To solve this projectile motion problem, we need to determine the horizontal distance (range) of the golf ball.

### Answer
The horizontal distance travelled by the ball is **79.5 m**.

### Explanation
To find the horizontal range ($R$) of a projectile launched from level ground, we use the formula:
$$R = \frac{v^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$

**1. Identify the given variables:**
*   Initial velocity ($v$) = $30\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Launch angle ($\theta$) = $30^\circ$
*   Acceleration due to gravity ($g$) $\approx 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$

**2. Set up the calculation:**
Substitute the values into the range formula:
$$R = \frac{30^2 \times \sin(2 \times 30^\circ)}{9.8}$$
$$R = \frac{900 \times \sin(60^\circ)}{9.8}$$

**3. Solve:**
Since $\sin(60^\circ) \approx 0.866$:
$$R = \frac{900 \times 0.866}{9.8}$$
$$R = \frac{779.4}{9.8}$$
$$R \approx 79.53\text{ m}$$

Rounding to appropriate significant figures based on the input (usually 2 or 3), the horizontal distance is approximately **79.5 m**.

***

*Note: If you use $g = 9.81\text{ m s}^{-2}$, the answer is approximately $79.45\text{ m}$. In HSC physics exams, $9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$ is the standard convention unless otherwise specified.*
