### Front
What is the relationship between the normal force ($F_N$) and gravity ($mg$) for a trolley at the bottom of a vertical circular dip?

### Back
- Key idea: The normal force must provide both the weight of the object and the net centripetal force required for circular motion.
- Formula / law: $F_N = F_c + mg$ (or $F_N = \frac{mv^2}{r} + mg$)
- When to use it: Solving vertical circular motion problems where the object is at the lowest point of its path.
- Common trap: Assuming $F_N = mg$. At the bottom of a dip, the object feels "heavier" because the normal force must overcome gravity *and* provide the centripetal acceleration.
