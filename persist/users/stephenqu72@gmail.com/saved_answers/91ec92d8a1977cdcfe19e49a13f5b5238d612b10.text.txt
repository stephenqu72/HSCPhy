To answer a 9-mark HSC physics question on the history of the atom, you must provide a structured, detailed discussion. You need to demonstrate how scientific principles—such as electromagnetism, conservation laws, or wave mechanics—enabled specific experimental discoveries.

### Suggested Response

**1. J.J. Thomson’s Cathode Ray Tube Experiment**
*   **Principle:** The interaction between moving charges and electromagnetic fields (Lorentz force).
*   **Discussion:** Thomson used the known principle that cathode rays could be deflected by electric and magnetic fields. By applying balanced perpendicular electric ($F_e = qE$) and magnetic ($F_m = qvB$) fields, he determined the velocity of the rays. By observing the deflection, he calculated the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$). This demonstrated that cathode rays were composed of subatomic, negatively charged particles (electrons) that were much smaller than a hydrogen atom, thus proving the atom was divisible.

**2. Rutherford’s Gold Foil Experiment**
*   **Principle:** Conservation of momentum and Coulombic repulsion.
*   **Discussion:** Rutherford built upon the principle of electrostatic repulsion between like charges. By firing alpha particles (positively charged, massive nuclei) at a thin gold foil, he expected them to pass through based on the "plum pudding" model. The unexpected wide-angle scattering of particles occurred because of the strong repulsive force between the alpha particle and the dense, concentrated positive nucleus. This validated the concept of a small, massive, positive central core, contradicting the uniform distribution of charge assumed previously.

**3. Chadwick’s Discovery of the Neutron**
*   **Principle:** Conservation of momentum and energy in particle collisions.
*   **Discussion:** Earlier experiments (like those by the Joliot-Curies) produced a mysterious, highly penetrating radiation when Beryllium was bombarded with alpha particles. Chadwick applied the laws of conservation of energy and momentum to the collision products. He realized that if the radiation consisted of gamma rays, the observed kinetic energy of ejected protons from a paraffin wax target would be physically impossible. By applying these conservation laws to the collision, he calculated that the neutral particle must have a mass nearly equal to that of a proton, confirming the existence of the neutron.

***

### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Structure:** Since it is a 9-mark "discuss" question, allocate 3 marks per experiment. Structure your answer using headings for clarity.
2.  **Focus on the "How":** Don’t just describe the experiment. Explicitly state the physical principle (e.g., "Conservation of momentum," "Electromagnetic deflection") and explain how that principle acted as the tool or framework that allowed the scientist to interpret the data.
3.  **Synthesis:** Ensure you connect the principle to the *structural realization* of the atom (i.e., identifying the electron, the nucleus, or the neutron).
