### The Answer
A well-labelled diagram should include:
1.  **A light source:** Representing unpolarised light.
2.  **First Polariser (The Polariser):** Showing unpolarised light entering and linearly polarised light exiting.
3.  **Second Polariser (The Analyser):** Showing the rotation relative to the first polariser.
4.  **A detector/screen:** Where the intensity of light is observed as the second polariser is rotated.

**Diagram Components:**
*   **Source:** A light bulb emitting light waves with oscillations in all directions ($360^\circ$ around the axis of propagation).
*   **Polariser 1:** A filter with vertical transmission axis lines. The light emerging has oscillations in only one plane.
*   **Polariser 2 (Rotatable):** A second filter. When the axis is parallel to the first, intensity is maximum. When rotated $90^\circ$ (crossed), intensity is zero (extinction).
