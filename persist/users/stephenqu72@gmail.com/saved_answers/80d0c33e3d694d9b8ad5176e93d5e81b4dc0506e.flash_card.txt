### Front
Why is the Michelson-Morley experiment considered "valid" despite producing a "null result" regarding the luminiferous aether?

### Back
- **Key idea:** A result that disproves a hypothesis does not make an experiment invalid; the experiment was highly precise and successfully tested the existence of the aether.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When evaluating scientific experimental design or the shift toward the Special Theory of Relativity.
- **Common trap:** Confusing a "null result" (failure to detect an effect) with an "invalid experiment" (flawed methodology).
