### Front
What happens to the atomic number ($Z$) and mass number ($A$) of a nucleus during $\beta^-$ (beta-minus) decay?

### Back
- **Key idea:** A neutron converts into a proton, an electron ($\beta^-$), and an electron antineutrino.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $Z \rightarrow Z + 1$; $A$ remains constant.
- **When to use it:** When a neutron-rich nucleus undergoes radioactive decay to increase stability.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that the mass number $A$ stays the same; students often incorrectly adjust $A$ because a particle is emitted.
