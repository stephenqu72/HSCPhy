### Front
Explain the origin and physical significance of the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation in the context of the Big Bang theory.

### Back
- **Key idea:** The CMB is the remnant "afterglow" of the Big Bang, representing the moment photons decoupled from matter during recombination ($T \approx 3000\text{ K}$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When asked for evidence supporting the Big Bang theory or explaining the evolution of the early universe.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to mention that the radiation has been **redshifted** into the microwave spectrum due to the expansion of the universe.
