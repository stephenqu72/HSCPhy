### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to identify the core principle defined by **Lenz's Law**, which is a fundamental concept in electromagnetism within the HSC Physics syllabus.

*   **Lenz's Law** states that the direction of an induced current is such that it creates a magnetic field that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it. This is essentially a manifestation of the **Law of Conservation of Energy**. If the induced current did not oppose the change, it would reinforce it, leading to an infinite loop of energy creation, which is physically impossible.

*   **(B)** refers to properties related to **self-induction** or electromagnetic principles, but it is not the definition of Lenz's Law.
*   **(D)** describes **Snell's Law**, which relates the angles of incidence and refraction to the refractive indices of the media through which a wave is passing.

When approaching questions about laws and definitions in Physics, ensure you distinguish between the "what" (definition) and the "how" (mathematical formula, e.g., Faraday's Law, which is $\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi_B}{\Delta t}$).
