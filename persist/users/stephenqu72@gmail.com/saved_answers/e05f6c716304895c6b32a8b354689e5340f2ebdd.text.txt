To solve this problem, we use the formula for Young's double-slit interference pattern, which relates the wavelength of light, the slit separation, the distance to the screen, and the fringe separation.

### Given Data:
*   Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $540\text{ nm} = 540 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$
*   Slit separation ($d_{slits}$) = $0.15\text{ mm} = 0.15 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$
*   Fringe separation ($\Delta x$) = $1.5\text{ cm} = 1.5 \times 10^{-2}\text{ m}$ (The distance between the central bright band and the 1st bright band is the fringe separation).

### Formula:
The distance between adjacent bright fringes is given by:
$$\Delta x = \frac{\lambda d}{d_{slits}}$$

Where:
*   $\Delta x$ is the fringe separation
*   $\lambda$ is the wavelength
*   $d$ is the distance from the slits to the screen
*   $d_{slits}$ is the distance between the slits

### Calculation:
We need to rearrange the formula to solve for $d$:
$$d = \frac{\Delta x \cdot d_{slits}}{\lambda}$$

Substitute the values:
$$d = \frac{(1.5 \times 10^{-2}\text{ m}) \times (0.15 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m})}{540 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}}$$
$$d = \frac{0.225 \times 10^{-5}}{540 \times 10^{-9}}$$
$$d = \frac{2.25 \times 10^{-6}}{5.4 \times 10^{-7}}$$
$$d = \frac{22.5 \times 10^{-7}}{5.4 \times 10^{-7}}$$
$$d \approx 4.166... \text{ m}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $d = 4.2\text{ m}$.

Comparing this to the provided options:
(A) 0.04 m
(B) 1.2 m
(C) 4.2 m
(D) 417 m

The correct answer is **(C)**.
