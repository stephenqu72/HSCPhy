To solve this problem, we use the formula for Young’s Double Slit interference pattern, which describes the fringe spacing (the distance between consecutive bright fringes):

$$\Delta y = \frac{\lambda d}{w}$$

Where:
*   $\Delta y$ is the distance between consecutive bright fringes (given as $1.5 \text{ cm} = 0.015 \text{ m}$)
*   $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the light ($540 \text{ nm} = 540 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$)
*   $w$ is the distance between the slits ($0.15 \text{ mm} = 0.15 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m} = 1.5 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}$)
*   $d$ is the distance between the slits and the screen (the value we need to find)

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $d$:**
    $$d = \frac{\Delta y \cdot w}{\lambda}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values into the equation:**
    $$d = \frac{0.015 \text{ m} \cdot 1.5 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}}{540 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}}$$

3.  **Perform the multiplication in the numerator:**
    $$0.015 \cdot 1.5 \times 10^{-4} = 2.25 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}^2$$

4.  **Perform the division:**
    $$d = \frac{2.25 \times 10^{-6}}{540 \times 10^{-9}}$$
    $$d = \frac{2.25}{540} \times 10^3$$
    $$d = 0.004166... \times 10^3$$
    $$d \approx 4.166... \text{ m}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $d = 4.2 \text{ m}$.
