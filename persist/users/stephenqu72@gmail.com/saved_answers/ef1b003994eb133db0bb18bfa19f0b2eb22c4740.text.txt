To determine the SI unit for the constant $k$, we use the principle of **dimensional consistency**. In any physically valid equation, the units on the left-hand side must equal the units on the right-hand side.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1. **Identify the units of the variables:**
   - $u$ is speed, so its SI unit is $m/s$ or $m \cdot s^{-1}$.
   - $l$ is length, so its SI unit is $m$.
   - $r$ is radius (a length), so its SI unit is $m$. The term $r^2$ will have the unit $m^2$.
   - $3$ and $2$ are dimensionless constants and do not affect the units.

2. **Rearrange the given equation to isolate $k$:**
   Given the equation:
   $$u = \frac{3kl}{2r^2}$$
   Multiply both sides by $2r^2$ and divide by $3l$:
   $$k = \frac{u \cdot 2r^2}{3l}$$

3. **Substitute the units into the expression for $k$:**
   Ignoring the dimensionless constants ($2$ and $3$), we look at the units:
   $$\text{Units of } k = \frac{\text{Units of } u \cdot \text{Units of } r^2}{\text{Units of } l}$$
   $$\text{Units of } k = \frac{(m \cdot s^{-1}) \cdot (m^2)}{m}$$

4. **Simplify the units:**
   $$\text{Units of } k = \frac{m^3 \cdot s^{-1}}{m}$$
   $$\text{Units of } k = m^2 \cdot s^{-1}$$
