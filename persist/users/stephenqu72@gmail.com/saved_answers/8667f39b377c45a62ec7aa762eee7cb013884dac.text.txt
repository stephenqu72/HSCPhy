### Answer: The Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) Radiation

**Selection:** The Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation.

**Outline:** The CMB is faint, uniform electromagnetic radiation filling the entire universe, with a spectrum corresponding to a blackbody temperature of approximately $2.7\text{ K}$. It was predicted as a remnant of the early, hot, dense universe. As the universe expanded, the high-energy radiation from the time of recombination (when electrons and nuclei first formed neutral atoms) was stretched (redshifted) by the expansion of space into the microwave region of the spectrum.

**Assessment:** The CMB is considered one of the strongest pieces of evidence for the Big Bang Theory because it is consistent with the prediction of a cooling, expanding universe. Its high degree of uniformity in all directions (isotropy) provides a "snapshot" of the early universe, effectively debunking steady-state models that could not explain the presence or the specific temperature characteristics of this residual heat.
