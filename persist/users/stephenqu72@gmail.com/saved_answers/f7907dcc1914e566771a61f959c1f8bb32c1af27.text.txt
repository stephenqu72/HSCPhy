### Answer
This apparatus is **J.J. Thomson’s cathode ray tube (CRT) experiment**. The experiment involves accelerating a beam of electrons using electric fields and then subjecting them to both electric and magnetic fields. By balancing the electric force ($F_E = qE$) and the magnetic force ($F_B = qvB$) so that the beam passes through undeflected, Thomson was able to determine the velocity of the electrons ($v = \frac{E}{B}$). By then turning off the electric field and measuring the circular deflection caused by the magnetic field alone, he determined the **charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$)** of the electron.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to identify the key components of the apparatus and link them to the historical physics discovery:

1.  **Identify the experiment:** Recognizing the cathode ray tube with both electric plates and electromagnets identifies this as the experimental setup Thomson used in 1897.
2.  **Outline the physics:**
    *   **Acceleration:** Electrons are emitted from the negative electrode (cathode) and accelerated by the positive electrodes (anode).
    *   **Deflection:** The beam passes through a region where an electric field and a magnetic field are applied. 
    *   **The "Null" Method:** Thomson adjusted the fields until the forces cancelled out ($qE = qvB$), allowing him to calculate the velocity of the electrons.
    *   **Measurement:** Using the deflection produced by the magnetic field ($F_B = qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$), he derived the ratio of the charge to the mass.
3.  **State the significance:** The most critical outcome of this experiment was the discovery that the $q/m$ ratio was constant regardless of the cathode material, providing evidence that electrons are universal subatomic particles with a very small mass compared to a hydrogen atom.
