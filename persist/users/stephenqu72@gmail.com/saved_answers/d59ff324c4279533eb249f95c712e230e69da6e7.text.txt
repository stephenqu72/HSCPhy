To determine the starting position of the generator coil, we must look at the standard relationship between magnetic flux and induced EMF in an AC generator.

### The Physics Principles
The magnetic flux $\Phi$ through a coil is given by:
$$\Phi = BA \cos(\theta)$$
where $\theta$ is the angle between the normal to the coil and the magnetic field lines. The induced EMF $\varepsilon$ is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux:
$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$$

1.  **When the coil is perpendicular to the field lines:** The normal to the coil is parallel to the field ($\theta = 0^\circ$). The flux is at its **maximum**, and the rate of change of flux is zero. Thus, the **EMF is zero**.
2.  **When the coil is parallel to the field lines:** The normal to the coil is perpendicular to the field ($\theta = 90^\circ$). The flux is **zero**, but the rate of change of flux is at its maximum. Thus, the **EMF is at its maximum**.

### Analyzing the Graph (Standard AC Generator)

### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Identify the y-axis:** Check if the graph represents Magnetic Flux ($\Phi$) or Induced EMF ($\varepsilon$).
2.  **Locate $t=0$:** Determine the value of the variable at the start.
3.  **Relate to Orientation:** 
    *   If $t=0$ is maximum EMF, the coil is parallel to the field (flat/horizontal).
    *   If $t=0$ is zero EMF, the coil is perpendicular to the field (vertical).
4.  **Visualize the normal:** Always remember that $\theta$ in the flux formula is the angle relative to the *normal* of the coil area, not the plane of the coil itself.
