### Explanation
To solve this, let's evaluate each option based on the known constraints of the Bohr model:

*   **(A) It cannot explain the observed line spectra of atoms with more than one electron:** This is **correct**. Bohr’s model relied on the electrostatic attraction between a single nucleus and a single electron. When more electrons are added, electron-electron repulsions make the system mathematically complex, and the simple energy level transitions Bohr predicted fail to accurately match experimental observations for multi-electron atoms.
*   **(B) It cannot account for the quantization of angular momentum in atoms:** This is **incorrect**. In fact, the *postulate* that angular momentum is quantized ($L = mvr = n\hbar$) is the very foundation of the Bohr model.
*   **(C) It cannot predict the correct value of the ground state energy for hydrogen:** This is **incorrect**. One of the major successes of the Bohr model was its ability to accurately predict the Rydberg constant and the ground state energy of the hydrogen atom ($-13.6 \text{ eV}$).
*   **(D) It does not consider the wave-particle duality of electrons:** While this is technically a true statement (Bohr treated electrons as classical particles in circular orbits), in the context of HSC Physics, **(A)** is the "classic" limitation that defines the model's inability to expand beyond the simplest atomic system (hydrogen). Furthermore, de Broglie's hypothesis later provided the theoretical justification for Bohr's quantization, effectively correcting this omission. Therefore, (A) is the primary and most significant limitation tested in this syllabus.

### How to approach these questions:
1.  **Recall the "Successes":** Remember that Bohr was successful for hydrogen (single electron).
2.  **Identify the "Failure":** Immediately look for options that mention multi-electron atoms or the Zeeman effect (splitting of spectral lines in magnetic fields), as these are the primary failures of the Bohr model.
3.  **Eliminate the "Postulates":** Recognize that the quantization of angular momentum was a *foundational assumption* of the model, not a limitation.
