### Answer
The correct answer is **C. $\frac{\lambda}{2}$**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must understand the definition of wavelength ($\lambda$) and the spatial arrangement of a transverse wave:

1.  **Define Wavelength:** The wavelength $\lambda$ is defined as the distance between two consecutive points that are in phase, such as the distance between two consecutive crests or two consecutive troughs.
2.  **Analyze the wave structure:** A transverse wave consists of a series of crests (maximum positive displacement) and troughs (maximum negative displacement). 
3.  **Identify the distance between a crest and a trough:**
    *   The distance from a crest to the equilibrium position (zero displacement) is $\frac{1}{4}$ of a wavelength ($\frac{\lambda}{4}$).
    *   The distance from the equilibrium position to the next trough is another $\frac{1}{4}$ of a wavelength ($\frac{\lambda}{4}$).
    *   Therefore, the total horizontal distance (measured in the direction of energy transfer) between a crest and its neighbouring trough is:
        $$\text{Distance} = \frac{\lambda}{4} + \frac{\lambda}{4} = \frac{2\lambda}{4} = \frac{\lambda}{2}$$

This confirms that the distance between a crest and the adjacent trough is exactly half of the wavelength.
