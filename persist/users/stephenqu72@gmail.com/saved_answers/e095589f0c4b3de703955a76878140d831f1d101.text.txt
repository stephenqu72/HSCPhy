To solve this problem, we must apply the concept of **time dilation** from Einstein's Special Theory of Relativity.

### Step-by-Step Solution

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   The "proper time" ($t_0$) is the lifetime of the muon measured in its own rest frame: $t_0 = 2.02 \times 10^{-6} \text{ s}$.
    *   The "dilated time" ($t$) is the time measured by an observer on Earth for the muon to travel from the atmosphere to the surface: $t = 1.64 \times 10^{-5} \text{ s}$.
    *   The formula for time dilation is: 
        $$t = \frac{t_0}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$

2.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $v$:**
    Divide by $t_0$:
    $$\frac{t}{t_0} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$
    Square both sides:
    $$\frac{t^2}{t_0^2} = \frac{1}{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
    Invert both sides:
    $$\frac{t_0^2}{t^2} = 1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$$
    Rearrange to isolate $\frac{v^2}{c^2}$:
    $$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 1 - \frac{t_0^2}{t^2}$$
    $$v = c \sqrt{1 - \frac{t_0^2}{t^2}}$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$\frac{t_0}{t} = \frac{2.02 \times 10^{-6}}{1.64 \times 10^{-5}} \approx 0.12317$$
    Calculate the square of this ratio:
    $$(0.12317)^2 \approx 0.01517$$
    Substitute back into the equation:
    $$v = c \sqrt{1 - 0.01517} = c \sqrt{0.98483} \approx 0.99238c$$
