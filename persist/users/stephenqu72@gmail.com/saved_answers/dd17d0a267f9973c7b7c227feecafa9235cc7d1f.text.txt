To find the work done on the proton by the electric field, we must use the relationship between work, charge, and electric potential difference.

### Explanation and Solution

**1. Understand the Physics:**
The work done ($W$) by an electric field on a charged particle as it moves between two points is given by the formula:
$$W = q \Delta V$$
where:
*   $q$ is the charge of the particle (for a proton, $q = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$).
*   $\Delta V$ is the potential difference between the entry point and the exit point.

**2. Determine the Electric Field Strength ($E$):**
The potential difference ($V_{total}$) across the plates is $25 \text{ V}$ over a total distance ($d_{total}$) of $2.3 \times 10^{-2} \text{ m}$. The electric field is uniform:
$$E = \frac{V_{total}}{d_{total}} = \frac{25}{2.3 \times 10^{-2}} \approx 1087 \text{ V/m}$$

**3. Determine the Potential Difference ($\Delta V$) experienced by the proton:**
The vertical displacement ($\Delta y$) of the proton is the difference between its entry position and its exit position.
*   Entry distance from positive plate: $6.5 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$
*   Exit distance from positive plate: $d_{total} - (\text{distance from negative plate}) = 2.3 \times 10^{-2} - 4.1 \times 10^{-3} = 1.89 \times 10^{-2} \text{ m}$

The vertical distance travelled by the proton is:
$$\Delta y = (1.89 \times 10^{-2}) - (6.5 \times 10^{-3}) = 1.24 \times 10^{-2} \text{ m}$$

The potential difference traversed is $\Delta V = E \times \Delta y$:
$$\Delta V = \left( \frac{25}{2.3 \times 10^{-2}} \right) \times (1.24 \times 10^{-2}) \approx 13.478 \text{ V}$$

**4. Calculate Work Done ($W$):**
$$W = (1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \times (13.478 \text{ V}) \approx 2.16 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $2.2 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J}$.
