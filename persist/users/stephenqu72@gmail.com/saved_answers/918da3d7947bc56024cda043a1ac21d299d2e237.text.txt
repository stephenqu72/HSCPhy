### Part (a): Features and Advantages of Geostationary Orbits

**Answer:**
A geostationary orbit is located directly above the Earth's equator and has an orbital period of exactly 24 hours (matching the Earth's rotation). Consequently, the satellite remains in a fixed position relative to a point on the Earth's surface. 

The main advantage for communications is that ground-based antennas (satellite dishes) do not need to track the satellite; they can remain pointed in a fixed direction. This significantly reduces the cost and complexity of the ground equipment and ensures a continuous, uninterrupted signal link.

**How to solve it:**
*   **Key concepts:** You must identify the "fixed position relative to the ground" and "24-hour period." 
*   **Marking:** To get 2 marks, you need to state the orbital characteristics (equatorial, 24-hour period) and connect that clearly to the practical benefit (no need for tracking antennas).

### Part (b): Calculation of Total Energy

**Answer:**
To calculate the total energy ($E_{total}$) of a satellite in orbit, we use the sum of kinetic energy ($K$) and gravitational potential energy ($U$):
$$E_{total} = K + U = \frac{GMm}{2r} - \frac{GMm}{r} = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$$

**Step 1: Identify constants and variables**
*   $m = 750\text{ kg}$
*   $h = 400\text{ km} = 4.0 \times 10^5\text{ m}$
*   $R_E = 6.37 \times 10^6\text{ m}$ (Radius of Earth)
*   $M = 5.97 \times 10^{24}\text{ kg}$ (Mass of Earth)
*   $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2\text{ kg}^{-2}$
*   $r = R_E + h = 6.77 \times 10^6\text{ m}$

**Step 2: Calculate the value**
$$E_{total} = -\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24}) \times 750}{2 \times (6.77 \times 10^6)}$$
$$E_{total} \approx -2.20 \times 10^{10}\text{ J}$$

**How to solve it:**
1.  **Define total energy:** Remember that the total energy of an orbiting body is negative, representing the energy required to remove the object to infinity.
2.  **Calculate $r$:** Always ensure you add the altitude to the Earth's radius. A common error is using only the altitude.
3.  **Use the correct formula:** The formula $E = -GMm / 2r$ is derived from circular orbit conditions ($v = \sqrt{GM/r}$).
