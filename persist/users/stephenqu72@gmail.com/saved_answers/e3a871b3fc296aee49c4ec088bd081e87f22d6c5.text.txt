### Explanation
To solve this question, we need to understand the nature of the light produced by these two sources:

1.  **Incandescent Light:** This is produced by thermal radiation (black-body radiation). As an object is heated, it emits light across a broad range of frequencies. This results in a **continuous spectrum**, meaning it contains all colors of the visible spectrum (and beyond into the infrared). According to Wien's Law, the peak wavelength $\lambda_{max}$ depends on the temperature, but it will always span a wide band of frequencies.

2.  **Laser Light:** The term LASER stands for "Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation." This process involves electrons transitioning between specific energy levels in an atom, which results in the emission of photons with a very specific energy $E$. Since $E = hf$ (where $h$ is Planck's constant and $f$ is frequency), the emitted light is **monochromatic**, meaning it has a very narrow frequency range. 

**Evaluating the options:**
*   **A is correct:** It accurately describes incandescent light as having a continuous spectrum and laser light as being limited to a narrow frequency range (monochromatic).
*   **B is incorrect:** Laser light is never a continuous spectrum, and incandescent light is not inherently "concentrated in the yellow region" (this depends on the temperature of the filament).
*   **C is incorrect:** It swaps the characteristics of the two sources.
*   **D is incorrect:** Incandescent light has a very wide spectrum, not a narrow one.
