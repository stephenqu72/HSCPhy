### Answer
The correct answer is **C. Neutrons**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must look at the physics concepts being modeled:

1.  **The Model:** This is a classic classroom demonstration used to model a **nuclear chain reaction**.
2.  **The Components:**
    *   **The Mousetraps:** These represent unstable, fissile nuclei (like Uranium-235). When "triggered," they release energy and additional particles.
    *   **The Balls:** These represent **neutrons**. In nuclear fission, a neutron strikes a nucleus, causing it to split (fission). This fission event releases further neutrons, which then strike other nearby nuclei, causing them to split as well. 
3.  **The Process:** 
    *   The "ball dropped onto the mousetraps" represents the *initial neutron* that induces the first fission.
    *   The fact that "all the mousetraps released their balls" represents the *chain reaction* process, where the neutrons released from one fission event trigger subsequent fission events in neighboring nuclei.

Since it is the **neutrons** that act as the agents to trigger fission and are subsequently released during the fission of heavy nuclei to sustain a chain reaction, the balls represent neutrons.
