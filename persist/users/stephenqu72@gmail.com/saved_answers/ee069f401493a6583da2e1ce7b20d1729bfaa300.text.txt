To solve this problem, we need to determine the fraction of the radioactive isotope that remains after 16 hours and then calculate the percentage that has decayed.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Standardize units:**
The time taken is $t = 16\text{ hours}$.
The half-life is $T_{1/2} = 2.2\text{ days}$.
Convert the half-life into hours:
$T_{1/2} = 2.2 \times 24 = 52.8\text{ hours}$.

**2. Calculate the amount remaining:**
We use the radioactive decay formula:
$$N(t) = N_0 \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{\frac{t}{T_{1/2}}}$$
Where $N(t)/N_0$ represents the fraction remaining.
$$\frac{N(t)}{N_0} = \left( 0.5 \right)^{\frac{16}{52.8}}$$
$$\frac{N(t)}{N_0} = \left( 0.5 \right)^{0.30303...} \approx 0.8105$$

This means approximately $81.05\%$ of the isotope remains.

**3. Calculate the percentage that has decayed:**
The percentage that has decayed is the initial amount ($100\%$) minus the amount remaining:
$$\text{Decayed \%} = 100\% - 81.05\% = 18.95\%$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number, we get $19\%$.

Comparing this result to the given options:
(A) $7\%$
(B) $13\%$
(C) $15\%$
(D) $19\%$

The correct answer is **(D)**.
