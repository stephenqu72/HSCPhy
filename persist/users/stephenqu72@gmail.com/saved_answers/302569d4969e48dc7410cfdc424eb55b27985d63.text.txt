The correct answer is **(D)**.

### Explanation

To understand why physicists collide particles at such high speeds, we must refer to Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle, $E = mc^2$. 

1.  **High Kinetic Energy:** When protons are accelerated to $0.9999c$, they possess an immense amount of kinetic energy. Due to relativistic effects, their total energy is significantly higher than their rest mass energy.
2.  **Matter-Energy Conversion:** In high-energy particle physics (such as at the Large Hadron Collider), the goal of these collisions is not simply to "bounce" particles off each other or to measure basic properties like repulsion. Instead, the total kinetic energy from the two colliding particles is concentrated into an extremely small volume during the impact. 
3.  **Formation of New Particles:** According to the laws of relativity, energy can be converted into mass. If the collision provides sufficient energy ($E_{total} > \sum m_{rest}c^2$), that energy can spontaneously transform into new, often short-lived subatomic particles (such as the Higgs boson, top quarks, or various mesons). This process allows scientists to study the fundamental constituents of matter and the forces that govern the universe.
