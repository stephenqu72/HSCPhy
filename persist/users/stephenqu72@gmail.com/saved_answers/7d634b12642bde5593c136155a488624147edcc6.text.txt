To solve this problem, we follow the principles of mass-energy equivalence.

### (a) Calculate the mass defect in MeV

**Step 1: Calculate the mass of the reactants.**
Mass of reactants $= \text{Mass}(^{12}\text{C}) + \text{Mass}(^{1}\text{H})$
Mass of reactants $= 12.000 + 1.0078 = 13.0078 \, \text{amu}$

**Step 2: Determine the mass defect ($\Delta m$).**
The mass defect is the difference between the mass of the reactants and the product ($^{13}\text{N}$). Note: The gamma photon ($\gamma$) has zero rest mass.
$\Delta m = (\text{Mass of reactants}) - (\text{Mass of products})$
$\Delta m = 13.0078 - 13.057 = -0.0492 \, \text{amu}$

*Note: In physics problems regarding nuclear fusion, if the result is negative, it indicates mass has been converted into energy. We use the magnitude of this difference for the energy calculation.*

**Step 3: Convert the mass defect into MeV.**
Using the conversion factor $1 \, \text{amu} \approx 931.5 \, \text{MeV}/c^2$:
Energy released $= 0.0492 \, \text{amu} \times 931.5 \, \text{MeV/amu}$
Energy released $\approx 45.83 \, \text{MeV}$

---

### (b) Calculate the energy released in Joules

To convert the energy from MeV to Joules, we use the conversion $1 \, \text{eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}$. Since $1 \, \text{MeV} = 10^6 \, \text{eV}$:

$E = 45.83 \times 10^6 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}$
$E \approx 7.34 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{J}$

---

### Summary of Steps
1. **Mass Defect:** Sum the masses of the inputs, subtract the mass of the outputs.
2. **Energy (MeV):** Multiply the mass difference (in amu) by the constant $931.5 \, \text{MeV/amu}$.
3. **Energy (Joules):** Multiply the energy in MeV by $10^6$ to get eV, then multiply by the elementary charge $1.602 \times 10^{-19}$ to convert to Joules.
