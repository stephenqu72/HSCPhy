To determine the correct path for the proton, we need to use the Right-Hand Rule to find the direction of the force and the formula for the radius of a charged particle's path in a magnetic field.

### 1. Determining the Path Direction
*   **The Right-Hand Rule:** Point your fingers in the direction of the velocity vector (to the right). Curl your fingers into the page (direction of the magnetic field $\vec{B}$, represented by the crosses). Your thumb points **upward**.
*   Since a proton is a positively charged particle, the force acts in the direction of your thumb. Therefore, the proton will deflect **upwards**, towards paths $P_1$ or $P_3$.

### 2. Calculating the Radius
When a charged particle moves perpendicular to a magnetic field, the magnetic force provides the centripetal force:
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Rearranging to solve for the radius $r$:
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

**Given constants for a proton:**
*   Mass $m \approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$
*   Charge $q \approx 1.60 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
*   Velocity $v = 4.0 \times 10^6\text{ m/s}$
*   Magnetic field $B = 0.55\text{ T}$

**Calculation:**
$$r = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27}) \times (4.0 \times 10^6)}{(1.60 \times 10^{-19}) \times (0.55)}$$
$$r = \frac{6.68 \times 10^{-21}}{8.8 \times 10^{-20}}$$
$$r \approx 0.0759 \text{ m} \approx 0.076 \text{ m}$$

Comparing this value to the options provided, $0.074\text{ m}$ is the correct order of magnitude, which corresponds to path $P_3$.

**Answer:**
The correct option is **C**.
