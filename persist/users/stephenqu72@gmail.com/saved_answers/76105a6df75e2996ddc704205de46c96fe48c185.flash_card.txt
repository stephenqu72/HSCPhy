### Front
What is the net force acting on a projectile at its maximum height?

### Back
- Key idea: The only force acting on a projectile (ignoring air resistance) is gravity, which remains constant throughout the entire flight.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{net} = mg$ ($g \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$)
- When to use it: Analyzing forces or acceleration in projectile motion and free fall.
- Common trap: Confusing zero vertical velocity ($v_y = 0$) at the peak with zero acceleration or zero net force.
