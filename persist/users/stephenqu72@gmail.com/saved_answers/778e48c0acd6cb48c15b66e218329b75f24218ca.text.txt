To solve this problem, we need to relate the variables shown in the graph to the physical principles of a uniform electric field.

### Explanation
1.  **Identify the relevant formula:** For a uniform electric field between two parallel plates, the relationship between voltage ($V$), electric field strength ($E$), and the distance between the plates ($d$) is given by:
    $$V = E \times d$$

2.  **Analyze the graph:** This formula represents a linear equation in the form of $y = mx + c$, where:
    *   $y = V$ (Voltage)
    *   $x = E$ (Electric Field)
    *   $m = d$ (The slope of the line is the distance between the plates)

3.  **Calculate the slope:** We can pick two points on the line to calculate the gradient ($m = \frac{\Delta V}{\Delta E}$):
    *   Point 1: $(1, 0.05)$
    *   Point 2: $(4, 0.20)$
    
    $$d = \frac{0.20 - 0.05}{4 - 1}$$
    $$d = \frac{0.15}{3}$$
    $$d = 0.05 \, \text{m}$$

4.  **Convert to the answer choices:** The calculated distance is $0.05 \, \text{m}$. Since $1 \, \text{m} = 100 \, \text{cm}$, then $0.05 \, \text{m} = 5.0 \, \text{cm}$.

Comparing this to the options provided, $5.0 \, \text{cm}$ matches option **D**.
