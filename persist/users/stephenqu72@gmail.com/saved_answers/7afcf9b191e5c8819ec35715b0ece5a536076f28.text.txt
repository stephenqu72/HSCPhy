1.  **Weight ($W = mg$):** This acts vertically downwards due to gravity.

3.  **Frictional Force ($f$):** Since the floor has fallen away and the rider is not sliding down, there must be an upward frictional force exerted by the wall on the rider. This force balances the weight of the rider ($f = mg$).

*   There is an upward force (Friction).
*   There is a horizontal force pointing towards the center (Normal force).

### How to solve it:
*   **Step 1: Identify the vertical equilibrium.** The rider is not moving vertically; therefore, the upward static friction must exactly equal the downward gravitational force. This requires two vertical arrows of equal magnitude in opposite directions.
*   **Step 2: Identify the centripetal requirement.** For the rider to move in a circle, there must be a net force directed toward the center of the rotation. The wall provides this via the Normal force ($N$), acting horizontally.
