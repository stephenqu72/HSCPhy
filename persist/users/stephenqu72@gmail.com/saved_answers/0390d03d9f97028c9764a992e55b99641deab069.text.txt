To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of circular motion and Newton’s Second Law.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(B)**.

### Explanation
1.  **Identify the Net Force:** For an object moving in a horizontal circle, the net force acting on the object is the **centripetal force**. By definition, the centripetal force must always point toward the center of the circular path. Since the vehicle is moving around a horizontal circular track, the center of this circle lies horizontally to the left of the vehicle. Therefore, the net force must point horizontally toward the left.
2.  **Analyze the Physics:**
    *   The forces acting on the vehicle are the gravitational force (pointing straight down) and the normal force (pointing perpendicular to the track surface).
    *   The vector sum of the gravitational force and the normal force results in a horizontal vector directed toward the center of the turn.
    *   According to Newton's Second Law, $F_{net} = ma$. Since the acceleration is centripetal (pointing horizontally toward the center), the net force $F_{net}$ must also be directed horizontally toward the center.
3.  **Evaluate the options:**
    *   **A** points perpendicular to the surface (this is the direction of the normal force, not the net force).
    *   **B** points horizontally toward the center of the circular path. This is the correct direction for centripetal force.
    *   **C** points down the slope.
    *   **D** points straight down (this is the direction of the gravitational force).

Since arrow **B** represents the direction of the net force (the horizontal centripetal force), option (B) is the correct choice.
