**a. Calculate the speed of the wave in the guitar string.**

The standing wave shown in Figure 12 represents the fundamental mode (the first harmonic), where the entire length of the string $L = 0.8\text{ m}$ corresponds to half a wavelength ($\lambda/2$).
Therefore, $\lambda = 2L = 2 \times 0.8\text{ m} = 1.6\text{ m}$.

Using the wave equation $v = f\lambda$:
$$v = 250\text{ Hz} \times 1.6\text{ m} = 400\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

**b. The frequency is tripled to 750 Hz. Draw the shape of the standing wave.**

Tripling the frequency ($3 \times 250\text{ Hz} = 750\text{ Hz}$) while keeping the speed of the wave constant means the wavelength must decrease by a factor of three. This creates the third harmonic ($n=3$). 
The string will now have 3 antinodes and 4 nodes (including the fixed ends). You should draw a shape with three distinct "loops" along the $0.8\text{ m}$ length.
