### Front
Compare the mass and charge of an alpha ($\alpha$) particle versus a beta ($\beta^-$) particle.

### Back
- Key idea: Alpha particles are heavy, doubly-charged helium nuclei; Beta particles are light, singly-charged high-speed electrons.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\alpha = {}^4_2\text{He}^{2+}$; $\beta^- = e^-$.
- When to use it: Identifying particles in radioactive decay or comparing deflection in magnetic/electric fields.
- Common trap: Confusing a positron ($\beta^+$) with a standard beta particle ($\beta^-$) or assuming beta particles have significant mass.
