To determine the correct answer, we must first define **uniform circular motion**. It is the motion of an object travelling at a **constant speed** in a circular path. For this to occur, there must be a constant net force (centripetal force) acting perpendicular to the object's velocity, pulling it towards the center of the circle.

### Analysis of Options:

*   **A. A ball thrown horizontally off a cliff:** This is projectile motion. The vertical velocity increases due to gravity, so the speed is not constant, and the path is parabolic, not circular.
*   **B. A proton moving parallel to a magnetic field:** The magnetic force on a charge is given by $F = qvB\sin(\theta)$. If the motion is parallel, $\theta = 0^\circ$ or $180^\circ$, so $\sin(\theta) = 0$. There is no force, and the proton continues in a straight line at constant velocity.
*   **C. A mass swinging horizontally on the end of a string:** As the mass swings in a horizontal circle, the tension in the string provides a constant centripetal force directed toward the center of the circular path. If we assume a frictionless environment (typical for these theoretical HSC physics questions), the speed remains constant. This satisfies the definition of uniform circular motion.
*   **D. An electron moving perpendicularly to an electric field:** An electric field exerts a constant force $F = qE$ on a charge. If the electron enters the field perpendicularly, it will follow a parabolic path (similar to projectile motion), not a circular one.

### Conclusion:
The correct answer is **C**.

### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Recall the definition:** Always check if the motion implies constant speed ($v = \text{constant}$) and a circular path.
2.  **Identify forces:** Ask yourself if there is a force acting constantly perpendicular to the direction of motion. 
3.  **Eliminate options:** Quickly disqualify motions involving gravity-induced acceleration (projectile motion) or straight-line motion (no force or force parallel to velocity).
