### Answer and Explanation

**a) Explain how this is possible with such different eccentricities. (2 marks)**

**Answer:**
Kepler’s Third Law states that the square of the orbital period $T$ is proportional to the cube of the semimajor axis $a$ ($T^2 \propto a^3$). The orbital period depends solely on the semimajor axis of the orbit and the mass of the central star, not on the eccentricity of the orbit. Since both planets orbit the same star and have the same semimajor axis, they must have the same orbital period, regardless of how elongated or circular their individual paths are.

**How to solve:**
To gain full marks, you must explicitly link the period to the semimajor axis. The key takeaway for HSC Physics is that eccentricity affects the *shape* and the *instantaneous velocity* of the planet at different points in its orbit (perihelion vs. aphelion), but it does not change the time taken to complete one full revolution if the average distance (semimajor axis) remains constant.

**b) Describe an investigation scientists can conduct to verify that Kepler’s Third Law is universal. (3 marks)**

**Answer:**
To verify the universality of Kepler's Third Law, scientists observe different systems orbiting the same central mass (e.g., the moons of Jupiter or the planets of our Solar System). 
1. **Method:** Measure the orbital period ($T$) and the average distance/semimajor axis ($r$) for various bodies orbiting the same central object.
2. **Analysis:** Plot a graph of $T^2$ against $r^3$. 
3. **Expected Results:** According to the law, the data should produce a straight line passing through the origin. The gradient of this line, which is equal to $\frac{4\pi^2}{GM}$, will be constant for all objects orbiting that same central mass, confirming that the relationship $T^2 \propto r^3$ holds true universally for those systems.

**How to solve:**
This is a standard "verify a law" question. For 3 marks, you need three components:
1. **The Procedure:** Measuring $T$ and $r$ for multiple objects.
2. **The Analysis:** Mentioning the graphical approach ($T^2$ vs $r^3$) which is the standard scientific way to verify a proportional relationship.
3. **The Result:** Explaining that a linear relationship confirms the proportionality.
