To find the force $F$ required to turn the bolt, we use the formula for torque:

$$\tau = r \cdot F \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

### Explanation:
1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   **Torque ($\tau$):** $200\text{ N m}$
    *   **Radius ($r$):** The length of the spanner is $25.0\text{ cm}$. We must convert this to SI units (meters): $25.0\text{ cm} = 0.25\text{ m}$.
    *   **Angle ($\theta$):** The angle between the force vector and the handle of the spanner is $30^\circ$.

2.  **Set up the equation:**
    Substitute the known values into the torque formula:
    $$200 = 0.25 \cdot F \cdot \sin(30^\circ)$$

3.  **Calculate:**
    *   Since $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$:
    $$200 = 0.25 \cdot F \cdot 0.5$$
    $$200 = 0.125 \cdot F$$
    
    *   Solve for $F$:
    $$F = \frac{200}{0.125}$$
    $$F = 1600\text{ N}$$

**Answer:** The force required is $1600\text{ N}$.
