To find the energy of a photon given its wavelength, we use the following relationship:

### 1. The Physics Formula
The energy $E$ of a photon is given by:
$$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$

Where:
*   $h$ is Planck's constant: $6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$
*   $c$ is the speed of light: $3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
*   $\lambda$ is the wavelength: $535 \text{ nm} = 535 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$

### 2. The Shortcut for HSC Physics
In HSC Physics, it is highly efficient to use the constant $hc = 1240 \text{ eV}\cdot\text{nm}$. This allows you to calculate the energy directly in electron volts (eV) without converting to Joules first:
$$E (\text{in eV}) = \frac{1240}{\lambda (\text{in nm})}$$

### 3. Calculation
Substitute the given wavelength ($\lambda = 535 \text{ nm}$) into the shortcut formula:
$$E = \frac{1240}{535}$$
$$E \approx 2.3177 \text{ eV}$$

Rounding to three significant figures, we get **$2.32 \text{ eV}$**.

Comparing this to the options provided:
*   A. $1.79 \text{ eV}$
*   B. $2.00 \text{ eV}$
*   C. $2.11 \text{ eV}$
*   D. $2.32 \text{ eV}$

The correct answer is **D**.
