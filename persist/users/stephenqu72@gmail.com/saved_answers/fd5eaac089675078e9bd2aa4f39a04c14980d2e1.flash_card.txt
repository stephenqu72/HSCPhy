### Front
How can you distinguish between the effect of random errors and systematic errors when repeating experimental measurements?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Random errors are unpredictable fluctuations (reduced by averaging), whereas systematic errors are consistent biases (not reduced by averaging).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** To evaluate the reliability and accuracy of experimental data during data analysis.
- **Common trap:** Assuming that taking multiple measurements will eliminate a systematic error; it only improves precision, not accuracy.
