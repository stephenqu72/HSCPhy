To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of Conservation of Mechanical Energy and the requirements for circular motion.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Condition for circular motion:** For the car to remain on the track at the top of the loop (where the loop has diameter $D$ and radius $R = D/2$), the gravitational force must be equal to or less than the required centripetal force. At the critical minimum velocity, the normal force $N$ becomes zero.
    The centripetal force equation at the top is:
    $$\frac{mv^2}{R} = mg + N$$
    Setting $N = 0$ for the minimum speed:
    $$\frac{mv^2}{R} = mg \implies v^2 = gR$$
    Since $R = D/2$, we have $v^2 = \frac{gD}{2}$.

2.  **Conservation of Energy:** The total mechanical energy at the starting height $h$ must equal the total mechanical energy at the top of the loop (height $2R = D$).
    Initial Energy: $E_i = mgh$
    Final Energy (at the top): $E_f = mgD + \frac{1}{2}mv^2$

    Setting $E_i = E_f$:
    $$mgh = mgD + \frac{1}{2}m\left(\frac{gD}{2}\right)$$
    $$gh = gD + \frac{gD}{4}$$
    $$h = D + \frac{D}{4}$$
    $$h = \frac{5D}{4}$$

### Conclusion:
The minimum height required is $\frac{5D}{4}$. Comparing this to the given options, the correct choice is **(A)**.
