To solve this problem, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which relates the absolute temperature $T$ of a black body to the wavelength $\lambda_{\text{max}}$ at which it emits the most radiation.

### The Formula
Wien's Displacement Law is given by:
$$\lambda_{\text{max}} T = b$$
Where:
- $\lambda_{\text{max}}$ is the peak wavelength in meters (m).
- $T$ is the temperature in Kelvin (K).
- $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$.

### Step-by-Step Calculation
1. **Identify the given values:**
   $$\lambda_{\text{max}} = 5.8 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$$
   $$b \approx 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$$

2. **Rearrange the formula to solve for $T$:**
   $$T = \frac{b}{\lambda_{\text{max}}}$$

3. **Substitute the values:**
   $$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{5.8 \times 10^{-7}}$$

4. **Perform the division:**
   $$T \approx 0.49965 \times 10^4 \text{ K}$$
   $$T \approx 4996.5 \text{ K}$$
