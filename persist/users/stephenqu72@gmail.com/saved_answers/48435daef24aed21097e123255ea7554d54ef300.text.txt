To determine the correct source for each spectrum, we must apply our knowledge of the three fundamental types of spectra described by Kirchhoff's laws:

1.  **Spectrum III (Continuous Spectrum):** This shows a smooth, unbroken band of all colors (wavelengths). This is characteristic of a hot, dense object, such as an **incandescent filament** (a blackbody radiator).
2.  **Spectrum II (Absorption Spectrum):** This shows a continuous background with discrete dark lines superimposed on it. This occurs when light from a hot source passes through a cooler, thinner gas. This is characteristic of **sunlight**, where the sun's core produces a continuous spectrum that is absorbed by cooler gases in its outer atmosphere (Fraunhofer lines).
3.  **Spectrum I (Emission Spectrum):** This shows bright, discrete lines on a dark background. This is produced by a low-density, excited gas where electrons are falling from higher to lower energy levels, emitting specific photons. This is characteristic of a **gas discharge tube**.
