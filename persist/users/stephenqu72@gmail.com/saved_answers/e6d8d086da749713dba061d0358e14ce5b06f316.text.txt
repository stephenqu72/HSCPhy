### Answer

**a) Which of these generators is a DC generator? Justify your answer.**

Type A is the DC generator. It uses a **split-ring commutator**, which reverses the connection of the coil to the external circuit every half-turn. This process ensures that the induced current flows in the same direction through the external load, effectively converting the induced AC into a pulsating DC output.

**b) Evaluate the effectiveness of each type of generator in large-scale power transmission.**

Type B (the AC generator with slip rings) is far more effective for large-scale power transmission. 
1. **Efficiency:** AC can be easily stepped up to high voltages using transformers. High-voltage transmission reduces the current ($I$) for a given power output, which significantly minimizes energy loss due to heat ($P_{loss} = I^2R$) in transmission lines. 
2. **Maintenance:** DC generators (Type A) suffer from mechanical wear due to the friction of brushes on the split-ring commutator, leading to sparking and high maintenance requirements. AC generators are more robust and can be built at much larger scales.
3. **Conclusion:** While DC generators are useful for specific local applications, AC generators are superior for global power grids because they allow for efficient voltage transformation and long-distance transmission.

### How to solve this
1. **Identify the components:** Recognize that the key difference in the diagrams is the connection at the armature output. Type A uses a split-ring (the hallmark of DC), while Type B uses two continuous rings (slip rings), which is the hallmark of AC.
2. **Apply physics principles:** Understand the role of the commutator in reversing polarity to maintain unidirectional current (DC) and the role of slip rings in maintaining the natural AC output generated by the coil's rotation in a magnetic field.
3. **Connect to the syllabus (Power Transmission):** For the evaluation part, recall the relationship between power, voltage, and current ($P=VI$). Understand that the ability of AC to utilize electromagnetic induction in transformers to step up voltage is the primary reason it dominates the power grid, as it minimizes $I^2R$ energy losses.
