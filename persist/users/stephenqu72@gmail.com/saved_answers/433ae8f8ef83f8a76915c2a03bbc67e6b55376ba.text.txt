### Answer
The graph shows that as motor speed increases, the torque generated by the DC motor decreases linearly. This occurs because as the motor spins faster, the armature coils cut through the magnetic field lines more rapidly, inducing a **back EMF** ($\varepsilon_{back}$) in the armature. According to Lenz’s Law, this back EMF opposes the supply voltage ($V$), resulting in a reduction of the net potential difference across the armature ($V_{net} = V - \varepsilon_{back}$). Consequently, the armature current ($I = \frac{V - \varepsilon_{back}}{R}$) decreases. Since the torque produced by a DC motor is directly proportional to the armature current ($\tau \propto I$), a decrease in current leads to a decrease in the torque produced.

### How to solve this
To gain full marks for this question, you must demonstrate a logical cause-and-effect relationship. Use this structure:

1.  **Identify the phenomenon:** Start by linking "speed" to "back EMF." Recall that a spinning motor acts as a generator. The faster it spins, the greater the induced back EMF.
2.  **Apply Circuit Theory:** Explain how back EMF affects the current. Use the relationship $I = \frac{V - \varepsilon_{back}}{R}$. State clearly that as back EMF increases, the net current flowing through the motor windings must decrease.
3.  **Link to Torque:** State the relationship between current and torque. In a DC motor, torque ($\tau$) is proportional to current ($I$). Therefore, as the current drops due to the increasing back EMF, the torque produced must also drop.
