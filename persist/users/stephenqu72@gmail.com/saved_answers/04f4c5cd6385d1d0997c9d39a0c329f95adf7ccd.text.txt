To solve this problem, we need to calculate the force exerted on the current-carrying rod by the magnetic field and then use Newton's Second Law to determine its acceleration.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Identify the Magnetic Force ($F_B$):**
A current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field experiences a force given by the formula:
$$F_B = BIl\sin(\theta)$$
Where:
- $B = 0.24\text{ T}$ (magnetic field strength)
- $I = 48\text{ A}$ (current)
- $l = d = 12.0\text{ cm} = 0.12\text{ m}$ (the length of the rod within the field, which corresponds to the distance $d$ between the rails)
- $\theta = 90^\circ$ (the field is perpendicular to the rod), so $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

Calculating the force:
$$F_B = 0.24 \times 48 \times 0.12$$
$$F_B = 1.3824\text{ N}$$

**2. Calculate the Acceleration ($a$):**
According to Newton's Second Law, $F_{net} = ma$. Since the rails are frictionless, the magnetic force is the net force:
$$a = \frac{F_B}{m}$$
Where $m = 0.72\text{ kg}$.

$$a = \frac{1.3824}{0.72}$$
$$a = 1.92\text{ m/s}^2$$

### Final Answer
The initial acceleration of the rod is **$1.92\text{ m/s}^2$**.
