### Front
What is the formula for the force per unit length between two parallel, current-carrying wires and how do you determine which measurement contributes the most to experimental uncertainty?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Ampere's Force Law and relative error analysis.
- **Formula:** $\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$ and $\text{Relative Error} = \frac{\Delta x}{x} \times 100\%$.
- **When to use it:** Calculating magnetic permeability ($\mu_0$) or assessing the precision of experimental measurements.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert all units to SI (e.g., cm to m, g to kg) or assuming the variable with the smallest absolute uncertainty has the largest relative error.
