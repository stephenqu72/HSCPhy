To solve this problem, we must understand that tidal forces are not just about the absolute gravitational force, but the **difference** in gravitational force across the diameter of the Earth.

### The Physics Principles
The tidal force $F_t$ exerted by a body on Earth is proportional to:
$$F_t \propto \frac{M}{r^3}$$
where $M$ is the mass of the body and $r$ is the distance between the center of the Earth and the center of the body.

### Step-by-Step Calculation
We need to compare the ratio of tidal forces between Jupiter and the Moon:
$$\frac{F_{t, \text{Jupiter}}}{F_{t, \text{Moon}}} = \frac{M_{\text{Jupiter}}}{M_{\text{Moon}}} \times \left( \frac{r_{\text{Moon}}}{r_{\text{Jupiter}}} \right)^3$$

**1. Given Data:**
*   $M_{\text{Moon}} = 7.3 \times 10^{22} \, \text{kg}$
*   $r_{\text{Moon}} = 3.6 \times 10^8 \, \text{m}$
*   $M_{\text{Jupiter}} = 1.9 \times 10^{27} \, \text{kg}$
*   $r_{\text{Jupiter}} = 5.9 \times 10^{11} \, \text{m}$

**2. Substituting values into the ratio:**
*   Mass ratio: $\frac{1.9 \times 10^{27}}{7.3 \times 10^{22}} \approx 26,027$
*   Distance ratio cubed: $\left( \frac{3.6 \times 10^8}{5.9 \times 10^{11}} \right)^3 \approx (6.1 \times 10^{-4})^3 \approx 2.27 \times 10^{-10}$

**3. Final Calculation:**
$$\text{Ratio} \approx 26,027 \times 2.27 \times 10^{-10} \approx 5.9 \times 10^{-6}$$

### Discussion
The tidal force exerted by Jupiter is approximately $6 \times 10^{-6}$ (or about $0.0006\%$) of the tidal force exerted by the Moon. Even though Jupiter is vastly more massive than the Moon, tidal force is determined by the **inverse-cube law** of distance. Because Jupiter is significantly further away from Earth than the Moon, the difference in its gravitational pull across the Earth’s diameter is negligible. Consequently, the Moon remains the dominant force in influencing Earth's tides.
