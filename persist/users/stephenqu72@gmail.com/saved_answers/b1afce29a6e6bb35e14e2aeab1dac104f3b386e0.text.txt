### Answer and Explanation

#### (a) Identify the purpose of the diffraction grating in this investigation.
**Answer:** The purpose of the diffraction grating is to act as a dispersive element that separates light into its component wavelengths (or colours) by diffracting different wavelengths at different angles. This allows for the observation and analysis of the emission spectrum of the gas in the discharge tube.

#### (b) Find the angular separation $\theta_r$ between the red 1 line and the central maxima.
**Explanation:** 
We use the diffraction grating formula: $d \sin \theta = n\lambda$.
1. **Find $d$:** The grating has $300 \text{ lines/mm}$, which is $3 \times 10^5 \text{ lines/m}$.
   $d = \frac{1}{3 \times 10^5} \text{ m} \approx 3.333 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$.
2. **Apply the formula for $n=1$ and $\lambda = 656 \text{ nm}$ ($656 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$):**
   $\sin \theta_r = \frac{n\lambda}{d} = \frac{1 \times 656 \times 10^{-9}}{3.333 \times 10^{-6}}$
   $\sin \theta_r \approx 0.1968$
   $\theta_r = \arcsin(0.1968) \approx 11.35^\circ$

**Answer:** The angular separation is approximately $11.4^\circ$.

#### (c) Explain quantitatively why the distance between the cyan 2 and red 2 lines on the photograph is double the distance between the cyan 1 and red 1 lines.
**Explanation:**
The grating equation is $d \sin \theta = n\lambda$. Given the small-angle approximation $\sin \theta \approx \theta$ (in radians), the angular position is $\theta \approx \frac{n\lambda}{d}$.
1. The angular separation between red and cyan in the first order ($n=1$) is:
   $\Delta \theta_1 = \theta_{r1} - \theta_{c1} = \frac{1 \cdot \lambda_r}{d} - \frac{1 \cdot \lambda_c}{d} = \frac{\lambda_r - \lambda_c}{d}$.
2. The angular separation between red and cyan in the second order ($n=2$) is:
   $\Delta \theta_2 = \theta_{r2} - \theta_{c2} = \frac{2 \cdot \lambda_r}{d} - \frac{2 \cdot \lambda_c}{d} = 2 \left( \frac{\lambda_r - \lambda_c}{d} \right)$.
3. Therefore, $\Delta \theta_2 = 2 \times \Delta \theta_1$. Since the physical distance $x$ on a screen or film at a distance $L$ from the grating is $x \approx L \tan \theta \approx L \theta$, if the angle is doubled, the separation distance on the photograph is also doubled.
