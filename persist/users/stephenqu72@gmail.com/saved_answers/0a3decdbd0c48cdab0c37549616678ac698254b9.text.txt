### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of **Einstein’s Relativity of Simultaneity**, a core concept in Special Relativity.

1.  **Perspective of Observer B (the stationary observer):** Observer B is stationary on the platform. At the moment the lightning strikes, B is exactly midway between the two points where the bolts hit the tracks. Since light from both bolts must travel the same distance to reach observer B, and light travels at a constant speed $c$ in all inertial frames, observer B perceives the two events as occurring **simultaneously**.

2.  **Perspective of Observer A (the moving observer):** Observer A is on the train, moving to the right. As the light from the two lightning bolts travels toward A, the train itself is moving. 
    *   Because the train is moving toward the light coming from the **front** of the train, observer A effectively "meets" that light sooner.
    *   Because the train is moving away from the light coming from the **back** of the train, that light has to travel "further" to catch up to observer A.
    *   Consequently, observer A perceives the light from the front bolt first and the light from the back bolt later. Therefore, observer A sees the bolt hit the **front** of the train first.

**Summary:** 
Simultaneity is relative. Events that are simultaneous in one inertial frame (B) are not necessarily simultaneous in another frame moving at a constant velocity (A).
