Torque is defined by the formula:
$$\tau = r \cdot F \cdot \sin(\theta)$$
where:
*   $r$ is the distance from the pivot to the point where the force is applied.
*   $F$ is the magnitude of the force.
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the position vector and the force vector.

Let's evaluate each force:

1.  **Force W:** This force is applied directly at the pivot. Therefore, the distance from the pivot $r = 0$. Since $r = 0$, the torque $\tau_W = 0 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$.
2.  **Force Z:** This force acts horizontally, pointing directly toward the pivot. The angle $\theta$ between the rod (the radial line from the pivot) and the force vector is $0^\circ$ (or $180^\circ$). Because $\sin(0^\circ) = 0$, the torque $\tau_Z = 0 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$.
3.  **Force X:** This force acts perpendicularly to the rod at a distance $r_X$ from the pivot. The torque is $\tau_X = r_X \cdot 5 \cdot \sin(90^\circ) = 5r_X$.
4.  **Force Y:** This force acts perpendicularly to the rod at the end of the rod. Let the total length of the rod be $L$. Then $\tau_Y = L \cdot 5 \cdot \sin(90^\circ) = 5L$.
