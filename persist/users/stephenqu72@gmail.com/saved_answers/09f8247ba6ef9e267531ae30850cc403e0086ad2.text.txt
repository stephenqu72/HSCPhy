### Answer
A proton is composed of two up quarks and one down quark ($uud$), giving it a net charge of $+\frac{2}{3} + \frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = +1e$. A neutron is composed of one up quark and two down quarks ($udd$), giving it a net charge of $+\frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = 0$.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to recall the quark compositions of nucleons as defined by the Standard Model of particle physics.

1.  **Identify the components:** Both protons and neutrons are baryons, meaning they are each made of three quarks held together by the strong nuclear force.
2.  **Define the proton:** The proton consists of two up ($u$) quarks and one down ($d$) quark. Since an up quark has a charge of $+\frac{2}{3}e$ and a down quark has a charge of $-\frac{1}{3}e$, the total charge is:
    $$+\frac{2}{3} + \frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = +1$$
3.  **Define the neutron:** The neutron consists of one up ($u$) quark and two down ($d$) quarks. The total charge is:
    $$+\frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = 0$$
4.  **Structure the comparison:** When answering a "compare" question in the HSC, always explicitly state the similarity (both are baryons made of three quarks) and the difference (the specific arrangement of up and down quarks resulting in different charges).
