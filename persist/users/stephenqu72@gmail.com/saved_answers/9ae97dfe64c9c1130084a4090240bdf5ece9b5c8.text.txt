To determine the direction of the force acting on a charged particle moving through a magnetic field, we use the **Right-Hand Rule** (also known as the Right-Hand Palm Rule) for magnetic forces.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) upwards**.

### Explanation
To solve this, follow these steps using your right hand:

1.  **Identify the variables:** 
    *   The magnetic field $B$ is directed to the **right**.
    *   The velocity $v$ of the positive charge is directed **out of the page**.
2.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule:**
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field $B$ (to the right).
    *   Point your thumb in the direction of the velocity $v$ (out of the page, towards your face).
    *   The direction in which your palm faces represents the direction of the force $F$ acting on a **positive** charge.
3.  **Result:** When your fingers point right and your thumb points out of the page, your palm will be facing **upwards**. 

Alternatively, you can use the vector cross product formula for the magnetic force:
$$F = q(v \times B)$$
Using the standard Cartesian coordinate system (where $x$ is right, $y$ is up, and $z$ is out of the page):
*   Velocity vector: $v = v \hat{k}$ (out of the page)
*   Magnetic field vector: $B = B \hat{i}$ (to the right)
*   Force: $F \propto (\hat{k} \times \hat{i})$
Since $\hat{k} \times \hat{i} = \hat{j}$ (where $\hat{j}$ is the upward unit vector), the direction of the force is **upwards**.
