### Front
What are the three mandatory requirements for a satellite to maintain a geostationary orbit?

### Back
- Key idea: The satellite must remain fixed relative to a point on Earth's surface.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Orbital Period ($T$) = 24 hours (specifically 23h 56m); Orbital Plane = Equator; Direction = West to East (prograde).
- When to use it: When identifying orbital characteristics or calculating orbital radius using $r^3/T^2 = GM/4\pi^2$.
- Common trap: Assuming a polar orbit can be geostationary; polar orbits are useful for global scanning, not fixed-point communication.
