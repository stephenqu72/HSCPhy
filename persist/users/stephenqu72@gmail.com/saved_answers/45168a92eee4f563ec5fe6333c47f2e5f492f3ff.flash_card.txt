### Front
Explain why an object on a rotating turntable eventually slides outward, and provide the formula for the centripetal acceleration involved.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Static friction provides the centripetal force. When $\omega$ increases, the required force ($F_c = mr\omega^2$) exceeds the maximum static friction ($\mu_s N$), causing the object to slide.
- **Formula:** $a_c = r\omega^2$
- **When to use it:** Problems involving objects on rotating platforms or banked curves where friction maintains the path.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that friction is a *limiting* force; the object doesn't fly "outward" due to centrifugal force, but due to insufficient centripetal force.
