To solve this problem, we need to understand the relationship between linear velocity ($v$) and angular velocity ($\omega$) for an object undergoing uniform circular motion.

### Explanation

1.  **Angular Velocity ($\omega$):** The angular velocity is defined as the rate of change of the angle with respect to time. Since Eva and Pam are on the same rigid circular platform, they complete one full rotation in the same amount of time. Therefore, they both rotate through the same angle in the same time interval, meaning their **angular velocities are equal** ($\omega_{Eva} = \omega_{Pam}$).

2.  **Linear Velocity ($v$):** The linear velocity of a point at a distance $r$ from the center of rotation is given by the formula:
    $$v = r\omega$$
    Since $\omega$ is constant for both, the linear velocity $v$ is directly proportional to the radius $r$ (the distance from the center). Looking at the diagram, Eva is positioned further from the center than Pam ($r_{Eva} > r_{Pam}$). Consequently:
    $$v_{Eva} = r_{Eva} \omega > v_{Pam} = r_{Pam} \omega$$
    Thus, Eva must have a larger linear velocity because she covers a larger circumference in the same amount of time.

3.  **Conclusion:** By comparing these findings, we see that they have the same angular velocity, but Eva has a larger linear velocity.
