To solve this problem, we must apply our knowledge of particle physics regarding the composition of nucleons (protons and neutrons).

### Answer
The correct answer is **B. 1 up, 2 down**.

### Explanation
1.  **Understand the Physical Property:** A neutron is a neutral subatomic particle, meaning its net electrical charge must be equal to $0$.
2.  **Use the Data:** From the provided table, we have the charge for an **Up ($u$)** quark as $+\frac{2}{3}$ and a **Down ($d$)** quark as $-\frac{1}{3}$.
3.  **Calculate the Charge for each option:** A nucleon is composed of three quarks. We need the combination that sums to zero:
    *   **A ($1u, 1d$):** This only accounts for two quarks. Charge: $+\frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = +\frac{1}{3}$ (Incorrect).
    *   **B ($1u, 2d$):** Three quarks. Charge: $1(+\frac{2}{3}) + 2(-\frac{1}{3}) = \frac{2}{3} - \frac{2}{3} = 0$. This confirms the neutron is neutral.
    *   **C ($2u, 1d$):** Three quarks. Charge: $2(+\frac{2}{3}) + 1(-\frac{1}{3}) = \frac{4}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3}{3} = +1$. This is the composition of a proton, not a neutron (Incorrect).
    *   **D ($2u, 2d$):** This is four quarks, which does not form a standard baryon (Incorrect).

Since the combination $udd$ results in a total charge of $0$, it correctly identifies the structure of a neutron.
