### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a): Identify TWO energy changes
To move a satellite from the surface into orbit, you must add energy to the system. The two primary energy changes are:
1.  **Increase in Gravitational Potential Energy ($U$):** Since $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$, as the distance $r$ from the centre of Mars increases, the potential energy becomes "less negative," representing an increase in total potential energy.
2.  **Increase in Kinetic Energy ($K$):** The satellite must gain enough speed to achieve orbital velocity. Kinetic energy increases from zero (or the rotational speed of the planet) to the required orbital velocity $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$.

#### Part (b): Calculate the orbital radius
To calculate the radius of a stationary (areostationary) orbit, we use Kepler's Third Law:
$$\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$$

**Step 1: Convert the period ($T$) to seconds.**
$T = 24 \text{ hours } 40 \text{ minutes} = (24 \times 3600) + (40 \times 60) = 86400 + 2400 = 88800 \text{ seconds}$.

**Step 2: State the constants.**
$G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3 \text{kg}^{-1} \text{s}^{-2}$
$M = 6.39 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$

**Step 3: Rearrange and solve for $r$.**
$$r^3 = \frac{GMT^2}{4\pi^2}$$
$$r^3 = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (6.39 \times 10^{23}) \times (88800)^2}{4\pi^2}$$
$$r^3 = \frac{(4.262 \times 10^{13}) \times (7.885 \times 10^9)}{39.478}$$
$$r^3 \approx 8.515 \times 10^{21}$$
$$r = \sqrt[3]{8.515 \times 10^{21}} \approx 2.04 \times 10^7 \text{ m}$$

The orbital radius is approximately $2.04 \times 10^7 \text{ m}$ (or $20,400 \text{ km}$).
