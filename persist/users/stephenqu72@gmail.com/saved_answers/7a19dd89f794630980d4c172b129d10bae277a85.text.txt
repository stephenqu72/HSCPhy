### Teacher's Explanation

This question tests your understanding of **Faraday’s Law of Induction** and **Lenz’s Law**, which are core components of the HSC Physics curriculum.

#### Part (a)
The key concept here is **Eddy Currents**. As the magnet rotates, the magnetic flux passing through the stationary metal disc is constantly changing.
1. According to Faraday’s Law, this changing magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (emf) in the disc.
2. Because the disc is a conductor, this emf drives circular currents known as **eddy currents**.
3. According to Lenz’s Law, these eddy currents create their own magnetic field that opposes the change that created them (the rotation of the magnet). This produces a torque on the disc, causing it to **rotate in the same direction as the magnet** to minimize the relative motion.

#### Part (b)
To sketch this graph, we must consider the formula for induced emf: $\varepsilon = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$.
1. **Frequency:** As the drill speeds up, the rate of change of magnetic flux ($d\Phi/dt$) increases. This means the frequency of the AC sine wave on your graph must increase over time.
2. **Amplitude:** The magnitude of the induced emf is proportional to the rate of change of flux. Since the magnet is rotating faster, the induced emf (the peak-to-peak amplitude) will also increase linearly as the drill accelerates.
3. **The Graph:** You should sketch a sine wave that starts at zero, where the peaks get **taller** (higher amplitude) and the waves get **closer together** (higher frequency) as time progresses, until it reaches a constant amplitude and frequency at "full speed."

---

### Answer

**(a)** As the magnet rotates, it creates a changing magnetic flux through the metal disc. This induces eddy currents in the disc in accordance with Faraday's Law. By Lenz's Law, these eddy currents create a magnetic field that opposes the motion of the magnet, exerting a torque that causes the disc to rotate in the same direction as the magnet.

**(b)** The graph should show an AC signal (a sine wave) starting from the origin. 
- The **amplitude** of the peaks should gradually increase from zero to a maximum value as the drill reaches full speed.
- The **frequency** (distance between peaks) should gradually increase (peaks get closer together) as the angular velocity of the magnet increases. 

*(Visually: Imagine a wave that is "stretched" at the start and becomes compressed and taller toward the right side of the graph.)*
