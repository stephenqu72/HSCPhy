To determine the correct description of the car's acceleration, we must apply the principles of Uniform Circular Motion.

### Answer
The correct option is **B) Magnitude is constant, Direction changes**.

### Explanation
When an object travels in a circular path at a constant speed, it undergoes **centripetal acceleration**.

1.  **Magnitude:** The formula for centripetal acceleration is $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$, where $v$ is the constant speed and $r$ is the radius of the circular track. Since both $v$ and $r$ are constant, the magnitude of the acceleration, $a_c$, remains constant throughout the motion.
2.  **Direction:** By definition, centripetal acceleration is always directed towards the **center** of the circular path. As the car moves around the track, its position relative to the center changes constantly. Therefore, the direction of the acceleration vector must also continuously change to always point toward the center of the circle.

Because the magnitude stays the same but the direction is always shifting, option B is the only correct choice.
