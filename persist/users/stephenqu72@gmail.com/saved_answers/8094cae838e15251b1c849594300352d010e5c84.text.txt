### Answer
James Clerk Maxwell made several pivotal contributions to electromagnetic theory that unified electricity and magnetism:

1.  **Unification of Electricity and Magnetism:** Maxwell synthesized the existing experimental work of scientists like Faraday, Ampere, and Gauss into a coherent set of four mathematical equations (Maxwell’s Equations). These equations demonstrated that electric and magnetic fields are intrinsically linked and form a single electromagnetic field.
2.  **The Displacement Current:** He introduced the concept of "displacement current" into Ampere’s Law. This correction accounted for the existence of magnetic fields in empty space (such as between the plates of a charging capacitor), ensuring the conservation of charge and allowing for wave propagation.
3.  **Prediction of Electromagnetic Waves:** By manipulating his equations, Maxwell derived the wave equation for electromagnetic fields. He showed that these waves move through space at a speed $v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$. 
4.  **Nature of Light:** Upon calculating the theoretical speed of these waves, Maxwell found it matched the known speed of light ($c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$), leading him to the revolutionary conclusion that light itself is an electromagnetic wave.
