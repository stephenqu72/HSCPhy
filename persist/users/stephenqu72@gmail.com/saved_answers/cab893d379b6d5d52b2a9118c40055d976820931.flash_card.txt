### Front
What are the two defining characteristics of the electric field between two charged parallel plates?

### Back
- Key idea: The electric field is uniform (constant magnitude and direction) between the plates, with a "fringing effect" (bulging) at the edges.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Direction is defined by the path of a positive test charge (positive to negative plate).
- When to use it: To identify the correct electric field diagram or calculate field strength ($E = V/d$).
- Common trap: Assuming field lines are perfectly straight at the edges (ignoring fringing) or reversing the direction (negative to positive).
