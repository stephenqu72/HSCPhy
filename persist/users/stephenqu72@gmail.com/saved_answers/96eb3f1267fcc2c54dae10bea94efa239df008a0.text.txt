### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) Wien**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to recall the key figures in the history of thermodynamics and quantum physics regarding black-body radiation:

1.  **Wien's Displacement Law:** Wilhelm Wien discovered that the wavelength at which a black body emits the most radiation (the peak intensity wavelength, $\lambda_{max}$) is inversely proportional to its absolute temperature ($T$). This is expressed by the equation:
    $$\lambda_{max} \cdot T = b$$
    where $b$ is Wien's displacement constant. This directly addresses the relationship asked in the question.

2.  **Why other options are incorrect:**
    *   **(A) Planck:** While Max Planck solved the "ultraviolet catastrophe" by introducing the idea of quantization of energy ($E = hf$), his work was built upon the empirical laws established by Wien.
    *   **(B) Maxwell:** James Clerk Maxwell is primarily known for his work on electromagnetic theory (Maxwell’s equations) and statistical mechanics (Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution), not for the displacement law of black-body radiation peaks.
    *   **(D) Young:** Thomas Young is best known for his double-slit experiment, which provided evidence for the wave theory of light.

In the HSC Physics curriculum, specifically under the "Quanta to Quarks" or "Advanced Mechanics" topics, it is essential to distinguish between Wien’s empirical law (the relationship of the peak) and Planck’s quantum solution (the explanation of the spectral curve).
