The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation
To understand this problem, we must apply Newton’s Second Law of Motion and the principles of kinematics.

1.  **Newton's Second Law:** For an object in free fall (where air resistance is ignored), the only force acting on the object is gravity. The force of gravity (weight) is given by $F_g = mg$, where $m$ is the mass and $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity ($9.8\ m/s^2$).
2.  **Calculating Acceleration:** According to $F_{net} = ma$, when the only force is gravity, we substitute $F_g$ for $F_{net}$:
    $$mg = ma$$
    Dividing both sides by $m$, we find:
    $$a = g$$
3.  **Independence of Mass:** As shown by the equation above, the acceleration $a$ of an object in free fall is equal to the acceleration due to gravity $g$, which is independent of the object's mass $m$. Whether an object is $60\ kg$ or $80\ kg$, it will accelerate downwards at the same rate of approximately $9.8\ m/s^2$.
4.  **Kinematics:** Since both individuals start from the same height with an initial velocity of $0\ m/s$ and undergo the same acceleration, they will cover the same vertical displacement in the same amount of time. Therefore, they will both reach the surface of the water simultaneously.

*   **Why the other options are incorrect:**
    *   **A** is incorrect because mass does not influence the time taken to fall in a vacuum or where air resistance is negligible.
    *   **B** is incorrect because while it is true that the net force on Sharukh is larger ($80 \times 9.8$ vs $60 \times 9.8$), her larger mass also gives her greater inertia, which exactly offsets the extra force.
    *   **C** is incorrect because they do not experience the same downward *force* (gravity pulls harder on the person with more mass); they experience the same downward *acceleration*.
