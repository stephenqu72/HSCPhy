### Front
How do you calculate the weight of an object in a different gravitational environment?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Weight is a force ($F_g$) that depends on local gravity; mass remains constant regardless of location.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $W = mg$
- **When to use it:** When comparing the weight of the same object in different gravitational fields (e.g., planets or moons).
- **Common trap:** Confusing mass (a scalar, constant property) with weight (a vector, location-dependent force).
