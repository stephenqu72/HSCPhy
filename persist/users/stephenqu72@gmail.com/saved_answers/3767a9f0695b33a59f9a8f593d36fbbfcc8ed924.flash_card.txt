### Front
What are the directions of the instantaneous velocity ($v$) and centripetal acceleration ($a$) for an object in uniform circular motion?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Velocity is tangential; acceleration is centripetal (radially inward).
- **Figure:** $v$ is a tangent to the circle at the object’s position; $a$ points directly to the center of the circle.
- **When to use it:** Any problem involving uniform circular motion (e.g., satellites, turning vehicles, particles in magnetic fields).
- **Common trap:** Confusing tangential velocity with the direction of acceleration; remember that $a$ is perpendicular to $v$, not parallel to the path.
