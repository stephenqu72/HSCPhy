### Answer
The correct answer is **D. The Universe is expanding at a constant rate.**

---

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we need to interpret the physical significance of the graph provided, which is a classic representation of **Hubble’s Law**.

1.  **Analyze the Graph:** The x-axis represents the distance of galaxies from Earth, and the y-axis represents their recessional velocity. The line of best fit is linear, passing through the origin $(0,0)$. This relationship is mathematically expressed as $v = H_0 d$, where $v$ is velocity, $d$ is distance, and $H_0$ is the Hubble constant.
2.  **Physical Interpretation:** A linear relationship between distance and velocity indicates that galaxies further away from us are moving away at proportionally higher speeds. This is the primary evidence for the expansion of the universe.
3.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   **A (Contracting):** If the universe were contracting, the recessional velocities would be negative (moving toward us).
    *   **B (Steady State):** A steady-state universe would not show this systematic correlation where distance correlates directly with velocity.
    *   **C (Accelerating):** While modern observations show the expansion *is* accelerating (due to dark energy), Hubble’s *original* data and the linear trend shown in this specific graph describe a uniform expansion rate at a single point in time, governed by the Hubble constant.
    *   **D (Constant Rate):** In the context of Hubble’s original findings represented by this linear graph, the relationship $v = H_0 d$ suggests that at any given epoch, the expansion follows a consistent linear trend, which is often interpreted as a uniform or "constant" rate of expansion across the observed distance scale in introductory physics.

**Key takeaway for HSC Physics:** Remember that Hubble's Law ($v = H_0 d$) provides evidence that the universe is expanding. The linearity of the graph is the crucial observation that proves the velocity is proportional to distance.
