### Front
What is the quark composition of a proton, and how is it determined?

### Back
- Key idea: A proton is a baryon with a total electric charge of +1.
- Formula / law: $Q_{proton} = 2(u) + 1(d) = 2(+\frac{2}{3}e) + 1(-\frac{1}{3}e) = +1e$.
- When to use it: To identify the internal structure of hadrons (baryons).
- Common trap: Confusing proton composition ($uud$) with neutron composition ($udd$), which results in a net charge of 0.
