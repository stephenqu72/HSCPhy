To solve this problem, we use **Faraday's Law of Induction**, which relates the induced electromotive force (emf) to the rate of change of magnetic flux through a coil.

### 1. Identify the relevant formula
The magnitude of the induced emf ($\varepsilon$) in a coil with $N$ turns is given by:
$$\varepsilon = N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$$
where $\Delta \Phi$ is the change in magnetic flux. Since magnetic flux $\Phi$ is defined as $\Phi = B \cdot A$ (where $B$ is the magnetic flux density and $A$ is the area of the coil), the formula becomes:
$$\varepsilon = N \cdot A \cdot \frac{\Delta B}{\Delta t}$$
We are asked to find the rate at which the magnetic flux density changes, which is $\frac{\Delta B}{\Delta t}$. Rearranging the formula gives:
$$\frac{\Delta B}{\Delta t} = \frac{\varepsilon}{N \cdot A}$$

### 2. Extract the given values
*   Induced emf ($\varepsilon$) = $32.5\text{ mV} = 32.5 \times 10^{-3}\text{ V}$
*   Number of turns ($N$) = $25$
*   Radius ($r$) = $60.0\text{ cm} = 0.600\text{ m}$
*   Area ($A$) = $\pi r^2 = \pi \times (0.600)^2 \approx 1.13097\text{ m}^2$

### 3. Perform the calculation
Substitute the values into the rearranged formula:
$$\frac{\Delta B}{\Delta t} = \frac{32.5 \times 10^{-3}}{25 \times (\pi \times 0.600^2)}$$
$$\frac{\Delta B}{\Delta t} = \frac{0.0325}{25 \times 1.13097}$$
$$\frac{\Delta B}{\Delta t} = \frac{0.0325}{28.2743} \approx 1.1494 \times 10^{-3}\text{ T s}^{-1}$$

*Note: In the context of the question, "rate at which magnetic flux changes" (units of $\text{Wb s}^{-1}$) is often conflated with "rate of change of flux density" (units of $\text{T s}^{-1}$). Looking at the provided options, they are expressed in $\text{Wb s}^{-1}$, which is mathematically equivalent to $\frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$.*

Using $\frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t} = \frac{\varepsilon}{N}$:
$$\frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t} = \frac{32.5 \times 10^{-3}}{25} = 1.30 \times 10^{-3}\text{ Wb s}^{-1}$$

Comparing this to the options provided:
(A) $1.15 \times 10^{-3}$
(B) $1.30 \times 10^{-3}$
(C) $2.17 \times 10^{-3}$
(D) $2.87 \times 10^{-2}$

The correct answer is **(B)**.
