How do you determine the mass ($M$) of a central body using orbital data?

### Back
- Key idea: The gradient of a $T^2$ vs $r^3$ graph equals $\frac{4\pi^2}{GM}$.
- Formula: $M = \frac{4\pi^2}{G \cdot \text{gradient}}$
- When to use it: To find the mass of a planet or star when given orbital period and radius data for its satellites.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., $T$ in seconds, $r$ in metres) or neglecting the powers of 10 when calculating the gradient from a scaled graph.
