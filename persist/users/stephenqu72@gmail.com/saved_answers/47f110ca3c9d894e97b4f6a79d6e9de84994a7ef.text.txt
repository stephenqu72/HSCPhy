### Step 1: Analyze the transformer turns ratio
Count the number of turns in each coil from the image:
- The **primary coil** has 6 turns.
- The **secondary coil** has 3 turns.

This gives us a turns ratio of:
$$\frac{N_p}{N_s} = \frac{6}{3} = 2$$

### Step 2: Relate current to the turns ratio
For an ideal transformer, the current and voltage are related to the turns ratio by $\frac{I_p}{I_s} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$. However, we are given an **efficiency ($\eta$) of 80%** (or 0.8). Efficiency is defined as the ratio of output power to input power:
$$\eta = \frac{P_{out}}{P_{in}} = \frac{V_s I_s}{V_p I_p}$$

From the transformer equation $\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$, we can substitute $\frac{V_s}{V_p}$ into the efficiency formula:
$$\eta = \left( \frac{N_s}{N_p} \right) \times \left( \frac{I_s}{I_p} \right)$$

### Step 3: Calculation
Substitute the known values into the equation:
- Efficiency ($\eta$) = $0.8$
- $\frac{N_s}{N_p} = \frac{3}{6} = 0.5$
- Primary current ($I_p$) = $3\text{ A}$

$$0.8 = 0.5 \times \left( \frac{I_s}{3} \right)$$

Now, solve for $I_s$:
$$0.8 = \frac{0.5 \times I_s}{3}$$
$$0.8 \times 3 = 0.5 \times I_s$$
$$2.4 = 0.5 \times I_s$$
$$I_s = \frac{2.4}{0.5}$$
$$I_s = 4.8\text{ A}$$
