As an HSC Physics teacher, let's break down this classic projectile motion problem.

### Answer
The correct statement is **C**.

### Explanation

To solve this, we must remember that in projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), the horizontal and vertical motions are **independent** of each other.

1.  **Vertical Motion governs time:** Since both objects are "flicked horizontally," their initial vertical velocity ($u_y$) is $0$ m/s. The time of flight ($t$) is determined solely by the vertical displacement ($h$) and the acceleration due to gravity ($g$).
    Using the equation of motion:
    $$h = u_y t + \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$
    Since $u_y = 0$, this simplifies to:
    $$h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$
    Rearranging for $t$:
    $$t = \sqrt{\frac{2h}{g}}$$

2.  **Compare the two objects:**
    *   Object 1: $h_1 = 150$ m
    *   Object 2: $h_2 = 50$ m

    The time for the first object is $t_1 = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 150}{g}}$ and the time for the second is $t_2 = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 50}{g}}$.

3.  **Find the ratio:**
    $$\frac{t_1}{t_2} = \frac{\sqrt{\frac{300}{g}}}{\sqrt{\frac{100}{g}}} = \sqrt{\frac{300}{100}} = \sqrt{3}$$
    Therefore, $t_1 = \sqrt{3} \times t_2$.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
*   **A:** False, because they fall from different heights.
*   **B:** False, the math shows the ratio is $\sqrt{3}$, not 3.
*   **D:** False. Mass does not affect the time of fall in a vacuum (ignoring air resistance), and the object dropped from the *lower* height (the lighter one) will actually reach the ground *faster* because it has less distance to travel.
