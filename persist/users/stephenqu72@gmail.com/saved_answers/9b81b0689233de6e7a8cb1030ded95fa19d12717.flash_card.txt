What are the fundamental conservation laws required to balance nuclear decay equations?

### Back
- Key idea: Total mass number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$) must remain constant on both sides of the equation.
- Formula / law: $\sum A_{reactants} = \sum A_{products}$ and $\sum Z_{reactants} = \sum Z_{products}$.
- When to use it: To identify unknown decay particles or products in $\alpha$, $\beta^-$, or $\beta^+$ decay equations.
- Common trap: Forgetting to include the antineutrino ($\bar{\nu}$) in $\beta^-$ decay or the neutrino ($\nu$) in $\beta^+$ decay to satisfy lepton number conservation.
