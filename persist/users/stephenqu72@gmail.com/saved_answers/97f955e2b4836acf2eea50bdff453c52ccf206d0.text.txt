To solve this problem, we need to substitute the given definitions into the expression for total relativistic energy.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the given definitions:**
    *   Relativistic kinetic energy: $K = (\gamma - 1)mc^2$
    *   Lorentz factor: $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$
    *   Total relativistic energy: $E_{\text{total}} = E_{\text{rest}} + K$

2.  **Recall the definition of rest energy ($E_{\text{rest}}$):**
    From Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence, the energy of an object at rest is:
    $$E_{\text{rest}} = mc^2$$

3.  **Substitute into the total energy equation:**
    $$E_{\text{total}} = mc^2 + (\gamma - 1)mc^2$$

4.  **Simplify the expression:**
    Distribute the $mc^2$:
    $$E_{\text{total}} = mc^2 + \gamma mc^2 - mc^2$$
    The $mc^2$ terms cancel out, leaving:
    $$E_{\text{total}} = \gamma mc^2$$

5.  **Substitute the definition of $\gamma$ back into the expression:**
    Substitute $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$:
    $$E_{\text{total}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}} \times mc^2$$
    $$E_{\text{total}} = \frac{mc^2}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$
