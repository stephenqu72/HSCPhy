### Answer and Explanation

To solve ray diagram problems for curved mirrors, we use two standard principal rays.

#### a) Ray Diagram Construction
To locate the image, draw at least two of the following rays starting from the top of the object:

1.  **Parallel Ray:** Draw a ray parallel to the principal axis until it hits the mirror. Upon reflection, it must pass through the principal focus ($f$).
2.  **Focal Ray:** Draw a ray that passes through the principal focus ($f$) until it hits the mirror. Upon reflection, it will travel parallel to the principal axis.
3.  **Center of Curvature Ray:** Draw a ray that passes through the center of curvature ($C$). This ray hits the mirror at a $90^\circ$ angle (normal to the surface) and reflects back along the same path.

**Result:** The intersection of these reflected rays will be beyond $C$. You should draw an inverted arrow pointing downwards at the intersection point to represent the image.

#### b) Nature of the Image
The image produced by placing an object between $f$ and $C$ in a concave mirror is:
*   **Real:** The light rays actually converge at the image position.
*   **Inverted:** The arrow points downwards relative to the principal axis.
*   **Magnified:** The image is larger than the original object.
*   **Location:** It is formed beyond the center of curvature ($C$).
