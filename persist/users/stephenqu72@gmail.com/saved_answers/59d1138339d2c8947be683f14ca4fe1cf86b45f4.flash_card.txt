### Front
What does the slope of a galaxy's recessional velocity ($v$) vs. distance ($d$) graph represent in the context of the Hubble-Lemaître Law?

### Back
- Key idea: The slope represents the rate of universal expansion.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = H_0 d$ ; Slope = Hubble constant ($H_0$).
- When to use it: To determine the recessional velocity of a distant galaxy or to estimate the expansion rate of the universe.
- Common trap: Confusing the slope ($H_0$) with the Hubble time ($1/H_0$), which represents the age of the universe.
