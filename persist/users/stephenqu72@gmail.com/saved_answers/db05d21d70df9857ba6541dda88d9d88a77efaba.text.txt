To solve this problem, we must apply the laws of light polarisation in sequence.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. Passing through the first polariser:**
When unpolarised light of intensity $I_A$ passes through the first polariser, its intensity is reduced by half. Let $I_1$ be the intensity of the light after the first polariser:
$$I_1 = \frac{1}{2} I_A$$

**2. Passing through the second polariser:**
According to **Malus's Law**, when polarised light of intensity $I_1$ passes through a second polariser with an angle $\theta$ between their transmission axes, the final intensity $I_B$ is given by:
$$I_B = I_1 \cos^2(\theta)$$

**3. Combining the equations:**
Substitute the expression for $I_1$ into the equation for $I_B$:
$$I_B = \left( \frac{1}{2} I_A \right) \cos^2(\theta)$$

We are given the ratio $\frac{I_B}{I_A} = 0.375$. Dividing both sides of our equation by $I_A$:
$$\frac{I_B}{I_A} = \frac{1}{2} \cos^2(\theta)$$
$$0.375 = 0.5 \cos^2(\theta)$$

**4. Solving for $\theta$:**
Divide both sides by $0.5$:
$$\cos^2(\theta) = \frac{0.375}{0.5} = 0.75$$
Take the square root of both sides:
$$\cos(\theta) = \sqrt{0.75} \approx 0.866$$
Now, find the angle using the inverse cosine ($\arccos$):
$$\theta = \arccos(0.866) \approx 30^\circ$$

**Conclusion:**
The angle between the transmission axes is $30^\circ$. Therefore, the correct option is **A**.
