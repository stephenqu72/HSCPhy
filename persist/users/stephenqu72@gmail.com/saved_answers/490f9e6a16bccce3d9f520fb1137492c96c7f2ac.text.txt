### Answer
The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation
To understand how an electromagnetic wave propagates, we look at Maxwell’s equations, which describe the relationship between electric and magnetic fields.

1.  **The Nature of the Wave:** An electromagnetic wave consists of time-varying electric and magnetic fields that are mutually perpendicular to each other and both perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation.
2.  **Mutual Induction:** According to Maxwell-Faraday’s law, a changing magnetic field induces a changing electric field. Conversely, the Ampère-Maxwell law states that a changing electric field induces a changing magnetic field. 
3.  **Self-Propagation:** Because these fields are oscillating (changing over time), they continuously generate one another. This "self-propagating" mechanism allows the wave to travel through a vacuum at the speed of light ($c$).
4.  **Geometry:** These fields are always **perpendicular** (at $90^\circ$ angles) to one another. 

**Why the others are incorrect:**
*   **A:** The fields must be perpendicular, not parallel.
*   **B & C:** The fields must be oscillating (changing over time) to sustain the wave; constant fields do not propagate as an electromagnetic wave.

When answering these questions in your HSC exams, always remember the "triad" structure: the Electric field vector ($\vec{E}$), the Magnetic field vector ($\vec{B}$), and the direction of propagation ($\vec{v}$) are all mutually orthogonal (at right angles to each other).
