### Front
How does the gravitational potential energy ($U$) of a mass $m$ change in relation to its distance $r$ from a central mass $M$?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational potential energy is negative; as $r$ increases, $U$ becomes less negative (approaches zero).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$
- When to use it: To calculate changes in energy for satellite orbits, escape velocity, or work done in a gravitational field.
- Common trap: Confusing "tripling" a negative value (which makes it more negative) with increasing the total energy toward zero (which requires decreasing $r$).
