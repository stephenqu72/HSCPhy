### Part a) i.
**Solution:**
The component of gravity acting down an inclined plane is given by $F_g = mg \sin(\theta)$.
Given: $m = 2.0\text{ kg}$, $g = 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$, $\theta = 25^\circ$.
$$F_g = 2.0 \times 9.8 \times \sin(25^\circ)$$
$$F_g = 19.6 \times 0.4226... \approx 8.284\text{ N}$$
Rounding to two significant figures (consistent with the prompt), we get **$8.3\text{ N}$**.

**Explanation:** In mechanics, we resolve the weight vector into two components: perpendicular to the slope ($mg \cos\theta$) and parallel to the slope ($mg \sin\theta$). Since the trolley is moving down the slope, the gravitational pull parallel to the incline is the force driving the motion.

---

### Part a) ii.
**Solution:**
Using Newton's Second Law: $F_{net} = ma$.
The net force is the driving force ($F_g$) minus the frictional force ($F_f$):
$$F_g - F_f = ma$$
$$8.284 - F_f = 2.0 \times 3.2$$
$$8.284 - F_f = 6.4$$
$$F_f = 8.284 - 6.4 = 1.884\text{ N}$$
Rounding, we get **$1.9\text{ N}$**.

**Explanation:** The acceleration provided by the light gates tells us the actual net force acting on the trolley. Any difference between the theoretical driving force ($8.3\text{ N}$) and the actual net force ($6.4\text{ N}$) must be accounted for by the opposing frictional force.

### Part b) i.
**Solution:**
We use the **Principle of Conservation of Momentum**: In the absence of external horizontal forces, the total momentum before collision equals total momentum after collision.
$p_{initial} = p_{final}$
$m_1 u_1 + m_2 u_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$
$2.0 \times 4.0 + 2.0 \times 0 = (2.0 + 2.0) \times v_f$
$8.0 = 4.0 \times v_f$
$$v_f = 2.0\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

**Explanation:** Because the trolleys stick together, this is a "perfectly inelastic" collision. We set the momentum of the moving trolley equal to the combined momentum of both trolleys moving at a new, shared velocity.

---

### Part b) ii.
**Solution:**
We compare the total kinetic energy ($KE$) before and after the collision.
$KE_{before} = \frac{1}{2}m_1 v_1^2 + 0 = \frac{1}{2} \times 2.0 \times (4.0)^2 = 16.0\text{ J}$
$KE_{after} = \frac{1}{2}(m_1 + m_2) v_f^2 = \frac{1}{2} \times 4.0 \times (2.0)^2 = 8.0\text{ J}$
Since $KE_{before} \neq KE_{after}$ ($16.0\text{ J} > 8.0\text{ J}$), kinetic energy is lost.
**Conclusion: The collision is inelastic.**

**Explanation:** In physics, a collision is perfectly elastic only if kinetic energy is conserved. Because the trolleys stick together, significant energy is converted into heat and sound, making the collision inelastic.
