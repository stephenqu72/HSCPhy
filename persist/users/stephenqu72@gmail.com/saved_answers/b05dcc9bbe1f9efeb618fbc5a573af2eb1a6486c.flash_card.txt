### Front
Sketching the frequency graph of a siren passing a stationary observer: What happens to the observed frequency and what physics principle does this demonstrate?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The Doppler Effect: moving towards an observer compresses wavefronts (higher $f$); moving away stretches wavefronts (lower $f$).
- **Law / Principle:** Doppler Effect.
- **When to use it:** Whenever there is relative motion between a wave source (sound/light) and an observer.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the frequency changes gradually; in a simple model with constant velocity, the graph is a step-down function with a sharp discontinuity at the moment of passing.
