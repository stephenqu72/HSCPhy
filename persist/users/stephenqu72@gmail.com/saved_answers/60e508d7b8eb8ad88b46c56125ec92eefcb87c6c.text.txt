To solve this problem, we need to apply the principle of equilibrium to a charged particle placed between two parallel plates.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
For the particle to be "exactly suspended," the upward electric force ($F_E$) must be equal in magnitude to the downward gravitational force ($F_g$).

- Gravitational force: $F_g = mg$
- Electric force: $F_E = qE$
- Electric field between plates: $E = \frac{V}{d}$

Equating the forces:
$$mg = qE = q\frac{V}{d}$$

### 2. Rearrange for distance ($d$)
$$d = \frac{qV}{mg}$$

### 3. Substitute the given values
- $q = 4.0 \times 10^{-8} \, \text{C}$ (We use the magnitude of the charge)
- $V = 35.0 \, \text{V}$
- $m = 2.5 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{kg}$
- $g \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$

$$d = \frac{(4.0 \times 10^{-8}) \times 35.0}{(2.5 \times 10^{-6}) \times 9.8}$$
$$d = \frac{1.4 \times 10^{-6}}{2.45 \times 10^{-5}}$$
$$d \approx 0.05714 \, \text{m}$$

### 4. Convert to appropriate units
To match the options provided:
$0.05714 \, \text{m} \approx 57 \, \text{mm}$

Comparing this to the given options:
(A) $56 \, \text{cm}$
(B) $57 \, \text{mm}$
(C) $17.5 \, \text{m}$
(D) $1.8 \, \text{m}$

The correct option is **(B)**.

### Explanation Summary
To solve this, recognize that for equilibrium, the electric force $qV/d$ must balance the weight $mg$. By rearranging the formula to solve for $d$ and plugging in the constants for gravity ($9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$) and the provided physical quantities, we find the required separation distance is approximately $0.057 \, \text{m}$, which converts to $57 \, \text{mm}$.
