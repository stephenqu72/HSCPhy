To find the minimum height $h$ required for the particle to complete the loop, we must analyze the physics at the top of the loop.

### 1. Condition at the top of the loop
For the particle to stay on the track at the highest point of the loop (a height of $2r$ from the ground), the centripetal force must be provided by at least the gravitational force. At the "critical" point where the particle is just about to lose contact with the track, the normal force is zero.
The centripetal force equation at the top is:
$$\frac{mv^2}{r} = mg + N$$
Setting $N = 0$ for the minimum speed ($v$):
$$\frac{mv^2}{r} = mg$$
$$v^2 = gr$$

### 2. Conservation of Energy
Since the particle is released from rest at height $h$, we use the conservation of mechanical energy between the starting position and the top of the loop. 
Total energy at the start:
$$E_i = mgh$$
Total energy at the top of the loop (where height is $2r$):
$$E_f = mg(2r) + \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
Equating $E_i = E_f$:
$$mgh = 2mgr + \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

### 3. Solving for $h$
Substitute $v^2 = gr$ into the energy equation:
$$mgh = 2mgr + \frac{1}{2}m(gr)$$
Divide by $mg$:
$$h = 2r + 0.5r$$
$$h = 2.5r$$
