To determine the colour of Star XLR8, we must identify its peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) and apply Wien’s Displacement Law, which states that the peak wavelength of a blackbody is inversely proportional to its temperature.

### Answer
The correct answer is **d) Blue**.

### Explanation
1.  **Identify the Peak Wavelength:** Looking at the provided graph, the blue "continuum" curve represents the blackbody radiation profile. We locate the highest point (the peak) of this curve on the x-axis, which represents the wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$). The peak occurs at approximately $460\text{ nm}$.
2.  **Relate Wavelength to Colour:** The visible spectrum roughly ranges from $380\text{ nm}$ (violet/blue) to $750\text{ nm}$ (red). 
    *   $400\text{--}450\text{ nm}$ is violet/deep blue.
    *   $450\text{--}495\text{ nm}$ is blue.
    *   $495\text{--}570\text{ nm}$ is green.
    *   $570\text{--}590\text{ nm}$ is yellow.
    *   $590\text{--}750\text{ nm}$ is orange/red.
3.  **Conclusion:** A peak wavelength of approximately $460\text{ nm}$ falls squarely into the blue region of the visible spectrum. Stars that peak in the blue region appear blue to the human eye, indicating that the star is very hot.
