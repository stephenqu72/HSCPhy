### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must understand the conventions of electric field lines:

1.  **Direction of Field Lines:** By definition, electric field lines originate from **positive** charges and terminate on **negative** charges. 
2.  **Visual Analysis:** Looking at the diagram provided, all the arrows are pointing **away** from the centers of both charges. Since the field lines are directed outward from both objects, both charges must be positive.
3.  **Repulsion:** The pattern of the lines—where they bend away from the space between the charges—demonstrates the repulsive force that exists between like charges. If one were positive and the other negative (Option D), the lines would travel directly from the positive charge to the negative charge.

While the *shape* of the electric field pattern is identical for two negative charges (except that the arrows would point inward), the diagram clearly shows the arrows pointing **outward**. Therefore, the charges are specifically identified as positive.
