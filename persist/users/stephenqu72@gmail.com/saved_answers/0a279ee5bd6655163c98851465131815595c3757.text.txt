To determine the new orbital velocity, we must use the formula for the orbital velocity of a satellite in a circular orbit:

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

where:
*   $G$ is the gravitational constant
*   $M$ is the mass of the Earth
*   $r$ is the distance from the centre of the Earth

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Define the initial state:**
    The initial velocity $v_1$ at distance $r_1 = r$ is given as $v$:
    $$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

2.  **Define the new state:**
    The new distance is $r_2 = 2r$. Let the new velocity be $v_2$:
    $$v_2 = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{2r}}$$

3.  **Compare the two states:**
    We can rewrite the expression for $v_2$ by pulling out the constant factors:
    $$v_2 = \sqrt{\frac{1}{2}} \times \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$
    Since $\sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} = v$, we can substitute this back into the equation:
    $$v_2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \times v$$

4.  **Calculate the value:**
    The value of $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$ is approximately $0.707$. Therefore:
    $$v_2 \approx 0.707v$$

Rounding to one decimal place as presented in the options, we get $0.7v$.
