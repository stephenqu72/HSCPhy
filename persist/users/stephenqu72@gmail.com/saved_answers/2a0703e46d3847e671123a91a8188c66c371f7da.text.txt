The correct answer is **A. The charge of an electron**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to recall the historical context of atomic physics experiments often covered in the HSC Physics syllabus:

1.  **Robert Millikan's Oil Drop Experiment:** By observing the motion of tiny charged oil droplets in an electric field, Millikan was able to measure the force required to balance the gravitational force acting on the drops. He discovered that the charge on the droplets was always an integer multiple of a fundamental unit. This value, approximately $1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$, was identified as the **elementary charge of an electron**.

2.  **Evaluating the distractors:**
    *   **B. The charge to mass ratio of an electron:** This was determined by J.J. Thomson using the cathode ray tube experiment, not Millikan.
    *   **C. Electrons orbiting far from the nucleus:** This relates to Rutherford's gold foil experiment, which discovered the nucleus.
    *   **D. The shell orbits of electrons in hydrogen:** This is associated with the Bohr model of the atom, which explains spectral lines, not the charge of the electron itself.

**Key takeaway:** Always associate the "Oil Drop" with the "quantization and magnitude of the elementary charge."
