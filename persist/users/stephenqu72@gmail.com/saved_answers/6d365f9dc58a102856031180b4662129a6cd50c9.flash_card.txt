### Front
What is the fundamental trigger condition for a nucleus to undergo $\beta^-$ decay?

### Back
- Key idea: Neutron-to-proton ratio is too high (nucleus is "neutron-rich").
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $n \rightarrow p + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e$
- When to use it: When a nucleus lies above the "band of stability" and needs to increase its atomic number ($Z$) by 1 to reach stability.
- Common trap: Confusing $\beta^-$ decay (triggered by excess neutrons) with $\beta^+$ decay (triggered by excess protons).
