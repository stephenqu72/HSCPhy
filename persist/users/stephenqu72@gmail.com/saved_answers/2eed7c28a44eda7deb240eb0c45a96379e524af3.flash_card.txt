### Front
What determines the escape velocity of a satellite, and how does distance from the center of a planet ($r$) affect it?

### Back
- Key idea: Escape velocity is independent of the satellite's mass and inversely proportional to the square root of the distance from the center of the planet.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$
- When to use it: To calculate or compare the minimum velocity required to escape a gravitational field from a specific orbital radius.
- Common trap: Assuming a more massive satellite requires more energy/speed to escape (the satellite's mass cancels out).
