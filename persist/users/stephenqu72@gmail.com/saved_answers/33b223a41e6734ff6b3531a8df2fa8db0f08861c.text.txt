To determine the point where the projectile has the minimum speed, we must analyze the components of its velocity.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) Y**.

### Explanation
In projectile motion, we break the velocity $v$ into two independent components:
1. **Horizontal velocity ($v_x$):** In the absence of air resistance, there is no horizontal acceleration. Therefore, the horizontal component remains constant throughout the entire flight: $v_x = v \cos(\theta) = \text{constant}$.
2. **Vertical velocity ($v_y$):** The projectile is subject to the acceleration due to gravity ($g$) acting downwards. This causes the vertical velocity to change according to $v_y = u_y - gt$.

The total speed $v$ is given by the Pythagorean theorem:
$$v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$$

Since $v_x$ is constant, the total speed $v$ is minimized when the vertical component $v_y$ is equal to zero. This occurs at the peak of the trajectory (the maximum height). Looking at the diagram provided, point **Y** represents the apex or the highest point of the path. At this specific point, the projectile is momentarily moving purely horizontally, meaning $v_y = 0$, resulting in the minimum possible speed for the entire flight path.
