### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must understand the physical phenomenon of **interference** in Young’s Double Slit Experiment.

1.  **Wave Nature of Light:** When plane light waves encounter the two narrow slits, each slit acts as a new point source of circular waves (Huygens' Principle). These waves propagate outward from both slits and overlap on the screen.
2.  **Superposition and Interference:** Where the waves overlap, they undergo superposition.
    *   **Constructive Interference:** Where two wave crests (or troughs) meet, they add together to produce a region of high intensity, resulting in a bright fringe on the screen.
    *   **Destructive Interference:** Where a crest meets a trough, they cancel each other out, resulting in a region of zero intensity (a dark fringe).
3.  **The Resulting Pattern:** This alternating pattern of bright and dark fringes is known as an **interference pattern**. Unlike single-slit diffraction (which produces a large central maximum) or simple geometric shadows, the double-slit experiment produces a series of equally spaced bright fringes across the screen.
4.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   Option A suggests a single spot, which would happen if the light passed through a single aperture without significant diffraction.
    *   Option B and C show only two or three fringes, which lacks the continuous characteristic of a wave-interference pattern.
    *   **Option D** represents a series of multiple, equally spaced fringes, which is the signature result of the interference of coherent light sources.

Therefore, D is the most accurate representation of the intensity distribution pattern observed on the screen.
