To solve this projectile motion problem, we need to consider the horizontal and vertical components of motion separately.

### Given Data:
*   Total time of flight, $t = 6.1\text{ s}$
*   Horizontal range, $R = 244\text{ m}$
*   Acceleration due to gravity, $g \approx 9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$

### Step 1: Find the horizontal velocity component ($v_x$)
Since there is no air resistance, the horizontal velocity is constant:
$$v_x = \frac{R}{t} = \frac{244}{6.1} = 40\text{ ms}^{-1}$$

### Step 2: Find the vertical velocity component ($v_{iy}$)
Using the vertical displacement formula $y = v_{iy}t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$. Since the projectile lands on flat ground, the vertical displacement at the time of landing is $0$:
$$0 = v_{iy}t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$
$$v_{iy} = \frac{gt}{2}$$
$$v_{iy} = \frac{9.8 \times 6.1}{2} = 4.9 \times 6.1 = 29.89 \approx 30\text{ ms}^{-1}$$

### Step 3: Find the magnitude of the initial velocity ($v$)
The magnitude of the resultant initial velocity is the vector sum of its components:
$$v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_{iy}^2}$$
$$v = \sqrt{40^2 + 30^2}$$
$$v = \sqrt{1600 + 900} = \sqrt{2500} = 50\text{ ms}^{-1}$$

**Conclusion:**
The magnitude of the initial velocity is $50\text{ ms}^{-1}$.

Comparing this to the given options:
A. $30\text{ ms}^{-1}$
B. $40\text{ ms}^{-1}$
C. $50\text{ ms}^{-1}$
D. $60\text{ ms}^{-1}$

The correct answer is **C**.
