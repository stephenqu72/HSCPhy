### Answer
The correct answer is **(B) Time dilation**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must understand the core of Einstein’s light-clock thought experiment:

1.  **The Scenario:** The image shows a light pulse traveling from a source on the ceiling to a mirror on the floor and back. 
    *   To an observer **inside the train**, the light travels a straight vertical path up and down.
    *   To an observer **outside on the platform**, the train is moving. Therefore, by the time the light hits the floor and returns to the ceiling, the train has shifted horizontally. To this external observer, the light must travel a diagonal (longer) path.

2.  **The Postulate:** A fundamental postulate of special relativity is that the speed of light ($c$) is constant for all inertial observers. 

3.  **The Deduction:** 
    *   Since speed is defined as $\text{distance} / \text{time}$ ($v = d/t$), and the speed of light $c$ is constant, if the observer on the platform sees the light travel a **greater distance** ($d$), they must also observe a **greater amount of time** ($t$) for that journey to occur.
    *   Mathematically, if $d_{\text{platform}} > d_{\text{train}}$ and $c$ remains constant, then $t_{\text{platform}} > t_{\text{train}}$.

4.  **Conclusion:** This demonstrates that time passes more slowly for the moving object relative to the stationary observer. This phenomenon is known as **time dilation**.

*   **(A) Length contraction** refers to the shortening of objects moving at relativistic speeds in the direction of motion.
*   **(C) Mass dilation** (relativistic mass increase) refers to the increase in measured momentum/inertia as an object approaches $c$.
*   **(D) Energy/mass equivalency** refers to the concept $E = mc^2$.
