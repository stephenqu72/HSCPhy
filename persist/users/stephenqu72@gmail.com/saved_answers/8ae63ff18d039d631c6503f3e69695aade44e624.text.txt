The correct answer is **A. Polarisation**.

### Explanation
To determine which wave behavior confirms the transverse nature of light, we must look at how light interacts with filters:

1.  **Transverse vs. Longitudinal:** A transverse wave oscillates perpendicular to the direction of energy propagation. A longitudinal wave (like sound) oscillates parallel to the direction of propagation.
2.  **The Evidence:** Polarisation is the process of restricting the oscillations of a wave to a single plane. Longitudinal waves cannot be polarised because their displacement is already along the axis of travel; there is no "side-to-side" component to filter out. 
3.  **Why others are incorrect:** 
    *   **Diffraction** (B) and **Interference** (C) are wave behaviors shared by both transverse (light) and longitudinal (sound) waves. Therefore, they demonstrate that light *is* a wave, but they do not prove that it is *transverse*.
    *   **Refraction** (D) occurs when a wave changes speed as it enters a different medium and can also occur in both transverse and longitudinal waves.

Because polarisation can only occur if the wave has a component perpendicular to the direction of motion, the observation that light can be polarised provides definitive evidence that light waves are transverse.
