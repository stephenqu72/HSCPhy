To solve these physics problems, we must use the standard gravitational constant, $G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11}\ \text{m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$.

### Part (a)
**Answer:**
Using the formula $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$:
$$g = \frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \times (18.98 \times 10^{26})}{(6.842 \times 10^7)^2}$$
$$g = \frac{1.2671 \times 10^{17}}{4.6813 \times 10^{15}}$$
$$g \approx 27.07\ \text{m s}^{-2}$$

**Explanation:**
1. Identify the given variables: $M = 18.98 \times 10^{26}\ \text{kg}$ and $r = 6.842 \times 10^7\ \text{m}$.
2. Substitute the values into the provided formula.
3. Calculate the numerator (product of $G$ and $M$) and the denominator (square of the radius).
4. Divide the results to find the gravitational field strength at the surface.

### Part (b)
**Answer:**
We use Kepler’s Third Law: $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$, which rearranges to $r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{GMT^2}{4\pi^2}}$.
Given $T = 29300\ \text{s}$:
$$r^3 = \frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \times (18.98 \times 10^{26}) \times (29300)^2}{4\pi^2}$$
$$r^3 = \frac{(1.2671 \times 10^{17}) \times (8.5849 \times 10^8)}{39.478}$$
$$r^3 \approx 2.755 \times 10^{24}$$
$$r \approx 1.40 \times 10^8\ \text{m}$$

**Explanation:**
1. Kepler’s Third Law relates the orbital radius $r$ and the orbital period $T$.
2. Substitute the known constants and values ($G$, $M$, and $T$) into the rearranged formula.
3. Solve for $r^3$ first, then take the cube root of the result to find the orbital radius.
