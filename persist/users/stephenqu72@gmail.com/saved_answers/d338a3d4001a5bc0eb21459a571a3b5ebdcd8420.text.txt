To answer this question effectively for an HSC Physics exam, you need to address two distinct parts: the qualitative mechanism (the wave model) and the quantitative application (the mathematical relationship).

### Answer

**Qualitative Support for the Wave Model:**
Thomas Young’s double-slit experiment demonstrated that light, when passed through two closely spaced narrow slits, produces an interference pattern of alternating bright and dark fringes on a screen. This provided clear evidence for the wave nature of light because:
*   **Constructive Interference:** When light waves from both slits arrive at a point in phase (path difference is a whole number of wavelengths, $n\lambda$), they reinforce each other, creating a bright fringe.
*   **Destructive Interference:** When waves arrive out of phase (path difference is a half-integer number of wavelengths, $(n + \frac{1}{2})\lambda$), they cancel each other out, creating a dark fringe.
*   Particles would simply pass through the slits and create two distinct bright bands directly behind the slits; only waves can spread out (diffract) and overlap to form an interference pattern.

**Quantitative Measurements:**
From the spacing of the fringes on the screen, one can calculate the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the light used. The relationship is given by the formula:
$$\lambda = \frac{xd}{L}$$
Where:
*   $\lambda$ = wavelength of the light.
*   $x$ = the distance between adjacent bright (or dark) fringes.
*   $d$ = the distance between the two slits.
*   $L$ = the perpendicular distance from the slits to the viewing screen.

### How to Solve
1.  **Understand the "Qualitative" requirement:** You must explicitly mention **interference**. If you explain it as "light bending," you are describing diffraction, which is a precursor, but the *interference* of those diffracted waves is what proves the wave nature. Focus on the concept of superposition—the adding and subtracting of wave amplitudes.
2.  **Understand the "Quantitative" requirement:** You are expected to show the mathematical formula that links the macroscopic, measurable variables ($x, d, L$) to the microscopic property ($\lambda$).
3.  **Structuring for 6 marks:** 
    *   Allocate 3 marks for the qualitative explanation (mentioning diffraction, superposition, constructive vs. destructive interference).
    *   Allocate 3 marks for the quantitative explanation (stating the formula, defining the variables, and explaining that the experiment allows for the determination of the light's wavelength).
