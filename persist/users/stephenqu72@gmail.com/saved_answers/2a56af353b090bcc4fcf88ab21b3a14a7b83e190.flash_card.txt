### Front
Why do larger, stable nuclei require a higher neutron-to-proton ratio compared to lighter nuclei?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The short-range strong nuclear force must overcome long-range electrostatic repulsion between protons. Neutrons provide additional attraction without adding repulsion.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** Explaining nuclear stability, alpha decay, or why the "belt of stability" curves towards higher neutron numbers as $Z$ increases.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to mention that the strong nuclear force is *short-range* while electrostatic repulsion is *long-range*, meaning protons on opposite sides of a large nucleus repel each other, but cannot attract each other.
