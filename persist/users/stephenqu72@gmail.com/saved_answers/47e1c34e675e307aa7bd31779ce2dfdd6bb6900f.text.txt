### Answer
The caesium-133 atom is used to define the **second**, the SI unit of time. One second is defined as the duration of exactly $9,192,631,770$ periods of the radiation corresponding to the transition between the two hyperfine levels of the ground state of the caesium-133 atom. 

Because the **metre** (the SI unit of distance) is defined as the distance light travels in a vacuum in $1/299,792,458$ of a second, the precision of the caesium atomic clock provides the necessary standard to define distance accurately.
