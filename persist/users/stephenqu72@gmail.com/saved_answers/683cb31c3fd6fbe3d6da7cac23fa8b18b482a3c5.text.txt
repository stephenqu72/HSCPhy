To determine the correct vector for the object's acceleration, we must apply the principles of uniform circular motion.

### Answer
The correct option is **(C)**.

### Explanation
1.  **Uniform Circular Motion Definition:** The object is moving in a circular path at a constant speed. In physics, even if the speed is constant, the velocity is changing because the *direction* of the motion is constantly changing.
2.  **Centripetal Acceleration:** Acceleration is defined as the rate of change of velocity ($\vec{a} = \frac{\Delta \vec{v}}{\Delta t}$). For an object in uniform circular motion, the acceleration vector is always directed toward the **center of the circle**. This is known as centripetal acceleration ($a_c$).
3.  **Analyzing the Diagram:** 
    *   The object is located at the leftmost point of the circular path.
    *   For the object to maintain its circular trajectory, the net force (and therefore the acceleration) must point toward the center of the circle.
    *   Looking at the circular path, the center lies to the right and slightly upward relative to the object's current position.
    *   Vector **(C)** points toward the interior/center of the circular path. 
    *   Vector (A) points straight down (tangent to the path at a different point or representing velocity).
    *   Vector (B) points away from the circle.
    *   Vector (D) points horizontally away from the center.

Therefore, vector **(C)** is the only vector that points toward the center of the circle, which is the required direction for centripetal acceleration.
