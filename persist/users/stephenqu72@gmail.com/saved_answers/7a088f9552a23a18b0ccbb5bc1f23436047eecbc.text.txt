To find the energy of a photon, we use the relationship between energy and frequency established by Planck's law:

$$E = hf$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   Frequency ($f$) = $102.8\text{ MHz} = 102.8 \times 10^6\text{ Hz} = 1.028 \times 10^8\text{ Hz}$
    *   Planck's constant ($h$) is a standard physical constant: $6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$

2.  **Substitute the values into the formula:**
    $$E = (6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J}\cdot\text{s}) \times (1.028 \times 10^8\text{ Hz})$$

3.  **Calculate the result:**
    $$E \approx 6.811528 \times 10^{-26}\text{ J}$$

Rounding this to four significant figures gives $6.812 \times 10^{-26}\text{ J}$.
