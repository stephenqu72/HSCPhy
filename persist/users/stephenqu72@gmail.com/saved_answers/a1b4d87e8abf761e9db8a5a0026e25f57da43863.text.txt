To solve this question, we need to understand the fundamental differences between light sources in terms of **coherence** (the maintenance of a constant phase relationship) and **monochromaticity** (the range of wavelengths produced).

### Analysis of the options:

*   **A. Light from a laser is coherent and has a very narrow range of wavelengths.**
    *   **Correct.** A laser produces light through stimulated emission, which results in light waves that are in phase (coherent) and typically of a single frequency or a very narrow band of wavelengths (monochromatic).
*   **B. Light from an incandescent lamp is coherent and has a range of wavelengths.**
    *   **Incorrect.** An incandescent lamp produces light through blackbody radiation (thermal agitation of atoms). This process produces light waves with random phases, meaning the light is **incoherent**, and it emits across a broad continuous spectrum of wavelengths (white light).
*   **C. Light from an incandescent lamp is incoherent and has a very narrow range of wavelengths.**
    *   **Incorrect.** While it is correctly identified as incoherent, it is false to say it has a narrow range of wavelengths. Incandescent lamps emit a broad, continuous range of wavelengths.
*   **D. Light from a single-colour light-emitting diode (LED) is coherent and contains a very wide range of wavelengths.**
    *   **Incorrect.** LEDs produce light via spontaneous emission (electroluminescence). This light is **incoherent**. While it has a narrower range of wavelengths than an incandescent bulb, it is not "wide," and it is certainly not coherent.

### How to approach this:
1.  **Identify the light source:** Recall the mechanism for each. Lasers (stimulated emission) are unique for their coherence and monochromatic nature. Incandescent bulbs (thermal) are broad-spectrum and incoherent.
2.  **Define Terms:** 
    *   **Coherent:** Waves have a fixed phase relationship (e.g., laser).
    *   **Incoherent:** Waves have random phase relationships (e.g., light bulb, the Sun).
    *   **Narrow range:** Near-monochromatic (e.g., laser).
3.  **Evaluate:** Match these definitions to the provided choices. Choice **A** is the only statement that accurately describes the physics of a laser.

The correct answer is **A**.
