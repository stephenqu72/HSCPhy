### Explanation
To solve this problem, you need to understand the phenomenon of **diffraction**, which is the bending of waves around obstacles or through openings.

1.  **The Principle of Diffraction:** For a wave to undergo significant diffraction, the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the wave must be comparable in size to the dimensions of the opening (the aperture) it is passing through. If the opening is much larger than the wavelength, the wave will primarily travel in a straight line (ray-like behavior).
2.  **Sound vs. Light:**
    *   **Sound:** Human speech has a wavelength typically in the range of $0.5\text{ m}$ to $2\text{ m}$. A doorway is also approximately $1\text{ m}$ wide. Because the wavelength of sound is **comparable** to the width of the doorway, sound waves bend significantly around the door frame, allowing them to reach the teacher in the corridor.
    *   **Light:** Visible light has an extremely small wavelength (in the order of $10^{-7}\text{ m}$). Since the doorway is vast compared to the wavelength of light, light undergoes negligible diffraction and travels in straight lines. This is why the teacher cannot "see" around the corner to observe her students.
3.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   (A) and (B) are physically incorrect regarding the speeds of sound and light.
    *   (C) is incorrect because if the wavelength were much smaller than the opening, diffraction would be minimal.
    *   **(D) is correct** because significant diffraction occurs when the wavelength is comparable to the width of the opening.
