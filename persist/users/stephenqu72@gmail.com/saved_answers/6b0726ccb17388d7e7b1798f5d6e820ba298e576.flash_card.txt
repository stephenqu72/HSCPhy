### Front
What is the condition for a vehicle to safely navigate a circular bend, and how is the required centripetal force calculated?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The available static friction ($F_f$) must be $\geq$ the required centripetal force ($F_c$).
- **Formula:** $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- **When to use it:** To determine if a vehicle will skid on a horizontal, unbanked curve.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that if $F_c > F_f$, the car will deviate from its circular path (skid) because there is insufficient friction to provide the necessary centripetal acceleration.
