### Front
For two projectiles launched horizontally from the same height, how are their times of flight related, and what determines this time?

### Back
- Key idea: Time of flight is independent of horizontal velocity and depends only on vertical displacement and gravity.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$ (assuming $u_y = 0$).
- When to use it: Any horizontal projectile motion problem where vertical displacement is known.
- Common trap: Assuming a higher horizontal velocity increases the time of flight; horizontal and vertical components are independent.
