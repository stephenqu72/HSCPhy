To solve this physics problem, we need to use the formula for torque ($\tau$):

$$\tau = r \cdot F \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   **Torque ($\tau$):** $175\text{ Nm}$
*   **Length of the wrench ($r$):** $60\text{ cm} = 0.6\text{ m}$ (We must convert to SI units)
*   **Angle ($\theta$):** $60^\circ$

### 2. Rearrange the formula to solve for Force ($F$):
$$F = \frac{\tau}{r \cdot \sin(\theta)}$$

### 3. Substitute the values:
$$F = \frac{175}{0.6 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)}$$

Since $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$:

$$F = \frac{175}{0.6 \cdot 0.866}$$
$$F = \frac{175}{0.5196}$$
$$F \approx 336.79\text{ N}$$

**Wait, let's re-evaluate the interpretation of "angle at the circumference":**
In some HSC-level mechanics contexts, if the force is applied "at the circumference" or perpendicular to the handle in a way that suggests the angle given is *relative to the handle* (which is the standard definition of $\theta$ in torque problems), the calculation above holds. However, looking at the provided options, if the question implies the component of force perpendicular to the handle, we have calculated correctly ($336.8\text{ N}$).

Let's check if there is a common interpretation error. If the angle $\theta$ is defined relative to the normal (90 degrees), then $\sin(\theta)$ becomes $\cos(\theta)$. If we use $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$:
$$F = \frac{175}{0.6 \cdot 0.5} = \frac{175}{0.3} \approx 583.33\text{ N}$$
