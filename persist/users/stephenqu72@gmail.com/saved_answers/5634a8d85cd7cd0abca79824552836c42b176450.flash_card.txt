### Front
How does the required centripetal force change if the angular velocity ($\omega$) of an object in uniform circular motion is doubled, while mass and radius remain constant?

### Back
- Key idea: Centripetal force is proportional to the square of angular velocity ($F_c \propto \omega^2$).
- Formula / law / constant: $F_c = mr\omega^2$
- When to use it: Any scenario involving circular motion where angular velocity or orbital speed is varied.
- Common trap: Assuming a linear relationship (e.g., guessing $2F$ instead of $2^2F = 4F$).
