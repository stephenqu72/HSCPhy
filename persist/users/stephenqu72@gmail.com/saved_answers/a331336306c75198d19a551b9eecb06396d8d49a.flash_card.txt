### Front
What is the physical definition and formula for escape velocity ($v_e$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The minimum initial velocity required for an object to reach an infinite distance from a gravitational source, where its total mechanical energy is zero.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$ (derived from $E_{total} = K + U = 0$)
- **When to use it:** When calculating the speed needed to overcome a gravitational field regardless of atmospheric conditions or trajectory.
- **Common trap:** Confusing it with orbital velocity ($v_o = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$); remember that escape velocity is $\sqrt{2}$ times larger than orbital velocity.
