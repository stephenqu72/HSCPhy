#### Question 11
**Answer: (C)**

**Explanation:**
1.  **Maximum Current:** In an AC generator, the induced emf ($\varepsilon$) is directly proportional to the current ($I = \varepsilon/R$). Therefore, the current is at a maximum when the magnitude of the emf is at its maximum. Looking at the graph, point $B$ corresponds to the peak value ($10\text{ V}$), so the maximum current occurs at $B$.
2.  **Maximum Flux:** Magnetic flux is defined as $\Phi_B = BA\cos(\theta)$. According to Faraday's Law, the induced emf is proportional to the rate of change of flux: $\varepsilon = -N \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt}$.
    *   When the emf is zero (at point $A$), the rate of change of flux is zero. This happens when the flux is at its maximum (or minimum) value because the slope of the flux-time graph is zero at those points.
    *   Therefore, the maximum flux occurs when the emf is zero, which is at point $A$.
