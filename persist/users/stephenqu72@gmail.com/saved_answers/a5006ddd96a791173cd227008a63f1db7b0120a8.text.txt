To find the acceleration due to gravity on the surface of Planet Y, we use the formula for gravitational acceleration at the surface of a spherical body:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Where:
- $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
- $M$ is the mass of the planet.
- $r$ is the radius of the planet.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1. **Define Earth's gravity:**
   Let the mass of Earth be $M_E$ and its radius be $R_E$. The acceleration due to gravity on Earth is:
   $$g = \frac{GM_E}{R_E^2}$$

2. **Define Planet Y's parameters:**
   We are told Planet Y has twice the mass of Earth ($M_Y = 2M_E$) and half the radius of Earth ($r_Y = \frac{1}{2}R_E$).

3. **Calculate gravity for Planet Y ($g_Y$):**
   Substitute the new values into the formula:
   $$g_Y = \frac{G(2M_E)}{(\frac{1}{2}R_E)^2}$$

4. **Simplify the expression:**
   $$g_Y = \frac{2GM_E}{\frac{1}{4}R_E^2}$$
   $$g_Y = 2 \times 4 \times \frac{GM_E}{R_E^2}$$
   $$g_Y = 8 \left( \frac{GM_E}{R_E^2} \right)$$

5. **Substitute $g$ back in:**
   Since $g = \frac{GM_E}{R_E^2}$, we get:
   $$g_Y = 8g$$
