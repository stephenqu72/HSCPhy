### Front
How can the speed of microwaves be determined using a standing wave pattern in a microwave oven?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The distance between consecutive "hot spots" (antinodes) is exactly half a wavelength ($\frac{\lambda}{2}$).
- **Formula:** $v = f\lambda$, where $\lambda = 2 \times (\text{distance between hot spots})$.
- **When to use it:** When calculating wave speed from standing wave interference patterns.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to double the measured distance between antinodes to get the full wavelength ($\lambda$).
