### **Part (a) Solution & Explanation**

**Question:** Describe the change in energy of the asteroid as it approaches the Earth. (2 marks)

**Answer:**
As the asteroid approaches Earth, its **gravitational potential energy decreases** (it becomes more negative because it is moving closer to the center of mass). Due to the law of conservation of energy, this loss in potential energy results in an **increase in the asteroid's kinetic energy**, meaning its speed increases.

**Teacher's Tip:**
To get both marks here, you must mention *both* types of energy. Don't just say "it speeds up"—explain *why* by referencing the conversion from gravitational potential energy to kinetic energy.

### **Part (b) Solution & Explanation**

**Question:** Use the law of conservation of energy to calculate the asteroid's speed when it reaches Earth's surface. You may neglect the effects of Earth's atmosphere on the asteroid. (4 marks)

**Step 1: Identify the Principle and Variables**
We use the **Law of Conservation of Mechanical Energy**. Since there is no friction (neglecting atmosphere), the total energy at the start must equal the total energy at the surface.

$$E_{\text{initial}} = E_{\text{final}}$$
$$K_i + U_i = K_f + U_f$$

Where:
*   $v_i = 12 \text{ km/s} = 12,000 \text{ m/s}$ (Initial velocity)
*   $r_i = 10 R_E$ (Initial distance from Earth's center)
*   $r_f = R_E$ (Final distance at the surface)
*   $m$ is the mass of the asteroid.
*   $M$ is the mass of the Earth.
*   $G$ is the gravitational constant.

**Step 2: Set up the Equation**
The formula for gravitational potential energy is $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$. Let's plug our values into the conservation equation:

$$\frac{1}{2}mv_i^2 - \frac{GMm}{10R_E} = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 - \frac{GMm}{R_E}$$

Notice that the mass of the asteroid ($m$) appears in every term, so we can cancel it out. This is a key concept: the motion of a falling object in a gravitational field does not depend on its own mass.

$$\frac{1}{2}v_i^2 - \frac{GM}{10R_E} = \frac{1}{2}v_f^2 - \frac{GM}{R_E}$$

**Step 3: Rearrange to solve for $v_f$**
Multiply the entire equation by $2$ to simplify:
$$v_i^2 - \frac{2GM}{10R_E} = v_f^2 - \frac{2GM}{R_E}$$

Move terms to isolate $v_f^2$:
$$v_f^2 = v_i^2 + \frac{2GM}{R_E} - \frac{2GM}{10R_E}$$
$$v_f^2 = v_i^2 + \frac{2GM}{R_E} (1 - 0.1)$$
$$v_f^2 = v_i^2 + 1.8\left(\frac{GM}{R_E}\right)$$

**Step 4: Substitute known values**
To solve this without being given $G$ or $M$, we can use the relationship for surface gravity: $g = \frac{GM}{R_E^2}$, which means $\frac{GM}{R_E} = g R_E$.
Using standard values: $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ and $R_E \approx 6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$.

$$v_f^2 = (12,000)^2 + 1.8(9.8 \times 6.37 \times 10^6)$$
$$v_f^2 = 144,000,000 + 112,366,800$$
$$v_f^2 = 256,366,800$$
$$v_f = \sqrt{256,366,800}$$
$$v_f \approx 16,011.46 \text{ m/s}$$

**Final Answer:**
The asteroid's speed when it reaches Earth's surface is approximately **$16.0 \text{ km/s}$**.
