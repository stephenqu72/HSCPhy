#### Part (a)
**Answer:**
All objects at a temperature above absolute zero emit electromagnetic radiation, known as black-body radiation. As the temperature of the tungsten increases, two key changes occur according to Wien’s Displacement Law and the Stefan-Boltzmann Law:
1. **Wien’s Law:** The peak wavelength of the emitted radiation shifts towards shorter wavelengths (higher frequencies) as temperature increases. Initially, the peak is in the infrared; as it heats to $700^\circ\text{C}$, the peak moves towards the red end of the visible spectrum. At $2700^\circ\text{C}$, the peak shifts towards the blue/violet end, causing the object to appear white (a mix of all visible colours).
2. **Stefan-Boltzmann Law:** The total power emitted (intensity) increases significantly with temperature ($P \propto T^4$). This explains why the object becomes "bright" as it gets hotter.
