To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of Special Relativity, specifically the concept of **length contraction**.

### Explanation

1.  **Understand Length Contraction:** According to Einstein's theory of Special Relativity, objects moving at relativistic speeds undergo length contraction in the direction of their motion. The formula for the contracted length $L'$ is:
    $$L' = L \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
    where $L$ is the proper length (the length measured by an observer at rest relative to the object) and $v$ is the velocity of the object.

2.  **Analyze the dimensions:**
    *   **Length ($L$):** The carriage is moving horizontally at $v = 0.8c$. Since this is the direction of motion, the measured length $L'$ will be contracted.
        $$L' = 5 \sqrt{1 - (0.8)^2}$$
        $$L' = 5 \sqrt{1 - 0.64}$$
        $$L' = 5 \sqrt{0.36}$$
        $$L' = 5 \times 0.6 = 3 \text{ m}$$
    *   **Height ($H$):** Length contraction **only** occurs in the direction of motion. The height $H$ is measured perpendicular to the direction of motion. In Special Relativity, dimensions perpendicular to the direction of motion do not change. Therefore, the measured height $H'$ remains the same as the proper height $H$.
        $$H' = H = 2.5 \text{ m}$$

3.  **Conclusion:** The observer will measure a length $L' = 3 \text{ m}$ and a height $H' = 2.5 \text{ m}$. Comparing this to the provided options, we find that **Option C** is the correct answer.
