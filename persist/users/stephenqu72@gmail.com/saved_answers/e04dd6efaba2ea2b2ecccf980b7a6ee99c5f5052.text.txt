To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of **Special Relativity**. Because the velocity of the UFO is a significant fraction of the speed of light ($c$), we cannot use the classical momentum formula ($p = mv$). Instead, we must use the relativistic momentum equation:

$$p = \gamma mv_0$$

Where:
*   $m_0$ is the rest mass ($1050\text{ kg}$)
*   $v$ is the velocity ($90,000\text{ km/s} = 9 \times 10^7\text{ m/s}$)
*   $c$ is the speed of light ($\approx 3 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$)
*   $\gamma$ (the Lorentz factor) is defined as $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$

### Step 1: Calculate the ratio $\frac{v}{c}$
$$\frac{v}{c} = \frac{9 \times 10^7}{3 \times 10^8} = 0.3$$

### Step 2: Calculate the Lorentz factor $\gamma$
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.3)^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.09}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.91}} \approx 1.04828$$

### Step 3: Calculate relativistic momentum $p$
$$p = \gamma \times m_0 \times v$$
$$p = 1.04828 \times 1050\text{ kg} \times (9 \times 10^7\text{ m/s})$$
$$p \approx 1.04828 \times 9.45 \times 10^{10}$$
$$p \approx 9.906 \times 10^{10}\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

Rounding to three significant figures, we get $9.91 \times 10^{10}\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$.
