To calculate the de Broglie wavelength of the tennis ball, we use the de Broglie equation:

$$\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$$

where:
*   $h$ is Planck's constant ($6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J s}$)
*   $m$ is the mass of the object (in kg)
*   $v$ is the velocity of the object (in m/s)

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Convert units to SI units:**
*   **Mass ($m$):** The mass is given as $59.4\text{ g}$. We must convert this to kilograms:
    $$m = 59.4\text{ g} = 0.0594\text{ kg}$$
*   **Velocity ($v$):** The velocity is given as $170\text{ km h}^{-1}$. To convert to m/s, divide by $3.6$:
    $$v = \frac{170}{3.6} \approx 47.222\text{ m/s}$$

**2. Substitute the values into the de Broglie equation:**
$$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J s}}{(0.0594\text{ kg}) \times (47.222\text{ m/s})}$$

**3. Perform the calculation:**
$$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{2.805}$$
$$\lambda \approx 2.362 \times 10^{-34}\text{ m}$$
