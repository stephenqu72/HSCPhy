To answer these questions, we use Wien’s Displacement Law, which is given by:
$$\lambda_{peak} T = b$$
Where $\lambda_{peak}$ is the peak wavelength, $T$ is the absolute temperature (in Kelvin), and $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant (approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m K}$).

### Question 18
**Analysis:**
The students measured the temperature of the *glass* ($1900\text{ K}$), not the *filament*. The filament must be significantly hotter than the glass to emit visible light. Therefore, the actual temperature of the filament $T_{actual} > 1900\text{ K}$.
Given $\lambda_{peak} = 2 \times 10^{-3}\text{ mm} = 2 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}$, we calculate the observed product:
$$\lambda_{peak} \times T_{measured} = (2 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}) \times (1900\text{ K}) = 3.8 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m K}$$
Since the true temperature $T_{actual}$ is higher than the measured $1900\text{ K}$, the real value of $b$ must be greater than the calculated product:
$$b = \lambda_{peak} \times T_{actual} > 3.8 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m K}$$
**Correct Answer: D**
