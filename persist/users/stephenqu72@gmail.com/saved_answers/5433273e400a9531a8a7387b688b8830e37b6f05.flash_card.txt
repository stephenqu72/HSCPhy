### Front
How do you determine the direction of the magnetic field ($B$) around a current-carrying wire?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The Right-Hand Grip Rule.
- **Law/Figure:** Point right thumb in direction of conventional current ($I$); fingers curl in direction of $B$-field lines.
- **When to use it:** Whenever a current flows through a conductor to find the orientation of the surrounding magnetic field.
- **Common trap:** Using your left hand instead of your right, or confusing conventional current ($I$) with electron flow (which is opposite).
