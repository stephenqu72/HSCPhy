### Front
What is the relationship between a galaxy’s recession velocity and its distance from Earth, and what does it indicate?

### Back
- Key idea: Recession velocity is directly proportional to distance ($v \propto d$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = H_0d$ (Hubble-Lemaître Law); $H_0$ is the Hubble constant.
- When to use it: To calculate the age of the universe or determine the rate of cosmic expansion from observational data.
- Common trap: Mistaking the linear relationship for an inverse relationship; remember that the constant of proportionality ($H_0$) represents the rate of expansion, not a gravitational force.
