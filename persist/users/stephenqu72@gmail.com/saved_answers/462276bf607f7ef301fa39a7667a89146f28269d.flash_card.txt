### Front
How do you determine the direction of the magnetic field around a long straight current-carrying wire?

### Back
- Key idea: The Right-Hand Grip Rule.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Point right thumb in the direction of conventional current ($I$); fingers curl in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$).
- When to use it: To find the orientation of circular magnetic field lines around a straight conductor.
- Common trap: Using the left hand instead of the right hand, or confusing electron flow with conventional current (always use conventional current $I$ for the thumb).
