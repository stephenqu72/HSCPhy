To determine the correct angle $\theta$ for a banked track where a car can travel without relying on friction, we use the physics of circular motion on a banked surface.

### Solution

For a vehicle to navigate a banked curve without friction, the horizontal component of the normal force must provide the required centripetal force. The formula relating banking angle, velocity, radius, and gravity is:

$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

**Given values:**
*   Velocity ($v$) = $20\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Radius ($r$) = $100\text{ m}$
*   Acceleration due to gravity ($g$) $\approx 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$

**Calculation:**
1. Substitute the values into the equation:
   $$\tan(\theta) = \frac{20^2}{100 \times 9.8}$$
   $$\tan(\theta) = \frac{400}{980}$$
   $$\tan(\theta) \approx 0.40816$$

2. Solve for $\theta$:
   $$\theta = \arctan(0.40816)$$
   $$\theta \approx 22.2^\circ$$

Comparing this result to the given options:
A. $15^\circ$
B. $22^\circ$
C. $33^\circ$
D. $40^\circ$

The most accurate angle is $22^\circ$. Therefore, the correct option is **B**.

### How to solve this type of question:
1. **Identify the Physics principle:** Recognise that "safe driving" on a banked track without friction implies that the net centripetal force is provided entirely by the horizontal component of the normal force.
2. **Recall the formula:** Always keep the banking angle formula $\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$ in your Physics reference toolkit.
3. **Check units:** Ensure velocity is in $\text{m s}^{-1}$ and radius is in metres. If they are not, convert them before plugging them into the formula.
4. **Calculate:** Use a calculator to find the inverse tangent ($\arctan$) of the calculated ratio.
5. **Select the closest match:** In multiple-choice questions, choose the option that most closely aligns with your calculation.
