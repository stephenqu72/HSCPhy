### Answer
Both nuclear fission and nuclear fusion release energy due to the conversion of mass into energy, a principle described by Einstein's mass-energy equivalence equation, $E = \Delta mc^2$. In both processes, the total mass of the products is slightly less than the total mass of the reactants; this missing mass is known as the **mass defect**.

**Nuclear Fission:**
Fission occurs when a heavy, unstable nucleus (such as Uranium-235) splits into smaller, more stable daughter nuclei after absorbing a neutron. Because the binding energy per nucleon is higher for these intermediate-mass nuclei than for the original heavy nucleus, the system moves to a state of lower potential energy. The "mass defect" ($\Delta m$) represents the energy released as kinetic energy of the products and gamma radiation.
*   **Example:** The fission of Uranium-235:
    $$^{235}_{92}\text{U} + ^1_0n \rightarrow ^{141}_{56}\text{Ba} + ^{92}_{36}\text{Kr} + 3^1_0n + \text{Energy}$$

**Nuclear Fusion:**
Fusion occurs when light nuclei (such as isotopes of hydrogen) combine to form a heavier nucleus. The resulting nucleus has a higher binding energy per nucleon than the original light nuclei. The mass of the resulting nucleus is less than the sum of the masses of the reacting nuclei. This difference in mass is released as significant amounts of energy.
*   **Example:** The fusion of Deuterium and Tritium in the Sun or experimental reactors:
    $$^2_1\text{H} + ^3_1\text{H} \rightarrow ^4_2\text{He} + ^1_0n + \text{Energy}$$

2.  **Explain the trend of Binding Energy:** Discuss the *binding energy per nucleon curve*. Mention that energy is released whenever the nucleons end up in a more stable configuration (higher binding energy per nucleon). Fission moves heavy nuclei toward the peak of the curve; fusion moves light nuclei toward the peak.
3.  **Differentiate the processes:** Clearly separate your explanation for fission and fusion.
4.  **Include Equations/Examples:** The question specifically asks for "examples." Providing a balanced nuclear equation for both processes is essential for full marks, as it demonstrates technical accuracy.
