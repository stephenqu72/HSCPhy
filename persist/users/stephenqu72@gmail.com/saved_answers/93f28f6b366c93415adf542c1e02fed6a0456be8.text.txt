### Answer

Maxwell’s prediction of the velocity of electromagnetic waves was a pivotal moment in the development of classical physics. It contributed to the theory in the following ways:

1.  **Unification of Physics:** By calculating the speed of electromagnetic waves using only electrical and magnetic constants ($\varepsilon_0$ and $\mu_0$), Maxwell demonstrated that light is an electromagnetic wave. This unified the previously separate fields of optics, electricity, and magnetism into one cohesive theoretical framework.
2.  **Theoretical Calculation:** Maxwell derived the speed of these waves using the formula:
    $$v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \varepsilon_0}}$$
    Substituting the known values for the permeability of free space ($\mu_0$) and the permittivity of free space ($\varepsilon_0$) yielded a result approximately equal to the experimentally measured speed of light ($c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$).
3.  **Validation of Wave Nature:** This discovery provided strong theoretical evidence that light propagates as an oscillating electromagnetic field, confirming the wave nature of light and setting the stage for the transition from Newtonian mechanics to the modern understanding of electromagnetism.
