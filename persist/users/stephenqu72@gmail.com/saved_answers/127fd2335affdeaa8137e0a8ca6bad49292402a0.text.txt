To find the magnitude of the centripetal force acting on the ball, we need to follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   Mass $m = 20\text{ g} = 0.020\text{ kg}$
*   Length of string $L = 1.10\text{ m}$
*   Angle with the horizontal $\theta = 30^\circ$
*   Angular velocity $\omega = 4\pi\text{ rad s}^{-1}$

### 2. Determine the radius of the circular path:
The radius $r$ of the horizontal circle is the horizontal component of the string's length:
$$r = L \cos(30^\circ)$$
$$r = 1.10 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 1.10 \times 0.866 = 0.9526\text{ m}$$

### 3. Calculate the centripetal force:
The formula for centripetal force $F_c$ in terms of angular velocity is:
$$F_c = m r \omega^2$$

Substitute the known values into the equation:
$$F_c = 0.020 \times (1.10 \cos 30^\circ) \times (4\pi)^2$$
$$F_c = 0.020 \times 0.9526 \times (16 \times \pi^2)$$
$$F_c \approx 0.019052 \times 157.91$$
$$F_c \approx 3.008\text{ N}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (consistent with the data provided), we get **$3.0\text{ N}$**.

Comparing this result to the given options:
A. $2.9\text{ N}$
B. $3.0\text{ N}$
C. $3.3\text{ N}$
D. $3.5\text{ N}$

The correct answer is **B**.

### Explanation Summary:
To solve this, we recognized that the ball undergoes uniform circular motion. The centripetal force required to keep the object in this motion is provided by the horizontal component of the tension in the string. We calculated the radius of the horizontal circular path using trigonometry ($r = L\cos\theta$), then applied the standard circular motion formula $F_c = mr\omega^2$.
