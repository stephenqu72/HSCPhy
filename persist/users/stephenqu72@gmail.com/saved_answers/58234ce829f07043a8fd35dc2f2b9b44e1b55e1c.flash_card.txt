### Front
Why should centripetal force ($F_c$) never be drawn as an individual vector on a free-body diagram (FBD)?

### Back
- Key idea: $F_c$ is not a "real" physical force; it is the **net (resultant) force** produced by the vector sum of existing real forces.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{net} = \sum F = F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: Whenever drawing FBDs for objects in circular motion (e.g., banked tracks, conical pendulums).
- Common trap: Drawing $F_c$ as an additional force vector, which double-counts the net effect of the actual forces (like gravity, tension, or normal force).
