To solve this problem, we need to resolve the weight force of the car into components relative to the inclined plane.

### Answer
The component of the weight force acting parallel to the ramp is $4900\text{ N}$ (or $4.9 \times 10^3\text{ N}$) acting down the slope.

### Explanation
1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   Mass of the car, $m = 1000\text{ kg}$
    *   Angle of inclination, $\theta = 30^{\circ}$
    *   Acceleration due to gravity, $g = 9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$

2.  **Understand the physics:**
    When an object is on an inclined plane, its weight force ($W = mg$) acts vertically downwards. We can resolve this force into two perpendicular components:
    *   The component perpendicular to the surface of the ramp: $W_{\perp} = mg \cos(\theta)$
    *   The component parallel to the surface of the ramp: $W_{\parallel} = mg \sin(\theta)$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    Using the formula for the parallel component:
    $$W_{\parallel} = mg \sin(30^{\circ})$$
    $$W_{\parallel} = 1000 \times 9.8 \times \sin(30^{\circ})$$
    Since $\sin(30^{\circ}) = 0.5$:
    $$W_{\parallel} = 1000 \times 9.8 \times 0.5$$
    $$W_{\parallel} = 4900\text{ N}$$

This component always acts directly down the slope of the ramp, independent of the car's circular motion.
