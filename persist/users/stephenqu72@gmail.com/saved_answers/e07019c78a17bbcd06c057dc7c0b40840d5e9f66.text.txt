### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) Absorption spectra**.

---

### Explanation
To determine the type of spectrum observed from the Sun, we must look at its physical structure:

1.  **The Core/Photosphere:** The dense, hot interior of the Sun acts as a black body, which emits a **continuous spectrum** containing all wavelengths of light.
2.  **The Solar Atmosphere:** Surrounding the photosphere is the Sun's cooler, thinner outer atmosphere (the chromosphere and corona). As the continuous light from the hot interior travels outward, it passes through the cooler gases of the solar atmosphere.
3.  **The Process:** The atoms in this cooler outer layer absorb specific wavelengths of light that correspond to their unique energy level transitions (electrons jumping between shells). 
4.  **The Result:** Because specific wavelengths are removed (absorbed) from the continuous spectrum, what we observe on Earth is a continuous rainbow-like spectrum interrupted by dark lines. These dark lines are known as **Fraunhofer lines**. 

Therefore, the spectrum of the Sun is classified as an **absorption spectrum** (or an absorption line spectrum).

*   **Why (A) is incorrect:** While the core produces a continuous spectrum, we do not see it in isolation; it is modified by the outer layers.
*   **Why (B) is incorrect:** Emission spectra consist of bright lines on a dark background, produced by hot, low-density gases (like a nebula), not a star's light passing through its own cooler atmosphere.
*   **Why (D) is incorrect:** "Broad spectra" is not standard terminology used to describe stellar classifications in this context.
