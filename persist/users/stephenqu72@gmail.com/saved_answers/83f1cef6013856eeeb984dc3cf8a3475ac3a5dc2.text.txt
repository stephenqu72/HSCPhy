The correct answer is **(D)**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must analyze the historical contributions of James Clerk Maxwell to the field of electromagnetism compared to other key physicists.

*   **(A) Prediction of the existence of electromagnetic waves:** Maxwell derived the "Maxwell's Equations," which demonstrated that electric and magnetic fields propagate as waves. This is a foundational part of his work.
*   **(B) Prediction of the speed of electromagnetic waves:** By solving his wave equations, Maxwell calculated the velocity of these waves to be $v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$. Upon substituting the known values for the permeability and permittivity of free space, he found the value was approximately $3 \times 10^8$ m/s, which matched the known speed of light. This led him to the conclusion that light itself is an electromagnetic wave.
*   **(C) Unification of electricity and magnetism:** Maxwell is famous for synthesizing the work of Faraday, Gauss, and Ampère into a single mathematical framework (Maxwell's Equations). This effectively unified electricity and magnetism into the single field of electromagnetism.
*   **(D) Discovery of electromagnetic induction:** This discovery is attributed to **Michael Faraday** in 1831, not Maxwell. While Maxwell provided the mathematical explanation for electromagnetic induction (represented by the Maxwell-Faraday equation: $\nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t}$), the actual physical discovery of the phenomenon belongs to Faraday.

Since the question asks which cannot be attributed to Maxwell, (D) is the correct choice.
