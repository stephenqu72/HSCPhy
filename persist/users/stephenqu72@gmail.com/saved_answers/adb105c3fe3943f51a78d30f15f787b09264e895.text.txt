### Explanation
To solve this, we must compare the two primary hydrogen-fusion mechanisms in main-sequence stars:

1.  **Proton-Proton (P-P) Chain:**
    *   This is the dominant reaction in lower-mass, cooler main-sequence stars (like our Sun).
    *   Because the electrostatic repulsion between protons is less severe at lower temperatures, this reaction proceeds at a **relatively slow** rate.
    *   It requires temperatures of at least $10^7$ K to begin.

2.  **CNO Cycle (Carbon-Nitrogen-Oxygen cycle):**
    *   This process uses carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen as catalysts to fuse hydrogen into helium.
    *   The CNO cycle is highly temperature-sensitive. It becomes the dominant process in **more massive, hotter** main-sequence stars where core temperatures exceed approximately $1.7 \times 10^7$ K.
    *   Because of its extreme temperature dependence, the reaction proceeds at a **relatively fast** rate compared to the P-P chain.
