### Answer
The correct answer is **C. 1.0c**.

---

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we apply one of the fundamental postulates of Albert Einstein's **Special Theory of Relativity**.

1.  **The Second Postulate:** Einstein’s second postulate states that the speed of light in a vacuum ($c$) is constant for all observers, regardless of their motion or the motion of the light source.
2.  **Application:** Even though the laser pointer is mounted on a jet moving at $0.4c$ relative to the Earth, the speed of the light emitted from that laser is independent of the jet's velocity.
3.  **Conclusion:** When the stationary observer on Earth measures the speed of the light emitted by the laser, they will measure the universal constant speed of light, which is exactly $c$ (or $1.0c$).

In classical (Galilean) mechanics, you might be tempted to add the speeds ($0.4c + 1.0c = 1.4c$), but this does not apply to electromagnetic radiation. In relativistic physics, the speed of light is an invariant quantity. Therefore, it remains $1.0c$ for all inertial observers.
