To solve this problem, we use the transformer turns ratio formula, which relates the number of turns in the coils to the voltages across them.

### Answer
The correct answer is **A. 70**.

### Explanation
The relationship between the primary voltage ($V_p$), secondary voltage ($V_s$), number of turns in the primary coil ($N_p$), and number of turns in the secondary coil ($N_s$) is given by the ideal transformer equation:

$$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Primary voltage ($V_p$) = $13.2\text{ kV} = 13,200\text{ V}$
*   Secondary voltage ($V_s$) = $240\text{ V}$
*   Primary turns ($N_p$) = $3,850$

**2. Rearrange the formula to solve for the secondary turns ($N_s$):**
$$N_s = N_p \times \frac{V_s}{V_p}$$

**3. Substitute the values into the equation:**
$$N_s = 3,850 \times \frac{240}{13,200}$$

**4. Perform the calculation:**
First, simplify the fraction:
$$\frac{240}{13,200} = \frac{24}{1,320} = \frac{1}{55}$$

Then:
$$N_s = 3,850 \times \frac{1}{55}$$
$$N_s = 70$$

Therefore, there are $70$ turns in the secondary coil.
