To solve for the energy released in this nuclear fusion process, we follow these steps based on the principle of mass-energy equivalence ($E = \Delta mc^2$).

### 1. Calculate the Mass Defect ($\Delta m$)
The mass defect is the difference between the total mass of the reactants and the total mass of the products.

**Reactants:** Two $^{2}_{1}H$ nuclei
Mass of reactants $= 2 \times 2.014102 \, \text{amu} = 4.028204 \, \text{amu}$

**Products:** One $^{3}_{2}He$ nucleus and one $^{1}_{0}n$
Mass of products $= 3.016050 \, \text{amu} + 1.008665 \, \text{amu} = 4.024715 \, \text{amu}$

**Mass Defect ($\Delta m$):**
$$\Delta m = (\text{Mass of reactants}) - (\text{Mass of products})$$
$$\Delta m = 4.028204 - 4.024715 = 0.003489 \, \text{amu}$$

### 2. Convert mass from amu to kg
To calculate energy in Joules, we must convert the mass defect into kilograms. We use the conversion factor $1 \, \text{amu} \approx 1.6605 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{kg}$.
$$\Delta m \, (\text{in kg}) = 0.003489 \times 1.6605 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{kg} \approx 5.7935 \times 10^{-30} \, \text{kg}$$

### 3. Calculate Energy ($E$)
Using Einstein's equation $E = \Delta mc^2$, where $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}$:
$$E = (5.7935 \times 10^{-30} \, \text{kg}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s})^2$$
$$E = 5.7935 \times 10^{-30} \times 9.00 \times 10^{16}$$
$$E \approx 5.21 \times 10^{-13} \, \text{J}$$
