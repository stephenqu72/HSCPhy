### Answer and Explanation

This question is based on the famous **Millikan’s Oil Drop Experiment**.

#### (a) Free Body Diagram
As the oil drop falls at a **constant speed**, the net force is zero. Therefore, there are two equal and opposite forces:
1.  **Weight ($mg$):** An arrow pointing straight down.
2.  **Drag force ($F_d$):** An arrow of the same length pointing straight up.

#### (b) Forces on the stationary drop
When the drop is stationary between the charged plates, the net force is zero. The two forces are:
1.  **Gravitational Force (Weight):** Acting downwards.
2.  **Electric Force ($F_e$):** Acting upwards (since the drop is negatively charged and must be repelled by the negative bottom plate or attracted by the positive top plate).

#### (c) Calculation of Charge
To hold the drop stationary, the electric force must balance the gravitational force:
$$F_e = F_g$$
$$qE = mg$$

Since the electric field between parallel plates is given by $E = \frac{V}{d}$, we substitute this into the equation:
$$q \left( \frac{V}{d} \right) = mg \implies q = \frac{mgd}{V}$$

**Given data:**
- $m = 2.93 \times 10^{-15} \text{ kg}$
- $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$
- $d = 7.71 \text{ mm} = 7.71 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$
- $V = 346 \text{ V}$

**Calculation:**
$$q = \frac{(2.93 \times 10^{-15}) \times 9.8 \times (7.71 \times 10^{-3})}{346}$$
$$q \approx 6.40 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$$

#### (d) Supporting the conclusion
Millikan concluded that charge is **quantized**, meaning it exists only in discrete packets. By observing the results $9.6 \times 10^{-19}$, $12.8 \times 10^{-19}$, and $3.2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$, we can see that each value is an integer multiple of $3.2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$ (specifically $3e$, $4e$, and $1e$ if we consider the elementary charge). This demonstrates that all measured charges are multiples of a fundamental base unit of charge.
