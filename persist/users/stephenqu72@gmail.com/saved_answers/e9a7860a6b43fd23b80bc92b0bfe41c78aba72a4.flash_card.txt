### Front
How does centripetal force ($F_c$) relate to velocity ($v$) and radius ($r$)?

### Back
- Key idea: $F_c$ is directly proportional to the square of the velocity ($v^2$) and inversely proportional to the radius ($r$).
- Formula: $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: To determine the net force required to maintain circular motion for an object of mass $m$.
- Common trap: Assuming $F_c \propto v$. $F_c$ is proportional to $v^2$; if velocity doubles, force quadruples.
