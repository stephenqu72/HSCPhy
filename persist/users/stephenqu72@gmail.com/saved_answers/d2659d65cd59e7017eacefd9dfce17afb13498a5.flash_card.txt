### Front
How do you determine the direction of the frictional force for a vehicle on a banked track?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Compare the vehicle's actual velocity ($v$) to the "ideal" velocity ($v_{ideal}$) where friction is zero.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v_{ideal} = \sqrt{rg \tan\theta}$
- **When to use it:** If $v > v_{ideal}$, the vehicle tends to slide *up* the slope, so friction acts *down* the slope. If $v < v_{ideal}$, the vehicle tends to slide *down* the slope, so friction acts *up* the slope.
- **Common trap:** Assuming friction always acts up the slope; direction depends entirely on whether the vehicle is traveling faster or slower than the design speed of the bank.
