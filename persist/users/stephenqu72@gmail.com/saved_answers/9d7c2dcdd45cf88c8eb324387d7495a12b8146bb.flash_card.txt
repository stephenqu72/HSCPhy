### Front
How does the density of gravitational field lines relate to gravitational field strength ($g$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Field line density is directly proportional to the magnitude of the gravitational field strength ($g$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$; closer lines = stronger field.
- When to use it: To compare gravitational fields of different planets or at different distances from a mass.
- Common trap: Mistaking the number of lines drawn for total mass; it is the *density* (lines per unit area) that determines field strength.
