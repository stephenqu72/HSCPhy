### Front
What two fundamental conservation laws must be applied to solve nuclear decay equations?

### Back
- Key idea: Conservation of nucleon number (mass number) and atomic number (charge).
- Formula / law: 
  $\sum A_{\text{reactants}} = \sum A_{\text{products}}$
  $\sum Z_{\text{reactants}} = \sum Z_{\text{products}}$
- When to use it: Whenever a particle or nucleus undergoes radioactive decay to identify unknown products.
- Common trap: Forgetting to include the neutrino ($\nu_e$) or antineutrino ($\bar{\nu}_e$) in $\beta$ decay equations, which are essential for conserving lepton number.
