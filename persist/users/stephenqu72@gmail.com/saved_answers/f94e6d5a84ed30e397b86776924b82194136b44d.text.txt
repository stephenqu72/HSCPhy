To answer this question effectively for an HSC Physics exam, you need to demonstrate an understanding of the progression of the atomic model.

### Answer

**Three historical experiments:**
1.  **J.J. Thomson’s Cathode Ray Tube Experiment:** Identified the electron as a subatomic particle, leading to the "plum pudding" model.
2.  **Geiger-Marsden (Rutherford) Gold Foil Experiment:** Investigated the internal structure of the atom using alpha particle scattering.
3.  **Millikan’s Oil Drop Experiment:** Determined the charge and mass of the electron, providing evidence for the quantized nature of electric charge.

**Explanation of observations (Rutherford’s Gold Foil Experiment):**
In this experiment, alpha particles were fired at a thin sheet of gold foil. The expected observation, based on the plum pudding model, was that particles would pass straight through with minimal deflection. However, the researchers observed that:
*   Most particles passed straight through, suggesting the atom is mostly empty space.
*   A small percentage were deflected at large angles.
*   A very small number were reflected directly backward.

These observations forced a change in the model: the deflection of positively charged alpha particles indicated the existence of a small, dense, positively charged **nucleus** at the center of the atom, rather than the positive charge being spread throughout the atom's volume.

### How to solve this
1.  **Identify and List (1-2 marks):** Simply naming the experiments is not enough for full marks. You must ensure they are distinctly "historical experiments" that contributed to the classical model (Thomson, Rutherford, Millikan, Bohr, etc.).
2.  **Explain the "Mechanism" (3 marks):** When explaining the second part, do not just list the findings. You must link the **observations** (what was seen) to the **conclusion** (how the model was updated). 
    *   *Observation:* Alpha particles deflected.
    *   *Reasoning:* Electrostatic repulsion must exist between the positive alpha particles and a concentrated positive charge.
    *   *Result:* The planetary model of the atom (a dense nucleus surrounded by orbiting electrons).
3.  **Structure:** Use a clear subheading or bullet points to ensure the marker can easily identify both parts of the question.
