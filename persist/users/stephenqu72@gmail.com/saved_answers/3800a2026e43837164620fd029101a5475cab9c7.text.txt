### Answer and Explanation

#### (a) Derive an equation for the orbital velocity $v$
To find the orbital velocity, we equate the centripetal force required to keep the satellite in orbit to the gravitational force acting on it. Let $m$ be the mass of the satellite, $M$ the mass of the planet, $r$ the orbital radius, and $v$ the velocity.

1. Set the forces equal:
   $$\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GmM}{r^2}$$
2. Cancel $m$ from both sides and multiply by $r$:
   $$v^2 = \frac{GM}{r}$$
3. Take the square root:
   $$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

---

#### (b) Show that the total energy is half the gravitational potential energy
1. The Gravitational Potential Energy ($U$) is:
   $$U = -\frac{GmM}{r}$$
2. The Kinetic Energy ($K$) is:
   $$K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
3. Substitute $v^2 = \frac{GM}{r}$ (from part a) into the kinetic energy equation:
   $$K = \frac{1}{2}m\left(\frac{GM}{r}\right) = \frac{GmM}{2r}$$
4. The Total Energy ($E_T$) is $K + U$:
   $$E_T = \frac{GmM}{2r} - \frac{GmM}{r} = -\frac{GmM}{2r}$$
5. Comparing $E_T$ to $U$:
   $$E_T = \frac{1}{2} \left( -\frac{GmM}{r} \right) = \frac{1}{2}U$$

---

#### (c) Explain why gravitational potential energy is negative
Gravitational potential energy is defined as the work done by an external agent to bring an object from infinity (where potential energy is zero) to a distance $r$ from the planet. 
1. Since the gravitational force is attractive, the object "falls" toward the planet.
2. Because the field does positive work as the object moves from infinity to $r$, the object loses potential energy.
3. Starting from a reference point of zero at infinity, moving to a closer distance must therefore result in a negative value. It signifies that the object is in a "bound state," meaning energy must be added to the system to remove the satellite to infinity.
