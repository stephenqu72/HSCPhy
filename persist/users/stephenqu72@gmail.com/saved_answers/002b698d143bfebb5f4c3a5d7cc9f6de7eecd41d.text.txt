To solve this problem, we need to apply the physics of circular motion. Specifically, we are looking for the linear velocity required to generate a specific centripetal acceleration.

### Answer
The correct option is **(A) $50 \text{ ms}^{-1}$**.

### Explanation
1. **Identify the given variables:**
   - The diameter $d$ of the ring is $513 \text{ m}$. Since the radius $r$ is half of the diameter:
     $$r = \frac{513}{2} = 256.5 \text{ m}$$
   - The required centripetal acceleration $a_c$ is equal to Earth's gravity:
     $$a_c = 9.8 \text{ ms}^{-2}$$

2. **Select the relevant formula:**
   The formula for centripetal acceleration in terms of linear velocity $v$ and radius $r$ is:
   $$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$$

3. **Rearrange for velocity $v$:**
   $$v^2 = a_c \times r$$
   $$v = \sqrt{a_c \times r}$$

4. **Substitute the values and calculate:**
   $$v = \sqrt{9.8 \times 256.5}$$
   $$v = \sqrt{2513.7}$$
   $$v \approx 50.1368 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (consistent with the provided options), we get $v \approx 50 \text{ ms}^{-1}$. This matches option **(A)**.
