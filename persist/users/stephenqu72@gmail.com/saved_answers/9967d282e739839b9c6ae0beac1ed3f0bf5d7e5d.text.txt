### Explanation
To solve this, let us review each option against the physics curriculum regarding planetary motion:

*   **Option A:** This describes the **Postulates of Special Relativity** (specifically the Principle of Relativity), not Kepler’s laws.
*   **Option B:** This is **Kepler’s First Law (The Law of Orbits)**. It states that all planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one of the two foci. This correctly identifies the geometry of planetary motion as established by Kepler.
*   **Option C:** This is incorrect. Kepler explicitly moved away from the idea of uniform circular motion (which was the Ptolemaic and Copernican view) to elliptical orbits.
*   **Option D:** This describes **Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation**, given by the formula $F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$. While Newton used Kepler's laws to derive this, it is not a law attributed to Kepler himself.

**Summary of Kepler's Laws for your revision:**
1.  **Law of Orbits:** Planets move in ellipses with the Sun at one focus.
2.  **Law of Areas:** A line segment joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time.
3.  **Law of Periods:** The square of the orbital period $T$ is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis $r$ of its orbit: $\frac{T^2}{r^3} = \text{constant}$.
