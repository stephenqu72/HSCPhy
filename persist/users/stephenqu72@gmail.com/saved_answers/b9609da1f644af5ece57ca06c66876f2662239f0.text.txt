To determine the correct answer, we must understand the physics of X-ray diffraction, a core component of the HSC Physics curriculum under the "From the Universe to the Atom" (or related modern physics) module.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(B) The spacing between the atoms of the crystalline solid.**

### Explanation
X-ray diffraction is a phenomenon that occurs when X-rays interact with a crystalline solid. To solve this, consider the following physical principles:

1.  **The Diffraction Condition:** For significant diffraction to occur, the wavelength of the incident wave ($\lambda$) must be of a similar order of magnitude to the spacing between the scattering centers (the atoms or planes of atoms in the crystal lattice).
2.  **Bragg's Law:** The diffraction pattern is governed by Bragg's Law, given by the formula:
    $$n\lambda = 2d \sin\theta$$
    Where:
    *   $d$ is the **spacing between the planes of atoms** in the crystal lattice.
    *   $\theta$ is the angle of incidence/diffraction.
    *   $n$ is an integer (order of reflection).
3.  **Analysis of Options:**
    *   **(A)** The photographic plate is simply the **detector** used to record the interference pattern; it does not cause the diffraction itself.
    *   **(B)** This is the fundamental cause. The regular arrangement of atoms creates a diffraction grating effect where the separation distance $d$ between atoms determines the constructive interference angles, thus creating the pattern.
    *   **(C)** While the arrangement is important, it is the specific *interatomic spacing* ($d$) that allows the math of Bragg’s law to result in the constructive interference observed. Option (B) is the most direct physical cause related to the wave interference.
    *   **(D)** The X-ray beam is the probe used, but it is the structure of the target (the crystal) that dictates how the waves interfere.

Therefore, the geometric spacing between the atoms acts as the "diffraction grating" for the X-rays.
