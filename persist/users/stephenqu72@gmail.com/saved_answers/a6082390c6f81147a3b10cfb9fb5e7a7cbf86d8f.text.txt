### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of projectile motion in the absence of significant air resistance:

1.  **Identify the Forces:** Once the tennis ball leaves the racket, the only force acting on it is gravity. According to Newton's Second Law, $F_{net} = ma$. Since the only force is the weight force ($F_g = mg$), the acceleration is determined by:
    $$ma = mg \implies a = g$$
2.  **Determine the Value of Acceleration:** Acceleration due to gravity ($g$) is a constant value of approximately $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ directed downwards throughout the entire flight of the ball.
3.  **Analyze the Motion:** 
    *   On the way up, the velocity is positive (upward), but the acceleration is negative (downward), causing the ball to slow down.
    *   At the maximum height, the velocity is momentarily zero, but the acceleration is still $g$ (downward).
    *   On the way down, both the velocity and acceleration are directed downward, causing the ball to speed up.
4.  **Evaluate the Graph:** Because the acceleration is constant (always $g$ pointing downward) regardless of the position of the ball, the acceleration vs. time graph must be a **horizontal straight line**. 
    *   Graph A shows variable acceleration.
    *   Graph B shows variable acceleration.
    *   Graph C shows changing acceleration.
    *   **Graph D** shows a constant negative value (assuming down is negative), which accurately represents the constant acceleration due to gravity throughout the duration of the flight.
