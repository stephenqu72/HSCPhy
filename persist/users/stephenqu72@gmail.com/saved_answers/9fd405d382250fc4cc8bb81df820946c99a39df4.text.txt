#### (b) Determining $g$ from the Gradient
**1. Theory:**
The period of a pendulum is given by $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{l}{g}}$.
Squaring both sides gives:
$$T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2}{g} \cdot l$$
This is in the form $y = mx + c$, where $y = T^2$, $x = l$, and the gradient $m = \frac{4\pi^2}{g}$.
Therefore, $g = \frac{4\pi^2}{m}$.

**2. Calculating the Gradient ($m$):**
Draw a line of best fit through the points. To find the gradient, pick two points on your line that are far apart (e.g., $(0.1, 0.5)$ and $(0.8, 3.2)$).
$$m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{3.2 - 0.5}{0.8 - 0.1} = \frac{2.7}{0.7} \approx 3.86 \text{ s}^2/\text{m}$$
