To solve this problem, we must understand the physics of orbital mechanics. For a satellite in circular orbit, the centripetal force is provided by the gravitational force:
$$\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GMm}{r^2} \implies v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$
where $r = R_E + h$ (Earth's radius + altitude).

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Determine the constants and initial conditions:**
*   Radius of Earth ($R_E$) $\approx 6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
*   Gravitational constant ($G$) $\approx 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2\text{kg}^{-2}$
*   Mass of Earth ($M$) $\approx 5.97 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$
*   Initial radius ($r_1$) = $6.37 \times 10^6 + 650 \times 10^3 = 7.02 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
*   Initial velocity ($v_1$) = $7.55 \times 10^3 \text{ m/s}$

**2. Calculate the required velocity for the new orbit ($v_2$):**
Using the ratio relationship $v \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{r}}$, we have:
$$\frac{v_2}{v_1} = \sqrt{\frac{r_1}{r_2}}$$
New radius ($r_2$) = $6.37 \times 10^6 + 550 \times 10^3 = 6.92 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
$$v_2 = v_1 \times \sqrt{\frac{7.02 \times 10^6}{6.92 \times 10^6}} = 7550 \times \sqrt{1.01445} \approx 7604 \text{ m/s}$$

**3. Calculate the change in speed ($\Delta v$):**
$$\Delta v = v_2 - v_1 = 7604 - 7550 = 54 \text{ m/s}$$
The satellite must increase its speed by approximately $54 \text{ m/s}$.

### Explanation of Physics:
According to **Kepler’s Third Law** ($T^2 \propto r^3$), a satellite in a lower orbit has a smaller orbital period. To maintain a circular orbit at a lower altitude, the satellite experiences a stronger gravitational pull. To prevent the satellite from falling toward Earth, it must travel at a higher tangential velocity to balance the increased gravitational force, effectively ensuring the centripetal acceleration remains sufficient for the tighter radius.
