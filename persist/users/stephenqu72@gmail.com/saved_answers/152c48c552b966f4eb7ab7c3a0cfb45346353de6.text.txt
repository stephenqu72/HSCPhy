### How to solve it

**Part (a):**
In physics, the net force ($F_{net}$) on an object is the vector sum of all forces acting on it. For a rocket moving through space near a planet and moon, the primary force is gravity ($F_g = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$). Since both the planet and the moon have mass, they exert a gravitational pull on the rocket at every point in the diagram. Even at a Lagrange point (where gravitational forces balance), the net force would be zero, but given the dynamic path shown, the rocket is always being accelerated by the gravitational influence of the nearby bodies. Thus, there is no point where the net force is zero.
