To solve this problem, we must analyze the physics of a car moving in a circular path on a frictionless, banked track.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
When a car travels around a frictionless banked curve, the only forces acting on the car are gravity (acting downwards) and the normal force (acting perpendicular to the track surface). The horizontal component of the normal force provides the necessary centripetal force to keep the car moving in a circle.

The relationship for the banking angle $\theta$ is derived from:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

### 2. Convert Units
The velocity $v$ is given in $\text{km h}^{-1}$, which must be converted to SI units ($\text{m s}^{-1}$):
$$v = 300 \times \frac{1000 \text{ m}}{3600 \text{ s}} = \frac{300}{3.6} \approx 83.33 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

### 3. Calculation
Given:
- $v \approx 83.33 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
- $r = 750 \text{ m}$
- $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$

Substitute these values into the equation:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{(83.33)^2}{750 \times 9.8}$$
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{6944.44}{7350}$$
$$\tan(\theta) \approx 0.9448$$

Now, find the inverse tangent ($\arctan$):
$$\theta = \arctan(0.9448) \approx 43.37^\circ$$

Rounding to the nearest degree, we get $43^\circ$.
