### Answer
The correct answer is **(D)**.

### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of the **Photoelectric Effect**, a fundamental concept in quantum physics.

1.  **The Core Principle:** The photoelectric effect states that electrons are emitted from a metallic surface only when the incident photons have an energy $E = hf$ that is greater than or equal to the metal's work function ($\Phi$). If the frequency ($f$) of the light is below the threshold frequency ($f_0$), no amount of light intensity ("power") or time will result in electron emission.
2.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   **(A) is incorrect:** The photoelectric effect is an instantaneous interaction between a single photon and a single electron. Electrons do not "accumulate" energy over time.
    *   **(B) is incorrect:** Increasing the power (intensity) of the light increases the number of photons, but not the energy of individual photons. If the individual photons are below the threshold energy, increasing the number of them will still result in no emission.
    *   **(C) is incorrect:** According to the relation $E = hf$, decreasing the frequency decreases the energy of the individual photons, making it even less likely for emission to occur.
    *   **(D) is correct:** The relationship between frequency ($f$) and wavelength ($\lambda$) is given by $c = f\lambda$, or $f = \frac{c}{\lambda}$. Decreasing the wavelength increases the frequency of the light. Since $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$, decreasing the wavelength increases the energy of the individual photons. If the wavelength is decreased sufficiently, the photon energy will exceed the work function ($\Phi$), and electrons will be emitted.
