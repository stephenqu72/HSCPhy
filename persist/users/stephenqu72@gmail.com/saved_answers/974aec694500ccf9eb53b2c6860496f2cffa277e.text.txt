The correct answer is **(B) Refraction**.

### Explanation

To solve this, we need to consider how a lens functions in relation to light.

1.  **Understanding the Lens:** A lens is a transparent optical component made of a material (like glass or plastic) with a different refractive index than the surrounding medium (usually air).
2.  **The Physics of Light Bending:** When light travels from one medium into another, it changes speed. If the light enters the second medium at an angle, this change in speed causes the light rays to bend. This phenomenon is known as **refraction**.
3.  **Lens Functionality:** Lenses are shaped (curved) specifically to control this refraction. By controlling the angle at which light enters and exits the lens surfaces, the lens forces the incoming light rays to converge or diverge.
4.  **Creating Images:** When light rays from an object pass through a convex lens, they are refracted inward. If the object is placed at an appropriate distance (specifically between the focal point and the lens for a magnifying glass), the refracted rays diverge in a way that, when traced back, create an enlarged, virtual image of the object.
5.  **Eliminating other options:**
    *   **Diffraction** involves the spreading of waves around obstacles or through slits.
    *   **Reflection** involves light bouncing off a surface (this is how mirrors work, not lenses).
    *   **Superposition** is the principle that describes how waves interact with each other when they overlap, but it is not the mechanism that shapes the path of light rays through a lens to form an image.

Therefore, because lenses work by bending light via the change in refractive indices, **refraction** is the correct wave behaviour.
