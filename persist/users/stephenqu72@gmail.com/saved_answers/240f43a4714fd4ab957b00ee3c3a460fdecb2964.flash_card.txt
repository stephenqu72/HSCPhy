### Front
What is the formula for the orbital speed ($v$) of a satellite, and how does orbital radius ($r$) affect its required speed?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Satellites require less speed at higher altitudes because gravitational force weakens according to the inverse-square law.
- **Formula:** $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$
- **When to use it:** When calculating the velocity required for a stable circular orbit around a central mass ($M$).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to add the Earth's radius ($R_e$) to the given altitude ($h$) to find the total orbital radius ($r = R_e + h$).
