To determine the banking angle $\theta$, we analyze the forces acting on the car.

### 1. Identify the Given Values
*   Radius of the curve ($r$) = $90\text{ m}$
*   Velocity ($v$) = $60\text{ km/h}$. We must convert this to meters per second:
    $$v = \frac{60 \times 1000}{3600} \approx 16.67\text{ m/s}$$
*   Acceleration due to gravity ($g$) $\approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$

### 2. Physics Principle
For a car on a banked track, the horizontal component of the normal force ($N$) provides the necessary centripetal force ($F_c$), while the vertical component balances the weight of the car ($mg$).

*   Vertical force equation: $N \cos\theta = mg$
*   Horizontal force equation: $N \sin\theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$

By dividing the two equations:
$$\frac{N \sin\theta}{N \cos\theta} = \frac{mv^2/r}{mg}$$
$$\tan\theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

### 3. Calculation
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$\tan\theta = \frac{(16.67)^2}{90 \times 9.8}$$
$$\tan\theta = \frac{277.89}{882}$$
$$\tan\theta \approx 0.315$$
$$\theta = \arctan(0.315) \approx 17.5^\circ$$

**Final Answer:** The road is banked at an angle of approximately $17.5^\circ$.

### How to solve this problem:
1.  **Unit Conversion:** Always ensure velocity is in standard SI units ($\text{m/s}$) before performing calculations.
2.  **Free Body Diagram:** Visualize the forces. The Normal force acts perpendicular to the surface. Resolve this vector into a vertical component ($N\cos\theta$) to counteract gravity and a horizontal component ($N\sin\theta$) to act as the centripetal force.
3.  **Ratio Method:** The standard derivation $\tan\theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$ is the most efficient way to link circular motion parameters to the banking angle.
4.  **Significant Figures:** Ensure your final answer reflects the precision of the input data (usually 2 or 3 significant figures in HSC Physics).
