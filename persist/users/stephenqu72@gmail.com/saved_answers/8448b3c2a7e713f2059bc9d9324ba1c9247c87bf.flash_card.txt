### Front
How does the centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) of an object change if the radius of its circular path increases by a factor of 3, while its speed remains constant?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Centripetal acceleration is inversely proportional to the radius ($a_c \propto 1/r$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$
- **When to use it:** Problems involving circular motion at constant speed with changing radii or velocities.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting the square on velocity ($v^2$) or confusing inverse proportionality (increasing radius decreases acceleration).
