### Explanation

To solve this problem, we must understand the physical origins of the different types of spectra shown:

1.  **Spectrum Z (Continuous Spectrum):** An incandescent globe (such as a traditional tungsten filament bulb) emits light due to heat, a process known as blackbody radiation. When a solid, liquid, or dense gas is heated to a high temperature, it emits radiation across a broad range of wavelengths, creating an unbroken band of color. This is a **continuous spectrum**.
2.  **Spectrum W (Absorption Spectrum):** This shows a continuous rainbow interrupted by dark lines. These dark lines occur when light from a continuous source passes through a cooler gas, which absorbs specific wavelengths corresponding to the energy transitions of the atoms in that gas. This is typical of light from a star passing through its cooler outer atmosphere.
3.  **Spectrum X and Y (Emission Spectra):** These show specific, discrete colored lines against a black background. This occurs when an excited gas emits light at specific wavelengths as electrons drop to lower energy states. Each element produces a unique "fingerprint" of lines.

Since an incandescent globe acts as a hot, dense thermal radiator, it produces a complete, continuous spectrum without discrete gaps or lines. Therefore, **Z** is the correct identification.
