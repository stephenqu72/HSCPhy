### Teacher's Explanation

**Part a) The Physics of Matter Waves**
To earn full marks here, you must demonstrate your understanding of the **de Broglie hypothesis**.
1.  **Concept:** Matter (like electrons) exhibits wave-particle duality, meaning particles can exhibit wave-like properties such as diffraction.
2.  **Mechanism:** The diffraction pattern spacing depends on the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the incident wave interacting with the crystal lattice structure.
3.  **Synthesis:** If the de Broglie wavelength of the electrons ($\lambda_e = h/p$) is equal to the wavelength of the X-rays ($\lambda_x$), they will undergo diffraction through the same crystal lattice structure in an identical manner, resulting in the same interference pattern.

**Part b) Step-by-Step Calculation**
To solve this, we must first find the wavelength of the electrons and then relate that to the frequency of the X-rays.

1.  **Calculate electron kinetic energy ($E_k$) in Joules:**
    $$E_k = 3.0 \times 10^3 \text{ eV} \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J/eV} = 4.806 \times 10^{-16} \text{ J}$$

2.  **Calculate electron momentum ($p$):**
    Using the non-relativistic relation $E_k = \frac{p^2}{2m}$:
    $$p = \sqrt{2mE_k} = \sqrt{2 \times (9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}) \times (4.806 \times 10^{-16} \text{ J})}$$
    $$p \approx 2.958 \times 10^{-23} \text{ kg m/s}$$

3.  **Calculate the wavelength ($\lambda$) using de Broglie’s equation:**
    $$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}}{2.958 \times 10^{-23} \text{ kg m/s}} \approx 2.24 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$$

4.  **Calculate frequency ($f$) for X-rays:**
    Since X-rays travel at the speed of light ($c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$), use $v = f\lambda$:
    $$f = \frac{c}{\lambda} = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}}{2.24 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}} \approx 1.34 \times 10^{19} \text{ Hz}$$

---

### Answers

**a)** According to the de Broglie hypothesis, particles such as electrons possess a wavelength given by $\lambda = h/p$. When an electron is accelerated, it acts as a wave. Because the spacing of a diffraction pattern is dependent on the wavelength of the incident radiation/particles, if the de Broglie wavelength of the electrons matches the wavelength of the X-rays, both will produce the same constructive and destructive interference pattern when diffracted by the same crystalline lattice structure.

**b)** 
1. Convert energy: $E_k = 4.806 \times 10^{-16} \text{ J}$
2. Momentum: $p = \sqrt{2 \times 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \times 4.806 \times 10^{-16}} = 2.958 \times 10^{-23} \text{ kg m/s}$
3. Wavelength: $\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{2.958 \times 10^{-23}} = 2.24 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$
4. Frequency: $f = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{2.24 \times 10^{-11}} = 1.34 \times 10^{19} \text{ Hz}$
