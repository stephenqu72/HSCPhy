To solve this problem, we must understand the physics of a charged particle moving through a uniform magnetic field.

### Explanation

When a charged particle $q$ enters a magnetic field $B$ moving with velocity $v$ perpendicular to the field, it experiences a magnetic force $F_B$ given by:
$$F_B = qvB$$

Since this force acts perpendicular to the velocity, it provides the centripetal force required to keep the particle in a circular path of radius $r$:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Equating the magnetic force to the centripetal force:
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Solving for the radius $r$:
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

From this formula, we can see the relationship between radius and velocity:
$$r \propto v$$

If the velocity is doubled ($v' = 2v$) while $m$, $q$, and $B$ remain constant, the new radius $r'$ becomes:
$$r' = \frac{m(2v)}{qB} = 2 \left( \frac{mv}{qB} \right) = 2r$$

Therefore, doubling the velocity results in doubling the radius of the circular path.

**Correct Answer: (C) $2r$**
