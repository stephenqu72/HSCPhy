### Front
What is the formula for centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) and how is it related to Newton’s Second Law in circular motion?

### Back
- Key idea: Objects moving in a circle undergo acceleration toward the center, even at constant speed.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$ and $F_{net} = ma_c$.
- When to use it: Any problem involving an object moving along a curved path or circular arc.
- Common trap: Forgetting that acceleration is a vector directed toward the center, or using the mass of the vehicle when the question specifically asks for the force on a passenger.
