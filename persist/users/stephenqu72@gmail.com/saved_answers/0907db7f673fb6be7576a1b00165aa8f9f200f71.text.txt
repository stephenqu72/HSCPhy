### Explanation of the Physics Concepts

To solve this problem, we must understand the balance of torques on the beam. The downward force of gravity $F_g$ on the riders is balanced by the upward magnetic force $F_B$ on the U-loop. Since these forces balance at the same distance from the pivot, we have:
$$F_g = F_B = B I_{U\text{-loop}} L$$
Where $L$ is the length of the conductor perpendicular to the magnetic field.

#### Part a: Plotting the graph
*   **Axes:** Plot $F_{g \text{ on riders}}$ on the x-axis and $I_{U\text{-loop}}$ on the y-axis.
*   **Units:** Label x-axis as $(10^{-4} \text{ N})$ and y-axis as $(\text{A})$.
*   **Scaling:** Choose a scale that spans the grid effectively. For x, use $0$ to $20 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}$. For y, use $0$ to $5 \text{ A}$.
*   **Best Fit:** Plot the points $(3.92, 1.3), (7.84, 2.0), (11.8, 2.4), (15.7, 3.1), (19.6, 4.6)$. Draw a straight line of best fit that passes through the origin (or near it, accounting for systematic errors).

#### Part b: Calculating the gradient
Pick two points on your line of best fit that are far apart, $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$. 
Gradient $m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{I_2 - I_1}{F_2 - F_1}$.
Using the data points (approximate for the line of best fit):
$m = \frac{4.6 - 1.3}{19.6 - 3.92} \times \frac{1}{10^{-4}} \approx \frac{3.3}{15.68 \times 10^{-4}} \approx 2104 \text{ A/N}$.
*(Note: Your value may vary slightly depending on your drawn line).*

#### Part c: Calculating magnetic field $B$
From our formula $F_g = B I_{U\text{-loop}} L$, we can rearrange it to:
$$I_{U\text{-loop}} = \left( \frac{1}{BL} \right) F_g$$
Comparing this to the equation of a line $y = mx$, we see that the gradient $m = \frac{1}{BL}$.
Therefore:
$$B = \frac{1}{m \cdot L}$$
Given $L = 2.5 \text{ cm} = 0.025 \text{ m}$ and $m \approx 2104 \text{ A/N}$:
$$B = \frac{1}{2104 \times 0.025} \approx \frac{1}{52.6} \approx 0.019 \text{ T}$$
