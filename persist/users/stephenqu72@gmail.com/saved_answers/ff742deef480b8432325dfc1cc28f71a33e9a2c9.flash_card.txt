### Front
Outline the role of bosons within the Standard Model.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Bosons are "force-carrier" particles that mediate fundamental interactions between fermions.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** 
    - Electromagnetic: Photon
    - Strong nuclear: Gluons
    - Weak nuclear: $W$ and $Z$ bosons
    - Higgs mechanism: Higgs boson (provides mass)
- **When to use it:** When asked to describe the structure of the Standard Model or the mechanism of fundamental forces.
- **Common trap:** Confusing fermions (matter particles) with bosons (force carriers). Note that the Higgs boson is a scalar boson, not a gauge boson, as it provides mass rather than mediating a force.
