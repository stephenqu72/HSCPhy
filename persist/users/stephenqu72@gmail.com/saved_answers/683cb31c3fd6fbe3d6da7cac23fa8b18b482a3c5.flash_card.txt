### Front
What is the direction of the acceleration vector for an object in uniform circular motion?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration in uniform circular motion is always centripetal (directed toward the center of the circular path).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\vec{a}_c$ points toward the center; $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$
- When to use it: Whenever an object moves in a circular path at constant speed.
- Common trap: Confusing the velocity vector (tangent to the circle) with the acceleration vector (directed toward the center).
