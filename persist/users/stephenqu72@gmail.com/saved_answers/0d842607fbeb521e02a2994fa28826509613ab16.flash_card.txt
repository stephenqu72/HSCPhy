### Front
How do you determine the horizontal range ($R$) of a projectile launched from level ground, and which two variables have the greatest impact?

### Back
- Key idea: Range is proportional to the square of initial velocity ($v^2$) and the sine of double the projection angle ($\sin 2\theta$).
- Formula: $R = \frac{v^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$
- When to use it: To compare the horizontal distances of projectiles launched at different speeds and angles on level ground.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the initial velocity ($v$) or confusing the range formula with those for time of flight or maximum height.
