To solve this problem, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which relates the surface temperature of a black body to the wavelength at which it emits the maximum intensity of radiation ($\lambda_{max}$).

### The Physics Principle
Wien’s Displacement Law is expressed as:
$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$
Where:
*   $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength in meters ($m$).
*   $T$ is the absolute temperature in Kelvin ($K$).
*   $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, m \cdot K$.

### Step-by-Step Calculation
1.  **Identify the given variables:**
    *   $T = 4000 \, K$
    *   $b \approx 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, m \cdot K$

2.  **Substitute into the formula:**
    $$\lambda_{max} = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{4000}$$

3.  **Perform the division:**
    $$\lambda_{max} = \frac{2.898}{4000} \times 10^{-3}$$
    $$\lambda_{max} = 0.0007245 \times 10^{-3} \, m$$
    $$\lambda_{max} = 7.245 \times 10^{-7} \, m$$

4.  **Convert to nanometers (nm) to match the options:**
    Since $1 \, m = 10^9 \, nm$:
    $$\lambda_{max} = 7.245 \times 10^{-7} \times 10^9 \, nm$$
    $$\lambda_{max} = 724.5 \, nm \approx 725 \, nm$$

### Conclusion
Comparing our result of $725 \, nm$ with the given options, option **(d)** is the correct answer.

*   Option (a) is $0.0725 \, \mu m$ (too small).
*   Option (b) is $7250 \, nm$ (too large).
*   Option (c) is $725 \times 10^{-7} \, nm = 7.25 \times 10^{-5} \, nm$ (too small).
*   Option (d) is $725 \, nm$.
