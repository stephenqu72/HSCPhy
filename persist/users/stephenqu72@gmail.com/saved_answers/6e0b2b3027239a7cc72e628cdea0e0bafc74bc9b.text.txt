To solve this problem, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, which is defined as:
$$F = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$$
Where:
- $F$ is the gravitational force
- $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{ kg}^{-2}$ (Universal Gravitational Constant)
- $M$ is the mass of the Moon
- $m = 1000 \text{ kg}$ (mass of the object)
- $r$ is the distance from the centre of the Moon

### (a) Determine the mass of the Moon
1. **Extract data from the graph:** At the surface of the Moon, $r = 1.7 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$ (the point where the dashed line starts). Reading the corresponding force on the $y$-axis, $F = 1.6 \times 10^3 \text{ N}$.
2. **Rearrange the formula to solve for $M$:**
   $$M = \frac{Fr^2}{Gm}$$
3. **Substitute the values:**
   $$M = \frac{(1.6 \times 10^3) \times (1.7 \times 10^6)^2}{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times 1000}$$
   $$M \approx \frac{4.624 \times 10^{15}}{6.67 \times 10^{-8}} \approx 6.93 \times 10^{22} \text{ kg}$$

### (b) Calculate the escape speed
The escape speed $v_e$ is the speed required for the kinetic energy to equal the gravitational potential energy, defined by:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$
Using $M = 6.93 \times 10^{22} \text{ kg}$ and $r = 1.7 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (6.93 \times 10^{22})}{1.7 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{9.24 \times 10^{12}}{1.7 \times 10^6}} = \sqrt{5.435 \times 10^6} \approx 2331 \text{ m/s}$$
Rounding to appropriate significant figures based on the graph, the escape speed is approximately $2.3 \times 10^3 \text{ m/s}$.

### Explanation of the approach:
*   **For (a):** The key is identifying the correct point on the graph to find $r$ and $F$. Since the question provides the mass $m$, we can isolate $M$ using the standard gravitational force equation.
*   **For (b):** We apply the conservation of energy principle. By equating $\frac{1}{2}mv^2$ to $\frac{GMm}{r}$, we derive the escape velocity formula. Note that the mass of the object $m$ cancels out, demonstrating that escape velocity is independent of the mass of the projectile.
