To determine the temperature of the blackbody radiator, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which states:

$$\lambda_{max} T = b$$

Where:
*   $\lambda_{max}$ is the wavelength at which the emission of radiation is at its peak.
*   $T$ is the absolute temperature of the blackbody in Kelvin (K).
*   $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}$.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$):**
    Looking at the graph, the curve reaches its maximum luminosity at a wavelength between $400 \text{ nm}$ and $600 \text{ nm}$. The peak is exactly halfway between the $400 \text{ nm}$ and $600 \text{ nm}$ grid lines (at the $500 \text{ nm}$ mark).
    Therefore, $\lambda_{max} = 500 \text{ nm} = 500 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$.

2.  **Rearrange Wien’s Law for temperature ($T$):**
    $$T = \frac{b}{\lambda_{max}}$$

3.  **Substitute the values:**
    $$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}}{500 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}}$$
    $$T = \frac{2.898}{500} \times 10^6 \text{ K}$$
    $$T \approx 0.005796 \times 10^6 \text{ K}$$
    $$T \approx 5796 \text{ K}$$

4.  **Evaluate the options:**
    The calculated temperature of $\approx 5796 \text{ K}$ is closest to $5600 \text{ K}$ (Option C). In physics problems of this type, small variations in reading the graph or the value used for Wien's constant are common, making $5600 \text{ K}$ the correct choice.

**Correct Answer:** **C. 5600 K.**
