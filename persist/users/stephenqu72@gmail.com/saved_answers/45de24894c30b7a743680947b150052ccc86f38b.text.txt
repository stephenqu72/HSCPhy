To solve this problem, we need to determine how many half-lives have passed within the total time given.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   Half-life ($t_{1/2}$) = $40\text{ s}$
    *   Total time ($T$) = $2\text{ minutes}$

2.  **Ensure consistent units:**
    Since the half-life is in seconds, convert the total time into seconds:
    $$T = 2\text{ minutes} \times 60\text{ seconds/minute} = 120\text{ s}$$

3.  **Calculate the number of half-lives ($n$):**
    The number of half-lives that have elapsed is the total time divided by the duration of one half-life:
    $$n = \frac{T}{t_{1/2}} = \frac{120\text{ s}}{40\text{ s}} = 3$$

4.  **Calculate the remaining fraction:**
    The fraction of a radioactive sample remaining after $n$ half-lives is given by the formula $(\frac{1}{2})^n$:
    $$\text{Remaining fraction} = \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^3 = \frac{1^3}{2^3} = \frac{1}{8}$$

**Conclusion:**
After three half-lives, $\frac{1}{8}$ of the original sample remains. Looking at the options provided, the correct answer is **(A)**.
