### Front
How do you calculate average speed from total distance ($d$) and total time ($t$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Average speed is a scalar quantity representing the total path length covered per unit of time.
- Formula: $v_{avg} = \frac{d}{t}$
- When to use it: Whenever calculating the rate of motion over a total interval, regardless of changes in instantaneous velocity.
- Common trap: Confusing average speed with average velocity (displacement/time) or failing to maintain consistent significant figures.
