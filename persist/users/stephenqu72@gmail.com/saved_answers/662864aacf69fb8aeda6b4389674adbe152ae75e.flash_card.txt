### Front
How do you calculate constant acceleration from initial velocity ($u$), final velocity ($v$), and time ($t$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity over time.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a = \frac{v - u}{t}$
- When to use it: Any kinematics problem involving uniform motion where acceleration is constant.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert units (e.g., km/h to m/s) before substituting into the formula.
