To solve this problem, we must first understand the physics of an electron beam moving undeflected through crossed electric ($E$) and magnetic ($B$) fields.

### Physics Principles
When an electron passes through a velocity selector, the electric force ($F_E = qE$) is balanced by the magnetic force ($F_B = qvB$). For the beam to remain undeflected:
$$qE = qvB$$
$$E = vB$$
$$v = \frac{E}{B}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Analyze the relationship:**
The equation $E = vB$ indicates that if we plot $B$ on the $y$-axis and $E$ on the $x$-axis, the relationship is $B = \frac{1}{v} E$. This is a linear relationship in the form $y = mx$, where the gradient $m = \frac{1}{v}$. Therefore, the velocity $v$ is the reciprocal of the gradient of the line of best fit:
$$v = \frac{1}{\text{gradient}}$$

**2. Plotting the data:**
*   $x$-axis: Electric Field ($E$) in $\times 10^5 \text{ N C}^{-1}$
*   $y$-axis: Magnetic Field ($B$) in mT ($10^{-3} \text{ T}$)

Plot the points: $(4.8, 16)$, $(5.5, 18)$, $(7.2, 24)$, $(7.9, 26)$, $(9.1, 30)$.
Draw a line of best fit through these points. Passing through the origin $(0,0)$, a suitable pair of points to calculate the gradient would be near $(9, 30)$ and $(3, 10)$.

**3. Calculating the Gradient:**
Using the points $(3, 10)$ and $(9, 30)$:
$$\text{gradient} = \frac{\Delta B}{\Delta E} = \frac{(30 - 10) \times 10^{-3} \text{ T}}{(9 - 3) \times 10^5 \text{ N C}^{-1}}$$
$$\text{gradient} = \frac{20 \times 10^{-3}}{6 \times 10^5} = 3.33 \times 10^{-9} \text{ T C N}^{-1}$$

**4. Determining Velocity:**
$$v = \frac{1}{\text{gradient}} = \frac{1}{3.33 \times 10^{-9}}$$
$$v \approx 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

*(Note: Depending on your exact line of best fit, your gradient might vary slightly, but it should result in a value close to the speed of light.)*
