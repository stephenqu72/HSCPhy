To solve this problem, we use the formula for torque:

$$\tau = rF \sin(\theta)$$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   Torque ($\tau$) = $200\text{ N m}$
    *   Distance ($r$) = $25.0\text{ cm} = 0.25\text{ m}$ (We must convert to SI units).
    *   Angle ($\theta$) = $30^\circ$ (This is the angle between the force vector and the handle of the spanner).

2.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for Force ($F$):**
    $$F = \frac{\tau}{r \sin(\theta)}$$

3.  **Substitute the values into the equation:**
    $$F = \frac{200}{0.25 \times \sin(30^\circ)}$$

4.  **Perform the calculation:**
    *   Since $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$
    *   $F = \frac{200}{0.25 \times 0.5}$
    *   $F = \frac{200}{0.125}$
    *   $F = 1600\text{ N}$

**Answer:** The force $F$ required is $1600\text{ N}$.
