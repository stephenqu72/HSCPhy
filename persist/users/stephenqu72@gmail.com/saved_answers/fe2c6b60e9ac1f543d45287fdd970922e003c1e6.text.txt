### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must identify the type of spectrum shown in the image and understand how different physical sources produce different types of spectra:

1.  **Analyze the image:** The spectrum provided shows a series of distinct, sharp coloured lines against a black background. This is a characteristic **emission spectrum** (or bright-line spectrum).
2.  **Understand emission spectra:** An emission spectrum is produced when an element in a gaseous state is excited (usually by heat or electricity), causing its electrons to transition from higher energy levels to lower energy levels. As the electrons drop, they emit photons with specific, discrete wavelengths (or frequencies) related to the energy difference between the levels ($E = hf$).
3.  **Evaluate the options:**
    *   **(A) Very hot object:** This would produce a **continuous spectrum** (a complete rainbow of colours) because the atoms are so tightly packed that their energy levels overlap.
    *   **(B) Star:** While stars contain hydrogen, observing the light from a star typically results in an **absorption spectrum** (a continuous spectrum with dark lines where the cooler outer atmosphere of the star has absorbed specific frequencies).
    *   **(C) Gas discharge tube:** This is the classic apparatus used to produce an **emission spectrum**. By passing a high-voltage electrical current through a low-pressure gas, the atoms are excited and emit light at characteristic wavelengths.
    *   **(D) Fluorescent lamp:** While these do involve gas discharge, they also rely on a phosphor coating inside the tube to convert UV light into a broader range of visible light, which produces a spectrum that is not identical to the pure emission lines of a simple hydrogen discharge tube.

Therefore, the spectrum of a gas discharge tube filled with hydrogen will match the spectrum provided in the image.
