### Answer

**Production:**
Electromagnetic waves are produced by the acceleration of charged particles (such as electrons). When a charge oscillates, it creates a time-varying electric field. According to Maxwell’s equations, this changing electric field induces a time-varying magnetic field in the surrounding space.

**Propagation:**
The changing electric field and changing magnetic field are mutually self-sustaining. The oscillating magnetic field induces a changing electric field, and the oscillating electric field induces a changing magnetic field. This cycle continues, allowing the electromagnetic wave to propagate through space (or a vacuum) at the speed of light, $c$. The electric and magnetic field vectors are perpendicular to each other and perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation (transverse wave).

### How to solve this question
To score full marks on a 3-mark question regarding electromagnetic wave theory, you must address both distinct parts of the prompt:

1.  **Production (1-1.5 marks):** You must mention that **acceleration of charges** is the fundamental requirement. Stating that "electrons moving" is insufficient; they must be accelerating (e.g., oscillating in an antenna or changing energy levels in an atom).
2.  **Propagation (1.5-2 marks):** You must explain the mechanism of mutual induction. Use terms like "time-varying" or "oscillating." Mention that the fields are **mutually perpendicular** and that they do not require a medium to travel, as the fields regenerate each other in the vacuum.
3.  **HSC Tip:** Use the phrase "self-propagating" or "mutually reinforcing" to demonstrate a high-level understanding of Maxwell's theory of electromagnetism.
