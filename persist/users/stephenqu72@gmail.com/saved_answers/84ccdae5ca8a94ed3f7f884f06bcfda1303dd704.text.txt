To solve this problem, we use the radioactive decay formula:

$$N(t) = N_0 \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{\frac{t}{t_{1/2}}}$$

Where:
*   $N_0 = 200 \text{ g}$ (initial amount)
*   $t = 8 \text{ days}$ (time elapsed)
*   $t_{1/2} = 3.63 \text{ days}$ (half-life)
*   $N(t)$ is the amount **remaining** after time $t$.

### Step 1: Calculate the amount remaining
First, calculate the number of half-lives that have passed:
$$n = \frac{8}{3.63} \approx 2.20386$$

Now, calculate the remaining mass ($N(t)$):
$$N(t) = 200 \times (0.5)^{2.20386}$$
$$N(t) = 200 \times 0.21689... \approx 43.378 \text{ g}$$

### Step 2: Calculate the amount decayed
The question asks for the amount that has **decayed**, which is the initial amount minus the remaining amount:
$$\text{Decayed} = N_0 - N(t)$$
$$\text{Decayed} = 200 - 43.378 = 156.622 \text{ g}$$

Rounding to one decimal place, we get $156.6 \text{ g}$.
