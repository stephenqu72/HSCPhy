### Part (a): Compare and describe the physical principles

**Answer:**
Both speakers operate by converting electrical energy into mechanical energy (vibration) to produce sound waves, but they rely on different fundamental forces:

*   **Magnetic Loudspeaker:** Operates on the **Motor Effect**. When an alternating current flows through the voice coil placed in a radial magnetic field, it experiences a varying magnetic force ($F = BIL\sin\theta$). The direction of the force reverses as the current alternates, causing the coil and the attached speaker cone to oscillate at the frequency of the input signal.
*   **Electrostatic Loudspeaker:** Operates on the principles of **Electrostatics (Coulomb’s Law)**. A thin, charged plastic sheet is suspended between two fixed conducting plates with high potential differences. When the charge on the central sheet varies, it experiences a varying electrostatic force of attraction and repulsion from the fixed plates. This causes the sheet to oscillate, creating sound.

**How to solve:**
1.  Identify the "physics" in each: One is electromagnetism (Motor Effect), the other is electrostatics (electric field forces).
2.  Use the "Compare" command: Mention the similarity (both convert electrical energy to mechanical oscillation).
3.  Use the "Describe" command: Provide the mechanism for movement for each type.

### Part (b): Contrast the loudspeaker with an induction motor

**Answer:**
*   **Energy Conversion:** A loudspeaker converts electrical energy into kinetic energy of a vibrating diaphragm (sound), whereas an induction motor converts electrical energy into rotational kinetic energy (torque).
*   **Principle of Operation:** The loudspeaker relies on the **Motor Effect** (Lorentz force on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field). In contrast, an induction motor relies on **Electromagnetic Induction**. It uses a rotating magnetic field to induce eddy currents in the rotor (Lenz's Law), which then interact with the stator's magnetic field to create torque.
*   **Motion:** The loudspeaker requires a back-and-forth (oscillatory) motion to produce waves, while the induction motor is designed for continuous rotational motion.

**How to solve:**
1.  Focus on the *principles* asked. Do not just list features; explain *why* they function differently.
2.  Key distinction: Loudspeaker = Motor Effect; Induction Motor = Faraday's Law/Lenz's Law + Induced Currents.
3.  Structure the answer by contrasting one against the other clearly.
