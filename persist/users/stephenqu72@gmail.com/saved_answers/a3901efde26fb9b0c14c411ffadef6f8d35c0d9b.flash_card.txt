### Front
How does the centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) of an object moving in a circle of constant radius change when the period ($T$) is varied?

### Back
- Key idea: For a fixed radius, centripetal acceleration is inversely proportional to the square of the period ($a_c \propto 1/T^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_c = \frac{4\pi^2 r}{T^2}$
- When to use it: To determine how changing the speed or duration of a circular orbit affects the required centripetal force/acceleration.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the period or confusing $T$ (period) with $f$ (frequency) when rearranging the proportional relationship.
