To find the acceleration of the car, we follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the given variables
*   Initial velocity ($u$): $20\text{ km/h}$
*   Final velocity ($v$): $60\text{ km/h}$
*   Time interval ($t$): $8\text{ s}$

### 2. Convert units to SI (meters per second)
The acceleration is requested in $\text{ms}^{-2}$, so we must convert the velocities from $\text{km/h}$ to $\text{m/s}$ by dividing by $3.6$:
*   $u = \frac{20}{3.6} \approx 5.556\text{ m/s}$
*   $v = \frac{60}{3.6} \approx 16.667\text{ m/s}$

### 3. Apply the acceleration formula
The formula for uniform acceleration is:
$$a = \frac{v - u}{t}$$

Substituting our values:
$$a = \frac{16.667 - 5.556}{8}$$
$$a = \frac{11.111}{8}$$
$$a \approx 1.389\text{ m/s}^2$$

Rounding to one decimal place, we get $1.4\text{ m/s}^2$.

Comparing this result to the given options:
(A) $1.4\text{ ms}^{-2}$
(B) $5.0\text{ ms}^{-2}$
(C) $9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$
(D) $144\text{ ms}^{-2}$

The correct answer is **(A)**.
