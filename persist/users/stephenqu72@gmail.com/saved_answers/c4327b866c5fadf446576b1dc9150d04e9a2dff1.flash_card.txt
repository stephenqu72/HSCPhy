How does radioactive decay relate to mass change and the decay constant?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Half-life ($t_{1/2}$) is inversely proportional to the decay constant ($\lambda$). Mass change in decay is governed by $E=\Delta mc^2$, but is experimentally undetectable due to the tiny mass-energy equivalence.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{t_{1/2}}$; $E = \Delta mc^2$.
- **When to use it:** Use the decay constant formula to determine decay rates from graphs; use mass-energy equivalence to explain why mass appears constant in lab settings.
- **Common trap:** Assuming mass is lost in a way that is measurable on a standard lab scale; confusing the decay constant with the decay rate ($A = \lambda N$).
