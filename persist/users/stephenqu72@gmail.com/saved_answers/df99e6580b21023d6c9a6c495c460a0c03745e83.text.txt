### Explanation
To solve this problem, you must apply **Newton's First Law of Motion**, which states that an object will maintain its state of constant velocity (speed and direction) unless acted upon by an external net force.

1.  **Uniform Circular Motion:** While the mass is moving in a circle, it is constantly being accelerated toward the center of the circle by the centripetal force provided by the tension in the string. At any specific point in time, the instantaneous velocity of the mass is directed **tangent** to the circular path at that point.
2.  **When the String Breaks:** The moment the string breaks, the centripetal force (the tension) is removed. According to Newton's First Law, since there is no longer a net force acting on the mass to pull it toward the center of the circle, the object will continue to move in a straight line at its current instantaneous velocity.
3.  **Determining the Direction:** The instantaneous velocity vector at any point on a circle is always at a $90^\circ$ angle to the radius vector at that point. Looking at the diagram, arrow **1** represents the path tangent to the circle at the point where the mass is located. Arrows 2, 3, and 4 suggest a continued curved or radial path, which would require an external force to maintain, but no such force exists once the string breaks.

Therefore, the mass will follow path **1** in a straight line.
