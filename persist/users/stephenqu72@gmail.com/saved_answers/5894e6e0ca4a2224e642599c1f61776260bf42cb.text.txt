To determine the correct answer, we must apply the principles of the **Doppler Effect**.

### Explanation
The Doppler Effect states that the observed frequency ($f_{obs}$) of a wave changes relative to the actual frequency ($f_s$) emitted by a source based on the relative motion between the source and the observer:
1. When the source moves **towards** the observer, the waves are compressed, leading to an observed frequency that is **higher** than the actual frequency ($f_{obs} > f_s$).
2. When the source moves **away from** the observer, the waves are stretched, leading to an observed frequency that is **lower** than the actual frequency ($f_{obs} < f_s$).

**Analyzing the graph:**
* The actual frequency of the siren is $880\text{ Hz}$.
* On the **west** side of the observer, the frequency is constant and **lower** than $880\text{ Hz}$. This means the ambulance is moving away from Mina while it is in the west.
* On the **east** side of the observer, the frequency is constant and **higher** than $880\text{ Hz}$. This means the ambulance is moving towards Mina while it is in the east.

Since the frequency values are constant (represented by horizontal lines), the speed of the ambulance is constant. Because the ambulance moves from a region where it is moving away (west) to a region where it is moving towards (east), it must be travelling from **west to east** (passing Mina in the middle).

Therefore, the ambulance is travelling at a constant speed from west to east.
