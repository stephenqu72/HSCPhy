### Answer
The correct answer is **c) Mutually propagating electric and magnetic fields perpendicular to each other.**

---

### Explanation
James Clerk Maxwell’s theoretical work on electromagnetism (Maxwell’s Equations) led to the prediction that electromagnetic disturbances propagate through space as waves. 

1.  **The Nature of the Fields:** Maxwell identified that a changing electric field produces a magnetic field, and a changing magnetic field produces an electric field. These two fields support one another in a "self-propagating" wave.
2.  **Directionality:** In this model, the oscillating electric field vector ($E$) and the oscillating magnetic field vector ($B$) are **perpendicular** to each other. Furthermore, both of these vectors are perpendicular to the direction of the wave's propagation (making them transverse waves).
3.  **Why the other options are incorrect:**
    *   **a)** If the fields were parallel, they could not induce the cyclic change required for a self-propagating wave in the vacuum.
    *   **b)** This describes a mechanical wave (like sound) requiring a medium, which contradicts Maxwell’s discovery that electromagnetic waves travel through a vacuum.
    *   **d)** This describes electrons (matter particles), whereas Maxwell's waves are field-based oscillations. The equation $E = hf$ refers to the energy of a photon (Einstein/Planck), not the wave propagation definition proposed by Maxwell.
