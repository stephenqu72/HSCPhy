### Question 10 Solution
The spring constant $k$ is the gradient of the Force vs. Compression graph ($F = kx$).
From the graph, we select a clear point: $F = 40.0\text{ kN}$ at $x = 8.0\text{ mm}$.

1.  **Convert units to SI base units:**
    *   $F = 40.0\text{ kN} = 40,000\text{ N} = 4.0 \times 10^4\text{ N}$
    *   $x = 8.0\text{ mm} = 0.008\text{ m} = 8.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$

2.  **Calculate $k$:**
    $$k = \frac{F}{x} = \frac{4.0 \times 10^4\text{ N}}{8.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}}$$
    $$k = 0.5 \times 10^7\text{ N m}^{-1} = 5.0 \times 10^6\text{ N m}^{-1}$$

**Answer: D**

### Question 11 Solution
The potential energy stored in a spring is represented by the area under the Force vs. Compression graph. The "additional potential energy" is the area of the trapezoid between $x_1 = 4.0\text{ mm}$ and $x_2 = 8.0\text{ mm}$.

1.  **Identify forces at these compressions:**
    *   At $x_1 = 4.0\text{ mm}$, $F_1 = 20.0\text{ kN} = 2.0 \times 10^4\text{ N}$
    *   At $x_2 = 8.0\text{ mm}$, $F_2 = 40.0\text{ kN} = 4.0 \times 10^4\text{ N}$
    *   $\Delta x = 8.0\text{ mm} - 4.0\text{ mm} = 4.0\text{ mm} = 0.004\text{ m}$

2.  **Calculate the area of the trapezoid:**
    $$\text{Energy} = \text{Area} = \frac{F_1 + F_2}{2} \times \Delta x$$
    $$\text{Energy} = \frac{(2.0 \times 10^4 + 4.0 \times 10^4)\text{ N}}{2} \times 0.004\text{ m}$$
    $$\text{Energy} = 3.0 \times 10^4\text{ N} \times 0.004\text{ m}$$
    $$\text{Energy} = 120\text{ J} = 1.2 \times 10^2\text{ J}$$

**Answer: B**
