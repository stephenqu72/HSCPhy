### Front
Why does an alpha particle carry away significantly more kinetic energy than the daughter nucleus during alpha decay?

### Back
- Key idea: Conservation of momentum requires the lighter particle (alpha) to have a much higher velocity to balance the momentum of the heavier daughter nucleus.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $p_P = p_{\alpha}$ and $K = \frac{p^2}{2m} \implies \frac{K_{\alpha}}{K_P} = \frac{m_P}{m_{\alpha}}$
- When to use it: To calculate the energy distribution of decay products or explain why the alpha particle is the primary carrier of kinetic energy.
- Common trap: Assuming equal kinetic energy for both particles; remember that since $K \propto \frac{1}{m}$, the lighter mass carries the majority of the energy.
