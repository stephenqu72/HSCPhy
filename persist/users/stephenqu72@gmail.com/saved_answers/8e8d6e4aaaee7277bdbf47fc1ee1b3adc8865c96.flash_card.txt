### Front
How do you determine the surface temperature of a distant star using its spectral intensity graph?

### Back
- Key idea: Wien’s Displacement Law relates the wavelength of peak emission ($\lambda_{max}$) to absolute temperature ($T$).
- Formula / law / constant: $\lambda_{max} T = 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}$ (Wien's Constant).
- When to use it: When given an intensity vs. wavelength graph to find a star's temperature.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert nanometers (nm) to meters (m) before substituting into the formula.
