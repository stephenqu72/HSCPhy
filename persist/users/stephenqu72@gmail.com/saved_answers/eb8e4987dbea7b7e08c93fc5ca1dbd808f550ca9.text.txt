### Answer
James Clerk Maxwell’s predictions are fundamental to the provided model of an electromagnetic (EM) wave. His core predictions include:

1.  **Mutual Induction:** Maxwell predicted that a time-varying electric field generates a time-varying magnetic field, and vice versa. This creates a self-propagating wave where the oscillating $E$ and $B$ fields sustain each other.
2.  **Transverse Nature:** He predicted that electromagnetic waves are transverse, meaning both the electric field ($E$) and the magnetic field ($B$) oscillate perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation.
3.  **Orthogonality:** The model shows that the $E$ and $B$ fields are perpendicular to each other as well as to the direction of propagation (forming a mutually orthogonal triad).
4.  **Constant Speed:** Maxwell calculated that these waves travel at a constant speed in a vacuum, given by the formula $c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$, which matched the experimentally determined speed of light.
