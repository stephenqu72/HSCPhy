### Analysis of the Physics
The graph shows a **linear relationship** passing through the origin, which means that the gravitational field strength is directly proportional to the variable on the $x$-axis ($g \propto x$).

Inside a uniform sphere (a planet of constant density $\rho$), the gravitational field strength at a distance $r$ from the centre is given by:
$$g = \frac{GMr}{R^3}$$
where:
*   $G$ is the gravitational constant
*   $M$ is the mass of the planet
*   $R$ is the radius of the planet
*   $r$ is the distance from the centre of the planet ($r \le R$)

Since $G$, $M$, and $R$ are constants for a specific planet, the equation simplifies to $g = kr$ (where $k = \frac{GM}{R^3}$). This is the equation of a straight line passing through the origin. Therefore, the gravitational field strength is directly proportional to the **distance from the centre of the planet** when moving through the interior of the planet.
