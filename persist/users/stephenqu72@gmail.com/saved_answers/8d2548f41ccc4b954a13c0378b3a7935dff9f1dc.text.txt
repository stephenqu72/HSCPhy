To correctly label the diagram of the electromagnetic wave, we must apply our knowledge of the properties of electromagnetic radiation:

### Answer and Explanation

1.  **$E$ (Electric field):** The electric field component oscillates in one plane perpendicular to the direction of propagation. In the diagram, the vertical wave (the one pointing up and down) represents the electric field. You should label the vertical amplitude arrows with an $E$.
2.  **$B$ (Magnetic field):** The magnetic field component oscillates in a plane perpendicular to both the direction of propagation and the electric field. In the diagram, the horizontal wave (the one pointing side-to-side) represents the magnetic field. You should label the horizontal amplitude arrows with a $B$.
3.  **$c$ (Speed of light):** This represents the velocity at which the wave propagates through space. You should label the horizontal axis (the straight line moving forward) with a $c$ to indicate the direction of wave travel.
4.  **$\lambda$ (Wavelength):** This is the distance between two consecutive identical points on the wave (e.g., peak to peak or trough to trough). You should draw a bracket between two consecutive peaks on either the electric or magnetic wave and label it with the symbol $\lambda$.

**Summary of identification:**
*   **Vertical oscillations:** $E$
*   **Horizontal oscillations:** $B$
*   **Direction of propagation:** $c$
*   **Distance between two crests:** $\lambda$
