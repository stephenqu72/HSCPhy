To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of circular motion from the HSC Physics syllabus.

### Answer
The correct statement is **(B)**: The net force depends on the $v$, $m$, and $r$ and is directed towards the centre.

### Explanation
1.  **Uniform Circular Motion:** When an object moves in a circular path at a constant speed, its direction of motion is constantly changing. Because velocity is a vector (including both magnitude and direction), a changing direction implies a changing velocity, which results in acceleration.
2.  **Centripetal Force:** According to Newton’s Second Law ($F = ma$), an object that is accelerating requires a net external force. In uniform circular motion, this net force is called the **centripetal force**. The centripetal force is always directed toward the center of the circular path.
3.  **The Formula:** The magnitude of the centripetal force ($F_c$) is given by the formula:
    $$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
    Where:
    *   $m$ is the mass of the car.
    *   $v$ is the velocity (speed).
    *   $r$ is the radius of the circular path.
4.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   (A) is incorrect because an object changing direction must have a net force acting upon it.
    *   (B) is correct as it identifies the dependence on the variables $m, v,$ and $r$ and correctly states the direction as being towards the centre.
    *   (C) is incorrect because the force is always directed toward the centre of the circle, not a fixed cardinal direction.
    *   (D) is incorrect because the "centripetal force" is not a separate force added to gravity; rather, the centripetal force *is* the resultant sum of all forces (like friction or normal force components) acting on the car.
