### Front
In a nuclear fission reactor, how do you compensate for a decrease in fission rate caused by a loss of moderator?

### Back
- Key idea: Withdraw control rods to increase neutron population density.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When reaction rate drops due to insufficient thermal neutrons; withdrawing rods reduces neutron absorption, increasing the probability of fission.
- Common trap: Thinking that moving control rods is purely for cooling (temperature control); their primary function is neutron flux regulation via absorption.
