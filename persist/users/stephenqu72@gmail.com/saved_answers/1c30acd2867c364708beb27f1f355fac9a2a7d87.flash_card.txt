### Front
Why does increasing transmission voltage reduce power loss in electrical lines?

### Back
- Key idea: Increasing voltage ($V$) for a fixed power ($P$) load inversely reduces line current ($I$). Since power loss is proportional to $I^2$, even small reductions in current cause massive decreases in heat loss.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $P = VI$ and $P_{loss} = I^2R$.
- When to use it: Any calculation involving power transmission, efficiency, or the function of step-up transformers.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the current is squared ($I^2$); students often incorrectly assume a linear relationship between current and power loss.
