### Answer

To receive full marks for this question, you must contrast the predictions of the classical wave model with the observed experimental evidence of the photoelectric effect.

**The failure of the wave model can be summarized as follows:**

1.  **Existence of a Threshold Frequency:** The wave model suggests that light intensity determines energy delivery. It predicts that if the light is bright enough, any frequency of light should eventually eject an electron. However, experiments show that no electrons are ejected below a specific "threshold frequency" ($f_0$), regardless of the light's intensity.
2.  **Lack of Time Delay:** According to the wave model, energy is distributed evenly across a wavefront. If light were a wave, a very dim light would take time to "build up" enough energy to eject an electron. Experimentally, photoemission occurs almost instantaneously ($< 10^{-9}$ seconds) upon illumination, even at extremely low intensities.
3.  **Dependence of Kinetic Energy on Frequency:** The wave model predicts that the maximum kinetic energy of the emitted electrons should depend on the intensity (amplitude) of the light. In reality, the maximum kinetic energy is found to be independent of intensity and depends solely on the frequency ($f$) of the incident radiation, following the relationship $E_k = hf - \Phi$.

### How to solve it
1.  **Understand the Keyword "Discuss":** In an HSC Physics context, "discuss" requires you to provide points for and/or against, or in this case, to identify the discrepancy between the classical theory and the physical evidence. You must provide a structured argument.
2.  **Structure your answer:** Aim for 4 distinct points or two well-developed paragraphs. Since it is a 4-mark question, treat it as "1 mark for identifying the theory's prediction" and "1 mark for the actual experimental observation" for each of the two main points you choose to discuss.
3.  **Use correct terminology:** Always reference "intensity" (which relates to amplitude/wave energy) versus "frequency" (which relates to photon energy). 
4.  **Connect to evidence:** Ensure you explicitly state that the wave model views energy as continuous, whereas the photoelectric effect demonstrates that energy is delivered in discrete "quanta" or packets (photons).
