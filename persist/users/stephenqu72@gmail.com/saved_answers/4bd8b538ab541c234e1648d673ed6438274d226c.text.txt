To solve this problem, we use the fundamental radioactive decay formula.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) 0.354 g**.

### Explanation
Radioactive decay can be calculated using the following formula:
$$N_t = N_0 \times \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^n$$

Where:
*   $N_t$ is the amount remaining.
*   $N_0$ is the initial amount ($4.0 \text{ g}$).
*   $n$ is the number of half-lives that have passed ($3.5$).

**Step-by-step calculation:**
1. Substitute the known values into the equation:
   $$N_t = 4.0 \times \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{3.5}$$

2. Calculate the decay factor:
   $$\left( 0.5 \right)^{3.5} \approx 0.088388$$

3. Multiply by the initial mass:
   $$N_t = 4.0 \times 0.088388$$
   $$N_t \approx 0.35355 \text{ g}$$

Rounding to three significant figures, we get **0.354 g**, which matches option (C).
