To solve this problem, we use the diffraction grating formula:

$$d \sin(\theta) = m\lambda$$

### Part (a): Calculation
**1. Find the slit separation ($d$):**
The grating has 8000 slits over a width of $4\text{ cm}$ ($0.04\text{ m}$). The distance $d$ between adjacent slits is:
$$d = \frac{\text{width}}{\text{number of slits}} = \frac{0.04\text{ m}}{8000} = 5 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}$$

**2. Identify the variables:**
*   Order $m = 5$ (as indicated in the diagram).
*   The angle $\theta = 90^\circ$ (relative to the normal).
*   $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

**3. Calculate the wavelength ($\lambda$):**
Using $d \sin(\theta) = m\lambda$:
$$\lambda = \frac{d \sin(\theta)}{m}$$
$$\lambda = \frac{(5 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}) \times \sin(90^\circ)}{5}$$
$$\lambda = \frac{5 \times 10^{-6}}{5} = 1 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}$$

**Result:** The wavelength is $1000\text{ nm}$ (or $1 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}$).

---

### Part (b): Identify the colour
Visible light ranges approximately from $380\text{ nm}$ (violet) to $750\text{ nm}$ (red). A wavelength of $1000\text{ nm}$ falls outside the visible spectrum in the **infrared** region.

---

### Teacher's Tips for HSC Physics
*   **Unit Conversion:** Always convert centimetres to metres before calculating $d$ to ensure your final answer is in standard SI units (metres).
*   **The Grating Formula:** Remember that $\theta$ is the angle relative to the normal (the line perpendicular to the grating surface), not the surface itself. 
*   **Physical Meaning:** If you calculate a wavelength significantly larger than $750\text{ nm}$ or smaller than $380\text{ nm}$, double-check your powers of ten, but do not be afraid to state that it is "infrared" or "ultraviolet" if your math is correct!
