In HSC Physics, it is critical to distinguish between **spontaneous** and **induced** reactions. 
1.  **Spontaneous transmutation (radioactive decay):** An unstable nucleus emits particles (like alpha, beta, or gamma radiation) on its own to reach a more stable state. This happens naturally to isotopes like $^{235}\text{U}$.
2.  **Induced transmutation:** An external particle (usually a neutron) must strike the nucleus to cause a change, such as in nuclear fission or neutron capture.
