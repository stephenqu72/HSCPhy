To solve this problem, we use the formula for Young's double-slit interference pattern, which relates the distance between bright fringes (or spots) to the slit separation, wavelength, and distance to the screen.

### 1. Identify the given values:
*   Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $536\text{ nm} = 536 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$
*   Distance to screen ($D$) = $5.54\text{ m}$
*   Fringe separation ($\Delta y$) = $3.30\text{ cm} = 3.30 \times 10^{-2}\text{ m}$

### 2. The Formula:
The relationship is given by:
$$\Delta y = \frac{\lambda D}{d}$$
where $d$ is the separation distance between the two slits. We need to rearrange this to solve for $d$:
$$d = \frac{\lambda D}{\Delta y}$$

### 3. Calculation:
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$d = \frac{(536 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}) \times (5.54\text{ m})}{3.30 \times 10^{-2}\text{ m}}$$
$$d = \frac{2.96944 \times 10^{-6}}{3.30 \times 10^{-2}}\text{ m}$$
$$d \approx 9.00 \times 10^{-5}\text{ m}$$

### 4. Convert to micrometers ($\mu\text{m}$):
Since $1\text{ m} = 10^6\text{ }\mu\text{m}$:
$$d = 9.00 \times 10^{-5} \times 10^6\text{ }\mu\text{m} = 90.0\text{ }\mu\text{m}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct choice is **D**.
