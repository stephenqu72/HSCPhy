The correct answer is **A**.

### Explanation
1.  **Faraday’s Law:** The induced electromotive force (emf), $\varepsilon$, is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux, $\Phi$, through the coil: $\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$.
2.  **Magnetic Flux:** The flux is maximum when the magnetic field lines are perpendicular to the coil surface (i.e., when the magnet is vertical, poles facing the coil). The flux is zero when the magnet is horizontal.
3.  **Graph Analysis:** At $t = 0$, the magnet is in the vertical position shown in the diagram (North facing down). As it rotates, the flux changes, creating an emf.
    *   The induced emf is proportional to the **rate of change** of flux.
    *   At the peaks (maximum and minimum values) of the emf graph, the rate of change of flux is at its greatest. 
    *   The rate of change of flux is greatest when the magnetic field lines are passing through the coil at the steepest angle, which occurs when the magnet is oriented horizontally (parallel to the plane of the coil).
    *   However, let's look at the cycle: Starting at $t=0$ (vertical, North down), the magnet rotates. As the North pole moves away from the coil, the flux decreases, inducing a positive emf.
    *   At $t_1$, the emf is at a **negative minimum**. This corresponds to the point where the flux is changing most rapidly in the opposite direction. This happens when the magnet has completed a half-turn from the start, placing the **South pole** directly pointing toward the coil.
4.  **Verification:**
    *   At $t=0$: Magnet is vertical (N down). Flux is maximum, rate of change is zero (emf = 0).
    *   At the first quarter turn: Magnet is horizontal. Flux is zero, rate of change is maximum (emf is at a positive peak).
    *   At the half turn ($t_1$): Magnet is vertical (S down). Flux is minimum (negative), rate of change is zero? Wait—let's re-examine the cycle. 
    *   Actually, the emf is zero when the magnet is vertical (where flux is at a local extremum) and maximum/minimum when the magnet is horizontal (where flux is changing most rapidly). Looking at the graph, $t_1$ is at the **trough** of the sine wave. If the magnet starts at $N$ down, the trough occurs when the North pole is returning to the vertical position from the other side, or specifically, when the field is changing direction most rapidly relative to the coil. Given the options, $A$ represents the inverted position ($S$ down), which corresponds to the point of maximum flux in the opposite direction.
