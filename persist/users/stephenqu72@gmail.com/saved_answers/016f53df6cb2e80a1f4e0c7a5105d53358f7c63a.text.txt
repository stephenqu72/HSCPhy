To answer this question effectively for an HSC Physics exam, you need to apply Faraday's Law and Lenz's Law to the principles of electromagnetic induction.

### Answer
Magnet B takes longer to fall through the aluminium tube because of the interaction between the moving magnetic field and the conductive material of the tube. As magnet B falls, the magnetic flux through the cross-section of the aluminium tube changes. According to **Faraday's Law of Induction**, this changing magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) in the aluminium tube, which drives **eddy currents** within the tube walls.

According to **Lenz's Law**, these eddy currents create their own magnetic field that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced them. This induced magnetic field exerts a repulsive force on the approaching pole of the magnet and an attractive force on the trailing pole, creating a magnetic braking effect. No such currents are induced in the plastic tube because plastic is an insulator. Therefore, magnet A falls freely under gravity, while magnet B experiences an opposing magnetic force that decelerates its fall.

### How to solve this (HSC Exam Strategy)
When approaching a 5-mark explanation question like this, follow this logical framework:

1.  **Identify the Physics Principles:** Immediately recall the relationship between moving magnets and conductors. The key concepts are **Faraday’s Law** (change in flux induces EMF) and **Lenz’s Law** (the opposition to that change).
2.  **Define the mechanism:** Explicitly mention "eddy currents." These are circular loops of current induced in the bulk material of the conductor (the aluminium tube).
3.  **Establish causality:** 
    *   Explain *why* the current is there: Change in magnetic flux ($\Delta \Phi_B / \Delta t$) as the magnet moves.
    *   Explain *the effect* of the current: The eddy currents produce a secondary magnetic field.
4.  **Connect to Lenz's Law:** State that the induced magnetic field acts to oppose the motion of the magnet. Use the phrase "opposes the change in magnetic flux" to ensure you hit the syllabus terminology.
5.  **Contrast:** Briefly state why this doesn't happen in the plastic tube (plastic is a non-conductor/insulator, so no EMF can drive a current).

**Structuring your 5 marks:**
*   **Mark 1:** Identifying the change in magnetic flux.
*   **Mark 2:** Mentioning the induction of eddy currents in the aluminium.
*   **Mark 3:** Referencing Lenz’s Law/the opposition to motion.
*   **Mark 4:** Describing the repulsive/attractive forces (the magnetic braking effect).
*   **Mark 5:** Explicitly contrasting with the non-conductive plastic tube.
