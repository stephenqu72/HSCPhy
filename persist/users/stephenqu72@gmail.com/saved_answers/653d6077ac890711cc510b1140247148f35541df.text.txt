To improve the efficiency of an electrical transformer, we need to minimize energy losses. These losses primarily occur due to:
1. **Resistance in the coils:** Causes heat dissipation (Joule heating).
2. **Eddy currents in the core:** Induced currents caused by the changing magnetic flux, which generate heat.
3. **Magnetic hysteresis:** Energy lost in repeatedly magnetizing and demagnetizing the core material.

### Analysis of the options:

*   **(A)** Reducing the induced flux would decrease the output voltage, not improve efficiency.
*   **(B)** While using thick wire helps reduce resistance in the coils, this is typically done to manage current levels. However, it is not the standard "textbook" method for core-related efficiency, and (C) is a more definitive structural improvement for transformer efficiency.
*   **(C) Correct:** Using **laminated** layers of a **soft iron** core is the standard method to improve efficiency.
    *   **Lamination:** The layers are separated by an insulator (like enamel paint) to increase the electrical resistance path for eddy currents, thereby minimizing their magnitude and reducing heat loss.
    *   **Soft Iron:** This material is used because it is "magnetically soft," meaning it has high magnetic permeability and low coercivity, which allows it to be magnetized and demagnetized easily. This significantly reduces energy losses due to **hysteresis**.
*   **(D)** Using "hard iron" is incorrect. Hard iron has high coercivity, making it difficult to magnetize and demagnetize, which would lead to large energy losses through hysteresis.

**Conclusion:** Option (C) is the correct answer because it simultaneously addresses both eddy current losses (through lamination) and hysteresis losses (by using soft iron).
