To solve this problem, we must apply the **Right-Hand Rule** for a moving positive charge in a magnetic field. This is a classic HSC Physics question involving the Lorentz force.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   **Velocity ($v$):** Directed from East to West.
    *   **Magnetic Field ($B$):** Directed North.
    *   **Charge ($q$):** Positive.

2.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule (or the vector cross-product $F = q(v \times B)$):**
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the velocity ($v$) which is **West**.
    *   Curl your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$) which is **North**.
    *   Your thumb points in the direction of the magnetic force ($F$).
    *   If you point West and curl towards North, your thumb will point **upwards (away from the Earth's surface)**.

3.  **Analyze the effect on the motion:**
    *   The uncharged ball is only affected by gravity and will follow a standard parabolic projectile trajectory.
    *   The charged ball experiences an additional upward magnetic force. This force acts as an "anti-gravity" lift, effectively reducing the net downward acceleration of the ball while it is in motion.
    *   Because the ball experiences an upward force, it will stay in the air for a longer duration than the uncharged ball.

4.  **Conclusion:**
    *   Since the horizontal velocity component remains the same for both, but the charged ball remains in the air for a longer period of time, it will travel a greater horizontal distance. 
    *   Therefore, the charged ball has a **longer range** than the uncharged ball.

The correct statement is **B**.
