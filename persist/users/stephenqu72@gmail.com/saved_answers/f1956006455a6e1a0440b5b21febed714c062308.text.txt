### Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Calculate the energy of the photon**
We use the Planck-Einstein relation: 
$$E = hf$$
Where:
- $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$ (Planck's constant)
- $f = 2.5 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$

$$E = (6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (2.5 \times 10^{14}) = 1.6565 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

**2. Convert the energy from Joules to electronvolts (eV)**
Since the energy levels are given in eV, we convert our result using the conversion factor $1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$:
$$\Delta E (\text{in eV}) = \frac{1.6565 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}}{1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J/eV}} \approx 1.034 \text{ eV}$$
