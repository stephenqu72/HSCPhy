### Explanation
To solve this question, we must analyze the physics principles represented in each figure:

1.  **Figure 1 (Blackbody Radiation):** This graph shows the spectral radiance of a blackbody at various temperatures. Classical physics (the wave model of light) predicted that a blackbody should emit infinite energy at short wavelengths (the "ultraviolet catastrophe"). This model failed to match experimental data. Max Planck resolved this by proposing that light energy is emitted in discrete "quanta" or packets, which is the foundational concept of the **particle model of light** (photons). Therefore, blackbody radiation is a key piece of evidence supporting the particle model.

2.  **Figure 2 (Double-Slit Interference):** This figure illustrates a classic double-slit experiment where wavefronts pass through two slits and interfere with each other to create a pattern of alternating maxima and minima on a screen. Interference and diffraction are exclusive characteristics of waves. Particles (like tiny marbles) would simply pass through the slits and create two distinct bands on the screen. Because light creates an interference pattern, this is primary evidence supporting the **wave model of light**.

By matching these:
*   Figure 1 $\rightarrow$ Particle model
*   Figure 2 $\rightarrow$ Wave model

This corresponds to option **D**.
