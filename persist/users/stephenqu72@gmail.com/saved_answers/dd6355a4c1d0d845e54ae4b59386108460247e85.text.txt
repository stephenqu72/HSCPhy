The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation
To determine which situation induces the greatest electromotive force (emf), we must refer to **Faraday’s Law of Induction**, which is mathematically expressed as:

$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$$

Where:
*   $\varepsilon$ is the induced emf.
*   $N$ is the number of turns in the coil.
*   $\Delta\Phi$ is the change in magnetic flux.
*   $\Delta t$ is the time interval.

To maximize the induced emf ($\varepsilon$), we need to maximize the numerator $N \cdot \Delta\Phi$. 

1.  **Analyze the diagrams:**
    *   **Diagrams A, B, and C** have a coil with relatively few turns (5 loops).
    *   **Diagram D** has a coil with significantly more turns (10 loops).
2.  **Evaluating the variables:**
    *   Assuming the speed at which the magnet is plunged is constant across all options, the rate of change of flux through a single loop remains roughly similar.
    *   However, the total induced emf is directly proportional to the number of turns $N$ in the coil. By having more turns in the coil, the total magnetic flux linkage changes more significantly as the magnet passes through, resulting in a higher total emf induced across the entire coil.
    *   The pole of the magnet (North vs. South) affects the *direction* of the induced current (Lenz's Law), but it does not change the *magnitude* of the induced emf in this context.

Therefore, since diagram **D** possesses the greatest number of turns ($N$), it will produce the greatest amount of induced emf.
