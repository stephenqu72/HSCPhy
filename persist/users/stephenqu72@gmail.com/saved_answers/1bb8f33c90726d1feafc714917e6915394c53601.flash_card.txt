### Front
In an experiment testing whether mass affects the acceleration of falling objects, what must be kept as a controlled variable to ensure a fair test?

### Back
- Key idea: The drop height (distance $d$) must remain constant so that any difference in time is solely due to the independent variable (mass).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a = \frac{2d}{t^2}$
- When to use it: When designing a controlled experiment to investigate motion under gravity.
- Common trap: Changing the height while changing the mass, which makes it impossible to determine if time differences are caused by mass or distance.
