To determine the correct trajectory of the toy helicopter, we must analyze the forces acting on it and the resulting components of its motion.

### Analysis of Motion
1.  **Horizontal Motion:** The helicopter is released from a plane moving with a constant horizontal velocity. Since air resistance is negligible and there are no horizontal forces acting on the toy (the thrusters act only vertically), the horizontal velocity component $v_x$ remains constant throughout the motion.
2.  **Vertical Motion:** Initially, the helicopter has an initial vertical velocity of $0$ (assuming it is "dropped"). Once the thrusters are activated, they apply a constant upward force $F_T$ that is greater than the downward force of gravity $F_g = mg$.
    *   The net vertical force is $F_{net,y} = F_T - mg$.
    *   Since $F_T > mg$, the net force is positive (upwards), leading to a constant upward acceleration $a_y$ according to Newton's Second Law ($F = ma$).
    *   Because the acceleration is constant and directed upwards, the vertical position $y$ will follow the kinematic equation $y = v_{iy}t + \frac{1}{2}a_y t^2$. Since $v_{iy} = 0$, the helicopter will accelerate upwards from its point of release.

### Determining the Path
*   Because the horizontal velocity is constant and there is an upward acceleration, the path is a **parabola** that opens **upwards**.
*   The toy starts with horizontal momentum, so it will initially travel forward, but because the vertical acceleration is upwards, its downward "drop" is immediately countered and reversed.
