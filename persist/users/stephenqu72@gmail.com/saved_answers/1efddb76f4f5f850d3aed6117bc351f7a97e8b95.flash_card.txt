### Front
Derive the expression for the total mechanical energy $E$ of a satellite of mass $m$ in a stable circular orbit of radius $r$ around a central mass $M$.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Total energy $E$ is the sum of kinetic energy ($K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$) and potential energy ($U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$), where $K = -U/2$.
- **Formula / law:** $\frac{GMm}{r^2} = \frac{mv^2}{r} \implies E = K + U = \frac{GMm}{2r} - \frac{GMm}{r} = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$.
- **When to use it:** To determine the energy required to change orbits, escape gravity (total energy must reach 0), or calculate the binding energy of a satellite.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting the negative sign for gravitational potential energy ($U$), which leads to an incorrect sign for total energy.
