### Answer
In both situation (A) and (B), the beam of light moves towards Fred at exactly the speed of light, $c$ (approximately $3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$).

### Explanation
To solve this, you must apply the **Second Postulate of Special Relativity**, which states that the speed of light in a vacuum is constant for all observers, regardless of the motion of the light source or the observer.

1.  **The Physics Principle:** Unlike classical (Galilean) mechanics where velocities add linearly (e.g., a ball thrown from a moving train), light does not obey these addition rules. Whether Joe is stationary or moving at high speed towards Fred, the measured velocity of the light beam remains constant at $c$.
2.  **Michelson-Morley Experiment:** This experiment was designed to detect the "luminiferous aether," a medium through which light was thought to travel. By measuring the speed of light in different directions relative to the Earth's motion, they expected to find a variation in speed (an "aether wind"). The null result—finding no change in the speed of light—suggested that light does not require a medium and that its speed is invariant.
3.  **Einstein’s Contribution:** Einstein proposed the Theory of Special Relativity, which accepted the null result of the Michelson-Morley experiment as a fundamental truth. He elevated the invariance of the speed of light to a primary postulate. This directly explains why Fred measures $c$ in both scenarios: the speed of light is a universal constant, independent of the reference frame.

### How to approach this in an exam:
*   **Identify the key concept:** You must explicitly state that the speed of light is invariant.
*   **Address the evidence:** Explicitly link the Michelson-Morley "null result" to the disproval of the aether theory.
*   **Link to Einstein:** Connect the empirical evidence (Michelson-Morley) to Einstein's theoretical framework (Special Relativity) to demonstrate a high-level understanding of how scientific knowledge evolves.
