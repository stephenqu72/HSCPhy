### Answer

James Clerk Maxwell’s theory proposed that light is an **electromagnetic wave**. He unified the concepts of electricity and magnetism, suggesting that a changing electric field produces a changing magnetic field, and vice versa. These oscillating, mutually regenerating fields propagate through space as a self-sustaining transverse wave.

**Key components of the theory:**
1.  **Electromagnetic Nature:** Maxwell mathematically demonstrated that electric and magnetic fields travel as waves. 
2.  **Transverse Waves:** Unlike sound waves (longitudinal), light is a transverse wave where the electric field vector $E$ and the magnetic field vector $B$ oscillate perpendicular to each other and to the direction of wave propagation.
3.  **Constant Speed:** Using his equations, Maxwell calculated the speed of these waves to be $v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$, where $\mu_0$ is the permeability of free space and $\epsilon_0$ is the permittivity of free space. This calculated value matched the experimentally measured speed of light ($c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8$ m/s), leading to the conclusion that light is an electromagnetic wave.

**Evidence supporting the theory:**
1.  **Hertz’s Experiment:** Heinrich Hertz provided the crucial experimental confirmation. By creating an oscillating spark in a transmitter, he generated electromagnetic waves (radio waves). He detected these waves using a loop receiver with a spark gap at a distance, proving that energy could be transferred through space via electromagnetic radiation.
2.  **Similarity of Properties:** Hertz demonstrated that these generated radio waves exhibited the same properties as light, including reflection, refraction, diffraction, and polarization.
3.  **Velocity Alignment:** The speed of the waves produced in Hertz's experiments was found to be consistent with the speed of light, validating Maxwell’s prediction that the speed of electromagnetic waves is a fundamental constant independent of the frequency.
