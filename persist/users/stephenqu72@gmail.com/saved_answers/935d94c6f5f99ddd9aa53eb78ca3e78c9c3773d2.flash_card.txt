### Front
Why is Lenz’s Law considered a requirement of the Law of Conservation of Energy?

### Back
- **Key idea:** If the induced magnetic field reinforced the flux change, the system would accelerate indefinitely without work input, creating energy from nothing. Lenz’s Law ensures the induced force *opposes* motion, meaning external mechanical work is required to generate electrical energy.
- **Law:** Law of Conservation of Energy (Work done = Electrical energy produced).
- **When to use it:** To explain why induced currents must create a repulsive/opposing magnetic field.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to mention that external work must be performed; the induced current is not "free energy," but a conversion of mechanical energy.
