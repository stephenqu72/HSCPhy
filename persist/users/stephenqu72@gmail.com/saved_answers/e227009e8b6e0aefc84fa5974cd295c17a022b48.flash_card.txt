### Front
In which type of frame of reference are Newton’s Laws (e.g., $F=ma$) valid?

### Back
- Key idea: Inertial vs. Non-inertial frames.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s Second Law ($F = ma$).
- When to use it: To determine if standard Newtonian mechanics can be applied to an observer in motion.
- Common trap: Assuming high velocity (e.g., $0.75c$) makes a frame non-inertial; only **acceleration** or **deceleration** creates a non-inertial frame where Newton's laws fail.
