### Front
How does the gravitational force ($F$) between two point masses change if the mass of both objects is doubled?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational force is directly proportional to the product of the two masses ($F \propto m_1m_2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$; $G \approx 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{kg}^{-2}$.
- When to use it: To calculate the force between two masses or determine proportionality changes.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the distance ($r^2$) when calculating changes in $r$, or confusing direct proportionality (mass) with inverse-square law (distance).
