### Front
In a system where a cube maintains a fixed position on a rotating backboard, how does the horizontal force exerted by the backboard on the cube compare to the force exerted by the cube on the backboard?

### Back
- Key idea: Newton’s Third Law states that all action-reaction pairs are always equal in magnitude and opposite in direction.
- Formula / law: Newton’s Third Law ($F_{A \text{ on } B} = -F_{B \text{ on } A}$)
- When to use it: To identify the relationship between interacting objects, regardless of their state of motion (stationary, linear, or circular).
- Common trap: Assuming the forces are unequal because the object is accelerating (centripetal motion). Action-reaction pairs are independent of the object's acceleration.
