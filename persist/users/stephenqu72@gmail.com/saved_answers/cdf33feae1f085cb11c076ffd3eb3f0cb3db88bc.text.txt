### Answer
*   **AC Generators (Slip Rings):** AC generators do not use a "commutator" in the sense of a reversing switch. Instead, they use two **slip rings**. Each end of the rotating coil is connected to its own continuous metal ring. As the coil rotates, the connection to the external circuit remains fixed to the same end of the coil, allowing the current to flow back and forth, producing an alternating current (AC) output.
*   **DC Generators (Split-Ring Commutator):** DC generators use a **split-ring commutator**. This is a single metal ring divided into two halves separated by an insulator. As the coil rotates, the split-ring swaps its contact between the brushes at the exact moment the current in the coil reverses direction. This ensures that the current flowing into the external circuit always flows in the same direction, resulting in a direct current (DC) output.
*   **Comparison:** Both devices function as a mechanical interface to extract electrical energy from a rotating coil within a magnetic field. The contrast lies in whether the device preserves the natural polarity of the induced current (slip rings in AC) or reverses the connections to rectify the current into a single direction (split-ring in DC).
