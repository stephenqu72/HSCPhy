To solve this problem, we must apply the principle of **time dilation** from Einstein's Special Theory of Relativity.

### Explanation

When an object is moving at a velocity $v$ relative to an observer, the time interval $\Delta t$ observed by the stationary observer will be longer than the proper time interval $\Delta t_0$ (the time measured in the frame of reference where the object is at rest). This is expressed by the formula:

$$\Delta t = \gamma \Delta t_0$$

Where $\gamma$ (the Lorentz factor) is defined as:

$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$

**Step-by-step calculation:**

1.  **Identify given values:**
    *   Proper half-life ($\Delta t_0$) = $6.00$ days
    *   Velocity ($v$) = $0.9c$

2.  **Calculate the Lorentz factor ($\gamma$):**
    $$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{(0.9c)^2}{c^2}}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.81}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.19}}$$
    $$\gamma \approx \frac{1}{0.43589} \approx 2.29416$$

3.  **Calculate the observed half-life ($\Delta t$):**
    $$\Delta t = 2.29416 \times 6.00 \text{ days}$$
    $$\Delta t \approx 13.765 \text{ days}$$

Rounding to two decimal places, we get **13.76 days**.

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **C**.

### ANSWER
**C. 13.76 days.**
