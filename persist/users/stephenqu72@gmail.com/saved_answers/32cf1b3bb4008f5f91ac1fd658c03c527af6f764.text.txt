To solve this problem, we apply the **principle of conservation of energy**. Since the electron is released from rest and accelerated by an electric field, the electrical potential energy lost by the electron is converted into its kinetic energy.

The work done by an electric field on a charge $q$ is given by $W = qV$, where $V$ is the potential difference. This work equals the change in kinetic energy ($KE$) of the electron:
$$qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

$$V = \frac{mv^2}{2q}$$
