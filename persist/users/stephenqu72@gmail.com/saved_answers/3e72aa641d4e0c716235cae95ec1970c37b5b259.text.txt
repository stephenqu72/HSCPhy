To determine which part of the plane develops a positive charge, we must apply the physics of **electromagnetic induction** in the Earth's magnetic field, specifically the principle of motional electromotive force (EMF).

### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) The top of the right wing**.

### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of the **Right-Hand Rule** in the context of a conductor moving through the Earth's magnetic field.

1.  **The Physics Principle:** As the plane moves through the Earth’s magnetic field, the metal wings act as a conductor moving through a magnetic field. According to Faraday’s Law of induction, a motional EMF is induced across the conductor given by $\varepsilon = Bvl$, where $B$ is the magnetic field strength, $v$ is the velocity, and $l$ is the length of the conductor (the wingspan).
2.  **Direction of the Magnetic Field:** In the Southern Hemisphere (travelling from Perth to Sydney), the Earth's magnetic field lines point **upwards** and towards the magnetic south.
3.  **Applying the Right-Hand Rule (Force on Electrons):** 
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$), which is **upwards**.
    *   Point your thumb in the direction of the plane's velocity ($v$), which is **forward** (towards Sydney).
    *   Your palm (for positive charges) points to the right. However, we are dealing with free **electrons** in the metal. The magnetic force ($F = qvB$) on a negative electron will be in the **opposite direction** to the force on a positive charge.
    *   Applying the left-hand rule for electrons (or the right-hand rule and reversing the direction), the electrons are pushed towards the **left wing**.
4.  **Resulting Charge:** Since electrons accumulate on the left wing, the left wing becomes negatively charged. By conservation of charge, the **right wing** is left with a deficit of electrons, meaning it develops a **positive charge**.
5.  **Top vs. Bottom:** Because the Earth's magnetic field has both horizontal and vertical components, and the wings have a specific orientation relative to the airflow and the magnetic dip, the induced potential difference effectively makes the "top" of the wing structure the positive terminal in this specific orientation of movement.

Therefore, the right wing becomes positively charged.
