### How to solve this:
*   **Temperature:** Increases toward the left of the x-axis ($O \rightarrow M$).
*   **Luminosity:** Increases toward the top of the y-axis.
*   **Radius:** Follows the relation $L = 4\pi R^2 \sigma T^4$. Stars in the top right are large (giants), and stars in the bottom left are small (dwarfs).
*   **Lifespan:** The lifespan of a star is proportional to $\frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Luminosity}}$. Massive stars (top left) live for millions of years; low-mass stars (bottom right) live for billions.
