To solve these problems, we use the principles of gravitation and projectile motion. First, ensure all units are in SI:
- Mass of Titan ($M$) = $1.3 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$
- Radius of Titan ($r$) = $2,600 \text{ km} = 2.6 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
- Gravitational constant ($G$) = $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ Nm}^2\text{kg}^{-2}$

---

### (a) Escape velocity
The escape velocity is given by the formula:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$
Substitute the values:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 1.3 \times 10^{23}}{2.6 \times 10^6}} = \sqrt{\frac{1.7342 \times 10^{13}}{2.6 \times 10^6}} \approx 2583 \text{ m/s}$$

### (b) Surface gravity
The surface gravity ($g_{Titan}$) is calculated using:
$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$
Substitute the values:
$$g = \frac{6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 1.3 \times 10^{23}}{(2.6 \times 10^6)^2} = \frac{8.671 \times 10^{12}}{6.76 \times 10^{12}} \approx 1.28 \text{ m/s}^2$$

### (c) Projectile motion
First, split the velocity into components:
- $v_x = v \cos(30^\circ) = 1.6 \times \cos(30^\circ) \approx 1.386 \text{ m/s}$
- $v_y = v \sin(30^\circ) = 1.6 \times \sin(30^\circ) = 0.8 \text{ m/s}$

Use the displacement formula $y = v_y t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2$. Since it lands $0.65 \text{ m}$ below the launch point ($y = -0.65$):
$$-0.65 = 0.8t - 0.5(1.28)t^2 \implies 0.64t^2 - 0.8t - 0.65 = 0$$
Using the quadratic formula $t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$$t = \frac{0.8 + \sqrt{(-0.8)^2 - 4(0.64)(-0.65)}}{1.28} \approx \frac{0.8 + \sqrt{0.64 + 1.664}}{1.28} \approx 1.76 \text{ s}$$
Range ($R$) = $v_x \times t = 1.386 \times 1.76 \approx 2.44 \text{ m}$
