To solve this problem, we need to relate the horizontal range ($R$) of a projectile to its maximum height ($H$).

### Given:
- Angle of projection, $\theta = 45^\circ$
- Horizontal range, $R = 20\text{ m}$
- Acceleration due to gravity, $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$

### Formulas:
1. The horizontal range formula: 
   $$R = \frac{v^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$
2. The maximum height formula: 
   $$H = \frac{v^2 \sin^2(\theta)}{2g}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**Step 1: Simplify the range formula.**
Since $\theta = 45^\circ$, then $2\theta = 90^\circ$. Because $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$:
$$R = \frac{v^2}{g} = 20$$
From this, we find that the ratio $\frac{v^2}{g} = 20$.

**Step 2: Substitute into the height formula.**
The formula for maximum height is:
$$H = \frac{v^2 \sin^2(\theta)}{2g}$$
We can rearrange this as:
$$H = \left( \frac{v^2}{g} \right) \cdot \frac{\sin^2(45^\circ)}{2}$$

**Step 3: Calculate the value.**
We know $\sin(45^\circ) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$, so $\sin^2(45^\circ) = \frac{1}{2}$.
Substituting the known values:
$$H = (20) \cdot \frac{1/2}{2}$$
$$H = 20 \cdot \frac{1}{4}$$
$$H = 5\text{ m}$$

**Conclusion:**
The maximum height of the projectile is $5\text{ m}$. Comparing this to the given options, the correct choice is **d**.
