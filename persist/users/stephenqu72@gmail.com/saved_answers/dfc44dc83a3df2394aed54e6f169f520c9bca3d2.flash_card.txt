### Front
Compare the gravitational force exerted by the Earth on the Moon to the force exerted by the Moon on the Earth.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational forces are mutual and equal in magnitude, acting in opposite directions (Newton’s Third Law).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation ($F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$) and Newton’s Third Law.
- **When to use it:** When asked about interactions between two bodies in a gravitational field.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the larger mass (Earth) exerts a greater force on the smaller mass (Moon). The force is always identical for both objects.
