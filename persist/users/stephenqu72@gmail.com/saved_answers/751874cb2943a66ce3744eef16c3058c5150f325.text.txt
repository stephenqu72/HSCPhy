To determine the correct order, we must recall the electromagnetic spectrum, ordered by increasing frequency (which corresponds to decreasing wavelength).

### The Full Electromagnetic Spectrum (Increasing Frequency)
The standard sequence of the electromagnetic spectrum from lowest frequency to highest frequency is:
1. **Radio waves** (lowest frequency, longest wavelength)
2. **Microwaves**
3. **Infrared**
4. **Visible light**
5. **Ultraviolet**
6. **X-rays**
7. **Gamma-rays** (highest frequency, shortest wavelength)

### Evaluating the Options
*   **(A) gamma-ray, microwave, visible:** Incorrect. Gamma-ray has the highest frequency of the three.
*   **(B) microwave, infrared, visible:** **Correct.** Looking at the sequence above, microwaves have a lower frequency than infrared, and infrared has a lower frequency than visible light.
*   **(C) visible, microwave, radio:** Incorrect. This is in order of *decreasing* frequency.
*   **(D) X-ray, ultraviolet, infrared:** Incorrect. This is in order of *decreasing* frequency (X-rays are higher than ultraviolet, which are higher than infrared).
