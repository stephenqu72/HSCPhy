To find the centripetal force acting on the cork, we use the formula for centripetal force:

$$F_c = mr\omega^2$$

### 1. Identify the given values and convert to SI units:
*   Mass of the cork ($m$): $200\text{ g} = 0.2\text{ kg}$
*   Radius of the path ($r$): $60\text{ cm} = 0.6\text{ m}$
*   Frequency ($f$): $5\text{ Hz}$

### 2. Calculate the angular velocity ($\omega$):
The angular velocity is related to the frequency by the formula $\omega = 2\pi f$:
$$\omega = 2 \times \pi \times 5 = 10\pi\text{ rad/s}$$

### 3. Calculate the centripetal force ($F_c$):
Now, substitute these values into the force equation:
$$F_c = (0.2\text{ kg}) \times (0.6\text{ m}) \times (10\pi\text{ rad/s})^2$$
$$F_c = 0.12 \times (100\pi^2)$$
$$F_c = 12\pi^2$$

Using the approximation $\pi^2 \approx 9.87$:
$$F_c \approx 12 \times 9.87 = 118.44\text{ N}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, $118.44\text{ N}$ is closest to **120 N**.

**Correct Answer: B**

***

### How to solve this problem:
1.  **Standardise Units:** Always convert grams to kilograms and centimetres to metres before plugging numbers into physics formulas.
2.  **Determine Angular Velocity:** Remember that $f$ (frequency in Hz) must be converted to $\omega$ (angular velocity in rad/s) using $\omega = 2\pi f$ because circular motion formulas require radians.
3.  **Choose the Correct Formula:** Since you are provided with frequency rather than linear velocity, the version $F_c = mr\omega^2$ is the most direct method.
4.  **Approximation:** If you do not have a calculator for $\pi^2$, use the standard approximation $\pi^2 \approx 10$, which gives $12 \times 10 = 120$. This confirms the selection of option B.
