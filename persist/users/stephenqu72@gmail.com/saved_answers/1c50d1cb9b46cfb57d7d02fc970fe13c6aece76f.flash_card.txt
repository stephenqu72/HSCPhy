### Front
Why can a synchrotron not accelerate particles starting from rest?

### Back
- Key idea: The magnetic field $B$ is used to keep particles in a *fixed* circular radius $r$; particles must enter with sufficient momentum to match this path.
- Formula / law: $p = qBr$
- When to use it: To explain the necessity of an "injector" (e.g., Linac) before entering a synchrotron.
- Common trap: Assuming all circular accelerators behave like cyclotrons (which can accelerate from near-rest) rather than recognizing the fixed-radius constraint of a synchrotron.
