### Answer
The correct answer is **(B) Einstein’s thought experiments**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we must evaluate the purpose of each experiment listed:

*   **(A) Millikan’s oil drop experiment:** This experiment was designed to determine the elementary charge of a single electron. While it confirmed the quantized nature of charge, it was not the experiment used to develop models of light.
*   **(B) Einstein’s thought experiments:** Einstein used thought experiments (gedankenexperiments) to analyze the photoelectric effect and the nature of light. Specifically, his theoretical work on the photoelectric effect led to the proposal that light consists of individual "quanta" or photons, possessing energy $E = hf$. This work provided the crucial evidence for the particle-like nature of light, which was a pivotal step in the development of the quantum model of light.
*   **(C) JJ Thomson’s cathode ray experiments:** These experiments were designed to study the properties of cathode rays, leading to the discovery of the electron and the determination of its charge-to-mass ratio. They describe the nature of matter, not light.
*   **(D) The Hafele-Keating experiment:** This experiment involved flying atomic clocks around the Earth to test the predictions of time dilation in Special and General Relativity. It confirmed the effects of relativity on time, rather than the fundamental nature of light itself.

By process of elimination and knowledge of the history of physics, Einstein's theoretical insights into light's interaction with matter (the photoelectric effect) represent the most direct contribution to the modern model of light among the choices provided.
