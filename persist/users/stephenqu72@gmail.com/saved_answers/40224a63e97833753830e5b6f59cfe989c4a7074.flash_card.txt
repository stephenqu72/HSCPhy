### Front
In a conical pendulum, how do the forces acting on the mass relate to its vertical and horizontal motion?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Tension is resolved into two components: the vertical component balances gravity ($mg$), and the horizontal component provides the centripetal force ($F_c$).
- **Formula / law:** $\tan \theta = \frac{v^2}{rg} = \frac{F_c}{mg}$
- **When to use it:** When calculating speed, angle, or radius for an object moving in a horizontal circle suspended by a string.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the object is in equilibrium. Because the direction is constantly changing, the object is accelerating; therefore, net force $\neq 0$.
