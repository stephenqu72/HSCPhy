### Answer
The correct answer is **(C)**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must consider the physics of uniform circular motion on a banked track.

1.  **Force Analysis:** When a train moves along a curved, banked track, there are two primary forces acting on it (and its occupants):
    *   The gravitational force ($F_g = mg$), which acts vertically downwards.
    *   The normal force ($F_N$), which acts perpendicular to the surface of the banked track.

2.  **Vector Components:** Because the track is banked at an angle $\theta$, the normal force ($F_N$) can be resolved into two perpendicular components:
    *   A vertical component: $F_N \cos\theta$
    *   A horizontal component: $F_N \sin\theta$

3.  **The Centripetal Requirement:** For an object to move in a circle, a net inward (centripetal) force is required. In the case of a banked track, the horizontal component of the normal force ($F_N \sin\theta$) points toward the center of the curve. Therefore, this component *provides* or *contributes to* the required centripetal force ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$).

4.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   (A) is incorrect because the gravitational force does not match the centripetal force; they are perpendicular to each other in the standard frame of reference.
    *   (B) is incorrect because the normal force is always greater than the centripetal force (it must also counteract gravity).
    *   (C) is **correct** because the normal force is the specific force that provides the horizontal component necessary for circular motion.
    *   (D) is incorrect because if the normal force aligned with the gravitational force, there would be no horizontal component to provide circular motion.
