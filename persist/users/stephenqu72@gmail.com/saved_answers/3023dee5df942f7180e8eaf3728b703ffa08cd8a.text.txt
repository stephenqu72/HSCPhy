To determine the direction of the force acting on the particle, we use the fundamental definition of an electric field.

### Answer
The correct answer is **C. Up the page**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, you need to apply the relationship between an electric field ($E$) and the force ($F$) exerted on a charged particle ($q$):

$$F = qE$$

1.  **Understand the Field Direction:** The arrows in the diagram represent the electric field lines, which point vertically upwards. By definition, an electric field vector points in the direction that a *positive* test charge would be pushed.
2.  **Determine the Force Direction:** The particle in the diagram is positively charged ($+$). 
    *   For a positive charge, the force vector ($F$) is in the same direction as the electric field vector ($E$).
    *   Since the electric field lines point "up the page," the force on the positive charge will also be directed "up the page."
3.  **Address Distractors:** Students often confuse this with magnetic field problems. If this were a magnetic field, we would use the Right-Hand Rule to find a force directed into or out of the page. However, for a stationary or moving particle in an **electric field**, the velocity ($v$) does not change the direction of the electric force; the force is always parallel (or anti-parallel) to the field lines. Therefore, the velocity $v$ is a distractor in this context.

Since the field points up and the charge is positive, the force is directed **up the page**.
