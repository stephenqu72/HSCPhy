To calculate the energy released in a nuclear reaction, we follow the principle of mass-energy equivalence, where the "missing" mass ($\Delta m$) is converted into energy ($E$) using Einstein’s equation, $E = \Delta mc^2$. In HSC Physics, when using atomic mass units ($u$), we use the conversion factor $1 \, u = 931.5 \, \text{MeV}$.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Calculate the total mass of the reactants:**
$$\text{Mass}_{\text{reactants}} = \text{Mass}(^{12}\text{C}) + \text{Mass}(^{1}\text{H})$$
$$\text{Mass}_{\text{reactants}} = 12.064 + 1.008 = 13.072 \, u$$

**2. Calculate the total mass of the products:**
$$\text{Mass}_{\text{products}} = \text{Mass}(^{9}\text{B}) + \text{Mass}(^{4}\text{He})$$
$$\text{Mass}_{\text{products}} = 9.013 + 4.003 = 13.016 \, u$$

**3. Calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$):**
The mass defect is the difference between the initial mass and the final mass:
$$\Delta m = \text{Mass}_{\text{reactants}} - \text{Mass}_{\text{products}}$$
$$\Delta m = 13.072 - 13.016 = 0.056 \, u$$

**4. Convert the mass defect into energy:**
Using the conversion $1 \, u = 931.5 \, \text{MeV}$:
$$E = \Delta m \times 931.5 \, \text{MeV}/u$$
$$E = 0.056 \times 931.5 = 52.164 \, \text{MeV}$$

**Answer:** The energy released in the reaction is **$52.164 \, \text{MeV}$**.

***

### How to solve this type of question:
*   **Identify the Reactants and Products:** Always sum the masses on the left side of the equation and compare them to the sum of the masses on the right side.
*   **Find the Mass Defect:** Subtract the final mass from the initial mass. If the result is positive, energy is released (exothermic); if negative, energy is absorbed (endothermic).
*   **Use the Conversion Factor:** In physics exams, you are expected to use the equivalence $1 \, u \approx 931.5 \, \text{MeV}$. Do not attempt to use $c^2$ in SI units unless specifically asked, as it is much more complex and prone to unit errors.
