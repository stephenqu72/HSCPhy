### Analysis of the Question
The question asks for two specific outcomes regarding photoemission:
1.  **"Produce more electrons per unit time":** The number of electrons emitted (the photocurrent) is directly proportional to the number of photons hitting the surface per unit time. This is determined by the **intensity** of the light. Therefore, to increase the number of electrons, we must **increase the intensity**.
2.  **"With less kinetic energy per electron":** The maximum kinetic energy ($E_k$) of the emitted photoelectrons is determined by Einstein’s photoelectric equation:
    $$E_k = hf - \Phi$$
    where $h$ is Planck’s constant, $f$ is the frequency of the light, and $\Phi$ is the work function of the metal. Since the frequency $f$ is related to wavelength $\lambda$ by $f = \frac{c}{\lambda}$, we can write:
    $$E_k = \frac{hc}{\lambda} - \Phi$$
    To decrease $E_k$, we need to decrease the energy of the incident photons ($hf$). This is achieved by **increasing the wavelength** ($\lambda$), as energy and wavelength are inversely proportional.

### Conclusion
*   To get more electrons: **Increase intensity**.
*   To get less kinetic energy per electron: **Increase wavelength**.
