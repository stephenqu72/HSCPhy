### Explanation
To solve this, we need to analyze the visual information provided in the diagram:

2.  **Equal Areas:** The shaded regions represent the area swept out by a line segment connecting the planet to the Sun during those equal time intervals. The diagram explicitly labels these areas as $A$, implying that $A_1 = A_2 = A_3$.

**Kepler's Second Law** states that a line segment joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time. Mathematically, this is expressed as:
$$\frac{dA}{dt} = \text{constant}$$

This law implies that planets must move faster when they are closer to the Sun (perihelion) and slower when they are further away (aphelion) to ensure the swept area remains constant over time.
