### Answer

**(a) Discuss the importance of James Clerk Maxwell’s contribution to the classical theory of electromagnetism.**

Maxwell’s most significant contribution was the formulation of **Maxwell's Equations**, which unified the separate phenomena of electricity and magnetism into a single, cohesive framework. 
1. **Unification:** He synthesized the work of predecessors (Gauss, Faraday, and Ampère) and introduced the "displacement current" term. This corrected Ampère's Law, accounting for the changing electric field in a capacitor, which was essential for theoretical consistency.
2. **Prediction of Waves:** Through his equations, he derived a wave equation for electromagnetic fields. He calculated the velocity of these waves to be $v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0}}$, which was numerically identical to the known speed of light ($c$).
3. **Nature of Light:** This led to his revolutionary hypothesis that light itself is an electromagnetic wave, fundamentally changing our understanding of the universe and paving the way for modern telecommunications and the theory of Special Relativity.

**(b) Describe two supporting observations that provided confirmation of Maxwell’s contribution to understanding electromagnetic waves.**

1. **Hertz’s Experiment (1887):** Heinrich Hertz provided the first experimental proof of electromagnetic waves. By using an oscillating spark-gap transmitter, he generated "Hertzian waves" (radio waves) and detected them at a distance using a receiver loop, proving that electromagnetic disturbances could travel through space as waves at the speed of light.
2. **Measurement of Wave Properties:** Hertz further demonstrated that these waves behaved exactly like light, exhibiting reflection, refraction, interference, and polarization. By measuring the wavelength and frequency of the standing waves he created, he confirmed that $v = f\lambda$ was equal to the speed of light, providing empirical validation for Maxwell's theoretical predictions.

### How to solve this
1. **Analyze the Verb:** "Discuss" in part (a) means you must explore the topic, identify key aspects (unification, prediction), and show why they matter (the link to light). 
2. **Allocate Marks:** Part (a) is worth 3 marks, so ensure you have 3 distinct, strong points. Part (b) is worth 2 marks, requiring two specific examples with enough detail to show you understand *how* they confirm the theory.
3. **Use Technical Terminology:** Mention "displacement current," "unification," "speed of light," and "Hertz's experiment." These are "buzzwords" that examiners look for in an HSC response.
4. **Link Theory to Experiment:** Always remember that in Physics, a theory (Maxwell) is only as good as the experiment that validates it (Hertz). Always make that connection explicit.
