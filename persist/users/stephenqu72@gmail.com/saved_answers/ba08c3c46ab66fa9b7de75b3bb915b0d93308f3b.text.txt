### Explanation

Electrical energy is transmitted from power stations to consumers over long distances. The power lost as heat in the transmission lines due to resistance $R$ is given by the formula:
$$P_{loss} = I^2 R$$
where $I$ is the current flowing through the lines.

To minimize energy loss ($P_{loss}$), it is crucial to keep the current ($I$) as low as possible. Since power transmitted is defined by $P = VI$ (where $V$ is voltage), for a constant power output, increasing the voltage $V$ allows us to decrease the current $I$ significantly.

1.  **Step-up transformers** are used at power stations to increase the voltage and decrease the current, which minimizes energy lost as heat during transmission. This makes (A) and (B) correct statements.
2.  **Step-down transformers** are used near residential areas to reduce the extremely high transmission voltage to a safe, usable level for households. This makes (D) a correct statement.
3.  **Option (C)** suggests that transformers reduce energy losses by *increasing* current. As shown by $P_{loss} = I^2 R$, increasing the current would exponentially *increase* the energy loss in the lines, which is the opposite of the intended function.
