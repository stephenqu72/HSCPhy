To solve this problem, we must apply the **Law of Conservation of Energy** for an object in a gravitational field.

### Explanation and Solution

In an elliptical orbit, the total mechanical energy (sum of kinetic and gravitational potential energy) is conserved at every point along the path. 

**1. Identify the Energy Equations:**
The total energy $E$ of a projectile of mass $m$ at a distance $r$ from the centre of the Earth is given by:
$$E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{GMm}{r}$$
where $G$ is the gravitational constant and $M$ is the mass of the Earth.

**2. Set up the conservation equation:**
Since energy is conserved, the energy at the launch point (distance $R$, velocity $v_0 = 8200 \text{ m/s}$) must equal the energy at the furthest point (distance $2R$, velocity $v$):
$$\frac{1}{2}mv_0^2 - \frac{GMm}{R} = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{GMm}{2R}$$

**3. Simplify the expression:**
We can divide the entire equation by $m$:
$$\frac{1}{2}v_0^2 - \frac{GM}{R} = \frac{1}{2}v^2 - \frac{GM}{2R}$$

**4. Use the relationship for orbital motion:**
From the conservation of angular momentum, for a projectile at the extremes of its orbit, $r_1v_1 = r_2v_2$ is NOT directly applicable here because the velocity at the start is not necessarily perpendicular to the radius. However, we can use the standard value for Earth's surface gravity: $g = \frac{GM}{R^2}$, meaning $GM = gR^2$. Given $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ and $R \approx 6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$:
$$GM \approx 3.98 \times 10^{14} \text{ m}^3/\text{s}^2$$

Rearranging for $v$:
$$\frac{1}{2}v^2 = \frac{1}{2}v_0^2 - \frac{GM}{R} + \frac{GM}{2R}$$
$$\frac{1}{2}v^2 = \frac{1}{2}v_0^2 - \frac{GM}{2R}$$
$$v^2 = v_0^2 - \frac{GM}{R}$$

**5. Calculation:**
Using $v_0 = 8200 \text{ m/s}$, $G \approx 6.67 \times 10^{-11}$, $M \approx 5.97 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$, $R \approx 6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$:
$$v^2 = (8200)^2 - \frac{3.98 \times 10^{14}}{6.37 \times 10^6}$$
$$v^2 = 67,240,000 - 62,480,376$$
$$v^2 \approx 4,759,624$$
$$v \approx 2181.6 \text{ m/s}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (based on the input), the speed is approximately **$2.2 \times 10^3 \text{ m/s}$**.
