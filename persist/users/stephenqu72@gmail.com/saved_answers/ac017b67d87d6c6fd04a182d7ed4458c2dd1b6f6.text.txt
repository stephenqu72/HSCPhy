To solve this problem, we need to determine two properties of the electric field: its **direction** and its **magnitude (strength)**.

### 1. Determining the Direction
By definition, electric field lines point from regions of higher electric potential to regions of lower electric potential.
*   Line Y has a potential of $+100\text{ V}$.
*   Line X has a potential of $+50\text{ V}$.

Since the field points from high potential to low potential, the direction of the electric field is from **Y to X**.

### 2. Determining the Strength
In a uniform electric field, the magnitude of the electric field ($E$) is given by the formula:
$$E = \frac{\Delta V}{d}$$
Where:
*   $\Delta V$ is the potential difference between the two points: $100\text{ V} - 50\text{ V} = 50\text{ V}$.
*   $d$ is the distance between the two lines: $2.0\text{ cm}$.

Plugging in the values:
$$E = \frac{50\text{ V}}{2.0\text{ cm}} = 25\text{ V cm}^{-1}$$

### Conclusion
Comparing our results to the options:
*   **Direction:** $Y \rightarrow X$
*   **Strength:** $25\text{ V cm}^{-1}$

This corresponds to **Option C**.
