### Answer
James Clerk Maxwell’s contribution was a foundational turning point in physics, as he unified the separate domains of electricity and magnetism into a single theory of electromagnetism. His primary achievement was the derivation of equations that predicted the existence of electromagnetic waves, which propagate through space at a speed $c$ determined by the permittivity ($\varepsilon_0$) and permeability ($\mu_0$) of free space:
$$c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\varepsilon_0 \mu_0}}$$
By calculating this speed, Maxwell demonstrated that it matched the experimentally measured speed of light. This was significant because it provided the first theoretical evidence that light is an electromagnetic wave rather than a mechanical wave requiring a medium. His work shifted the scientific paradigm, leading to the dismissal of the "luminiferous aether" hypothesis and laying the theoretical groundwork for the later development of special relativity and quantum mechanics.
