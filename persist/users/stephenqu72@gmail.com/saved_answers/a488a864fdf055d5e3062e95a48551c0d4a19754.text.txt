### Explanation
To determine the charge of these particles as they move through a magnetic field, we use the **Right-Hand Rule** (for positive charges) or the **Left-Hand Rule** (for negative charges) to find the direction of the magnetic force $F_m$ acting on them, defined by the formula:
$$F = qvB \sin(\theta)$$

1.  **Particle L:** It travels in a straight line. This indicates that it experiences no magnetic force. According to the force formula, if $v \neq 0$ and $B \neq 0$, the force can only be zero if the charge $q$ is zero. Therefore, **L has no charge** (it is a gamma ray).
2.  **Particle K:** Using the Right-Hand Rule:
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the velocity (to the right).
    *   Curl your fingers into the page (direction of $B$).
    *   Your thumb points **upwards**.
    *   Since the particle actually deflects upwards, it behaves as a **positive charge** (e.g., an alpha particle).
3.  **Particle M:** 
    *   If we use the Right-Hand Rule, the force on a positive charge would be upwards. Since particle M deflects downwards (the opposite direction), it must be a **negative charge** (e.g., a beta particle). Alternatively, using the Left-Hand Rule for a negative charge, your thumb will point downwards, confirming it is negative.

Comparing these results to the options provided:
*   K: positive
*   L: no charge
*   M: negative
