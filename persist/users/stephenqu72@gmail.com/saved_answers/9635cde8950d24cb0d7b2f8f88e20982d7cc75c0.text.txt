### 1. Identify the peak frequency from the graph
Looking at the graph, the peak of the curve (maximum energy output) occurs at a frequency of $f_{peak} = 2.0 \times 10^{14}\text{ Hz}$.

### 2. Convert frequency to wavelength
Wien's Displacement Law is typically expressed in terms of wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$):
$$\lambda_{max} T \approx 2.898 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$$
Since we have frequency, we use the relationship $c = f\lambda$, where $c$ is the speed of light ($3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$):
$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{c}{f_{peak}} = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{2.0 \times 10^{14}} = 1.5 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}$$

### 3. Calculate the Temperature ($T$)
Rearrange Wien's Displacement Law to solve for $T$:
$$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{\lambda_{max}}$$
$$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{1.5 \times 10^{-6}}$$
$$T \approx 1.932 \times 10^3\text{ K}$$
