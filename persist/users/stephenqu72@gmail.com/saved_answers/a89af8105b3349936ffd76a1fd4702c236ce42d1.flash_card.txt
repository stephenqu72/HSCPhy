### Front
What evidence did Edwin Hubble use to determine the recession velocity of galaxies in the context of the expanding universe?

### Back
- Key idea: Redshift of spectral lines (Doppler effect) observed in galactic spectra.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = H_0d$ (Hubble’s Law)
- When to use it: To calculate the recession velocity ($v$) of a galaxy when the distance ($d$) and Hubble's constant ($H_0$) are known.
- Common trap: Confusing luminosity (used for distance) with spectra (used for velocity); they are two distinct measurements required to prove the expansion.
