### Front
How does the magnetic field magnitude $B$ vary along the central axis of an ideal solenoid?

### Back
- Key idea: The magnetic field inside an ideal solenoid is uniform (constant).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $B = \mu_0 n I$ (for an infinite/ideal solenoid).
- When to use it: Use for solenoid interior problems unless the question specifies a "finite" or "short" solenoid requiring end-effect analysis.
- Common trap: Assuming the field drops to zero or varies significantly inside; in HSC physics, treat the interior field of a standard solenoid as constant.
