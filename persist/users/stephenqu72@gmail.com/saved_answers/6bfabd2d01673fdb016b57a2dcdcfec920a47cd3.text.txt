### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) $\text{m s}^{-2}$**.

### Explanation
To determine the S.I. unit for gravitational field strength ($g$), we look at its definition: gravitational field strength is the force exerted per unit mass on an object in a gravitational field.

We can express this mathematically as:
$$g = \frac{F}{m}$$

Where:
*   $F$ is the gravitational force, measured in Newtons ($\text{N}$).
*   $m$ is the mass, measured in kilograms ($\text{kg}$).

So, the unit for $g$ is $\text{N/kg}$. However, we can break this down further into fundamental S.I. base units using Newton's Second Law ($F = ma$):
*   Force ($F$) has the unit $\text{kg m s}^{-2}$.
*   Therefore, $g = \frac{\text{kg m s}^{-2}}{\text{kg}}$.

By canceling out the $\text{kg}$ from the numerator and the denominator, we are left with:
$$g = \text{m s}^{-2}$$

This unit is equivalent to an acceleration, which makes sense because gravitational field strength is numerically equal to the acceleration due to gravity.
