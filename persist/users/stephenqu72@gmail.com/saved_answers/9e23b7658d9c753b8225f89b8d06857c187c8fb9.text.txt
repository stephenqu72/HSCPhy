The correct diagram is **A**.

### Explanation
To determine which path a proton takes through an electric field, we use the relationship between the electric force and the electric field. The force $F$ on a charge $q$ in an electric field $E$ is given by:
$$F = qE$$

1.  **Analyze the Physics:**
    *   Since a proton has a positive charge ($q > 0$), the force exerted on it by the electric field must be in the **same direction** as the electric field vector.
    *   If a particle is moving with an initial velocity parallel to the electric field (or if the force is acting in the same or opposite direction as the motion), the particle will continue to move in a **straight line** (it will simply accelerate or decelerate).
    *   If the force is perpendicular to the motion, the particle would follow a parabolic path.

2.  **Evaluate the options:**
    *   **Diagram A:** The proton is moving to the left, and the electric field is also pointing to the left. Since the force $F = qE$ acts in the direction of $E$, the proton experiences a force to the left. This force acts along the same line as the velocity, causing the proton to accelerate in a straight line. This is correct.
    *   **Diagram B:** The velocity is downward and the field is to the left. The force would pull the proton to the left, causing it to curve (a parabolic trajectory).
    *   **Diagrams C and D:** These show magnetic fields (indicated by the '$\times$' notation). The question specifically asks for a "uniform electric field," so these are incorrect contexts for this specific question.

Therefore, because the force on the proton is collinear with its velocity in diagram A, it continues to move in a straight line.
