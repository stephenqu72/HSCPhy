### Front
How does centripetal force ($F$) change if the velocity ($v$) of an object in a circular path is halved, while mass and radius remain constant?

### Back
- Key idea: Centripetal force is directly proportional to the square of the velocity ($F \propto v^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: Any problem involving uniform circular motion where speed changes but the radius of the path remains constant.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the velocity change; many students incorrectly assume the force halves instead of quartering.
