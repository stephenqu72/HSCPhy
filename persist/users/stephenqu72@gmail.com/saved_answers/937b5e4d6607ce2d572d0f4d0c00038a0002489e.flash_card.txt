### Front
Why does sound bend around a doorway while light does not?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Diffraction (the bending of waves around obstacles).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the wave is comparable to the size of the aperture/obstacle ($d$). Significant diffraction occurs when $\lambda \approx d$.
- **Common trap:** Assuming light doesn't diffract at all; it does, but the wavelength of visible light is so small ($10^{-7}\text{ m}$) that diffraction through a doorway is negligible.
