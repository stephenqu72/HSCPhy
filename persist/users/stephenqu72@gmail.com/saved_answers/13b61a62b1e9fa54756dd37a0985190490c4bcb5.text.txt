### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a): Determine the gradient of the graph
To find the gradient ($m$) of a linear graph passing through the origin $(0,0)$, we use the formula $m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}$. 

Looking at the graph, we can select the point $(x = 8 \times 10^6 \text{ ms}^{-1}, y = 3 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m})$:
$$m = \frac{3 \times 10^{-3} - 0}{8 \times 10^6 - 0} = \frac{3}{8} \times 10^{-9}$$
$$m = 0.375 \times 10^{-9} = 3.75 \times 10^{-10} \text{ s/C}$$

#### Part (b): Calculate the magnetic field strength
1. **Derive the relationship:** When an electron moves through a magnetic field perpendicular to its velocity, the magnetic force provides the centripetal force:
   $$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
   Rearranging for radius ($r$):
   $$r = \left( \frac{m}{qB} \right) v$$
   Comparing this to the equation of a straight line ($y = mx$), the gradient is:
   $$\text{gradient} = \frac{m}{qB}$$

2. **Substitute known values:**
   - Mass of an electron ($m$): $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
   - Charge of an electron ($q$): $1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
   - Gradient: $3.75 \times 10^{-10} \text{ s/C}$

   Rearranging for $B$:
   $$B = \frac{m}{q \times \text{gradient}}$$
   $$B = \frac{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}{(1.60 \times 10^{-19}) \times (3.75 \times 10^{-10})}$$
   $$B = \frac{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}{6 \times 10^{-29}} \approx 0.01518 \text{ T}$$

Rounding to appropriate significant figures (consistent with the data), we get **$B \approx 1.52 \times 10^{-2} \text{ T}$**.
