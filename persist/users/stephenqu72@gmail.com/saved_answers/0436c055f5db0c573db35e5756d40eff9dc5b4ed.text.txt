To solve this problem, we must apply the Doppler effect formula for light.

### Answer
The relative speed $v$ required is approximately $2.77 \times 10^7 \, \text{m/s}$ (or $0.092c$).

### Explanation

**1. Understand the relationship between frequency and wavelength:**
The speed of light $c$ is given by $c = f \lambda$. Therefore, frequency $f$ can be written as $f = \frac{c}{\lambda}$. Substituting this into the provided formula $f = \frac{f_0}{1 \pm v/c}$:
$$\frac{c}{\lambda} = \frac{c / \lambda_0}{1 \pm v/c}$$
$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = \frac{1}{\lambda_0 (1 \pm v/c)}$$
$$\lambda = \lambda_0 (1 \pm v/c)$$

**2. Determine the correct sign:**
The observer is moving **toward** the source, which causes a "blueshift" (the observed wavelength $\lambda$ is smaller than the emitted wavelength $\lambda_0$). Therefore, the denominator term must result in a smaller wavelength. Using the formula derived above, we use the minus sign to represent decreasing wavelength:
$$\lambda = \lambda_0 (1 - v/c)$$

**3. Substitute the given values:**
*   $\lambda_0 = 6.5 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}$ (Emitted red light)
*   $\lambda = 5.9 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}$ (Observed yellow light)
*   $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}$

Rearranging for $v$:
$$\frac{\lambda}{\lambda_0} = 1 - \frac{v}{c}$$
$$\frac{v}{c} = 1 - \frac{\lambda}{\lambda_0}$$
$$v = c \left( 1 - \frac{5.9 \times 10^{-7}}{6.5 \times 10^{-7}} \right)$$
$$v = 3.00 \times 10^8 \times \left( 1 - 0.9077 \right)$$
$$v \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \times 0.0923$$
$$v \approx 2.77 \times 10^7 \, \text{m/s}$$
