### Front
Why does the actual trajectory of a projectile differ from the theoretical parabolic model?

### Back
- Key idea: Air resistance (drag) acts as a non-conservative force that dissipates mechanical energy, causing $v_x$ to decrease and altering $v_y$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Any question comparing theoretical ("vacuum") vs. real-world projectile motion.
- Common trap: Forgetting that air resistance affects both horizontal range (due to deceleration) and vertical height/symmetry, not just the range.
