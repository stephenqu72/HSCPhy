To answer this question correctly, we must understand the physics governing the motion of a charged particle within a cyclotron.

### Correct Answer:
**B. It only accelerates while between the dees.**

---

### Explanation:

1.  **Motion inside the dees:** Inside the hollow electrode chambers (the "dees"), the particle is shielded from the electric field. It is moving solely through a uniform magnetic field ($B$). The force acting on the particle is the magnetic Lorentz force, given by $F = qvB \sin(\theta)$. Since the velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field, this force acts as a centripetal force, causing the particle to move in a semicircular path. Because the magnetic force is always perpendicular to the direction of motion, it changes the *direction* of the velocity but **cannot change its magnitude** (speed). Thus, the particle does not accelerate inside the dees.

2.  **Motion between the dees:** In the gap between the two dees, there is an alternating electric field ($E$). When the particle crosses this gap, it experiences an electric force ($F = qE$). This force is parallel (or anti-parallel) to the direction of motion, which performs work on the particle. According to the work-energy theorem, this work results in an increase in the kinetic energy of the particle, which corresponds to an **increase in speed (acceleration)**.

3.  **Why the other options are incorrect:**
    *   **A is incorrect** because the magnetic field does no work on the particle while it is inside the dees.
    *   **C is incorrect** because there is no electric field inside the hollow dees to cause linear acceleration.
    *   **D is incorrect** because the particle maintains a constant speed while inside the dees, it does not slow down.

### Summary
The cyclotron design uses the gap for acceleration via an electric field and the dees for uniform circular motion via a magnetic field. Therefore, the particle only gains speed when crossing the gap.
