To solve this problem, we use **Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion**. 

### Explanation
Kepler’s Third Law states that the square of the orbital period ($T$) of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis ($r$) of its orbit:
$$\frac{T^2}{r^3} = k$$

When using units of **Earth years** for the period and **Astronomical Units (AU)** for the distance, the constant $k$ becomes $1$. Therefore, we can express the relationship as:
$$T^2 = r^3$$

Given in the question:
- The average distance of the dwarf planet is 13 times that of Earth, so $r = 13\text{ AU}$.

Now, substitute $r$ into the equation:
$$T^2 = 13^3$$
$$T^2 = 2197$$

To find the orbital period $T$, take the square root of both sides:
$$T = \sqrt{2197}$$
$$T \approx 46.87\text{ years}$$
