To solve this problem, we need to calculate the **centripetal force** required for the car to safely navigate the bend and then compare it to the available frictional forces provided in the table.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
When a car moves in a circle of radius $r$ at a constant speed $v$, it requires a centripetal force ($F_c$) to keep it on the circular path. This force is provided by the friction between the tires and the road. For the car to drive safely (i.e., without skidding), the available frictional force ($F_f$) must be greater than or equal to the required centripetal force:
$$F_f \ge F_c$$
The formula for centripetal force is:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

### 2. Calculate the Required Centripetal Force
Given values:
- Mass ($m$) = $2200 \text{ kg}$
- Speed ($v$) = $10.5 \text{ m/s}$
- Radius ($r$) = $23 \text{ m}$

Substitute these into the formula:
$$F_c = \frac{2200 \times (10.5)^2}{23}$$
$$F_c = \frac{2200 \times 110.25}{23}$$
$$F_c = \frac{242550}{23} \approx 10545.65 \text{ N}$$

### 3. Compare with the Table
The car requires a minimum of approximately **$10546 \text{ N}$** of frictional force to safely stay on the bend at this speed.

Looking at the table:
- **Dry:** $9500 \text{ N}$ (Less than $10546 \text{ N}$)
- **Heavy rain:** $8000 \text{ N}$ (Less than $10546 \text{ N}$)
- **Chemical spill:** $6500 \text{ N}$ (Less than $10546 \text{ N}$)

Since the required force ($10546 \text{ N}$) exceeds the maximum available friction in all three scenarios, it is not safe to drive around the bend under any of these conditions.
