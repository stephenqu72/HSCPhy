### Front
Explain the common physical origin of energy release in both nuclear fission and fusion.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Energy is released when nucleons transition to a more stable state (higher binding energy per nucleon) by converting "mass defect" into energy.
- **Formula / law:** $E = \Delta mc^2$ (Einstein's mass-energy equivalence).
- **When to use it:** Whenever asked to compare the energetic outcomes of fission (heavy nuclei splitting) or fusion (light nuclei combining).
- **Common trap:** Confusing "mass defect" with the mass of the particles themselves; the mass of products is *less* than the mass of reactants.
