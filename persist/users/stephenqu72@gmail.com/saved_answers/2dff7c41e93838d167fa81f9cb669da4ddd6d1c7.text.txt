To determine the correct graph for the induced electromotive force (EMF), we must analyze the physics of a conductor rotating in a uniform magnetic field using Faraday's Law of Induction.

### Answer
The correct answer is **A**.

### Explanation
1.  **Faraday’s Law and Magnetic Flux:** The induced EMF ($\varepsilon$) in a conductor is equal to the rate of change of magnetic flux through the area swept out by the conductor:
    $$\varepsilon = - \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt}$$
    where $\Phi_B = B \cdot A \cdot \cos(\theta)$, with $\theta$ being the angle between the magnetic field lines and the normal to the area swept by the conductor.

2.  **Analyzing the Geometry:** 
    *   In the starting position shown in the diagram, the conductor $PQ$ is perpendicular to the magnetic field $B$. 
    *   As the conductor rotates, the area "swept" by the conductor changes. Specifically, the component of the velocity vector $v$ that is perpendicular to the magnetic field $B$ determines the EMF. The motional EMF is given by $\varepsilon = B \cdot l \cdot v_{\perp}$, where $v_{\perp} = v \cdot \sin(\theta)$ (or $\cos(\theta)$ depending on the reference).
    *   At the start ($t=0$), the conductor is moving parallel to the magnetic field lines at point $Q$. When the velocity is parallel to the field lines, the magnetic force on the charges is zero, so the **EMF is 0**. This immediately eliminates options B and D.

3.  **Determining the Shape:**
    *   As the conductor rotates further, it sweeps through the magnetic field lines. The rate at which the conductor "cuts" the field lines varies sinusoidally with the angle of rotation because the effective length cutting the field changes as $L \sin(\omega t)$.
    *   At $t=0$, the EMF is 0. As it rotates to the horizontal position, it cuts the maximum number of field lines per unit time, reaching a maximum EMF. It then returns to 0 at the bottom, and reverses sign as it moves through the other half of the circle.
    *   This periodic sinusoidal variation with an initial value of 0 corresponds to graph **A**.

4.  **Why others are incorrect:**
    *   **B:** Starts at a maximum, which would only happen if the conductor were already moving perpendicular to the field at $t=0$.
    *   **C:** Suggests no EMF, which is incorrect as the conductor is actively cutting through magnetic field lines.
    *   **D:** Suggests a linear change, which does not reflect the circular nature of the rotation.
