### How to solve this:
To earn the full 4 marks, your diagram must be neat, clear, and include the following key features:
1.  **Initial Wavefront:** Draw a line (or curve) representing the initial position of the wavefront (e.g., a straight line for a plane wave).
2.  **Secondary Sources:** Place several points (dots) along this wavefront.
3.  **Secondary Wavelets:** Draw small semi-circles (or spheres) originating from these points. Ensure they all have the same radius, representing distance $d = vt$, where $v$ is the wave speed and $t$ is the time interval.
4.  **New Wavefront:** Draw a tangent line that touches the outermost edge of all these secondary wavelets. This represents the new position of the wavefront.
5.  **Directional Rays:** Draw arrows perpendicular to the wavefronts to show the direction of propagation (rays).

### Explanation:
Huygens’ Principle provides a geometric method for determining the future position of a wavefront. By treating every point on a wavefront as a point source, we can see how the energy of the wave propagates forward. The envelope tangent to all the secondary wavelets at any later time $t$ defines the new wavefront. This model is fundamental in explaining diffraction and reflection phenomena in wave optics.
