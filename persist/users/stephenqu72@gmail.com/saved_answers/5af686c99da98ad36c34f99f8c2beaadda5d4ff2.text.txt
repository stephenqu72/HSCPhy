### Answer

Back emf and the current produced in a generator are fundamentally linked through the principles of electromagnetic induction and energy conservation.

1.  **Origin and Nature:** The current produced in a generator is the output caused by the change in magnetic flux through the coil (Faraday’s Law). Back emf (electromotive force) is an induced voltage that opposes the change in the magnetic flux that produced it (Lenz’s Law).
2.  **Relationship:** The back emf is a direct consequence of the current flowing in the generator. As the generator supplies current to an external load, that current creates its own magnetic field, which interacts with the external magnetic field of the generator.
3.  **The "Opposition" Effect:** While the generator produces a terminal voltage to drive current, the back emf acts to oppose the motion of the generator (the mechanical torque). If the load current increases, the back emf becomes more significant, requiring more mechanical input energy (torque) to maintain the rotation speed, which illustrates the Law of Conservation of Energy.
4.  **Mathematical Link:** Both are governed by the rate of change of magnetic flux, $\Phi_B$. The induced emf is given by $\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi_B}{\Delta t}$. The current $I$ is related by Ohm’s Law, $I = \frac{V - \varepsilon}{R}$, where $V$ is the supply or terminal voltage and $R$ is the internal resistance.
