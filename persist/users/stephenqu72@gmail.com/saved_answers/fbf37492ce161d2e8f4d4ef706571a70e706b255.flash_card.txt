### Front
What is the procedure for solving a particle undergoing uniform circular motion in a horizontal plane (e.g., a ball in a bowl)?

### Back
- Key idea: Resolve forces into vertical (equilibrium) and horizontal (centripetal) components.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $N \cos\theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ and $N \sin\theta = mg$ (assuming $\theta$ is measured from the horizontal).
- When to use it: Whenever an object moves in a horizontal circle due to a normal force or tension.
- Common trap: Misidentifying the angle $\theta$. Always draw a Free Body Diagram to check if your angle is relative to the horizontal or vertical.
