To find the acceleration due to gravity ($g$) on the surface of a planet, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation and the concept of weight. The gravitational force between a planet of mass $M$ and an object of mass $m$ on its surface is:

$$F = \frac{G M m}{r^2}$$

Since the weight of the object is $F = mg$, we can equate these to solve for $g$:

$$mg = \frac{G M m}{r^2}$$
$$g = \frac{G M}{r^2}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the constants and variables:**
    *   Universal Gravitational Constant, $G \approx 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3 \text{kg}^{-1} \text{s}^{-2}$
    *   Mass of Venus, $M = 4.87 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$
    *   Diameter of Venus $= 12\,100 \text{ km} = 1.21 \times 10^7 \text{ m}$
    *   Radius of Venus, $r = \frac{\text{diameter}}{2} = 6.05 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$

2.  **Substitute the values into the formula:**
    $$g = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (4.87 \times 10^{24})}{(6.05 \times 10^6)^2}$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$g = \frac{3.248 \times 10^{14}}{3.660 \times 10^{13}}$$
    $$g \approx 8.874 \text{ m s}^{-2}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches option (A).

**Answer:** (A) $8.87 \text{ m s}^{-2}$
