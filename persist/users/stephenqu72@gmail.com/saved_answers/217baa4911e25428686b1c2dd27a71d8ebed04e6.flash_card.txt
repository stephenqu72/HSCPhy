### Front
How can you calculate the mass of a star ($M_*$) if you are given the orbital radius ($r$) and period ($T$) of an orbiting exoplanet?

### Back
- Key idea: Use Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$
- When to use it: When determining the mass of a central body based on the orbital characteristics of a satellite or planet.
- Common trap: Failing to convert the orbital period ($T$) into SI units (seconds).
