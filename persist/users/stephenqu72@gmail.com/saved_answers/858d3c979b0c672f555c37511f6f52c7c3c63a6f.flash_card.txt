### Front
How do you calculate the initial velocity ($v$) of a projectile launched at an angle ($\theta$) on flat ground when the horizontal range ($R$) is known?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The horizontal range formula for projectile motion.
- **Formula / law:** $v = \sqrt{\frac{R \cdot g}{\sin(2\theta)}}$
- **When to use it:** When solving for launch velocity given range and angle on level ground.
- **Common trap:** Neglecting that this is an "idealised" model; it lacks **validity** in real-world scenarios because it ignores air resistance (drag), which would require a higher initial velocity to reach the same range.
