### Front
What is the work done by an electric field on a charge $q$ when moving it through a potential difference $\Delta V$?

### Back
- Key idea: Work done is equal to the negative change in potential energy ($W = -q\Delta V$), or the energy gained by the charge as it moves to a lower potential.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $W = -q(V_f - V_i)$
- When to use it: Whenever a charge moves between two points in an electric field (e.g., parallel plates).
- Common trap: Confusing work done *by the field* with work done *by an external agent*; remember that if a positive charge moves to a lower potential, the field does positive work (energy is released).
