### Explanation

To solve this, we must evaluate the contributions of James Clerk Maxwell to the field of electromagnetism as per the HSC Physics curriculum:

*   **(B) Unified the fields of Electricity, magnetism and light:** This is correct. Maxwell's equations unified these phenomena by showing that electric and magnetic fields travel through space as waves at the speed of light.
*   **(C) Calculated the speed of light:** This is correct. By using the constants of permittivity ($\epsilon_0$) and permeability ($\mu_0$) from his equations, Maxwell derived the formula $c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0}}$, which yielded a value for the speed of light that matched experimental measurements.
*   **(D) Explained how EMR propagates:** This is correct. Maxwell’s equations described the self-propagating nature of electromagnetic waves, where oscillating electric fields generate magnetic fields and vice versa.
*   **(A) Proved that EMR exists beyond the visible spectrum:** This is **not** a contribution of Maxwell. While Maxwell's theory *predicted* the existence of other electromagnetic waves, he did not experimentally prove the existence of EMR beyond the visible spectrum. That discovery was made later by **Heinrich Hertz**, who experimentally generated and detected radio waves in 1887, providing the empirical proof of Maxwell's theory.

Since the question asks which was **not** a contribution, (A) is the correct choice.
