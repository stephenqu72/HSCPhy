### Front
How does using a spanner facilitate the removal of a tight nut in terms of torque?

### Back
- Key idea: Increasing the radial distance ($r$) from the pivot point increases the torque ($\tau$) for a constant applied force ($F$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\tau = F \cdot r \cdot \sin(\theta)$ (Principle of Moments).
- When to use it: Any problem involving levers, pivots, or rotational effects of force.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the force must be applied perpendicular to the spanner to achieve maximum torque ($\sin 90^\circ = 1$).
