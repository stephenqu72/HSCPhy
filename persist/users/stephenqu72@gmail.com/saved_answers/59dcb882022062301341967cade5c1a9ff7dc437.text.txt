### Answer and Explanation

This problem requires us to analyze the kinematics of a projectile and linearize a non-linear relationship.

#### (i) Completing the table
To complete the last column $(y/t)$, we divide the value in the $y$ column by the corresponding $t$ value for each row.
*   Row 1: $9.3 / 0.05 = 186$
*   Row 2: $21.0 / 0.10 = 210$
*   Row 3: $35.0 / 0.15 \approx 233.3$
*   Row 4: $51.8 / 0.20 = 259$
*   Row 5: $71.0 / 0.25 = 284$
*   Row 6: $92.2 / 0.30 \approx 307.3$

#### (ii) Plotting the linear relationship
The equation given is $\frac{y}{t} = u + kt$. This is in the form of a linear equation $Y = mX + c$, where:
*   $Y = \frac{y}{t}$ (on the vertical axis)
*   $X = t$ (on the horizontal axis)
*   $m = k$ (the slope)
*   $c = u$ (the vertical intercept)

To plot this, you would label the y-axis "$y/t$ ($\text{cm s}^{-1}$)" from $150$ to $350$ and the x-axis "$t$ (s)" from $0$ to $0.35$. Plot the calculated points and draw a line of best fit through them.

#### (iii) Finding $u$ and $k$
To find the constants, we use the graph from part (ii):
1.  **Vertical Intercept ($u$):** Extend your line of best fit to the y-axis (where $t=0$). The value at this point is the intercept $u$. From the data trend, $u \approx 160\text{ cm s}^{-1}$.
2.  **Gradient ($k$):** Select two points $(t_1, Y_1)$ and $(t_2, Y_2)$ on your line of best fit. Calculate the slope:
    $$k = \frac{Y_2 - Y_1}{t_2 - t_1}$$
    Using Row 1 $(0.05, 186)$ and Row 6 $(0.30, 307.3)$:
    $$k = \frac{307.3 - 186}{0.30 - 0.05} = \frac{121.3}{0.25} \approx 485.2\text{ cm s}^{-2}$$

*Note: In physics theory, $y = v_{iy}t + \frac{1}{2}gt^2$, so $y/t = v_{iy} + \frac{1}{2}gt$. Thus, $u$ represents the initial vertical velocity and $k$ represents half the acceleration due to gravity ($g/2$).*
