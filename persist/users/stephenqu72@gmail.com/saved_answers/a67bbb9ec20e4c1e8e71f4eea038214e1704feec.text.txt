To determine the correct diagram, we apply the **Right-Hand Grip Rule** for a current-carrying straight wire.

### Answer
The correct answer is **A**.

### Explanation
1.  **The Rule:** The Right-Hand Grip Rule states that if you point the thumb of your right hand in the direction of the conventional current ($I$), your curled fingers will indicate the direction of the magnetic field lines ($B$) around the wire.
2.  **Application:** 
    *   The current is flowing **upwards**.
    *   Point your right thumb upward along the wire.
    *   Your fingers curl around the wire in a **counter-clockwise** direction when viewed from above (or moving from right-to-left in front of the wire).
3.  **Evaluating the diagrams:**
    *   **Diagram A:** Shows the magnetic field lines circling the wire. If you use your right hand with your thumb pointing up, your fingers emerge from behind the wire to the left and go behind the wire from the right, which matches the counter-clockwise rotation shown.
    *   **Diagram B:** Represents a clockwise rotation, which would be correct if the current were flowing downwards.
    *   **Diagram C:** Incorrectly shows magnetic field lines that are vertically parallel to the wire.
    *   **Diagram D:** Represents a uniform field (like that found inside a solenoid), not the field produced by a single straight wire.

By following the Right-Hand Grip Rule, we confirm that A is the only diagram that accurately represents the physics of a current-carrying straight wire.
