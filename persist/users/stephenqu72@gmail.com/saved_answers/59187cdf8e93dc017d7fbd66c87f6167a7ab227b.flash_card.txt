### Front
How do you determine the magnitude of torque ($\tau$) acting on a rotating object like a bicycle wheel?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Torque is the rotational equivalent of force, dependent on the magnitude of force and its perpendicular distance from the pivot (lever arm).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\tau = rF\sin(\theta)$ or $\tau = r_{\perp}F$
- **When to use it:** To calculate the turning effect of a force; use $\tau = rF$ when force is applied perpendicularly at maximum radius.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the distance from the pivot ($r$) with the perpendicular distance ($r_{\perp}$); always ensure you use the component of force perpendicular to the radius.
