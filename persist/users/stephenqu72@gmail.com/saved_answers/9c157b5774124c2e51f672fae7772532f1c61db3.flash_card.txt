### Front
How does the binding energy per nucleon curve determine whether a nucleus undergoes fission or fusion?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Nuclei move toward the peak of the curve (Iron-56) to reach a state of higher stability and lower energy.
- **Figure:** Binding energy per nucleon vs. mass number (A) graph.
- **When to use it:** To identify stable decay products or energy release in nuclear reactions.
    - **Fission:** Heavy nuclei ($A > 56$) split to increase binding energy per nucleon.
    - **Fusion:** Light nuclei ($A < 56$) combine to increase binding energy per nucleon.
- **Common trap:** Confusing "total binding energy" with "binding energy per nucleon." Stability is defined solely by the latter.
