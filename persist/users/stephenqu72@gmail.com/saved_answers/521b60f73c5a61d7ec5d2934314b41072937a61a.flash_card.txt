### Front
Why do astronauts in an orbiting spacecraft experience "weightlessness"?

### Back
- Key idea: Both the astronaut and spacecraft are in a state of constant freefall, accelerating toward Earth at the same rate ($a = \frac{GM}{r^2}$), resulting in zero normal force ($F_N = 0$).
- Formula / law / constant: Newton’s Second Law ($F=ma$); $a = \frac{GM}{r^2}$.
- When to use it: Explaining the sensation of weightlessness in orbit or freefall environments.
- Common trap: Assuming weightlessness means there is no gravity; gravity is actually very strong at orbital altitudes.
