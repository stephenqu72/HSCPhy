To determine the correct answer, we must recall the physics of **uniform circular motion**.

### Analysis
Work done is defined by the formula:
$$W = F \cdot s \cdot \cos(\theta)$$
where:
- $F$ is the magnitude of the force (in this case, the centripetal force).
- $s$ is the displacement.
- $\theta$ is the angle between the force vector and the displacement vector.

In uniform circular motion, the centripetal force always acts toward the center of the circle, while the instantaneous displacement (the velocity vector) is always tangent to the circle. Because the radius is perpendicular to the tangent at any point, the force and displacement are **perpendicular** ($\theta = 90^\circ$). Since $\cos(90^\circ) = 0$, the work done is exactly $0$.
