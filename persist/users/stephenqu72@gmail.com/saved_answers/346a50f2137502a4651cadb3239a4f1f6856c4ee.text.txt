To determine the daughter nuclide produced by the alpha decay of bismuth-211, we follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the parent nucleus
Bismuth (Bi) has an atomic number of 83. The mass number is given as 211. Therefore, the parent nuclide is written as:
$$_ {83}^{211} \text{Bi}$$

### 2. Understand alpha decay
An alpha particle is a helium nucleus, consisting of 2 protons and 2 neutrons. It is represented as:
$$_ {2}^{4} \alpha \quad \text{or} \quad _ {2}^{4} \text{He}$$
When a nucleus undergoes alpha decay, it loses 2 protons and 2 neutrons. Consequently:
- The **mass number** (top number) decreases by 4.
- The **atomic number** (bottom number) decreases by 2.

### 3. Calculate the daughter nuclide
Let the daughter nuclide be $X$:
$$_ {83}^{211} \text{Bi} \rightarrow _ {Z}^{A} X + _ {2}^{4} \text{He}$$

*   **For the mass number ($A$):** $211 - 4 = 207$
*   **For the atomic number ($Z$):** $83 - 2 = 81$

The element with atomic number 81 is Thallium (Tl). Therefore, the daughter nuclide is:
$$_ {81}^{207} \text{Tl}$$

### 4. Evaluate the options
Comparing our result ($_{81}^{207} \text{Tl}$) to the provided options:
*   A: $_{81}^{207} \text{Tl}$ (This matches our calculation)
*   B: $_{85}^{207} \text{Tl}$ (Incorrect atomic number)
*   C: $_{85}^{215} \text{At}$ (This represents alpha *addition* or another process)
*   D: $_{87}^{215} \text{Fr}$ (Incorrect numbers)

**Correct Answer: A**
