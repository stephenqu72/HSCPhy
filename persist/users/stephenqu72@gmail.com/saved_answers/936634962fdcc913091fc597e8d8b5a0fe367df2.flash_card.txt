### Front
What type of error is caused by unpredictable, inconsistent fluctuations in an experimental apparatus (e.g., a "homemade" launcher producing varying initial velocities)?

### Back
- Key idea: Random error
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure
- When to use it: When measurements show unpredictable scatter or lack precision due to uncontrolled variables.
- Common trap: Confusing it with "systematic error," which occurs when measurements are consistently biased in one direction (e.g., zero-offset error).
