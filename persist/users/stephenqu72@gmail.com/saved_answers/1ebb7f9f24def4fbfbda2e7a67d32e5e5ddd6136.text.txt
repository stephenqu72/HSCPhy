To determine which trajectory has the longest time-of-flight, we must consider the physics of vertical motion in projectile motion. 

1.  **Understanding the Vertical Component:** The time-of-flight ($t$) of a projectile launched from level ground depends solely on the initial vertical component of the velocity ($u_y$) and the acceleration due to gravity ($g$). The formula for time-of-flight is:
    $$t = \frac{2u \sin(\theta)}{g}$$
    where $u$ is the initial launch speed and $\theta$ is the angle of projection.

2.  **The Role of Launch Angle:** Since all projectiles in the diagram are launched with the same speed ($u$), the time-of-flight is directly proportional to $\sin(\theta)$. As the launch angle $\theta$ increases (up to $90^\circ$), the vertical velocity component $u_y = u \sin(\theta)$ increases. 

3.  **Connecting to the Diagram:** 
    *   A greater vertical velocity component results in the projectile reaching a higher maximum height ($h_{max} = \frac{(u \sin\theta)^2}{2g}$). 
    *   By looking at the diagram, Trajectory A reaches the greatest vertical height. This indicates that it has the largest vertical velocity component $u_y$.
