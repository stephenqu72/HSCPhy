To find the strength of the magnetic field, we use the formula for the magnetic force experienced by a moving charged particle:

$$F = qvB \sin(\theta)$$

### Given Information:
*   **Force ($F$):** $3.2 \times 10^{-14}\text{ N}$
*   **Velocity ($v$):** $2.0 \times 10^6\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   **Charge of a proton ($q$):** $1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$ (standard physical constant)
*   **Angle ($\theta$):** $90^\circ$ (since the proton moves "perpendicular" to the magnetic field, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$)

### Step-by-Step Calculation:
1.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $B$:**
    $$B = \frac{F}{qv \sin(\theta)}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values:**
    $$B = \frac{3.2 \times 10^{-14}}{(1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times (2.0 \times 10^6) \times 1}$$

3.  **Simplify the denominator:**
    $$(1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times (2.0 \times 10^6) \approx 3.204 \times 10^{-13}$$

4.  **Calculate $B$:**
    $$B \approx \frac{3.2 \times 10^{-14}}{3.2 \times 10^{-13}} \approx 0.1\text{ T}$$

Comparing our result to the options provided:
(A) $0.08\text{ T}$
(B) $0.10\text{ T}$
(C) $0.24\text{ T}$
(D) $0.32\text{ T}$

The correct option is **(B)**.
