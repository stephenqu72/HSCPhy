To solve this problem, we must analyze the forces acting on the ball at the top and bottom of its vertical path.

### 1. Identify Given Information
*   Mass ($m$) = $0.130\text{ kg}$
*   Radius ($r$) = $95.5\text{ cm} = 0.955\text{ m}$
*   Frequency ($f$) = $5.00\text{ rev/s}$

First, calculate the angular velocity ($\omega$):
$$\omega = 2\pi f = 2 \times \pi \times 5.00 = 10\pi \approx 31.416\text{ rad/s}$$

### 2. Force Analysis
The net force required for circular motion is the centripetal force: $F_c = m r \omega^2$.

*   **At the top:** Both tension ($T_{top}$) and gravity ($mg$) act downwards, providing the centripetal force:
    $$T_{top} + mg = m r \omega^2 \implies T_{top} = m r \omega^2 - mg$$

*   **At the bottom:** Tension ($T_{bottom}$) acts upwards and gravity ($mg$) acts downwards:
    $$T_{bottom} - mg = m r \omega^2 \implies T_{bottom} = m r \omega^2 + mg$$

### 3. Determine the Difference
We want to find $T_{bottom} - T_{top}$:
$$\text{Difference} = (m r \omega^2 + mg) - (m r \omega^2 - mg)$$
$$\text{Difference} = m r \omega^2 + mg - m r \omega^2 + mg = 2mg$$

Note: Because the angular velocity is constant (5.00 revolutions per second), the centripetal force $m r \omega^2$ is the same at both points. Therefore, the centripetal terms cancel out.

### 4. Calculation
$$\text{Difference} = 2 \times 0.130\text{ kg} \times 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$$
$$\text{Difference} = 2.548\text{ N}$$

Rounding to 3 significant figures (consistent with the data provided):
**The difference in tension is $2.55\text{ N}$.**
