### Front
How do you determine the net magnetic field at a point between two parallel current-carrying wires?

### Back
- Key idea: Superposition of magnetic field vectors calculated via the Right-Hand Grip Rule.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$; Right-Hand Grip Rule.
- When to use it: When calculating the resultant magnetic field magnitude and direction from multiple sources.
- Common trap: Failing to consider the vector direction (in vs. out of page); if currents are in the same direction, fields cancel between the wires; if opposite, they add.
