### Answer

**(a) Explain how the cosmic microwave background (CMB) provides evidence for the Big Bang.**
The CMB is the "afterglow" of the Big Bang, representing thermal radiation left over from the early universe when it was hot and dense. As the universe expanded, this radiation stretched (redshifted) due to the expansion of space itself, cooling down to its current temperature of approximately $2.7\text{ K}$. Its detection confirms the Big Bang theory's prediction that the early universe was a hot, opaque plasma that became transparent once it cooled sufficiently for atoms to form (recombination).

**(b) Explain how measuring the redshift of a distant galaxy can provide information about its distance from Earth.**
Redshift ($z$) occurs when light from a distant galaxy is shifted toward longer (redder) wavelengths because the galaxy is moving away from us as the space between us expands. According to Hubble’s Law, defined by $v = H_0d$, the velocity ($v$) at which a galaxy recedes is directly proportional to its distance ($d$) from Earth (where $H_0$ is the Hubble constant). By measuring the redshift, astronomers calculate the recession velocity, which then allows them to determine the distance to the galaxy.

### Explanation and Strategy

To solve these types of HSC Physics questions (Cosmology module), follow this strategy:

1.  **Understand the Keyword:** Both questions ask you to "Explain." This means you must go beyond just stating a fact; you need to provide a cause-and-effect relationship.
2.  **Part (a) Strategy:** Focus on the connection between the early high-temperature state of the universe and the current observation. Key terms to include are: "recombination," "expansion of space," and "blackbody radiation." Mentioning that the CMB is uniform in all directions is a good way to secure both marks.
3.  **Part (b) Strategy:** You must link the observation to the theory.
    *   **Observation:** Redshift occurs because light waves stretch as space expands.
    *   **Relationship:** State Hubble's Law. Even if you don't write the formula, explain that "velocity is proportional to distance."
    *   **Logic:** Since $v \propto d$, knowing the speed allows us to solve for the distance.
