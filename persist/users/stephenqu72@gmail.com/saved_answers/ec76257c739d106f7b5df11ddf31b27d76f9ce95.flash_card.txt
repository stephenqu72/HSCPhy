### Front
What is the relationship between the speed ($v$) and radius ($r$) of an electron moving in a uniform magnetic field, and how is it graphed?

### Back
- Key idea: Velocity is directly proportional to the radius ($v \propto r$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \left( \frac{qB}{m} \right)r$; graph is a straight line through the origin.
- When to use it: Circular motion of a charged particle in a uniform magnetic field where $B$ is constant.
- Common trap: Confusing this with $r = \frac{mv}{qB}$ and mistakenly thinking $v$ and $r$ are inversely proportional if $B$ or $p$ were variables.
