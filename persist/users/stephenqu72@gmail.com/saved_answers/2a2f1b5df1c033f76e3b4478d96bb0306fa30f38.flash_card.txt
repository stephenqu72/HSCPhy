### Front
What is the quark composition of a proton ($p$) and a negative pion ($\pi^-$), and what principle determines this?

### Back
- Key idea: Net charge is the sum of constituent quark charges.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $Q_{total} = \sum q_{quarks}$; Proton ($uud = +1e$), Negative pion ($\bar{u}d = -1e$).
- When to use it: Whenever determining the composition of subatomic particles based on their charge.
- Common trap: Confusing quark charges with antiquark charges (e.g., an anti-up quark $\bar{u}$ has a charge of $-\frac{2}{3}e$, not $+\frac{2}{3}e$).
