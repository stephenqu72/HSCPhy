To determine the ratio of the torque of Motor 2 to Motor 1, we use the formula for the torque $\tau$ on a DC motor armature:

$$\tau = nIAB \sin(\theta)$$

Since the motors are of similar design and we are comparing them at their operating states, the torque is proportional to the product of the number of loops ($n$), the current ($I$), the area of the armature ($A$), and the magnetic field strength ($B$). 

Note that the area $A$ is proportional to the square of the diameter ($d^2$), since $A = \pi (\frac{d}{2})^2$. Thus, we can express the proportionality as:
$$\tau \propto n \cdot I \cdot d^2 \cdot B$$

### Step 1: Calculate the proportionality for Motor 1
$$\text{Value}_1 = n_1 \cdot I_1 \cdot d_1^2 \cdot B_1$$
$$\text{Value}_1 = 200 \cdot 3.0 \cdot (0.10)^2 \cdot 0.50$$
$$\text{Value}_1 = 600 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.50 = 3.0$$

### Step 2: Calculate the proportionality for Motor 2
$$\text{Value}_2 = n_2 \cdot I_2 \cdot d_2^2 \cdot B_2$$
$$\text{Value}_2 = 300 \cdot 2.0 \cdot (0.20)^2 \cdot 0.30$$

$$\text{Value}_2 = 24 \cdot 0.30 = 7.2$$

### Step 3: Find the ratio
To compare Motor 2 to Motor 1:
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{\text{Value}_2}{\text{Value}_1} = \frac{7.2}{3.0} = 2.4$$
