To find the energy of a photon given its wavelength, we use the relationship between energy ($E$), Planck's constant ($h$), the speed of light ($c$), and the wavelength ($\lambda$).

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1.  **Identify the formula:**
    The energy of a photon in Joules is given by:
    $$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$
    However, since the options are in electron-volts (eV), we can use the convenient constant $hc \approx 1240 \text{ eV}\cdot\text{nm}$. This allows for a much faster calculation in HSC physics problems.

2.  **Convert values:**
    *   Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $414 \text{ nm}$
    *   $hc \approx 1240 \text{ eV}\cdot\text{nm}$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$E = \frac{1240 \text{ eV}\cdot\text{nm}}{414 \text{ nm}}$$
    $$E \approx 2.995 \text{ eV}$$

4.  **Conclusion:**
    Rounding our result gives $3.0 \text{ eV}$, which matches option (C).

---

### Alternative Method (using SI units):
If you prefer using SI units, you calculate energy in Joules first and then convert to eV:
*   $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$
*   $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
*   $\lambda = 414 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$

$$E = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34})(3.00 \times 10^8)}{414 \times 10^{-9}} \approx 4.80 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

To convert Joules to eV, divide by $1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J/eV}$:
$$E = \frac{4.80 \times 10^{-19}}{1.602 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 3.0 \text{ eV}$$

The correct answer is **(C)**.
