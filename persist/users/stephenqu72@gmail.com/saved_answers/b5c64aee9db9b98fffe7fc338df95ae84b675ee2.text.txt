To solve this problem, we use the principles of projectile motion. Given $h = 3.0\text{ m}$, the maximum height $H = 2h = 6.0\text{ m}$. We assume standard gravity $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$.

### (a) Initial vertical velocity ($v_{iy}$)
Using the kinematic equation $v_f^2 = v_i^2 + 2a\Delta y$, at maximum height $v_f = 0$ and $\Delta y = H = 6.0\text{ m}$:
$$0 = v_{iy}^2 - 2gH$$
$$v_{iy} = \sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 6.0} \approx 10.84\text{ m/s}$$

### (b) Time to reach maximum height ($t_{up}$)
Using $v_f = v_i + at$:
$$0 = v_{iy} - gt_{up}$$
$$t_{up} = \frac{10.84}{9.8} \approx 1.11\text{ s}$$

### (c) Initial launch angle ($\theta$)
To find $\theta$, we need $v_{ix}$. The ball passes the tree at $x = 8.6\text{ m}$ at height $y = 2h = 6.0\text{ m}$ (the peak). Thus, the peak is exactly at the tree.
Horizontal distance $R_{peak} = 8.6\text{ m}$.
$x = v_{ix} t_{up} \implies v_{ix} = \frac{8.6}{1.106} \approx 7.78\text{ m/s}$.
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{v_{iy}}{v_{ix}} = \frac{10.84}{7.78} \approx 1.393$$
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}(1.393) \approx 54.3^\circ$$

### (d) Distance $d$
The ball lands on the green at height $y = h = 3.0\text{ m}$.
Using $y = v_{iy}t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$:
$$3.0 = 10.84t - 4.9t^2 \implies 4.9t^2 - 10.84t + 3.0 = 0$$
Using the quadratic formula: $t = \frac{10.84 \pm \sqrt{(-10.84)^2 - 4(4.9)(3.0)}}{9.8}$
$t \approx 1.87\text{ s}$ (we take the larger root as it is the landing time).
Total horizontal distance $x_{total} = v_{ix} \times t = 7.78 \times 1.87 \approx 14.55\text{ m}$.
Since the tree is at $x = 8.6\text{ m}$, $d = x_{total} - x = 14.55 - 8.6 = 5.95\text{ m}$.

### Summary of method:
1. **Vertical velocity:** Use energy conservation or kinematics ($v^2 = u^2 + 2as$) at the peak.
2. **Time:** Use $v = u + at$ to find the time to the peak.
3. **Angle:** Relate $v_{ix}$ (using distance to peak) and $v_{iy}$ to find the vector angle.
4. **Distance:** Solve the trajectory equation $y(t)$ for $y=3$ to find total time, calculate total range, and subtract $x$.
