**Answer:**
The correct answer is **(A) X-rays**.

**Explanation:**
To solve this problem, you need to understand the relationship between the energy of a photon and its frequency or wavelength, as described by the Planck-Einstein relation:

$$E = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$

Where:
*   $E$ is the energy of the photon.
*   $h$ is Planck's constant.
*   $f$ is the frequency.
*   $\lambda$ (lambda) is the wavelength.
*   $c$ is the speed of light.

From these equations, we can see that energy is **directly proportional to frequency** and **inversely proportional to wavelength**. Therefore, the form of electromagnetic radiation with the highest energy must have the highest frequency and the shortest wavelength.

Looking at the electromagnetic spectrum in order of increasing frequency and energy:
1. Microwaves (Lowest energy)
2. Infrared
3. Ultraviolet
4. X-rays (Highest energy among the options provided)

Because X-rays have the shortest wavelength and highest frequency of the choices listed, they possess the highest energy per photon.
