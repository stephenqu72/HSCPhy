As an HSC Physics teacher, I can tell you that this question tests your understanding of **Newton’s First Law of Motion** (Inertia) within a non-inertial reference frame.

### Answer
The correct answer is **B**.

---

### Explanation
To solve this, you need to apply the principle of **inertia**, which states that an object will maintain its state of motion (velocity) unless acted upon by an external net force.

1.  **The Perspective:** When you are inside the car, you are in a non-inertial reference frame. As the car turns right, the car itself changes its direction of motion towards the right.
2.  **The Object's Motion:** The basketball, according to Newton’s First Law, wants to continue moving in a straight line at a constant velocity. 
3.  **The Relative Movement:** As the car accelerates to the right, the car "moves away" from the ball’s original straight-line path. To an observer inside the car, it appears as though the ball is moving to the **left**.
4.  **Why the other options are incorrect:**
    *   **A and D:** These are incorrect because the ball does not move to the right relative to the car; the car moves right while the ball continues straight.
    *   **Centrifugal Force:** In physics, "centrifugal force" is considered a *fictitious* force used to explain motion in a rotating frame. HSC Physics curriculum emphasizes that the observed motion is a direct consequence of the object's inertia, not a real physical force acting on it.
    *   **Centripetal Force:** This is the net force that causes circular motion. Since there is insufficient friction between the seat and the ball to provide the necessary centripetal force to make the ball turn with the car, the ball does not follow the turn.

Therefore, the ball simply continues in a straight line while the seat of the car moves underneath it to the right, making the ball appear to shift to the **left** of the car seat due to **inertia**.
