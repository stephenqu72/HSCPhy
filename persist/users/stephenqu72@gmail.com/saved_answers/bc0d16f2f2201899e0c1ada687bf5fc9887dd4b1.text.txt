To find the decay constant ($\lambda$) for the radioactive sample, we follow these steps:

### 1. Determine the Half-life ($t_{1/2}$) from the graph
The half-life is the time it takes for the radioactivity to drop to half of its initial value.
*   Initial activity at $t = 0$ is $100$.
*   Half of the initial activity is $50$.
*   Looking at the graph, the activity reaches $50$ at $t = 2$ days.
*   Therefore, the half-life $t_{1/2} = 2$ days.

### 2. Convert units
Since the options provided are in seconds ($s^{-1}$), we must convert the half-life from days into seconds:
$$t_{1/2} = 2 \text{ days} \times 24 \text{ hours/day} \times 60 \text{ minutes/hour} \times 60 \text{ seconds/minute}$$
$$t_{1/2} = 2 \times 86,400 \text{ s} = 172,800 \text{ s}$$

### 3. Calculate the decay constant ($\lambda$)
The relationship between the decay constant and half-life is given by the formula:
$$\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{t_{1/2}}$$

Substitute the value in seconds:
$$\lambda = \frac{0.693}{172,800 \text{ s}}$$
$$\lambda \approx 4.01 \times 10^{-6} \text{ s}^{-1}$$
