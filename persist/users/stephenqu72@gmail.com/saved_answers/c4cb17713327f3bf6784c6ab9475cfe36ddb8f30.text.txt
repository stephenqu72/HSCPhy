To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of **Special Relativity**, specifically **length contraction**.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Identify the given information:**
*   Proper length ($L_0$): The distance between Earth and Sirius as measured by an observer on Earth is $8.6 \text{ light years}$.
*   Velocity ($v$): The spaceship is traveling at $0.70c$ (where $c$ is the speed of light).

**2. Understand the perspective of the observer inside the spaceship:**
For the observer inside the spaceship, Earth and Sirius are moving toward them at $0.70c$. Because of length contraction, the distance between Earth and Sirius ($L$) will appear shorter to them than it does to an observer on Earth.

**3. Calculate the length contracted distance ($L$):**
The formula for length contraction is:
$$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
Substituting our values:
$$L = 8.6 \times \sqrt{1 - (0.70)^2}$$
$$L = 8.6 \times \sqrt{1 - 0.49}$$
$$L = 8.6 \times \sqrt{0.51}$$
$$L \approx 8.6 \times 0.71414 \approx 6.1416 \text{ light years}$$

**4. Calculate the time taken ($t$):**
The observer inside the spaceship measures the distance to be $L = 6.1416 \text{ light years}$. Since they are traveling at a velocity of $v = 0.70c$, the time taken is:
$$t = \frac{d}{v} = \frac{6.1416 \text{ ly}}{0.70c}$$
Since $1 \text{ light year}$ is the distance light travels in $1 \text{ year}$, the units work out such that:
$$t = \frac{6.1416}{0.70} \approx 8.7737 \text{ years}$$

**Conclusion:**
Rounding to two decimal places, the time taken is approximately **$8.77 \text{ years}$**.

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct choice is **(A)**.
