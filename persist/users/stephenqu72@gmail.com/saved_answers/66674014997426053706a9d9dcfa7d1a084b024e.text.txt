### Part (a)
**Answer:** As the resistance $r$ of the cables increases, the total resistance of the series circuit ($R_{total} = r + R$) increases. Since the supply voltage is constant ($V = 24\text{ V}$), the total current $i$ decreases according to Ohm’s Law ($i = V / R_{total}$). Because the brightness of the globe depends on the power dissipated ($P = i^2 R$), a decrease in current leads to a decrease in the power dissipated in the globe, thus the globe becomes dimmer.

### Part (b)
**Answer:**
*   **Independent variable:** Resistance of the cables ($r$). Reasoning: This is the variable the students actively change to observe its effect.
*   **Dependent variable:** Current in the cables ($i$). Reasoning: This is the variable being measured as a consequence of changing the cable resistance.

### Part (c)
**Answer:** In the series circuit:
1.  The total voltage supplied is $V = 24\text{ V}$.
2.  The total resistance is $R_{total} = r + R$.
3.  By Ohm's Law, $V = i(r + R)$.
4.  Rearrange for $r$:
    $$24 = i(r + R)$$
    $$\frac{24}{i} = r + R$$
    $$r = \frac{24}{i} - R$$
This confirms the validity of the equation.

### Part (d)
**Calculations:**
We calculate $\frac{1}{i}$ for each given $i$:
1. $1 / 2.4 \approx 0.417\text{ A}^{-1}$
2. $1 / 2.0 = 0.500\text{ A}^{-1}$
3. $1 / 1.7 \approx 0.588\text{ A}^{-1}$
4. $1 / 1.5 \approx 0.667\text{ A}^{-1}$
5. $1 / 1.3 \approx 0.769\text{ A}^{-1}$

### Part (e) & (f)
**Guidance:** 
To solve **(f)**, note that the equation $r = 24(\frac{1}{i}) - R$ is in the form $y = mx + c$.
*   $y = r$
*   $x = 1/i$
*   Slope ($m$) $= 24$
*   $y$-intercept ($c$) $= -R$

**How to solve (f):** Once you have drawn the line of best fit in part (e), extend the line until it crosses the $y$-axis (where $1/i = 0$). The value of $r$ at this intersection represents $-R$. Therefore, your $y$-intercept value will be $-R$. To find $R$, simply take the negative of your $y$-intercept value.
