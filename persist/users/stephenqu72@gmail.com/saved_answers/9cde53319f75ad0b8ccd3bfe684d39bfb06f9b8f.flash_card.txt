### Front
How do $\alpha$, $\beta^-$, and $\beta^+$ decays appear on an $A$ (mass number) vs $Z$ (atomic number) nuclear decay plot?

### Back
- Key idea: Changes in $A$ and $Z$ determine the direction of movement on the decay graph.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: 
  - $\alpha$ (alpha): $\Delta Z = -2, \Delta A = -4$ (2 left, 2 down)
  - $\beta^-$ (beta minus): $\Delta Z = +1, \Delta A = 0$ (1 up)
  - $\beta^+$ (positron): $\Delta Z = -1, \Delta A = 0$ (1 down)
- When to use it: To identify parent-daughter relationships on nuclear stability graphs.
- Common trap: Confusing the axes; always confirm if the graph plots $Z$ on the x-axis or y-axis.
