To solve this physics problem, we must apply the principle of **moments** (torque) in rotational equilibrium. Since the arm is held in a stationary position, the sum of all clockwise moments must equal the sum of all counter-clockwise moments about the pivot point (the elbow).

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the Pivot:** The elbow acts as the pivot point. We calculate all moments relative to this point.
2.  **Identify the forces and their distances (lever arms) from the pivot:**
    *   **Bicep Force ($F_B$):** Acts upwards at a distance of $50\text{ mm}$ from the pivot. This creates a counter-clockwise moment.
    *   **Forearm weight ($30\text{ N}$):** Acts downwards at a distance of $50 + 90 = 140\text{ mm}$ from the pivot. This creates a clockwise moment.
    *   **Dumbbell weight ($140\text{ N}$):** Acts downwards at a distance of $50 + 90 + 160 = 300\text{ mm}$ from the pivot. This also creates a clockwise moment.
3.  **Set up the Equilibrium Equation:**
    $$\sum \tau_{\text{clockwise}} = \sum \tau_{\text{counter-clockwise}}$$
    $$(30\text{ N} \times 140\text{ mm}) + (140\text{ N} \times 300\text{ mm}) = F_B \times 50\text{ mm}$$
4.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$4200 + 42000 = F_B \times 50$$
    $$46200 = F_B \times 50$$
    $$F_B = \frac{46200}{50}$$
    $$F_B = 924\text{ N}$$
