To show that the maximum range of a projectile on level ground occurs at $45^\circ$ regardless of $g$, we must derive the range formula and find its maximum.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Derive the Range Formula:**
Let a projectile be launched with initial velocity $v$ at an angle $\theta$. We split the motion into horizontal and vertical components:
- Horizontal velocity: $v_x = v \cos \theta$
- Vertical velocity: $v_y = v \sin \theta$

The time of flight $t$ for a projectile returning to the same level is found by setting the vertical displacement to zero:
$$y = v \sin \theta \cdot t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2 = 0$$
Solving for $t$ (ignoring $t=0$):
$$t = \frac{2v \sin \theta}{g}$$

The horizontal range $R$ is $v_x \cdot t$:
$$R = (v \cos \theta) \cdot \left(\frac{2v \sin \theta}{g}\right) = \frac{v^2 (2 \sin \theta \cos \theta)}{g}$$

Using the trigonometric identity $\sin(2\theta) = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta$, we get:
$$R = \frac{v^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$

**2. Find the Maximum:**
To maximize $R$, we need to maximize the term $\sin(2\theta)$. The maximum value of the sine function is $1$, which occurs when the argument is $90^\circ$:
$$\sin(2\theta) = 1$$
$$2\theta = 90^\circ$$
$$\theta = 45^\circ$$

**Conclusion:**
Because $g$ is a constant in the denominator, the value of $g$ scales the entire range but does not affect the angle at which the sine function reaches its maximum. Thus, for any value of $g$, the range is maximized when $\sin(2\theta) = 1$, which is always at $\theta = 45^\circ$.
