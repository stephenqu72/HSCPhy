### Explanation
In the study of Special Relativity, the **proper length** ($L_0$) of an object is defined as the length of an object measured in the frame of reference in which the object is at rest.

Here is why the other options are incorrect:
*   **(A)** "Proper standard measuring stick" is a distractor. The definition of proper length relies on the relative motion between the observer and the object, not the type of instrument used.
*   **(C)** While measuring both ends at the same time is a requirement for measuring length in any frame, it is not the *definition* of proper length. If you measure an object that is moving relative to you at the same time, you would be measuring its *contracted* length ($L$), not its proper length.
*   **(D)** This is too broad. Any measurement of length, whether it is the contracted length or the proper length, is taken in an inertial frame of reference. This does not uniquely identify "proper length."

**Key Takeaway for your exams:** Always remember that "proper" quantities (proper time, proper length) are measured by an observer who is **co-moving** with the object or event in question. For length, this means the observer is at rest relative to the object.
