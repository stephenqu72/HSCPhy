To find the radius $r$ of the circular arc, we analyze the forces acting on the roller coaster cart at the peak of the track.

### Physics Analysis
1. **Identify the forces:** At the peak, the two forces acting on the cart are the downward gravitational force ($mg$) and the upward normal force ($F_N$) exerted by the track.
2. **Circular motion condition:** The net force providing the centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) must be directed toward the center of the circle (downward). Thus:
   $$F_{net} = mg - F_N = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
3. **Condition of "no upward force":** The problem states the occupant feels no upward force. This means the normal force $F_N = 0$. This is the condition of "weightlessness" or "free fall" at the peak.
4. **Solve for $r$:** Substituting $F_N = 0$ into the equation:
   $$mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
   Dividing both sides by $m$:
   $$g = \frac{v^2}{r}$$
   Rearranging for $r$:
   $$r = \frac{v^2}{g}$$

### Calculation
Using standard gravity $g \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ and the given speed $v = 28 \, \text{m/s}$:
$$r = \frac{28^2}{9.8}$$
$$r = \frac{784}{9.8}$$
$$r = 80 \, \text{m}$$

Comparing this result to the given options:
(A) 60 m
(B) 70 m
(C) 80 m
(D) 90 m

The correct answer is **(C)**.
