To find the linear velocity at which the string breaks, we must analyze the forces acting on the weight when it is in a conical pendulum motion.

### 1. Identify the Forces
The two forces acting on the weight are:
*   **Gravity ($mg$):** Acting downwards, where $m = 2\text{ kg}$ and $g \approx 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$. So, $F_g = 2 \times 9.8 = 19.6\text{ N}$.
*   **Tension ($T$):** Acting along the string. At the breaking point, $T = 110\text{ N}$.

### 2. Decompose the Tension
The tension force provides both the vertical balance against gravity and the horizontal centripetal force:
*   **Vertical component:** $T \cos(\theta) = mg$
*   **Horizontal component:** $T \sin(\theta) = F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$

In this setup, the radius $r$ of the horizontal circle is related to the length of the string ($L=3\text{ m}$) by $r = L \sin(\theta)$. Substituting this into the centripetal force equation:
$$T \sin(\theta) = \frac{mv^2}{L \sin(\theta)}$$
$$v^2 = \frac{T L \sin^2(\theta)}{m}$$

### 3. Solve for the missing variable ($\theta$)
From the vertical component, $\cos(\theta) = \frac{mg}{T}$:
$$\cos(\theta) = \frac{19.6}{110} \approx 0.1782$$
Using the trigonometric identity $\sin^2(\theta) = 1 - \cos^2(\theta)$:
$$\sin^2(\theta) = 1 - (0.1782)^2 \approx 1 - 0.03175 = 0.96825$$

### 4. Calculate the velocity
Now substitute the values into the equation for $v^2$:
$$v^2 = \frac{110 \times 3 \times 0.96825}{2}$$
$$v^2 = \frac{319.52}{2} = 159.76$$
$$v = \sqrt{159.76} \approx 12.64\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **(A)**.
