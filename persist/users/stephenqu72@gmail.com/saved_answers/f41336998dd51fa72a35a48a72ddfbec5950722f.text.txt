To achieve full marks on this 6-mark HSC Physics question, you must demonstrate a deep understanding of how Maxwell unified disparate phenomena and provided the theoretical framework that made modern technology possible.

### Answer
James Clerk Maxwell’s contributions are central to modern physics, primarily through his unification of electricity and magnetism into a single field: electromagnetism.

**1. Unification of Electromagnetism:**
Before Maxwell, the work of Oersted, Ampère, and Faraday showed that electricity and magnetism were linked. Maxwell mathematically synthesized these observations into his four equations. His most significant contribution was the addition of the "displacement current" term to Ampère's Law, which accounted for changing electric fields producing magnetic fields, even in a vacuum. This led to the theoretical prediction that electromagnetic waves could propagate through space.

**2. Theoretical Prediction of Electromagnetic Waves:**
Maxwell calculated the speed of these waves using the constants of permittivity ($\epsilon_0$) and permeability ($\mu_0$) of free space, using the formula:
$$c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\epsilon_0 \mu_0}}$$
His calculation yielded a speed approximately equal to the known speed of light. This was a monumental breakthrough as it proved that light itself is an electromagnetic wave, unifying optics with electromagnetism.

**3. Development of Wireless Communication:**
Maxwell’s work provided the theoretical "blueprint" for wireless communication. By mathematically demonstrating that oscillating charges produce self-propagating electromagnetic waves, he paved the way for experimentalists like Heinrich Hertz to prove their existence. Hertz’s later success in generating and detecting radio waves validated Maxwell’s theory, directly enabling the invention of radio, television, mobile phones, and Wi-Fi. Without Maxwell's foundational mathematics, the fundamental mechanism for transmitting information through space without wires would not have been understood.
