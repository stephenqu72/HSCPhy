To solve this physics problem, we need to extract the peak-to-peak voltage and the period of the wave from the provided oscilloscope trace.

### Step 1: Calculate the peak-to-peak voltage
The peak-to-peak voltage ($V_{p-p}$) is the vertical distance from the crest (top) to the trough (bottom) of the wave.
*   Counting the vertical divisions on the screen, the wave spans from $+3$ to $-3$ units, totaling $6$ vertical divisions.
*   We are given that each vertical division represents $20\text{ V}$.
*   Therefore, $V_{p-p} = 6\text{ divisions} \times 20\text{ V/division} = 120\text{ V}$.

### Step 2: Calculate the frequency
First, we find the period ($T$), which is the time taken for one full cycle.
*   Looking at the trace, one complete cycle (from one peak to the next) spans $4$ horizontal divisions.
*   We are given that each horizontal division represents $10\text{ ms} = 0.01\text{ s}$.
*   Therefore, the period $T = 4\text{ divisions} \times 0.01\text{ s/division} = 0.04\text{ s}$.
*   The frequency ($f$) is the reciprocal of the period:
$$f = \frac{1}{T} = \frac{1}{0.04\text{ s}} = 25\text{ Hz}$$

### Conclusion
The peak-to-peak voltage is $120\text{ V}$ and the frequency is $25\text{ Hz}$. Comparing this to the given options:
*   A. $42\text{ V}$ and $10\text{ Hz}$
*   B. $60\text{ V}$ and $25\text{ Hz}$
*   C. $120\text{ V}$ and $10\text{ Hz}$
*   **D. $120\text{ V}$ and $25\text{ Hz}$**

The correct answer is **D**.
