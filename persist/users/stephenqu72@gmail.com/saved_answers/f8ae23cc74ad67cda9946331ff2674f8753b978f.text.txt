To solve this problem, we use the formula for the escape velocity ($v_e$) of an object:

$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$$

Where:
*   $G$ is the gravitational constant.
*   $M$ is the mass of the asteroid.
*   $R$ is the radius of the asteroid.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Define the variables:**
    Let $M_F$ and $R_F$ be the mass and radius of Fios.
    Let $M_S$ and $R_S$ be the mass and radius of Stratos.
    From the problem statement:
    *   $M_S = 3M_F$
    *   Since diameter is proportional to radius, if the diameter of Stratos is twice that of Fios, then $R_S = 2R_F$.

2.  **Set up the ratio:**
    We want to find the ratio $\frac{v_S}{v_F}$:
    $$\frac{v_S}{v_F} = \frac{\sqrt{\frac{2GM_S}{R_S}}}{\sqrt{\frac{2GM_F}{R_F}}}$$

3.  **Simplify the expression:**
    The constant terms ($2G$) cancel out:
    $$\frac{v_S}{v_F} = \sqrt{\frac{M_S}{R_S} \cdot \frac{R_F}{M_F}} = \sqrt{\frac{M_S}{M_F} \cdot \frac{R_F}{R_S}}$$

4.  **Substitute the known values:**
    $$\frac{v_S}{v_F} = \sqrt{\left(\frac{3M_F}{M_F}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{R_F}{2R_F}\right)}$$
    $$\frac{v_S}{v_F} = \sqrt{3 \cdot \frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}}$$

Comparing our result to the given options, the correct answer is **D**.
