To solve this problem, we apply **Faraday's Law of Induction**, which states that the induced electromotive force (EMF) is equal to the negative rate of change of magnetic flux:
$$\varepsilon = - \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$$

### Step 1: Identify the net magnetic flux
The wire is twisted into a figure-eight. Because the loops are wound in a figure-eight configuration, their magnetic fluxes effectively oppose each other in the circuit. The net flux $\Phi_{net}$ is the difference between the flux in the two loops:
$$\Phi_{net} = |A_1 - A_2| \cdot B$$
where $A_1 = \pi r_1^2$ and $A_2 = \pi r_2^2$.

### Step 2: Determine the change in flux ($\Delta \Phi$)
The magnetic field changes from $0.1\text{ T}$ to $-0.06\text{ T}$ (since it is in the opposite direction). 
The total change in magnetic field is:
$$\Delta B = B_f - B_i = -0.06 - 0.1 = -0.16\text{ T}$$
The change in flux is:
$$\Delta \Phi = (A_2 - A_1) \cdot \Delta B = \pi (r_2^2 - r_1^2) \cdot (-0.16)$$

### Step 3: Use the EMF equation
Given $\varepsilon = 2.3\text{ V}$, $\Delta t = 0.015\text{ s}$, and the magnitude of induced voltage $|\varepsilon| = \left| \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t} \right|$:
$$2.3 = \left| \frac{\pi (r_2^2 - r_1^2) \cdot (-0.16)}{0.015} \right|$$
$$2.3 = \frac{\pi (r_2^2 - (0.07)^2) \cdot 0.16}{0.015}$$

### Step 4: Solve for $r_2$
First, multiply by $0.015$:
$$0.0345 = \pi (r_2^2 - 0.0049) \cdot 0.16$$
Divide by $0.16\pi$:
$$\frac{0.0345}{0.16\pi} = r_2^2 - 0.0049$$
$$0.06864 = r_2^2 - 0.0049$$
$$r_2^2 = 0.07354$$
$$r_2 = \sqrt{0.07354} \approx 0.271\text{ m}$$

Converting to centimetres, the radius $r$ is approximately **$27.1\text{ cm}$**.
