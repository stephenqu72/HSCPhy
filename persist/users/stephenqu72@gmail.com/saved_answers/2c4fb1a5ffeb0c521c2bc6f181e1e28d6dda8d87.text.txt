### Step-by-Step Explanation

1.  **Analyze the Rubber Stopper:** The rubber stopper is moving in a uniform horizontal circle. The centripetal force required to keep it in this circular path is provided by the tension ($T$) in the string. The formula for centripetal force is:
    $$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
    Therefore, the tension in the string is $T = \frac{mv^2}{r}$.

2.  **Analyze the Hanging Mass:** The hanging mass $M$ is stationary (in equilibrium). This means the upward force (tension $T$ from the string) must exactly balance the downward force (the weight of the mass, $Mg$).
    $$T = Mg$$

3.  **Equate the two expressions:** Since the tension $T$ is the same throughout the string, we can set the two expressions for $T$ equal to each other:
    $$Mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

4.  **Solve for M:** To find the expression for the unknown mass $M$, divide both sides by $g$:
    $$M = \frac{mv^2}{rg}$$
