### Explanation
To identify the correct answer, we must recall the primary pillars of evidence that support the Big Bang Theory in HSC Physics:

1.  **Hubble’s Law ($v = H_0d$):** Edwin Hubble observed that galaxies are moving away from us at speeds proportional to their distance. This observation provides direct evidence that the universe is expanding. If we extrapolate this expansion backward in time, it implies the universe originated from a singular, extremely dense, and hot point (the "Big Bang").
2.  **Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) Radiation:** This is the uniform, faint glow of microwave radiation detected in all directions in the sky. It is the "afterglow" or residual thermal radiation left over from the recombination era, about 380,000 years after the Big Bang, when the universe cooled sufficiently for atoms to form and light to travel freely. Its discovery is considered one of the strongest confirmations of the Big Bang model.
