To calculate the magnitude and direction of the magnetic force acting on the wire, we use the formula for the force on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field:

$$F = BIl\sin(\theta)$$

### 1. Identify the given values:

*   Current ($I$): $2.5\text{ A}$
*   Angle ($\theta$): The angle between the current-carrying wire and the magnetic field lines is $48^\circ$.
*   Length ($l$): The wire length is not given directly, but we can find it using the vertical component ($0.82\text{ m}$) and the angle. Since $\sin(48^\circ) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \frac{0.82}{l}$, then $l = \frac{0.82}{\sin(48^\circ)}$.

### 2. Calculation:

$$F = B \cdot I \cdot \left( \frac{0.82}{\sin(48^\circ)} \right) \cdot \sin(48^\circ)$$

The $\sin(48^\circ)$ terms cancel out:
$$F = B \cdot I \cdot 0.82$$
$$F = 3.6 \times 2.5 \times 0.82$$
$$F = 7.38\text{ N}$$
