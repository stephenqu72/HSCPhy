### Front
How does the total mechanical energy of a satellite change as it moves to a higher circular orbit, and why?

### Back
- Key idea: Total mechanical energy increases (becomes less negative) as orbital radius ($r$) increases, requiring external work.
- Formula / law / constant: $E_{total} = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$
- When to use it: When calculating energy requirements for orbital maneuvers or comparing satellite states at different radii.
- Common trap: Assuming that because kinetic energy decreases, total energy must also decrease; you must account for the increase in gravitational potential energy.
