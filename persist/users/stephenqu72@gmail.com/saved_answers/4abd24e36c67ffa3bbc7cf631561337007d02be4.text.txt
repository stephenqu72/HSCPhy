Maxwell’s theory of electromagnetism describes how electric and magnetic fields interact to propagate through space as electromagnetic waves. Let’s evaluate each option:

*   **A and B:** According to Maxwell’s equations (specifically the Ampère-Maxwell law and Faraday's law of induction), a time-varying electric field generates a magnetic field, and a time-varying magnetic field generates an electric field. Therefore, statements A and B are **correct** descriptions of how these fields sustain one another in a wave.
*   **C:** In an electromagnetic wave, the electric field ($E$) and the magnetic field ($B$) oscillate **in phase** with each other. This means they reach their maximum and minimum values (zeros) at the same points in space and time. Because statement C claims they are "out of phase," it is **incorrect**.
