### Front
How does the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation provide evidence for the Big Bang?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The expansion of the universe caused high-energy photons emitted shortly after the Big Bang to undergo cosmological redshift, stretching them into the microwave spectrum.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When explaining evidence for an expanding, cooling universe (Hubble’s cosmological principle).
- **Common trap:** Assuming the Big Bang was an "explosion" that pushed galaxies through space; remember that the expansion of space itself causes the wavelength stretching.
