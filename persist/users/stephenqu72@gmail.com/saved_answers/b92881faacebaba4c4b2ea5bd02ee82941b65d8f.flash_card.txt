### Front
In uniform circular motion, what is the orientation of the velocity vector relative to the centripetal force vector?

### Back
- Key idea: Velocity is tangential; centripetal force is radially inward.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: The vectors are always orthogonal ($90^\circ$).
- When to use it: Any orbital or circular motion problem involving direction of motion and force.
- Common trap: Assuming velocity points in the direction of the force; remember that centripetal force changes the *direction* of velocity, not its *magnitude*.
