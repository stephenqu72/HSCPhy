### Answer
The correct answer is **(D)**.

### Explanation
An **inertial frame of reference** is a frame in which Newton's First Law of Motion holds true. This means that an object at rest remains at rest, and an object in motion continues to move at a constant velocity (constant speed in a straight line) unless acted upon by a net external force. Essentially, an inertial frame is one that is **not accelerating**.

Let's evaluate each option:

*   **(A) A lift slowing down:** Slowing down implies an acceleration (deceleration) in the opposite direction of motion. Because the frame is accelerating, it is a non-inertial frame.
*   **(B) A cyclist riding downhill:** Gravity acts as an accelerating force on the cyclist. As they move down the hill, their velocity is increasing, meaning they are accelerating. Therefore, this is a non-inertial frame.
*   **(C) A ball at maximum height:** Even though the instantaneous velocity of the ball is $0$ m/s at its peak, it is still subject to the acceleration due to gravity ($g \approx 9.8$ m/s$^2$ downwards). Because it is accelerating, it is not an inertial frame.
*   **(D) A sky diver at terminal velocity:** Terminal velocity occurs when the upward force of air resistance exactly balances the downward force of gravity. Because the net force is zero, the sky diver is no longer accelerating. Since the sky diver is moving at a **constant velocity**, the frame of reference is inertial.
