To determine the correct graph, we must analyze the mathematical relationships for the potential energy ($U$) and kinetic energy ($K$) of a satellite of mass $m$ orbiting a planet of mass $M$ at a radius $r$.

### Physics Principles

1.  **Gravitational Potential Energy ($U$):** 
    The formula is given by:
    $$U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$$
    Since $G$, $M$, and $m$ are positive constants, as $r$ increases, the denominator increases, making the magnitude of $U$ smaller. Because $U$ is negative, becoming "less negative" means the value of $U$ **increases** toward zero. Thus, the graph for $U$ must be a curve starting from a negative value and rising toward the horizontal axis as $r$ increases.

2.  **Kinetic Energy ($K$):** 
    For a satellite in a circular orbit, the centripetal force is provided by gravity:
    $$\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GMm}{r^2} \implies mv^2 = \frac{GMm}{r}$$
    Since $K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$, we have:
    $$K = \frac{GMm}{2r}$$
    As $r$ increases, $K$ **decreases**. Additionally, note that $|U| = 2K$. This means that at any given radius $r$, the magnitude of the potential energy is exactly twice the magnitude of the kinetic energy.

### Analysis of the Options
*   **$U$**: Must be a negative value that increases (moves up toward zero) as $r$ increases.
*   **$K$**: Must be a positive value that decreases as $r$ increases.
*   **Relationship**: $|U| = 2K$. Visually, the curve for $U$ must be twice as far from the horizontal axis ($0$ energy) as the curve for $K$.

Looking at the options:
*   In all graphs, $U$ is the upper curve (closer to zero) and $K$ is the lower curve. However, $U$ must be negative and $K$ must be positive. In standard physics convention, $U$ is negative. In the provided graphs, the labels represent the magnitudes or the relative positions.
