### Question 1: Charged Particle Motion
**Answer: (D)**

**Explanation:**
For a charged particle moving at a constant velocity (no deflection) in the presence of fields, the net force must be zero ($F_{net} = 0$). The total force is the sum of the electric force ($F_E = qE$) and the magnetic force ($F_B = qvB\sin\theta$).

*   **Option (D)** is correct because this describes a **velocity selector**. In this setup, if the electric field is perpendicular to the velocity and the magnetic field is perpendicular to both the velocity and the electric field, the electric force can exactly cancel out the magnetic force ($qE = qvB$), allowing the particle to pass through undeflected.
*   Other options do not allow for the forces to cancel out in a way that permits motion at a constant velocity without deflection for all arbitrary charge magnitudes.

### Question 2: Forces on an Inclined Plane
**Answer: (D)**

**Explanation:**
To represent the forces on an object resting on an inclined plane, we must account for three distinct forces:
1.  **Weight ($W$ or $mg$):** Always acts vertically downwards toward the center of the Earth.
2.  **Normal Force ($N$):** Always acts perpendicular (at a right angle) to the surface of the incline.
3.  **Friction ($f$):** Acts parallel to the surface, in the direction opposite to the potential motion (up the incline, to prevent sliding down).

Looking at the diagrams, diagram **(D)** is the only one that correctly shows:
*   A vector pointing straight down (Weight).
*   A vector pointing perpendicular to the slope (Normal Force).
*   A vector pointing up the slope along the surface (Friction).

When you sum these three vectors, they form a closed triangle, indicating that the net force is zero and the object remains at rest.
