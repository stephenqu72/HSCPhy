To solve this problem, we need to relate the linear distance traveled by the tip of the blade to its angular velocity.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the given variables:**
*   Radius of the blade, $r = 1.5\text{ m}$
*   Arc length (distance), $s = 2.5\text{ m}$
*   Time taken, $t = 0.5\text{ s}$

**2. Calculate the angular displacement ($\theta$):**
The relationship between arc length $s$, radius $r$, and angular displacement in radians $\theta$ is:
$$s = r\theta$$
$$\theta = \frac{s}{r} = \frac{2.5\text{ m}}{1.5\text{ m}} = 1.666... \text{ radians}$$

**3. Calculate the angular velocity in rad/s:**
Angular velocity ($\omega$) is defined as the rate of change of angular displacement:
$$\omega = \frac{\theta}{t} = \frac{1.666... \text{ rad}}{0.5\text{ s}} = 3.333... \text{ rad/s}$$

**4. Convert from radians per second to degrees per second:**
Since the options are given in degrees per second ($\text{° s}^{-1}$), we must convert the angular velocity using the conversion factor $1\text{ radian} = \frac{180^\circ}{\pi}$:
$$\omega (\text{in } ^\circ/\text{s}) = 3.333... \times \frac{180}{\pi}$$
$$\omega \approx 3.333... \times 57.2958$$
$$\omega \approx 190.98... ^\circ/\text{s}$$

Rounding this to the nearest whole number, we get $191 ^\circ/\text{s}$.

**Conclusion:**
