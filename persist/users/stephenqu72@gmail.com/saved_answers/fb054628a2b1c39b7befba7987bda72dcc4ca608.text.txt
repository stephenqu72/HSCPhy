### Answer
**Motion Analysis:**
As the magnet swings, the changing magnetic flux near the surface of the aluminium can induces eddy currents within the metal (Faraday’s Law). According to Lenz’s Law, these eddy currents create a magnetic field that opposes the change in flux. Consequently, the interaction creates a magnetic drag force on the magnet, causing it to lose mechanical energy and come to rest faster than it would due to air resistance alone. Simultaneously, the force exerted by the magnet on the can creates a torque, causing the aluminium can to rotate in the direction of the magnet's swing.

**Energy Transformations:**
1. **For the Magnet:** The initial gravitational potential energy is converted into kinetic energy as it swings. Due to the interaction with the can, mechanical energy is dissipated as heat within the aluminium via resistance ($P = I^2R$ losses from eddy currents).
2. **For the Can:** The work done by the magnetic force on the aluminium transfers energy from the magnet's kinetic energy into rotational kinetic energy of the can and thermal energy (heat) generated by the circulating eddy currents.
