### Front
How does a smaller Hubble constant ($H_0$) affect the estimated age of the universe ($t$)?

### Back
- Key idea: The age of the universe is inversely proportional to the Hubble constant.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $t \approx \frac{1}{H_0}$
- When to use it: To estimate the age of the universe based on current expansion rates.
- Common trap: Assuming a smaller $H_0$ means a younger universe; remember that a slower expansion rate requires more time to reach current distances.
