### Answer
Maxwell predicted that light is an electromagnetic wave consisting of time-varying electric and magnetic fields that propagate through space. This model relates to his predictions in the following ways:

1.  **Self-Propagating Nature:** Maxwell predicted that a changing electric field generates a changing magnetic field (Ampère-Maxwell law), and a changing magnetic field generates a changing electric field (Faraday’s Law). This allows the wave to sustain itself as it travels through a vacuum.
2.  **Transverse Orientation:** The diagram shows the electric field ($E$) and magnetic field ($B$) oscillating perpendicular to each other and to the direction of propagation. Maxwell’s equations confirm that electromagnetic waves are transverse waves.
3.  **Constant Velocity:** Maxwell calculated that these waves travel at a constant speed, $c$, given by $c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$, where $\mu_0$ is the permeability of free space and $\epsilon_0$ is the permittivity of free space. He identified this calculated speed as the speed of light.
4.  **Origin:** The diagram correctly identifies an oscillating charge as the source of these waves. Maxwell predicted that accelerated charges produce electromagnetic radiation.
