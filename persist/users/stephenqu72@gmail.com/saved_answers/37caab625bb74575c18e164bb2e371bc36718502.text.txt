To solve this problem, we use the principles of Young's Double Slit interference. First, let's identify our known variables and convert them into SI units:

*   Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $589\text{ nm} = 589 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$
*   Slit separation ($d$) = $0.100\text{ mm} = 1.00 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m}$
*   Distance to screen ($L$) = $1.50\text{ m}$
*   Order of maximum ($n$) = $1$

### Part a) Determine the angle of the first-order maximum

The condition for constructive interference (bright fringes) is given by the formula:
$$d \sin(\theta) = n\lambda$$

Rearranging for $\sin(\theta)$:
$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{n\lambda}{d}$$
$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{1 \times 589 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}}{1.00 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m}}$$
$$\sin(\theta) = 5.89 \times 10^{-3}$$

Taking the inverse sine:
$$\theta = \arcsin(5.89 \times 10^{-3}) \approx 0.337^\circ$$

---

### Part b) Determine the distance from the central bright maxima

The distance $y$ on the screen for small angles can be found using the relationship $\tan(\theta) = \frac{y}{L}$, or more commonly via the small-angle approximation $\sin(\theta) \approx \tan(\theta) = \frac{y}{L}$.

Using the small-angle approximation:
$$y = L \tan(\theta) \approx L \sin(\theta)$$
$$y = 1.50\text{ m} \times 5.89 \times 10^{-3}$$
$$y = 0.008835\text{ m}$$

Converting to millimeters:
$$y \approx 8.84\text{ mm}$$

**Summary of steps:**
1. **Identify the formula:** Use $d \sin(\theta) = n\lambda$ for double-slit interference.
2. **Convert units:** Always ensure $d$ and $\lambda$ are in meters.
3. **Calculate angle:** Solve for $\theta$ using the inverse sine function.
4. **Calculate distance:** Use trigonometry ($\tan(\theta) = y/L$) or the small-angle approximation ($y \approx L \cdot \sin(\theta)$) to find the position on the screen.
