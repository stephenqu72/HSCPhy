### Front
How does changing the initial horizontal velocity ($v_x$) of a projectile launched horizontally affect its range ($R$) and time of flight ($t$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Time of flight is independent of horizontal velocity; range is directly proportional to horizontal velocity.
- Formula: $t = \sqrt{\frac{2h}{g}}$ and $R = v_x t$
- When to use it: Any horizontal projectile motion problem where height is constant.
- Common trap: Assuming a higher horizontal velocity increases the time of flight.
