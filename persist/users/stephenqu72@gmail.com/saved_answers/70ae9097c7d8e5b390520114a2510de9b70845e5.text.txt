To determine which segment takes the greatest travel time, we must apply **Kepler’s Second Law of Planetary Motion** (the Law of Equal Areas).

### The Physical Principle
Kepler’s Second Law states that a line segment joining a planet and its star sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time. Mathematically, this implies that a planet moves fastest when it is closest to the star (perihelion) and slowest when it is furthest from the star (aphelion).

Since the question states that the path distances between consecutive points ($P, Q, R, S$) are equal, the travel time for each segment is inversely proportional to the planet's orbital speed:
$$\text{Time} \approx \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}}$$
