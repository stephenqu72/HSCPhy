To determine the direction of the force on the electron, we use the **Right-Hand Rule (RHR)** for moving charges in a magnetic field.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the Magnetic Field ($B$) direction:** Outside a bar magnet, magnetic field lines always emerge from the North pole and enter the South pole. Since the electron is passing the South pole, the magnetic field lines near the electron are directed **towards the right** (pointing into the South pole).
2.  **Identify the Velocity ($v$) direction:** The diagram shows the electron moving **upwards** (towards the top of the page).
3.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule (for a positive charge):** 
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the velocity ($v$) — **upwards**.
    *   Curl your fingers towards the magnetic field direction ($B$) — **to the right**.
    *   Your thumb points **out of the page**. This is the direction of the force on a *positive* charge.
4.  **Adjust for a negative charge:** The electron is a negatively charged particle. Therefore, the force on the electron is exactly opposite to the direction determined by the RHR for a positive charge. 
    *   Since the RHR gives "out of the page" for a positive charge, the force on the electron is **into the page**.

**Conclusion:** The correct answer is **(D) Into the page**.
