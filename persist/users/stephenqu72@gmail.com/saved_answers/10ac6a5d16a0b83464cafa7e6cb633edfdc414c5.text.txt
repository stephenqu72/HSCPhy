### Question 4: Calculation of Magnetic Field Strength

**Answer:** **a) $3.33 \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}$**

**Explanation:**
For the electron to travel in a straight line, the electric force ($F_E$) must be equal in magnitude and opposite in direction to the magnetic force ($F_B$).
The formulas are:
- $F_E = qE$
- $F_B = qvB$ (assuming the velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field)

Setting these forces equal:
$$qE = qvB$$
$$E = vB$$
$$B = \frac{E}{v}$$

Given:
- $E = 10\text{ NC}^{-1}$
- $v = 3 \times 10^7\text{ ms}^{-1}$

Substitute the values:
$$B = \frac{10}{3 \times 10^7}$$
$$B = 3.33 \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}$$

### Question 5: Direction of the Electric Field

**Answer:** **c) Up**

**Explanation:**
1. **Determine Magnetic Force direction:** The electron is travelling to the right, and the magnetic field is "into the page." Using the **Right-Hand Palm Rule** (for a positive charge): thumb points right, fingers point into the page, palm faces **up**. However, because the electron has a **negative charge**, we reverse the direction. Thus, the magnetic force on the electron is **down**.
2. **Balance the forces:** For the electron to travel in a straight line, the electric force must oppose the magnetic force. Since the magnetic force is pulling the electron **down**, the electric force must pull the electron **up**.
3. **Determine Electric Field direction:** An electric field $E$ exerts a force $F = qE$ on a charge. For an electron (negative charge), the force is in the **opposite direction** to the electric field lines. To get an upward force on an electron, the electric field must be pointing **downwards**.
