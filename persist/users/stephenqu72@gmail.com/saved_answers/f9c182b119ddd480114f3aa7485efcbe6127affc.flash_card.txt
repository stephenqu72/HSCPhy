### Front
How is the magnitude of torque ($\tau$) calculated, and what do the variables represent?

### Back
- Key idea: Torque is the rotational equivalent of force (the turning effect).
- Formula: $\tau = rF \sin(\theta)$
    - $r$: distance from pivot to point of application.
    - $F$: magnitude of applied force.
    - $\theta$: angle between the position vector ($r$) and force vector ($F$).
- When to use it: Whenever a force acts on an object at a distance from a pivot point.
- Common trap: Failing to convert units (e.g., cm to m) or using the wrong angle (ensure it is the angle *between* the vectors $r$ and $F$).
