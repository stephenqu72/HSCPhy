### (a) Show that $\epsilon_{\max} = 2\pi f NBA$

To derive this, we start with Faraday’s Law of Induction:
$$\epsilon = -N \frac{d\Phi_b}{dt}$$
where the magnetic flux is $\Phi_b = BA \cos(\theta)$. Since the coil rotates at a constant angular velocity $\omega$, we substitute $\theta = \omega t$:
$$\Phi_b = BA \cos(\omega t)$$
Differentiating this with respect to time $t$:
$$\epsilon = -N \frac{d}{dt}(BA \cos(\omega t)) = -NBA (-\omega \sin(\omega t)) = NBA\omega \sin(\omega t)$$
The maximum emf ($\epsilon_{\max}$) occurs when $\sin(\omega t) = 1$:
$$\epsilon_{\max} = NBA\omega$$
Since angular velocity is related to frequency $f$ by $\omega = 2\pi f$, we substitute this into the expression:
$$\epsilon_{\max} = NBA(2\pi f) = 2\pi f NBA$$

### (b) Calculate the maximum emf

**1. Identify the variables:**
*   $N = 150$ turns
*   $B = 1.85 \times 10^2 \text{ mT} = 0.185 \text{ T}$
*   Side length $s = 6.00 \text{ cm} = 0.06 \text{ m}$
*   Area $A = s^2 = (0.06)^2 = 0.0036 \text{ m}^2$
*   Frequency $f$: The speed is $240 \text{ rpm}$ (rotations per minute).
    *   $f = \frac{240}{60} = 4 \text{ Hz}$

**2. Calculation:**
$$\epsilon_{\max} = 2 \times \pi \times 4 \times 150 \times 0.185 \times 0.0036$$
$$\epsilon_{\max} \approx 2.506 \text{ V}$$

### (c) Sketch the output for doubled speed

When the speed of rotation is doubled, two things happen according to $\epsilon = NBA\omega \sin(\omega t)$:
1.  **Amplitude doubles:** The peak voltage ($V_{\max}$) goes from $2.0 \text{ V}$ to $4.0 \text{ V}$.
2.  **Frequency doubles:** The period ($T$) is halved. The original graph shows one full cycle every $0.20 \text{ s}$ ($T = 0.20 \text{ s}$). The new period will be $0.10 \text{ s}$.
