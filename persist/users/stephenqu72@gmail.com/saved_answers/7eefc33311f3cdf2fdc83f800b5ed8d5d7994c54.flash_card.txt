### Front
How does "experimental uncertainty" differ from "experimental error"?

### Back
- Key idea: Uncertainty is a range of potential values due to measurement limitations; error is the difference between a measured value and the (unknown) true value.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When evaluating measurement reliability and analyzing limitations of experimental data.
- Common trap: Confusing uncertainty with error; remember that "true values" are theoretical, while uncertainty is an objective, measurable parameter of the process.
