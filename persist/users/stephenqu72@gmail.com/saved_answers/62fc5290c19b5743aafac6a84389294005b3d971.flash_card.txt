### Front
According to the Standard Model of particle physics, which common subatomic particle is considered a composite particle rather than a fundamental particle?

### Back
- Key idea: Protons are hadrons composed of quarks (uud), whereas leptons and bosons are elementary.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When identifying particles with internal structure versus elementary particles.
- Common trap: Assuming all subatomic particles (protons, neutrons, electrons) are "fundamental."
