### Front
How does vertical drop ($y$) relate to horizontal distance ($d$) for a projectile fired horizontally with constant initial velocity ($v$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Vertical drop is proportional to the square of the horizontal distance ($y \propto d^2$).
- **Formula:** $y = \frac{1}{2}g(\frac{d}{v})^2$
- **When to use it:** To compare how different horizontal ranges affect the vertical displacement of projectiles fired horizontally.
- **Common trap:** Assuming linear proportionality ($y \propto d$); remember the time of flight also increases with distance, making the drop quadratic.
