### Front
What does a graph of recessional velocity ($v$) versus distance ($d$) for galaxies look like according to Hubble’s Law?

### Back
- Key idea: Recessional velocity is directly proportional to distance ($v \propto d$), resulting in a straight line through the origin.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = H_0d$; Hubble constant ($H_0$).
- When to use it: To determine the age of the universe or the expansion rate based on galactic data.
- Common trap: Assuming a non-linear (curved) relationship or using non-standard distance units like light years instead of megaparsecs (Mpc).
