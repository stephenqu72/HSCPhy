To solve this problem, we use the fundamental formula for torque ($\tau$):

$$\tau = F \cdot r \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

In this context, assuming the force is applied perpendicularly to the door, $\theta = 90^{\circ}$ and $\sin(90^{\circ}) = 1$, simplifying the formula to:
$$\tau = F \cdot r$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given variables:**
    *   Force ($F$) = $15\text{ N}$
    *   Distance from the pivot (the handle's distance $r$) = $85\text{ cm}$

2.  **Ensure units are in SI units:**
    *   Torque is measured in Newton-meters ($\text{N m}$), so distance must be in meters.
    *   $r = 85\text{ cm} = 0.85\text{ m}$

3.  **Calculate the torque:**
    $$\tau = 15\text{ N} \times 0.85\text{ m}$$
    $$\tau = 12.75\text{ N m}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **B**.

### Explanation of the physics:
Torque is a measure of the rotational force applied to an object around a pivot point (the hinges). By pulling the handle, you are exerting a force at a distance from the hinge. Since torque depends on the product of the force and the perpendicular distance from the pivot, increasing that distance (by placing the handle far from the hinges) makes it significantly easier to rotate the door.
