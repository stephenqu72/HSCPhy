To solve this photoelectric effect problem, we use the wave equation $c = f\lambda$ and Einstein’s photoelectric equation:
$$eV_s = hf - \Phi$$
where $V_s$ is the stopping potential, $f$ is frequency, $h$ is Planck's constant ($6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$), and $\Phi$ is the work function.

### (a) Completing the table
We use $f = \frac{c}{\lambda}$, where $c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$.
1. For $\lambda = 6.0 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$: $f = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{6.0 \times 10^{-7}} = 0.5 \times 10^{15} = 5.0 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$
2. For $\lambda = 4.0 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$: $f = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{4.0 \times 10^{-7}} = 0.75 \times 10^{15} = 7.5 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$
3. For $\lambda = 3.0 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$: $f = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{3.0 \times 10^{-7}} = 1.0 \times 10^{15} = 10.0 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$

| Stopping Potential (V) | Wavelength ($\times 10^{-7}$ m) | Frequency ($\times 10^{14}$ Hz) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1.0 | 6.0 | **5.0** |
| 2.0 | 4.0 | **7.5** |
| 3.0 | 3.0 | **10.0** |

### (b) Plotting the Graph
Plot the points $(5, 1)$, $(7.5, 2)$, and $(10, 3)$. The resulting plot is a straight line. The line of best fit passes through these points. Extrapolating the line back to the x-axis (where $V_s = 0$) gives the threshold frequency, $f_0 \approx 2.5 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$.

### (c) Determining the Work Function
The work function is defined as $\Phi = hf_0$.
Using the threshold frequency from the intercept:
$$\Phi = (6.63 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}) \times (2.5 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz})$$
$$\Phi \approx 1.66 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$
To convert to electron-volts ($1 \text{ eV} = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$):
$$\Phi \approx \frac{1.66 \times 10^{-19}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 1.04 \text{ eV}$$
