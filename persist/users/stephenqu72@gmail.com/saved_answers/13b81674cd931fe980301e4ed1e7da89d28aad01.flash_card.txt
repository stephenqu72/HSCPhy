### Front
How do you solve for the initial velocity ($u$) of a projectile given a specific displacement $(\Delta x, \Delta y)$ and launch angle ($\theta$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Analyze horizontal and vertical motion independently, linked by a common time ($t$).
- Formula / law: $\Delta x = (u \cos\theta)t$ and $\Delta y = (u \sin\theta)t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$.
- When to use it: When you need to find initial speed or trajectory variables for an object under constant gravitational acceleration.
- Common trap: Forgetting that $t$ is the same for both dimensions; failing to substitute $t = \frac{\Delta x}{u \cos\theta}$ into the vertical equation correctly.
