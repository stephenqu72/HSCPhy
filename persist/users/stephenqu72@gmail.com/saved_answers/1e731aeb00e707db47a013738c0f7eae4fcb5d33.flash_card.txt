### Front
How do you determine the time and position where two objects with different motion profiles meet?

### Back
- Key idea: Set the displacement equations ($s_A = s_B$) equal to each other and solve for $t$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$ (Kinematic equations).
- When to use it: When comparing the motion of two objects moving from the same starting point to find their point of intersection.
- Common trap: Forgetting to account for different initial velocities ($u$) or canceling the variable $t$ without considering the $t=0$ solution.
