To identify the predominant nuclear reactions in stars A and U, we must use our knowledge of stellar evolution and the Hertzsprung-Russell (H-R) diagram.

### Answer
The correct option is **B**.

### Explanation
1.  **Analyze the H-R Diagram:** Both stars A and U are located on the **Main Sequence** (the diagonal band stretching from the top-left to the bottom-right). Stars on the Main Sequence derive their energy from the fusion of hydrogen into helium.
2.  **Determine Temperature/Mass:**
    *   **Star A** is located in the upper-left of the Main Sequence. This region corresponds to hot, massive, and highly luminous stars (spectral class O/B). Because of their high core temperatures, these stars primarily fuse hydrogen via the **CNO cycle** (Carbon-Nitrogen-Oxygen cycle), which is highly temperature-dependent.
    *   **Star U** is located toward the center-right of the Main Sequence (near spectral class G, similar to our Sun). These stars are cooler and less massive than Star A. In such stars, core temperatures are lower, making the **proton-proton chain reaction** the most efficient and dominant process for hydrogen fusion.
3.  **Conclusion:** As we move along the Main Sequence, there is a transition point (typically around 1.3 solar masses, or spectral type F) where the dominant mechanism switches from the CNO cycle to the proton-proton chain. Since Star A is significantly more massive and hotter than Star U, it utilizes the CNO cycle, while Star U utilizes the proton-proton chain.

Therefore, Star A uses the CNO cycle and Star U uses the proton-proton chain reaction, matching option B.
