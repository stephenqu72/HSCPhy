### Explanation
1.  **Understanding Transformer Ratios:** The principle of a transformer is governed by the transformer equation:
    $$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$
    where $V_p$ and $V_s$ are the primary and secondary voltages, and $N_p$ and $N_s$ are the number of turns in the primary and secondary coils, respectively.

2.  **Defining "Step-up":** For a transformer to be a **step-up** transformer, the output voltage ($V_s$) must be greater than the input voltage ($V_p$). According to the ratio above, this requires the number of turns in the secondary coil ($N_s$) to be **greater** than the number of turns in the primary coil ($N_p$).

3.  **Analyzing the Diagram:** Looking at the provided image, we can count the turns visible on each side of the iron core:
    *   The primary coil (left) has 5 visible turns.
    *   The secondary coil (right) has 5 visible turns.
    Since $N_p = N_s$, the ratio $\frac{N_p}{N_s} = 1$. This would result in $V_s = V_p$. This is a 1:1 ratio, which does not "step up" the voltage. Therefore, the design is incorrect for the stated purpose.

    *   **B is incorrect** because the direction of the windings (winding sense) does not determine whether it is a step-up or step-down transformer; it only affects the phase relationship of the output voltage.
