#### Part (a)
**Answer:** The most important component of an AC generator is the **stator/rotor system (the combination of the magnetic field and the rotating coil)** or specifically the **rotating coil (armature)**. Its function is to rotate within a constant magnetic field, causing a continuous change in magnetic flux ($\Phi$) through the coil. According to Faraday’s Law of Induction, this change in flux induces an electromotive force (emf) in the coil.

**Explanation:** To secure the full 2 marks, you must link the physical movement to the electromagnetic principle. Mentioning **Faraday’s Law** ($\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$) is essential. Explain that as the coil rotates, the angle between the area vector of the coil and the magnetic field lines changes, which changes the flux and generates the voltage.
