### Answer
The graph represents the **Lorentz factor** $\gamma$ as a function of velocity. The significance of this shape in the special theory of relativity is that as the velocity of an object ($X$) approaches the speed of light ($c$, represented here by the value 3), the Lorentz factor ($Y$) approaches infinity. This indicates that time dilation becomes extreme, meaning that as an object approaches the speed of light, time for that object appears to slow down relative to a stationary observer, reaching a point where acceleration to or beyond the speed of light is impossible for massive objects.

$$t = \gamma t_0 = \frac{t_0}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$

2. **Analyze the trend:** Observe the horizontal asymptote. As $X$ approaches $3$, the denominator $\sqrt{1 - \frac{X^2}{3^2}}$ approaches zero, causing the value of $Y$ ($\gamma$) to approach infinity.
3. **Physical interpretation:**
   - At low speeds ($X \approx 0$), $Y \approx 1$, meaning time flows at the standard rate (classical mechanics).
   - As $X \to c$ (the graph approaches $X=3$), $Y \to \infty$. This implies that the duration of an event, measured from an external frame, becomes infinitely long—a phenomenon known as time dilation. 
   - Consequently, it would require infinite energy to accelerate an object with mass to the speed of light, which is why $c$ is the universal speed limit.
