### Front
What is the mathematical relationship between terminal velocity ($v$) and mass ($m$) if a graph of $v^2$ vs $m$ yields a straight line through the origin?

### Back
- Key idea: Proportionality derived from Newton’s Second Law at equilibrium ($\Sigma F = 0$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v \propto \sqrt{m}$ (No fixed constant or figure).
- When to use it: To interpret linear graphs involving squared velocity and mass, or to predict terminal velocity for different masses.
- Common trap: Assuming a linear relationship between $v$ and $m$ instead of the square root relationship ($v \propto \sqrt{m}$).
