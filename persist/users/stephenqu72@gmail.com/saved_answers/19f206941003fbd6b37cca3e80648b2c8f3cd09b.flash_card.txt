### Front
How do you calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$) if the energy released ($Q$) is known?

### Back
- Key idea: Mass-energy equivalence (mass is converted into kinetic/radiant energy).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\Delta m = \frac{Q}{c^2}$ (where $c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ ms}^{-1}$).
- When to use it: In nuclear physics or problems involving energetic fragmentation/explosions.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the speed of light ($c^2$) or failing to convert energy into Joules before calculating.
