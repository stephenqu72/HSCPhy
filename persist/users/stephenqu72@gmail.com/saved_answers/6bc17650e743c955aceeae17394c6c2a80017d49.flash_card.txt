### Front
What is the relationship between centripetal force ($F_c$) and the radius of curvature ($r$) for an object moving at a constant speed?

### Back
- Key idea: $F_c$ is inversely proportional to $r$ ($F_c \propto 1/r$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: To determine how force requirements change as a circular path widens or tightens at a constant speed.
- Common trap: Assuming $F_c$ is proportional to $r$. Remember that a smaller radius requires a larger force to maintain the same velocity.
