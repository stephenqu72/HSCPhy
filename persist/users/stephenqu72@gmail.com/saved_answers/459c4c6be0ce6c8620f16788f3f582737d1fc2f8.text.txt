### Explanation
According to Einstein's theory of Special Relativity, the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames of reference. This leads to two critical phenomena:

1.  **Time Dilation:** From the perspective of a stationary observer, a clock moving at relativistic speeds relative to them will appear to run more slowly. Crucially, due to the **Principle of Relativity**, this is reciprocal. An observer on the platform sees time slowing down for the passenger on the train, and the passenger on the train sees time slowing down for the observer on the platform. This is represented by the formula $t = \gamma t_0$, where $\gamma$ (the Lorentz factor) is always $\ge 1$.
2.  **Length Contraction:** Objects moving at relativistic speeds appear shortened in the direction of motion to a stationary observer. The formula is $L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma}$.
    *   The platform observer sees the train as shortened (contradicting D).
    *   The passenger on the train sees the platform as moving past them at high speed, so the passenger perceives the platform to be shortened (contradicting C).

**Evaluating the options:**
*   **A is false:** Time always appears to run *slower* for an object in motion relative to the observer, not faster.
*   **B is true:** This is a fundamental consequence of the Principle of Relativity; observers in different inertial frames each perceive the other's "clocks" as running slowly.
*   **C is false:** The passenger sees the platform moving, so the platform undergoes length contraction (appears shorter), not longer.
*   **D is false:** The platform observer sees the train moving, so the train undergoes length contraction (appears shorter), not longer.
