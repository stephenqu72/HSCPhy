### Front
Why does the momentum of an object in a stable circular orbit change, while its kinetic energy remains constant?

### Back
- Key idea: Momentum is a vector (dependent on direction); kinetic energy is a scalar (dependent only on speed).
- Formula / law / constant: $\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$ (vector); $K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$ (scalar).
- When to use it: Analyzing orbital mechanics where gravitational force acts perpendicular to velocity.
- Common trap: Assuming a constant speed implies a constant momentum; momentum changes because the velocity *direction* is constantly changing.
