To determine the quark composition of a proton, we need to recall that a proton has an overall electric charge of $+1$ (relative to the charge of an electron, which is $-1$, or using the elementary charge $e$).

### Explanation:
The goal is to find a combination of Up ($u = +\frac{2}{3}$) and Down ($d = -\frac{1}{3}$) quarks that sums to a total charge of $+1$.

Let's test the provided options:

*   **a) 2 Up Quarks and 1 Down Quark:**
    $$Charge = 2 \left( +\frac{2}{3} \right) + 1 \left( -\frac{1}{3} \right) = \frac{4}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3}{3} = +1$$
    This matches the charge of a proton.

*   **b) 1 Up Quark and 2 Down Quarks:**
    $$Charge = 1 \left( +\frac{2}{3} \right) + 2 \left( -\frac{1}{3} \right) = \frac{2}{3} - \frac{2}{3} = 0$$
    This is the quark composition of a neutron.

*   **c) 3 Up Quarks:**
    $$Charge = 3 \left( +\frac{2}{3} \right) = +2$$

*   **d) 3 Down Quarks:**
    $$Charge = 3 \left( -\frac{1}{3} \right) = -1$$

Since a proton must have a charge of $+1$, the correct combination is **2 Up Quarks and 1 Down Quark**.

**Correct Answer:** a) 2 Up Quarks and 1 Down Quark
