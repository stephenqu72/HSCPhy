### Front
How do you determine the gravitational field strength ($g$) at varying distances ($r$) from the centre of a planet?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational field strength follows the inverse-square law ($g \propto 1/r^2$).
- **Formula:** $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- **When to use it:** To calculate field strength at a specific point or to extrapolate/interpolate data points on a $g$ vs $r$ graph.
- **Common trap:** Confusing distance from the **surface** ($h$) with distance from the **centre** ($r$). Always ensure $r = R_{planet} + h$.
