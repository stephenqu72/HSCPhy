To solve this question, we need to apply our knowledge of spectroscopy and how different light sources interact with matter.

### Explanation

1.  **Discharge Tubes:** These tubes contain low-pressure gases that emit light when an electrical discharge passes through them. Because the atoms in the gas have discrete energy levels, electrons transition between these levels, releasing specific photons. This creates an **emission spectrum** (a series of distinct colored lines).
2.  **Reflected Sunlight:** Sunlight originates from the hot core of the Sun (a continuous spectrum). However, as this light passes through the cooler gases in the Sun's atmosphere (the photosphere and chromosphere), specific wavelengths are absorbed by the atoms present. This results in an **absorption spectrum** (a continuous rainbow of colors interrupted by dark lines).
3.  **Incandescent Filaments:** An incandescent bulb works via thermal radiation (blackbody radiation). As the filament is heated, it glows, emitting light across a broad range of wavelengths. This produces a **continuous spectrum**.
