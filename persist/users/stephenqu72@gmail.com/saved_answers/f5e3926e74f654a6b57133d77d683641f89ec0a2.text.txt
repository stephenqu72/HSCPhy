To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of electromagnetism regarding the force between two parallel current-carrying conductors, combined with Newton's laws of motion as observed by the electronic balance.

### Part (a): Calculate the force between the two rods
When the current is applied, the balance reading changes. The change in the reading indicates the magnetic force exerted on the bottom rod.

1.  **Identify the change in mass ($\Delta m$):**
    $\Delta m = 25.003\text{ g} - 25.000\text{ g} = 0.003\text{ g} = 3 \times 10^{-6}\text{ kg}$
2.  **Calculate the force ($F$):**
    The force measured by the balance is the gravitational force acting on the "extra" mass recorded.
    $F = \Delta m \times g$
    Using $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$:
    $F = 3 \times 10^{-6}\text{ kg} \times 9.8\text{ m/s}^2 = 2.94 \times 10^{-5}\text{ N}$

### Part (b): Determine the magnitude of current
We use the formula for the magnetic force per unit length between two parallel conductors:
$$F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 L}{2 \pi d}$$
Since the currents are equal ($I_1 = I_2 = I$), the formula becomes:
$$F = \frac{\mu_0 I^2 L}{2 \pi d}$$

**Given values:**
*   $F = 2.94 \times 10^{-5}\text{ N}$
*   $L = 0.218\text{ m}$
*   $d = 5.38\text{ cm} = 0.0538\text{ m}$
*   $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

**Rearranging to solve for $I$:**
$$I^2 = \frac{F \times 2 \pi d}{\mu_0 \times L}$$
$$I^2 = \frac{2.94 \times 10^{-5} \times 2 \pi \times 0.0538}{4\pi \times 10^{-7} \times 0.218}$$
$$I^2 = \frac{2.94 \times 10^{-5} \times 0.0538}{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 0.218} \approx 36.275$$
$$I = \sqrt{36.275} \approx 6.02\text{ A}$$

The magnitude of the current flowing in each rod is approximately **6.02 A**.
