To solve this problem, we must analyze the forces acting on the ball undergoing uniform circular motion in a horizontal plane (a conical pendulum).

### 1. Identify the Forces
There are two forces acting on the ball:
*   **Gravity ($mg$)** acting vertically downwards.
*   **Tension ($T$)** in the string acting at an angle $\theta$ to the vertical.

### 2. Resolve the Tension Force
We resolve the tension $T$ into two components:
*   **Vertical component:** $T \cos(\theta)$, which balances the weight of the ball so it does not move vertically:
    $$T \cos(\theta) = mg \quad \text{--- (Equation 1)}$$
*   **Horizontal component:** $T \sin(\theta)$, which provides the necessary centripetal force ($F_c$) to keep the ball in a circular path:
    $$T \sin(\theta) = \frac{mv^2}{r} \quad \text{--- (Equation 2)}$$

### 3. Derive the Expression for $\theta$
To eliminate $T$ and find $\theta$, we divide Equation 2 by Equation 1:
$$\frac{T \sin(\theta)}{T \cos(\theta)} = \frac{\frac{mv^2}{r}}{mg}$$

The $T$ terms cancel out, and the mass $m$ cancels out:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

To solve for $\theta$, we take the inverse tangent of both sides:
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{v^2}{rg}\right)$$

Comparing this to the given options, it matches **Option A**.

The correct option is **A**.
