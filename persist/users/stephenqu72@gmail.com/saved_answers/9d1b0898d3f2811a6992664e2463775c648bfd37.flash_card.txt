### Front
How do you relate the orbital radii and periods of two satellites orbiting the same central body?

### Back
- Key idea: Kepler’s Third Law states the square of the period is proportional to the cube of the orbital radius ($r^3 \propto T^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\frac{r_1^3}{T_1^2} = \frac{r_2^3}{T_2^2}$
- When to use it: Any problem involving two orbiting bodies (e.g., planets or satellites) sharing the same primary mass.
- Common trap: Forgetting to cube the radii or square the periods, or failing to ensure units are consistent.
