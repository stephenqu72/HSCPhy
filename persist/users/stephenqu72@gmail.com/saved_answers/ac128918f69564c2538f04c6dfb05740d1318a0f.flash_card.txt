### Front
In ideal projectile motion (no air resistance), how does the horizontal component of velocity ($v_x$) change throughout the flight?

### Back
- Key idea: Horizontal velocity remains constant.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s First Law ($a_x = 0$).
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem to simplify calculations by treating horizontal and vertical components independently.
- Common trap: Assuming velocity is zero at maximum height; only the *vertical* component is zero at the peak, while $v_x$ persists.
