To solve this problem, we need to convert the wavelength of the light into its corresponding frequency using the wave equation.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. Identify the wavelength ($\lambda$) from the diagram:**
Looking at the spectrum, the arrow points exactly to the line at $650\text{ nm}$.
Convert this value into standard SI units (metres):
$$\lambda = 650\text{ nm} = 650 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m} = 6.5 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m}$$

**2. State the wave equation:**
The relationship between the speed of light ($c$), frequency ($f$), and wavelength ($\lambda$) is given by:
$$c = f \lambda$$
Where $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$.

**3. Rearrange to solve for frequency ($f$):**
$$f = \frac{c}{\lambda}$$

**4. Substitute the known values:**
$$f = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}}{6.5 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m}}$$

**5. Perform the calculation:**
$$f \approx 0.4615 \times 10^{15}\text{ Hz}$$
To express this in standard scientific notation ($a \times 10^n$):
$$f \approx 4.6 \times 10^{14}\text{ Hz}$$

**Conclusion:**
Comparing this result to the given options:
A. $6.5 \times 10^2\text{ Hz}$
B. $4.6 \times 10^{14}\text{ Hz}$
C. $6.5 \times 10^{14}\text{ Hz}$
