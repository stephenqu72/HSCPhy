### Front
How do you determine the frequency ($f$) of a waveform from an oscilloscope trace?

### Back
- Key idea: Frequency is the inverse of the period ($T$), which is the time taken for one complete oscillation.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $f = \frac{1}{T}$
- When to use it: Whenever given an oscilloscope trace with a calibrated time base to find the frequency of a signal.
- Common trap: Failing to convert the time base units (e.g., milliseconds) into standard SI units (seconds) before calculating.
