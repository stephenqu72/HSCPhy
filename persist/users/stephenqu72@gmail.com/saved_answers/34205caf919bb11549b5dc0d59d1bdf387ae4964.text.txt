To determine which lines represent the highest energy light, we must use the relationship between the energy of a photon and its wavelength.

### Explanation
1. **The relationship between energy and wavelength:** According to the Planck-Einstein relation, the energy ($E$) of a photon is inversely proportional to its wavelength ($\lambda$):
   $$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$
   where $h$ is Planck's constant and $c$ is the speed of light.
2. **Analysis:** Because energy and wavelength are inversely proportional, the shortest wavelength corresponds to the highest energy.
3. **Application:** Looking at the provided emission spectrum, the scale runs from $400\text{ nm}$ to $700\text{ nm}$. The wavelength $400\text{ nm}$ is the shortest wavelength on the scale. Therefore, the emission lines located near $400\text{ nm}$ possess the highest energy.

**Correct Answer: (A) The lines near $400\text{ nm}$.**
