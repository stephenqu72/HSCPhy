### Answer
The correct statement is **(C)**: The momentum changes, but the kinetic energy remains constant throughout the orbital path.

### Explanation
To solve this, we need to analyze the properties of an object in a stable circular orbit:

1.  **Kinetic Energy ($E_k$):** The kinetic energy of an object in a circular orbit is given by $E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$. For a stable circular orbit, the orbital speed $v$ is determined by the formula $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$. Since $G$ (gravitational constant), $M$ (mass of Earth), and $r$ (orbital radius) are all constant, the speed $v$ remains constant. Consequently, the kinetic energy is also constant.

2.  **Momentum ($p$):** Momentum is a **vector quantity**, defined as $\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$. While the *magnitude* of the velocity (speed) is constant, the *direction* of the velocity vector is constantly changing as the satellite orbits the Earth. Because the direction of the velocity vector is changing, the momentum vector must also be changing at every point along the path.

**Summary:**
*   **Kinetic Energy** is a scalar value (depends only on speed squared), which remains constant.
*   **Momentum** is a vector value (depends on velocity, which includes direction), which changes because the direction of travel changes continuously in a circular path.

Therefore, statement (C) is the only correct conclusion.
