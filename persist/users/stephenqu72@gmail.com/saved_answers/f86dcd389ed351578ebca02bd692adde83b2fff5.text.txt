To calculate the work done on an electron accelerated between two charged plates, we use the relationship between work, charge, and electric potential difference (voltage).

### Explanation
The work done ($W$) on a charge ($q$) moving through a potential difference ($V$) is given by the formula:
$$W = qV$$

In this problem:
*   The charge of an electron is a fundamental constant: $q = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
*   The potential difference (voltage) is given as: $V = 20\text{ V}$

Now, substitute these values into the formula:
$$W = (1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (20\text{ V})$$
$$W = 32 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$$

To express this in scientific notation (standard form):
$$W = 3.2 \times 10^{-18}\text{ J}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches option **B**.

### Answer
The correct option is **B) $3.2 \times 10^{-18}\text{ J}$**.
