To solve this problem, we need to understand the relationship between the properties of electromagnetic radiation as we move across the spectrum from radio waves (left) to gamma rays (right).

### Answer
The correct answer is **C. Wavelength decreases, energy increases**.

### Explanation
To arrive at this answer, we must recall the fundamental equations governing electromagnetic waves:

1.  **The Wave Equation:** $c = f \lambda$
    Where $c$ is the speed of light (constant), $f$ is frequency, and $\lambda$ is wavelength. Since $c$ is constant, frequency and wavelength are inversely proportional. As we move to the right (toward gamma rays), the **frequency increases**, which means the **wavelength must decrease**.

2.  **Planck’s Equation:** $E = hf$
    Where $E$ is energy, $h$ is Planck's constant, and $f$ is frequency. Because energy is directly proportional to frequency, as the frequency increases moving to the right of the spectrum, the **energy increases**.

**Summary of the trend from left to right:**
*   **Wavelength ($\lambda$):** Decreases (from long radio waves to short gamma rays).
*   **Frequency ($f$):** Increases.
*   **Energy ($E$):** Increases.

Comparing this to our options:
*   A is incorrect because energy increases.
*   B is incorrect because wavelength decreases.
*   **C is correct: Wavelength decreases, energy increases.**
*   D is incorrect because wavelength decreases.
