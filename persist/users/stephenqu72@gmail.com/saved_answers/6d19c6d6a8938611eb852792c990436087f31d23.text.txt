$$350^2 = 200^2 + 2 \cdot \alpha \cdot 45$$

### (c) Calculate the work done to increase from setting 1 to setting 2.
By the Work-Energy Theorem, the work done equals the change in rotational kinetic energy: $W = \Delta K = K_f - K_i = \frac{1}{2}I\omega_f^2 - \frac{1}{2}I\omega_i^2$.

First, calculate the moment of inertia ($I$):
$m = 400 \text{ g} = 0.4 \text{ kg}$, $L = 0.04 \text{ m}$.
$$I = \frac{1}{3}mL^2 = \frac{1}{3} \cdot 0.4 \cdot (0.04)^2 = \frac{1}{3} \cdot 0.4 \cdot 0.0016 \approx 2.133 \times 10^{-4} \text{ kg m}^2$$

Now calculate the work:
$$W = \frac{1}{2} \cdot I \cdot (\omega_f^2 - \omega_i^2)$$
$$W = 0.5 \cdot 2.133 \times 10^{-4} \cdot (350^2 - 200^2)$$
