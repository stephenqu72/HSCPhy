To answer this question, we must apply the principles of projectile motion under the influence of gravity (ignoring air resistance).

### Analysis
1.  **Nature of Acceleration:** For a projectile in flight, the only force acting on the object is gravity, which acts downwards towards the centre of the Earth. According to Newton's Second Law, $F = ma$, the acceleration is $a = g$. This value is constant throughout the entire flight.
2.  **Direction and Sign Convention:** The problem explicitly states that the **upwards direction is positive**. Since gravity acts downwards, the acceleration must be negative.
3.  **Conclusion:** The acceleration of the projectile is a constant negative value throughout the entire flight. Therefore, on a graph of acceleration versus time, the line must be horizontal (constant) and positioned below the time axis (negative).

### Evaluating the Options
*   **(A)** shows a constant positive acceleration.
*   **(B)** shows a changing acceleration.
*   **(C)** shows a constant negative acceleration. This matches our derivation.
*   **(D)** shows a changing acceleration.

The correct choice is **(C)**.

### How to solve this type of question
*   **Identify the forces:** Always identify the forces acting on the object. In standard HSC projectile motion questions, we assume gravity is the only force.
*   **Determine constancy:** Gravity is constant near the Earth's surface, so acceleration $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ is constant.
*   **Respect signs:** Always look for the coordinate system provided in the question. If "up" is positive, gravity must be written as $-g$.
*   **Visualise:** A "constant" value is always represented by a horizontal line on a graph. If it is negative, that line must be below the horizontal axis.
