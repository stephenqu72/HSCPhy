### Front
In a perfectly inelastic collision, what happens to linear momentum and kinetic energy?

### Back
- Key idea: Linear momentum is conserved, but kinetic energy is not (it transforms into internal energy, such as heat or deformation).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\sum p_{\text{before}} = \sum p_{\text{after}}$
- When to use it: To calculate final velocities when objects stick together after impact.
- Common trap: Assuming that because momentum is conserved, kinetic energy must also be conserved.
