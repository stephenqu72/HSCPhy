### 1. Analysis of the Experiments
*   **Experiment 1 (Incandescent Lamp):** An incandescent lamp contains a hot filament that emits a continuous range of wavelengths. This produces a **continuous spectrum** (a rainbow of colors).
*   **Experiment 2 (Sodium Lamp):** A low-pressure sodium discharge lamp emits light due to specific electronic transitions within sodium atoms. These specific energy transitions result in the emission of light at discrete wavelengths, which appear as bright, distinct lines on a dark background. This is an **emission spectrum** (the characteristic "two yellow lines" for sodium).
*   **Experiment 3 (Incandescent Lamp + Sodium Vapour):** When the continuous white light from the lamp passes through cooler sodium vapour, the sodium atoms absorb the exact same specific wavelengths they would otherwise emit. This removes those specific frequencies from the continuous spectrum, leaving dark gaps at those precise locations. This is an **absorption spectrum** (a continuous background with black absorption lines).
