To find the speed of the proton when it leaves the field, we can use the principle of **Conservation of Energy**.

### Explanation

1.  **Identify the Forces and Energy:** A proton (a positive charge) entering an electric field directed to the left experiences an electric force $F = qE$ acting to the left. Since the proton is moving to the right, this force acts in the opposite direction of motion, acting as a retarding force that does work on the proton, reducing its kinetic energy.
2.  **Work-Energy Theorem:** The work done by the electric field on the charge is $W = F \times d = -qEd$ (the negative sign indicates the force opposes the displacement).
3.  **Conservation of Energy Equation:**
    The final kinetic energy $K_f$ is equal to the initial kinetic energy $K_i$ plus the work done by the field:
    $$K_f = K_i + W$$
    $$\frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - qEd$$
4.  **Solve for $v_f$:**
    Multiply the entire equation by $\frac{2}{m}$:
    $$v_f^2 = v^2 - \frac{2qEd}{m}$$
    Take the square root of both sides:
    $$v_f = \sqrt{v^2 - \frac{2qEd}{m}}$$
