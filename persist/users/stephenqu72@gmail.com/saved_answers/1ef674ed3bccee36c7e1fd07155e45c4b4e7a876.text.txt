To determine the correct expression for the tension force $F_T$ acting on the ball, we must analyze the forces acting in the radial direction (towards the center of the circle). Recall that for circular motion, the net force directed toward the center must equal the centripetal force: $\sum F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$.

### 1. At the Top of the Path
At the top, both the tension ($F_T$) and the gravitational force ($mg$) are acting **downwards** (towards the center). Therefore, they both contribute to the centripetal force:
$$F_T + mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Rearranging to solve for tension:
$$F_T = \frac{mv^2}{r} - mg$$

### 2. At the Bottom of the Path
At the bottom, the tension ($F_T$) acts **upwards** (towards the center), while the gravitational force ($mg$) acts **downwards** (away from the center). Therefore, the net centripetal force is:
$$F_T - mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Rearranging to solve for tension:
$$F_T = \frac{mv^2}{r} + mg$$

### Conclusion
Comparing these derivations with the options provided:
*   **Top of path:** $F_T = \frac{mv^2}{r} - mg$
*   **Bottom of path:** $F_T = \frac{mv^2}{r} + mg$
