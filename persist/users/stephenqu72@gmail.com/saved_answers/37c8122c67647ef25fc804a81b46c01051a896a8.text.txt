### Answer
**Propelling the vehicle:**
When the electric motor is used as a motor, it utilizes the **Motor Effect**. A current is supplied to the motor coils, which are placed within a magnetic field. According to the formula $F = BIL \sin(\theta)$, the interaction between the current-carrying conductors and the magnetic field creates a torque, which propels the vehicle forward by converting electrical energy into kinetic energy.

**Braking the vehicle (Regenerative Braking):**
When the motor acts as a generator, the vehicle's kinetic energy forces the motor coils to rotate within the magnetic field, inducing an EMF (electromagnetic induction). According to **Lenz’s Law**, the direction of the induced current creates a magnetic force that opposes the change in motion that produced it. This magnetic braking force exerts a torque in the opposite direction of the rotation, slowing the vehicle down while converting kinetic energy back into electrical energy.
