### Explanation
To solve this, you need to recall the fundamental physics definition of gravitational field strength ($g$). 

1.  **Definition:** The gravitational field strength at a specific point in space is defined as the gravitational force ($F_g$) acting per unit of mass ($m$) placed at that point. Mathematically, this is expressed as:
    $$g = \frac{F_g}{m}$$

2.  **Units:** Since the force is measured in Newtons ($N$) and mass is measured in kilograms ($kg$), the units for gravitational field strength are Newtons per kilogram ($N/kg$).

3.  **Evaluating the distractors:**
    *   **(A)** is incorrect because it describes "weight" (a force measured in Newtons), not the field strength.
    *   **(C)** is incorrect because gravitational force is not measured in kilograms; kilograms measure mass.
    *   **(D)** is incorrect because "force per unit area" defines *pressure*, not gravitational field strength.

By using the formula $g = F/m$, it becomes clear that "force per unit mass" is the correct physical description, and $N/kg$ is the correct SI unit.
