To determine the direction of the force on the electron, we use the principles of electrostatics.

### Answer
The correct answer is **A**.

### Explanation
1.  **Understand the Electric Field Direction:** The electric field $E$ is defined by the direction a **positive** test charge would move if placed in the field. The diagram shows the electric field lines pointing upwards (from the bottom plate to the top plate).
2.  **Understand the Force on a Charge:** The force $F$ on a charge $q$ in an electric field $E$ is given by the formula:
    $$F = qE$$
3.  **Apply to an Electron:** An electron has a **negative** charge ($q = -e$). Because of the negative sign, the force exerted on an electron will be in the **opposite direction** to the electric field.
4.  **Conclusion:** Since the electric field $E$ points upwards, the force $F$ on the electron must point downwards. Looking at the options provided, arrow $\downarrow$ corresponds to option A.
