To solve this problem, we must identify the physics principles governing an object at the top of a vertical circular path.

### Explanation

When the motorcycle is at the very top of the vertical loop, two forces are acting on it:
1.  **Gravity ($mg$)**: Pulling downward.
2.  **Normal force ($N$)**: The contact force from the track, also acting downward.

The net force acting toward the center of the circle is the centripetal force ($F_c$), given by:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

At the **minimum speed** required to maintain contact with the track, the normal force $N$ effectively drops to zero (the motorcycle is "weightless" at the peak). At this point, gravity alone provides the necessary centripetal force:
$$mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

We can cancel the mass $m$ from both sides, showing that the minimum speed is independent of the mass:
$$g = \frac{v^2}{r}$$

Now, rearrange the formula to solve for velocity $v$:
$$v = \sqrt{gr}$$

Using standard gravity $g = 9.8 \, \text{m s}^{-2}$ and the given radius $r = 4.2 \, \text{m}$:
$$v = \sqrt{9.8 \times 4.2}$$
$$v = \sqrt{41.16}$$
$$v \approx 6.415... \, \text{m s}^{-1}$$

Comparing this to the provided options, $6.4 \, \text{m s}^{-1}$ is the correct choice.

**Correct Answer:** C
