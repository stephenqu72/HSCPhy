### Front
What factors determine a planet's orbital period ($T$) and how are they mathematically related according to Kepler’s Third Law?

### Back
- Key idea: $T$ depends only on the orbital radius ($r$) and the central star’s mass ($M$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{GM}$
- When to use it: To calculate orbital periods or compare orbits around the same central body.
- Common trap: Assuming the mass of the orbiting planet affects its period; it cancels out and has no effect.
