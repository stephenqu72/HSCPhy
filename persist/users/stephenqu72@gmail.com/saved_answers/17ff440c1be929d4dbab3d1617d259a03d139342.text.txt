### Answer
The correct option is **(B)**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we use the standard formula for the force per unit length between two parallel, straight, current-carrying conductors.

1.  **Recall the formula:** The force $F$ acting on a length $l$ of a conductor carrying current $I_2$ in the magnetic field $B_1$ produced by another conductor carrying current $I_1$ is given by:
    $$F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 l}{2\pi r}$$
    where $r$ is the distance between the conductors.

2.  **Simplify for identical conductors:** Since the conductors are identical, their currents are equal: $I_1 = I_2 = I$. Substituting this into the formula gives:
    $$F = \frac{\mu_0 I^2 l}{2\pi r}$$

3.  **Rearrange for current $I$:** We want to isolate $I$, so we perform the following algebraic steps:
    *   Multiply both sides by $2\pi r$: 
        $$F \cdot 2\pi r = \mu_0 I^2 l$$
    *   Divide by $(\mu_0 \cdot l)$:
        $$I^2 = \frac{F \cdot 2\pi r}{\mu_0 \cdot l}$$
    *   Take the square root of both sides:
        $$I = \sqrt{\frac{F \cdot 2\pi r}{l \cdot \mu_0}}$$

This expression matches option **(B)**.
