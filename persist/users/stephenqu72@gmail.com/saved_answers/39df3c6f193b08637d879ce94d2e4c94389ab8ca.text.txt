The device shown in the diagram is an **electrical transformer**.

### Answer
A transformer operates based on the principle of electromagnetic induction. When an alternating current (AC) flows through the primary coil, it creates a continuously changing magnetic flux within the iron core. This changing magnetic flux is channeled through the core to the secondary coil. According to Faraday’s Law of Induction, the changing magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) or voltage across the secondary coil.

### How to solve this type of question
To secure full marks (3/3) for an "Explain" question about a transformer in an HSC Physics context, you must demonstrate a logical cause-and-effect chain. Use the following structure:

1.  **Identify the primary cause:** State that an **alternating current** (AC) in the primary coil generates a changing magnetic field.
2.  **Explain the transmission:** Mention the **laminated iron core**, which serves to concentrate and guide the magnetic flux from the primary to the secondary coil.
3.  **Identify the effect:** State that the changing magnetic flux links with the secondary coil, and by **Faraday’s Law**, induces an EMF in the secondary coil.

**Pro-tip for HSC exams:** Always explicitly use the term "changing" or "varying" magnetic flux. A static magnetic field will not induce a current; emphasizing that it is an *alternating* current is essential for demonstrating a deep understanding of the physics involved.
