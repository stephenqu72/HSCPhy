To solve this problem, we need to find the ratio of turns in the primary coil ($N_p$) to the turns in the secondary coil ($N_s$). We use the ideal transformer equation:

$$\frac{N_p}{N_s} = \frac{V_p}{V_s}$$

### Step 1: Identify the known values
*   Primary voltage ($V_p$) = $240\text{ V}$
*   Secondary power ($P_s$) = $12\text{ W}$
*   Secondary current ($I_s$) = $1.2\text{ A}$

### Step 2: Calculate the secondary voltage ($V_s$)
Using the power formula $P = VI$, we can rearrange it to solve for secondary voltage:
$$V_s = \frac{P_s}{I_s} = \frac{12\text{ W}}{1.2\text{ A}} = 10\text{ V}$$

### Step 3: Calculate the turns ratio
Now, substitute the voltages into the transformer ratio formula:
$$\frac{N_p}{N_s} = \frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{240\text{ V}}{10\text{ V}} = \frac{24}{1}$$

The ratio of turns in the primary coil to the secondary coil is $24:1$.
