### Front
How do you calculate the altitude ($h$) of a satellite given its orbital period ($T$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The orbital radius ($r$) is measured from the center of the Earth; altitude is measured from the surface.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Kepler’s Third Law: $r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{GMT^2}{4\pi^2}}$ and $h = r - R_e$.
- **When to use it:** Whenever calculating the height or radius of a circular orbit given a specific time period.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert the orbital period into **seconds** or forgetting to **subtract the Earth's radius ($R_e$)** when the question asks for altitude rather than orbital radius.
