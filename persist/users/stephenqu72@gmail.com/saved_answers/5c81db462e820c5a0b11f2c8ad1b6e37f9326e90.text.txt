### Explanation
To solve this problem, we rely on the fundamental principles of **Uniform Circular Motion**:

1.  **Definition of Centripetal Force:** An object moving in a circle at a constant speed is constantly changing its velocity because its direction is changing. According to Newton's Second Law ($F = ma$), this acceleration requires a net force. This net force is called the **centripetal force**.
2.  **Direction of the Force:** By definition, the centripetal force always points toward the **center of the circular path**. It acts perpendicular to the object's instantaneous velocity (which is tangent to the circle).
3.  **Applying to the Diagram:**
    *   The object $P$ is located on the leftmost edge of the circular path.
    *   To maintain circular motion, the net force must pull the object toward the center of the circle.
    *   Looking at the options provided:
        *   Arrow $W$ points away from the circle.
        *   Arrows $X$ and $Z$ are tangent to the circle (these would correspond to the direction of velocity).
        *   Arrow $Y$ points directly toward the center of the circle.

Therefore, arrow $Y$ correctly represents the direction of the net centripetal force acting on the object at position $P$.
