### Front
In a ball rolling along a smooth vertical semi-circular wall, why is the magnitude of the net force minimal at the highest point?

### Back
- Key idea: Net force magnitude is the vector sum of gravity ($mg$) and the normal force ($N$), where $N$ provides the required centripetal force ($mv^2/r$).
- Formula / law: $F_{net} = \sqrt{(mg)^2 + (mv^2/r)^2}$
- When to use it: Analyzing circular motion in a vertical plane where speed varies due to gravitational potential energy changes.
- Common trap: Assuming the net force is constant or purely perpendicular to the wall; it is the resultant of both gravity and the normal force.
