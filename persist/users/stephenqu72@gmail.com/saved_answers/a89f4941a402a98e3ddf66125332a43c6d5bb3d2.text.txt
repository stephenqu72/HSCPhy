### Answer

**(a) State de Broglie’s proposal.**
Louis de Broglie proposed that all matter, including particles such as electrons, exhibits wave-like properties in addition to their particle-like properties. He postulated that the wavelength $\lambda$ of a particle is related to its momentum $p$ by the equation $\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$, where $h$ is Planck's constant.

**(b) Describe the experimental evidence for de Broglie’s proposal.**
The primary experimental evidence is the **Davisson-Germer experiment**. In this experiment, a beam of electrons was directed at a crystalline nickel target. It was observed that the electrons scattered at specific angles, creating a diffraction pattern identical to the patterns produced by X-rays (which are known to be waves) diffracting off the crystal lattice. This confirmed that electrons, which were previously considered purely particles, exhibit wave interference and diffraction behavior, providing direct evidence for de Broglie’s wave-particle duality hypothesis.

### How to solve this type of question
*   **For Part (a):** This is a "recall" question. You must explicitly link the particle nature to the wave nature. Using the formula $\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$ or $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$ is essential to demonstrate your understanding of the relationship between mass, velocity, and wavelength.
*   **For Part (b):** This is an "explain/describe" question worth 3 marks. To get full marks, you should:
    1.  **Identify the experiment:** Clearly name the Davisson-Germer experiment.
    2.  **Explain the method:** Mention that they fired electrons at a nickel crystal.
    3.  **Explain the observation/conclusion:** State that the resulting diffraction patterns confirmed that electrons behave as waves, as diffraction is a wave-specific phenomenon.
