To solve this problem, we use the transformer turns ratio formula, which relates the number of turns in the primary and secondary coils to their respective voltages.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the given variables:**
    *   Number of turns in the primary coil ($N_p$) = $2000$
    *   Number of turns in the secondary coil ($N_s$) = $60$
    *   Primary voltage ($V_p$) = $240\text{ V}$
    *   Secondary voltage ($V_s$) = $?$

2.  **Recall the transformer equation:**
    The relationship between voltage and the number of turns is given by the formula:
    $$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$

3.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $V_s$:**
    $$V_s = V_p \times \left( \frac{N_s}{N_p} \right)$$

4.  **Substitute the values into the equation:**
    $$V_s = 240\text{ V} \times \left( \frac{60}{2000} \right)$$
    $$V_s = 240 \times 0.03$$
    $$V_s = 7.2\text{ V}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **(B)**.
