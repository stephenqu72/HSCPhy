To determine the acceleration due to gravity on the new planet, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) 0.75 g**.

### Explanation
1.  **Recall the formula:** The acceleration due to gravity ($g$) on the surface of a planet of mass $M$ and radius $r$ is given by:
    $$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$
    where $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.

2.  **Define Earth's gravity:** For Earth, let the mass be $M$ and the radius be $r$. Thus:
    $$g_{\text{earth}} = \frac{GM}{r^2} = g$$

3.  **Define the new planet's parameters:** We are given that the new planet has:
    *   Mass $M' = 3M$
    *   Radius $r' = 2r$

4.  **Calculate the new gravity ($g'$):** Substitute these new values into the gravity formula:
    $$g' = \frac{G(3M)}{(2r)^2}$$
    $$g' = \frac{3GM}{4r^2}$$

5.  **Relate $g'$ to $g$:** By factoring out the constants, we see:
    $$g' = \frac{3}{4} \left( \frac{GM}{r^2} \right)$$
    Since $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$, we can substitute $g$ back into the equation:
    $$g' = \frac{3}{4} g = 0.75g$$

Therefore, the acceleration due to gravity on the new planet is $0.75g$.
