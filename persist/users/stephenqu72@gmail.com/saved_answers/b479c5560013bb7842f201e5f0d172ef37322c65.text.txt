The correct answer is **(C) W and Z bosons**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to recall the Standard Model of particle physics, specifically the exchange particles (gauge bosons) responsible for the fundamental forces:

1.  **The Weak Nuclear Force:** This force is responsible for processes like beta decay. Quantum field theory predicts that this force is mediated by the **W and Z bosons**. These were famously predicted in the 1960s and 70s as part of the electroweak theory and were later experimentally confirmed at CERN in 1983.
2.  **Evaluating the other options:**
    *   **(A) Gluon:** This is the exchange particle for the strong nuclear force, which holds quarks together inside protons and neutrons.
    *   **(B) Photon:** This is the exchange particle for the electromagnetic force, which acts between electrically charged particles.
    *   **(D) Graviton:** This is a hypothetical elementary particle that is proposed to mediate the force of gravity in quantum gravity theories. As of now, it has not been experimentally detected.

Therefore, because the W and Z bosons are the specific exchange particles for the weak force, option (C) is the only correct choice.
