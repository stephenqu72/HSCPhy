### Front
Why does the experimental range of a projectile often differ from the theoretical range calculated using $R = \frac{u^2 \sin 2\theta}{g}$?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Air resistance (drag force) acts on the projectile, reducing horizontal velocity.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When experimental data (like a tennis ball's range) is significantly lower than theoretical calculations, or when justifying why models fail in real-world scenarios.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the discrepancy is purely due to measurement error; always check if the experimental value is consistently *lower* than the theoretical, which confirms air resistance is the cause.
