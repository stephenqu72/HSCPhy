### Front
How does Hubble’s Law explain the observed redshift of distant galaxies?

### Back
- Key idea: Galaxies are not moving *through* space; rather, the fabric of spacetime itself is expanding, stretching the wavelengths of light (cosmological redshift).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $z = \frac{\Delta \lambda}{\lambda}$; Hubble’s Law ($v = H_0d$)
- When to use it: To explain why distant galaxies recede from us in all directions without a central point of origin.
- Common trap: Mistaking cosmological redshift for a standard Doppler effect (which implies movement *through* space rather than the expansion *of* space).
