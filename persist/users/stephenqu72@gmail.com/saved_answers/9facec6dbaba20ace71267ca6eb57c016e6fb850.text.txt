### Part (a): How to solve
To achieve full marks (4 marks), you need to demonstrate the conversion of electrical energy into mechanical energy. 

**Steps to construct your diagrams:**
1.  **Diagram 1 (The Setup):** Draw a rectangular coil positioned between two permanent magnets (North and South poles). Label the split-ring commutator and the brushes.
2.  **Diagram 2 (The Motor Effect):** Apply the **Right-Hand Motor Rule** (or Fleming’s Left-Hand Rule). Show the current $I$ flowing through the coil. Note that the force $F = IlB\sin\theta$ acts in opposite directions on opposite sides of the coil, creating a torque that causes rotation.
3.  **Diagram 3 (Commutation):** Show the coil rotated by $180^\circ$. Annotate the importance of the **split-ring commutator**: it reverses the direction of current in the coil every half-turn, ensuring the torque always acts in the same rotational direction. 

**Key annotations to include:**
*   Magnetic field direction (N to S).
*   Force vectors acting on the coil sides.
*   The role of the split-ring commutator in maintaining continuous rotation.

### Part (b): How to solve
This question tests your understanding of the output of a DC generator.

**Explanation:**
A DC generator uses a split-ring commutator. Unlike an AC generator (which uses slip rings and produces a sine wave), the split-ring commutator "flips" the negative half of the AC waveform into the positive region.

**The Sketch:**
*   **Axes:** The $y$-axis should be labeled "Output Voltage ($V$)" and the $x$-axis should be "Angle ($\theta$)" from $0$ to $360^\circ$ (or $2\pi$ radians).
*   **The Waveform:** Draw two "humps" (like a rectified sine wave).
*   The first hump goes from $0$ to $180^\circ$ and the second hump goes from $180$ to $360^\circ$. 
*   Ensure both humps are **entirely above the $x$-axis** (positive voltage). 
*   The peak voltage occurs at $90^\circ$ and $270^\circ$, while the voltage is $0$ at $0^\circ$, $180^\circ$, and $360^\circ$.
