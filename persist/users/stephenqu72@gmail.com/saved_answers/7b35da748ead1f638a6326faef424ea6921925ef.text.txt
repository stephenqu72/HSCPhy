### Answer
Planck’s investigation into black body radiation was a paradigm-shifting moment in physics that resolved the "Ultraviolet Catastrophe" and fundamentally changed our understanding of the interaction between matter and light.

1.  **The Classical Failure:** Classical physics, based on the equipartition theorem, predicted that as the frequency of radiation increased, the intensity emitted by a black body would increase towards infinity (the Ultraviolet Catastrophe). This contradicted experimental evidence.
2.  **Planck’s Hypothesis:** To reconcile theory with experimental data, Max Planck proposed a radical idea: the energy of oscillators in the cavity walls was not continuous but quantized. He suggested that energy is exchanged in discrete packets or "quanta," given by the relationship:
    $$E = nhf$$
    where $n$ is an integer, $h$ is Planck’s constant, and $f$ is the frequency of radiation.
3.  **Understanding of Light and Matter:**
    *   **Matter:** Planck’s work shifted the understanding of matter at the microscopic scale, moving away from continuous classical oscillations to discrete, quantized energy states.
    *   **Light:** This established the foundation for the quantum theory of light. It suggested that light behaves as if it is composed of discrete packets of energy, which Einstein later refined in his explanation of the photoelectric effect (the photon model).
4.  **Legacy:** This initiated the field of quantum mechanics, challenging the classical assumption that energy and matter were purely continuous, and paving the way for the wave-particle duality of light.
