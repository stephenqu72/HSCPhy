The correct answer is **(B)**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must understand the physical mechanism behind "orbital decay."

1.  **What is orbital decay?** Orbital decay is the gradual decrease in the altitude of a satellite's orbit. This primarily occurs due to **atmospheric drag**. As a satellite travels through the thin upper layers of the atmosphere (the thermosphere or exosphere), it experiences small amounts of friction with gas molecules. This drag converts some of the satellite's kinetic energy into thermal energy, causing it to lose speed. When a satellite loses speed, it can no longer maintain its current orbital radius, causing it to drop to a lower altitude, where the atmosphere is denser, which increases drag further—eventually leading to re-entry.

2.  **Evaluating the options:**
    *   **(A) Incorrect:** Velocity is not the determining factor for atmospheric drag; the density of the medium through which the object travels is the primary cause.
    *   **(B) Correct:** Geostationary orbits are located at an altitude of approximately $35,786 \text{ km}$ above the Earth's surface. At this extreme distance, the density of the Earth's atmosphere is negligible (effectively a vacuum). Because there are essentially no gas particles to create frictional drag, there is no force to sap the satellite's energy, meaning it does not experience significant orbital decay.
    *   **(C) Incorrect:** All orbiting satellites must have a specific orbital velocity to maintain their orbit. If they did not move, they would fall directly back to Earth due to gravity.
    *   **(D) Incorrect:** Satellites in geostationary orbit are still well within the influence of the Earth's magnetic field (the magnetosphere).
