To answer this question, we must identify the physical phenomenon occurring when a plane wave passes through a narrow gap or opening.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) Diffraction**.

### Explanation
To solve this, let’s analyze the behavior of the waves shown in the diagram:

1.  **Observation:** The diagram shows straight, parallel wavefronts (plane waves) traveling toward a barrier. As these waves pass through the narrow gap (the aperture) in the barrier, the wavefronts curve and spread out into the region behind the barrier.
2.  **Definition of Diffraction:** Diffraction is defined as the bending or spreading out of waves as they encounter an obstacle or pass through an opening (aperture). The degree of diffraction is most significant when the size of the opening is comparable to the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the incident wave. 
3.  **Evaluating the options:**
    *   **(A) Diffraction:** This perfectly matches our observation of waves spreading out after passing through an opening.
    *   **(B) Refraction:** This involves the change in direction of a wave as it passes from one medium to another due to a change in wave speed. This is not depicted here.
    *   **(C) Reflection:** This occurs when a wave bounces off a surface. The waves in the diagram are passing *through* the gap, not bouncing off the barrier.
    *   **(D) Superposition:** This refers to the principle where two or more waves overlap to form a resultant wave. While interference (a consequence of superposition) often occurs *after* diffraction, the primary process illustrated by the bending of the wavefronts at the gap is diffraction.

In the HSC Physics syllabus, recognizing these fundamental wave characteristics is crucial for understanding how waves behave in various environments.
