To determine the correct frequency and amplitude, we need to analyze the provided displacement-time graph.

### 1. Finding the Amplitude
The amplitude is defined as the maximum displacement from the equilibrium position ($d=0$). Looking at the graph, the wave reaches a maximum peak of $1.0\text{ cm}$ and a minimum trough of $-1.0\text{ cm}$. Therefore, the amplitude is $1.0\text{ cm}$.

### 2. Finding the Frequency
To find the frequency, we first need to determine the period ($T$), which is the time taken for one full cycle. 
* Starting at $t = 0\text{ ms}$, the wave is at a maximum. 
* The wave returns to a maximum at $t = 20\text{ ms}$.
* Therefore, the period $T = 20\text{ ms} = 20 \times 10^{-3}\text{ s} = 0.02\text{ s}$.

The relationship between frequency ($f$) and period is:
$$f = \frac{1}{T}$$
$$f = \frac{1}{0.02\text{ s}} = 50\text{ Hz}$$
