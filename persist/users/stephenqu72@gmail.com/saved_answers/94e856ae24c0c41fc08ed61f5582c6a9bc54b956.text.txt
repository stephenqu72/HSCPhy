To achieve full marks on this 5-mark HSC Physics question, you need to link the physical setup of the apparatus to the quantum mechanical explanation of electron transitions.

### Answer
To produce an absorption spectrum in the laboratory, a continuous spectrum of light (produced by a hot, dense light source like a filament lamp) is passed through a cooler, dilute gas. When the transmitted light is passed through a prism or diffraction grating and viewed, it appears as a continuous spectrum interrupted by specific dark lines.

These dark lines occur because the atoms in the cooler gas absorb specific photons. An electron in a ground-state atom absorbs a photon with an energy exactly equal to the difference between two energy levels ($E_2 - E_1 = hf$). The electron is "excited" to a higher energy level. Because these energy levels are quantized and unique to the specific element (the "fingerprint"), only photons of very specific wavelengths are removed from the continuous spectrum. The gas then re-emits these photons in all directions, causing the observed dark lines against the bright background.

### How to solve this
1.  **Identify the Components (1 mark):** Clearly state that you need a continuous light source and a cooler gas.
2.  **Describe the Mechanism (2 marks):** Use the term "quantized energy levels." Explain that photons are absorbed only if their energy corresponds exactly to the energy gap between electron orbitals ($E = \Delta E$).
3.  **Explain the Result (2 marks):** Explain that the missing light creates dark lines at those specific wavelengths. Mention that this is diagnostic of the element's atomic structure (the "atomic fingerprint").

**Teacher's Tip:** In the HSC, examiners look for the specific relationship $E = hf$ or $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$. Mentioning that the energy levels are **quantized** is the "key" word that will secure you the marks for the explanation section of this response.
