To determine the correct answer, we must identify which of these experiments is not associated with the confirmation of Special Relativity (the constancy of the speed of light and the principle of relativity in all inertial frames).

### Answer
The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we evaluate each option based on its role in the development or validation of physics theories:

*   **A. The Michelson-Morley experiment:** This experiment attempted to detect the "luminiferous ether." Its null result was pivotal, as it suggested that the speed of light is constant regardless of the Earth's motion, which served as a primary foundation for Einstein's postulates.
*   **B. The Hafele-Keating experiment:** This experiment used atomic clocks on commercial airliners to measure time dilation. It provided direct experimental proof for the time dilation predicted by the Special Theory of Relativity.
*   **C. Cosmological studies:** Studies involving time dilation in distant objects (such as observations of Type Ia supernovae) provide evidence that confirms the effects of relativity at a cosmological scale.
*   **D. The Geiger-Marsden experiment:** This is also known as the **Rutherford gold foil experiment**. This experiment involved firing alpha particles at a thin gold foil to observe their deflection. It provided the evidence required to propose the **nuclear model of the atom** (the discovery of the dense, positively charged nucleus). It has nothing to do with Special Relativity.

Since the Geiger-Marsden experiment is a pillar of atomic structure rather than relativity, it is the correct choice.
