To solve this problem, we use the transformer ratio formula, which relates the number of turns in the coils to the voltages across them.

### Answer
The correct answer is **D. 400**.

### Explanation
For an ideal transformer, the ratio of the primary voltage ($V_p$) to the secondary voltage ($V_s$) is equal to the ratio of the number of turns in the primary coil ($N_p$) to the number of turns in the secondary coil ($N_s$). This is expressed by the formula:

$$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$

From the diagram, we have:
- Primary voltage ($V_p$) = $12\text{ V}$
- Primary turns ($N_p$) = $20$
- Secondary voltage ($V_s$) = $240\text{ V}$
- Secondary turns ($N_s$) = $?$

Substitute the known values into the formula:
$$\frac{12}{240} = \frac{20}{N_s}$$

Simplify the fraction on the left:
$$\frac{1}{20} = \frac{20}{N_s}$$

Now, solve for $N_s$ by cross-multiplying:
$$1 \times N_s = 20 \times 20$$
$$N_s = 400$$

Since the voltage increased from $12\text{ V}$ to $240\text{ V}$ (a step-up transformer), the number of turns must increase proportionally, confirming that $400$ is the correct result.
