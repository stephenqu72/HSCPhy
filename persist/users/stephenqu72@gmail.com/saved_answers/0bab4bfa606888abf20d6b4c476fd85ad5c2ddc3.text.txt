To solve this question, we must refer to the **Hertzsprung-Russell (H-R) diagram**, which plots the luminosity and temperature of stars.

### Explanation:

1.  **Red Giants:** These stars are located in the upper-right region of the H-R diagram.
    *   **Temperature:** Because they are "red," their surface temperatures are relatively low (cooler than the Sun).
    *   **Luminosity:** Despite being cool, they are very large in radius, which gives them a high luminosity compared to the Sun.
2.  **White Dwarfs:** These stars are found in the lower-left region of the H-R diagram.
    *   **Temperature:** They are the exposed hot cores of stars, so their surface temperatures are very high (hotter than the Sun).
    *   **Luminosity:** Because they are extremely small in radius, their total light output (luminosity) is very low compared to the Sun.

### Comparison to the Sun:
*   **Red Giant:** Lower temperature than the Sun, Higher luminosity than the Sun.
*   **White Dwarf:** Higher temperature than the Sun, Lower luminosity than the Sun.

Looking at the options provided:
*   Option (A) matches these properties: Red Giant (Lower temperature, Higher luminosity) and White Dwarf (Higher temperature, Lower luminosity).

Therefore, the correct choice is **(A)**.
