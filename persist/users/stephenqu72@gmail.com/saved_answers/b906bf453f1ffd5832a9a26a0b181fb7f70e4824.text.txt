### Analysis of the Diagrams:
1.  **Diagram 1 (Reference):** This is the absorption spectrum of hydrogen. It shows specific, sharp lines at exact wavelengths.
2.  **Diagram 2 (Croesus):** The dark absorption lines align perfectly with the hydrogen reference lines. This indicates that the star contains hydrogen and is not moving significantly relative to us in a way that causes line shifting (or is at a neutral velocity).
3.  **Diagram 3 (Dromus):** The absorption lines are "smeared" or broadened. Furthermore, they are shifted slightly toward the blue end (left) of the spectrum compared to Diagram 1.

### Evaluating the Options:
*   **(A) Dromus is rotating faster than Croesus:** This is a **correct conclusion**. The broadening of spectral lines (as seen in Diagram 3) is often caused by the Doppler effect resulting from the rapid rotation of the star. Different parts of the star's equator are moving toward and away from the observer, broadening the absorption lines.
*   **(B) Both stars contain hydrogen:** This is a **correct conclusion**. Both spectra show the same characteristic pattern of lines as the hydrogen reference in Diagram 1.
*   **(C) Dromus is moving towards us:** This is a **correct conclusion**. The shift of the absorption lines toward the blue end (the left) relative to the reference spectrum indicates a blueshift, which occurs when a source is moving toward the observer.
*   **(D) Croesus has a higher surface temperature than Dromus:** This is **NOT a conclusion that can be drawn**. Spectral line broadening can also be caused by high pressure in a star's atmosphere or high temperatures, but looking at these diagrams alone, we cannot determine the relative surface temperatures. Temperature is typically determined by the peak wavelength of the continuous spectrum (Wien’s Law), which is not explicitly provided here.

**Conclusion:** The statement that CANNOT be drawn is (D).
