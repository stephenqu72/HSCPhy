### Front
What were the two key principles established by Galileo regarding motion and gravity?

### Back
- Key idea: Projectile motion components are independent, and objects under gravity undergo uniform acceleration ($d \propto t^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $d = \frac{1}{2}at^2$; Principle of Independence of Perpendicular Motions.
- When to use it: To explain projectile motion or kinematics of falling bodies.
- Common trap: Attributing the Universal Law of Gravitation ($1/r^2$) or the specific value $g = 9.8 \, \text{ms}^{-2}$ to Galileo; he discovered the *concept* of constant acceleration, not the specific modern numerical value.
