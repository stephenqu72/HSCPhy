### Front
If two students pull on opposite ends of a rope with $400\text{ N}$ of force each, what is the magnitude of the force exerted by the rope on each student?

### Back
- Key idea: Newton's Third Law states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction; the tension in the rope equals the magnitude of the applied force.
- Formula / law: Newton’s Third Law ($F_{AB} = -F_{BA}$).
- When to use it: To determine interaction forces between objects in equilibrium or tension problems.
- Common trap: Adding the forces together ($800\text{ N}$) or incorrectly assuming they cancel to $0\text{ N}$.
