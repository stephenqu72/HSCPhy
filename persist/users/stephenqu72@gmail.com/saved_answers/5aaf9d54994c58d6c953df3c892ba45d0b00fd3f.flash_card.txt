### Front
Explain why alpha decay produces a discrete energy spectrum while beta decay produces a continuous one.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Alpha decay is a two-body process (fixed energy share), whereas beta decay is a three-body process (energy shared between electron and antineutrino).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Conservation of energy, momentum, and angular momentum.
- **When to use it:** When asked to explain experimental evidence for the existence of the neutrino or to contrast decay energy spectra.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to explicitly mention the antineutrino; continuous spectra occur because the antineutrino carries away a variable amount of energy.
