### Front
Why did early DC electrical grids require power stations to be located close to consumers?

### Back
- Key idea: DC cannot be efficiently stepped up in voltage, forcing high current transmission and significant resistive heat loss ($P_{loss} = I^2R$) over long distances.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $P_{loss} = I^2R$
- When to use it: To explain the limitations of DC and the efficiency of AC transmission (which uses transformers to lower $I$ by increasing $V$).
- Common trap: Thinking DC is inherently less efficient at the point of use; it is inefficient only due to *transmission* logistics.
