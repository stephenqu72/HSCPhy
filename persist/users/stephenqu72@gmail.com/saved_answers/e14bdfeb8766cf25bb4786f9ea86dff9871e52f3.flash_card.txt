### Front
State Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion and its mathematical expression for comparing two orbiting bodies.

### Back
- **Key idea:** The square of the orbital period ($T$) is directly proportional to the cube of the orbital radius ($r$).
- **Formula:** $\frac{T_1^2}{r_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{r_2^3}$
- **When to use it:** To calculate an unknown period or orbital radius when comparing two objects orbiting the same central body.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to cube the radii or square the periods; ensure units are consistent (e.g., both in years or both in seconds) on both sides of the equation.
