To find the peak wavelength of electromagnetic radiation emitted by a black body at a given temperature, we use **Wien's Displacement Law**.

### The Formula
Wien's Displacement Law is given by:
$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$

Where:
*   $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength in meters ($m$).
*   $T$ is the absolute temperature in Kelvin ($K$).
*   $b$ is Wien's displacement constant, which is approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, m \cdot K$.

### Step-by-Step Solution
1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   $T = 310 \, K$
    *   $b \approx 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, m \cdot K$

2.  **Substitute the values into the formula:**
    $$\lambda_{max} = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{310}$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$\lambda_{max} \approx 0.009348 \times 10^{-3} \, m$$
    Converting this to scientific notation:
    $$\lambda_{max} \approx 9.3 \times 10^{-6} \, m$$

Comparing our result to the given options, $9.3 \times 10^{-6} \, m$ corresponds to option **A**.

### Conclusion
The peak wavelength of the infrared radiation emitted by a person at $310 \, K$ is approximately $9.3 \, \mu m$, which is $9.3 \times 10^{-6} \, m$.

**Correct Option: A**
