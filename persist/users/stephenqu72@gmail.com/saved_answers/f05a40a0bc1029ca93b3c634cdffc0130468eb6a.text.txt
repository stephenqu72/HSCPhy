### Answer
The correct answer is **a) $C$**.

### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of one of the fundamental postulates of **Albert Einstein's Special Theory of Relativity**.

1.  **The Second Postulate of Special Relativity:** Einstein proposed that the speed of light in a vacuum ($C$) is constant for all inertial observers, regardless of the motion of the source or the observer.
2.  **Application:** In classical (Galilean) physics, you might be tempted to add the velocities together ($0.7C + 0.7C = 1.4C$). However, in the realm of Special Relativity, light does not obey Galilean velocity addition. 
3.  **Radio Waves:** Radio waves are part of the electromagnetic spectrum and therefore travel at the speed of light ($C$). Because the speed of light is an absolute constant in all inertial frames of reference, no matter how fast Ship A or Ship B are moving towards or away from each other, the radio wave emitted by Ship A will always be measured by an observer on Ship B to be traveling at exactly $C$.

Even though the ships are moving toward each other, the speed of the signal (the light wave) remains invariant. Therefore, the velocity of the radio wave relative to Ship B is simply $C$.
