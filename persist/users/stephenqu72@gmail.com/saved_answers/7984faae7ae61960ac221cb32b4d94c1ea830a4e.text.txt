To determine the direction of the force experienced by the current-carrying conductor, we use the **Right-Hand Palm Rule** (also known as Fleming's Left-Hand Rule).

### Explanation of the Solution

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   **Magnetic Field ($B$):** Represented by the parallel arrows pointing towards the top-right of the page.
    *   **Current ($I$):** Represented by the symbol $\otimes$, which means the current is flowing **into the page**.

2.  **Apply the Right-Hand Palm Rule:**
    *   Point your **thumb** in the direction of the current ($I$): Point your thumb **into the page** (away from your face).
    *   Point your **fingers** in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$): Align your fingers so they point towards the top-right of the page.
    *   Your **palm** now faces the direction of the magnetic force ($F$). Alternatively, if using Fleming's Left-Hand Rule:
        *   Point your index finger in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$) (top-right).
        *   Point your middle finger in the direction of the current ($I$) (into the page).
        *   Your thumb will point towards the bottom-right of the page.

3.  **Analyze the Options:**
    *   The thumb (for Right-Hand Palm Rule) or the thumb (for Left-Hand Rule) points perpendicular to the plane formed by the current and the magnetic field.
    *   Given the orientation, the force acts perpendicular to the magnetic field lines in a direction that corresponds to **Option D**.

### Conclusion
The force is directed towards the bottom-right. Therefore, the correct option is **D**.
