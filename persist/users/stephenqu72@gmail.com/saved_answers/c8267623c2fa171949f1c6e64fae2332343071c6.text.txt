To calculate the wavelength of an electron, we use the **de Broglie wavelength** formula, which relates the momentum of a particle to its associated wavelength.

### 1. Identify the given values:
*   Velocity ($v$) = $0.78 \times 10^6 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Mass of an electron ($m$) = $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$ (This is a standard physical constant provided in your HSC Physics Data Sheet).
*   Planck's constant ($h$) = $6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$ (Also from the Data Sheet).

### 2. The Formula:
The de Broglie wavelength ($\lambda$) is given by:
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$

### 3. Calculation:
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{(9.11 \times 10^{-31}) \times (0.78 \times 10^6)}$$

First, calculate the denominator (momentum, $p$):
$$p = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \times 0.78 \times 10^6 \approx 7.1058 \times 10^{-25} \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$$

Now, divide $h$ by $p$:
$$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{7.1058 \times 10^{-25}}$$
$$\lambda \approx 9.32 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}$$

**Final Answer:**
The wavelength of the electron is approximately $9.3 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}$ (or $0.93 \text{ nm}$).

***

### How to solve this:
1.  **Recognise the physics concept:** When asked for the "wavelength of a particle," always use the de Broglie equation.
2.  **Refer to your Data Sheet:** Ensure you retrieve the correct values for the mass of the electron and Planck's constant.
3.  **Check units:** Ensure velocity is in $\text{m s}^{-1}$ and mass is in $\text{kg}$ to maintain SI unit consistency so that the resulting wavelength is in metres.
4.  **Significant Figures:** Since the given velocity ($0.78$) has two significant figures, your final answer should ideally be rounded to two significant figures ($9.3 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}$).
