### Part (a)
**Answer:**
In the extremely early universe (the Quark Epoch), the temperature was so high that quarks existed as a free "quark-gluon plasma." As the universe expanded and cooled (during the Hadron Epoch), quarks combined via the strong nuclear force to form hadrons, such as protons and neutrons. Subsequently, during the period of Nucleosynthesis (a few minutes after the Big Bang), these protons and neutrons fused to form the first simple atomic nuclei, primarily hydrogen (protons) and helium.

**How to solve it:**
*   **Key Concept:** Hierarchy of structure. You must state the progression: Quarks $\rightarrow$ Hadrons $\rightarrow$ Nuclei.
*   **Context:** Explicitly mention the cooling of the universe as the driver for this progression.
*   **Marking criteria:** 2 marks require both the structural hierarchy and the chronological formation process.

### Part (b)
**Answer:**
Particle accelerators (like the Large Hadron Collider) are essential for validating the Standard Model of particle physics because they allow us to recreate the extreme energy conditions of the early universe. By smashing particles together at near-light speeds, we observe the conversion of kinetic energy into mass ($E=mc^2$), allowing for the detection of short-lived, high-mass particles.

A primary example is the discovery of the **Higgs Boson** in 2012. The theoretical prediction of the Higgs field, which grants particles mass, was a cornerstone of the Standard Model. Without the collider, we could not have achieved the high energy densities required to produce and confirm the existence of this particle. Similarly, accelerators have confirmed the existence of quarks and the nature of the strong and weak nuclear forces. By providing empirical data that matches theoretical predictions, accelerators transform speculative mathematical models into verified scientific knowledge, cementing our understanding of the fundamental building blocks of the universe.

**How to solve it:**
*   **Assessment:** To "assess" something means to make a judgment about its value. You must explain *why* it is important (it bridges the gap between theory and observation).
*   **Physics Principle:** Connect the high-energy environment of accelerators to the Big Bang using Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence, $E=mc^2$.
*   **Evidence:** You must include a specific example (Higgs Boson is the strongest choice) to gain full marks.
*   **Structure:** Start with the mechanism (acceleration), move to the theoretical connection, provide the specific example, and conclude with the impact on scientific knowledge.
