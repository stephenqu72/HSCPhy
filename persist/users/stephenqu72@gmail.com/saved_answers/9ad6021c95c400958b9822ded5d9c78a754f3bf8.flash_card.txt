### Front
What is the Doppler Effect and how does an observer moving towards a stationary sound source affect the perceived frequency?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The change in frequency/wavelength of a wave due to relative motion between the source and observer. Moving towards a source increases the frequency (pitch).
- **Formula:** $f_{obs} = f_s \left( \frac{v \pm v_{obs}}{v \mp v_s} \right)$
- **When to use it:** When analyzing wave phenomena (sound or light) involving moving observers or sources.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the sign convention; remember that moving *towards* always increases the observed frequency, while moving *away* always decreases it.
