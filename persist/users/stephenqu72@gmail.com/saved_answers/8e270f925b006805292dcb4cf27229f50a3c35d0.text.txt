### Answer

**Experimental Method:**
1.  **Setup:** Use a projectile launcher (such as a spring-loaded cannon) mounted on a laboratory bench. Ensure the launcher can be adjusted to specific angles $(\theta)$ and that the launch velocity $(v)$ remains constant for all trials.
2.  **Procedure:** 
    *   Set the launcher to an initial angle (e.g., $15^{\circ}$).
    *   Fire the projectile and measure the horizontal distance (range, $R$) from the point directly below the release to the landing spot using a measuring tape. 
    *   Repeat the trial at least three times for each angle to determine an average range.
    *   Increase the angle by increments (e.g., $15^{\circ}, 30^{\circ}, 45^{\circ}, 60^{\circ}, 75^{\circ}$) and repeat the measurement process.
3.  **Variables:** 
    *   *Independent variable:* Launch angle ($\theta$). 
    *   *Dependent variable:* Horizontal range ($R$). 
    *   *Controlled variables:* Launch velocity ($v$), height of launch (same as landing height), and air resistance (minimized).

**Graph:**
The relationship is defined by the range formula:
$$R = \frac{v^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$

The graph of $R$ vs $\theta$ is a sine wave starting at $0^{\circ}$ (Range = 0), peaking at $45^{\circ}$ (maximum range), and returning to 0 at $90^{\circ}$.

```text
Range (R)
 ^          _
 |        /   \
 |      /       \
 |     /         \
 |____/___________\____> Angle (theta)
 0   45          90
```
