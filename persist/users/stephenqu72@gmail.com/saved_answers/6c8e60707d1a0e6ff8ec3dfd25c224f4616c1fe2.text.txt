To solve this physics problem, we need to analyze the forces acting on the cyclist.

### Answer
On a flat track, the centripetal force is provided solely by the frictional force between the tires and the track. However, on a banked track, the normal force ($F_N$) acts perpendicular to the surface of the track. Because the track is tilted at an angle ($\theta$), the horizontal component of this normal force points toward the center of the circular path. This horizontal component, $F_{N, horizontal} = F_N \sin(\theta)$, acts as an additional centripetal force. Therefore, the banked track allows for a larger total centripetal force, enabling the cyclist to maintain a higher speed without relying solely on friction.
