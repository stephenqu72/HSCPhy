To secure full marks for this question, you need to provide five distinct pieces of empirical evidence and briefly explain how each confirms Einstein’s postulates of special relativity.

### Answer
1.  **Michelson-Morley Experiment:** This landmark experiment attempted to detect the "luminiferous ether" through which light was thought to travel. The null result demonstrated that the speed of light is independent of the Earth's motion through space, providing foundational evidence that $c$ is constant.
2.  **Muon Decay:** Muons created in the upper atmosphere have a short half-life and should decay before reaching the surface. However, due to time dilation (a direct consequence of the constant speed of light), they travel at relativistic speeds, allowing them to survive long enough to be detected at sea level.
3.  **Atomic Clocks on Jet Aircraft (Hafele-Keating Experiment):** By flying ultra-precise atomic clocks around the world, physicists observed time differences consistent with relativistic predictions. This confirms that time is relative to the frame of reference, supporting the equivalence of inertial frames.
4.  **Particle Accelerator Data:** In cyclotrons or synchrotrons, particles are accelerated to speeds near $c$. As they approach $c$, it requires exponentially more energy to increase their velocity, confirming the relativistic mass increase and the limit of $c$ as a universal speed constant.
5.  **Global Positioning System (GPS):** GPS satellites move at high orbital velocities and are in a different gravitational potential than Earth-based receivers. The system must apply both special and general relativity corrections to its internal clocks to maintain sub-meter accuracy, providing real-world, everyday validation of relativistic time effects.

### How to solve this
*   **Identify vs. Analyse:** The command word is "Identify and analyse." Simply listing the evidence is not enough; you must explicitly state *what* the evidence is and *how* it proves the postulates (e.g., "The result proves $c$ is constant" or "This confirms time dilation").
*   **Structure:** Since this is a 5-mark question, aim for a clear, dot-point structure. One mark is allocated to the identification and brief analysis of each point.
*   **Broaden your scope:** Ensure your examples cover both postulates. The Michelson-Morley experiment specifically targets the speed of light, while the Hafele-Keating and Muon experiments highlight the consequences of the postulates on time.
*   **HSC Tip:** Always link back to the specific postulate mentioned in the prompt. Using keywords like "time dilation," "null result," and "reference frames" will signal to the examiner that you have a high-level understanding of the physics.
