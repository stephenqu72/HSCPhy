To solve this problem, we use the formula for the magnetic force on a current-carrying conductor:

$$F = BIl \sin(\theta)$$

### Explanation:
1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   Magnetic field strength, $B = 6.5\text{ T}$
    *   Current, $I = 2.5\text{ A}$
    *   Angle, $\theta = 30^\circ$ (the angle between the conductor and the magnetic field lines)
2.  **Analyze the question:** The options are given in $\text{N m}^{-1}$, which represents force per unit length ($F/l$). Dividing the original formula by length $l$ gives:
    $$\frac{F}{l} = BI \sin(\theta)$$
3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$\frac{F}{l} = 6.5 \times 2.5 \times \sin(30^\circ)$$
    Since $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$:
    $$\frac{F}{l} = 6.5 \times 2.5 \times 0.5$$
    $$\frac{F}{l} = 8.125\text{ N m}^{-1}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (matching the precision of the values provided in the diagram), we get $8.1\text{ N m}^{-1}$.

Comparing this to the given choices:
(A) $6.2\text{ N m}^{-1}$
(B) $8.1\text{ N m}^{-1}$
(C) $14\text{ N m}^{-1}$
(D) $16\text{ N m}^{-1}$

The correct option is **(B)**.
