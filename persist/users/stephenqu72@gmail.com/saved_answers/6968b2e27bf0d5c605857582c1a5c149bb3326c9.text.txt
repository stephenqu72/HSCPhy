To explain why the spinning aluminium disc comes to rest, we must apply the principles of electromagnetic induction and Lenz's Law.

### Answer
The spinning aluminium disc enters a region of perpendicular magnetic field. As the metal moves through this field, the free electrons in the aluminium experience a magnetic force (Lorentz force) due to their motion. This causes a separation of charge, creating **eddy currents** within the disc. According to **Lenz’s Law**, these eddy currents generate their own magnetic field that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced them. Consequently, an electromagnetic force acts on the eddy currents, producing a torque that opposes the rotation of the disc, effectively acting as a magnetic brake that brings the disc to rest.

1.  **Identify the cause (Faraday’s Law/Lorentz force):** State that as the disc rotates through the magnetic field, the changing magnetic flux (or the motion of charges in a magnetic field) induces eddy currents within the conductive aluminium disc.
2.  **State Lenz’s Law:** Mention that the direction of these eddy currents is determined by Lenz’s Law, which states that the induced current will create a magnetic field that opposes the motion that caused it.
3.  **Explain the interaction:** Explain that there is an interaction between the external magnetic field and the magnetic field created by the eddy currents. This results in a magnetic force (Lorentz force) acting on the disc.
4.  **Conclude with the effect:** Explicitly state that this force exerts a torque in the opposite direction to the disc's rotation, resulting in the dissipation of rotational kinetic energy as thermal energy (due to resistance in the aluminium), causing the disc to decelerate and come to rest.
