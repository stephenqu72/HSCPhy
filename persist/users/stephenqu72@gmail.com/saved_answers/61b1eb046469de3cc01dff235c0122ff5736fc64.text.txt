### Identification of Experimental Setups

1.  **Setup Y:** This diagram shows a radiation source (Po) emitting alpha particles into a target (Be), resulting in the ejection of neutral radiation that is then detected. This is the **Chadwick experiment (1932)**. James Chadwick used this setup to demonstrate the existence of the neutron by bombarding beryllium with alpha particles and measuring the properties of the resulting neutral radiation. While Joliot-Curie performed similar experiments earlier, they misinterpreted the neutral radiation as high-energy gamma rays; Chadwick correctly identified it as a new particle, the neutron.

While Ernest Rutherford conceived the theory based on the results, the actual experimental work was carried out by his assistants, **Hans Geiger and Ernest Marsden**, under his supervision.
