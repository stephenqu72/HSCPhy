### Answer
James Clerk Maxwell’s contribution to our understanding of light was monumental, as he unified the theories of electricity and magnetism into a single electromagnetic framework. His key contributions include:

1.  **Maxwell’s Equations:** He formulated a set of four partial differential equations that described how electric and magnetic fields are generated and altered by each other and by charges and currents.
2.  **Prediction of Electromagnetic Waves:** By manipulating these equations, he discovered that oscillating electric and magnetic fields would propagate through space as a transverse wave.
3.  **Calculation of Velocity:** He calculated the speed of these waves using the constants $\epsilon_0$ (permittivity of free space) and $\mu_0$ (permeability of free space) through the formula:
    $$v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$$
4.  **Identification of Light as an EM Wave:** He found that the calculated speed of these waves was approximately $3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$, which matched the experimentally determined speed of light. He concluded that light itself is an electromagnetic wave, moving the study of optics into the realm of electromagnetism.
