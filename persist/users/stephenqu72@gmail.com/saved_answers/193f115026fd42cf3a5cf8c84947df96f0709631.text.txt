### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) Light is a form of an electromagnetic wave.**

---

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to understand the historical context of James Clerk Maxwell’s work in the late 19th century regarding electromagnetism:

1.  **The Unification:** Before Maxwell, electricity and magnetism were treated as separate phenomena. Maxwell developed a set of equations (Maxwell's Equations) that unified these two fields, demonstrating that a changing electric field produces a magnetic field and vice versa.
2.  **Theoretical Prediction:** When Maxwell solved these equations for free space, he found that the resultant wave equation predicted that electromagnetic disturbances travel through space as waves. 
3.  **The Constant $c$:** Maxwell calculated the speed of these predicted waves using the permittivity ($\epsilon_0$) and permeability ($\mu_0$) of free space:
    $$c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\epsilon_0 \mu_0}}$$
    When he calculated this value, he found it to be approximately $3 \times 10^8$ m/s, which matched the experimentally determined speed of light at the time.
4.  **Conclusion:** Based on this result, Maxwell proposed that light itself is an electromagnetic wave. This was a profound discovery because it shifted the scientific understanding of light from a mechanical or corpuscular theory to an electromagnetic one.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
*   **(A)** Maxwell's theory showed that light does not require a medium to travel (it is not mechanical).
*   **(B)** The corpuscular theory was championed by Newton; Maxwell’s work actually solidified the wave nature of light.
*   **(D)** While light does travel slower in denser materials (refraction), this was already known through experiments and Snell's Law; it was not the fundamental finding of Maxwell's unification.
