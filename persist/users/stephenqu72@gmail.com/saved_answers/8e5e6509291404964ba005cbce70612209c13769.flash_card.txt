### Front
Derive the expression for the angle $\theta$ of a conical pendulum moving in a horizontal circle with velocity $v$ and radius $r$.

### Back
- **Key idea:** The horizontal component of tension provides the centripetal force, while the vertical component balances gravity.
- **Formula:** $\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$
- **When to use it:** When analyzing an object moving in a horizontal circular path suspended by a string (conical pendulum).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that $m$ (mass) cancels out, or confusing $\sin(\theta)$ and $\cos(\theta)$ components relative to the vertical vs. horizontal.
