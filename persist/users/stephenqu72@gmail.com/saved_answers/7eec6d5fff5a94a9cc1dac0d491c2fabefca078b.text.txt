### Explanation:

To solve this problem, we must apply **Wien's Displacement Law**. This fundamental principle of thermal physics states that the wavelength at which the emission of a black body reaches its maximum intensity ($\lambda_{max}$) is inversely proportional to the absolute temperature ($T$) of the black body.

The mathematical relationship is given by:
$$\lambda_{max} T = b$$
Or, rewritten to solve for $\lambda$:
$$\lambda = \frac{b}{T}$$

Where:
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength of peak intensity.
- $T$ is the absolute temperature (in Kelvin).
- $b$ is Wien's displacement constant ($\approx 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}$).

**Analyzing the graph:**
- Because $\lambda$ is inversely proportional to $T$ (i.e., $\lambda \propto \frac{1}{T}$), as the temperature $T$ increases, the peak wavelength $\lambda$ must decrease.
- A graph showing an inverse relationship between two variables where one is a constant divided by the other results in a **rectangular hyperbola** that never touches either axis (as $T \to \infty$, $\lambda \to 0$, and as $T \to 0$, $\lambda \to \infty$).

Comparing this to the options:
- **Graph A** represents an inverse relationship ($\lambda \propto \frac{1}{T}$), which correctly depicts the curve of a rectangular hyperbola.
- **Graph B** represents a direct power relationship (like a parabola).
- **Graph C** represents a linear inverse relationship, which is incorrect because the relationship is not linear.
- **Graph D** represents a direct linear relationship ($\lambda \propto T$), which is incorrect.

Therefore, Graph A is the only correct representation.
