### Front
How does the binding energy per nucleon change during the gamma decay of a metastable isotope (e.g., $^{192m}\text{Ir} \rightarrow ^{192}\text{Ir} + \gamma$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Binding energy per nucleon remains unchanged because the number of protons and neutrons remains constant and the nucleus does not undergo transmutation.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When distinguishing between gamma decay (internal energy level transition) and beta/alpha decay (which change nucleon composition/binding energy).
- Common trap: Assuming the release of gamma radiation implies a change in the binding energy per nucleon; energy release is due to the transition between excited and ground states, not a change in the nucleus's fundamental binding configuration.
