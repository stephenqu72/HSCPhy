### Front
How does the magnitude of centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) change for a point on a tire rotating at a constant rate, and what would its graphical representation vs. time look like?

### Back
- Key idea: Since angular velocity ($\omega$) and radius ($r$) are constant, $a_c$ is constant over time.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_c = r\omega^2$; represented by a horizontal line (constant graph).
- When to use it: To determine acceleration for circular motion with uniform rotation.
- Common trap: Assuming $a_c$ changes because the direction of the acceleration vector changes (the *magnitude* remains constant even though the *direction* is centripetal).
