### Front
How must the force vectors be drawn in a free-body diagram for an object accelerating downwards through air?

### Back
- Key idea: The downward vector (Weight) must be visibly longer than the upward vector (Air Resistance) to represent an unbalanced net force.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{net} = ma$ (Newton’s Second Law).
- When to use it: Representing objects experiencing non-zero acceleration in free-body diagrams.
- Common trap: Drawing arrows of equal length, which incorrectly implies zero net force and constant velocity (terminal velocity).
