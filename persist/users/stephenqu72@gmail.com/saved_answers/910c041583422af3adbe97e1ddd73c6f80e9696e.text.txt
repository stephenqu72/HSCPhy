To solve this physics problem, we must apply the principles of electromagnetism, specifically the **Motor Effect**.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(B)**.

### Explanation
To understand why (B) is correct, we need to analyze the relationship between the potential difference, current, and the magnetic force acting on a conductor.

1.  **Ohm's Law:** When a potential difference ($V$) is applied across a conductor of resistance ($R$) in a closed circuit, a current ($I$) flows through it according to the equation:
    $$I = \frac{V}{R}$$
    As the potential difference ($V$) is gradually increased, the current ($I$) flowing through the conductor also increases proportionally.

2.  **The Motor Effect:** A current-carrying conductor placed in an external magnetic field ($B$) experiences a magnetic force ($F$). The magnitude of this force is given by:
    $$F = BIL \sin(\theta)$$
    Where:
    *   $B$ is the magnetic field strength.
    *   $I$ is the current.
    *   $L$ is the length of the conductor.
    *   $\theta$ is the angle between the conductor and the magnetic field (which is $90^\circ$ in this case, meaning $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$).

3.  **Synthesis:** Since the force $F$ is directly proportional to the current $I$ ($F \propto I$), and the current $I$ is directly proportional to the potential difference $V$ ($I \propto V$), it follows that as the potential difference is increased, the current increases, leading to a **gradually increasing magnetic force** acting on the conductor.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **(A):** While the conductor will move due to the force, it will move in one direction (governed by the Right-Hand Rule), not "back and forth" unless the current direction is being alternated.
*   **(C) and (D):** While induction can occur in dynamic situations (e.g., changing magnetic flux), the primary effect described here is the direct application of a force due to an external potential difference causing a current to flow through a magnetic field.
