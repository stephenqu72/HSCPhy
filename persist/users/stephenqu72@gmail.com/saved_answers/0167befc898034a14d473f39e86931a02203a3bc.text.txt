**Step 2: Use the classical formula for time**
Since the Earth observers are measuring the time it takes for the object to cover a distance in their own frame, we simply use the standard relationship:
$$t = \frac{d}{v}$$

**Step 3: Calculate the time in years**
$$t = \frac{3 \text{ light years}}{0.95c}$$
Since a light year is defined as the distance light travels in one year ($c \times 1 \text{ year}$):
$$t = \frac{3 \times c \times 1 \text{ year}}{0.95c}$$
$$t = \frac{3}{0.95} \text{ years}$$
$$t \approx 3.15789 \text{ years}$$

**Step 4: Convert years to days**
To convert the time into days, we multiply by the number of days in a year (using 365.25 days to account for leap years, or 365 for standard calculation):
$$t_{\text{days}} = 3.15789 \times 365.25 \approx 1153.37 \text{ days}$$
