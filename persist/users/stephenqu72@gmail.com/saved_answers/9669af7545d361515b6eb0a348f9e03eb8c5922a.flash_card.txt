### Front
What physical assumption must be re-evaluated when performing projectile motion calculations for altitudes above 100 km?

### Back
- Key idea: The Earth’s gravitational field strength ($g$) is not constant at high altitudes.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$ (Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation).
- When to use it: Whenever the altitude is significant relative to the Earth's radius (typically $>100\text{ km}$).
- Common trap: Assuming $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$ remains constant; for large displacements, the curvature of the Earth and atmospheric drag also become non-negligible variables.
