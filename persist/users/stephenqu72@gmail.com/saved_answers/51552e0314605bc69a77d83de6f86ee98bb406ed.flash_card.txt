### Front
Define gravitational field strength ($g$) and state its SI units.

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational field strength is the gravitational force acting per unit mass at a specific point.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{F_g}{m}$ (units: $N/kg$ or $m/s^2$).
- When to use it: Whenever calculating the force of gravity on an object or describing the intensity of a gravitational field.
- Common trap: Confusing gravitational field strength ($N/kg$) with weight (a force measured in $N$) or pressure ($N/m^2$).
