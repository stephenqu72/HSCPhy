To solve this problem, we use the formula for constructive interference in Young’s double-slit experiment:

$$d \sin(\theta) = n\lambda$$

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   **Wavelength ($\lambda$):** $621\text{ nm} = 621 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$
*   **Slit separation ($d$):** $100\text{ }\mu\text{m} = 100 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m} = 1.0 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m}$
*   **Order ($n$):** We are looking for the $2^{\text{nd}}$ bright spot, so $n = 2$.
*   **Screen distance ($L$):** $1.50\text{ m}$ (Note: This is provided as extra information, as the question asks for the *angle*, which is independent of screen distance in the path difference formula).

### 2. Rearrange the formula:
We need to solve for the angle $\theta$:
$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{n\lambda}{d}$$
$$\theta = \arcsin\left(\frac{n\lambda}{d}\right)$$

### 3. Substitute the values:
$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{2 \times (621 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m})}{1.0 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m}}$$
$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{1242 \times 10^{-9}}{10^{-4}}$$
$$\sin(\theta) = 1.242 \times 10^{-2} = 0.01242$$

Now, take the inverse sine:
$$\theta = \arcsin(0.01242)$$
$$\theta \approx 0.7119^\circ$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $0.71^\circ$.
