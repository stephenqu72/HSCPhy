### Front
What does a linear graph of recessional velocity ($v$) versus distance ($d$) for distant galaxies represent in cosmology?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The universe is undergoing a uniform expansion (Hubble's Law).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v = H_0 d$ (Hubble's Law); $H_0$ is the Hubble constant.
- **When to use it:** To calculate the age of the universe ($t \approx 1/H_0$) or determine the distance/velocity of galaxies.
- **Common trap:** Confusing this linear "uniform expansion" with *accelerated* expansion (dark energy), which requires non-linear data from Type Ia supernovae.
