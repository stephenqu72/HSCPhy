### Explanation
To solve this question, we must understand the fundamental definition of a **hypothesis** within the scientific method:

*   **What is a hypothesis?** It is a proposed explanation for a phenomenon. Its defining characteristic is that it must be **falsifiable** or **testable**. In science, we formulate a hypothesis before conducting an experiment to see if the data supports or refutes it.
*   **Why option A is correct:** A hypothesis is essentially a "tentative" or "proposed" explanation that must be able to be subjected to experimental testing.
*   **Why the other options are incorrect:**
    *   **B:** A "well-tested" explanation that has gained broad acceptance is more accurately described as a **theory** or a **scientific law**. A hypothesis has not yet been "well-tested."
    *   **C:** Science is based on empirical evidence, not on the authority of the individual proposing the idea. A statement is not a hypothesis simply because a famous person said it.
    *   **D:** Popular belief or plausibility does not define a hypothesis. Many plausible-sounding ideas are not scientific hypotheses because they cannot be tested or falsified.

In summary, the core requirement of a hypothesis is its **testability**.
