To calculate the magnitude of the torque, we use the formula:

$$\tau = r \cdot F \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
*   $r$ is the distance from the pivot to the point where the force is applied (the length of the spanner).
*   $F$ is the magnitude of the applied force.
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the position vector ($r$) and the force vector ($F$).

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   The length of the spanner $r = 10 \text{ cm}$. We must convert this to standard SI units (meters) for torque calculation: $r = 0.1 \text{ m}$.
    *   The applied force $F = 250 \text{ N}$.
    *   The angle $\theta$: Looking at the diagram, the force is applied vertically. The spanner makes an angle of $30^\circ$ with the vertical. Therefore, the angle between the spanner and the vertical force vector is $30^\circ$.

2.  **Calculate the Torque:**
    $$\tau = 0.1 \text{ m} \times 250 \text{ N} \times \sin(30^\circ)$$

3.  **Evaluate:**
    *   We know that $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$.
    *   $\tau = 0.1 \times 250 \times 0.5$
    *   $\tau = 25 \times 0.5$
    *   $\tau = 12.5 \text{ Nm}$

Rounding to the nearest whole number as presented in the options, we get $13 \text{ Nm}$.

The correct option is **(A)**.
