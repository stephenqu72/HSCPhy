To solve this problem, we apply the principle of **Conservation of Mechanical Energy**. Since the rocket is moving away from the planet with its engine off, the total mechanical energy (sum of kinetic and gravitational potential energy) remains constant between Point 1 and Point 2.

### Given Data:
- Mass of the planet ($M$) = $8.6 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$
- Gravitational constant ($G$) = $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{ kg}^{-2}$
- Point 1: $r_1 = 4.3 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$, $v_1 = 5500 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
- Point 2: $r_2 = ?$, $v_2 = 2900 \text{ m s}^{-1}$

### Calculation:
The law of conservation of energy states:
$$E_{k1} + E_{p1} = E_{k2} + E_{p2}$$
$$\frac{1}{2}mv_1^2 - \frac{GMm}{r_1} = \frac{1}{2}mv_2^2 - \frac{GMm}{r_2}$$

We can cancel the mass of the rocket ($m$) from all terms:
$$\frac{1}{2}v_1^2 - \frac{GM}{r_1} = \frac{1}{2}v_2^2 - \frac{GM}{r_2}$$

Rearranging to solve for the potential energy term at Point 2:
$$\frac{GM}{r_2} = \frac{1}{2}v_2^2 - \frac{1}{2}v_1^2 + \frac{GM}{r_1}$$

Substituting the values:
1. $\frac{1}{2}(2900)^2 = 4,205,000$
2. $\frac{1}{2}(5500)^2 = 15,125,000$
3. $\frac{GM}{r_1} = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(8.6 \times 10^{23})}{4.3 \times 10^6} \approx 13,340,000$

Now plug these into the equation:
$$\frac{GM}{r_2} = 4,205,000 - 15,125,000 + 13,340,000$$
$$\frac{GM}{r_2} = 2,420,000$$

Solve for $r_2$:
$$r_2 = \frac{GM}{2,420,000} = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(8.6 \times 10^{23})}{2,420,000}$$
$$r_2 \approx 2.37 \times 10^7 \text{ m}$$

**Answer:** The rocket's distance from the planet's centre at Point 2 is approximately $2.37 \times 10^7 \text{ m}$.

### Explanation of Steps:
1. **Identify the Principle:** Recognize that in a gravitational field without external forces, the total energy of the rocket is conserved.
2. **Set up the Energy Equation:** Use the kinetic energy formula ($\frac{1}{2}mv^2$) and the gravitational potential energy formula ($-\frac{GMm}{r}$).
3. **Algebraic Manipulation:** Cancel the mass of the rocket ($m$) and rearrange the equation to isolate the unknown distance $r_2$.
4. **Substitution and Computation:** Calculate the values for each term carefully, ensuring you use the correct scientific notation, and solve for $r_2$.
