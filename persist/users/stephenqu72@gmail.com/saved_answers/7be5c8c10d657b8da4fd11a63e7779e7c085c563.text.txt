### Answer
The correct answer is **D. diffraction and interference.**

### Explanation
To understand why this happens, we have to look at the wave nature of light:

1.  **Diffraction:** According to Huygens' principle, every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary wavelets. As the light wave encounters the edges of the circular metal disc, the light waves "bend" around the obstacle. This bending of waves around corners or obstacles is the phenomenon of **diffraction**.
2.  **Interference:** Because the obstacle is circular, the light diffracting around all points of the disc's circumference travels an equal distance to reach the very center point on the screen. Because these light waves have travelled equal distances, they arrive at the center in phase with one another. When waves arrive in phase, they undergo **constructive interference**, resulting in the bright spot observed.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **Refraction** involves light changing speed and bending as it passes from one medium to another (like glass or water), which is not the primary cause of this phenomenon.
*   **Polarisation** refers to the orientation of the oscillations of the light wave and is not required for the formation of Poisson's spot.

In the context of the HSC syllabus, this experiment is a crucial piece of evidence that supports the **wave model of light** over the particle model, as particles would simply be blocked by the disc, creating a dark center.
