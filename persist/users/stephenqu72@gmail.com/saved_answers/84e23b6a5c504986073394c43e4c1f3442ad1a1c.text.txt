### Part (a)
**Answer:** The primary evidence was the observation of **redshift** in the light spectra of distant galaxies. Astronomers observed that the light from these galaxies was shifted toward the longer (red) wavelengths compared to light from stationary sources. This indicated that galaxies were moving away from us. When Edwin Hubble combined this with distance measurements, he observed a linear relationship known as **Hubble’s Law** ($v = H_0d$), which showed that the speed of recession increases with distance, providing evidence for a uniformly expanding universe.

**How to solve:** To get full marks (2), you must mention:
1. The observation of galactic redshift (Doppler effect).
2. The interpretation that galaxies are receding from us, implying an expanding space-time continuum.

### Part (b)
**Answer:** Hubble's work revolutionized our knowledge in two major ways:
1. **The Extragalactic Nature of Nebulae:** Before Hubble, many believed the universe was limited to the Milky Way galaxy. By using Cepheid variable stars to measure the distance to the Andromeda "nebula," Hubble proved it was far outside our galaxy, demonstrating that our galaxy is just one of many in a vast universe.
2. **The Expansion of the Universe:** By establishing the relationship between distance ($d$) and recession velocity ($v$), Hubble provided the empirical foundation for the Big Bang theory. His work confirmed that the universe is not static (as previously assumed by Einstein and others), but dynamic and expanding, which allows us to calculate the age of the universe.

**How to solve:** To get full marks (3), you need to demonstrate an understanding of the paradigm shift:
1. Mention the discovery that "nebulae" were actually separate galaxies (showing the universe is much larger than previously thought).
2. Explain the discovery of the expansion (Hubble’s Law).
3. Connect these findings to a broader shift in scientific understanding—moving from a static, single-galaxy universe to an expanding, multi-galactic one.
