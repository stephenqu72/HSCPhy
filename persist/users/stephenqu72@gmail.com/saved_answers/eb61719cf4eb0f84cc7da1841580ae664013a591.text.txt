### Explanation
To solve this, we need to recall **Hubble's Law**, which is a fundamental pillar of modern cosmology.

1.  **Hubble's Law:** Edwin Hubble observed that galaxies are moving away from Earth (and from each other) at velocities that are directly proportional to their distances. This is expressed mathematically as:
    $$v = H_0 d$$
    Where:
    - $v$ is the recessional velocity of the galaxy.
    - $d$ is the distance to the galaxy.
    - $H_0$ is the Hubble constant.

2.  **Analysis of Options:**
    *   **Option A:** Since $v$ is proportional to $d$ ($v \propto d$), this statement is correct.
    *   **Option B:** This incorrectly suggests an inverse relationship, which contradicts Hubble's observed expansion of the universe.
    *   **Options C and D:** These refer to the **Tully-Fisher relation**, which relates a spiral galaxy's rotational velocity to its luminosity. While this is a significant astrophysical principle, it is not "Hubble's discovery" regarding the expansion of the universe. Therefore, Option A is the intended answer in the context of Hubble’s primary work on the recessional velocity of galaxies.
