### Explanation
To find the final speed of the proton, we apply the principle of **conservation of energy**. When a charged particle is accelerated through a potential difference $V$, the electrical potential energy lost is converted entirely into kinetic energy.

1.  **Electrical Potential Energy ($E_p$):**
    The work done on a charge $e$ moving through a potential difference $V$ is given by:
    $$E_p = eV$$

2.  **Kinetic Energy ($E_k$):**
    The kinetic energy of a mass $m$ moving at speed $v$ is given by:
    $$E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

3.  **Equating Energy:**
    Assuming the proton starts from rest (initial kinetic energy is zero), we equate the electrical potential energy to the final kinetic energy:
    $$eV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

4.  **Solving for $v$:**
    Multiply both sides by $2$ to isolate the $mv^2$ term:
    $$2eV = mv^2$$
    Divide both sides by $m$:
    $$v^2 = \frac{2eV}{m}$$
    Finally, take the square root of both sides to find the speed $v$:
    $$v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m}}$$

This matches option **A**.
