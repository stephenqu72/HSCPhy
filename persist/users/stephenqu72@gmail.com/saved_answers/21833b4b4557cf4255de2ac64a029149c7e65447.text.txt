To determine the correct answer, we must identify the wave characteristics of sound and light:

1.  **Sound waves:** Sound is a mechanical wave that requires a medium (like air) to travel. Particles in the medium oscillate parallel to the direction of energy propagation. This characteristic identifies sound as a **longitudinal wave**.
2.  **Visible light waves:** Light is an electromagnetic wave. It does not require a medium and consists of oscillating electric and magnetic fields that are perpendicular to the direction of energy propagation. This characteristic identifies light as a **transverse wave**.

Comparing these facts to the options provided:
*   A is incorrect because sound is not transverse.
*   B is incorrect because light is not longitudinal.
*   C is incorrect because it swaps the classifications.
*   **D is correct** because it accurately labels sound as longitudinal and light as transverse.

### Answer
The correct option is **D**.

### Explanation
To solve this, recall the fundamental differences between wave types in the HSC Physics syllabus:
*   **Longitudinal waves:** Particle displacement is parallel to the direction of wave propagation (e.g., sound waves, which consist of compressions and rarefactions).
*   **Transverse waves:** Particle or field displacement is perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation (e.g., all electromagnetic waves, including visible light).

Since sound relies on the vibration of medium particles in the direction of the sound's path, and light is an electromagnetic wave where fields oscillate perpendicularly to the direction of travel, only statement **D** is physically correct.
