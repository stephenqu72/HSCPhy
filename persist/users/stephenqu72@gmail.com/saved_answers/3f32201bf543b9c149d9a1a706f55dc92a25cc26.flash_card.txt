### Front
How does the required speed $v$ change if the radius $r$ of a curve is doubled while maintaining a constant centripetal acceleration ($a_c$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Since $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$, if $a_c$ is constant, $v$ is directly proportional to $\sqrt{r}$.
- **Formula:** $v_2 = v_1 \sqrt{\frac{r_2}{r_1}}$. For $r_2 = 2r_1$, then $v_2 = v_1 \sqrt{2}$.
- **When to use it:** Problems involving uniform circular motion where centripetal force requirements are linked to changing track geometry.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units (e.g., $\text{km h}^{-1}$ to $\text{m s}^{-1}$) before calculating banking angles or force magnitudes.
