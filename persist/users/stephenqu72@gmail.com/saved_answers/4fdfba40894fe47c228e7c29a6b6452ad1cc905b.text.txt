### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a) Derivation
To derive Kepler’s Third Law, we equate the gravitational force providing the centripetal force for a satellite in a circular orbit:

1.  **Gravitational Force:** $F_g = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$
2.  **Centripetal Force:** $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
3.  Equating them: $\frac{GMm}{r^2} = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
4.  Simplify to find $v^2$: $v^2 = \frac{GM}{r}$
5.  Since the satellite travels a circumference $2\pi r$ in period $T$, $v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$. Substituting this into the equation:
    $$\left(\frac{2\pi r}{T}\right)^2 = \frac{GM}{r}$$
    $$\frac{4\pi^2 r^2}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{r}$$
    $$\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$$

#### Part (b) Calculation
To find the mass $M$ of the planet, we determine the gradient of the graph, where $T^2$ is on the $y$-axis and $r^3$ is on the $x$-axis. From the formula, the gradient $k = \frac{T^2}{r^3} = \frac{4\pi^2}{GM}$.

1.  **Calculate the gradient ($m$):**
    Using points $(100 \times 10^{12}, 0.075)$ and $(400 \times 10^{12}, 0.315)$:
    $$k = \frac{0.315 - 0.075}{(400 - 100) \times 10^{12}} = \frac{0.24}{300 \times 10^{12}} = 8 \times 10^{-16} \text{ days}^2/\text{km}^3$$
2.  **Convert units:** 
    $T$ is in days. Convert to seconds: $1 \text{ day} = 86,400 \text{ s}$. 
    So, $1 \text{ day}^2 = (86,400)^2 \approx 7.465 \times 10^9 \text{ s}^2$.
    $r$ is in km. Convert to meters: $1 \text{ km}^3 = 10^9 \text{ m}^3$.
    $$k = (8 \times 10^{-16}) \times \frac{7.465 \times 10^9}{10^9} \approx 5.972 \times 10^{-15} \text{ s}^2/\text{m}^3$$
3.  **Calculate Mass ($M$):**
    Since $k = \frac{4\pi^2}{GM}$, then $M = \frac{4\pi^2}{Gk}$:
    $$M = \frac{4 \times \pi^2}{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.972 \times 10^{-15})}$$
    $$M \approx \frac{39.478}{3.983 \times 10^{-25}} \approx 9.9 \times 10^{25} \text{ kg}$$
