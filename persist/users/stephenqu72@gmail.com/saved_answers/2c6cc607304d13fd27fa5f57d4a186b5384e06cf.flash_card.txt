### Front
Calculate the centripetal force ($F_c$) for a $800\text{ kg}$ car travelling at $40\text{ m s}^{-1}$ around a curve with a radius of $80\text{ m}$, and state the force responsible.

### Back
- Key idea: Net force directed towards the centre is required for circular motion.
- Formula / law / constant: $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$; Static friction provides this force.
- When to use it: Any object changing direction while moving at a constant speed in a circular path.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the force is provided by static friction, not "centrifugal" force (which is non-existent).
- Answer: $16,000\text{ N}$ directed towards the centre.
