To find the magnitude of the force acting on the conductors, we use the formula for the force between two parallel current-carrying wires:

$$F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 L}{2 \pi r}$$

### 1. Identify the given values:
*   Permeability of free space: $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \, \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A}$
*   Current 1: $I_1 = 4.2 \, \text{A}$
*   Current 2: $I_2 = 6.8 \, \text{A}$
*   Length of conductors: $L = 5.9 \, \text{m}$
*   Separation distance: $r = 19 \, \text{cm} = 0.19 \, \text{m}$

### 2. Substitute the values into the formula:
$$F = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times 4.2 \times 6.8 \times 5.9}{2 \pi \times 0.19}$$

### 3. Simplify and calculate:
First, cancel out the $2\pi$ from the numerator and denominator:
$$F = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 4.2 \times 6.8 \times 5.9}{0.19}$$

Now, multiply the values in the numerator:
$$4.2 \times 6.8 \times 5.9 = 168.504$$
$$F = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 168.504}{0.19}$$
$$F = \frac{337.008 \times 10^{-7}}{0.19}$$
$$F \approx 1773.7 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{N}$$
$$F \approx 1.77 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{N}$$
