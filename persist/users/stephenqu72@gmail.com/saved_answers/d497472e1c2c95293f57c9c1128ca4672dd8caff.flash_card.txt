### Front
How do particle accelerators provide evidence for the Standard Model?

### Back
- **Key idea:** High-energy collisions convert kinetic energy into mass, creating short-lived particles and confirming predicted force-mediators (bosons) and sub-atomic structures.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $E = mc^2$ (Energy-mass equivalence).
- **When to use it:** When asked to explain the role of accelerators in experimental physics or the validation of modern particle theory.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to mention specific examples (e.g., Higgs boson, $W/Z$ bosons, or quarks). Don't just mention the accelerator; link the machine's energy to the creation of matter.
