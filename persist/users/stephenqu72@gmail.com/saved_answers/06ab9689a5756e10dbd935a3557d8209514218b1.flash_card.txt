### Front
Rank alpha, beta, and gamma radiation by their ionising ability and penetrating power.

### Back
- Key idea: Ionising ability is inversely proportional to penetrating power.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Ionising: $\alpha > \beta > \gamma$; Penetrating: $\gamma > \beta > \alpha$.
- When to use it: To compare the safety risks, shielding requirements, or interaction of radiation with matter.
- Common trap: Confusing "ionising ability" (interaction strength) with "penetrating power" (ability to pass through materials).
