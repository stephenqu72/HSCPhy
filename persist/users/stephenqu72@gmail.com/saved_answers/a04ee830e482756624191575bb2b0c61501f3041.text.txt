### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) the discovery of the nucleus.**

### Explanation
This diagram illustrates the famous **Geiger-Marsden experiment** (often referred to as the **Rutherford Gold Foil experiment**), conducted in 1909 under the direction of Ernest Rutherford.

1.  **The Setup:** A radioactive source (Polonium, which emits $\alpha$-particles) is directed at a very thin sheet of gold foil. The zinc sulfide screen acts as a detector, producing a tiny flash of light whenever an $\alpha$-particle hits it.
2.  **The Observations:** Most $\alpha$-particles passed straight through the foil, as expected by the "Plum Pudding" model of the atom prevalent at the time. However, a small fraction of the particles were deflected at large angles, and a very small number were reflected directly backward.
3.  **The Conclusion:** Rutherford reasoned that since $\alpha$-particles are positively charged and relatively massive, the only way they could be repelled backwards is if they encountered a dense, positively charged concentration of mass within the atom. This led to the rejection of the Plum Pudding model and the proposal of the **nuclear model of the atom**, where most of the atom's mass and its positive charge are concentrated in a tiny central volume called the **nucleus**.
