To determine what happens to the interference fringes when the screen is moved further away, we refer to the formula for fringe separation in a double-slit experiment:

$$\Delta x = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$

Where:
*   $\Delta x$ is the fringe separation (the distance between adjacent bright fringes).
*   $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the light used.
*   $L$ is the distance from the slits to the screen.
*   $d$ is the distance between the two slits.

### Explanation:
1.  **Analyze the relationship:** The formula shows a direct proportionality between the fringe separation $\Delta x$ and the screen distance $L$. Because $\Delta x \propto L$, as the screen distance ($L$) increases, the fringe separation ($\Delta x$) must also increase.
2.  **Conceptual understanding:** As the light rays travel a longer distance to reach the screen, the small angle of divergence between the rays results in a larger linear separation between the constructive interference points on the screen.
3.  **Conclusion:** Since the fringes become more widely spaced as the screen is moved further away, they are "further apart."

Therefore, the correct option is **(B)**.
