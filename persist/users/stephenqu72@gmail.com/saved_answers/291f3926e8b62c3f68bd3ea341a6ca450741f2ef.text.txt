### Answer

James Chadwick confirmed the existence of the neutron (the particle Rutherford predicted) by bombarding a beryllium target with alpha particles ($\alpha$), which produced a neutral radiation. When this radiation struck a paraffin wax target, it ejected protons. By applying the laws of **conservation of momentum** and **conservation of energy** to the collisions between the unknown radiation and the protons, Chadwick was able to calculate the mass of the unknown particle. He demonstrated that the mass of this neutral particle was approximately equal to that of a proton, thereby confirming the existence of the neutron.

2.  **The Interaction:** Explain that this radiation was directed at hydrogen-rich material (paraffin wax), which resulted in the ejection of protons.
3.  **The Conservation Laws:** This is the core of the question. Explain that Chadwick analyzed the collision between the unknown neutral particle and the protons. By treating the collision as an elastic scattering event, he used:
    *   **Conservation of Momentum:** $p_{initial} = p_{final}$
    *   **Conservation of Kinetic Energy:** $E_{k(initial)} = E_{k(final)}$
4.  **The Conclusion:** Explain that solving these simultaneous equations for the mass of the unknown particle showed it had a mass nearly identical to the proton, proving it was a neutral nucleon (the neutron).
