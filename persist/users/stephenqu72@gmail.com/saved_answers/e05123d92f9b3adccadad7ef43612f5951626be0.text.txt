The correct answer is **B. Cathode rays travel in straight lines.**

### Explanation:
This specific apparatus is known as a **Maltese Cross tube**. It is a classic experiment used to investigate the properties of cathode rays (which were later identified as a stream of electrons). 

1.  **Observation:** Inside the vacuum tube, there is a metal Maltese cross positioned in the path of the cathode rays. When the high voltage is applied, the cathode rays are emitted from the cathode, travel across the tube, and strike the fluorescent screen at the end.
2.  **The Shadow:** Because the Maltese cross acts as an obstruction, it prevents the cathode rays from reaching the fluorescent screen directly behind it. This creates a sharp, well-defined shadow of the cross on the screen.
3.  **The Conclusion:** The formation of a sharp shadow is direct evidence that the rays travel in straight lines. If they did not travel in straight lines (for example, if they curved or scattered significantly), the shadow would be distorted or non-existent.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **A:** While cathode rays do possess momentum (demonstrated by paddle-wheel tubes), this specific apparatus shows the path of the rays, not their ability to exert force.
*   **C and D:** While it is true that cathode rays are charged particles and are indeed deflected by electric and magnetic fields, this particular experimental setup does not contain external deflection plates or magnets to demonstrate these properties.
