To increase the lifting capacity of a DC motor, we must increase the **motor torque** ($\tau$). The torque generated by a DC motor is given by the formula:

$$\tau = nIAB \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
*   $n$ = number of turns in the coil
*   $I$ = current in the coil
*   $A$ = area of the coil
*   $B$ = magnetic field strength
*   $\theta$ = angle between the normal to the coil and the magnetic field

Based on this principle, here are 5 modifications to increase the lifting capacity:

### Answer: 5 Modifications
1.  **Increase the number of windings ($n$):** Increasing the number of turns in the coil directly increases the total torque produced by the motor.
2.  **Increase the current ($I$):** By increasing the voltage of the power supply or using a power source with lower internal resistance, you increase the current flowing through the coil, which increases the magnetic force on the wires.
3.  **Increase the magnetic field strength ($B$):** Replace the current magnets with stronger permanent magnets (e.g., Neodymium magnets) or bring the magnets closer to the rotor to reduce the air gap.
4.  **Increase the area of the coil ($A$):** Using a larger frame for the cardboard cube (increasing the coil area) will increase the torque, as torque is directly proportional to the area.
5.  **Use a ferromagnetic core:** Inserting a soft iron core inside the cardboard cube will concentrate the magnetic flux lines through the coil, significantly increasing the effective magnetic field strength ($B$) acting on the windings.
