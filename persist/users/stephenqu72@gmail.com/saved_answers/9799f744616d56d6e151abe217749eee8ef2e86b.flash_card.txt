### Front
When a projectile is launched with an initial downward angle, how do you correctly define the initial vertical velocity component ($v_y$) and the displacement ($y$) for projectile motion calculations?

### Back
- Key idea: Vector components and sign conventions must be consistent with the coordinate system.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v_y = -v \sin\theta$; $g = -9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$.
- When to use it: Whenever a projectile is launched at an angle relative to the horizontal.
- Common trap: Forgetting that a downward launch angle makes the initial vertical velocity ($v_y$) and vertical displacement ($y$) negative relative to the launch point.
