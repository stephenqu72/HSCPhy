### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of **diffraction**, which is the bending of waves around obstacles or through openings.

1.  **The Physics of Diffraction:** The degree of diffraction (how much the wave spreads out after passing through a gap) is determined by the ratio of the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the wave to the width of the gap ($w$).
2.  **The Relationship:** The extent of diffraction is inversely proportional to the width of the gap. Mathematically, for a single slit, the first minimum of diffraction occurs at an angle $\theta$ given by:
    $$\sin(\theta) \approx \frac{\lambda}{w}$$
    (where $\theta$ is half the angular spread).
3.  **Applying the logic:** 
    *   If you **narrow the gap** ($w$ decreases), the ratio $\frac{\lambda}{w}$ increases.
    *   As $\frac{\lambda}{w}$ increases, the angle $\theta$ must also increase.
    *   This means the waves will spread out more significantly, resulting in a wider angular spread.
4.  **Wavelength:** Changing the physical gap width does not change the properties of the wave itself (such as its frequency or speed). Therefore, the wavelength remains constant. This allows us to immediately discard options (C) and (D).

In summary, as the gap size $w$ gets smaller, the waves are forced to bend more as they emerge, leading to a greater angular spread of the wave pattern.
