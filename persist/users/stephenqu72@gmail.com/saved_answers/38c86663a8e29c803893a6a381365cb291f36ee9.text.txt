### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) $1.0c$**.

---

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to apply Einstein’s **Second Postulate of Special Relativity**.

1.  **The Principle:** Einstein’s second postulate states that the speed of light in a vacuum, denoted by $c$, is constant for all inertial observers, regardless of the motion of the source or the motion of the observer.
2.  **Application:** Even though the spacecraft is moving at a significant fraction of the speed of light ($0.8c$) relative to the Earth, the laser beam (light) is emitted at speed $c$ from the perspective of the observer on the spacecraft. According to the theory of relativity, when the scientist on Earth measures the speed of that same laser beam, they will also measure it as exactly $c$.
3.  **Why others are incorrect:** In classical (Galilean) mechanics, you might be tempted to add the velocities together ($0.8c + 1.0c = 1.8c$), but this does not apply to electromagnetic radiation. In Special Relativity, the speed of light is the universal speed limit and remains invariant. Therefore, $1.0c$ is the only scientifically accurate result.
