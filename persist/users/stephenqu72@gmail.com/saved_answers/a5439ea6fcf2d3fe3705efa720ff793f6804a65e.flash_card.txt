### Front
Does the mass of a satellite affect its orbital period? State the underlying principle.

### Back
- **Key idea:** No; the period is independent of the satellite's mass because the satellite's mass ($m$) cancels out when equating centripetal force to gravitational force.
- **Formula / law / constant:** Kepler’s Third Law: $T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2r^3}{GM}$
- **When to use it:** When calculating orbital period or comparing the motion of satellites at the same radius.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the satellite's mass ($m$) with the central body's mass ($M$); only the central body's mass affects the orbital period.
