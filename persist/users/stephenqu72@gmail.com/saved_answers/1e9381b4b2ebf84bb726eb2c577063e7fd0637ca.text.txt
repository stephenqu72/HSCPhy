To solve this problem, we use Kepler's Third Law of planetary motion, which relates the orbital period $T$ and orbital radius $r$ to the mass of the central body $M$:

$$\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Convert units to SI (meters and seconds):**
*   **Radius ($r$):** $121,511\text{ km} = 121,511,000\text{ m} = 1.21511 \times 10^8\text{ m}$
*   **Period ($T$):** $12\text{ hours} = 12 \times 3600\text{ s} = 43,200\text{ s}$

**2. Rearrange the formula to solve for Mass ($M$):**
$$M = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{G T^2}$$
Where $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2/\text{kg}^2$.

**3. Substitute the values into the equation:**
$$M = \frac{4 \times \pi^2 \times (1.21511 \times 10^8)^3}{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (43,200)^2}$$

*   Numerator: $4 \times 9.8696 \times 1.7937 \times 10^{24} \approx 7.081 \times 10^{25}$
*   Denominator: $(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (1.86624 \times 10^9) \approx 1.2448 \times 10^{-1}$
*   $M \approx \frac{7.081 \times 10^{25}}{0.12448} \approx 5.688 \times 10^{26}\text{ kg}$

Rounding this to two significant figures, we get $5.7 \times 10^{26}\text{ kg}$.
