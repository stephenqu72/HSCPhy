### Answer

Geostationary satellites orbit at an altitude of approximately $35\,800\text{ km}$ directly above the equator, allowing them to remain fixed relative to a point on the Earth's surface.

**Uses:**
*   **Telecommunications and Broadcasting:** Because they remain in a fixed position relative to the ground, satellite dishes do not need to track the satellite, making them ideal for television broadcasting and point-to-point communication.
*   **Weather Monitoring:** They provide continuous monitoring of the same hemisphere, allowing for the real-time tracking of weather patterns and storm systems.

**Limitations:**
*   **Signal Latency:** The extreme altitude results in a significant time delay (latency) for signals to travel to and from the satellite, which is unsuitable for real-time voice calls or high-speed data requiring low latency.
*   **Limited Coverage:** Because they are locked to the equator, they have poor signal reception at very high latitudes (the polar regions).
