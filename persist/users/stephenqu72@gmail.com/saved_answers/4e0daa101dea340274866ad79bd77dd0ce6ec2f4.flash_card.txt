### Front
What is the acceleration of a projectile (ignoring air resistance) at its maximum height?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration is constant throughout the entire flight.
- Formula / law / constant: $a_x = 0$, $a_y = -g \approx -9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$.
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem to determine velocity or displacement at any point.
- Common trap: Assuming acceleration is zero at the peak because vertical velocity is zero. Acceleration is always $g$ downwards.
