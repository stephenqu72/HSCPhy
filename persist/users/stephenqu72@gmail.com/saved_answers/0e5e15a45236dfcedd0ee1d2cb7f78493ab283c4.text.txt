To solve this problem, we must recognise that when a charged particle enters a uniform magnetic field perpendicularly, the magnetic force acts as the centripetal force required to maintain circular motion.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the governing equation:**
    The magnetic force ($F_B$) is given by $F_B = qvB$, and the centripetal force ($F_c$) is given by $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$. Setting these equal to each other:
    $$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

2.  **Rearrange for velocity ($v$):**
    By dividing both sides by $v$ and multiplying by $r/m$, we get:
    $$v = \frac{qBr}{m}$$

3.  **List the given values and convert to SI units:**
    *   Mass ($m$) = $6.692 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$
    *   Charge ($q$) = $1.6 \times 10^{-16}\text{ C}$
    *   Radius ($r$) = $10\text{ cm} = 0.10\text{ m}$
    *   Magnetic field strength ($B$) = $0.10\text{ T}$

4.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$v = \frac{(1.6 \times 10^{-16}\text{ C}) \times (0.10\text{ T}) \times (0.10\text{ m})}{6.692 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}}$$
    $$v = \frac{1.6 \times 10^{-18}}{6.692 \times 10^{-27}}$$
    $$v \approx 0.23909 \times 10^9\text{ m/s}$$
    $$v \approx 2.4 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$$
