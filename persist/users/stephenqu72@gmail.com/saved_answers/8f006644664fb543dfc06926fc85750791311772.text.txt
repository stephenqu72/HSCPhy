As an HSC Physics teacher, I will guide you through these fundamental cosmological concepts. Here are the answers and the strategy for approaching them.

### Answer Key

**a) Describe the main features of the Big Bang model (3 marks)**
The Big Bang model describes a universe that originated from a state of extremely high density and temperature, roughly $13.8$ billion years ago. Since that initial singularity, the universe has been continuously expanding and cooling. As it cooled, energy transitioned into matter, eventually allowing for the formation of subatomic particles, atomic nuclei (nucleosynthesis), and later, neutral atoms, stars, and galaxies.

**b) How does the red-shift of light from galaxies support the Big Bang model? (1 mark)**
Hubble’s Law observes that light from distant galaxies is red-shifted, meaning they are moving away from us. This indicates that the universe is expanding; if we "rewind" this expansion, it implies the universe originated from a single, dense point.

**c) Outline why the time was short and why nucleosynthesis did not reoccur (2 marks)**
Nucleosynthesis required extremely high temperatures and pressures to overcome the electrostatic repulsion between protons. As the universe expanded, it rapidly cooled and the density of matter decreased. Once the temperature dropped below the threshold required for fusion, the process ceased, and the expanding, thinning gas prevented the conditions for nucleosynthesis from ever occurring again.

**d) State features of the CMB radiation which are consistent with the Big Bang model (2 marks)**
The CMB radiation is observed as a nearly perfect black-body spectrum with a temperature of approximately $2.7$ K, which is consistent with the predicted "afterglow" of a cooling, expanding universe. Additionally, the CMB is highly uniform (isotropic) across the sky, providing evidence for the hot, dense state of the early universe.

### How to approach these questions
1.  **Understand the "Why" (Parts a & b):** For cosmology, always link your answer to the evolution of the universe over time. For red-shift, don't just say "galaxies move away"; connect it to the *expansion of space itself* over time.
2.  **Logic and Causality (Part c):** In HSC physics, "outline" questions require a logical progression. You must mention the *conditions* (high temp/density) and the *cause of change* (expansion leading to cooling/dilution).
3.  **Keyword recall (Part d):** Always link the CMB to the term "black-body radiation." Memorizing the temperature ($2.7$ K) and the term "isotropic" is essential for gaining full marks in this section.
