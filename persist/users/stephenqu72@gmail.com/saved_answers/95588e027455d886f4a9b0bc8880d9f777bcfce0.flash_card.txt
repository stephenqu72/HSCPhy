### Front
What is the general nuclear equation for alpha decay and how do the mass and atomic numbers change?

### Back
- Key idea: A heavy nucleus emits a helium-4 nucleus ($^4_2He$), resulting in the daughter nucleus having a lower mass and atomic number.
- Formula / law: ${}^{A}_{Z}X \rightarrow {}^{A-4}_{Z-2}Y + {}^{4}_{2}He$
- When to use it: To identify alpha decay reactions or calculate missing isotopes in a decay series.
- Common trap: Confusing alpha decay (emission of $^4_2He$) with beta decay (emission of $^0_{-1}e$) or nuclear fission (splitting of a nucleus).
