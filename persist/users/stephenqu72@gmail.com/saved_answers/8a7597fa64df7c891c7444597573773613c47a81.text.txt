### Answer
The correct answer is **(B) Interference and diffraction**.

### Explanation
To arrive at this answer, we need to identify the two distinct wave phenomena occurring in the diagram:

1.  **Diffraction:** As the plane wave fronts encounter the narrow gaps (slits) in the barrier, the waves do not simply pass straight through. Instead, they spread out into semicircular patterns on the other side. This bending of waves around an obstacle or through an opening is known as **diffraction**.
2.  **Interference:** Once the waves have diffracted through the slits, they overlap in the region to the right of the barrier. Where the circular wave fronts (the crests) meet, they interact with each other. This interaction creates regions of constructive interference (where the waves reinforce each other) and destructive interference (where the waves cancel each other out). This superposition of waves from multiple sources is known as **interference**.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **Dispersion** refers to the separation of light into its component colors (like a prism), which is not happening here.
*   **Refraction** involves the bending of light as it passes from one medium to another (e.g., air to glass) due to a change in speed. In this diagram, the waves remain in the same medium.

When you see a diagram showing plane waves passing through slits and creating a complex pattern of overlapping semicircular fronts, always look for **diffraction** (at the slit) and **interference** (where the diffracted waves overlap).
