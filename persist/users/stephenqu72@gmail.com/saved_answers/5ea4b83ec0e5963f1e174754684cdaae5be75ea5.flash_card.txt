### Front
Explain how pions traveling at relativistic speeds reach the Earth's surface despite their short half-life.

### Back
- Key idea: Time dilation; moving clocks run slower relative to a stationary observer, extending the particle's lifetime in the laboratory frame.
- Formula / law: $t_v = \gamma t_0$ and $d_{lab} = \gamma d_{proper}$
- When to use it: When solving problems involving relativistic particles (e.g., muons/pions) where measured distance or lifetime exceeds classical expectations.
- Common trap: Confusing proper time ($t_0$) with dilated time ($t_v$); ensure $\gamma \ge 1$ (the dilated time/distance must be larger).
