To answer this question effectively for an HSC Physics exam, you need to describe the common "Arago’s Disc" or "Rotating Magnet" experiment used to model an AC induction motor.

### Answer

**Experimental Procedure:**
1. Suspend a permanent magnet above a metal disc (e.g., aluminium or copper) that is free to rotate on a central pivot/bearing.
2. Quickly rotate the magnet around the central axis of the disc (e.g., by hand or using a motor).
3. Observe the motion of the metal disc.

**Results Observed:**
The metal disc begins to rotate in the same direction as the rotating magnet. As the magnet spins, its changing magnetic field induces eddy currents in the disc (according to Faraday’s Law and Lenz’s Law). These eddy currents create their own magnetic fields that oppose the change in magnetic flux, resulting in a force (Lorentz force) that drags the disc along with the rotating magnetic field, effectively creating torque.

### How to solve this question
1.  **Understand the syllabus requirement:** This question assesses your ability to recall a mandatory first-hand investigation. You must clearly link the practical procedure to the physics principle of electromagnetic induction.
2.  **Structure for 3 marks:**
    *   **Mark 1:** Describe the experimental setup (The apparatus used: Magnet + Disc + Pivot).
    *   **Mark 2:** Describe the action taken (Spinning the magnet).
    *   **Mark 3:** Explain the observation using physics terminology (Eddy currents, Lenz's Law, and torque).
3.  **Diagram Tip:** When providing the diagram, draw a top-down or side view showing the magnet, the axis of rotation, and an arrow indicating the direction of the rotating magnetic field. Ensure you label the disc and the magnet clearly.
4.  **Key Physics Terminology:** Examiners look for "Eddy currents" and "Lenz’s Law." Without these, it is difficult to achieve full marks, as they explain *why* the observation occurs.
