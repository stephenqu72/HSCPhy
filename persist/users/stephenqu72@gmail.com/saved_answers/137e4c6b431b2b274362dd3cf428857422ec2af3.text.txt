To find the magnitude of the force required to keep the carriage in uniform circular motion, we need to calculate the **centripetal force** ($F_c$) acting on the system consisting of the carriage and its occupant.

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   Mass of Sophie ($m_s$) = $65.00\text{ kg}$
*   Mass of the carriage ($m_c$) = $550.0\text{ kg}$
*   Total mass ($m_{total}$) = $m_s + m_c = 65.00 + 550.0 = 615.0\text{ kg}$
*   Radius of motion ($r$) = $15.00\text{ m}$
*   Frequency ($f$) = $10\text{ rotations per minute} = \frac{10}{60}\text{ Hz} = \frac{1}{6}\text{ Hz}$

### 2. Calculate the angular velocity ($\omega$):
$$\omega = 2\pi f = 2 \times \pi \times \left(\frac{1}{6}\right) = \frac{\pi}{3} \approx 1.0472\text{ rad/s}$$

### 3. Calculate the centripetal force ($F_c$):
The formula for centripetal force is:
$$F_c = m_{total} \times r \times \omega^2$$

Substitute the values:
$$F_c = 615.0 \times 15.00 \times \left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right)^2$$
$$F_c = 615.0 \times 15.00 \times \left(\frac{\pi^2}{9}\right)$$
$$F_c = 9225 \times 1.0966...$$
$$F_c \approx 10115.9\text{ N}$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number, we get $10116\text{ N}$.

Comparing this to the provided options, the correct answer is **(c)**.

### How to solve this problem:
1.  **System Identification:** Recognize that the arm must support the combined mass of the occupant and the carriage.
2.  **Unit Conversion:** Convert the rotational frequency from "rotations per minute" (RPM) to Hertz (rotations per second) by dividing by 60.
3.  **Formula Selection:** Use the circular motion force formula $F = mr\omega^2$. Alternatively, you could find the velocity ($v = 2\pi rf$) and use $F = \frac{mv^2}{r}$, which will yield the same result.
4.  **Calculation:** Plug in the total mass and the radius to determine the net centripetal force required for the uniform circular motion.
