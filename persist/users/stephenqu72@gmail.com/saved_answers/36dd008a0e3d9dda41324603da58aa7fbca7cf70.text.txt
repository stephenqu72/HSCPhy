### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a) Force Diagrams
To draw these correctly, always ensure the vectors originate from the centre of mass of the car.

**(i) No Friction:**
*   **Weight ($mg$):** A vertical arrow pointing straight down.
*   **Normal Force ($N$):** An arrow perpendicular to the surface of the track, pointing up and to the left (towards the centre of the turn).

**(ii) With Friction:**
*   **Weight ($mg$):** A vertical arrow pointing straight down.
*   **Normal Force ($N$):** An arrow perpendicular to the surface of the track, pointing up and to the left.
*   **Friction ($f$):** An arrow pointing **parallel to the track surface**, directed down the slope (assuming the car is at the design speed where it doesn't slide, the friction acts to supplement the centripetal force). *Note: If the car were moving faster than the design speed, friction would act down the slope; if slower, it would act up the slope. For a general representation at design speed, include it parallel to the track.*

---

#### Part (b) Calculation
To find the banking angle $\theta$ for a car on a frictionless surface, we use the standard banking formula derived from equating the centripetal force to the horizontal component of the normal force:

$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

**Given:**
*   $v = 18.0\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   $r = 125\text{ m}$
*   $g \approx 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$

**Calculation:**
1. Substitute the values into the formula:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{18.0^2}{125 \times 9.8}$$
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{324}{1225}$$
$$\tan(\theta) \approx 0.26449$$

2. Solve for $\theta$ by taking the inverse tangent:
$$\theta = \arctan(0.26449)$$
$$\theta \approx 14.8^\circ$$

The turn should be banked at approximately **$14.8^\circ$**.
