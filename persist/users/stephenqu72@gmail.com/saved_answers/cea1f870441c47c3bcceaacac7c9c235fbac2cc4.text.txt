*   **Explanation:** Comparing the absorption spectrum of Yopitrix to the known spectrum of Element A, we can see that all the spectral lines for Yopitrix have shifted towards the longer wavelength (red) end of the spectrum. This phenomenon is known as a **redshift**.
*   **Determining velocity:** The velocity of the star can be determined by measuring the shift in wavelength ($\Delta\lambda$) of the spectral lines. By comparing the observed wavelength ($\lambda_{obs}$) of a specific line to its known rest wavelength ($\lambda_{rest}$) from Element A, the velocity ($v$) can be calculated using the Doppler effect formula for non-relativistic speeds:
    $$\frac{v}{c} = \frac{\Delta\lambda}{\lambda_{rest}}$$
    where $c$ is the speed of light, $\Delta\lambda = \lambda_{obs} - \lambda_{rest}$, and $\frac{\Delta\lambda}{\lambda_{rest}}$ is the redshift parameter ($z$).
