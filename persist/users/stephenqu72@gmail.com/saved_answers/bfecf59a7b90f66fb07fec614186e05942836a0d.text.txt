To solve this problem, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, specifically the formula for gravitational field strength ($g$) at the surface of a spherical body:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

    *   Gravitational field strength ($g$) = $1.35 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$
    *   Radius ($r$) = $2575 \text{ km} = 2.575 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
    *   Gravitational constant ($G$) $\approx 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{ kg}^{-2}$

2.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for the mass ($M$):**
    $$M = \frac{g \cdot r^2}{G}$$

3.  **Substitute the values into the equation:**
    $$M = \frac{1.35 \times (2.575 \times 10^6)^2}{6.67 \times 10^{-11}}$$

4.  **Perform the calculation:**
    *   First, square the radius: $(2.575 \times 10^6)^2 \approx 6.63 \times 10^{12} \text{ m}^2$
    *   Multiply by $g$: $1.35 \times 6.63 \times 10^{12} \approx 8.95 \times 10^{12}$
    *   Divide by $G$: $\frac{8.95 \times 10^{12}}{6.67 \times 10^{-11}} \approx 1.34 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$
