To solve this projectile motion problem, we first convert the initial velocity into SI units:
$v = 90 \text{ km.h}^{-1} = \frac{90}{3.6} \text{ m.s}^{-1} = 25 \text{ m.s}^{-1}$.

### (a) How long is the ball in the air?
The time of flight $t$ for a projectile returning to the same vertical level is given by the formula:
$$t = \frac{2v \sin(\theta)}{g}$$
Using $v = 25 \text{ m.s}^{-1}$, $\theta = 40^\circ$, and $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m.s}^{-2}$:
$$t = \frac{2 \times 25 \times \sin(40^\circ)}{9.8}$$
$$t \approx \frac{50 \times 0.6428}{9.8} \approx 3.28 \text{ seconds}$$

### (b) How much further would the ball travel at $45^\circ$?
First, calculate the range $R$ for both angles using the formula:
$$R = \frac{v^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$

**For $40^\circ$:**
$$R_{40} = \frac{25^2 \times \sin(80^\circ)}{9.8} = \frac{625 \times 0.9848}{9.8} \approx 62.81 \text{ m}$$

**For $45^\circ$:**
$$R_{45} = \frac{25^2 \times \sin(90^\circ)}{9.8} = \frac{625 \times 1}{9.8} \approx 63.78 \text{ m}$$

**Difference in distance:**
$$\Delta R = R_{45} - R_{40} = 63.78 - 62.81 = 0.97 \text{ m}$$

The ball would travel approximately **0.97 metres** further.
