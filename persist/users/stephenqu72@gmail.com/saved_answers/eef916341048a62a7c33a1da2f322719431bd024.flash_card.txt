### Front
Explain why a planet’s velocity decreases as it moves from perihelion to aphelion in terms of work and energy.

### Back
- Key idea: The Sun's gravitational force acts at an obtuse angle to the displacement, doing negative work, which converts kinetic energy into gravitational potential energy.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Principle of Conservation of Mechanical Energy; Work-Energy Theorem ($W = \Delta KE$).
- When to use it: Any question regarding orbital speed variation or energy changes in elliptical orbits.
- Common trap: Stating that velocity changes because the distance increases, without identifying the *mechanism* (negative work/energy conversion) required for full marks.
