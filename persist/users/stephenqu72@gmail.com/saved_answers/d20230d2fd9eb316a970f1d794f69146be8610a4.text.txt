To determine the strength of the magnetic field, we use the formula for the magnetic force experienced by a moving charged particle:

$$F = qvB\sin(\theta)$$

### Given Information:
*   **Force ($F$):** $3.2 \times 10^{-14} \text{ N}$
*   **Velocity ($v$):** $2.0 \times 10^6 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   **Charge of a proton ($q$):** $1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$ (standard physical constant)
*   **Angle ($\theta$):** $90^\circ$ (since the proton moves perpendicular to the field, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$)

### Calculation Steps:
1.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for the magnetic field strength ($B$):**
    $$B = \frac{F}{qv\sin(\theta)}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values:**
    $$B = \frac{3.2 \times 10^{-14}}{(1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times (2.0 \times 10^6) \times 1}$$

3.  **Perform the multiplication in the denominator:**
    Denominator $= 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \times 2.0 \times 10^6 \approx 3.204 \times 10^{-13}$

4.  **Complete the division:**
    $$B = \frac{3.2 \times 10^{-14}}{3.204 \times 10^{-13}} \approx 0.099875 \text{ T}$$

Rounding this result, we get **$0.10 \text{ T}$**.

**Answer:** The correct option is **B**.
