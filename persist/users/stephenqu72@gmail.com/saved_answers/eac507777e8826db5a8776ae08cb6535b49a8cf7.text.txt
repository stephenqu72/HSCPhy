To answer these questions, we need to interpret the positions of specific star types on the Hertzsprung-Russell (H-R) diagram.

### Answers

**18. Which letter corresponds to a Sun-like star?**
*   **Answer: (B)**
*   **Explanation:** The Sun is a G2 main-sequence star. Looking at the H-R diagram, the diagonal band running from top-left to bottom-right is the main sequence. Point **B** lies directly on the main sequence in the region corresponding to a G-type spectral class and $1 L_{\odot}$, which defines a Sun-like star.

**19. Which letter corresponds to a blue supergiant?**
*   **Answer: (C)**
*   **Explanation:** Blue supergiants are hot (high surface temperature) and have very high luminosity. On the H-R diagram, these stars are found in the top-left region. Point **C** is located high up on the far left, representing a high-temperature (blue) and extremely luminous star.

**20. Which letter corresponds to an old star that once was a Sun-like, main-sequence star?**
*   **Answer: (A)**
*   **Explanation:** When a Sun-like star exhausts its hydrogen fuel, it leaves the main sequence and eventually evolves into a white dwarf. White dwarfs are small, hot, but have very low luminosity due to their small surface area. They are located in the bottom-left region of the H-R diagram. Point **A** is located in this "white dwarf" region.

### How to solve H-R Diagram questions:
1.  **Identify the Axes:** Always check the labels. The Y-axis represents **Luminosity** (or absolute magnitude), and the X-axis represents **Surface Temperature** (or spectral type).
2.  **Locate the Regions:** 
    *   **Main Sequence:** The thick diagonal band where most stars reside.
    *   **Giants/Supergiants:** Found above the main sequence (high luminosity).
    *   **White Dwarfs:** Found below the main sequence (low luminosity, high temperature).
3.  **Cross-reference:** Match the known characteristics of the star mentioned (e.g., "blue" means high temperature on the left, "supergiant" means high luminosity at the top) to the position on the grid.
