### Answer
Back EMF and the current in a generator are both phenomena governed by Faraday’s Law and Lenz’s Law, but they serve different purposes and act in opposite ways:

1.  **Nature and Origin:** The current produced in a generator is the useful electrical output, driven by the EMF induced when the coil rotates through a magnetic field. Back EMF, however, is a counter-voltage that appears in a motor (or sometimes a generator under load) as the device rotates, opposing the original change in magnetic flux.
2.  **Lenz’s Law:** Both are applications of Lenz’s Law. The current is induced to create a magnetic field that opposes the change in flux causing the motion. Back EMF specifically acts to oppose the source EMF (in motors) or the motion of the generator (due to the torque required to maintain rotation), ensuring the conservation of energy.
3.  **Effect:** The current represents the flow of charge providing power to a circuit. The Back EMF acts as an internal "resistance" to the motion or current flow; it effectively limits the magnitude of current based on the rotational speed of the generator.
4.  **Relationship:** As the generator spins faster, the frequency and magnitude of the current increase, and the potential for Back EMF (which opposes the drive) also increases, meaning more torque is required to keep the generator turning at a constant rate.
