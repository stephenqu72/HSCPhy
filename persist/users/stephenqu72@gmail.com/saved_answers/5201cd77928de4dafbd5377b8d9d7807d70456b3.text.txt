### Explanation
To solve this, we need to apply the principles of projectile motion, specifically the independence of horizontal and vertical motion when air resistance is ignored.

1.  **Analyze (A):** The ball rolls off the table with a horizontal velocity $v_x$. Since it is moving as it leaves the table, its initial velocity is not zero.
2.  **Analyze (B):** Because we are ignoring air resistance, there is no horizontal force acting on the ball. According to Newton's First Law, the horizontal acceleration is zero ($a_x = 0$), not a constant non-zero value.
3.  **Analyze (C):** The total velocity $v$ is given by the vector sum of the horizontal and vertical components: $v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$. 
    *   $v_x$ remains constant throughout the flight.
    *   $v_y$ increases linearly due to the constant acceleration of gravity ($g$).
    *   As the ball falls, $v_y$ increases, which means the total magnitude of velocity $v$ increases. Therefore, the velocity reaches its maximum value just before the ball hits the ground. This statement is correct.
4.  **Analyze (D):** The vertical acceleration is caused by gravity, which is constant ($g \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$). It does not increase as the ball approaches the ground.

### Summary
By understanding that horizontal acceleration is zero and vertical acceleration is constant, we can identify that the vertical velocity component grows over time, leading to an increasing total velocity until impact.
