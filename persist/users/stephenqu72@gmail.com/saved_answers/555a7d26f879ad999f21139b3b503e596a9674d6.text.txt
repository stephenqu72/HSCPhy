### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of the **Special Theory of Relativity**, specifically the principle of **Length Contraction** and the **Postulates of Special Relativity**.

1.  **The Concept:** According to Special Relativity, length contraction states that an object measured by an observer in motion relative to that object will appear shorter than its "proper length" (the length measured in the object's own rest frame). 
    *   The platform observer measures the train as moving, so they see the train contracted.
    *   The train observer measures the platform as moving (in the opposite direction), so they see the platform contracted.

2.  **Addressing the "Contradiction":** Students often think this is a paradox, but it is a consistent feature of relativity. The reason both observations are physically "correct" is rooted in Einstein's First Postulate of Special Relativity: **The laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames of reference.** There is no "privileged" or "absolute" frame of reference. Because both the platform and the train are inertial frames (they are not accelerating), both observers are equally correct in their measurements of length relative to their own frame.

3.  **Evaluating the Distractors:**
    *   **(B) is incorrect** because the train is an inertial frame of reference. Motion does not invalidate a frame of reference.
    *   **(C) is incorrect** because the speed of light ($c$) is a universal constant in all inertial frames of reference, regardless of the motion of the source or the observer.
    *   **(D) is incorrect** because both frames are standard inertial frames where the laws of physics hold true.

Therefore, the principle that justifies the validity of both measurements is that all inertial frames are equally valid.
