To solve this problem, we use **Faraday's Law of Induction**, which describes the relationship between the induced electromotive force (EMF) and the rate of change of magnetic flux in a coil with multiple turns.

### The Physics Principle
The average induced EMF ($\varepsilon$) in a coil with $N$ turns is given by the formula:
$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$$

Where:
- $\varepsilon$ is the magnitude of the induced EMF ($1.2\text{ V}$)
- $N$ is the number of turns ($6$)
- $\Delta\Phi$ is the change in magnetic flux ($0.05\text{ Wb}$)
- $\Delta t$ is the time interval we need to find

### Calculation
We want to find $\Delta t$, so we rearrange the formula (ignoring the negative sign as we are looking for the magnitude of the time interval):
$$\Delta t = \frac{N \Delta\Phi}{\varepsilon}$$

Now, substitute the given values into the equation:
$$\Delta t = \frac{6 \times 0.05\text{ Wb}}{1.2\text{ V}}$$
$$\Delta t = \frac{0.3}{1.2}\text{ s}$$
$$\Delta t = 0.25\text{ s}$$

Comparing this result to the given options:
A. $0.04\text{ s}$
B. $0.01\text{ s}$
**C. $0.25\text{ s}$**
D. $0.50\text{ s}$

The correct option is **C**.

### Step-by-Step Summary
1.  **Identify the variables:** $N = 6$, $\Delta\Phi = 0.05\text{ Wb}$, and $\varepsilon = 1.2\text{ V}$.
2.  **Select the formula:** Use Faraday’s Law, $\varepsilon = N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$.
3.  **Rearrange:** Isolate $\Delta t$ to get $\Delta t = \frac{N \Delta\Phi}{\varepsilon}$.
4.  **Compute:** Calculate the product $6 \times 0.05 = 0.3$, then divide by $1.2$ to arrive at $0.25\text{ s}$.
