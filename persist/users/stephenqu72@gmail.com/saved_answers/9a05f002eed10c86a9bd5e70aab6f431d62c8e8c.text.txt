### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
To find the total number of orders, we use the diffraction grating formula:
$$d \sin(\theta) = n \lambda$$
Where:
- $d$ is the slit separation (the reciprocal of the lines per metre).
- $\theta$ is the angle of diffraction.
- $n$ is the order number.
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength.

**1. Calculate $d$:**
$$d = \frac{1}{6.0 \times 10^5 \text{ lines/m}} \approx 1.667 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$$

**2. Determine the maximum order $n$:**
The maximum possible value for $\sin(\theta)$ is $1$ (which occurs at $90^\circ$).
$$n = \frac{d \sin(\theta)}{\lambda} < \frac{d(1)}{\lambda}$$
$$n < \frac{1.667 \times 10^{-6}}{590 \times 10^{-9}}$$
$$n < 2.825$$
Since $n$ must be an integer, the maximum observable order on one side is $n = 2$.

**3. Calculate total orders:**
The observable orders are $n = 0$, $n = \pm 1$, and $n = \pm 2$.
Total orders = $2 (\text{left}) + 2 (\text{right}) + 1 (\text{zero order}) = 5$.

**Answer:** There are **5** observable orders.

---

#### Part (b)
We look at the relationship $d \sin(\theta) = n \lambda$. Rearranging for $\theta$: $\sin(\theta) = \frac{n \lambda}{d}$. As $n$ increases, $\sin(\theta)$ increases, meaning the angles become larger.

1. **Increased angular separation (dispersion):** The separation between the two wavelengths ($590\text{ nm}$ and $589\text{ nm}$) will be greater in the second-order spectrum compared to the first-order spectrum because the angular position is directly proportional to $n$.
2. **Increased diffraction angle:** Both wavelengths will be diffracted at larger angles from the central zero-order beam in the second-order spectrum compared to the first-order spectrum.
