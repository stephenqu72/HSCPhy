### Front
What are quarks, and how do they form matter according to the Standard Model?

### Back
- Key idea: Quarks are fundamental fermions with fractional charges that combine via the strong nuclear force to form hadrons.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Hadrons consist of Baryons (3 quarks; e.g., proton $uud$, neutron $udd$) or Mesons (quark-antiquark pair).
- When to use it: To explain the subatomic composition of matter or the interaction of fundamental particles via the strong force.
- Common trap: Forgetting that quarks are never found in isolation due to "color confinement."
