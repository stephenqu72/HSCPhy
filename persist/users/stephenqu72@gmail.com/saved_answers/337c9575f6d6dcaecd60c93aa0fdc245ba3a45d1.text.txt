To solve this problem, we use the relationship between the linear speed ($v$) of an object moving in a circle and its angular velocity ($\omega$).

### Formula
The relationship is given by the formula:
$$v = r \omega$$

Where:
- $v$ is the linear speed of the tip of the hand.
- $r$ is the radius of the circular path, which corresponds to the length of the second hand.
- $\omega$ is the angular velocity.

### Calculation
1. **Identify the given values:**
   - Angular velocity ($\omega$) = $0.105 \text{ rad s}^{-1}$
   - Radius ($r$) = $1.8 \text{ cm}$

2. **Substitute the values into the formula:**
   $$v = 1.8 \text{ cm} \times 0.105 \text{ rad s}^{-1}$$
   $$v = 0.189 \text{ cm s}^{-1}$$

3. **Compare with the options:**
   The calculated value is $0.189 \text{ cm s}^{-1}$, which matches option (A).

### Conclusion
The correct option is **(A)**.
