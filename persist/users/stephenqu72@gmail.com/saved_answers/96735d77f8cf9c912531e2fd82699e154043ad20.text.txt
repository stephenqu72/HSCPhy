To solve this problem, we use **Fleming's Right-Hand Rule** (also known as the Generator Rule). This rule helps us determine the direction of the induced current in a conductor moving through a magnetic field.

### Explanation of the Rule
The rule states:
*   **Thumb:** Direction of the **motion** of the conductor (to the right in the diagram).
*   **First Finger (Index):** Direction of the **magnetic field** ($B$).
*   **Second Finger:** Direction of the **induced current** ($I$) (from $X$ to $Y$, which is upwards).

### Step-by-Step Analysis
1.  **Motion:** Point your right thumb to the **right**.
2.  **Current:** Point your middle finger **upwards** (representing the direction $X$ to $Y$).
3.  **Magnetic Field:** Observe where your index finger is pointing. 
    *   With your thumb to the right and your middle finger pointing up, your index finger must point **out of the page**.

Now let's check the options:
*   **(A) Crosses ($\times$):** Represents a magnetic field pointing **into** the page.
*   **(B) Dots ($\bullet$):** Represents a magnetic field pointing **out of** the page.
*   **(C) and (D):** Represent magnetic fields acting horizontally. Since the wire is moving horizontally (parallel to the field lines), there would be no "cutting" of magnetic flux, and thus no induced EMF/current.

By applying the right-hand rule, we see that option **(B)** is the only one that satisfies the condition where the magnetic field is directed out of the page to induce an upward current.

**Correct Answer: (B)**
