### Front
For a conical pendulum, how do you determine the velocity $v$ of the mass in terms of the radius $r$, angle $\theta$, and gravitational acceleration $g$?

### Back
- Key idea: The horizontal component of tension provides the necessary centripetal force ($T \sin\theta = mv^2/r$) while the vertical component balances gravity ($T \cos\theta = mg$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \sqrt{rg \tan\theta}$
- When to use it: Any circular motion problem involving a mass suspended by a string or rod at a fixed angle to the vertical.
- Common trap: Confusing $\sin\theta$ and $\cos\theta$ components—always draw a clear free-body diagram to check which side is adjacent to the angle.
