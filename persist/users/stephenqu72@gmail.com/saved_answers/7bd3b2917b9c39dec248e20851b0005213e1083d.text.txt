### Answer
James Clerk Maxwell’s work in the 1860s led to the fundamental prediction that light is an **electromagnetic wave**. His key predictions include:

1.  **Electromagnetic Nature:** Maxwell predicted that light consists of oscillating electric and magnetic fields that propagate through space as a self-sustaining wave.
2.  **Wave Velocity:** He calculated the speed of these electromagnetic waves using the constants of free space (the permittivity of free space $\epsilon_0$ and the permeability of free space $\mu_0$). The theoretical speed $c$ is given by:
    $$c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0}}$$
    When calculated, this value matched the experimentally measured speed of light, leading him to conclude that light is indeed an electromagnetic wave.
3.  **Transverse Wave Properties:** He predicted that these waves are transverse, meaning the oscillating electric and magnetic field vectors are perpendicular to each other and to the direction of wave propagation.
4.  **Propagation through Vacuum:** Unlike mechanical waves (like sound), Maxwell predicted that these electromagnetic waves do not require a material medium to propagate; they can travel through a vacuum.
