To calculate the energy released during this fusion reaction, we follow the principle of mass-energy equivalence, where the "mass defect" (the difference in mass between the reactants and products) is converted into energy according to Einstein's equation, $E = \Delta mc^2$.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Calculate the mass of the reactants:**
Reactants = $2 \times {}^{3}\text{He}$
Mass $= 2 \times (5.008238 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg})$
Mass $= 10.016476 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$

**2. Calculate the mass of the products:**
Products = $1 \times {}^{4}\text{He} + 2 \times {}^{1}\text{H}$
Mass $= 6.646483 \times 10^{-27} + 2 \times (1.673534 \times 10^{-27})\text{ kg}$
Mass $= 6.646483 \times 10^{-27} + 3.347068 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$
Mass $= 9.993551 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$

**3. Calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$):**
$\Delta m = \text{Mass}_{\text{reactants}} - \text{Mass}_{\text{products}}$
$\Delta m = (10.016476 - 9.993551) \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$
$\Delta m = 0.022925 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg} = 2.2925 \times 10^{-29}\text{ kg}$

**4. Convert the mass defect into energy in Joules ($E = \Delta mc^2$):**
Using $c = 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$:
$E = (2.2925 \times 10^{-29}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)^2$
$E = 2.2925 \times 10^{-29} \times 9.00 \times 10^{16}$
$E \approx 2.06325 \times 10^{-12}\text{ J}$

**5. Convert Joules to MeV:**
We use the conversion factor $1\text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$, so $1\text{ MeV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-13}\text{ J}$.
$E(\text{MeV}) = \frac{2.06325 \times 10^{-12}\text{ J}}{1.602 \times 10^{-13}\text{ J/MeV}}$
$E \approx 12.88\text{ MeV}$

**Final Answer:**
The energy released in the reaction is approximately **12.88 MeV**.
