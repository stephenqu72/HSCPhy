To answer an "Assess" question in the HSC, you must provide a judgment of value, effectiveness, or contribution. You need to show how each scientist shifted our understanding of the atom, creating the foundation for the next, ultimately leading to our modern quantum mechanical model.

### Answer

The development of the modern atomic model was a cumulative process, where each scientist corrected the limitations of their predecessors.

**1. Ernest Rutherford (The Nuclear Model):**
Through the 1909 Geiger-Marsden gold foil experiment, Rutherford disproved the "plum pudding" model. He identified the existence of a dense, positively charged nucleus surrounded by vast empty space containing electrons.
*   **Contribution:** He provided the first experimental evidence for a nucleus.
*   **Limitation:** Classically, electrons orbiting a nucleus should lose energy through electromagnetic radiation and spiral into the nucleus, meaning Rutherford’s atom was theoretically unstable.

**2. Niels Bohr (The Planetary Model):**
Bohr integrated Planck’s quantum theory to solve the stability issue. He proposed that electrons exist in fixed, discrete energy levels ($n=1, 2, 3...$) and do not radiate energy while in a "stationary state."
*   **Contribution:** He explained the emission spectra of hydrogen by showing that photons are emitted when electrons transition between these discrete energy levels.
*   **Limitation:** His model only worked accurately for single-electron systems (hydrogen) and could not explain the fine structure of spectral lines or the behavior of multi-electron atoms.

**3. Erwin Schrödinger (The Quantum Mechanical Model):**
Schrödinger advanced the theory by proposing that electrons behave as standing waves rather than point particles. He formulated the Schrödinger wave equation to calculate the probability of finding an electron in a specific region of space, known as an orbital.
*   **Contribution:** He replaced the fixed "orbits" of Bohr with "orbitals" (regions of high electron density). This accounted for the Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle and provided the mathematical framework that defines our currently accepted model of the atom.

**Assessment:**
Rutherford’s work was the vital starting point, providing the structural foundation. Bohr’s work was a necessary bridge that introduced quantization, but it was Schrödinger’s mathematical shift from classical mechanics to wave mechanics that finally provided a comprehensive model capable of explaining all atomic behavior. Each contribution was essential; without the experimental proof of the nucleus, quantum models would have lacked an anchor.

### How to solve this
1.  **Deconstruct the Verb:** "Assess" requires you to make a judgment. Use evaluative language (e.g., "crucial foundation," "necessary bridge," "mathematical breakthrough").
2.  **Chronological Structure:** Address them in order to show the *evolution* of the model. 
3.  **The "Why":** For each scientist, mention:
    *   What they proposed.
    *   The limitation they solved from the previous model.
    *   The new limitation they introduced (this highlights the progression).
4.  **Connect to the "Currently Accepted Model":** Ensure your conclusion links back to the quantum mechanical model (electron clouds/orbitals) as the final state.
