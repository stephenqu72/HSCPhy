### Answer

**a. Which of these models was proposed by Newton?**
Model 1.

**b. With reference to general characteristics of scientific models, why was Newton’s model rejected in favour of the model proposed by Huygens?**
A scientific model is judged by its ability to explain observed phenomena and make accurate predictions. Newton’s corpuscular model (Model 1) successfully explained reflection and refraction (though incorrectly predicting that light speeds up in denser media), but it struggled to account for phenomena such as diffraction, interference, and polarization. Huygens’ wave model (Model 2) provided a superior explanation for these wave-like properties. Over time, the wave model was accepted because it possessed greater **explanatory power** and **predictive validity** for a broader range of experimental observations, fulfilling the criteria of a robust scientific model.

### How to solve this question
1.  **Identify the models:** Recognize that Model 1 depicts light as discrete particles (corpuscles), which is consistent with Isaac Newton's Corpuscular Theory. Model 2 depicts light as wavefronts, which is consistent with Christiaan Huygens' Wave Theory.
2.  **Recall the historical context:** Newton and Huygens held competing views in the late 17th century. Newton argued that light consists of tiny particles, while Huygens argued that light acts as a wave traveling through an "aether."
3.  **Apply the nature of scientific models:** When answering part (b), move beyond simply stating that "waves were proven correct." Frame your answer around the *philosophy of science*. Scientific models are rejected or refined when new evidence emerges that the existing model cannot explain (a lack of empirical adequacy).
4.  **Connect to evidence:** Mention specific phenomena (like interference or diffraction) that Newton’s particles could not explain, but waves could. This demonstrates to the marker that you understand the mechanism of scientific paradigm shifts.
