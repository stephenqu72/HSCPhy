### Front
Why might a radioactive sample produce fewer beta particles than predicted while emitting a high volume of photons?

### Back
- Key idea: The isotope is a gamma ($\gamma$) emitter, not a beta ($\beta$) emitter.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When observed beta activity is lower than the predicted sum of multiple isotope decay rates.
- Common trap: Assuming all radioactive isotopes in a mixture are beta emitters just because one is.
