### Part (a)

**1. Calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$):**
The mass defect is the difference between the mass of the reactants and the mass of the products:
$$\Delta m = m(\text{Pu}) - [m(\text{U}) + m(\alpha)]$$
$$\Delta m = 238.0495u - (234.0409u + 4.0026u)$$
$$\Delta m = 238.0495u - 238.0435u = 0.0060u$$

**2. Convert mass to Joules:**
Use the conversion $1u = 1.661 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$ and $E = \Delta mc^2$ (where $c = 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$):
$$\Delta E = (0.0060 \times 1.661 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s})^2$$
$$\Delta E \approx 8.969 \times 10^{-13}\text{ J} \approx 9.0 \times 10^{-13}\text{ J}$$

**1. Calculate the number of nuclei decayed ($N_{\text{decayed}}$):**
The number of nuclei remaining after time $t$ is $N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$. The number decayed is $N_{\text{decayed}} = N_0 - N(t) = N_0(1 - e^{-\lambda t})$.
- $N_0 = 9.0 \times 10^{24}$
- Half-life $T_{1/2} = 87.7\text{ years}$
- Decay constant $\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{87.7} \approx 0.007903\text{ year}^{-1}$
- Time $t = 10\text{ years}$

$$N_{\text{decayed}} = 9.0 \times 10^{24} \times (1 - e^{-(0.007903)(10)})$$
$$N_{\text{decayed}} = 9.0 \times 10^{24} \times (1 - 0.9240) \approx 6.84 \times 10^{23}\text{ nuclei}$$
