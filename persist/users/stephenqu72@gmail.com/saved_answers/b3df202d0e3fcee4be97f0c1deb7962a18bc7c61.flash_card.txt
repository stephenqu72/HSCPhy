### Front
How is centripetal force provided for a vehicle on a frictionless banked track, and what is the resulting formula for the banking angle $\theta$?

### Back
- Key idea: The horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$ or $F_c = mg \tan(\theta)$.
- When to use it: When a vehicle is moving at a constant speed on a banked curve without relying on friction.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the vertical component of the normal force must balance gravity ($N \cos\theta = mg$); never resolve $mg$ horizontally.
