To determine the linear speed of the seats, we must analyze the forces acting on the seat during uniform circular motion.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Identify the Radius of the Circular Path**
The seat does not rotate at a radius of $6.0\text{ m}$. The cable length is $L = 3.0\text{ m}$ and it hangs at an angle $\theta = 25^\circ$ to the vertical. The additional horizontal distance created by the swing is $L\sin\theta$.
The total radius $r$ of the circular path is:
$$r = 6.0 + L\sin(25^\circ)$$
$$r = 6.0 + 3.0 \sin(25^\circ) \approx 6.0 + 1.2678 = 7.2678\text{ m}$$

**2. Analyze the Forces**
The forces acting on the seat are tension ($T$) and weight ($mg$). The net force providing the centripetal acceleration is the horizontal component of tension ($T\sin\theta$). The vertical component of tension balances gravity ($T\cos\theta = mg$).
Dividing these two equations gives:
$$\frac{T\sin\theta}{T\cos\theta} = \frac{F_c}{mg} = \tan\theta$$
$$F_c = mg\tan\theta$$

**3. Relate Centripetal Force to Linear Speed**
The formula for centripetal force is $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$. Equating this to $mg\tan\theta$:
$$\frac{mv^2}{r} = mg\tan\theta$$
$$v^2 = rg\tan\theta$$
$$v = \sqrt{rg\tan\theta}$$

**4. Calculation**
Using $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$:
$$v = \sqrt{7.2678 \times 9.8 \times \tan(25^\circ)}$$
$$v = \sqrt{71.224 \times 0.4663}$$
$$v = \sqrt{33.212}$$
$$v \approx 5.76\text{ m/s}$$

The linear speed of the seats is approximately **$5.8\text{ m/s}$**.
