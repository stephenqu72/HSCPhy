To calculate the mass of the particle, we use the principles of circular motion in a magnetic field.

### Answer
The mass of the particle is approximately $3.77 \times 10^{-25} \text{ kg}$.

1.  **Identify the physics principles:** When a charged particle enters a perpendicular magnetic field, the magnetic force acts as a centripetal force, causing the particle to move in a circular path.
    *   Magnetic Force: $F_B = qvB$
    *   Centripetal Force: $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$

2.  **Equate the forces:** Since the magnetic force provides the centripetal force:
    $$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
    Solving for mass ($m$):
    $$m = \frac{qBr}{v}$$

    $$r = \frac{9.96 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}}{2} = 4.98 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}$$
