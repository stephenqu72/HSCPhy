### Front
What is the direction of the net force acting on an object in uniform circular motion, and which direction does it point relative to the path?

### Back
- Key idea: Centripetal force is the net force required to change the direction of an object's velocity, causing circular motion.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$; points radially inward toward the center of the circle.
- When to use it: Whenever an object moves in a circular path at constant speed.
- Common trap: Mistaking the direction of velocity (tangential) for the direction of force (centripetal/radial).
