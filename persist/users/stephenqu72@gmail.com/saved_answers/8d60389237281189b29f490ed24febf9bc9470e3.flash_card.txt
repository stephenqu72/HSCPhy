### Front
Define the Electric Field Strength ($E$) at a point in space.

### Back
- **Key idea:** The electrostatic force ($F$) experienced per unit of a stationary positive test charge ($q$).
- **Formula:** $E = \frac{F}{q}$
- **When to use it:** To determine the magnitude and direction of an electric field at any coordinate in space.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that $E$ is a vector quantity; the direction is defined by the motion of a *positive* test charge.
