### Part (a)
**Answer:**
We assume an ideal transformer where input power equals output power ($P_{in} = P_{out}$).
Using the formula $P = VI$:
$$V_{in}I_{in} = V_{out}I_{out}$$
$$240 \times I_{in} = 6 \times 1$$
$$I_{in} = \frac{6}{240}$$
$$I_{in} = 0.025 \text{ A}$$

**How to solve it:**
1. **Identify the principle:** In an ideal transformer, energy is conserved, meaning the power entering the primary coil equals the power leaving the secondary coil.
2. **Set up the equation:** Use the relationship $V_{in}I_{in} = V_{out}I_{out}$.
3. **Substitute:** Plug in the known values ($V_{in}=240\text{V}$, $V_{out}=6\text{V}$, $I_{out}=1\text{A}$).
4. **Calculate:** Solve for $I_{in}$. Always include units in your final answer.

### Part (c)
**Answer:**
To maximize efficiency and minimize energy losses, transformers are designed with the following features:
*   **Laminated soft-iron core:** The core is made of thin, insulated sheets to reduce "eddy currents," which minimize thermal energy loss.
*   **Soft magnetic material:** Using materials like silicon steel allows for easy magnetization and demagnetization, reducing "hysteresis loss."
*   **Thick copper windings:** Using low-resistance wire (copper) minimizes heat loss due to the resistance of the wires ($I^2R$ heating or "copper loss").
*   **Tight coupling:** Winding coils closely around each other ensures almost all magnetic flux passes through both coils, minimizing "flux leakage."
