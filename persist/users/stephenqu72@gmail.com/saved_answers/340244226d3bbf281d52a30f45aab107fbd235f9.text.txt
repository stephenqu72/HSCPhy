To solve this question, we must understand the fundamental physical differences between how laser light and incandescent light are produced.

### Answer
The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation

1.  **Laser Light:** Lasers produce "monochromatic" light. This means the light emitted consists of a very narrow range of wavelengths (frequencies). Because of the stimulated emission process, photons are emitted with almost identical energies, resulting in a very sharp, narrow spectral line (often referred to as coherent light).
2.  **Incandescent Light:** This type of light is produced by thermal radiation (blackbody radiation). When a filament is heated to a high temperature, atoms vibrate and collide, emitting photons across a broad range of frequencies. This creates a **continuous spectrum** that covers a wide portion of the electromagnetic spectrum (typically including visible, infrared, and ultraviolet light, depending on the temperature).

**Evaluating the choices:**
*   **A is incorrect** because incandescent light has a broad (wide), not narrow, spectrum.
*   **B is incorrect** because it flips the definitions of laser and incandescent light spectra.
*   **C is incorrect** because laser light is highly monochromatic (not a continuous spectrum), and incandescent light is not restricted to the "yellow region."
*   **D is correct** because it accurately identifies that laser light is confined to a narrow frequency range (monochromatic) and incandescent light follows the broad profile of a continuous blackbody spectrum.
