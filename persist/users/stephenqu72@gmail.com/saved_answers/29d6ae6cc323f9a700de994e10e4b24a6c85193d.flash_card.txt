### Front
In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), how does the horizontal velocity change throughout the flight?

### Back
- Key idea: Horizontal velocity remains constant because there is no net horizontal force.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s First Law ($a_x = 0$).
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem where horizontal velocity is required at any point in the trajectory.
- Common trap: Attempting to use vertical displacement ($y$) or gravity ($g$) to calculate horizontal velocity; these only affect vertical velocity ($v_y$).
