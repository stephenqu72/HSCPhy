### Part (a)
**Explanation:** Kepler’s Third Law states $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$, which can be rearranged as $r^3 = (\frac{GM}{4\pi^2})T^2$. This matches the equation of a straight line $y = mx + c$, where the gradient $m = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$.

1. **Calculate the gradient ($m$):** Using the line of best fit, take two points. The line passes through $(0,0)$ and approximately $(20 \times 10^{11}, 650 \times 10^{25})$.
   $$m = \frac{650 \times 10^{25}}{20 \times 10^{11}} = 32.5 \times 10^{14} \text{ m}^3/\text{s}^2$$
2. **Solve for $M$:**
   $$M = \frac{m \cdot 4\pi^2}{G} = \frac{32.5 \times 10^{14} \cdot 4\pi^2}{6.67 \times 10^{-11}} \approx 1.92 \times 10^{26} \text{ kg}$$

### Part (b)(i)
**Explanation:** The escape velocity formula is $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$.
1. **Identify variables:** $M = 5.7 \times 10^{26} \text{ kg}$. The radius of Earth is $R_E \approx 6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$. Since $R_{P} = 2R_E$, $R_P = 1.274 \times 10^7 \text{ m}$.
2. **Calculate:**
   $$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \cdot 5.7 \times 10^{26}}{1.274 \times 10^7}} \approx \sqrt{5.97 \times 10^9} \approx 7.73 \times 10^4 \text{ m/s}$$

### Part (b)(ii)
**Explanation:** This is a direct application of Kepler’s Second Law (the Law of Equal Areas), which states that a line joining a planet and an orbiting body sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time.
**Answer:** The areas of segment $AOB$ and $COD$ are equal. Since the problem specifies that the spaceship travels from $A$ to $B$ and $C$ to $D$ in equal time intervals, Kepler's Second Law dictates that the areas swept out must be identical, regardless of the distance from the planet.
