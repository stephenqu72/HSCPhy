To determine the relationship between $R_1$ and $R_2$, we must look at the rotational dynamics of the lever system (torque).

### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) $R_2 > 2R_1$**.

### Explanation
1. **Understanding Torque:** Torque ($\tau$) is calculated as the product of the force and the perpendicular distance from the pivot (fulcrum): $\tau = F \cdot d$. Here, the force is the weight of the masses ($W = mg$).
   - The torque produced by the mass $2M$ is $\tau_1 = (2M)g \cdot R_1$.
   - The torque produced by the mass $M$ is $\tau_2 = Mg \cdot R_2$.

2. **Analyzing the Motion:** The problem states that when the system is released, the mass $M$ accelerates **downwards**. This means the lever is rotating in a clockwise direction.
   - For the system to rotate clockwise, the clockwise torque must be greater than the counter-clockwise torque:
     $$\tau_2 > \tau_1$$

3. **Setting up the inequality:**
   - Substitute the expressions for torque into the inequality:
     $$Mg \cdot R_2 > (2M)g \cdot R_1$$

4. **Simplifying:**
   - We can cancel out the common terms $M$ and $g$ from both sides of the inequality:
     $$R_2 > 2R_1$$

This confirms that for the lighter mass side to accelerate downwards, its lever arm $R_2$ must be more than twice the length of the lever arm $R_1$ associated with the heavier mass.
