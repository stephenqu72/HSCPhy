### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must understand the fundamental nature of sound waves:

1.  **Nature of the wave:** Sound waves are **longitudinal waves**. In a longitudinal wave, the displacement of the medium's particles occurs in a direction **parallel** to the direction of energy propagation (wave travel).
2.  **Wave travel:** The sound is emitted by the loudspeaker and travels toward the dust particle. Therefore, the direction of the wave is horizontal (along the line connecting the loudspeaker and the particle).
3.  **Particle motion:** Because sound is longitudinal, the air molecules (and by extension, the tiny dust particle suspended in that air) oscillate back and forth about their equilibrium position. This oscillation occurs along the same line as the wave's path.
4.  **Analyzing the options:**
    *   **A:** Shows vertical oscillation (perpendicular). This describes a transverse wave.
    *   **B:** Shows the particle moving only in one direction, which is incorrect as wave motion involves oscillation (periodic movement).
    *   **C:** Shows no motion, which is incorrect as the sound wave exerts pressure variations that cause the particle to oscillate.
    *   **D:** Shows the particle oscillating back and forth horizontally. This accurately represents the motion of particles in a longitudinal wave.

Therefore, the dust particle will move in a back-and-forth motion parallel to the direction of the sound wave.
