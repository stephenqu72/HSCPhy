### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) $\text{rad s}^{-1}$**.

### Explanation
To determine the unit of any physical quantity, it is best to look at its definition. 

Angular velocity, denoted by the Greek letter omega ($\omega$), is defined as the rate of change of angular displacement ($\theta$) over time ($t$). Mathematically, this is expressed as:
$$\omega = \frac{\Delta\theta}{\Delta t}$$

1.  **Identify the unit for angular displacement ($\theta$):** In physics, angular displacement is measured in **radians** (rad). Note that radians are dimensionless, but they are included in the unit to clarify that we are dealing with rotation.
2.  **Identify the unit for time ($t$):** The standard SI unit for time is **seconds** (s).
3.  **Combine the units:** Since $\omega = \frac{\Delta\theta}{\Delta t}$, the units will be $\frac{\text{rad}}{\text{s}}$. 

In scientific notation, dividing by a unit is equivalent to multiplying by that unit raised to the power of $-1$. Therefore, $\frac{\text{rad}}{\text{s}}$ becomes **$\text{rad s}^{-1}$**.

*   **(B) rad** is the unit for angular displacement, not velocity.
*   **(C) $\text{m s}^{-1}$** is the unit for linear (tangential) velocity.
*   **(D) $\theta$** is a symbol for angular displacement, not a unit.
