### Answer
To represent the electric field between these two charged parallel plates, you should draw a series of **straight, parallel lines** pointing from the positive plate toward the negative plate.

*   **Direction:** The arrows on the lines must point from right to left (from the $+$ plate to the $-$ plate), as electric field lines are defined by the direction a positive test charge would move.
*   **Uniformity:** The lines should be spaced equally apart between the plates to represent a **uniform electric field**.
*   **Edges:** You may draw slight "fringing" or curving of the lines at the top and bottom ends of the plates to indicate that the field is non-uniform outside the immediate central region.

### How to solve it
1.  **Recall the definition:** By convention, electric field lines represent the direction of the force on a hypothetical positive test charge. Since positive charges are repelled by positive plates and attracted to negative plates, the field lines must originate at the positive plate and terminate at the negative plate.
2.  **Understand the setup:** A pair of parallel plates with opposite charges creates a **uniform electric field** in the central region between them.
3.  **Application:**
    *   Draw straight lines spanning the gap between the plates.
    *   Place arrows on each line pointing left (towards the negative plate).
    *   Ensure the spacing between the lines is consistent, as the field strength $E$ is constant between parallel plates: $E = \frac{V}{d}$, where $V$ is the potential difference and $d$ is the distance between plates.
