### Part (a): Calculation
To solve for the secondary current in an ideal transformer, we use the principle of conservation of energy (power in = power out). For an ideal transformer:
$$V_p I_p = V_s I_s$$

Since the voltage ratio is proportional to the turns ratio ($\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$), we can derive the relationship between current and turns:
$$\frac{I_s}{I_p} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$

**Given:**
- $N_p = 1200$
- $N_s = 900$
- $I_p = 4.0\text{ A}$

**Calculation:**
$$I_s = I_p \times \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$
$$I_s = 4.0 \times \frac{1200}{900}$$
$$I_s = 4.0 \times \frac{4}{3}$$
$$I_s \approx 5.33\text{ A}$$

### Part (b): Descriptive Response
In non-ideal transformers, energy is lost as heat. You should identify the specific mechanisms and their solutions:

1.  **Resistance of the coils ($I^2R$ loss/Copper loss):**
    *   **Where:** In the copper wire windings due to electrical resistance.
    *   **Improvement:** Use thicker copper wire to reduce the resistance $R$.
2.  **Eddy Currents:**
    *   **Where:** In the iron core, induced by the changing magnetic flux.
    *   **Improvement:** Use a laminated core (thin sheets of iron insulated from each other) to break the path of eddy currents.
3.  **Hysteresis Loss:**
    *   **Where:** In the magnetic core material due to the repeated magnetization and demagnetization cycles.
    *   **Improvement:** Use "soft" magnetic materials (like silicon steel) which are easier to magnetize and demagnetize.
4.  **Flux Leakage:**
    *   **Where:** Magnetic flux escaping outside the core.
    *   **Improvement:** Wind the secondary coil directly over the primary coil on a closed-loop core to ensure maximum flux linkage.
