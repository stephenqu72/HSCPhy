### Front
On an $N$ vs $Z$ graph, what displacement vector represents alpha ($\alpha$) decay?

### Back
- Key idea: Alpha decay releases a helium nucleus ($^{4}_{2}\text{He}$), reducing both proton count ($Z$) and neutron count ($N$) by 2.
- Figure: A vector pointing down and to the left (shift of $-2$ on both axes).
- When to use it: Any $N$ vs $Z$ plot identifying radioactive decay modes.
- Common trap: Confusing alpha decay (down/left) with beta-minus decay (up/left) or beta-plus decay (down/right).
