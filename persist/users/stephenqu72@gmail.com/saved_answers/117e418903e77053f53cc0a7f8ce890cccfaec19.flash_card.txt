### Front
How does changing launch velocity ($u$) or platform height ($h$) affect the horizontal range ($R$) of a horizontally launched projectile?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Range is directly proportional to both initial horizontal velocity and the square root of height ($R = u\sqrt{2h/g}$).
- **Formula:** $R = u \cdot t$ where $t = \sqrt{2h/g}$.
- **When to use it:** To determine landing position changes in projectile motion problems.
- **Common trap:** Assuming mass affects the range (it does not, as $g$ is constant for all masses) or ignoring that changing $h$ affects the time of flight, not just vertical displacement.
