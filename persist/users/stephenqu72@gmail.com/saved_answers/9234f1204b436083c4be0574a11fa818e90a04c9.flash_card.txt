### Front
How do you derive the linear speed ($v$) of a conical pendulum (e.g., a swing ride seat)?

### Back
- Key idea: The net horizontal centripetal force is provided by the horizontal component of tension ($T\sin\theta$).
- Formula: $v = \sqrt{rg\tan\theta}$
- When to use it: Circular motion involving a tethered object moving at a constant angle to the vertical.
- Common trap: Forgetting to add the swing's horizontal displacement ($L\sin\theta$) to the central axle radius ($R_{axle}$) to find the total radius $r$.
