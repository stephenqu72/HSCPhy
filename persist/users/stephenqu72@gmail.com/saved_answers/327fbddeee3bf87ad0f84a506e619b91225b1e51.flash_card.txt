### Front
What does the gradient of a velocity-distance graph for galaxies represent in the context of Hubble’s Law?

### Back
- Key idea: The gradient represents the rate of expansion of the universe.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = H_0 d$ (Hubble's Law); $H_0$ (Hubble constant) is the gradient.
- When to use it: When interpreting observational data of galactic recessional velocities plotted against their distances.
- Common trap: Confusing the gradient ($H_0$) with the inverse ($1/H_0$), which represents the approximate age of the universe.
