To answer this question, we must apply Newton's Second Law of Motion:
$$F_{net} = ma$$

### Answer:
The correct diagram is **A**.

### Explanation:
1.  **The Physics Principle:** A satellite in orbit is experiencing a gravitational force exerted by the Earth. According to Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation, this force always acts directly toward the center of mass of the Earth.
2.  **Direction of Acceleration:** Newton's Second Law states that the net force acting on an object is in the same direction as the acceleration ($F = ma$). Since the only significant force acting on the satellite is the gravitational force pulling it toward the center of the Earth, the satellite’s acceleration must also be directed toward the center of the Earth.
3.  **Analyzing the Options:**
    *   **Diagram A:** Shows an acceleration vector pointing directly toward the center of the Earth. This matches the direction of the gravitational force.
    *   **Diagrams B and C:** Show vectors pointing toward points other than the center of the Earth.
    *   **Diagram D:** Shows a vector tangent to the orbit, which would represent the direction of the satellite's velocity, not its acceleration.

Therefore, because gravitational force is centripetal in nature (directed toward the center of the orbit), the acceleration is likewise directed toward the center of the Earth.
