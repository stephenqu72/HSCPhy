To solve this projectile motion problem, we must analyze the horizontal and vertical components of motion independently.

### Part (a)
**Given:**
*   Horizontal displacement, $\Delta x = 2.80\text{ m}$
*   Vertical displacement, $\Delta y = 1.71\text{ m}$
*   Launch angle, $\theta = 60.0^\circ$
*   Acceleration due to gravity, $g = 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$

**Step 1: Define equations of motion.**
The horizontal velocity is constant: $v_x = u \cos\theta$.
The time $t$ to reach the dish is: $t = \frac{\Delta x}{u \cos\theta} = \frac{2.80}{u \cos 60^\circ} = \frac{2.80}{0.5u} = \frac{5.6}{u}$

The vertical displacement is: $\Delta y = (u \sin\theta)t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$

**Step 2: Substitute and solve for $u$.**
Substitute $t = \frac{5.6}{u}$ into the vertical displacement equation:
$$1.71 = (u \sin 60^\circ) \left( \frac{5.6}{u} \right) - \frac{1}{2}(9.8) \left( \frac{5.6}{u} \right)^2$$
$$1.71 = 5.6 \sin 60^\circ - 4.9 \left( \frac{31.36}{u^2} \right)$$
$$1.71 = 4.8497 - \frac{153.664}{u^2}$$
$$\frac{153.664}{u^2} = 4.8497 - 1.71 = 3.1397$$
$$u^2 = \frac{153.664}{3.1397} \approx 48.94$$
$$u \approx 7.00\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

### Part (b)
**Explanation:**
If the initial speed $u$ is increased while keeping the angle constant at $60.0^\circ$, the vertical component of the velocity will also increase. This causes the coin to travel higher into the air, reaching a point well above the shelf before descending. Consequently, the coin will miss the dish by landing behind it (the horizontal range will increase).

**Proposal:**
To ensure the coin still lands in the dish, Daniel must **decrease the launch angle** ($\theta$) alongside increasing the initial speed. By flattening the trajectory, the coin can travel faster horizontally while keeping its maximum height low enough to hit the target.
