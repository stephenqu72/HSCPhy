To answer this question, you need to represent the forces acting on the box as vectors originating from the point. 

### The Solution

You should draw two arrows starting from the center dot:
1.  **A downward arrow:** This represents the force of gravity (Weight, $W$ or $mg$). This arrow must be **longer** than the upward arrow to indicate that there is a net force in the downward direction (resulting in acceleration).
2.  **An upward arrow:** This represents air resistance (Drag, $F_d$). This arrow must be **shorter** than the downward arrow.

### Explanation

When an object accelerates, it means there is an unbalanced net force acting upon it (Newton’s Second Law, $F_{net} = ma$).

*   **Gravity ($mg$):** Gravity always acts downwards towards the centre of the Earth.
*   **Air Resistance ($F_d$):** Since the box is moving downwards, air resistance acts in the opposite direction (upwards) to oppose the motion.
*   **The "HSC Trick":** A common mistake is to draw the arrows equal in length. However, if the arrows were equal, the forces would be balanced, the net force would be zero, and the box would be moving at a constant velocity (terminal velocity) rather than accelerating. Because the box is **accelerating downwards**, the downward force vector must be strictly greater in magnitude (longer) than the upward force vector.

### Summary for your diagram:
*   Label the downward arrow as "Weight", "$mg$", or "Gravity".
*   Label the upward arrow as "Air resistance" or "$F_{drag}$".
*   Ensure the downward arrow is visibly longer than the upward arrow.
