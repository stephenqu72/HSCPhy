To solve this problem, we use Einstein's mass-energy equivalence principle, defined by the formula:
$$E = mc^2$$

Where $E$ is energy, $m$ is mass, and $c$ is the speed of light. Rearranging this to solve for mass, we get:
$$m = \frac{E}{c^2}$$

### Step-by-step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given information:**
    The energy released is $E = 0.42\text{ MeV}$.

2.  **Convert MeV to Joules:**
    In physics, $1\text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$. 
    Therefore, $1\text{ MeV} = 10^6\text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-13}\text{ J}$.
    So, $E = 0.42 \times 10^6 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$.

3.  **Calculate the mass in kg:**
    Using the formula $m = \frac{E}{c^2}$:
    $$m = \frac{0.42 \times 10^6 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19}}{c^2}\text{ kg}$$

        $$m = \frac{0.42\text{ MeV}}{931.5\text{ MeV/u}} = \frac{0.42}{931.5}\text{ u}$$
