### Front
How do you determine the mass ($M$) of a central body using the orbital radius ($r$) and period ($T$) of a satellite?

### Back
- Key idea: Equate gravitational force ($F_g$) to centripetal force ($F_c$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Kepler’s Third Law rearranged: $M = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{G T^2}$
- When to use it: When calculating the mass of a planet or star based on the orbital data of a moon or planet.
- Common trap: Failing to convert units (e.g., radius in km to meters, or period in hours/days to seconds) or forgetting to square the period $T$.
