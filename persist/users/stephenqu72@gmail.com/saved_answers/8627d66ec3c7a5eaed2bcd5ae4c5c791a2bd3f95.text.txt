### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Answer:** The acceleration is constant at all points in the projectile's trajectory. 
**Justification:** Under the assumptions of a uniform gravitational field and no air resistance, the only force acting on the projectile is gravity. Since $F_g = mg$, the acceleration is $a = g$ downwards. As neither the mass of the projectile nor the gravitational field strength changes, the acceleration remains constant at $9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ throughout the entire flight.

#### Part (b)
**Explanation of how to solve:**
To find the launch angle $\theta$, we use the kinematic equations for projectile motion. 
1. **Define the coordinate system:** Let the launch point be $(0,0)$. 
2. **Find the displacement:** 
   - Horizontal distance ($x$): $0.85 \, \text{m} + 0.60 \, \text{m} - 0.080 \, \text{m} = 1.37 \, \text{m}$.
   - Vertical distance ($y$): $0.90 \, \text{m} + 0.060 \, \text{m} = 0.96 \, \text{m}$.
3. **Use kinematic equations:**
   - Horizontal: $x = u_x t \implies u_x = \frac{x}{t} = \frac{1.37}{0.66} \approx 2.076 \, \text{m/s}$.
   - Vertical: $y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$. Taking upward as positive, $a_y = -9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$.
     $$0.96 = u_y(0.66) + 0.5(-9.8)(0.66)^2$$
     $$0.96 = 0.66 u_y - 2.134$$
     $$3.094 = 0.66 u_y \implies u_y \approx 4.688 \, \text{m/s}$$
4. **Calculate the angle:**
   $$\tan(\theta) = \frac{u_y}{u_x} = \frac{4.688}{2.076} \approx 2.258$$
   $$\theta = \arctan(2.258) \approx 66.1^\circ$$
   Rounding to significant figures provided in the prompt, this confirms the launch angle is approximately $65^\circ$.
