### Front
What is the fundamental difference between natural transmutation and induced transmutation?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Natural transmutation is spontaneous radioactive decay (e.g., alpha/beta decay) occurring without external triggers. Induced transmutation requires an external particle (usually a neutron) to strike a nucleus to initiate the change.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** Use when distinguishing between radioactive decay series (natural) and nuclear reactor processes like fission or neutron capture (induced).
- **Common trap:** Assuming all nuclear reactions are the same; always check if an external "neutron" reactant is present—if it is, the reaction is induced, not natural.
