To solve this problem, we must break the rocket's flight into two distinct stages: the powered ascent and the projectile motion phase.

### Step 1: Calculate state at end of motor burn
During the motor burn (constant acceleration), the rocket travels at an angle of $20.0^{\circ}$. 
Initial velocity $u = 0$, final velocity $v = 82.0 \text{ m s}^{-1}$, time $t = 2.50 \text{ s}$.

**Position at the end of burn:**
Assuming constant acceleration, the distance covered is $s = \frac{u+v}{2} \times t = \frac{0 + 82.0}{2} \times 2.50 = 102.5 \text{ m}$.
- Horizontal displacement: $x_0 = 102.5 \times \cos(20^{\circ}) \approx 96.33 \text{ m}$
- Vertical displacement: $y_0 = 102.5 \times \sin(20^{\circ}) \approx 35.06 \text{ m}$

**Velocity components at end of burn:**
- $v_x = 82.0 \times \cos(20^{\circ}) \approx 77.04 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
- $v_y = 82.0 \times \sin(20^{\circ}) \approx 28.04 \text{ m s}^{-1}$

### Step 2: Projectile motion
The rocket acts as a projectile from $(x_0, y_0)$ with initial velocity $v_x$ and $v_y$. We need the time $t_f$ it takes to hit the ground ($y = 0$).
Using $y = y_0 + v_y t_f - \frac{1}{2}g t_f^2$:
$$0 = 35.06 + 28.04 t_f - 4.9 t_f^2$$
Using the quadratic formula $t_f = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$$t_f = \frac{-28.04 \pm \sqrt{28.04^2 - 4(-4.9)(35.06)}}{2(-4.9)}$$
$$t_f = \frac{-28.04 \pm \sqrt{786.24 + 687.18}}{-9.8} = \frac{-28.04 \pm 38.39}{-9.8}$$
We take the positive root: $t_f \approx 6.78 \text{ s}$.

### Step 3: Total Horizontal Range
The total range $R$ is the horizontal distance covered during the burn plus the distance covered during projectile motion:
$$R = x_0 + (v_x \times t_f)$$
$$R = 96.33 + (77.04 \times 6.78)$$
$$R = 96.33 + 522.33 = 618.66 \text{ m}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (based on $20.0^{\circ}$ and $2.50\text{ s}$), the horizontal range is approximately **$620 \text{ m}$**.
