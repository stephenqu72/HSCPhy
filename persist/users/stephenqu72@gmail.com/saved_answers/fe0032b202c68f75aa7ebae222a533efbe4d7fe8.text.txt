**1. Production of Neutrons**
Neutrons are produced in nuclear reactors primarily through fission. A common reaction involves Uranium-235:
$${}_{92}^{235}\text{U} + {}_{0}^{1}\text{n} \rightarrow {}_{56}^{141}\text{Ba} + {}_{36}^{92}\text{Kr} + 3({}_{0}^{1}\text{n}) + \text{Energy}$$

**2. Evidence for Matter Waves**
The diffraction pattern observed in the image provides empirical evidence that neutrons behave as waves. Diffraction is a wave-specific phenomenon that occurs when waves encounter obstacles or slits of comparable size to their wavelength. Since neutrons produce a pattern analogous to X-ray diffraction, they must possess a de Broglie wavelength defined by $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$.

**3. Calculation of Neutron Velocity**
Using the Bragg diffraction condition for constructive interference:
$$n\lambda = 2d \sin(\theta)$$
Assuming first-order diffraction ($n=1$) and that the angular separation of $4^{\circ}$ corresponds to the diffraction angle ($\theta = 2^{\circ}$):
$$\lambda = 2 \times (0.282 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}) \times \sin(2^{\circ})$$
$$\lambda \approx 1.967 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$$
Using de Broglie's equation $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$, we solve for velocity $v$:
$$v = \frac{h}{m\lambda}$$
Where $m = 1.675 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$ (mass of neutron) and $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$:
$$v = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{(1.675 \times 10^{-27})(1.967 \times 10^{-11})} \approx 20,110 \text{ m/s}$$

**4. Benefit of Using Neutrons**
Neutrons have no charge, allowing them to penetrate deep into materials, whereas X-rays interact primarily with electron clouds. This makes neutron diffraction superior for identifying light elements (like Hydrogen) and investigating magnetic structures, as neutrons have a magnetic moment.
