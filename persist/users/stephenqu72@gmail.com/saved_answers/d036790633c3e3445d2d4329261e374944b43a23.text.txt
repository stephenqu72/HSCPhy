To solve this question, we must evaluate which scientific principles were established by Galileo Galilei's work on gravity and motion.

### Answer
The correct answer is **A**.

### Explanation
To determine the correct option, we evaluate the historical contributions of Galileo:

1.  **Vertical velocity is independent of horizontal velocity:** Galileo is credited with the principle of the independence of perpendicular motions. He demonstrated that in projectile motion, the horizontal and vertical components of motion act independently of each other.
2.  **Distance travelled for a falling body increases at an increasing rate:** Through his experiments with inclined planes, Galileo determined that for a body starting from rest, the distance traveled $d$ is proportional to the square of the time elapsed ($d \propto t^2$). This means the distance increases at an increasing rate (acceleration).
3.  **Why the other options are incorrect:**
    *   **Acceleration due to gravity is $9.8 \, \text{ms}^{-2}$:** While Galileo established that all objects fall with constant acceleration regardless of mass, the specific value of $9.8 \, \text{ms}^{-2}$ is a modern measurement of $g$ that was not defined by Galileo. However, in the context of many HSC-level multiple-choice questions, "Acceleration due to gravity" is often used as a conceptual placeholder for the constant acceleration he discovered.
    *   **"The more mass an object has, the larger the gravitational force":** This is a statement of Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation, not Galileo's work. Galileo specifically argued *against* the Aristotelian view that heavier objects fall faster, proving that all objects fall at the same rate in a vacuum.
    *   **$1/r^2$ relationship:** This is the Inverse Square Law, which was formulated by Isaac Newton, not Galileo.

Option **A** is the best fit because it correctly identifies the independence of perpendicular motion and the relationship between distance and time for falling bodies (acceleration).
