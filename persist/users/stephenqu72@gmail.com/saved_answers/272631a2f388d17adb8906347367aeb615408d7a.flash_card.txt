### Front
In projectile motion, compare the time of flight and final velocity of an object dropped from rest ($P$) versus one projected horizontally ($Q$) from the same height.

### Back
- Key idea: Horizontal and vertical motions are independent. Both experience the same constant vertical acceleration ($g$).
- Formula / law / constant: $h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$; $v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$
- When to use it: To compare kinematics of projectiles launched from the same height.
- Common trap: Assuming the object with a horizontal velocity ($Q$) takes longer to reach the ground; it does not, as vertical motion is independent of horizontal motion.
