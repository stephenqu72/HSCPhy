### Front
How do gravitational potential ($V$) and gravitational field strength ($g$) relate to the distance ($r$) from a central mass?

### Back
- **Key idea:** $V$ is inversely proportional to $r$ ($V \propto 1/r$), while $g$ is inversely proportional to the square of $r$ ($g \propto 1/r^2$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $V = -GM/r$; $g = GM/r^2$.
- **When to use it:** Use when comparing potential or field strength at two different radial distances from a point mass.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that gravitational potential ($V$) is always negative; when comparing values, ensure you are comparing their magnitudes ($|V|$) to avoid sign errors in ratios.
