### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to recall the classification of subatomic particles as defined by the Standard Model of particle physics, which is a key component of the HSC Physics syllabus (specifically within the "From the Universe to the Atom" or "Quanta to Quarks" context).

1.  **Define Hadrons:** Hadrons are composite subatomic particles made of quarks held together by the strong nuclear force. They are divided into two main categories:
    *   **Baryons:** Made of three quarks (e.g., protons are $uud$, and neutrons are $udd$).
    *   **Mesons:** Made of a quark and an antiquark pair.
2.  **Evaluate the options:**
    *   **(A) Neutrinos:** These are leptons, which are fundamental particles and are not constituents of hadrons.
    *   **(B) Electrons:** These are also leptons, which are fundamental particles, not composed of quarks.
    *   **(C) Quarks:** According to the Standard Model, hadrons are defined specifically as particles composed of quarks.
    *   **(D) Bosons:** While gluons (which are gauge bosons) mediate the force that binds quarks together, they are not considered the "matter constituents" of the hadron itself in the way that quarks are.

Therefore, quarks are the fundamental building blocks that make up all hadrons.
