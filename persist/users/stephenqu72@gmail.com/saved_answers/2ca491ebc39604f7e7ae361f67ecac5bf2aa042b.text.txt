To find the surface gravitational field of Mars compared to Earth, we use the formula for gravitational field strength $g$:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

### Step-by-step Solution:

1.  **Define the variables in terms of Earth:**
    *   Let $M_E$ be the mass of Earth and $R_E$ be the radius of Earth.
    *   The mass of Mars is given as $M_M = 0.1 M_E$.
    *   The diameter of Mars is $0.5$ times that of Earth. Since radius is half of diameter, the radius of Mars is also $0.5$ times that of Earth: $R_M = 0.5 R_E$.

2.  **Set up the ratio:**
    The gravitational field of Mars ($g_M$) relative to Earth ($g_E$) is:
    $$\frac{g_M}{g_E} = \frac{\frac{G M_M}{R_M^2}}{\frac{G M_E}{R_E^2}}$$

3.  **Simplify the expression:**
    The constant $G$ cancels out:
    $$\frac{g_M}{g_E} = \frac{M_M}{M_E} \times \left( \frac{R_E}{R_M} \right)^2$$

4.  **Substitute the given values:**
    $$\frac{g_M}{g_E} = 0.1 \times \left( \frac{1}{0.5} \right)^2$$
    $$\frac{g_M}{g_E} = 0.1 \times (2)^2$$
    $$\frac{g_M}{g_E} = 0.1 \times 4$$
    $$\frac{g_M}{g_E} = 0.4$$

Therefore, the gravitational field of Mars is $0.4g$.
