To answer this question effectively for the HSC Physics exam, you need to provide a clear, annotated sketch that distinguishes the two processes. Since this is a 2-mark question, each diagram is worth 1 mark.

### Answer Sketch Description

**Nuclear Fission:**
Draw a large nucleus (e.g., Uranium-235) being struck by a small particle (a neutron). Show it splitting into two smaller "daughter" nuclei, releasing two or three neutrons and energy.
*   **Visual:** `Neutron + Large Nucleus -> 2 Smaller Nuclei + 3 Neutrons + Energy`

**Nuclear Fusion:**
Draw two small, light nuclei (e.g., isotopes of Hydrogen like Deuterium and Tritium) coming together to form a single, larger nucleus (e.g., Helium). Show a neutron being released alongside energy.
*   **Visual:** `Small Nucleus + Small Nucleus -> Larger Nucleus + Neutron + Energy`

---

### How to solve it
1.  **Understand the core concepts:** 
    *   **Fission:** The process of *splitting* a heavy, unstable nucleus into two lighter ones. This is the basis of current nuclear power plants.
    *   **Fusion:** The process of *combining* two light nuclei to form a single, heavier nucleus. This is the process that powers the Sun and other stars.
2.  **Focus on labels:** Always label your sketches. Ensure you indicate the incoming and outgoing neutrons. 
3.  **Represent Mass/Energy:** Clearly show that in fission, you go from one big circle to two smaller ones, and in fusion, you go from two small circles to one big one.
4.  **Marking Criteria:** In an HSC exam, you get 1 mark for the correct representation of fission and 1 mark for the correct representation of fusion. Including the emission of neutrons and energy is vital for full marks.
