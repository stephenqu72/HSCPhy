### Solution and Explanation

**Given Data:**
*   Mass $m = 2.4 \times 10^{-7} \text{ kg}$
*   Charge $q = +1.4 \times 10^{-2} \text{ C}$
*   Velocity $v = 2.7 \times 10^2 \text{ m/s}$
*   Magnetic field $B = 3.6 \times 10^{-2} \text{ T}$

---

#### (a) Determine the force acting on the particle
When a charged particle moves through a magnetic field, it experiences a magnetic force given by $F = qvB \sin(\theta)$. Since the velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field lines (into the page), $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

$$F = (1.4 \times 10^{-2} \text{ C}) \times (2.7 \times 10^2 \text{ m/s}) \times (3.6 \times 10^{-2} \text{ T})$$
$$F = 0.13608 \text{ N} \approx 0.14 \text{ N}$$
Using the Right-Hand Rule (fingers point into page, thumb points right), the force direction is **upwards**.

---

#### (b) Calculate the radius of the circular path
The magnetic force provides the centripetal force required for circular motion: $F_B = F_c$.
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Rearranging for $r$:
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$
$$r = \frac{(2.4 \times 10^{-7} \text{ kg}) \times (2.7 \times 10^2 \text{ m/s})}{(1.4 \times 10^{-2} \text{ C}) \times (3.6 \times 10^{-2} \text{ T})}$$
$$r = \frac{6.48 \times 10^{-5}}{5.04 \times 10^{-4}} \approx 0.1286 \text{ m} \approx 0.13 \text{ m}$$

---

#### (c) Similarities and differences in motion
*   **Similarities:** All three fields can exert a force on an object leading to acceleration. They can all be described using field intensity concepts ($E$, $B$, and $g$).
*   **Differences:**
    *   **Electric Fields:** Exert a force $F = qE$ on charges. If the field is uniform and the charge enters perpendicular to it, the particle follows a **parabolic** path (similar to projectile motion). The force is independent of velocity.
    *   **Magnetic Fields:** Exert a force $F = qvB\sin(\theta)$ only on moving charges. The force is always perpendicular to velocity, resulting in **uniform circular motion** (no change in kinetic energy). 
    *   **Gravitational Fields:** Exert a force $F = mg$ on mass. It acts on all matter (not just charged). If the field is uniform, a mass moves in a **parabolic** path (projectile motion) in a vacuum. Unlike magnetic fields, gravity performs work on objects if they move in the direction of the field.
