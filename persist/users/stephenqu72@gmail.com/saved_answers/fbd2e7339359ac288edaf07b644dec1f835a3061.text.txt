### Answer
The correct answer is **A**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to understand the historical context of Newton's corpuscular (particle) theory of light:

1.  **The Mechanism:** Newton proposed that light consisted of tiny particles ("corpuscles"). He theorized that these particles experienced an attractive force directed perpendicularly toward the surface of a denser medium.
2.  **Vector Analysis:** As a light particle approached a denser medium (like glass or water), this attractive force would accelerate the particle in the direction normal to the interface. While the component of the particle's velocity parallel to the surface remained constant, the component perpendicular to the surface increased.
3.  **Resulting Change:** This increase in the perpendicular velocity component caused the overall velocity vector of the light particle to bend toward the normal, explaining why light "refracts" (bends) when entering a denser medium.
4.  **Flaw in the Theory:** A crucial, though incorrect, prediction of Newton's model was that light must travel **faster** in a denser medium to satisfy the conservation of energy and momentum required for this acceleration. We now know from Foucault's later experiments that light actually slows down in denser media, which eventually led to the abandonment of the particle theory in favor of the wave theory.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **B** is incorrect because Newton’s model actually predicted that particles would *speed up* in a denser medium, not slow down.
*   **C** describes scattering, which is not the mechanism for refraction.
*   **D** is incorrect because the angle of incidence is never equal to the angle of refraction (unless the light is hitting the surface at $0^\circ$ normal incidence).
