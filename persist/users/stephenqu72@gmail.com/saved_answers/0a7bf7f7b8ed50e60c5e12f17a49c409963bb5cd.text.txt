To answer this question, we apply the **Right-Hand Rule** (also known as the motor rule) for the magnetic force on a current-carrying wire.

### The Physics Principle
The magnetic force $\vec{F}$ on a current-carrying conductor is given by the cross product:
$$\vec{F} = I(\vec{L} \times \vec{B})$$
Where:
*   $I$ is the current.
*   $\vec{L}$ is the length vector in the direction of the current.
*   $\vec{B}$ is the external magnetic field.

Using the **Right-Hand Rule**:
1.  Point your **fingers** in the direction of the conventional **current ($I$)**.
2.  Curl your fingers towards the direction of the **magnetic field ($B$)**.
3.  Your **extended thumb** points in the direction of the **force ($F$)**.
