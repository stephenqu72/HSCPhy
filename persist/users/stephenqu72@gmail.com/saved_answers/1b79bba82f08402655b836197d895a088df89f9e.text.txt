### Answer
To successfully negotiate a circular curve, a centripetal force is required to change the direction of the car's velocity. This is given by:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

1.  **On the flat icy road:** The only horizontal force available to provide this centripetal force is static friction between the tires and the road. Since the surface is icy, the coefficient of friction is effectively zero (or negligible). Without friction, there is no horizontal force to act as the centripetal force, meaning the car cannot maintain a circular path and will continue moving in a straight line (Newton's First Law).
2.  **On the banked road:** When the road is banked at an angle $\theta$, the normal force $N$ exerted by the road is no longer vertical. The vertical component of the normal force, $N \cos \theta$, balances the weight of the car ($mg$), while the horizontal component, $N \sin \theta$, acts toward the centre of the circular path. This horizontal component provides the necessary centripetal force even in the absence of friction. Therefore, the car can safely navigate the curve without relying on the icy surface for traction.
