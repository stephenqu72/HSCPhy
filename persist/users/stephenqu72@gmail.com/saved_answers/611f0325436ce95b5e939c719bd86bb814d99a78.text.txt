To solve this problem, we must understand the physics of single-slit diffraction.

### Explanation

The condition for the first dark fringe (minimum) in a single-slit diffraction pattern is given by the formula:
$$a \sin \theta = n \lambda$$
For the first minimum ($n=1$), we have:
$$a \sin \theta = \lambda$$
For small angles, $\sin \theta \approx \tan \theta \approx \frac{y}{D}$, where $y$ is the distance from the central maximum to the first minimum, and $D$ is the distance to the screen. Thus, the angular width of the central maximum is proportional to:
$$\theta \approx \frac{\lambda}{a}$$

**Analyzing the image:**

$$\theta_1 > \theta_2$$
2. Since $\theta$ is proportional to the ratio $\frac{\lambda}{a}$, we can conclude that:
$$\frac{\lambda_1}{a_1} > \frac{\lambda_2}{a_2}$$

This relationship shows that for $S_1$, the ratio of wavelength to slit width is larger, which is consistent with its broader diffraction pattern.
