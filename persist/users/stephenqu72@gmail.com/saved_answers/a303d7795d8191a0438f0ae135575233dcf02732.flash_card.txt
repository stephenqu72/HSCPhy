### Front
What principles link the energy and motion of a mass falling onto a vertical spring?

### Back
- Key idea: Conservation of Mechanical Energy ($\Delta E_g = \Delta E_s$) and Equilibrium at maximum speed ($\sum F=0$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $mgh = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$ (energy) and $mg = kx$ (equilibrium).
- When to use it: Calculating spring constant ($k$), maximum compression, or velocity in vertical mass-spring systems.
- Common trap: Forgetting that total height $h$ must include both the initial drop and the final compression distance $x$.
