To solve this problem, we use the formula for the magnetic force on a current-carrying conductor: $F = nILB\sin(\theta)$, where $n$ is the number of turns, $I$ is current, $L$ is length, $B$ is magnetic field strength, and $\theta$ is the angle between the current and the magnetic field.

### Part (a)
Side $AB$ has a length of $0.12\text{ m}$. The current flows horizontally to the right, and the magnetic field is directed vertically upwards. Therefore, the angle between them is $90^\circ$ ($\sin(90^\circ) = 1$).

**Calculation:**
$$F = nILB = 150 \times 1.5\text{ A} \times 0.12\text{ m} \times 0.06\text{ T} = 1.62\text{ N}$$

**Direction:** Using the Right-Hand Rule (fingers point up, thumb points right, palm faces out), the force is directed **out of the page**.

### Part (b)
Side $BC$ has a length of $0.08\text{ m}$. The current flows downwards, and the magnetic field points upwards. These two vectors are anti-parallel (the angle between them is $180^\circ$).

**Calculation:**
Since $\sin(180^\circ) = 0$:
$$F = nILB \sin(180^\circ) = 0\text{ N}$$
**Direction:** There is **no force** exerted on side $BC$.

### Part (c)
Since the force on $AB$ is directed out of the page and the force on $DC$ (by symmetry/Left-hand rule) will be directed into the page, the coil will experience a torque. Looking from above, the coil will rotate **clockwise**.

### Summary of Answers:
(a) Magnitude: $1.62\text{ N}$; Direction: Out of the page.
