### Front
How does the launch angle ($\theta$) affect the time taken for a projectile to reach its maximum height?

### Back
- Key idea: Time to peak is directly proportional to the vertical velocity component ($v \sin\theta$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $t = \frac{v \sin\theta}{g}$
- When to use it: To compare the trajectory duration of projectiles with different launch angles and the same initial velocity.
- Common trap: Confusing time to reach peak height with total time of flight or horizontal range.
