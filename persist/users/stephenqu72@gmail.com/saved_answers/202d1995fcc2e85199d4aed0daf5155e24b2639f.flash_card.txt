### Front
How did the evolution of particle detection technology influence the development of the Standard Model?

### Back
- Key idea: Increased collision energy in accelerators/detectors revealed a "zoo" of new particles, necessitating a unified framework to explain their classification and interactions.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: To answer 5-mark "Discuss" or "Explain" questions linking technological advancements (cyclotrons, bubble chambers) to theoretical physics.
- Common trap: Forgetting to explicitly link the **experimental data** from high-energy collisions to the **theoretical requirement** for a model like the Standard Model; don't just describe the tech in isolation.
