### Answer
The correct answer is **D. Proton**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must understand the definition of a "fundamental particle" within the context of the Standard Model of particle physics. A fundamental particle is a particle that is not composed of any smaller constituent parts; it is "elementary."

1.  **Electrons** (Option A) are leptons and are classified as fundamental particles because they have no known internal structure.
2.  **Gluons** (Option B) are gauge bosons (force carriers) and are fundamental particles because they mediate the strong nuclear force and are not composed of smaller sub-particles.
3.  **Muons** (Option C) are second-generation leptons and, like electrons, are fundamental particles.
4.  **Protons** (Option D) are **not** fundamental particles. They are composite particles classified as hadrons (specifically baryons). According to the Standard Model, a proton is composed of three valence quarks held together by gluons: two **up quarks** and one **down quark** (represented as $uud$).

Since the question asks to identify which particle is **NOT** fundamental, the proton is the correct choice because it has an internal structure.
