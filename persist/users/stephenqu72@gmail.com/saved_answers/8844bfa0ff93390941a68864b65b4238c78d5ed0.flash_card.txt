### Front
Explain how the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) provides evidence for the Big Bang model in an HSC Physics context.

### Back
- **Key idea:** The CMB is the "afterglow" of the hot, dense early universe, stretched into microwaves by the cosmological expansion of space.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\approx 2.7\text{ K}$ (current temperature of the CMB).
- **When to use it:** When asked for evidence supporting the Big Bang theory or the evolution of the early universe (recombination/decoupling).
- **Common trap:** Confusing "cosmological redshift" with the Doppler effect; clarify that it is space itself expanding, not the motion of galaxies through space.
