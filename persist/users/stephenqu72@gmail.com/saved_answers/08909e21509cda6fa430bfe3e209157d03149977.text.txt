### Explanation
To understand why **A** is the correct answer, we must recall James Clerk Maxwell’s synthesis of electromagnetism:

1.  **The Source of EM Waves:** Maxwell unified earlier discoveries (by Gauss, Faraday, and Ampère) and proposed his "displacement current" theory. He concluded that an **accelerating charged particle** is the fundamental source of an electromagnetic wave.
2.  **The Mechanism:**
    *   An accelerating charge creates a time-varying (changing) electric field.
    *   According to the Ampère-Maxwell law, a changing electric field creates a changing magnetic field.
    *   A changing magnetic field, in turn, creates a changing electric field (Faraday’s Law).
    *   This self-sustaining cycle of mutually inducing electric and magnetic fields propagates through space as an electromagnetic wave.

**Evaluating the other options:**
*   **B is incorrect** because stationary charges produce constant electric fields, not changing ones, and they do not generate electromagnetic waves.
*   **C and D are incorrect** because Maxwell’s theory of electromagnetism deals with electric and magnetic fields. Gravitational fields are a distinct domain described by Newtonian physics or General Relativity and do not play a role in the production of EM waves.

### Study Tip
In the HSC exam, remember the "Acceleration" rule:
*   **Stationary charge:** Static electric field.
*   **Constant velocity charge:** Electric field plus a constant magnetic field (no EM wave).
*   **Accelerating charge:** Oscillating electric and magnetic fields (EM wave).
