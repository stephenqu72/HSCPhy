To solve this problem, we use Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion, which states that the square of the orbital period ($T$) is proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis ($r$):
$$T^2 \propto r^3 \implies T \propto r^{3/2}$$

### Step 1: Calculate the orbital periods in Earth years
Given the orbital radii $r_A = 1\text{ au}$, $r_P = 4\text{ au}$, and $r_{Ar} = 9\text{ au}$:
*   For Athos: $T_A = (1)^{3/2} = 1\text{ Earth year}$
*   For Porthos: $T_P = (4)^{3/2} = 8\text{ Earth years}$
*   For Aramis: $T_{Ar} = (9)^{3/2} = 27\text{ Earth years}$

### Step 2: Determine the angular velocities
The angular velocity $\omega$ (in radians per Earth year) is given by $\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T}$:
*   $\omega_A = 2\pi$
*   $\omega_P = \frac{2\pi}{8} = \frac{\pi}{4}$
*   $\omega_{Ar} = \frac{2\pi}{27}$

### Step 3: Find the time for the next alignment
The planets realign when the relative angular displacements are integer multiples of $2\pi$. We look for the smallest time $t > 0$ such that:
1.  $(\omega_A - \omega_P)t = 2\pi n$
2.  $(\omega_A - \omega_{Ar})t = 2\pi m$
where $n, m$ are integers.

Using the first condition:
$$(2\pi - \frac{\pi}{4})t = 2\pi n \implies \frac{7\pi}{4}t = 2\pi n \implies t = \frac{8}{7}n$$
Using the second condition:
$$(2\pi - \frac{2\pi}{27})t = 2\pi m \implies \frac{25\pi}{27}t = 2\pi m \implies t = \frac{54}{25}m$$
Setting them equal: $\frac{8}{7}n = \frac{54}{25}m \implies \frac{n}{m} = \frac{54 \times 7}{8 \times 25} = \frac{378}{200} = \frac{189}{100}$
The smallest integer solution is $n = 189, m = 100$.
$$t = \frac{8}{7} \times 189 = 8 \times 27 = 216\text{ Earth years}$$

### Step 4: Convert to Athos years
Since the period of Athos is 1 Earth year, 216 Earth years equals 216 Athos years.
