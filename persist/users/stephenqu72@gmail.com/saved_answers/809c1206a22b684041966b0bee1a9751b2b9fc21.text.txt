### Answer
Both charged particles in a uniform electric field and projectiles in a uniform gravitational field exhibit **parabolic motion** when entering the field perpendicularly to the field lines.

1.  **Similarities:**
    *   **Constant Acceleration:** In both cases, the particle experiences a constant force acting in one direction only ($F_g = mg$ for gravity; $F_e = qE$ for electric fields). According to Newton’s Second Law ($F = ma$), this results in a constant acceleration in the direction of the field.
    *   **Independence of Motion:** Both types of motion can be analyzed using independent components: uniform velocity in the horizontal direction and uniformly accelerated motion in the vertical direction.
    *   **Trajectory:** Both follow a parabolic path, which can be described mathematically by the kinematic equation $y = \frac{1}{2}at^2$, leading to a quadratic relationship between vertical displacement and horizontal distance.

2.  **Differences:**
    *   **Force Origin:** Gravitational force acts on mass, whereas electric force acts on charge.
    *   **Directionality:** Gravity is strictly attractive (towards the centre of the Earth). Electric fields can be attractive or repulsive depending on the polarity of the charge relative to the field.
    *   **Magnitude:** Electric forces on subatomic particles are typically many orders of magnitude stronger than gravitational forces, meaning gravitational effects are often negligible when calculating the motion of an electron in an electric field.

### How to solve this question
To score full marks on a "Compare" question in the HSC, follow this strategy:

1.  **Use a structured approach:** Use a table or clear bullet points to explicitly identify "Similarities" and "Differences." The command term "Compare" requires you to look at both.
2.  **Use Physics Equations:** Link the concepts by showing the mathematical similarities.
    *   For projectiles: $a = g$
    *   For charged particles: $a = \frac{qE}{m}$
    *   By showing both lead to $a = \text{constant}$, you prove they are mathematically analogous.
3.  **Reference the Kinematics:** Mention that horizontal velocity remains constant in both scenarios because there is no component of force acting in the horizontal direction (assuming no air resistance for projectiles).
