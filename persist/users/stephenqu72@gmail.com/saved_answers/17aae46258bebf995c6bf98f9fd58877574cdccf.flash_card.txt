### Front
What is the direction of the net force acting on an object undergoing uniform circular motion, and why?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The net force (centripetal force) is directed towards the centre of the circle to constantly change the object's direction (velocity vector), despite constant speed.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Newton’s Second Law ($F_{net} = ma_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$)
- **When to use it:** Whenever an object moves along a curved path or circular trajectory.
- **Common trap:** Confusing centripetal force with "centrifugal force"—an imaginary force that does not exist in an inertial frame of reference.
