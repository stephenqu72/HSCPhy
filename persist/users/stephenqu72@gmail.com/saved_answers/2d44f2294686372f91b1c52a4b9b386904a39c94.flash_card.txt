### Front
What is the quark composition of a neutron, and why?

### Back
- **Key idea:** A neutron is a baryon (three-quark particle) with a net charge of 0.
- **Formula:** Charge of Up ($u$) = $+2/3e$; Charge of Down ($d$) = $-1/3e$. Calculation: $u + d + d \rightarrow (+2/3) + (-1/3) + (-1/3) = 0$.
- **When to use it:** When asked about the subatomic structure of baryons or the Standard Model of matter.
- **Common trap:** Confusing a neutron ($udd$) with a proton ($uud$, which sums to $+1$).
