### Front
For a rigid body rotating at a constant angular speed ($\omega$), how do the linear velocity ($v$) and centripetal acceleration ($a$) of two points at different radial distances ($r_P$ vs $r_Q$) compare?

### Back
- Key idea: Points on a rotating rigid body share the same angular speed ($\omega$), but linear properties scale linearly ($v \propto r$) or linearly ($a \propto r$).
- Formula / law: $v = r\omega$ and $a = r\omega^2$.
- When to use it: Circular motion problems involving rotating disks, rods, or pulleys where multiple points are fixed to the same body.
- Common trap: Assuming points have the same linear speed or acceleration; always remember that $\omega$ is the shared constant, not $v$.
