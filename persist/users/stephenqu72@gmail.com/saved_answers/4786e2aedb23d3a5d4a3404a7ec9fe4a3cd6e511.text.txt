To solve this problem, we use the radioactive decay formula:

$$N(t) = N_0 \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{\frac{t}{T_{1/2}}}$$

Where:
*   $N(t)$ is the remaining amount of the substance.
*   $N_0$ is the initial amount (we can set this to $100\%$ to find the percentage remaining).
*   $t$ is the elapsed time ($5.50$ minutes).
*   $T_{1/2}$ is the half-life ($10.0$ minutes).

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    $t = 5.50$
    $T_{1/2} = 10.0$

2.  **Calculate the number of half-lives elapsed ($n$):**
    $$n = \frac{t}{T_{1/2}} = \frac{5.50}{10.0} = 0.55$$

3.  **Apply the formula:**
    $$N(t) = 100 \times (0.5)^{0.55}$$

4.  **Solve the exponent:**
    Using a calculator: $0.5^{0.55} \approx 0.6817$

5.  **Convert to percentage:**
    $$0.6817 \times 100 \approx 68.17\%$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number provided in the options, we get $68\%$.
