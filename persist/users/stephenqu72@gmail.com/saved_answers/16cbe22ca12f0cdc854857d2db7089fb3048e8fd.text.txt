### Explanation:
To solve this, we must recall Johannes Kepler's three laws of planetary motion, which describe the movement of planets around the Sun:

1.  **The Law of Ellipses (Kepler's First Law):** All planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one of the focal points. This matches option **(B)** perfectly.
2.  **The Law of Equal Areas (Kepler's Second Law):** A line segment joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time.
3.  **The Law of Harmonies (Kepler's Third Law):** The square of the orbital period $T$ of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis $r$ of its orbit: $T^2 \propto r^3$.
