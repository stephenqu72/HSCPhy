### Answer
The correct answer is **C**.

### Explanation

1.  **Understand the frame of reference:** We are observing the wires from the frame of reference of a positive charge in Wire $Y$. In this frame, the positive charges in Wire $Y$ are stationary, while the positive charges in Wire $X$ are moving at a velocity $v$ (due to the current flowing in the opposite direction).

2.  **Length Contraction:** According to special relativity, objects moving relative to an observer undergo length contraction in the direction of motion. The length $L$ of an object as measured by an observer moving at velocity $v$ is given by:
    $$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
    where $L_0$ is the proper length (the length measured in the object's rest frame).

3.  **Analyze Wire $Y$:** Since we are in the frame of reference of the positive charges in Wire $Y$, these charges are at rest. They maintain their "proper" spacing, which is what we see in the original diagram.

4.  **Analyze Wire $X$:** The positive charges in Wire $X$ are moving relative to us. Because of length contraction, the space *between* the moving charges in Wire $X$ appears contracted. When the spacing between charges decreases, the **linear charge density** ($\lambda = \frac{q}{L}$) appears to **increase**. Therefore, from our frame of reference, Wire $X$ appears to have a higher density of positive charges.

5.  **Conclusion:** 
    *   Wire $Y$ (stationary relative to us) maintains its original charge distribution.
    *   Wire $X$ (moving relative to us) appears to have a higher concentration of positive charges due to length contraction.
