### Explanation
To solve this, we need to understand the **Doppler Effect** as it applies to light waves.

1.  **Understanding the Physics:** In the electromagnetic spectrum, red light has a longer wavelength and a lower frequency compared to blue or violet light.
2.  **The Doppler Effect:** When an object (like a galaxy) is moving away from an observer, the light waves it emits are "stretched" as they travel through space. This stretching results in a longer wavelength.
3.  **The Terminology:** Because the wavelength has increased (shifted toward the red end of the spectrum), we call this phenomenon **redshift**. 
4.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   (A) describes stellar evolution, not light behavior.
    *   (C) refers to physical equipment (filters), not a fundamental astronomical observation.
    *   (D) is incorrect because an increase in frequency would correspond to a *decrease* in wavelength (blueshift), which happens when an object is moving *towards* the observer.

Therefore, redshift is specifically the observed increase in wavelength as a result of an object moving away from Earth.
