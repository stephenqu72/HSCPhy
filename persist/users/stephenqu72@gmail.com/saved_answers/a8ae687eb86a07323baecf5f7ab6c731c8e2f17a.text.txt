To determine the correct answer, we must apply the principles of electromagnetism, specifically the **Right-Hand Rule** for magnetic forces on charges.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the Force on Charges:**
    When a conductive bar moves through a magnetic field $B$ at a velocity $v$, the free electrons inside the bar experience a magnetic force given by the Lorentz force law:
    $$F = q(v \times B)$$
    In this case, $q$ is the charge of an electron (which is negative).

2.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule (RHR):**
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the velocity vector $v$ (to the right).
    *   Curl your fingers towards the direction of the magnetic field $B$ (up the page).
    *   Your thumb points in the direction of the force on a **positive** charge. This would be **out of the page**.
    *   Since electrons have a **negative** charge, the force on them is in the opposite direction. Therefore, the electrons are pushed **into the page**.

3.  **Determine Electron Density:**
    Because the electrons are pushed towards one side of the bar (into the page), that side of the bar will accumulate a higher concentration of electrons, while the opposite side will become positively charged (depleted of electrons). Therefore, the measured electron density increases at the end of the bar moving **into the page**.

### Conclusion:
The magnetic force pushes the free electrons towards the "back" of the bar relative to the cross-product direction, which points them into the page.

**Correct Answer: C. Moving into the page**
