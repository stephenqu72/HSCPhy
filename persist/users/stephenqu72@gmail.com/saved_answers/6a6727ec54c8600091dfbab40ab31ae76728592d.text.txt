To solve this problem, we apply the principles of electromagnetism, specifically the **Motor Effect** (force on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field) and **Right-Hand Rules**.

### Part (a)
**Answer:** The current flows from the positive terminal of the battery, along the top rail, **down through the metal rod**, and back along the bottom rail to the negative terminal.

**Explanation:**
The motor effect states that the magnetic force $F$ is given by $F = IlB \sin\theta$. According to the **Right-Hand Rule for the Motor Effect** (or Fleming’s Left-Hand Rule):
*   Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field (vertically **upwards**).
*   Point your thumb in the direction of the magnetic force (to the **right**, as stated in the question).
*   Your palm will face in the direction of the current through the rod.
*   By placing your palm facing the rod, your fingers point into the page/downward relative to the rod's position. Since the force is to the right, the current must flow through the rod from the top rail toward the bottom rail.

### Part (b)
**Answer:** The electromagnetic force is $100\text{ N}$.

**Explanation:**
1.  **Calculate the current ($I$):** Use Ohm's Law ($V = IR$).
    $$I = \frac{V}{R} = \frac{20\text{ V}}{0.02\ \Omega} = 1000\text{ A}$$
2.  **Calculate the magnetic force ($F$):** Use the motor effect formula $F = IlB \sin\theta$. 
    *   The rod is perpendicular to the magnetic field, so $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.
    *   Convert length to meters: $L = 8\text{ cm} = 0.08\text{ m}$.
    $$F = 1000\text{ A} \times 0.08\text{ m} \times 0.005\text{ T}$$
    $$F = 80 \times 0.005 = 0.4\text{ N}$$

*(Correction note: Based on the provided values $20\text{ V}$, $0.02\ \Omega$, $8\text{ cm}$, and $0.005\text{ T}$, the force is $0.4\text{ N}$.)*
