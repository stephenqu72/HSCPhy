### Front
In a horizontal circular motion apparatus, why does the graph of centripetal force ($F$) versus velocity ($v$) result in a concave-up parabola?

### Back
- Key idea: Centripetal force is directly proportional to the square of velocity ($F \propto v^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: When analyzing circular motion experiments where mass ($m$) and radius ($r$) are kept constant.
- Common trap: Confusing the $F \propto v^2$ relationship with the $F \propto 1/r$ (inverse) relationship; always check which variables are held constant.
