To solve these problems, we use the standard physical constants for Earth:
- Mass of Earth, $M = 5.97 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$
- Gravitational constant, $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2/\text{kg}^2$
- Radius of Earth, $R_E = 6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
- Average altitude, $h = 300 \text{ km} = 3.00 \times 10^5 \text{ m}$
- Orbital radius, $r = R_E + h = 6.67 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
- Satellite mass, $m = 100 \text{ kg}$

---

### (a) Calculate the predicted period of the satellite
Use Kepler’s Third Law: $\frac{T^2}{r^3} = \frac{4\pi^2}{GM}$.

$$T = \sqrt{\frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{GM}} = \sqrt{\frac{4\pi^2 (6.67 \times 10^6)^3}{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(5.97 \times 10^{24})}}$$
$$T \approx \sqrt{2.79 \times 10^7} \approx 5285 \text{ seconds} \approx 88.1 \text{ minutes}$$

### (b) Determine the total energy of the satellite
The total energy of a satellite in orbit is given by the sum of kinetic and gravitational potential energy, which simplifies to:
$$E = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$$
$$E = -\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(5.97 \times 10^{24})(100)}{2(6.67 \times 10^6)}$$
$$E \approx -2.98 \times 10^9 \text{ J}$$

### (c) Calculate the average orbital speed of the satellite in orbit
Orbital speed $v$ is calculated using the circumference divided by the period:
$$v = \frac{2\pi r}{T} = \frac{2\pi (6.67 \times 10^6)}{5285}$$
$$v \approx 7927 \text{ m/s} \approx 7.93 \text{ km/s}$$

---

### Explanation of Approach
1. **Identify parameters:** Always convert altitudes to distances from the center of the Earth ($r = R_E + h$).
2. **Kepler's Law:** For period, rearrange the orbital mechanics formula. Ensure all units are in SI (meters, seconds, kg).
3. **Energy:** Remember that total mechanical energy in a bound orbit is always negative, representing the energy required to remove the satellite to infinity.
4. **Velocity:** Since the path is circular (or approximated as such for average speed), the simplest calculation is the total distance of one orbit ($2\pi r$) divided by the orbital period ($T$).
