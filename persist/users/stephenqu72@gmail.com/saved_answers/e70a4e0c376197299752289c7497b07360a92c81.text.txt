To solve this problem, we apply **Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion**, which states that the square of the orbital period ($T$) of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the average distance ($r$) from the star:

$$T^2 \propto r^3 \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{T_1^2}{T_2^2} = \frac{r_1^3}{r_2^3}$$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the given information:**
    Let $r_X$ be the distance of Planet X and $r_Y$ be the distance of Planet Y. We are told that $r_X = 5r_Y$.
    Therefore, the ratio of the distances is:
    $$\frac{r_X}{r_Y} = 5$$

2.  **Set up the ratio for the orbital periods:**
    Using Kepler's Third Law:
    $$\left( \frac{T_X}{T_Y} \right)^2 = \left( \frac{r_X}{r_Y} \right)^3$$

3.  **Substitute the known value:**
    Substitute $\frac{r_X}{r_Y} = 5$ into the equation:
    $$\left( \frac{T_X}{T_Y} \right)^2 = 5^3$$

4.  **Solve for the ratio of the orbital periods:**
    Take the square root of both sides to isolate $\frac{T_X}{T_Y}$:
    $$\frac{T_X}{T_Y} = \sqrt{5^3}$$
    Using exponent notation, $\sqrt{5^3} = (5^3)^{\frac{1}{2}} = 5^{\frac{3}{2}}$.

5.  **Match with the given options:**
    Looking at the options, we need to express the result to match the form provided. 
    Option D is $\sqrt{5^3}$.
    Calculation check: 
    $\frac{T_X}{T_Y} = (5^3)^{1/2} = 5^{3/2}$.
    Option D matches this mathematical value exactly.

**Correct Answer:** **D**
