To solve this problem, we must apply the principle of the conservation of energy in atomic transitions. When an electron transitions from a higher energy level $E_n$ to a lower energy level $E_m$, it emits a photon with energy equal to the difference between these levels:

$$\Delta E = E_n - E_m = hf$$

where $h$ is Planck's constant and $f$ is the frequency of the emitted photon. Therefore, the frequency of the emitted light is directly proportional to the energy gap between the levels: $f \propto \Delta E$.
