To determine the path of the ball as seen by Mary, we must apply the principle of **Relative Motion**.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(C)**.

### Explanation
1.  **Reference Frames:** 
    *   From **John’s perspective** (stationary on the platform), the ball has no horizontal velocity. It simply accelerates downwards due to gravity. The path is a straight vertical line.
    *   From **Mary’s perspective** (moving in the train at a constant velocity $v$), the ball is not just moving downwards; it also has a relative horizontal velocity of $-v$ (it appears to be moving backward relative to her).

2.  **Equations of Motion:**
    *   Vertical motion ($y$): The ball accelerates under gravity ($g$). The vertical displacement is given by $y = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$. This results in a downward path where the velocity increases over time.
    *   Horizontal motion ($x$): Relative to the train, the ball moves horizontally at a constant speed $v$ in the opposite direction of the train's travel. The displacement is $x = vt$.

3.  **Analyzing the Path:**
    *   If we combine these motions, the horizontal displacement $x$ is proportional to time $t$, and the vertical displacement $y$ is proportional to $t^2$.
    *   Substituting $t = \frac{x}{v}$ into the vertical equation $y = \frac{1}{2}g(\frac{x}{v})^2$, we get:
        $$y = \left(\frac{g}{2v^2}\right)x^2$$
    *   This is the equation of a **parabola**. 

4.  **Direction:**
    *   As the train moves forward, the ball will appear to "sweep" backward relative to the train.
