**Answer:**
One major error is **parallax error**. Because the ruler is placed on the ground significantly in front of the wall where the trajectory occurs, the camera will record different positions for the object relative to the markings on the ruler depending on the camera's exact angle.

**Explanation:**
When measuring objects in a plane (the wall) using a scale in a different plane (the ground), the line of sight from the camera creates a projection error. This means the measured horizontal distances will be inaccurate, as the ruler does not lie in the same plane as the motion of the ball. To fix this, the ruler should be placed against the wall directly behind the trajectory path.
