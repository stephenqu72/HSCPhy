To solve this problem, we apply the concept of **length contraction** from Einstein’s theory of Special Relativity.

### Answer
The correct answer is **B. $\sqrt{\frac{8}{9}}c$**.

---

### Step-by-Step Explanation

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   Let $L_0$ be the rest length of the spaceship.
    *   Let $L$ be the observed length of the spaceship as it passes the station.
    *   We are given that $L = \frac{1}{3}L_0$.

2.  **Recall the length contraction formula:**
    The relationship between the observed length and the rest length is given by:
    $$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$

3.  **Substitute the given values into the formula:**
    $$\frac{1}{3}L_0 = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$

4.  **Solve for $v$:**
    *   Divide both sides by $L_0$:
        $$\frac{1}{3} = \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
    *   Square both sides to remove the square root:
        $$\frac{1}{9} = 1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$$
    *   Rearrange the equation to isolate the velocity term:
        $$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 1 - \frac{1}{9}$$
        $$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = \frac{8}{9}$$
    *   Take the square root of both sides:
        $$\frac{v}{c} = \sqrt{\frac{8}{9}}$$
        $$v = \sqrt{\frac{8}{9}}c$$

Thus, the speed of the spaceship is $\sqrt{\frac{8}{9}}c$.
