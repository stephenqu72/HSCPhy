As an HSC teacher, here is the breakdown of how to approach these two physics questions, which focus on Astrophysics.

### Answer

**(a)**
An **emission spectrum** consists of discrete bright lines of specific wavelengths (colors) against a dark background, produced when electrons in excited atoms drop to lower energy levels and emit photons. 
An **absorption spectrum** consists of a continuous spectrum of light with dark lines (gaps) at specific wavelengths, produced when light passes through a cooler gas, causing electrons in atoms to absorb specific energy photons to jump to higher energy levels.

**(b)**
Two factors found on the axes of a Hertzsprung–Russell diagram are:
1. **Luminosity** (plotted on the vertical axis).
2. **Surface Temperature** (plotted on the horizontal axis).

***

### Explanation of how to solve it

**Part (a):**
*   **The Concept:** This question tests your understanding of atomic energy levels. 
*   **How to structure your answer:** Always contrast the two by mentioning the appearance (bright lines vs. dark lines) and the physical mechanism (emission of energy vs. absorption of energy). 
*   **Key terminology:** Use terms like "excited state," "ground state," "discrete energy levels," and "photon" to ensure you gain full marks for scientific accuracy.

**Part (b):**
*   **The Concept:** You need to recall the standard structure of the H-R diagram.
*   **How to solve it:** Remember that the horizontal axis represents "Temperature" (decreasing from left to right) or "Spectral Class," and the vertical axis represents "Luminosity" (or "Absolute Magnitude"). 
*   **Teacher's Tip:** When a question asks for "two factors," provide clear, distinct labels. Avoid ambiguous terms like "brightness," as "Luminosity" is the precise physical quantity used in astrophysics.
