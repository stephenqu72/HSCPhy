### Front
Why does a satellite move faster when it drops to a lower circular orbit?

### Back
- Key idea: Orbital velocity is inversely proportional to the square root of the orbital radius.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$
- When to use it: To explain the relationship between orbital speed, gravitational force, and radius.
- Common trap: Assuming a lower orbit implies a lower speed; remember that lower potential energy requires higher kinetic energy to maintain a stable circular path.
