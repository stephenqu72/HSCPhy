### Answer
The correct answer is **(B) length**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we must look at the postulates of Einstein’s theory of special relativity. One of the fundamental postulates is that the speed of light in a vacuum, $c$, is a universal constant for all inertial observers.

Prior to modern redefinitions, the meter was defined based on a physical artifact (a metal bar) or specific wavelengths of light. However, because the speed of light is now defined as an exact value ($c = 299,792,458 \text{ m/s}$), the SI unit for **length** (the meter) is defined as the distance that light travels in a vacuum in $1/299,792,458$ of a second.

This redefinition was necessary because if $c$ is constant, length and time become linked. By fixing the value of $c$, we anchor the definition of length to the behavior of light itself, which is consistent with the relativistic framework where the speed of light is invariant.

    *   (C) Mass is related to energy ($E=mc^2$) in relativity, but the definition of the kilogram was recently redefined based on the Planck constant, not primarily because of the postulates of special relativity.
    *   (D) While time is also affected by relativity (time dilation), the second is defined based on the atomic transition frequency of cesium-133, not by the speed of light.
