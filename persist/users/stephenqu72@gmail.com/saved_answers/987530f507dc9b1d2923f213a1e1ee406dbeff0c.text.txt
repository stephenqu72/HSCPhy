### Answer
The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation
To understand this question, we must look at the fundamental principles of electromagnetism as described by Maxwell’s equations.

1.  **Fundamental Principle:** An electromagnetic wave is generated by an **accelerating charge**. According to classical electrodynamics (specifically the Larmor formula), an electric charge that undergoes acceleration will radiate energy in the form of electromagnetic waves.
2.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   **A (Stationary):** A stationary charge only produces a static electric field. It has no acceleration, so it does not produce an electromagnetic wave.
    *   **B (Stable hydrogen atom):** According to the Bohr model, an electron in a stable orbit is considered a stationary state where it does not radiate energy (an exception to classical mechanics necessitated by quantum observations). Therefore, it does not produce electromagnetic waves while in its stable ground state.
    *   **C (Moving at constant velocity):** An object moving at a constant velocity has an acceleration of $a = 0$. While it produces both an electric and a magnetic field, it does not radiate electromagnetic waves.
    *   **D (Moving at a constant speed in a circular path):** Even though the *speed* (magnitude of velocity) is constant, the *direction* of the electron is constantly changing. Because velocity is a vector, a change in direction constitutes an acceleration (centripetal acceleration). Since the electron is accelerating, it will radiate electromagnetic waves.

Therefore, because the electron is undergoing centripetal acceleration in option D, it is the only scenario provided where the electron will produce electromagnetic radiation.
