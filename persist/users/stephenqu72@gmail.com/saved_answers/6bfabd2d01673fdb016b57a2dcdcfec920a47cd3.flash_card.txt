### Front
What are the SI base units for gravitational field strength ($g$), and how are they derived?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational field strength is force per unit mass ($g = F/m$), which is equivalent to acceleration.
- **Formula / law / constant:** $g = F/m$; $F=ma \implies \text{N/kg} = \text{m s}^{-2}$. No fixed constant.
- **When to use it:** Converting between N/kg and $\text{m s}^{-2}$ or performing dimensional analysis for gravitational problems.
- **Common trap:** Confusing N/kg (units of $g$) with N (units of weight/force) or J/kg (units of gravitational potential).
