### Answer to Question 35

**(a) Contrast the processes that lead to the production of absorption and emission spectra from stars.**

*   **Absorption spectra:** These occur when light from the hot, dense core of a star passes through the cooler, less dense gases of the stellar atmosphere (photosphere). Atoms in the atmosphere absorb specific wavelengths of light that correspond to the energy required to excite electrons to higher energy levels. This results in dark lines appearing on a continuous background spectrum.
*   **Emission spectra:** These occur when hot, low-density gases (such as those in a nebula or the chromosphere during an eclipse) are excited. Electrons in these atoms fall from higher energy states to lower energy states, releasing energy as photons of specific wavelengths. This results in bright lines appearing on a dark background.
*   **Contrast:** The key difference is the source and path of the light: absorption occurs due to the subtraction of specific wavelengths from a continuous source, while emission occurs due to the addition of specific wavelengths from an excited gas.

**(b) Explain how the rotational velocity of a star can be deduced from spectra.**

*   As a star rotates, one side of the star moves toward the observer (blueshift) and the opposite side moves away (redshift).
*   According to the Doppler effect, the light from the side rotating toward the observer is shifted to shorter wavelengths, while the light from the side rotating away is shifted to longer wavelengths.
*   This causes the spectral lines (which are typically sharp) to broaden into a wider, flatter profile. By measuring the extent of this **Doppler broadening** (the width of the absorption lines), astronomers can calculate the star's rotational velocity.

### How to solve this
1.  **For part (a):** The command word is "Contrast." Do not just define the terms; you must explicitly state the difference in the physical mechanism. Focus on the *source* (continuous vs. gas) and the *interaction* (absorption of specific photons vs. emission of specific photons). Using a table structure or clear comparative language (e.g., "In contrast to...") is highly recommended.
2.  **For part (b):** You must link the motion of the star to the Doppler effect. The core physical concept is that rotation creates a relative velocity shift in the emitted photons. Key terms to include are "Doppler effect," "Doppler broadening," and "redshift/blueshift." Mentioning that the broadening magnitude is proportional to the speed of rotation will secure full marks.
