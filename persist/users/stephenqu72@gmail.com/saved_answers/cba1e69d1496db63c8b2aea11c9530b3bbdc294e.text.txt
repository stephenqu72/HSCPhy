To excel in HSC Physics, you must always look for the underlying mechanism (what is happening) and the context (where it is happening). Here is the breakdown for these two questions.

### Answer to Question 25(a)
The fundamental difference lies in the nuclear process:

*   **The Sun (Nuclear Fusion):** The Sun produces energy through nuclear fusion, where light nuclei (primarily hydrogen isotopes, deuterium and tritium) combine to form a heavier nucleus (helium). This process releases massive amounts of energy due to the mass defect (where the mass of the products is less than the mass of the reactants, converted into energy via $E = mc^2$). Fusion requires extreme temperatures and pressures to overcome the electrostatic repulsion between positively charged protons.
*   **Built Reactors (Nuclear Fission):** Human-made nuclear reactors operate via nuclear fission, where a heavy, unstable nucleus (such as Uranium-235) is split into two smaller, lighter nuclei upon bombardment by a neutron. This also results in a mass defect, releasing energy. Unlike fusion, fission does not require extreme stellar temperatures and can be sustained in a controlled environment.

### Answer to Question 25(b)
The difference between controlled and uncontrolled fission lies in the chain reaction process:

*   **Controlled Fission:** This occurs in nuclear power plants. It is "controlled" because the chain reaction is managed using control rods (made of materials like cadmium or boron) that absorb excess neutrons. This ensures that, on average, only one neutron from each fission event triggers another event, maintaining a steady, constant power output.
*   **Uncontrolled Fission:** This occurs in nuclear weapons (atomic bombs). The process is designed to be explosive. No control rods are used; instead, the system is designed so that the number of neutrons causing fission increases exponentially. This releases a massive amount of energy in a fraction of a second, resulting in a rapid, self-sustaining chain reaction.

### How to solve these questions
1.  **Identify the keywords:** In part (a), the command word is "Explain," which means you must provide both the mechanism (fusion vs. fission) and the reason/context. Use clear comparative language ("while," "in contrast to," "whereas").
2.  **Use correct terminology:** Always mention the processes (Fusion vs. Fission) and the reactants (Light nuclei vs. Heavy, unstable nuclei). Mentioning the mass defect $E = mc^2$ demonstrates a deep understanding of the physics involved.
3.  **For part (b), use a structured comparison:** A great tip for "Compare" questions is to explicitly state the mechanism of control (e.g., "The use of control rods to absorb neutrons"). By explaining how one is managed (steady) and the other is exponential (explosive), you cover the necessary physics to earn full marks.
