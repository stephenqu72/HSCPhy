### Answer
The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must apply the de Broglie hypothesis and the concept of electron orbital stability as proposed by Louis de Broglie.

1.  **De Broglie's Hypothesis:** De Broglie proposed that all matter exhibits wave-like properties, where the wavelength ($\lambda$) is related to the momentum ($p$) of the particle by the equation $\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$. This applies to electrons within an atom.
2.  **Quantised Energy Levels:** Niels Bohr's original model of the atom postulated quantised orbits but could not explain *why* they were quantised. De Broglie provided the physical justification: electrons orbiting the nucleus act as **standing waves**.
3.  **Constructive Interference:** For an electron to exist in a stable, non-radiating orbit around a nucleus, its wave must undergo constructive interference with itself. This happens only if the circumference of the orbit ($2\pi r$) is an integer multiple ($n$) of its wavelength: 
    $$n\lambda = 2\pi r$$
    If the wave did not close perfectly (i.e., if it weren't a standing wave), destructive interference would occur, and the electron could not exist in that state.
4.  **Eliminating other options:**
    *   **A** is incorrect because individual particles do not explain the discrete, quantised nature of energy levels on their own; they follow classical physics which would allow any energy level.
    *   **B** is incorrect because energy levels are not caused by "diffraction patterns" in this context.
    *   **C** is incorrect because it identifies the wrong particle (protons) as the ones responsible for atomic energy levels; it is the electrons that surround the nucleus.

Therefore, electrons behaving as waves that form standing waves around the nucleus at particular discrete wavelengths (corresponding to specific energy levels) is the correct explanation.
