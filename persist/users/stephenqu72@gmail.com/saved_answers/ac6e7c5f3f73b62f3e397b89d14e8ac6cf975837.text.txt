To determine the correct nuclear equation for the $\beta$-decay of thallium-210, we must apply the laws of conservation of mass number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$).

### Explanation

1.  **Understand $\beta$-decay:** In beta-minus ($\beta^-$) decay, a neutron in the nucleus turns into a proton and an electron. The electron (a beta particle, denoted as $_{-1}^{0}e$) is ejected from the nucleus.
    *   The mass number ($A$) remains constant because the total number of nucleons (protons + neutrons) does not change.
    *   The atomic number ($Z$) increases by 1 because a neutron has become a proton.

2.  **Analyze the Parent Nucleus:**
    *   Thallium ($Tl$) is given as $_{81}^{210}Tl$.
    *   Mass number $A = 210$.
    *   Atomic number $Z = 81$.

3.  **Apply Conservation Laws:**
    *   **New Atomic Number:** Since $Z$ increases by 1, the new element must have an atomic number of $81 + 1 = 82$. Looking at the periodic table, element 82 is Lead ($Pb$).
    *   **New Mass Number:** The mass number remains $210$.
    *   **Beta Particle:** The decay equation includes the electron $_{-1}^{0}e$.

4.  **Evaluate Options:**
    *   **A:** $_{81}^{210}Tl \rightarrow _{82}^{210}Pb + _{-1}^{0}e$. Here, $210 = 210 + 0$ (balanced mass) and $81 = 82 - 1$ (balanced atomic number). This is correct.
    *   **B:** The atomic number decreases, which describes positron emission or alpha decay, not $\beta^-$-decay.
    *   **C:** This is an alpha decay equation, not $\beta$-decay.
    *   **D:** The element does not change, which is physically incorrect for this process.

Therefore, the correct equation is **A**.
