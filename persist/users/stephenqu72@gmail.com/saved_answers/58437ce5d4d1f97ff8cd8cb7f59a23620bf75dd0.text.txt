### Answer
The correct answer is **B. Observations that light from distant galaxies is red shifted.**

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must understand the core principles of Hubble's discovery, which is a fundamental concept in the HSC Physics syllabus under the "From the Universe to the Atom" or "Astrophysics" modules.

1.  **Doppler Effect and Spectroscopy:** Edwin Hubble studied the light spectra emitted by distant galaxies. By comparing the observed absorption lines in the spectra of these galaxies to known laboratory standards, he observed that the lines were shifted toward the longer wavelength (red) end of the electromagnetic spectrum. This is known as **redshift**.
2.  **Hubble's Law:** Hubble interpreted this redshift as a Doppler effect caused by the galaxies moving away from Earth. He found a direct correlation: the further away a galaxy is, the faster it is receding from us ($v = H_0 d$).
3.  **Expansion of the Universe:** If galaxies are moving away from us in all directions, and the speed of recession is proportional to their distance, it logically follows that the space between galaxies is expanding. This observation provided the empirical evidence required to formulate the theory of the expanding universe.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **A:** The "cosmological constant" was a term added by Einstein to his equations to force a static universe model. Hubble’s observational data actually *disproved* the need for a static model.
*   **C:** Blueshift indicates an object is moving *towards* the observer. This is observed in some local galaxies (like Andromeda), but it contradicts the general trend of universal expansion.
*   **D:** The distribution of matter is a separate cosmological principle (the Cosmological Principle assumes homogeneity and isotropy on large scales), and it was not the specific evidence Hubble used to discover the expansion.
