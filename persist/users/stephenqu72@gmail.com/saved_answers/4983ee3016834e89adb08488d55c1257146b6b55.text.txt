To solve this problem, we must calculate the total change in mechanical energy required to move the Soyuz capsule from the Earth's surface to the ISS orbit.

### 1. Identify the Constants and Variables
*   Mass of Earth ($M$): $5.97 \times 10^{24}\text{ kg}$
*   Radius of Earth ($R_E$): $6.37 \times 10^6\text{ m}$
*   Gravitational Constant ($G$): $6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2\text{ kg}^{-2}$
*   Mass of capsule ($m$): $9000\text{ kg}$
*   Altitude ($h$): $4.00 \times 10^2\text{ km} = 4.00 \times 10^5\text{ m}$
*   Orbital radius ($r$): $R_E + h = 6.77 \times 10^6\text{ m}$

### 2. The Physics Principles
The "minimum energy" refers to the difference between the total mechanical energy in the final orbit ($E_f$) and the total mechanical energy on the Earth's surface ($E_i$).
*   **Initial energy ($E_i$):** This is purely gravitational potential energy: $E_i = -\frac{GMm}{R_E}$
*   **Final energy ($E_f$):** This includes both potential and kinetic energy in a circular orbit: $E_f = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$

### 3. Calculation
**Step A: Calculate $E_i$**
$$E_i = -\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(5.97 \times 10^{24})(9000)}{6.37 \times 10^6} \approx -5.623 \times 10^{11}\text{ J}$$

**Step B: Calculate $E_f$**
$$E_f = -\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(5.97 \times 10^{24})(9000)}{2(6.77 \times 10^6)} \approx -2.645 \times 10^{11}\text{ J}$$

**Step C: Calculate $\Delta E$**
$$\Delta E = E_f - E_i$$
$$\Delta E = (-2.645 \times 10^{11}) - (-5.623 \times 10^{11})$$
$$\Delta E \approx 2.98 \times 10^{11}\text{ J}$$

The minimum energy required is approximately **$2.98 \times 10^{11}\text{ J}$**.

### How to solve this as an HSC student:
1.  **State your formula:** Always start by defining the energy at both points. Remember that potential energy is $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$ and kinetic energy for a circular orbit is $K = \frac{GMm}{2r}$.
2.  **Use correct units:** Convert altitude in km to meters immediately to ensure consistency with $G$ and Earth's radius.
3.  **Check signs:** Remember that gravitational potential energy is negative. Subtracting a negative number is the same as adding a positive one, which correctly increases the energy of the system.
