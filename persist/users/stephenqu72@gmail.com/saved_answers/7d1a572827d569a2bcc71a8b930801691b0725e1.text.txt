The correct answer is **A. photoelectric effect**.

### Explanation:
To solve this question, we must distinguish between phenomena that demonstrate the **wave nature** of light and those that demonstrate the **particle (photon) nature** of light.

1.  **The Photoelectric Effect (A):** This occurs when light shining on a metal surface causes electrons to be emitted. Classical wave theory failed to explain why emission depends on the frequency of light rather than its intensity. Albert Einstein explained this by proposing that light consists of discrete "packets" of energy called photons, where the energy of each photon is given by $E = hf$. This interaction is a one-to-one collision between a photon and an electron, which is clear evidence of light behaving as a particle.

2.  **White light through a prism (B):** This is the phenomenon of **dispersion**. It occurs because different frequencies (colors) of light travel at different speeds through the medium, causing them to refract by different amounts. This is a property explained by the wave model of light.

3.  **Diffraction (C) and Interference (D):** These are classic **wave phenomena**. Diffraction is the bending of waves around obstacles, and interference is the superposition of waves (constructive and destructive). These effects can only be explained by considering light as a continuous wave capable of overlapping and creating patterns.

Therefore, only the photoelectric effect provides evidence for the particle model of light.
