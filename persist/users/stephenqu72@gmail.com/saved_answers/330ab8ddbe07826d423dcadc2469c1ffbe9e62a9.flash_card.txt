### Front
How do you derive the mass ($M$) of a central body (e.g., a pulsar) using the orbital data of a satellite?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Equate centripetal force ($F_c$) to gravitational force ($F_g$).
- **Formula / law:** $\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GMm}{r^2} \implies M = \frac{4\pi^2r^3}{GT^2}$
- **When to use it:** When given an orbital radius ($r$) and period ($T$) to calculate the mass of a primary body.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., hours/days to seconds, km to meters) or forgetting to square the period ($T^2$).
