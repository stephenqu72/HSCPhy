### Answer

The Hafele-Keating experiment (1971) provided empirical evidence for Einstein’s theory of special and general relativity by using highly accurate caesium atomic clocks. 

1.  **The Methodology:** Clocks were flown on commercial airliners twice around the world, once in the direction of Earth's rotation (eastward) and once against it (westward), and then compared to a stationary reference clock at the U.S. Naval Observatory.
2.  **Special Relativity:** Due to time dilation, clocks moving at high speeds relative to an observer tick more slowly. The experiment confirmed that the moving clocks experienced a time difference consistent with the Lorentz factor:
    $$\Delta t' = \gamma \Delta t = \frac{\Delta t}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$
3.  **General Relativity:** The experiment also accounted for gravitational time dilation, as the clocks were at different altitudes where the gravitational potential differs. The results matched Einstein's predictions to a high degree of precision, verifying that time is relative and dependent on both velocity and gravitational potential.
