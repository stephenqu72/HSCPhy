### Front
At the maximum height (peak) of a projectile's trajectory, what are the horizontal and vertical components of acceleration?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration is independent of velocity; gravity acts constantly regardless of vertical motion.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_x = 0$; $a_y = g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ (downwards).
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem (ignoring air resistance).
- Common trap: Assuming acceleration is zero at the peak because the vertical velocity is zero.
