To answer this question, we must understand Isaac Newton's **corpuscular theory of light**, which proposed that light consists of tiny particles called "corpuscles."

### Explanation

*   **(A) Colours:** Newton successfully explained colours by proposing that different "corpuscles" of light had different sizes, which allowed them to be refracted by different amounts through a prism.
*   **(B) Interference:** Interference is a phenomenon that depends on the **wave nature of light**. When light waves meet, they can undergo constructive or destructive interference (superposition). Newton’s particle model viewed light as discrete, independent entities, which could not explain how two beams of light could cancel each other out to create dark regions. This phenomenon was famously demonstrated by Thomas Young’s double-slit experiment, which provided the evidence needed to displace the corpuscular theory in favor of the wave theory.
*   **(C) Reflection:** Newton explained reflection using his particle model by suggesting that the particles of light bounce off a surface in a way similar to a perfectly elastic collision of a ball against a wall.
*   **(D) Refraction:** Newton explained refraction by arguing that light particles accelerate when entering a denser medium due to an attractive force from the particles of the medium. Although this led to the incorrect conclusion that light travels faster in denser media (like glass) than in a vacuum, it was still an attempt to account for the phenomenon within his model.

**Correct Answer:** **(B) Interference**
