### Front
What is the systematic criteria used to evaluate the **reliability** of secondary sources in a Depth Study?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Reliability refers to the consistency and trustworthiness of information.
- **Criteria (The 4 Cs):**
    1. **Credibility:** Verify author qualifications and publisher authority (e.g., peer-reviewed, CSIRO).
    2. **Cross-referencing:** Compare data across at least two independent, reputable sources.
    3. **Currency/Objectivity:** Ensure data is up-to-date and free from commercial or political bias.
    4. **Clear Methodology:** Assess if the source explains its data collection/analysis.
- **When to use it:** When evaluating secondary data, literature reviews, or research depth studies.
- **Common trap:** Confusing "reliability" (consistency) with "validity" (how well the data answers the specific research question).
