### Front
How do you calculate the centripetal force ($F_c$) for a conical pendulum given mass ($m$), length ($L$), angle ($\theta$), and angular velocity ($\omega$)?

### Back
- Key idea: The centripetal force is provided by the horizontal component of the tension.
- Formula: $F_c = mr\omega^2$, where $r = L\cos\theta$ (radius of the horizontal circle).
- When to use it: Circular motion problems where an object moves in a horizontal plane suspended by a string.
- Common trap: Using $\sin\theta$ instead of $\cos\theta$ for the radius; failing to convert mass to SI units (kg).
