### Answer

**(a) Identify an assumption of EACH model that determines the results on the screen.**

*   **Prediction X (Particle Model):** Assumes that light consists of streams of tiny particles (corpuscles). These particles travel in straight lines through the two slits, resulting in two distinct, bright bands on the screen corresponding to the paths of the slits.
*   **Prediction Y (Wave Model):** Assumes that light behaves as a wave. When light passes through two closely spaced slits, the waves diffract and interfere with each other (constructive and destructive interference), creating an interference pattern of alternating bright and dark fringes.

**(b) How did Young's results help lead to the reconceptualisation of the model of light?**

Young’s Double Slit Experiment provided clear empirical evidence of **interference**, a phenomenon unique to wave motion. At the time, Newton’s corpuscular theory of light was the dominant model. Because particles cannot interfere with one another to produce the dark fringes observed in the experimental results (Prediction Y), the corpuscular model was unable to explain the observed pattern.

The observation of the interference pattern demonstrated that light possesses wave-like properties (diffraction and interference). This forced the scientific community to move away from the strictly Newtonian particle model and accept that light travels as a wave. This reconceptualisation fundamentally shifted the understanding of light, eventually leading to the development of wave optics and laying the groundwork for the future discovery of wave-particle duality in quantum physics.
