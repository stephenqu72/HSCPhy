### Explanation
When an object is fired from a slingshot, it becomes a projectile. To determine the correct vector diagram, we must identify the forces acting on the object **after** it has left the slingshot:

1.  **Ignoring Air Resistance:** In standard HSC physics problems involving projectile motion, we assume the object is in free fall. This means the only force acting on the object throughout its entire flight is the gravitational force ($F_g = mg$).
2.  **Direction of Gravity:** The gravitational force always acts vertically downwards toward the center of the Earth.
3.  **Independence of Motion:** A common misconception is that a "force" is required to keep the object moving horizontally. However, according to Newton’s First Law, an object in motion will continue in motion with a constant horizontal velocity unless acted upon by an external horizontal force (like air resistance). Since there are no horizontal forces, there should be **no horizontal arrows** in the vector diagram.
4.  **Analysis of Options:**
    *   **Option A** shows only a downward force vector at various points along the trajectory. This correctly represents the constant gravitational force acting on the object.
    *   **Options B, C, and D** are incorrect because they include extra forces or variable force magnitudes that do not exist in standard projectile motion. For instance, horizontal arrows suggest an applied force is still acting on the projectile, which is incorrect after the object has left the slingshot.

Therefore, because gravity is a constant downward force acting on the object regardless of its horizontal position, **Option A** is the only correct representation.
