### Front
Compare the effects of alpha, beta, and gamma decay on atomic transmutation.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Alpha and beta decay result in transmutation (changing $Z$); gamma decay does not, as it only releases excess nuclear energy.
- **Law/Figure:** 
  - $\alpha$: $Z-2, A-4$
  - $\beta^-$: $Z+1, A$ (unchanged)
  - $\gamma$: $Z, A$ (unchanged)
- **When to use it:** When asked to explain nuclear stability or the conversion of elements during radioactive decay.
- **Common trap:** Assuming all forms of nuclear radiation result in the formation of a new element; always remember gamma radiation only shifts the nucleus to a lower energy state.
