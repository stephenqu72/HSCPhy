To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of electromagnetism as they relate to DC motors.

### Given Data:
- Number of turns ($N$) = $100$
- Magnetic field strength ($B$) = $25.0\text{ mT} = 0.025\text{ T}$
- Length of coil side ($l$) = $0.32\text{ m}$
- Width of coil ($w$) = $0.1\text{ m}$
- Driving voltage ($V$) = $120\text{ V}$
- Resistance per turn ($R_{turn}$) = $0.018\text{ }\Omega$

---

### (a) Current flow from the power supply
First, calculate the total resistance of the coil ($R_{total}$):
$$R_{total} = N \times R_{turn} = 100 \times 0.018 = 1.8\text{ }\Omega$$

Using Ohm's Law ($V = IR$):
$$I = \frac{V}{R_{total}} = \frac{120}{1.8} = 66.67\text{ A}$$

**Answer:** The current flow from the power supply is $66.7\text{ A}$ (to 3 significant figures).

---

### (b) Effective torque supplied by the motor
The formula for the torque ($\tau$) on a motor coil is given by:
$$\tau = N I A B$$
where $A$ is the area of the coil ($l \times w$).

1. Calculate the area ($A$):
$$A = 0.32\text{ m} \times 0.1\text{ m} = 0.032\text{ m}^2$$

2. Calculate the torque:
$$\tau = 100 \times 66.67\text{ A} \times 0.032\text{ m}^2 \times 0.025\text{ T}$$
$$\tau = 6667 \times 0.032 \times 0.025$$
$$\tau = 5.33\text{ Nm}$$

**Answer:** The effective torque supplied by the motor is $5.33\text{ Nm}$.

---

### How to solve this:
1. **Identify the circuit parameters:** Always start by finding the total resistance of the coil by multiplying the turns by the resistance per turn. Then use Ohm's law to find the total current.
2. **Apply the motor torque formula:** Recognize that for a coil in a radial magnetic field, the torque is constant and determined by $\tau = NIAB$. Ensure your units are in SI (meters, Tesla, Amperes) before calculating.
