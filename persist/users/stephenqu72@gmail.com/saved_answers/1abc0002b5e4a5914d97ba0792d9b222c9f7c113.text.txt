To calculate the torque ($\tau$), we use the formula:

$$\tau = F \cdot L \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
- $F = 20\text{ N}$
- $L = 2\text{ km} = 2000\text{ m}$
- $\theta = 35^\circ$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the component of force perpendicular to the bar:**
    The torque is generated by the component of the force perpendicular to the lever arm. Looking at the diagram, the perpendicular component is $F \cdot \sin(\theta)$.
    $$F_{\perp} = 20 \cdot \sin(35^\circ)$$
    $$F_{\perp} \approx 20 \cdot 0.5736 \approx 11.47\text{ N}$$

2.  **Calculate the Torque:**
    Multiply the perpendicular force by the length of the bar. Since the answer options involve $\text{kNm}$ (kilo-Newton meters), it is easiest to keep $L$ in $\text{km}$ and $F$ in $\text{kN}$, or calculate in $\text{Nm}$ and convert.
    Using $L = 2\text{ km}$:
    $$\tau = 20\text{ N} \cdot 2\text{ km} \cdot \sin(35^\circ)$$
    $$\tau = 40 \cdot 0.573576... \approx 22.943\text{ kNm}$$

3.  **Conclusion:**
    The calculated torque is approximately $22.94\text{ kNm}$. Matching this to the given options, the correct choice is **D**.

### Explanation:
The key to this problem is recognizing that torque is the product of the force and the perpendicular distance from the pivot. The angle $\theta$ provided is between the bar and the force vector. Therefore, we use the sine component to find the force acting perpendicular to the radius $L$. Finally, we must pay close attention to the units; because $L$ is given in kilometers, the resulting torque magnitude is in $\text{kNm}$.
