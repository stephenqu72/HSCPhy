To solve this problem, we must apply **Malus's Law**, which states that the intensity of light passing through a polarising filter is given by:

$$I = I_0 \cos^2(\phi)$$

where:
*   $I_0$ is the intensity of the light incident on the filter.
*   $\phi$ is the angle between the light's initial plane of polarisation and the filter's axis.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the incident angle $\phi_1$:**
    The first filter is parallel to the $y$-axis. Therefore, the light emerging from the first filter is polarised vertically along the $y$-axis.

2.  **Determine the angles $\phi$ for the second filter:**
    *   **Case 1 ($\theta = 90^\circ$):** When the second filter is at an angle of $90^\circ$ to the $x$-axis, it is parallel to the $y$-axis. The angle between the light (vertical) and the filter (vertical) is $\phi = 0^\circ$. 
    *   *Correction/Refinement:* Wait, let's look at the geometry carefully. If the light is polarised along the $y$-axis and the second filter is at angle $\theta$ relative to the $x$-axis, the angle $\phi$ between the light (vertical) and the filter (at angle $\theta$ to the $x$-axis) is $\phi = 90^\circ - \theta$.
    *   For $\theta = 90^\circ$, $\phi = 90^\circ - 90^\circ = 0^\circ$. Malus's Law gives $I = I_0 \cos^2(0^\circ) = I_0$. We are told this intensity is $210 \text{ lux}$. So, $I_0 = 210 \text{ lux}$.
    *   For $\theta = 35^\circ$, the angle between the vertical light and the filter is $\phi = 90^\circ - 35^\circ = 55^\circ$.

3.  **Calculate the intensity for $\theta = 35^\circ$:**
    $$I = I_0 \cos^2(55^\circ)$$
    $$I = 210 \times (\cos(55^\circ))^2$$
    $$I = 210 \times (0.57357...)^2$$
    $$I = 210 \times 0.32899... \approx 69.09 \text{ lux}$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number, we get $69 \text{ lux}$.
