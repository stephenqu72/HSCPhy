### Answer
**Concept of Flux Linkage:**
Flux linkage refers to the total magnetic flux passing through the turns of a coil. In an ideal transformer, all the magnetic flux ($\Phi$) generated by the primary coil should pass through the secondary coil. The flux linkage is defined as $N\Phi$, where $N$ is the number of turns. 

**Improving Flux Linkage:**
In practical transformers, "magnetic leakage" occurs when some flux lines do not complete the path through the secondary coil, leading to energy loss and reduced efficiency. To improve this:
1.  **Laminated Soft Iron Core:** A high-permeability soft iron core provides a low-reluctance path for the magnetic field, "guiding" the flux from the primary to the secondary coil effectively.
2.  **Coil Arrangement:** Placing the primary and secondary coils on top of each other (coaxial winding) on the same limb of the core minimizes the distance flux must travel through air, significantly reducing leakage.
