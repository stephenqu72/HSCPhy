### Front
What law and formula describe the relationship between orbital period ($T$) and orbital radius ($r$) for two moons orbiting the same central body?

### Back
- Key idea: Kepler’s Third Law states the ratio $r^3/T^2$ is constant for all objects orbiting the same mass.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\frac{r_1^3}{T_1^2} = \frac{r_2^3}{T_2^2}$ (Kepler's Third Law).
- When to use it: To find an unknown period or radius of a moon/satellite without needing to know the mass of the central planet ($M$) or the gravitational constant ($G$).
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert units (e.g., days to seconds or km to meters) if using the absolute formula ($M = \frac{4\pi^2r^3}{GT^2}$); however, ratios work as long as units are consistent on both sides.
