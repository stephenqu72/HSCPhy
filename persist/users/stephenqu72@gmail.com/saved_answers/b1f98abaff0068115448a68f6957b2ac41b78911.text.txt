### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) Gravitational and Electric**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must understand the physics behind Robert Millikan’s famous "Oil Drop Experiment."

1.  **The Goal:** Millikan aimed to determine the magnitude of the elementary charge of an electron by suspending tiny, charged oil droplets in a uniform electric field.
2.  **The Forces at Play:** In the experiment, Millikan balanced two specific forces to keep the oil drop stationary (or moving at a constant terminal velocity):
    *   **Gravitational Force ($F_g$):** This force acts downwards on the oil drop, calculated by $F_g = mg$, where $m$ is the mass of the droplet and $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity.
    *   **Electric Force ($F_e$):** By applying a potential difference across two horizontal metal plates, Millikan created a uniform electric field. The electric force acting on the charged drop is $F_e = qE$, where $q$ is the charge of the droplet and $E$ is the strength of the electric field.
3.  **The Equilibrium:** When the drop was suspended in mid-air (equilibrium), the downward gravitational force was perfectly balanced by the upward electric force:
    $$F_g = F_e$$
    $$mg = qE$$

Because Millikan's experiment relied solely on the balance between these two forces to determine the charge $q$, **Magnetic forces were not involved.** Therefore, (C) is the only correct choice.
