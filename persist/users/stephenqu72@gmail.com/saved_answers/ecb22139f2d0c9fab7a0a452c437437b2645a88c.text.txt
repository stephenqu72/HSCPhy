### Explanation
To determine the direction of the induced current, we apply **Lenz's Law**, which states that the direction of an induced current is such that it opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it.

1.  **As the ring falls towards the magnet:** The magnetic flux through the ring increases. The magnetic field lines are pointing from the North pole to the South pole. To oppose this increase in downward/horizontal magnetic flux, the ring must create its own magnetic field directed upwards (or opposite to the external field). Using the **Right-Hand Grip Rule**, an upward-directed magnetic field produced by the loop requires an **anticlockwise** current.

2.  **As the ring leaves the magnet:** The magnetic flux through the ring decreases. According to Lenz’s Law, the induced current must now oppose this decrease by creating a magnetic field in the same direction as the external field (to "pull" the flux back). This requires a **clockwise** current.

Therefore, the current is first **anticlockwise** (as it enters the field) and then **clockwise** (as it leaves the field).
