To answer this 5-mark HSC Physics question, you need to link the evolution of particle detection technology with the theoretical advancements in particle physics.

### Suggested Answer
From the 1930s onwards, the development of high-energy particle accelerators (such as the cyclotron and later synchrotrons) and advanced detection systems (like cloud chambers, bubble chambers, and modern multi-layer detectors) allowed physicists to smash particles together at increasingly high energies. This technology enabled the discovery of a "zoo" of new subatomic particles that could not be explained by the simple proton-neutron-electron model. 

The accumulation of experimental data from these high-energy collisions revealed consistent patterns in particle interactions, necessitating a unifying theoretical framework. The Standard Model was developed to classify these subatomic particles and describe their fundamental interactions. It explains that all matter is composed of fundamental building blocks (quarks and leptons) and that the forces between them are mediated by exchange particles called gauge bosons (such as photons, gluons, and $W$ and $Z$ bosons). Finally, it incorporates the Higgs boson to explain how these particles acquire mass.

1.  **Identify the link (Technology $\rightarrow$ Theory):** You must state that technology (accelerators/detectors) provided the *evidence*. As energy increased, we saw more particles; this forced theorists to create a model that could account for this complexity.
2.  **Mention specific technological milestones:** Referring to the progression from early cloud chambers to modern particle accelerators demonstrates a strong understanding of the "investigating the nature of matter" aspect.
3.  **Explain the Standard Model:** You must define it as a framework that categorizes fundamental particles. Use key terms like "quarks," "leptons," "gauge bosons," and "Higgs boson."
4.  **Structure for marks:** Since it is a 5-mark question, aim for 3 marks on the technological history/link and 2 marks on the definition of the Standard Model. 
5.  **Conciseness:** Stick to the guide of 10 lines to ensure you remain focused on the key historical and theoretical connections without rambling.
