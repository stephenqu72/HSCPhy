To answer this question, we must look at the physical definition of centripetal force.

### Answer
The correct answer is **A. No change**.

### Explanation
To solve this, consider the formula for centripetal force required to keep an object of mass $m$ moving at speed $v$ in a circle of radius $r$:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

The question specifies that the mass of the car ($m$) remains constant ($1.3$ tonnes) and the speed of the car ($v$) remains constant ($40 \text{ km/h}$). Assuming the radius of the circular track ($r$) also remains constant, the **required centripetal force** must remain identical.

**Why does this happen?**
1. **Definition of Centripetal Force:** Centripetal force is not a new or unique type of force; it is a "net force requirement" dictated by kinematics. Any object moving in a circle *must* experience a net force toward the center equal to $\frac{mv^2}{r}$ to maintain that path.
2. **The role of banking:** On a flat track, the friction between the tires and the road provides the entirety of the centripetal force. On a banked track, the horizontal component of the normal force provides some (or all) of that required centripetal force.
3. **Conclusion:** While the *source* of the force changes (from purely friction to a combination of normal force and friction), the *total magnitude* of the centripetal force required to sustain a circular motion with the same mass, speed, and radius does not change.

Since the parameters $m$, $v$, and $r$ are unchanged, the centripetal force remains the same.
