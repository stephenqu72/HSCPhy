To determine the correct answer, we must apply two fundamental principles of blackbody radiation: **Wien's Displacement Law** and the **Stefan-Boltzmann Law**.

### 1. Identifying the Hotter Object
Wien's Displacement Law states that the wavelength at which the peak emission occurs ($\lambda_{max}$) is inversely proportional to the temperature ($T$) of the object:
$$\lambda_{max} \propto \frac{1}{T}$$
Looking at the graph, curve **A** has a peak at approximately $600\text{ nm}$, while curve **B** has a peak at approximately $700\text{ nm}$. Since curve **A** has a shorter peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max, A} < \lambda_{max, B}$), it must correspond to the higher temperature. Therefore, **A is the hotter object**.

### 2. Identifying the Object with Greater Total Energy Output
The total energy output (total power radiated) of a blackbody is represented by the area under its radiation curve. According to the Stefan-Boltzmann Law, the total power radiated is proportional to the fourth power of the temperature ($P \propto T^4$). 
Visually, curve **A** lies entirely above curve **B** across the entire wavelength spectrum. Because curve **A** has a significantly larger area under its curve than curve **B**, object **A** has a greater total energy output.
