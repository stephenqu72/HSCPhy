The correct answer is **C**.

### Explanation
Newton's law of universal gravitation, which is stated as $F = G \frac{Mm}{d^2}$ (or in the proportional form provided in the question, $Fd^2 \propto Mm$), is strictly derived for **point masses**. 

When dealing with large, spherical celestial bodies like the Sun and the Earth, we can treat them as point masses located at their respective centers of mass due to the **Shell Theorem**. This theorem states that a spherically symmetric body affects external objects as if all of its mass were concentrated at its center. 

However, for this approximation to be physically accurate and for the formula to remain valid as given, the distance between the two objects ($d$) must be very large compared to the radii of the objects themselves. Because the distance between the Earth and the Sun is approximately 150 million kilometers, which is vastly greater than the radii of either body, the "point mass" approximation is highly accurate. 

*   **Option A** is incorrect because the gravitational constant $G$ is always required for the proportionality to become an equality.
*   **Option B** is irrelevant; the magnitude of the force does not dictate the validity of the point-mass approximation.
*   **Option D** explains why the Earth orbits the Sun, but it is not the reason we are permitted to treat these extended bodies as point masses.
