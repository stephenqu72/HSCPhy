### Answer

**Investigation Outline:**
To examine the spectra, place a spectroscope or a diffraction grating in front of a white light source (such as an incandescent bulb) and then in front of a discharge tube containing a gas (such as hydrogen or helium). View the light through the diffraction grating to observe the resulting patterns.

**Description of Spectra:**
1.  **Incandescent Bulb (Continuous Spectrum):** When the white light passes through the grating, it produces a continuous band of colors, transitioning smoothly from red to violet without any gaps or dark lines. This represents all visible wavelengths.
2.  **Gas Discharge Tube (Emission Spectrum):** When the light from the gas discharge tube passes through the grating, it produces a series of distinct, sharp colored lines against a dark background. Each element produces a unique pattern of lines (spectral signature) corresponding to specific electronic transitions within the atoms.
