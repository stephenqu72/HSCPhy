### Front
How do you determine the direction of force between a current-carrying solenoid and a nearby bar magnet?

### Back
- Key idea: Interaction between induced poles and the magnet’s poles (like poles repel, opposite poles attract).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Right-Hand Grip Rule (for solenoid polarity) & Newton’s Third Law.
- When to use it: Whenever a current flows through a coil near a magnetic material to predict relative motion.
- Common trap: Misapplying the Right-Hand Grip Rule by curling fingers in the direction of the magnetic field instead of the conventional current.
