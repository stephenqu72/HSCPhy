### Answer

In the Standard Model of matter, a hydrogen atom consists of the following components:

1.  **The Proton (Nucleus):** The nucleus of a hydrogen atom is a single proton. A proton is a baryon composed of three valence quarks: two **up quarks** (each with charge $+2/3e$) and one **down quark** (with charge $-1/3e$), held together by the **strong nuclear force** mediated by **gluons**.
2.  **The Electron:** The atom contains one electron, which is a **lepton** (a fundamental particle). The electron occupies the region surrounding the nucleus.
3.  **Fundamental Interactions:** The electron is held in orbit around the proton by the **electromagnetic force**, which is mediated by the exchange of **photons**. 
4.  **Mass Generation:** Both the quarks and the electron acquire their mass through their interaction with the **Higgs field**, mediated by the **Higgs boson**.
