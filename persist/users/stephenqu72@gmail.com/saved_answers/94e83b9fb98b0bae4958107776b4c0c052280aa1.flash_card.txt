### Front
How do you structure a "Working Scientifically" answer regarding risk assessment for a physics experiment?

### Back
- Key idea: Identify the hazard and explicitly state the control measure to mitigate it.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: For any exam question asking to justify or describe safety procedures.
- Common trap: Being vague (e.g., "be careful") instead of specifying a concrete hazard (e.g., "projectile striking someone") and a specific control (e.g., "landing zone padding").
