### Part (b): Calculation for Manganese-56 after 6 hours
To find the number of remaining Manganese-56 atoms ($N_t$) after $t = 6$ hours, use the radioactive decay formula:
$$N_t = N_0 \times \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{\frac{t}{t_{1/2}}}$$

**Given:**
- Initial atoms $N_0 = 1.5 \times 10^6$
- Elapsed time $t = 6$ hours
- Half-life $t_{1/2} = 2.6$ hours

**Step-by-step calculation:**
1. Determine the number of half-lives passed: $n = \frac{6}{2.6} \approx 2.3077$
2. Substitute into the formula:
$$N_t = 1.5 \times 10^6 \times (0.5)^{2.3077}$$
3. Calculate the exponent: $(0.5)^{2.3077} \approx 0.2023$
4. Final calculation: $N_t = 1.5 \times 10^6 \times 0.2023 \approx 3.03 \times 10^5$

**Result:** After 6 hours, there are approximately $3.03 \times 10^5$ atoms of Manganese-56 remaining.
