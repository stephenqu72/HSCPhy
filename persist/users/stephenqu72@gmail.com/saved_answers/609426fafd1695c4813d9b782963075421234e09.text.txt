To determine the correct statements, we must refer to the electromagnetic spectrum. In the spectrum, as we move from radio waves to microwaves, then to infrared, and finally to visible light (where red light is at the low-frequency/long-wavelength end), the frequency increases and the wavelength decreases.

### Analysis of the Physics
The energy of a quantum (photon) is given by the Planck-Einstein relation:
$$E = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$
Where:
- $E$ is the energy
- $h$ is Planck’s constant
- $f$ is frequency
- $\lambda$ is wavelength

1.  **Comparing frequencies:** Red light has a higher frequency than microwave radiation. Therefore, statement **II is incorrect**.
2.  **Comparing wavelengths:** Microwaves have significantly longer wavelengths than red light. Therefore, statement **III is incorrect**.
3.  **Comparing energy:** Since red light has a higher frequency than microwave radiation, it follows from $E=hf$ that a quantum of red light is more energetic than a quantum of microwave radiation. Therefore, statement **I is correct** and statement **IV is incorrect**.

### Conclusion
Only statement **I** is correct.

Looking at the options provided:
(A) I only
(B) I and II only
(C) I and III only
(D) II and IV only

The correct answer is **(A)**.

### How to solve this
1.  **Memorize the EM Spectrum order:** Remember that frequency increases and wavelength decreases as you move towards the high-energy end (Gamma rays). Microwaves are "to the left" of visible red light on the spectrum, meaning they have lower frequency, longer wavelengths, and lower energy.
2.  **Use the Proportionality Rules:** 
    - Energy is directly proportional to frequency ($E \propto f$).
    - Frequency is inversely proportional to wavelength ($f \propto \frac{1}{\lambda}$).
3.  **Evaluate each statement:** systematically check if the relationship aligns with the relative positions of the waves on the spectrum.
