To find the orbital velocity of the Earth, we treat the orbit as a circle and use the relationship between distance, time, and speed.

### Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Radius of orbit ($r$): $1.5 \times 10^8 \text{ km} = 1.5 \times 10^{11} \text{ m}$
*   Time period ($T$): $365 \text{ days}$

**2. Convert units to SI (meters and seconds):**
*   Convert distance to meters: $1.5 \times 10^8 \times 10^3 = 1.5 \times 10^{11} \text{ m}$
*   Convert time to seconds: $365 \text{ days} \times 24 \text{ hours/day} \times 3600 \text{ seconds/hour} = 31,536,000 \text{ s}$

**3. Use the orbital velocity formula:**
The circumference of the circular orbit is $2\pi r$. The velocity $v$ is the total distance divided by the time period:
$$v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$$

**4. Perform the calculation:**
$$v = \frac{2 \times \pi \times (1.5 \times 10^{11} \text{ m})}{31,536,000 \text{ s}}$$
$$v = \frac{9.42477 \times 10^{11}}{31,536,000}$$
$$v \approx 29,885.7 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$

Rounding this to the nearest whole number gives **$29,886 \text{ ms}^{-1}$**.
