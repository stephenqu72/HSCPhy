### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) an absorption spectrum is produced.**

### Explanation
To understand this, we look at the interaction between photons and electrons within atoms:

1.  **The Process:** When white light (which contains a continuous range of wavelengths/colors) passes through a cool, low-density gas, the atoms in the gas interact with the photons.
2.  **Quantum Energy Levels:** Electrons in the gas atoms occupy specific, quantized energy levels. They can only absorb a photon if its energy ($E = hf$) exactly matches the difference between two energy levels ($\Delta E$) within the atom.
3.  **Absorption:** Because the atoms absorb these specific discrete frequencies, those corresponding colors are "removed" from the continuous white light spectrum. 
4.  **The Result:** When the remaining light is passed through a prism or diffraction grating, we see a continuous spectrum with dark lines corresponding to the specific frequencies that were absorbed by the gas. This is known as an **absorption spectrum**.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **(A)** Gas does not absorb all light; it only absorbs photons with energies matching its specific electronic transitions.
*   **(B)** An **emission spectrum** is produced when an excited gas emits light as its electrons drop from higher energy levels to lower ones. 
*   **(D)** While electrons are indeed "energised" (promoted to higher states), this describes the *process* of absorption, but the *observable result* in the context of spectroscopy is the formation of an absorption spectrum.
