### Front
Explain the difference in electrical conduction between a pure metal at room temperature and a superconductor below its critical temperature ($T_c$).

### Back
- **Key idea:** Normal metals experience resistance due to electron-ion scattering; superconductors exhibit zero resistance due to Cooper pair formation.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $P = I^2R$ (Energy loss).
- **When to use it:** When asked to contrast conduction mechanisms or discuss the efficiency of superconducting power transmission.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to mention that normal metal resistance increases with temperature due to increased lattice vibrations.
