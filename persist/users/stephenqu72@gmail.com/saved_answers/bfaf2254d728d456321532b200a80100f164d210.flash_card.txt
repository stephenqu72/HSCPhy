### Front
Why do Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites experience orbital decay, while geostationary satellites do not?

### Back
- Key idea: Orbital decay is caused by atmospheric drag, which converts a satellite's kinetic energy into thermal energy, lowering its altitude.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Explaining satellite mission longevity or the effect of atmospheric density on orbital mechanics.
- Common trap: Assuming velocity changes or gravitational pull are the primary causes of orbital decay, rather than the non-conservative force of atmospheric friction.
