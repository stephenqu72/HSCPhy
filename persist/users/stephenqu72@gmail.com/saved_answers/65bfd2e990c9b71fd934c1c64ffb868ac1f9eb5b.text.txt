To solve this problem, we need to calculate the magnetic force between two parallel current-carrying wires and then use Newton's Second Law to determine the mass of the trolley.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
*   **Magnetic Force between two parallel wires:** The force per unit length $F/L$ is given by the formula:
    $$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$
    where:
    *   $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T m/A}$ (permeability of free space)
    *   $I_1 = 25.0 \text{ A}$
    *   $I_2 = 50.0 \text{ A}$
    *   $r = 2.00 \text{ mm} = 2.00 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$
    *   $L = 2.00 \text{ m}$

*   **Newton's Second Law:**
    $$F = ma \implies m = \frac{F}{a}$$

### 2. Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step A: Calculate the force $F$**
$$\frac{F}{2.00} = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times 25.0 \times 50.0}{2\pi \times (2.00 \times 10^{-3})}$$

Simplify the expression:
$$\frac{F}{2.00} = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 1250}{2.00 \times 10^{-3}}$$
$$\frac{F}{2.00} = \frac{2.5 \times 10^{-4}}{2 \times 10^{-3}}$$
$$\frac{F}{2.00} = 0.125 \text{ N/m}$$

Now, multiply by the length $L = 2.00 \text{ m}$:
$$F = 0.125 \times 2.00 = 0.250 \text{ N}$$

**Step B: Calculate the mass $m$**
Using $F = ma$ where $a = 0.200 \text{ m/s}^2$:
$$0.250 = m \times 0.200$$
$$m = \frac{0.250}{0.200}$$
$$m = 1.25 \text{ kg}$$

Comparing our result to the given options:
(A) 0.625 kg
(B) 1.25 kg
(C) 2.50 kg
(D) 5.00 kg

The correct answer is **(B)**.
