To prove that the dart hits the monkey, we must analyze the vertical positions of both objects as a function of time, $t$. Let the horizontal distance from the thrower to the tree be $R$ and the initial height of the monkey be $h_T$.

### Proof

**1. Position of the monkey:**
The monkey starts at height $h_T$ and undergoes free fall with acceleration $-g$. Its vertical position $y_m$ at time $t$ is:
$$y_m(t) = h_T - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$

**2. Position of the dart:**
The dart is thrown at an angle $\theta$. The horizontal component of velocity is $v_x = v \cos \theta$ and the vertical component is $v_y = v \sin \theta$. 
From the geometry of the triangle, we know that $\tan \theta = \frac{h_T}{R}$. 
The horizontal position of the dart at time $t$ is $x_d(t) = (v \cos \theta)t$. The dart reaches the tree when $x_d(t) = R$, which occurs at time $t = \frac{R}{v \cos \theta}$.

The vertical position of the dart $y_d$ at time $t$ is:
$$y_d(t) = (v \sin \theta)t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$

**3. Comparison:**
Substitute the time of flight $t = \frac{R}{v \cos \theta}$ into the equation for $y_d$:
$$y_d = v \sin \theta \left( \frac{R}{v \cos \theta} \right) - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$
$$y_d = R \tan \theta - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$

Since $\tan \theta = \frac{h_T}{R}$, we substitute $R \tan \theta = R \left( \frac{h_T}{R} \right) = h_T$:
$$y_d = h_T - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$

Because $y_d(t) = y_m(t)$ at the moment the dart reaches the tree, the dart and the monkey are at the same vertical position. Thus, the dart hits the monkey.

### Explanation
To solve this, use the principle of independent horizontal and vertical motion. 
1. **Define the frames:** Establish coordinate systems for both objects.
2. **Kinematic equations:** Write the displacement equations for both objects. Note that both are subject to the same downward gravitational acceleration $g$.
3. **Equate positions:** Show that both the dart and the monkey fall by the same distance $\frac{1}{2}gt^2$ from their "line-of-sight" paths. Because the initial velocity vector was pointed directly at the monkey, this common "drop" ensures they coincide at the same point in space.
