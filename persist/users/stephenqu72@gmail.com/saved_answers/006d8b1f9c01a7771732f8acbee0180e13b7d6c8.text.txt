**Answer:**

The correct answer is **C. Perpendicular to each other and to the direction of wave travel.**

---

**Explanation:**

To solve this question, you must understand the fundamental properties of an electromagnetic (EM) wave as described by Maxwell’s equations.

1.  **Nature of the Wave:** An electromagnetic wave consists of an oscillating electric field ($E$) and an oscillating magnetic field ($B$). 
2.  **Transverse Nature:** These waves are **transverse**, meaning the oscillations of the fields occur in a direction perpendicular to the direction of energy propagation (the direction of wave travel).
3.  **Orthogonality:** In a vacuum, the electric field vector and the magnetic field vector are always perpendicular to one another. Furthermore, the direction of wave propagation is determined by the cross product of the electric and magnetic fields ($\vec{E} \times \vec{B}$). This means the wave travels in a direction that is mutually perpendicular to both the electric and magnetic fields.

Visualizing this, if the wave travels along the $x$-axis, the electric field might oscillate along the $y$-axis, while the magnetic field oscillates along the $z$-axis. Because all three axes ($x, y, z$) are at right angles to one another, the fields are perpendicular to each other and perpendicular to the direction of motion.
