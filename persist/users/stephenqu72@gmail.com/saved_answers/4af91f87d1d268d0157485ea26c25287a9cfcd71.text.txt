To find the escape velocity of an object from Earth's surface, we use the principle of conservation of energy.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Understand the Concept:** Escape velocity is the minimum speed an object needs to break free from a planet's gravitational pull without further propulsion. The mass of the object ($m = 800\text{ kg}$) is irrelevant because it cancels out in the derivation of the formula.
2.  **The Formula:** The escape velocity $v_e$ is given by:
    $$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$
    Where:
    *   $G$ (Gravitational constant) $\approx 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2/\text{kg}^2$
    *   $M$ (Mass of Earth) $\approx 5.97 \times 10^{24}\text{ kg}$
    *   $r$ (Radius of Earth) $\approx 6.37 \times 10^6\text{ m}$
3.  **Calculation:**
    $$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24})}{6.37 \times 10^6}}$$
    $$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{7.965 \times 10^{14}}{6.37 \times 10^6}}$$
    $$v_e = \sqrt{1.25 \times 10^8} \approx 11,180\text{ m/s}$$
    Converted to scientific notation, this is approximately $1.12 \times 10^4\text{ m/s}$.
