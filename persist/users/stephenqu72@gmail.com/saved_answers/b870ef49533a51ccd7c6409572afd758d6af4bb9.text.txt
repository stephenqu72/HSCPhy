To determine which transition corresponds to the emission spectrum, we use the relationship between the energy of a photon ($\Delta E$) and its wavelength ($\lambda$):

$$\Delta E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$

Where:
- $h \approx 4.136 \times 10^{-15} \text{ eV s}$ (Planck's constant)
- $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$ (speed of light)
- $hc \approx 1241 \text{ eV nm}$

### Step-by-step calculation:

We need to calculate the energy change $\Delta E$ for each option and see which wavelength $\lambda$ from the spectrum ($565, 569, 575, 582, 587, 594, 600 \text{ nm}$) matches:

**Option A: $-1.45 \text{ eV}$ to $-3.54 \text{ eV}$**
$\Delta E = -1.45 - (-3.54) = 2.09 \text{ eV}$
$\lambda = \frac{1241}{2.09} \approx 593.8 \text{ nm}$ (Matches the **594 nm** line)

**Option B: $-1.45 \text{ eV}$ to $-5.74 \text{ eV}$**
$\Delta E = -1.45 - (-5.74) = 4.29 \text{ eV}$
$\lambda = \frac{1241}{4.29} \approx 289 \text{ nm}$ (Not in the visible spectrum shown)

**Option C: $-1.47 \text{ eV}$ to $-5.74 \text{ eV}$**
$\Delta E = -1.47 - (-5.74) = 4.27 \text{ eV}$
$\lambda = \frac{1241}{4.27} \approx 291 \text{ nm}$ (Not in the visible spectrum shown)

**Option D: $-1.45 \text{ eV}$ to $-1.47 \text{ eV}$**
$\Delta E = 0.02 \text{ eV}$
$\lambda = \frac{1241}{0.02} = 62050 \text{ nm}$ (Infrared, not in spectrum)

### Conclusion
The transition from $-1.45 \text{ eV}$ to $-3.54 \text{ eV}$ produces a photon with a wavelength of approximately $594 \text{ nm}$, which is clearly visible in the provided emission spectrum.

**The correct answer is A.**
