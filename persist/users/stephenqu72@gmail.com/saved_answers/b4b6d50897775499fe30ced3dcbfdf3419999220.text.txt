To solve this problem, we need to analyze the physical principles behind nuclear fission and nuclear fusion.

### Analysis of the Statements:

*   **A. Neutrons are released:** While fission reactions (like $U-235$ splitting) typically release neutrons, fusion reactions (like deuterium-tritium fusion) can release neutrons, but not all fusion processes do. Therefore, this is not a universal rule for all fusion reactions.
*   **B. No harmful radiation is produced:** This is incorrect. Both processes involve high-energy particles and electromagnetic radiation (gamma rays), both of which are forms of harmful ionizing radiation.
*   **C. The binding energy per nucleon increases:** **This is the correct statement.** The stability of a nucleus is determined by its binding energy per nucleon.
    *   In **fission**, a very heavy, less stable nucleus splits into lighter, more stable nuclei.
    *   In **fusion**, very light, less stable nuclei join to form a more stable, heavier nucleus.
    *   In both cases, the resulting products are closer to the "peak" of the binding energy curve (near Iron-56), meaning the binding energy per nucleon increases, which results in a release of energy ($E = \Delta mc^2$).
*   **D. The total mass increases:** This is incorrect. In both fission and fusion, the products have a lower total mass than the reactants. This "mass defect" ($\Delta m$) is converted into energy.

### Conclusion:
Because both fission and fusion reactions move nuclei toward a more stable configuration (higher binding energy per nucleon), statement **C** is the correct answer.
