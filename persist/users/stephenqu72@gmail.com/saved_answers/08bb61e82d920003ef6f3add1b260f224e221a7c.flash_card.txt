### Front
State Kepler’s Second Law and its implication regarding the time taken for a planet to sweep through equal orbital areas.

### Back
- **Key idea:** A line joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas in equal time intervals.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\frac{dA}{dt} = \text{constant}$; No fixed constant.
- **When to use it:** To compare the orbital speed or time taken for a planet to travel between different points in an elliptical orbit.
- **Common trap:** Assuming orbital velocity is constant; it is only constant in a perfectly circular orbit. Velocity is greatest at perihelion and slowest at aphelion.
