### (a) Calculate the radius of orbit of Planet A in AU
**Method:** Kepler’s Third Law states that for objects orbiting the same central mass, the ratio $\frac{r^3}{T^2}$ is constant:
$$\frac{r_A^3}{T_A^2} = \frac{r_B^3}{T_B^2}$$

**Calculation:**
Given $T_A = 43 \text{ days}$, $T_B = 145 \text{ days}$, and $r_B = 0.2 \text{ AU}$:
$$r_A^3 = r_B^3 \times \left(\frac{T_A}{T_B}\right)^2$$
$$r_A^3 = (0.2)^3 \times \left(\frac{43}{145}\right)^2$$
$$r_A^3 = 0.008 \times (0.29655)^2 \approx 0.008 \times 0.08794 \approx 0.0007035$$
$$r_A = \sqrt[3]{0.0007035} \approx 0.0889 \text{ AU}$$

### (b) Calculate the diameter of Planet B
**Method:** The distance from the center of the star to the surface of the planet is given as $0.195 \text{ AU}$. The orbital radius $r_B$ is the distance from the center of the star to the center of the planet.
$r_B = 0.2 \text{ AU} = 0.2 \times 150 \times 10^6 \text{ km} = 30 \times 10^6 \text{ km}$.
The distance from the center of the star to the surface is $r_B - \text{radius of planet} (R_p) = 0.195 \text{ AU}$.

**Calculation:**
$0.195 \text{ AU} = 0.195 \times 150 \times 10^6 = 29.25 \times 10^6 \text{ km}$.
$$R_p = r_B - 29.25 \times 10^6 = 30 \times 10^6 - 29.25 \times 10^6 = 0.75 \times 10^6 \text{ km}$$
Diameter $= 2 \times R_p = 1.5 \times 10^6 \text{ km}$.

### (c) Calculate the change in total mechanical energy of Planet B
**Method:** The total mechanical energy $E$ of a satellite in a circular orbit is $E = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$.
When $r$ doubles ($r_2 = 2r_1$), the new energy $E_2$ is:
$$E_2 = -\frac{GMm}{2(2r_1)} = \frac{1}{2} E_1$$
The change in energy is $\Delta E = E_2 - E_1 = -\frac{1}{2} E_1 - (-E_1) = +\frac{1}{2} E_1$.
Essentially, the energy becomes half as negative, meaning it increases by an amount equal to half the initial absolute value.

**Calculation:**
Initial energy $E_1 = -\frac{GMm}{2r_1}$.
$\Delta E = \frac{GMm}{4r_1}$
Using $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}$, $M = 1.02 \times 10^{29}$, $m = 7.0 \times 10^{22}$, $r_1 = 3 \times 10^{10} \text{ m}$ ($0.2 \text{ AU}$ converted to meters):
$$\Delta E = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(1.02 \times 10^{29})(7.0 \times 10^{22})}{4 \times (3 \times 10^{10})} \approx 3.98 \times 10^{30} \text{ J}$$
