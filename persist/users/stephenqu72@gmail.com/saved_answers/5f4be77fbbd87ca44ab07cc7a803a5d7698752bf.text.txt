### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a): Thomson's Experiment
To outline Thomson's experiment for 4 marks, you must describe the setup and the logic behind how he isolated the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$).

**Outline:**
1. **Setup:** A cathode ray tube (CRT) produces a beam of electrons accelerated by an electric potential.
2. **Deflection:** The beam is passed through perpendicular electric ($E$) and magnetic ($B$) fields.
3. **Velocity Selection:** Thomson adjusted the fields so that the electric force ($F_E = qE$) and magnetic force ($F_B = qvB$) were equal and opposite, resulting in zero net deflection. This allows the velocity of the electrons to be determined: $v = E/B$.
4. **Circular Motion:** The electric field is then turned off, leaving only the magnetic field. The electrons undergo circular motion where the magnetic force acts as a centripetal force: $qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$.
5. **Ratio Calculation:** By measuring the radius ($r$) of the circular path and knowing $v$ and $B$, the ratio $q/m$ can be calculated as:
   $$\frac{q}{m} = \frac{v}{Br}$$

---

#### Part (b): Calculation
To find the force on the electron, we use the magnetic force formula for a charged particle:
$$F = qvB\sin(\theta)$$

**1. Identify given variables:**
- $q = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$ (charge of an electron)
- $v = 8.0 \times 10^6\text{ m/s}$
- $B = 1.5 \times 10^{-2}\text{ T}$
- $\theta = 40^\circ$ (the angle between the velocity vector and the magnetic field lines)

**2. Calculation:**
$$F = (1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \times (8.0 \times 10^6) \times (1.5 \times 10^{-2}) \times \sin(40^\circ)$$
$$F = (1.92 \times 10^{-14}) \times \sin(40^\circ)$$
$$F \approx 1.23 \times 10^{-14}\text{ N}$$

**Final Answer:** The magnitude of the force on the electron is $1.23 \times 10^{-14}\text{ N}$. Using the right-hand rule (remembering to reverse the direction for a negative electron), the force acts directed out of the plane of the motion/perpendicular to the velocity.
