### 2. Determine the fringe spacing ($\Delta y$):
Looking at the diagram, $R$ is a bright fringe and $T$ is the *next* dark fringe. The distance between a bright fringe and the adjacent dark fringe is exactly half the fringe spacing ($\Delta y$).
$$\frac{\Delta y}{2} = 18 \text{ mm} \implies \Delta y = 36 \text{ mm} = 0.036 \text{ m}$$

### 3. Use the fringe spacing formula:
The formula for the spacing between adjacent bright fringes is:
$$\Delta y = \frac{\lambda D}{d}$$
Rearranging to solve for the wavelength ($\lambda$):
$$\lambda = \frac{\Delta y \cdot d}{D}$$
