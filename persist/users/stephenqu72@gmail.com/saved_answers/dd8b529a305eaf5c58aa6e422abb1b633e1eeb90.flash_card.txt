### Front
How do you calculate the potential difference ($V$) required to accelerate a proton between D-chambers in a cyclotron?

### Back
- Key idea: Work done by the electric field equals the change in the proton's kinetic energy.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $qV = \Delta E_k = \frac{1}{2}m(v_f^2 - v_i^2)$ where $v = \frac{rqB}{m}$.
- When to use it: When finding the accelerating voltage required for a specific increase in the cyclotron path radius ($r$).
- Common trap: Forgetting that the distance between entry and exit points in a D-chamber is the **diameter** ($2r$), not the radius ($r$).
