To solve this problem, we must derive the expression for the acceleration of the particle placed in the uniform electric field between two parallel metal plates.

### Derivation
1.  **Electric Field:** The electric field $E$ between two parallel plates with a potential difference $V$ separated by a distance $d$ is given by:
    $$E = \frac{V}{d}$$
2.  **Electric Force:** The force $F$ exerted by this electric field on a particle with charge $q$ is:
    $$F = qE = q\left(\frac{V}{d}\right)$$
3.  **Acceleration:** According to Newton's Second Law, $F = ma$. Therefore:
    $$ma = \frac{qV}{d}$$
    Solving for acceleration $a$:
    $$a = \frac{qV}{md}$$

### Analysis of the Statements
We compare our formula $a = \frac{qV}{md}$ to the options provided:
*   **A:** The acceleration is proportional to $qV$. Since $q$ and $V$ are in the numerator, this is **correct**.
*   **B:** The acceleration is proportional to $\frac{q}{m}$. Since both are variables in the expression, this is **correct**.
*   **C:** The acceleration is proportional to $md$. Since $m$ and $d$ are in the denominator, the acceleration is actually *inversely* proportional to the product $md$. Therefore, saying it is "proportional to $md$" is **NOT correct**.
*   **D:** The acceleration is proportional to $\frac{V}{d}$. Since $V$ is in the numerator and $d$ is in the denominator, this represents the electric field strength, and the acceleration is indeed proportional to this ratio. This is **correct**.

The statement that is **NOT correct** is **C**.
