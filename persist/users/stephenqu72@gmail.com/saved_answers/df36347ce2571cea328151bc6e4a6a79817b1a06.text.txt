To solve this problem, we apply the principle of **Conservation of Mechanical Energy**. Since air resistance is negligible, the total mechanical energy (kinetic + gravitational potential) at the point of release must equal the total mechanical energy at the moment of impact with the sea.

### Part (a)
Let the release point be $h = 0$ m. Therefore, the sea level is at $h = -28$ m.
The total energy at the release point is:
$$E_{total} = K_i + U_i = \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2 + mgh_i$$
At the sea level:
$$E_{total} = K_f + U_f = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 + mgh_f$$

Setting $E_i = E_f$ and cancelling the mass $m$:
$$\frac{1}{2}v_i^2 + gh_i = \frac{1}{2}v_f^2 + gh_f$$

Given $v_i = 14 \text{ m s}^{-1}$, $h_i = 0$, $h_f = -28 \text{ m}$, and using $g = 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$:
$$\frac{1}{2}(14)^2 + 0 = \frac{1}{2}v_f^2 + (9.8)(-28)$$
$$98 = \frac{1}{2}v_f^2 - 274.4$$
$$v_f^2 = 2 \times (98 + 274.4) = 744.8$$
$$v_f = \sqrt{744.8} \approx 27.3 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

### Part (b)
We are plotting the **magnitude** of the vertical component of velocity $v_y$.
1. **At $t=0$**: The stone is thrown upwards, so $v_y$ is positive. The magnitude starts at some value $v_{y0} > 0$.
2. **At $t=T_H$**: The stone reaches maximum height, so the vertical velocity is $0$. The magnitude of the speed is $0$.
3. **From $t=T_H$ to $t=T_S$**: The stone falls. The vertical velocity becomes increasingly negative. Since we are plotting **magnitude** ($|v_y|$), the value starts at $0$ at $T_H$ and increases linearly as the stone accelerates downwards.
4. **Graph shape**: A V-shape (or two straight lines) meeting at the x-axis at $t=T_H$.
