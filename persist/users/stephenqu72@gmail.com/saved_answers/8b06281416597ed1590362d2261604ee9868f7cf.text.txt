To solve this problem, we use the formula for the gravitational field strength $g$ at the surface of a planet:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Where:
*   $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
*   $M$ is the mass of the planet.
*   $r$ is the radius of the planet.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Define the variables for Earth:**
    Let the mass of Earth be $M_E$ and the radius of Earth be $r_E$. The gravitational field strength at Earth's surface is:
    $$g = \frac{GM_E}{r_E^2}$$

2.  **Define the variables for the new planet:**
    We are given that the new planet has:
    *   Mass $M_p = 2M_E$
    *   Radius $r_p = \frac{1}{2}r_E$

3.  **Calculate the new gravitational field strength ($g_p$):**
    Substitute the new values into the formula:
    $$g_p = \frac{G(2M_E)}{(\frac{1}{2}r_E)^2}$$

4.  **Simplify the expression:**
    $$g_p = \frac{2GM_E}{\frac{1}{4}r_E^2}$$
    $$g_p = 2 \times 4 \times \frac{GM_E}{r_E^2}$$
    $$g_p = 8 \times \left(\frac{GM_E}{r_E^2}\right)$$

5.  **Relate it back to $g$:**
    Since $g = \frac{GM_E}{r_E^2}$, we can see that:
    $$g_p = 8g$$

Therefore, the correct option is **(D)**.
