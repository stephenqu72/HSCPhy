To solve this problem, we use the de Broglie wavelength formula:
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$
where:
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength
- $h$ is Planck's constant
- $m$ is the mass of the particle
- $v$ is the velocity of the particle

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1. **State the given condition:**
   The proton ($p$) and alpha particle ($\alpha$) have the same wavelength:
   $$\lambda_p = \lambda_\alpha$$

2. **Equate the expressions:**
   Using the formula $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$:
   $$\frac{h}{m_p v_p} = \frac{h}{m_\alpha v_\alpha}$$
   Since $h$ is constant on both sides, we can cancel it out:
   $$m_p v_p = m_\alpha v_\alpha$$

3. **Rearrange to find the ratio of speeds:**
   We want to find the ratio $\frac{v_\alpha}{v_p}$:
   $$\frac{v_\alpha}{v_p} = \frac{m_p}{m_\alpha}$$

4. **Identify the masses:**
   - A proton has a mass of approximately $1 \, \text{amu}$ ($m_p \approx 1$).
   - An alpha particle consists of two protons and two neutrons, giving it a mass of approximately $4 \, \text{amu}$ ($m_\alpha \approx 4$).
   - Therefore, $m_\alpha \approx 4 m_p$.

5. **Calculate the ratio:**
   Substitute the mass ratio into the velocity ratio equation:
   $$\frac{v_\alpha}{v_p} = \frac{m_p}{4 m_p} = \frac{1}{4}$$

**Conclusion:**
The ratio of the speed of the alpha particle to the speed of the proton is $1/4$. This corresponds to option **(A)**.
