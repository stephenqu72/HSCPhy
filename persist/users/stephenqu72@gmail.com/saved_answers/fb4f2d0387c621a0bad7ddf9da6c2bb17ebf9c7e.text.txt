To solve for the magnetic force experienced by a moving charge in a magnetic field, we use the formula for the Lorentz force (the magnetic component):

$$F = qvB \sin(\theta)$$

### Given values:
*   Charge ($q$) = $+2.5\text{ C}$
*   Velocity ($v$) = $3.6 \times 10^5\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Magnetic field strength ($B$) = $7.4 \times 10^{-2}\text{ T}$
*   Angle ($\theta$) = $30^\circ$

### Step-by-step Calculation:

1.  **Identify the variables:** Ensure all units are in SI units (Coulombs, m/s, Tesla). They are already in the correct units.
2.  **Substitute the values into the equation:**
    $$F = (2.5) \times (3.6 \times 10^5) \times (7.4 \times 10^{-2}) \times \sin(30^\circ)$$
3.  **Perform the multiplication:**
    *   $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$
    *   $F = 2.5 \times 360,000 \times 0.074 \times 0.5$
    *   $F = 900,000 \times 0.074 \times 0.5$
    *   $F = 66,600 \times 0.5$
    *   $F = 33,300\text{ N}$
4.  **Convert to scientific notation:**
    $$F = 3.33 \times 10^4\text{ N}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches **(B)**.

***

### Answer:
The magnitude of the magnetic force experienced by the charge is **$3.3 \times 10^4\text{ N}$**. Therefore, the correct option is **(B)**.
