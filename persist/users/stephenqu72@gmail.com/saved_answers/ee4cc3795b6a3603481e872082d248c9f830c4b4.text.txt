To solve this problem, we must first calculate the force per unit length exerted on conductor X by conductor Y, and then determine which of the external magnetic field scenarios (A, B, C, or D) produces an equivalent force.

### 1. Calculate the original force per unit length
The force per unit length $f$ between two parallel conductors carrying currents $I_1$ and $I_2$ separated by a distance $r$ is given by:
$$f = \frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$

Given:
- $I_1 = I_2 = 5.0\text{ A}$
- $r = 5.0\text{ cm} = 0.05\text{ m}$
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

Substituting these values:
$$f = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(5.0)(5.0)}{2\pi(0.05)} = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 25}{0.05} = 1.0 \times 10^{-4}\text{ N/m}$$

### 2. Analyze the external magnetic field scenarios
The force on a conductor in an external magnetic field is given by $F = B I L \sin(\theta)$, or force per unit length:
$$f = \frac{F}{L} = B I \sin(\theta)$$

For the force to be $1.0 \times 10^{-4}\text{ N/m}$, we need:
$$1.0 \times 10^{-4} = B(5.0)\sin(90^\circ)$$
$$B = \frac{1.0 \times 10^{-4}}{5.0} = 2.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$$

Now, let's look at the magnetic field produced by conductor Y at the position of conductor X. According to the right-hand grip rule, current flowing to the right in Y produces a magnetic field that points **out of the page** at the position of conductor X.

The magnitude of this field is:
$$B_Y = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r} = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(5.0)}{2\pi(0.05)} = 2.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$$

Since the original force was generated by a magnetic field of $2.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$ pointing **out of the page**, we look for an option where the external magnetic field is $2.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$ pointing **out of the page**.

### 3. Conclusion
- Scenarios (A) and (B) involve fields pointing up/down, which would result in a force directed into or out of the page, not toward or away from the conductor as in the original parallel wire configuration.
- Scenario (C) is a field directed **into** the page.
- Scenario (D) is a field directed **out of** the page.

Therefore, **(D)** is the correct situation.
