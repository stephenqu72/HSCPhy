To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of the **Doppler Effect**, which describes the change in frequency of a wave in relation to an observer who is moving relative to the wave source.

### Explanation

The observed frequency $f_{obs}$ when the source is moving relative to a stationary observer is given by the formula:
$$f_{obs} = f_s \left( \frac{v_w}{v_w \mp v_s} \right)$$
Where:
*   $f_s$ is the source frequency.
*   $v_w$ is the speed of sound.
*   $v_s$ is the speed of the source (the ambulance).

**1. Analyzing Ambulance A:**
Ambulance A is moving **directly towards** the observer. As it moves, it "catches up" to the sound waves it is emitting, causing them to compress. This results in a shorter wavelength and a **higher** frequency being detected by the observer.

**2. Analyzing Ambulance B:**
Ambulance B is moving **directly away** from the observer. As it moves, it increases the distance between successive wave crests, effectively stretching the sound waves. This results in a longer wavelength and a **lower** frequency being detected by the observer.

Comparing these results to the options provided, the frequency of ambulance A's siren is measured to be higher, and the frequency of ambulance B's siren is measured to be lower.

### Answer
The correct option is **D**.
