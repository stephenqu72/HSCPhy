To solve this problem, we need to determine the magnitude and direction of the magnetic force acting on a moving charge.

### 1. Identify the Direction of the Magnetic Field ($B$)
Using the **Right-Hand Grip Rule** for the current-carrying loop:
- Point your right thumb in the direction of the current ($I$) at any point on the loop (e.g., the top wire flows to the left).
- Curl your fingers through the center of the loop.
- Your fingers point **out of the page** at the center of the loop. Therefore, the magnetic field $B$ is directed out of the page.

### 2. Determine the Direction of the Force ($F$)
We use the **Right-Hand Palm Rule** (or Fleming's Left-Hand Rule for negative charges) for the magnetic force on a moving charge $q$:
- **Thumb:** Direction of velocity $v$ (to the right).
- **Fingers:** Direction of magnetic field $B$ (out of the page).
- **Palm:** Direction of force $F$ for a *positive* charge. 
- For a positive charge, the palm points **up**.
- Since the electron is a **negative** charge, we reverse the direction. Thus, the force on the electron is **down the page**.

### 3. Calculate the Magnitude of the Force
The formula for the magnetic force on a moving charge is:
$$F = qvB\sin(\theta)$$
Where:
- $q = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$ (charge of an electron)
- $v = 1.5 \times 10^7\text{ m/s}$
- $B = 30\ \mu\text{T} = 30 \times 10^{-6}\text{ T}$
- $\theta = 90^\circ$ (since $v$ is in the plane of the page and $B$ is perpendicular to the page), so $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

Calculation:
$$F = (1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times (1.5 \times 10^7) \times (30 \times 10^{-6})$$
$$F \approx 1.6 \times 1.5 \times 30 \times 10^{-19+7-6}$$
$$F \approx 7.2 \times 10^{-17}\text{ N}$$

Combining the magnitude and direction, the correct answer is **A**.
