To solve this problem, we need to consider the energy transformation of the charged particle as it moves through the electric field.

### Explanation

1.  **Understand the Physics:** The particle is a positive charge entering a downward-directed electric field (created by a positive plate on top and a negative plate on the bottom). A positive charge experiences a force in the direction of the electric field. Therefore, the charge is accelerated downwards.
2.  **Energy Principle:** The total work done on the charge by the electric field will increase its kinetic energy. The work done ($W$) by an electric field on a charge ($q$) moving through a potential difference ($V$) is given by $W = qV$. Alternatively, using the electric field strength ($E$) and displacement ($d$):
    $$W = F \times d = (qE) \times d$$
3.  **Identify Given Values:**
    *   Charge $q = 1.2\text{ C}$
    *   Electric field $E = 1400\text{ N C}^{-1}$
    *   Vertical deflection $d = 3.5\text{ cm} = 0.035\text{ m}$
    *   Initial Kinetic Energy $KE_i = 131\text{ J}$
4.  **Calculate the Work Done:**
    $$W = qEd = 1.2 \times 1400 \times 0.035$$
    $$W = 1680 \times 0.035 = 58.8\text{ J}$$
5.  **Calculate Final Kinetic Energy:** Since the field does positive work on the particle (accelerating it in the direction of the field), the final kinetic energy ($KE_f$) is the sum of the initial kinetic energy and the work done:
    $$KE_f = KE_i + W$$
    $$KE_f = 131\text{ J} + 58.8\text{ J} = 189.8\text{ J}$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number provided in the options, we get $190\text{ J}$.
