### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to recall the three primary "pillars" of evidence that support the Big Bang Theory within the HSC Physics curriculum:

1.  **Redshift (A):** Observations, most notably by Edwin Hubble, showed that the light from distant galaxies is shifted toward the red end of the spectrum. This indicates that these galaxies are moving away from us. This expansion of space is a direct consequence of the Big Bang model, which describes an evolving, expanding universe starting from a dense, hot singularity.
2.  **Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) Radiation (B):** This is often considered the "smoking gun" of the Big Bang. It is the remnant heat left over from the early universe (about 380,000 years after the Big Bang), which has cooled and stretched into microwave wavelengths as the universe expanded. Its discovery in 1964 confirmed the prediction that the universe was once extremely hot and dense.
3.  **Relative Abundance of Lighter Elements (C):** The Big Bang model predicts that the early universe would have been hot and dense enough for a brief period of nuclear fusion. This process, known as Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, accounts for the observed high abundance of light elements, specifically hydrogen (about 75%) and helium (about 25%), with trace amounts of lithium. The observed ratios in the universe match the theoretical predictions of this model perfectly.

Since options (A), (B), and (C) are all key pieces of scientific evidence that validate the Big Bang Theory, option (D) is the correct choice.
