### Answer
The correct answer is **D. Downwards**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must apply the fundamental principles of **uniform circular motion**:

1.  **Definition of Centripetal Force:** An object undergoing uniform circular motion is constantly changing its direction of velocity. According to Newton's Second Law ($F = ma$), this acceleration requires a net force. In circular motion, this net force is called the **centripetal force**.
2.  **Direction of the Force:** By definition, the centripetal force always acts **perpendicular** to the object's instantaneous velocity ($v$) and is directed towards the **center of the circular path**.
3.  **Applying to the diagram:**
    *   Locate the position of the object at velocity $v_1$ (at the top of the circular path).
    *   The center of the circle is located directly below this position.
    *   Since the net force (centripetal force) must point towards the center of the circle, at the top position, the force vector must point straight **downwards**.

Therefore, at the position of $v_1$, the net force points towards the center, which is in the downwards direction.
