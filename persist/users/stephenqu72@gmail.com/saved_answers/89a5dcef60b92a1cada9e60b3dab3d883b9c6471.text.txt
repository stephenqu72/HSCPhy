### Answer
The correct option is **(C)**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we use the formula for the fringe width ($\Delta x$) in Young’s double-slit experiment:

$$\Delta x = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$

Where:
*   $\Delta x$ is the fringe separation (the distance between adjacent dark or bright bands).
*   $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the light used.
*   $L$ is the distance from the double slits to the screen.
*   $d$ is the separation distance between the two slits.

To **increase** the value of $\Delta x$, we must manipulate the variables in the equation:
*   **Increase** $\lambda$ (the wavelength).
*   **Increase** $L$ (the distance to the screen).
*   **Decrease** $d$ (the slit separation).

Let's evaluate the options provided:
*   **(A) Decrease the distance between the slits and the screen ($L$):** According to the formula, $\Delta x$ is directly proportional to $L$. Decreasing $L$ will **decrease** $\Delta x$.
*   **(B) Decrease the wavelength of the light ($\lambda$):** $\Delta x$ is directly proportional to $\lambda$. Decreasing $\lambda$ will **decrease** $\Delta x$.
*   **(C) Decrease the slit separation ($d$):** $\Delta x$ is inversely proportional to $d$. Therefore, **decreasing** the denominator ($d$) will **increase** the value of $\Delta x$. This is the correct action.
*   **(D) Decrease the slit width:** The slit width affects the intensity profile and the single-slit diffraction envelope, but it does not appear in the primary formula for the spacing of the interference fringes in an ideal double-slit experiment.

Thus, decreasing the slit separation ($d$) is the correct way to increase the fringe spacing.
