### Front
How do you determine the net magnetic field at a point between two parallel current-carrying wires?

### Back
- Key idea: The Principle of Superposition—the net magnetic field is the vector sum of the individual fields produced by each wire.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Right-Hand Grip Rule ($B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$); Vector addition.
- When to use it: Whenever multiple current-carrying conductors are present in the same region.
- Common trap: Ignoring the direction of the fields (into vs. out of page). You must treat fields as vectors; fields in opposite directions subtract, while fields in the same direction add.
