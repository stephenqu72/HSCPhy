### Front
What is the condition required for an object to be in rotational equilibrium?

### Back
- Key idea: The net torque ($\sum \tau$) acting about a pivot point must be zero.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\sum \tau = 0$ (or $\tau_{\text{clockwise}} = \tau_{\text{counter-clockwise}}$)
- When to use it: To calculate unknown forces, distances, or angles when an object is not rotating (e.g., stable flight or static beams).
- Common trap: Forgetting to use only the component of the force that is perpendicular to the lever arm ($F\sin\theta$ or $F\cos\theta$).
