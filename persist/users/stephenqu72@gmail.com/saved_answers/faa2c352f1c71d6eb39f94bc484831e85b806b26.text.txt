To solve this problem, we must analyze the forces acting on the driver at the peak of the loop.

### Physics Principles
At the top of a vertical loop, the forces acting on the driver (directed downwards toward the center of the circle) are the normal force ($F_N$) and the gravitational force ($mg$). These two forces provide the net centripetal force required for circular motion.

The equation for centripetal force is:
$$\sum F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

At the top of the loop:
$$F_N + mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

### Given Data
*   Mass of the driver ($m$): $75\text{ kg}$ (Note: the mass of the car is extraneous information).
*   Diameter of the loop ($d$): $12\text{ m}$, so the radius ($r$) is $6\text{ m}$.
*   Normal force required ($F_N$): Equivalent to weight force, so $F_N = mg$.
*   Acceleration due to gravity ($g$): $9.8\text{ m/s}^2$.

### Step-by-Step Calculation
1.  **Substitute the condition $F_N = mg$ into the force equation:**
    $$mg + mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
    $$2mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

2.  **Cancel the mass ($m$) from both sides:**
    $$2g = \frac{v^2}{r}$$

3.  **Solve for $v$:**
    $$v^2 = 2gr$$
    $$v = \sqrt{2gr}$$

4.  **Plug in the values:**
    $$v = \sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 6}$$
    $$v = \sqrt{117.6}$$
    $$v \approx 10.84\text{ m/s}$$

**Answer:** The car must travel at approximately **$10.8\text{ m/s}$** for the driver to experience a normal force equal to their weight at the peak of the loop.
