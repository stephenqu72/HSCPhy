To solve this problem, we must treat the motion of the bowling ball as projectile motion, independent in the vertical and horizontal directions.

### Step 1: Calculate the time of flight ($t$)
The ball falls vertically from a height of $700\text{ m}$. We use the kinematic equation for vertical displacement, assuming initial vertical velocity $u_y = 0$:
$$s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$
$$700 = 0 + \frac{1}{2}(9.8)t^2$$
$$t^2 = \frac{700}{4.9} \approx 142.86$$
$$t \approx 11.95\text{ s}$$

### Step 2: Calculate the required horizontal range ($R$)
The ball will continue to move horizontally at the same speed as the plane ($v_x = 120\text{ m/s}$) for the duration of its fall:
$$R = v_x \times t$$
$$R = 120 \times 11.95 \approx 1434\text{ m}$$
This means the scientist must release the ball when the plane is exactly $1434\text{ m}$ away from the target horizontally.

### Step 3: Calculate the waiting time
The plane is currently $2000\text{ m}$ away. It needs to travel until it is only $1434\text{ m}$ away from the target before releasing the ball.
Distance to travel before dropping:
$$d_{travel} = 2000 - 1434 = 566\text{ m}$$
Time to wait ($t_{wait}$):
$$t_{wait} = \frac{d_{travel}}{v_x} = \frac{566}{120} \approx 4.72\text{ s}$$

**Answer:** The scientist needs to wait approximately **$4.72\text{ seconds}$** before dropping the ball.
