### Front
How do $\beta^-$ and $\alpha$ decay affect a nucleus's position on an $A$ vs $Z$ graph?

### Back
- Key idea: $\beta^-$ decay shifts the nucleus horizontally right ($\Delta Z = +1, \Delta A = 0$); $\alpha$ decay shifts it diagonally down-left ($\Delta Z = -2, \Delta A = -4$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\beta^-$: $(A, Z) \rightarrow (A, Z+1)$; $\alpha$: $(A, Z) \rightarrow (A-4, Z-2)$.
- When to use it: To trace or predict decay chains on a Segrè plot (or $A$ vs $Z$ graph).
- Common trap: Confusing the axes; remember the vertical axis is $A$ (nucleon number) and the horizontal axis is $Z$ (proton number).
