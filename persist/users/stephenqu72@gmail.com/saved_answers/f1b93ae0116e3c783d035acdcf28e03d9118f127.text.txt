To solve this problem, we use the formula for the gravitational field strength $g$ of a spherical body:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Where:
*   $g = 1.35 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$ (the gravitational field strength)
*   $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{ kg}^{-2}$ (the universal gravitational constant)
*   $M$ is the mass of Titan (what we need to find)
*   $r = 2575 \text{ km} = 2.575 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$ (the radius in standard SI units)

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $M$:**
    $$M = \frac{g \cdot r^2}{G}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values:**
    $$M = \frac{1.35 \times (2.575 \times 10^6)^2}{6.67 \times 10^{-11}}$$

3.  **Calculate $r^2$:**
    $$(2.575 \times 10^6)^2 \approx 6.63 \times 10^{12}$$

4.  **Complete the calculation:**
    $$M = \frac{1.35 \times 6.63 \times 10^{12}}{6.67 \times 10^{-11}}$$
    $$M = \frac{8.95 \times 10^{12}}{6.67 \times 10^{-11}}$$
    $$M \approx 1.34 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, $1.34 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$ is closest to $1.35 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$.
