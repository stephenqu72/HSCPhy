### Front
How do you determine the velocity of a particle undergoing time dilation when given its proper half-life ($t_0$) and observed half-life ($t$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Time dilation increases the observed half-life of particles moving at relativistic speeds.
- **Formula:** $t = \gamma t_0$, where $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}}$.
- **When to use it:** When solving problems involving subatomic particles (e.g., muons/pions) traveling at a significant fraction of the speed of light.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that distance $d$ in the observer's frame must be calculated using the *observed* time ($t$) and the particle's velocity ($v$), not the proper time ($t_0$).
