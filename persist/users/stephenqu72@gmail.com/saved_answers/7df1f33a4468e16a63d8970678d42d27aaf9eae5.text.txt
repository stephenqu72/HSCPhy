To determine the correct answer, we need to recall the standard SI units for these two physical quantities.

### Analysis:
1.  **Acceleration**: Acceleration is defined as the rate of change of velocity over time. 
    *   Velocity is measured in $\text{m s}^{-1}$.
    *   Acceleration is $\frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}$, which is $\frac{\text{m s}^{-1}}{\text{s}} = \text{m s}^{-2}$.
    *   Therefore, the SI unit for acceleration is $\text{m s}^{-2}$.

2.  **Gravitational Potential Energy**: Energy is defined as the capacity to do work. The SI unit for all forms of energy (including kinetic, gravitational potential, and elastic potential) is the Joule ($J$). 
    *   One Joule is equivalent to $1\text{ kg m}^2\text{ s}^{-2}$.

### Evaluating the Options:
*   (A) Acceleration is incorrect ($\text{m s}^{-1}$ is velocity).
*   **(B) Acceleration is $\text{m s}^{-2}$ and Energy is $J$. This matches our definitions.**

*   (D) Acceleration is correct; Energy is incorrect ($\text{m s}^{-1}$ is velocity).

The correct answer is **(B)**.
