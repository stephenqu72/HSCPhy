### Front
How does an elastic band investigation model Hubble’s Law in the context of universal expansion?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Expansion is uniform and isotropic; recessional velocity ($v$) is proportional to distance ($d$) from the observer.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Hubble's Law: $v = H_0d$.
- **When to use it:** To explain how space expands uniformly, causing distant objects to appear to recede faster than nearby ones.
- **Common trap:** Assuming galaxies themselves stretch; the model only represents the space *between* gravitationally bound objects increasing.
