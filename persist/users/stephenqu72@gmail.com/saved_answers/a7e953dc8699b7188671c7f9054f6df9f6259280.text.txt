### Answer

**(a)**
To find the input current, we assume the transformer is ideal (100% efficient), meaning power input equals power output:
$$P_{in} = P_{out}$$
$$V_{in} \cdot I_{in} = V_{out} \cdot I_{out}$$

Given:
$V_{in} = 240\text{ V}$
$V_{out} = 6\text{ V}$
$I_{out} = 1\text{ A}$

Rearranging for input current ($I_{in}$):
$$I_{in} = \frac{V_{out} \cdot I_{out}}{V_{in}}$$
$$I_{in} = \frac{6 \times 1}{240} = \frac{6}{240} = 0.025\text{ A}$$

The input current is **$0.025\text{ A}$** (or $25\text{ mA}$).

**(b)**
One way transformers are designed to maximise efficiency is by using a **laminated iron core**. 
By using thin, insulated layers of iron rather than a single solid block, the transformer reduces the flow of eddy currents. Eddy currents generate unwanted heat (thermal energy loss) when induced in the core by the changing magnetic flux. Minimising these currents ensures more electrical energy is transferred to the secondary coil rather than being lost as heat.
