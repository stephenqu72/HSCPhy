To solve this problem, we must analyze the Hertzsprung-Russell (HR) diagram provided. The x-axis represents surface temperature (in K) and the y-axis represents solar luminosity ($L_{\odot}$).

### Step-by-Step Analysis:

1.  **Identify the Regions:**
    *   **Region $X$** contains stars with high luminosity ($> 10^3 L_{\odot}$) and high temperature (near $10,000$ K). These are **supergiants**.
    *   **Region $Y$** contains stars with low luminosity ($< 10^{-2} L_{\odot}$) and high temperature (near $10,000$ K). These are **white dwarfs**.

2.  **Evaluate the Statements:**
    *   **(A) Stars in region $X$ and $Y$ have similar temperatures:** Looking at the x-axis, both clusters are located at approximately the same position on the temperature scale ($10,000$ K). This statement is **true**.
    *   **(B) Stars in region $X$ and $Y$ have different luminosities due to the different sizes of the stars:** According to the Stefan-Boltzmann Law, $L = 4\pi R^2 \sigma T^4$. Since both groups have similar temperatures ($T$), the vast difference in luminosity must be attributed to the surface area, i.e., the radius ($R$). Supergiants have very large radii, while white dwarfs have very small radii. This statement is **true**.
    *   **(C) Stars in region $X$ are nearing the end of their lives, while stars in region $Y$ have died:** Stars in region $X$ are indeed in a late stage of stellar evolution (post-main sequence). Stars in region $Y$ (white dwarfs) are technically the "remnants" or "corpses" of stars that have finished nuclear fusion. This statement is **true**.
    *   **(D) Stars in region $Y$ were once stars in region $X$ before they died:** This is **false**. Region $X$ represents supergiants (high-mass stars). When these high-mass stars die, they typically end as supernovae, leaving behind neutron stars or black holes. White dwarfs (region $Y$) are the end states of low-to-medium mass stars (like our Sun), which evolve through the red giant phase, not the high-luminosity supergiant phase shown in region $X$.

### Conclusion:
The false statement is **(D)**.
