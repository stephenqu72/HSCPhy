A **blue shift** occurs when a light source moves toward the observer, causing the observed wavelength of the light to decrease (shift toward the blue end of the spectrum). This is a result of the **Doppler effect**.

*   In the other options, the labels or arrows indicate different states of motion (receding or motion perpendicular to the line of sight), which do not produce a blue shift.

### Answer to Question 5
The correct answer is **(A)**.

**Explanation:**
According to **classical electrodynamics** (Maxwell’s equations), an accelerating charged particle must emit electromagnetic radiation. An electron orbiting a nucleus is undergoing centripetal acceleration to maintain its circular path. Therefore, Maxwell's theory predicts that the electron should constantly lose energy by radiating electromagnetic waves. This would cause the electron to spiral into the nucleus, which contradicts the observed stability of atoms—a paradox that eventually led to the development of the Bohr model and quantum mechanics.
