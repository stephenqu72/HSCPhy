To solve this problem, we must understand the physics of Young’s double-slit interference pattern. The distance between adjacent bright fringes (maxima) on the screen is known as the fringe separation, denoted by $\Delta y$.

The formula for the distance between fringes in a double-slit experiment is:
$$\Delta y = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$

Where:
*   $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the light used.
*   $L$ is the distance from the slits to the screen.
*   $d$ is the separation between the two slits.

To **increase** the distance between maxima ($\Delta y$), we need to either increase the wavelength ($\lambda$), increase the screen distance ($L$), or decrease the slit separation ($d$).

### Evaluating the Options:
*   **A. Decreasing the distance between slits ($d$):** Looking at the formula $\Delta y = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$, since $d$ is in the denominator, decreasing $d$ will result in an increase in $\Delta y$. **This is correct.**
