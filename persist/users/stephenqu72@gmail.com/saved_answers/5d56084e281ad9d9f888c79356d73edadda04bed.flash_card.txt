### Front
How is the gravitational field strength ($g$) defined, and how does it differ from the gravitational force ($F$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** $g$ is the gravitational field strength, representing acceleration due to gravity or force per unit mass.
- **Formula / law / constant:** $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$ (where $F = mg$).
- **When to use it:** When calculating the acceleration of a free-falling body or the intensity of a gravitational field at a distance $r$ from a mass $M$.
- **Common trap:** Confusing $g$ (a constant for a specific distance $r$) with the total gravitational force $F$ (which scales linearly with the mass $m$ of the object).
