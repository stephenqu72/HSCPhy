### Explanation
To solve for the speed of the propeller tip, we are dealing with uniform circular motion. The speed $v$ of an object moving in a circle is defined as the distance traveled in one full revolution (the circumference of the circle) divided by the time it takes to complete that revolution (the period $T$).

**1. Identify the given variables:**
*   Radius of the motion ($r$) = $8\text{ m}$
*   Period ($T$) = $0.32\text{ s}$

**2. State the formula:**
The circumference of the circle is $2\pi r$. Therefore, the linear speed $v$ is given by:
$$v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$$

**3. Substitute the values into the formula:**
$$v = \frac{2 \times \pi \times 8}{0.32}$$

**4. Perform the calculation:**
$$v = \frac{16\pi}{0.32}$$
$$v = 50\pi$$
Using the value of $\pi \approx 3.14159$:
$$v \approx 157.079\dots\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

Rounding to the appropriate number of significant figures (consistent with the options provided), we get **$157\text{ m s}^{-1}$**.

This matches option (C).
