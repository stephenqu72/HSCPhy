### Model Answer

**(a) Compare Newton’s and Huygens’ models of light, including ONE similarity and ONE difference.**

*   **Similarity:** Both models successfully explained the laws of reflection and refraction. Newton used the particle model to show that light obeys the law of reflection (like elastic collisions) and refraction (by assuming light travels faster in denser media). Huygens used his wave theory and the principle of secondary wavelets to explain reflection and refraction at boundaries.
*   **Difference:** The fundamental difference is the nature of light propagation. Newton proposed the **corpuscular theory**, suggesting light consists of discrete particles (corpuscles) traveling in straight lines. Conversely, Huygens proposed the **wave theory**, suggesting light propagates as longitudinal mechanical waves through a medium known as the "luminiferous aether."

**(b) Justify whose model of light was correct. Give reasons for your answer.**

Neither model was entirely correct. Historically, Huygens’ wave model gained precedence in the 19th century through Young’s Double Slit experiment (demonstrating interference and diffraction, which particles cannot explain). However, Newton’s corpuscular model was partially vindicated in the 20th century by Einstein’s explanation of the Photoelectric Effect, which proved that light behaves as discrete packets of energy called photons. Modern physics accepts the **wave-particle duality** of light, meaning light exhibits both wave-like and particle-like properties depending on the context of the experiment.

### Teacher's Explanation: How to Solve This
1.  **Analyze the Verb:** The question asks you to "Compare." This means you must show both the links between them (similarities) and the contrasts (differences). 
2.  **Structuring Part (a):** For a 3-mark question, clear signposting is essential. State the similarity (the phenomena they both explained) and the difference (the core physical nature of their hypotheses) explicitly. 
3.  **Answering Part (b):** Avoid the trap of saying "Huygens was right" or "Newton was right." Since the syllabus emphasizes the evolution of scientific understanding, you must demonstrate "HSC-level" maturity by acknowledging the **wave-particle duality**.
    *   Start by clarifying that neither was strictly "correct" by modern standards.
    *   Provide the historical context: Huygens explains diffraction/interference (wave), while Newton/Einstein explains the Photoelectric Effect (particle).
    *   Conclude with the modern synthesis: quantum mechanics dictates that light possesses a dual nature.
