### Front
In a "rotating drum" ride where the floor drops away, what forces act on the passenger in the vertical and horizontal planes to keep them in position?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Vertical equilibrium (friction vs. gravity) and horizontal circular motion (normal force as centripetal force).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $F_f = mg$ (vertical); $F_{net} = N = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ (horizontal).
- **When to use it:** Problems involving banked tracks, conical pendulums, or rotating cylinders where normal force provides centripetal acceleration.
- **Common trap:** Assuming friction provides the centripetal force; it does not—friction acts vertically to oppose gravity. The wall's normal force provides the centripetal force.
