To determine the correct answer, we use the formula for the magnetic field strength $B$ at a distance $r$ from a long, straight current-carrying wire:

$$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$

Where:
- $B$ is the magnetic field strength (given as $2\text{ T}$)
- $\mu_0$ is the permeability of free space ($4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$)
- $I$ is the current (which we need to find)
- $r$ is the distance from the wire ($10\text{ m}$)

### Step-by-step Solution:

1. **Rearrange the formula to solve for current ($I$):**
   $$I = \frac{B \cdot 2\pi r}{\mu_0}$$

2. **Substitute the known values into the equation:**
   $$I = \frac{2 \cdot 2\pi \cdot 10}{4\pi \times 10^{-7}}$$

3. **Simplify the calculation:**
   - The $2\pi$ in the numerator and the $4\pi$ in the denominator can be simplified:
   $$I = \frac{2 \cdot 10}{2 \cdot 10^{-7}}$$
   $$I = \frac{20}{2 \times 10^{-7}}$$
   $$I = 10 \times 10^7$$
   $$I = 1 \times 10^8\text{ A}$$

The calculated current required to produce exactly $2\text{ T}$ at $10\text{ m}$ is $1 \times 10^8\text{ A}$. Looking at the provided options:
