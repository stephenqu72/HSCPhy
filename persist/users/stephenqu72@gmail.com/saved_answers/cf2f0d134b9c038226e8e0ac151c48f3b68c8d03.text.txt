### 1. The Definition
A geostationary satellite appears stationary relative to a fixed point on the Earth's surface. To achieve this, the satellite must satisfy three primary conditions:
*   **Orbital Period:** The satellite must orbit at the same angular velocity as the Earth. Since the Earth completes one rotation in approximately 24 hours (a sidereal day), the satellite must have an orbital period of exactly **24 hours**.
*   **Orbital Plane:** The satellite must orbit directly above the Earth's **equator**. If it were in a polar orbit, it would move across the sky relative to the ground observer as the Earth rotates, rather than appearing fixed in one spot.
*   **Direction:** The satellite must orbit in the same direction as the Earth's rotation (west to east).
