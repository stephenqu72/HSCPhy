To solve for the torque applied to the wrench, we use the formula for torque ($\tau$):

$$\tau = r \cdot F \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

    *   **Distance ($r$):** The length of the wrench is given as $25\text{ cm}$. We must convert this into SI units (meters) to match the standard unit of torque ($N\cdot m$):

    *   **Angle ($\theta$):** The angle given between the handle of the wrench and the force vector is $60^\circ$. In the torque formula, $\theta$ represents the angle between the lever arm and the force vector.

2.  **Substitute the values into the formula:**
    $$\tau = 0.25\text{ m} \cdot 50\text{ N} \cdot \sin(60^\circ)$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
