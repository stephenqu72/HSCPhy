To answer this question effectively for the HSC Physics curriculum, you need to identify a specific phenomenon and explain how it demonstrates wave-like properties (such as superposition and interference).

### Answer
Young’s Double Slit Experiment is the primary experimental evidence supporting the wave model of light. In this experiment, monochromatic light is passed through two narrow, closely spaced slits, acting as coherent point sources. The light waves emerging from these slits overlap and undergo superposition. Where the path difference between the waves is an integer multiple of the wavelength ($n\lambda$), constructive interference occurs, creating bright fringes. Where the path difference is a half-integer multiple ($(n + 0.5)\lambda$), destructive interference occurs, creating dark fringes. This pattern of light and dark bands can only be explained by the wave nature of light, as particles would simply produce two distinct lines of light behind the slits.

### How to solve this question
1.  **Identify the phenomenon:** You must select a phenomenon that explicitly demonstrates wave behavior. Young’s Double Slit experiment is the "gold standard" for this. Other valid examples include diffraction around obstacles or polarization.
2.  **Explain the "Why":** For 3 marks, you cannot just name the experiment. You must link the experiment to the fundamental wave properties. 
    *   **Mark 1:** Identifying the experiment (Young’s Double Slit).
    *   **Mark 2:** Explaining the mechanism (superposition/interference of waves). 
    *   **Mark 3:** Describing the result (the alternating pattern of bright and dark fringes/maxima and minima) that confirms the wave model.
3.  **Use precise terminology:** Ensure you use terms like *superposition*, *constructive/destructive interference*, and *coherent sources*. Avoid vague descriptions like "it creates a pattern"—be specific about what the pattern represents.
