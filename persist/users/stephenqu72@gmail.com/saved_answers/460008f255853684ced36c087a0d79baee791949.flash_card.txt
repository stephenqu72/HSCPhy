### Front
How does doubling the speed of an object in uniform circular motion affect its centripetal acceleration ($a_c$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Centripetal acceleration is proportional to the square of velocity ($a_c \propto v^2$). Doubling velocity results in a 4-fold increase in acceleration.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$
- When to use it: Any problem involving uniform circular motion where speed or radius changes.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the velocity factor (e.g., incorrectly assuming $a_c$ only doubles).
