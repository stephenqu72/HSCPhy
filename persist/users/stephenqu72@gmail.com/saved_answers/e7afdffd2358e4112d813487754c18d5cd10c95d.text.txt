### Answer

Louis de Broglie proposed that matter exhibits wave-like properties, with a wavelength given by $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$. For an electron to exist in a stable orbit around a nucleus without radiating energy, it must form a **standing wave**.

For a standing wave to form in a circular orbit of radius $r$, the circumference of the orbit must be an integer multiple of the de Broglie wavelength:
$$2\pi r = n\lambda$$
where $n = 1, 2, 3, \dots$

Substituting the de Broglie relation $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$ into the standing wave condition:
$$2\pi r = n \left( \frac{h}{mv} \right)$$

Rearranging the equation to solve for angular momentum ($mvr$):
$$mvr = \frac{nh}{2\pi}$$

This derivation provides a theoretical physical basis for Bohr’s empirical postulate that angular momentum is quantized.
