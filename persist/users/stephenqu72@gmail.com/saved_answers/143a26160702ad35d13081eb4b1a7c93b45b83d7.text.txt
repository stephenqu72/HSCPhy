### Answer
The correct answer is **B. The temperature of the object**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to apply **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which describes the relationship between the temperature of a black body and the wavelength at which it emits the most radiation (the peak wavelength, $\lambda_{max}$).

Wien’s Displacement Law is given by the formula:
$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$

Where:
*   $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength of radiation.
*   $b$ is Wien's displacement constant ($2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m}\cdot\text{K}$).
*   $T$ is the absolute temperature of the object in Kelvin.

**Key concepts for the HSC Physics exam:**
1.  **Independence of material:** A defining characteristic of a "black body" in physics is that its emission spectrum depends *only* on its temperature, not on the material from which it is constructed or its surface properties.
2.  **Inverse proportionality:** As the temperature ($T$) of an object increases, the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) decreases, which is why objects shift from glowing dull red (longer wavelength) to bright white/blue (shorter wavelength) as they get hotter.
3.  **Elimination:** 
    *   Options A and C are incorrect because the material type does not affect the peak wavelength of a true black body.
    *   Option D is incorrect because the shape of the object does not change the peak wavelength of the radiation emitted by a black body.
