To solve this problem, we must interpret the standard features of the Hertzsprung-Russell (H-R) diagram:

### 1. Identify the Regions
*   **Region R:** This lies on the lower end of the main sequence. These are low-mass, cool, low-luminosity stars (likely red dwarfs).
*   **Region S:** This lies at the top left of the main sequence. These are high-mass, hot, high-luminosity stars (likely blue giants).
*   **Region Q:** This lies in the top right of the diagram. These are high-luminosity, cool stars, which identifies them as **Red Supergiants**.

### 2. Analyze the Physical Processes
*   **The p-p chain (Proton-Proton chain):** This is the dominant nuclear fusion process in stars with core temperatures below approximately $15 \times 10^6\text{ K}$. This includes the Sun and, more importantly, low-mass stars on the lower main sequence (Region R). High-mass stars (Region S) primarily use the CNO cycle. Thus, the $p-p$ chain is dominant in Region $R$.
*   **Greatest Size:** According to the Stefan-Boltzmann law, luminosity $L$ is related to radius $r$ and temperature $T$ by the formula:
    $$L = 4\pi r^2 \sigma T^4$$
    Rearranging for radius, we get $r \propto \sqrt{L} / T^2$. Stars in Region $Q$ (Red Supergiants) have extremely high luminosities but low temperatures. Because of the $1/T^2$ dependence, a low temperature combined with a high luminosity results in a very large radius. Therefore, stars in Region $Q$ are physically the largest.

### 3. Conclusion
*   Region where the $p-p$ chain is dominant: **R**
*   Region where stars have the greatest size: **Q**
