To answer this HSC Physics question, you must demonstrate an understanding of the historical progression of cathode ray research and how these findings collectively shifted the scientific consensus toward the discovery of the subatomic electron.

### Model Answer

The nature of cathode rays was a central debate in late 19th-century physics, primarily between those who believed they were electromagnetic waves (ether pulses) and those who believed they were material particles.

1.  **Hertz vs. Perrin/Thomson:** Early German physicists (like Hertz) argued cathode rays were waves because they could pass through thin metal foils without leaving holes. However, Jean Perrin proved they carried a negative charge by collecting them in a Faraday cage. 
2.  **Deflection Experiments:** J.J. Thomson utilized a cathode ray tube with both electric and magnetic fields. He observed that cathode rays were deflected by electric fields—a property incompatible with neutral electromagnetic waves. 
3.  **The $q/m$ Ratio:** By measuring the deflection in both fields, Thomson calculated the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$) of the rays. He found this ratio to be approximately 2000 times larger than that of a hydrogen ion.
4.  **Conclusion:** Thomson concluded that these "corpuscles" were much smaller than the smallest known atom (hydrogen). Since the rays were identical regardless of the cathode material used, he proposed that these corpuscles (electrons) were universal constituents of all matter.

This provided the experimental evidence required to replace the "billiard ball" model of the atom with the "plum pudding" model, proving the atom was divisible.

### How to solve this question:
*   **Identify the two-part requirement:** The question asks for (1) the experiments and (2) their role in the discovery of the electron. Ensure you don't just list experiments; you must explain how the results *led* to the conclusion of the electron's existence.
*   **The "Discuss" command term:** In HSC terms, "discuss" requires more than a recount. You must present the conflicting viewpoints (wave vs. particle) and show how the evidence resolved the conflict.
*   **Structure for 5 marks:**
    *   **1 mark:** Mention the nature of the debate (wave vs. particle).
    *   **2 marks:** Detail specific experiments (e.g., Faraday cage, electric/magnetic deflection).
    *   **2 marks:** Clearly link these results to the conclusion that particles exist with a mass much smaller than an atom, leading to the discovery of the electron.
