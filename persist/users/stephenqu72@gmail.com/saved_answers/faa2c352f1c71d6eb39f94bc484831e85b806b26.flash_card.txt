### Front
What is the minimum speed required at the top of a vertical loop for a driver to experience a normal force ($F_N$) equal to their own weight ($mg$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** At the top of a loop, the centripetal force is the vector sum of weight and the normal force.
- **Formula:** $v = \sqrt{2gr}$ (derived from $F_N + mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ where $F_N = mg$).
- **When to use it:** Vertical circular motion problems involving apparent weight or "feeling" a certain number of $g$'s at the peak.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to include the weight force ($mg$) in the centripetal force equation, or confusing the diameter ($d$) with the radius ($r$).
