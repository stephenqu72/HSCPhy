### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a): Free Body Diagram
To represent a vehicle on a frictionless banked turn, you must draw two vectors originating from the center of the box:
1.  **Weight ($mg$):** A vertical arrow pointing straight down.
2.  **Normal Force ($F_N$):** An arrow pointing perpendicular (at $90^{\circ}$) to the surface of the banked road.

Ensure these are clearly labelled. The vertical component of $F_N$ will balance $mg$, and the horizontal component will provide the centripetal force.

---

#### Part (b): Calculation and Assessment

**1. Identify Given Data:**
*   Radius $r = 80.0\text{ m}$
*   Angle $\theta = 23^{\circ}$
*   Acceleration due to gravity $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$
*   Government limit $v_{limit} = 55\text{ km/h} = \frac{55}{3.6} \approx 15.28\text{ m/s}$

**2. Derivation:**
For a frictionless banked turn, the horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force:
$$F_{N} \sin(\theta) = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
The vertical component balances the weight:
$$F_{N} \cos(\theta) = mg$$
Dividing the two equations:
$$\frac{\sin(\theta)}{\cos(\theta)} = \frac{v^2}{rg} \implies \tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$
Rearranging for $v$:
$$v = \sqrt{rg \tan(\theta)}$$

**3. Calculation:**
$$v = \sqrt{80.0 \times 9.8 \times \tan(23^{\circ})}$$
$$v = \sqrt{784 \times 0.42447}$$
$$v = \sqrt{332.78} \approx 18.24\text{ m/s}$$
Convert back to $\text{km/h}$:
$$18.24 \times 3.6 \approx 65.7\text{ km/h}$$

**4. Assessment:**
The ideal speed for this curve is approximately $66\text{ km/h}$. Since the government regulation states that motorists will travel at *no more than* $55\text{ km/h}$, and the design speed ($66\text{ km/h}$) is significantly higher than the allowed limit, the design is **inappropriate** (or potentially dangerous). A car traveling at $55\text{ km/h}$ would require inward friction to prevent sliding down the slope, whereas the design is intended to be frictionless at $66\text{ km/h}$.
