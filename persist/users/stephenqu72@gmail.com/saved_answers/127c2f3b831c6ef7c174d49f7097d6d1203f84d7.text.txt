To solve this problem, we first determine the speed of the hammer while it is rotating, then use projectile motion equations to find the range.

### (a) Show that the vertical component of the velocity is $14.2 \text{ ms}^{-1}$

**Step 1: Calculate the orbital speed ($v$) of the hammer.**
The speed of an object in uniform circular motion is given by:
$$v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$$
Given $r = 1.6 \text{ m}$ and $T = 0.50 \text{ s}$:
$$v = \frac{2 \times \pi \times 1.6}{0.50} = \frac{3.2\pi}{0.50} \approx 20.106 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$

**Step 2: Calculate the vertical component ($v_y$).**
The velocity vector at $P$ makes an angle of $45^\circ$ to the horizontal. The vertical component is found using trigonometry:
$$v_y = v \sin(45^\circ)$$
$$v_y = 20.106 \times \sin(45^\circ) \approx 20.106 \times 0.7071 \approx 14.217 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$
Rounding to three significant figures, we get **$14.2 \text{ ms}^{-1}$**.

---

### (b) Calculate the horizontal range

**Step 1: Find the horizontal velocity ($v_x$).**
$$v_x = v \cos(45^\circ) = 20.106 \times \cos(45^\circ) \approx 14.217 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$

**Step 2: Find the time of flight ($t$).**
Using the displacement equation for the vertical direction: $s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$.
Setting $s_y = -1.2 \text{ m}$ (since it lands below the release point), $u_y = 14.217 \text{ ms}^{-1}$, and $a_y = -9.8 \text{ ms}^{-2}$:
$$-1.2 = 14.217t - 4.9t^2$$
$$4.9t^2 - 14.217t - 1.2 = 0$$
Using the quadratic formula $t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$$t = \frac{14.217 + \sqrt{(-14.217)^2 - 4(4.9)(-1.2)}}{9.8} \approx \frac{14.217 + 15.013}{9.8} \approx 2.983 \text{ s}$$

**Step 3: Calculate range ($R$).**
$$R = v_x \times t = 14.217 \times 2.983 \approx 42.4 \text{ m}$$
The horizontal range of the projectile is approximately **$42.4 \text{ m}$**.
