### Answer
The correct answer is **D) emission spectra**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we need to understand the interaction between electrons and energy levels within an atom, a core concept in the HSC Physics module "From the Universe to the Atom."

1.  **Energy Absorption (Excitation):** When an atom absorbs energy (such as an electrical current), its electrons move from a lower energy state (the ground state) to a higher energy state. This is called excitation.
2.  **Energy Release (De-excitation):** Electrons are naturally unstable in higher energy states. They quickly return to a lower energy state (eventually the ground state). When they drop down, they must lose the extra energy they absorbed. This energy is released as a photon (a packet of light).
3.  **The Resulting Observation:** Because the energy levels within an atom are "quantized" (meaning they exist at specific, fixed levels), the photons released have specific, discrete energies. This results in the emission of light at specific wavelengths. When this light is passed through a prism or diffraction grating, it produces an **emission spectrum**, which appears as a series of bright, coloured lines on a black background.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **A) Absorption spectra** occur when electrons absorb specific wavelengths of light from a continuous source, resulting in dark lines against a continuous rainbow background.
*   **B) Continuous spectra** occur from hot, dense objects (like the core of a star or a standard incandescent filament) where all wavelengths are emitted.
*   **C) Radioactive decay** involves changes to the nucleus of an atom, not the electron energy transitions described in the question.
