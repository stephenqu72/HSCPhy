The mass-energy equivalence principle states that mass and energy are interchangeable. The relationship is given by the famous equation:
$$E = mc^2$$

Where:
*   $E$ is the energy produced (in Joules, $\text{J}$).
*   $m$ is the mass converted (in kilograms, $\text{kg}$).
*   $c$ is the speed of light in a vacuum, approximately $3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$.

**Step-by-step calculation:**
1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   $m = 4.6 \text{ kg}$
    *   $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

2.  **Substitute the values into the equation:**
    $$E = 4.6 \times (3.00 \times 10^8)^2$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    *   First, square the speed of light: $(3.00 \times 10^8)^2 = 9.00 \times 10^{16} \text{ m}^2/\text{s}^2$
