### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of projectile motion for an object launched horizontally. 

**1. Time of Flight:**
The horizontal motion and vertical motion of a projectile are independent. For an object launched horizontally, the time of flight $t$ is determined by the distance the object needs to travel horizontally ($d$) and its horizontal velocity ($v_x$):
$$t = \frac{d}{v_x}$$
Since the horizontal velocity $v_x$ is constant and identical for both shots, the time of flight is directly proportional to the distance $d$. Because the second event covers a shorter distance ($50.0\text{ m}$ vs $70.0\text{ m}$), the **time of flight for the second arrow will be shorter**.

**2. Vertical Displacement (Drop):**
The vertical drop $y$ of an object launched horizontally (assuming initial vertical velocity $u_y = 0$) is given by:
$$y = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$
where $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity.
*   Since the first arrow ($70\text{ m}$) is in the air for a longer time than the second arrow ($50\text{ m}$), it will experience a larger vertical displacement downward.
*   Therefore, the first arrow will hit the target **lower** than the second arrow.

**Conclusion:**
Comparing these points to the options provided:
*   The first arrow hits lower than the second.
*   The time of flight is shorter for the second arrow.
This matches option **C**.
