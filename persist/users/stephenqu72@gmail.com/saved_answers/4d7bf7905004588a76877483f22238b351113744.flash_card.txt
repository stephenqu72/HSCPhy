### Front
How does gravitational field strength ($g$) vary with distance ($r$) from the centre of a planet?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational field strength follows an inverse-square law ($g \propto \frac{1}{r^2}$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$; the graph is a curve asymptotic to both axes.
- When to use it: Any question involving orbital mechanics or gravitational field intensity outside a spherical mass.
- Common trap: Confusing the inverse-square curve ($1/r^2$) with a simple inverse linear curve ($1/r$) or a linear relationship.
