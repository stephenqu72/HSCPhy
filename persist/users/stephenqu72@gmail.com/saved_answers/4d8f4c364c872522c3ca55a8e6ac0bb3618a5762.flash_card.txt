### Front
What is the fundamental physical cause of radioactive decay in an unstable nucleus?

### Back
- Key idea: The nucleus decays to achieve a more stable configuration by balancing the strong nuclear force (attractive) and electrostatic force (repulsive) between nucleons.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Use when asked to explain why isotopes undergo radioactive decay (alpha/beta emission).
- Common trap: Assuming particles originate from electron shells (beta decay involves a neutron-to-proton transformation within the nucleus).
