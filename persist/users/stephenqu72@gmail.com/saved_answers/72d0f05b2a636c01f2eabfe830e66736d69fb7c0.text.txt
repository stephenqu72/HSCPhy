### Solutions and Explanations

**a. Alternator**
The device uses slip rings to output an AC current. A DC generator requires a split-ring commutator.

**b. i. $0\text{ Wb}$**
**ii.** Magnetic flux is defined by $\Phi = BA\cos(\theta)$, where $\theta$ is the angle between the **normal** to the loop and the magnetic field. In Figure 5, the plane of the loop is parallel to the magnetic field lines. Therefore, the normal to the loop is perpendicular ($90^\circ$) to the field. Since $\cos(90^\circ) = 0$, the flux is zero.

**c. $0.05\text{ s}$**
The period $T$ is the reciprocal of frequency $f$.
$$T = \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{20} = 0.05\text{ s}$$

**d. $0.05\text{ Wb}$**
Maximum flux occurs when the loop is perpendicular to the field ($\theta = 0^\circ$):
$$\Phi_{\text{max}} = B \times A = 0.40\text{ T} \times (0.50\text{ m} \times 0.25\text{ m}) = 0.40 \times 0.125 = 0.05\text{ Wb}$$

**e. $4\text{ V}$**
Average induced emf is given by Faraday's Law: $\varepsilon_{\text{avg}} = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$.
$\Delta\Phi = \Phi_{\text{final}} - \Phi_{\text{initial}} = 0.05 - 0 = 0.05\text{ Wb}$.
$\Delta t = \frac{1}{4}$ of a period $= \frac{0.05}{4} = 0.0125\text{ s}$.
$$\varepsilon_{\text{avg}} = \frac{0.05}{0.0125} = 4\text{ V}$$

**f. Ways to increase amplitude:**
1. Increase the rate of rotation (frequency).
2. Increase the strength of the magnetic field ($B$).
3. Increase the area of the loop ($A$).
4. Increase the number of turns of wire ($N$).

**g. Sketching the output:**
When a split-ring commutator is used, it reverses the connection every half-cycle, ensuring the output current flows in the same direction. The negative half-cycle of the sine wave is "flipped" to become positive. You should draw a series of positive-only humps (rectified AC).
