To find the time of flight for a projectile launched from and landing at the same height, we use the principles of kinematics.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) 3.02 s**.

### Explanation
To solve for the time of flight ($t$), we use the vertical motion component of the projectile.

**1. Identify the given variables:**
*   Initial velocity ($v$) = $28\text{ ms}^{-1}$
*   Launch angle ($\theta$) = $30^\circ$
*   Acceleration due to gravity ($g$) $\approx 9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$
*   Vertical displacement ($\Delta y$) = $0$ (since it lands at the same height)

**2. Resolve the initial velocity into the vertical component:**
The vertical component of the velocity ($v_{iy}$) is given by:
$$v_{iy} = v \cdot \sin(\theta)$$
$$v_{iy} = 28 \cdot \sin(30^\circ)$$
Since $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$:
$$v_{iy} = 28 \cdot 0.5 = 14\text{ ms}^{-1}$$

**3. Use the kinematic equation for displacement:**
The formula for vertical displacement is:
$$\Delta y = v_{iy}t + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$
Substituting $\Delta y = 0$ and $a = -g$:
$$0 = v_{iy}t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$
We can factor out $t$:
$$0 = t(v_{iy} - \frac{1}{2}gt)$$
Since $t = 0$ is the launch time, the time of flight is found by solving:
$$v_{iy} = \frac{1}{2}gt$$
$$t = \frac{2 \cdot v_{iy}}{g}$$

**4. Calculate:**
$$t = \frac{2 \cdot 14}{9.8}$$
$$t = \frac{28}{9.8} \approx 2.857\text{ s}$$

Rounding to two decimal places, we get **$2.86\text{ s}$**. 
*(Note: If using $g = 9.81\text{ ms}^{-2}$, $t \approx 2.854\text{ s}$. However, checking the options provided, $2.86\text{ s}$ is clearly the intended answer calculated via $g = 9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$.)*
