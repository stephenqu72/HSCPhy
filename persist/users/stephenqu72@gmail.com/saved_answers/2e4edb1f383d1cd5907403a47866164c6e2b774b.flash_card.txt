### Front
In a nuclear transformation like beta decay ($n \rightarrow p + \text{product}$), how do you determine the identity of the missing product?

### Back
- Key idea: Law of conservation of charge.
- Formula / law: $\sum Q_{initial} = \sum Q_{final}$
- When to use it: To identify unknown decay particles by balancing total electric charge on both sides of a nuclear equation.
- Common trap: Forgetting that beta decay also involves an antineutrino (which is neutral, so it doesn't change the charge balance but is required for lepton conservation).
