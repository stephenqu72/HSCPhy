### Front
Distinguish between an inertial and a non-inertial frame of reference.

### Back
- Key idea: Inertial frames have zero acceleration ($a = 0$); non-inertial frames are accelerating ($a \neq 0$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s First Law holds true in inertial frames; fictitious (pseudo) forces are required to explain motion in non-inertial frames.
- When to use it: To determine if Newton's Laws can be applied directly or if "fictitious" forces must be introduced.
- Common trap: Assuming all moving objects are non-inertial. Constant velocity ($v = \text{constant}$) is an inertial frame.
