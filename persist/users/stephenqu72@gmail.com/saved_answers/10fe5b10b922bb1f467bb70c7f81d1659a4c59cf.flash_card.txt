### Front
Which of the following is NOT a fundamental particle: Electron, Muon, Meson, or Strange quark?

### Back
- Key idea: Fundamental particles (quarks/leptons) are indivisible; composite particles are made of sub-components.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When classifying subatomic particles under the Standard Model.
- Common trap: Confusing composite hadrons (like mesons: $q\bar{q}$ or baryons: $qqq$) with fundamental particles.
