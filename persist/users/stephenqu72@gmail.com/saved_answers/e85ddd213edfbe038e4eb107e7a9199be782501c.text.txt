### Answer and Explanation

**Part 1: The speed of light in both situations**
In both situations (A and B), the beam of light moves towards Fred at exactly the same speed: $c$ (approximately $3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$). According to Einstein’s second postulate of Special Relativity, the speed of light in a vacuum is a universal constant and is independent of the motion of the source or the observer. Therefore, even though Joe is moving in situation B, he does not "add" his velocity to the light beam.

**Part 2: Relevance of the Michelson-Morley Experiment**
The Michelson-Morley experiment was designed to detect the existence of the "luminiferous aether," the medium through which light was thought to travel. If the aether existed, the measured speed of light should have varied depending on the Earth’s motion through it. However, the experiment yielded a "null result," meaning the speed of light was found to be the same in all directions regardless of the Earth's motion. This provided the experimental foundation for Einstein to discard the concept of the aether and postulate that $c$ is invariant.

**Part 3: Relevance to Einstein's contributions**
This scenario directly relates to Einstein’s **Theory of Special Relativity** (1905). Specifically, it confirms the second postulate: the speed of light in a vacuum is constant for all inertial observers, regardless of the relative motion of the source or observer. This led to the fundamental shift in our understanding of space and time, moving away from Galilean relativity (where velocities simply add linearly) to relativistic velocity addition, where space and time become "flexible" to keep the speed of light constant.

### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Identify the Core Principle:** Recognize that the question tests the invariance of the speed of light—a cornerstone of Special Relativity.
2.  **State the Postulate Clearly:** Always explicitly mention that the speed of light $c$ is constant. Avoid the temptation to use classical Newtonian logic (i.e., do not add $v_{light} + v_{car}$).
3.  **Link to History:** When asked about the Michelson-Morley experiment, focus on the "null result." Explain that it proved there was no aether and that light does not require a medium to travel.
4.  **Synthesize:** Connect the historical failure to find a medium (Michelson-Morley) to Einstein's radical solution (Special Relativity), which accepted the constancy of light as a fundamental law of nature rather than a variable dependent on an aether.
