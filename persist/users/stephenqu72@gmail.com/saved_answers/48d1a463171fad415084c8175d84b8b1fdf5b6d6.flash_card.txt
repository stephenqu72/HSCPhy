### Front
In circular motion, how do net work and total mechanical energy differ between horizontal and vertical paths (assuming constant speed)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Net work is zero in both cases (constant speed); mechanical energy is constant for horizontal, but varies for vertical (due to $E_p$ change).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Work-Energy Theorem ($W_{net} = \Delta E_k$), $E_{mech} = E_k + E_p$.
- **When to use it:** To analyze energy conservation and work done in circular orbital systems.
- **Common trap:** Assuming constant speed implies constant total mechanical energy; it only does so if potential energy ($h$) also remains constant.
