To solve this problem, we must first extract the relevant data:
*   Mass ($m$) = $250\text{ g} = 0.25\text{ kg}$
*   Frequency of the stroboscope ($f_s$) = $12\text{ Hz}$
*   Orbital speed ($v$) = $3.5\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   There are 8 positions shown in one full rotation ($360^\circ$).

---

### (a) What is the radius of the circle of motion?
First, find the period of rotation ($T$). Since there are 8 intervals in one full rotation captured by a 12 Hz strobe, the time taken for one full revolution is:
$$T = \frac{8}{f_s} = \frac{8}{12} = \frac{2}{3}\text{ s}$$

We know that speed $v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$, so:
$$r = \frac{vT}{2\pi} = \frac{3.5 \times (2/3)}{2\pi} \approx \frac{2.333}{6.283} \approx 0.37\text{ m}$$

---

### (b) What is the tension in the string connecting the mass to the centre?
The tension ($T_s$) provides the centripetal force required for circular motion:
$$T_s = F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
$$T_s = \frac{0.25 \times (3.5)^2}{0.3713} \approx \frac{3.0625}{0.3713} \approx 8.25\text{ N}$$

---

### (c) Vector analysis of the change in velocity ($\Delta v$)
To show the direction of $\Delta v$:
1.  Draw a vector $\vec{v}_A$ tangent to the circle at point $A$ (pointing right).
2.  Draw a vector $\vec{v}_B$ tangent to the circle at point $B$ (pointing down-right).
3.  To find $\Delta \vec{v} = \vec{v}_B - \vec{v}_A$, place the tail of $\vec{v}_B$ at the head of $(-\vec{v}_A)$.
4.  The resultant vector $\Delta \vec{v}$ will point toward the centre of the circle, confirming the centripetal nature of the acceleration.
