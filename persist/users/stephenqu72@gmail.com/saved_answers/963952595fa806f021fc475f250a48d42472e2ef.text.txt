### Solution and Explanation

To solve these problems, we use Kepler’s Third Law and the principles of orbital mechanics.

#### (a) Calculate the mass of Saturn
**Formula:** Kepler’s Third Law is given by:
$$\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$$
Rearranging for $M$:
$$M = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{GT^2}$$

**Step 1: Convert units to SI (meters and seconds).**
*   $r = 1.22 \times 10^6\text{ km} = 1.22 \times 10^9\text{ m}$
*   $T = 15.9\text{ days} = 15.9 \times 24 \times 3600 = 1,373,760\text{ s}$
*   $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2\text{ kg}^{-2}$

**Step 2: Substitute and solve.**
$$M = \frac{4 \times \pi^2 \times (1.22 \times 10^9)^3}{6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times (1,373,760)^2}$$
$$M \approx \frac{7.16 \times 10^{28}}{1.258 \times 10^2} \approx 5.69 \times 10^{26}\text{ kg}$$

---

#### (b) Calculate the total mechanical energy of Titan
**Formula:** The total mechanical energy ($E$) of an orbiting body is half the potential energy:
$$E = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$$
*   $m = 1.35 \times 10^{23}\text{ kg}$

**Calculation:**
$$E = -\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.69 \times 10^{26}) \times (1.35 \times 10^{23})}{2 \times (1.22 \times 10^9)}$$
$$E \approx -2.10 \times 10^{30}\text{ J}$$

---

#### (c) Identify the energy changes as the satellite moves into orbit
As the satellite moves from the surface to orbit, the following energy transformations occur:
1.  **Gravitational Potential Energy (GPE):** Increases, as the satellite moves further away from the center of Saturn ($U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$, so as $r$ increases, the value becomes less negative).
2.  **Kinetic Energy (KE):** Increases, because the satellite must gain velocity to maintain a stable circular orbit ($v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$).
3.  **Total Mechanical Energy:** Increases, because external work (from fuel combustion/engines) is done on the satellite system to elevate it and increase its orbital speed.
