### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Answer:** The amplitude is $0 \text{ m}$ (destructive interference).

**Explanation:** 
Point $P$ is located on a nodal line. In the provided diagram, the solid lines represent wave crests. The point $P$ is marked exactly midway between two crests originating from source $X$. This indicates that point $P$ is at a position of a trough (or more generally, at a point of zero displacement for the wave from $X$). Furthermore, by examining the intersection of the waves, we can see that point $P$ lies on a line where a crest from one source meets a trough from the other. When two waves of identical frequency and amplitude overlap such that a crest of one meets a trough of the other (a path difference of $(n + 0.5)\lambda$), destructive interference occurs. Since the waves are identical, $A_1 = A_2 = 0.26 \text{ m}$, the resultant amplitude is $A_{net} = |A_1 - A_2| = 0.26 - 0.26 = 0 \text{ m}$.

#### Part (b)
**Discussion:**
Interference is a phenomenon exclusive to waves. Historically, this provided crucial evidence for the wave nature of light and, later, the wave nature of matter.

*   **Light:** The Young’s Double Slit Experiment provided the definitive evidence for the wave model of light. Newton’s corpuscular theory could not explain the bright and dark fringes observed on a screen. The observation of an interference pattern—resulting from the superposition of light waves—demonstrated that light undergoes diffraction and interference, proving its wave-like nature.
*   **Matter:** De Broglie proposed that all matter exhibits wave-particle duality. The wave nature of matter (specifically electrons) was confirmed by the Davisson-Germer experiment. They observed the diffraction and interference of electrons reflected off a nickel crystal. Because electrons created an interference pattern similar to X-rays, it provided experimental evidence that matter particles possess an associated wavelength (the de Broglie wavelength), confirming that matter also behaves as a wave.
