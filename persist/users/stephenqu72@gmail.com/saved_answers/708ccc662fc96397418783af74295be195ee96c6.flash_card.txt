### Front
What is the horizontal distance between a consecutive crest and trough of a transverse wave in terms of wavelength ($\lambda$)?

### Back
- Key idea: A crest and the adjacent trough are exactly half a wavelength apart.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Distance = $\frac{\lambda}{2}$
- When to use it: When calculating spatial separation for wave features in transverse mechanics.
- Common trap: Mistaking the distance between a crest and its *next* crest (a full $\lambda$) for the distance to the trough ($\frac{\lambda}{2}$).
