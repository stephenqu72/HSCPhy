### Front
In the Essen and Gordon-Smith experiment to determine the speed of light ($c$), what physical parameter must be measured in addition to the known frequency ($f$)?

### Back
- Key idea: The length of the resonance cavity.
- Formula / law: $c = f \lambda$.
- When to use it: When using standing waves in a cavity to calculate the wavelength ($\lambda$) of electromagnetic radiation.
- Common trap: Confusing "oscillation rate" or "period" with wavelength; these are derived from the already known frequency ($f$).
