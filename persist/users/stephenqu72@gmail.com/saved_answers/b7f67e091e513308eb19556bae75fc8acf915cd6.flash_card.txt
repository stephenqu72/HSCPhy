### Front
What is the definition of a baryon according to the Standard Model?

### Back
- Key idea: A baryon is a composite hadron consisting of three quarks ($qqq$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When classifying subatomic particles or determining the composition of protons and neutrons.
- Common trap: Confusing baryons (3 quarks) with mesons (1 quark, 1 antiquark) or mislabeling leptons as composite particles.
