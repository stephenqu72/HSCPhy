### Analysis
Planck’s hypothesis successfully resolved the "Ultraviolet Catastrophe" by proposing that energy is radiated in discrete "quanta," where $E = nhf$. This leads to two critical behaviors for black-body radiation curves plotted with **Intensity vs. Wavelength**:

1.  **Total Intensity:** According to the Stefan-Boltzmann Law, the total power radiated (the area under the curve) is proportional to the fourth power of temperature ($P \propto T^4$). Therefore, as the temperature increases, the entire curve must shift **upward** because the object radiates more energy at every wavelength.
2.  **Peak Wavelength:** According to Wien’s Displacement Law, $\lambda_{max} T = \text{constant}$. This means that as the temperature $T$ increases, the peak wavelength $\lambda_{max}$ must shift toward the **left** (shorter wavelengths).
