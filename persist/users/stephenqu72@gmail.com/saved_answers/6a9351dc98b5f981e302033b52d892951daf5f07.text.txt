To solve these problems, we use Newton’s Second Law and the definitions of impulse and kinematics.

**Given data:**
*   Mass $m = 2.0\text{ kg}$
*   Force $F = 4.0\text{ N}$
*   Initial velocity $u = 0\text{ m s}^{-1}$

---

### Question 3: Magnitude of acceleration
**Solution:**
Using Newton's Second Law, $F = ma$, we rearrange to solve for acceleration $a$:
$$a = \frac{F}{m}$$
$$a = \frac{4.0\text{ N}}{2.0\text{ kg}} = 2.0\text{ m s}^{-2}$$
**Correct Option: (C)**

---

### Question 4: Magnitude of impulse
**Solution:**
Impulse ($J$) is defined as the product of force and time, or the change in momentum:
$$J = F \cdot \Delta t$$
Given $\Delta t = 5.0\text{ s}$:
$$J = 4.0\text{ N} \times 5.0\text{ s} = 20\text{ Ns}$$
**Correct Option: (C)**

---

### Question 5: Final speed
**Solution:**
We use the kinematic equation $v = u + at$, where $t = 10\text{ s}$, $u = 0\text{ m s}^{-1}$, and $a = 2.0\text{ m s}^{-2}$ (calculated in Question 3):
$$v = 0 + (2.0\text{ m s}^{-2})(10\text{ s})$$
$$v = 20\text{ m s}^{-1}$$
**Correct Option: (C)**
