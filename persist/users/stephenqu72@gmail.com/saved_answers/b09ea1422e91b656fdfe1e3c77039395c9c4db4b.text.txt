### Explanation
To identify the independent variable in any scientific investigation, you need to look at what the experimenter is deliberately changing or manipulating to observe an effect.

1.  **The Independent Variable:** This is the factor that you change or control in the experiment. In this investigation, the question explicitly states that the "strength of a magnet is varied." Therefore, the strength of the magnet is the independent variable.
2.  **The Dependent Variable:** This is the factor that you measure as a result of changing the independent variable. In this case, we are measuring the "size of the generated current," so this is the dependent variable (Option C).
3.  **Controlled Variables:** These are factors that must be kept constant to ensure a fair test (such as the speed of the magnet or the distance between the coil and the magnet). Options (A) and (D) are typically controlled variables in this type of induction experiment to ensure the results are valid.

**Summary Strategy:**
Whenever you encounter an experimental design question, use the acronym **C.O.D.E.** to keep your variables organized:
*   **C**hange = Independent Variable
*   **O**bserve/Measure = Dependent Variable
*   **D**o the same (Control) = Controlled Variables
*   **E**xperiment

In this scenario:
*   We **change** the magnet's strength (Independent).
*   We **measure** the generated current (Dependent).
