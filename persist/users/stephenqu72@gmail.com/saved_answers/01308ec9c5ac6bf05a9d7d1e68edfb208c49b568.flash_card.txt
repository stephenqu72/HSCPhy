### Front
How do you calculate the energy released during a nuclear fission reaction?

### Back
- Key idea: Mass-energy equivalence; the energy released equals the mass defect ($\Delta m$) converted into energy.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E = \Delta mc^2$, where $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$.
- When to use it: Whenever a change in mass occurs during a nuclear reaction (fission or fusion).
- Common trap: Forgetting to include the mass of *all* products (including every individual neutron released) when calculating total mass.
