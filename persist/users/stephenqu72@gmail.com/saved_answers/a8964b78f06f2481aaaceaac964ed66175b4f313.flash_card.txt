### Front
How can you use a graph of $r^3$ vs $T^2$ for a satellite orbiting a central body to determine that body's mass?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kepler’s Third Law rearranged: $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$.
- **Formula:** $M = \frac{4\pi^2 \cdot \text{gradient}}{G}$, where the gradient is $\frac{\Delta r^3}{\Delta T^2}$.
- **When to use it:** When given orbital data (radius and period) for satellites orbiting a central mass.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., $km$ to $m$, hours/days to seconds) or confusing the gradient $m$ for $M$.
