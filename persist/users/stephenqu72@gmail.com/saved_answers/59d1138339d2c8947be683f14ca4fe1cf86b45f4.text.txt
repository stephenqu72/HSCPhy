### Answer
The correct answer is **A**.

### Explanation
This graph represents the **Hubble-Lemaître Law**, which describes the relationship between the recessional velocity of a galaxy and its distance from an observer. The mathematical expression for this relationship is:

$$v = H_0 d$$

Where:
*   $v$ is the recessional velocity of the galaxy (the vertical axis).
*   $d$ is the distance of the galaxy from the Earth (the horizontal axis).
*   $H_0$ is the Hubble constant.

In a linear graph where $y = mx$, the slope $m$ corresponds to the constant of proportionality. Since the graph plots $v$ on the y-axis and $d$ on the x-axis, the slope of the line of best fit represents the **Hubble constant** ($H_0$).

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **B:** The age of the universe is related to the Hubble constant by the expression $t \approx \frac{1}{H_0}$, not the slope itself.
*   **C:** The inverse of the Hubble constant ($1/H_0$) is related to the Hubble time (an estimate of the age of the universe), but the slope of this specific graph is $H_0$.
*   **D:** The slope can indeed be used to find the age of the universe by taking its reciprocal (assuming a constant expansion rate).
