To solve this problem, we need to understand the dynamics of an object moving in a horizontal circle. When the stone is being spun, the tension in the string provides the necessary centripetal force to maintain its circular path.

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   Radius of the circle ($r$) = $1.5\text{ m}$
*   Mass of the stone ($m$) = $50\text{ g} = 0.05\text{ kg}$
*   Tension ($T$) = $3.3\text{ N}$

### 2. Physics Principle:
The centripetal force ($F_c$) required to keep an object moving in a circle is provided by the tension in the string:
$$F_c = T = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Where:
*   $m$ is the mass in kilograms
*   $v$ is the tangential speed in meters per second
*   $r$ is the radius in meters

### 3. Rearrange the formula to solve for $v$:
$$v^2 = \frac{T \cdot r}{m}$$
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{T \cdot r}{m}}$$

### 4. Substitute the values:
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{3.3 \cdot 1.5}{0.05}}$$
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{4.95}{0.05}}$$
$$v = \sqrt{99}$$
$$v \approx 9.95\text{ m/s}$$

Rounding to the nearest significant figure provided in the options, the speed is $10\text{ m/s}$. When the warrior releases the sling, the stone's velocity is tangent to the circle at that instant, and it will continue to move at this constant speed (ignoring air resistance and gravity).

**Correct Option: (B) $10\text{ m/s}$**
