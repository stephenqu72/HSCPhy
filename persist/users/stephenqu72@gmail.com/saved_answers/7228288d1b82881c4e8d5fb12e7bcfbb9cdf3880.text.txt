To solve this problem, we must apply the physical principles of how light interacts with matter:

1.  **Nature of the Source:** A "main sequence star" emits a continuous spectrum of light (all wavelengths across the visible range).
2.  **Interaction with Nebulae:** As this continuous light passes through the cool gas of the nebulae, the atoms in the gas absorb specific wavelengths of light that correspond to their electronic energy level transitions. This process creates an **absorption spectrum**, where dark lines appear on a continuous background at the exact wavelengths where the gas atoms absorb energy.
3.  **Principle of Complementarity:** The wavelengths absorbed by a gas to create an absorption spectrum are exactly the same wavelengths that the gas would emit if it were hot and excited (an emission spectrum). Therefore, the dark lines in the final observed spectrum must match the bright lines provided in the "Emission Spectra" reference diagrams for both Nitrogen and Hydrogen.
