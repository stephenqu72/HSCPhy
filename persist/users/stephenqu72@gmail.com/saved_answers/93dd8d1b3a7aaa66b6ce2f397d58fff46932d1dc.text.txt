To find the surface temperature of Rigel based on its peak wavelength, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**.

### The Formula
Wien’s Displacement Law states that the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) of black-body radiation is inversely proportional to the temperature ($T$):
$$\lambda_{max} T = b$$
Where:
*   $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength in meters ($m$).
*   $T$ is the temperature in Kelvin ($K$).
*   $b$ is Wien's displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m}\cdot\text{K}$.

### Step-by-Step Solution

1.  **Convert the wavelength to standard units:**
    The given wavelength is $550 \, \text{nm}$. Since $1 \, \text{nm} = 10^{-9} \, \text{m}$:
    $$\lambda_{max} = 550 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{m} = 5.5 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}$$

2.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $T$:**
    $$T = \frac{b}{\lambda_{max}}$$

3.  **Substitute the values:**
    $$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m}\cdot\text{K}}{5.5 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}}$$
    $$T \approx 5269.09 \, \text{K}$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number provided in the options, we get $5269 \, \text{K}$.
