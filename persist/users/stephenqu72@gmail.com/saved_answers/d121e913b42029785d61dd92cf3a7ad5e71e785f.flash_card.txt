### Front
Derive the formula for the "design speed" ($v$) of a frictionless banked track of radius ($r$) and angle ($\theta$).

### Back
- Key idea: At design speed, the horizontal component of the normal force provides the total centripetal force, and the vertical component balances gravity.
- Formula: $v = \sqrt{rg \tan \theta}$
- When to use it: When asked for the speed where no friction is required to maintain a circular path on a banked surface.
- Common trap: Assuming the normal force ($N$) equals $mg$; in reality, $N = \frac{mg}{\cos \theta}$, which is greater than the weight of the object.
