To calculate the escape velocity of a planet, we use the principle of conservation of energy. The escape velocity is the minimum speed an object must reach to escape a planet's gravitational field, meaning its kinetic energy must be equal to the magnitude of its gravitational potential energy.

### 1. Identify the Formula
The formula for escape velocity ($v_e$) is derived from equating kinetic energy ($\frac{1}{2}mv^2$) to gravitational potential energy ($\frac{G M m}{r}$):
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

Where:
*   $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N m}^2 \text{kg}^{-2}$ (Universal gravitational constant)
*   $M = 6.42 \times 10^{23} \, \text{kg}$ (Mass of Mars)
*   $r = 3.40 \times 10^{6} \, \text{m}$ (Radius of Mars)

### 2. Calculation
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (6.42 \times 10^{23})}{3.40 \times 10^{6}}}$$

First, calculate the numerator:
$$2 \times 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 6.42 \times 10^{23} \approx 8.567 \times 10^{13}$$

Divide by the radius:
$$\frac{8.567 \times 10^{13}}{3.40 \times 10^{6}} \approx 2.5198 \times 10^{7}$$

Take the square root:
$$v_e = \sqrt{2.5198 \times 10^{7}} \approx 5019.7 \, \text{m/s}$$

Rounding to three significant figures (matching the data provided in the question):
$$v_e \approx 5.02 \times 10^{3} \, \text{m/s} \, (\text{or } 5.02 \, \text{km/s})$$
