### Front
Two projectiles are launched horizontally from the same height. If Projectile B has twice the initial horizontal velocity of Projectile A, how do their times of flight and horizontal ranges compare?

### Back
- Key idea: Time of flight is independent of horizontal velocity; range is directly proportional to horizontal velocity.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $t = \sqrt{\frac{2h}{g}}$ and $R = u_x t$.
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem where objects are launched horizontally from the same height.
- Common trap: Assuming higher horizontal velocity results in a longer time of flight.
