To solve this problem, we need to apply the concepts of de Broglie wavelength and the diffraction of particles through a lattice (similar to the Davisson-Germer experiment).

### Step 1: Calculate the de Broglie wavelength of the electrons
The kinetic energy $K$ gained by an electron accelerated through a potential difference $V$ is given by $K = eV$. The de Broglie wavelength $\lambda$ is related to momentum $p$ by $\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$. Since $K = \frac{p^2}{2m}$, we have $p = \sqrt{2mK} = \sqrt{2meV}$.
Thus, the wavelength is:
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2meV}}$$
Using $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$, $m = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$, $e = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$, and $V = 83 \text{ V}$:
$$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{\sqrt{2 \times 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \times 83}}$$
$$\lambda \approx 1.347 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m} = 0.1347 \text{ nm}$$

### Step 2: Use the diffraction geometry
The diffraction pattern shows the central maximum and the first-order maxima separated by $2 \text{ cm}$ over a distance $L = 5 \text{ cm}$. The angle $\theta$ of the first-order maximum relative to the central axis is:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{half-distance}}{L} = \frac{1 \text{ cm}}{5 \text{ cm}} = 0.2$$
$$\theta = \arctan(0.2) \approx 11.31^\circ$$

### Step 3: Calculate the lattice spacing $d$
For diffraction by a grid (or atomic surface), the condition for constructive interference is $d \sin(\theta) = n\lambda$. For the first order ($n=1$):
$$d = \frac{\lambda}{\sin(\theta)}$$
$$d = \frac{0.1347 \text{ nm}}{\sin(11.31^\circ)}$$
Since $\sin(11.31^\circ) \approx 0.196$:
$$d \approx \frac{0.1347}{0.196} \approx 0.687 \text{ nm}$$

Rounding to the nearest provided option, we get **0.69 nm**.

**Correct Option: D**
