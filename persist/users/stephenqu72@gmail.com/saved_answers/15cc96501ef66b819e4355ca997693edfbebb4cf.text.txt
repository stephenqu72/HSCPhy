### Explanation
To solve this question, we need to recall Johannes Kepler’s three laws of planetary motion, which describe the motion of planets around the Sun:

1.  **Kepler's First Law (Law of Ellipses):** All planets move in elliptical orbits, with the Sun at one of the focal points. This matches statement **(A)**.
2.  **Kepler's Second Law (Law of Equal Areas):** A line segment joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time. This matches statement **(B)**.
3.  **Kepler's Third Law (Law of Harmonies):** The square of the orbital period of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis of its orbit. Mathematically, this is expressed as $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = k$. This matches statement **(C)**.

Statement **(D)**, "The weight of an object on the Earth's surface is due to the gravitational attraction of Earth," describes the definition of weight (or the concept of universal gravitation as applied to an object near a planet), but it is a principle of Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, not one of Kepler’s Laws of Planetary Motion. Therefore, (D) is the statement that is **NOT** one of Kepler's Laws.
