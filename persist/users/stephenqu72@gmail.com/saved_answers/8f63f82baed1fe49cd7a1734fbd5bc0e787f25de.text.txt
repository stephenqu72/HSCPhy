### Answer and Explanation

To solve this problem, we first need to convert all units into standard SI units.

*   Mass $m = 1.05 \text{ tonne} = 1050 \text{ kg}$
*   Radius $r = 30 \text{ m}$
*   Velocity $v = 70 \text{ km/h} = \frac{70}{3.6} \approx 19.444 \text{ m/s}$

---

#### Part (a)
**Objective:** Determine if the car crashes.
The car stays on the road if the required centripetal force ($F_c$) is less than or equal to the maximum available friction ($f_{max} = 3800 \text{ N}$).

1.  **Calculate required centripetal force:**
    $$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
    $$F_c = \frac{1050 \times (19.444)^2}{30}$$
    $$F_c = \frac{1050 \times 378.086}{30} \approx 13233 \text{ N}$$

2.  **Compare:**
    Since the required centripetal force ($13233 \text{ N}$) is significantly greater than the maximum available frictional force ($3800 \text{ N}$), the tires cannot provide enough grip to maintain the path.
    **Conclusion:** The driver will crash.

---

#### Part (b)
**Objective:** Calculate the banking angle ($\theta$) for a frictionless turn.
For a banked corner with no friction, the horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force:
$$N \sin\theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
The vertical component balances gravity:
$$N \cos\theta = mg$$

Dividing the two equations gives:
$$\tan\theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

1.  **Substitute the values:**
    $$\tan\theta = \frac{(19.444)^2}{30 \times 9.8}$$
    $$\tan\theta = \frac{378.086}{294} \approx 1.286$$

2.  **Calculate $\theta$:**
    $$\theta = \tan^{-1}(1.286) \approx 52.1^\circ$$

The required angle of incline is approximately $52.1^\circ$.
