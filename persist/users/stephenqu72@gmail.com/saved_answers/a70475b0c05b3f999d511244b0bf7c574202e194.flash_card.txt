### Front
How can you relate the orbital periods and radii of two satellites orbiting the same central body?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kepler’s Third Law states the square of the orbital period ($T$) is proportional to the cube of the orbital radius ($r$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\frac{T_1^2}{r_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{r_2^3}$
- **When to use it:** When comparing the orbital mechanics of two bodies orbiting the *same* central mass (e.g., moons around a planet).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to cube the radii or square the periods before solving; also, ensure the orbital radius is measured from the *centre* of the central body.
