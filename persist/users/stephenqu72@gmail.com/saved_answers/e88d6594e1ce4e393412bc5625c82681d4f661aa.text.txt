To determine the correct representation of the magnetic field around a current-carrying wire, we apply the **Right-Hand Grip Rule**.

### Answer:
The correct diagram is **(A)**.

### Explanation:
1.  **The Physics Principle:** According to Ampère's Law, a current $I$ flowing through a long, straight wire creates a magnetic field $B$ that forms concentric circles around the wire. 
2.  **Applying the Rule:** To find the direction of this field, use the Right-Hand Grip Rule:
    *   Point the thumb of your right hand in the direction of the conventional current ($10\text{ A}$ flowing upwards).
    *   Curl your fingers around the wire. The direction your fingers curl indicates the direction of the magnetic field lines.
3.  **Analysis of the Options:**
    *   With your thumb pointing **upward**, your fingers curl around the wire in a **counter-clockwise** direction when viewed from above (or moving from right to left across the front of the wire). 
    *   Looking at the diagrams, diagram **(A)** shows the field lines circulating in a way that is consistent with the right-hand rule for an upward current. 
    *   Diagram (B) shows a clockwise rotation, which corresponds to a current flowing downward.
    *   Diagrams (C) and (D) are incorrect representations, as they do not show the concentric circular nature of the magnetic field around a straight conductor.

By following the right-hand grip rule, you can quickly identify the correct orientation of the circular magnetic field lines relative to the direction of the current.
