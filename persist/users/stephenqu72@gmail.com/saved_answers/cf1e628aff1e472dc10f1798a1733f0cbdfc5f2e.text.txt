### Answer
The correct answer is **D. The temperature of its surface**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, you need to apply **Wien's Displacement Law**. 

Wien's Displacement Law states that the wavelength at which the emission of radiation from a black body is at its peak ($\lambda_{max}$) is inversely proportional to the absolute temperature ($T$) of the body. The relationship is given by the formula:

$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$

Where:
- $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength.
- $T$ is the absolute temperature (measured in Kelvin).
- $b$ is Wien's displacement constant (approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}$).

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
- **A (Density):** Density does not determine the spectral distribution of thermal radiation.
- **B (Area):** The surface area affects the *total intensity* or *power* of the radiation emitted (according to the Stefan-Boltzmann Law, $P = \sigma A T^4$), but it does not shift the peak wavelength.
- **C (Nature of the surface):** While the emissivity of a surface affects how much radiation is emitted compared to an ideal black body, the peak wavelength is fundamentally defined by the temperature of the body.

Therefore, because the peak wavelength is determined solely by the temperature $T$, option D is the correct answer.
