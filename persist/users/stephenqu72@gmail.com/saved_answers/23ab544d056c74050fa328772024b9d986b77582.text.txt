To determine the de Broglie wavelength of the recoiling electron, we must apply the principle of **Conservation of Energy**. 

### Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Calculate the energy of the incident photon ($E_i$):**
The maximum energy of an X-ray photon produced by the tube is equal to the kinetic energy gained by the electron accelerated through $70,000\text{ V}$.
$$E_i = qV = (1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (70,000\text{ V}) = 1.1214 \times 10^{-14}\text{ J}$$

**2. Calculate the energy of the scattered photon ($E_s$):**
Using $E = hf$, where $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$ and $f = 1.64 \times 10^{19}\text{ Hz}$:
$$E_s = (6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (1.64 \times 10^{19}) = 1.0867 \times 10^{-14}\text{ J}$$

**3. Determine the kinetic energy of the recoiling electron ($K_e$):**
Since energy is conserved in the collision ($E_i = E_s + K_e$):
$$K_e = E_i - E_s = (1.1214 \times 10^{-14}) - (1.0867 \times 10^{-14}) = 3.47 \times 10^{-16}\text{ J}$$

**4. Determine the momentum of the electron ($p$):**
Using the non-relativistic relation $K_e = \frac{p^2}{2m}$, where $m = 9.109 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$:
$$p = \sqrt{2mK_e} = \sqrt{2 \times (9.109 \times 10^{-31}) \times (3.47 \times 10^{-16})}$$
$$p = 2.515 \times 10^{-23}\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

**5. Calculate the de Broglie wavelength ($\lambda$):**
Using the de Broglie equation $\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$:
$$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{2.515 \times 10^{-23}} \approx 2.63 \times 10^{-11}\text{ m}$$

**Final Answer:** The de Broglie wavelength of the electron is approximately $2.63 \times 10^{-11}\text{ m}$.
