To solve this HSC Physics question, we need to understand how electrical power is transmitted over long distances and why transformers are used to step up the voltage.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) The current decreases, reducing thermal energy losses.**

### Explanation
The goal of power transmission is to deliver electrical energy from a power station to homes and businesses with minimal energy loss.

1.  **The Power Formula:** Electrical power ($P$) transmitted through a line is given by:
    $$P = VI$$
    where $V$ is the voltage and $I$ is the current. For a fixed amount of power $P$ that needs to be delivered, if we increase the voltage ($V$), the current ($I$) must decrease proportionally to keep the power constant.

2.  **Energy Loss:** The power lost as heat (thermal energy) in the transmission wires is determined by the formula for resistive power dissipation:
    $$P_{\text{loss}} = I^2 R$$
    where $I$ is the current flowing through the cable and $R$ is the resistance of the cable.

3.  **The Relationship:** Because the current $I$ is squared in the power loss formula, even a small reduction in current leads to a very significant reduction in energy loss as heat. By stepping up the voltage using transformers, the current in the transmission lines is kept very low, which keeps $I^2 R$ losses to a minimum.

*   (A) and (D) are incorrect because the resistance $R$ of the transmission cable is a physical property of the wire (material, length, cross-sectional area) and does not change when we alter the transmission voltage.
*   (B) is incorrect because increasing voltage decreases current, it does not increase it.
