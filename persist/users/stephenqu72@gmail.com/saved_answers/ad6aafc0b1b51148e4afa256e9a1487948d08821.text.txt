To solve this problem, we apply the physics of moving charges in a magnetic field using the Right-Hand Rule (RHR).

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the variables:** 
    *   The particle is an alpha particle ($\alpha$), which is a helium nucleus. It has a **positive charge**.
    *   The magnetic field ($B$) is represented by the 'X' symbols, which signify that the field is pointing **into the page**.
    *   The velocity ($v$) of the particle is directed to the **left** (as indicated by the arrow).

2.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule:**
    *   Point your right thumb in the direction of the velocity vector (to the left).
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field (into the page).
    *   Your palm (or the force vector perpendicular to your palm) will point in the direction of the magnetic force ($F$) exerted on a positive charge.
    *   Using this orientation, your palm faces **upwards**.

3.  **Analyze the Physics:**
    *   The magnetic force ($F$) acting on a charged particle is given by $F = qvB\sin(\theta)$. Because the force is always perpendicular to the velocity, the magnetic field cannot change the speed of the particle; it only changes the direction, causing circular motion. 
    *   Since the particle is positively charged, the right-hand rule indicates an upward force. Therefore, the particle will deflect upwards as it enters the magnetic field.

**Correct Answer:** **C. It will be deflected upwards**
