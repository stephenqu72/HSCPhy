### Answer
The correct answer is **A. 4F**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we look at the physics of circular motion. An object moving in a horizontal circle requires a centripetal force to maintain its path. 

1.  **Identify the relationship:** For an object of mass $m$ moving in a circle of radius $r$ with an angular velocity $\omega$, the centripetal force $F_c$ is provided by the tension in the string ($F$). The formula for centripetal force is:
    $$F = m r \omega^2$$

2.  **Analyze the variables:** The problem states that the mass $m$ and the radius $r$ are kept constant. Therefore, the tension $F$ is directly proportional to the square of the angular velocity:
    $$F \propto \omega^2$$

3.  **Apply the change:** We are told that the new angular velocity $\omega'$ is double the original:
    $$\omega' = 2\omega$$

4.  **Calculate the new tension ($F'$):** Substitute the new angular velocity into the proportionality relationship:
    $$F' \propto (\omega')^2$$
    $$F' \propto (2\omega)^2$$
    $$F' \propto 4\omega^2$$

Since the original tension was $F = m r \omega^2$, the new tension is:
$$F' = 4(m r \omega^2) = 4F$$

Therefore, when the angular velocity is doubled, the centripetal force (tension) increases by a factor of 4.
