### Front
At maximum speed on a banked track, in which direction does the frictional force act and why?

### Back
- Key idea: Friction acts **down the slope** to prevent the car from sliding up the track due to excessive centripetal force.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\sum F_y = 0$ (vertical equilibrium) and $\sum F_x = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ (centripetal requirement).
- When to use it: When calculating the **maximum** speed on a banked curve where friction is non-negligible.
- Common trap: Assuming friction acts *up* the slope; this only happens at the *minimum* speed (when the car is on the verge of sliding down).
