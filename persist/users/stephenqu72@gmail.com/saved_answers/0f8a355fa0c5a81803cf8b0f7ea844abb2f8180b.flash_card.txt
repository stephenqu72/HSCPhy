### Front
At the highest point of a projectile's trajectory, what is the net force acting on the projectile, and what is its vertical acceleration?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravity acts constantly on a projectile, regardless of its position or velocity.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{net} = mg = ma$; $a_y = g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ (downwards).
- When to use it: To analyze forces and acceleration at any point in a projectile's flight path.
- Common trap: Assuming that because vertical velocity is zero at the peak, the force or acceleration must also be zero.
