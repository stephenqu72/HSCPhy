### Answer
The correct answer is **(B) electrostatic charge**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we must identify the four fundamental forces that govern all physical interactions in the universe according to the Standard Model of particle physics. They are:

1.  **Gravity:** The force of attraction between masses.
2.  **Electromagnetic force:** The force that acts between electrically charged particles. Note that "electrostatic charge" is a property of matter, not a force itself; the *force* exerted by these charges is the electromagnetic force.
3.  **Weak nuclear force:** Responsible for particle decay (such as beta decay).
4.  **Strong nuclear force:** Responsible for holding the protons and neutrons together in the atomic nucleus.

Since (A), (C), and (D) are all fundamental forces, and (B) refers to a property of matter (charge) rather than the fundamental force itself (the electromagnetic force), (B) is the odd one out and therefore the correct answer to the question.
