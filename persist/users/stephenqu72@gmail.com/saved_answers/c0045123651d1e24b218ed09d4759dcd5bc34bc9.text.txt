To solve this problem, we need to understand the physics of standing waves. 

### Answer
The correct option is **C. $2.94 \times 10^8 \text{ ms}^{-1}$**.

### Explanation

**1. Understand the Physics:**
In a standing wave pattern, the "hot spots" (where the chocolate melts) correspond to the antinodes of the wave. The distance between two consecutive antinodes is equal to half a wavelength ($\frac{\lambda}{2}$).
Given the distance between melted areas is $6.00 \text{ cm}$:
$$\frac{\lambda}{2} = 6.00 \text{ cm} = 0.06 \text{ m}$$
Therefore, the full wavelength is:
$$\lambda = 0.12 \text{ m}$$

**2. Identify the Variables:**
*   Frequency ($f$) = $2450 \text{ MHz} = 2450 \times 10^6 \text{ Hz} = 2.45 \times 10^9 \text{ Hz}$
*   Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $0.12 \text{ m}$

**3. Apply the Wave Equation:**
The relationship between speed ($v$), frequency ($f$), and wavelength ($\lambda$) is given by:
$$v = f \times \lambda$$

Substitute the values:
$$v = (2.45 \times 10^9 \text{ Hz}) \times (0.12 \text{ m})$$
$$v = 0.294 \times 10^9 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$

To express this in standard scientific notation:
$$v = 2.94 \times 10^8 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$

This result is consistent with the speed of electromagnetic waves in a microwave cavity, which is slightly less than the speed of light in a vacuum ($3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ ms}^{-1}$).
