### Front
Explain how Hubble’s Law links redshift observations to the distance of a galaxy.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Galaxies move away from us as space expands; recession velocity ($v$) is directly proportional to distance ($d$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Hubble's Law: $v = H_0d$ ($H_0 \approx 70 \text{ km/s/Mpc}$).
- **When to use it:** To determine the distance of extragalactic objects or justify the expansion of the universe.
- **Common trap:** Confusing Doppler-induced motion through space with the expansion of space itself; redshift is caused by the *stretching* of photons as space expands.
