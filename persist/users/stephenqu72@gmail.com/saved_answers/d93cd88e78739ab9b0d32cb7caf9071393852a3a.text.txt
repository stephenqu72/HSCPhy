### Answer
The correct answer is **A. Electromagnetic induction**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, let's break down the physical principles being demonstrated in the image:

1.  **Observation:** The diagram shows that when a magnet is moved relative to a coil of wire, an electric current ($I$) is induced in the circuit, as indicated by the movement of the galvanometer/ammeter needle. When the magnet is stationary ($I=0$), no current is induced.
2.  **Defining the Concepts:**
    *   **Electromagnetic induction (Option A):** This is the overarching principle that describes the generation of an electromotive force (EMF) and current across an electrical conductor in a changing magnetic field. This is exactly what is occurring here.
    *   **Ampere's law (Option B):** This relates the integrated magnetic field around a closed loop to the electric current passing through the loop. It is about how currents *create* magnetic fields, not how moving magnets create currents.
    *   **Faraday's law (Option C):** While this is a specific mathematical law that *quantifies* electromagnetic induction ($$\mathcal{E} = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$$), in the context of HSC Physics multiple-choice questions, "Electromagnetic induction" is the broader, more appropriate term for the *phenomenon* being demonstrated by the experimental setup. 
    *   **The motor effect (Option D):** This describes the force exerted on a current-carrying conductor placed within an external magnetic field ($F = BIL \sin\theta$). This is the reverse process of what is shown.

**Conclusion:** The experiment demonstrates the conversion of mechanical energy (moving the magnet) into electrical energy (current) via a changing magnetic flux. This entire process is known as **electromagnetic induction**.
