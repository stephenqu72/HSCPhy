To solve this problem, we first establish our constants and perform necessary unit conversions.

**Given:**
*   Mass $m = 1 \text{ tonne} = 1000 \text{ kg}$
*   Radius $r = 500 \text{ m}$
*   Angle $\theta = 10^\circ$
*   Velocity $v = 250 \text{ kph} = \frac{250}{3.6} \approx 69.44 \text{ m/s}$
*   Gravity $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$

---

### (A) Calculate the resultant force on the vehicle
The resultant force on a vehicle in uniform circular motion is the **centripetal force** ($F_c$), which is directed horizontally toward the center of the circular path.

$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
$$F_c = \frac{1000 \times (69.44)^2}{500}$$
$$F_c = \frac{1000 \times 4822.5}{500} = 9645 \text{ N}$$

The resultant force is **$9645 \text{ N}$ directed horizontally toward the center of the track.**

---

### (B) Calculate the value and direction of the frictional force
For a vehicle on a banked track, the forces acting are the Normal force ($N$), weight ($mg$), and friction ($f$). We resolve forces into vertical ($y$) and horizontal ($x$) components:
1.  **Vertical:** $N \cos\theta - f \sin\theta = mg$
2.  **Horizontal:** $N \sin\theta + f \cos\theta = F_c = 9645 \text{ N}$

First, we find the "ideal" speed ($v_{ideal}$) where friction is zero:
$\tan\theta = \frac{v^2}{rg} \Rightarrow v_{ideal} = \sqrt{rg \tan\theta} = \sqrt{500 \times 9.8 \times \tan(10^\circ)} \approx 29.3 \text{ m/s}$.
Since our actual speed ($69.44 \text{ m/s}$) is much higher than the ideal speed, the vehicle wants to skid **up** the track. Therefore, **friction acts down the slope.**

From the horizontal equation: $N \sin\theta + f \cos\theta = 9645$  (Eq 1)
From the vertical equation: $N \cos\theta - f \sin\theta = mg = 9800$ (Eq 2)

Solving for $N$ and $f$:
$N = \frac{9645 + f \cos\theta}{\sin\theta}$ 
Substitute into Eq 2: $(\frac{9645 + f \cos\theta}{\sin\theta})\cos\theta - f \sin\theta = 9800$
$9645 \cot\theta + f \frac{\cos^2\theta}{\sin\theta} - f \sin\theta = 9800$
$f (\frac{\cos^2\theta - \sin^2\theta}{\sin\theta}) = 9800 - 9645 \cot(10^\circ)$
$f (\frac{\cos(20^\circ)}{\sin(10^\circ)}) = 9800 - 5468 = 4332$
$f \approx 4332 \times \frac{0.1736}{0.9397} \approx 800 \text{ N}$

The frictional force is approximately **$800 \text{ N}$ directed down the slope of the track.**
