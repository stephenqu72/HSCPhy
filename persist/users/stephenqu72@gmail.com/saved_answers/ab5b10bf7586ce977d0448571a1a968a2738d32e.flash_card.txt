### Front
On a vertical velocity-time graph for projectile motion, what does it signify if the final velocity magnitude is greater than the initial velocity magnitude?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The projectile has landed at a point lower than its launch elevation (e.g., launched from a cliff to a lower level).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When interpreting the symmetry of a $v$-$t$ graph to determine displacement relative to launch height.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the final velocity magnitude is equal to the initial velocity (only true for ground-to-ground projectile motion).
