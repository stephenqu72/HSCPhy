### Front
How do you calculate the initial velocity ($u$) of a projectile given its range ($R$), time of flight ($t$), and launch angle ($\theta$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Horizontal velocity ($v_x$) is constant because horizontal acceleration is zero.
- **Formula:** $u = \frac{R}{t \cdot \cos(\theta)}$
- **When to use it:** When solving projectile motion problems where air resistance is neglected.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the total initial velocity ($u$) with the horizontal velocity component ($v_x$). Always resolve the components using trigonometry.
