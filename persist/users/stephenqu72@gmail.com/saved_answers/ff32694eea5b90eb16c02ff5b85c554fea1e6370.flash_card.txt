### Front
State Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion and its mathematical expression for two satellites orbiting the same central body.

### Back
- **Key idea:** The square of the orbital period ($T$) is directly proportional to the cube of the mean orbital radius ($r$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\frac{T_1^2}{r_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{r_2^3}$ (or $T^2 \propto r^3$).
- **When to use it:** When comparing the orbital periods and distances of two satellites (or planets) orbiting the same central mass.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to cube the radius ($r^3$) or square the period ($T^2$) during calculations; or confusing this with circular motion formulas like $v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$.
