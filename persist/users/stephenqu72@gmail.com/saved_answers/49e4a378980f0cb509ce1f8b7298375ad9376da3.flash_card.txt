### Front
What does a stroboscopic photograph of a fired projectile demonstrate regarding the components of its motion?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Galileo’s principle of the independence of vertical and horizontal motion.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Vertical: $y = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$ (constant $a_y$); Horizontal: $v_x = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}$ (constant $v_x$).
- **When to use it:** To analyze projectile motion components; specifically when identifying that vertical fall rate is unaffected by horizontal launch velocity.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the horizontal velocity changes; remember that in the absence of air resistance, $a_x = 0$ and $v_x$ remains constant throughout the flight.
