To solve this problem, we will use the principles of energy conservation in an electric field and the dynamics of a charged particle in a magnetic field.

### Part (a): Calculating the speed of the proton
When a proton (charge $q = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C}$, mass $m = 1.673 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{kg}$) is accelerated by an electric field, the work done on the proton by the field equals its gain in kinetic energy.

1. **Work done ($W$):** $W = qEd$, where $E = 500 \, \text{V/m}$ and $d = 0.20 \, \text{m}$.
2. **Kinetic Energy ($K$):** $K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.
3. **Equate and solve for $v$:**
   $$qEd = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \implies v = \sqrt{\frac{2qEd}{m}}$$
4. **Substitution:**
   $$v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times 500 \times 0.20}{1.673 \times 10^{-27}}}$$
   $$v = \sqrt{\frac{3.204 \times 10^{-17}}{1.673 \times 10^{-27}}} \approx \sqrt{1.915 \times 10^{10}} \approx 1.384 \times 10^5 \, \text{m/s}$$

**Answer (a):** The speed of the proton is approximately $1.38 \times 10^5 \, \text{m/s}$.

---

### Part (b): Calculating the magnetic field strength
When the proton enters a perpendicular magnetic field, it moves in a circular path because the magnetic force acts as the centripetal force:
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r} \implies B = \frac{mv}{qr}$$

1. **Given values:**
   - $v = 1.384 \times 10^5 \, \text{m/s}$
   - $r = 500 \, \mu\text{m} = 5 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m}$
2. **Calculation:**
   $$B = \frac{(1.673 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{kg}) \times (1.384 \times 10^5 \, \text{m/s})}{(1.602 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C}) \times (5 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m})}$$
   $$B = \frac{2.315 \times 10^{-22}}{8.01 \times 10^{-23}} \approx 2.89 \, \text{T}$$

**Answer (b):** The magnetic field strength is approximately $2.89 \, \text{T}$.

### How to solve it
*   **Part (a):** Recognize that electric potential energy is converted into kinetic energy. Use the work-energy theorem ($W = \Delta K$) to derive the final velocity. Ensure units are converted to SI (e.g., cm to m).
*   **Part (b):** Apply the centripetal force formula for a charged particle in a magnetic field. Equate the magnetic force ($F_B = qvB$) to the centripetal force ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$) and rearrange the equation to isolate the unknown, $B$. Always carry the precision from part (a) to avoid rounding errors.
