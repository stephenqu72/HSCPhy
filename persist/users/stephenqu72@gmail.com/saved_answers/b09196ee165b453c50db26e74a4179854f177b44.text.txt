To solve this problem, we look at the physics of circular motion on a level track.

### Physics Principles
When a train rounds a curve, the "sideways force" mentioned in the question is the centripetal force required to keep the train moving in a circle. This force ($F_c$) is provided by the rails pushing against the wheels:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

The problem states that the speed limit is determined by the "maximum safe sideways force." This means that $F_c$ is a constant value for the track system. We can set up a relationship between the velocity $v$ and the radius $r$:
$$F_c \propto \frac{v^2}{r}$$

Since $F_c$ is constant, we can express this as:
$$\frac{v_1^2}{r_1} = \frac{v_2^2}{r_2}$$

### Calculation
1. **Identify the variables:**
   - $v_1 = 60\text{ km h}^{-1}$
   - $r_1 = 200\text{ m}$
   - $v_2 = 120\text{ km h}^{-1}$
   - $r_2 = ?$

2. **Rearrange for $r_2$:**
   $$r_2 = r_1 \times \left( \frac{v_2}{v_1} \right)^2$$

3. **Substitute the values:**
   $$r_2 = 200 \times \left( \frac{120}{60} \right)^2$$
   $$r_2 = 200 \times (2)^2$$
   $$r_2 = 200 \times 4$$
   $$r_2 = 800\text{ m}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **(D)**.
