To solve this problem, we use the formula for gravitational potential energy ($U$) of an object in a gravitational field:

$$U = -\frac{G M m}{r}$$

### Given constants and values:
*   Mass of the Hubble Telescope ($m$) = $11,000\text{ kg} = 1.1 \times 10^4\text{ kg}$
*   Mass of the Earth ($M$) $\approx 5.97 \times 10^{24}\text{ kg}$ (standard HSC physics constant)
*   Gravitational constant ($G$) $\approx 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2/\text{kg}^2$
*   Radius of Earth ($R_E$) = $6371\text{ km} = 6,371,000\text{ m}$
*   Altitude ($h$) = $559\text{ km} = 559,000\text{ m}$

### Step 1: Calculate the orbital radius ($r$)
The distance $r$ must be measured from the center of the Earth to the object:
$$r = R_E + h = 6,371,000\text{ m} + 559,000\text{ m} = 6,930,000\text{ m} = 6.93 \times 10^6\text{ m}$$

### Step 2: Calculate Gravitational Potential Energy ($U$)
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$U = -\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24}) \times (1.1 \times 10^4)}{6.93 \times 10^6}$$

First, multiply the numerator:
$$(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24}) \times (1.1 \times 10^4) \approx 4.38 \times 10^{19}$$

Now, divide by $r$:
$$U \approx -\frac{4.38 \times 10^{19}}{6.93 \times 10^6} \approx -6.32 \times 10^{12}\text{ J}$$
