### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Expected Result:** The magnet will fall through the copper pipe significantly slower than through the PVC pipe. 

**Explanation:**
1.  **Electromagnetic Induction:** As the magnet falls through the copper pipe, the changing magnetic flux induces eddy currents in the walls of the copper pipe (Faraday's Law).
2.  **Lenz's Law:** Lenz’s Law states that these induced eddy currents will create a magnetic field that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced them. Therefore, the magnetic field created by the eddy currents exerts an upward repulsive force on the falling magnet, opposing gravity.
3.  **Relation to Giant Drop:** The "giant drop" uses this exact principle. As the permanent magnets on the seats pass the stationary copper fins, eddy currents are induced in the fins. These currents create a magnetic opposing force that slows the seat down without physical contact. PVC, being an insulator, does not allow significant eddy currents to flow, so the magnet falls at near-gravitational acceleration.

#### Part (b)
**Yes, the teacher's comment is correct.**

**Explanation:**
The braking force generated by eddy currents is **velocity-dependent**. According to the law of electromagnetic induction, the magnitude of the induced EMF (and thus the induced current and the braking force) is directly proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux. 
- When the ride is moving very fast, the rate of change of flux is high, resulting in a strong braking force.
- As the ride slows down, the rate of change of flux decreases, which automatically reduces the braking force.
This provides a "smooth" deceleration, as the force naturally tapers off rather than being a sudden, fixed mechanical friction (like a traditional clamp brake) that would cause a harsh, jerky stop regardless of the ride's speed.

### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Identify the Physics Principles:** Always look for keywords like "magnets," "falling," "moving through conductors," and "stopping." These are immediate signals to discuss **Electromagnetic Induction**, **Faraday’s Law**, and **Lenz’s Law**.
2.  **Lenz’s Law structure:** When explaining Lenz's law, always mention three steps: 
    *   Change in flux.
    *   Induction of currents (Eddy currents).
    *   The opposing force generated.
3.  **Linking concepts:** To score full marks in HSC Physics, you must always explicitly link the theoretical model (the pipe experiment) to the real-world application (the ride).
4.  **Analyze the "Why":** For evaluation questions (like Part b), focus on the *dynamic* nature of the force. Traditional brakes are static/fixed, but magnetic braking is proportional to speed, which is the key to a smooth deceleration.
