To solve this problem, we must calculate the magnitude of the force produced by a magnetic field and compare it to the magnitude of the force produced by an electric field on a proton.

### 1. Identify the given values:
*   Charge of a proton ($q$) = $1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
*   Magnetic field strength ($B$) = $1.50 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$
*   Velocity of proton ($v$) = $4.00 \times 10^5\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Distance between plates ($d$) = $0.30\text{ m}$
*   Potential difference ($V$) = $200\text{ V}$

### 2. Calculate the Magnetic Force ($F_B$):
The force on a charged particle moving through a magnetic field is given by $F_B = qvB \sin(\theta)$. Since the proton moves perpendicular to the field, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.
$$F_B = (1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times (4.00 \times 10^5) \times (1.50 \times 10^{-5})$$
$$F_B = 9.612 \times 10^{-19}\text{ N}$$

### 3. Calculate the Electric Force ($F_E$):
The electric force is given by $F_E = qE$, where $E$ is the electric field strength. The electric field between parallel plates is $E = \frac{V}{d}$.
$$E = \frac{200}{0.30} = 666.67\text{ V m}^{-1}$$
$$F_E = qE = (1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times 666.67$$
$$F_E \approx 1.068 \times 10^{-16}\text{ N}$$

### 4. Comparison:
To compare them, we find the ratio $\frac{F_E}{F_B}$:
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{1.068 \times 10^{-16}}{9.612 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 111.1$$

**Conclusion:** The force experienced by the proton due to the electric field ($1.07 \times 10^{-16}\text{ N}$) is approximately **111 times larger** than the force experienced due to the magnetic field ($9.61 \times 10^{-19}\text{ N}$).
