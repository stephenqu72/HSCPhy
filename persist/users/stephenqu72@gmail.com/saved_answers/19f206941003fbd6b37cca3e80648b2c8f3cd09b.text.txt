To solve this problem, we use the principles of projectile motion, conservation of momentum, and mass-energy equivalence. Let $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$.

### a) Determine the maximum height $H$
At maximum height, the vertical velocity component is zero. We use the formula $v_y^2 = u_y^2 - 2gH$.
The initial vertical component is $u_y = u \sin(60^\circ) = 30 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 25.98\text{ m/s}$.
$$0 = (u \sin 60^\circ)^2 - 2gH$$
$$H = \frac{(30 \sin 60^\circ)^2}{2 \times 9.8} = \frac{675}{19.6} \approx 34.44\text{ m}$$

### b) Calculate the mass defect
1. **Conservation of Momentum:**
At peak height, the vertical velocity is $0$, and horizontal velocity is $v_x = u \cos(60^\circ) = 30 \times 0.5 = 15\text{ m/s}$.
Momentum before = Momentum after.
$$M v_x = m_A v_A + m_B v_B$$
$$1.0 \times 15 = 0.5(0) + 0.5(v_B) \implies v_B = 30\text{ m/s}$$

2. **Energy Change ($\Delta E$):**
Initial kinetic energy $K_i = \frac{1}{2} M v_x^2 = 0.5 \times 1 \times 15^2 = 112.5\text{ J}$.
Final kinetic energy $K_f = \frac{1}{2} m_A v_A^2 + \frac{1}{2} m_B v_B^2 = 0 + 0.5 \times 0.5 \times 30^2 = 225\text{ J}$.
The energy released $Q = K_f - K_i = 225 - 112.5 = 112.5\text{ J}$.

3. **Mass Defect:**
Using $E = mc^2$, where $c \approx 3 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$:
$$\Delta m = \frac{Q}{c^2} = \frac{112.5}{(3 \times 10^8)^2} = 1.25 \times 10^{-15}\text{ kg}$$

### c) Is the momentum of piece B conserved?
**No.** Momentum is only conserved for a system if the net external force acting on it is zero. After the explosion, gravity acts on piece B. Since there is a non-zero external force (weight), the momentum of piece B changes over time.
