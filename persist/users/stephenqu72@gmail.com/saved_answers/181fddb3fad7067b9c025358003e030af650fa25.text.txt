### Part (a)
**Answer:**
The gradient of a graph is the change in the vertical axis divided by the change in the horizontal axis. 
- Vertical axis units: $\text{J kg}^{-1}$
- Horizontal axis units: $\text{m}$ (Note: the graph uses km, but for dimensional analysis, we use the base unit of length, $\text{m}$)

The units of the gradient are:
$$\text{Gradient units} = \frac{\text{J kg}^{-1}}{\text{m}} = \frac{\text{J}}{\text{kg} \cdot \text{m}}$$
Since work (energy) is Force $\times$ distance ($W = Fd$), the units of Joule are $\text{N} \cdot \text{m}$. Substituting this:
$$\frac{\text{N} \cdot \text{m}}{\text{kg} \cdot \text{m}} = \frac{\text{N}}{\text{kg}}$$
From Newton's second law ($F = ma$), $1 \text{ N} = 1 \text{ kg} \cdot \text{m/s}^2$. Therefore, $1 \text{ N/kg} = 1 \text{ m/s}^2$. Since $\text{m/s}^2$ is the unit of acceleration, the gradient represents the acceleration due to gravity ($g$).
