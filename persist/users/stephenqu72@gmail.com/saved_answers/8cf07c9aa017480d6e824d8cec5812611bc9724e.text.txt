### Explanation
To determine the relationship between the maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons ($E_{k \text{ max}}$) and the wavelength of the incident photons ($\lambda$), we use the photoelectric equation provided:

$$E_{k \text{ max}} = E - W$$

We know that the energy of a photon $E$ is related to its wavelength $\lambda$ by the equation:
$$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$
where $h$ is Planck’s constant and $c$ is the speed of light.

Substituting this into the initial equation, we get:
$$E_{k \text{ max}} = \frac{hc}{\lambda} - W$$

**Analysis:**
1. **Inverse proportionality:** Since $E_{k \text{ max}}$ is proportional to $1/\lambda$, as the wavelength $\lambda$ increases, the energy of the photon decreases, which leads to a decrease in the maximum kinetic energy of the electrons. This rules out graph C.
2. **Threshold Wavelength:** Photoelectric emission only occurs when the photon energy $E \geq W$. This means there is a maximum wavelength (the threshold wavelength, $\lambda_0$) beyond which no electrons are emitted. When $\lambda = \lambda_0$, $E_{k \text{ max}} = 0$.
3. **Shape of the curve:** The equation $E_{k \text{ max}} = \frac{hc}{\lambda} - W$ is in the form of a hyperbola shifted downwards by the constant $W$. As $\lambda$ approaches zero, $E_{k \text{ max}}$ approaches infinity. As $\lambda$ increases towards the threshold wavelength, the kinetic energy decreases following a reciprocal curve.

Graph **B** correctly represents this inverse relationship, showing $E_{k \text{ max}}$ decreasing asymptotically toward the x-axis as $\lambda$ increases.
