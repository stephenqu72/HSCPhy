### Answer
When the tapping key is closed, the conducting rod will experience a magnetic force that causes it to **deflect (swing) outwards**, away from the magnet (in this case, horizontally to the right).

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we apply the **Motor Effect** principle, which describes the force exerted on a current-carrying conductor in an external magnetic field.

1.  **Identify the direction of the Magnetic Field ($B$):** The magnetic field lines flow from the North pole ($N$) to the South pole ($S$). In this diagram, the $N$ pole is at the bottom and the $S$ pole is at the top, so the magnetic field points **upwards**.
2.  **Identify the direction of the Current ($I$):** Current flows from the positive terminal of the battery to the negative terminal. Following the circuit, the current enters the rod from the left side and flows towards the right side through the conductor.
3.  **Apply the Right-Hand Palm Rule:**
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$) (straight **up**).
    *   Point your thumb in the direction of the current ($I$) (to the **right**).
    *   Your palm (the direction of the force) now faces **outwards** (away from the magnet).
4.  **Justification:** According to the formula for the magnetic force on a current-carrying wire, $$F = IlB \sin\theta$$ (where $F$ is force, $I$ is current, $l$ is length of the wire, and $B$ is magnetic field strength), the conductor experiences a force perpendicular to both the current and the magnetic field. Since the wire is free to swing on its conducting suspension wires, the net force causes it to displace from its equilibrium position.
