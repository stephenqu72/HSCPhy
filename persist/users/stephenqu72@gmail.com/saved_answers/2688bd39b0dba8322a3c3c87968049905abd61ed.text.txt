The correct answer is **A. $0\text{ V}$**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, you must understand the fundamental physical principle behind how a transformer operates: **electromagnetic induction**.

1.  **The Principle of Operation:** A transformer works based on Faraday’s Law of Induction, which states that an electromotive force (EMF) is induced in a coil only when there is a *changing* magnetic flux through it.
2.  **DC vs. AC:** Transformers require a time-varying input voltage (like Alternating Current, AC) to create a changing magnetic flux in the primary coil, which then induces a voltage in the secondary coil.
3.  **Applying to the Question:** The problem states that the input is a **DC voltage**. A steady DC current creates a constant magnetic field (static magnetic flux). Since the magnetic flux is not changing, there is no change in magnetic flux over time ($\frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t} = 0$).
4.  **Conclusion:** Because there is no change in flux, no EMF is induced in the secondary coil. Therefore, the output voltage is $0\text{ V}$.

Common trap: Many students immediately apply the transformer turns ratio formula:
$$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$
Using this formula would give:
$$V_s = \frac{V_p \times N_s}{N_p} = \frac{240 \times 80}{2000} = 9.6\text{ V}$$
However, this formula **only applies to AC circuits**. Because this circuit uses DC, the transformer cannot function, making **B** a distractor designed to test your conceptual understanding rather than just your ability to perform a calculation.
