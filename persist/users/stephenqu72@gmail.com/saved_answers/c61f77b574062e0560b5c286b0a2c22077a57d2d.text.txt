To solve this problem, we must equate the gravitational force acting on the bottom wire with the magnetic force of repulsion between the two wires.

### (a) Determining current and direction

**1. Direction:** 
To "levitate," the bottom wire must experience an upward magnetic force to counteract gravity (downwards). According to the right-hand grip rule and the motor effect, currents flowing in **opposite directions** produce a repulsive force. Therefore, the current in the bottom wire must flow to the **left**.

**2. Calculation:**
We are given the mass per unit length, $\mu = \frac{m}{L} = 18.5 \text{ g/m} = 0.0185 \text{ kg/m}$.
The gravitational force per unit length is:
$$F_g / L = \mu g = 0.0185 \times 9.8 = 0.1813 \text{ N/m}$$
The magnetic force per unit length between two parallel wires is given by:
$$\frac{F_m}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$
Since $I_1 = I_2 = I$ and $r = 0.1 \text{ mm} = 1 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}$:
$$0.1813 = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) I^2}{2\pi \times 10^{-4}}$$
Simplifying the expression:
$$0.1813 = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} I^2}{10^{-4}} = 2 \times 10^{-3} I^2$$
$$I^2 = \frac{0.1813}{2 \times 10^{-3}} = 90.65$$
$$I = \sqrt{90.65} \approx 9.52 \text{ A}$$

### (b) Newton's Law explanation

According to **Newton’s Third Law**, for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. 
1. The top wire exerts a magnetic force on the bottom wire to push it away (upwards). 
2. Simultaneously, the bottom wire exerts an equal magnitude force on the top wire in the opposite direction (downwards). 
3. In this equilibrium state, the upward magnetic force on the bottom wire equals the downward gravitational force, satisfying **Newton’s First Law**, as the net force on the bottom wire is zero, allowing it to remain stationary (levitate).
