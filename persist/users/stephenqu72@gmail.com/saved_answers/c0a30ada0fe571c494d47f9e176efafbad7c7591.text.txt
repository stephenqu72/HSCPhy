### Explanation
To determine which metals are present in the unknown mixture, you must perform a **spectral line analysis**. An emission spectrum acts as a "chemical fingerprint" for an element; if an element is present in a mixture, all of its characteristic emission lines will appear in the spectrum of that mixture.

1.  **Analyze the "Unknown mixture":** Look at the lines present in the final spectrum.
2.  **Compare with individual metals:**
    *   **Magnesium (Mg):** If you align the lines of the Mg spectrum with the unknown mixture, you will observe that every single line found in the Mg spectrum is present in the unknown mixture.
    *   **Aluminium (Al):** If you align the lines of the Al spectrum, you will notice its lines (specifically the green and red lines) also perfectly match lines found in the unknown mixture.
    *   **Lithium (Li):** The Li spectrum has a characteristic orange-red line. If you look at the position of that line in the Li spectrum and compare it to the unknown mixture, there is no corresponding line in the mixture. Therefore, Lithium is not present.
    *   **Copper (Cu):** The Cu spectrum has several distinct lines, particularly in the violet and blue regions, that do not appear in the unknown mixture. Therefore, Copper is not present.

Since the unknown mixture contains all the lines of Magnesium and all the lines of Aluminium, and no lines from the other elements, the mixture consists of **Aluminium and magnesium**.
