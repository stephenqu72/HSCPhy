To solve this problem, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, which is given by the formula:

$$F = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Initial State:** The initial force is $F$, where the mass of the objects are $m_1$ and $m_2$, and the distance is $r$.
    $$F = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

2.  **Identify Changes:**
    *   The distance is decreased **by** one-third. This means the new distance $r'$ is $r - \frac{1}{3}r = \frac{2}{3}r$.
    *   The mass of ONE object is increased by a factor of 4. Let the new mass be $m_1' = 4m_1$.

3.  **Calculate New Force ($F_1$):**
    Substitute the new values into the gravitational formula:
    $$F_1 = \frac{G (4m_1) m_2}{(\frac{2}{3}r)^2}$$

4.  **Simplify:**
    $$F_1 = \frac{4 \cdot G m_1 m_2}{\frac{4}{9}r^2}$$
    $$F_1 = \frac{4}{\frac{4}{9}} \cdot \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$
    $$F_1 = 4 \cdot \frac{9}{4} \cdot F$$
    $$F_1 = 9F$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **D**.

***

*Note: If the question meant the distance was decreased **to** one-third (i.e., $r' = \frac{1}{3}r$), the force would be $F_1 = 4 \cdot 9F = 36F$. However, based on the phrasing "decreased by one third," the calculation $F_1 = 9F$ is the mathematically correct interpretation of the prompt.*
