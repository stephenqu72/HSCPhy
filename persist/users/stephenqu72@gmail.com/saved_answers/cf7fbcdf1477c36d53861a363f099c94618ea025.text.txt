### Answer
The correct answer is **B. The spacing would decrease.**

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must understand the relationship between the momentum of an electron and the diffraction pattern it creates. This involves two core physics principles:

1.  **De Broglie Wavelength:** The wavelength ($\lambda$) of an electron is inversely proportional to its momentum ($p$):
    $$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$
    Where $h$ is Planck's constant, $m$ is the mass of the electron, and $v$ is its velocity (speed). If the speed $v$ is doubled, the momentum $p$ also doubles, which causes the de Broglie wavelength $\lambda$ to be halved.

2.  **Diffraction Spacing:** The spacing of the diffraction pattern ($y$) is directly proportional to the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the wave passing through the grid (aperture). The relationship can be expressed by the diffraction equation for a grid or slit:
    $$y \approx \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$
    Where:
    *   $L$ is the distance to the screen.
    *   $d$ is the spacing of the grid.
    *   $\lambda$ is the wavelength.

**Logical steps:**
*   Since the speed $v$ is doubled, the wavelength $\lambda$ becomes $\lambda' = \frac{\lambda}{2}$.
*   Substituting this into the diffraction spacing equation: $y' \propto \frac{\lambda}{2}$.
*   Because the wavelength is halved, the fringe spacing $y$ also decreases (specifically, it becomes half of the original spacing).

Therefore, increasing the speed of the electrons results in a shorter wavelength, which leads to a more compressed (decreased) diffraction pattern.
