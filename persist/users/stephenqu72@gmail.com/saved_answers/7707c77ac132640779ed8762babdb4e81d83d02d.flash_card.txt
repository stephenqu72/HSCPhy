### Front
How does the electric field strength $E$ between parallel plates change if the potential difference $V$ is doubled and the plate separation $d$ is halved?

### Back
- Key idea: Electric field is directly proportional to voltage and inversely proportional to distance ($E \propto V/d$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E = \frac{V}{d}$
- When to use it: To calculate or compare the uniform electric field strength between parallel conducting plates.
- Common trap: Forgetting that dividing by a fraction (halving $d$) is equivalent to multiplying by its reciprocal, leading to a factor of 4 increase rather than 1.
