### Answer
The correct answer is **(B) electrons transitioning between energy levels.**

### Explanation
To solve this, we rely on the **Bohr Model of the atom**, which is central to the HSC Physics syllabus regarding atomic structure.

1.  **Understanding the Balmer Series:** The Balmer series represents the specific spectral lines produced when electrons in a hydrogen atom transition from higher energy levels ($n > 2$) down to the second energy level ($n = 2$).
2.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   **(A) is incorrect:** This describes the classical Rutherford model. If an electron spiraled down, it would lose energy continuously, resulting in a continuous spectrum rather than the discrete spectral lines observed in the Balmer series.
    *   **(B) is correct:** Bohr’s model postulates that electrons exist in quantized stationary states. When an electron falls from a higher energy state ($E_{high}$) to a lower energy state ($E_{low}$), it emits a photon with energy equal to the difference between these states:
        $$\Delta E = E_{high} - E_{low} = hf$$
        This discrete change in energy corresponds to the specific wavelengths (colors) observed in the Balmer series.
    *   **(C) is incorrect:** The Balmer series is an electronic phenomenon, not a nuclear one. Nuclear radiation involves processes like alpha, beta, or gamma decay from the nucleus.
    *   **(D) is incorrect:** The spectral lines are caused by emission (or absorption) due to transitions, not the scattering or reflection of light photons.

By recognizing that atomic spectra are a result of quantized energy transitions as defined by Bohr, we can confidently identify (B) as the correct explanation.
