### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) $v = 0.97c$**.

---

### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of **time dilation** in special relativity. 

1. **Understand Time Dilation:**
   When an object is moving at relativistic speeds, time passes more slowly for that object relative to a stationary observer. This is described by the time dilation formula:
   $$t = \gamma t_0$$
   where:
   - $t$ is the dilated time (observed by the stationary observer).
   - $t_0$ is the proper time (time measured in the moving frame).
   - $\gamma$ (Lorentz factor) is defined as $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$.

2. **Set up the variables:**
   The problem states the second-hand is "four times slower." This means for every 1 second of proper time ($t_0$) that passes on the spacecraft, 4 seconds pass for the stationary observer ($t$). Therefore:
   $$\frac{t}{t_0} = \gamma = 4$$

3. **Solve for $v$:**
   Substitute $\gamma = 4$ into the Lorentz factor equation:
   $$4 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$
   Square both sides:
   $$16 = \frac{1}{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
   Rearrange the equation:
   $$1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2} = \frac{1}{16}$$
   $$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 1 - 0.0625 = 0.9375$$
   Take the square root:
   $$\frac{v}{c} = \sqrt{0.9375} \approx 0.968$$
   $$v \approx 0.97c$$

Rounding to two decimal places, we get $0.97c$, which matches option (D).
