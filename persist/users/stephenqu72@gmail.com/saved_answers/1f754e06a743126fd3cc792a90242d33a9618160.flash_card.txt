### Front
How does changing a planet's mass ($M$) and radius ($r$) affect its surface gravity ($g$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** $g$ is directly proportional to $M$ and inversely proportional to $r^2$.
- **Formula:** $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- **When to use it:** To compare surface gravity between two celestial bodies when their relative masses and radii are provided.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to square the radius factor (e.g., halving the radius increases gravity by $2^2=4$, not 2).
