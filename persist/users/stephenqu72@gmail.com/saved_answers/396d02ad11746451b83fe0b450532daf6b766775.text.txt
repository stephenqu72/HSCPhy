### Answer
Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites are used extensively despite their drawbacks primarily because they are much closer to the Earth's surface compared to satellites in higher orbits (such as Geostationary Earth Orbit). This proximity provides two major technical advantages:

1.  **Reduced Signal Latency:** Because the distance the signal must travel is significantly shorter, the time delay (latency) for communication is minimized, making them ideal for high-speed internet and real-time telecommunications.
2.  **Higher Resolution and Sensitivity:** Their closeness allows for higher resolution Earth observation (imaging) and more sensitive remote sensing data collection, as the signal-to-noise ratio is improved and the satellite can capture more detailed data of the Earth's surface.
