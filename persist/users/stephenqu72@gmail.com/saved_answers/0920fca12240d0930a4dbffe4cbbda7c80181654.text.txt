To determine the ratio of the gravitational forces, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, which is given by the formula:

$$F = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

Where:
- $G$ is the gravitational constant.
- $m_1$ and $m_2$ are the masses of the two objects.
- $r$ is the distance between the centers of the two objects.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Calculate the force exerted by $x$ on $y$ ($F_{xy}$):**
- Mass of $x$ ($m_x$) = $200\text{ g}$
- Mass of $y$ ($m_y$) = $400\text{ g}$
- Distance ($r_{xy}$) = $5\text{ m}$

$$F_{xy} = \frac{G \cdot 200 \cdot 400}{5^2} = \frac{G \cdot 80,000}{25} = 3,200G$$

**2. Calculate the force exerted by $z$ on $y$ ($F_{zy}$):**
- Mass of $z$ ($m_z$) = $400\text{ g}$
- Mass of $y$ ($m_y$) = $400\text{ g}$
- Distance ($r_{zy}$) = $5\text{ m}$

$$F_{zy} = \frac{G \cdot 400 \cdot 400}{5^2} = \frac{G \cdot 160,000}{25} = 6,400G$$

**3. Determine the ratio ($F_{xy} : F_{zy}$):**
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{F_{xy}}{F_{zy}} = \frac{3,200G}{6,400G} = \frac{1}{2}$$

The ratio of the gravitational forces is $1:2$.

**Correct Option: b)**

### Teacher's Tip:
Note that since $G$ and $r^2$ are identical for both calculations, you can simplify the problem by recognizing that the force is directly proportional to the product of the masses ($m_1 \cdot m_2$). Therefore, the ratio is simply $(200 \cdot 400) : (400 \cdot 400)$, which simplifies to $200 : 400$, or $1:2$.
