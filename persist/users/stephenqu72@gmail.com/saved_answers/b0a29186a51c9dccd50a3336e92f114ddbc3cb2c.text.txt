To determine which factors affect the escape velocity of a rocket, we must look at the formula for escape velocity, which is derived from the conservation of energy.

### Explanation
The escape velocity ($v_e$) is the minimum speed an object must reach to break free from the gravitational influence of a massive body, such as the Earth. It occurs when an object's total mechanical energy (kinetic energy + gravitational potential energy) is zero at an infinite distance.

The formula for escape velocity is:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$$

Where:
*   $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
*   $M$ is the mass of the planet (or massive body).
*   $R$ is the radius (or distance from the center of mass) of the planet.

**Analyzing the factors:**
*   **I. The mass of the Earth ($M$):** This appears in the numerator of the formula, so it affects escape velocity.
*   **II. The mass of the rocket ($m$):** Note that $m$ cancels out during the derivation ($ \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{G M m}{R} $). Therefore, the mass of the rocket **does not** affect the escape velocity.
*   **III. The universal gravitational constant ($G$):** This appears in the formula and affects the magnitude of the velocity.
*   **IV. The radius of the Earth ($R$):** This appears in the denominator, so it affects the escape velocity.

Since I, III, and IV are in the formula and II is not, the correct answer is **(B)**.

### Answer
The correct option is **(B)**.
