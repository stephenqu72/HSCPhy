To solve this problem, we must analyze the forces acting on the baby. The baby is moving in a circular path, which requires a centripetal force. In this scenario, the centripetal force is provided entirely by the static frictional force between the baby and the merry-go-round.

### 1. Identify the given values
*   Radius of the merry-go-round, $r = 1\text{ m}$
*   Period of rotation, $T = 4\text{ s}$
*   Acceleration due to gravity, $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$

### 2. Determine the centripetal acceleration
The centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) is given by the formula:
$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} \quad \text{or} \quad a_c = \omega^2 r$$
First, calculate the angular velocity $\omega$:
$$\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{2\pi}{4} = \frac{\pi}{2} \text{ rad/s}$$
Now, calculate the acceleration:
$$a_c = \left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)^2 \times 1 = \frac{\pi^2}{4} \approx 2.467 \text{ m/s}^2$$

### 3. Set up the force equations
For the baby to stay in place, the static friction ($f_s$) must equal the required centripetal force:
$$f_s = m \times a_c$$
The maximum static friction is defined as:
$$f_{s(\text{max})} = \mu_s \times N$$
Where $N$ is the normal force. On a horizontal surface, $N = mg$. Therefore:
$$\mu_s \times m \times g = m \times a_c$$

### 4. Solve for the coefficient of static friction ($\mu_s$)
Notice that the mass $m$ cancels out:
$$\mu_s \times g = a_c$$
$$\mu_s = \frac{a_c}{g}$$
$$\mu_s = \frac{\pi^2 / 4}{9.8} \approx \frac{2.467}{9.8} \approx 0.252$$

**Final Answer:** The least coefficient of static friction required is approximately **$0.25$**.
