### Answer
The correct answer is **B**.

### Explanation
To solve this projectile motion problem, we must analyze the vertical and horizontal components of the initial velocity separately.

**1. Vertical Component ($v_y$):**
The maximum height reached by a projectile is determined by its initial vertical velocity. The relevant kinematic equation is $v_f^2 = v_y^2 + 2a\Delta y$. At the peak, $v_f = 0$, so:
$$0 = v_y^2 - 2g \Delta y_{max} \implies \Delta y_{max} = \frac{v_y^2}{2g}$$
Since Sam’s ball went higher than Kim’s ball ($\Delta y_{Sam} > \Delta y_{Kim}$), Sam must have a **larger initial vertical velocity** than Kim. Therefore, the vector for Sam's vertical velocity must be longer than Kim's.

**2. Horizontal Component ($v_x$):**
The horizontal distance (range $R$) is given by $R = v_x \times t_{total}$, where $t_{total}$ is the time of flight. 
The time of flight is determined by the vertical motion: $t_{total} = \frac{2v_y}{g}$. 
Substituting this into the range equation:
$$R = v_x \times \left(\frac{2v_y}{g}\right)$$
We are told that both balls travelled the same distance ($R = 100\text{m}$). This means the product of the horizontal and vertical velocity components must be the same for both golfers:
$$v_{x,Sam} \times v_{y,Sam} = v_{x,Kim} \times v_{y,Kim}$$
Because Sam has a larger vertical component ($v_{y,Sam} > v_{y,Kim}$), he must have a **smaller horizontal component** ($v_{x,Sam} < v_{x,Kim}$) to ensure the range remains identical.

**Conclusion:**
- Sam: Higher vertical component, lower horizontal component.
- Kim: Lower vertical component, higher horizontal component.

Looking at the options, **Option B** is the only one where Sam’s vertical arrow is longer than Kim’s, and Sam’s horizontal arrow is shorter than Kim’s.
