To determine the proper length of Spaceship $Y$, we use the principle of **length contraction** from Einstein's Special Theory of Relativity.

### Answer
The correct option is **C. 450 m**.

### Explanation

**1. Understand the concepts:**
*   **Proper length ($L_0$):** This is the length of an object measured in its own rest frame (the frame where the object is stationary).
*   **Contracted length ($L$):** This is the length measured by an observer who is moving relative to the object.
*   **The formula:** The relationship between these two is given by:
    $$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
    Alternatively, this can be written using the Lorentz factor $\gamma$ as $L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma}$, where $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}}$.

**2. Identify the given values:**
*   The contracted length ($L$) = $150\text{ m}$.
*   The velocity ($v$) = $0.95c$.

**3. Set up the calculation:**
We need to solve for $L_0$:
$$L_0 = \frac{L}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$

Substitute the values:
$$L_0 = \frac{150}{\sqrt{1 - (0.95)^2}}$$
$$L_0 = \frac{150}{\sqrt{1 - 0.9025}}$$
$$L_0 = \frac{150}{\sqrt{0.0975}}$$

Calculate the square root:
$$\sqrt{0.0975} \approx 0.31225$$

Now, solve for $L_0$:
$$L_0 \approx \frac{150}{0.31225} \approx 480.38\text{ m}$$

**4. Select the best fit:**
Comparing our result of approximately $480\text{ m}$ to the options provided:
A. 50 m
B. 150 m
C. 450 m
D. 900 m

Option **C (450 m)** is the closest value to our calculated result.
