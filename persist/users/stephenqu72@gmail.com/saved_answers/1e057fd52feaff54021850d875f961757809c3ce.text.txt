The correct answer is **C. Photon**.

### Explanation

To solve this, we must differentiate between **fundamental particles** (those that are not made of smaller components) and **composite particles** (those made of smaller components) within the context of the Standard Model of particle physics.

1.  **Analyze the options:**
    *   **A. Hadron:** A hadron is a composite particle made of quarks held together by the strong nuclear force. Examples include protons and neutrons. Therefore, it is not a fundamental particle.
    *   **B. Neutron:** A neutron is a baryon (a type of hadron) composed of three quarks (specifically, one 'up' quark and two 'down' quarks). Because it has internal structure, it is not fundamental.
    *   **C. Photon:** The photon is an elementary particle. It is the quantum of the electromagnetic field and the gauge boson that mediates the electromagnetic force. It is not composed of any smaller particles, making it a fundamental particle in the Standard Model.
    *   **D. Proton:** Like the neutron, the proton is a hadron made of three quarks (two 'up' quarks and one 'down' quark). Because it has an internal quark structure, it is not fundamental.

2.  **Conclusion:** The Standard Model classifies elementary particles into fermions (quarks and leptons) and gauge bosons. Since the photon is a gauge boson (a force carrier) that cannot be broken down into smaller components, it satisfies the definition of a fundamental particle.
