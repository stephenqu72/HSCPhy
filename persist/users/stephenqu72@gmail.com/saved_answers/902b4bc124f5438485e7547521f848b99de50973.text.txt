### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
To determine the mass of the car, we must analyze the forces acting on it. For a vehicle on a frictionless banked track, the net centripetal force ($F_c$) is provided by the horizontal component of the normal force ($N$).

**1. Identify the physics principles:**
*   The vertical component of the normal force balances gravity: $N \cos(\theta) = mg$
*   The horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force: $N \sin(\theta) = F_c$

**2. Relate the two equations:**
Divide the horizontal force equation by the vertical force equation:
$$\frac{N \sin(\theta)}{N \cos(\theta)} = \frac{F_c}{mg}$$
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{F_c}{mg}$$

**3. Solve for mass ($m$):**
Rearranging the formula to solve for $m$:
$$m = \frac{F_c}{g \tan(\theta)}$$

**4. Calculation:**
Using $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, $\theta = 15.0^\circ$, and $F_c = 3040 \text{ N}$:
$$m = \frac{3040}{9.8 \times \tan(15.0^\circ)}$$
$$m = \frac{3040}{9.8 \times 0.2679...}$$
$$m = \frac{3040}{2.6258...}$$
$$m \approx 1157.7... \text{ kg}$$

Rounding to three significant figures (based on the input data):
**Mass $\approx 1160 \text{ kg}$**

#### Part (b)
**Justification:**
If friction is introduced, the car is no longer restricted to a single "design speed" to maintain its circular path. 
*   **Impact:** Friction allows the car to safely navigate the corner at a range of speeds higher or lower than the ideal design speed. If the car travels faster than the design speed, friction acts down the slope to prevent the car from sliding up the track. Conversely, if the car travels slower, friction acts up the slope to prevent it from sliding down. Effectively, friction increases the stability of the car’s motion on the banked corner.
