### Front
How does the binding energy per nucleon curve dictate the energy released in nuclear reactions (fission vs. fusion)?

### Back
- Key idea: Energy is released whenever the product nucleus has a higher binding energy per nucleon (is more stable) than the reactants.
- Figure: Binding energy per nucleon ($BE/A$) vs. nucleon number ($A$) graph; peak occurs at Iron-56.
- When to use it: To determine if a nuclear reaction (fission, fusion, or decay) is exothermic or endothermic.
- Common trap: Assuming all nuclear reactions release energy; fusion of heavy nuclei or fission of light nuclei is endothermic and requires an energy input.
