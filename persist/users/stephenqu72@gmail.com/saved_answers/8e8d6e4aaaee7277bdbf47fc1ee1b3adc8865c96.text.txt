To determine if humans could live on this exoplanet, we must evaluate three criteria provided in the table: the temperature of the star, the orbital velocity of the planet, and the orbital radius of the planet.

### 1. Temperature of the Star
Wien’s Displacement Law states that $\lambda_{max} T = 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}$. Looking at the intensity graph, the peak wavelength $\lambda_{max}$ is approximately $500 \text{ nm}$ ($5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$).
$$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{5 \times 10^{-7}} \approx 5796 \text{ K}$$
This falls within the habitable range of $4000 - 6000 \text{ K}$.

### 2. Orbital Velocity ($v$)
The orbital period $T_{orbit}$ is determined by the time between brightness dips in the first graph. The dips occur at approximately $350$, $850$, and $1350$ days. The period is $T_{orbit} = 500 \text{ days} = 500 \times 24 \times 3600 \approx 4.32 \times 10^7 \text{ s}$.
Using the circular orbit formula $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$, where $T_{orbit} = \frac{2\pi r}{v} \Rightarrow r = \frac{v T_{orbit}}{2\pi}$. Substituting $r$:
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{G M 2\pi}{v T_{orbit}}} \Rightarrow v^3 = \frac{2\pi G M}{T_{orbit}}$$
$$v^3 = \frac{2\pi \times (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (7.96 \times 10^{30})}{4.32 \times 10^7} \approx 7.72 \times 10^{13}$$
$$v \approx 42,570 \text{ m/s} = 42.57 \text{ km/s}$$
This falls within the habitable range of $2 - 80 \text{ km/s}$.

### 3. Orbital Radius ($r$)
$$r = \frac{v T_{orbit}}{2\pi} = \frac{42570 \times 4.32 \times 10^7}{2\pi} \approx 2.92 \times 10^{11} \text{ m}$$
Earth's orbital radius is approximately $1.5 \times 10^{11} \text{ m}$. Thus, $r \approx \frac{2.92}{1.5} \approx 1.95 \text{ Earth radii}$.
This falls within the habitable range of $0.5 - 2.5 \text{ Earth radii}$.

### Conclusion
Since the star's temperature, the planet's orbital velocity, and the orbital radius all fall within the specified "habitable" ranges, it is scientifically plausible that the environment could support human habitation, provided other atmospheric and geological factors are favorable.
