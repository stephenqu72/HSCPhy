To answer this question effectively, you must identify how a nuclear chain reaction is maintained and regulated. The key concept is the **neutron cycle** within a fission reactor.

### Answer
Sustained and controlled nuclear energy is produced through a **fission chain reaction**, where the fission of a heavy nucleus, such as $^{235}\text{U}$, releases energy and additional neutrons, which go on to trigger further fission events.

**Two examples of control mechanisms:**
1. **Control Rods:** These are made of neutron-absorbing materials like cadmium or boron. By inserting these rods into the reactor core, they absorb excess neutrons, reducing the number available to cause further fission. This keeps the neutron multiplication factor ($k$) at exactly $1$, ensuring a steady, sustained reaction rather than an exponential explosion.
2. **Moderators:** Materials like graphite or heavy water are used to slow down fast-moving neutrons produced during fission. Thermal (slow) neutrons have a much higher probability of being captured by $^{235}\text{U}$ to cause fission. By regulating the speed of neutrons, the moderator ensures the chain reaction is maintained efficiently and predictably.
