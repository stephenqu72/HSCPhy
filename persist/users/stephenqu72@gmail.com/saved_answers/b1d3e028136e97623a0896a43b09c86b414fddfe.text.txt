### Analysis

**1. Greatest Centripetal Force:**
The centripetal force required for an object of mass $m$ to maintain a circular orbit of radius $r$ is given by:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} = m \omega^2 r$$
Since all three points (L1, L2, L3) share the same angular velocity ($\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T}$) as the Earth, the required centripetal force is directly proportional to the orbital radius $r$. 
Looking at the diagram, the distance of the Lagrange points from the Sun is:
*   $r_{L1} < r_{Earth}$
*   $r_{L2} > r_{Earth}$
*   $r_{L3} \approx r_{Earth}$ (on the opposite side)

Therefore, $r_{L2} > r_{L3} > r_{L1}$. Since $F_c \propto r$, the point with the **greatest radius (L2)** requires the greatest centripetal force to keep it in a circular orbit with the same period as the Earth.

**2. Greatest Linear Velocity:**
The linear velocity $v$ of an object in circular motion is given by:
$$v = \omega r$$
Again, because all three points share the same angular velocity $\omega$, the linear velocity is directly proportional to the orbital radius $r$.
As established, $r_{L2}$ is the largest radius among the three points. Therefore, the point at **L2** will have the greatest linear velocity.

### Conclusion
Comparing these results to the options provided:
*   Greatest centripetal force: **L2**
