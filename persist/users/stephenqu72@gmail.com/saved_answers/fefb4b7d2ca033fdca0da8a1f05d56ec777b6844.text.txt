To solve this physics problem, we must apply Newton’s First Law of Motion. The problem states that in both scenarios, the oil droplet moves at a **constant speed**.

According to Newton’s First Law, if an object moves at a constant velocity (which includes a constant speed in a straight line), the **net force acting on the object must be zero**.

### Step-by-Step Analysis:

**1. Downwards motion (Switch is open):**
*   The forces acting on the droplet are gravity ($F_G$) pulling it downwards and the frictional force ($F_F$) acting against the motion (upwards).
*   Since the speed is constant, the net force is zero:
    $$F_{net} = F_G - F_F = 0$$
    Therefore, $$F_G = F_F$$

**2. Upwards motion (Switch is closed):**
*   The forces acting on the droplet are the electric force ($F_E$) pulling it upwards, gravity ($F_G$) pulling it downwards, and the frictional force ($F_F$) acting against the motion (downwards).
*   Since the speed is constant, the net force is zero:
    $$F_{net} = F_E - F_G - F_F = 0$$
    Rearranging this, we get:
    $$F_E = F_G + F_F$$

**Conclusion:**
By comparing these equations to the options provided, we see that row **D** correctly represents the relationship between the forces in both scenarios.

*   Downwards: $F_G = F_F$
*   Upwards: $F_E = F_G + F_F$

The correct answer is **D**.
