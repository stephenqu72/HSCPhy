### Front
How does repeating measurements affect the quality of experimental data?

### Back
- Key idea: Repeating measurements improves the **accuracy** of the mean and reduces the impact of **random error**.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When evaluating the validity and reliability of experimental results in a physics report or exam question.
- Common trap: Assuming that repeating measurements improves **precision**. Precision is determined by the quality of equipment and technique; repetition only allows you to *assess* precision, not improve it.
