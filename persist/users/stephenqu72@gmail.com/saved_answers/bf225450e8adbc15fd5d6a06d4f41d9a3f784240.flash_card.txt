### Front
What is the formula for calculating the electrical force ($F$) exerted on a charged particle ($q$) within an electric field ($E$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Force is the product of charge and electric field strength.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $F = qE$
- **When to use it:** To calculate the force on a charge in a uniform electric field (e.g., Millikan’s oil drop experiment).
- **Common trap:** Confusing electric field strength ($E$) with voltage ($V$) or failing to ensure units are in SI (e.g., converting $\text{kC}$ or $\text{nC}$ to $\text{C}$).
