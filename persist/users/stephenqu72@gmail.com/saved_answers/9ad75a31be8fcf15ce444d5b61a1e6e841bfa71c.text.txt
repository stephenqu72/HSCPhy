To solve this problem, we use the relationship between the energy of a photon and its wavelength.

### 1. Identify the relevant formula
The energy $E$ of a photon is related to its wavelength $\lambda$ by the equation:
$$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$
Where:
*   $E = 8.3 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}$ (given)
*   $h$ is Planck's constant $\approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J s}$
*   $c$ is the speed of light $\approx 3.0 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}$

### 2. Rearrange the formula
We need to solve for $\lambda$:
$$\lambda = \frac{hc}{E}$$

### 3. Substitute the values
$$\lambda = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J s}) \times (3.0 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s})}{8.3 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}}$$

First, multiply the constants in the numerator:
$$hc \approx 1.9878 \times 10^{-25} \, \text{J m}$$

Now, divide by the energy:
$$\lambda = \frac{1.9878 \times 10^{-25}}{8.3 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$\lambda \approx 2.395 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}$$

Rounding this to two significant figures, we get $2.4 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}$.

Comparing this to the given options:
(A) $2.4 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}$
(B) $5.2 \, \text{m}$
(C) $4.2 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}$
(D) $1.3 \times 10^{15} \, \text{m}$

The correct option is **(A)**.
