### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Answer:** The new speed limit should be approximately $56.6 \text{ km h}^{-1}$.

**Explanation:**
The formula for centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) is:
$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$$
We are told the centripetal acceleration remains constant, so if we compare the initial state ($1$) and the new state ($2$):
$$\frac{v_1^2}{r_1} = \frac{v_2^2}{r_2}$$
We know that the radius is doubled, so $r_2 = 2r_1$. Substituting this in:
$$\frac{v_1^2}{r_1} = \frac{v_2^2}{2r_1}$$
$$v_2^2 = 2v_1^2$$
$$v_2 = v_1 \sqrt{2}$$
Given $v_1 = 40 \text{ km h}^{-1}$:
$$v_2 = 40 \times 1.414 \approx 56.57 \text{ km h}^{-1}$$

#### Part (b)
**Answer:** The required banking angle $\theta$ is approximately $12.1^\circ$.

**Explanation:**
To traverse a curve without relying on friction, the horizontal component of the normal force must provide the required centripetal force. The formula for the banking angle is:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$
First, convert the speed from part (a) to SI units ($\text{m s}^{-1}$):
$$v = 56.57 \text{ km h}^{-1} = \frac{56570}{3600} \approx 15.71 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$
Given $r = 150 \text{ m}$ and $g = 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{(15.71)^2}{150 \times 9.8}$$
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{246.8}{1470} \approx 0.1679$$
$$\theta = \arctan(0.1679) \approx 12.1^\circ$$
