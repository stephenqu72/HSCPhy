### Front
How does the total mechanical energy of a satellite change when moving from a lower to a higher circular orbit?

### Back
- Key idea: Total mechanical energy ($E = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$) increases (becomes less negative) as the orbital radius increases.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E_{total} = K + U = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$.
- When to use it: To explain orbital manoeuvres; while potential energy increases, kinetic energy decreases, but the net work done by engines is positive.
- Common trap: Assuming that because the satellite slows down (lower $K$), it loses total energy. In reality, the gain in $U$ is twice the loss in $K$.
