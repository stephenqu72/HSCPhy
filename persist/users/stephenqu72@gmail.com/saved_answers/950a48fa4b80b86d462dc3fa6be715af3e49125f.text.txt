### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) The atom is mostly empty space.**

---

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must understand the historical context of the Rutherford Gold Foil experiment and what it specifically sought to test.

1.  **Context (The Plum Pudding Model):** Before Rutherford's experiment, J.J. Thomson’s "Plum Pudding" model suggested that atoms were spheres of positive charge with negatively charged electrons embedded throughout. If this model were correct, alpha particles (which are positively charged) should have passed straight through the gold foil with minimal deflection.
2.  **The Observation:** Rutherford observed that while most alpha particles passed straight through the gold foil, a small fraction were deflected at large angles, and some were even reflected directly back toward the source.
3.  **The Conclusion:** 
    *   The fact that most particles passed through undeflected led to the revolutionary conclusion that the **atom is mostly empty space**.
    *   The fact that some were deflected at sharp angles led to the conclusion that there is a dense, positively charged center, which he named the **nucleus**.

**Why the other options are not the "most significant" answer:**
*   **(A)** Electrons were already known to exist in atoms (discovered by Thomson).
*   **(B)** The concept of "energy shells" was introduced later by **Niels Bohr**, not Rutherford.
*   **(C)** While Rutherford did discover the nucleus was positive, option (D) is the broader foundational realization that forced the abandonment of the "Plum Pudding" model and paved the way for the planetary model of the atom. In HSC Physics, the discovery that the atom is mostly empty space is the key structural takeaway from this specific experiment.
