### Answer and Explanation

#### (a) Calculate the rest mass of Kaon in kg.
**Solution:**
The total energy released by the annihilation of the Kaon ($m_0$) and its anti-particle ($m_0$) is $E_{total} = 987\text{ MeV}$.
Since the particles annihilate at rest, $E_{total} = 2m_0c^2$, so the mass of a single Kaon is given by:
$$m_0c^2 = \frac{987\text{ MeV}}{2} = 493.5\text{ MeV}$$
Convert MeV to Joules: $493.5 \times 10^6 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J} \approx 7.906 \times 10^{-11}\text{ J}$.
Using $E = m_0c^2$:
$$m_0 = \frac{7.906 \times 10^{-11}}{(3.00 \times 10^8)^2} \approx 8.78 \times 10^{-28}\text{ kg}$$

#### (b) Compare the work required
**Solution:**
Work required is equal to the change in Kinetic Energy ($\Delta KE$).
$KE = (\gamma - 1)m_0c^2$, where $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}}$.
1. **From $0$ to $0.900c$:** $\gamma_{0.9} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.9^2}} \approx 2.294$.
   $\Delta KE_1 = (2.294 - 1)m_0c^2 = 1.294 m_0c^2$.
2. **From $0.900c$ to $0.999c$:** $\gamma_{0.999} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.999^2}} \approx 22.366$.
   $\Delta KE_2 = (22.366 - 2.294)m_0c^2 = 20.072 m_0c^2$.

**Comparison:** The work required to accelerate the particle from $0.900c$ to $0.999c$ is significantly greater (approx. $15.5$ times more) than the work required to accelerate it from rest to $0.900c$.

---

#### (c) Explain the implications
**Solution:**
As an object's velocity approaches the speed of light ($c$), its relativistic mass (or total energy) increases toward infinity. Consequently, it requires an exponentially increasing amount of energy (work) to achieve even small further increases in velocity. This implies that no particle with mass can ever be accelerated to or beyond the speed of light.
