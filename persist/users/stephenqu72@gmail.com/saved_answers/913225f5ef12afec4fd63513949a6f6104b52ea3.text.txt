To determine which quantity does not affect the escape velocity, we must look at the mathematical definition of escape velocity.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(B) The mass of the object being projected.**

### Explanation
The escape velocity ($v_e$) is defined as the minimum speed needed for an object to break free from the gravitational attraction of a massive body (like a planet) without further propulsion.

We derive this by equating the kinetic energy of the object to the magnitude of the gravitational potential energy of the object at the planet's surface:
$$\frac{1}{2}mv_e^2 = \frac{GMm}{r}$$

Where:
- $m$ is the mass of the object being projected.
- $M$ is the mass of the planet.
- $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
- $r$ is the radius of the planet (distance from the center of mass).

As you can see, the mass of the object ($m$) appears on both sides of the equation and cancels out:
$$v_e^2 = \frac{2GM}{r}$$
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

Because the mass of the object ($m$) cancels out, the escape velocity is **independent** of the mass of the object being projected. Conversely, $M$, $r$, and $G$ all appear in the final formula, meaning they all influence the magnitude of the escape velocity.
