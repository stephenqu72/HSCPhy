To find the distance between adjacent maxima (often called the "fringe width") in Young's double-slit experiment, we use the formula:

$$\Delta x = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$

### Given values:
*   Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $589\text{ nm} = 589 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$
*   Distance between slits ($d$) = $2.4 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m}$
*   Slit-to-screen distance ($L$) = $0.75\text{ m}$

### Calculation:
Substitute the values into the formula:

$$\Delta x = \frac{(589 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}) \times (0.75\text{ m})}{2.4 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m}}$$

First, multiply the numerator:
$589 \times 0.75 = 441.75$
So, the numerator is $441.75 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}^2$.

Now, divide by $d$:
$$\Delta x = \frac{441.75 \times 10^{-9}}{2.4 \times 10^{-4}}$$
$$\Delta x = 184.0625 \times 10^{-5}\text{ m}$$

To convert this to scientific notation or standard units:
$$\Delta x \approx 1.84 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$$

Since $10^{-3}\text{ m}$ is equivalent to $1\text{ mm}$, we get:
$$\Delta x \approx 1.84\text{ mm}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches **(A)**.
