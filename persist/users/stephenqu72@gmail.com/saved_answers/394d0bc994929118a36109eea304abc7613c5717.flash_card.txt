### Front
How do you calculate the velocity of an object at the bottom of a frictionless slope given an initial height $h$?

### Back
- Key idea: Conservation of mechanical energy ($PE_{top} = KE_{bottom}$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \sqrt{2gh}$
- When to use it: When an object slides or rolls down a frictionless incline starting from rest.
- Common trap: Forgetting to cancel the mass ($m$) or failing to account for any initial velocity if the object wasn't starting from rest.
