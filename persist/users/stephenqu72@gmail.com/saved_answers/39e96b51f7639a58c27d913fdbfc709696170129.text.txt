### Explanation
To solve this problem, we need to consider two distinct concepts related to an object's position on a rotating Earth:

**1. Gravitational Potential ($U$):**
The gravitational potential at a distance $r$ from the center of the Earth is defined by the formula:
$$U = -\frac{GM}{r}$$
where $G$ is the gravitational constant, $M$ is the mass of the Earth, and $r$ is the distance from the center of the Earth. 
*   Point X is at a higher altitude than point Y, meaning $r_X > r_Y$.
*   As $r$ increases, the magnitude of the negative value $\frac{GM}{r}$ decreases, making the value "less negative" (closer to zero). Therefore, the gravitational potential is **greater** at X than at Y.

**2. Angular Velocity ($\omega$):**
The Earth rotates as a rigid body. For any point on the surface of the Earth, the angular velocity $\omega$ is defined as:
$$\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T}$$
where $T$ is the period of rotation (one day). Since every point on the Earth completes one rotation in the same amount of time, the **angular velocity is the same** for all points on the Earth's surface regardless of their height or latitude.
