### Front
How does changing the Hubble constant ($H_0$) affect the estimated age of the universe ($t$)?

### Back
- Key idea: The age of the universe is inversely proportional to the Hubble constant.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $t \approx \frac{1}{H_0}$
- When to use it: To calculate the age of the universe given a change in the expansion rate (Hubble constant).
- Common trap: Assuming a direct relationship (e.g., doubling $H_0$ doubles the age) instead of an inverse one.
