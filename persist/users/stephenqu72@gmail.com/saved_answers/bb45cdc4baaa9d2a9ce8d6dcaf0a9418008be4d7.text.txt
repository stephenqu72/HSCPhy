### Part (a): Calculation
To find the acceleration due to gravity ($g$) at the surface of a planet, we use the formula derived from Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation:
$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

**Given:**
- $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{ kg}^{-2}$
- $M = 6.4 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$
- $r = 3.4 \times 10^{6} \text{ m}$

**Calculation:**
$$g = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (6.4 \times 10^{23})}{(3.4 \times 10^{6})^2}$$
$$g = \frac{4.2688 \times 10^{13}}{1.156 \times 10^{13}}$$
$$g \approx 3.69 \text{ m s}^{-2}$$

---

### Part (b): Graphing
1. **Find Initial Potential Energy ($U_g$):**
   At the surface, $U_g = 0$ J. At a height $h = 10$ m, the potential energy is:
   $$U_g = mgh = 0.20 \text{ kg} \times 3.69 \text{ m s}^{-2} \times 10 \text{ m} = 7.38 \text{ J}$$
   - **Sketching $U_g$:** Draw a straight line starting from $(0,0)$ up to $(10, 7.38)$.

2. **Sketching Kinetic Energy ($E_K$):**
   By the Law of Conservation of Energy, total mechanical energy is constant. Since the probe is "released" (starts from rest), the initial $E_K = 0$. As it falls, $E_K$ increases as $U_g$ decreases.
   - **Sketching $E_K$:** Draw a straight line starting from $(0, 7.38)$ (at height 10m) down to $(10, 0)$ (at height 0m).

**Summary for graph:**
- Line $U_g$: Passes through $(0,0)$ and $(10, 7.38)$.
- Line $E_K$: Passes through $(0, 7.38)$ and $(10, 0)$.

### How to solve these types of questions:
1. **Identify the variables:** Always list your given values with units before plugging them into the formula.
2. **Formula selection:** For gravitational fields, remember that surface gravity depends on the planet's mass and radius.
3. **Energy principles:** When sketching energy graphs for objects in a uniform field, remember that total energy is conserved. Since $U_g = mgh$ is a linear function of $h$, the energy graphs will be straight lines with equal and opposite gradients.
