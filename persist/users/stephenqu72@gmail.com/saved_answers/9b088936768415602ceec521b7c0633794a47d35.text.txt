### Answer

**Escape velocity** is the minimum speed required for an object to break free from the gravitational attraction of a massive body (like a planet or star) and never return. Once an object reaches this speed, its total mechanical energy becomes zero, allowing it to move to an infinite distance from the body.
The formula for escape velocity ($v_e$) is:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

**Orbital velocity** is the constant speed required for an object (like a satellite) to maintain a stable, circular orbit at a specific distance from the center of a massive body. At this velocity, the gravitational force provides the necessary centripetal force to keep the object in orbit.
The formula for orbital velocity ($v_o$) is:
$$v_o = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

*(Where $G$ is the gravitational constant, $M$ is the mass of the central body, and $r$ is the distance from the center of the mass.)*
