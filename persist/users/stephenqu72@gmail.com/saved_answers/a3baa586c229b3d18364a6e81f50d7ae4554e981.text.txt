To solve this problem, we use the principle that the work done on a charged particle by an electric field is equal to the change in its kinetic energy.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the relationship:**
    The work done $W$ on a charge $q$ accelerated through a potential difference $V$ is given by:
    $$W = qV$$
    This work results in a change in the kinetic energy ($\Delta E_k$) of the charge:
    $$\Delta E_k = qV$$

2.  **Calculate the change in kinetic energy:**
    The kinetic energy increases from $2.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ J}$ to $6.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ J}$.
    $$\Delta E_k = E_{k,final} - E_{k,initial}$$
    $$\Delta E_k = (6.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ J}) - (2.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ J}) = 4.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ J}$$

3.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for charge ($q$):**
    Since $qV = \Delta E_k$, we have:
    $$q = \frac{\Delta E_k}{V}$$

4.  **Substitute the known values:**
    Given $V = 500 \text{ V}$ and $\Delta E_k = 4.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ J}$:
    $$q = \frac{4.0 \times 10^{-5}}{500}$$
    $$q = \frac{4.0 \times 10^{-5}}{5.0 \times 10^2}$$
    $$q = 0.8 \times 10^{-7} \text{ C}$$
    $$q = 8.0 \times 10^{-8} \text{ C}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct choice is **(A)**.
