### Front
How do you calculate centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) when given the rotational frequency ($f$) and radius ($r$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Centripetal acceleration is proportional to the square of the angular velocity.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_c = \omega^2 r$ where $\omega = 2\pi f$.
- When to use it: Circular motion problems involving rotation rates in Hz or rotations per second.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert diameter to radius, or using linear velocity ($v$) when only frequency ($f$) is provided.
