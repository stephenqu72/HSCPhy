### Front
How does the gravitational force between two objects change if both masses are doubled and the distance between their centres is also doubled?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational force is proportional to the product of masses ($m_1m_2$) and inversely proportional to the square of the distance ($1/d^2$).
- Formula: $F = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{d^2}$
- When to use it: To calculate the change in gravitational force when variables in the universal gravitation formula are scaled.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the distance change in the denominator—ensure you square the entire term $(2d)^2 = 4d^2$.
