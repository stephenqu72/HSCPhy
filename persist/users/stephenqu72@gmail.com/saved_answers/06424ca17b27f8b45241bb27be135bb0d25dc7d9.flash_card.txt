### Front
How do you calculate the orbital velocity of a satellite when the mass of the central body ($M$) is unknown?

### Back
- Key idea: Use the data from another satellite orbiting the same central body to determine the gravitational parameter ($GM$).
- Formula / law / constant: $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$ rearranged to $GM = v^2r$.
- When to use it: When given orbital data for one satellite and asked to find the velocity of another orbiting the same body.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert units (e.g., km to m) or failing to derive $GM$ before substituting into the velocity formula.
