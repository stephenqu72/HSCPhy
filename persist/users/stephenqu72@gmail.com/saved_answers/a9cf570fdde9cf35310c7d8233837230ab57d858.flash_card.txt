### Front
How do you determine the direction of a magnetic field produced by a current-carrying wire, and what is the net field at the midpoint between two parallel wires carrying equal current in the same direction?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Current-carrying wires generate circular magnetic fields; vectors add/subtract based on direction.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Right-Hand Grip Rule.
- **When to use it:** To find the direction of $B$-fields around straight conductors and determine vector superposition.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to account for the 3D orientation (into vs. out of the page)—vectors in opposite directions at the midpoint cancel to zero.
