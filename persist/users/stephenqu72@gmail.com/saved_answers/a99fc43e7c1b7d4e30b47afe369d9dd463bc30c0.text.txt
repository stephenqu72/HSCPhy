### Answer and Explanation

To solve this problem, we need to apply the transformer equation, which relates the primary voltage ($V_p$), secondary voltage ($V_s$), primary turns ($N_p$), and secondary turns ($N_s$):

$$\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Primary voltage ($V_p$) = $40\text{ V}$
*   Primary turns ($N_p$) = $1000$
*   Secondary turns ($N_s$) = $6000$

**2. Calculate the secondary voltage ($V_s$):**
Rearranging the formula to solve for $V_s$:
$$V_s = V_p \times \left( \frac{N_s}{N_p} \right)$$
$$V_s = 40 \times \left( \frac{6000}{1000} \right)$$
$$V_s = 40 \times 6$$
$$V_s = 240\text{ V}$$

**3. Determine the type of transformer:**
*   A **step-up** transformer is one where the secondary voltage is greater than the primary voltage ($V_s > V_p$), which corresponds to having more turns in the secondary coil than in the primary coil ($N_s > N_p$).
*   Since $N_s (6000) > N_p (1000)$ and $V_s (240\text{ V}) > V_p (40\text{ V})$, this is a **step-up** transformer.

**Conclusion:**
The secondary voltage is $240\text{ V}$ and the transformer is a step-up transformer. This matches option **D**.
