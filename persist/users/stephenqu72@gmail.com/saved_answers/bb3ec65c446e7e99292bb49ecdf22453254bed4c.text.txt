### Answer
As the magnet falls through the aluminium pipe, the changing magnetic flux induces eddy currents in the conductive walls of the pipe, according to **Faraday’s Law of Induction**. In accordance with **Lenz’s Law**, these eddy currents create their own magnetic fields that oppose the change in magnetic flux that produced them. This interaction creates a repulsive magnetic force that acts against the direction of the magnet's motion (gravity), effectively slowing its descent. Because plastic is a non-conductive insulator, no eddy currents are induced, and the magnet falls at the standard rate of acceleration due to gravity.

1.  **Identify the Inductive Cause:** Mention that aluminium is a conductor and as the magnet falls, it creates a *changing magnetic flux* through the walls of the pipe.
2.  **Apply Faraday’s and Lenz’s Laws:**
    *   State that this changing flux induces **eddy currents** in the pipe (Faraday's Law).
    *   Explicitly mention **Lenz's Law**, noting that the direction of these currents creates a magnetic field that *opposes* the motion of the falling magnet.
3.  **Explain the Result:** Describe how this creates an upward magnetic force (opposing gravity) which results in a net force decrease, causing the magnet to reach a terminal velocity or simply fall slower than it would in a vacuum or a non-conductive (plastic) pipe.

**Key Tip:** Ensure you contrast this with the plastic pipe. By stating that plastic is an insulator, you demonstrate an understanding of why the phenomenon is specific to conductive materials.
