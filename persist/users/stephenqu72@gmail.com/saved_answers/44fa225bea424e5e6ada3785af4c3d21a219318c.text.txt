The correct answer is **B. X**.

### Explanation

To solve this, we must understand the physics of James Chadwick’s 1932 experiment that led to the discovery of the neutron:

1.  **The Source (Polonium):** Polonium emits alpha particles ($\alpha$), which are helium nuclei.
2.  **The Beryllium Target:** When these alpha particles strike the Beryllium target, a nuclear reaction occurs:
    $$_2^4\text{He} + _4^9\text{Be} \rightarrow _6^{12}\text{C} + _0^1\text{n}$$
    This reaction produces high-energy, neutral radiation, which Chadwick identified as **neutrons**.
3.  **Position W:** In this region, you would primarily find the alpha particles emitted by the Polonium source. Neutrons have not yet been produced here.
4.  **Position X:** This is the space immediately after the Beryllium target. Since the neutrons are created by the collision of alpha particles with Beryllium, the neutrons are emitted from the target and travel into the space marked **X**.
5.  **The Paraffin Block (and beyond):** Chadwick placed a paraffin block (rich in hydrogen/protons) in the path of this radiation. The neutrons struck the paraffin and knocked protons out of it. The "ionising chamber" was used to detect these ejected protons. Therefore, while neutrons exist in region X, they interact with the paraffin at region Y and Z, causing protons to be detected in the chamber.

Since neutrons are generated the moment the alpha particles strike the Beryllium, they are present in the space immediately following the target, which is **X**.
