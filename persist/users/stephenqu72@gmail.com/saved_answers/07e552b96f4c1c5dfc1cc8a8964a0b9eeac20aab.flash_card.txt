### Front
Which categories of particles in the Standard Model are considered "fundamental"?

### Back
- Key idea: Fundamental particles have no substructure and are not composed of smaller particles.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Use to identify which particles cannot be broken down further (e.g., in multiple-choice questions about particle classification).
- Common trap: Assuming baryons (protons, neutrons) are fundamental; they are composite particles made of quarks.
