To determine the correct answer, we must calculate the radius of the circular path of the ion and use the Right-Hand Rule to determine its charge.

### 1. Determining the Sign of the Ion
- The magnetic field ($\vec{B}$) is directed **out of the page**.
- The velocity ($\vec{v}$) of the ion is **downwards**.
- According to the right-hand rule for a **positive** charge moving in a magnetic field: point your fingers in the direction of velocity (down), curl them towards the magnetic field (out of the page), and your thumb points towards the direction of the force.
- Following this, a positive charge would experience a force to the **left**.
- Looking at the diagram, the particle curves to the left. Therefore, the ion must have a **positive** charge.

### 2. Calculating the Radius
When a charged particle moves perpendicularly into a magnetic field, the magnetic force acts as the centripetal force:
$$F_B = F_c$$
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Solving for $r$:
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

**Given data:**
- Mass ($m$) = $12.00\text{ amu} = 12.00 \times 1.66 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg} = 1.992 \times 10^{-26}\text{ kg}$
- Velocity ($v$) = $3.135 \times 10^6\text{ ms}^{-1}$
- Charge ($q$) = $1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$ (charge of one electron/proton)
- Magnetic field ($B$) = $0.25\text{ T}$

**Calculation:**
$$r = \frac{(1.992 \times 10^{-26}) \times (3.135 \times 10^6)}{(1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times (0.25)}$$
$$r = \frac{6.24492 \times 10^{-20}}{0.04005 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$r = \frac{6.24492 \times 10^{-20}}{4.005 \times 10^{-20}} \approx 1.559\dots \approx 1.560\text{ m}$$

Comparing our results to the given options:
- The radius is $1.560\text{ m}$.
- The charge is positive.

This matches **Option D**.
