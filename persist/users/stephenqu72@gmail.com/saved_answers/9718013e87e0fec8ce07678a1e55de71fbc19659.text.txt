### Solution and Explanation

#### Part (a) Calculate the orbital period
To calculate the orbital period, we use Kepler's Third Law: $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$.
1. **Identify variables:** 
   - $R_E = 6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
   - $h = 600 \text{ km} = 0.6 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
   - Orbital radius $r = R_E + h = 6.97 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
   - $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{ kg}^{-2}$
   - $M = 6.0 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$

2. **Calculate:**
   $$T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{GM} = \frac{4\pi^2 (6.97 \times 10^6)^3}{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(6.0 \times 10^{24})} \approx 3.35 \times 10^7 \text{ s}^2$$
   $$T \approx 5790 \text{ seconds} \approx 96.5 \text{ minutes}$$

#### Part (b) Explain how the satellite stays in orbit
The satellite remains in orbit because it possesses a high **tangential velocity**.
1. It is in a state of **continuous free-fall** toward the Earth due to gravity, which provides the necessary **centripetal force**.
2. Because it has sufficient forward speed, the curvature of its path matches the curvature of the Earth's surface.
3. As long as it is above the dense part of the atmosphere (to avoid drag), gravity acts as the centripetal force keeping it in a circular path, requiring no fuel to maintain the orbit.

#### Part (c) Determine the change in gravitational potential energy
The change in gravitational potential energy ($\Delta U$) is the area under the force-distance graph, or specifically, the integral of $F(h) dh$. Since we are given the field strength ($g = F/m$), we use the average value or the work done formula. 
1. **Method:** The change in potential energy is $\Delta U = m \times (\text{area under the gravity graph})$.
2. The graph shows a near-linear change in $g$ from $9.8 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$ to $8.2 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$.
3. **Calculate Average $g$:** $g_{avg} = \frac{9.8 + 8.2}{2} = 9.0 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$.
4. **Calculate Work:** $\Delta U = m \cdot g_{avg} \cdot \Delta h$
   $$\Delta U = 288 \text{ kg} \times 9.0 \text{ N kg}^{-1} \times (600 \times 10^3 \text{ m})$$
   $$\Delta U \approx 1.555 \times 10^9 \text{ J}$$

*(Note: While the exact potential energy formula is $U = -GMm/r$, using the graph area is the standard approach for this specific HSC-style question format.)*
