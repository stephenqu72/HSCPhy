*   Look at the battery connected to the solenoid. The long line represents the positive terminal and the short line represents the negative terminal.
*   Conventional current flows from the positive terminal to the negative terminal. Therefore, the current enters the solenoid from the right side and flows "up and over" the front of the coils.
*   Use the **Right-Hand Grip Rule**: If you curl the fingers of your right hand in the direction of the current (up and over the front of the coils), your thumb points to the left end of the solenoid.
*   Therefore, the left end of the solenoid acts as the **North (N)** pole, and the right end of the solenoid acts as the **South (S)** pole.

**2. Determine the interaction with the external magnet:**
*   The external magnet is placed to the right of the solenoid.
*   The South pole of the solenoid is facing the external magnet.
*   Magnetic field lines exit the North pole and enter the South pole. Therefore, the magnetic field produced by the solenoid at the location of the small magnet points toward the left (attracted to the solenoid's South pole).

*   Since the solenoid's right side is a South pole, the magnet will experience an attractive force. Its **North pole** will be pulled toward the solenoid's South pole.

*   In option (A), the North (N) pole of the small magnet is on the left side, facing the solenoid's South pole. This is the correct alignment because opposite poles attract.
