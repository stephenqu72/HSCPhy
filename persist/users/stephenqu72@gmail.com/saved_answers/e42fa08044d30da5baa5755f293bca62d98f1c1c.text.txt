To solve for the speed of a car on a banked curve without the assistance of friction, we use the formula derived from the balance of forces in uniform circular motion.

### 1. The Physics Principle
When a car travels around a banked curve with no friction, the horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force required to keep the car moving in a circle.

The formula is:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

Where:
- $\theta = 15^\circ$ (banking angle)
- $r = 70\text{ m}$ (radius of the curve)
- $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$ (acceleration due to gravity)
- $v$ = velocity (the unknown we need to find)

### 2. Rearranging the Formula
To solve for $v$, we rearrange the equation:
$$v^2 = rg \cdot \tan(\theta)$$
$$v = \sqrt{rg \cdot \tan(\theta)}$$

### 3. Calculation
Substitute the given values into the equation:
$$v = \sqrt{70 \times 9.8 \times \tan(15^\circ)}$$

Calculate the values:
$$\tan(15^\circ) \approx 0.2679$$
$$v = \sqrt{70 \times 9.8 \times 0.2679}$$
$$v = \sqrt{686 \times 0.2679}$$
$$v = \sqrt{183.8194}$$
$$v \approx 13.56\text{ m/s}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (consistent with the provided data):
**The speed is approximately $14\text{ m/s}$.**
