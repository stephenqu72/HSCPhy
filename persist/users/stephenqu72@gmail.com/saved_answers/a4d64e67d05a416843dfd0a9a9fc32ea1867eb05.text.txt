To find the electromotive force (emf) produced by a rotating coil in a magnetic field, we use the formula for the peak emf ($\varepsilon_{peak}$) of an AC generator:

$$\varepsilon_{peak} = N B A \omega$$

### 1. Identify the given values:
*   Number of turns ($N$) = $200$
*   Magnetic field strength ($B$) = $0.05\text{ T}$
*   Side length of the square coil = $16\text{ cm} = 0.16\text{ m}$
*   Area ($A$) = $side^2 = 0.16\text{ m} \times 0.16\text{ m} = 0.0256\text{ m}^2$
*   Frequency ($f$) = $46.8\text{ rotations s}^{-1} = 46.8\text{ Hz}$

### 2. Calculate the angular velocity ($\omega$):
The relationship between angular velocity and frequency is:
$$\omega = 2\pi f$$
$$\omega = 2 \times \pi \times 46.8 \approx 294.05\text{ rad s}^{-1}$$

### 3. Calculate the peak emf:
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$\varepsilon_{peak} = 200 \times 0.05 \times 0.0256 \times (2 \times \pi \times 46.8)$$
$$\varepsilon_{peak} = 10 \times 0.0256 \times 294.05$$
$$\varepsilon_{peak} = 0.256 \times 294.05 \approx 75.28\text{ V}$$
