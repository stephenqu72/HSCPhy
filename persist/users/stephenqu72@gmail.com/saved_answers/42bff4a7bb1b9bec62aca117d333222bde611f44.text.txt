To determine the direction of the magnetic field at point $X$, we use the **Right-Hand Grip Rule** for solenoids.

### Answer
The correct answer is **B. right**.

### Explanation
To solve this, follow these steps using your right hand:

1.  **Analyze the current direction:** Observe the diagram of the solenoid. The current enters from the left and flows "over the top" of the coils towards the right. You can see this by looking at the wire segments that appear in front of the central axis—they are carrying current downwards relative to the top of the coil.
2.  **Apply the Right-Hand Grip Rule:** Curl the fingers of your right hand in the direction of the conventional current flow around the coils. Your fingers should be pointing "down" on the front side of the loops and "up" on the back side.
3.  **Determine the thumb's direction:** With your fingers curled in the direction of the current, your extended right thumb points towards the direction of the magnetic field lines inside the solenoid. In this configuration, your thumb will point towards the **right**.
4.  **Consider the field lines:** The magnetic field lines inside a solenoid travel from the South pole to the North pole, effectively exiting the North pole. Point $X$ is located outside the solenoid near the right end, where the magnetic field lines emerge and loop back around. Therefore, the field at $X$ is directed towards the right.
