### a) Thomson’s Experiment (4 Marks)
**Answer:**
Thomson used a cathode ray tube equipped with both electric and magnetic fields. 
1. **Deflection Balance:** He first applied an electric field ($E$) and a magnetic field ($B$) perpendicular to each other and to the path of the electrons. He adjusted them until the forces balanced, meaning the electron passed through undeflected. This occurs when the electric force ($F_E = qE$) equals the magnetic force ($F_B = qvB$). From this, he calculated the velocity of the electrons: $v = \frac{E}{B}$.
2. **Magnetic Deflection:** He then switched off the electric field, causing the magnetic field to exert a centripetal force ($F_B = qvB$) that deflected the electrons into a circular path with radius $r$.
3. **Ratio Calculation:** Setting the magnetic force equal to the centripetal force ($qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$), he rearranged the variables to find the charge-to-mass ratio: $\frac{q}{m} = \frac{v}{Br}$. By substituting $v = \frac{E}{B}$, he arrived at $\frac{q}{m} = \frac{E}{B^2r}$.

**Teacher’s Advice:** To secure all 4 marks, you must link the physical "force balancing" concept to the final mathematical derivation. Using equations is essential here.

### b) Rutherford’s Model (5 Marks)
**Answer:**
Rutherford’s nuclear model accounts for these findings by separating the charge carriers spatially:
1. **Thomson’s Result:** Thomson’s experiment proved the existence of subatomic particles (electrons) with a very high charge-to-mass ratio. Rutherford’s model incorporates these as small, light, negatively charged particles orbiting a dense, positively charged nucleus.
2. **Geiger-Marsden (Gold Foil) Result:** The experiment showed that most alpha particles passed through, but some deflected at large angles. Rutherford’s model explains this by proposing that the atom is mostly empty space (explaining the transmission) but contains a very small, dense, positively charged core (the nucleus) that causes the electrostatic repulsion of alpha particles (explaining the large-angle deflections).
3. **Synthesis:** While Thomson’s "Plum Pudding" model failed to account for the alpha scattering, Rutherford provided a structure that positioned the electrons discovered by Thomson outside the nucleus, explaining why electrons could be "emitted" while the bulk of the mass remained in the center.

**Teacher’s Advice:** In a 5-mark question, structure is key. Explicitly state how the model solves the specific "conflict" raised by the two experiments—don't just describe the model; evaluate its success against the data.
