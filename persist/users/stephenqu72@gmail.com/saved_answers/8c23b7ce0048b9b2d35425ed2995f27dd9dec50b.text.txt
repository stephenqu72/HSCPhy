### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Understand the Relationship:**
    The magnitude of the gravitational field $g$ at a distance $r$ from the centre of a planet is given by:
    $$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$
    This means $g$ is inversely proportional to the square of the distance from the centre: $g \propto \frac{1}{r^2}$.

2.  **Analyze the Given Data:**
    The problem states the field is $5\text{ N kg}^{-1}$ at a distance of $2R$ from the **surface**. 
    Assuming the radius of Planet $X$ is $R_{planet} = 1R$ (implied by the visual scale where the planet occupies one unit of $R$), a distance of $2R$ from the *surface* corresponds to a distance of $3R$ from the **centre**.

    $$5 = \frac{k}{(2R)^2} = \frac{k}{4R^2} \implies k = 20R^2$$
