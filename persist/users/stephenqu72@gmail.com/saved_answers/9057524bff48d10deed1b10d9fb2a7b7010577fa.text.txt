To determine where the greatest diffraction effects occur, we must apply the physical principles governing wave diffraction through an aperture.

### Answer
The correct answer is **B**.

### Explanation
Diffraction is the bending of waves around obstacles or through openings. The extent of this "spreading" or diffraction depends on the relationship between the wavelength of the wave ($\lambda$) and the size of the gap ($w$).

1.  **The Principle:** The diffraction effect is most significant when the size of the gap is approximately equal to or smaller than the wavelength of the wave ($w \approx \lambda$ or $w < \lambda$). When the gap is much larger than the wavelength ($w \gg \lambda$), the waves pass through mostly in a straight line with minimal bending at the edges.
2.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   **A:** $\lambda = 1.0\text{ cm}$, $w = 2.0\text{ cm}$. Here, $w > \lambda$.
    *   **B:** $\lambda = 2.0\text{ cm}$, $w = 2.0\text{ cm}$. Here, $w = \lambda$. This satisfies the condition for maximum diffraction, where the wave effectively becomes a circular wave front as it passes through the aperture.
    *   **C:** $\lambda = 1.0\text{ cm}$, $w = 3.0\text{ cm}$. Here, $w > \lambda$ (and the ratio $w/\lambda$ is larger than in option A).
    *   **D:** $\lambda = 2.0\text{ cm}$, $w = 3.0\text{ cm}$. Here, $w > \lambda$.

In **Option B**, the gap size is equal to the wavelength. This is the optimal condition for the waves to spread out significantly into a semi-circular pattern, resulting in the greatest diffraction effect among the choices provided.
