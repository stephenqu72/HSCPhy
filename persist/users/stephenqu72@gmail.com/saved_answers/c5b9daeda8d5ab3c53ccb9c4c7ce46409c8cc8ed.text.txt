To solve this problem, we must analyze the physics of projectile motion, specifically focusing on the independent horizontal and vertical components of the marble's motion.

### Explanation

1.  **Vertical Motion (Time of Flight):**
    The marble is projected horizontally from a bench of height $h$. The vertical motion is governed by gravity ($g$) and is independent of the initial horizontal velocity. The equation for the vertical displacement is:
    $$h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$
    Solving for the time of flight ($t$):
    $$t = \sqrt{\frac{2h}{g}}$$
    Since $h$ (height of the bench) and $g$ (acceleration due to gravity) remain constant, the **time of flight is independent of the initial horizontal speed**. Therefore, options C and D are incorrect.

2.  **Horizontal Motion (Range):**
    The range ($R$) is the horizontal distance traveled while the marble is in the air. Since there is no horizontal acceleration (ignoring air resistance), the horizontal velocity ($v_x$) remains constant. The range is given by:
    $$R = v_x \times t$$
    If we roll the marble at a **slower speed**, the value of $v_x$ decreases. Since $t$ remains constant, a smaller $v_x$ will result in a smaller product ($R$). Thus, the range will decrease.

### Conclusion
Rolling the marble at a slower speed results in a smaller horizontal distance covered during the same time of flight. Therefore, it decreases the range.
