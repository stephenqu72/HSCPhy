To determine which pair of conductors has the weakest force acting between them, we use the formula for the force per unit length between two parallel current-carrying wires:

$$F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 L}{2 \pi r}$$

Where $I_1$ and $I_2$ are the currents, $L$ is the length of the wires, and $r$ is the separation distance. Since we are comparing the total force, we can ignore the constants $\frac{\mu_0}{2 \pi}$ and focus on the proportionality:

$$F \propto \frac{I_1 I_2 L}{r}$$

Let's calculate the relative strength for each option:

*   **(A)** $I_1 = I$, $I_2 = 2I$, $L = l$, $r = r$
    $$F_A \propto \frac{(I)(2I)(l)}{r} = 2 \left( \frac{I^2 l}{r} \right)$$
*   **(B)** $I_1 = I$, $I_2 = I$, $L = l$, $r = 2r$
    $$F_B \propto \frac{(I)(I)(l)}{2r} = 0.5 \left( \frac{I^2 l}{r} \right)$$
*   **(C)** $I_1 = I$, $I_2 = I$, $L = 2l$, $r = 2r$
    $$F_C \propto \frac{(I)(I)(2l)}{2r} = 1 \left( \frac{I^2 l}{r} \right)$$
*   **(D)** $I_1 = 2I$, $I_2 = 2I$, $L = l$, $r = 2r$
    $$F_D \propto \frac{(2I)(2I)(l)}{2r} = 2 \left( \frac{I^2 l}{r} \right)$$

Comparing the coefficients:
*   (A) $= 2$
*   (B) $= 0.5$
*   (C) $= 1$
*   (D) $= 2$

The smallest value is $0.5$, which corresponds to option (B).

**Answer:** (B)
