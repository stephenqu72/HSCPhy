To solve this problem, we need to apply the relationships between angular motion and linear motion for a rotating rigid body.

### Analysis

1.  **Linear Speed ($v$):**
    For a rigid body rotating with a constant angular speed $\omega$, the linear speed $v$ of a point at a radial distance $r$ from the axis of rotation is given by:
    $$v = r\omega$$
    *   For point P, located at distance $r_P = \frac{r}{2}$:
        $$v_P = \left(\frac{r}{2}\right)\omega = \frac{1}{2}r\omega$$
    *   For point Q, located at distance $r_Q = r$:
        $$v_Q = r\omega$$
    Comparing the two, we clearly see that $v_P = \frac{1}{2}v_Q$, therefore **$v_P < v_Q$**.

2.  **Acceleration ($a$):**
    In uniform circular motion, the acceleration is the centripetal acceleration, defined by the formula:
    $$a = r\omega^2$$
    *   For point P:
        $$a_P = \left(\frac{r}{2}\right)\omega^2 = \frac{1}{2}r\omega^2$$
    *   For point Q:
        $$a_Q = r\omega^2$$
    Comparing the two, we see that $a_P = \frac{1}{2}a_Q$.

### Conclusion
Based on the derivations above, we have $v_P < v_Q$ and $a_P = \frac{1}{2}a_Q$. Looking at the provided options, this corresponds to **Option B**.
