To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of Special Relativity, specifically time dilation and relativistic energy.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Identify the Lorentz Factor ($\gamma$)**
The problem states that the clocks on the spaceship run eight times slower than the clocks on Earth. This refers to time dilation, where the observed time interval $t$ is related to the proper time interval $t_0$ by the Lorentz factor $\gamma$:
$$t = \gamma t_0$$
Since the spaceship's clocks run 8 times slower, $\gamma = 8$.

**2. Relate $\gamma$ to the Velocity ($v$)**
The Lorentz factor is defined as:
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$
Given $\gamma = 8$, we can find the value of $\frac{v^2}{c^2}$:
$$8 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}} \implies \frac{1}{64} = 1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$$
$$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 1 - \frac{1}{64} = \frac{63}{64}$$

**3. Calculate Kinetic Energy ($E_k$)**
The relativistic kinetic energy is given by the difference between the total energy and the rest energy:
$$E_k = E_{total} - E_{rest} = \gamma mc^2 - mc^2 = (\gamma - 1)mc^2$$
We are given:
- $m = 10,000\text{ kg}$
- $\gamma = 8$
- $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$

Substitute the values:
$$E_k = (8 - 1) \times 10,000 \times (3.00 \times 10^8)^2$$
$$E_k = 7 \times 10,000 \times (9.00 \times 10^{16})$$
$$E_k = 7 \times 10^4 \times 9.00 \times 10^{16}$$
$$E_k = 6.3 \times 10^{21}\text{ Joules}$$

**Answer:**
The kinetic energy of the spaceship is $6.3 \times 10^{21}\text{ J}$.
