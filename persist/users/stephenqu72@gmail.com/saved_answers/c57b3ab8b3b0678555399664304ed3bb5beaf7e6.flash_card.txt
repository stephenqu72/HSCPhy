### Front
Define and differentiate Hadrons, Baryons, and Leptons.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Hadrons are composite particles (quarks) affected by the strong nuclear force. Baryons are Hadrons with 3 quarks. Leptons are fundamental particles (no quarks) unaffected by the strong nuclear force.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When classifying subatomic particles; e.g., identify that a neutron ($udd$) is a Baryon/Hadron, whereas an electron is a Lepton.
- **Common trap:** Confusing Mesons (quark-antiquark pair) with Baryons; both are Hadrons, but only Baryons have three quarks.
