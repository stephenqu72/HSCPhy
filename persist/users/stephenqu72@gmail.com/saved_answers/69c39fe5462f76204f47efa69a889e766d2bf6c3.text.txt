To determine the correct statement, we must apply the magnetic force formula for a current-carrying wire:
$$F = BIL \sin(\theta)$$
where $B$ is the magnetic field strength, $I$ is the current, $L$ is the length of the conductor, and $\theta$ is the angle between the conductor and the magnetic field lines.

### Analysis of Rod $P$ (Diagram $X$):
*   The magnetic field $B$ is directed out of the page (perpendicular to the horizontal plane of rotation).
*   The current $I$ flows along the rod.
*   Because the rod is rotating in a horizontal plane and the field is vertical, the rod is **always at $90^\circ$** to the magnetic field lines regardless of its position.
*   Since $\theta = 90^\circ$, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$. The force magnitude is $F = BIL$.
*   Since $B, I,$ and $L$ are constant, the **magnitude of the force on $P$ is constant.**

### Analysis of Rod $Q$ (Diagram $Y$):
*   The magnetic field $B$ is uniform and oriented vertically in the plane.
*   The rod rotates, meaning its orientation relative to the field lines changes continuously.
*   Let $\phi$ be the angle between the rod and the magnetic field lines. As the rod rotates, the angle $\phi$ changes over time.
*   The force magnitude is given by $F = BIL \sin(\phi)$.
*   Because $\sin(\phi)$ changes as the rod sweeps through the circle, the **magnitude of the force on $Q$ varies continually.**

### Conclusion:
Comparing our findings, the magnitude of the force on $P$ is constant, and the magnitude of the force on $Q$ varies continually as the rod's angle relative to the field lines changes.

The correct statement is **B**.
