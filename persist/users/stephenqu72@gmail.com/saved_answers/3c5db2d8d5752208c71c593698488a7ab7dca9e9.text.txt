To find the frequency of the gamma radiation, we use the relationship between photon energy ($E$) and frequency ($f$):

$$E = hf$$

where:
- $E$ is the energy in Joules ($J$)
- $h$ is Planck's constant ($6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$)
- $f$ is the frequency in Hertz ($Hz$)

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Convert the energy from MeV to Joules:**
The given energy is $1.33 \text{ MeV}$. 
Since $1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$, and $1 \text{ MeV} = 10^6 \text{ eV}$:
$$E = 1.33 \times 10^6 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$
$$E \approx 2.13 \times 10^{-13} \text{ J}$$

**2. Rearrange the formula to solve for frequency ($f$):**
$$f = \frac{E}{h}$$

**3. Substitute the values:**
$$f = \frac{2.13 \times 10^{-13} \text{ J}}{6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}}$$
$$f \approx 3.21 \times 10^{20} \text{ Hz}$$
