### Part (a)
**Answer:**
Maxwell’s equations predicted that light travels at a constant speed $c = 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$. In the 19th century, physicists believed that waves required a medium to propagate (like sound in air). They proposed the existence of the "luminiferous aether," a stationary medium that permeated the universe. The challenge arose because, according to Galilean relativity, if you moved through the aether, you should observe light moving at different speeds depending on your velocity. However, experiments (like the Michelson-Morley experiment) failed to detect any relative motion of the Earth through the aether.

Einstein’s Theory of Special Relativity resolved this by discarding the aether entirely. He postulated that the speed of light in a vacuum is invariant (constant) for all inertial observers, regardless of their motion or the motion of the source. This meant that the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames, rendering the concept of a stationary "aether" unnecessary and resolving the contradiction between Maxwell’s equations and classical mechanics.

**How to solve it:**
1.  **Identify the conflict:** Focus on the clash between Maxwell’s constant $c$ and the "Galilean transformation" (which assumes speeds add up, like $v_{total} = v_{obj} + v_{observer}$).
2.  **Define the aether:** Briefly explain that physicists thought light needed a medium.
3.  **Explain the resolution:** Explicitly state Einstein's **Second Postulate of Special Relativity**: the speed of light is constant for all observers. Mention that this removed the need for the aether.

### Part (b)
**Answer:**
A primary piece of experimental evidence is the observation of time dilation using muons. Muons are unstable subatomic particles with a very short half-life. They are created in the upper atmosphere by cosmic rays and, due to their high velocity (near $c$), they reach the Earth’s surface in numbers much higher than classical physics would predict. According to our frame, their internal "clocks" run slower, allowing them to travel further before decaying. Another example is the Michelson-Morley experiment, which proved that the speed of light is independent of the Earth's motion through space.

**How to solve it:**
1.  **Select a concrete example:** Muon decay or the Michelson-Morley experiment are the standard HSC syllabus examples.
2.  **Link to theory:** Ensure you explain *why* it supports the theory. For muons: mention high speed + time dilation = increased lifespan relative to an Earth observer.
