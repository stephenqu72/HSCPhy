To determine the correct answer, let’s analyze the physics principles behind the observation of stellar spectra:

### Answer
The correct option is **(C) Doppler effect**.

### Explanation
1.  **Analyze the Data:** If you look closely at the absorption spectra provided, you will notice that the positions of the dark lines (absorption lines) shift towards the longer wavelengths (the red end of the spectrum, towards 800 nm) as time progresses from $t_1$ to $t_3$.
2.  **Identify the Phenomenon:** The shift of spectral lines towards longer wavelengths is known as a **redshift**. Conversely, a shift towards shorter wavelengths is known as a **blueshift**.
3.  **Apply the Physics Principle:** The **Doppler effect** describes the change in frequency or wavelength of a wave in relation to an observer who is moving relative to the wave source. For light, when a source (like a star) moves away from an observer, the light waves it emits are "stretched," resulting in a longer observed wavelength (redshift).
4.  **Evaluate Other Options:**
    *   **Malus' law:** Relates to the intensity of polarized light passing through a polarizer.
    *   **Wien's law:** Relates the temperature of a blackbody to the wavelength at which it emits the most radiation ($\lambda_{max} T = \text{constant}$). It does not explain the shifting of fixed spectral lines due to motion.
    *   **Photoelectric effect:** Relates to the emission of electrons when light hits a material.

Since the observation involves the shifting of wavelengths due to the relative motion between the star and the Earth, the Doppler effect is the correct physical principle.
