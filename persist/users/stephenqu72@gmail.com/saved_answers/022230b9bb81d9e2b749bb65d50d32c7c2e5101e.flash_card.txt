### Front
How does the motion of a medium (e.g., wind) affect the velocity of sound, and what does this imply about the wave's frame of reference?

### Back
- Key idea: The medium's velocity is added vectorially to the speed of sound; sound is not invariant to the motion of its medium.
- Formula / law / constant: $v_{effective} = v_s \pm v_{medium}$
- When to use it: When calculating relative velocities for waves that require a mechanical medium (unlike light).
- Common trap: Assuming the speed of the wave remains constant regardless of the observer or medium motion (the Michelson-Morley error).
