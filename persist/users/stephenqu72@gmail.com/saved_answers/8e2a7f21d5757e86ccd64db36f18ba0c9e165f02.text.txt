### Answer
The correct statement is **A**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must apply Newton's Laws of Motion and his Law of Universal Gravitation.

1.  **Newton’s Third Law:** This law states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. Therefore, the gravitational force exerted by the Earth on the Moon is exactly equal in magnitude and opposite in direction to the gravitational force exerted by the Moon on the Earth. This immediately makes option **D incorrect**, as the forces are equal.
2.  **Newton’s Second Law ($F = ma$):** While the forces are equal, the masses of the two objects are very different ($m_{Earth} \gg m_{Moon}$). 
    *   Since $F = ma$, the acceleration of an object is defined by $a = \frac{F}{m}$.
    *   Because the Earth has a much larger mass, its acceleration is extremely small, but it is not zero. Because the Moon has a smaller mass, its acceleration towards the Earth is significantly larger. 
3.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   **A is correct:** Because the Earth experiences a non-zero gravitational force from the Moon, it must undergo an acceleration towards the Moon, as per $F = ma$. The fact that this acceleration is tiny does not make the statement false.
    *   **B is incorrect:** The Earth is constantly acted upon by the Moon’s gravity, so the net force is not zero.
    *   **C is incorrect:** While the forces are equal and opposite, the accelerations are inversely proportional to the masses ($a \propto \frac{1}{m}$). Since their masses are not equal, their accelerations cannot be equal.
    *   **D is incorrect:** As per Newton's Third Law, the gravitational forces between the two bodies are equal in magnitude.
