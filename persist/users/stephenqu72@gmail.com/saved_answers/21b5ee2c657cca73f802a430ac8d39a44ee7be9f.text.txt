To analyse the motion of particles $X$ and $Y$ in a uniform electric field, we must apply the principles of electrostatics and classical mechanics.

### Answer
1.  **Particle $X$:** Particle $X$ travels in a straight line, indicating that the net force acting on it is zero. Since it is within an electric field, this implies particle $X$ is **neutral** (has no charge).
2.  **Particle $Y$:** Particle $Y$ experiences a downward deflection (parabolic motion). The electric field $E$ points from the positive top plate to the grounded (negative) bottom plate. Since the particle is deflected towards the negative plate, particle $Y$ must be **positively charged**.
3.  **Comparison:** Particle $X$ is neutral and unaffected by the electric field, whereas particle $Y$ is charged and experiences an electrostatic force $F = qE$.

### How to solve this question
To score full marks on this type of HSC physics problem, structure your response as follows:

*   **Identify the direction of the field:** The top plate is positive ($+V$) and the bottom plate is grounded ($0V$). Therefore, the electric field lines point vertically downwards.
*   **Analyse Particle $X$:** State that because $X$ undergoes no change in velocity or direction, the electric force acting on it must be zero. Conclude that $X$ has a charge of $q = 0$ (it is a neutral particle, such as a neutron).
*   **Analyse Particle $Y$:** Observe that $Y$ follows a parabolic trajectory bending towards the bottom (negative) plate. By the laws of electrostatics, like charges repel and opposite charges attract. Since the particle is attracted to the negative plate, $Y$ must be positively charged.
*   **Apply Physics Principles:** Reference the relevant physics relationship, $F = qE$, where $F$ is the electric force, $q$ is the charge, and $E$ is the electric field strength. Mention that the vertical acceleration $a = \frac{qE}{m}$ causes the parabolic path, assuming the particle has mass $m$.
