### Answer

**1. Chadwick’s Discovery of the Neutron**
Chadwick’s experiment (bombarding beryllium with alpha particles) solved the "missing mass" problem in the nucleus. By observing the ejection of protons from paraffin wax, he identified a neutral particle with mass similar to the proton: the neutron.
*Calculation:* To show the neutral nature, we note the conservation of energy and momentum. If the unknown radiation was a $\gamma$-ray (as initially thought), the mass would be insufficient to knock out a proton. Chadwick’s calculation of energy conservation proved the particle had mass: 
$E = mc^2$. Given the kinetic energy of the incident particles and the resulting protons, the neutral particle's mass was calculated to be $\approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$.

**2. Millikan’s Oil Drop Experiment**
Millikan determined the elementary charge $q$ of the electron. By balancing the electric force $F_E = qE = q(\frac{V}{d})$ with gravity $F_g = mg$, he proved that charge is quantized.
*Calculation:*
$qE = mg \implies q(\frac{V}{d}) = mg$
$q = \frac{mgd}{V} = \frac{(1.47 \times 10^{-12} \text{ kg}) \times (9.8 \text{ m/s}^2) \times (0.006 \text{ m})}{10,000 \text{ V}}$
$q = 8.64 \times 10^{-18} \text{ Coulombs}$
(This value is a multiple of the elementary charge $e \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$, confirming quantization).

**3. Cosmic-Origin Muons and Special Relativity**
Muons are unstable and should decay before reaching the surface. Their detection at sea level provides experimental evidence for **time dilation** from Einstein’s Theory of Special Relativity.
*Calculation:*
The Lorentz factor $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.995^2}} \approx 10$.
The dilated half-life $t_{observed} = \gamma t_{proper} = 10 \times 2.2 \text{ }\mu\text{s} = 22 \text{ }\mu\text{s}$.
This allows the muon to travel a distance $d = v \times t \approx c \times 22 \times 10^{-6} \text{ s} \approx 6600 \text{ m}$, explaining why they are detected at the surface despite the 2000m mountain height.
