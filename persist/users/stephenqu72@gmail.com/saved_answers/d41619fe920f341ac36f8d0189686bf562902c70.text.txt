To solve this problem, we use the formula for the magnetic force on a current-carrying conductor:

$$F = BIl \sin(\theta)$$

### Step 1: Identify the given values
*   Magnetic field strength, $B = 1.0\text{ T}$
*   Current, $I = 2\text{ A}$
*   Length of the conductor, $l = 5.0\text{ cm} = 0.05\text{ m}$ (remember to convert to SI units)
*   Angle, $\theta = 60^\circ$

### Step 2: Calculate the magnitude of the force
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$F = 1.0 \times 2 \times 0.05 \times \sin(60^\circ)$$
$$F = 0.1 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$$
$$F \approx 0.1 \times 0.866$$
$$F \approx 0.0866\text{ N}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $0.087\text{ N}$.

### Step 3: Determine the direction
Using the Right-Hand Palm Rule:
1.  Point your **thumb** in the direction of the conventional current ($+$ to $-$), which is down and to the right.
2.  Point your **fingers** in the direction of the magnetic field ($N$ to $S$), which is to the right.
3.  Your **palm** (or the resulting force vector) will face **into the page**.

Matching this with the options, the correct answer is **A**.
