### Front
How do you determine the radius of a planet if you know its mass and gravitational acceleration ($g$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational acceleration $g$ is proportional to $M/r^2$. Use a ratio comparison between the target planet and Earth.
- **Formula:** $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- **When to use it:** When comparing gravitational field strengths between two celestial bodies with different masses and radii.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to square the radius ratio or forgetting to take the square root when solving for $r$.
