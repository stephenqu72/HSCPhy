### Front
Why do two objects of different masses fall at the same rate in a vacuum?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration due to gravity is independent of an object's mass.
- Formula / law / constant: Newton’s Second Law ($F=ma$); $g \approx 9.8\ m/s^2$.
- When to use it: Any kinematics problem involving objects in free fall where air resistance is negligible.
- Common trap: Assuming a greater gravitational force on a larger mass results in a faster acceleration; mass increases both the force and the inertia (resistance to acceleration) proportionally.
