### Front
What defines uniform circular motion, and what is the primary requirement for an object to maintain it?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Motion at a constant speed in a circular path where a net force (centripetal force) acts perpendicular to the velocity, directed toward the center.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- **When to use it:** When analyzing objects in stable circular orbits or horizontal swings where speed is constant.
- **Common trap:** Confusing circular motion with projectile motion (parabolic paths) or assuming velocity is constant (velocity changes because direction changes).
