To solve this problem, we use the formula for the force per unit length ($F/L$) between two parallel current-carrying wires:

$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$

In this arrangement, all currents are magnitude $I$, and the distance between wires is $a$. Therefore, the magnitude of the force exerted by wire B on wire A, and by wire C on wire A, is the same:
$$F_{BA} = F_{CA} = \frac{\mu_0 I^2}{2\pi a}$$

**Step 1: Determine the direction of the forces on A**
*   Wire A carries current *into* the page ($\otimes$).
*   Wire B carries current *out of* the page ($\odot$).
*   Since the currents are in opposite directions, the force between A and B is **repulsive**. Thus, wire B pushes wire A away along the line connecting them (up and to the right).
*   Similarly, the current in wire C is opposite to wire A, so wire C exerts a **repulsive** force on wire A (up and to the left).

**Step 2: Vector addition**
We have two equal force vectors, each of magnitude $F_0 = \frac{\mu_0 I^2}{2\pi a}$, acting on wire A. The angle between the line connecting B-A and C-A is $60^\circ$ (equilateral triangle). By geometry, the horizontal components of these two forces are equal and opposite, so they cancel out. The vertical components both point upwards and add together.

The angle each force vector makes with the vertical is $30^\circ$. The net vertical force is:
$$F_{net} = F_{BA} \cos(30^\circ) + F_{CA} \cos(30^\circ)$$
$$F_{net} = 2 \left( \frac{\mu_0 I^2}{2\pi a} \right) \cos(30^\circ)$$

Since $\cos(30^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$:
$$F_{net} = 2 \left( \frac{\mu_0 I^2}{2\pi a} \right) \left( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \right) = \frac{\mu_0 \sqrt{3} I^2}{2\pi a}$$

The direction of the net force is **upwards**.

Comparing this to the given options, **Option B** is the correct answer.
