To determine the direction of the magnetic field at point $P$, we must analyze the motion of the charges and the resulting magnetic field they produce.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) Out of the page**.

### Explanation
1.  **Identify the Current:** In the vacuum tube, electrons are flowing from the negative cathode to the positive anode. This means the electrons are moving from left to right.
2.  **Define Conventional Current:** By physics convention, the direction of "conventional current" ($I$) is defined as the direction in which positive charges would flow. Since electrons move to the right, the conventional current is effectively flowing from the anode to the cathode, or from **right to left**.
3.  **Apply the Right-Hand Grip Rule:** To find the direction of the magnetic field generated by a current-carrying path, we use the Right-Hand Grip Rule:
    *   Point your right thumb in the direction of the conventional current (to the left).
    *   Your fingers curl around the wire in the direction of the magnetic field lines.
    *   Since point $P$ is located *below* the tube, imagine your hand gripping the wire. As your fingers wrap around, they will be pointing **out of the page** at the region below the wire.

Alternatively, you can visualize the magnetic field lines as concentric circles around the wire. For a current flowing to the left, the magnetic field loops come out of the page below the wire and go into the page above the wire. Therefore, at point $P$, the field is directed out of the page.
