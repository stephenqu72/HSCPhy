To find the peak wavelength of emitted radiation from a person, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**. 

### Answer
The peak wavelength is approximately **$9.35 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$** (or $9.35 \text{ } \mu\text{m}$).

### Explanation
To solve this, follow these steps:

**1. Convert temperature to Kelvin:**
Physics laws involving blackbody radiation, such as Wien’s Displacement Law, require temperature to be in absolute units (Kelvin).
$$T = 37^\circ\text{C} + 273.15 = 310.15 \text{ K}$$

**2. Apply Wien’s Displacement Law:**
The formula is given by:
$$\lambda_{\text{max}} = \frac{b}{T}$$
Where:
- $\lambda_{\text{max}}$ is the peak wavelength in meters (m).
- $b$ is Wien's displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$.
- $T$ is the absolute temperature in Kelvin (K).

**3. Calculate:**
$$\lambda_{\text{max}} = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}}{310.15 \text{ K}}$$
$$\lambda_{\text{max}} \approx 9.3438... \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$$

Rounding to appropriate significant figures based on the input, we get:
$$\lambda_{\text{max}} \approx 9.35 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$$

This result places the radiation in the **far-infrared** region of the electromagnetic spectrum, which is why specialized infrared (thermal) cameras are required to "see" a person at night.
