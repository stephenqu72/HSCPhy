### Front
How does acceleration change during the flight of a projectile (ignoring air resistance)?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration is constant in both magnitude and direction throughout the entire trajectory.
- Formula / law / constant: $a = g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ (downwards).
- When to use it: To simplify projectile motion problems into independent horizontal (constant velocity) and vertical (constant acceleration) components.
- Common trap: Assuming vertical acceleration is zero at the peak of the trajectory; it remains $9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ downwards at all times.
