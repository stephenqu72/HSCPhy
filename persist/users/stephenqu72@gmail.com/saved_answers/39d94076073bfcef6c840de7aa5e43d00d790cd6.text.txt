To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of circular motion and the conservation of mechanical energy.

### Step 1: Determine the required velocity at the top of the loop
At the top of the loop, the forces acting on the passenger are the normal force ($N$) and gravity ($mg$), both acting downwards towards the center of the circular path. The net force provides the centripetal acceleration:
$$N + mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
The problem states that the passengers should "feel weightless," which occurs when the normal force $N = 0$. In this state, gravity alone provides the centripetal acceleration:
$$mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
$$g = \frac{v^2}{r} \implies v^2 = gr$$
Given the radius $r = 12\text{ m}$, the square of the velocity at the top of the loop is:
$$v^2 = 12g$$

### Step 2: Use the Conservation of Mechanical Energy
The roller coaster starts from rest ($u = 0$) at an initial height $H$. At the top of the loop, the height is $h = 2r$ (the diameter of the loop), and the velocity is $v$.
The total mechanical energy at the start equals the total mechanical energy at the top of the loop:
$$mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + mg(2r)$$
Wait—let us define $d$ as the distance from the **start** to the **top of the loop**. Thus, the starting height relative to the ground is $H = d + 2r$.
$$mg(d + 2r) = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + mg(2r)$$
Subtract $mg(2r)$ from both sides:
$$mgd = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
$$gd = \frac{1}{2}v^2$$

### Step 3: Solve for $d$
Substitute the expression $v^2 = gr$ into the energy equation:
$$gd = \frac{1}{2}(gr)$$
$$d = \frac{1}{2}r$$
Given $r = 12\text{ m}$:
$$d = \frac{1}{2} \times 12\text{ m} = 6.0\text{ m}$$
