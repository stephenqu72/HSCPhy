### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) High pressure and temperature.**

### Explanation
To understand why (D) is the correct answer, we must consider the physics of atomic nuclei. 

1.  **The Coulomb Barrier:** Atomic nuclei are positively charged. According to the laws of electrostatics, like charges repel each other. This repulsive force is known as the Coulomb barrier. For two nuclei to fuse, they must get close enough for the strong nuclear force to overcome this repulsion.
2.  **The Role of Temperature:** Temperature is a measure of the average kinetic energy of particles. To overcome the Coulomb barrier, the nuclei must be moving at extremely high speeds. High temperatures in a star's core provide the particles with the necessary kinetic energy to approach each other closely enough to fuse.
3.  **The Role of Pressure:** Even with high kinetic energy, the probability of fusion is low unless the density of particles is high. High pressure (resulting from the immense gravitational collapse of the star) compresses the core, bringing nuclei into closer proximity and increasing the collision frequency. 

**Why other options are incorrect:**
*   **(A)** Low pressure would mean particles are too far apart to collide frequently, and there would not be enough heat to overcome the barrier.
*   **(B)** While hydrogen and helium are the fuel, simply having them present does not guarantee fusion. Fusion requires the extreme environment of a star's core.
*   **(C)** Heavy elements are typically the products of fusion (nucleosynthesis), not the primary requirement for initiating the sustained fusion process in main-sequence stars.
