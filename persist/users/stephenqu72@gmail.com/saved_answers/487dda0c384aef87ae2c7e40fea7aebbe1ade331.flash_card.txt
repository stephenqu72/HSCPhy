### Front
How do the magnitudes of the gravitational forces exerted by two objects on each other compare?

### Back
- Key idea: Newton’s Third Law (interaction forces are equal in magnitude and opposite in direction).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_g = \frac{G M_1 M_2}{r^2}$ (Newton's 3rd Law)
- When to use it: Comparing the interaction forces between two bodies of different masses.
- Common trap: Assuming a larger mass exerts a greater force; while the **force** is equal, the **acceleration** ($a=F/m$) will be much smaller for the larger mass.
