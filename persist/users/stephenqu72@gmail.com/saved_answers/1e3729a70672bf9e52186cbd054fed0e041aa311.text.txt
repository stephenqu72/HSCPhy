To solve this problem, we use the universal law of gravitation, which states that the acceleration due to gravity $g$ at a distance $r$ from the center of the Earth is given by:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

### Step 1: Identify constants
- Radius of the Earth ($R_E$) $\approx 6371 \text{ km}$
- Altitude ($h$) $= 300 \text{ km}$
- Distance from center at surface ($r_1$) $= R_E$
- Distance from center at altitude ($r_2$) $= R_E + h = 6371 + 300 = 6671 \text{ km}$

### Step 2: Set up the ratio
The acceleration at the surface is $g_1 = \frac{GM}{R_E^2}$.
The acceleration at altitude is $g_2 = \frac{GM}{(R_E + h)^2}$.

The ratio of the acceleration at altitude to the surface is:
$$\frac{g_2}{g_1} = \frac{R_E^2}{(R_E + h)^2} = \left( \frac{6371}{6671} \right)^2$$

### Step 3: Calculation
$$\frac{g_2}{g_1} \approx (0.95503)^2 \approx 0.91208$$

This means that $g_2$ is approximately $91.21\%$ of $g_1$.

### Step 4: Determine the percentage decrease
The question asks by what percentage the acceleration is **less than** that on the surface.
$$\text{Percentage decrease} = \left(1 - \frac{g_2}{g_1}\right) \times 100\%$$
$$\text{Percentage decrease} = (1 - 0.91208) \times 100\% = 0.08792 \times 100\% \approx 8.79\%$$
