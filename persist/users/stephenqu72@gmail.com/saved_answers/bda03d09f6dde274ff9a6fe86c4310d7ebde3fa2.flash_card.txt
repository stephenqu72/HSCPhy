### Front
How does doubling the radius of a spherical body affect its surface gravitational field strength ($g$), assuming mass remains constant?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational field strength follows an inverse-square relationship with distance ($g \propto 1/r^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- When to use it: Any problem involving changes to a planet's or star's radius or mass.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the scaling factor (e.g., doubling the radius results in $1/2^2 = 1/4$ the field strength, not $1/2$).
