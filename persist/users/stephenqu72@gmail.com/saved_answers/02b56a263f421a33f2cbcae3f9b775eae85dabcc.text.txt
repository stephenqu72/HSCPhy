This problem requires an understanding of Hooke’s Law ($F = k\Delta x$), mechanical energy in springs ($PE = \frac{1}{2}k\Delta x^2$), and data analysis.

### Part a: Graphing
To solve this, calculate $F = mg$ for each data point (ensure $m$ is in kg, e.g., $300\text{ g} = 0.3\text{ kg} \rightarrow F = 3\text{ N}$).
*   **The trend:** The graph will be a straight line until $\Delta x = 60\text{ mm}$ (Spring A only). After $60\text{ mm}$, Spring B engages, causing the gradient to increase (it becomes steeper).
*   **Uncertainty:** Add bars of total length $4\text{ mm}$ (centered at the point) for all horizontal $\Delta x$ values.

### Part b: Spring Constants
Convert all units: $\Delta x$ to meters, $F$ to Newtons.
**i. Spring A:** Use the linear portion before $60\text{ mm}$.
At $\Delta x = 60\text{ mm} = 0.06\text{ m}$, $m = 900\text{ g} = 0.9\text{ kg} \rightarrow F = 9\text{ N}$.
$$k_A = \frac{F}{\Delta x} = \frac{9}{0.06} = 150\text{ N m}^{-1}$$

**ii. Spring B:** At $80\text{ mm}$, the total force is $19\text{ N}$. Spring A has compressed $80\text{ mm}$, so its force is $F_A = 150 \times 0.08 = 12\text{ N}$.
The force provided by Spring B is $F_B = F_{total} - F_A = 19 - 12 = 7\text{ N}$.
Spring B only compressed $20\text{ mm}$ ($0.02\text{ m}$).
$$k_B = \frac{F_B}{\Delta x_B} = \frac{7}{0.02} = 350\text{ N m}^{-1}$$

### Part c: Potential Energy and Work
**i. $PE_A$ at $80\text{ mm}$:**
$$PE_A = \frac{1}{2}k_A(\Delta x)^2 = 0.5 \times 150 \times (0.08)^2 = 0.48\text{ J}$$

**ii. $PE_{A+B}$ at $80\text{ mm}$:**
Area under the graph or sum of energies:
$$PE_B = \frac{1}{2}k_B(\Delta x_B)^2 = 0.5 \times 350 \times (0.02)^2 = 0.07\text{ J}$$
$$PE_{total} = 0.48 + 0.07 = 0.55\text{ J}$$

**iii. Work done on Spring B:**
This is equal to the potential energy stored in Spring B: $0.07\text{ J}$.

### Part d: Application
This is a **progressive rate suspension system**.
1.  **Small bumps:** Only the softer spring (Spring A) compresses, providing a comfortable, compliant ride.
2.  **Severe bumps:** Once the platform travels $60\text{ mm}$, the stiffer spring (Spring B) engages. This increases the total stiffness, preventing the suspension from "bottoming out" and keeping the car stable under high loads or heavy impacts.
