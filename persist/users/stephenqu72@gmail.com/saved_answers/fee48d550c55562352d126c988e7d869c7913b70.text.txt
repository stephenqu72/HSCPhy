To calculate the work done on a charged particle accelerating through a potential difference, we use the relationship between work ($W$), charge ($q$), and voltage ($V$):

$$W = qV$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   The particle is a proton, which has an elementary charge of $q = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C}$.
    *   The potential difference (voltage) is $V = 1 \, \text{kV} = 1000 \, \text{V} = 1.0 \times 10^{3} \, \text{V}$.

2.  **Substitute the values into the formula:**
    $$W = (1.6 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C}) \times (1.0 \times 10^{3} \, \text{V})$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$W = 1.6 \times 10^{-19 + 3} \, \text{J}$$
    $$W = 1.6 \times 10^{-16} \, \text{J}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches option (B).

**Answer:** (B) $1.6 \times 10^{-16} \, \text{J}$
