To find the radius of the circular path of an electron in a magnetic field, we equate the magnetic force to the centripetal force.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
When a charged particle moves perpendicularly into a uniform magnetic field, the magnetic force acts as the centripetal force, causing the particle to move in a circle.
*   **Magnetic Force ($F_B$):** $F_B = qvB$
*   **Centripetal Force ($F_c$):** $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$

Equating these:
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Rearranging to solve for the radius ($r$):
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

### 2. Identify the Variables
*   Mass of an electron ($m$): $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
*   Charge of an electron ($q$): $1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
*   Velocity ($v$): $6.5 \times 10^3 \text{ m/s}$
*   Magnetic Field ($B$): $2.4 \times 10^{-3} \text{ T}$

### 3. Calculation
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}) \times (6.5 \times 10^3 \text{ m/s})}{(1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \times (2.4 \times 10^{-3} \text{ T})}$$

First, calculate the numerator:
$$(9.11 \times 6.5) \times 10^{-31+3} = 59.215 \times 10^{-28} = 5.9215 \times 10^{-27}$$

Next, calculate the denominator:
$$(1.60 \times 2.4) \times 10^{-19-3} = 3.84 \times 10^{-22}$$

Finally, divide the results:
$$r = \frac{5.9215 \times 10^{-27}}{3.84 \times 10^{-22}} \approx 1.54 \times 10^{-5} \text{ m}$$
