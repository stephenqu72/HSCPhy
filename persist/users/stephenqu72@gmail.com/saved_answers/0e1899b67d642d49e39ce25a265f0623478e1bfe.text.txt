To solve this problem, we use the relationship between energy ($E$), power ($P$), and time ($t$):

$$E = P \times t$$

### Step-by-step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   Power ($P$) = $2500\text{ W}$
    *   Energy ($E$) = $9\text{ MJ} = 9 \times 10^6\text{ J}$

2.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for time ($t$):**
    $$t = \frac{E}{P}$$

3.  **Substitute the values into the equation:**
    $$t = \frac{9,000,000\text{ J}}{2500\text{ W}}$$
    $$t = 3600\text{ seconds}$$

4.  **Convert seconds to hours:**
    Since there are $3600$ seconds in one hour ($60\text{ minutes} \times 60\text{ seconds}$):
    $$t = \frac{3600\text{ seconds}}{3600\text{ seconds/hour}} = 1\text{ hour}$$

**Answer:** The correct option is **(A)**.
