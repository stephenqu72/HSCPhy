### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Identify the component:** Recognize the standard components of a mass spectrometer. Parallel plates are the hallmark of an electrostatic accelerator (Electric Field). The semi-circular trajectory in a large chamber is the hallmark of magnetic deflection (Magnetic Field).
2.  **Apply Physics Principles:** Recall that electric fields change the kinetic energy/speed of a charge ($W = qV = \Delta K$), while magnetic fields change the direction of a moving charge without doing work on it ($F = qvB\sin\theta$).
3.  **Visual Evidence:** Look for visual cues, such as parallel plate symbols (indicating electric fields) and the characteristic curved path in a vacuum chamber (indicating a perpendicular magnetic field).
