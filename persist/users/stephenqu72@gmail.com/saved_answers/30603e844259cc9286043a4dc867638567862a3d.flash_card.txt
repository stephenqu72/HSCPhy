### Front
How do you calculate the mass of an object undergoing circular motion when given centripetal force ($F$), radius ($r$), and angular velocity ($\omega$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Mass is determined by rearranging the centripetal force formula.
- **Formula / law:** $F = mr\omega^2 \implies m = \frac{F}{r\omega^2}$
- **When to use it:** When an object follows a circular path and angular velocity is provided instead of linear velocity ($v$).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., cm to m) or failing to square the entire angular velocity term ($\omega^2$).
