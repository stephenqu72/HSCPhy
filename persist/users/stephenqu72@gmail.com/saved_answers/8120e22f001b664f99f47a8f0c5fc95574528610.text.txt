To solve this projectile motion problem, we must treat the horizontal and vertical motions of the ball independently.

### 1. Identify the given information:
*   Horizontal velocity ($v_x$): $2\text{ ms}^{-1}$
*   Vertical displacement ($h$): $1\text{ m}$
*   Acceleration due to gravity ($g$): Approximately $9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$
*   Initial vertical velocity ($u_y$): $0\text{ ms}^{-1}$ (since it rolls horizontally off the table)

### 2. Find the time of flight ($t$):
We use the vertical displacement formula:
$$h = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} g t^2$$
Since $u_y = 0$, this simplifies to:
$$h = \frac{1}{2} g t^2$$
$$1 = \frac{1}{2} \times 9.8 \times t^2$$
$$1 = 4.9 \times t^2$$
$$t^2 = \frac{1}{4.9} \approx 0.2041$$
$$t = \sqrt{0.2041} \approx 0.4518\text{ s}$$

### 3. Calculate horizontal distance ($d$):
Now, use the horizontal velocity and the time calculated above:
$$d = v_x \times t$$
$$d = 2 \times 0.4518$$
$$d \approx 0.9036\text{ m}$$

Converting this to centimeters:
$$0.9036\text{ m} \times 100 = 90.36\text{ cm}$$

Looking at the options provided, **b) 90cm** is the correct answer.

***

### Explanation of steps:
1.  **Separate the motion:** In projectile motion, the horizontal velocity remains constant (ignoring air resistance), while the vertical motion is subject to constant acceleration due to gravity.
2.  **Determine time:** Use the height of the table to calculate how long the ball is in the air. 
3.  **Calculate range:** Once the time is determined, multiply that time by the constant horizontal speed to find the horizontal displacement (range).
