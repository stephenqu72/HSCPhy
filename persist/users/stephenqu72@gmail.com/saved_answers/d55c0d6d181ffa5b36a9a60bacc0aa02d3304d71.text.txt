To find the frequency of the photon, we use the relationship between the energy of a photon and its frequency, defined by the Planck-Einstein relation:

$$E = hf$$

Where:
*   $E$ is the energy of the photon ($9.0 \times 10^{-24} \text{ J}$)
*   $h$ is Planck's constant ($6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$)
*   $f$ is the frequency in Hertz ($\text{Hz}$)

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for frequency ($f$):**
    $$f = \frac{E}{h}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values into the equation:**
    $$f = \frac{9.0 \times 10^{-24} \text{ J}}{6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}}$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$f \approx 1.35828 \dots \times 10^{10} \text{ Hz}$$

4.  **Round to two significant figures** (matching the precision of the provided energy value):
    $$f \approx 1.4 \times 10^{10} \text{ Hz}$$
