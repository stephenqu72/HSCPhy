To solve this projectile motion problem, we must treat horizontal and vertical motion independently. We will use $g = 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$ for the acceleration due to gravity.

### (a) Calculate the distance from the base of the cliff (Range)

**1. Find the time of flight ($t$):**
Using the vertical displacement formula $s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$, where the initial vertical velocity $u_y = 0$:
$$70 = 0 + \frac{1}{2} (9.8) t^2$$
$$70 = 4.9 t^2$$
$$t^2 = \frac{70}{4.9} \approx 14.286$$
$$t \approx 3.78\text{ s}$$

**2. Calculate horizontal distance (Range):**
Using $d = v_x t$:
$$d = 40 \times 3.7796...$$
$$d \approx 151.18\text{ m}$$

---

### (b) Calculate the velocity of the object as it hits the water

Velocity is a vector, so we need the final horizontal velocity ($v_x$) and the final vertical velocity ($v_y$).

**1. Components of velocity:**
*   $v_x = 40\text{ m s}^{-1}$ (remains constant)
*   $v_y = u_y + a_y t = 0 + (9.8)(3.7796) \approx 37.04\text{ m s}^{-1}$

**2. Magnitude of resultant velocity ($v$):**
Using Pythagoras' theorem:
$$v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2} = \sqrt{40^2 + 37.04^2}$$
$$v = \sqrt{1600 + 1371.96} = \sqrt{2971.96} \approx 54.52\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

**3. Direction ($\theta$):**
$$\tan \theta = \frac{v_y}{v_x} = \frac{37.04}{40} \approx 0.926$$
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}(0.926) \approx 42.8^\circ \text{ below the horizontal.}$$

**Final Answer:** The object hits the water at approximately $54.5\text{ m s}^{-1}$ at an angle of $42.8^\circ$ below the horizontal.
