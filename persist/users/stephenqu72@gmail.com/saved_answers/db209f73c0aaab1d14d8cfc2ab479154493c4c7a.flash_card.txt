### Front
What is the net momentum of two identical particles (like protons) moving toward each other with equal speeds, and what is the quark composition of a proton?

### Back
- Key idea: Momentum is a vector; a proton is a baryon composed of quarks.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: 
    - Momentum: $\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$ (Net $p = 0$ for equal and opposite velocities).
    - Composition: Proton = $uud$ (up, up, down).
- When to use it: Any collision problem in a center-of-momentum frame or Standard Model theory questions.
- Common trap: Assuming high kinetic energy implies high total momentum; remember momentum is a vector sum.
