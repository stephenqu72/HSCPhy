### Answer and Explanation

#### Part a.
**Calculation:**
1. Determine the current drawn by one kettle ($P = VI \implies I = \frac{P}{V}$):
   $$I_1 = \frac{2200\text{ W}}{240\text{ V}} \approx 9.17\text{ A}$$
2. Determine the total current for two kettles:
   $$I_{total} = 2 \times 9.17\text{ A} = 18.34\text{ A}$$

**Discussion:**
Since the total current required ($18.34\text{ A}$) exceeds the rated limit of the fuse ($15\text{ A}$), the fuse will **blow (melt)**. This creates an open circuit, cutting off power to the kettles to prevent the wires from overheating and potentially causing a fire.

#### Part b.
**Calculation:**
1. Determine the resistance of the power tool at $240\text{ V}$ ($P = \frac{V^2}{R} \implies R_{tool} = \frac{V^2}{P}$):
   $$R_{tool} = \frac{240^2}{3000} = \frac{57600}{3000} = 19.2\text{ }\Omega$$
2. Determine the resistance of the extension cord ($50\text{ m}$ cord, usually implying a go-and-return path, so $100\text{ m}$ of wire):
   $$R_{cord} = 50\text{ m} \times 0.025\text{ }\Omega/\text{m} \times 2 = 2.5\text{ }\Omega$$
3. Determine total circuit resistance and the resulting current:
   $$R_{total} = 19.2 + 2.5 = 21.7\text{ }\Omega$$
   $$I = \frac{V}{R_{total}} = \frac{240}{21.7} \approx 11.06\text{ A}$$
4. Calculate the voltage drop across the tool ($V_{tool} = I \times R_{tool}$):
   $$V_{tool} = 11.06\text{ A} \times 19.2\text{ }\Omega \approx 212.35\text{ V}$$

**Conclusion:**
The operating voltage at the power tool is approximately $212.4\text{ V}$. Since the specified voltage range is $220\text{ V} - 240\text{ V}$, the tool will **not be operational** (or will operate poorly/unsafe) because the voltage drop caused by the resistance of the long extension cord has pulled the voltage below the minimum requirement.
