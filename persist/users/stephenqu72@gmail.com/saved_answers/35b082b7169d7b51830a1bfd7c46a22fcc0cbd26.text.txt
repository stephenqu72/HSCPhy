### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of **Einstein's Postulates of Special Relativity**. Specifically, it refers to the **Second Postulate of Special Relativity**, which states:

> "The speed of light in a vacuum, $c$, is constant for all inertial observers, regardless of the motion of the light source or the observer."

In classical (Galilean) physics, velocities are additive. If you throw a ball from a moving train, you add the velocity of the ball to the velocity of the train. However, electromagnetism and experimental results (most notably the Michelson-Morley experiment) demonstrate that light does not obey these Galilean transformation rules. 

*   **Option A** is a direct statement of the second postulate of special relativity. No matter how fast you are moving or how fast the source is moving, you will always measure the speed of light as exactly $c \approx 3 \times 10^8$ m/s in a vacuum.
*   **Options B, C, and D** are incorrect because they imply that the speed of light is relative to the motion of the source or the observer, which contradicts the fundamental principle that the speed of light is invariant.

When approaching questions about special relativity in the HSC Physics syllabus, always remember: **$c$ is a universal constant.**
