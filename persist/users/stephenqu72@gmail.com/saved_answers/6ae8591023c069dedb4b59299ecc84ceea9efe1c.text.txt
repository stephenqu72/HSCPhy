To solve this problem, we must consider the vector addition of two magnetic fields at point $P$.

### 1. Identify the Direction of the Fields
*   **Earth's Magnetic Field ($B_E$):** By standard convention, Earth's magnetic field lines point towards the geographic North. Therefore, $B_E$ points **North** with a magnitude of $B$.
*   **Bar Magnet's Magnetic Field ($B_M$):** The bar magnet has its South pole facing eastward (towards point $P$). Magnetic field lines point *into* a South pole. Since point $P$ is to the east of the magnet, the field lines produced by the magnet at point $P$ point **West** (back towards the South pole). The problem states the magnitude of this field is also $B$.

### 2. Perform Vector Addition
We have two vectors of equal magnitude $B$:
*   $\vec{B}_E = B$ (North)
*   $\vec{B}_M = B$ (West)

To find the resultant magnetic field $\vec{B}_{net}$, we add these two perpendicular vectors using the Pythagorean theorem and trigonometry:
*   **Magnitude:** Using the Pythagorean theorem, the magnitude is:
    $$B_{net} = \sqrt{B^2 + B^2} = \sqrt{2B^2} = B\sqrt{2} \approx 1.414B$$
*   **Direction:** Since both components are equal, the resultant vector points exactly halfway between North and West, which is **Northwest (NW)**.

### 3. Conclusion
The resultant magnetic field has a magnitude of approximately $1.4B$ and points in the North-West direction. Comparing this to the given options, **D** is the correct answer.
