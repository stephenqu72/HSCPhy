To solve this problem, we must calculate the maximum torque produced by the DC motor and compare it to the torque required to turn the spanner (load).

### 1. Calculate the required torque to turn the spanner ($\tau_{load}$)
The torque is given by $\tau = r \cdot F \cdot \sin(\theta)$. 
- Length of spanner ($r$) = $8\text{ cm} = 0.08\text{ m}$
- Force ($F$) = $14\text{ N}$
- Angle ($\theta$) = $32^\circ$

$$\tau_{load} = 0.08 \times 14 \times \sin(32^\circ)$$
$$\tau_{load} \approx 1.12 \times 0.5299 \approx 0.593\text{ Nm}$$

### 2. Calculate the maximum torque produced by the DC motor ($\tau_{motor}$)
The torque produced by a DC motor is given by $\tau = n \cdot B \cdot I \cdot A$, where $n$ is the number of loops, $B$ is the magnetic field strength, $I$ is the current, and $A$ is the area of the coil.
- $n = 2$
- $B = 3\text{ T}$
- $I = 2.5\text{ A}$
- Area ($A$) = $240\text{ mm} \times 300\text{ mm} = 0.24\text{ m} \times 0.30\text{ m} = 0.072\text{ m}^2$

$$\tau_{motor} = 2 \times 3 \times 2.5 \times 0.072$$
$$\tau_{motor} = 15 \times 0.072 = 1.08\text{ Nm}$$

### 3. Conclusion and Comparison
Comparing the two values:
- $\tau_{motor} = 1.08\text{ Nm}$
- $\tau_{load} = 0.593\text{ Nm}$

Since the motor produces a torque of $1.08\text{ Nm}$, which is greater than the required $0.593\text{ Nm}$ to turn the spanner, the student's hypothesis is **verified**. The difference is $1.08 - 0.593 = 0.487\text{ Nm}$, meaning the motor provides sufficient torque to overcome the load and rotate the spanner.
