### Answer

Galileo Galilei proposed that the motion of a projectile could be understood by separating it into two independent, simultaneous components:

1.  **Horizontal component:** An object in motion maintains a constant velocity ($v_x = \text{constant}$) because no force acts in the horizontal direction (ignoring air resistance).
2.  **Vertical component:** An object experiences a constant acceleration due to gravity ($g$) acting downwards, causing the object to undergo vertical free-fall motion ($v_y$ changes uniformly).

**Diagram:**
Imagine a projectile launched horizontally from a height. The motion results in a parabolic trajectory:

```text
       (Horizontal velocity is constant)
          --->   --->   --->   --->
        o      o      o      o      o  <-- Horizontal distance
        | \    |  \   |   \  |    \
        |  \   |    \ |     \|      \
        v   \  v      v        v        v <-- Vertical acceleration (g)
                                          (Trajectory)
```
