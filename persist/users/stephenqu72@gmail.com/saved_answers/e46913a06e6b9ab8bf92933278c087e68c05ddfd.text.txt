To solve this problem, we need to apply the standard projectile motion formulas for range ($R$) and maximum height ($H$):

1.  **Range formula:** $R = \frac{v^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$
2.  **Maximum height formula:** $H = \frac{v^2 \sin^2(\theta)}{2g}$

Where $v$ is the initial velocity, $\theta$ is the launch angle, and $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity.

### Analysis of the options:

*   **Range ($R$):** Complementary angles (angles that add up to $90^{\circ}$) produce the same range. Since $30^{\circ} + 60^{\circ} = 90^{\circ}$, the projectiles launched at $30^{\circ}$ and $60^{\circ}$ will have the **same range**. The $45^{\circ}$ launch angle provides the maximum possible range for a given velocity.
*   **Maximum Height ($H$):** The maximum height depends on $\sin^2(\theta)$. Since $\sin(30^{\circ}) = 0.5$ and $\sin(60^{\circ}) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$, the heights will be different. Specifically, $H_{60} > H_{30}$. The height increases as the launch angle increases toward $90^{\circ}$.

### Evaluating the statements:
