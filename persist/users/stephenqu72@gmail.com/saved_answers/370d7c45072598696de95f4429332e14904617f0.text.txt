### Answer
The stability of the nucleus cannot be accounted for by the gravitational or electrostatic forces acting alone.

2. **Electrostatic Force:** Graph 2 shows a significant repulsive force between protons. As distance decreases, this force increases rapidly, which would act to push the nucleus apart rather than hold it together.
3. **The Strong Nuclear Force:** Since gravity is too weak and the electrostatic force is repulsive, the stability of the nucleus must be provided by the **Strong Nuclear Force**. It acts to overcome the electrostatic repulsion between protons, thereby binding both protons and neutrons together to maintain nuclear stability.
