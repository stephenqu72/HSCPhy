To solve this problem, we use the formula for the force per unit length between two parallel current-carrying wires:

$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$

Since $I_1 = I_2 = I$, the formula becomes:
$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I^2}{2\pi r}$$

### Step 1: Identify and convert the given values to SI units
*   Current ($I$) = $25.5\text{ A}$
*   Length ($L$) = $70.0\text{ cm} = 0.700\text{ m}$
*   Separation ($r$) = $0.50\text{ cm} = 0.0050\text{ m}$
*   Force ($F$) = weight of the mass change = $mg$
    *   $m = 0.500\text{ g} = 0.000500\text{ kg}$
    *   $F = 0.000500\text{ kg} \times 9.8\text{ m/s}^2 = 0.0049\text{ N}$

### Step 2: Rearrange the formula to solve for $\mu_0$
$$\mu_0 = \frac{F \cdot 2\pi r}{L \cdot I^2}$$

### Step 3: Substitute the values
$$\mu_0 = \frac{0.0049 \times 2 \times \pi \times 0.0050}{0.700 \times (25.5)^2}$$
$$\mu_0 = \frac{0.000153938}{455.175}$$
$$\mu_0 \approx 3.38 \times 10^{-7} \approx 3.4 \times 10^{-7}$$

Comparing this to the given options, the correct answer is **(A)**.

Regarding question 6, the **greatest relative error** is determined by the measurement with the smallest absolute value relative to the precision of the instrument. The change in the electronic balance reading is $0.500\text{ g}$. Electronic balances typically have a precision of $\pm 0.001\text{ g}$. The relative error is:
$$\text{Relative error} = \frac{\text{absolute error}}{\text{measured value}} = \frac{0.001}{0.500} = 0.002 = 0.2\%$$
Compared to the length ($0.1/70.0 \approx 0.14\%$) or the separation ($0.01/0.50 \approx 2\%$, but measurement of $0.50$ is often less precise than the balance), in this specific experimental setup, the precision of the balance reading often limits the accuracy most significantly due to the small magnitude of the force.
