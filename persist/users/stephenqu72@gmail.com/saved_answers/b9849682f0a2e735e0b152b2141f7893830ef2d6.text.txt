### Answer and Explanation

This question examines the **Velocity Selector** principle (Region X) and circular motion in a magnetic field (Region Y).

**(a) State the direction of the magnetic field in Region X.**
*   **Direction:** Out of the page.
*   **Explanation:** The electric field points from the positive top plate to the negative bottom plate (downward). The force on the electron ($F_E = qE$) is upward because the electron is negative. To remain undeflected, the magnetic force must be downward. Using the Left-Hand Rule (for negative charges): thumb points down (force), fingers point right (velocity), so the palm must face the direction of the field. This indicates the field is **out of the page**.

**(b) Show that the speed can be determined as $v = \frac{E}{B}$.**
*   For the electron to travel undeflected, the magnetic force ($F_B$) must equal the electric force ($F_E$):
    $$F_E = F_B$$
    $$qE = qvB$$
*   Divide both sides by $q$ and $B$:
    $$v = \frac{E}{B}$$

**(c) Calculate the speed of the undeflected electron.**
*   Given: $E = 1.20 \times 10^5 \text{ N/C}$, $B = 0.5 \text{ T}$.
    $$v = \frac{1.20 \times 10^5}{0.5} = 2.4 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s}$$

**(d) Sketch a possible trajectory in Region Y.**
*   The electron enters Region Y (magnetic field into the page). By the Left-Hand Rule, the magnetic force acts **upwards** initially. The electron will follow a **circular path curving upwards** (a counter-clockwise semi-circle or arc) from the entrance point.

**(e) Determine the strength of the magnetic field ($B_Y$) in Region Y.**
*   The magnetic force provides the centripetal force:
    $$qvB_Y = \frac{mv^2}{r} \implies B_Y = \frac{mv}{qr}$$
*   Constants: $m = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$, $q = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$.
*   Conversion: $r = 22.3 \text{ mm} = 0.0223 \text{ m}$.
    $$B_Y = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}) \times (2.4 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s})}{(1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \times (0.0223 \text{ m})}$$
    $$B_Y \approx 6.12 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$$
