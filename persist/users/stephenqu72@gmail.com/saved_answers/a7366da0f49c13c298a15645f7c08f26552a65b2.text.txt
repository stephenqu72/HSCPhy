To solve this projectile motion problem, we must focus on the vertical motion of the box, as we are given the vertical displacement required to reach the shelf.

### 1. Identify given information
*   Initial velocity: $u = 12 \text{ ms}^{-1}$
*   Launch angle: $\theta = 55^\circ$
*   Vertical displacement: $s_y = 3.68 \text{ m}$
*   Acceleration due to gravity: $g \approx 9.8 \text{ ms}^{-2}$ (acting downwards, so $a_y = -9.8 \text{ ms}^{-2}$)

### 2. Resolve the initial velocity into vertical components
The vertical component of the initial velocity ($u_y$) is given by:
$$u_y = u \sin(\theta)$$
$$u_y = 12 \sin(55^\circ) \approx 9.8298 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$

### 3. Use the displacement formula
We use the kinematic equation for displacement in the vertical direction:
$$s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$$
Substituting our values:
$$3.68 = 9.8298t - 4.9t^2$$

Rearrange this into a standard quadratic equation $at^2 + bt + c = 0$:
$$4.9t^2 - 9.8298t + 3.68 = 0$$

### 4. Solve using the quadratic formula
The quadratic formula is $t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$$t = \frac{9.8298 \pm \sqrt{(-9.8298)^2 - 4(4.9)(3.68)}}{2(4.9)}$$
$$t = \frac{9.8298 \pm \sqrt{96.6248 - 72.128}}{9.8}$$
$$t = \frac{9.8298 \pm \sqrt{24.4968}}{9.8}$$
$$t = \frac{9.8298 \pm 4.9494}{9.8}$$

This gives two possible values for $t$:
*   $t_1 = \frac{9.8298 - 4.9494}{9.8} \approx 0.498 \text{ s}$
*   $t_2 = \frac{9.8298 + 4.9494}{9.8} \approx 1.508 \text{ s}$

Since the box lands on the shelf on its way up (the first time it reaches that height), we select the smaller value.

**Final Answer:**
The time it takes for the box to land on the shelf is approximately **$0.50 \text{ seconds}$**.
