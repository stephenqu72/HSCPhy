### Front
Contrast the net work done on a passenger in a Ferris wheel versus a spinning cone (horizontal circular motion).

### Back
- Key idea: Ferris wheel involves vertical displacement (changing $E_p$, net work $\neq 0$), while a horizontal cone maintains constant $E_p$ and $E_k$ (net work $= 0$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Work-Energy Theorem ($W_{net} = \Delta E_k$).
- When to use it: Whenever analyzing energy changes in circular motion or non-inertial reference frames.
- Common trap: Assuming that because an object is moving at constant speed, no work is being done; you must check if the height (and thus $E_p$) is changing.
