### Answer

To explain time dilation using these diagrams, consider the following points:

1.  **Constancy of the speed of light:** According to Einstein's second postulate of special relativity, the speed of light ($c$) is constant for all inertial observers, regardless of the motion of the source or the observer.
2.  **Observations in Diagram X (Proper Time):** For the passenger on the train, the light travels a straight vertical path of length $2L$ (down and up). Since $v = d/t$, the time interval measured is $\Delta t_0 = 2L/c$. This is the proper time because the start and end points of the event occur at the same location in the passenger's reference frame.
3.  **Observations in Diagram Y (Dilated Time):** For the observer outside the train, the train moves horizontally while the light is in transit. Therefore, the light follows a diagonal path of length $2D$. Since $D > L$, the total distance traveled by the light ($2D$) is greater than the distance observed by the passenger ($2L$).
4.  **Conclusion:** Because the speed of light $c$ must remain constant, and the outside observer measures a longer distance for the light to travel, they must necessarily measure a longer time interval ($\Delta t$) to complete the journey. Thus, $\Delta t > \Delta t_0$, which is the phenomenon of time dilation.
