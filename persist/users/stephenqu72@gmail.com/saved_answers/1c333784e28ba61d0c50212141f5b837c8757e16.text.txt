To solve this problem, we must analyze the physical principles governing the circular motion experiment.

### Explanation

1.  **Analyze the Physics:**
    The centripetal force ($F$) acting on the rubber bung is provided by the weight of the masses hanging on the mass carrier. The formula for centripetal force is:
    $$F = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
    where $m$ is the mass of the bung, $v$ is its velocity, and $r$ is the radius of rotation.

2.  **Identify the Constants and Variables:**
    In this experiment, the mass of the bung ($m$) and the radius of rotation ($r$) are held constant. Therefore, the relationship between force ($F$) and velocity ($v$) can be expressed as:
    $$F \propto v^2$$
    This is a quadratic relationship, which mathematically takes the form $y = kx^2$ (a parabola).

3.  **Interpret the Graph:**
    The graph provided shows a curve that passes through the origin and increases at an increasing rate. This is the characteristic shape of a $y = x^2$ graph. 
    *   If we graph $F$ vs $v$, we get a parabola.
    *   If we graph $F$ vs $v^2$, we would get a **straight line** because $F$ is directly proportional to $v^2$. 
    
    Since the graph shows a curve, it represents the relationship between the force ($F$) and the velocity ($v$) itself. 

4.  **Evaluate Options:**
    *   **(A) F and v:** This matches the parabolic relationship ($F = \frac{m}{r}v^2$).
    *   **(B) F and $v^2$:** This would yield a linear graph (a straight line passing through the origin).
    *   **(C) v and r:** The experiment keeps $r$ fixed, so this does not represent the trend investigated.
    *   **(D) a and v/r:** Since $a = \frac{v^2}{r}$, this would not produce the curve shown in the context of the experiment's variables.

Therefore, the graph represents the relationship between force ($F$) and velocity ($v$).

**Correct Option: (A)**
