### Front
State the Standard Model composition of protons and neutrons and explain why their masses differ.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Protons ($uud$) and neutrons ($udd$) are baryons held by the strong nuclear force.
- **Formula / Law:** Proton charge: $+\frac{2}{3} + \frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = +1e$. Neutron charge: $+\frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = 0$.
- **When to use it:** When asked to justify nucleon properties using the Standard Model (Module 8: Quanta to Quarks).
- **Common trap:** Attributing mass solely to the sum of quark masses; must include the energy from gluon interactions and the fact that a $d$ quark is intrinsically heavier than a $u$ quark.
