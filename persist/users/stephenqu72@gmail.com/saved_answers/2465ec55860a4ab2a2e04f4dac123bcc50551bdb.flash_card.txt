### Front
How do you calculate the decay constant ($\lambda$) when given the half-life ($t_{1/2}$) of a radioactive source?

### Back
- Key idea: The decay constant represents the probability of decay per unit time.
- Formula: $\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{t_{1/2}}$
- When to use it: Whenever you need to convert between half-life and the rate of radioactive decay in an exponential model.
- Common trap: Forgetting to ensure the units of time (seconds, days, years) are consistent throughout the calculation.
