### Front
How do you calculate the uniform electric field ($E$) between two parallel plates given a potential difference ($V$) and separation distance ($d$)?

### Back
- Key idea: The electric field strength is defined as the potential gradient between parallel conductors.
- Formula: $E = \frac{V}{d}$
- When to use it: When calculating the field between parallel plate electrodes (e.g., spark plugs, capacitors, or Millikan oil drop apparatus).
- Common trap: Failing to convert the distance ($d$) from millimeters (mm) to meters (m) before calculating.
