### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to recall the fundamental properties of electromagnetic (EM) waves:

1.  **Nature of the fields:** EM waves consist of time-varying electric fields ($\vec{E}$) and magnetic fields ($\vec{B}$). These fields are not constant; they oscillate sinusoidally. This immediately invalidates option (A).
2.  **Orientation:** A key characteristic of EM waves is that the electric field vector and the magnetic field vector are always **perpendicular** to each other.
3.  **Propagation:** The wave propagates (travels) in a direction that is perpendicular to both the electric field vector and the magnetic field vector. This is often described mathematically using the cross product, where the direction of propagation is given by the Poynting vector $\vec{S} \propto \vec{E} \times \vec{B}$.

Because the electric field, magnetic field, and the direction of propagation form a mutually perpendicular set of axes (often visualized as an $x, y, z$ coordinate system), option (C) is the only statement that correctly describes the geometry of an EM wave.

*   (A) is incorrect because the fields oscillate in magnitude and are not parallel.
*   (B) is incorrect because the fields are perpendicular, not parallel.
*   (D) is incorrect because the fields are not randomly aligned; they have a very specific, defined orientation relative to each other.
