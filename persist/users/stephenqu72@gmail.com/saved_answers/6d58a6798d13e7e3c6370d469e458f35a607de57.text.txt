To find the correct answer, we need to extract two pieces of information from the graph and then use the wave equation.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Determine the Amplitude:**
The amplitude ($A$) is defined as the maximum displacement from the equilibrium (zero) position. Looking at the y-axis, the graph goes from $0$ to $8 \text{ cm}$. Therefore, the amplitude is:
$$A = 8 \text{ cm}$$

**2. Determine the Wavelength ($\lambda$):**
The wavelength is the distance between two consecutive points in phase (e.g., crest to crest or trough to trough).
*   Looking at the graph, the first crest is at $1.5 \text{ cm}$ and the second crest is at $7.5 \text{ cm}$.
*   $\lambda = 7.5 \text{ cm} - 1.5 \text{ cm} = 6 \text{ cm}$.
*   Convert this to SI units (meters) to match the wave speed: $\lambda = 0.06 \text{ m}$.

**3. Calculate the Frequency ($f$):**
We are given the wave speed $v = 18 \text{ m s}^{-1}$. We use the universal wave equation:
$$v = f \lambda$$
Rearranging for $f$:
$$f = \frac{v}{\lambda}$$
$$f = \frac{18 \text{ m s}^{-1}}{0.06 \text{ m}}$$
$$f = 300 \text{ Hz}$$

**Conclusion:**
Comparing our results ($A = 8 \text{ cm}$, $f = 300 \text{ Hz}$) to the given options, the correct choice is **C**.
