### Front
How do you determine the charge ($q$) of a stationary oil drop in a Millikan-style experiment?

### Back
- **Key idea:** At equilibrium, the upward electric force balances the downward gravitational force ($F_E = F_g$).
- **Formula:** $q = \frac{mg}{E}$
- **When to use it:** When an oil drop is suspended (stationary) between two parallel charged plates.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to check the direction of the electric field ($E$); if $E$ is downward and the drop is suspended, the charge must be negative to experience an upward force.
