### Answer

**(a)** According to classical electromagnetic theory (specifically Maxwell’s equations), an accelerating charged particle, such as an electron orbiting a nucleus, must continuously emit electromagnetic radiation. This loss of energy would cause the electron to spiral inward, resulting in the collapse of the atom in a fraction of a second.

**(b)** Bohr proposed that electrons exist in specific, "stationary" energy states or orbits. He postulated that while in these stable orbits, electrons do not radiate energy. They only emit or absorb electromagnetic radiation when transitioning between these discrete energy levels, with the energy of the photon given by $E = hf$.
