### Explanation
To solve this, we compare the coherence and monochromatic nature of the two light sources:

1.  **Laser Light:** Laser stands for "Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation." This process involves stimulated emission, where photons are emitted with the same frequency, phase, and direction. Because all photons produced are essentially at the same energy level (and thus the same wavelength), laser light is **highly monochromatic**, meaning it has a **very narrow spectrum**.
2.  **Incandescent Light:** Incandescent light is produced through thermal radiation (blackbody radiation). Atoms in the filament are heated, causing them to vibrate and emit photons across a continuous range of energies as they collide and settle into different energy states. This results in a broad range of wavelengths covering a large portion of the electromagnetic spectrum (typically visible, infrared, and ultraviolet), which is described as a **very wide spectrum**.
