### Front
In uniform circular motion, what is the direction of the net force (centripetal force) relative to the object’s instantaneous velocity?

### Back
- Key idea: The net force must act perpendicular to the velocity and point directly toward the center of the circular path.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s Second Law ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$)
- When to use it: Whenever an object moves in a circular path at a constant speed to determine the direction of acceleration/force at any specific point.
- Common trap: Confusing centripetal force with "centrifugal force" (a non-existent outward force) or assuming the force acts in the direction of motion.
