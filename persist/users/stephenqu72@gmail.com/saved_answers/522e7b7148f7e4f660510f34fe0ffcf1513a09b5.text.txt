To determine what the range of the projectile depends on, we must look at the fundamental equations of motion for a projectile launched from an initial height $h > 0$.

### Answer
The correct answer is **A. The initial velocity and starting height**.

### Explanation
In projectile motion, the range ($R$) is defined as the total horizontal distance covered. The horizontal displacement is governed by the equation:
$$R = v_x \times t_{total}$$
where $v_x$ is the constant horizontal velocity ($v_0 \cos \theta$) and $t_{total}$ is the total time of flight.

1.  **Initial Velocity ($v_0$ and $\theta$):** The horizontal velocity $v_x$ is directly determined by the magnitude of the initial velocity $v_0$ and the launch angle $\theta$. 
2.  **Starting Height ($h$):** Because the projectile is launched from a height above the ground (as shown in the diagram), the time of flight $t_{total}$ is not simply determined by the vertical component of the velocity ($v_y = v_0 \sin \theta$) and gravity alone. It is also dependent on the initial height $h$. You can see this by using the vertical displacement equation:
    $$\Delta y = v_{0y}t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$
    Setting $\Delta y = -h$ (the final position relative to the launch height), we get:
    $$-h = (v_0 \sin \theta)t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$
    This quadratic equation shows that the time of flight $t$ is a function of $v_0$, $\theta$, and $h$.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
*   **B and C:** While the components of the launch velocity and the angle are critical, they are incomplete without considering the starting height. If the starting height were zero (launching from the ground), these options might be partially correct, but in this specific physical scenario, the height $h$ is a necessary independent variable.
*   **D:** The "maximum height above the launch position" and "time of flight" are *outcomes* or *derivatives* of the initial conditions, not the independent variables that determine the range in the first place.

By choosing **A**, we encompass all the independent variables ($v_0$, $\theta$, and $h$) required to calculate the range of a projectile launched from an elevated position.
