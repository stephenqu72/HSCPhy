### Front
What factors determine the electric force ($F$) acting on a charged particle between two parallel plates, and how does position affect this force?

### Back
- Key idea: The force is directly proportional to voltage and inversely proportional to plate separation; it is uniform (constant) regardless of position ($x$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F = qE = \frac{qV}{d}$
- When to use it: Any scenario involving a point charge moving within a uniform electric field created by parallel plates.
- Common trap: Assuming the force changes based on the particle's position ($x$) between the plates; it does not.
