To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of the **Doppler Effect** in astrophysics.

### Answer
The correct choice is **A**.

### Explanation
1.  **Analyze the spectral lines:** Observe the positions of the spectral lines for Star X compared to Star Y. You will notice that the lines in Star Y are shifted toward the right end of the spectrum relative to Star X.
2.  **Apply the Doppler Effect:** In astronomical spectra, the right side usually represents the longer wavelengths (the red end of the visible spectrum), and the left represents the shorter wavelengths (the blue/violet end). A shift toward longer wavelengths is known as a **redshift**.
3.  **Interpret the redshift:** A redshift indicates that the object is moving away from the observer. However, notice that the *pattern* and *spacing* of the lines remain identical. This confirms that the two stars are likely the same type of object, and the difference in their spectra is due to motion, not an intrinsic difference in temperature or chemical composition that would suggest different life stages.
4.  **Evaluate the options:** Since the pattern of the lines is the same, both stars represent the same evolutionary stage. A redshift (motion) does not change the classification of a star's evolutionary stage (e.g., from Main Sequence to Red Supergiant). Therefore, since the spectra represent the same star-type experiencing a Doppler shift, both stars are in the **Main sequence** stage.
