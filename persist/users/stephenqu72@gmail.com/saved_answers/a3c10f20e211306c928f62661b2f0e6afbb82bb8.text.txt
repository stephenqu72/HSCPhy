To find the angular velocity of the Ferris wheel car, we follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the given values
*   Arc distance ($s$) = $62.8\text{ m}$
*   Radius ($r$) = $10\text{ m}$
*   Time ($t$) = $2\text{ minutes} = 2 \times 60 = 120\text{ seconds}$

### 2. Calculate the angular displacement in radians
The relationship between arc length, radius, and angular displacement ($\theta$) in radians is given by:
$$s = r\theta \implies \theta = \frac{s}{r}$$
$$\theta = \frac{62.8}{10} = 6.28\text{ radians}$$

### 3. Convert radians to degrees
Since one full circle is $2\pi$ radians ($6.28$ radians is approximately $2\pi$), we know:
$$1\text{ radian} \approx \frac{180^\circ}{\pi}$$
$$\theta \text{ (in degrees)} = 6.28 \times \frac{180^\circ}{3.14} \approx 2 \times 180^\circ = 360^\circ$$

### 4. Calculate angular velocity ($\omega$)
Angular velocity is the angular displacement per unit time:
$$\omega = \frac{\theta}{t}$$
$$\omega = \frac{360^\circ}{120\text{ s}} = 3.00^\circ\text{ s}^{-1}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **(C)**.

### How to solve it:
*   **Step 1:** Convert the time into standard SI units (seconds) so the final unit matches the question requirements ($^\circ\text{s}^{-1}$).
*   **Step 2:** Use the arc length formula to find the angular displacement in radians.
*   **Step 3:** Use the conversion factor ($180^\circ/\pi$) to change radians into degrees. Note that $6.28$ is effectively $2\pi$, representing one full rotation ($360^\circ$).
*   **Step 4:** Divide the total degrees by the total time in seconds to obtain the rate of rotation.
