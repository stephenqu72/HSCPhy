### Front
Account for the use and limitations of geostationary satellites.

### Back
- **Key idea:** They orbit above the equator with a 24-hour period, remaining fixed relative to Earth's surface.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Orbital altitude $\approx 35\,800\text{ km}$.
- **When to use it:** To explain why they are ideal for continuous broadcasting/weather monitoring, but unsuitable for high-speed, real-time data.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that their high altitude (required for a 24-hour period) is the direct cause of signal latency and poor polar coverage.
