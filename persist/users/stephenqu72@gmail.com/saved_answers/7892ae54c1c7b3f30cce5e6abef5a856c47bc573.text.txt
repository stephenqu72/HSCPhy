### Explanation

In astrophysics, the spectral classification system (O, B, A, F, G, K, M) is used to categorize stars based on their surface temperature. The mnemonic "Oh Be A Fine Girl/Guy, Kiss Me" orders stars from hottest to coolest:

*   **O:** Hottest ($\approx 30,000$ K or more)
*   **B:** Hot ($\approx 10,000 - 30,000$ K)
*   **A:** Warm ($\approx 7,500 - 10,000$ K)
*   **F:** $\approx 6,000 - 7,500$ K
*   **G:** $\approx 5,200 - 6,000$ K
*   **K:** $\approx 3,700 - 5,200$ K
*   **M:** Coolest ($\approx 2,400 - 3,700$ K)

**Analysis of the stars in the table:**
1.  **Star X (M2):** This is an M-type star, which is the coolest category in the sequence.
2.  **Star Y (B5):** This is a B-type star, which is very hot.
3.  **Star Z (A2):** This is an A-type star, which is warmer than F, G, K, and M stars, but cooler than B stars.

Comparing the spectral types B, A, and M: The B-type star (Star Y) has the highest surface temperature.

Therefore, statement **B** is correct.

### Answer
The correct option is **B**.
