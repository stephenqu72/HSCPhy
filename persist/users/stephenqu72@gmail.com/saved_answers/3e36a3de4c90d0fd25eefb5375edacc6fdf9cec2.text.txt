To solve this problem, we apply **Malus's Law**, which states that the intensity of polarized light transmitted through a polarizer is given by:

$$I = I_0 \cos^2(\theta)$$

where:
*   $I_0$ is the initial intensity of the polarized light.
*   $I$ is the transmitted intensity.
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the plane of polarization of the incident light and the transmission axis of the polarizer.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Determine the angle $\theta$**:
    The reflected light is horizontally polarized. The windshield (the polarizer) is polarized at $30^\circ$ to the vertical. This means the angle between the horizontal polarization of the light and the transmission axis of the windshield is:
    $$\theta = 90^\circ - 30^\circ = 60^\circ$$

2.  **Calculate the transmitted intensity ($I$)**:
    Using Malus's Law:
    $$I = I_0 \cos^2(60^\circ)$$
    Since $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$, we have:
    $$I = I_0 (0.5)^2 = 0.25 I_0$$
    This means the intensity seen by the driver is $25\%$ of the original reflected light.

3.  **Calculate the percentage reduction**:
    The reduction in intensity is the difference between the initial intensity and the transmitted intensity:
    $$\text{Reduction} = I_0 - I = I_0 - 0.25 I_0 = 0.75 I_0$$
    Converted to a percentage, this is a **75% reduction**.

**Correct Option: (A)**
