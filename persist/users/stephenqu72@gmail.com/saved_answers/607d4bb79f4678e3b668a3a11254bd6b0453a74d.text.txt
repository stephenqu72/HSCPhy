### Answer
The correct answer is **B. Horizontal range**.

### Explanation

To solve this, we must examine the effect of changing the launch angle ($\theta$) on various properties of a projectile, assuming the initial launch speed ($v$) remains constant.

1.  **Horizontal Range ($R$):** The formula for the range of a projectile on flat ground is:
    $$R = \frac{v^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$
    At $\theta = 45^\circ$, the range is at its maximum because $\sin(2 \times 45^\circ) = \sin(90^\circ) = 1$. If we increase the angle beyond $45^\circ$ (e.g., to $60^\circ$), the value of $\sin(2\theta)$ will decrease, thus decreasing the total horizontal range.

2.  **Final Velocity ($v_f$):** Since energy is conserved (assuming no air resistance), the final speed at the same height as the launch is equal to the initial speed. Increasing the angle does not change the magnitude of the final velocity.

3.  **Maximum Height ($H$):** The formula for maximum height is:
    $$H = \frac{v^2 \sin^2(\theta)}{2g}$$
    As $\theta$ increases from $45^\circ$ towards $90^\circ$, the value of $\sin(\theta)$ increases. Therefore, the maximum height will increase.

4.  **Time of Flight ($T$):** The formula for time of flight is:
    $$T = \frac{2v \sin(\theta)}{g}$$
    As $\theta$ increases from $45^\circ$ towards $90^\circ$, $\sin(\theta)$ increases, meaning the projectile spends more time in the air. Therefore, the time of flight will increase.

Since the question asks which variable will **decrease** when the angle is increased from $45^\circ$, the horizontal range is the only correct option.
