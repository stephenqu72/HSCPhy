### Explanation
1. **Understanding the Physics**: The diagram shows two parallel conducting plates with opposite charges. This configuration creates a **uniform electric field** ($E$) in the region between the plates (assuming the plates are large and the distance between them is small, which is the standard assumption in HSC physics).
2. **The Force Equation**: The force ($F$) experienced by a charged particle in an electric field is given by the formula:
   $$F = qE$$
   where $q$ is the charge of the particle and $E$ is the electric field strength.
3. **Uniformity**: Because the electric field $E$ between two large, parallel, uniformly charged plates is constant at every point between them, and the charge $q$ of the particle does not change, the force $F$ remains constant in both magnitude and direction.
4. **Addressing the particle type**: Note that the prompt asks about an "electron," while the diagram displays a positive charge. Regardless of whether the particle is positive or negative, the magnitude of the electric field is uniform throughout the space. Therefore, the force experienced remains constant regardless of the particle's position between the plates.

Since the field does not vary with distance in this idealized parallel plate setup, the force does not increase or decrease as the particle moves; it remains constant.
