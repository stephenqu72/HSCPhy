### Front
In a radioactive decay process, how do you calculate the mass of the stable daughter product formed after time $t$?

### Back
- Key idea: The mass of the daughter product is equal to the mass of the parent isotope lost.
- Formula / law: Conservation of mass ($m_{daughter} = m_{initial} - m_{remaining}$), where $m_{remaining} = m_0(0.5)^n$.
- When to use it: Whenever asked for the amount of stable decay product rather than the amount of radioactive parent remaining.
- Common trap: Providing the remaining mass of the parent isotope ($m_{remaining}$) instead of the mass of the decayed product ($m_{initial} - m_{remaining}$).
