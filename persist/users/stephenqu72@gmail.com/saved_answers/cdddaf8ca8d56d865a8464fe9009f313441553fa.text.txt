### Solution and Explanation

To solve these projectile motion problems, we first resolve the initial velocity into vertical and horizontal components.
Given:
- Initial velocity $u = 9.3\text{ m s}^{-1}$
- Angle $\theta = 45^\circ$
- Initial height $h_0 = 15\text{ m}$
- Acceleration due to gravity $g = 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$

Vertical component: $u_y = u \sin(45^\circ) = 9.3 \times \sin(45^\circ) \approx 6.576\text{ m s}^{-1}$.

---

#### (a) Calculate the maximum height from the ground the ball can reach.

To find the maximum height, we consider the vertical motion until the point where the vertical velocity $v_y = 0$.
Using the kinematic equation $v_y^2 = u_y^2 + 2a\Delta y$:
$$0 = (6.576)^2 + 2(-9.8)(\Delta y)$$
$$\Delta y = \frac{-(6.576)^2}{-19.6} \approx 2.207\text{ m}$$

The maximum height from the ground is the initial height plus the vertical displacement:
$$H_{max} = 15 + 2.207 = 17.207\text{ m} \approx 17.21\text{ m}$$

---

#### (b) How long does it take for the ball to reach the ground?

We use the vertical displacement formula $s = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$. When the ball reaches the ground, its vertical displacement relative to the starting point is $s = -15\text{ m}$.
$$-15 = 6.576t - 4.9t^2$$
Rearrange into a quadratic equation:
$$4.9t^2 - 6.576t - 15 = 0$$
Using the quadratic formula $t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$$t = \frac{6.576 \pm \sqrt{(-6.576)^2 - 4(4.9)(-15)}}{9.8}$$
$$t = \frac{6.576 \pm \sqrt{43.24 + 294}}{9.8} = \frac{6.576 \pm 18.36}{9.8}$$
Since time must be positive:
$$t = \frac{6.576 + 18.36}{9.8} \approx 2.544\text{ s}$$
The ball takes approximately $2.54\text{ seconds}$ to reach the ground.
