### Answer

**(a) Outline the key features of the Rutherford model of the atom.**
*   The atom consists of a tiny, positively charged, dense central nucleus that contains almost all of the atom's mass.
*   The remainder of the atom is mostly empty space, with negatively charged electrons orbiting the nucleus like planets orbiting the Sun.

**(b) Explain how the Bohr model of the atom overcomes an identified limitation of the Rutherford model.**
*   **Limitation:** According to classical electromagnetic theory (Maxwell’s equations), an accelerating charge (the orbiting electron) must continuously radiate energy as electromagnetic waves. This would cause the electron to lose kinetic energy, spiral into the nucleus, and result in the collapse of the atom in a fraction of a second.
*   **Bohr’s Solution:** Bohr proposed that electrons exist in specific, "stationary" energy states (stable orbits) where they do *not* radiate energy. Radiation is only emitted or absorbed when an electron makes a "quantum leap" (transition) between two allowed energy levels. This postulate successfully explained why atoms are stable and do not collapse.
