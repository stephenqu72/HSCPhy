### Front
Derive the formula for the velocity ($v$) of a car on a frictionless banked track at angle $\theta$ and radius $r$.

### Back
- Key idea: The horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force ($N \sin \theta = mv^2/r$), while the vertical component balances gravity ($N \cos \theta = mg$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \sqrt{rg \tan \theta}$
- When to use it: To calculate the design speed of a banked curve where friction is negligible.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the centripetal force is not the normal force itself, but its horizontal component. Do not confuse $\sin \theta$ and $\cos \theta$.
