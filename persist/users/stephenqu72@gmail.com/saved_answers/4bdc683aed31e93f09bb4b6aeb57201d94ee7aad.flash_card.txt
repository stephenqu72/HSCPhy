### Front
How do the horizontal ($v_x$) and vertical ($v_y$) velocity-time graphs appear for a projectile (ignoring air resistance)?

### Back
- Key idea: $v_x$ is constant (no horizontal force); $v_y$ changes linearly due to gravity.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_x = 0$; $a_y = -g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$.
- When to use it: To identify the correct motion graph for a projectile.
- Common trap: Assuming $v_y = 0$ at the start or that $v_x$ changes; remember $v_y$ crosses zero at peak height, but $v_x$ never reaches zero.
