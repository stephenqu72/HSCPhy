### Explanation

The escape velocity $v_e$ of an object from the surface or orbit of a planetary body is given by the formula:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

In this question, we are comparing four different scenarios. Since $2$ and $G$ are constants for all astronauts, the escape velocity is directly proportional to the square root of the ratio of the mass ($M$) to the orbital distance ($r$):
$$v_e \propto \sqrt{\frac{M}{r}}$$

We can calculate the relative value of $\frac{M}{r}$ for each astronaut to determine which is the largest:

*   **Astronaut P:** $\frac{M}{r} = 1$
*   **Astronaut Q:** $\frac{M}{r} = \frac{3M}{2r} = 1.5$
*   **Astronaut R:** $\frac{M}{r} = \frac{6M}{3r} = 2$
*   **Astronaut S:** $\frac{M}{r} = \frac{3M}{r} = 3$

Comparing these values, the ratio $\frac{M}{r}$ is greatest for **Astronaut S** ($3$). Therefore, Astronaut S requires the greatest escape velocity to leave the orbit.
