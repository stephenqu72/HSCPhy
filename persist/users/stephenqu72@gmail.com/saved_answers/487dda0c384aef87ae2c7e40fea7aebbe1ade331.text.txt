### Answer
The correct answer is **(C)**.

### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of **Newton’s Third Law of Motion**. 

Newton's Third Law states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. In terms of gravitational interaction:
1. When two objects interact, they exert forces on each other that are **equal in magnitude** and **opposite in direction**.
2. Even though the Earth is much more massive than the Moon ($6.0 \times 10^{24}\text{ kg}$ vs $7.3 \times 10^{22}\text{ kg}$), the gravitational force ($F_g$) is calculated using Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation:
   $$F_g = \frac{G M_1 M_2}{r^2}$$
   Because the formula involves the product of both masses ($M_1 \times M_2$), the force exerted by the Earth on the Moon is identical to the force exerted by the Moon on the Earth.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
* **(A) and (B):** These contradict Newton’s Third Law. The forces are always equal in magnitude, regardless of the mass of the objects.
* **(D):** While it is true that the Moon experiences a greater *acceleration* (because $a = F/m$ and the Moon has a smaller mass), this statement is not the primary description of the *interaction* (the force) itself, and it incorrectly claims the *Earth* experiences the greater acceleration. Actually, because the Earth is much more massive, its acceleration is negligible compared to the Moon's acceleration.
