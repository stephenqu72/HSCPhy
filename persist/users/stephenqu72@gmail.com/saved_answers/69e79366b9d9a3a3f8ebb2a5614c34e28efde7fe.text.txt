### Answer
Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence formula, $E=mc^2$, explains that mass and energy are interchangeable. In an atom, the mass of the nucleus is always less than the sum of the individual masses of its constituent protons and neutrons; this difference is the "mass defect" ($\Delta m$). According to the formula, this lost mass has been converted into the binding energy ($E = \Delta m c^2$) that holds the nucleons together within the nucleus.

2.  **Apply the Formula:** State the formula $E=mc^2$ and link it to the mass defect. You must explain that the "missing" mass is not actually lost, but rather transformed into energy.
3.  **Identify the Energy:** Explicitly mention that this energy is the **binding energy**, which is responsible for the strong nuclear force that overcomes the electrostatic repulsion between protons to keep the nucleus stable.
