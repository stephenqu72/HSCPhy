To find the magnitude of the force required, we use the formula for torque ($\tau$):

$$\tau = rF\sin(\theta)$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   Torque ($\tau$) = $51\text{ Nm}$
    *   Distance ($r$) = $28\text{ cm}$. We must convert this to SI units (meters) to match the torque units: $r = 0.28\text{ m}$.
    *   Angle ($\theta$) = $45^\circ$

2.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for Force ($F$):**
    $$F = \frac{\tau}{r\sin(\theta)}$$

3.  **Substitute the values into the equation:**
    $$F = \frac{51}{0.28 \times \sin(45^\circ)}$$

4.  **Perform the calculation:**
    *   $\sin(45^\circ) \approx 0.7071$
    *   $F = \frac{51}{0.28 \times 0.7071}$
    *   $F = \frac{51}{0.19798...}$
    *   $F \approx 257.6\text{ N}$

5.  **Round to the appropriate significant figures:**
    Rounding $257.6\text{ N}$ to two significant figures gives $2.6 \times 10^2\text{ N}$.
