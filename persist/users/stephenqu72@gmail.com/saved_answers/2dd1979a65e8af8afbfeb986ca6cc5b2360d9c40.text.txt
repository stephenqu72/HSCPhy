### Explanation
To solve this, consider the forces acting on the ball at point $X$:

1.  **Identify the forces:** Since air resistance is negligible, the only force acting on the ball after it leaves the wall is the gravitational force ($F_g = mg$). This force acts vertically downwards toward the center of the Earth.
2.  **Newton's Second Law:** According to Newton's Second Law of Motion, $\vec{F}_{net} = m\vec{a}$. Since the only force present is the weight of the ball, which acts vertically downwards, the acceleration vector $\vec{a}$ must also be directed vertically downwards.
3.  **Horizontal vs. Vertical Motion:** In projectile motion, the horizontal acceleration is zero because there are no horizontal forces. The vertical acceleration is constant and equal to the acceleration due to gravity ($g$). Therefore, at any point along the trajectory (including point $X$), the acceleration vector points straight down.

Diagram (A) is the only one that shows the acceleration vector pointing vertically downwards, matching the direction of gravitational force.
