To solve this problem, we use the standard physical constants for Earth:
*   Mass of Earth ($M$) $\approx 5.97 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$
*   Radius of Earth ($R_E$) $\approx 6.371 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
*   Gravitational constant ($G$) $\approx 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{ kg}^{-2}$

The orbital radius is $r = R_E + \text{altitude}$. For the initial orbit:
$r_1 = 6.371 \times 10^6 + 780,000 = 7.151 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$

### (a) Calculate the gravitational force
Using Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, $F = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$:
$$F = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24}) \times 1500}{(7.151 \times 10^6)^2}$$
$$F \approx \frac{5.97 \times 10^{17}}{5.11 \times 10^{13}} \approx 11,680 \text{ N}$$

### (b) Calculate the speed of the satellite
In a circular orbit, the gravitational force provides the centripetal force ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$), so $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$:
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24})}{7.151 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v = \sqrt{5.568 \times 10^7} \approx 7,462 \text{ m/s}$$

### (c) Calculate the change in gravitational potential energy
The new altitude is $780 - 67 = 713 \text{ km}$, so $r_2 = 6.371 \times 10^6 + 713,000 = 7.084 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$.
The change in potential energy is $\Delta U = U_f - U_i = -\frac{GMm}{r_2} - (-\frac{GMm}{r_1}) = GMm \left( \frac{1}{r_1} - \frac{1}{r_2} \right)$.

$$GMm = (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24}) \times 1500 \approx 5.97 \times 10^{17}$$
$$\Delta U = 5.97 \times 10^{17} \left( \frac{1}{7.151 \times 10^6} - \frac{1}{7.084 \times 10^6} \right)$$
$$\Delta U \approx 5.97 \times 10^{17} \times (-1.322 \times 10^{-8}) \approx -7.89 \times 10^9 \text{ J}$$
*(The negative value indicates a loss in gravitational potential energy as the satellite moves closer to Earth.)*
