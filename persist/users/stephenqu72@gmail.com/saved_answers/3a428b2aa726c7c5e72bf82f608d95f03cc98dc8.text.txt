### Teacher's Guide to Answering the Question

To achieve full marks on this 6-mark "Analyse" question, you must break down the relationship between the motor effect and its consequences. You cannot simply list devices; you must **identify components** and **relate them to the broader implications** for both society and the environment.

#### Model Answer

The motor effect, defined by the force $F = IlB\sin\theta$ exerted on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field, is the fundamental principle behind electric motors. Its discovery has transformed human civilization by enabling the efficient conversion of electrical energy into mechanical work.

**Societal Impact:**
The motor effect has been the primary driver of industrial and domestic automation. By replacing manual labor with electric motors, society has seen a massive increase in productivity, lower costs for consumer goods, and a higher standard of living. From household appliances (fridges, fans, washing machines) to essential industrial equipment (conveyor belts, electric trains, and manufacturing robotics), the motor effect has fundamentally altered how we work and live, enabling urbanization and high-density transport systems.

**Environmental Impact:**
The environmental impact is dual-natured. On one hand, the motor effect is a key component of sustainable energy solutions. Electric vehicles (EVs) utilize motors to reduce reliance on internal combustion engines, which directly reduces greenhouse gas emissions and improves urban air quality. Furthermore, electric motors are significantly more energy-efficient than thermal engines, leading to lower overall energy wastage. 

However, there is a negative environmental impact linked to the generation of the electricity required to power these motors. If the electricity is sourced from fossil fuels, the "green" benefits are mitigated by the carbon footprint of power plants. Additionally, the manufacturing of motors requires rare-earth minerals and heavy metals, leading to mining-related environmental degradation and challenges in recycling electronic waste. In summary, the motor effect is a cornerstone of modern technological progress, but its net benefit to the environment is contingent upon the transition to renewable energy sources.

### How to Solve This Question

1.  **Deconstruct the Verb:** The command word is "Analyse." This means you must identify the key components (the motor effect) and investigate the relationship between them (how they impact society/environment). You are not just describing; you are evaluating the cause-and-effect relationship.
2.  **Structure your Response:** Use a clear structure. Dedicate one paragraph to societal impacts and another to environmental impacts. Use a concluding sentence to synthesize the two.
3.  **Use Physics Terminology:** Even in a humanities-style analysis, ensure you reference the core concept. Mentioning the transformation of electrical potential energy into kinetic energy shows the marker you understand the underlying physics.
4.  **Balance your argument:** For "Analyse" questions, it is critical to show the "pro" and the "con." For the environment, discuss both the efficiency/reduction of emissions (positive) and the issues of energy source dependency and mineral extraction (negative).
5.  **Check the Marks:** Since it is worth 6 marks, aim for at least 3 distinct, well-explained points for each category (Society and Environment).
