### Explanation
To calculate the remaining amount of a radioactive substance after a certain number of half-lives, we use the following formula:

$$N_t = N_0 \times \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n$$

Where:
*   $N_t$ is the final amount remaining.
*   $N_0$ is the initial amount of the sample.
*   $n$ is the number of half-lives that have elapsed.

**Step-by-step calculation:**
1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   $N_0 = 4.0\text{ g}$
    *   $n = 3.5$
2.  **Substitute the values into the formula:**
    $$N_t = 4.0 \times \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{3.5}$$
3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    *   $\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{3.5} = 0.5^{3.5} \approx 0.088388$
    *   $N_t = 4.0 \times 0.088388$
    *   $N_t = 0.35355\dots\text{ g}$

Looking at the provided options, the value $0.35355$ is closest to **$0.35\text{ g}$**.
