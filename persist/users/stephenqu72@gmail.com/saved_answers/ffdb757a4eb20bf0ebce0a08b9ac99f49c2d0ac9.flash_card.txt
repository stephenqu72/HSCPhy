### Front
How does the evolution of scientific theories progress in Physics (specifically regarding model status)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Models evolve through a cycle of: **abandoning** those contradicted by empirical evidence, **developing** new models to explain anomalies, and **validating** models through rigorous experimentation.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** To evaluate the impact of an experiment (e.g., Michelson-Morley) on the scientific community or to explain the nature of scientific progress.
- **Common trap:** Focusing only on the "new" discovery and neglecting to explain *why* the previous model failed or how the new model was *experimentally validated*.
