To solve this problem, we need to determine the threshold wavelength ($\lambda$) corresponding to the work function ($\Phi$) of tungsten.

### 1. Identify the given values
*   Work function ($\Phi$) = $4.5\text{ eV}$
*   We need to convert this into Joules (J) because standard physical constants are in SI units. 
    *   $1\text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$
    *   $\Phi = 4.5 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J} = 7.209 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$

### 2. Choose the correct formula
The relationship between the energy of a photon ($E$) and its wavelength ($\lambda$) is given by:
$$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$
Where:
*   $h$ (Planck's constant) $\approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J s}$
*   $c$ (speed of light) $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$

Rearranging to solve for $\lambda$:
$$\lambda = \frac{hc}{E}$$

### 3. Calculate
$$\lambda = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)}{7.209 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$\lambda = \frac{1.9878 \times 10^{-25}}{7.209 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$\lambda \approx 2.757 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m}$$

Rounding to match the provided options, we get approximately $2.76 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m}$.

Comparing this to the given choices:
A) $4.42 \times 10^{-16}\text{ m}$
B) $2.74 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$
C) $1.76 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m}$
D) $2.76 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m}$

**The correct answer is D.**
