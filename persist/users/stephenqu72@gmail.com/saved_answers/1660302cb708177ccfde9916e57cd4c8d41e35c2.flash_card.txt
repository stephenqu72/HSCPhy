### Front
How does an increase in gravitational acceleration ($g$) affect the range ($R$) and maximum height ($h$) of a projectile?

### Back
- Key idea: Both $R$ and $h$ are inversely proportional to $g$.
- Formula / law: $h = \frac{v^2 \sin^2 \theta}{2g}$ and $R = \frac{v^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem comparing different environments (e.g., Earth vs. Moon) or variable gravity.
- Common trap: Assuming horizontal velocity $v_x$ is affected by $g$; remember $v_x$ remains constant while $g$ only influences vertical flight time.
