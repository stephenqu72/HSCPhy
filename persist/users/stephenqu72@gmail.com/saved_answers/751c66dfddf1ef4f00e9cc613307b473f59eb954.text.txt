To solve this problem, we must analyze the horizontal and vertical components of projectile motion independently.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Analyze the Initial Velocity ($v_x$):**
In horizontal projection, there is no air resistance, meaning horizontal velocity is constant. The formula for horizontal distance is:
$$x = v_x \cdot t$$
At $t = 0.5\text{ s}$, the distance is $x$:
$$x = v_x \cdot 0.5$$
$$v_x = \frac{x}{0.5} = 2x \text{ m/s}$$
This eliminates options A and B.

**2. Analyze the Vertical Distance ($d_y$):**
Vertical motion is governed by gravity with an initial vertical velocity of $0\text{ m/s}$. The distance fallen is given by the formula:
$$d_y = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$
We are given that at $t = 0.5\text{ s}$, the vertical distance is $y$:
$$y = \frac{1}{2}g(0.5)^2 = \frac{1}{2}g(0.25) = 0.125g$$
Now, we want to find the distance fallen at $t = 1.0\text{ s}$. Let this distance be $d_{1.0}$:
$$d_{1.0} = \frac{1}{2}g(1.0)^2 = 0.5g$$
To express $d_{1.0}$ in terms of $y$, we can find the ratio:
$$\frac{d_{1.0}}{y} = \frac{0.5g}{0.125g} = 4$$
$$d_{1.0} = 4y$$

**Conclusion:**
The initial velocity is $2x$ and the vertical distance at $1.0\text{ s}$ is $4y$. Therefore, the correct option is **D**.
