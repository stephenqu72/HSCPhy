To solve this problem, we use **Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion**, which states that the square of the orbital period $T$ of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis $r$ of its orbit:

$$T^2 \propto r^3$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Set up the ratio:** Since both planets orbit the same star, the constant of proportionality is the same for both. We can write the relationship as:
    $$\frac{T_A^2}{r_A^3} = \frac{T_B^2}{r_B^3}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values:**
    *   For Planet A: $r_A = r$ and $T_A = T$.
    *   For Planet B: $r_B = \frac{r}{4}$. Let the unknown period be $T_B$.

    $$\frac{T^2}{r^3} = \frac{T_B^2}{(\frac{r}{4})^3}$$

3.  **Simplify the expression:**
    $$\frac{T^2}{r^3} = \frac{T_B^2}{\frac{r^3}{64}}$$

    Multiply both sides by $\frac{r^3}{64}$:
    $$T_B^2 = \frac{T^2}{r^3} \times \frac{r^3}{64}$$
    $$T_B^2 = \frac{T^2}{64}$$

4.  **Solve for $T_B$:** Take the square root of both sides:
    $$T_B = \sqrt{\frac{T^2}{64}}$$
    $$T_B = \frac{T}{8}$$

**Conclusion:** The period of Planet B is $\frac{T}{8}$. Therefore, the correct option is **(A)**.
