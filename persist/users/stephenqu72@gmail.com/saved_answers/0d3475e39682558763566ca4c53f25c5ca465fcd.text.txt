### Explanation

To solve this question, we must understand the historical development of the nature of light in physics:

1.  **Young’s Double Slit Experiment:** This experiment demonstrated the phenomenon of interference and diffraction. These are wave-like properties. When light passes through two narrow slits, it creates an interference pattern (fringes) that can only be explained if light behaves as a **wave**.
2.  **The Photoelectric Effect:** This experiment involves the emission of electrons from a metal surface when light of a sufficient frequency shines on it. Classical wave theory failed to explain why electron emission was dependent on the frequency ($f$) rather than the intensity of the light. Albert Einstein proposed that light consists of discrete packets of energy called photons, where the energy of a photon is given by $E = hf$. This interaction demonstrated that light behaves as a **particle** (or quantum).
