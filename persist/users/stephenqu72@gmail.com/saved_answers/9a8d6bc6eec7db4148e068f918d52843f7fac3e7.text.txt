### Answer
The correct answer is **A. 12.5 lux**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we apply **Malus's Law**, which describes how the intensity of polarized light changes as it passes through a second polarizing filter (the analyzer).

**1. Understand the Physics Principles:**
*   When two polaroid filters are aligned to transmit the maximum amount of light, their transmission axes are parallel. In this state, the intensity $I_0$ is 50 lux.
*   When the second filter is rotated by an angle $\theta$, Malus's Law states that the new intensity $I$ is given by:
    $$I = I_0 \cos^2(\theta)$$
    where $I_0$ is the initial intensity and $\theta$ is the angle between the transmission axes of the two polarizers.

**2. Identify the given variables:**
*   Initial intensity $I_0 = 50 \text{ lux}$
*   Angle of rotation $\theta = 60^\circ$

**3. Perform the calculation:**
*   Substitute the values into the formula:
    $$I = 50 \times \cos^2(60^\circ)$$
*   Recall that $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$ (or $\frac{1}{2}$):
    $$I = 50 \times (0.5)^2$$
    $$I = 50 \times 0.25$$
    $$I = 12.5 \text{ lux}$$

Therefore, after rotating the filter by $60^\circ$, the transmitted light intensity is 12.5 lux.
