### Front
How does a longer tap handle reduce the force required to turn the tap?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Increasing the lever arm distance ($d$) from the pivot reduces the force ($F$) required to produce the same torque ($\tau$).
- **Formula / law:** $\tau = F \times d$ (Principle of Moments).
- **When to use it:** Any question involving rotational motion, levers, or mechanical advantage.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to state that the required torque ($\tau$) to turn the internal valve remains constant.
