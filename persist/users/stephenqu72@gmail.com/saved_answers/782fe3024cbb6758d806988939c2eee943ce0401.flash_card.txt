### Front
How do you relate the orbital radius ($r$) and orbital period ($T$) of a planet or satellite orbiting the Sun?

### Back
- Key idea: Kepler’s Third Law (Law of Periods).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$ (where $M$ is the mass of the central body).
- When to use it: To calculate the period or orbital radius of an object in a stable circular orbit.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., $km \rightarrow m$ or years $\rightarrow$ seconds) before substituting into the formula.
