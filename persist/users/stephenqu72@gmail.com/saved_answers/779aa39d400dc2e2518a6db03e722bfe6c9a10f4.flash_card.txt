### Front
When plotting potential difference ($V_a$) against velocity ($v$) for a charged particle accelerating from rest, what function should be on the horizontal axis to achieve a linear graph?

### Back
- Key idea: Kinetic energy gained equals electrical work done ($qV_a = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $V_a = (\frac{m}{2q})v^2$ (Linear form $y=mx$).
- When to use it: When linearising data to find the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$) from a gradient.
- Common trap: Plotting $v$ instead of $v^2$ will result in a parabolic curve rather than a linear relationship.
