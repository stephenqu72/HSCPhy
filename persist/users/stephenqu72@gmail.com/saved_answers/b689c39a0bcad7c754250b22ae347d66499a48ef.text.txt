### Answer
The correct answer is **B. Hadrons**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we need to recall the classification of subatomic particles based on the Standard Model of particle physics:

1.  **Quarks** are fundamental particles that experience the strong nuclear force.
2.  **Hadrons** are composite particles made of quarks held together by the strong force. By definition, any particle formed by a combination of quarks is a **hadron**.
3.  **Baryons** (like protons and neutrons) are a specific sub-category of hadrons made of exactly three quarks.
4.  **Mesons** are another specific sub-category of hadrons made of one quark and one antiquark.
5.  **Leptons** (such as electrons and neutrinos) are fundamental particles; they are **not** made of quarks.

The question asks for the "general name" given to particles formed when two or more quarks combine. Since baryons and mesons are both types of composite particles formed by quarks, they fall under the umbrella term of **hadrons**. Therefore, "Hadron" is the correct general classification.
