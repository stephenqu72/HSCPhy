### Front
Why is the proton not considered a "fundamental particle" in the Standard Model?

### Back
- Key idea: Fundamental particles have no substructure; protons are composite particles made of quarks.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When identifying the hierarchy of matter (Hadrons vs. Elementary particles) in particle physics questions.
- Common trap: Assuming that because a proton is a stable, building block of an atom, it must be indivisible or fundamental.
