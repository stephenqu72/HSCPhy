### Front
How do the velocity components behave during ideal projectile motion?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Horizontal velocity is constant (Newton’s 1st Law); vertical velocity changes linearly due to constant gravity.
- **Formula / Law / Constant:** $v_x = \text{constant}$; $v_y(t) = v_{0y} - gt$.
- **When to use it:** To determine velocity at any time $t$ during flight or to identify matching motion graphs.
- **Common trap:** Assuming horizontal velocity changes or that vertical velocity is zero at the start/end of the flight rather than just at the peak.
