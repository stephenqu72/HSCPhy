### Front
How does the torque on a rotating system (like a bicycle pedal or generator coil) vary throughout one full revolution?

### Back
- Key idea: Torque varies sinusoidally as the effective lever arm changes with the angle of rotation.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\tau = rF\sin(\theta)$
- When to use it: Whenever a force is applied to a pivot at a changing angle or when calculating rotational mechanics.
- Common trap: Assuming torque is constant; always remember torque is zero when the force vector is parallel to the lever arm ($\sin(0^\circ) = 0$).
