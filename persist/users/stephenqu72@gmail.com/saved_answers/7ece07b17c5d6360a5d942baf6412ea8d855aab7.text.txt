To solve this problem, we use the formula for escape velocity $v_e$:

$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

Where:
- $v_e$ is the escape velocity in m/s
- $G$ is the gravitational constant ($6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ Nm}^2\text{kg}^{-2}$)
- $M$ is the mass of the Moon (the value we need to find)
- $r$ is the radius of the Moon in meters

### Step 1: Convert units to SI units
- Radius $r = 1.73 \times 10^3 \text{ km} = 1.73 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
- Escape velocity $v_e = 2.38 \text{ km s}^{-1} = 2.38 \times 10^3 \text{ m s}^{-1}$

### Step 2: Rearrange the formula to solve for $M$
Square both sides of the escape velocity formula:
$$v_e^2 = \frac{2GM}{r}$$

Isolate $M$:
$$M = \frac{v_e^2 \times r}{2G}$$

### Step 3: Substitute the values and calculate
$$M = \frac{(2.38 \times 10^3)^2 \times (1.73 \times 10^6)}{2 \times (6.67 \times 10^{-11})}$$

Calculate the numerator:
$$(5.6644 \times 10^6) \times (1.73 \times 10^6) \approx 9.799 \times 10^{12}$$

Calculate the denominator:
$$2 \times 6.67 \times 10^{-11} = 1.334 \times 10^{-10}$$

Calculate $M$:
$$M = \frac{9.799 \times 10^{12}}{1.334 \times 10^{-10}} \approx 7.345 \times 10^{22} \text{ kg}$$

Rounding to three significant figures, we get $7.35 \times 10^{22} \text{ kg}$.
