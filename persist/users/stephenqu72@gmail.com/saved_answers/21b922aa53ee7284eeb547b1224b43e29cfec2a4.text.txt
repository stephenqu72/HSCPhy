To solve this question, we must understand the composition of an atom at both the **atomic level** and the **subatomic (fundamental particle) level**.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the atoms' components:**
    A helium (He) atom consists of:
    *   $2$ protons
    *   $2$ neutrons (in the nucleus)
    *   $2$ electrons (orbiting the nucleus)

2.  **Identify the "fundamental particles":**
    In physics and chemistry, the term "fundamental particles" in this context refers to the **quarks** that make up the nucleons (protons and neutrons) and the **electrons** themselves (which are elementary particles).
    *   An **electron** is an elementary particle. There are $2$ electrons in a He atom.
    *   A **proton** consists of $3$ quarks ($2$ up quarks and $1$ down quark). Since there are $2$ protons, that is $2 \times 3 = 6$ quarks.
    *   A **neutron** consists of $3$ quarks ($1$ up quark and $2$ down quarks). Since there are $2$ neutrons, that is $2 \times 3 = 6$ quarks.

3.  **Calculate the total:**
    Summing these up:
    $$\text{Total particles} = (\text{Electrons}) + (\text{Quarks in protons}) + (\text{Quarks in neutrons})$$
    $$\text{Total particles} = 2 + 6 + 6 = 14$$

**Conclusion:**
There are $14$ fundamental particles required to construct a helium atom. Therefore, the correct option is **(C)**.
