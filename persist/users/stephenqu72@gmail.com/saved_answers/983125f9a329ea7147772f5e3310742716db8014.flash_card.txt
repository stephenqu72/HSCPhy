### Front
How does the normal force ($N$) compare to weight ($mg$) for a passenger at the bottom of a vertical Ferris wheel?

### Back
- Key idea: At the bottom of a vertical circle, the seat must support the passenger's weight *and* provide the upward centripetal force.
- Formula / law: $\sum F = N - mg = \frac{mv^2}{r} \implies N = mg + F_c$
- When to use it: Circular motion problems involving vertical planes (e.g., Ferris wheels, roller coasters).
- Common trap: Assuming $N = mg$ (the weight); remember that $N > mg$ at the bottom to allow for upward acceleration toward the center.
