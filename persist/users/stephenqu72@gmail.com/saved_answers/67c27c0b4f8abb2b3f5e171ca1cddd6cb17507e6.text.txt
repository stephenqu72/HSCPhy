### Answer

**(a)(i) Role of paraffin:**
The paraffin wax is rich in hydrogen atoms (protons). Its role is to act as a target for the "unknown radiation" emitted from the beryllium. When the neutral radiation strikes the hydrogen atoms in the paraffin, it knocks protons out of the wax. Detecting these protons allowed Chadwick to calculate the mass and properties of the unknown radiation through conservation of momentum and energy.

**(a)(ii) Change to the model of the atom:**
Chadwick’s discovery confirmed the existence of the **neutron**. Prior to this, the nucleus was thought to consist only of protons and electrons (to account for mass and charge). By identifying a neutral particle with a mass similar to the proton, Chadwick provided the missing component of the nucleus, leading to the current model where the nucleus consists of protons and neutrons, thereby resolving the discrepancy between the atomic number and atomic mass.

**(b) De Broglie’s hypothesis:**
The Bohr-Rutherford model postulated that electrons orbit the nucleus in discrete energy levels but could not explain *why* these orbits were stable or why only certain energy levels were permitted (the quantization of angular momentum). 

De Broglie proposed that matter (like electrons) has a wave-particle duality, governed by the relation $\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$, where $\lambda$ is the wavelength, $h$ is Planck's constant, and $p$ is momentum. This implied that electrons behave as standing waves confined around the nucleus. For a stable orbit to exist, the circumference of the orbit must be an integer multiple of the electron's wavelength ($2\pi r = n\lambda$). This provides a physical explanation for Bohr's quantization postulate, as it dictates that only specific, discrete orbits are allowed, otherwise destructive interference would occur.

3.  **Analyze (b):** You must link the *limitation* (the arbitrary nature of Bohr's orbits) to the *solution* (de Broglie's wave interpretation). The transition from "particle orbiting" to "standing wave" is the essential conceptual leap.
