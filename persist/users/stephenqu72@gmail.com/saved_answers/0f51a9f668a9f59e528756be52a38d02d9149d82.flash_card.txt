### Front
How is a standing wave produced on a string fixed at both ends?

### Back
- Key idea: Superposition of an incident wave and its reflected wave traveling in opposite directions.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $L = n\frac{\lambda}{2}$ (for $n$th harmonic); Principle of Superposition.
- When to use it: Explaining string vibrations, resonance, or musical instrument acoustics.
- Common trap: Forgetting to mention that the waves must have the same frequency and amplitude to create distinct nodes and antinodes.
