To estimate the radius of a black hole (specifically the Schwarzschild radius), we use the relationship derived from equating the escape velocity to the speed of light $c$.

### Answer
The radius of the black hole is approximately $1.48 \times 10^{23}$ meters.

### Explanation
To solve this, we use the formula for the **Schwarzschild radius** ($r_s$), which defines the radius of the event horizon for a non-rotating black hole:

$$r_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2}$$

Where:
*   $G$ is the universal gravitational constant $\approx 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$
*   $M$ is the mass of the black hole $= 10^{50} \text{ kg}$
*   $c$ is the speed of light in a vacuum $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

**Step-by-step calculation:**

1.  **Substitute the known values into the equation:**
    $$r_s = \frac{2 \times (6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \times 10^{50}}{(3.00 \times 10^8)^2}$$

2.  **Calculate the numerator:**
    $$2 \times 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 10^{50} = 1.3348 \times 10^{40}$$

3.  **Calculate the denominator (square of the speed of light):**
    $$(3.00 \times 10^8)^2 = 9.00 \times 10^{16}$$

4.  **Perform the division:**
    $$r_s = \frac{1.3348 \times 10^{40}}{9.00 \times 10^{16}} \approx 1.483 \times 10^{23} \text{ meters}$$

Rounding to appropriate significant figures based on the provided data, the radius is approximately $1.48 \times 10^{23} \text{ m}$.
