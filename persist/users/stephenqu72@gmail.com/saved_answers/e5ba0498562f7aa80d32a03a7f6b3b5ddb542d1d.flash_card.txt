### Front
How does the time of flight ($t$) of a projectile vary with the launch angle ($\theta$) on level ground?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Time of flight is directly proportional to the sine of the launch angle.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $t = \frac{2v \sin(\theta)}{g}$
- **When to use it:** To determine flight duration given initial velocity and launch angle.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the "time of flight vs. angle" graph (which increases monotonically to $90^\circ$) with the "range vs. angle" graph (which is symmetrical and peaks at $45^\circ$).
