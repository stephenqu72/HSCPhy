To solve for the torque created on the hinges of the door, we use the fundamental physics formula for torque:

$$\tau = F \cdot r \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

Assuming the force is applied perpendicular to the door (which is standard for this type of problem unless stated otherwise), $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$, simplifying the formula to:

$$\tau = F \cdot r$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   Force ($F$) = $15\text{ N}$
    *   Distance/Radius ($r$) = $85\text{ cm}$

2.  **Convert units to SI base units:**
    *   Torque is measured in Newton-meters ($\text{Nm}$), so the distance must be in meters.
    *   $r = 85\text{ cm} = 0.85\text{ m}$

3.  **Calculate the torque:**
    *   $$\tau = 15\text{ N} \times 0.85\text{ m}$$
    *   $$\tau = 12.75\text{ Nm}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **B**.

### Explanation:
When calculating torque, always ensure that your distance measurement matches the units required for the final answer. Since the options are in Newton-meters ($\text{Nm}$), converting centimeters to meters is a critical step that students often miss. Multiplying $15$ by $0.85$ yields $12.75$.
