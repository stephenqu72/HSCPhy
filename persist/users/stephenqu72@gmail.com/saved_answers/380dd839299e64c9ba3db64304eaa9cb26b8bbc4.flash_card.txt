### Front
What is Kepler's Third Law and how is it used to compare the orbits of two planets around the same central body?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The square of the orbital period ($T$) is directly proportional to the cube of the orbital radius ($r$).
- **Formula / law:** $\frac{r_1^3}{T_1^2} = \frac{r_2^3}{T_2^2}$ or $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$
- **When to use it:** When comparing two objects orbiting the same central mass, or to find an unknown radius/period given the other parameters.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to cube the radii and square the periods; ensure units are consistent (e.g., $r$ in metres, $T$ in seconds) before calculating.
