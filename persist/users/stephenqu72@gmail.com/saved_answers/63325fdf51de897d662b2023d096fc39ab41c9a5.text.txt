To solve this problem, we use the principle of **time dilation** from Einstein's Special Theory of Relativity.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   The proper time, $t_0$, is the time interval measured by the crew on the spacecraft (since the event occurs at the same location in their reference frame): $t_0 = 3.1 \times 10^{-9} \text{ s}$.
    *   The relative velocity between the spacecraft and Earth is $v = 0.9c$.
    *   We need to find the dilated time, $t$, measured by an observer on Earth.

2.  **Use the time dilation formula:**
    The formula is given by:
    $$t = \frac{t_0}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$

3.  **Calculate the Lorentz factor (the denominator):**
    First, find the term $\frac{v^2}{c^2}$:
    $$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = \frac{(0.9c)^2}{c^2} = 0.9^2 = 0.81$$
    Now, calculate the denominator:
    $$\sqrt{1 - 0.81} = \sqrt{0.19} \approx 0.43589$$

4.  **Solve for $t$:**
    $$t = \frac{3.1 \times 10^{-9}}{0.43589}$$
    $$t \approx 7.11 \times 10^{-9} \text{ s}$$

### Answer:
The time between the pulses as measured by an observer on Earth is approximately **$7.1 \times 10^{-9} \text{ s}$**.
