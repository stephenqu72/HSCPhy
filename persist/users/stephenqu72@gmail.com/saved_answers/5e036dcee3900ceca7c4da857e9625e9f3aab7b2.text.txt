To solve this conical pendulum problem, we identify the given parameters:
*   Mass $m = 0.5\text{ kg}$
*   Angular velocity $\omega = 20\text{ rad/s}$
*   Radius $r = 0.3\text{ m}$

---

### (a) Calculate the linear speed of the bob

**Solution:**
The linear speed $v$ is related to angular velocity $\omega$ and radius $r$ by the formula:
$$v = r\omega$$
Substituting the given values:
$$v = 0.3\text{ m} \times 20\text{ rad/s} = 6.0\text{ m/s}$$

**Explanation:**
Linear speed represents the distance covered per unit time along the circular path. Since the bob completes a circle of radius $0.3\text{ m}$ at an angular rate of $20\text{ rad/s}$, we simply multiply these values to find the tangential speed.

---

### (b) Determine the angle $\theta$

**Solution:**
For a conical pendulum, the forces are:
1.  Gravity ($mg$) acting vertically downward.
2.  Tension ($F_T$) acting along the string at an angle $\theta$ to the vertical.

The horizontal component of tension provides the centripetal force ($F_c$), and the vertical component balances the weight:
*   $F_T \sin(\theta) = F_c = m r \omega^2$
*   $F_T \cos(\theta) = mg$

Dividing the first equation by the second:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{m r \omega^2}{mg} = \frac{r \omega^2}{g}$$
Using $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{0.3 \times 20^2}{9.8} = \frac{120}{9.8} \approx 12.24$$
$$\theta = \arctan(12.24) \approx 85.3^\circ$$

**Explanation:**
To determine the angle, draw a vector diagram where the weight vector points down, the centripetal force vector points horizontally toward the center, and the tension vector is the resultant. Because the centripetal force is $m r \omega^2$, the tangent of the angle $\theta$ (opposite side $F_c$ over adjacent side $mg$) is equivalent to $\frac{r\omega^2}{g}$. Note that the mass cancels out, showing the angle is independent of the bob's mass.
