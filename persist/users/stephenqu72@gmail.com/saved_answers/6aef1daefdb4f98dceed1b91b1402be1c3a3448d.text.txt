To answer this question correctly, you must apply **Newton’s Third Law of Motion**, which states that for every action force, there is a reaction force equal in magnitude, opposite in direction, and acting on a different object.

### Answer
The reaction force is the **gravitational force exerted by Liesel on the Earth**, which is $500\text{ N}$ directed **upwards**.

### Explanation
To solve this, follow these steps:

1.  **Understand Newton’s Third Law:** The law states that if body A exerts a force on body B, then body B exerts a force of equal magnitude and opposite direction on body A.
2.  **Identify the "Action":** The question identifies the action force $F_g$ as the force exerted by the **Earth on Liesel** (gravity pulling her down). 
3.  **Identify the "Reaction":** According to the law, the reaction must be the "equal and opposite" pair. Since the Earth pulls Liesel down, the reaction is Liesel pulling the Earth up with the same magnitude ($500\text{ N}$).
4.  **Avoid Common Misconceptions:** A common mistake is to identify the "normal force" (the floor pushing up on Liesel) as the reaction force. This is incorrect. The normal force is the reaction to the force Liesel exerts on the *floor*, not the reaction to the gravitational force exerted by the *Earth*.

**Summary of the force pair:**
*   **Action:** Earth pulls Liesel down ($500\text{ N}$).
*   **Reaction:** Liesel pulls Earth up ($500\text{ N}$).
