### Answer

**(a) Benefit and Limitation**
*   **Benefit:** Superconductors have zero electrical resistance, meaning they allow electricity to be transmitted without any energy loss due to heating ($P = I^2R$). This significantly increases the efficiency of the power grid.
*   **Limitation:** Superconductors require extreme cooling (liquid nitrogen or helium) to maintain temperatures below their critical temperature ($T_c$), which is both expensive and technically difficult to implement over long distances.

**(b) Contrast of Electrical Conduction**
*   **Pure metal at room temperature:** Electrons flow through a lattice of vibrating positive ions. As temperature increases, the lattice vibrations increase, causing more collisions between electrons and the ions. This results in measurable electrical resistance, leading to energy dissipation as heat.
*   **Pure metallic superconductor below $T_c$:** The material undergoes a phase change where electrons form "Cooper pairs." These pairs move through the lattice without scattering off the ions or defects. Consequently, the material exhibits zero resistance and zero energy loss.

### How to solve this

1.  **For part (a):** The key here is to identify the "ideal" property of the material vs. the "practical" reality.
    *   *Tip:* Always link the "zero resistance" property to the reduction of $I^2R$ energy loss. When thinking of limitations, focus on the "coolant requirement" and the high cost of cryogenic infrastructure.

2.  **For part (b):** The word **"Contrast"** means you must discuss both sides. A good response structure is:
    *   Explain the mechanism of resistance in a normal metal (electron-ion scattering).
    *   Explain the mechanism of conduction in a superconductor (Cooper pair formation and zero scattering).
    *   Explicitly mention the role of temperature (vibrations vs. $T_c$).
    *   Use a table or clear comparative language (e.g., "In contrast to...", "Whereas..."). Since it is worth 4 marks, aim for at least two clear points of comparison.
