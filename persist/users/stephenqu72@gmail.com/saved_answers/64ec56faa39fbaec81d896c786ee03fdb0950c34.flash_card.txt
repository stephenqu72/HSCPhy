### Front
For two planets with the same radius, what is the relationship between gravitational field strength ($g$) and planetary mass ($M$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Direct linear proportionality ($g \propto M$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$; Graph is a straight line passing through the origin.
- When to use it: Comparing gravitational field strengths of different planets with identical radii.
- Common trap: Confusing this with the inverse-square relationship between $g$ and distance ($r$), which only applies when comparing positions relative to the *same* mass.
