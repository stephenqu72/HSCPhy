### Front
Explain the origin of nuclear stability and why the neutron-to-proton ratio increases in heavier elements.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Stability arises from the competition between the short-range attractive **strong nuclear force** and the long-range repulsive **electrostatic force** between protons.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed formula; graph of $N$ vs $Z$ (the "band of stability").
- **When to use it:** When explaining why heavy nuclei require more neutrons ($N>Z$) to remain stable or why there is an upper limit to the size of stable nuclei ($Z > 82$).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to state that neutrons provide the attractive strong nuclear force *without* adding electrostatic repulsion.
