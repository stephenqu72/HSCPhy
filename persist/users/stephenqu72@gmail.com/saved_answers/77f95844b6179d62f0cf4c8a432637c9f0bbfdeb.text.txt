To solve this problem, we use the de Broglie wavelength formula, which relates the wavelength ($\lambda$) of a particle to its momentum ($p$):

$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$

Where:
*   $\lambda$ is the de Broglie wavelength (given as $1\text{ mm} = 1 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$)
*   $h$ is Planck’s constant ($6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J s}$)
*   $m$ is the mass of an electron ($9.109 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$)
*   $v$ is the velocity of the electron

### Step-by-step Solution:

1.  **Rearrange the formula** to solve for velocity ($v$):
    $$v = \frac{h}{m\lambda}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values** into the equation:
    $$v = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J s}}{(9.109 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}) \times (1 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m})}$$

3.  **Perform the calculation**:
    $$v = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{9.109 \times 10^{-34}}$$
    $$v \approx 0.7274\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $0.73\text{ m s}^{-1}$.
