To solve these problems, we analyze the projectile motion of the package relative to the motion of the helicopter.

### Question 1
**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
The key concept here is **Galilean relativity**. When the package is released, it possesses the same initial velocity as the helicopter. In the absence of air resistance, the horizontal component of the package's velocity remains constant ($v_x = v \cos \theta$). Since the helicopter continues to fly at the same constant velocity, its horizontal position $x_h$ and the package's horizontal position $x_p$ will both change at the exact same rate over time $t$. 

Mathematically, at any time $t$:
$$x_h(t) = v \cos \theta \cdot t$$
$$x_p(t) = v \cos \theta \cdot t$$
Because their horizontal positions are identical at every moment, the helicopter will remain directly above the package throughout the entire flight. Therefore, when the package lands, the helicopter is directly above it.

### Question 2
**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
The speed of the package is the magnitude of its velocity vector: $v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$.
1.  **Horizontal velocity ($v_x$):** Remains constant at $10 \cos 30^\circ \approx 8.66 \text{ m s}^{-1}$.
2.  **Vertical velocity ($v_y$):** Changes due to gravity according to $v_y(t) = v_y(0) - gt$. Here, $v_y(0) = 10 \sin 30^\circ = 5 \text{ m s}^{-1}$. So, $v_y(t) = 5 - 10t$.
3.  **Speed equation:** $v(t) = \sqrt{(8.66)^2 + (5 - 10t)^2}$.

*   At $t = 0$, speed is $10 \text{ m s}^{-1}$.
*   At $t = 0.5 \text{ s}$, the vertical velocity is $5 - 10(0.5) = 0 \text{ m s}^{-1}$, which is the minimum speed. The speed is not zero because the horizontal component ($8.66 \text{ m s}^{-1}$) is still present.
*   As time increases past $0.5 \text{ s}$, the vertical speed increases, causing the total speed to increase again.

Graph **B** is the only one that shows a non-zero minimum speed (the curve does not touch the x-axis) and represents the parabolic nature of the speed-time function.
