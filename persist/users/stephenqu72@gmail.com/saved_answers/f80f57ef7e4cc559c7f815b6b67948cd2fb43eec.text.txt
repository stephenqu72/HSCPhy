This question explores the historical development of atomic theory through the study of cathode rays. Here is the breakdown of the answers and the underlying physics principles.

### Answers and Explanations

**(a) Briefly explain how this shadow is formed.**

*   **Answer:** The shadow is formed because cathode rays (a stream of high-speed electrons) travel in straight lines from the cathode. When these rays encounter the opaque metal cross, they are blocked, preventing them from striking the fluorescent screen behind it. The surrounding areas of the screen are bombarded by the electrons, causing them to glow, which leaves a dark silhouette of the cross.
*   **How to solve:** Identify the two key properties demonstrated here: that cathode rays originate from the cathode and that they travel in straight lines (rectilinear propagation).

**(b) Briefly explain this observation.**

*   **Answer:** The movement of the shadow indicates that the cathode rays are deflected by the magnetic field. This demonstrates that cathode rays consist of moving charged particles (electrons). Since a moving charge in a magnetic field experiences a Lorentz force ($F = qvB\sin\theta$), the path of the cathode rays is bent, causing the shadow of the cross to shift position on the screen.
*   **How to solve:** Connect the observation of the shifting shadow to the fundamental principle that magnetic fields exert a force on moving electric charges. This proves that cathode rays are not just light, but particles with mass and charge.

**(c) Briefly describe ONE way that work involving gas discharge tubes contributed to our understanding of atomic structure.**

*   **Answer:** Work with gas discharge tubes, specifically J.J. Thomson’s experiments, led to the discovery of the electron. By measuring the deflection of cathode rays in electric and magnetic fields, Thomson calculated the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$) of the particles, proving they were subatomic particles much smaller than a hydrogen atom. This invalidated the "indivisible atom" model of Dalton and led to the "Plum Pudding" model of the atom.
*   **How to solve:** Recall the key milestones in atomic physics. Focus on the transition from the idea of the atom as a solid sphere to the understanding that it contains smaller, negatively charged components (electrons).
