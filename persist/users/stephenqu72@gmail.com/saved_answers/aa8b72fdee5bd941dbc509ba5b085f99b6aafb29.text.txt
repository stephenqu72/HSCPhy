As an HSC Physics teacher, here is how you should approach this transformer question.

### Answer and Explanation

**a. Which part of the transformer is represented by part A?**
*   **Answer:** Part A represents the **primary coil**.
*   **Explanation:** In a transformer, the primary coil is the side that receives the input energy (often from an AC power supply). The diagram shows the coil on the left (A) is open-ended, ready to connect to a load or circuit, whereas the right side (B) is connected to the AC source. In standard transformer notation, the side connected to the source is the input/primary, and the other is the output/secondary. *Note: Even if the source is shown on the right, the identification relies on the function of the coils; A is the secondary coil if B is the input, but in standard textbook diagrams where power is delivered to a load at A, A is the secondary.* Looking closely at the diagram, the AC source is at B, making **B the primary coil** and **A the secondary coil**. 
*   *Correction based on diagram:* Since the AC source symbol is connected to the right side (B), **B is the primary coil** and **A is the secondary coil**. A transformer works by induction; the coil connected to the source is the primary.

**b. Does this model represent a step-up or a step-down transformer?**
*   **Answer:** This is a **step-up transformer**.
*   **Explanation:** By observing the number of turns in the coils, you can see that the coil at A has significantly more turns than the coil at B. According to the transformer equation: 
    $$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$
    Since $N_s$ (number of turns at A) is greater than $N_p$ (number of turns at B), the output voltage $V_s$ will be greater than the input voltage $V_p$. Therefore, it "steps up" the voltage.

**c. Describe the role of the laminated iron frame.**
*   **Answer:** The laminated iron core serves two main purposes:
    1.  **Magnetic Flux Linkage:** The iron core has high magnetic permeability, which confines the magnetic field produced by the primary coil and directs it through the secondary coil, ensuring maximum magnetic flux linkage.
    2.  **Reducing Eddy Currents:** The core is "laminated" (made of thin, insulated sheets) rather than a solid block. This increases the electrical resistance path for eddy currents, which are induced by the changing magnetic field. By breaking up the paths for these currents, energy loss due to heating (Joule heating) is significantly reduced, increasing the efficiency of the transformer.
