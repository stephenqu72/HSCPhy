To solve this problem, we use the universal formula for the acceleration due to gravity on the surface of a planet:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Where:
- $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity
- $G$ is the gravitational constant
- $M$ is the mass of the planet
- $r$ is the radius of the planet

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. Identify the given relationships:**
Let the properties of Earth be $g_E$, $M_E$, and $r_E$.
For Planet X, we are told:
- $r_X = 2r_E$
- $g_X = \frac{1}{2}g_E$

**2. Set up the ratio equation:**
We can express the gravitational acceleration of Planet X as:
$$g_X = \frac{GM_X}{r_X^2}$$

Substitute the known values for Planet X ($r_X = 2r_E$ and $g_X = \frac{1}{2}g_E$):
$$\frac{1}{2}g_E = \frac{GM_X}{(2r_E)^2}$$
$$\frac{1}{2}g_E = \frac{GM_X}{4r_E^2}$$

**3. Relate to Earth's gravity:**
Since $g_E = \frac{GM_E}{r_E^2}$, we can substitute $g_E$ in the equation:
$$\frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{GM_E}{r_E^2} \right) = \frac{GM_X}{4r_E^2}$$

**4. Solve for $M_X$:**
Cancel out common terms ($G$ and $r_E^2$ from both sides):
$$\frac{M_E}{2} = \frac{M_X}{4}$$
$$M_X = \frac{4}{2}M_E = 2M_E$$

**5. Calculate the final value:**
The mass of Earth ($M_E$) is approximately $5.972 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$. (Using the value implied by option A, $5.962 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$):
$$M_X = 2 \times (5.962 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}) = 1.1924 \times 10^{25} \text{ kg}$$

Comparing this to the provided options, **(B)** $1.193 \times 10^{25} \text{ kg}$ is the correct answer.
