To solve this problem, we use Einstein's mass-energy equivalence principle, which states that mass and energy are interchangeable according to the formula:

$$E = mc^2$$

Where:
- $E$ is the energy produced in Joules ($3.8 \times 10^{26} \text{ J}$)
- $m$ is the mass lost in kilograms ($\text{kg}$)
- $c$ is the speed of light in a vacuum, approximately $3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for mass ($m$):**
    $$m = \frac{E}{c^2}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values into the equation:**
    $$m = \frac{3.8 \times 10^{26}}{(3.00 \times 10^8)^2}$$
    $$m = \frac{3.8 \times 10^{26}}{9.00 \times 10^{16}}$$
    $$m \approx 4.222 \times 10^9 \text{ kg}$$

3.  **Convert kilograms to tonnes:**
    Since $1 \text{ tonne} = 1,000 \text{ kg}$ (or $10^3 \text{ kg}$), we divide the mass by $1,000$:
    $$m = \frac{4.222 \times 10^9 \text{ kg}}{10^3}$$
    $$m \approx 4.22 \times 10^6 \text{ tonnes}$$

4.  **Interpret the result:**
    $4.22 \times 10^6$ tonnes is equivalent to **4.22 million tonnes**.
