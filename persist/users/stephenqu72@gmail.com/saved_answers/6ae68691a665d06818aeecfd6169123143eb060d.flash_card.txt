### Front
Why are transformers used to increase voltage for long-distance power transmission?

### Back
- Key idea: Increasing voltage decreases current, which significantly reduces $I^2R$ thermal energy losses in transmission lines.
- Formula / law: $P = VI$ and $P_{\text{loss}} = I^2R$
- When to use it: When analyzing energy efficiency in electrical power distribution systems.
- Common trap: Assuming the resistance ($R$) of the cables changes; resistance is a fixed physical property of the wire and remains constant.
