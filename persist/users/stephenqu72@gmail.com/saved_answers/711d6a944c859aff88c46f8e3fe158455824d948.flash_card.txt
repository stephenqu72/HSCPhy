### Front
What is the quark composition of a neutron, and how is it determined?

### Back
- Key idea: A neutron is a baryon with a total electric charge of 0.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Charge summation: $q(\text{up}) = +2/3e$; $q(\text{down}) = -1/3e$. Composition = $udd$ ($+2/3 - 1/3 - 1/3 = 0$).
- When to use it: To identify or verify baryon identities based on quark charges.
- Common trap: Confusing the neutron ($udd$) with the proton ($uud$).
