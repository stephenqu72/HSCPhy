### Answer
The correct answer is **B. Decreasing only the vertical component**.

---

### Explanation
To understand how the time of flight for a projectile is determined, we must look at the equations of motion for the vertical direction. 

1.  **Vertical Motion:** The time of flight ($t$) of a projectile launched from the ground and returning to the same ground level is determined solely by the initial vertical component of the velocity ($u_y$) and the acceleration due to gravity ($g$). 
2.  **The Formula:** Using the kinematic equation for displacement in the vertical direction ($s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$), where displacement $s_y = 0$ at the end of the flight and acceleration $a_y = -g$, we get:
    $$0 = u_y t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2$$
    Rearranging for $t$:
    $$t = \frac{2 u_y}{g}$$
3.  **Analysis:** The formula $t = \frac{2 u_y}{g}$ shows that the time of flight is directly proportional to the initial vertical velocity component ($u_y$). Therefore, if you decrease the initial vertical component, the time of flight will decrease. 
4.  **Why others are incorrect:** 
    *   The horizontal component of velocity ($u_x$) affects how far the object travels (range), but it has no impact on how long the object stays in the air in a vacuum. Therefore, changing the horizontal component (C and D) will not change the time of flight.
    *   Increasing the vertical component (A) would increase the time of flight.
