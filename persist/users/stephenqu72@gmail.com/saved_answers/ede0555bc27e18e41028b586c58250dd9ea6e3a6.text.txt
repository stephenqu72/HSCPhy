To find the ratio of the gravitational acceleration of Planet Falcon to Planet Unicorn, we use the formula for gravitational acceleration at the surface of a planet:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Where:
- $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
- $M$ is the mass of the planet.
- $r$ is the radius of the planet.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Define the variables:**
    Let $M_U$ be the mass of Planet Unicorn and $r_U$ be its radius.
    The gravitational acceleration on Planet Unicorn is $g_U = \frac{GM_U}{r_U^2}$.

2.  **Express the variables for Planet Falcon:**
    According to the problem, Planet Falcon has 12 times the mass and 4 times the radius of Planet Unicorn:
    - $M_F = 12M_U$
    - $r_F = 4r_U$

3.  **Calculate the acceleration for Planet Falcon ($g_F$):**
    Substitute the values for $M_F$ and $r_F$ into the gravitational acceleration formula:
    $$g_F = \frac{G(12M_U)}{(4r_U)^2}$$
    $$g_F = \frac{12GM_U}{16r_U^2}$$
    Simplify the fraction by dividing both numerator and denominator by 4:
    $$g_F = \frac{3}{4} \left( \frac{GM_U}{r_U^2} \right)$$

4.  **Find the ratio:**
    Since $g_U = \frac{GM_U}{r_U^2}$, we can write:
    $$g_F = \frac{3}{4} g_U$$
    Therefore, the ratio of $g_F : g_U$ is $3 : 4$.

**Correct Option:**
The correct answer is **(C) 3 : 4**.
