To find the torque produced by the weight of the book, we follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the given values
*   **Mass of the book ($m$):** $800\text{ g}$. We must convert this to SI units (kilograms) for our calculation:
    $$m = 800\text{ g} = 0.8\text{ kg}$$
*   **Total length of the shelf:** $1\text{ m}$
*   **Position of the book ($r$):** The book is halfway along the length of the shelf:
    $$r = \frac{1\text{ m}}{2} = 0.5\text{ m}$$
*   **Acceleration due to gravity ($g$):** Standard gravity is $9.8\text{ m/s}^2$.

### 2. Calculate the Force (Weight)
The force ($F$) acting on the shelf is the weight of the book:
$$F = m \times g$$
$$F = 0.8\text{ kg} \times 9.8\text{ m/s}^2 = 7.84\text{ N}$$

### 3. Calculate the Torque ($\tau$)
Torque is defined as the product of the force and the perpendicular distance from the pivot (the hinge at the wall):
$$\tau = F \times r$$
$$\tau = 7.84\text{ N} \times 0.5\text{ m}$$
$$\tau = 3.92\text{ Nm}$$

Comparing our result to the options provided:
A. $3.92$
B. $7.84$
C. $3920$
D. $7840$

The correct option is **A**.

### Explanation Summary
In physics, torque is the rotational equivalent of force. To solve this, we first ensured all units were in the standard SI system ($kg$, $m$, and $s$). We calculated the weight of the book using $F=mg$, and then multiplied that force by the distance from the pivot point to determine the rotational effect (torque) being applied to the hinge.
