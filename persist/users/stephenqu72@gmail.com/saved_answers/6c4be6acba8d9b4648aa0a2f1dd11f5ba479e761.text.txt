To find the magnitude of the force $F_A$, we must use the condition for static equilibrium. Since the net force on the ring is $0\text{ N}$, the sum of the horizontal components ($\Sigma F_x$) and the sum of the vertical components ($\Sigma F_y$) must both be zero.

### Step 1: Define a Coordinate System
Let:
*   West be the negative $x$-direction ($-x$).
*   East be the positive $x$-direction ($+x$).
*   South be the negative $y$-direction ($-y$).
*   North be the positive $y$-direction ($+y$).

### Step 2: Resolve Forces into Components
*   **Force $F_A$**: Pointing West.
    *   $F_{Ax} = -F_A$
    *   $F_{Ay} = 0$
*   **Force $F_B$**: Pointing South ($65\text{ N}$).
    *   $F_{Bx} = 0$
    *   $F_{By} = -65\text{ N}$
*   **Force $F_C$**: Magnitude $190\text{ N}$ at $110^\circ$ to $F_B$.
    Since $F_B$ is at $270^\circ$ (or $-90^\circ$ on a unit circle), $F_C$ is at an angle of $270^\circ - 110^\circ = 160^\circ$ from the positive $x$-axis.
    *   $F_{Cx} = 190 \cdot \cos(160^\circ) \approx 190 \cdot (-0.9397) \approx -178.54\text{ N}$
    *   $F_{Cy} = 190 \cdot \sin(160^\circ) \approx 190 \cdot (0.3420) \approx 64.98\text{ N}$

### Step 3: Solve for $F_A$
Using the equilibrium condition for the horizontal components ($\Sigma F_x = 0$):
$$-F_A + F_{Bx} + F_{Cx} = 0$$
$$-F_A + 0 + (-178.54) = 0$$
$$F_A = -178.54\text{ N}$$

The magnitude is the absolute value of this component, which is approximately $179\text{ N}$.

**Note on direction:** The negative sign indicates the direction is West, as stated in the problem. The magnitude is $179\text{ N}$.
