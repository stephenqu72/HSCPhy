To solve this problem, we use the formula for the gravitational acceleration $g$ at the surface of a planet:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

where:
- $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
- $M$ is the mass of the planet.
- $r$ is the radius of the planet.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1. **Define the Earth's conditions:**
   For Earth, the acceleration is:
   $$g_{\text{Earth}} = \frac{GM_E}{r_E^2} = g$$

2. **Define Planet X's conditions:**
   We are told Planet X has twice the mass ($M_X = 2M_E$) and twice the radius ($r_X = 2r_E$).

3. **Substitute these values into the formula:**
   $$g_X = \frac{G(2M_E)}{(2r_E)^2}$$

4. **Simplify the expression:**
   $$g_X = \frac{2GM_E}{4r_E^2}$$
   $$g_X = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{GM_E}{r_E^2} \right)$$

5. **Relate back to Earth's gravity:**
   Since $\frac{GM_E}{r_E^2} = g$, we find:
   $$g_X = \frac{1}{2} g$$
