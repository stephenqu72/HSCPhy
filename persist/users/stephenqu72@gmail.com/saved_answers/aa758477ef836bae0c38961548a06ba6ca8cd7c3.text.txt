To solve this question, we must look at the historical and theoretical significance of James Clerk Maxwell's work in the context of the HSC Physics syllabus (specifically "Electromagnetism"):

1.  **Understanding the unification:** Before Maxwell, electricity (studied by Coulomb, Ampère, and Faraday) and magnetism (studied by Oersted and others) were often viewed as separate, though related, phenomena. Maxwell’s four equations mathematically linked these forces, showing that a changing electric field creates a magnetic field and vice versa. This effectively combined them into the unified field of **electromagnetism**.
