### Front
How do you determine unknown reactants or products in a nuclear reaction (such as in the CNO cycle)?

### Back
- Key idea: Conservation of atomic number ($Z$) and nucleon number ($A$).
- Formula / law: $\sum Z_{\text{reactants}} = \sum Z_{\text{products}}$ and $\sum A_{\text{reactants}} = \sum A_{\text{products}}$.
- When to use it: Any nuclear decay, fusion, or fission problem to identify unknown particles or balance equations.
- Common trap: Forgetting to account for all emitted particles (like $\gamma$ rays, $\beta$ particles, or neutrinos) when balancing the numbers.
