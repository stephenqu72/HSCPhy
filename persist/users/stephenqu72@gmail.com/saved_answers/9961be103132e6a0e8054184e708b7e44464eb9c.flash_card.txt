### Front
At the peak of a projectile's trajectory, what is the angle between the velocity vector and the acceleration vector?

### Back
- Key idea: At the peak, vertical velocity is zero, leaving only horizontal velocity; acceleration due to gravity is always vertical.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $90^{\circ}$ (orthogonal vectors).
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem asking for the orientation of vectors at maximum height.
- Common trap: Assuming velocity is zero at the peak; the horizontal component remains constant.
