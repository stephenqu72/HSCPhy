### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must identify the mathematical relationship between the energy of a photon ($E$) and its wavelength ($\lambda$).

1.  **Recall the relevant physics formula:**
    The energy of a photon is related to its frequency ($f$) by the Planck-Einstein relation:
    $$E = hf$$
    where $h$ is Planck's constant.

2.  **Incorporate wavelength:**
    Since the speed of light ($c$) is equal to the product of frequency and wavelength ($c = f\lambda$), we can express frequency as $f = \frac{c}{\lambda}$. Substituting this into the energy equation gives:
    $$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$

3.  **Analyze the relationship:**
    In this equation, $h$ and $c$ are constants. Therefore, the energy $E$ is inversely proportional to the wavelength $\lambda$ (i.e., $E \propto \frac{1}{\lambda}$).

4.  **Interpret the graph:**
    *   An inverse relationship (where $y = \frac{k}{x}$) produces a **hyperbolic curve** that decreases as $x$ increases. 
    *   Graph (A) shows a bell curve, which is incorrect.

    *   **Graph (C) shows a curve that starts high on the y-axis and approaches zero as x increases, which represents an inverse relationship.**
    *   Graph (D) shows a direct linear relationship ($y = mx$), which would represent energy vs. frequency, not energy vs. wavelength.

Thus, graph (C) correctly represents the inverse relationship between the energy and the wavelength of a photon.
