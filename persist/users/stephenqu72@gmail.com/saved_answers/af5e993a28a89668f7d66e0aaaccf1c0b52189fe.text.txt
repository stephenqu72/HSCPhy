To determine the direction of the force on the electron, we use the **Right-Hand Rule** for magnetic forces acting on a moving charge.

### The Answer
The correct answer is **A. Up**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we apply the magnetic force formula $F = qvB \sin(\theta)$, where the direction is determined by the Right-Hand Rule.

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   **Velocity ($v$):** The electron is moving to the right.
    *   **Magnetic Field ($B$):** The crosses ($\times$) indicate that the magnetic field is directed **into the page**.
    *   **Charge ($q$):** The electron has a **negative charge**.

2.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule (for a positive charge):**
    *   Point your right-hand fingers in the direction of the velocity (to the right).
    *   Curl your fingers into the page (direction of $B$).
    *   Your thumb points **down**. This is the direction of the force for a *positive* charge.

3.  **Adjust for the electron's charge:**
    *   Since an electron is negatively charged ($q < 0$), the force acts in the **opposite direction** to what the right-hand rule suggests for a positive charge.
    *   Opposite to "down" is **up**.

Therefore, the electron will experience a force directed **upwards** as it enters the field.
