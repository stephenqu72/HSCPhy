To solve this problem, we apply **Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion**, which states that the square of the orbital period ($T$) of a planet or satellite is directly proportional to the cube of the average distance ($r$) from the central body. This is expressed by the formula:

$$\frac{T^2}{r^3} = k$$

Since all three satellites are orbiting the same central body (Earth), the constant $k$ is the same for all of them. Therefore, we can set up a ratio between any two satellites:

$$\frac{T_1^2}{r_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{r_2^3}$$

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1. **Identify the knowns for Alpha:**
   - $T_{Alpha} = 1$
   - $r_{Alpha} = 2$

2. **Identify the unknowns for Beta:**
   - $T_{Beta} = x$
   - $r_{Beta} = 3.41$

3. **Set up the equation:**
   $$\frac{1^2}{2^3} = \frac{x^2}{3.41^3}$$
   $$\frac{1}{8} = \frac{x^2}{39.653941}$$

4. **Solve for $x^2$:**
   $$x^2 = \frac{39.653941}{8}$$
   $$x^2 = 4.95674$$

5. **Solve for $x$:**
   $$x = \sqrt{4.95674}$$
   $$x \approx 2.226$$

Rounding to two decimal places, we get **$2.23$**.
