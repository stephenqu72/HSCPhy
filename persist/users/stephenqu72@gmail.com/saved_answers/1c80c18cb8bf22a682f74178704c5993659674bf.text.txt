To solve this projectile motion problem, we must treat horizontal and vertical components of motion independently.

### Part (a): Calculating Initial Velocity

**1. Given Data:**
*   Range ($R$) = $205\text{ m}$
*   Total time ($t$) = $4.3\text{ s}$
*   Acceleration due to gravity ($g$) = $9.8\text{ m/s}^2$

**2. Horizontal Velocity ($u_x$):**
Since horizontal velocity is constant (ignoring air resistance), we use the range formula:
$$R = u_x \times t$$
$$u_x = \frac{205}{4.3} \approx 47.67\text{ m/s}$$

**3. Vertical Velocity ($u_y$):**
Using the vertical displacement formula $s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$. Since the total displacement in the vertical direction is $0$ at the end of the flight:
$$0 = u_y(4.3) + \frac{1}{2}(-9.8)(4.3)^2$$
$$0 = 4.3u_y - 90.601$$
$$u_y = \frac{90.601}{4.3} \approx 21.07\text{ m/s}$$

**4. Resultant Initial Velocity ($u$):**
$$u = \sqrt{u_x^2 + u_y^2} = \sqrt{47.67^2 + 21.07^2}$$
$$u = \sqrt{2272.43 + 443.95} = \sqrt{2716.38} \approx 52.12\text{ m/s}$$
The initial velocity is approximately **$52.1\text{ m/s}$**.

### Part (b): Justification for Range Selection

**Answer:** The archers might choose not to maximize range to achieve **greater precision and accuracy (grouping).**

**Justification:** 
To maximize range (on flat ground), a projectile must be launched at an angle of $45^\circ$. However, in archery, a lower trajectory is often preferred because:
1.  **Reduced Flight Time:** A flatter trajectory minimizes the time the arrow is in the air. This reduces the time the arrow is subjected to external environmental factors such as wind, which could push the arrow off course.
2.  **Velocity Control:** Launching at an angle higher than required for a specific distance necessitates a higher release velocity or a more complex arc. A flatter, more direct path (higher horizontal velocity component) makes the arrow’s path more predictable and less sensitive to small variations in the launch angle, leading to tighter groupings on the target.
