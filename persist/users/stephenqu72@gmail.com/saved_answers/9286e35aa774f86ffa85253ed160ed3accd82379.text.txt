To solve this problem, we need to consider the physics of how a hydroelectric generator produces power.

### Explanation

1.  **Identify the variables:** The power ($P$) produced by a hydroelectric turbine is proportional to the rate of energy transfer. The fundamental equation for the power available from flowing water is:
    $$P \propto \dot{m} \cdot v^2$$
    where $\dot{m}$ is the mass flow rate (proportional to the volume flow rate $x$) and $v$ is the velocity of the water.

2.  **Relate Flow and Turbine Speed:** The question states that the flow rate $x$ is doubled to $2x$ and the turbine speed (which is directly proportional to the water velocity $v$) is also doubled to $2v$.

3.  **Calculate the effect on power:**
    *   The power output $E$ (per unit time) is proportional to the mass flow rate ($\dot{m}$) and the square of the velocity ($v^2$):
        $$P \propto \text{flow} \cdot (\text{speed})^2$$
    *   If the flow doubles ($2x$) and the turbine speed doubles ($2v$), the new power $P'$ becomes:
        $$P' \propto (2x) \cdot (2v)^2$$
        $$P' \propto 2x \cdot 4v^2$$
        $$P' \propto 8(x \cdot v^2)$$
    *   Since $P \propto x \cdot v^2 = E$, the new power output is $8E$.

Alternatively, thinking about it simply: 
*   Doubling the amount of water (mass) per second doubles the energy available ($2 \times E$).
*   Doubling the turbine speed increases the kinetic energy extraction by a factor of $2^2 = 4$.
*   Combining these factors: $2 \times 4 = 8$.

Therefore, the total energy output produced per unit of time increases by a factor of 8.

**Correct Answer:** **(D) 8E**
