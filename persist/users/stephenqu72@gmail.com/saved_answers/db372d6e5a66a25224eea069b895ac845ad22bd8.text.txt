### Explanation
When a vehicle travels around a circular banked track at a constant speed, the two primary forces acting on it are:
1.  **Weight ($W$):** Acts vertically downwards from the center of mass of the vehicle.
2.  **Normal Reaction Force ($R$):** Acts perpendicular to the surface of the track.

For the vehicle to undergo uniform circular motion, it requires a net force directed toward the center of the turn, known as the **centripetal force ($F_c$)**. In this context, $F_c$ is the *resultant* (vector sum) of the weight $W$ and the reaction force $R$.

Mathematically, this is expressed as:
$$F_c = W + R$$

To determine the correct diagram, we look for the vector addition of $W$ and $R$:
*   The reaction force $R$ must be perpendicular to the banked surface.
*   The weight $W$ must point straight down.
*   The horizontal resultant $F_c$ must point toward the center of the circular path (horizontally toward the left in the provided diagram).

Looking at the options:
*   **Diagram (D)** correctly shows the vector addition. If you place the tail of the weight vector $W$ at the tip of the reaction vector $R$ (or vice versa), the resultant vector is the horizontal centripetal force $F_c$ pointing to the left. Specifically, $R$ is directed perpendicular to the slope, and the combination of $R$ and $W$ results in the horizontal centripetal force $F_c$.

Therefore, **(D)** is the correct representation because it accurately depicts $R$ perpendicular to the track, $W$ vertically downward, and the resultant $F_c$ as the horizontal component required for circular motion.
