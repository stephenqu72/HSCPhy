To determine the factors affecting the banking angle $\theta$, we must look at the forces acting on the car.

### Analysis of Forces
1.  **Gravity ($mg$):** Acts vertically downwards.
2.  **Normal Force ($N$):** Acts perpendicular to the banked surface.

For a car on a frictionless banked curve, the horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force ($F_c$) required for circular motion:
$$N \sin(\theta) = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

The vertical component of the normal force balances the weight of the car:
$$N \cos(\theta) = mg$$

### Derivation
Dividing the first equation by the second to eliminate $N$ and $m$:
$$\frac{N \sin(\theta)}{N \cos(\theta)} = \frac{mv^2/r}{mg}$$
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

### Conclusion
From the final equation $\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$, we can see that the angle $\theta$ depends only on the velocity ($v$), the radius of the curve ($r$), and gravity ($g$). Crucially, **mass ($m$) cancels out**, meaning it does not affect the required banking angle. Friction is assumed to be zero, so the surface material and tire shape are irrelevant.

Therefore, the speed of the car is the correct factor among the choices provided.

**Correct Option: (B)**
