To solve this problem, we must identify the physics principle that allows a car to turn on a flat corner. For a car to move in a circle, it requires a centripetal force directed toward the center of the turn. On a flat road, this centripetal force is provided solely by the **static friction** between the tires and the road.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the Physics Principles:**
The maximum speed occurs when the static friction reaches its maximum possible value, given by $F_f = \mu_s N$. Since the road is flat, the normal force $N$ is equal to the weight of the car: $N = mg$.
The centripetal force required is $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$.
Setting the maximum static friction equal to the centripetal force:
$$\mu_s mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

**2. Rearrange to solve for velocity ($v$):**
The mass $m$ cancels out from both sides:
$$\mu_s g = \frac{v^2}{r}$$
$$v = \sqrt{\mu_s \cdot g \cdot r}$$

**3. Substitute the given values:**
*   $\mu_s = 0.8$
*   $g = 9.8 \, \text{m s}^{-2}$
*   $r = 50 \, \text{m}$

$$v = \sqrt{0.8 \times 9.8 \times 50}$$
$$v = \sqrt{392}$$
$$v \approx 19.8 \, \text{m s}^{-1}$$

**4. Convert the units:**
The options are provided in $\text{km h}^{-1}$. To convert from $\text{m s}^{-1}$ to $\text{km h}^{-1}$, we multiply by $3.6$:
$$19.8 \times 3.6 \approx 71.28 \, \text{km h}^{-1}$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number provided in the options, we get $71 \, \text{km h}^{-1}$.

Comparing this to the choices provided:
A. $19 \, \text{km h}^{-1}$
B. $20 \, \text{km h}^{-1}$
C. $67 \, \text{km h}^{-1}$
D. $71 \, \text{km h}^{-1}$

The correct answer is **D**.
