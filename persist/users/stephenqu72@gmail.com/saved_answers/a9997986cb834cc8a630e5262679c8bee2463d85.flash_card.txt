### Front
What is the formula for the Total Mechanical Energy ($E$) of a satellite in a circular orbit, and how do you calculate the energy required to move between two different orbits?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The Total Mechanical Energy is the sum of gravitational potential and kinetic energy.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $E = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$. Energy required = $\Delta E = E_{final} - E_{initial}$.
- **When to use it:** Use when calculating work done or fuel energy required for orbital maneuvers (changing altitude).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting the factor of $2$ in the denominator of the mechanical energy formula (which differentiates it from potential energy, $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$).
