### Front
What is the nature of the acceleration of a projectile in motion (neglecting air resistance)?

### Back
- Key idea: The acceleration is constant and directed vertically downwards ($g$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_x = 0$, $a_y = g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$.
- When to use it: To resolve motion into independent horizontal (constant velocity) and vertical (constant acceleration) components.
- Common trap: Assuming the total speed of the projectile is constant or that acceleration changes; also, mistaking the projectile’s path as having a constant velocity.
