### Explanation
1.  **Nathaniel’s Frame of Reference:** Nathaniel is in a train moving at a constant velocity of $0.75c$. This is an **inertial frame of reference**. According to the principle of relativity, the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames. Therefore, Nathaniel can conduct experiments (such as verifying $F = ma$) just as if he were stationary on the ground, and he will successfully verify Newton’s second law.
2.  **Chelsea’s Frame of Reference:** Chelsea is in a train that is "slowing down" (decelerating). This is a **non-inertial frame of reference** because she is experiencing an acceleration. In a non-inertial frame, Newton’s laws do not hold in their standard form. An object inside the train would appear to accelerate even if no net external force is applied (due to "fictitious forces" or "inertial forces"). Consequently, Chelsea would be unable to verify Newton’s second law using standard methods because her observations would be skewed by the non-inertial nature of her environment.
3.  **Evaluating the options:**
    *   **A** is incorrect because Chelsea is in a non-inertial frame.
    *   **B is correct** because only Nathaniel is in an inertial frame where Newton's laws are valid.
