### Front
Differentiate between "accuracy" and "precision" in experimental measurements.

### Back
- Key idea: Accuracy is proximity to the true value; precision is the consistency (closeness) of repeated measurements.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When evaluating the validity and reliability of experimental data or analysing sets of results.
- Common trap: Assuming high precision implies high accuracy; a systematic error can lead to results that are highly precise but significantly inaccurate.
