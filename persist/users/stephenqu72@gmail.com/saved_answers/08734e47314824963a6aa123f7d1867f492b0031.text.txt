To find the temperature of the blackbody, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which relates the wavelength at which the emission of a blackbody is at its peak ($\lambda_{max}$) to its absolute temperature ($T$):

$$\lambda_{max} T = b$$

Where $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}$.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$):**
    Looking at the graph, the peak of the energy density curve occurs at $0.50 \text{ \mu m}$.
    We must convert this to meters:
    $$\lambda_{max} = 0.50 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$$

2.  **Rearrange Wien’s Law to solve for $T$:**
    $$T = \frac{b}{\lambda_{max}}$$

3.  **Substitute the values:**
    $$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}}{0.50 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}}$$
    $$T \approx 5796 \text{ K}$$

4.  **Compare with the options:**
    The calculated value is approximately $5800 \text{ K}$.
    *   (A) is far too low.
    *   (B) $5800 \text{ K}$ matches our calculation.
    *   (C) $5800 ^\circ\text{C}$ is equivalent to $6073 \text{ K}$, which is incorrect because the law requires absolute temperature (Kelvin).
    *   (D) is far too high.

The correct answer is **(B)**.
