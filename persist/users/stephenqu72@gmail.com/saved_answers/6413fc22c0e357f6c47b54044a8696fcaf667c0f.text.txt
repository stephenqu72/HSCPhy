To calculate the binding energy per nucleon of Nickel-62 ($_{28}^{62}\text{Ni}$), we follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the constituent particles
Nickel-62 has an atomic number ($Z$) of 28 (protons) and a mass number ($A$) of 62.
*   Number of protons ($Z$) = 28
*   Number of neutrons ($N$) = $A - Z = 62 - 28 = 34$

### 2. Calculate the theoretical mass
Using standard values:
*   Mass of a proton ($m_p$) $\approx 1.007276\text{ u}$
*   Mass of a neutron ($m_n$) $\approx 1.008665\text{ u}$

Total theoretical mass = $(28 \times 1.007276) + (34 \times 1.008665)$
Total theoretical mass = $28.203728 + 34.29461 = 62.498338\text{ u}$

### 3. Calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$)
Mass defect = Theoretical mass - Observed mass
$\Delta m = 62.498338\text{ u} - 61.9283449\text{ u} = 0.5699931\text{ u}$

### 4. Convert mass defect to energy
Using the conversion factor $1\text{ u} \approx 931.5\text{ MeV/c}^2$:
Total Binding Energy ($BE$) = $0.5699931 \times 931.5\text{ MeV} \approx 530.954\text{ MeV}$

### 5. Calculate binding energy per nucleon
$BE/\text{nucleon} = \frac{BE}{A} = \frac{530.954}{62} \approx 8.56\text{ MeV/nucleon}$

***

### How to solve this type of problem:
1.  **Determine the composition:** Always identify the number of protons and neutrons first.
2.  **Calculate the mass defect:** Find the difference between the sum of the individual parts and the actual measured nucleus mass. Ensure you use precise values for proton and neutron masses.
3.  **Use the correct energy conversion:** Use $931.5\text{ MeV}$ per atomic mass unit.
4.  **Normalize:** Divide the total binding energy by the mass number ($A$) to find the energy "per nucleon."
