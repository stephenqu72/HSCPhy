To solve this problem, we must analyze the forces acting on the stone at the top (Point B) and the bottom (Point A) of its circular path.

### 1. Identify the given values
*   Mass $m = 245\text{ g} = 0.245\text{ kg}$
*   Velocity $v = 4.00\text{ ms}^{-1}$
*   Radius $r = 120\text{ cm} = 1.20\text{ m}$
*   Acceleration due to gravity $g \approx 9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$

### 2. Force analysis
The net centripetal force $F_c$ is provided by the combination of tension $T$ and gravity $mg$. The formula for centripetal force is $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$.

**At the bottom (Point A):**
Tension acts upwards, and weight acts downwards.
$$T_A - mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
$$T_A = \frac{mv^2}{r} + mg$$

**At the top (Point B):**
Both tension and weight act downwards.
$$T_B + mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
$$T_B = \frac{mv^2}{r} - mg$$

### 3. Calculate the difference
We want the difference $T_A - T_B$:
$$T_A - T_B = \left( \frac{mv^2}{r} + mg \right) - \left( \frac{mv^2}{r} - mg \right)$$
$$T_A - T_B = 2mg$$

Substitute the values:
$$T_A - T_B = 2 \times 0.245\text{ kg} \times 9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$$
$$T_A - T_B = 4.802\text{ N}$$

Rounding to appropriate significant figures based on the provided data ($4.00$ and $120$ imply 3 sig figs):
**The difference in tension is $4.80\text{ N}$.**

***

### How to solve this type of problem:
1.  **Identify the Forces:** Always draw a free-body diagram for the points of interest. Remember that centripetal force is not a physical force itself, but the *resultant* of all forces acting toward the center.
2.  **Set up Equations:** Use Newton’s Second Law for circular motion ($F_{net} = \frac{mv^2}{r}$).
3.  **Algebraic Simplification:** Instead of calculating $T_A$ and $T_B$ separately (which can lead to rounding errors), subtract the equations algebraically first. You will find that for a constant speed, the centripetal component cancels out, leaving you with $2mg$.
