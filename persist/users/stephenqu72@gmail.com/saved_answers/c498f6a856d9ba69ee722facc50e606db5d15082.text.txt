To find the minimum speed $v_{min}$ for a car on a banked curve, we must consider the forces acting on the car: gravity ($mg$), the normal force ($N$), and static friction ($f_s = \mu_s N$).

### Derivation
At the minimum speed, the car tends to slide *down* the slope, so the frictional force $f_s$ acts *up* the slope. We resolve forces into vertical ($y$) and horizontal ($x$) components:

1. **Vertical balance ($y$-axis):**
   $$N \cos\theta - f_s \sin\theta - mg = 0 \implies N \cos\theta - \mu_s N \sin\theta = mg$$
   $$N(\cos\theta - \mu_s \sin\theta) = mg \implies N = \frac{mg}{\cos\theta - \mu_s \sin\theta}$$

2. **Horizontal (Centripetal) force ($x$-axis):**
   The net force towards the center of the curve is:
   $$N \sin\theta + f_s \cos\theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
   $$N(\sin\theta + \mu_s \cos\theta) = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

3. **Substitute $N$ into the horizontal equation:**
   $$\left( \frac{mg}{\cos\theta - \mu_s \sin\theta} \right) (\sin\theta + \mu_s \cos\theta) = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
   $$v^2 = rg \frac{\sin\theta + \mu_s \cos\theta}{\cos\theta - \mu_s \sin\theta} = rg \left( \frac{\tan\theta + \mu_s}{1 - \mu_s \tan\theta} \right)$$

### Calculation
Given: $r = 147 \text{ m}$, $\theta = 34.6^\circ$, $\mu_s = 0.382$, $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$.

First, calculate $\tan(34.6^\circ) \approx 0.6896$.
$$v^2 = 147 \times 9.8 \times \left( \frac{0.6896 + 0.382}{1 - (0.382 \times 0.6896)} \right)$$
$$v^2 = 1440.6 \times \left( \frac{1.0716}{1 - 0.2634} \right) = 1440.6 \times \left( \frac{1.0716}{0.7366} \right)$$
$$v^2 \approx 1440.6 \times 1.4548 \approx 2095.8$$
$$v = \sqrt{2095.8} \approx 45.78 \text{ m/s}$$

The minimum speed is approximately **$45.8 \text{ m/s}$**.
