### Part (a) Answer and Explanation

**Answer:**
Anna measures the length of the ruler to be $0.66\text{ m}$ (or $0.661\text{ m}$ to three significant figures).

**Explanation:**
1. **Identify the variables:** 
   - Proper length ($L_0$) = $1.00\text{ m}$ (this is the length measured by Matthew, who is in the same inertial frame as the ruler).
   - Velocity ($v$) = $0.75c$.
   - Lorentz factor ($\gamma$) = $\frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$.

2. **Calculate the Lorentz factor:**
   $$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.75)^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.5625}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.4375}} \approx 1.51186$$

3. **Apply the Length Contraction formula:**
   $$L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma} = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
   $$L = 1.00 \times \sqrt{0.4375} \approx 0.6614\text{ m}$$

**Answer:**

**Explanation:**
To earn full marks, your answer must include these three points:
1. **Reference Frames:** Explain that the measurement of length is relative to the observer's motion. Because Matthew is stationary relative to the ruler, he measures the "proper length." Because Anna is in a different inertial frame moving at relativistic speeds relative to the ruler, she measures a "contracted length."
2. **Postulates of Relativity:** Mention that the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames, and the speed of light is constant for all observers.
3. **No Contradiction:** Emphasize that there is no absolute spatial measurement. The discrepancy is not a "mistake" or a physical change in the ruler itself, but a fundamental property of space-time where physical dimensions are observed differently depending on the relative velocity of the observers.
