To solve this problem, we use the formula for the acceleration due to gravity on the surface of a planet:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Where:
- $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity
- $G$ is the gravitational constant
- $M$ is the mass of the planet
- $r$ is the radius of the planet

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1. **State the given information:**
   - The acceleration due to gravity is equal for both planets: $g_1 = g_2$
   - Planet 1 has 4 times the mass of Planet 2: $M_1 = 4M_2$

2. **Set up the equality:**
   Since $g_1 = g_2$, we can write:
   $$\frac{GM_1}{r_1^2} = \frac{GM_2}{r_2^2}$$

3. **Simplify the equation:**
   We can cancel out the gravitational constant $G$ from both sides:
   $$\frac{M_1}{r_1^2} = \frac{M_2}{r_2^2}$$

4. **Substitute the mass relationship ($M_1 = 4M_2$):**
   $$\frac{4M_2}{r_1^2} = \frac{M_2}{r_2^2}$$

5. **Solve for $r_1$ in terms of $r_2$:**
   Divide both sides by $M_2$ (assuming $M_2 \neq 0$):
   $$\frac{4}{r_1^2} = \frac{1}{r_2^2}$$
   
   Cross-multiply to isolate the radii:
   $$r_1^2 = 4r_2^2$$
   
   Take the square root of both sides:
   $$r_1 = \sqrt{4r_2^2}$$
   $$r_1 = 2r_2$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **A**.
