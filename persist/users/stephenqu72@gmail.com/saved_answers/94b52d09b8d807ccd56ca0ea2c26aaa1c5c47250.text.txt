To solve this problem, we will use the principles of uniform circular motion.

### (a) Calculate the angular velocity

**Step 1: Find the linear velocity ($v$)**
The plane travels $250\text{ m}$ in $2\text{ s}$.
$$v = \frac{\Delta d}{\Delta t} = \frac{250}{2} = 125\text{ m/s}$$

**Step 2: Find the angular velocity ($\omega$) in radians per second**
Using the relationship $v = r\omega$, where $r = 2100\text{ m}$:
$$\omega = \frac{v}{r} = \frac{125}{2100} \approx 0.05952\text{ rad/s}$$

**Step 3: Convert to degrees per second ($^\circ\text{s}^{-1}$)**
Since $1\text{ radian} = \frac{180^\circ}{\pi}$:
$$\omega = 0.05952 \times \frac{180}{\pi} \approx 3.41^\circ\text{s}^{-1}$$

### (b) At what angle are the wings tilted?

To turn in a horizontal circle, the horizontal component of the lift force must provide the centripetal force, while the vertical component must balance the weight of the plane ($mg$). 

If $\theta$ is the angle of tilt (bank angle), the forces are:
1. $L \cos\theta = mg$ (vertical balance)
2. $L \sin\theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ (centripetal force)

Dividing the two equations gives:
$$\tan\theta = \frac{mv^2/r}{mg} = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

Using $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$:
$$\tan\theta = \frac{125^2}{2100 \times 9.8}$$
$$\tan\theta = \frac{15625}{20580} \approx 0.7592$$
$$\theta = \arctan(0.7592) \approx 37.2^\circ$$

The wings are tilted at approximately **$37.2^\circ$**.
