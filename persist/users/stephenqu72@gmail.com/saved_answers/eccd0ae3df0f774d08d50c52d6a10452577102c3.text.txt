To calculate the maximum current flowing through the ring, we need to apply Faraday's Law of Induction in the context of a rotating coil.

### 1. Identify the given variables
*   Resistance $R = 2\ \Omega$
*   Magnetic field strength $B = 0.04\ \text{T}$
*   Angular velocity $\omega = 15\ \text{rad/s}$
*   Diameter $d = 15\ \text{cm} = 0.15\ \text{m}$
*   Radius $r = \frac{d}{2} = 0.075\ \text{m}$
*   Area $A = \pi r^2 = \pi (0.075)^2 \approx 0.01767\ \text{m}^2$

### 2. The Physics Principle
When a loop rotates in a uniform magnetic field, the magnetic flux $\Phi$ through the loop changes according to:
$$\Phi(t) = B A \cos(\omega t)$$
According to Faraday's Law, the induced electromotive force (EMF), $\varepsilon$, is the negative rate of change of magnetic flux:
$$\varepsilon = -\frac{d\Phi}{dt} = BA\omega \sin(\omega t)$$
The maximum EMF ($\varepsilon_{\text{max}}$) occurs when $\sin(\omega t) = 1$:
$$\varepsilon_{\text{max}} = BA\omega$$

### 3. Calculation
First, calculate the maximum EMF:
$$\varepsilon_{\text{max}} = 0.04\ \text{T} \times (\pi \times 0.075^2\ \text{m}^2) \times 15\ \text{rad/s}$$
$$\varepsilon_{\text{max}} = 0.04 \times 0.01767 \times 15 \approx 0.0106\ \text{V}$$

Now, use Ohm’s Law ($I = \frac{V}{R}$) to find the maximum current ($I_{\text{max}}$):
$$I_{\text{max}} = \frac{\varepsilon_{\text{max}}}{R} = \frac{0.0106\ \text{V}}{2\ \Omega}$$
$$I_{\text{max}} \approx 0.0053\ \text{A} = 5.3\ \text{mA}$$

**Answer:** The maximum current flowing through the ring is approximately **$5.3\ \text{mA}$**.
