To solve this problem, we must apply the formula for the magnetic force between two parallel current-carrying wires and Newton's Second Law of Motion.

### 1. Identify the given values:
*   Length of wires ($L$) = $2.00\text{ m}$
*   Current in wire 1 ($I_1$) = $25.0\text{ A}$
*   Current in wire 2 ($I_2$) = $50.0\text{ A}$
*   Separation distance ($r$) = $2.00\text{ mm} = 2.00 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$
*   Acceleration ($a$) = $0.200\text{ m s}^{-2}$
*   Permeability of free space ($\mu_0$) = $4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T m A}^{-1}$

### 2. Calculate the magnetic force ($F$):
The formula for the force between two parallel wires is:
$$F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 L}{2\pi r}$$

Substitute the values:
$$F = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times 25.0 \times 50.0 \times 2.00}{2\pi \times (2.00 \times 10^{-3})}$$

Simplify the expression:
$$F = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 25.0 \times 50.0 \times 2.00}{2.00 \times 10^{-3}}$$
$$F = \frac{5 \times 10^{-4}}{2 \times 10^{-3}} = 0.25\text{ N}$$

### 3. Calculate the mass ($m$):
Using Newton’s Second Law ($F = ma$), we can find the mass of one trolley:
$$m = \frac{F}{a}$$
$$m = \frac{0.25\text{ N}}{0.200\text{ m s}^{-2}}$$
$$m = 1.25\text{ kg}$$

Comparing our result to the options provided:
(A) $0.625\text{ kg}$
(B) $1.25\text{ kg}$
(C) $2.50\text{ kg}$
(D) $5.00\text{ kg}$

**The correct answer is (B).**
