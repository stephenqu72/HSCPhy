### Front
What does Kepler’s Second Law of planetary motion state regarding orbital motion?

### Back
- Key idea: A line segment joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas in equal time intervals.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\frac{dA}{dt} = \text{constant}$.
- When to use it: To relate a planet’s orbital speed to its distance from the Sun; planets move faster at perihelion (closer) and slower at aphelion (further).
- Common trap: Assuming orbital speed is constant; it is only constant in a circular orbit.
