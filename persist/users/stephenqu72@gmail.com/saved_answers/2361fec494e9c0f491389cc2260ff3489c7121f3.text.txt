To solve this problem, we use the principles of an **ideal transformer**. In an ideal transformer, we assume there is no power loss, meaning the power in the primary coil equals the power in the secondary coil ($P_p = P_s$).

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   Number of turns in the primary coil ($N_p$) = $1600$
*   Number of turns in the secondary coil ($N_s$) = $800$
*   Current in the secondary coil ($I_s$) = $3.34\text{ A}$ (Note: The phrasing "draws a current" in this context refers to the output load/secondary side).

### 2. The Relationship:
For an ideal transformer, the ratio of currents is inversely proportional to the ratio of turns:
$$\frac{I_p}{I_s} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$

Rearranging for the primary current ($I_p$):
$$I_p = I_s \times \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$

### 3. Calculation:
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$I_p = 3.34\text{ A} \times \frac{800}{1600}$$
$$I_p = 3.34\text{ A} \times 0.5$$
$$I_p = 1.67\text{ A}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **(B)**.
