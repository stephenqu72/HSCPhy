To solve this problem, we use Einstein's photoelectric equation, which relates the energy of incoming photons to the work function of the metal and the maximum kinetic energy of the ejected electrons:

$$E_k = E_{photon} - \Phi$$

Where:
- $E_{photon} = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$
- $\Phi = hf_0$ (Work function)

### 1. Identify the given values:
- Wavelength of light ($\lambda$) = $400\text{ nm} = 400 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$
- Threshold frequency ($f_0$) = $2 \times 10^7\text{ Hz}$
- Planck’s constant ($h$) $\approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$
- Speed of light ($c$) $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$

### 2. Calculate the energy of the incoming photon:
$$E_{photon} = \frac{hc}{\lambda} = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34})(3.00 \times 10^8)}{400 \times 10^{-9}} \approx 4.97 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$$

### 3. Calculate the work function ($\Phi$):
$$\Phi = hf_0 = (6.626 \times 10^{-34})(2 \times 10^7) \approx 1.325 \times 10^{-26}\text{ J}$$

### 4. Calculate Maximum Kinetic Energy ($E_k$):
$$E_k = 4.97 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J} - 1.325 \times 10^{-26}\text{ J}$$

Because $10^{-26}$ is significantly smaller than $10^{-19}$, the subtraction results in:
$$E_k \approx 4.97 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$$

Comparing this to the provided options, the value is dominated by the energy of the incident photon. Option **B** is the correct answer.
