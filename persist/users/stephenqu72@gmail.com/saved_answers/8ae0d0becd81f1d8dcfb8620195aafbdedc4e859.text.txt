To find the distance between the slits, we use the double-slit interference formula for constructive interference (maxima):

$$d \sin \theta = m \lambda$$

### Given Values:
*   Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $425\text{ nm} = 425 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$
*   Order of maximum ($m$) = $1$ (for the "first maximum")
*   Angle of deflection ($\theta$) = $5^{\circ}$

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $d$ (slit separation):**
    $$d = \frac{m \lambda}{\sin \theta}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values into the equation:**
    $$d = \frac{1 \times (425 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m})}{\sin 5^{\circ}}$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    *   $\sin 5^{\circ} \approx 0.087156$
    *   $d = \frac{425 \times 10^{-9}}{0.087156}$
    *   $d \approx 4876.3 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$
    *   $d \approx 4.8763 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}$

Comparing this to the provided options, it matches **(A) $4.88 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}$**.

In HSC Physics, when dealing with Young's Double Slit Experiment, always ensure your units are in standard SI units (meters). Convert nanometers ($10^{-9}$) to meters immediately to avoid calculation errors. The formula $d \sin \theta = m \lambda$ is valid here because the problem defines the "first maximum" ($m=1$) at a specific diffraction angle.
