### Answer

**(a) Describe the features of the spectra of stars that can be used to calculate their recessional velocity.**
To calculate recessional velocity, astronomers observe the **absorption lines** (or emission lines) in the star's spectrum. By comparing the observed wavelength of these lines to the known laboratory (rest) wavelengths, they measure the **Doppler shift**. Because distant galaxies are moving away, these spectral lines are shifted towards longer wavelengths, a phenomenon known as **redshift**. The magnitude of this shift ($z$) is directly proportional to the object’s recessional velocity.

**(b) With reference to the graph and your understanding of the Big Bang Theory, explain why Hubble’s Law provides evidence for the Big Bang.**
The graph shows a positive linear relationship between distance ($D$) and recessional velocity ($v$), represented by $v = H_0D$. This indicates that galaxies are moving away from us, with more distant galaxies receding at higher velocities. This implies that the universe is **expanding**. If we extrapolate this expansion backward in time, it suggests that all matter and energy in the universe originated from a single, hot, dense point (a singularity). Therefore, the observable expansion described by Hubble's Law is a primary pillar of evidence supporting the Big Bang Theory, as it contradicts a static, unchanging universe.
