To solve this problem, we use the principles of an **ideal transformer**, where the power input equals the power output ($P_{in} = P_{out}$), and the ratio of voltages is equal to the ratio of the number of turns.

### Step 1: Find the secondary voltage ($V_s$)
The transformer equation is:
$$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$
Given $V_p = 120\text{ V}$, $N_p = 1500$, and $N_s = 50$:
$$\frac{120}{V_s} = \frac{1500}{50}$$
$$\frac{120}{V_s} = 30$$
$$V_s = \frac{120}{30} = 4\text{ V}$$

### Step 2: Find the secondary current ($I_s$)
Since the resistor $R$ dissipates $35\text{ W}$, we use the power formula $P = V_s \times I_s$:
$$35 = 4 \times I_s$$
$$I_s = \frac{35}{4} = 8.75\text{ A} \approx 8.8\text{ A}$$

### Step 3: Find the primary current ($I_p$)
For an ideal transformer, the power in the primary circuit equals the power in the secondary circuit:
$$P_p = P_s$$
$$V_p \times I_p = 35\text{ W}$$
$$120 \times I_p = 35$$
$$I_p = \frac{35}{120} \approx 0.29\text{ A}$$

### Conclusion
The current in the primary coil is $0.29\text{ A}$ and the current in the secondary coil is $8.8\text{ A}$. Comparing this to the given options, the correct choice is **B**.
