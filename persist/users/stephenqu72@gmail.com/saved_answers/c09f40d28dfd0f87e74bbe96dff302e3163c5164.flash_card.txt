### Front
Explain the change in kinetic energy of a comet as it moves from aphelion to perihelion.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Conservation of mechanical energy ($E = K + U$). As the distance to the Sun decreases, gravitational potential energy ($U$) becomes more negative, causing kinetic energy ($K$) to increase.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\Delta K + \Delta U = 0$; $U = - \frac{G M m}{r}$
- **When to use it:** To explain changes in orbital speed due to work done by the gravitational field.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that gravitational potential energy is negative; as $r$ decreases, $U$ becomes *more* negative (decreases), which is why $K$ must *increase*.
