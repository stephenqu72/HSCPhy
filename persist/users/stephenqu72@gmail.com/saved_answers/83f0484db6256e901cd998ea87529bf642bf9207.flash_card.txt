### Front
How do you calculate the power dissipated by a specific resistor in a circuit branch?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Power ($P$) is the rate of energy transfer, calculated using current ($I$) and resistance ($R$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $P = I^2R$; where $I$ is the current flowing *through that specific component*.
- **When to use it:** When calculating energy consumption per second ($1\ W = 1\ J/s$) for a specific resistor.
- **Common trap:** Using the *total* circuit current ($I_{total}$) instead of the specific branch current ($I_{branch}$) when calculating power for a single resistor in a parallel circuit.
