### Front
What is the scientific term for the consistency of data obtained through repeated trials of an investigation?

### Back
- Key idea: Reliability refers to how consistent results are when an investigation is repeated under the same conditions.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Use when assessing if repeated trials yield similar data; it is improved by increasing the sample size or number of trials.
- Common trap: Confusing it with **accuracy** (closeness to the "true" value) or **validity** (whether the experiment tests the intended hypothesis/controls all variables).
