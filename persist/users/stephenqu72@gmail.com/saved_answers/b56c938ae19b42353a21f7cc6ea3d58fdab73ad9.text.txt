### Explanation
To understand why this is the correct answer, we must analyze the physics of banked curves in uniform circular motion.

1.  **The Physics of Banked Curves:** When a train travels around a curved track that is banked at an angle $\theta$ to the horizontal, it experiences two primary forces:
    *   **Weight ($mg$):** Acting vertically downwards.
    *   **Normal Force ($N$):** Acting perpendicular to the surface of the track.

2.  **Resolving the Normal Force:** The normal force ($N$) acts at an angle to the vertical. We can resolve this force into two components:
    *   The **vertical component** ($N\cos\theta$): This must balance the weight of the train so that it does not move vertically. Therefore, $N\cos\theta = mg$.
    *   The **horizontal component** ($N\sin\theta$): This component points toward the center of the circular path. This is the net force providing the required centripetal force ($F_c$) to keep the train on the curved track.

3.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   (A) is incorrect because the gravitational force is constant, while the centripetal force depends on the train's speed and the radius of the turn.
    *   (B) is incorrect because the normal force is larger than the centripetal force (the centripetal force is only a *component* of the normal force).
    *   **(C) is correct** because the horizontal component of the normal force is exactly what provides the centripetal force required for circular motion.
    *   (D) is incorrect because the normal force is perpendicular to the track surface, while the gravitational force is always vertical.

By banking the track, we allow the normal force to "contribute" its horizontal component to act as the centripetal force, reducing the reliance on friction to keep the train on the tracks.
