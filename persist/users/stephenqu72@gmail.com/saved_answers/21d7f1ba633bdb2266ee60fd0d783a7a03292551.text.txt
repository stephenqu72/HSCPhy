### Answer
The correct answer is **(B) 1.2 N**.

### Explanation
To calculate the magnitude of the magnetic force acting on a current-carrying wire in a uniform magnetic field, we use the formula:
$$F = BIl \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
*   $F$ is the magnetic force (in Newtons)
*   $B$ is the magnetic field strength ($3\text{ T}$)
*   $I$ is the current ($4\text{ A}$)
*   $l$ is the length of the wire in the magnetic field
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the wire and the magnetic field ($30^\circ$)

**Step 1: Determine the length of the wire ($l$)**
From the diagram, we are given a perpendicular height $h = 10\text{ cm} = 0.1\text{ m}$. This represents the opposite side of a right-angled triangle formed by the wire and the magnetic field lines. We can find the length of the wire ($l$) using trigonometry:
$$\sin(30^\circ) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \frac{0.1}{l}$$
$$l = \frac{0.1}{\sin(30^\circ)}$$

**Step 2: Calculate the force**
Substitute the values into the force formula:
$$F = B \times I \times l \times \sin(\theta)$$
Since $l = \frac{0.1}{\sin(30^\circ)}$, we substitute this into the equation:
$$F = 3 \times 4 \times \left( \frac{0.1}{\sin(30^\circ)} \right) \times \sin(30^\circ)$$

The $\sin(30^\circ)$ terms cancel out:
$$F = 3 \times 4 \times 0.1$$
$$F = 12 \times 0.1$$
$$F = 1.2\text{ N}$$

Thus, the magnetic force acting on the wire is **1.2 N**.
