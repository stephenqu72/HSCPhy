To determine the acceleration due to gravity on the surface of Planet Y, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation.

### Answer
The correct answer is **D. $8g$**.

### Explanation
1. **Identify the formula:** The acceleration due to gravity $g$ on the surface of a planet is given by:
   $$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$
   where $G$ is the gravitational constant, $M$ is the mass of the planet, and $r$ is the radius of the planet.

2. **Define the variables for Earth:**
   $$g_{\text{Earth}} = \frac{GM_E}{r_E^2} = g$$

3. **Define the variables for Planet Y:**
   According to the problem, Planet Y has:
   - Mass $M_Y = 2M_E$
   - Radius $r_Y = 0.5r_E$ (or $\frac{1}{2}r_E$)

4. **Calculate $g$ for Planet Y:**
   Substitute these values into the gravitational formula:
   $$g_Y = \frac{G(2M_E)}{(\frac{1}{2}r_E)^2}$$

5. **Simplify the expression:**
   $$g_Y = \frac{2GM_E}{\frac{1}{4}r_E^2}$$
   $$g_Y = 2 \times 4 \times \frac{GM_E}{r_E^2}$$
   $$g_Y = 8 \times \frac{GM_E}{r_E^2}$$

6. **Relate back to Earth's gravity:**
   Since $\frac{GM_E}{r_E^2} = g$, we conclude:
   $$g_Y = 8g$$

This demonstrates that because the acceleration is proportional to the mass and inversely proportional to the square of the radius, doubling the mass increases gravity by a factor of 2, and halving the radius increases gravity by a factor of $2^2 = 4$. Combining these, $2 \times 4 = 8$.
