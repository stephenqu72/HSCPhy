The correct answer is **(D)**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must understand the historical and scientific context of quantum mechanics:

1.  **Distinguishing the Options:**
    *   **(A) Postulating that particles can behave as waves:** This is attributed to **Louis de Broglie** ($p = \frac{h}{\lambda}$), who proposed that matter possesses wave-like properties.
    *   **(B) Postulating that waves can behave as particles:** This refers to the wave-particle duality of light, primarily established by **Albert Einstein** (photoelectric effect) and **Max Planck**.
    *   **(C) Explaining the wavelengths of the emission lines in the spectrum of hydrogen:** This is primarily the work of **Niels Bohr**, who developed the Bohr model of the atom to explain quantized energy levels and spectral lines.
    *   **(D) Describing the likelihood of finding an electron in a certain position:** This is the core contribution of **Erwin Schrödinger**. He developed the Schrödinger equation, which describes how the quantum state of a physical system changes over time. The solutions to this equation are wave functions ($\psi$), and the square of the magnitude of the wave function ($|\psi|^2$) provides the probability density—the probability of finding an electron at a specific point in space. This shifted the atomic model from "fixed orbits" (Bohr) to "probability clouds" (quantum mechanical model).

2.  **Summary:**
    Schrödinger’s work moved physics away from deterministic trajectories toward a probabilistic interpretation of quantum systems. Therefore, option (D) is the most accurate description of his contribution.
