### Front
How does the fringe spacing in Young’s double-slit experiment change when light enters a medium with a higher refractive index?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The wavelength of light decreases in a denser medium ($\lambda_n = \lambda/n$), causing the diffraction pattern to compress.
- **Formula:** $d \sin(\theta) = n\lambda$
- **When to use it:** To predict shifts in interference patterns when changing the medium between the slits and the screen.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the frequency changes; frequency is constant, only wavelength and speed decrease.
