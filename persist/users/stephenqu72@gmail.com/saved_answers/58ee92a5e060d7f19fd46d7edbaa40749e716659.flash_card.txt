### Front
What is the relationship between applied force, distance from the pivot, and torque?

### Back
- Key idea: Torque ($\tau$) is the rotational equivalent of force; it is the turning effect produced by a force acting at a distance from a pivot.
- Formula / law: $\tau = F \cdot d$ (where $F$ is perpendicular to the lever arm).
- When to use it: To calculate the rotational effectiveness of a force in equilibrium problems or mechanical advantage scenarios.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert distances from cm to SI units (metres) or failing to account for multiple forces acting on the same lever arm.
