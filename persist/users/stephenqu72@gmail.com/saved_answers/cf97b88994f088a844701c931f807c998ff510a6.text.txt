To solve this question, we must apply Einstein’s photoelectric equation, which is fundamental to the study of quantum physics in the HSC Physics syllabus.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(A)**.

### Explanation
Einstein's photoelectric equation is given by:
$$K_{max} = hf - \Phi$$

Where:
*   $K_{max}$ is the maximum kinetic energy of the emitted photoelectrons.
*   $h$ is Planck’s constant.
*   $f$ is the frequency of the incident light.
*   $\Phi$ (phi) is the work function of the metal (the minimum energy required to remove an electron from the surface).

To increase $K_{max}$, we must either increase the energy of the incident photons ($hf$) or decrease the energy required to remove the electron ($\Phi$).

**Analyzing the options:**
*   **(A) Changing the cathode material to a metal with a lower work function:** If $\Phi$ decreases, the term $(hf - \Phi)$ increases, resulting in a higher $K_{max}$. This is correct.
*   **(B) Changing yellow light to red light:** Red light has a longer wavelength and therefore a lower frequency than yellow light. Decreasing $f$ decreases the photon energy ($hf$), which decreases $K_{max}$.
*   **(C) Increasing the intensity of the incident yellow light:** Intensity affects the *number* of photons hitting the surface per second, which increases the *number* of photoelectrons emitted (the current), but it does not change the energy of individual photons. Therefore, $K_{max}$ remains unchanged.
*   **(D) Decreasing the distance between the cathode and the anode:** This may affect the collection efficiency of the photoelectrons, but it has no effect on the energy with which they are emitted from the cathode surface.
