To solve this question, we must first define what "freefall" means in a physics context.

### Physics Concept: Freefall
An object is in **freefall** if the only force acting upon it is gravity. Because the particle in the question is neutral, it is not subject to electromagnetic forces (like the Lorentz force). Therefore, as long as it is in space near Earth, the only significant force acting on it is the gravitational pull of the Earth.

According to Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation, the force of gravity is given by:
$$F_g = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$$
This force is always directed toward the center of the Earth. Regardless of the particle's velocity, if no other forces (like air resistance or propulsion) are acting on it, it is in freefall. 

### Analyzing the Options
*   **A, B, and D:** These describe paths where an object is moving under the influence of gravity alone. An object dropped straight down (A), orbiting in a curved path (B), or passing by the Earth on a hyperbolic trajectory (D) are all experiencing only gravitational acceleration.
*   **C:** If an object is moving in a "curved path away from Earth," it implies the object is moving against the direction of the gravitational field. For a neutral particle to move away from Earth while experiencing *only* the downward pull of gravity, it must have had significant initial velocity. However, the path described as "curving away" (deflecting away from the gravitational source) usually implies a repulsive force or an external maneuver. Since the only force present is gravity (which is strictly attractive), an object cannot "curve away" from Earth under gravity alone. It can be *moving* away, but its path would be curving *towards* the Earth (the gravitational pull is always tugging it back).

The statement in **C** is physically impossible for a neutral particle under the sole influence of Earth's gravity.

**Correct Answer: C**
