### Part a)
**Solution:**
We use the equations of motion (SUVAT). Let the upward direction be positive.
- Initial velocity ($u$) = ?
- Displacement ($s$) = $1.8\text{ m}$ (at peak B)
- Final velocity at peak ($v$) = $0\text{ m/s}$
- Acceleration ($a$) = $-9.8\text{ m/s}^2$

Using $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$:
$$0 = u^2 + 2(-9.8)(1.8)$$
$$u^2 = 35.28 \implies u = 5.94\text{ m/s}$$

Now, find the time to reach peak B using $v = u + at$:
$$0 = 5.94 - 9.8t_{up}$$
$$t_{up} = 0.606\text{ s}$$

Since the motion is symmetrical, the total time in the air is $t_{total} = 2 \times 0.606 \approx 1.2\text{ s}$.

### Part b)
**Solution:**
The ball takes $0.606\text{ s}$ to travel from A to B. The racquet head moves from C to B in this same time.
The distance traveled by the racquet is a quarter-circle arc of radius $r = 1.8\text{ m}$.
$$Distance = \frac{1}{4} \times (2\pi r) = \frac{1}{2} \pi (1.8) \approx 2.827\text{ m}$$
$$Speed = \frac{Distance}{time} = \frac{2.827}{0.606} \approx 4.66\text{ m/s}$$

### Part c)
**Solution:**
First, find the time ($t$) it takes for the ball to reach the net ($12\text{ m}$ away) using horizontal velocity ($v_x = 24\text{ m/s}$):
$$t = \frac{d}{v_x} = \frac{12}{24} = 0.5\text{ s}$$

Next, find the vertical displacement ($s_y$) of the ball in $0.5\text{ s}$. Initial vertical velocity ($u_y$) is $0$ because it is launched horizontally:
$$s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$$
$$s_y = 0 + \frac{1}{2}(-9.8)(0.5)^2 = -1.225\text{ m}$$

The ball was launched at a total height of $3.6\text{ m}$ ($1.8\text{ m} + 1.8\text{ m}$).
Height above ground = $3.6 - 1.225 = 2.375\text{ m}$.
The net is $0.90\text{ m}$ high. The clearance above the net is:
$$2.375 - 0.90 = 1.475\text{ m} \approx 1.48\text{ m}$$
