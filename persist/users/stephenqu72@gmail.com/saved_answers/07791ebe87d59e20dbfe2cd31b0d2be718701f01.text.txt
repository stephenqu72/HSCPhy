### Explanation
In nuclear physics, the stability of a nucleus is determined by its **binding energy per nucleon**. A higher binding energy per nucleon indicates a more stable nucleus. 

1.  **Analyze the stability:**
    *   Binding energy per nucleon for $W = 1.11 \text{ MeV}$
    *   Binding energy per nucleon for $X = 2.83 \text{ MeV}$
    *   Binding energy per nucleon for $Y = 7.07 \text{ MeV}$
    Comparing these values, $Y$ has a significantly higher binding energy per nucleon than both $W$ and $X$. This means that the product $Y$ is much more stable than the reactants $W$ and $X$.

2.  **Energy change in reactions:**
    A nuclear reaction releases energy (exothermic) if the total binding energy of the products is greater than the total binding energy of the reactants. 
    *   Total binding energy of reactants ($W + X$) = $2.224566 + 8.481798 = 10.706364 \text{ MeV}$.
    *   The total binding energy of the products is $BE(Y) + BE(Z)$. 
    Since $BE(Y)$ alone ($28.29566 \text{ MeV}$) is already significantly greater than the sum of the reactants' binding energies ($10.706364 \text{ MeV}$), the total binding energy of the products will inevitably be greater than that of the reactants.
