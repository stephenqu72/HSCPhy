### Answer
The correct answer is **(C)**: L is negative, R is positive.

### Explanation
To determine the polarity of point charges based on an electric field diagram, you must apply the standard convention for electric field lines:

1.  **Direction of Field Lines:** By definition, electric field lines originate from positive charges and terminate on negative charges. In other words, the arrows on the lines always point **away** from a positive charge and **towards** a negative charge.
2.  **Analyzing Charge L:** Look at the arrows near charge L. The arrows are pointing directly towards the center of L. Since field lines point towards negative charges, **L must be negative**.
3.  **Analyzing Charge R:** Look at the arrows near charge R. The arrows are pointing directly away from the center of R. Since field lines point away from positive charges, **R must be positive**.
4.  **Confirming the interaction:** We can also see that the field lines travel from R to L, which is consistent with the movement of a field from a positive source to a negative sink.

Therefore, L is negative and R is positive, which matches option (C).
