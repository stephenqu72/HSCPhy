To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of circular motion and Newton’s Second Law.

### Answer
The car must travel at a speed of approximately $10.84 \text{ m/s}$.

### Explanation
**1. Identify the forces at the peak:**
At the peak of the loop, both gravity ($mg$) and the normal force ($N$) act downwards on the driver toward the center of the circular path. The net force provides the centripetal force required for circular motion:
$$\sum F = F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
$$N + mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

**2. Interpret the condition:**
The question states the driver feels a normal force equivalent to his weight force, meaning $N = mg$. Substituting this into our equation:
$$mg + mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
$$2mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

**3. Simplify and solve for $v$:**
Note that the mass of the driver ($m$) cancels out on both sides:
$$2g = \frac{v^2}{r}$$
$$v^2 = 2gr$$
$$v = \sqrt{2gr}$$

**4. Calculate:**
*   The diameter is $12 \text{ m}$, so the radius $r = 6 \text{ m}$.
*   We use the acceleration due to gravity $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$.
*   $v = \sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 6}$
*   $v = \sqrt{117.6} \approx 10.84435 \text{ m/s}$

Rounding to appropriate significant figures, the speed is $10.8 \text{ m/s}$. (Note: The mass of the car is irrelevant to the driver's experience of the normal force, as the motion is independent of the car's mass in this specific calculation).
