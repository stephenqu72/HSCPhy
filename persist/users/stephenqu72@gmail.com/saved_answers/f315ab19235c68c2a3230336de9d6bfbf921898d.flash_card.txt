### Front
Why are space launch sites ideally located near the Earth's equator?

### Back
- Key idea: Launching eastward at the equator provides the maximum "free" tangential velocity boost from Earth’s rotation, reducing the fuel required to reach orbital velocity.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \omega r$ (where $\omega$ is constant and $r$ is max at the equator).
- When to use it: When asked about orbital mechanics, launch efficiency, or satellite deployment strategies.
- Common trap: Attributing the benefit to lower gravity ($g$) or thinner air; while both are true, the rotational velocity boost is significantly more impactful.
