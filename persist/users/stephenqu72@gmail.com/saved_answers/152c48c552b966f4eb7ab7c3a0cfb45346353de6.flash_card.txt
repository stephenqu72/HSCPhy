### Front
What are the standard criteria for evaluating a scientific model of a complex system (e.g., orbital mechanics) in an HSC Physics context?

### Back
- Key idea: Models simplify reality to predict outcomes but always involve trade-offs.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Any question asking to "evaluate," "justify," or "assess" a model, diagram, or simulation.
- Common trap: Only listing limitations. You **must** also state a "usefulness" (e.g., visualising complex motion, calculating trajectory) to gain full marks. Always address 3D vs 2D, scale, and ignored variables (e.g., N-body effects).
