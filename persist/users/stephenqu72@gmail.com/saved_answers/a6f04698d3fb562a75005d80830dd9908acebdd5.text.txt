To determine the magnitude of the force on a current-carrying conductor in a uniform magnetic field, we use the formula:

$$F = BIl\sin(\theta)$$

Where:
- $F$ is the magnetic force (in Newtons)
- $B$ is the magnetic field strength (in Tesla)
- $I$ is the current (in Amperes)
- $l$ is the length of the conductor (in meters)
- $\theta$ is the angle between the **direction of the current** and the **direction of the magnetic field**.

    *   $B = 0.12\text{ T}$

    *   $l = l$
2.  **Determine the angle $\theta$:**
    *   The magnetic field is represented by the 'x' symbols, which indicate that the field is directed **into the page**.
    *   The conductor lies on the plane of the page at an angle of $25^\circ$ to the horizontal.
    *   Crucially, because the magnetic field points directly into the page (perpendicular to the plane of the page) and the wire lies within the plane of the page, the wire is perpendicular to the magnetic field.
    *   When a wire lies in a plane and the magnetic field is perpendicular to that plane, the angle $\theta$ between the wire and the magnetic field lines is $90^\circ$.
    *   Since $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$, the formula simplifies to $F = BIl$.

3.  **Evaluate the options:**
