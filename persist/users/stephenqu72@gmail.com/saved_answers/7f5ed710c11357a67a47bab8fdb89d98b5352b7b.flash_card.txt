### Front
How do you experimentally determine the horizontal launch speed ($v_x$) of a projectile?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Use vertical displacement ($h$) to find time of flight ($t$), then use horizontal range ($d$) to find velocity.
- **Formula:** $v_x = \frac{d}{\sqrt{2h/g}}$
- **When to use it:** Projectile motion experiments where the launch is perfectly horizontal ($u_y = 0$).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that horizontal velocity ($v_x$) is constant, while vertical velocity ($v_y$) is affected by gravity ($g$). Ensure you average multiple trials of $d$ to maintain reliability.
