### Front
Which foundational principles of motion are attributed to Galileo Galilei in the context of HSC Physics?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Objects fall with constant acceleration ($d \propto t^2$) and projectile motion consists of independent horizontal and vertical components.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $d = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$ (kinematic foundation); No fixed constant (Galileo did not determine $g = 9.8 \text{ ms}^{-2}$).
- **When to use it:** To distinguish Galileo’s kinematic observations from Newton’s later dynamical laws (Universal Gravitation).
- **Common trap:** Attributing the inverse-square law ($1/r^2$) or the mass-dependent force of gravity to Galileo; these are Newton’s contributions.
