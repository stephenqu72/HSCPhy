### Analysis

1.  **Velocity:**
    *   The velocity vector is always **tangent** to the path of the projectile at any given point.
    *   At point **X** (on the way up), the velocity is directed diagonally upwards and to the right ($\nearrow$).
    *   At point **Y** (the peak), the vertical component of velocity is zero, so the velocity is purely horizontal, directed to the right ($\rightarrow$).
    *   At point **Z** (on the way down), the velocity is directed diagonally downwards and to the right ($\searrow$).

2.  **Acceleration:**
    *   In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), the **only** force acting on the object is gravity, which acts vertically downwards.
    *   According to Newton's Second Law ($F = ma$), the acceleration is always in the direction of the net force.
    *   Therefore, the acceleration is constant, pointing **vertically downwards** ($\downarrow$) at all points along the trajectory (including at the peak, point Y).

### Explanation Summary
*   Velocity is tangent to the path, so its direction changes throughout the flight.
*   Acceleration is constant and always directed towards the center of the Earth (downwards) because gravity is the only force acting on the projectile. A common misconception is that acceleration is zero at the peak; however, if acceleration were zero at the peak, the object would continue to move in a straight horizontal line forever and never return to the ground.
