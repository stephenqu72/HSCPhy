To solve these physics problems, we must first convert all units to the SI base units: meters ($m$), seconds ($s$), and kilograms ($kg$).

**Given values:**
- Orbital radius $r = 23\,500 \text{ km} = 2.35 \times 10^7 \text{ m}$
- Period $T = 30.4 \text{ hours} = 30.4 \times 3600 \text{ s} = 109\,440 \text{ s}$
- Radius of Mars $R_M = 3.4 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
- Gravitational constant $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{ kg}^{-2}$

---

### a) What is the orbital velocity of Deimos?
The orbital velocity $v$ for a circular orbit is the circumference divided by the period:
$$v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$$
$$v = \frac{2 \pi \times 2.35 \times 10^7}{109\,440} \approx 1349.3 \text{ m/s}$$
**Answer:** The orbital velocity is approximately $1.35 \times 10^3 \text{ m/s}$.

---

### b) What is the mass of Mars?
We use the formula for orbital velocity derived from equating gravitational force to centripetal force ($v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$), which rearranges to:
$$M = \frac{v^2 r}{G}$$
$$M = \frac{(1349.3)^2 \times 2.35 \times 10^7}{6.67 \times 10^{-11}}$$
$$M \approx \frac{1.82 \times 10^6 \times 2.35 \times 10^7}{6.67 \times 10^{-11}} \approx 6.41 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$$
**Answer:** The mass of Mars is approximately $6.41 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$.

---

### c) What is the gravitational field strength at the surface of Mars?
The gravitational field strength $g$ is given by:
$$g = \frac{GM}{R_M^2}$$
$$g = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (6.41 \times 10^{23})}{(3.4 \times 10^6)^2}$$
$$g = \frac{4.275 \times 10^{13}}{1.156 \times 10^{13}} \approx 3.70 \text{ m/s}^2$$
**Answer:** The gravitational field strength at the surface of Mars is $3.70 \text{ m/s}^2$.
