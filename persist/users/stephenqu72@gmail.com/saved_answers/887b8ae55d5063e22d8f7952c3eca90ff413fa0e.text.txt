To solve this problem, we must break the missile's flight into two distinct phases: the powered phase and the projectile (free-fall) phase.

### Part (a): Distance travelled during the first 10 seconds
The missile starts with an initial velocity $u = 300.0\text{ ms}^{-1}$ and undergoes constant acceleration $a = 25.0\text{ ms}^{-2}$ for time $t = 10.0\text{ s}$. Using the displacement formula:
$$s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$
$$s = (300.0)(10.0) + \frac{1}{2}(25.0)(10.0)^2$$
$$s = 3000 + 1250 = 4250\text{ m}$$
**The distance travelled during the first 10 seconds is $4250\text{ m}$.**

---

### Part (b): Determine the total horizontal distance
First, find the final velocity of the missile at the end of the 10 seconds:
$$v = u + at = 300.0 + (25.0)(10.0) = 550.0\text{ ms}^{-1}$$
This $v$ is the horizontal velocity $v_x$ when the engine cuts out.

Next, find the time it takes to hit the ground from the altitude of $h = 200.0\text{ m}$. Using the vertical motion equation $h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$ (assuming $g = 9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$):
$$200.0 = \frac{1}{2}(9.8)t^2$$
$$t^2 = \frac{400.0}{9.8} \approx 40.816$$
$$t \approx 6.389\text{ s}$$

Now, calculate the horizontal distance covered during this free-fall phase:
$$d_2 = v_x \times t = 550.0 \times 6.389 \approx 3513.95\text{ m}$$

Total horizontal distance $D = d_1 + d_2$:
$$D = 4250 + 3513.95 = 7763.95\text{ m}$$
**Maverick should fire the missile from approximately $7764\text{ m}$ away.**

---

### Explanation of the Method
1.  **Phase 1 (Kinematics):** Use standard SUVAT equations to find displacement ($s$) and final velocity ($v$) for the powered phase.
2.  **Phase 2 (Projectile Motion):** Recognize that after 10s, the missile becomes a projectile with initial horizontal velocity $v$ and zero initial vertical velocity.
3.  **Vertical-Horizontal Coupling:** Calculate the time to hit the ground using the vertical drop distance. Since horizontal velocity remains constant in projectile motion, multiply the final horizontal velocity from phase 1 by the time calculated from the drop height to find the second leg of the journey. Sum both distances for the final answer.
