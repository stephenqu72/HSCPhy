To solve this problem, we must first derive the expression for the total energy of an orbiting satellite and then represent the relationships graphically.

### 1. Derivation of Total Energy
For a satellite of mass $m$ in a stable circular orbit around a planet of mass $M$ at a radius $r$, the centripetal force is provided by the gravitational force:
$$\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$$
From this, we find the expression for kinetic energy ($E_k$):
$$mv^2 = \frac{GMm}{r}$$
$$E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{GMm}{2r}$$

The gravitational potential energy ($E_p$) is defined as:
$$E_p = -\frac{GMm}{r}$$

The total energy ($E_T$) is the sum of kinetic and potential energy:
$$E_T = E_k + E_p$$
$$E_T = \frac{GMm}{2r} - \frac{GMm}{r}$$
$$E_T = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$$

### 2. Graphical Representation
To graph these relationships, note that all three energies are proportional to $1/r$:
*   **$E_p$ (Gravitational Potential Energy):** A negative curve approaching 0 as $r \to \infty$. It is the "deepest" (most negative).
*   **$E_k$ (Kinetic Energy):** A positive curve, exactly half the magnitude of the potential energy.
*   **$E_T$ (Total Energy):** A negative curve, which is the sum of $E_k$ and $E_p$. It lies exactly halfway between the $x$-axis and the $E_p$ curve.

**Sketch description:**
*   **Y-axis:** Energy ($E$)
*   **X-axis:** Radius ($r$)
*   Draw $E_p = -k/r$ (in the negative quadrant).
*   Draw $E_k = +k/(2r)$ (in the positive quadrant).
*   Draw $E_T = -k/(2r)$ (in the negative quadrant, above the $E_p$ line).

### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Start with Physics Fundamentals:** Always begin with the equivalence of gravitational force and centripetal force ($F_g = F_c$).
2.  **Define Energy expressions:** Clearly write down the standard equations for $E_k$ and $E_p$.
3.  **Algebraic Substitution:** Substitute the velocity term derived from the force balance into the kinetic energy equation so that all terms are functions of $r$.
4.  **Summation:** Combine the terms to find $E_T$.
5.  **Graphing Logic:** When drawing, emphasize the magnitudes ($E_p$ is twice $E_T$ in magnitude, and $E_k$ is equal in magnitude to $E_T$ but positive). Label your axes clearly.
