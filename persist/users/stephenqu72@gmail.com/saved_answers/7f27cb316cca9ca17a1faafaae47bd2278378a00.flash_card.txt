### Front
Define electric potential difference ($V$) in terms of work and charge.

### Back
- Key idea: The work done per unit positive charge to move it between two points.
- Formula / law: $V = \frac{W}{q}$
- When to use it: To calculate the energy required to move a charge or determine the potential drop across a component.
- Common trap: Confusing potential difference (energy per unit charge) with total work (Joules) or power (Watts).
