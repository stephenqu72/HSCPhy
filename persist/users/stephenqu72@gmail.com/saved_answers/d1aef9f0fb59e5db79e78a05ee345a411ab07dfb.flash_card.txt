### Front
How do you derive the constant power ($P$) of an electric kettle given a linear temperature increase over time ($T = kt$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Power is the rate of energy transfer ($P = Q/t$), where $Q$ is the heat absorbed ($mc\Delta T$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $P = \frac{mc\Delta T}{t}$ where $\Delta T = kt$; thus $P = mc_wk$.
- When to use it: To determine power from temperature-time graphs where the gradient ($k$) represents the rate of heating.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the time $t$ cancels out when the temperature increase is perfectly linear.
