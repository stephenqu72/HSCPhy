To solve this problem, we use **Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion**, which states that the square of the orbital period ($T$) of a planet or moon is directly proportional to the cube of its mean orbital radius ($r$):

$$\frac{r^3}{T^2} = k$$

Since both moons orbit the same central body (Jupiter), the constant $k$ is the same for both. Therefore, we can set up the following ratio:

$$\frac{r_I^3}{T_I^2} = \frac{r_E^3}{T_E^2}$$

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   $T_I = 1.77 \text{ days}$
*   $T_E = 3.55 \text{ days}$
*   $r_E = 671,000 \text{ km}$
*   $r_I = ?$

**2. Rearrange the formula to solve for $r_I$:**
$$r_I^3 = r_E^3 \times \frac{T_I^2}{T_E^2}$$
$$r_I = \sqrt[3]{r_E^3 \times \left(\frac{T_I}{T_E}\right)^2}$$
$$r_I = r_E \times \left(\frac{T_I}{T_E}\right)^{2/3}$$

**3. Substitute the values:**
$$r_I = 671,000 \times \left(\frac{1.77}{3.55}\right)^{2/3}$$
$$r_I \approx 671,000 \times (0.49859)^{0.6667}$$
$$r_I \approx 671,000 \times 0.62877$$
$$r_I \approx 421,909 \text{ km}$$

Comparing our result to the options provided, the correct answer is **(B)**.
