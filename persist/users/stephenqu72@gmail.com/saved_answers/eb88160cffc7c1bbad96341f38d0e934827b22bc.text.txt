To determine which graph shows a wheel slowing down, we must analyze the relationship between angular velocity ($\omega$) and time ($t$).

### Explanation
"Slowing down" means that the magnitude of the angular velocity, $|\omega|$, is decreasing over time as it approaches zero.

*   **Graph A:** The angular velocity starts at zero and increases linearly. This represents uniform angular acceleration (speeding up).
*   **Graph B:** The angular velocity is negative and the magnitude $|\omega|$ is increasing (it is becoming "more negative"). This represents the wheel speeding up in the opposite direction of rotation.
*   **Graph C:** The angular velocity is positive and increasing at an accelerating rate. This represents the wheel speeding up.
*   **Graph D:** The angular velocity starts at a negative value (representing rotation in a specific direction) and approaches zero over time. As the value of $\omega$ moves from a negative number toward zero, the absolute value $|\omega|$ is decreasing. This means the wheel is losing speed.

Therefore, Graph D is the correct representation of a wheel slowing down.

**Correct Answer: (D)**
