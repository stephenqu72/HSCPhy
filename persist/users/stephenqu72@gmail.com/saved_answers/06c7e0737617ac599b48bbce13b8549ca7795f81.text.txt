To solve this problem, we need to analyze the physics of projectile motion in the absence of air resistance.

### Analysis of the Motion
When the ball leaves the table, it is under the influence of only one force: **gravity**, acting vertically downwards.

1.  **Horizontal Motion:** Since there is no air resistance and no horizontal force acting on the ball, the horizontal acceleration is $a_x = 0$. Consequently, the horizontal velocity $v_x$ remains constant throughout the flight.
2.  **Vertical Motion:** Gravity acts as a constant downward force, resulting in a constant vertical acceleration $a_y = g$ (approximately $9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ downwards).
3.  **Resultant Acceleration:** Since the horizontal acceleration is zero and the vertical acceleration is constant ($g$), the net acceleration of the ball is constant and directed downwards.

### Evaluating the Options
*   **A: The ball's speed increases at a constant rate.** This is incorrect. While the vertical velocity increases at a constant rate, the total speed $v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$ changes in a non-linear way because $v_y$ is squared.
*   **B: The momentum of the ball is conserved.** This is incorrect. Momentum ($\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$) is only conserved if the net external force on the system is zero. Here, gravity provides a net external force.
*   **C: The acceleration of the ball is constant.** This is **correct**. Throughout the flight, the ball experiences a constant acceleration $g$ acting vertically downward.
*   **D: The ball travels at constant speed.** This is incorrect. The vertical velocity increases due to gravity, meaning the total speed of the ball increases as it falls.

### Correct Answer
The correct statement is **C**.
