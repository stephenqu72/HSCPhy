### Answer and Explanation

#### Part a)
**Solution:**
1.  **Understand the condition for maxima:** At point $P_3$, we have a maximum intensity, which indicates constructive interference. For constructive interference in a double-slit experiment, the path difference must be an integer multiple of the wavelength: $\Delta L = m\lambda$.
2.  **Calculate path difference:**
    $$\Delta L = |S_2P_3 - S_1P_3| = 80.6\text{ cm} - 72.3\text{ cm} = 8.3\text{ cm} = 0.083\text{ m}$$
3.  **Identify the order ($m$):** $P_0$ is the central maximum ($m=0$). $P_1, P_2, P_3$ are consecutive maxima, so $P_3$ corresponds to the third order, $m=3$.
4.  **Find the wavelength ($\lambda$):**
    $$3\lambda = 0.083\text{ m} \implies \lambda = \frac{0.083}{3} \approx 0.02767\text{ m}$$
5.  **Calculate frequency ($f$):** Use the wave equation $v = f\lambda$, where $v = 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m s}^{-1}$.
    $$f = \frac{v}{\lambda} = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{0.02767} \approx 1.084 \times 10^{10}\text{ Hz}$$

**Answer:** $1.08 \times 10^{10}\text{ Hz}$ (or $10.8\text{ GHz}$)

#### Part b)
**Explanation:**
The signal strength is at a minimum due to **destructive interference**. At a point midway between $P_0$ and $P_1$, the waves traveling from $S_1$ and $S_2$ arrive with a path difference of approximately half a wavelength ($\Delta L \approx \frac{\lambda}{2}$). When waves are $180^\circ$ out of phase, the crest of one wave aligns with the trough of the other, resulting in the waves cancelling each other out to produce a node of zero or minimum intensity.

#### Part c)
**Explanation:**
Polarisation refers to the orientation of the oscillations of a transverse wave. Specifically, it describes waves in which the electric field vector is vibrating in only one plane. For a source of polarised microwaves, all the electric field oscillations occur in a single, fixed direction (e.g., vertical or horizontal), rather than oscillating randomly in all directions perpendicular to the direction of propagation.
