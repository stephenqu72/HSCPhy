To solve this problem, we need to determine the energy of a photon at the peak wavelength of a blackbody radiating at a temperature of $T = 2.7\text{ K}$.

### Step 1: Find the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$)
We use Wien’s Displacement Law, which states:
$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$
Where:
*   $b \approx 2.898 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$ (Wien's displacement constant)
*   $T = 2.7\text{ K}$

$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{2.7} \approx 1.073 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$$

### Step 2: Calculate the photon energy ($E$)
The energy of a photon is given by the equation:
$$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda_{max}}$$
Where:
*   $h \approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$ (Planck's constant)
*   $c \approx 3.0 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$ (Speed of light)

Substituting the values:
$$E = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3.0 \times 10^8)}{1.073 \times 10^{-3}}$$
$$E = \frac{1.9878 \times 10^{-25}}{1.073 \times 10^{-3}}$$
$$E \approx 1.85 \times 10^{-22}\text{ J}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $1.9 \times 10^{-22}\text{ J}$.

Comparing this to the given options:
A. $1.9 \times 10^{-22}\text{ J}$
B. $2.3 \times 10^{-23}\text{ J}$
C. $7.4 \times 10^{-26}\text{ J}$
D. $6.2 \times 10^{-31}\text{ J}$

The correct option is **A**.
