### Front
How does the redshift of distant galaxies provide evidence for the Big Bang theory?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Redshift indicates a Doppler shift towards longer wavelengths, showing galaxies are receding; Hubble’s Law ($v = H_0d$) confirms this expansion, which implies a smaller, denser origin (Big Bang) when extrapolated backward.
- **Formula / law:** Hubble's Law: $v = H_0d$
- **When to use it:** When asked to link spectral data or observational evidence to cosmological models or the expansion of the universe.
- **Common trap:** Confusing redshift with a change in the light's velocity; remember that the speed of light ($c$) is constant—only the wavelength is stretched due to the expansion of space itself.
