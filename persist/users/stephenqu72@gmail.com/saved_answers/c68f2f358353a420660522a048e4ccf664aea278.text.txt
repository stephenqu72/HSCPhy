To calculate the relativistic momentum of an object, we use the formula:

$$p = \gamma m_0 v$$

Where:
*   $p$ is the relativistic momentum.
*   $m_0$ is the rest mass ($35\,000\text{ kg}$).
*   $v$ is the velocity ($0.8c$).
*   $\gamma$ is the Lorentz factor, calculated as $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Calculate the Lorentz factor ($\gamma$):**
Given $v = 0.8c$, then $v^2 = 0.64c^2$.
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{0.64c^2}{c^2}}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.64}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.36}} = \frac{1}{0.6} = \frac{5}{3} \approx 1.667$$

**2. Calculate the relativistic momentum ($p$):**
Substitute the values into the momentum formula:
$$p = \left( \frac{5}{3} \right) \times 35\,000\text{ kg} \times 0.8c$$
$$p = 1.667 \times 35\,000 \times 0.8 \times (3 \times 10^8\text{ m/s})$$
$$p = 1.667 \times 28\,000 \times 3 \times 10^8$$
$$p \approx 1.4 \times 10^{13}\text{ kg m/s}$$

**Final Answer:**
The relativistic momentum of the spaceship is approximately $1.4 \times 10^{13}\text{ kg m/s}$.

### Teacher's Tip:
In HSC Physics, always remember that as an object approaches the speed of light, its momentum increases significantly compared to its classical momentum ($mv$). If your result is smaller than $35\,000 \times 0.8c$ ($8.4 \times 10^{12}$), you know you've made a calculation error!
