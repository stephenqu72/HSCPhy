### Answer

According to Maxwell’s classical theory of electromagnetism, the production of radio waves occurs as follows:

1.  **Accelerating Charges:** The AC voltage source causes the electrons within the metal antenna to oscillate back and forth (accelerate and decelerate).
2.  **Varying Fields:** According to Maxwell, an oscillating electric charge creates a time-varying electric field. This changing electric field, in turn, induces a time-varying magnetic field (Ampère-Maxwell law).
3.  **Self-Propagating Wave:** These oscillating, mutually perpendicular electric and magnetic fields regenerate each other in a self-sustaining cycle, propagating outwards from the antenna as an electromagnetic wave (radio wave) at the speed of light $c$.

### How to solve it

To secure full marks for this 3-mark HSC Physics question, you need to link the physical mechanism (oscillation) to Maxwell’s specific theoretical predictions:

*   **Step 1: Identify the source.** You must mention that the AC voltage causes **accelerating charges** (oscillating electrons) in the antenna. This is the fundamental requirement for electromagnetic radiation.
*   **Step 2: Reference Maxwell’s theory.** You must explain that a changing electric field creates a changing magnetic field. You don't necessarily need to quote the equations, but you must demonstrate an understanding of the **interdependence** of the fields (i.e., a time-varying field generates the other).
*   **Step 3: State the result.** Conclude by stating that this interaction creates a self-propagating electromagnetic wave that travels away from the source.

**Teacher's Tip:** In the HSC, examiners look for the term "accelerating charges." Whenever a question asks about the production of electromagnetic waves, always start your response by mentioning that charges are accelerating or oscillating.
