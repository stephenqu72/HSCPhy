$$\tan(60^\circ) = \frac{v_y}{v_x}$$

$$\tan(60^\circ) = \frac{29.4}{u}$$
Since $\tan(60^\circ) = \sqrt{3} \approx 1.732$:
$$u = \frac{29.4}{1.732} \approx 16.97 \text{ m/s}$$

### 3. Find the magnitude of the velocity ($v$)
The magnitude of the resultant velocity vector is given by the hypotenuse of the velocity triangle:
$$v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$$
From our trigonometric relationship $\cos(60^\circ) = \frac{v_x}{v}$, we know that:
$$v = \frac{v_x}{\cos(60^\circ)}$$
Since $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$:
$$v = \frac{u}{0.5} = 2u$$
