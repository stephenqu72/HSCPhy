### Part (a)
**Solution:**
To find the length of the string ($r$), we use the principle of **Conservation of Mechanical Energy**. As the plane swings from the horizontal position to the bottom point $P$, its gravitational potential energy is converted into kinetic energy.

1. The change in height is equal to the length of the string, $r$.
2. The initial energy at the top is $E_i = mgh = mgr$.
3. The kinetic energy at the bottom is $E_f = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.
4. Equating $mgr = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$:
$$r = \frac{v^2}{2g}$$
Using $v = 3.56\text{ m s}^{-1}$ and $g = 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$:
$$r = \frac{3.56^2}{2 \times 9.8} = \frac{12.6736}{19.6} \approx 0.647\text{ m}$$

**Answer:** The length of the string is approximately $0.65\text{ m}$.

### Part (b)
**Solution:**
After the string is cut at point $P$, the plane is at height $h = 3.10 - r = 3.10 - 0.647 = 2.453\text{ m}$. It has an initial horizontal velocity $v_x = 3.56\text{ m s}^{-1}$.

1. **Find vertical acceleration ($a_y$):** The net vertical force is $F_{net} = F_L - mg = ma_y$.
   $$a_y = \frac{F_L - mg}{m} = \frac{F_L}{m} - g$$
2. **Kinematics for vertical displacement:** Using $s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2}a_y t^2$ where $u_y = 0$:
   $$-2.453 = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{F_L}{m} - g \right) t^2 \implies 2.453 = \frac{1}{2} \left( g - \frac{F_L}{m} \right) t^2$$
3. **Kinematics for horizontal displacement:** $s_x = v_x t \implies 3.56 = 3.56t \implies t = 1\text{ s}$.
4. **Substitute $t = 1$ into the vertical equation:**
   $$2.453 = \frac{1}{2} \left( 9.8 - \frac{F_L}{m} \right) (1)^2$$
   $$4.906 = 9.8 - \frac{F_L}{m}$$
   $$\frac{F_L}{m} = 9.8 - 4.906 \approx 4.9\text{ m s}^{-2}$$
   Since $g = 9.8$, then $4.9 = \frac{1}{2}g$.
   Therefore, $F_L = \frac{1}{2}mg$.
