To achieve full marks on this HSC Physics question, you need to identify a specific cause of energy loss in a transformer and explain the engineering solution used to mitigate it.

### Answer
**Reason for inefficiency:** Resistance within the copper windings (Joule heating).

**How it is minimised:** Transformers use thick copper wires with low resistivity for the windings. By increasing the cross-sectional area of the wire, the resistance $R$ is reduced (based on the formula $R = \rho \frac{L}{A}$), which minimizes the power loss given by $P = I^2R$ when current flows through the coils.

*(Alternative acceptable answer: Magnetic leakage/flux leakage. This is minimized by winding the primary and secondary coils on top of each other on a common soft iron core.)*

    *   **Hysteresis losses:** Solved by using "soft" iron cores that are easily magnetized and demagnetized.
