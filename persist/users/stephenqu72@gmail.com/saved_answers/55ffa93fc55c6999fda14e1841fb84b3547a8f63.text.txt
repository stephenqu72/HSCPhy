### Answer
The correct answer is **(B)**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must analyze the nuclear decay process provided:
$${}^{64}_{29}\text{Cu} \rightarrow {}^{64}_{28}\text{Ni} + {}^{0}_{+1}e + \nu$$

1.  **Identify the decay type:** The equation shows a nucleus with atomic number $29$ (Copper) changing into a nucleus with atomic number $28$ (Nickel) while emitting a positron (${}^{0}_{+1}e$). This is **positron emission** (or $\beta^+$ decay).
2.  **Analyze the nucleon change:** A proton in the nucleus is converting into a neutron to change the element from Copper to Nickel.
3.  **Apply the Standard Model:**
    *   A **proton** is composed of two "up" quarks and one "down" quark ($uud$).
    *   A **neutron** is composed of one "up" quark and two "down" quarks ($udd$).
    *   For a proton ($uud$) to transform into a neutron ($udd$), one of the **up quarks must change into a down quark**.
    *   This transformation is mediated by the weak nuclear force, involving the emission of a $W^+$ boson, which then decays into a positron and a neutrino.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **(A)** Electrons do not "become" positrons; this is a fundamental misunderstanding of particle identity.
*   **(C)** This represents $\beta^-$ decay (a neutron becoming a proton), which is the opposite of the process shown.
*   **(D)** This describes $\beta^-$ decay, not positron emission.
