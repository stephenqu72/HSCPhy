The correct answer is **(D) The existence of the nucleus.**

### Explanation

To solve this, we need to recall the historical context of the **Geiger-Marsden experiment** (often referred to as the Rutherford Gold Foil experiment).

1.  **The Context:** At the time, the prevailing model of the atom was J.J. Thomson's "Plum Pudding" model, which suggested that positive charge was spread uniformly throughout the atom with electrons embedded within it.
2.  **The Experiment:** Hans Geiger and Ernest Marsden, working under Ernest Rutherford, fired high-speed alpha particles ($\alpha$) at a very thin sheet of gold foil.
3.  **The Observations:** 
    *   Most alpha particles passed straight through the foil, supporting the idea that the atom is mostly empty space.
    *   A small but significant number of alpha particles were deflected at large angles.
    *   Some were even deflected backwards, which would be impossible if the positive charge were spread out as Thomson proposed.
4.  **The Conclusion:** Rutherford concluded that the positive charge and the vast majority of the atom's mass must be concentrated in a tiny, dense, central region he called the **nucleus**.
