To determine the composition of the star, we must perform a spectral analysis by comparing the absorption lines of the reference elements to the absorption lines present in the "Star's Spectrum."

### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) Helium and Magnesium**.

### Explanation
An absorption spectrum acts as a "fingerprint" for an element. Because each element has a unique electronic structure, it absorbs specific wavelengths of light. To solve this, we compare the position of the black lines (representing absorbed wavelengths) in the reference spectra to those in the "Star's Spectrum."

1.  **Analyze Helium:** If you align the vertical lines from the Helium spectrum with the Star's spectrum, you will see that every line present in the Helium spectrum matches a corresponding line in the Star's spectrum at the exact same horizontal position. Therefore, the star contains Helium.
2.  **Analyze Magnesium:** If you look at the single line in the Magnesium spectrum and move your eye down to the Star's spectrum, you will see a line aligned in that exact position. Therefore, the star contains Magnesium.
3.  **Elimination:** 
    *   **Hydrogen:** The leftmost lines in the Hydrogen spectrum do not match the spacing/position of the leftmost lines in the Star's spectrum.
    *   **Sodium:** The distinct double lines in the Sodium spectrum do not align with the lines in the Star's spectrum.

Since the star's spectrum is a composite of the lines present in the reference elements, and the lines for Helium and Magnesium align perfectly with the star's observed lines, the composition must be Helium and Magnesium.
