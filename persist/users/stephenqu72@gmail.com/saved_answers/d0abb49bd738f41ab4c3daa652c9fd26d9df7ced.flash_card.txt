### Front
In uniform vertical circular motion, why does the tension in the string vary at the top and bottom of the path?

### Back
- Key idea: Tension must adjust to provide the required centripetal force while accounting for the vector direction of gravity.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{net} = T \pm mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: To calculate tension at specific points in a vertical circle.
- Common trap: Forgetting that at the top, gravity *aids* centripetal force ($T = \frac{mv^2}{r} - mg$), while at the bottom, tension must *overcome* gravity ($T = \frac{mv^2}{r} + mg$).
