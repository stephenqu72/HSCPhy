### Front
Distinguish between **accuracy** and **precision** when analysing experimental data or graphs.

### Back
- Key idea: Accuracy refers to how close the average value is to the true value; Precision refers to how closely measurements are grouped together (consistency).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Evaluating systematic error (accuracy) vs. random error (precision) in experimental results.
- Common trap: Confusing a "centered but scattered" cluster (accurate, low precision) with a "tightly packed but offset" cluster (low accuracy, high precision).
