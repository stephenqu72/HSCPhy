To solve this problem, we must apply the concept of **time dilation** from Einstein's Theory of Special Relativity. 

### The Physics Principles
When an object moves at a significant fraction of the speed of light ($c$), time for that object appears to "slow down" relative to a stationary observer. The relationship between the "rest time" ($t_0$) and the "dilated time" ($t$) is given by the Lorentz transformation:
$$t = \gamma t_0 = \frac{t_0}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Rest lifetime ($t_0$) = $1 \times 10^{-6} \text{ s}$
*   Velocity ($v$) = $2.97 \times 10^8 \text{ ms}^{-1}$
*   Speed of light ($c$) $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ ms}^{-1}$

**2. Calculate the Lorentz factor ($\gamma$):**
First, determine the ratio $\frac{v}{c}$:
$$\frac{v}{c} = \frac{2.97 \times 10^8}{3.00 \times 10^8} = 0.99$$
Now calculate $\gamma$:
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.99)^2}}$$
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.9801}}$$
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.0199}} \approx \frac{1}{0.141067} \approx 7.0888$$

**3. Calculate the dilated time ($t$):**
$$t = \gamma \times t_0$$
$$t = 7.0888 \times 1 \times 10^{-6} \text{ s}$$
$$t \approx 7.09 \times 10^{-6} \text{ s}$$

**Answer:**
The lifetime of the muon at this speed is approximately $7.09 \times 10^{-6} \text{ s}$ (or $7.09 \text{ \mu s}$).

In the HSC Physics exam, always ensure you state the formula you are using. Showing the calculation of the Lorentz factor ($\gamma$) clearly will guarantee you get full marks. Remember that because $v$ is very close to $c$, the lifetime will be significantly longer than the rest lifetime.
