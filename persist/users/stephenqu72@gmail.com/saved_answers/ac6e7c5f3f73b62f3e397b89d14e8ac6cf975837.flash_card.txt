### Front
What are the conservation laws applied when determining the nuclear equation for $\beta^-$-decay?

### Back
- Key idea: Total nucleon number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$) must be conserved across the equation.
- Formula / law: $A_{parent} = A_{daughter} + A_{particle}$ and $Z_{parent} = Z_{daughter} + Z_{particle}$.
- When to use it: To predict or verify decay products in nuclear transformation problems.
- Common trap: Forgetting that in $\beta^-$-decay, the atomic number increases ($Z+1$) because a neutron becomes a proton; do not mistake it for alpha decay ($Z-2$).
