### Answer

James Clerk Maxwell’s contribution was pivotal in unifying the fields of electricity and magnetism, providing the theoretical foundation for understanding the nature of light. His significance can be summarized in the following points:

1.  **Unification of Electromagnetic Phenomena:** Maxwell synthesized existing laws (such as Gauss’s Laws, Ampère’s Law, and Faraday’s Law) into a cohesive set of equations known as Maxwell's Equations. He introduced the "displacement current" to correct Ampère’s Law, which accounted for the generation of magnetic fields by changing electric fields.
2.  **Theoretical Prediction of Electromagnetic Waves:** Through his equations, Maxwell demonstrated that a changing electric field generates a magnetic field and vice versa, creating a self-sustaining, transverse electromagnetic wave.
3.  **Determining the Velocity of Light:** Maxwell calculated the speed of these electromagnetic waves using the permittivity ($\epsilon_0$) and permeability ($\mu_0$) of free space, given by:
    $$c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$$
    The resulting value was approximately $3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$, which matched the experimentally determined speed of light.
4.  **Conclusion on the Nature of Light:** This match led Maxwell to propose the landmark hypothesis that light is not a mechanical wave requiring an "aether," but an electromagnetic wave. This shifted the paradigm of physics, moving away from corpuscular or purely mechanical wave theories and laying the groundwork for both Special Relativity and Quantum Mechanics.
