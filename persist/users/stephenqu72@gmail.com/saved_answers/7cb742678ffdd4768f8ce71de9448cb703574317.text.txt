#### Part (a)
**Answer:**
An inertial frame of reference is a frame of reference in which an object remains at rest or moves at a constant velocity unless acted upon by a net external force (consistent with Newton’s First Law). It is a non-accelerating frame.
**Everyday example:** A person standing on the ground (ignoring the Earth's rotation) is considered to be in an inertial frame of reference.

**How to solve:**

$$L = L_0\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
$$70 = 220\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
$$\frac{70}{220} = \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
$$0.3182^2 = 1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$$
$$0.1012 = 1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$$
$$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 0.8988$$
$$\frac{v}{c} = \sqrt{0.8988} \approx 0.948$$
$$v \approx 0.948c \approx 2.84 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$$
