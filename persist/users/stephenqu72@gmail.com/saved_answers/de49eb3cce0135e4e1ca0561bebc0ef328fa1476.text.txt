To solve this problem, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which relates the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) of electromagnetic radiation emitted by a black body to its absolute temperature ($T$).

### The Formula
Wien's Displacement Law is expressed as:
$$\lambda_{max} T = b$$
Where:
*   $\lambda_{max} = 450 \text{ nm} = 450 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$
*   $T$ is the temperature in Kelvin (K)
*   $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}$

### Step-by-Step Calculation

1.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $T$:**
    $$T = \frac{b}{\lambda_{max}}$$

2.  **Substitute the values:**
    $$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}}{450 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}}$$

3.  **Perform the division:**
    $$T = 6440 \text{ K}$$

**Answer:** The surface temperature of the star is $6440 \text{ K}$.
