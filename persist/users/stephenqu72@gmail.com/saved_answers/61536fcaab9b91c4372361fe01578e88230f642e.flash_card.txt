### Front
Why is it impossible to accelerate a neutral particle like the $K^0$ kaon using a standard synchrotron?

### Back
- Key idea: Synchrotrons rely on electromagnetic interaction with charged particles to provide acceleration and steering.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F = qE$ (acceleration) and $F = qvB\sin\theta$ (magnetic steering/Lorentz force).
- When to use it: When discussing the limitations of particle accelerators or the properties of subatomic particles.
- Common trap: Assuming neutral particles can be manipulated via magnetic fields; they must be produced as secondary decay products from charged particle collisions instead.
