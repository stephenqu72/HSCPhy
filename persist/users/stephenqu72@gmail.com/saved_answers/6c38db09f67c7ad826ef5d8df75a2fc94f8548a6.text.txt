### Answer
The correct answer is **B. $400\text{ N}$**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we apply **Newton’s Third Law of Motion**, which states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

1.  **Understand the interaction:** When a student pulls on one end of the rope with a force of $400\text{ N}$, the student is exerting a force on the rope. 
2.  **Apply Newton’s Third Law:** According to the law, the rope exerts an equal and opposite force back on the student. If the student pulls the rope with $400\text{ N}$ of force (the action), the rope pulls back on the student with exactly $400\text{ N}$ of force (the reaction).
3.  **Analyze the misconception:** A common mistake is to add the forces together ($400\text{ N} + 400\text{ N} = 800\text{ N}$) or to assume the forces cancel out to $0\text{ N}$. However, the force of the rope on each student depends only on the tension within the rope, which is established by the magnitude of the force applied at the ends. Since both students are pulling with $400\text{ N}$, the tension in the rope is $400\text{ N}$.
4.  **Conclusion:** The rope pulls on the left student with $400\text{ N}$ and on the right student with $400\text{ N}$. Therefore, the magnitude of the force of the rope on each student is $400\text{ N}$.
