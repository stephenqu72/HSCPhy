To solve this problem, we use the principles of gravitational potential energy and orbital mechanics. 

**Constants required:**
* Mass of Earth ($M$): $5.97 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$
* Radius of Earth ($R$): $6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
* Gravitational constant ($G$): $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ Nm}^2\text{kg}^{-2}$
* Mass of ISS ($m$): $4.2 \times 10^5 \text{ kg}$
* Altitude ($h$): $400 \text{ km} = 4.0 \times 10^5 \text{ m}$
* Orbital radius ($r = R + h$): $6.77 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$

---

### (a) Calculate energy to raise the ISS to its altitude
The energy required is the change in gravitational potential energy ($\Delta U$):
$$\Delta U = U_f - U_i = -\frac{GMm}{R+h} - \left(-\frac{GMm}{R}\right)$$
$$\Delta U = GMm \left(\frac{1}{R} - \frac{1}{R+h}\right)$$
$$\Delta U = (6.67 \times 10^{-11})(5.97 \times 10^{24})(4.2 \times 10^5) \left(\frac{1}{6.37 \times 10^6} - \frac{1}{6.77 \times 10^6}\right)$$
$$\Delta U \approx 1.56 \times 10^{12} \text{ J}$$

### (b) Calculate Kinetic Energy and Total Energy
**Kinetic Energy ($E_k$):** For a stable circular orbit, $E_k = \frac{GMm}{2r}$.
$$E_k = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(5.97 \times 10^{24})(4.2 \times 10^5)}{2(6.77 \times 10^6)} \approx 1.24 \times 10^{12} \text{ J}$$

**Total Energy ($E_{total}$):** $E_{total} = E_k + U = \frac{GMm}{2r} - \frac{GMm}{r} = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$.
$$E_{total} = -1.24 \times 10^{12} \text{ J}$$

### (c) Explain why Total Energy is less than Kinetic Energy
The total energy of an orbiting object is the sum of its kinetic energy ($E_k > 0$) and its gravitational potential energy ($U < 0$). Since the potential energy is negative (representing a "bound state" within Earth's gravity) and its magnitude is twice that of the kinetic energy, the addition of the negative potential energy reduces the total energy to a value lower than the kinetic energy alone. Specifically, $E_{total} = -E_k$.
