To solve this problem, we must apply the two fundamental laws governing the intensity of polarized light: Malus's Law and the rule for unpolarized light passing through a first polarizer.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. Passing through the first polarizer:**
When unpolarized light with an initial intensity $I_0$ passes through a single polarizer, exactly half of the light intensity is transmitted regardless of the polarizer's orientation.
$$I_1 = \frac{I_0}{2}$$
Given $I_0 = 50.0$ lumens:
$$I_1 = \frac{50.0}{2} = 25.0 \text{ lumens}$$

**2. Passing through the analyser:**
When the now-polarized light (with intensity $I_1$) passes through a second polarizer (the analyser) set at an angle $\theta$ relative to the first, we use **Malus's Law**:
$$I_2 = I_1 \cos^2(\theta)$$
Given $\theta = 30^\circ$ and $I_1 = 25.0$ lumens:
$$I_2 = 25.0 \times \cos^2(30^\circ)$$
Since $\cos(30^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$, then $\cos^2(30^\circ) = \frac{3}{4} = 0.75$.

**3. Final Calculation:**
$$I_2 = 25.0 \times 0.75 = 18.75 \text{ lumens}$$
