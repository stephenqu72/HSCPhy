To solve this projectile motion problem, we must analyze the motion in the horizontal direction.

### Analysis and Calculation
In projectile motion, horizontal velocity ($v_x$) remains constant because there is no air resistance acting on the projectile (assuming standard HSC physics assumptions).

1. **Identify the variables:**
   - Horizontal distance (range), $R = 200\text{ m}$
   - Total time of flight, $t = 2.4\text{ s}$
   - Angle of elevation, $\theta = 50^\circ$

2. **Calculate the horizontal component of the velocity ($v_x$):**
   Since horizontal velocity is constant:
   $$v_x = \frac{R}{t}$$
   $$v_x = \frac{200}{2.4} \approx 83.33\text{ ms}^{-1}$$

3. **Relate horizontal velocity to the initial velocity ($u$):**
   The horizontal component of the initial velocity is given by the formula:
   $$v_x = u \cos(\theta)$$
   Therefore:
   $$u = \frac{v_x}{\cos(\theta)}$$
   $$u = \frac{83.33}{\cos(50^\circ)}$$

4. **Compute the result:**
   Using $\cos(50^\circ) \approx 0.6428$:
   $$u \approx \frac{83.33}{0.6428} \approx 129.64\text{ ms}^{-1}$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number, we get $130\text{ ms}^{-1}$.

*   **Step 2:** Use the horizontal distance and time to calculate the horizontal velocity component ($v_x$).
*   **Step 3:** Use trigonometry to resolve this horizontal component back into the total initial velocity ($u$) using the angle of projection.
