### Front
In uniform circular motion, how much work is done on the mass by the centripetal force during one full revolution?

### Back
- Key idea: Work done is zero because the centripetal force is always perpendicular to the instantaneous velocity.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $W = Fd\cos\theta$ where $\theta = 90^\circ$; Work-Energy Theorem ($\Delta E_k = W_{net}$).
- When to use it: Any uniform circular motion scenario to determine energy changes.
- Common trap: Assuming that because a force acts on the object, work must be done. Work is only done if there is a component of force parallel to the displacement.
