To find the peak wavelength of a star's radiation, we use **Wien's Displacement Law**. This law states that the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) of blackbody radiation is inversely proportional to its absolute temperature ($T$):

$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$

Where:
*   $b$ is Wien's displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$.
*   $T$ is the surface temperature of the star in Kelvin.

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   $T = 3500 \text{ K}$
    *   $b = 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$

2.  **Apply the formula:**
    $$\lambda_{max} = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}}{3500 \text{ K}}$$

3.  **Perform the division:**
    $$\lambda_{max} \approx 8.28 \times 10^{-7} \text{ meters}$$

4.  **Convert meters to nanometers (nm):**
    Since $1 \text{ m} = 10^9 \text{ nm}$:
    $$\lambda_{max} = 8.28 \times 10^{-7} \times 10^9 \text{ nm}$$
    $$\lambda_{max} = 828 \text{ nm}$$

Comparing our result to the given options, the correct answer is **D**.
