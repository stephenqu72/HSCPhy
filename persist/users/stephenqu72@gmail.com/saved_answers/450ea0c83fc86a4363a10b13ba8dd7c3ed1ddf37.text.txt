To solve this problem, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, which is given by the formula:
$$F = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$
where $G$ is the gravitational constant, $m_1$ and $m_2$ are the masses of the two objects, and $r$ is the distance between their centres.

### Step 1: Calculate the force between Star B and Star C ($F_{BC}$)
*   Mass of Star B = $M$
*   Mass of Star C = $8M$
*   Distance ($r_{BC}$) = $3d$

$$F_{BC} = \frac{G \cdot M \cdot 8M}{(3d)^2} = \frac{8 G M^2}{9 d^2} \approx 0.888 \frac{G M^2}{d^2}$$

### Step 2: Calculate the force between Star A and Star B ($F_{AB}$)
*   Mass of Star A = $6M$
*   Mass of Star B = $M$
*   Distance ($r_{AB}$) = $4d$

$$F_{AB} = \frac{G \cdot 6M \cdot M}{(4d)^2} = \frac{6 G M^2}{16 d^2} = \frac{3 G M^2}{8 d^2} = 0.375 \frac{G M^2}{d^2}$$

### Step 3: Find the ratio
To find how many times greater the force between B and C is compared to A and B, we divide $F_{BC}$ by $F_{AB}$:

$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{F_{BC}}{F_{AB}} = \frac{\frac{8}{9} \frac{G M^2}{d^2}}{\frac{3}{8} \frac{G M^2}{d^2}}$$

$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{8}{9} \div \frac{3}{8} = \frac{8}{9} \times \frac{8}{3} = \frac{64}{27}$$

$$\text{Ratio} \approx 2.37037...$$

Rounding to two decimal places, the result is **2.37**.

Comparing this to the given options:
A. 1.78
B. 5.12
C. 2.16
D. 2.37

The correct answer is **D**.
