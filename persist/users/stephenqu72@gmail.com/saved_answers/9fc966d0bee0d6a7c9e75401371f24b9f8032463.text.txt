### Explanation
To solve this question, you must understand the historical context of Newton's corpuscular (particle) theory of light. Newton proposed that light consists of tiny particles called "corpuscles." 

Newton attempted to explain the refraction of light (the bending of light as it enters a denser medium, like glass or water) by assuming that these corpuscles were subject to forces exerted by the particles of the medium. 

1.  **The Mechanism:** Newton argued that when light particles approach the interface of a denser medium, they experience an attractive force perpendicular to the surface.
2.  **Vector Components:** This force increases the component of the particle's velocity that is perpendicular to the interface. Since the component of velocity parallel to the surface remains unchanged, the resultant velocity vector changes direction, causing the path of the light to bend toward the normal (the hallmark of refraction).
3.  **The Flaw:** Crucially, Newton's theory incorrectly predicted that light must *accelerate* when entering a denser medium. While this explanation was eventually proven wrong by Foucault's later measurements showing that light travels slower in denser media, the question specifically asks how Newton's theory *attempted* to explain it at the time.

*   **B is incorrect** because Newton's theory predicted light would speed up, not slow down, in a denser medium.
*   **C is incorrect** because scattering describes reflection/diffusion, not refraction.
*   **D is incorrect** because Newton did provide a mechanical explanation for refraction, even if it was physically incorrect by modern standards.
