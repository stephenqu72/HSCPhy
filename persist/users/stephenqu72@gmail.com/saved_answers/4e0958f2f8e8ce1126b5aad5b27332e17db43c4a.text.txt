To determine the direction of the particle's velocity, we must analyze the forces acting on the positively charged particle.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Analyze the Electric Force ($F_E$):**
    The electric field points from the positive plate to the negative plate (down the page). Since the particle has a positive charge, the electric force $F_E = qE$ acts in the direction of the electric field. Therefore, the **electric force is directed down the page**.

2.  **Analyze the Condition for Constant Velocity:**
    For the particle to move at a constant velocity, the net force acting on it must be zero ($F_{net} = 0$). This means the magnetic force ($F_B$) must exactly oppose the electric force. Since $F_E$ is directed down, the **magnetic force must be directed up the page**.

3.  **Use the Right-Hand Rule (RHR):**
    We apply the right-hand rule to find the direction of the velocity ($v$) that results in an upward magnetic force:
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$): **into the page** (represented by the $\times$ symbols).
    *   Point your thumb in the direction of the magnetic force ($F_B$): **up the page**.
    *   The palm of your hand (for a positive charge) points in the direction of the velocity ($v$).
    *   With your fingers pointing into the page and your palm/thumb pointing up, your fingers (velocity) must point to the **right**.

4.  **Conclusion:**
    If the particle moves to the right, the magnetic force acts upwards, balancing the downward electric force, allowing the particle to move at a constant velocity.

The correct answer is **D. To the right**.
