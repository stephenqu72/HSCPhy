### Front
How can gravitational acceleration ($g$) be determined experimentally using only a horizontal projectile launcher (without a stopwatch)?

### Back
- Key idea: Use the independence of horizontal and vertical motion; equate time $t$ in both planes.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{2hu^2}{R^2}$ (derived from $R=ut$ and $h=\frac{1}{2}gt^2$).
- When to use it: When asked to design an experiment to measure $g$ or verify kinematic equations.
- Common trap: Forgetting to measure both height ($h$) and horizontal range ($R$) accurately, or failing to ensure the initial launch is perfectly horizontal.
