### Explanation

To solve this problem, we must look at the definition of magnetic flux, denoted by the symbol $\Phi_B$. 

1.  **Definition**: Magnetic flux is defined as the measure of the total magnetic field passing through a given surface area. Mathematically, it is expressed as:
    $$\Phi_B = B \cdot A \cdot \cos(\theta)$$
    where $B$ is the magnetic field strength, $A$ is the area, and $\theta$ is the angle between the magnetic field lines and the normal to the surface.

2.  **Visualizing Field Lines**: In physics, magnetic field lines (or lines of magnetic force) are used as a visual tool to represent the magnetic field. The "number of lines" passing through a surface is a direct, qualitative representation of the total magnetic flux. 

3.  **Evaluating the Options**:
    *   **(A) Number of lines of magnetic force:** This is the correct definition. The more lines that pass through an area, the greater the magnetic flux.
    *   **(B) Density of the lines of magnetic force:** The *density* of these lines (number of lines per unit area) represents the **magnetic field strength ($B$)**, not the total flux.
    *   **(C) Direction of the lines of magnetic force:** The direction indicates the orientation of the field, not the quantity of flux.
    *   **(D) Size of the area:** While the area is a *factor* in the calculation of flux, it does not define the flux itself. A large area with very few field lines could have less flux than a small area with a high concentration of field lines.

Therefore, "number of lines of magnetic force" is the most accurate conceptual description of magnetic flux.
