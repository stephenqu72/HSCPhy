To solve this problem, we must distinguish between **mass** and **apparent weight**. Mass is constant ($65\text{ kg}$), while apparent weight is the normal force ($F_N$) exerted by the floor of the elevator. Using Newton’s Second Law: 
$$F_{net} = F_N - mg = ma \implies F_N = m(g + a)$$
(Taking upward acceleration as positive and gravity $g \approx 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$).

### Step-by-step Solution:

**Stage 1: Constant velocity**
When velocity is constant, acceleration $a = 0$.
$$F_N = m(g + 0) = 65 \times 9.8 = 637\text{ N}$$
This eliminates options (a) and (b), which express the answer in kilograms (mass), not Newtons (force).

**Stage 2: Accelerating upwards at $5\text{ m s}^{-2}$**
Here, $a = +5\text{ m s}^{-2}$.
$$F_N = 65 \times (9.8 + 5) = 65 \times 14.8 = 962\text{ N}$$

**Stage 3: Accelerating downwards at $5\text{ m s}^{-2}$**
Here, $a = -5\text{ m s}^{-2}$ (downward is negative relative to the upward normal force).
$$F_N = 65 \times (9.8 - 5) = 65 \times 4.8 = 312\text{ N}$$

Comparing these results to the given options:
* Stage 1: $637\text{ N}$
* Stage 2: $962\text{ N}$
* Stage 3: $312\text{ N}$

This corresponds to **option (d)**.

### Explanation Summary:
1. **Identify the goal:** We are calculating the apparent weight, which is the normal force ($F_N$) the scale reads. The units must be in Newtons ($N$).
2. **Apply Newton’s Law:** For any vertical elevator movement, the normal force is $F_N = m(g + a)$.
3. **Analyze stages:**
   - **Constant velocity:** $a=0$, so $F_N = mg$.
   - **Upward acceleration:** $a$ is positive, so $F_N$ increases.
   - **Downward acceleration:** $a$ is negative, so $F_N$ decreases.
