### Solutions and Explanations

#### Part (a)
**Goal:** Derive an expression for the vertical component of the electron's velocity ($v_y$) as it leaves the field.

**Explanation:**
1.  **Identify Forces:** The electron experiences an upward electric force $F = eE$. Since $F = ma$, the vertical acceleration is $a_y = \frac{eE}{m}$.
2.  **Time in field:** The electron travels horizontally at a constant velocity $v_x$. The time $t$ taken to traverse the length $l$ is $t = \frac{l}{v_x}$.
3.  **Kinematics:** Using the equation $v_y = u_y + a_yt$, and noting the initial vertical velocity $u_y = 0$:
    $$v_y = \left(\frac{eE}{m}\right) \left(\frac{l}{v_x}\right) = \frac{eEl}{mv_x}$$

#### Part (b)(ii)
**Question:** Compare the work needed to move identical negative charges from $X$ to $Y$ and $X$ to $Z$.
**Answer:** The work done to move the charge from $X$ to $Y$ is **greater** than the work done to move the charge from $X$ to $Z$.
**Justification:**
1.  Work done is equal to the change in electric potential energy: $W = q\Delta V$. 
2.  The electric potential $V$ at a distance $r$ is given by $V = \frac{kQ}{r}$. Potential is a scalar; it only depends on distance from the source.
3.  Point $Z$ is further from the source than $X$ (so $V_X > V_Z$), but point $Y$ is even further away than $Z$ (so $V_X > V_Z > V_Y$). 
4.  The potential difference for $X \to Y$ is $|V_Y - V_X|$, and for $X \to Z$ is $|V_Z - V_X|$. Since the distance between $X$ and $Y$ is greater than the distance between $X$ and $Z$ along the field lines, the potential difference magnitude $\Delta V$ is larger for the path $X \to Y$, requiring more work.
