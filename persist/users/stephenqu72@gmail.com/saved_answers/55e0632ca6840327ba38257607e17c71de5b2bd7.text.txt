### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) 6.85 billion years**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must understand the inverse relationship between the Hubble constant ($H_0$) and the age of the universe ($t$).

1.  **The Relationship:** The age of the universe is inversely proportional to the Hubble constant, expressed by the relationship:
    $$t \propto \frac{1}{H_0}$$
    This means if the value of the Hubble constant increases, the estimated age of the universe decreases proportionally.

2.  **Analyze the Change:**
    *   Initial Hubble constant ($H_1$) = $82 \text{ km/s/Mpc}$
    *   Initial Age ($t_1$) = $13.7 \text{ billion years}$ (Note: The prompt text says 13.8, but option A refers to 13.7; we will use the proportional change).
    *   New Hubble constant ($H_2$) = $164 \text{ km/s/Mpc}$

3.  **Calculate the Ratio:**
    The new Hubble constant is exactly double the original:
    $$\frac{H_2}{H_1} = \frac{164}{82} = 2$$

4.  **Apply the Inverse Relationship:**
    Since $t$ is inversely proportional to $H_0$, doubling the Hubble constant will halve the age of the universe:
    $$t_2 = \frac{t_1}{2}$$
    $$t_2 = \frac{13.7 \text{ billion years}}{2} = 6.85 \text{ billion years}$$

By doubling the expansion rate ($H_0$), the universe reaches its current size in half the time, leading to an age of $6.85 \text{ billion years}$.
