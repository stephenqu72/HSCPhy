### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the Half-life ($t_{1/2}$):**
    The decay constant is related to the half-life by the formula:
    $$\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{t_{1/2}}$$
    The half-life is the time it takes for the percentage of radioactive nuclides to decrease from 100% to 50%. Looking at the graph:
    *   At $t = 0\text{ s}$, the activity is 100%.
    *   We need to find the time $t$ where the activity is 50%.
    *   Tracing the horizontal line from 50 on the y-axis to the curve and dropping down to the x-axis, we see it hits at $t \approx 125\text{ s}$.

2.  **Calculate the Decay Constant ($\lambda$):**
    Using the half-life $t_{1/2} = 125\text{ s}$:
    $$\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{125}$$
    $$\lambda \approx \frac{0.6931}{125}$$
    $$\lambda \approx 0.005545\text{ s}^{-1}$$
