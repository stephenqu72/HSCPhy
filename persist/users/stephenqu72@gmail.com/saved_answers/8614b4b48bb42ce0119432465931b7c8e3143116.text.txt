### Answer
The correct answer is **B. Electrostatic**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, you need to understand the physical interaction between the subatomic particles in the Bohr model of a hydrogen atom:

1.  **Understand the Nature of the Particles:** A hydrogen atom consists of a positively charged proton at the center and a negatively charged electron orbiting it.
2.  **Identify the Force:** According to Coulomb's Law, two point charges exert an electrostatic force on each other. Since the proton and electron have opposite charges, they experience an attractive electrostatic force. This is given by the formula:
    $$F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$
    where $q_1$ is the charge of the proton, $q_2$ is the charge of the electron, and $r$ is the distance between them.
3.  **The Role of Centripetal Force:** While the electron is in a circular orbit, it requires a **centripetal force** to maintain that path. However, centripetal force is a *requirement* for circular motion, not a *type* of fundamental force. In the Bohr model, the **electrostatic force** acts as the centripetal force provider.
4.  **Why others are incorrect:** 
    *   **Gravitational:** While there is a gravitational force between the proton and electron, it is many orders of magnitude weaker than the electrostatic force and is negligible at the atomic scale.
    *   **Strong Nuclear:** This force acts over very short distances to bind nucleons (protons and neutrons) together within the nucleus; it does not hold the electron in orbit.
    *   **Centripetal:** As noted above, this is a label for the *effect* of a force on an object in circular motion, not the *source* of the force itself in this context.

Therefore, the fundamental physical force responsible for keeping the electron in orbit is the **electrostatic** force.
