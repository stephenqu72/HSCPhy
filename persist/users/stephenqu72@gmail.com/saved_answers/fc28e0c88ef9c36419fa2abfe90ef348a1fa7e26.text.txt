### Answer
The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation
To understand why the velocity at maximum height is represented by a horizontal arrow (D), we must analyze the two components of projectile motion:

1.  **Vertical Component ($v_y$):** As the projectile travels upwards, gravity acts against it, causing its vertical velocity to decrease. At the exact moment the projectile reaches its maximum height, its vertical velocity is momentarily zero ($v_y = 0$).
2.  **Horizontal Component ($v_x$):** Assuming negligible air resistance, there are no forces acting horizontally on the projectile. Therefore, the horizontal velocity remains constant throughout the entire flight. 

Since the total velocity vector $\vec{v}$ is the vector sum of its horizontal and vertical components, at maximum height:
$$\vec{v} = v_x \hat{i} + v_y \hat{j}$$
Substituting $v_y = 0$:
$$\vec{v} = v_x \hat{i}$$

This means that at the highest point of the trajectory, the velocity vector is directed purely horizontally. Among the choices provided, arrow **D** represents a purely horizontal vector pointing to the right, which corresponds to the direction of the projectile's travel.
