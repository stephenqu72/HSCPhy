### Answer

**Part (a): Calculation**

To find the average induced EMF, we use Faraday’s Law of Induction:
$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$$

1.  **Identify given values:**
    *   Number of turns, $N = 10$
    *   Area, $A = 1.6 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2$
    *   Magnetic field strength, $B = 2.0 \times 10^{-2} \text{ T}$
    *   Time interval, $\Delta t = 0.50 \text{ s}$

2.  **Calculate change in flux ($\Delta \Phi$):**
    *   Initial flux ($\Phi_i$) at position X (outside field) = $0 \text{ Wb}$
    *   Final flux ($\Phi_f$) at position Y (inside field) = $B \times A = (2.0 \times 10^{-2}) \times (1.6 \times 10^{-3}) = 3.2 \times 10^{-5} \text{ Wb}$
    *   $\Delta \Phi = 3.2 \times 10^{-5} - 0 = 3.2 \times 10^{-5} \text{ Wb}$

3.  **Calculate EMF ($\varepsilon$):**
    *   $\varepsilon = 10 \times \frac{3.2 \times 10^{-5}}{0.50}$
    *   $\varepsilon = 6.4 \times 10^{-4} \text{ V}$

**Part (b): Sketch**

*   **Outside field:** EMF = $0$.
*   **Entering field:** Magnetic flux is increasing, so there is a constant negative (or positive, depending on convention) EMF.
*   **Inside field:** Magnetic flux is constant ($\Delta \Phi = 0$), so EMF = $0$.
*   **Leaving field:** Magnetic flux is decreasing, creating an EMF equal in magnitude but opposite in sign to the "entering" phase.
*   **Outside field:** EMF = $0$.

### How to solve it

1.  **Understand Faraday’s Law:** The magnitude of induced EMF is directly proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux linkage. Remember that flux is $\Phi = B \times A \times \cos(\theta)$. Since the loop is perpendicular to the field, $\cos(0^\circ) = 1$.
2.  **Handle the signs:** In HSC Physics, we often focus on the magnitude. If you use Lenz's Law, ensure your direction is consistent. The entering phase generates a flux change that opposes the incoming field, and the exiting phase generates a flux change that tries to maintain it.
3.  **Graphing logic:**
    *   The EMF is the **gradient** of the flux-time graph.
    *   When the loop is fully inside the field, the flux is constant. The derivative of a constant is zero, meaning the EMF must return to the x-axis (zero) while the loop is completely submerged in the magnetic field.
