### Front
What is a key piece of evidence for the Big Bang theory, and why does it support the model?

### Back
- Key idea: The Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) is the "afterglow" of the Big Bang; its blackbody spectrum ($2.7\text{ K}$) confirms the universe transitioned from a hot, dense state to an expanding, cooling one.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $T \approx 2.7\text{ K}$ (Blackbody radiation).
- When to use it: Any question asking for evidence supporting the Big Bang (e.g., "Select, outline and assess").
- Common trap: Don't just define it; you must **assess** it by explaining how it refutes the Steady-State model, which could not account for this uniform background heat.
