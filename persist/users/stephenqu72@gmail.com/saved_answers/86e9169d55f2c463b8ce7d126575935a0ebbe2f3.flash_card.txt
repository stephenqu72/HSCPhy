### Front
What are the conservation rules for Alpha ($\alpha$) and Beta-minus ($\beta^-$) decay?

### Back
- Key idea: Conservation of nucleon number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$).
- Formula / law: 
  - $\alpha$ decay: $^{A}_{Z}X \rightarrow ^{A-4}_{Z-2}Y + ^{4}_{2}He$
  - $\beta^-$ decay: $^{A}_{Z}X \rightarrow ^{A}_{Z+1}Y + ^{0}_{-1}e + \bar{\nu}_e$
- When to use it: To determine the daughter nucleus or identify radioactive decay products.
- Common trap: Forgetting that $\beta^-$ decay also emits an antineutrino ($\bar{\nu}_e$) and that the atomic number increases by 1 (not decreases).
