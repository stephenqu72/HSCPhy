### Front
How does the orbital velocity ($v$) of a satellite change if its orbital radius ($r$) is doubled?

### Back
- Key idea: Orbital velocity is inversely proportional to the square root of the orbital radius ($v \propto 1/\sqrt{r}$).
- Formula: $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$
- When to use it: To determine how a change in orbital altitude or distance affects the velocity required to maintain a stable circular orbit.
- Common trap: Forgetting to take the square root of the factor change (e.g., assuming if $r$ doubles, $v$ halves; the correct factor is $1/\sqrt{2} \approx 0.707$).
