To find the frequency of the longest wavelength of light in the Balmer Series from the provided spectrum, follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the longest wavelength
Looking at the provided spectrum, the wavelengths are labeled in nanometers ($nm$) at the bottom. The wavelengths listed are $397, 410, 434, 486,$ and $656$ $nm$. The longest wavelength is clearly $656$ $nm$.

### 2. Convert units to SI
To use the wave equation, we must convert the wavelength ($\lambda$) from nanometers to meters:
$$\lambda = 656 \text{ nm} = 656 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$$

### 3. Use the wave equation
The relationship between the speed of light ($c$), frequency ($f$), and wavelength ($\lambda$) is:
$$c = f \lambda$$
Rearranging to solve for frequency ($f$):
$$f = \frac{c}{\lambda}$$

Using the speed of light $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$:
$$f = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{656 \times 10^{-9}}$$
$$f \approx 4.57 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$$
