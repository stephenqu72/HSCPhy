To solve this problem, we must account for the relativistic effects on the proton's momentum because its speed is $v = 0.85c$, which is a significant fraction of the speed of light.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
The magnetic force $F_B$ acts as the centripetal force $F_c$ to keep the proton in a circular orbit:
$$qvB = \frac{\gamma m_0 v^2}{r}$$
Where:
- $q = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$ (charge of a proton)
- $v = 0.85c$ (where $c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$)
- $r = 100 \text{ m}$
- $m_0 = 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$ (rest mass of a proton)
- $\gamma$ is the Lorentz factor: $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$

### 2. Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step A: Calculate the Lorentz factor ($\gamma$)**
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.85^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.7225}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.2775}} \approx 1.898$$

**Step B: Rearrange the force equation for $B$**
$$B = \frac{\gamma m_0 v}{qr}$$

**Step C: Plug in the values**
$$B = \frac{1.898 \times (1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) \times (0.85 \times 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s})}{(1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \times 100 \text{ m}}$$
$$B = \frac{1.898 \times 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \times 2.55 \times 10^8}{1.602 \times 10^{-17}}$$
$$B = \frac{8.085 \times 10^{-19}}{1.602 \times 10^{-17}}$$
$$B \approx 0.05047 \text{ T}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $0.051 \text{ T}$.
