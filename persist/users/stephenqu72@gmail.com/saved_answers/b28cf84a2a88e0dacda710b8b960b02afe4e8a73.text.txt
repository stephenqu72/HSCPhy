2.  **Concept:**
    A sound wave consists of alternating compressions and rarefactions. The distance between two consecutive compressions is defined as one wavelength ($\lambda$). The time it takes for one full wavelength to pass a fixed point is defined as the period ($T$) of the wave.

3.  **Calculation:**
    The period ($T$) is given as $1\text{ second}$.
    The frequency ($f$) is the reciprocal of the period:
    $$f = \frac{1}{T}$$
    $$f = \frac{1}{1\text{ s}} = 1\text{ Hz}$$
