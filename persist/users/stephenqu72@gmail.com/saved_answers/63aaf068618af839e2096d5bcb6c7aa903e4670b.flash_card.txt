### Front
What is the formula for the banking angle ($\theta$) of a frictionless track, and does the mass of the vehicle affect it?

### Back
- Key idea: The centripetal force is provided by the horizontal component of the normal force.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$. Mass cancels out; therefore, it does not affect $\theta$.
- When to use it: To determine the ideal banking angle for a specific velocity ($v$) and radius ($r$) without relying on friction.
- Common trap: Assuming a heavier car requires a steeper banking angle. Mass is independent of the required angle.
