### Answer
The results demonstrate **Malus’s Law**, which states that the intensity of light $I$ transmitted through an analyzer is proportional to the square of the cosine of the angle $\theta$ between the transmission axes of the polarizer and the analyzer:
$$I = I_0 \cos^2(\theta)$$
where $I_0$ is the intensity of the light after passing through the first polarizer.

As the angle $\theta$ increases from $20^\circ$ to $80^\circ$, the value of $\cos(\theta)$ decreases. Since the intensity depends on $\cos^2(\theta)$, the light intensity $I$ decreases significantly. The data follows this mathematical relationship: as $\theta$ approaches $90^\circ$, $\cos(90^\circ) = 0$, meaning the intensity of light reaching the sensor approaches zero.
