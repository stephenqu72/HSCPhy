### Front
How do you calculate the time required for a device of known power to consume a specific amount of energy?

### Back
- Key idea: Energy is the product of power and time.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E = Pt$ (where $t = E/P$). No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Problems involving energy consumption, electrical work, or power ratings over time.
- Common trap: Failing to convert units (e.g., leaving energy in MJ instead of Joules, or calculating time in seconds instead of hours).
