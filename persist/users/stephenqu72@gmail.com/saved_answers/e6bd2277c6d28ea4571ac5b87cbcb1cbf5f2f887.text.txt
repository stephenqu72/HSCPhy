To answer this question effectively, you need to explain how the process of nuclear fission scales and how human intervention (or the lack thereof) alters the outcome of the reaction.

### Answer
The diagram depicts a self-sustaining **nuclear chain reaction**, where the fission of one $^{235}\text{U}$ nucleus releases neutrons that trigger further fission events in adjacent nuclei.

*   **Uncontrolled Chain Reaction:** This occurs when, on average, more than one neutron released from each fission event successfully triggers another fission event ($k > 1$). In such a scenario, the number of fissions increases exponentially, releasing a massive amount of energy in a very short time, as seen in nuclear weapons.
*   **Controlled Chain Reaction:** This occurs in nuclear reactors where the reaction is kept stable by maintaining the reproduction constant at exactly one ($k = 1$). This is achieved using control rods (made of materials like boron or cadmium) that absorb excess neutrons, preventing the reaction from escalating while ensuring a steady, manageable release of thermal energy for electricity generation.
