### Front
What is the formula for calculating the banking angle ($\theta$) of a frictionless track, and which forces provide the centripetal force?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force ($F_c$).
- **Formula:** $\tan \theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$
- **When to use it:** When a vehicle rounds a banked corner at a specific speed such that no friction is required to maintain the path.
- **Common trap:** Confusing $\sin\theta$ and $\tan\theta$ components, or forgetting that the normal force ($N$) is *not* equal to weight ($mg$) when the track is banked.
