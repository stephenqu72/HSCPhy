### Answer and Explanation

**Explanation:**
To analyze the motion of a spectroscopic binary star system, we look at the **Doppler Effect**. When a star moves toward Earth, its light is blue-shifted (spectral lines shift to shorter wavelengths/left). When it moves away, its light is red-shifted (spectral lines shift to longer wavelengths/right).

1.  **Periodic Nature:** The spectral lines shift away from the central rest position and return to it in a repeating cycle. At 0, 14, and 28 days, the line is at the central rest position, indicating the star has no radial velocity relative to Earth (moving perpendicular to the line of sight). The full cycle from rest to maximum shift and back to rest occurs every 14 days; therefore, the orbital period of the system is 28 days.
2.  **Magnitude of Changes:** The shift away from the center represents the radial velocity component of the star's orbital motion. At 7 days, the line is shifted to the right (red-shift), indicating the star is moving away from Earth at its maximum orbital speed. At 21 days, the line is shifted to the left (blue-shift), indicating the star is moving toward Earth at its maximum orbital speed. The magnitude of the shift is directly proportional to the line-of-sight orbital velocity $v_r$ of the star.

**Summary Answer:**
The changes in the spectral lines are caused by the Doppler Effect resulting from the orbital motion of the stars. The periodic nature is evident as the spectral line returns to its original position every 28 days, confirming the orbital period of the system. The magnitude of the displacement from the rest position indicates the radial velocity of the star. At 7 days, a red-shift indicates maximum velocity away from Earth, and at 21 days, a blue-shift indicates maximum velocity toward Earth. When the line is at the center (0, 14, 28 days), the star is moving perpendicular to the observer's line of sight.
