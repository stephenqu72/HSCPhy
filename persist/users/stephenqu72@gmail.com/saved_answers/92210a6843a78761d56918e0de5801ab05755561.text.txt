To find the acceleration due to gravity of an object at the midpoint between the Earth and the Moon, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, which states the gravitational field strength $g$ produced by a mass $M$ at a distance $r$ is:
$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$
where $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N m}^2 \text{kg}^{-2}$.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the parameters:**
    *   Distance between Earth and Moon ($d$) = $3.84 \times 10^8 \, \text{m}$
    *   Distance from midpoint to either body ($r$) = $d / 2 = 1.92 \times 10^8 \, \text{m}$
    *   Mass of Earth ($M_E$) = $6.0 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg}$
    *   Mass of Moon ($M_M$) = $7.35 \times 10^{22} \, \text{kg}$

2.  **Calculate acceleration due to Earth ($g_E$):**
    $$g_E = \frac{6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 6.0 \times 10^{24}}{(1.92 \times 10^8)^2} \approx 1.085 \times 10^{-2} \, \text{m s}^{-2} \, \text{(towards Earth)}$$

3.  **Calculate acceleration due to Moon ($g_M$):**
    $$g_M = \frac{6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 7.35 \times 10^{22}}{(1.92 \times 10^8)^2} \approx 0.133 \times 10^{-2} \, \text{m s}^{-2} \, \text{(towards Moon)}$$

4.  **Find the net acceleration:**
    Since the forces act in opposite directions, we subtract the smaller acceleration from the larger one:
    $$g_{net} = g_E - g_M = 1.085 \times 10^{-2} - 0.133 \times 10^{-2} = 0.952 \times 10^{-2} \, \text{m s}^{-2} \, \text{(towards Earth)}$$
