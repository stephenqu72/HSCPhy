To solve this question, we must apply the principles of the photoelectric effect, a cornerstone of quantum physics in the HSC Physics syllabus.

### Answer
The model supported is the **particle (or photon) model**.

### Explanation
To gain full marks, you need to contrast the two models based on how they explain the transfer of energy:

1.  **The Particle (Photon) Model:** According to this model, light consists of discrete packets of energy called photons. Each photon interacts with a single electron in the metal surface instantaneously. If a photon has enough energy (i.e., its frequency is above the threshold frequency $f_0$), it will transfer that energy to the electron immediately, regardless of the overall light intensity. This explains why photoelectrons are emitted "rapidly" (essentially instantaneously) even at very low intensities.

2.  **The Wave Model (The Flaw):** Classical wave theory suggests that light energy is spread continuously across the wavefront. At very low intensities, it would take a significant amount of time for an electron to "absorb" enough energy from the incident light waves to overcome the metal's work function ($\Phi$) and be ejected. The wave model predicts a significant time delay at low intensities, which is **not** observed experimentally.

**Summary for your exam:** State clearly that the particle model is supported because the energy transfer is one-to-one and instantaneous, whereas the wave model incorrectly predicts a time delay as the electron slowly accumulates energy from the wavefront.
