### Front
How do you determine the acceleration of an object rolling on an inclined plane?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Only the component of gravity acting parallel to the surface contributes to acceleration.
- **Formula:** $a = g \sin(\theta)$
- **When to use it:** When an object moves on a tilted surface; resolve velocity into components parallel and perpendicular to the slope.
- **Common trap:** Using $\cos(\theta)$ instead of $\sin(\theta)$ for the acceleration component; remember that $\sin(\theta)$ is used for the "downhill" component.
