### Part (a)
**Answer:**
To calculate the energy $E$ of a photon, we use the formula $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$.
- Planck's constant $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$
- Speed of light $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
- Wavelength $\lambda = 415 \text{ nm} = 415 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$

$$E = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)}{415 \times 10^{-9}} \approx 4.79 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

**Explanation:** Always ensure your units match. Since $\lambda$ is in nanometers, you must convert it to meters ($10^{-9}$) before substituting it into the equation.
