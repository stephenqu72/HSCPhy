To find the magnitude of the electromotive force (emf) produced in the coil, we use **Faraday's Law of Induction**.

### 1. The Physics Principle
Faraday's Law states that the induced emf ($\varepsilon$) in a coil with $N$ turns is equal to the negative rate of change of magnetic flux ($\Phi$) over time ($t$):
$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$$
Since the question asks for the **magnitude**, we can ignore the negative sign:
$$|\varepsilon| = N \times \left| \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t} \right|$$

### 2. Extracting Data from the Graph
*   **Number of turns ($N$):** $1000$
*   **Gradient ($\frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$):** We calculate the slope of the linear graph.
    *   At $t = 0 \text{ ms}$, $\Phi = 1 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb}$.
    *   At $t = 5 \text{ ms}$, $\Phi = 3 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb}$.
    *   Change in flux ($\Delta \Phi$) = $(3 - 1) \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb} = 2 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb}$.
    *   Change in time ($\Delta t$) = $5 \text{ ms} = 5 \times 10^{-3} \text{ s}$.

### 3. Calculation
Calculate the rate of change of flux:
$$\frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t} = \frac{2 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb}}{5 \times 10^{-3} \text{ s}} = 0.4 \times 10^{-1} \text{ Wb/s} = 0.04 \text{ Wb/s}$$

Now, multiply by the number of turns ($N = 1000$):
$$|\varepsilon| = 1000 \times 0.04 \text{ V} = 40 \text{ V}$$

Comparing this result to the given options:
(A) 0.04 V
(B) 0.06 V
(C) 40 V
(D) 60 V

The correct option is **(C)**.
