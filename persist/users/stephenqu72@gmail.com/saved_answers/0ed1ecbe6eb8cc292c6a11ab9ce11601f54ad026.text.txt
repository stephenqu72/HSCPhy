### Answer
1. **Change in Magnetic Flux:** As the magnet falls through the aluminium tube, the magnetic flux through the cross-section of the tube changes.
2. **Induction (Faraday’s Law):** According to Faraday’s Law, this changing magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) and creates eddy currents within the conductive aluminium tubing.
3. **Opposition (Lenz’s Law):** Lenz’s Law states that the direction of the induced eddy currents will be such that they create their own magnetic fields to oppose the change that produced them. Therefore, the magnetic field created by the eddy currents exerts an upward magnetic force on the falling magnet, opposing gravity.
4. **Conservation of Energy:** The kinetic energy of the falling magnet is being converted into electrical energy (the eddy currents), which is then dissipated as thermal energy (heat) in the aluminium tube due to its electrical resistance. 
5. **Conclusion:** Because some of the magnet's gravitational potential energy is diverted into thermal energy, the net downward force on the magnet is reduced, resulting in a slower acceleration and a longer fall time compared to the non-magnetic steel.

### How to solve this
When approaching HSC Physics questions on Lenz's Law, use a structured "Cause and Effect" sequence:

*   **Step 1: Identify the "Cause".** State that the moving magnet creates a changing magnetic field relative to the aluminium conductor.
*   **Step 2: Identify the "Induction".** Mention Faraday's Law to explain the creation of eddy currents.
*   **Step 3: State Lenz's Law explicitly.** Ensure you write: "The induced currents create a magnetic field that *opposes* the change in flux."
*   **Step 4: Energy Linkage.** Always explicitly state the energy transformation: $Gravitational\ Potential\ Energy \rightarrow Electrical\ Energy\ (Eddy\ Currents) \rightarrow Thermal\ Energy$. This is the standard syllabus requirement for "explaining in terms of conservation of energy."
