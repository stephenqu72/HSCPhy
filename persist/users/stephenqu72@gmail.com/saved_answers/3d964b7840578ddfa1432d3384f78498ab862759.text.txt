To solve this nuclear fission equation, we must apply the **Law of Conservation of Nucleon Number** (mass number) and the **Law of Conservation of Atomic Number** (charge). 

The reaction is:
$${}^{239}_{94}Pu + {}^{1}_{0}n \rightarrow {}^{134}_{Z}Xe + {}^{M}_{40}Zr + 3({}^{1}_{0}n)$$

### 1. Conservation of Atomic Number (bottom numbers)
The total atomic number (number of protons) before the reaction must equal the total atomic number after the reaction:
$$94 + 0 = Z + 40 + 3(0)$$
$$94 = Z + 40$$
$$Z = 94 - 40$$
$$Z = 54$$

### 2. Conservation of Mass Number (top numbers)
The total number of nucleons (protons + neutrons) before the reaction must equal the total number of nucleons after the reaction:
$$239 + 1 = 134 + M + 3(1)$$
$$240 = 137 + M$$
$$M = 240 - 137$$
$$M = 103$$

### Conclusion
We found that $Z = 54$ and $M = 103$. Comparing this to the given options:
(A) $Z=103, M=54$
(B) $Z=54, M=103$
(C) $Z=105, M=54$
(D) $Z=54, M=105$

The correct choice is **(B)**.
