To solve this problem, we must understand **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which describes the relationship between the temperature of a black body and the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) of the electromagnetic radiation it emits.

### Explanation
Wien’s law is mathematically expressed as:
$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$
Where:
- $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength of emission.
- $T$ is the absolute temperature (in Kelvin).
- $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant ($2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m}\cdot\text{K}$).

From this formula, we can see that the peak wavelength is **inversely proportional** to the absolute temperature. This means:
1. As the temperature ($T$) **increases**, the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) **decreases** (the light shifts toward the blue/ultraviolet end of the spectrum).
2. As the temperature ($T$) **decreases**, the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) **increases** (the light shifts toward the red/infrared end of the spectrum).
