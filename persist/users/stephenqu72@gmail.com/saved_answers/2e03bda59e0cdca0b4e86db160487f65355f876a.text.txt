### Answer
The missile will strike the cliff at a height of **$45.4\text{ m}$**.

### Explanation

**1. Identify the given components:**
*   Initial velocity, $u = 35\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Angle, $\theta = 55^\circ$
*   Horizontal distance (range), $x = 40.0\text{ m}$
*   Acceleration due to gravity, $g = 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$

**2. Resolve the initial velocity into components:**
*   Horizontal component: $u_x = u \cos(\theta) = 35 \cos(55^\circ) \approx 20.075\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Vertical component: $u_y = u \sin(\theta) = 35 \sin(55^\circ) \approx 28.670\text{ m s}^{-1}$

**3. Determine the time taken to reach the cliff:**
Since horizontal velocity is constant (ignoring air resistance), we use:
$$x = u_x t$$
$$t = \frac{x}{u_x} = \frac{40.0}{20.075} \approx 1.9925\text{ s}$$

**4. Calculate the vertical height ($y$) at that time:**
Using the displacement formula for vertical motion:
$$y = u_y t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2$$
Substitute the values:
$$y = (28.670)(1.9925) - 0.5(9.8)(1.9925)^2$$
$$y = 57.135 - 4.9(3.970)$$
$$y = 57.135 - 19.453$$
$$y = 37.682\text{ m}$$

*(Self-correction note: Let's re-calculate more precisely)*
$u_y \approx 28.6701$
$t \approx 1.9925$
$y = (28.6701 \times 1.9925) - (0.5 \times 9.8 \times 1.9925^2) \approx 57.135 - 19.453 = 37.68\text{ m}$.

Rounding to 3 significant figures, the height is **$37.7\text{ m}$**.
