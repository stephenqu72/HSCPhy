To design an experiment to find the angle of elevation for maximum horizontal range, follow this structured approach:

### Experimental Design
*   **Independent Variable:** The angle of launch (e.g., $15^{\circ}, 30^{\circ}, 45^{\circ}, 60^{\circ}, 75^{\circ}$).
*   **Dependent Variable:** The horizontal range (distance from launch point to landing point).
*   **Controlled Variables:** Launch velocity (initial speed), the height of the launcher, and the object being projected.
*   **Method:** Using a spring-loaded projectile launcher, set it to a fixed launch velocity. Launch the projectile at a specific angle and use a measuring tape to record the horizontal displacement where it hits the ground. Repeat for a range of angles.
*   **Reliability:** Repeat the experiment at least three times for each angle to calculate an average and minimize random error.
*   **Validity:** Ensure the launch height remains constant throughout (launch from floor level or a fixed table height). Use carbon paper/tape at the landing zone to accurately pinpoint the landing spot. Keep all environmental factors (like air resistance/wind) constant by conducting the test indoors.
