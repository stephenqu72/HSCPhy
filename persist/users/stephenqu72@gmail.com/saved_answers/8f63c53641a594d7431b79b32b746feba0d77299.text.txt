### Answer
**Emission spectra** are produced when excited electrons in an atom drop to a lower energy level, releasing a photon with energy equal to the difference between the two levels ($E = \Delta E = hf$). This creates bright lines on a dark background.

**Absorption spectra** are produced when white light passes through a cool gas. The atoms in the gas absorb specific frequencies of light that match the energy difference between their ground state and excited states, resulting in dark lines on a continuous rainbow background.

For any specific element, the emission and absorption spectral lines occur at the **same** wavelengths/frequencies, because they correspond to the same allowed electronic energy transitions within the atom.

### How to solve this
To achieve full marks (2/2) for this HSC-style question, you need to structure your response to address the three distinct parts of the prompt:

1.  **Explain Emission:** Focus on the "downward" transition. Mention that electrons drop from a higher energy state to a lower one, emitting a photon.
2.  **Explain Absorption:** Focus on the "upward" transition. Mention that photons of specific energy are absorbed by atoms, promoting electrons from a lower to a higher energy level.
3.  **Compare the two:** Explicitly state that they produce the **same** spectral lines. You should justify this by noting that the energy transitions ($\Delta E$) are identical for both processes for a given element, leading to the same characteristic wavelengths.

**Teacher's Tip:** In the HSC, always use the keyword "transition" when discussing spectra. Connecting the physical process to the conservation of energy equation ($E = hf$) demonstrates a deeper understanding of the physics involved.
