### Front
How does the kinetic energy ($K$) of a satellite in a circular orbit relate to its orbital radius ($r$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kinetic energy is inversely proportional to the orbital radius.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $K = \frac{GMm}{2r} \implies K \propto \frac{1}{r}$
- **When to use it:** Comparing kinetic energies or speeds of satellites at different orbital radii (e.g., in a "drawn to scale" diagram question).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to measure the radius $r$ from the **center of the planet** (Earth) rather than the surface.
