### Front
What is the direction and cause of acceleration for a projectile in flight (neglecting air resistance)?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration is constant and acts only vertically downwards.
- Formula / law / constant: Newton’s Second Law ($F_{net} = ma$); $a = g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ downwards.
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem to determine the net force or acceleration vector at any point in the trajectory.
- Common trap: Assuming acceleration changes at the peak or has a horizontal component.
