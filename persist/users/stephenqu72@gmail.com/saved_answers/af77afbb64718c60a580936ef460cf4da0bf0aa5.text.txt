To find the speed of the tip of the second hand, we use the relationship between linear speed ($v$) and angular velocity ($\omega$):

$$v = r\omega$$

### Given Data:
- Angular velocity ($\omega$) = $0.105\text{ rad s}^{-1}$
- Length of the hand (which acts as the radius, $r$) = $1.8\text{ cm}$

### Calculation:
Substitute the given values into the formula:
$$v = 1.8\text{ cm} \times 0.105\text{ rad s}^{-1}$$
$$v = 0.189\text{ cm s}^{-1}$$

### Explanation:
1. **Identify the variables:** The problem provides the angular velocity and the radius of the circular path (the length of the clock hand).
2. **Apply the formula:** The linear speed of an object moving in a circle is the product of its radius and its angular velocity.
3. **Calculate:** By multiplying $1.8$ by $0.105$, we obtain $0.189$.
4. **Units:** Since the radius was in $\text{cm}$ and the angular velocity in $\text{s}^{-1}$, the resulting unit is $\text{cm s}^{-1}$. Comparing this to the given options, it matches option (A).

The correct answer is **(A)**.
