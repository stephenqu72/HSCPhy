### Explanation
To solve this problem, we need to analyze the relationship between voltage, current, and power loss in electrical transmission lines.

**1. Relationship between Power, Voltage, and Current:**
The power transmitted ($P$) is given by the formula:
$$P = VI$$
If the power $P$ remains constant and the voltage $V$ is doubled ($V' = 2V$), the current $I$ must be halved to maintain the same power:
$$P = (2V) \times I' \implies I' = \frac{I}{2}$$

**2. Power Loss in Transmission Lines:**
Power loss ($P_{loss}$) due to resistance ($R$) in the transmission wires is calculated using:
$$P_{loss} = I^2 R$$

**3. Calculating the new Power Loss:**
If the current is halved ($I' = 0.5I$), we substitute this into the power loss formula:
$$P'_{loss} = (0.5I)^2 R = 0.25 I^2 R$$
$$P'_{loss} = 0.25 P_{loss}$$

**4. Conclusion:**
The new power loss is $25\%$ of the original power loss. This represents a **$75\%$ reduction** in the energy wasted due to the resistance in the transmission lines (calculated as $100\% - 25\% = 75\%$).
