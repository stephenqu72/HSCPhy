### Answer

**(a) Describe the four main components of a DC motor.**
1.  **Stator (Field Magnets):** Provides a constant external magnetic field (usually permanent magnets in small motors) within which the rotor spins.
2.  **Rotor (Armature):** A coil of wire wrapped around an iron core, which rotates when current flows through it within the magnetic field.
3.  **Commutator:** A split-ring device that reverses the direction of the current in the armature coil every half-turn, ensuring the torque acts in the same rotational direction.
4.  **Brushes:** Carbon blocks that maintain electrical contact with the rotating commutator, allowing DC current to be transferred from the external circuit to the spinning armature.

**(b) Briefly analyse the functions of a DC motor as it applies to this system. Include relevant energy transformations and transfers.**
The DC motor functions as a transducer that converts **electrical energy** into **mechanical kinetic energy**. In this system, the handheld controller regulates the current supplied to the motor. As the current flows through the armature in the presence of the stator's magnetic field, a force is exerted on the wires ($F = BIL\sin\theta$). This creates a torque that causes the rotor to spin. The energy is transferred from the power pack through the circuit to the motor, where it is transformed into mechanical work to drive the car's wheels.

**(c) After running a slot car for a length of time, the slot cars heat up immensely. Explain, using physics principles, why this is the case.**
The heating is primarily due to **Joule heating (Ohmic heating)** and mechanical friction.
1.  **Joule Heating:** According to the formula $P = I^2R$, electrical energy is dissipated as heat due to the internal resistance of the copper wire coils in the armature.
2.  **Eddy Currents:** Changing magnetic flux in the iron core of the armature induces small circulating currents (eddy currents), which produce additional heat.
3.  **Friction:** Mechanical friction at the bearings and between the brushes and the commutator converts a portion of the mechanical kinetic energy back into thermal energy.

### How to solve it
*   **Part (a):** This is a recall question. Memorize the four "pillars" of a DC motor: Magnets (Field), Armature (Rotor), Split-ring (Commutator), and Brushes. Always mention the *function* of the commutator (reversing current) as it is the most critical for marks.
*   **Part (b):** Always explicitly state the energy transformation: $E_{electrical} \rightarrow E_{kinetic}$. Mentioning the Motor Effect (Force on a current-carrying conductor) links the theory to the application (the slot car moving).
*   **Part (c):** Use the formula $P = I^2R$ to justify why energy is lost as heat in the coils. Adding the concept of "energy efficiency" or "energy dissipation" shows the examiner you understand that no real-world system is 100% efficient.
