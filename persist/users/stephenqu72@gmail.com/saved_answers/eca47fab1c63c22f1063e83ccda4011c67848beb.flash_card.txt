### Front
How do you compare the orbital periods ($T$) and radii ($r$) of two satellites orbiting the same central body?

### Back
- Key idea: Kepler’s Third Law states $T^2 \propto r^3$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\frac{T_1^2}{r_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{r_2^3}$
- When to use it: When comparing satellites or planets orbiting the same central mass (e.g., Earth or the Sun).
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the periods or cube the radii; ensure units for $T$ and $r$ are consistent across both satellites.
