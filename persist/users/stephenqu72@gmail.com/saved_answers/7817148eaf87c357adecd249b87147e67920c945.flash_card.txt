### Front
In Rutherford scattering, what happens to the electric potential energy and the magnitude of momentum as an alpha particle approaches a gold nucleus?

### Back
- Key idea: Kinetic energy is converted into electric potential energy due to electrostatic repulsion.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $U = \frac{kq_1q_2}{r}$ and $K = \frac{p^2}{2m}$ (Conservation of Energy).
- When to use it: Any problem involving charged particles moving within an electrostatic field.
- Common trap: Assuming momentum is conserved; momentum is **not** conserved for the alpha particle alone because the electrostatic repulsive force acts as an external force.
