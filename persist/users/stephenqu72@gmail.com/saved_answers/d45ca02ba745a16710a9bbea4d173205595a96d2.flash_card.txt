### Front
How can random errors be minimised in an experiment, and why does this method not work for systematic errors?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Random errors are unpredictable fluctuations; systematic errors are consistent biases.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** Use repeated readings (trials) to calculate a mean, which causes random positive/negative fluctuations to cancel out.
- **Common trap:** Assuming repeated trials reduce systematic error; systematic error requires recalibration or method adjustment, not more trials.
