### Front
How do you calculate the change in gravitational potential energy ($\Delta U$) for an object of mass $m$ moving between two points of gravitational potential $V_1$ and $V_2$?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational potential energy ($U$) is the product of mass ($m$) and gravitational potential ($V$). The change in $U$ is the difference between final and initial states.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\Delta U = m(V_2 - V_1)$
- **When to use it:** When calculating work done or energy changes as an object moves within a gravitational field (e.g., satellite orbital changes).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting the negative signs of gravitational potential; ensure you subtract initial from final ($V_f - V_i$) to determine if energy was gained or lost.
