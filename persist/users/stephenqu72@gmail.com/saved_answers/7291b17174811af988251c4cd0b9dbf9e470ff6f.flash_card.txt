### Front
How can you distinguish between an elastic and inelastic collision using kinetic energy?

### Back
- Key idea: Compare total kinetic energy ($\sum E_k$) before vs. after the collision.
- Formula / law: If $\sum E_{k, \text{initial}} = \sum E_{k, \text{final}}$, it is **elastic**. If $\sum E_{k, \text{initial}} > \sum E_{k, \text{final}}$, it is **inelastic**.
- When to use it: To determine if energy has been dissipated (e.g., into heat, sound, or deformation).
- Common trap: Confusing momentum with kinetic energy; while momentum is always conserved in a closed system, kinetic energy is often lost in inelastic collisions.
