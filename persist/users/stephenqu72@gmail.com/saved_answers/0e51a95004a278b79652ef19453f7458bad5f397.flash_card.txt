### Front
What is magnetic permeability ($\mu$) and what does it represent in the context of a material's magnetic response?

### Back
- Key idea: A measure of a material's ability to support the formation of a magnetic field within itself.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $B = \mu H$ (where $B$ is magnetic flux density, $H$ is magnetic field intensity).
- When to use it: To determine how easily a material allows magnetic flux to pass through it or how much it magnifies an external magnetic field.
- Common trap: Confusing $\mu$ with magnetic force between wires or electrical resistance; it refers to magnetic field "permeation," not resistance to current.
