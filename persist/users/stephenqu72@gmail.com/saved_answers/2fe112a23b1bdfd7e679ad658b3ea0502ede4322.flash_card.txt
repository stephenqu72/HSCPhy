### Front
What quantity changes due to the Doppler Effect when a sound source moves relative to an observer?

### Back
- **Key idea:** As a source moves towards an observer, wavefronts "bunch up," decreasing the observed wavelength ($\lambda$) and increasing the observed frequency ($f$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v = f \lambda$ (where $v$ is the speed of sound in the medium).
- **When to use it:** When a wave source and an observer have relative motion.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the **speed** of the wave changes; the speed remains constant as it depends only on the medium, not the source motion.
