This question requires a clear, comparative analysis of the historical debate regarding the nature of light. To score the full 6 marks, you must accurately identify the models and link them to the specific experimental observations that supported them at the time.

### Answer

**1. Newton's Corpuscular Model**
*   **Model:** Sir Isaac Newton proposed that light consists of a stream of tiny, fast-moving particles called "corpuscles" emitted by light sources.
*   **Supporting Evidence:** Newton’s model successfully explained **rectilinear propagation** (light travelling in straight lines), as particles follow paths governed by inertia. It also accounted for **reflection** (particles bouncing off surfaces like elastic balls) and **refraction** (particles accelerating as they enter a denser medium, with the normal force component increasing their speed).

**2. Huygens' Wave Model**
*   **Model:** Christiaan Huygens proposed that light behaves as a longitudinal mechanical wave propagating through a hypothetical medium called the "luminiferous aether."
*   **Supporting Evidence:** Huygens' model explained phenomena that the particle model struggled with, such as **diffraction** (the bending of waves around obstacles) and **refraction** (by predicting that light slows down in denser media). His model also explained how waves could pass through each other without interacting, unlike particles.

*   **Identify the components:** The question has two parts for two different scientists. Structure your response with clear headings or paragraphs to ensure you address both parts equally (3 marks each).
*   **Connect evidence to the model:** Don't just list phenomena (e.g., "reflection, refraction"). You must explicitly explain *how* the model interprets the observation (e.g., "particles behave like elastic spheres during reflection").
*   **Focus on the "Why":** For 6 marks, the examiner is looking for the reasoning behind why these models were adopted in the 17th century. A strong answer will contrast their interpretations of refraction, as this was the critical observation that ultimately helped distinguish the two models later on.
*   **Be concise:** Since this is a 6-mark question, avoid fluff. Focus on:
    *   Newton: Particles + Straight lines/Reflection/Refraction (elastic collisions).
    *   Huygens: Waves + Diffraction/Refraction (slower speed in denser media).
