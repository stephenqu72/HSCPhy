To solve this problem, we need to balance the downward force of gravity with the upward electrostatic force exerted on the charge.

### 1. Identify the forces
For the particle to be "exactly suspended," the net force must be zero. This means the upward electrostatic force must equal the downward gravitational force:
$$F_e = F_g$$

### 2. Set up the equations
*   The gravitational force is given by $F_g = mg$.
*   The electrostatic force between two parallel plates is given by $F_e = qE$, where $E$ is the electric field strength.
*   The electric field between parallel plates is related to the potential difference ($V$) and the distance between plates ($d$) by $E = \frac{V}{d}$.

Combining these, we get:
$$q \left( \frac{V}{d} \right) = mg$$

### 3. Rearrange for distance ($d$)
$$d = \frac{qV}{mg}$$

### 4. Substitute the values
*   $q = 4.0 \times 10^{-8} \, \text{C}$ (We use the magnitude for the force calculation)
*   $V = 35.0 \, \text{V}$
*   $m = 2.5 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{kg}$
*   $g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$

$$d = \frac{(4.0 \times 10^{-8}) \times 35.0}{(2.5 \times 10^{-6}) \times 9.8}$$
$$d = \frac{1.4 \times 10^{-6}}{2.45 \times 10^{-5}}$$
$$d \approx 0.05714 \, \text{m}$$

### 5. Convert units
To match the options provided:
$0.05714 \, \text{m} = 5.714 \, \text{cm} = 57.14 \, \text{mm}$

Rounding to the closest value provided in the options, we get $57 \, \text{mm}$.
