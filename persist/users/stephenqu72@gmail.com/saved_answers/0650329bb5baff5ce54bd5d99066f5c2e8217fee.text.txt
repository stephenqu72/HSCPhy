To solve this problem, we need to analyze the vertical motion of the stone. In projectile motion, the horizontal and vertical components of motion are independent of each other.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   Vertical displacement ($s_y$): $10\text{ m}$
    *   Initial vertical velocity ($u_y$): $0\text{ m/s}$ (since it is thrown horizontally)
    *   Acceleration due to gravity ($g$): $9.8\text{ m/s}^2$
    *   Time ($t$): ?

2.  **Choose the kinematic equation:**
    We use the equation for displacement under constant acceleration:
    $$s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} g t^2$$

3.  **Substitute the known values:**
    Since $u_y = 0$, the equation simplifies to:
    $$10 = 0 \cdot t + \frac{1}{2} \cdot 9.8 \cdot t^2$$
    $$10 = 4.9 t^2$$

4.  **Solve for $t$:**
    $$t^2 = \frac{10}{4.9}$$
    $$t^2 \approx 2.0408$$
    $$t = \sqrt{2.0408}$$
    $$t \approx 1.428\text{ s}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (consistent with the provided options), we get **$1.4\text{ s}$**.
