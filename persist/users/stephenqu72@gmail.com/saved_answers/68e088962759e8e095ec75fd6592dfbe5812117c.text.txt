To solve this, we need to distinguish between the contributions of key physicists in the development of quantum mechanics:

*   **Option A (Correct):** Erwin Schrödinger developed the **wave equation** (Schrödinger equation). This is a complex mathematical equation that describes how the quantum state of a physical system changes with time. In the context of the atom, it allows us to calculate the probability of finding an electron in a specific region of space, which we refer to as an "orbital" or an "electron cloud" model. This is essentially a mathematical model of electron distribution.
*   **Option B (Incorrect):** This refers to **Louis de Broglie**, who proposed the matter-wave hypothesis (that particles like electrons have wave-like properties, where $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$). Schrödinger built upon this idea to create his wave mechanics, but the initial hypothesis belongs to de Broglie.
*   **Option C (Incorrect):** This is primarily associated with **Niels Bohr**. Bohr’s model of the atom was built on the premise that electrons move in fixed, quantized orbits and that their energy is quantized.
*   **Option D (Incorrect):** The mass of the electron was determined by **J.J. Thomson** (via charge-to-mass ratio experiments) and later confirmed more precisely by **Robert Millikan** through his oil-drop experiment.

**Exam Tip:** In HSC Physics, it is crucial to clearly distinguish between these contributors. Remember: **Bohr** = Orbits (quantized energy levels), **de Broglie** = Wave-particle duality, and **Schrödinger** = Mathematical wave equations/probability clouds.
