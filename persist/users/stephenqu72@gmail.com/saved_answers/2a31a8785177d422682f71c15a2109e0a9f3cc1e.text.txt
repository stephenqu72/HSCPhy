### Answer
**Feature 1: Laminations**
The iron core is laminated (made of thin, insulated sheets) rather than being a single solid block. This increases the electrical resistance of the path for induced eddy currents. By breaking up the current paths, laminations significantly reduce the magnitude of eddy currents, thereby minimizing energy loss due to resistive heating ($P = I^2R$).

**Feature 2: Copper Wire**
The coils are made of copper wire, which has a very low electrical resistivity. This minimizes the resistance of the windings, which reduces energy lost as heat (Joule heating) when the current flows through the primary and secondary coils.
