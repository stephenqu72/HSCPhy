### Explanation

To solve this, we must consider the physics of power transmission. Power is transmitted from power stations to consumers through long cables that have an inherent electrical resistance, denoted as $R$.

1.  **Power Loss:** The energy lost as heat during transmission is given by the formula for power dissipation in a resistor:
    $$P_{\text{loss}} = I^2R$$
    where $I$ is the current flowing through the transmission lines.

2.  **The Relationship between Voltage and Current:** The power transmitted by the system is given by $P = VI$. For a fixed amount of power, if we increase the voltage ($V$), the current ($I$) must decrease proportionally.

3.  **The Advantage:** By using "step-up" transformers at the power station, the voltage is significantly increased. Because $P = VI$, a higher voltage allows the same amount of power to be transmitted at a much lower current. Since the power loss is proportional to the **square** of the current ($I^2$), reducing the current results in a massive reduction in energy loss due to heating in the wires.

*   (B) is incorrect because stepping up voltage results in *lower* current, not larger current.
*   (D) is correct because it identifies the crucial link: stepping up the voltage allows for lower current, which minimizes resistive power loss ($I^2R$), thereby increasing the efficiency of the power grid.
