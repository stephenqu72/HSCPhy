### Front
What is the acceleration of a projectile at the peak of its trajectory (ignoring air resistance)?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration is constant throughout the entire flight.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a = g \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ (downwards).
- When to use it: For any projectile motion problem where air resistance is negligible.
- Common trap: Assuming acceleration is zero at the peak because vertical velocity is zero.
