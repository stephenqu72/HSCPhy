### Answer

**(a) Differences between Newton and Huygens**
*   **Newton’s Corpuscular Model:** Newton proposed that light consists of a stream of tiny, fast-moving particles called "corpuscles" that travel in straight lines and obey the laws of reflection and refraction.
*   **Huygens’ Wave Model:** Huygens proposed that light is a longitudinal wave that propagates through a hypothetical medium called the "aether." He used his principle of secondary wavelets to explain propagation.

**(b) Young’s Double Slit Experiment**
*   Young’s double slit experiment provided definitive evidence for the **wave model of light**. 
*   When monochromatic light passes through two closely spaced slits, it produces an interference pattern on a screen consisting of alternating bright and dark fringes. 
*   This pattern is caused by the superposition (constructive and destructive interference) of waves originating from the two slits. 
*   Newton’s corpuscular model could not explain this observation, as particles would simply pass through the slits and create two distinct bright regions rather than a complex interference pattern. Therefore, the experiment supported Huygens’ wave theory over Newton’s particle theory.
