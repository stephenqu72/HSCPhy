### Front
How do you calculate the work required to move an object between two points in a gravitational field?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Work done equals the change in gravitational potential energy ($\Delta U$).
- **Formula:** $W = \Delta U = -GMm \left( \frac{1}{r_f} - \frac{1}{r_i} \right)$
- **When to use it:** When calculating energy changes for objects moving within a radial gravitational field (e.g., spacecraft, satellites).
- **Common trap:** Confusing the sign of the work. Moving against gravity (increasing $r$) requires positive external work, while the gravitational field itself does negative work.
