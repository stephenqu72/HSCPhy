### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must understand the Einstein photoelectric equation provided:
$$K_{\text{max}} = hf - \phi$$

In this equation, each term represents a specific energy component:
*   $hf$ represents the **energy of the incident photon**, where $h$ is Planck's constant and $f$ is the frequency of the light.
*   $K_{\text{max}}$ represents the **maximum kinetic energy** of the emitted photoelectron.
*   $\phi$ (phi) represents the **work function** of the material.

The **work function** ($\phi$) is defined as the minimum amount of energy required to remove an electron from the surface of a metal. If the incoming photon energy ($hf$) is less than the work function ($\phi$), no electrons are emitted. If it is greater, the excess energy becomes the kinetic energy of the emitted electron.

*   **Option A** describes the term $hf$.
*   **Option B** is incorrect because the electron's remaining energy relates to $K_{\text{max}}$, not $\phi$.
*   **Option C** correctly defines the work function as the energy required to release an electron from a material.
