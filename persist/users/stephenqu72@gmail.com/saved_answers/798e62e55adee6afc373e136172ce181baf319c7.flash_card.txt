### Front
How do you determine if an object will escape a planet's gravitational field, and what is the formula for escape velocity?

### Back
- Key idea: Compare the object's current velocity ($v$) to the required escape velocity ($v_e$); if $v \ge v_e$, it escapes.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$
- When to use it: To calculate the minimum speed needed to move an object from a planet's surface to infinity.
- Common trap: Using the mass of the object ($m$) instead of the mass of the planet ($M$), or confusing escape velocity with orbital velocity ($\sqrt{GM/R}$).
