### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must apply the physics of a charged particle moving in a magnetic field.

1.  **Understanding the Magnetic Force:** A charged particle moving with velocity $v$ in a magnetic field $B$ experiences a magnetic force given by $F = qvB \sin \theta$. Since the particle is moving in a plane perpendicular to the field, this force acts as a centripetal force:
    $$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
    Rearranging for the radius of the path ($r$):
    $$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

2.  **Analyzing the Spiral:** The image shows a spiral path where the radius $r$ is clearly **decreasing** as the particle moves toward the center. 
    *   Since $m$ (mass of the electron), $q$ (charge), and $B$ (the uniform magnetic field) are constant, the equation $r = \frac{mv}{qB}$ shows that $r$ is directly proportional to the speed $v$.
    *   As the radius $r$ decreases, the speed $v$ must also **decrease**.

3.  **Analyzing the Magnitude of the Force:** Now consider the magnetic force $F = qvB$. 
    *   We have established that the speed $v$ is decreasing as the electron spirals inward due to the energy lost in collisions with hydrogen molecules.
    *   Since $F$ is directly proportional to $v$ ($F \propto v$), a decrease in speed $v$ results in a **decrease** in the magnitude of the magnetic force $F$.

Therefore, as the electron spirals inward, its speed decreases, and the force acting on it also decreases.
