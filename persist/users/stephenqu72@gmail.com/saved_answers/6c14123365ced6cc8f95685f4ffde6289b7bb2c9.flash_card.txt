### Front
How do you calculate the number of helium nuclei produced by the Sun per second?

### Back
- Key idea: Total Power output divided by the energy released per single fusion event.
- Formula / law / constant: $N = \frac{P}{E}$, where $E = \Delta mc^2$ (typically $4.29 \times 10^{-12} \text{ J}$ or $26.7 \text{ MeV}$).
- When to use it: When calculating solar power output, mass-energy equivalence, or fuel consumption rates in stars.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the energy released corresponds to the fusion of *four* hydrogen nuclei into *one* helium nucleus.
