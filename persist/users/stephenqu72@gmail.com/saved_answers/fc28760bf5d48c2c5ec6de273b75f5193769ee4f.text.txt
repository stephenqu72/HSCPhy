### Answer

The development of the atomic model transitioned from classical mechanical descriptions to quantum mechanical understanding through the application of circular motion and wave theory.

1.  **Rutherford and Circular Motion:** Rutherford’s gold foil experiment proposed a planetary model where electrons orbit a dense, positive nucleus. Scientists applied the physics of **uniform circular motion** to this model, equating the centripetal force ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$) to the electrostatic force ($F_e = \frac{kq_1q_2}{r^2}$). However, classical electromagnetic theory predicted that an accelerating charge (the orbiting electron) must radiate energy, causing it to spiral into the nucleus. This highlighted a flaw in the planetary model.

2.  **Bohr’s Model and Circular Orbits:** Niels Bohr resolved this by postulating "stationary states." He kept the idea of circular motion but constrained it using the wave nature of electrons. He proposed that electrons only exist in stable orbits where their angular momentum is quantized: $mvr = \frac{nh}{2\pi}$. This prevented the collapse of the atom but was still essentially a classical orbit model.

3.  **De Broglie and Wave Mechanics:** Louis de Broglie introduced the concept that matter has wave-like properties, where the wavelength is given by $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$. This provided the theoretical justification for Bohr’s quantization; an electron can only exist in an orbit if its circumference is an integer multiple of its wavelength ($2\pi r = n\lambda$). This ensures constructive interference, creating a standing wave.

4.  **The Quantum Mechanical Model:** This transition from "particle orbits" to "wave functions" culminated in the Schrödinger model. Electrons are no longer viewed as particles tracing circular paths, but as 3D standing wave patterns (orbitals) described by the Schrödinger wave equation. The focus shifted from definite trajectories to the probability of finding an electron in a specific region of space.

*   **Structure:** Use a chronological approach. Break your response into: Rutherford (Classical Mechanics/Circular Motion) $\rightarrow$ Bohr (Quantized circular orbits) $\rightarrow$ De Broglie/Schrödinger (Wave-particle duality).
*   **Key Physics:** Ensure you explicitly mention the contradiction of classical mechanics (accelerating charges radiate energy) to show *why* the models had to change.
*   **Terminology:** Use high-level terms like "quantization," "standing waves," "electrostatic force," and "wave function."
*   **Linking:** Always explicitly link the physical principle (e.g., "standing waves") to the atomic model (e.g., "transition from discrete orbits to probability clouds").
