### Answer
The correct answer is **C. Increasing the distance between the nut and the point of application of the force.**

---

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must understand the physical definition of torque ($\tau$). Torque is the turning effect of a force and is defined by the formula:

$$\tau = rF \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
*   $r$ is the distance (lever arm length) from the pivot point (the nut) to the point where the force is applied.
*   $F$ is the magnitude of the applied force.
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the force vector and the wrench (lever arm).

**Analyzing the options:**
*   **A & B (Angles):** Torque is maximized when $\theta = 90^\circ$ (which is the case shown in the diagram). If the current angle is already $90^\circ$, changing the angle (increasing or decreasing) will actually **decrease** the value of $\sin(\theta)$ and thus reduce the torque. Therefore, A and B are incorrect.
*   **D (Distance):** Decreasing the distance ($r$) will directly decrease the torque because $\tau$ is directly proportional to $r$. 
*   **C (Distance):** Increasing the distance ($r$) from the nut to the point of application increases the lever arm. Since torque is directly proportional to the radius $r$, increasing the distance will increase the magnitude of the torque. This is why long-handled wrenches are used to loosen tight nuts—they provide more leverage.
