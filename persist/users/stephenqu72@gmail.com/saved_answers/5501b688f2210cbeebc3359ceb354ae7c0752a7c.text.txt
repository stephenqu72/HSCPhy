To determine the compression required to store $0.9\text{ J}$ of potential energy, we follow these steps:

### 1. Find the spring constant ($k$)
The graph shows a linear relationship between force ($F$) and compression ($x$), which follows Hooke's Law: $F = kx$.
From the graph, we can see that when $x = 0.08\text{ m}$, $F = 40\text{ N}$.
Rearranging to find $k$:
$$k = \frac{F}{x} = \frac{40\text{ N}}{0.08\text{ m}} = 500\text{ N/m}$$

### 2. Relate potential energy to compression
The elastic potential energy ($U$) stored in a spring is given by the formula:
$$U = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$$

### 3. Solve for $x$
We are given $U = 0.9\text{ J}$ and we found $k = 500\text{ N/m}$. Substitute these into the formula:
$$0.9 = \frac{1}{2}(500)x^2$$
$$0.9 = 250x^2$$
$$x^2 = \frac{0.9}{250}$$
$$x^2 = 0.0036$$
$$x = \sqrt{0.0036} = 0.06\text{ m}$$

Comparing this result to the given options:
A. $0.05\text{ m}$
B. $0.06\text{ m}$
C. $0.07\text{ m}$
D. $0.08\text{ m}$

The correct option is **B**.

***

**Teacher's Tip:** Alternatively, you can solve this using the area under the force-displacement graph. Since $U$ is the area of the triangle ($Area = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$), and height $F = kx$, then $U = \frac{1}{2} \times x \times (kx) = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$. Using the graph to find the spring constant is the most reliable method for this type of problem.
