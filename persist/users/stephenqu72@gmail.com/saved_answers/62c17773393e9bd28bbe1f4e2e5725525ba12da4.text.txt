To solve this physics problem, we need to apply the principles of wave interference and the wave equation.

### Part a: Calculate the wavelength
The relationship between speed ($v$), frequency ($f$), and wavelength ($\lambda$) is given by the wave equation:
$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$
Rearranging to solve for $\lambda$:
$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$
Given $v = 340\text{ m s}^{-1}$ and $f = 340\text{ Hz}$:
$$\lambda = \frac{340}{340} = 1.0\text{ m}$$

---

### Part b: Calculate the displacement for the second region of quietness
When two sources emit identical waves, interference occurs. A "region of quietness" corresponds to **destructive interference**. This occurs when the path difference ($\Delta d$) between the waves from the two speakers is an odd multiple of half-wavelengths:
$$\Delta d = (n - 0.5)\lambda$$
where $n = 1, 2, 3, \dots$ (the order of the nodes).

Let the student move a distance $x$ from the centre toward speaker B.
*   Distance from A: $d_A = 5.0 + x$
*   Distance from B: $d_B = 5.0 - x$
*   Path difference: $\Delta d = |d_A - d_B| = |(5.0 + x) - (5.0 - x)| = 2x$

The **first** quiet region ($n=1$) occurs at $\Delta d = 0.5\lambda$.
The **second** quiet region ($n=2$) occurs at $\Delta d = 1.5\lambda$.

Setting the path difference equal to the second node condition:
$$2x = 1.5\lambda$$
Since $\lambda = 1.0\text{ m}$:
$$2x = 1.5 \times 1.0 = 1.5$$
$$x = \frac{1.5}{2} = 0.75\text{ m}$$

**Reasoning:** The student is experiencing destructive interference. The second quiet spot corresponds to a path difference of $1.5$ wavelengths. Because moving $x$ meters increases the distance to one speaker by $x$ and decreases the distance to the other by $x$, the path difference is $2x$. Solving $2x = 1.5\lambda$ gives a distance of $0.75\text{ m}$.
