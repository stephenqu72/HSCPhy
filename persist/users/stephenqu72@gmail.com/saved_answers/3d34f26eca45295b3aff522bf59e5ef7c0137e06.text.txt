To determine the correct relationship between the force between two parallel current-carrying conductors and the distance between them, we must look at the relevant physics formula.

### The Physics Principle
The magnitude of the force per unit length ($F/L$) between two long, straight, parallel conductors separated by a distance $r$ is given by the formula:

$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2 \pi r}$$

Where:
*   $F$ is the force
*   $L$ is the length of the conductor
*   $\mu_0$ is the permeability of free space
*   $I_1$ and $I_2$ are the currents in the two conductors
*   $r$ is the separation distance

### Analysis
From the formula, we can see that for constant currents, the force per unit length is inversely proportional to the distance between the wires:

$$\frac{F}{L} \propto \frac{1}{r}$$

This is an **inverse relationship**. Mathematically, as $r$ increases, the force decreases rapidly, and as $r$ approaches zero, the force approaches infinity.
