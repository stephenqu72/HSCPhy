### Front
Do all fundamental fields (gravitational, electric, and magnetic) follow the inverse square law? Explain.

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational and electric fields follow the inverse square law, but magnetic fields do not.
- Formula / law: $F_g \propto 1/r^2$ and $F_e \propto 1/r^2$; magnetic fields (dipoles) typically follow $1/r^3$ dependence.
- When to use it: When comparing field strength vs. distance relationships for point sources or dipoles.
- Common trap: Assuming all force fields obey the inverse square law simply because gravity and electrostatics do.
