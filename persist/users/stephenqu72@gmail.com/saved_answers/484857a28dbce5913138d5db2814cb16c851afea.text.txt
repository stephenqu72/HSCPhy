To answer this question, we apply the principles of the **Doppler Effect** in astronomy.

### Answer
The correct option is **(D)**.

### Explanation
1.  **Understand the Shift:** 
    *   The stationary hydrogen $\alpha$ line is at $656.3\text{ nm}$.
    *   The observed wavelength from the star is $686.2\text{ nm}$.
    *   Since $686.2\text{ nm} > 656.3\text{ nm}$, the light has shifted towards a longer wavelength. On the visible spectrum, longer wavelengths correspond to the red end of the spectrum. This phenomenon is called a **red-shift**.

2.  **Relate the Shift to Motion:**
    *   The Doppler effect for light states that when an object is moving away from an observer, the waves it emits are "stretched," resulting in an increase in wavelength (red-shift).
    *   Conversely, if an object were moving towards an observer, the waves would be "compressed," resulting in a decrease in wavelength (blue-shift).

3.  **Conclusion:**
    *   Because the observed light is red-shifted (wavelength has increased), the star must be moving **away from Earth**. 

Therefore, comparing this to the given options:
*   (A) Incorrect: It is not blue-shifted.
*   (B) Incorrect: It is not blue-shifted or moving towards Earth.
*   (C) Incorrect: It is not moving towards Earth.
*   (D) **Correct**: It is red-shifted and moving away from Earth.
