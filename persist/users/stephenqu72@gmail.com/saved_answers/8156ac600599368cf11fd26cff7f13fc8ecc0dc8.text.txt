To find the magnitude of the force on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field, we use the formula:

$$F = BIl \sin(\theta)$$

### Given data:
*   Magnetic field strength, $B = 2.5\text{ T}$
*   Current, $I = 1.5\text{ A}$
*   Length of the conductor, $l = 0.24\text{ m}$
*   Angle between the conductor and the magnetic field lines, $\theta = 25^\circ$

### Calculation:
Substitute the values into the formula:

$$F = 2.5 \times 1.5 \times 0.24 \times \sin(25^\circ)$$

First, calculate the product of the constants:
$$2.5 \times 1.5 \times 0.24 = 0.9$$

Now, multiply by the sine of the angle:
$$F = 0.9 \times \sin(25^\circ)$$
$$F \approx 0.9 \times 0.4226$$
$$F \approx 0.3803\text{ N}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the magnitude of the force is **0.38 N**.

The correct option is **B**.

### Explanation:
1.  **Identify the physical principle**: The force exerted on a current-carrying wire in a uniform magnetic field is given by the Lorentz force law for conductors.
2.  **Determine the angle**: The angle $\theta$ in the formula $F = BIl \sin(\theta)$ is defined as the angle between the direction of the current (the wire) and the direction of the magnetic field lines. As shown in the diagram, this is $25^\circ$.
3.  **Perform the calculation**: Ensure your calculator is set to **degrees** mode before calculating $\sin(25^\circ)$. Multiplying the values gives the force in Newtons.
