### Front
How does gravitational field strength ($g$) vary with distance ($r$) from the centre of a planet, and what does the graph look like?

### Back
- Key idea: $g$ follows an inverse-square relationship with distance ($g \propto 1/r^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$; the graph is a non-linear curve that flattens as $r$ increases.
- When to use it: Any question involving orbital mechanics or planetary gravity.
- Common trap: Confusing the $1/r^2$ graph with a linear inverse relationship ($1/r$) or a straight line.
