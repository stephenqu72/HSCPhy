### Front
In the classroom mousetrap-and-ball demonstration of a nuclear chain reaction, what do the balls represent?

### Back
- Key idea: Neutrons (the particles that trigger and sustain nuclear fission).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: To explain how a self-sustaining chain reaction occurs in fissile material (e.g., U-235).
- Common trap: Confusing the balls with the nuclei; the mousetrap is the unstable nucleus, while the ball is the neutron ejected from it.
