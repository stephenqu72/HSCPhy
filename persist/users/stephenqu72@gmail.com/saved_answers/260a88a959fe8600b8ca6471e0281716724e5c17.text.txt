### Answer
Louis de Broglie proposed the hypothesis of **wave-particle duality** for matter, suggesting that all moving particles (like electrons) exhibit wave-like properties. He postulated that the wavelength of a particle is inversely proportional to its momentum. This theory was fundamental in explaining the discrete energy levels of electrons in the Bohr model of the atom by treating them as standing waves.

The relevant equation is:
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$
Where:
*   $\lambda$ is the de Broglie wavelength
*   $h$ is Planck's constant
*   $p$ is the momentum of the particle
*   $m$ is the mass of the particle
*   $v$ is the velocity of the particle
