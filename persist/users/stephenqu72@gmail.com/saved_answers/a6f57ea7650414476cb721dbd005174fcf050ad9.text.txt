To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of polarized light passing through two polarising filters (a polariser and an analyser).

### Explanation

1.  **First Polariser:** When unpolarised light of intensity $I_0$ passes through the first polariser, the intensity of the light is reduced by half regardless of the orientation of the plate.
    $$I_1 = \frac{1}{2}I_0$$
    The light emerging from the first plate is now linearly polarised.

2.  **Second Polariser (Malus' Law):** Malus' Law states that the intensity of light transmitted through a second polariser is given by:
    $$I = I_1 \cos^2(\theta)$$
    where $\theta$ is the angle between the transmission axes of the two polarisers. Given $\theta = 60^\circ$:
    $$\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5 \implies \cos^2(60^\circ) = (0.5)^2 = 0.25 = \frac{1}{4}$$

3.  **Calculation:** Substituting $I_1$ into the equation:
    $$I = \left(\frac{1}{2}I_0\right) \times \left(\frac{1}{4}\right)$$
    $$I = \frac{1}{8}I_0$$
