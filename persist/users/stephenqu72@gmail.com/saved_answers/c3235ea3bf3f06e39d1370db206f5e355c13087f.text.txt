To solve this question, we must understand the scientific definitions of **accuracy** and **precision**:

*   **Accuracy:** How close the measured values are to the "true" or expected value (in this case, the bullseye at the very centre of the dartboard).
*   **Precision:** How close the measured values are to each other (how tightly grouped they are, regardless of where they land).

### Analysis of the players:
*   **Player A:** The darts are clustered tightly together very close to the bullseye. This is **accurate** and **precise**.
*   **Player B:** The darts are scattered widely across the board. This is **neither accurate nor precise**.
*   **Player C:** The darts are scattered widely across the board. This is **neither accurate nor precise**.

2.  **Assess Grouping (Precision):** Observe which player has their darts in a tight cluster. Player A and Player D show high precision.
3.  **Assess Proximity to Target (Accuracy):** Check if the tight cluster is on the bullseye. Player A's cluster is on the bullseye (accurate), while Player D's cluster is significantly off-centre (inaccurate).
