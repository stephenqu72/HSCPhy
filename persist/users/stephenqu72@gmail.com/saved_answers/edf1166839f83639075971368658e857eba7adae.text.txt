### Step 1: Analyze the decay processes

1.  **$\beta^-$ decay:** A neutron is converted into a proton and an electron (which is emitted). 
    *   The nucleon number $A$ remains unchanged because one neutron is lost and one proton is gained ($A = N_{neutrons} + Z_{protons}$).
    *   The proton number $Z$ increases by 1 ($Z \rightarrow Z + 1$).
    *   On a graph of $A$ vs $Z$, this corresponds to a horizontal movement to the **right**.

2.  **$\alpha$ decay:** The nucleus emits an alpha particle ($^4_2\text{He}$).
    *   The nucleon number $A$ decreases by 4.
    *   The proton number $Z$ decreases by 2.
    *   On a graph of $A$ vs $Z$, this corresponds to a diagonal movement **down and to the left** (specifically, a slope of $\frac{\Delta A}{\Delta Z} = \frac{-4}{-2} = 2$).
