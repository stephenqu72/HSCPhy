### Front
In uniform circular motion, what is the path of an object immediately after the centripetal force is removed?

### Back
- Key idea: An object in motion continues in a straight line at its instantaneous velocity (tangent to the circle) due to inertia.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s First Law of Motion.
- When to use it: When a tether breaks or centripetal force ceases abruptly.
- Common trap: Assuming the object continues to curve or flies radially outward (away from the center); it actually travels tangent to the path.
