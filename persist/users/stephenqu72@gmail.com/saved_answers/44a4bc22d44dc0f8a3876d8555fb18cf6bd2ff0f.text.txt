### Explanation
To determine the correct nuclear equation for the proton-proton chain (the primary fusion process in stars like the Sun), we must ensure that the conservation laws of physics—specifically the conservation of **nucleon number (mass number)** and **atomic number (charge)**—are satisfied.

1.  **Reactants:** The reaction starts with four hydrogen nuclei (protons), denoted as $4 \times {}_1^1\text{H}$.
2.  **Products:** The goal is to form one helium nucleus, ${}_2^4\text{He}$.
3.  **Conservation of Charge:** The total atomic number on the left is $4 \times 1 = 4$. The helium nucleus has an atomic number of 2. To conserve charge, we must account for a positive charge of $+2$ on the right side. This is achieved by the emission of two positrons ($2e^+$), as each positron carries a charge of $+1$.
4.  **Conservation of Leptons:** In the process of fusion, specifically during the weak nuclear interaction (where protons convert to neutrons), neutrinos ($\nu$) are emitted to conserve lepton number. Therefore, two electron neutrinos are required.
5.  **Energy:** Gamma rays ($\gamma$) are released to conserve energy (momentum and mass-energy equivalence).

The balanced equation is:
$$4_1^1\text{H} \rightarrow {}_2^4\text{He} + 2e^+ + 2\gamma + 2\nu$$

*   **Option A** correctly includes the positrons, gamma rays, and neutrinos necessary for physical conservation laws.
*   **Option B** is incorrect because it lacks the neutrinos required to conserve lepton number.
*   **Options C and D** suggest electron capture (involving $e^-$), which is not the standard representation of the net proton-proton chain fusion process.
