The correct answer is **B. Proton-proton chain**.

### Explanation

To solve this question, you need to understand the nuclear fusion processes that power stars of different masses and stages of evolution:

1.  **Proton-proton (p-p) chain:** This is the primary fusion process in stars with masses similar to or smaller than our Sun (stars with core temperatures below approximately 15 million Kelvin). It involves the fusion of hydrogen nuclei (protons) into helium. Because our Sun is a main-sequence star of average mass, this is its dominant energy-production mechanism.
2.  **CNO cycle (Carbon-Nitrogen-Oxygen cycle):** While this also fuses hydrogen into helium, it requires higher core temperatures than those found in the Sun. It is the dominant process in stars more massive than the Sun.
3.  **Triple alpha process:** This process involves the fusion of helium nuclei into carbon. It occurs in red giant stars after they have exhausted their core hydrogen, not in a main-sequence star like the Sun.
4.  **Positron-electron annihilation:** While this process does occur *within* the Sun (as a byproduct of the beta-plus decay steps in the p-p chain), it is not a "nucleosynthesis reaction" itself; it is a particle interaction that releases energy, not a process that builds heavier nuclei from lighter ones.

By process of elimination and knowledge of stellar evolution, the proton-proton chain is the standard process for the Sun's core nucleosynthesis.
