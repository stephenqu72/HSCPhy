To solve this problem, you must understand the physics of wave diffraction. Diffraction is the bending of waves around obstacles or through openings. The extent of diffraction—how much the waves "spread out"—is determined by the ratio of the wavelength ($\lambda$) to the slit width ($w$).

1.  **The Physics Principle:** The degree of diffraction is most significant when the slit width $w$ is approximately equal to or smaller than the wavelength $\lambda$ ($w \approx \lambda$). When the slit width is much larger than the wavelength ($w \gg \lambda$), the waves pass through with very little diffraction, resulting in a mostly straight wavefront with slight bending at the edges.
2.  **Analyzing the "Original" State:** In the provided original diagram, the wavelength is roughly comparable to the slit width, resulting in semi-circular wavefronts (a moderate amount of diffraction).
