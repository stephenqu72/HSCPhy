### Explanation

In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), we treat the horizontal and vertical components of motion independently:

*   **Horizontal Motion:** There is no horizontal force acting on the projectile. Therefore, the horizontal acceleration is zero, and the horizontal velocity ($v_x$) remains constant throughout the entire flight. This makes statement **(B)** correct.
*   **Vertical Motion:** The only force acting on the projectile is gravity. This results in a constant downward acceleration of $g \approx 9.8 \, m/s^2$. Because the acceleration is constant and identical at all points, statement **(A)** is correct.
*   **Energy:** In a closed system without air resistance, the total mechanical energy (sum of kinetic and gravitational potential energy) is conserved. Since the projectile moves between different heights, kinetic energy is converted to potential energy and vice versa, but the sum remains constant. This makes statement **(C)** correct.
*   **Vertical Velocity:** The vertical component of velocity changes continuously due to gravity. The projectile is moving upward at point $P$ and downward at point $Q$. Unless points $P$ and $Q$ are at the exact same height on the trajectory (which they clearly are not in this diagram), their vertical velocities will be different. Even if they were at the same height, the velocities would have opposite signs ($+v_y$ and $-v_y$), meaning they are not equal. Therefore, statement **(D)** is incorrect.
