### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Explanation:** 
In the absence of air resistance, all objects fall under the influence of gravity with the same constant acceleration, $g$, regardless of their mass. The vertical motion is governed by the kinematic equation $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$. Assuming the particles leave the hopper with zero initial vertical velocity ($u_y = 0$), the equation becomes $h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$. 

Rearranging for time $t$:
$$t = \sqrt{\frac{2h}{g}}$$
Since $h$ (vertical distance) and $g$ (acceleration due to gravity) are constants for all particles, the time $t$ is independent of the mass $m$.

---

#### Part (b)
**Step 1: Calculate the time of flight ($t$).**
Using $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$ and $h = 4.5\text{ m}$:
$$t = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 4.5}{9.8}} \approx 0.9578\text{ s}$$

**Step 2: Determine the electric field strength ($E$).**
The plates are separated by $d = 0.35\text{ m}$ with a potential difference $V = 65,000\text{ V}$:
$$E = \frac{V}{d} = \frac{65000}{0.35} \approx 185714.29\text{ V/m}$$

**Step 3: Calculate horizontal acceleration ($a_x$).**
The force on the particle is $F = qE$, and by Newton's Second Law, $F = ma$. Therefore, $ma = qE$, which leads to $a_x = \frac{q}{m}E$.
Given the specific charge $\frac{q}{m} = 1.2 \times 10^{-6}\text{ C/kg}$:
$$a_x = (1.2 \times 10^{-6}) \times 185714.29 \approx 0.22286\text{ m/s}^2$$

**Step 4: Calculate horizontal deflection ($x$).**
Using the kinematic equation for horizontal motion starting from rest ($u_x = 0$):
$$x = \frac{1}{2}a_xt^2$$
$$x = 0.5 \times 0.22286 \times (0.9578)^2 \approx 0.102\text{ m}$$

**Final Answer:** The total horizontal deflection is approximately **$0.10\text{ m}$** (or $10.2\text{ cm}$).
