### Answer

**(a) Transformer Calculations**

1.  **Secondary Voltage ($V_s$):**
    For an ideal (100% efficient) transformer, power input equals power output:
    $$P_p = P_s$$
    $$V_p I_p = V_s I_s$$
    Given $V_p = 120\text{ V}$, $I_p = 3.0\text{ mA}$, and $I_s = 30\text{ mA}$:
    $$120 \times 3.0 = V_s \times 30$$
    $$360 = 30V_s$$
    $$V_s = 12\text{ V}$$

2.  **Number of turns in primary coil ($N_p$):**
    Using the transformer ratio formula:
    $$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$
    Given $V_p = 120\text{ V}$, $V_s = 12\text{ V}$, and $N_s = 5$:
    $$\frac{120}{12} = \frac{N_p}{5}$$
    $$10 = \frac{N_p}{5}$$
    $$N_p = 50$$

**(b) Faraday's Observations**

Faraday's experiment relies on **Faraday's Law of Induction**, which states that an electromotive force (EMF) is induced only when there is a **change in magnetic flux** through the circuit.

*   **Switch open or closed (steady state):** When the switch is either fully open or closed, the current in Coil A is either zero or constant DC. A constant current produces a constant magnetic field. Since the magnetic flux through Coil B is not changing, no current is induced, and the compass needle does not deflect.
*   **Switch initially opened or closed:** When the switch is opened or closed, the current in Coil A changes rapidly from zero to maximum or vice-versa. This causes the magnetic field generated by Coil A to change. This changing magnetic flux is transmitted through the iron ring to Coil B. According to Faraday’s Law, this change in flux induces an EMF (and thus a current) in Coil B, which creates its own magnetic field, causing the compass needle to deflect momentarily.
