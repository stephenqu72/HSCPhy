### Front
How are Newton's Second Law and the concept of Impulse related when a constant force acts on an object over time $\Delta t$?

### Back
- Key idea: A constant force produces constant acceleration, resulting in a change in momentum (Impulse) that is proportional to the duration of the force.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F = ma$ and $J = F\Delta t = \Delta p$.
- When to use it: To calculate acceleration, final velocity ($v = u + at$), or the total momentum change from a known force and time interval.
- Common trap: Confusing impulse ($J$) with final velocity ($v$) or final momentum ($p$).
