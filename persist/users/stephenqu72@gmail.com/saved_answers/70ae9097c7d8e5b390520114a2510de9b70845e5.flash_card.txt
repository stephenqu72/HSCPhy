### Front
Which law explains why a planet takes longer to travel equal distances at different points in its elliptical orbit, and how does proximity to the star affect this?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Orbital speed is inversely proportional to the planet's distance from the star (perihelion = max speed; aphelion = min speed).
- **Formula / law:** Kepler’s Second Law (Law of Equal Areas).
- **When to use it:** When comparing travel times or orbital speeds of a planet at different positions along an elliptical path.
- **Common trap:** Assuming constant orbital speed; always identify the focus (star) position first to determine if the planet is near perihelion or aphelion.
