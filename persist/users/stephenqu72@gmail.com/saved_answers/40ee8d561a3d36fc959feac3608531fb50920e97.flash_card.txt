### Front
How do you determine the decay equation for Cobalt-60 ($^{60}_{27}\text{Co}$) transitioning to Nickel-60 ($^{60}_{28}\text{Ni}$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Conservation of mass number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$) determines the decay type ($\beta^-$ decay).
- **Formula / law:** $^{60}_{27}\text{Co} \rightarrow ^{60}_{28}\text{Ni} + _{-1}^{0}\beta + \bar{\nu}$
- **When to use it:** When the atomic number increases by 1 while the mass number remains constant.
- **Common trap:** Confusing $\beta^-$ decay (electron emission) with $\beta^+$ decay (positron emission), which would decrease the atomic number.
