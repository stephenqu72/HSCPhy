### Front
What is the relationship between the period ($T$) of a wave and its frequency ($f$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Frequency is the reciprocal of the period (the number of cycles per second).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $f = \frac{1}{T}$
- When to use it: To convert between the time taken for one full oscillation ($T$) and the frequency ($f$).
- Common trap: Forgetting to ensure the period ($T$) is in seconds (s) before calculating frequency in Hertz (Hz).
