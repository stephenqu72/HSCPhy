To solve this problem, we must apply the fundamental principles of electrostatics regarding parallel plate capacitors.

### Explanation

1.  **Uniform Electric Field:** In an ideal parallel plate capacitor (where the separation $d$ is small compared to the dimensions of the plates), the electric field is produced by the superposition of the fields from the two charged plates.
2.  **Derivation:** A single infinite charged plate produces a field strength of $E = \frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_0}$, where $\sigma$ is the surface charge density. For two parallel plates with equal and opposite charges, the field between the plates is the sum of the fields from each plate:
    $$E_{total} = E_{positive} + E_{negative} = \frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_0} + \frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_0} = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_0}$$
3.  **Result:** Since $\sigma$ (charge per unit area) and $\epsilon_0$ (permittivity of free space) are both constants, the magnitude of the electric field strength $E$ is constant at every point between the plates.
4.  **Graph Analysis:** A constant value for $E$ as $x$ increases from $0$ to $d$ corresponds to a horizontal straight line.
    *   Graph A shows a decreasing field.
    *   Graph B shows a field that peaks in the middle.
    *   Graph C shows a **constant** field strength, which correctly represents the uniform field between parallel plates.
    *   Graph D shows an increasing field.

Therefore, the correct graph is **C**.
