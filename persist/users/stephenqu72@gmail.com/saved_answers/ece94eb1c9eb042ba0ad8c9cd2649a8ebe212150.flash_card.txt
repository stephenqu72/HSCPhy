### Front
How can you determine if a nuclear reaction releases energy based on the masses of the reactants and products?

### Back
- **Key idea:** If mass is lost (mass defect), the reaction is exothermic (energy released).
- **Formula / law:** $\Delta m = (\Sigma m_{\text{products}}) - (\Sigma m_{\text{reactants}})$ and $E = \Delta mc^2$.
- **When to use it:** To determine the energetics of a nuclear transmutation or decay when atomic masses are provided.
- **Common trap:** Confusing mass defect with binding energy per nucleon; always ensure the final $\Delta m$ is negative for energy release (products lighter than reactants).
