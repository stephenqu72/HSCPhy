### Front
How do you calculate the gravitational field strength ($g$) at the surface of a spherical body like Triton?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational field strength depends on the mass of the body and the square of the distance from its center.
- Formula: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- When to use it: To find the acceleration due to gravity on the surface of a planet or moon.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the radius ($r$) or using the diameter instead of the radius.
