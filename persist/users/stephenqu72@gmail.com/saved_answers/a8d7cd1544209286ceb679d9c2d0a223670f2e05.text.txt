To answer this question, you need to apply the Photoelectric Effect, which is a cornerstone of quantum physics and Einstein's particle model of light.

### Answer
According to Einstein’s model, light consists of discrete packets of energy called photons, where the energy of each photon is given by $E = hf$. 

1.  **Energy Transfer:** A single photon interacts with a single electron on the metal surface. The photon transfers all its energy to that electron instantaneously.
2.  **Work Function:** For an electron to be emitted (photoelectric effect), the photon's energy must be greater than or equal to the work function ($\Phi$) of the metal, which is the minimum energy required to remove an electron from the surface.
3.  **Threshold Frequency:** Since $E = hf$, if the frequency of the incident light ($f$) is less than the threshold frequency ($f_0$), the photon energy is insufficient to overcome the work function, and no electrons are emitted. If $f \ge f_0$, the energy is sufficient to liberate the electrons, which are then accelerated by the circuit's potential difference to create a detectable current.

### How to solve this
When approaching HSC Physics questions on the photoelectric effect, always follow these steps to secure full marks:

*   **Step 1: Define the model.** State that light acts as a particle (photon) with energy proportional to its frequency ($E = hf$). This is the core requirement for "Einstein's model."
*   **Step 2: Explain the "One-to-One" interaction.** Explicitly mention that one photon interacts with one electron. This refutes the classical wave model, which suggested energy could build up over time.
*   **Step 3: Connect to the threshold.** Explain that emission only occurs if the photon energy is greater than the work function ($\Phi$). Link this directly to frequency because $E = hf$.
*   **Step 4: Mention the current.** Conclude by stating that the freed electrons (photoelectrons) are then collected by the anode, completing the circuit and resulting in a measurable current.
