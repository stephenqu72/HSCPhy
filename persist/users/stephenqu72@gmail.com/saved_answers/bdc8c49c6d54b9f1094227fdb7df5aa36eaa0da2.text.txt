### Answer

James Clerk Maxwell’s primary contribution was the unification of electricity and magnetism into a single, cohesive electromagnetic field theory. His work is best summarized by the following four points:

1.  **Unification of Laws:** Maxwell synthesized existing laws (Gauss’s Law for electricity, Gauss’s Law for magnetism, Faraday’s Law of induction, and Ampère’s Law) into a set of unified mathematical equations, now known as Maxwell’s Equations.
2.  **Introduction of Displacement Current:** Maxwell recognized that a changing electric field produces a magnetic field. He introduced the concept of "displacement current" to modify Ampère’s Law, allowing for consistent results in circuits involving changing electric fields (such as capacitors).
3.  **Prediction of Electromagnetic Waves:** By manipulating his equations, Maxwell demonstrated that oscillating electric and magnetic fields would propagate through space as self-sustaining electromagnetic waves.
4.  **Determination of the Speed of Light:** Maxwell calculated the theoretical speed of these electromagnetic waves using the permittivity ($\epsilon_0$) and permeability ($\mu_0$) of free space, finding that $c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0}}$. The result was consistent with the known speed of light, leading him to correctly propose that light is an electromagnetic wave.
