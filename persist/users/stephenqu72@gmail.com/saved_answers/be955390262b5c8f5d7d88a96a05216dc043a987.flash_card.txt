### Front
How is artificial gravity created in a space station and what is the formula for the magnitude of this acceleration?

### Back
- Key idea: Artificial gravity is generated by centripetal acceleration through rotation, creating a normal force against the outer hull that simulates weight.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{v^2}{r} = \omega^2 r$
- When to use it: Use when calculating required rotation speed ($\omega$) or radius ($r$) to simulate Earth’s gravity ($9.8\text{ m/s}^2$) for occupants.
- Common trap: Confusing an orbiting station (free-fall/weightlessness) with a rotating station (artificial gravity).
