To solve this problem, we need to determine the energy difference between two quantum states that corresponds to an absorbed photon with a wavelength of $\lambda = 122\text{ nm}$.

### Step 1: Calculate the energy of the photon
The energy $E$ of a photon is given by the formula:
$$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$

Using the constants:
- $h \approx 4.136 \times 10^{-15}\text{ eV s}$
- $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$
- $\lambda = 122 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$

$$E = \frac{(4.136 \times 10^{-15}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)}{122 \times 10^{-9}}$$
$$E \approx 10.17\text{ eV}$$

For a photon to be **absorbed**, the electron must move from a lower energy level to a higher energy level. This eliminates options C and D (which represent emission).
