To solve this problem, we use Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion, which states that the square of the orbital period ($T$) is proportional to the cube of the orbital radius ($r$):

$$\frac{T_X^2}{r_X^3} = \frac{T_Y^2}{r_Y^3}$$

### Step 1: Determine the orbital period of Planet $X$ ($T_X$)
We are told that in $5$ years, Planet $X$ rotates through $120^\circ$. Since a full orbit is $360^\circ$:
$$\frac{120^\circ}{360^\circ} = \frac{1}{3} \text{ of an orbit}$$
If $1/3$ of an orbit takes $5$ years, then:
$$T_X = 5 \times 3 = 15 \text{ years}$$

### Step 2: Relate the periods and radii
We are given the ratio of the radii $r_X : r_Y = 2 : 5$, so:
$$\frac{r_X}{r_Y} = \frac{2}{5}$$
Using Kepler's Third Law rearranged to solve for $T_Y$:
$$\frac{T_Y^2}{T_X^2} = \left(\frac{r_Y}{r_X}\right)^3$$
$$T_Y^2 = T_X^2 \times \left(\frac{r_Y}{r_X}\right)^3$$

### Step 3: Calculation
Substitute the known values ($T_X = 15$, $r_Y/r_X = 5/2 = 2.5$):
$$T_Y^2 = 15^2 \times (2.5)^3$$
$$T_Y^2 = 225 \times 15.625$$
$$T_Y^2 = 3515.625$$
$$T_Y = \sqrt{3515.625} \approx 59.3 \text{ years}$$

**Final Answer:** It takes Planet $Y$ approximately **59.3 years** to orbit the star.
