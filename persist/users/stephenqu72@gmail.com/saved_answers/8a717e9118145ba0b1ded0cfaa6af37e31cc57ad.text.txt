To determine which loop experiences the largest torque, we use the formula for the torque $\tau$ on a current-carrying loop in a magnetic field:

$$\tau = nIAB \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
- $n$ is the number of turns (which is 1 for all loops).
- $I$ is the current (1.0 A for all loops).
- $A$ is the area of the loop.
- $B$ is the magnetic flux density.
- $\theta$ is the angle between the normal to the plane of the loop and the magnetic field direction.

### Analysis of the Physics
In this problem, the magnetic field is **parallel to the plane of the loops**. 
1. The normal vector of a loop is always perpendicular to its plane.
2. If the magnetic field is parallel to the plane of the loop, the angle between the normal vector and the magnetic field is $90^\circ$.
3. Since $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$, the formula simplifies to $\tau = IAB$.

However, we must look at the geometry of these specific loops. The torque produced by a magnetic field on a loop is generated by the forces on the sides **perpendicular** to the magnetic field.
- The torque $\tau = F \times d$, where $F = IlB$.
- The force $F$ acts on the sides of length $l$ that are perpendicular to the field. These sides are separated by a distance $w$ (the width of the loop parallel to the field).
- Therefore, the torque is $\tau = (IlB) \times w = I(lw)B = IAB$.

### Calculating for each loop
Since $I$ and $B$ are constant for all loops, the torque is directly proportional to the area $A$ of the loop:

*   **Loop A:** Area $= L \times 0.2L = 0.2L^2$
*   **Loop B:** Area $= 0.5L \times 0.5L = 0.25L^2$
*   **Loop C:** Area $= 0.5L \times 0.3L = 0.15L^2$
*   **Loop D:** Area $= 0.8L \times 0.2L = 0.16L^2$

Comparing the areas: $0.25L^2 > 0.2L^2 > 0.16L^2 > 0.15L^2$.

Loop B has the largest area, and therefore, it experiences the largest torque.

**Answer:** Loop B
