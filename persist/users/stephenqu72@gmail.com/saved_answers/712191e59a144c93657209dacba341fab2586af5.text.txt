### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) 105 nm**.

### Explanation

To solve this problem, we need to understand the energy transitions involved in the hydrogen atom's Lyman series and how to calculate the wavelength of the emitted photon.

**1. Identify the energy levels:**
The problem provides four energy levels ($n=1, 2, 3, 4$):
*   $E_1 = -21.8 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$
*   $E_2 = -5.4 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$
*   $E_3 = -2.8 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$
*   $E_4 = -1.4 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$

**2. Determine the specific transition:**
*   The **Lyman series** corresponds to electrons falling to the ground state ($n=1$).
*   The lines in the series are defined by the starting energy level $n_{initial}$.
*   The "long wavelength end" corresponds to the smallest energy difference (since $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$).
*   The first line of the Lyman series is $n=2 \rightarrow n=1$.
*   The **second line** of the Lyman series is $n=3 \rightarrow n=1$.

**3. Calculate the energy difference ($\Delta E$):**
$$\Delta E = E_3 - E_1$$
$$\Delta E = (-2.8 \times 10^{-19}) - (-21.8 \times 10^{-19})$$
$$\Delta E = 19.0 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$$

**4. Calculate the wavelength ($\lambda$):**
Using the formula $\Delta E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$, rearranged to $\lambda = \frac{hc}{\Delta E}$:
*   $h \approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J s}$
*   $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$

$$\lambda = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)}{19.0 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$\lambda \approx \frac{1.9878 \times 10^{-25}}{1.90 \times 10^{-18}}$$
$$\lambda \approx 1.046 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m}$$

Converting to nanometers ($1\text{ nm} = 10^{-9}\text{ m}$):
$$\lambda \approx 104.6\text{ nm} \approx 105\text{ nm}$$

Comparing this to the given options, (A) is the correct value.
