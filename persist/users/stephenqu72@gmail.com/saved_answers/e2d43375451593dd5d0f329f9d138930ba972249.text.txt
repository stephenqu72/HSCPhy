To determine which coil experiences the greatest torque, we use the formula for the torque $\tau$ on a current-carrying coil in a magnetic field:

$$\tau = nIAB \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
- $n$ is the number of loops.
- $I$ is the current.
- $A$ is the area of the coil.
- $B$ is the magnetic field strength.
- $\theta$ is the angle between the normal to the coil and the magnetic field.

Since $I$ and $B$ are constant for all four options, the torque is proportional to the product of the number of loops ($n$) and the area ($A$): 
$$\tau \propto n \times A$$

We calculate the product $n \times A$ for each option:

*   **A:** $1 \times 0.18 = 0.18 \text{ m}^2$
*   **B:** $2 \times 0.045 = 0.090 \text{ m}^2$
*   **C:** $3 \times 0.020 = 0.060 \text{ m}^2$
*   **D:** $4 \times 0.011 = 0.044 \text{ m}^2$

**Explanation:**
The torque produced by a motor effect is dependent on the magnetic moment of the coil, defined as $m = nIA$. Because the total length of the wire is fixed, the perimeter ($P$) of each coil is constant ($P = n \times \text{circumference of one loop}$). As the number of loops ($n$) increases, the radius (and thus the area $A$) must decrease significantly. Mathematically, for a fixed length of wire $L$, $A \propto 1/n^2$. Therefore, the product $nA$ is proportional to $n(1/n^2) = 1/n$. This means that as the number of loops increases, the total magnetic moment decreases.

Comparing the calculated values, option A yields the largest value.

**Correct Answer:** A
