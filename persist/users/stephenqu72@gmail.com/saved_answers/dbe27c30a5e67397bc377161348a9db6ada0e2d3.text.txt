### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to understand the relationship between stellar mass, core temperature, and the mechanisms of hydrogen fusion in main sequence stars.

1.  **Proton-Proton (p-p) Chain:** This process is the dominant source of energy in stars like our Sun (stars with a mass $\leq 1.3 M_{\odot}$). Because the nuclei involved (protons) have lower charge ($Z=1$), the electrostatic repulsion (Coulomb barrier) is lower. Therefore, the p-p chain can proceed at lower core temperatures. Because it is less temperature-sensitive, the reaction rate is relatively slower compared to the CNO cycle.
2.  **CNO Cycle:** This process is the dominant source of energy in stars more massive than the Sun (stars with a mass $> 1.3 M_{\odot}$). The CNO cycle involves nuclei with higher atomic numbers ($Z > 1$), such as Carbon, Nitrogen, and Oxygen. These nuclei have higher positive charges, resulting in a much stronger Coulomb barrier. Consequently, the CNO cycle requires significantly higher core temperatures to overcome this repulsion. Once these temperatures are reached, the reaction rate of the CNO cycle increases very rapidly with temperature, making it a much faster and more efficient energy production mechanism in hotter, more massive stars.
