To solve this problem, we need to understand the definitions of **accuracy** and **reliability** (often referred to as precision in scientific contexts) in the HSC Physics syllabus.

### 1. Definitions
*   **Accuracy:** How close a measured value is to the **true value**. We determine this by looking at how close the average of the readings is to $7.33\text{ mA}$.
*   **Reliability (Precision):** How close the individual measurements are to each other. We determine this by looking at the **spread** or range of the data. A smaller spread indicates higher reliability.

### 2. Analysis
**Accuracy:**
*   **True value:** $7.33\text{ mA}$
*   **Rob’s average:** $7.35\text{ mA}$
    *   Difference: $|7.35 - 7.33| = 0.02\text{ mA}$
*   **Jan’s average:** $7.28\text{ mA}$
    *   Difference: $|7.28 - 7.33| = 0.05\text{ mA}$
*   Since Rob's average is closer to the true value, **Rob's results are more accurate.**

**Reliability:**
*   **Rob’s range:** Highest ($7.60$) - Lowest ($7.10$) = $0.50\text{ mA}$
*   **Jan’s range:** Highest ($7.36$) - Lowest ($7.20$) = $0.16\text{ mA}$
*   Since Jan's readings are clustered much more closely together, her results have a smaller spread, meaning **Jan's results are more reliable.**

### 3. Conclusion
Comparing the options:
*   A is incorrect because the averages differ from the true value by different amounts.
*   B is correct because $0.02 < 0.05$.
*   C is incorrect because the spreads are significantly different.
*   D is incorrect because Rob's spread is much larger than Jan's.

The correct answer is **B**.
