To solve this problem, we use the kinematic equations for motion under constant acceleration (gravity). Since the ball is thrown downwards, we define the downward direction as positive.

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   Initial velocity ($u$): $2.25\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Time ($t$): $1.80\text{ s}$
*   Acceleration due to gravity ($g$): $9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$ (acting downwards, so it is positive)
*   Displacement ($s$): This represents the height of the building ($h$)

### 2. Choose the correct kinematic formula:
We use the displacement equation:
$$s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$

### 3. Substitute the values:
$$h = (2.25)(1.80) + \frac{1}{2}(9.8)(1.80)^2$$

Calculate each term:
*   Term 1: $2.25 \times 1.80 = 4.05$
*   Term 2: $0.5 \times 9.8 \times 3.24 = 4.9 \times 3.24 = 15.876$

Summing these together:
$$h = 4.05 + 15.876 = 19.926\text{ m}$$

Rounding to significant figures consistent with the provided data, we get approximately $20\text{ m}$.

Comparing this to the options:
(A) $16\text{ m}$
(B) $20\text{ m}$
(C) $25\text{ m}$
(D) $30\text{ m}$

The correct answer is **(B)**.
