To solve this problem, we apply the **Motor Effect**, which describes the force exerted on a current-carrying conductor placed within a magnetic field. This force is determined using the **Right-Hand Palm Rule** (also known as Fleming's Left-Hand Rule).

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the Magnetic Field ($B$):** Magnetic field lines always point from the North pole to the South pole. In the diagram, the North pole is behind the foil and the South pole is in front of the foil. Therefore, the magnetic field is directed **out of the page** (towards you).
2.  **Identify the Direction of Conventional Current ($I$):** Look at the battery symbol. The long line represents the positive terminal and the short line the negative. Following the circuit from the positive terminal, the current travels from the right side of the foil to the left side. Thus, the current is flowing **to the left**.
3.  **Apply the Right-Hand Palm Rule:**
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$): **Out of the page** (towards your face).
    *   Point your thumb in the direction of the current ($I$): **To the left**.
    *   Your palm (or the force vector $F$) will now be pointing **downwards**.

Alternatively, using **Fleming’s Left-Hand Rule**:
*   **F**irst finger (Field): Point out of the page.
*   **Se**cond finger (Current): Point to the left.
*   **Th**umb (Motion): Will point **downwards**.

The force acting on the aluminium foil is directed downwards, causing it to move in that direction.

**Conclusion:** The correct answer is **(A)**.
