### Answer

Erwin Schrödinger made a pivotal contribution to the quantum mechanical model of the atom by proposing that electrons exhibit wave-like properties. He developed the **Schrödinger wave equation**, which treated the electron as a standing wave rather than a discrete particle orbiting the nucleus in a fixed path. 

The solutions to his wave equation, known as wave functions ($\psi$), describe the probability density of finding an electron in a specific region of space around the nucleus. This shifted the atomic model from the Bohr model’s planetary orbits to the concept of **atomic orbitals** (electron clouds), where there is a high probability of finding an electron. By mathematically defining these regions of high probability, Schrödinger provided the theoretical foundation for our modern understanding of electron configurations and subshells.
