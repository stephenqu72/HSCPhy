### Answer

**(a)**
*   **Star P:** White Dwarf
*   **Star R:** Main Sequence Star
*   **Star S:** Red Giant (or Red Supergiant)

**(b)**
*   **Star R (Main Sequence):** This star is fusing hydrogen into helium. This primarily occurs via the proton-proton chain (in stars like the Sun) or the CNO cycle (in more massive stars). 
    *   **Reactants:** Hydrogen nuclei (protons).
    *   **Products:** Helium nucleus, energy (gamma rays), and neutrinos/positrons.
*   **Star S (Red Giant):** This star has exhausted its core hydrogen and is currently fusing helium into heavier elements like carbon and oxygen via the triple-alpha process.
    *   **Reactants:** Helium nuclei (alpha particles).
    *   **Products:** Carbon (and potentially oxygen), and energy.
*   **Star P (White Dwarf):** This star is in its final evolutionary stage and is no longer undergoing significant nuclear fusion. It is simply radiating away residual thermal energy.
    *   **Reactants/Products:** None (nuclear fusion has ceased).

To excel in HSC Physics questions regarding the Hertzsprung-Russell (H-R) diagram, follow these steps:

1.  **Identify regions on the H-R diagram:**
    *   The prominent diagonal band from top-left to bottom-right is the **Main Sequence**. Star **R** sits here, representing a star in its stable hydrogen-fusing phase.
    *   The region to the right and above the main sequence is the **Giant/Supergiant branch**. Star **S** is here, indicating a star that has expanded and cooled after leaving the main sequence.
    *   The bottom-left region consists of hot but low-luminosity stars, which are **White Dwarfs**. Star **P** is located here.

2.  **Recall the nucleosynthesis cycle:**
    *   **Main Sequence:** Remember the core principle is the conversion of Hydrogen to Helium ($4^1H \rightarrow ^4He$).
    *   **Red Giant:** Once hydrogen is depleted, the core collapses and heats up, allowing helium fusion. The primary reaction is the triple-alpha process ($3^4He \rightarrow ^{12}C$).
    *   **White Dwarf:** Understand that this is the "dead" core of a low-to-medium mass star. Since the gravitational pressure is balanced by electron degeneracy pressure rather than thermal pressure from fusion, no active nucleosynthesis is occurring.
