### Front
What is the formula for the banking angle ($\theta$) of a frictionless track required for a vehicle to traverse a circular path of radius $r$ at velocity $v$?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force required for circular motion.
- **Formula:** $\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$
- **When to use it:** When analyzing circular motion on banked tracks where friction is negligible.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert velocity from $\text{km/h}$ to $\text{m/s}$ ($v \div 3.6$) before substituting into the formula.
