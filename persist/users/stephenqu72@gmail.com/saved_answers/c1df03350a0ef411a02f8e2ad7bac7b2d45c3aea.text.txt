### Question 11

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:**
The spring constant $k$ is defined by Hooke's Law: $F = kx$, where $F$ is the force and $x$ is the displacement (compression). Therefore, $k$ is the gradient of the force-compression graph.

1.  Identify the coordinates from the graph: At $x = 0.04\text{ m}$, the force $F = 4.0\text{ kN}$.
2.  Convert the units to SI units:
    *   $x = 0.04\text{ m}$
    *   $F = 4.0\text{ kN} = 4000\text{ N} = 4.0 \times 10^3\text{ N}$
3.  Calculate the gradient ($k$):
    $$k = \frac{F}{x} = \frac{4.0 \times 10^3\text{ N}}{0.04\text{ m}} = \frac{4000}{0.04} = 100,000\text{ N m}^{-1} = 1.0 \times 10^5\text{ N m}^{-1}$$
Matching this to the options, **D** is correct.

### Question 12
