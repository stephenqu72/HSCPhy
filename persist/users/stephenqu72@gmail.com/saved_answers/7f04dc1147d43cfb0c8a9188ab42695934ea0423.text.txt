To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of circular motion and Newton’s Second Law to the rollercoaster trolley at the lowest point of the dip.

### Analysis of Forces
1.  **Identify the forces:** At the bottom of the dip, there are two primary forces acting on the trolley:
    *   The normal force ($F_N$), which acts **upwards** (perpendicular to the track).
    *   The gravitational force ($mg$), which acts **downwards**.
2.  **Determine the net force:** Since the trolley is moving in a circular path, there must be a net centripetal force ($F_c$) directed toward the center of the circle (which is **upwards** at this point).
3.  **Apply Newton's Second Law:** The net force is the vector sum of all forces acting on the object. Taking "up" as the positive direction:
    $$F_{net} = F_N - mg = F_c$$
    Rearranging this, we get:
    $$F_N = F_c + mg$$

### Evaluating the Options
*   **(A) Incorrect:** Since $F_N = F_c + mg$, the normal force is actually **greater** than the weight ($mg$) of the trolley, which is the force experienced on a flat surface.
*   **(B) Incorrect:** The centripetal force is the *net* force required for circular motion. It is provided by the difference between the normal force and gravity, not just the normal force alone.
*   **(C) Correct:** The "sum of the normal force and gravitational force" refers to the vector sum. Since these two forces act in opposite directions, the magnitude of their sum is $F_N - mg$, which is exactly equal to the centripetal force required to keep the trolley in its circular path.
*   **(D) Incorrect:** Since (C) is correct, this is false.

**Answer:** (C)
