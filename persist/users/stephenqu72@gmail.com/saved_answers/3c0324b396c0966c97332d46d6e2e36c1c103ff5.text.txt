*   **Change in Neutron Number ($N$):** $142 \rightarrow 140$. This is a decrease of $2$.

When an isotope emits an $\alpha$ particle (a helium nucleus, $_2^4\text{He}$), the atomic number decreases by $2$ and the mass number ($A = Z + N$) decreases by $4$. Since both $Z$ and $N$ decreased by $2$, the total mass number decreased by $4$. This confirms that process $R$ is **$\alpha$ decay**.

### 2. Identifying Product $Q$

*   **Atomic Number ($Z$):** $90$
*   **Neutron Number ($N$):** $140$

The mass number $A$ is the sum of protons and neutrons:
$$A = Z + N = 90 + 140 = 230$$

In the periodic table, the element with atomic number $90$ is Thorium (Th). Therefore, the isotope is **Th-230**.
