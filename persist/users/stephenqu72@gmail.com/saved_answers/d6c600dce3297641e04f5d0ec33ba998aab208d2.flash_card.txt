### Front
Explain why Gamma ($\gamma$) radiation travels in a straight line through a uniform magnetic field, whereas Beta ($\beta$) particles curve.

### Back
- Key idea: The magnetic force on a moving charge ($F = qvB\sin\theta$) is zero if $q=0$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F = qvB\sin\theta$ (Magnetic Force on a moving charge).
- When to use it: To determine the nature (charge) of ionizing radiation when passed through a magnetic field.
- Common trap: Assuming all radiation curves; remember that $\gamma$-rays are neutral (EM photons) and remain undeflected.
