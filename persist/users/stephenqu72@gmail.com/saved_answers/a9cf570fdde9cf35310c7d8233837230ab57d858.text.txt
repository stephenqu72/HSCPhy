To determine the direction of the magnetic field at point $P$, we use the **Right-Hand Grip Rule**.

### Explanation

1.  **Understand the Right-Hand Grip Rule**: If you point your right thumb in the direction of the current $I$ in a wire, your fingers curl in the direction of the magnetic field lines generated by that wire.
2.  **Analyze the top wire**: The current is flowing to the right. Applying the right-hand grip rule, the magnetic field lines will emerge **out of the page** above the wire and go **into the page** below the wire (at point $P$).
3.  **Analyze the bottom wire**: The current is also flowing to the right. Applying the rule here, your fingers will be pointing **out of the page** at point $P$, which is above this wire.
4.  **Vector Addition**:
    *   The magnetic field from the top wire ($B_{top}$) is directed **into the page**.
    *   The magnetic field from the bottom wire ($B_{bottom}$) is directed **out of the page**.
5.  **Conclusion**: Since the wires carry equal currents and point $P$ is at the midpoint, the magnitudes of the magnetic fields are equal ($|B_{top}| = |B_{bottom}|$). Because the vectors point in exactly opposite directions, they cancel each other out completely.

Therefore, the net magnetic field at $P$ is **zero**.
