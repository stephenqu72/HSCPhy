### Answer
The correct answer is **B. towards C.**

### Explanation
To understand why the net force acts towards point $C$, we must analyze the forces acting on the mass $M$ during its uniform circular motion:

1.  **Identify the forces:** There are two forces acting on the mass $M$:
    *   **Gravity ($F_g = Mg$):** This force acts vertically downwards.
    *   **Tension ($T$):** This force acts along the string towards the pivot point $P$.

2.  **Determine the resultant force:** According to Newton's Second Law ($F_{net} = ma$), the net force acting on an object in uniform circular motion must be the **centripetal force**. The centripetal force is directed towards the center of the circular path.

3.  **Vector Decomposition:**
    *   The vertical component of the tension ($T \cos\theta$) must balance the gravitational force ($Mg$) because the mass has no vertical acceleration ($a_y = 0$).
    *   The horizontal component of the tension ($T \sin\theta$) provides the necessary centripetal force to keep the mass moving in a circle.
    *   Since the center of the circular motion is point $C$, the horizontal component of the tension (the net force) must point exactly towards $C$.

Because the vertical forces cancel out, the vector sum of gravity and tension is a resultant vector that points horizontally towards the center of the circular orbit, which is point $C$.
