To solve these problems, we use the principles of mass-energy equivalence, radioactive decay, and thermal physics.

### (a) Rest mass of the $\beta$-particle
**Solution:**
The rest mass of an electron ($m_e$) is $9.109 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$. Using Einstein’s equation $E = mc^2$:
$$E = (9.109 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}) \times (2.998 \times 10^8\text{ m/s})^2 = 8.187 \times 10^{-14}\text{ J}$$
Convert Joules to eV by dividing by $1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J/eV}$:
$$E = \frac{8.187 \times 10^{-14}}{1.602 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 511,000\text{ eV} = 0.511\text{ MeV}$$

### (b) Mass of Nickel-60
**Solution:**
The energy released ($Q$) in the decay is the sum of the kinetic energy of the $\beta$-particle ($0.31\text{ MeV}$) and the energies of the two gamma photons ($1.17\text{ MeV} + 1.33\text{ MeV}$):
$$Q = 0.31 + 1.17 + 1.33 = 2.81\text{ MeV}$$
In mass units (where $1\text{ u} \approx 931.5\text{ MeV}$):
$$\Delta m = \frac{2.81\text{ MeV}}{931.5\text{ MeV/u}} \approx 0.003016\text{ u}$$
Since $M_{Co} = M_{Ni} + \Delta m$, then $M_{Ni} = 59.919015 - 0.003016 = 59.915999\text{ u}$.

### (c) Half-life calculation
**Solution:**
Use the decay formula $A = A_0(0.5)^{t/t_{1/2}}$:
$$7.0 = 18.5(0.5)^{t/5.27}$$
$$\frac{7.0}{18.5} = 0.5^{t/5.27} \implies \ln(0.3784) = \frac{t}{5.27} \ln(0.5)$$
$$t = \frac{\ln(0.3784)}{\ln(0.5)} \times 5.27 \approx 1.403 \times 5.27 \approx 7.40\text{ years}$$

### (d)(i) Temperature change
**Solution:**
1. Find total decays in 10 minutes ($600\text{ s}$): $N = A \times t = (18.5 \times 10^9\text{ Bq}) \times 600\text{ s} = 1.11 \times 10^{13}\text{ decays}$.
2. Total energy $E = N \times Q = (1.11 \times 10^{13}) \times (2.81 \times 10^6\text{ eV}) \times (1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J/eV}) \approx 4.997\text{ J}$.
3. Using $Q = mc\Delta T$:
$$\Delta T = \frac{Q}{mc} = \frac{4.997}{60 \times 3.5 \times 10^3} \approx 2.38 \times 10^{-5}\text{ K}$$
