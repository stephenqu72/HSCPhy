*   **Magnetic Flux ($\Phi$):** Magnetic flux is defined by the formula $\Phi = B \cdot A \cos \theta$, where $A$ is the area of the coil. Since both coils are perpendicular to the field ($\theta = 0^\circ$), the flux is simply $\Phi = BA$. Because coil $Q$ has a larger surface area than coil $P$, and the magnetic flux density ($B$) is the same for both, coil $Q$ encompasses more magnetic field lines, resulting in a larger total magnetic flux.
