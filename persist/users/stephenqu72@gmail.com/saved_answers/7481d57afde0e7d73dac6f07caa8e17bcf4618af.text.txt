To determine which alternative produces the maximum torque, we use the formula for torque:
$$\tau = r \cdot F \cdot \sin(\theta)$$
where:
- $r$ is the distance from the pivot point (axis of rotation) to the point where the force is applied.
- $F$ is the magnitude of the force.
- $\theta$ is the angle between the force vector and the beam (or the component of force perpendicular to the beam).

Let's calculate the torque for each option:

**(A)** $r = 0.7\text{ m}$, $F = 2.9\text{ N}$, $\theta = 15^\circ$ (angle between force and beam):
$$\tau_A = 0.7 \cdot 2.9 \cdot \sin(15^\circ) \approx 2.03 \cdot 0.2588 \approx 0.526\text{ Nm}$$

**(B)** $r = 2.0 - 1.4 = 0.6\text{ m}$, $F = 5.3\text{ N}$, $\theta = 90^\circ$:
$$\tau_B = 0.6 \cdot 5.3 \cdot \sin(90^\circ) = 3.18 \cdot 1 = 3.18\text{ Nm}$$

**(C)** $r = 2.0\text{ m}$, $F = 3.4\text{ N}$, $\theta = 30^\circ$:
$$\tau_C = 2.0 \cdot 3.4 \cdot \sin(30^\circ) = 6.8 \cdot 0.5 = 3.4\text{ Nm}$$

**(D)** $r = 2.0\text{ m}$, $F = 4.2\text{ N}$, $\theta = 45^\circ$:
$$\tau_D = 2.0 \cdot 4.2 \cdot \sin(45^\circ) = 8.4 \cdot 0.7071 \approx 5.94\text{ Nm}$$

Comparing the values:
- $\tau_A \approx 0.53\text{ Nm}$
- $\tau_B = 3.18\text{ Nm}$
- $\tau_C = 3.40\text{ Nm}$
- $\tau_D \approx 5.94\text{ Nm}$

The maximum torque is applied in alternative (D).
