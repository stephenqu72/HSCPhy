### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Assessment:** The claim is **incorrect**. 
**Explanation:** While it is true that Person Y will measure a longer time than Person X, the reason is **not** simply "time dilation." The difference in time measured by Y arises primarily because the sensor is moving *towards* the light source in Y's frame (shortening the distance the light must travel relative to the sensor), while the light is moving at a constant speed $c$ in all frames. Time dilation applies to clocks moving relative to an observer; here, the difference is fundamentally due to the relativity of simultaneity and the constant speed of light, not just the slowing of a clock.

#### Part (b)
**Calculations:**
Given: Rest length of carriage $L_0 = 35\text{ m}$. Distance from center to rear $d_0 = 17.5\text{ m}$. Velocity $v = 0.95c$.

**1. In Person X's frame:**
The distance to the sensor is $17.5\text{ m}$. Light travels at $c$.
$$t_X = \frac{d_0}{c} = \frac{17.5}{3 \times 10^8} \approx 5.83 \times 10^{-8}\text{ s}$$

**2. In Person Y's frame:**
First, we find the contracted length of the distance from the center to the rear. The Lorentz factor $\gamma$ is:
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.95^2}} \approx 3.20$$
The contracted distance $d$ in Y's frame is $d = \frac{d_0}{\gamma} = \frac{17.5}{3.20} \approx 5.47\text{ m}$.

However, in Y's frame, the sensor is moving toward the light pulse. If the light is sent at $t=0$ when X is at position $x=0$, the sensor is at $x = -5.47\text{ m}$ and moving at $v = 0.95c$. The light pulse position is $x_L = -ct$. The sensor position is $x_S = -5.47 + 0.95ct$.
Setting $x_L = x_S$:
$$-ct = -5.47 + 0.95ct$$
$$1.95ct = 5.47$$
$$t_Y = \frac{5.47}{1.95 \times 3 \times 10^8} \approx 9.35 \times 10^{-9}\text{ s}$$

*(Note: In many curriculum contexts, students are expected to show that $t_Y$ is calculated by accounting for the sensor moving into the path of the light, confirming that $t_Y \neq \gamma t_X$ in this specific geometry.)*
