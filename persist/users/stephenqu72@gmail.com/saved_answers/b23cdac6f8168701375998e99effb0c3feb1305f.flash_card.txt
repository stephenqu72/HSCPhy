### Front
How did J.J. Thomson determine the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$) of an electron?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Thomson used a cathode ray tube to balance electric ($F_E=qE$) and magnetic ($F_B=qvB$) forces, then observed the magnetic deflection of electrons in a circular path ($F_B=mv^2/r$).
- **Formula / law:** $\frac{q}{m} = \frac{E}{B^2r}$ (derived from $v = E/B$ and $r = mv/qB$).
- **When to use it:** When asked to explain the derivation of $q/m$ or the experimental evidence for subatomic particles.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to mention that the electric and magnetic fields were perpendicular, or omitting the step where velocity ($v$) is calculated first.
