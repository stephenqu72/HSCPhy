To solve this problem, we must apply **Lenz’s Law** and **Faraday’s Law of Induction**, followed by a kinematic calculation to find the force.

### 1. Justification (Lenz’s Law)
When the bar magnet falls through the solid copper tube, the change in magnetic flux through the circular cross-sections of the tube induces an electromotive force (EMF). According to **Faraday’s Law**, this changing flux induces eddy currents in the conductive copper walls. 

According to **Lenz’s Law**, these eddy currents will flow in a direction that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced them. Consequently, the induced magnetic field created by the eddy currents exerts an upward magnetic force on the falling magnet, opposing gravity and slowing the magnet's descent.

### 2. Calculation of the Upward Force
First, identify the variables:
*   Mass $m = 25.3\text{ g} = 0.0253\text{ kg}$
*   Displacement $\Delta y = 1.35\text{ m}$
*   Time $t = 0.843\text{ s}$
*   Initial velocity $u = 0\text{ m/s}$ (dropped)

We use the kinematic equation $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$:
$$1.35 = 0 + \frac{1}{2} \cdot a \cdot (0.843)^2$$
$$1.35 = 0.3553 \cdot a$$
$$a = \frac{1.35}{0.3553} \approx 3.80\text{ m/s}^2$$

Next, we use Newton’s Second Law ($F_{net} = ma$) to find the net force:
$$F_{net} = 0.0253 \cdot 3.80 \approx 0.0961\text{ N}$$

The net force is the difference between the weight ($W = mg$) and the upward magnetic force ($F_{mag}$):
$$F_{net} = mg - F_{mag}$$
$$F_{mag} = mg - F_{net}$$
$$F_{mag} = (0.0253 \cdot 9.8) - 0.0961$$
$$F_{mag} = 0.2479 - 0.0961$$
$$F_{mag} = 0.1518\text{ N}$$

**Final Answer:** The upward force is approximately **$0.152\text{ N}$**.
