This physics problem relates to **electromagnetic induction**, specifically **Lenz's Law** and the **repulsive force** generated by changing magnetic flux.

### (a) Velocity-Time Graph Sketch
To sketch the graph for the first 4 seconds:
1.  **$t = 0$ to $1$ s:** The switch is open. The ring is at rest. $v = 0$.
2.  **$t = 1$ s:** The switch closes. A surge of current creates a rapidly increasing magnetic field. According to Faraday’s Law, an induced current in the ring creates a magnetic field that opposes this change (Lenz’s Law). This results in a strong repulsive force, causing the ring to jump upward. The velocity becomes positive and increases.
3.  **$t = 1$ to $3$ s:** Once the current in the DC circuit stabilizes, the magnetic flux through the ring becomes constant. Since $\Delta \Phi / \Delta t = 0$, no further induced current flows in the ring, and the magnetic force vanishes. The ring now moves only under the influence of gravity, slowing down, stopping momentarily at its peak, and falling back down.
4.  **$t = 3$ s:** The switch opens. The magnetic field collapses rapidly. This changing flux induces a current in the ring. However, because the field is already collapsing to zero, the ring no longer experiences the upward impulse. It continues to fall due to gravity.

**Graph Shape:**
- From $0$ to $1$ s: Horizontal line on the t-axis ($v=0$).
- At $t = 1$ s: A sharp vertical spike (or steep positive gradient) moving into positive velocity.
- From $1$ to $3$ s: A line with a negative gradient (decelerating upward, then accelerating downward).
- At $t = 3$ s: The motion remains that of an object in free fall (the velocity continues to become more negative).

### (b) Explanation (Switch remains closed)
If the switch remains closed, the ring would experience an initial upward "kick" as the magnetic field is established, but it would not remain hovering or levitating. 

1.  **Induction:** As the switch closes at $t = 1$ s, the current in the solenoid increases from zero to a maximum. This change in magnetic flux ($\Delta \Phi$) induces an EMF in the aluminium ring (Faraday's Law).
2.  **Lenz’s Law:** The induced current flows in a direction such that its magnetic field opposes the change in flux produced by the solenoid. This creates a magnetic north pole on the underside of the ring if the top of the solenoid is a north pole, resulting in a **repulsive force**.
3.  **Motion:** This repulsive force acts momentarily as an impulse, launching the ring upward.
4.  **Steady State:** Once the current in the DC circuit reaches a steady value, the magnetic flux through the ring becomes constant ($\Delta \Phi / \Delta t = 0$). With no change in flux, no further current is induced in the ring. With no induced current, there is no magnetic force. The ring will simply fall back down due to gravity. It does not hover because a DC field provides no *change* in flux, which is a requirement for the repulsive electromagnetic force.
