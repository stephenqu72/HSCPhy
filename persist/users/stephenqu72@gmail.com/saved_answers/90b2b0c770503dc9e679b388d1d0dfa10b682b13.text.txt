To solve this problem, we need to determine the number of photons emitted per second, which corresponds to the total power of the laser divided by the energy of a single photon.

### 1. Identify the given values and convert to SI units:
*   Power ($P$) = $30\text{ mW} = 30 \times 10^{-3}\text{ W} = 0.03\text{ J/s}$
*   Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $650\text{ nm} = 650 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$
*   Planck's constant ($h$) $\approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$
*   Speed of light ($c$) $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$

### 2. Calculate the energy of a single photon ($E$):
Using the equation $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$:
$$E = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)}{650 \times 10^{-9}}$$
$$E \approx \frac{1.9878 \times 10^{-25}}{6.5 \times 10^{-7}} \approx 3.058 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$$

### 3. Calculate the number of photons per second ($n$):
The total power is the energy of one photon multiplied by the number of photons emitted per second:
$$P = n \times E \implies n = \frac{P}{E}$$
$$n = \frac{0.03}{3.058 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$n \approx 9.81 \times 10^{16}\text{ photons/s}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches **Option B**.

### Answer:
**B. $9.8 \times 10^{16}$**
