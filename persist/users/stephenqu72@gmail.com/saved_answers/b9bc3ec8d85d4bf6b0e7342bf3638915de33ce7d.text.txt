### Answer
The correct answer is **A. Galileo**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to understand the historical development of the principle of relativity in physics:

1.  **Galileo Galilei (Galilean Relativity):** In his 1632 work, *Dialogue Concerning the Two Chief World Systems*, Galileo proposed that the laws of physics are the same for any system moving at a constant velocity. He used the famous example of a ship moving smoothly at a constant speed to explain that an observer inside the ship cannot determine if they are moving or at rest through mechanical experiments alone. This is the foundation of classical relativity.
2.  **Newton:** While Isaac Newton formalised the laws of motion and gravitation, he built upon the existing framework of Galilean relativity. He did not originate the principle of relativity itself.
3.  **Maxwell:** James Clerk Maxwell is famous for his equations of electromagnetism. In fact, his work actually highlighted a conflict with the principle of relativity (specifically regarding the speed of light), which eventually led to the development of Special Relativity.
4.  **Einstein:** Albert Einstein expanded the principle of relativity. He proposed *Special Relativity* (1905), which extended the principle to include electromagnetic phenomena, asserting that the laws of physics are invariant for all inertial observers and that the speed of light is constant. Later, he proposed *General Relativity* to include gravity. While Einstein is the most famous figure associated with "relativity," he was not the *first* to propose a principle of relativity.

Therefore, historically, the concept originated with Galileo.
