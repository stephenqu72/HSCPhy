### Teacher's Solution

#### (a) What is the direction of the magnetic field?
**Answer:** Using the Right-Hand Grip Rule, the magnetic field between the wires is directed **out of the page** (assuming the top wire carries current to the left and the bottom wire carries current to the right, or vice-versa, depending on the orientation). More generally, the field lines circulate around each wire; between the two wires, the field from the top wire will point in one direction, and the field from the bottom wire will point in the same direction, resulting in a net field **perpendicular to the plane of the wires.**

#### (b) Determine the distance between the two wires.
**Explanation:** We use the formula for the force per unit length between two parallel current-carrying wires:
$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2 \pi d}$$
Given:
- $F = 0.0138\text{ N}$
- $L = 1\text{ m}$
- $I_1 = I_2 = 14.4\text{ A}$
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

Rearranging for $d$:
$$d = \frac{\mu_0 I^2 L}{2 \pi F}$$
$$d = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times (14.4)^2 \times 1}{2 \pi \times 0.0138}$$
$$d = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 207.36}{0.0138} \approx 0.003005\text{ m}$$
**Answer:** The distance between the wires is approximately **3.0 mm**.

---

#### (c) Explain why the reading of the force meter increased.
**Explanation:** 
1. When the switch is closed, current flows through both wires. According to Ampère's Law, each wire creates a magnetic field.
2. When current flows in opposite directions in two parallel wires, they experience a mutual **repulsive force**. 
3. In this setup, the bottom wire is fixed to the force meter. The repulsive force pushes the bottom wire downward against the sensor of the force meter, causing the increase in the measured weight/force reading.
