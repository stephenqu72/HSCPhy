To determine the correct answer, we must refer to the mathematical formula that describes Young's double-slit experiment. The fringe separation (the distance between consecutive bright or dark bands), denoted by $\Delta x$, is given by the formula:

$$\Delta x = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$

Where:
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the light used.
- $L$ is the distance between the double slits and the screen.
- $d$ is the distance of separation between the two slits.

### Analysis of the Options:
We want to **increase** $\Delta x$. Looking at the relationship in the formula:
*   $\Delta x$ is directly proportional to $\lambda$ (increasing $\lambda$ increases $\Delta x$).
*   $\Delta x$ is directly proportional to $L$ (increasing $L$ increases $\Delta x$).
*   $\Delta x$ is inversely proportional to $d$ (decreasing $d$ increases $\Delta x$).

Now, let's evaluate the choices:
*   (A) Slit width affects the intensity and diffraction envelope, but not significantly the fringe separation $\Delta x$.
*   (B) **Decreasing $d$**: Since $\Delta x \propto \frac{1}{d}$, making $d$ smaller results in a larger $\Delta x$. This is correct.
*   (C) Decreasing $L$: Since $\Delta x \propto L$, this would decrease the fringe separation.
*   (D) Decreasing $\lambda$: Since $\Delta x \propto \lambda$, this would decrease the fringe separation.

The correct action to increase the distance between the bands is to decrease the distance of separation between the slits.

**Correct Answer: (B)**
