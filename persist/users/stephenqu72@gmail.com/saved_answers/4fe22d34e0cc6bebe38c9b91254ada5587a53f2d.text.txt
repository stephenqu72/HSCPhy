To determine the torque acting on the wrench, we use the formula for torque:

$$\tau = rF \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
*   $r$ is the distance from the pivot point (the nut) to the point where the force is applied.
*   $F$ is the magnitude of the applied force.
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the position vector ($r$) and the force vector ($F$).

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the Force ($F$):**
    The magnitude of the applied force is $17.0\text{ N}$.

2.  **Identify the Distance ($r$):**
    The distance from the nut to the handle tip is $25.0\text{ cm}$. In physics calculations, we must use standard SI units (meters). Therefore, we convert $25.0\text{ cm}$ to meters:
    $$r = 25.0 \text{ cm} = 0.25 \text{ m}$$

3.  **Identify the Angle ($\theta$):**
    The angle provided in the diagram ($37^\circ$) is the angle between the force vector and the handle of the wrench. This is exactly the angle $\theta$ required for the torque formula $\tau = rF \sin(\theta)$.

4.  **Substitute into the formula:**
    $$\tau = 0.25 \times 17 \times \sin(37^\circ)$$
    Rearranging this to match the format of the options:
    $$\tau = 17 \sin(37^\circ) \times 0.25$$
