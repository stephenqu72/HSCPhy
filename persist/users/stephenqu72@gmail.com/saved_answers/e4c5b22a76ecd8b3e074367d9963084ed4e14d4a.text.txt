### Answer

**(a) Describe what Einstein meant by an "inertial" frame of reference.**
An inertial frame of reference is a coordinate system that is either stationary or moving at a constant velocity (constant speed in a straight line). In such a frame, Newton’s First Law of Motion holds true: an object will remain at rest or continue to move at a constant velocity unless acted upon by a net external force. Essentially, an inertial frame is one that is not accelerating.

**(b) Using an example, describe how observations of the same event made from two different inertial frames of reference can be different. A diagram should be used to illustrate your answer.**

*Example: The path of a projectile.*
Imagine a person standing on the ground (Frame A) and a person standing on a train moving at a constant velocity (Frame B). If a passenger on the train drops a ball:
*   **Observer on the train (Frame B):** Sees the ball fall in a perfectly straight vertical line because both the passenger and the ball share the same horizontal velocity.
*   **Observer on the ground (Frame A):** Sees the ball follow a parabolic trajectory because the ball retains its horizontal velocity while simultaneously accelerating downward due to gravity.

**Diagram:**
```
Frame B (Train):        Frame A (Ground):
    |  (Ball drops)         / \
    |  (straight)          /   \  (Parabolic path)
    |                     /     \
    v                    v       v
```
The observations differ because velocity is relative; the displacement of the ball relative to the train is different from its displacement relative to the ground.
