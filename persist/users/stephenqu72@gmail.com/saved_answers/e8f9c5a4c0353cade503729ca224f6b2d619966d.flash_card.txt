### Front
How do you calculate the required radius ($r$) for a rotating spacecraft to simulate Earth's gravity ($g$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Artificial gravity is achieved by equating centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) to $g$.
- Formula: $r = \frac{g}{(2\pi f)^2}$ or $r = \frac{g}{\omega^2}$
- When to use it: Circular motion problems involving rotating space stations or centrifuges where a specific acceleration is required.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert frequency ($f$) to angular velocity ($\omega = 2\pi f$) or failing to square the entire $\omega$ term.
