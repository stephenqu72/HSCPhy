### Answer
The Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) provides strong evidence for the Big Bang model because it represents the "afterglow" of the early, hot, dense universe. 

1. **Origin:** According to the Big Bang theory, the early universe was so hot and dense that it was opaque to radiation. As it expanded and cooled to approximately $3000\text{ K}$ (about 380,000 years after the Big Bang), electrons and protons combined to form neutral hydrogen atoms. This process, known as recombination, allowed photons to travel freely through space for the first time.
2. **Expansion/Redshift:** As the universe has expanded over billions of years, the wavelength of these original high-energy photons has been stretched (cosmological redshift) by the expansion of space itself. 
3. **Consistency:** This stretching has shifted the radiation from the visible/infrared spectrum into the microwave region of the electromagnetic spectrum, corresponding to a temperature of approximately $2.7\text{ K}$, which perfectly matches the predictions made by Big Bang cosmologists.

### How to solve this question
To secure full marks (3 marks) in an HSC-style physics response, you must link the observation of the CMB to the theoretical requirements of the Big Bang model. Use the following structure:

*   **Point 1: The "Why" (1 mark):** Explain that the CMB is the remnant radiation from the early, hot phase of the universe (decoupling/recombination). 
*   **Point 2: The "Mechanism" (1 mark):** Explain how the expansion of the universe caused this radiation to stretch. Use the term "cosmological redshift" or explain that the wavelength increased.
*   **Point 3: The "Evidence" (1 mark):** Conclude by noting that the current observation of this radiation in the microwave region (at $\approx 2.7\text{ K}$) matches the theoretical calculations derived from the Big Bang model, confirming the model's validity.
