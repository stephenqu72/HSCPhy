### Front
How does centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) change if an object’s speed doubles and its orbital radius is halved?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration is directly proportional to $v^2$ and inversely proportional to $r$. Doubling $v$ quadruples $a_c$ ($2^2=4$), and halving $r$ doubles $a_c$ again ($4 \times 2 = 8$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$
- When to use it: Any uniform circular motion problem involving changes in velocity or radius.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the velocity change ($2v \rightarrow 4v^2$) or incorrectly handling the fraction in the denominator.
