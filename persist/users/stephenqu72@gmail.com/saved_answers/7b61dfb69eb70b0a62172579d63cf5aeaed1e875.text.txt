To solve this problem, we apply **Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion**, which states that for objects orbiting the same central body, the ratio of the square of the orbital period ($T$) to the cube of the orbital radius ($r$) is constant:

$$\frac{T^2}{r^3} = k$$

This means that for two moons orbiting the same planet (Xenos), we can set up the following ratio:

$$\frac{T_1^2}{r_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{r_2^3}$$

    $$\frac{(31 \times 10^4)^2}{(12 \times 10^3)^3} = \frac{T^2}{(4 \times 10^7)^3}$$

3.  **Solve for $T^2$:**
    To isolate $T^2$, multiply both sides by $(4 \times 10^7)^3$:
    $$T^2 = \frac{(31 \times 10^4)^2 \times (4 \times 10^7)^3}{(12 \times 10^3)^3}$$
