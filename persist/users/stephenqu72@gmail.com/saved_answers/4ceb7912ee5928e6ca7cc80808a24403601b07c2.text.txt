### Answer
The correct answer is **(B)**.

### Explanation

**1. Identifying the Observation:**
The student is shining monochromatic light at a sample of gas and observing it through a spectroscope. When light passes through a cool gas, the atoms in the gas will absorb photons that match the energy difference between their electron energy levels. This produces an **absorption spectrum**, which is characterized by dark lines (missing wavelengths) appearing on a continuous background of light. Therefore, the student would observe a "black line on a continuous background." This eliminates options (C) and (D).

**2. Calculating the Energy Transition:**
We must determine which electron transition corresponds to a photon with a wavelength of $\lambda = 656 \text{ nm}$. We use the Rydberg formula for the hydrogen atom:
$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$
Where $R \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \text{ m}^{-1}$ is the Rydberg constant. 

For the hydrogen Balmer series (which includes the red light at $656 \text{ nm}$), the transition ends at the $n=2$ level. We solve for the initial state $n_i$:
$$\frac{1}{656 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \text{ m}^{-1} \left( \frac{1}{2^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$
$$1,524,390 \approx 10,970,000 \times \left( 0.25 - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$
$$0.1389 \approx 0.25 - \frac{1}{n_i^2}$$
$$\frac{1}{n_i^2} \approx 0.1111$$
$$n_i^2 \approx 9 \implies n_i = 3$$

**3. Conclusion:**
The transition is from the $n=2$ level to the $n=3$ level, as the electron is *absorbing* the incoming photon to jump to a higher energy state. This matches option (B).
