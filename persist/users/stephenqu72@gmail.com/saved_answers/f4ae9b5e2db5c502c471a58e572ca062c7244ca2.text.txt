To solve this projectile motion problem, we first resolve the initial velocity vector into its horizontal and vertical components:
$u_x = u \cos(\theta) = 40 \cos(30^{\circ}) = 40 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = 20\sqrt{3} \approx 34.64\text{ m/s}$
$u_y = u \sin(\theta) = 40 \sin(30^{\circ}) = 40 \times 0.5 = 20\text{ m/s}$

### Part a: Calculate the maximum height, $h$
At the maximum height, the vertical component of the velocity is zero ($v_y = 0$). Using the kinematic equation $v_y^2 = u_y^2 + 2a_y \Delta y$:
$$0 = (20)^2 + 2(-9.8)h$$
$$19.6h = 400$$
$$h = \frac{400}{19.6} \approx 20.41\text{ m}$$

### Part b: Calculate the vertical drop, $d$
First, we find the time of flight ($t$) by using the horizontal displacement ($x = 173\text{ m}$):
$$x = u_x t$$
$$173 = (20\sqrt{3})t$$
$$t = \frac{173}{34.64} \approx 5.00\text{ s}$$

Now, use the vertical displacement formula $\Delta y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$ to find the vertical position relative to the start:
$$\Delta y = (20)(5) + \frac{1}{2}(-9.8)(5)^2$$
$$\Delta y = 100 - 4.9(25)$$
$$\Delta y = 100 - 122.5 = -22.5\text{ m}$$

Since the displacement is $-22.5\text{ m}$ (indicating it is below the starting point), the vertical drop $d$ is **$22.5\text{ m}$**.

1. **Resolve Velocity:** Break the initial speed into $u_x$ and $u_y$ using trigonometry.
2. **Maximum Height:** Use the vertical motion formula where final vertical velocity is zero.
3. **Time of Flight:** Use the horizontal motion formula since horizontal velocity is constant.
