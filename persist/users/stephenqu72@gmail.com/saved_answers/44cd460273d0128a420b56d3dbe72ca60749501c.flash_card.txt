### Front
How is Hubble’s Law used as evidence for an expanding universe, and how is the Hubble constant ($H_0$) determined from observational data?

### Back
- Key idea: Galaxies show a positive linear correlation between recessional velocity and distance, indicating space itself is expanding.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = H_0D$; $H_0$ is the gradient of the velocity-distance graph.
- When to use it: When interpreting cosmological redshift data or calculating the rate of cosmic expansion.
- Common trap: Confusing the units of $H_0$ or failing to convert velocity (km/s) and distance (Mpc or m) to consistent SI units before calculating the gradient.
