### Front
How do you calculate the horizontal component of tension ($T_x$) for an object moving in a horizontal circle?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The horizontal component of tension provides the required centripetal force ($F_c$).
- **Formula:** $T_x = F_c = m r \omega^2$ (or $mv^2/r$)
- **When to use it:** Problems involving conical pendulums or objects in horizontal circular motion.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units (e.g., grams to kg) or confusing the total tension ($T$) with the horizontal component ($T_x$).
