To find the correct expression for the binding energy per nucleon of $^3_2\text{He}$, follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the components of the nucleus
The nucleus $^3_2\text{He}$ consists of:
*   $Z = 2$ protons (each with mass $m_p$)
*   $A - Z = 3 - 2 = 1$ neutron (with mass $m_n$)
*   Total number of nucleons $A = 3$

### 2. Calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$)
The mass defect is the difference between the sum of the masses of the individual nucleons and the actual mass of the nucleus ($m$).
$$\Delta m = (\text{total mass of nucleons}) - (\text{mass of nucleus})$$
$$\Delta m = (2m_p + 1m_n) - m$$
$$\Delta m = (2m_p + m_n - m)$$

### 3. Convert mass defect to Binding Energy ($BE$)
Using Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle, $E = \Delta mc^2$:
$$BE = (2m_p + m_n - m)c^2$$

### 4. Determine Binding Energy per nucleon
The "binding energy per nucleon" is defined as the total binding energy divided by the number of nucleons ($A$):
$$\text{Binding energy per nucleon} = \frac{BE}{A}$$
Since $A = 3$:
$$\text{Binding energy per nucleon} = \frac{(2m_p + m_n - m)c^2}{3}$$
