To determine the correct expression for the upward force exerted by the seat on the occupant, we must apply Newton’s Second Law to the occupant at the top of the circular track.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the forces acting on the occupant:**
    *   The gravitational force ($mg$) acting **downwards**.
    *   The normal force ($F_{\text{seat}}$) exerted by the seat on the occupant, acting **upwards**.

2.  **Determine the direction of the net force:**
    *   Since the occupant is moving in a circular path, there must be a net centripetal force ($F_c$) directed towards the **center of the circle** (downwards in this scenario).
    *   The magnitude of the centripetal force is given by $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$.

3.  **Apply Newton’s Second Law ($F_{\text{net}} = ma_c$):**
    *   Taking the downward direction (towards the center) as positive:
        $$mg - F_{\text{seat}} = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

4.  **Solve for $F_{\text{seat}}$:**
    *   Rearrange the equation to isolate $F_{\text{seat}}$:
        $$F_{\text{seat}} = mg - \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
    *   Factor out the mass $m$:
        $$F_{\text{seat}} = m\left(g - \frac{v^2}{r}\right)$$

Comparing this result to the given options, we find that it matches option **B**.

**Conclusion:**
At the top of a hill, the seat must provide less support than the person's weight because the centripetal force is provided by the difference between gravity and the normal force. Therefore, the upward force $F_{\text{seat}}$ is $m(g - \frac{v^2}{r})$.

**Correct Option: B**
