### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) all waves**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to understand the fundamental definition of **diffraction**.

Diffraction is the bending of waves around the corners of an obstacle or through an aperture (opening) into the region of geometrical shadow. 

In physics, diffraction is a **characteristic property of all wave motion**, regardless of the medium or the type of wave.
*   **Electromagnetic waves** (like light or radio waves) exhibit diffraction when they pass through narrow slits or around obstacles.
*   **Sound waves** (which are mechanical, longitudinal waves) exhibit diffraction, which is why you can hear someone speaking even if they are around a corner.
*   **Mechanical waves** (like water waves) are perhaps the most common example we observe in nature, where ripples bend around obstacles in a pond.

Since diffraction is a universal behavior of any phenomenon that satisfies the wave equation, it applies to all wave types. Therefore, option (D) is the most comprehensive and correct choice.
