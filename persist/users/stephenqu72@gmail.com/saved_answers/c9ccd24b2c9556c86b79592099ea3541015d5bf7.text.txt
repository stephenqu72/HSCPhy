2.  **Rotational Velocity:** Rapid rotation causes "Doppler broadening" of spectral lines. Because one side of the star rotates toward the observer and the other away, the spectral lines are smeared out into wider bands. Comparing Star 1 and Star 3, the lines in Star 3 are noticeably sharper (thinner), while the lines in Star 1 are broader. This indicates Star 1 is rotating faster than Star 3.
