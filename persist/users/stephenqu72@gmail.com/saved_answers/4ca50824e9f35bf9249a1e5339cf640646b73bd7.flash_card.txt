### Front
How do you calculate the period ($T$) of a rotating spacecraft required to simulate Earth's gravity ($g$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The required centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) is equivalent to the acceleration due to gravity ($g$).
- **Formula:** $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{r}{g}}$ (derived from $a_c = \frac{4\pi^2r}{T^2}$).
- **When to use it:** When calculating rotational frequency or period to simulate gravity in a space habitat or centrifuge.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units (e.g., using km instead of m) or failing to square root the entire fraction inside the radical.
