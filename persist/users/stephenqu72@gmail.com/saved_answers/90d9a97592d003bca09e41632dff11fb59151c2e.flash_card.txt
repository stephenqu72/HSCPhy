### Front
How does the gravitational potential energy ($U$) of a mass change as it moves from between two stars toward the line connecting them?

### Back
- Key idea: The mass moves into a deeper "potential well," causing $U$ to become more negative (decrease).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $U = -\frac{GmM}{r}$; gravitational potential energy is scalar and additive.
- When to use it: When determining the shape of a potential energy graph between multiple point masses.
- Common trap: Assuming $U$ increases because the object is moving; $U$ only becomes "less negative" (increases) as $r$ increases toward infinity.
