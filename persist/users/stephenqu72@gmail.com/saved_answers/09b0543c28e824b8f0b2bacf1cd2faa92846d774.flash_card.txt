### Front
Explain the physical significance of the term $g$ in the formula $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$.

### Back
- **Key idea:** $g$ is the gravitational field strength (acceleration due to gravity), defined as force per unit mass.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $g = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- **When to use it:** To calculate the gravitational field strength at a specific distance from a mass or the acceleration of an object in free-fall.
- **Common trap:** Confusing $g$ (field strength/acceleration) with $mg$ (the gravitational force/weight of an object). $g$ is independent of the mass being acted upon.
