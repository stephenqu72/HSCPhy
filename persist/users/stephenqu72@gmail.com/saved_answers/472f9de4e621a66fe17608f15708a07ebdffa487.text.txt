To solve this problem, we need to determine the amount of radioactive isotope remaining after a specific time period and then find the percentage that has decayed.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Standardize the units of time**
The decay time is given in hours, and the half-life is given in days. We must convert them to the same unit. Let’s convert the half-life ($t_{1/2}$) into hours:
$$t_{1/2} = 2.2 \text{ days} \times 24 \text{ hours/day} = 52.8 \text{ hours}$$

**2. Calculate the amount remaining**
We use the radioactive decay formula:
$$N(t) = N_0 \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{\frac{t}{t_{1/2}}}$$
Where:
- $N(t)$ is the remaining amount
- $N_0$ is the initial amount (which we can treat as $100\%$)
- $t = 16 \text{ hours}$
- $t_{1/2} = 52.8 \text{ hours}$

$$N(t) = 100 \times (0.5)^{\frac{16}{52.8}}$$
$$N(t) = 100 \times (0.5)^{0.30303}$$
$$N(t) \approx 100 \times 0.8106$$
$$N(t) \approx 81.06\%$$

**3. Calculate the percentage decayed**
The question asks for the percentage that has **decayed**, not the amount remaining. 
$$\text{Decayed} = 100\% - \text{Remaining} \%$$
$$\text{Decayed} = 100\% - 81.06\% = 18.94\%$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number, we get **$19\%$**.
