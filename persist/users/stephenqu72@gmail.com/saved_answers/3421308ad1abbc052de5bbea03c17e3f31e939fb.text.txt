### Part a: Balanced Nuclear Equation for Step 3

Looking at the diagram for **Step 3**, we see two $^3_2\text{He}$ nuclei entering the reaction, and one $^4_2\text{He}$ nucleus and two $^1_1\text{H}$ protons exiting the reaction.

$$^3_2\text{He} + ^3_2\text{He} \rightarrow ^4_2\text{He} + 2(^1_1\text{H})$$

### Part b: Energy Produced in Step 3

To find the energy produced, we calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$) of the reaction using the provided table, then convert it to energy using Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle.

**1. Calculate the total mass of the reactants:**
$$\text{Mass}_{\text{reactants}} = 2 \times \text{mass}(^3_2\text{He})$$
$$\text{Mass}_{\text{reactants}} = 2 \times 3.016029\,\text{u} = 6.032058\,\text{u}$$

**2. Calculate the total mass of the products:**
$$\text{Mass}_{\text{products}} = \text{mass}(^4_2\text{He}) + 2 \times \text{mass}(^1_1\text{H})$$
$$\text{Mass}_{\text{products}} = 4.002603\,\text{u} + 2 \times 1.007825\,\text{u} = 6.018253\,\text{u}$$

**3. Calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$):**
$$\Delta m = \text{Mass}_{\text{reactants}} - \text{Mass}_{\text{products}}$$
$$\Delta m = 6.032058\,\text{u} - 6.018253\,\text{u} = 0.013805\,\text{u}$$

**4. Convert mass defect to energy:**
In nuclear physics problems, $1\,\text{u}$ is equivalent to approximately $931.5\,\text{MeV}$.
$$E = 0.013805\,\text{u} \times 931.5\,\text{MeV/u}$$
$$E \approx 12.86\,\text{MeV}$$
