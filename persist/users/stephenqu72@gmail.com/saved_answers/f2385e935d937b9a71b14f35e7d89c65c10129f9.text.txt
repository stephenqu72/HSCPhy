### Part (a)
**Experiment:** Geiger-Marsden (Gold Foil) experiment.
*   **Diagram description:** An alpha particle source is directed at a thin gold foil inside an evacuated chamber. A movable zinc sulfide screen (or detector) tracks the scattered alpha particles.
*   **Observations:** Most alpha particles passed straight through; some were deflected at small angles; very few were reflected back (>90 degrees).
*   **Rutherford’s Model:** The atom consists of a tiny, dense, positively charged nucleus containing most of the mass, with electrons orbiting at a distance, leaving most of the atom as empty space.

### Part (b)
1.  **Stability of the atom:** Classical electromagnetism predicts an accelerating electron should radiate energy and spiral into the nucleus, yet atoms are stable.
2.  **Origin of spectral lines:** Classical physics could not explain why specific discrete frequencies of light are emitted rather than a continuous spectrum.

### Part (c)
1.  Electrons exist in stationary orbits (discrete energy levels) where they do not radiate energy.
2.  Transitions between orbits involve the absorption or emission of a photon with energy equal to the difference between the two levels: $E = hf = E_2 - E_1$.
3.  The angular momentum of an electron is quantized in integral multiples of $h/2\pi$ ($mvr = n\hbar$).

### Part (d)
The first line of the Balmer series is $n=3 \rightarrow n=2$.
Energy formula: $E = 13.6 \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right) \text{ eV}$.
$$E = 13.6 \left( \frac{1}{2^2} - \frac{1}{3^2} \right) = 13.6 \left( 0.25 - 0.111 \right) \approx 1.89 \text{ eV}$$
Convert to Joules: $1.89 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \approx 3.03 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$.

### Part (e)
Ionisation is $n=1 \rightarrow n=\infty$.
$$E = 13.6 \left( \frac{1}{1^2} - \frac{1}{\infty^2} \right) = 13.6 \text{ eV}$$
$$13.6 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \approx 2.18 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J}$$

### Part (f)
De Broglie wavelength: $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$
$$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{(9.11 \times 10^{-31}) \times (5.5 \times 10^6)} \approx 1.32 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}$$
