### Front
How do you determine the radioactive decay constant ($\lambda$) from a half-life ($t_{1/2}$)?

### Back
- Key idea: The decay constant is the probability of decay per unit time.
- Formula / law / constant: $\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{t_{1/2}}$
- When to use it: When finding the rate of decay from a half-life or extracting $\lambda$ from an activity/count-rate decay graph.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert the time units of $t_{1/2}$ to match the required units of $\lambda$ (e.g., converting days to seconds).
