To solve this problem, we must analyze the forces acting on the ball at the specific instant it is at the **bottom** of its circular path.

### Analysis of Forces
1.  **Gravity ($mg$):** Acts downwards towards the center of the Earth.
2.  **Tension ($T$):** The string pulls the ball towards the center of the circular path. Since the ball is at the bottom, the center of the circle is directly above the ball. Therefore, the tension acts **upwards**.

There are no horizontal forces acting on the ball (as we are ignoring air resistance), so there is no horizontal arrow in the correct diagram.

### Physics Principles
The ball is moving in a uniform circular motion, which requires a **net centripetal force** directed towards the center of the circle. At the bottom of the path:
*   The net force $F_{net}$ must point upwards (towards the center).
*   $F_{net} = T - mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
*   Because the ball is accelerating towards the center, the upward tension force ($T$) must be **greater** in magnitude than the downward gravitational force ($mg$).

### Evaluating the Options
*   We are looking for a diagram with an upward arrow (Tension) and a downward arrow (Gravity).
*   The upward arrow must be **longer** than the downward arrow to represent the net centripetal force directed upwards.
*   Option C shows two arrows of equal length, which would imply zero net force (constant velocity in a straight line, not circular motion).
*   **Option D** shows an upward arrow that is longer than the downward arrow. This correctly represents the tension and gravity, and their vector sum provides the necessary upward centripetal force.

**Correct Answer: D**
