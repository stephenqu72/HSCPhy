To solve this projectile motion problem, we separate the horizontal and vertical components of the motion. Note that since the boxes leave a horizontal conveyor belt, the initial vertical velocity $u_y = 0$.

### Part a
To find the time taken, we use the vertical displacement formula:
$$y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2}g t^2$$
Given $y = 1.4\text{ m}$, $u_y = 0$, and $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$:
$$1.4 = 0 + \frac{1}{2}(9.8) t^2$$
$$1.4 = 4.9 t^2$$
$$t^2 = \frac{1.4}{4.9} = \frac{1}{3.5} \approx 0.2857$$
$$t = \sqrt{0.2857} \approx 0.5345\text{ s}$$
Rounding to two significant figures, **$t = 0.53\text{ s}$**.

### Part b
The horizontal motion is constant velocity. We use $x = u_x t$:
$$1.4 = u_x \times 0.5345$$
$$u_x = \frac{1.4}{0.5345} \approx 2.619\text{ m/s}$$
Rounding to two significant figures, the speed is **$2.6\text{ m/s}$**.

### Part c
**i. Answer:** keep speed the same

**ii. Explanation:**
In a vacuum (ignoring air resistance), the horizontal and vertical motions are independent of the object's mass. The vertical displacement $y = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$ depends only on gravity and height; therefore, the time of flight remains unchanged when mass is altered. Since horizontal displacement $x = u_xt$ also remains unchanged and the time of flight is constant, the horizontal velocity $u_x$ must remain the same to land in the same spot. Acceleration due to gravity acts equally on all masses regardless of their size.
