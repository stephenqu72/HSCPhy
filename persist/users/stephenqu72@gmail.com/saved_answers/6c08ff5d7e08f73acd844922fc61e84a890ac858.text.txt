### Answer
*   **Part A (Permanent Magnet):** Provides a constant, strong radial magnetic field. The radial shape ensures that the magnetic field lines are always perpendicular to the plane of the coil, regardless of the coil's rotation.
*   **Part B (Coil/Armature):** Carries the current to be measured. When current flows through the coil, the magnetic field exerts a magnetic force ($F = nBIL\sin\theta$) on the sides of the coil, creating a torque that causes it to rotate.
*   **Part C (Hairspring):** Provides a restoring torque that opposes the magnetic torque. As the coil rotates, the spring tightens, creating a counter-torque proportional to the angle of rotation ($\tau_{restoring} = k\theta$).

**How it achieves a linear scale:** 
The rotation continues until the magnetic torque equals the restoring torque. Because the magnetic field is radial, the magnetic torque remains proportional to the current ($I$) even as the coil rotates. Since the restoring torque is proportional to the angle ($\theta$), and the magnetic torque is proportional to current ($I$), then $\theta \propto I$. This linear relationship ensures the needle moves across an evenly spaced scale.
