### Front
What is the direction of the net force acting on an object undergoing uniform circular motion, and why?

### Back
- Key idea: The net force (centripetal force) must be directed toward the centre to provide the acceleration required to constantly change the object's direction.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s Second Law ($F_{net} = ma$); $a_c = v^2/r$
- When to use it: Any scenario involving circular motion (e.g., car on a curve, satellite in orbit, banked track).
- Common trap: Mistaking "centrifugal force" (outward) for the net force; or assuming constant speed means zero acceleration.
