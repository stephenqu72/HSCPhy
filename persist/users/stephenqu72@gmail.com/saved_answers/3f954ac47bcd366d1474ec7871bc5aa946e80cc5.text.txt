To answer a 9-mark "Analyse" question, you must demonstrate the relationship between experimental data, the failure of existing models, and the resulting theoretical improvements.

### Model Answer

**1. Rutherford and the Geiger-Marsden Experiment:**
J.J. Thomson’s "plum pudding" model proposed that positive charge was spread uniformly throughout the atom. The Geiger-Marsden experiment involved firing alpha particles at a thin gold foil. Most particles passed through, but a small fraction were deflected at large angles, with some rebounding backwards. Rutherford analysed this and concluded that the atom must contain a small, dense, positively charged nucleus. This led to the planetary model, though it remained unstable as classical physics predicted electrons would spiral into the nucleus due to energy loss.

**2. Bohr and Atomic Spectra:**
Bohr addressed the instability of the planetary model by incorporating Planck’s quantum hypothesis. He analysed the experimental line emission spectra of hydrogen, which revealed discrete, quantized energy levels rather than a continuous spectrum. Bohr postulated that electrons exist in stable "stationary states" or orbits where they do not radiate energy. He proposed that electrons only emit or absorb photons of energy $E = hf$ when transitioning between these quantized levels. This successfully explained the Rydberg formula and the stability of the hydrogen atom.

**3. de Broglie and Wave-Particle Duality:**
While Bohr’s model worked for hydrogen, it lacked a theoretical basis for *why* orbits were quantized. Louis de Broglie proposed that matter exhibits wave-like properties, with a wavelength $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$. Experimental evidence, such as the Davisson-Germer electron diffraction experiment, confirmed this wave nature. De Broglie explained that electrons orbit as standing waves. For an orbit to be stable, the circumference must be an integer multiple of the wavelength: $2\pi r = n\lambda$. This provided the physical justification for Bohr’s quantization, shifting the model from circular orbits to modern quantum mechanical probability clouds.

### Teacher’s Guide: How to solve this
1.  **Understand "Analyse":** In HSC Physics, "analyse" requires you to break down the topic into parts and identify the relationships and implications between them. Don't just list facts; explain *why* the old model failed and *how* the new experiment fixed it.
2.  **Structure for 9 Marks:** Aim for 3 marks per physicist. Use the "Problem-Experiment-Solution" flow for each:
    *   **Problem:** What did the current model fail to explain?
    *   **Experiment:** What data contradicted the model?
    *   **Solution:** How did the new model resolve the contradiction?
3.  **Key Terminology:** Ensure you mention "quantization," "discrete energy levels," "standing waves," and "Coulomb forces" to elevate your response to the top band (Level 4/5).
