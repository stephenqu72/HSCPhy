According to Faraday’s Law, a changing magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) in a conductor. Because the plate is conductive, this EMF causes circulating currents to flow within the material of the plate. These are known as **eddy currents**.
2.  **Lenz's Law:** Lenz’s Law states that the direction of the induced current is such that it creates a magnetic field that opposes the *change* in the original magnetic flux.
