### Explanation
To ensure total internal reflection (TIR) occurs at the glass-water interface, the angle of incidence ($\theta_i$) must be greater than or equal to the critical angle ($\theta_c$).

1.  **Identify the angle of incidence:**
    Looking at the diagram, the light ray hits the hypotenuse of the $45^\circ$ prism at an angle of incidence of $45^\circ$ relative to the normal. Thus, $\theta_i = 45^\circ$.

2.  **Define the condition for TIR:**
    For TIR to occur, the critical angle must be less than or equal to the angle of incidence:
    $$\theta_c \leq 45^\circ$$

3.  **Use the formula for the critical angle:**
    The formula for the critical angle at an interface between two media with refractive indices $n_1$ (glass) and $n_2$ (water) is:
    $$\sin(\theta_c) = \frac{n_2}{n_1}$$
    Given $n_2 = 1.33$ (the refractive index of water), we set the condition:
    $$\sin(45^\circ) \geq \frac{n_2}{n_1}$$
    $$\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \geq \frac{1.33}{n_1}$$

4.  **Solve for $n_1$:**
    $$n_1 \geq 1.33 \times \sqrt{2}$$
    $$n_1 \geq 1.33 \times 1.414...$$
    $$n_1 \geq 1.8806...$$

Rounding to two decimal places, the lowest refractive index required is $1.88$. Comparing this to the given options, **D** is the only value that satisfies the condition $n_1 \geq 1.88$.
