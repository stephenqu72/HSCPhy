### Front
How do you determine the direction of the magnetic field surrounding a straight current-carrying wire?

### Back
- Key idea: The **Right-Hand Grip Rule**.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Point right thumb in direction of conventional current ($I$); fingers curl in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$).
- When to use it: Whenever determining the orientation of magnetic field lines around a straight conductor.
- Common trap: Using the left hand instead of the right, or forgetting that the rule applies to **conventional current** (positive to negative).
