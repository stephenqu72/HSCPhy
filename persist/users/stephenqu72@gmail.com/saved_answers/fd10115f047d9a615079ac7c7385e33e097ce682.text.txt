To answer this question effectively for the HSC Physics exam, you must demonstrate a clear understanding of **Huygens’ Principle** and its application to **diffraction**.

### Answer
Huygens’ Principle states that every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary spherical wavelets. When a plane wavefront reaches a narrow aperture, only the portion of the wavefront passing through the opening continues to propagate. Because the aperture is narrow, it acts effectively as a single point source for the secondary wavelets. Consequently, instead of continuing as a plane wave, the light spreads out in all directions beyond the aperture, creating a circular or spherical wavefront pattern. This phenomenon is known as **diffraction**.

### How to solve it (Teacher's Tips)
To secure all 3 marks, you need to structure your answer logically:

1.  **Define the principle (1 mark):** State clearly that according to Huygens' Principle, every point on an existing wavefront acts as a source of secondary wavelets.
2.  **Apply it to the aperture (1 mark):** Explain that when the wavefront hits the barrier, most of it is blocked. The light reaching the narrow aperture becomes the only source of "active" secondary wavelets. 
3.  **Identify the outcome (1 mark):** Link this behavior to the observation of **diffraction**. Because the aperture is small (roughly comparable to the wavelength $\lambda$), these wavelets expand into a circular pattern rather than continuing straight, explaining why light "bends" around corners or spreads out after passing through a slit.
