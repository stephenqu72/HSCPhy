**Question 12**
*   **Answer: A**
*   **Explanation:**
    *   **The Commutator:** The generator uses a *split-ring commutator*. This device reverses the connections of the coil to the external circuit every half-turn, forcing the current (and thus the voltage) to flow in one direction only. This turns an AC sine wave into a pulsating DC output (all positive peaks). This eliminates options C and D.
    *   **Faraday's Law of Induction:** The induced $emf$ is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux, given by $\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$. Increasing the rotational speed increases the rate of change of flux, which results in a **higher maximum voltage**.
    *   **Frequency:** The frequency of the output signal is proportional to the rotational speed. Doubling the rpm from 50 to 100 means the waves will appear twice as frequently (twice as many peaks in the same time interval).
    *   Comparing the options: Option A shows a higher voltage peak and higher frequency for the 100 rpm graph compared to the 50 rpm graph, while maintaining the "all-positive" profile created by the split-ring commutator.
