### Front
What must be true about the acceleration of a carriage at the highest point of a vertical circular loop?

### Back
- Key idea: Objects undergoing circular motion always experience centripetal acceleration directed toward the center of the circle.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$
- When to use it: To determine the forces or acceleration acting on an object moving in a curved path.
- Common trap: Assuming acceleration is zero at the top because gravity and normal forces might balance; acceleration is non-zero because the velocity vector is constantly changing direction.
