To solve this problem, we use **Malus's Law**, which describes how the intensity of polarized light changes when it passes through a second polarizer (analyzer).

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Understand Malus's Law:**
    The intensity of light emerging from the second polarizer is given by:
    $$I = I_0 \cos^2(\theta)$$
    Where:
    - $I_0$ is the intensity of the light incident on the second polarizer.
    - $I$ is the transmitted intensity.
    - $\theta$ is the angle between the transmission axes of the two polarizers.

2.  **Translate the condition:**
    The question asks to "reduce the intensity by ninety percent." This means the light that *remains* is $100\% - 90\% = 10\%$ of the initial intensity.
    Therefore, the transmitted intensity $I$ is $10\%$ (or $0.1$) of $I_0$:
    $$I = 0.1 I_0$$

3.  **Set up the equation:**
    Substitute $I = 0.1 I_0$ into Malus's Law:
    $$0.1 I_0 = I_0 \cos^2(\theta)$$
    Cancel $I_0$ from both sides:
    $$0.1 = \cos^2(\theta)$$

4.  **Solve for $\theta$:**
    Take the square root of both sides:
    $$\sqrt{0.1} = \cos(\theta)$$
    Then, take the inverse cosine:
    $$\theta = \cos^{-1}(\sqrt{0.1})$$
