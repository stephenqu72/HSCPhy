### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a): Derivation
To derive the equation for orbital velocity, we equate the centripetal force required to keep the satellite in a circular orbit to the gravitational force provided by the Earth.

1.  The centripetal force is given by:
    $$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
2.  The gravitational force (Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation) is:
    $$F_g = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$$
3.  Since the gravitational force provides the centripetal force ($F_c = F_g$):
    $$\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$$
4.  Cancel the mass of the satellite ($m$) from both sides and simplify the radius ($r$):
    $$v^2 = \frac{GM}{r}$$
5.  Taking the square root gives the final expression:
    $$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

#### Part (b): Calculation
**1. Identify the given values:**
*   $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$
*   $M = 6.0 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$
*   Radius of Earth ($R_E$) = $6370 \text{ km} = 6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
*   Altitude ($h$) = $1000 \text{ km} = 1.0 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
*   The orbital radius $r$ is the distance from the *center* of the Earth, so $r = R_E + h$:
    $$r = 6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m} + 1.0 \times 10^6 \text{ m} = 7.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$$

**2. Substitute into the derived formula:**
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(6.0 \times 10^{24})}{7.37 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{4.002 \times 10^{14}}{7.37 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v = \sqrt{5.43 \times 10^7} \approx 7369 \text{ m/s}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (consistent with the provided data):
$$v \approx 7.4 \times 10^3 \text{ m/s} \text{ or } 7.4 \text{ km/s}$$
