### Front
In HSC Physics, how does empirical evidence influence the status of scientific theories?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Physics is an empirical science where evidence acts as the final arbiter. High-quality data that contradicts an existing model necessitates the modification or rejection (falsification) of that theory to better align with reality.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** Use when asked about the "nature of science," the "scientific method," or the impact of specific discoveries (e.g., Hubble’s Law or the Photoelectric Effect) on established paradigms.
- **Common trap:** Describing the history of a discovery without explicitly explaining *how* the evidence caused a paradigm shift from an old model to a new one. Always link evidence to the *falsification* or *refinement* of a theory.
