To solve this problem, we use the formula for the force per unit length between two parallel current-carrying conductors:

$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$

### 1. Identify the given information:
*   Force per unit length $\frac{F}{L} = 2.5 \times 10^{-5}\text{ N/m}$
*   Current in wire $P$, $I_P = 2.0\text{ A}$
*   Separation distance, $r = 4.0\text{ cm} = 0.04\text{ m}$
*   Permeability of free space, $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

### 2. Calculate the magnitude of current in wire $Q$ ($I_Q$):
Rearranging the formula to solve for $I_Q$:
$$I_Q = \frac{(F/L) \times 2\pi r}{\mu_0 I_P}$$

Substitute the values:
$$I_Q = \frac{(2.5 \times 10^{-5}) \times 2\pi \times 0.04}{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times 2.0}$$

Simplify the expression:
$$I_Q = \frac{2.5 \times 10^{-5} \times 0.08}{4 \times 10^{-7} \times 2.0}$$
$$I_Q = \frac{2.0 \times 10^{-6}}{8 \times 10^{-7}} = \frac{20}{8} = 2.5\text{ A}$$

### 3. Determine the direction:
According to the Right-Hand Grip Rule and the laws of electromagnetism, currents flowing in the **same direction** result in an **attractive force** between the conductors. Since the problem states the force is attractive, the current in wire $Q$ must flow in the same direction as the current in wire $P$ (which is **Up**).

### Conclusion:
The magnitude is $2.5\text{ A}$ and the direction is **Up**. This corresponds to option A (and C, which is identical).
