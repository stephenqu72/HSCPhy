To solve this problem, we must understand how particles in a medium move as a transverse wave propagates through it.

### Explanation
1.  **Understand Wave Motion:** When a wave moves to the left at speed $u$, the entire wave profile shifts to the left over time.
2.  **Visualize the Shift:** Imagine the curve shifting slightly to the left. 
    *   **Point X:** Point X is located on the upward slope (left of a trough, moving toward a peak). As the wave moves to the left, the "trough" to the left of X moves closer to X's horizontal position. Consequently, the portion of the wave currently to the right of X (which is higher than X) will move over X. Therefore, **Point X is rising**.
    *   **Point Y:** Point Y is located on the downward slope (right of a peak, moving toward a trough). As the wave moves to the left, the "peak" to the right of Y moves closer to Y's horizontal position. Consequently, the portion of the wave currently to the left of Y (which is higher than Y) will shift to the left, and the portion of the wave to the right (which is lower than Y) will move over Y. Therefore, **Point Y is falling**.

Alternatively, you can visualize the "next" position of the wave: if you draw the wave shifted slightly to the left, the dot at X will be higher than its current position, and the dot at Y will be lower than its current position.

**Conclusion:** X is rising and Y is falling.
