To determine the most appropriate measure of uncertainty for the given ammeter, we need to analyze the scale and the precision of the instrument.

### Explanation

1.  **Determine the scale division:**
    Look at the interval between $0$ and $5$. Counting the markings, there are $5$ subdivisions between $0$ and $5$. Therefore, each small mark represents $1\text{ A}$ ($5 \div 5 = 1$).

2.  **Apply the rule for analogue scale uncertainty:**
    In physics, for an analogue instrument, the standard convention is that the uncertainty is generally taken as **half of the smallest scale division**.
    *   Smallest scale division = $1\text{ A}$
    *   Uncertainty = $\frac{1}{2} \times 1\text{ A} = 0.5\text{ A}$

3.  **Evaluate the options:**
    *   **A ($0.05\text{ A}$):** This implies a level of precision much higher than what this scale can visually resolve.
    *   **B ($0.5\text{ A}$):** This matches the standard rule for reading an analogue scale to "half a division."
    *   **C ($0.8\text{ A}$):** An arbitrary value that does not align with standard scientific measurement practices.
    *   **D ($1\text{ A}$):** This would be the uncertainty if we were restricted to reading only to the nearest marked whole division, but we can interpolate between divisions.

Therefore, the most appropriate measure of uncertainty is $0.5\text{ A}$.

**Correct Answer: B**
