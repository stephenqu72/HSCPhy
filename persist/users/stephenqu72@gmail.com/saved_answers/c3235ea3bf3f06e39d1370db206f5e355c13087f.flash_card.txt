### Front
Distinguish between the terms **accuracy** and **precision** in experimental physics.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Accuracy refers to how close a measurement is to the true value; precision refers to the consistency (closeness) of repeated measurements to each other.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When evaluating experimental validity and reliability in your investigation reports.
- **Common trap:** Confusing high precision (consistent results) with high accuracy (correct results). A set of measurements can be precise but still inaccurate due to systematic error.
