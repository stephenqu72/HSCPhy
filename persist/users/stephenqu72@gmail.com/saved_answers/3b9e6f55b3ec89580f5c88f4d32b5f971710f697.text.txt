To solve this physics problem, we need to analyze the forces acting on the children when the floor of the rotating drum is removed.

### Analysis of Forces
1.  **Vertical Direction:** When the floor is removed, the children are prevented from falling downwards by the **frictional force** acting between their backs/clothes and the wall of the drum. This upward frictional force ($F_f$) must balance the downward gravitational force ($mg$) to keep them suspended in the air. Therefore, $F_f = mg$.
2.  **Horizontal Direction:** As the drum rotates, the wall of the drum exerts a normal reaction force ($N$) on the children directed toward the center of the drum. This normal force provides the **centripetal force** required to keep the children moving in a circle.

### Evaluating the Options
*   **A is incorrect:** Gravitational force acts vertically; it cannot balance a horizontal centripetal force.
*   **B is the correct statement:** It correctly identifies that the frictional force (acting vertically) balances gravity, while the wall's normal force (acting horizontally) provides the centripetal force required for circular motion. Together, these forces keep the children in their position against the wall.
*   **C is incorrect:** The centripetal force is not an "external" force added to the system; it is the *result* of the net force (the normal force) acting on the children. The normal force and the inertia of the children are not "in balance" in a way that causes the motion; rather, the normal force is the net force providing acceleration.
*   **D is incorrect:** While the children feel "pressed" against the wall due to their inertia, the physics perspective describes the normal force as the centripetal force. Furthermore, "unbalanced force" usually relates to a change in speed or state of motion; here, the net force is required to maintain the circular path.

**Correct Answer: B**

### How to solve this type of problem
1.  **Identify the system:** Recognize that the children are in circular motion.
2.  **Draw a Free Body Diagram:** 
    *   Vertical: Gravity ($mg$) acts down, Friction ($F_f$) acts up. Since they don't slide, $F_f = mg$.
    *   Horizontal: The normal force ($N$) from the wall acts toward the center. This is the net force providing the centripetal acceleration ($a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$).
3.  **Match with statements:** Look for the option that correctly identifies the vertical equilibrium (gravity and friction) and the horizontal requirement (normal force as the centripetal agent).
