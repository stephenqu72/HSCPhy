To answer this HSC Physics question for full marks (6 marks), you must demonstrate a clear understanding of both the **experimental setup** and the **mathematical reasoning** (conservation laws) James Chadwick used.

### Answer

James Chadwick discovered the neutron in 1932 by investigating mysterious radiation emitted when beryllium was bombarded with alpha particles. 

1.  **Experimental Setup:** Chadwick bombarded a target of beryllium ($^{9}_{4}\text{Be}$) with alpha particles ($\alpha$ or $^{4}_{2}\text{He}$). This produced a highly penetrating, electrically neutral radiation that was capable of ejecting protons from a paraffin wax target.
2.  **The Problem:** Scientists initially hypothesized this radiation to be high-energy gamma rays. However, the energy required to eject protons via a photon collision would have been impossibly high, violating the conservation of energy and momentum.
3.  **Discovery:** Chadwick proposed that the radiation consisted of uncharged particles with a mass approximately equal to that of a proton. He called these particles "neutrons."
4.  **Deriving the Mass:** Chadwick used the laws of **conservation of energy** and **conservation of momentum** in elastic collisions. By directing the unknown radiation at two different targets (hydrogen and nitrogen atoms) and measuring the kinetic energy of the resulting "recoil" nuclei, he set up a system of equations.
5.  **Mathematical proof:** By analyzing the velocities of the recoil protons ($v_p$) and recoil nitrogen nuclei ($v_n$), he could determine the mass of the incident neutral particle ($m_n$). Because the conservation equations depend on the mass of the colliding bodies, he was able to calculate $m_n \approx 1.008 \text{ u}$, confirming it was slightly heavier than a proton.

### How to solve this question

To secure all 6 marks, structure your response as follows:

*   **1-2 marks (Experimental Context):** Mention the beryllium bombardment experiment. You must state that alpha particles hit beryllium to release "neutral radiation."
*   **2-3 marks (The Evidence):** Explain *why* it wasn't gamma radiation. You need to mention that the radiation ejected protons from paraffin wax. If it were a photon, the energy required would have been inconsistent with known physics.
*   **4-6 marks (Scientific Reasoning):** This is the "deriving the mass" section. 
    *   Explicitly state that he applied the **Law of Conservation of Momentum** and **Law of Conservation of Kinetic Energy**.
    *   Mention that he used different targets (e.g., hydrogen and nitrogen) to create simultaneous equations.
    *   Conclude that the mass of the neutron was found to be approximately the same as the proton. 

**Tip for HSC exams:** Always link the "discovery" to the "mathematical derivation" by stating that the conservation laws provided the quantitative proof for his hypothesis.
