To identify the correct equation for alpha decay, we must look for the specific nuclear reaction pattern where a parent nucleus emits an alpha particle (a helium-4 nucleus, denoted as $^4_2He$).

### The Physics of Alpha Decay
In alpha decay, a heavy nucleus emits an alpha particle. The general equation is:
$${}^{A}_{Z}X \rightarrow {}^{A-4}_{Z-2}Y + {}^{4}_{2}He$$

Where:
- $A$ is the mass number (decreases by 4).
- $Z$ is the atomic number (decreases by 2).

### Evaluation of Options
*   **a) ${}^{233}_{92}U \rightarrow {}^{229}_{90}Th + {}^{4}_{2}He$**: This follows the rule perfectly. The mass number changes from $233$ to $229+4=233$, and the atomic number changes from $92$ to $90+2=92$. This is an **alpha decay**.
*   **b) ${}^{131}_{53}I \rightarrow {}^{131}_{54}Xe + {}^{0}_{-1}e + {}^{0}_{0}\bar{\nu}$**: This represents beta-minus ($\beta^-$) decay, where a neutron turns into a proton and emits an electron and an antineutrino.
*   **c) ${}^{235}_{92}U + {}^{1}_{0}n \rightarrow {}^{141}_{56}Ba + {}^{92}_{36}Kr + 3{}^{1}_{0}n$**: This represents nuclear fission, where a heavy nucleus splits into smaller nuclei after neutron bombardment.
*   **d) ${}^{4}_{2}He + {}^{4}_{2}He \rightarrow {}^{8}_{4}Be + \gamma$**: This represents nuclear fusion, specifically the process of creating Beryllium from Helium.

### Conclusion
The correct equation is **a**.
