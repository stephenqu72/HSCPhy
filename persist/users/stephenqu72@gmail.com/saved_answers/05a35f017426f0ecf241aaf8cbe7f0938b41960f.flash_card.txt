How do you determine if a collision is elastic or inelastic?

### Back
- Key idea: Kinetic energy ($KE$) conservation.
- Formula / law: $KE_{total} = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$. If $KE_{initial} \neq KE_{final}$, it is inelastic.
- When to use it: Any collision problem where you must identify the type of impact or energy loss.
- Common trap: Assuming momentum conservation (which holds for both) implies kinetic energy is also conserved. Momentum is conserved in all closed-system collisions; kinetic energy is only conserved in perfectly elastic ones.
