The correct answer is **(B) Motor Effect**.

### Explanation

To solve this question, we must analyze the physics principles described:

1.  **The Context:** The question refers to a charged particle moving through a magnetic field and experiencing a force. This force is known as the **Lorentz force** ($F = qvB \sin\theta$). 
2.  **The Right-Hand Palm Rule:** As stated in the question, the right-hand palm rule is used to determine the direction of this force on a positive charge (or conventional current).
3.  **The Motor Effect:** The "Motor Effect" is the phenomenon where a current-carrying conductor (a flow of charged particles) placed in an external magnetic field experiences a force. Because a current is simply a collection of individual moving charges, the force experienced by an individual charge (Lorentz force) is the microscopic origin of the macroscopic Motor Effect.
4.  **Eliminating the Distractors:**
    *   **Back EMF (A):** This is an induced voltage that opposes the change in current, typically associated with inductors or motors spinning. It does not explain the direction of the force on a single particle.
    *   **Lenz’s Law (C):** This law dictates the direction of an *induced* current to oppose the change in magnetic flux that created it. It is associated with generators and induction, not the force exerted on a current by an external field.
    *   **Faraday’s Law (D):** This law describes the magnitude of the electromotive force (EMF) induced in a circuit by a changing magnetic field.

Since the scenario describes the physical force exerted on a moving charge (current) due to a magnetic field, the underlying principle is the **Motor Effect**.
