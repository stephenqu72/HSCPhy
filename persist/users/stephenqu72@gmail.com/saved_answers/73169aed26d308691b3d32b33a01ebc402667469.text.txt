### Answer
This experimental setup, famously used by Heinrich Hertz in 1887, validates Maxwell’s theory of electromagnetism by providing empirical evidence for the existence of electromagnetic waves. 

The validation follows this sequence:
1. **Production of EM Waves:** The high-voltage spark at the transmitter creates an oscillating electric current. According to Maxwell’s equations, an accelerating charge produces a time-varying electric field and a corresponding time-varying magnetic field that propagate as an electromagnetic wave through space.
2. **Propagation:** The wave travels from the transmitter to the receiver.
3. **Detection:** When the electromagnetic wave reaches the receiver, its oscillating electric field induces an alternating electromotive force (EMF) in the receiver ring. If the intensity is sufficient, this induced EMF causes electrons to jump across the receiver's spark gap, creating a secondary spark.
4. **Conclusion:** Since the receiver is not physically connected to the transmitter, the energy must have been transferred through space via electromagnetic radiation. This confirmed Maxwell’s prediction that light is an electromagnetic wave and that such waves could be generated and detected experimentally.
