### Front
How did James Chadwick use conservation laws to prove the existence of the neutron?

### Back
- **Key idea:** By applying the laws of conservation of momentum and energy to collisions between unknown radiation and protons, he demonstrated that the incident particle could not be a massless gamma ray, but must have a mass similar to a proton.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** 
  Conservation of Momentum ($p_{initial} = p_{final}$) and Kinetic Energy; ${}_2^4\text{He} + {}_4^9\text{Be} \rightarrow {}_6^{12}\text{C} + {}_0^1\text{n}$
- **When to use it:** When explaining the experimental evidence for the neutron or the breakdown of the proton-electron model of the nucleus.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the radiation was gamma rays; the "trap" is failing to explain *why* gamma rays were mathematically impossible (they lack the mass required to transfer the observed kinetic energy to protons).
