### Front
What are the SI base units for one Joule ($J$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Deriving energy/work units from fundamental quantities (mass, length, time).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $1\text{ J} = 1\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m}^2\cdot\text{s}^{-2}$ (derived from $W = Fd$ and $F = ma$)
- When to use it: To check dimensional consistency or convert units in complex calculations.
- Common trap: Confusing the units for Joule ($kg\cdot m^2\cdot s^{-2}$) with Newton ($kg\cdot m\cdot s^{-2}$).
