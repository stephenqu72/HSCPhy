To answer this 5-mark HSC-style question effectively, you must address two distinct parts: the discovery of the neutron and the reason for its delayed detection.

### Answer

The particle responsible for the variation in atomic mass is the **neutron**. Its discovery was made by **James Chadwick in 1932**. 

**How the discovery was made:**
Chadwick bombarded a thin sheet of beryllium with alpha particles ($\alpha$ radiation). This resulted in the emission of a highly penetrating, neutral radiation. By allowing this radiation to strike other materials, such as paraffin wax, protons were ejected. By applying the laws of conservation of momentum and energy to the collisions between these unknown particles and the protons, Chadwick determined that the mass of the emitted particles was approximately equal to that of a proton. Since these particles could eject protons but were unaffected by electric or magnetic fields, he concluded they were electrically neutral, identifying them as neutrons.

**Why the discovery was delayed:**
The discovery came significantly later than other subatomic particles (the electron and proton) primarily because the neutron possesses **no net electric charge**. Early experimental techniques for detecting subatomic particles relied heavily on electromagnetic interaction—such as observing the deflection of particles in electric or magnetic fields or their ability to ionize gases. Because the neutron is neutral, it did not interact with these fields, leaving no "track" in cloud chambers or ionization detectors, rendering it essentially "invisible" to the detection methods available to earlier physicists like Rutherford.

### How to solve this question
1.  **Identify the Particle:** You must immediately recognize that the "particle responsible for this variation in mass" refers to the neutron.
2.  **Outline the Experiment (Part 1):** Focus on the core components of Chadwick’s experiment:
    *   The source: Alpha particles striking Beryllium.
    *   The observations: Neutral radiation and the ejection of protons from target material (like paraffin).
    *   The deduction: Using mass/energy conservation to conclude the particles had a mass similar to the proton but lacked charge.
3.  **Explain the Delay (Part 2):** Connect the nature of the neutron (electrically neutral) to the limitations of historical technology. Since previous discoveries (like the electron) relied on charge, the lack of charge meant the neutron could not be manipulated or detected by traditional electromagnetic equipment.
4.  **HSC Exam Tip:** A 5-mark question requires structure. Allocate roughly 2–3 marks for the experimental process and 2–3 marks for the explanation of the delay. Use scientific terminology like "neutral," "electromagnetic interaction," and "conservation laws" to gain full marks.
