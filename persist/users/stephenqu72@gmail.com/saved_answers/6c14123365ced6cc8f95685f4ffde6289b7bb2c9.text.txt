To solve this problem, we must understand the nuclear fusion process in the Sun, where four protons (hydrogen nuclei) fuse to form one helium nucleus.

### 1. Identify the energy released per helium nucleus
The mass of four protons ($4 \times 1.007276\text{ amu}$) is $4.029104\text{ amu}$. The mass of the resulting helium nucleus is given as $4.002602\text{ amu}$. The mass defect ($\Delta m$) is:
$$\Delta m = 4.029104 - 4.002602 = 0.026502\text{ amu}$$

To convert this mass to energy (Joules), we use $E = \Delta m c^2$. First, convert $\text{amu}$ to $\text{kg}$ using $1\text{ amu} = 1.660539 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$:
$$\Delta m (\text{kg}) = 0.026502 \times 1.660539 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg} \approx 4.4007 \times 10^{-29}\text{ kg}$$

Using $E = \Delta m c^2$ with $c = 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$:
$$E = (4.4007 \times 10^{-29}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)^2 \approx 3.96 \times 10^{-12}\text{ J}$$

*(Note: In standard physics textbooks, this energy is often approximated as $26.7\text{ MeV}$, which is $4.27 \times 10^{-12}\text{ J}$. We will use the standard energy release value for this calculation).*

### 2. Calculate the number of nuclei per second
The total power output of the Sun is $3.85 \times 10^{26}\text{ W}$ (Joules per second). The number of helium nuclei ($N$) produced per second is:
$$N = \frac{\text{Total Power}}{\text{Energy per nucleus}} = \frac{3.85 \times 10^{26}\text{ J/s}}{4.29 \times 10^{-12}\text{ J/nucleus}}$$
$$N \approx 8.97 \times 10^{37}\text{ nuclei/s}$$
