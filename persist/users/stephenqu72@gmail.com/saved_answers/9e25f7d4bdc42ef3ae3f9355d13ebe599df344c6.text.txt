### Solution and Explanation

To solve this problem, we use Einstein’s photoelectric equation:
$$E_k = hf - \Phi$$
Where $E_k$ is the maximum kinetic energy, $h$ is Planck's constant, $f$ is the frequency, and $\Phi$ is the work function. This is in the form of a linear equation $y = mx + c$.

---

#### (a) Complete the third column
To convert Joules ($J$) to electronvolts ($eV$), divide by the elementary charge $e \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C}$.
*   $1.2 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J} \implies \frac{1.2 \times 10^{-19}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}} = 0.75 \, \text{eV}$
*   $2.2 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J} \implies \frac{2.2 \times 10^{-19}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}} = 1.375 \approx 1.38 \, \text{eV}$
*   $3.5 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J} \implies \frac{3.5 \times 10^{-19}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}} = 2.1875 \approx 2.19 \, \text{eV}$
*   $4.2 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J} \implies \frac{4.2 \times 10^{-19}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}} = 2.625 \approx 2.63 \, \text{eV}$

#### (b) Graphing the data
To get Planck's constant in S.I. units, plot **Kinetic Energy ($J$)** on the y-axis and **Frequency ($Hz$)** on the x-axis.
*   **Gradient ($m$):** Represents Planck's constant ($h$). Using two points on your line of best fit (e.g., $(7.0 \times 10^{14}, 0.8 \times 10^{-19})$ and $(12 \times 10^{14}, 4.2 \times 10^{-19})$):
    $$h = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{(4.2 - 0.8) \times 10^{-19}}{(12 - 7.0) \times 10^{14}} = \frac{3.4 \times 10^{-19}}{5.0 \times 10^{14}} = 6.8 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J s}$$
*   **y-intercept ($c$):** Represents $-\Phi$ (negative work function). By extrapolating the line to $f=0$, you find the magnitude of the intercept equals $\Phi$.

#### (i) Planck's constant
Based on the gradient calculation above, the value is approximately $6.8 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J s}$. (Note: Experimental values vary; ensure it is close to the accepted $6.63 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J s}$).

#### (ii) Threshold frequency
The threshold frequency ($f_0$) occurs where $E_k = 0$. Using the equation $0 = hf_0 - \Phi$:
$$f_0 = \frac{\Phi}{h}$$
From the graph, find the x-intercept. Using the data provided:
Using the slope $6.8 \times 10^{-34}$ and a point: $0.8 \times 10^{-19} = (6.8 \times 10^{-34})(7.0 \times 10^{14}) - \Phi \implies \Phi \approx 4 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}$.
$$f_0 \approx \frac{4 \times 10^{-19}}{6.63 \times 10^{-34}} \approx 6.0 \times 10^{14} \, \text{Hz}$$
