To determine the magnetic forces acting on the wire segments, we use the formula for the magnetic force on a current-carrying conductor:
$$F = ILB \sin(\theta)$$
where $I$ is the current, $L$ is the length of the wire, $B$ is the magnetic field strength, and $\theta$ is the angle between the current and the magnetic field. The direction is determined by the **Right-Hand Rule (RHR)**: point your thumb in the direction of the current ($I$), your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$), and your palm (or the push of your hand) points in the direction of the force ($F$).

Since the current has a component perpendicular to the magnetic field, a force is exerted. 3.  **Section $NO$:** The current flows from $N$ to $O$ (horizontally to the left). Here, the direction of the current is **parallel** (specifically anti-parallel) to the magnetic field. When the current and magnetic field are parallel ($\theta = 0^\circ$ or $180^\circ$), $\sin(\theta) = 0$, meaning the magnetic force $F$ is **zero**.
