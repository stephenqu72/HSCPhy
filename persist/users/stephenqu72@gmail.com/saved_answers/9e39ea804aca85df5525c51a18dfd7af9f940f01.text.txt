To solve this problem, we use the law of universal gravitation, which states that the gravitational field strength $g$ at a distance $r$ from the center of a planet is given by the formula:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

where:
- $G$ is the gravitational constant
- $M$ is the mass of the Earth
- $r$ is the distance from the center of the Earth

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the initial state:**
    At distance $r = R$, the gravitational field strength $g_1$ is:
    $$g_1 = \frac{GM}{R^2} = 9.76\text{ N kg}^{-1}$$

2.  **Identify the new state:**
    We need to find the gravitational field strength $g_2$ at a distance $r = 2R$:
    $$g_2 = \frac{GM}{(2R)^2}$$

3.  **Simplify the expression for $g_2$:**
    $$g_2 = \frac{GM}{4R^2} = \frac{1}{4} \times \left( \frac{GM}{R^2} \right)$$

4.  **Substitute the known value:**
    Since $\frac{GM}{R^2} = 9.76\text{ N kg}^{-1}$, we substitute this into our equation:
    $$g_2 = \frac{9.76}{4}$$
    $$g_2 = 2.44\text{ N kg}^{-1}$$

Comparing our result to the given options, $2.44\text{ N kg}^{-1}$ matches option (B).

**Answer:** (B)
