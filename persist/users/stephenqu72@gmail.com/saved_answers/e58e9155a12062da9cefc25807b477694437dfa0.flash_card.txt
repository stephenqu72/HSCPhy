### Front
How do you determine an unknown mass $M$ in a system involving both static vertical equilibrium and uniform horizontal circular motion?

### Back
- Key idea: Equate the tension ($T$) acting on the static hanging mass to the centripetal force ($F_c$) required for the rotating mass.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $T = Mg$ and $T = \frac{mv^2}{r} \implies M = \frac{mv^2}{gr}$
- When to use it: When a system is connected by a string where one mass hangs vertically and the other orbits horizontally.
- Common trap: Confusing tension ($T$) with the mass ($M$) or forgetting to divide the centripetal force equation by gravity ($g$) when isolating $M$.
