### Front
How does changing the horizontal launch velocity of a projectile affect its time of flight ($t$) and horizontal range ($R$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Horizontal and vertical motions are independent; $t$ is determined solely by vertical displacement ($h$) and $g$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $t = \sqrt{2h/g}$ and $R = v_x t$.
- When to use it: Any horizontal projectile motion problem (ignoring air resistance).
- Common trap: Assuming an increase in horizontal velocity increases the time of flight.
