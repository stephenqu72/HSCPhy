### Front
How do you determine the orbital period of a planet if its orbital radius relative to Earth is known?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kepler’s Third Law states the square of the orbital period ($T$) is proportional to the cube of the mean orbital radius ($r$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\frac{T_1^2}{r_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{r_2^3}$; No fixed constant.
- **When to use it:** When comparing the orbital periods or distances of two objects orbiting the same central body (e.g., planets around the Sun).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to square the period ratio and cube the radius ratio, or failing to use consistent units for $T$ and $r$.
