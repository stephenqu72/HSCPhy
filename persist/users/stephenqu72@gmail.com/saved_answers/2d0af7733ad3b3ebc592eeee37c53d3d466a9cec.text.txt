To find the speed of the proton before the collision, we apply the **Law of Conservation of Momentum**. This law states that for any closed system, the total momentum before the collision must equal the total momentum after the collision.

### 1. Identify the Variables
Let $m$ be the mass of the proton.
The mass of the alpha particle is $4m$.
Let $v_{p,i}$ be the initial velocity of the proton (what we are trying to find).
The initial velocity of the alpha particle is $0\text{ m s}^{-1}$ (it is stationary).
After the collision:
- The proton moves in the opposite direction (rebound), so its velocity $v_{p,f} = -6.0 \times 10^6\text{ m s}^{-1}$.
- The alpha particle moves forward at $v_{\alpha,f} = 4.0 \times 10^6\text{ m s}^{-1}$.

### 2. Set up the Equation
The total momentum before collision equals the total momentum after:
$$p_{total, i} = p_{total, f}$$
$$(m \cdot v_{p,i}) + (4m \cdot 0) = (m \cdot v_{p,f}) + (4m \cdot v_{\alpha,f})$$

### 3. Solve the Equation
Notice that mass $m$ appears in every term, so we can divide the entire equation by $m$:
$$v_{p,i} = v_{p,f} + 4(v_{\alpha,f})$$

Substitute the values:
$$v_{p,i} = (-6.0 \times 10^6) + 4(4.0 \times 10^6)$$
$$v_{p,i} = -6.0 \times 10^6 + 16.0 \times 10^6$$
$$v_{p,i} = 10.0 \times 10^6\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

**Answer:**
The speed of the proton before the collision was $1.0 \times 10^7\text{ m s}^{-1}$ (or $10.0 \times 10^6\text{ m s}^{-1}$).

***

### How to solve this type of problem:
1. **Define a direction as positive:** Usually, the direction of the initial motion is taken as positive.
2. **Account for vector directions:** Always check the direction of objects after a collision. If an object reverses direction, its velocity must be assigned a negative sign.
3. **Cancel common variables:** In momentum problems involving ratios of mass, $m$ will often cancel out, simplifying the calculation significantly.
4. **Units:** Ensure all units are consistent (here, everything is already in SI units).
