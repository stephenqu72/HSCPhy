### Answer
Yes, the traveller will be able to see their reflection in the mirror.

### Explanation
To solve this, we rely on the **First Postulate of Special Relativity**, which states that the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames of reference.

1.  **Frame of Reference:** The traveller is in an inertial frame of reference (the train) moving at a constant velocity. Within this frame, the traveller is at rest relative to the mirror.
2.  **Physics Consistency:** Because the train is an inertial frame, the laws of optics and reflection work exactly the same way as they would if the train were stationary on the ground.
3.  **Light Path:** Light from the traveller’s face travels to the mirror, reflects, and returns to their eyes. Since the traveller and the mirror are both moving at the same constant velocity, the geometry of this reflection is identical to the situation where both are stationary. There is no relative motion between the observer and the mirror, so the image appears exactly as it would in everyday life.

In summary, Einstein’s Principle of Relativity ensures that you cannot perform an experiment (including looking into a mirror) to detect your own constant velocity; therefore, the reflection must be perfectly visible.
