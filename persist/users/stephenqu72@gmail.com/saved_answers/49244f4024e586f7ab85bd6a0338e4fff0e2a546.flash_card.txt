### Front
What is the direction of the net force acting on a projectile in flight (ignoring air resistance)?

### Back
- Key idea: The only force acting on a projectile is gravity, which acts exclusively in the vertical direction.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s Second Law ($F_{net} = ma$); $a = g \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ downward.
- When to use it: To determine acceleration, net force, or trajectory characteristics in projectile motion problems.
- Common trap: Assuming the force changes direction or that a horizontal force is required to maintain horizontal velocity (Newton’s First Law).
