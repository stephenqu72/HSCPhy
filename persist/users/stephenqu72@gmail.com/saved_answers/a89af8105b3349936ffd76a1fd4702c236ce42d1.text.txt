To determine the correct answer, we must look at the scientific methodology Edwin Hubble used to establish the expansion of the universe.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) stellar spectra.**

### Explanation
Edwin Hubble’s discovery was rooted in two primary components: determining distances to galaxies and measuring how fast they were moving. 

1.  **Distance Measurement:** Hubble used the period-luminosity relationship of Cepheid variable stars (discovered by Henrietta Leavitt) to calculate the distances to various galaxies.
2.  **Velocity Measurement:** To determine how fast these galaxies were moving relative to Earth, Hubble analyzed the light coming from them using spectroscopy. By observing the **stellar spectra** (or more accurately, the spectra of galaxies), Hubble identified the phenomenon of **redshift**. 
3.  **The Conclusion:** Hubble observed that the absorption lines in the spectra of distant galaxies were shifted toward the red end of the electromagnetic spectrum. According to the Doppler effect, this indicated that these galaxies were moving away from us. By plotting the distance of these galaxies (calculated via luminosity) against their recession velocity (calculated via their spectral redshift), he formulated Hubble's Law: $v = H_0d$, where $v$ is the recession velocity, $d$ is the distance, and $H_0$ is the Hubble constant.

While stellar luminosities were essential for calculating distance, it was the observation of **stellar spectra** (specifically the redshift of spectral lines) that provided the evidence for the recession velocity, confirming that the universe is expanding.
