### Front
What determines the maximum height ($h_{max}$) of a projectile in motion?

### Back
- Key idea: $h_{max}$ depends exclusively on the initial vertical velocity component ($u_y$) and gravity ($g$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $h_{max} = \frac{u_y^2}{2g}$
- When to use it: To calculate the peak vertical displacement of any projectile.
- Common trap: Assuming horizontal velocity ($u_x$) affects vertical height; it only influences horizontal range.
