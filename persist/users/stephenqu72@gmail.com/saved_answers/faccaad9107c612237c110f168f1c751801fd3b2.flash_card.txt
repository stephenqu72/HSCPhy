### Front
For a mass on a string at the **bottom** of a vertical circular path, why must Tension ($T$) be greater than Weight ($mg$)?

### Back
- Key idea: The net force must act as a centripetal force directed toward the center of the circle.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $T - mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: Any vertical circular motion problem where you need to calculate tension or force at the lowest point.
- Common trap: Assuming $T = mg$; this only occurs in static equilibrium, but the object is accelerating upwards towards the center.
