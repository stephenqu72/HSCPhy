### Front
What is the equation for the net centripetal force acting on a car at the very top of a vertical circular loop?

### Back
- Key idea: At the top of a loop, both the normal force ($N$) and gravity ($mg$) act towards the centre, providing the required centripetal force ($F_c$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_c = N + mg$ (or $\frac{mv^2}{r} = N + mg$)
- When to use it: Circular motion problems where an object is at the highest point of a vertical path.
- Common trap: Assuming the normal force is zero or that weight force acts in the opposite direction to the centripetal force.
