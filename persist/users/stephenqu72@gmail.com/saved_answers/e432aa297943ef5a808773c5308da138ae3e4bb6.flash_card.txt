### Front
How does the escape velocity ($v_e$) of an object change relative to the mass ($M$) and radius ($r$) of a planet?

### Back
- Key idea: Escape velocity is directly proportional to $\sqrt{M}$ and inversely proportional to $\sqrt{r}$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$
- When to use it: To determine the minimum speed required to escape a gravitational field from a specific distance ($r$).
- Common trap: Confusing $r$ (distance from center of mass) with altitude (height above surface). Always ensure $r = R_{planet} + h$.
