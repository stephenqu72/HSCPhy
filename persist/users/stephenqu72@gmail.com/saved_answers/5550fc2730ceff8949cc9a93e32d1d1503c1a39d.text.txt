### Answer
The correct answer is **C. into the page**.

### Explanation
To determine the direction of the magnetic field at the centre of a current-carrying loop, we use the **Right-Hand Grip Rule** (also known as the Right-Hand Rule for solenoids or loops).

1.  **Identify the current direction:** Look at the arrow on the circular wire. The current $I$ is flowing in a **clockwise** direction.
2.  **Apply the Right-Hand Grip Rule:** 
    *   Curl the fingers of your right hand in the direction of the current (clockwise). 
    *   Your thumb will naturally point in the direction of the magnetic field $B$ within the centre of the loop.
3.  **Visualize the result:** As your fingers follow the clockwise circle, your thumb will point away from you, directly **into the page**.

Alternatively, you can visualize the Right-Hand Grip Rule for a straight wire segment at any point on the loop. If you place your thumb in the direction of the current at any point on the wire and curl your fingers, your fingers will point into the page at the centre of the circle. Because the current flows in a clockwise direction, all segments of the loop contribute to a magnetic field vector that points into the page at the centre.
