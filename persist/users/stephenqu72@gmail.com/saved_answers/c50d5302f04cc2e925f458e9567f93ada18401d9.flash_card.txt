### Front
What is the most efficient method to calculate the final velocity or kinetic energy of an object in projectile motion when air resistance is ignored?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Law of Conservation of Mechanical Energy.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $E_{total, \text{initial}} = E_{total, \text{final}} \implies \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2 + mgh_i = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 + mgh_f$.
- **When to use it:** Use it as a "shortcut" for energy/velocity problems to avoid complex vector kinematics.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to include initial kinetic energy if the object is thrown at an angle, or mistakenly assuming initial velocity is zero.
