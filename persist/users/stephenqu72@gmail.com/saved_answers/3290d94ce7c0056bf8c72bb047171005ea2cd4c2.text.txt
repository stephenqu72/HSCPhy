### c. Assuming there are no frictional forces, derive an equation for the radius of mass A in terms of $v$ and $g$.
*Note: In this specific apparatus setup, the tension $T$ in the string provides the centripetal force. $T = m_B g$.*

1.  For mass A, the centripetal force required is:
    $$F_c = \frac{m_A v^2}{r}$$
2.  Since the system is balanced, the tension $T$ equals the weight of mass B:
    $$T = m_B g$$
3.  Equating the two (assuming the string is horizontal):
    $$m_B g = \frac{m_A v^2}{r}$$
4.  Rearrange the formula to solve for $r$:
    $$r = \frac{m_A v^2}{m_B g}$$

*Self-correction/Refinement for HSC level:* If the prompt specifically asks for $r$ in terms of $v$ and $g$ only, we observe that for this specific balance, $\frac{m_A}{m_B}$ is a ratio of the masses. If the experiment is designed such that $m_A = m_B$, then $m_A/m_B = 1$, simplifying the expression to:
$$r = \frac{v^2}{g}$$
