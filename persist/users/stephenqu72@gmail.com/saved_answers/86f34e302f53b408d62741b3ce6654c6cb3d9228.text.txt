### Explanation
To solve this question, we must understand the fundamental classification of particles within the **Standard Model of Physics**.

The Standard Model categorizes all known elementary particles into two primary groups based on their quantum properties (specifically their spin):

1.  **Fermions:** These are the "building blocks" of matter. Fermions have half-integer spin and include **quarks** (which form protons and neutrons) and **leptons** (such as the electron, muon, and neutrinos).
2.  **Bosons:** These are "force-carrier" particles that mediate the fundamental interactions (forces) of nature. They have integer spin and include the photon (electromagnetic force), gluons (strong nuclear force), $W$ and $Z$ bosons (weak nuclear force), and the Higgs boson (responsible for mass).

**Evaluating the distractors:**
*   **B is incorrect:** Mesons are not elementary particles; they are composite particles made of a quark and an antiquark.
*   **C is incorrect:** Electrons are a *type* of lepton, making the list redundant rather than comprehensive of the Standard Model.
*   **D is incorrect:** Again, electrons are leptons, so this list is redundant and fails to include the force-carrying bosons, which are an essential component of the Standard Model.

Therefore, the two fundamental categories that encompass all elementary particles are fermions and bosons.
