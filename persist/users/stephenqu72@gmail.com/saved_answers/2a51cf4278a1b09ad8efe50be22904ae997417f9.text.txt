To determine which energy level the electron relaxed to, we use the Bohr model formula for the energy levels of a hydrogen atom:

$$E_n = -\frac{13.6\text{ eV}}{n^2}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Calculate the energy of the electron at the starting level ($n=6$):**
    $$E_6 = -\frac{13.6}{6^2} = -\frac{13.6}{36} \approx -0.378\text{ eV}$$

2.  **Understand the emission process:**
    When an electron transitions from a higher energy level ($E_i$) to a lower energy level ($E_f$), the energy of the emitted photon ($\Delta E$) is the difference between these levels:
    $$\Delta E = E_i - E_f = 3.025\text{ eV}$$
    Since $E_i = E_6 = -0.378\text{ eV}$, we can set up the equation:
    $$-0.378 - E_f = 3.025$$
    $$E_f = -0.378 - 3.025 = -3.403\text{ eV}$$

3.  **Determine the final energy level ($n_f$):**
    Now we set $E_f$ equal to the general formula for the energy level $n_f$:
    $$-3.403 = -\frac{13.6}{n_f^2}$$
    $$n_f^2 = \frac{13.6}{3.403} \approx 4$$
    $$n_f = \sqrt{4} = 2$$

**Conclusion:**
