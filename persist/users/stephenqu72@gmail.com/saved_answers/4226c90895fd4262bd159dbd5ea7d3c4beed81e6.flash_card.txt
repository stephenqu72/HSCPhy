### Front
Why does an object appear to move to the left when a car turns right, according to Newton’s First Law?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The object maintains its original straight-line velocity due to inertia, while the car's frame changes direction.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Newton’s First Law of Motion (Law of Inertia).
- **When to use it:** Analyzing motion in non-inertial reference frames (e.g., accelerating or turning vehicles).
- **Common trap:** Attributing the motion to "centrifugal force"; instead, identify it as inertia and avoid treating centrifugal force as a real physical force.
