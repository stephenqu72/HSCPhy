### Front
How do you determine the weakest gravitational field strength ($g$) when given multiple mass ($M$) and distance ($r$) scenarios?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational field strength is directly proportional to mass and inversely proportional to the square of the distance.
- **Formula:** $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- **When to use it:** When comparing $g$ at specific points relative to different celestial bodies or distances.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to square the distance ($r^2$) in the denominator, especially when the distance is a fraction or a multiple.
