To solve this problem, we need to apply the conservation laws of nuclear physics regarding radioactive decay. Actinium ($Ac$) has an atomic number of $Z = 89$.

### 1. Alpha Decay
In alpha decay, a nucleus emits an alpha particle, which is a helium nucleus ($^{4}_{2}\alpha$ or $^{4}_{2}He$).
*   **Mass number ($A$):** Decreases by 4.
*   **Atomic number ($Z$):** Decreases by 2.

The equation for Actinium-227 is:
$$^{227}_{89}Ac \rightarrow ^{223}_{87}Fr + ^{4}_{2}He$$
The element with atomic number $87$ is Francium ($Fr$). Therefore, the product of alpha decay is $^{223}Fr$.

### 2. Beta Decay
In beta-minus decay, a neutron is converted into a proton, emitting an electron ($^{0}_{-1}e$).
*   **Mass number ($A$):** Remains unchanged.
*   **Atomic number ($Z$):** Increases by 1.

The equation for Actinium-227 is:
$$^{227}_{89}Ac \rightarrow ^{227}_{90}Th + ^{0}_{-1}e$$
The element with atomic number $90$ is Thorium ($Th$). Therefore, the product of beta decay is $^{227}Th$.

### Conclusion
Comparing our findings ($^{223}Fr$ for alpha decay and $^{227}Th$ for beta decay) to the options provided:
- Option A: $^{223}Fr$ and $^{227}Th$

The correct answer is **A**.
