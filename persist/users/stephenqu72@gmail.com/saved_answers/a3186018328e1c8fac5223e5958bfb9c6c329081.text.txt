To determine how to achieve the maximum effect when turning a spanner, we must look at the physical concept of **torque** (or the moment of a force).

### Answer
The correct answer is **(D)**.

### Explanation
The "maximum effect" in turning a bolt refers to maximizing the **torque** ($\tau$) applied to it. The formula for torque is:
$$\tau = rF \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
*   $r$ is the distance from the pivot point (the bolt) to the point where the force is applied.
*   $F$ is the magnitude of the force applied.
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the force vector and the lever arm (the spanner).

To maximize $\tau$:
1.  **Maximize $r$:** We should apply the force as far away from the pivot as possible. This is why a longer spanner makes it easier to turn a bolt. Thus, we want the **largest distance possible**.
2.  **Maximize $\sin(\theta)$:** The value of $\sin(\theta)$ reaches its maximum of $1$ when $\theta = 90^\circ$ (a right angle). Applying the force at a right angle ensures that the entire force contributes to rotating the bolt.

Comparing this to the options:
*   (A) and (B) are incorrect because they suggest the smallest distance, which minimizes torque.
*   (C) is incorrect because it suggests applying the force at the middle distance, which does not provide the maximum lever arm.
*   **(D)** correctly identifies both the **right-angle** application (for maximum efficiency) and the **largest distance** (for maximum leverage).
