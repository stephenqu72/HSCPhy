### Front
How does the gravitational potential energy ($U$) of an object change as it moves further away from a planet?

### Back
- Key idea: $U$ is negative; as distance $r$ increases, $U$ becomes less negative, meaning it increases towards a maximum of zero.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$
- When to use it: Any question involving gravitational potential energy changes or work done in a gravitational field.
- Common trap: Assuming that "increasing" energy means becoming more negative (e.g., $-100 \to -200$). Remember that $-100$ is mathematically greater than $-200$.
