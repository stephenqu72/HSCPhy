### Front
What are the fundamental components and interactions of a hydrogen atom according to the Standard Model?

### Back
- Key idea: Hydrogen comprises a proton (baryon; $uud$ quarks held by **gluons**) and an electron (lepton), held by the **electromagnetic force** (photons). All particles acquire mass via the **Higgs field** (Higgs boson).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Questions regarding the Standard Model, atomic structure, or subatomic particle classification.
- Common trap: Confusing force carriers (bosons) with matter particles (fermions) or failing to distinguish between the strong force (nucleus) and electromagnetic force (atomic structure).
