### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to understand the historical context of Newton's corpuscular (particle) theory of light, which was a major topic in the study of classical physics.

1.  **Newton's Hypothesis:** Newton proposed that light consisted of tiny particles called "corpuscles." To explain why light changes direction (refracts) when passing from one medium into a denser one (e.g., from air into glass), he suggested that the particles of light experience an attractive force perpendicular to the surface of the denser medium.
2.  **The Mechanism:** As the light corpuscles approach the denser medium, this attractive force pulls them towards the surface. This causes the component of their velocity perpendicular to the surface to increase, while the component parallel to the surface remains unchanged.
3.  **Result:** This change in the velocity components results in the light bending toward the normal, which is the observed effect of refraction.
4.  **Flaw in the Theory:** It is important to note that Newton’s theory incorrectly predicted that light travels *faster* in a denser medium. While this prediction was later proven wrong (by Foucault and Fizeau), the explanation provided by Newton *was* that forces within the medium changed the direction of the light particles.

*   **(B)** is incorrect because, while Newton predicted a change in speed, he believed the particles would *speed up* in a denser medium, not slow down.
*   **(C)** describes scattering, which is not the primary mechanism proposed by Newton for refraction.
*   **(D)** is incorrect because Newton did attempt, and provided a mechanism, to explain refraction; it just happens to be a physically incorrect one by modern standards.
