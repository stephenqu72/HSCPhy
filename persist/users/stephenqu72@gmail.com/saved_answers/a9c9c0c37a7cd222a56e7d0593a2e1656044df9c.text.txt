The correct answer is **(D) Diffraction.**

### Explanation

To solve this problem, you need to identify the physical process occurring when light interacts with a narrow aperture.

1.  **Analyze the image:** The image shows a bright central fringe flanked by a series of smaller, less intense bright spots arranged in a horizontal line. This specific pattern is the characteristic "diffraction pattern" created when monochromatic light (like a laser) passes through a single narrow slit.
2.  **Evaluate the options:**
    *   **(A) Refraction:** This is the bending of light as it passes between media of different refractive indices. It does not produce this fringe pattern.
    *   **(B) Reflection:** This is the bouncing of light off a surface. It does not explain the spread of light into fringes.
    *   **(C) Coherence:** While the laser light is coherent, coherence is a *property* of the light source, not the *phenomenon* causing the pattern itself. The pattern occurs because the light waves interfere with themselves after being spread out by the slit.
    *   **(D) Diffraction:** This is the bending or spreading of waves around an obstacle or through an aperture. When light passes through a narrow slit, each point across the slit acts as a secondary source of waves (Huygens' Principle). These waves interfere constructively and destructively, resulting in the characteristic intensity distribution observed on the screen.

**Key takeaway for HSC Physics:** Whenever you see a "single slit" or "small aperture" setup resulting in a series of bright and dark spots, you are looking at **diffraction**. If it were a "double slit," the primary phenomenon would be interference, though diffraction would still be occurring at each slit.
