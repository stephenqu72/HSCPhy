To solve this projectile motion problem, we must treat the horizontal and vertical components of motion independently. The key principle is that the horizontal velocity $v_x$ remains constant, while the vertical motion is governed by gravity with an initial vertical velocity of $u_y = 0 \text{ m s}^{-1}$.

### Part a: Horizontal distance
Since the horizontal velocity is constant, we use the formula $d_x = v_x \times t$.
Given:
$v_x = 3.0 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
$t = 0.40 \text{ s}$

$$d_x = 3.0 \times 0.40 = 1.2 \text{ m}$$

### Part b: Height of the table
To find the height $h$ (vertical displacement), we use the kinematic equation for vertical motion:
$d_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$
Since $u_y = 0$, $a_y = g = 10 \text{ m s}^{-2}$, and $t = 0.40 \text{ s}$:

$$h = 0 \times 0.40 + \frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times (0.40)^2$$
$$h = 5 \times 0.16 = 0.8 \text{ m}$$

### Part c: Speed at which the ball hits the floor
The final speed $v$ is the vector sum of the final horizontal velocity $v_{fx}$ and the final vertical velocity $v_{fy}$.
1. Horizontal: $v_{fx} = v_x = 3.0 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
2. Vertical: $v_{fy} = u_y + g t$
$$v_{fy} = 0 + 10 \times 0.40 = 4.0 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

The magnitude of the resultant velocity is:
$$v = \sqrt{v_{fx}^2 + v_{fy}^2}$$
$$v = \sqrt{3.0^2 + 4.0^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25}$$
$$v = 5.0 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

***

### Summary of Answers:
*   **a:** $1.2 \text{ m}$
*   **b:** $0.8 \text{ m}$
*   **c:** $5.0 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
