To determine the maximum height of a projectile, we look at the physics of vertical motion under the influence of gravity.

### Answer
The correct answer is **A: Only the vertical component of the launch velocity.**

### Explanation
In projectile motion, we treat the horizontal and vertical components of motion independently:

1.  **Vertical Motion:** The projectile reaches its maximum height ($h_{max}$) when its vertical velocity component $v_y$ becomes zero. We can use the kinematic equation:
    $$v^2 = u_y^2 + 2ay$$
    At maximum height, $v = 0$, $a = -g$, and $y = h_{max}$. Substituting these values:
    $$0 = u_y^2 - 2gh_{max}$$
    Rearranging to solve for $h_{max}$:
    $$h_{max} = \frac{u_y^2}{2g}$$
    As we can see from this formula, the maximum height depends solely on the initial vertical velocity component ($u_y$) and the acceleration due to gravity ($g$).

2.  **Horizontal Motion:** The horizontal component of the velocity ($u_x$) determines how far the projectile travels horizontally (the range) and how long it stays in the air, but it has no impact on the vertical reach of the projectile.

Because $h_{max}$ is a function of $u_y$ only, the horizontal component does not affect it. Therefore, statement A is correct.
