### Front
What is the relationship between linear velocity ($v$), angular velocity ($\omega$), and radius ($r$) for an object moving in a circle?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Linear velocity is the product of the radius and the angular velocity.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v = r\omega$
- **When to use it:** Converting between rotational speed (rad/s) and tangential speed (m/s) in circular motion problems.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units (e.g., cm to m) or confusing angular velocity ($\omega$ in rad/s) with frequency ($f$ in Hz).
