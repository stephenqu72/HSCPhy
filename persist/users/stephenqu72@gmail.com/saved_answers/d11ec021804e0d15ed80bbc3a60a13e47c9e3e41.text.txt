To account for the increase in the amplitude of oscillation of magnet B, we must apply the principles of electromagnetic induction and resonance.

### Answer
The increase in the amplitude of magnet B is due to the transfer of energy through an electromagnetic circuit leading to mechanical resonance.

1.  **Electromagnetic Induction:** As magnet A oscillates, the changing magnetic flux through its respective solenoid induces an electromotive force (EMF) and a corresponding alternating current in the circuit, according to Faraday’s Law of Induction.
2.  **Motor Effect:** This alternating current flows into the second solenoid, creating a time-varying magnetic field within it. This field exerts a magnetic force on the oscillating south pole of magnet B (the Motor Effect).
3.  **Resonance:** Because the two systems (spring-magnet assemblies) are identical, they have the same natural frequency. The alternating magnetic force acts as a driving force on magnet B. Since the driving frequency matches the natural frequency of the second system, the system enters a state of **resonance**.
4.  **Energy Transfer:** At resonance, the amplitude of the driven oscillation (magnet B) increases over time as energy is efficiently transferred from the induced current into the mechanical motion of magnet B, until limited by damping forces.
