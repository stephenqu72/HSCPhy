### Front
How does a suspended dust particle move when a sound wave passes through the air?

### Back
- Key idea: Sound waves are longitudinal; particles oscillate parallel to the direction of wave propagation.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Longitudinal wave motion (back-and-forth oscillation).
- When to use it: To describe the medium's particle displacement in sound, seismic P-waves, or ultrasound.
- Common trap: Confusing longitudinal displacement with transverse wave motion (up-and-down oscillation).
