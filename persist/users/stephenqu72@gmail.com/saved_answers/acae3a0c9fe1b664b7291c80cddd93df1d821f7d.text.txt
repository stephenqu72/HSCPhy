### Answer

**(a) What is the force exerted on side AB?**

The magnetic force $F$ on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field is given by:
$$F = nBIL\sin(\theta)$$
Where:
- $n = 150$ (number of turns)
- $B = 0.060\text{ T}$
- $I = 1.5\text{ A}$
- $L = 0.12\text{ m}$ (length of side AB)
- $\theta$ is the angle between the current direction and the magnetic field.

In the diagram, the current in segment AB is moving horizontally to the right, and the magnetic field is directed vertically upward. Therefore, the angle $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

$$F = 150 \times 0.060 \times 1.5 \times 0.12 \times \sin(90^\circ)$$
$$F = 150 \times 0.060 \times 1.5 \times 0.12 \times 1 = 1.62\text{ N}$$

**The force exerted on side AB is $1.62\text{ N}$.**

**(b) Sketch a labelled graph of the force on side AB over one full rotation**

As the coil rotates, the angle $\theta$ between the current direction (which stays fixed relative to the coil frame) and the vertical magnetic field changes. The force is described by $F = F_{max} \sin(\theta)$, where $\theta$ is the angle the wire segment makes with the magnetic field lines. 

Starting from the position shown ($\theta = 90^\circ$):
1. At $0^\circ$ (start): $\theta = 90^\circ$, $F = 1.62\text{ N}$.
2. At $90^\circ$ rotation: The wire becomes parallel to the field, $\theta = 0^\circ$, $F = 0\text{ N}$.
3. At $180^\circ$ rotation: The wire has flipped direction relative to the field, $F = -1.62\text{ N}$.
4. At $270^\circ$ rotation: $F = 0\text{ N}$.
5. At $360^\circ$ rotation: Returns to $1.62\text{ N}$.

The graph should be a **cosine wave** (or a sine wave phase-shifted) starting at $+1.62\text{ N}$ at time $t=0$, crossing zero at the quarter and three-quarter marks, and reaching $-1.62\text{ N}$ at the half-rotation mark.

### How to solve it
1.  **Identify the variables:** Extract $n, B, I,$ and $L$ from the question text. Ensure units are converted to SI units (e.g., $12\text{ cm} = 0.12\text{ m}$).
2.  **Determine the angle:** Visualize the Right-Hand Rule. Since the wire AB is perpendicular to the field lines, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.
3.  **Calculate:** Apply the formula $F = nBIL$. Always remember the number of turns ($n$) is a multiplier for the total force on the coil side.
4.  **Graphing logic:** Understand that as the coil rotates, the orientation of the wire relative to the magnetic field changes according to the sine of the angle of rotation. Since the force is proportional to the perpendicular component of the field, it will oscillate between maximum and zero throughout the rotation.
