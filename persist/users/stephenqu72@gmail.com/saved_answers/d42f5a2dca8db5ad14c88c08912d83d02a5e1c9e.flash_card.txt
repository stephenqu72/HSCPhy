### Front
How does an increase in the acceleration due to gravity ($g$) affect the maximum height ($H$) and horizontal range ($R$) of a projectile launched at a constant velocity?

### Back
- Key idea: Both $H$ and $R$ are inversely proportional to $g$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $H = \frac{v^2 \sin^2 \theta}{2g}$ and $R = \frac{v^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$.
- When to use it: To compare projectile motion across different gravitational fields (e.g., Earth vs. Moon).
- Common trap: Assuming that gravity only affects the vertical component—it also restricts the time of flight, which proportionally reduces the horizontal range.
