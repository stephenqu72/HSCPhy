### Answer
The correct answer is **A. The speed of light**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must understand the core historical and physical significance of James Clerk Maxwell’s work in electromagnetism:

1.  **Analyze the Diagram:** The diagram depicts an electromagnetic wave consisting of oscillating perpendicular electric ($\vec{E}$) and magnetic ($\vec{B}$) fields. This is the fundamental model for all electromagnetic radiation.
2.  **Recall Maxwell's Contribution:** Maxwell synthesized existing laws of electricity and magnetism (Gauss’s laws, Ampère’s law, and Faraday’s law) into what are now known as "Maxwell’s Equations."
3.  **The Theoretical Discovery:** By manipulating these equations, Maxwell derived a wave equation for the electromagnetic field. He discovered that the theoretical speed ($v$) of these waves was determined by the permittivity of free space ($\epsilon_0$) and the permeability of free space ($\mu_0$):
    $$v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$$
4.  **Connecting to Light:** When Maxwell calculated this value using the known constants of his time, he found it to be approximately $3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$, which matched the experimental measurements for the speed of light. This led him to the groundbreaking conclusion that **light itself is an electromagnetic wave**.
5.  **Evaluating Other Options:**
    *   **B (Speed of sound):** Sound is a mechanical wave requiring a medium, which behaves fundamentally differently than electromagnetic waves.
    *   **C (Bat sonar):** Sonar relies on mechanical sound waves (ultrasound).
    *   **D (Ocean waves):** Ocean waves are mechanical surface waves governed by fluid dynamics and gravity, not Maxwell's electromagnetic equations.

By recognizing that Maxwell's model describes the propagation of electromagnetic disturbances, we can identify that it specifically allows for the calculation of the speed at which these disturbances travel through a vacuum, which is the speed of light.
