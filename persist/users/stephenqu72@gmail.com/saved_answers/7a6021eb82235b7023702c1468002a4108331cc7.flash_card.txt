### Front
In the "Rotor" amusement ride, what is the direction of the net force acting on a rider once the floor is removed, and why?

### Back
- Key idea: The net force is the centripetal force ($F_c$) required for uniform circular motion.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{net} = F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ directed toward the center.
- When to use it: To analyze horizontal circular motion where vertical forces (gravity and friction) are in equilibrium.
- Common trap: Assuming the normal force is the only force; the net force is strictly horizontal (perpendicular to the wall), while vertical forces cancel out.
