### Front
How does the relative motion between a wave source and an observer affect the observed frequency ($f_{obs}$) according to the Doppler Effect?

### Back
- Key idea: Motion towards compresses waves (higher $f_{obs}$); motion away stretches waves (lower $f_{obs}$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $f_{obs} = f_s (\frac{v}{v \mp v_s})$ (No fixed constant).
- When to use it: Any scenario involving moving wave sources or observers (sound, light/redshift).
- Common trap: Confusing the direction of motion—remember that a *higher* frequency always indicates the source is moving *towards* the observer.
