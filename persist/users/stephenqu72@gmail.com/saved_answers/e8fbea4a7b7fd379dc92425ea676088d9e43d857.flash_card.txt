### Front
How do you determine velocity from a displacement-time graph, and what does it reveal about the acceleration of a projectile?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The velocity is the gradient (slope) of a displacement-time graph; the acceleration is the gradient of the resulting velocity-time graph.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v = \frac{ds}{dt}$; for a projectile, $a = g \approx -9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ (constant).
- **When to use it:** When analyzing kinematics graphs to identify motion states (e.g., peak height, constant acceleration).
- **Common trap:** Confusing a curved displacement-time graph (constant acceleration) with a curved velocity-time graph (changing acceleration). Projectile velocity-time graphs must be straight lines.
