### Answer and Explanation

#### Part a)
**Procedure:**
1. Place the $1\text{ kg}$ wooden block at a specific measured radius $r$ from the center of the turntable using the ruler.
2. Gradually increase the angular velocity $\omega$ of the turntable until the block just begins to slide outward.
3. Record the value of $\omega$ displayed on the motor unit at the exact moment sliding occurs.
4. Repeat the experiment for at least five different values of $r$ to gather sufficient data.
5. Ensure the turntable surface is clean and the mass of the block remains constant throughout to ensure $r$ is the only independent variable.

**Risk Management:**
* **Risk:** The wooden block could fly off the turntable at high speeds, potentially striking the student or nearby equipment.
* **Management:** Place a protective screen or transparent guard around the perimeter of the turntable, or wear safety goggles during the operation to protect eyes from flying objects.

#### Part b)
**Given:**
* Mass $m = 1\text{ kg}$
* Frictional force $F_f = 5\text{ N}$
* Angular velocity $\omega = 2\pi\text{ rad s}^{-1}$

**Explanation:**
The force causing the block to move in a circular path is friction, which provides the centripetal force ($F_c$). The block will start to slide when the required centripetal force exceeds the maximum static frictional force. Therefore, at the threshold of sliding:
$$F_c = F_f$$
Since $F_c = mr\omega^2$, we equate them:
$$mr\omega^2 = 5$$
Substitute the known values into the equation:
$$1 \cdot r \cdot (2\pi)^2 = 5$$
$$r \cdot 4\pi^2 = 5$$
$$r = \frac{5}{4\pi^2}$$
Using $\pi^2 \approx 9.87$:
$$r \approx \frac{5}{39.48} \approx 0.1266\text{ m}$$

**Final Answer:**
The maximum distance the block can be placed is approximately **$0.127\text{ m}$** (or $12.7\text{ cm}$).
