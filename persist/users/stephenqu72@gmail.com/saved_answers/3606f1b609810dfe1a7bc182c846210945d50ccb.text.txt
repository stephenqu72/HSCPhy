### Part a: Calculate the electric field strength at point P due to $q_1$

**Formula:** The electric field strength $E$ created by a point charge is given by:
$$E = \frac{k |q|}{r^2}$$
Where:
*   $k \approx 8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N m}^2 \text{ C}^{-2}$
*   $q_1 = 2.5 \text{ mC} = 2.5 \times 10^{-3} \text{ C}$
*   $r = 0.2 \text{ m}$

**Calculation:**
$$E = \frac{(8.99 \times 10^9) \times (2.5 \times 10^{-3})}{(0.2)^2}$$
$$E = \frac{2.2475 \times 10^7}{0.04}$$
$$E = 5.62 \times 10^8 \text{ N C}^{-1}$$

### Part b: Calculate the magnitude of the force on $q_1$ due to $q_2$

**i. Calculation:**
We use Coulomb's Law, $F = \frac{k |q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$, where $r = 1.0 \text{ m}$:
*   $q_1 = 2.5 \times 10^{-3} \text{ C}$
*   $q_2 = 5.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ C}$

$$F = \frac{(8.99 \times 10^9) \times (2.5 \times 10^{-3}) \times (5.0 \times 10^{-3})}{(1.0)^2}$$
$$F = \frac{8.99 \times 10^9 \times 1.25 \times 10^{-5}}{1}$$
$$F = 1.12 \times 10^5 \text{ N}$$

**ii. Direction:**
Since both charges are positive, they exert a **repulsive force** on each other. Therefore, the force on $q_1$ due to $q_2$ acts to the **left** (away from $q_2$). You should draw an arrow starting from $q_1$ pointing to the left.

### Teacher's Tips for Success:
1.  **Unit Conversions:** Always check for prefixes. "mC" means milli-Coulombs ($10^{-3}$), not Micro-Coulombs ($\mu\text{C}$, which is $10^{-6}$). Missing this is the most common error in HSC exams.
2.  **Significant Figures:** Ensure your final answer matches the precision of the input data. Given the values have 2 significant figures, you could round to $5.6 \times 10^8 \text{ N C}^{-1}$ and $1.1 \times 10^5 \text{ N}$.
3.  **Vector Nature:** Always remember that electric field and force are vectors. If asked for direction, check the signs of the charges (like charges repel, opposite charges attract).
