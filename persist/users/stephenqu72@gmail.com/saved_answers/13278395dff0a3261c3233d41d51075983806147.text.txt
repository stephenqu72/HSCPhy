To solve this problem, we first extract the given values and convert them to standard SI units:
*   Number of turns ($N$) = $8$
*   Side length ($s$) = $12.00\text{ cm} = 0.12\text{ m}$
*   Area ($A$) = $s^2 = (0.12)^2 = 0.0144\text{ m}^2$
*   Current ($I$) = $3.00\text{ A}$
*   Magnetic field strength ($B$) = $2.50 \times 10^{-4}\text{ T}$ (Note: The prompt image says $10^4$, but in standard HSC physics contexts, this is a typo for a realistic magnetic field $10^{-4}\text{ T}$).
*   The angle provided is $40^\circ$ between the coil plane and the magnetic field.

---

### (a) Calculate the torque on the coil
The formula for torque is $\tau = NIAB\sin(\theta)$, where $\theta$ is the angle between the **normal to the coil** and the magnetic field. 
If the coil makes an angle of $40^\circ$ with the field, the angle with the normal is $\theta = 90^\circ - 40^\circ = 50^\circ$.

$$\tau = 8 \times 3.00 \times 0.0144 \times (2.50 \times 10^{-4}) \times \sin(50^\circ)$$
$$\tau = 0.000864 \times 0.766$$
$$\tau \approx 6.62 \times 10^{-4}\text{ Nm}$$

---

### (b) Calculate the magnetic flux
The magnetic flux $\Phi$ is given by $\Phi = BA\cos(\theta_{plane})$, where $\theta_{plane}$ is the angle between the field and the plane of the coil ($40^\circ$).

$$\Phi = (2.50 \times 10^{-4}) \times 0.0144 \times \cos(40^\circ)$$
$$\Phi = (3.6 \times 10^{-6}) \times 0.766$$
$$\Phi \approx 2.76 \times 10^{-6}\text{ Wb}$$

*(Note: Flux is generally defined for a single turn unless calculating total flux linkage. If total flux linkage is required, multiply the result by $N=8$.)*

---

### Explanation
1.  **Torque:** Always ensure you identify the correct angle. The torque formula uses the angle between the normal vector (perpendicular to the coil face) and the magnetic field. Subtracting the given $40^\circ$ from $90^\circ$ gives the correct angle to use in the sine function.
2.  **Flux:** The magnetic flux formula uses the angle between the magnetic field and the surface area vector's alignment with the field (which is effectively the angle the field makes with the surface of the coil). Using the cosine of the angle given ($40^\circ$) directly provides the flux through the cross-section.
