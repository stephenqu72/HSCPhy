To find the speed of the ball, we use the **Impulse-Momentum Theorem**, which states that the impulse applied to an object is equal to its change in momentum:
$$J = \Delta p = m(v_f - v_i)$$

### 1. Calculate the Impulse ($J$)
The impulse is equal to the area under the force-time ($F-t$) graph. The graph consists of two rectangles:
*   **Rectangle 1 (from $0.0$ to $0.2$ s):**
    $$Area_1 = \text{base} \times \text{height} = 0.2\,\text{s} \times 10\,\text{N} = 2.0\,\text{N}\cdot\text{s}$$
*   **Rectangle 2 (from $0.2$ to $0.5$ s):**
    $$Area_2 = \text{base} \times \text{height} = (0.5 - 0.2)\,\text{s} \times 30\,\text{N} = 0.3\,\text{s} \times 30\,\text{N} = 9.0\,\text{N}\cdot\text{s}$$

Total Impulse ($J$) = $2.0 + 9.0 = 11.0\,\text{N}\cdot\text{s}$.

### 2. Calculate the change in velocity
The ball has a mass of $250\,\text{g}$, which must be converted to SI units (kilograms):
$$m = 250\,\text{g} = 0.25\,\text{kg}$$

Given the ball starts at rest, initial velocity $v_i = 0\,\text{m}\cdot\text{s}^{-1}$. Using $J = m \Delta v$:
$$11.0 = 0.25 \times (v_f - 0)$$
$$v_f = \frac{11.0}{0.25}$$
$$v_f = 44\,\text{m}\cdot\text{s}^{-1}$$

Comparing this result to the given options:
(A) $10\,\text{m}\cdot\text{s}^{-1}$
(B) $40\,\text{m}\cdot\text{s}^{-1}$
(C) $44\,\text{m}\cdot\text{s}^{-1}$
(D) $80\,\text{m}\cdot\text{s}^{-1}$

The correct option is **(C)**.
