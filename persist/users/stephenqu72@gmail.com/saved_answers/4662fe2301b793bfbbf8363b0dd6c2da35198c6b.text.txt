### Answer

J. J. Thomson's 1897 experiment involved the use of a cathode ray tube (CRT) to investigate the nature of cathode rays. He applied electric and magnetic fields to a beam of cathode rays. By balancing the electric force ($F_E = qE$) and the magnetic force ($F_B = qvB$), he was able to determine the velocity of the particles. Subsequently, by measuring the deflection caused by these fields, he calculated the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$) of the particles.

**Importance to Physics:**
1.  **Discovery of the Electron:** Thomson concluded that cathode rays were composed of negatively charged particles (electrons) that were much smaller than the hydrogen atom, debunking the idea that atoms were indivisible.
2.  **Revision of Atomic Models:** His discovery forced a move away from Dalton’s "billiard ball" model of the atom, leading to his proposal of the "plum pudding" model, where electrons were embedded in a positively charged sphere.
3.  **Foundation for Modern Particle Physics:** By proving that these subatomic particles existed and were universal components of all matter, he provided the experimental foundation for the development of modern quantum mechanics and electronics.
