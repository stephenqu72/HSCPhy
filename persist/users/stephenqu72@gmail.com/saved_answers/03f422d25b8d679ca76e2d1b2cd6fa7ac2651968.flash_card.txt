### Front
In a stable circular orbit, why does a satellite’s momentum change while its kinetic energy remains constant?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Momentum is a vector; Kinetic Energy is a scalar.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$ (vector) vs $E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$ (scalar). No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** To distinguish between vector and scalar properties in uniform circular motion.
- **Common trap:** Assuming constant speed implies constant momentum. Momentum is *never* constant in uniform circular motion because the direction of the velocity vector is constantly changing.
