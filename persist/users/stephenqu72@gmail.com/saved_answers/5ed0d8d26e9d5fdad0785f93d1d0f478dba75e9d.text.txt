To answer this question effectively for an HSC Physics exam, you must contrast the particle (Newton) and wave (Huygens) models regarding the speed of light in a denser medium.

### Answer
Newton’s particle model and Huygens’s wave model provide conflicting explanations for the refraction of light:

*   **Newton’s Particle Model:** Newton proposed that light consists of corpuscles (particles). To explain why light bends toward the normal when entering a denser medium (glass), he argued that the glass exerts an attractive force on the light particles, increasing their velocity component perpendicular to the surface. Consequently, this model **incorrectly predicts that light travels faster in a denser medium** (glass) than in a rarer medium (air).
*   **Huygens’s Wave Model:** Huygens proposed that light behaves as a wave. Using his principle, he explained refraction by suggesting that as the wavefront enters the denser medium, the part of the wavefront inside the glass slows down, while the part still in the air continues at the original speed. This change in speed causes the direction of the wavefront (and thus the ray) to change, bending it toward the normal. This model **correctly predicts that light travels slower in a denser medium** than in air.
