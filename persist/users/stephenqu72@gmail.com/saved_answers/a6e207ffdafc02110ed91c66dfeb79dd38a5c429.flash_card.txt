### Front
How do you determine the final velocity of an object using a force-time ($F$-$t$) graph and the Impulse-Momentum Theorem?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The impulse ($J$) is equal to the area under the $F$-$t$ graph and is also equal to the change in momentum ($\Delta p$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $J = \Delta p = m(v_f - v_i) = \text{Area under } F\text{-}t \text{ graph}$
- **When to use it:** When a force varies over time (e.g., a graph with multiple segments) and you need to find changes in velocity or momentum.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert mass from grams (g) to kilograms (kg), or failing to calculate the total area by summing all individual shapes under the graph.
