### Front
How does the initial vertical velocity component ($u_y$) affect the total time of flight ($t$) for a projectile launched and landing at the same height?

### Back
- Key idea: Time of flight is directly proportional to the initial vertical velocity component ($u_y$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $t = \frac{2u_y}{g}$
- When to use it: To calculate or compare the duration of projectile motion in a vacuum.
- Common trap: Assuming the horizontal velocity component ($u_x$) affects the time of flight; it only affects the horizontal range.
