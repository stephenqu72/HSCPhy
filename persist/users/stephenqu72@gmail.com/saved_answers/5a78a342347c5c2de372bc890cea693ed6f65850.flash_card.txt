### Front
How do you calculate the centripetal force acting on an object moving in a circle, and what is the primary unit conversion requirement?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Centripetal force is the net force required to maintain circular motion, directed toward the centre of the circle.
- **Formula / law:** $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- **When to use it:** When an object (e.g., a car on a bend) moves with a constant speed in a circular path.
- **Common trap:** Failing to convert velocity from $\text{km/h}$ to $\text{m/s}$ (divide by $3.6$) before substituting into the formula.
