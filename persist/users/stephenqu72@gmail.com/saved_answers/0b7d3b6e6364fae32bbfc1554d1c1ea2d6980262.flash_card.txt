### Front
In Uniform Circular Motion, what are the characteristics of the centripetal acceleration vector?

### Back
- Key idea: The magnitude remains constant, but the direction constantly changes.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$ (Directed toward the center).
- When to use it: Any object moving in a circular path at a constant speed.
- Common trap: Assuming acceleration is zero because the speed is constant; remember that velocity is a vector, so a changing direction means acceleration exists.
