To solve this problem, we must remember that the distance $r$ used in orbital mechanics formulas is measured from the **center of the Earth**, not the surface.

**1. Determine the orbital radii:**
*   Satellite A is at altitude $R$, so its distance from the center is $r_A = R_{Earth} + R = 2R$.
*   Satellite B is at altitude $3R$, so its distance from the center is $r_B = R_{Earth} + 3R = 4R$.

**2. Compare Gravitational Potential Energy ($U$):**
The formula for gravitational potential energy is $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$.
*   $U_A = -\frac{GMm}{2R}$
*   $U_B = -\frac{GMm}{4R}$
To compare them, note that $U_A = \frac{1}{2} \cdot (-\frac{GMm}{R})$ and $U_B = \frac{1}{4} \cdot (-\frac{GMm}{R})$.
Dividing $U_A$ by $U_B$:
$$\frac{U_A}{U_B} = \frac{-GMm / 2R}{-GMm / 4R} = \frac{4}{2} = 2$$
Thus, $U_A = 2U_B$.

**3. Compare Orbital Velocity ($v$):**
The formula for orbital velocity is $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$.
*   $v_A = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{2R}}$
*   $v_B = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{4R}}$
Dividing $v_A$ by $v_B$:
$$\frac{v_A}{v_B} = \frac{\sqrt{\frac{GM}{2R}}}{\sqrt{\frac{GM}{4R}}} = \sqrt{\frac{4R}{2R}} = \sqrt{2}$$
Thus, $v_A = \sqrt{2}v_B$.

Comparing these results to the given options, we find that $U_A = 2U_B$ and $v_A = \sqrt{2}v_B$, which corresponds to **Option D**.
