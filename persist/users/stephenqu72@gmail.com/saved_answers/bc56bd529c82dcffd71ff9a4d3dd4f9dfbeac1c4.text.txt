To determine the correct decay equation for Polonium-210 undergoing alpha decay, we must apply the laws of conservation of mass number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$).

### Explanation

1.  **Understand Alpha Decay:** An alpha particle is a helium nucleus, which contains 2 protons and 2 neutrons. It is represented as ${}_{2}^{4}\text{He}$. When a nucleus undergoes alpha decay, it loses 4 from its mass number and 2 from its atomic number.
2.  **Analyze the Parent Nucleus:** The parent nucleus is Polonium-210, represented as ${}_{84}^{210}\text{Po}$.
    *   Mass number ($A$) = $210$
    *   Atomic number ($Z$) = $84$
3.  **Apply Conservation Laws:** The sum of the mass numbers and the sum of the atomic numbers must be the same on both sides of the equation.
    *   **Mass Number:** The daughter nucleus ($X$) must have a mass number of $210 - 4 = 206$.
    *   **Atomic Number:** The daughter nucleus ($X$) must have an atomic number of $84 - 2 = 82$.
4.  **Evaluate the Options:** We are looking for an equation that follows the form:
    $${}_{84}^{210}\text{Po} \rightarrow {}_{82}^{206}X + {}_{2}^{4}\text{He}$$
    *   Comparing this to the provided options, option **C** matches these values perfectly.

**Conclusion:** The correct decay equation is:
$${}_{84}^{210}\text{Po} \rightarrow {}_{82}^{206}X + {}_{2}^{4}\text{He}$$

The correct answer is **C**.
