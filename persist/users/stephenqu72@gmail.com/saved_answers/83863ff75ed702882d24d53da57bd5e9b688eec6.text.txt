To identify the correct diagram for a DC generator, we must analyze the components and the function of each option:

1.  **Fundamental Difference between AC and DC Generators:**
    *   An **AC generator** (alternator) uses **slip rings**, which maintain a continuous connection between the rotating coil and the external circuit. This results in the current reversing direction every half-turn (producing Alternating Current).
    *   A **DC generator** uses a **split-ring commutator**. This device reverses the connections of the coil to the external circuit every half-turn. Because the current in the coil naturally reverses direction every half-turn, the action of the split-ring commutator ensures that the current flowing into the external circuit remains in a single direction (producing Direct Current).

### Summary for your HSC Exam:
Always look for the **split-ring commutator** (a ring split into two halves) when identifying a DC device. If it is a motor, it will be an "input" of current; if it is a generator, it will be an "output" of current.
