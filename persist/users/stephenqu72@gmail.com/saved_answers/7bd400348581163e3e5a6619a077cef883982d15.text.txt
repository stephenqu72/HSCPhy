### Answer

To determine the wavelength of the laser, Mr. Warner should follow this procedure:

1.  **Set up the apparatus:** Place the laser so that it shines directly onto a wall or screen. Position the diffraction grating in the path of the laser beam, ensuring the laser hits the grating perpendicularly.
2.  **Align components:** Use the tape measure to ensure the diffraction grating is at a known distance ($D$) from the screen.
3.  **Identify diffraction patterns:** The laser light will diffract, creating a series of bright spots on the screen (a central maximum and various higher-order maxima).
4.  **Take measurements:** Use the tape measure to record the distance from the central maximum (the point where the laser would hit if the grating were absent) to the first-order bright spot ($x_1$) on either side.
5.  **Calculate the grating spacing:** Convert the grating density (lines per cm) into the slit separation distance ($d$) using the formula $d = \frac{1}{\text{lines per unit length}}$.
6.  **Geometry:** Use the distance between the grating and the screen ($D$) and the distance from the central spot to the order $n$ spot ($x_n$) to determine the angle of diffraction ($\theta$) using trigonometry, specifically $\tan(\theta) = \frac{x_n}{D}$.
