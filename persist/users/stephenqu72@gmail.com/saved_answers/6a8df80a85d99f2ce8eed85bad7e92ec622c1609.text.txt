### Answer and Explanation

#### Part a. Calculate the power drawn by the lighting system.

**Answer:** $12\text{ W}$

**Explanation:**
To calculate the power ($P$) drawn by the system, we use the formula involving voltage ($V$) and resistance ($R$):
$$P = \frac{V^2}{R}$$
Given $V = 12\text{ V}$ and $R = 12\text{ }\Omega$:
$$P = \frac{12^2}{12} = \frac{144}{12} = 12\text{ W}$$

---

#### Part b. Give one possible reason for this and support your answer with calculations.

**Answer:**
The extension lead has internal resistance, which causes a "voltage drop" across the wire, leaving less than $12\text{ V}$ available for the lights.

**Explanation:**
1. First, find the current ($I$) flowing through the circuit. The total resistance of the circuit is the sum of the extension lead ($3\text{ }\Omega$) and the lighting system ($12\text{ }\Omega$):
   $$R_{\text{total}} = 3 + 12 = 15\text{ }\Omega$$
2. Using Ohm's Law ($I = V/R$) with the source voltage of $12\text{ V}$:
   $$I = \frac{12}{15} = 0.8\text{ A}$$
3. Calculate the voltage drop ($V_{\text{drop}}$) across the extension lead:
   $$V_{\text{drop}} = I \times R_{\text{lead}} = 0.8\text{ A} \times 3\text{ }\Omega = 2.4\text{ V}$$
4. The voltage actually delivered to the lighting system is:
   $$V_{\text{lights}} = 12\text{ V} - 2.4\text{ V} = 9.6\text{ V}$$
Since the lights are receiving $9.6\text{ V}$ instead of the required $12\text{ V}$, their power output is significantly reduced ($P = \frac{9.6^2}{12} = 7.68\text{ W}$), making them appear dimmer.

#### Part c. Using the same equipment, what changes could the home owner make to improve the brightness of the lights?

**Answer:**
The homeowner should move the transformer closer to the entertainment area.

**Explanation:**
The power loss and voltage drop are caused by the resistance of the $200\text{ m}$ cable. Resistance is directly proportional to length ($R = \rho L/A$). By shortening the length of the extension lead between the transformer and the lights, the resistance of the lead decreases. This reduces the voltage drop across the wire, ensuring that a voltage closer to the required $12\text{ V}$ reaches the lighting system, thereby increasing brightness.
