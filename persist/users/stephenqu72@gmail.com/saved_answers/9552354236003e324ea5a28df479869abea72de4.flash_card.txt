### Front
Derive the escape velocity $v_e$ of an object in a circular orbit of radius $r$ and period $T$ expressed solely in terms of $r$ and $T$.

### Back
- Key idea: Combine Kepler’s Third Law with the escape velocity formula to eliminate the mass ($M$) and gravitational constant ($G$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v_e = \frac{2\pi r\sqrt{2}}{T}$
- When to use it: When you need to calculate escape velocity from an orbiting body but the mass ($M$) of the central body is unknown.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert the orbital period ($T$) into SI units (seconds) or neglecting the $\sqrt{2}$ factor derived from substituting $GM = \frac{4\pi^2r^3}{T^2}$.
