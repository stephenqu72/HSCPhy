### Front
Why should you measure the time for multiple oscillations ($N$) rather than a single oscillation when calculating the period of a pendulum?

### Back
- Key idea: Reduction of relative percentage uncertainty caused by human reaction time.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $T = \frac{\text{total time}}{N}$
- When to use it: Any timing experiment involving high-frequency or rapid events where manual reaction time is a significant source of error.
- Common trap: Thinking it reduces physical errors like air resistance or parallax error; it only minimizes the impact of human reaction time.
