To excel in HSC Physics, you must clearly distinguish between the **photoelectric effect** (vacuum photocell) and the **photovoltaic effect** (semiconductor solar cell).

### (a) How does light contribute to the flow of current in each of these two cells?

**Answer:**
*   **Vacuum Photocell:** Light incident on the cathode (A) transfers energy to electrons. If the frequency of the light is above the threshold frequency of the metal, the photoelectric effect occurs, and electrons are ejected from the surface of the metal into the vacuum. These photoelectrons are then accelerated toward the anode (B) by the external voltage supply, completing the circuit and creating a current.
*   **Solar Cell:** Light incident on the $p-n$ junction provides sufficient energy to excite electrons from the valence band to the conduction band, creating electron-hole pairs. Due to the built-in electric field at the depletion region of the $p-n$ junction, these charge carriers are separated—electrons are swept toward the $n$-type side and holes toward the $p$-type side. This creates a potential difference that drives a current through the external load.

**How to solve:** 
1. Identify the physical mechanism: For the vacuum photocell, recall Einstein's Photoelectric Effect. For the solar cell, recall semiconductor band theory.
2. Structure your answer: Dedicate specific sentences to the "Photoelectric Effect" (vacuum) and the "Photovoltaic Effect" (semiconductor).
3. Use keywords: Mention "threshold frequency," "ejection," "electron-hole pairs," and "built-in electric field."

### (b) Explain how ONE advance in scientific understanding and ONE advance in technology have led to the use of semiconductors.

**Answer:**
*   **Scientific Advance:** The development of **Quantum Mechanics** and **Band Theory** allowed scientists to explain the electronic properties of materials. Understanding that electrons exist in energy bands (valence and conduction) and that impurities (doping) could create intermediate energy levels allowed for the deliberate manipulation of electrical conductivity.
*   **Technological Advance:** The development of **zone refining** (or Czochralski process) enabled the production of ultra-pure silicon crystals. This purity is essential because even minute amounts of unwanted impurities would interfere with the controlled doping process required to create reliable $p-n$ junctions for transistors and solar cells.

**How to solve:**
1. Focus on the distinction between *theory* (science) and *method/manufacturing* (technology).
2. The scientific advance should refer to the fundamental understanding of matter (Band Theory).
3. The technological advance should refer to the ability to manipulate matter at scale (purification/doping).
