### Explanation
This process is an alpha decay, which is a spontaneous nuclear reaction. We can analyze it using Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle, $E = mc^2$.

1.  **Total Mass:** In any spontaneous radioactive decay, the mass of the products is slightly less than the mass of the parent nucleus. This "missing mass" is known as the **mass defect** ($\Delta m$), and it is converted into kinetic energy and gamma radiation. Therefore, the total mass of the system **decreases**.
2.  **Total Binding Energy:** The binding energy of a nucleus is the energy required to disassemble it into its constituent protons and neutrons. In nuclear reactions, a more stable product nucleus has a higher binding energy per nucleon than the parent nucleus. Because the products (Lead-206 and Helium-4) are more tightly bound (more stable) than the parent nucleus (Polonium-210), the total binding energy of the system **increases**.

Essentially, the decrease in mass ($\Delta m$) is released as energy, and the resulting configuration of nucleons is more stable, corresponding to a higher total binding energy.
