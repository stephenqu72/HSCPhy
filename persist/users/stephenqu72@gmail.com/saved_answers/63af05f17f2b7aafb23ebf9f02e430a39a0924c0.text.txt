### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) Main sequence, red giant, white dwarf, black dwarf.**

### Explanation
To solve this, we categorize stars into two main pathways based on their mass compared to our Sun:

1.  **Low to Medium Mass Stars (like the Sun):** These stars spend the majority of their lives on the **Main sequence** burning hydrogen into helium. Once they exhaust their core hydrogen, they expand to become a **Red giant**. After the red giant phase, the star sheds its outer layers to form a planetary nebula, leaving behind a hot, dense core known as a **White dwarf**. Over a vast amount of time, this white dwarf radiates away its remaining heat until it cools down completely to become a theoretical **Black dwarf**.
2.  **High Mass Stars:** These stars follow a different evolutionary track. They become **Red supergiants** and eventually undergo a supernova explosion, resulting in either a neutron star or a black hole.

By identifying the star as being the "same size as the Sun," we immediately rule out any options containing "red supergiant." This eliminates (A) and (C). Between (B) and (D), we look at the final cooling stages: the star must become a white dwarf *before* it can cool down to become a black dwarf, making (D) the only logically correct sequence.
