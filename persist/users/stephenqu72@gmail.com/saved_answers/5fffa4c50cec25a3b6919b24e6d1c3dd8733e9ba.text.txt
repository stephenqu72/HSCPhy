To solve this problem, we need to apply our knowledge of the Bohr model of the hydrogen atom and the Rydberg formula.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) $656.3 \text{ nm}$**.

### Explanation
1.  **Identify the physics:** The Balmer series represents electronic transitions in a hydrogen atom where an electron falls from a higher energy level ($n > 2$) to the $n=2$ energy level. The wavelength $\lambda$ of the emitted photon is given by the Rydberg formula:
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$
    Where $R_H \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \text{ m}^{-1}$, $n_f = 2$, and $n_i = 3, 4, 5, \dots$

2.  **Determine the longest wavelength:** In physics, wavelength and energy are inversely proportional ($E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$). The longest wavelength corresponds to the transition with the **smallest energy change**. This occurs when the electron falls from the closest possible energy level to the final level, which is from $n_i = 3$ to $n_f = 2$.

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \times \left( \frac{1}{2^2} - \frac{1}{3^2} \right)$$
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \times \left( \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{9} \right)$$
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \times \left( \frac{5}{36} \right)$$
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} \approx 1,523,611 \text{ m}^{-1}$$
    $$\lambda \approx 6.563 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m} = 656.3 \text{ nm}$$

4.  **Visual Verification:** In the visible spectrum, red light has the longest wavelength ($700\text{--}635 \text{ nm}$) and violet light has the shortest. The diagram clearly shows a red line, which corresponds to this $n=3 \rightarrow n=2$ transition, confirming $656.3 \text{ nm}$ is the logical choice. Note that options (A) and (B) have units of meters, which would represent wavelengths the size of buildings, making them physically impossible for visible light.
