### Answer

**(a)** The pattern shown in Diagram 2 (known as Poisson's spot or Arago's spot) is produced by the **diffraction** of light waves around the edges of the small spherical object. When the wavefronts encounter the sphere, they are diffracted at the edges. Because the light is coherent, the diffracted waves travel equal distances to the center point behind the sphere, resulting in **constructive interference**, which creates the bright central spot. The surrounding concentric rings are created by alternating constructive and destructive interference of the diffracted wavefronts.

**(b)** This experiment demonstrates that light exhibits **wave-like properties**, specifically the ability to diffract and interfere. It provides evidence against the corpuscular (particle) theory of light, which would suggest that the light should be completely blocked by the sphere, leaving only a geometric shadow.
