### Front
How does the radius of a planet change if its mass increases by a factor of 4, assuming gravity ($g$) remains constant?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational field strength is directly proportional to mass and inversely proportional to the square of the radius ($g \propto M/r^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- When to use it: When comparing gravitational fields of different planets or calculating surface gravity based on planetary dimensions.
- Common trap: Forgetting to take the square root of the radius relationship; if $M$ increases by 4, $r$ must increase by $\sqrt{4} = 2$ to keep $g$ constant.
