### Answer and Explanation

To solve this problem, we first manipulate the given formula:
$$hf = q(V_0 + k)$$
Since $f = \frac{c}{\lambda}$ (where $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$), we substitute this into the equation:
$$h\left(\frac{c}{\lambda}\right) = qV_0 + qk$$
Rearranging to isolate $V_0$ (to fit the $y = mx + c$ format for a graph of $V_0$ vs $\frac{1}{\lambda}$):
$$V_0 = \left(\frac{hc}{q}\right)\frac{1}{\lambda} - k$$
This shows that the gradient of the graph, $m = \frac{hc}{q}$. Therefore, $h = \frac{mq}{c}$.
