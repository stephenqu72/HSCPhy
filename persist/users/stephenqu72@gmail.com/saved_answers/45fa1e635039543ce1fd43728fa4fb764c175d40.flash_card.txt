### Front
How do you calculate the centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) of an object in uniform circular motion when given only the radius ($r$) and period ($T$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Centripetal acceleration is the inward acceleration required to keep an object moving in a circle.
- **Formula:** $a_c = \frac{4\pi^2r}{T^2}$
- **When to use it:** When the time for one complete revolution (period) and the orbital radius are known.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert the given **diameter** to **radius** ($r = d/2$) or using the wrong units for time.
