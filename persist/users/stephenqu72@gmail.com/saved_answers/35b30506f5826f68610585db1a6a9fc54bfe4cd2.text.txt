To solve this problem, we apply the principles of electromagnetism and vector resolution.

### (a) Calculation of the Magnetic Force
The magnitude of the magnetic force $F_B$ on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field is given by:
$$F_B = B I L \sin(\theta)$$
where:
- $B = 4.80 \times 10^{-3} \text{ T}$
- $I = 5.0 \text{ A}$
- $L = 2.0 \text{ m}$
- $\theta$ is the angle between the current and the magnetic field. Since the magnetic field is vertical and the rod is horizontal, they are perpendicular, so $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

$$F_B = (4.80 \times 10^{-3}) \times 5.0 \times 2.0 = 0.048 \text{ N}$$
The force acts horizontally (due to the right-hand rule) perpendicular to the magnetic field and the current.

### (b) Procedure to find the mass
When gravity is included, the rod is held in equilibrium on the incline by the magnetic force, the normal force from the rails, and the gravitational force ($mg$).

1. **Setup:** Place the rod on the rails and connect the power source.
2. **Method:** Adjust the current $I$ until the rod remains stationary (it is neither sliding up nor down the rails).
3. **Physics Principle:** At equilibrium, the components of forces acting parallel to the incline must sum to zero. The magnetic force $F_B$ is horizontal. Its component parallel to the incline is $F_B \cos(30^\circ)$. The component of gravity acting parallel to the incline is $mg \sin(30^\circ)$.
4. **Equation:**
   $$F_B \cos(30^\circ) = mg \sin(30^\circ)$$
   $$mg = F_B \frac{\cos(30^\circ)}{\sin(30^\circ)} = F_B \cot(30^\circ)$$
5. **Calculation:** Since $F_B = BIL$, we have:
   $$m = \frac{BIL \cos(30^\circ)}{g \sin(30^\circ)}$$
   By measuring the current $I$ required to maintain equilibrium, the mass $m$ can be calculated using this formula.
