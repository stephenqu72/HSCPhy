### Explanation

To understand why nuclear fission releases energy, we look to Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle, defined by the equation:
$$E = \Delta m c^2$$

In a nuclear fission reaction, a heavy nucleus (such as Uranium-235) splits into smaller, more stable daughter nuclei. If you carefully measure the total mass of the reactants (the heavy nucleus and the incident neutron) and compare it to the total mass of the products (the daughter nuclei and the released neutrons), you will find that the mass of the products is slightly **less** than the mass of the reactants.

This missing mass is known as the **mass defect** ($\Delta m$). This "lost" mass has been converted into energy according to the equation above. Since the speed of light ($c$) is a very large number, even a tiny mass defect results in a tremendous release of energy.

*   **A is incorrect:** Refining uranium is an industrial/chemical process, not the source of nuclear energy.
*   **B is incorrect:** If the product mass were greater, energy would be consumed rather than released.
*   **D is incorrect:** Mass is not conserved in nuclear reactions; it is converted into energy (or vice versa).
