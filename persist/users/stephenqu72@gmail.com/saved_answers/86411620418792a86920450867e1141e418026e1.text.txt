To calculate the mass defect, we must compare the sum of the individual masses of the protons and neutrons to the actual measured mass of the nucleus.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

1.  **Identify the masses of subatomic particles:**
    Using standard physical constants:
    *   Mass of a proton ($m_p$) $\approx 1.007276\text{ u}$
    *   Mass of a neutron ($m_n$) $\approx 1.008665\text{ u}$

2.  **Calculate the theoretical mass of the nucleons:**
    Carbon-12 consists of 6 protons and 6 neutrons.
    $$\text{Theoretical mass} = (6 \times m_p) + (6 \times m_n)$$
    $$\text{Theoretical mass} = (6 \times 1.007276\text{ u}) + (6 \times 1.008665\text{ u})$$
    $$\text{Theoretical mass} = 6.043656\text{ u} + 6.051990\text{ u} = 12.095646\text{ u}$$

3.  **Calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$):**
    The mass defect is the difference between the sum of the masses of the individual nucleons and the actual mass of the atom ($12.000\text{ u}$):
    $$\Delta m = \text{Theoretical mass} - \text{Actual mass}$$
    $$\Delta m = 12.095646\text{ u} - 12.000\text{ u}$$
    $$\Delta m = 0.095646\text{ u}$$

**Answer:**
The mass defect of one carbon-12 atom is approximately **$0.0956\text{ u}$**.
