### Answer

Newton proposed a **corpuscular (particle) model**, while Huygens proposed a **wave model**. Their models competed for over a century until experimental evidence shifted the scientific consensus.

**1. Evidence supporting Newton’s Corpuscular Model:**
*   **Rectilinear Propagation:** The fact that light travels in straight lines and creates sharp shadows is easily explained by particles moving at high speeds in straight paths.
*   **Reflection:** Particles striking a surface and bouncing off (like a billiard ball) explained the laws of reflection perfectly.
*   **Refraction (Initially):** Newton argued that light particles speed up when entering a denser medium (due to an attractive force), which correctly explained the bending of light toward the normal.

**2. Evidence supporting Huygens’ Wave Model:**
*   **Diffraction:** Francesco Grimaldi observed that light spreads when passing through narrow slits. Particle theory could not explain this, whereas wave theory could (as waves "bend" around corners).
*   **Interference (Thomas Young’s Double Slit Experiment, 1801):** This was the "death blow" to the pure corpuscular theory. Young demonstrated that light passing through two narrow slits creates an interference pattern of bright and dark fringes. This phenomenon can only occur through the superposition of waves.
*   **Refraction (Foucault’s Experiment, 1850):** Leon Foucault measured the speed of light in water and found it to be slower than in air. This contradicted Newton’s prediction, providing definitive evidence that light behaves as a wave, as waves slow down in denser media.
