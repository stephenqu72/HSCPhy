### Answer
The correct answer is **C) V/1.4**.

---

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we use the formula for the orbital velocity ($v$) of a satellite in a circular orbit around a central body:

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

Where:
- $G$ is the gravitational constant
- $M$ is the mass of the Earth
- $r$ is the radius of the orbit from the centre of the Earth

**Step 1: Define the velocity of Aries**
For satellite Aries at distance $R$:
$$V_{Aries} = V = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{R}}$$

**Step 2: Define the velocity of Gemini**
For satellite Gemini at distance $2R$:
$$V_{Gemini} = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{2R}}$$

**Step 3: Relate Gemini's velocity to V**
We can factor out the constant terms from Gemini's expression:
$$V_{Gemini} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{2}} \times \sqrt{\frac{GM}{R}}$$
$$V_{Gemini} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \times V$$

Since $\sqrt{2} \approx 1.414$, we can approximate this as:
$$V_{Gemini} \approx \frac{V}{1.4}$$

Thus, Gemini's orbital velocity is $V$ divided by approximately $1.4$.
