To solve this problem, we must apply the principle of **conservation of mass-energy** and the relationship between the energy of a photon and its frequency.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. Determine the total energy available:**
When a helium nucleus annihilates with an anti-helium nucleus, the total mass-energy of both particles is converted into energy. Since each nucleus has a rest energy of $3728\text{ MeV}$, the total energy $E_{total}$ released is:
$$E_{total} = 3728\text{ MeV} + 3728\text{ MeV} = 7456\text{ MeV}$$

**2. Convert energy to Joules:**
To calculate frequency, we need the energy in Joules. Use the conversion factor $1\text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$.
$$E_{total} = 7456 \times 10^6 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$$
$$E_{total} \approx 1.1945 \times 10^{-9}\text{ J}$$

**3. Determine the energy of one photon:**
The problem states that two photons are formed. Assuming the energy is split equally to conserve momentum in the centre-of-mass frame, the energy of a single photon $E_{photon}$ is:
$$E_{photon} = \frac{E_{total}}{2} = \frac{1.1945 \times 10^{-9}\text{ J}}{2} \approx 5.9725 \times 10^{-10}\text{ J}$$

**4. Calculate the frequency:**
Using the Planck-Einstein relation $E = hf$, where $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$:
$$f = \frac{E_{photon}}{h}$$
$$f = \frac{5.9725 \times 10^{-10}}{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}$$
$$f \approx 9.01 \times 10^{23}\text{ Hz}$$

### Final Answer:
The maximum frequency of the photons produced is approximately **$9.01 \times 10^{23}\text{ Hz}$**.
