### Front
How do you calculate the mass ($M$) of a central body using an orbiting satellite's data?

### Back
- Key idea: Use Kepler’s Third Law derived from equating gravitational force to centripetal force.
- Formula: $M = \frac{4\pi^2r^3}{GT^2}$
- When to use it: When given the orbital radius ($r$) and period ($T$) of a satellite to determine the mass of the central planet/star.
- Common trap: Forgetting to cube the radius ($r^3$) or square the period ($T^2$), or failing to ensure units are SI (meters and seconds).
