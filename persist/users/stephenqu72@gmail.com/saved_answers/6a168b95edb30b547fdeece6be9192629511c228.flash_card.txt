### Front
How does the mass of a system change during an exothermic process according to special relativity?

### Back
- Key idea: Energy released by a system results in a corresponding decrease in its total mass.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\Delta m = \frac{\Delta E}{c^2}$ (Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence).
- When to use it: To calculate the mass defect in nuclear reactions or quantify mass changes during energy release.
- Common trap: Assuming mass is only conserved in chemical reactions; at the subatomic level, mass and energy are interchangeable.
