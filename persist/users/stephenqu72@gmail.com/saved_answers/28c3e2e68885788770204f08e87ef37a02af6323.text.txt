To solve this projectile motion problem, we first break the initial velocity $u = 25\text{ m/s}$ into its vertical and horizontal components. Let the upward direction be positive.

- Initial vertical velocity: $u_y = 25 \sin(32^\circ) \approx 13.248\text{ m/s}$
- Initial horizontal velocity: $u_x = 25 \cos(32^\circ) \approx 21.201\text{ m/s}$
- Vertical displacement: $\Delta y = -450\text{ m}$ (since it lands below the launch point)
- Acceleration due to gravity: $g = -9.8\text{ m/s}^2$

### (a) Calculate the total time of flight
We use the displacement formula for vertical motion: 
$$\Delta y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$$
$$-450 = 13.248t - 4.9t^2$$
Rearranging into a standard quadratic form $4.9t^2 - 13.248t - 450 = 0$.

Using the quadratic formula $t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$$t = \frac{13.248 \pm \sqrt{(-13.248)^2 - 4(4.9)(-450)}}{9.8}$$
$$t = \frac{13.248 \pm \sqrt{175.51 + 8820}}{9.8}$$
$$t = \frac{13.248 \pm 94.84}{9.8}$$
Since time must be positive, we take the plus sign:
$$t = \frac{108.088}{9.8} \approx 11.03\text{ seconds}$$

### (b) Calculate the final range
The range is the horizontal distance covered during the time of flight. Since horizontal velocity $u_x$ is constant (ignoring air resistance):
$$\text{Range} = u_x \times t$$
$$\text{Range} = 21.201 \times 11.03$$
$$\text{Range} \approx 233.85\text{ metres}$$

---

### Teacher's Tips for Success:
1. **Define signs:** Always state which direction is positive (e.g., up is positive). This makes the vertical displacement $-450\text{ m}$ and gravity $-9.8\text{ m/s}^2$ logical.
2. **Component analysis:** Always split your velocity into $x$ and $y$ components immediately.
3. **Quadratic awareness:** When the final vertical position is not the same as the start, you will almost always need the quadratic formula to solve for time.
