To solve this problem, we must apply Wien’s Displacement Law.

### Answer
The correct graph is **A**.

### Explanation
Wien’s Displacement Law describes the relationship between the temperature of a black body ($T$) and the wavelength at which it emits the maximum intensity of radiation ($\lambda_{max}$):
$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$
where $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant. This equation shows that $\lambda_{max}$ is inversely proportional to $T$.

However, the question asks for the relationship between temperature ($T$) and the **frequency** ($f$) at which maximum intensity is emitted. We know the relationship between frequency and wavelength for electromagnetic waves is:
$$c = f \lambda \implies \lambda = \frac{c}{f}$$
Substituting this into Wien’s Displacement Law:
$$\frac{c}{f} = \frac{b}{T}$$
Rearranging to solve for $f$:
$$f = \left( \frac{c}{b} \right) T$$
Since $c$ (the speed of light) and $b$ (Wien’s constant) are both constants, the term $\frac{c}{b}$ is also a constant. Therefore, the relationship is:
$$f \propto T$$
This represents a direct linear relationship, which corresponds to a straight line passing through the origin. Among the options provided, graph **A** correctly depicts this linear, directly proportional relationship.
