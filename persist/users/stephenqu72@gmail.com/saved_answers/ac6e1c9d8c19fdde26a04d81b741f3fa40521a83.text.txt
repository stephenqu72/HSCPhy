To answer this question, we must look at the historical development of the theories of light, specifically the competition between Isaac Newton’s corpuscular theory and Christiaan Huygens’ wave theory.

### Answer
The correct answer is **B**.

### Explanation
Newton’s corpuscular model proposed that light consisted of tiny particles ("corpuscles"). A key prediction of this model was based on the mechanism of refraction: Newton argued that for light to bend towards the normal when entering a denser medium (like glass or water), the corpuscles must accelerate due to an attractive force from the medium. This mathematically required the speed of light to be **greater** in a denser medium than in a vacuum or air.

As technology and experimental precision improved, Foucault (in 1850) performed an experiment measuring the speed of light in water. He found that the speed of light was **slower** in water than in air. This experimental evidence directly contradicted the prediction of the corpuscular model, providing the definitive reason for the scientific community to favor the wave theory, which correctly predicted that light travels slower in denser, optically refractive media.
