To answer this question effectively for an HSC Physics exam, you should focus on **J.J. Thomson’s Cathode Ray experiment**.

### Answer
J.J. Thomson’s 1897 cathode ray experiment provided definitive evidence for the existence of the electron. Thomson passed a beam of cathode rays through a vacuum tube equipped with both electric and magnetic plates. By balancing the electric force ($F = qE$) and the magnetic force ($F = qvB$), he was able to determine the velocity of the particles. By measuring the deflection caused by the electric field alone, he calculated the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$) of the particles. Because this ratio was found to be constant regardless of the material of the cathode or the gas in the tube, he concluded that these particles were universal constituents of matter, which he called "corpuscles" (now known as electrons).

### How to solve this question
To secure the full 3 marks, structure your response to address these three areas:
1.  **Identify the Scientist and the Experiment:** Clearly state that J.J. Thomson performed the cathode ray experiment.
2.  **Describe the Mechanism:** Mention the use of perpendicular electric and magnetic fields. Explain that by manipulating these fields, Thomson could deflect the beam and calculate the velocity and, subsequently, the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$).
3.  **Explain the Conclusion/Evidence:** State that the independence of the $q/m$ value from the material used proved that these particles were fundamental to all matter. 

**Pro-tip for HSC students:** Always link the experimental procedure (deflection by fields) to the physical conclusion (charge-to-mass ratio). This shows you understand the *physics* behind the history.
