2.  **Apply the Stefan-Boltzmann Law:** $L = 4\pi R^2 \sigma T^4$. This implies that for a given temperature, a higher luminosity requires a larger radius. This is why stars in the top right (giants) are large, and stars in the bottom right (dwarfs) are small.
