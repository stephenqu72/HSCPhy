### Front
How do you calculate the height difference ($h$) between two points on a frictionless track if an object starts from rest at height $h_P$ and reaches velocity $v$ at height $h_Q$?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Conservation of Mechanical Energy ($PE_{initial} = KE_{final}$).
- **Formula:** $h = \frac{v^2}{2g}$
- **When to use it:** When a system is "frictionless" and you need to relate gravitational potential energy to kinetic energy.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that mass ($m$) cancels out on both sides of the energy equation; do not waste time searching for the mass of the object.
