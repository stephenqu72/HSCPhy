### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to be familiar with the key historical experiments in atomic physics:

1.  **Millikan’s Oil Drop Experiment:** Robert Millikan and Harvey Fletcher measured the terminal velocity of charged oil droplets in a uniform electric field. By balancing the gravitational force ($F_g = mg$) against the electric force ($F_e = qE$), they were able to determine the magnitude of the charge $q$ on individual oil droplets. They discovered that these charges were always integer multiples of a fundamental constant, which is the elementary charge of an electron ($e \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C).

    *   **(D) Charge on a proton:** While the magnitude of the charge on a proton is equal to that of an electron, the experiment specifically investigated the quantized nature of charge using electrons attached to oil droplets.
