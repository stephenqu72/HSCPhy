To find the correct arrangement that produces the greatest angle $\theta$, we use the diffraction grating formula:

$$d \sin \theta = n\lambda$$

Where:
- $d$ is the distance between adjacent slits (grating spacing).
- $\theta$ is the angle of diffraction.
- $n$ is the order of the fringe (here $n=1$).
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the light.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the relationship for $\theta$:**
    Rearranging the formula for $\sin \theta$, we get:
    $$\sin \theta = \frac{n\lambda}{d}$$
    To maximize $\theta$ (and thus $\sin \theta$), we need to maximize $\lambda$ and minimize $d$.

2.  **Analyze the Grating Spacing ($d$):**
    The spacing $d$ is inversely proportional to the number of lines per mm ($N$).
    $$d = \frac{1}{N}$$
    Therefore, to minimize $d$, we must select the higher number of lines per mm. Between 400 lines/mm and 600 lines/mm, **600 lines/mm** will result in a smaller $d$, which increases the angle $\theta$.

3.  **Analyze the Wavelength ($\lambda$):**
    Different colours have different wavelengths. Visible light ranges from violet (shorter wavelength, $\approx 400$ nm) to red (longer wavelength, $\approx 700$ nm). Yellow light has a longer wavelength than violet light.
    To maximize $\theta$, we need the largest $\lambda$, so **Yellow** light is the correct choice.

4.  **Conclusion:**
    To get the greatest angle, we require the highest lines per mm (600) and the longest wavelength (Yellow). This corresponds to option **D**.

**Correct Answer: D**
