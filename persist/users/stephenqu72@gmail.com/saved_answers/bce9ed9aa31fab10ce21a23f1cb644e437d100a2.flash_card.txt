### Front
What are the two defining characteristics of an electric field between two oppositely charged parallel plates?

### Back
- Key idea: The field is **uniform** in both magnitude and direction.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E = \frac{V}{d}$ (where $E$ is field strength, $V$ is potential difference, $d$ is plate separation).
- When to use it: To determine the electric field between parallel plates where field lines are parallel and equally spaced.
- Common trap: Assuming the field is uniform at the very edges of the plates (where "fringing" or non-uniformity occurs).
