### Solution and Explanation

When sketching electric field lines for two like charges (in this case, two positive charges), keep these three core rules in mind:

1.  **Direction:** Electric field lines always point **away** from positive charges and **towards** negative charges. Since these are positive, all your arrows must point outward from the charges.
2.  **Repulsion:** Because the charges have the same sign, the electric field lines will repel each other. This means the lines originating between the two charges will curve away from the center line between the charges, creating a "dead zone" or a neutral point exactly in the middle where the field strength is zero.
3.  **Density and Symmetry:** The lines must be symmetric around the horizontal axis and the vertical midpoint between the charges. Ensure you draw at least eight lines as requested.

**How to sketch it:**
*   **Around each charge:** Draw lines emanating radially outwards (like the spokes of a wheel).
*   **Between the charges:** As lines approach the space between the two charges, curve them outwards away from each other. They should never cross or touch.
*   **Far field:** As you move further away from the pair, the field lines should begin to look like the field of a single point charge with double the magnitude.
*   **Arrows:** Place clear arrows on every line pointing directly away from the center of each positive charge.
