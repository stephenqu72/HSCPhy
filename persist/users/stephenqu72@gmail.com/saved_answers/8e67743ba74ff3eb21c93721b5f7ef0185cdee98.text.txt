To solve these questions, we apply the principles of electromagnetism concerning the force on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field.

### Question 6: Calculation of Force Magnitude

**Formula:**
The magnitude of the force ($F$) on a straight wire of length $L$ carrying a current $I$ in a uniform magnetic field $B$ is given by:
$$F = BIL \sin(\theta)$$
where $\theta$ is the angle between the wire and the magnetic field lines.

**Given values:**
*   $I = 10\text{ A}$
*   $B = 4.0 \times 10^{-4}\text{ T}$
*   $L = 10\text{ cm} = 0.1\text{ m}$
*   Since the magnetic field lines go from North to South (horizontally) and the wire is vertical, $\theta = 90^\circ$, so $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

**Calculation:**
$$F = (4.0 \times 10^{-4}\text{ T}) \times (10\text{ A}) \times (0.1\text{ m})$$
$$F = 4.0 \times 10^{-4} \times 10^0 \times 10^{-1} = 4.0 \times 10^{-4}\text{ N}$$

The correct answer is **B**.

### Question 7: Determination of Force Direction

**Method:**
We use the **Right-Hand Palm Rule** (or Fleming’s Left-Hand Rule for motors):
1.  **Fingers:** Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$), which is from the North pole to the South pole (in the diagram, from right to left).
2.  **Thumb:** Point your thumb in the direction of the conventional current ($I$), which is upward.
3.  **Palm:** The force ($F$) pushes out from your palm.

Applying this:
*   Magnetic field ($B$) is pointing to the **left**.
*   Current ($I$) is pointing **up**.
*   The palm faces **into the page**.

The correct answer is **B**.
