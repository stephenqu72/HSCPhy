To determine the direction of the force acting on the transmission line, we use the **Right-Hand Rule (RHR)** for a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) Towards the ground**.

### Explanation
The force $F$ on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field is given by the formula:
$$F = ILB\sin(\theta)$$
The direction of this force is determined by the Right-Hand Rule:
1.  **Point your fingers** in the direction of the conventional current ($I$). In this scenario, the current is flowing from **east to west**.
2.  **Curl your fingers** toward the direction of the magnetic field ($B$). The Earth's magnetic field points **north**. 
3.  Because the current (West) and the magnetic field (North) are perpendicular to each other on the horizontal plane, we must orient our hand correctly:
    *   Point your right thumb (or fingers) West.
    *   Point your fingers (or palm) North.
4.  Your thumb points in the direction of the force. If you point your fingers west and curl them toward the north, your thumb will point **downward**, which is **towards the ground**.

Visualizing the compass rose on the ground:
*   North is "up" the page.
*   West is to the "left" of the page.
*   The cross product of West and North (using the right-hand coordinate system where $x=$ East, $y=$ North, $z=$ Up) results in a vector pointing down into the page (negative $z$-direction).

Thus, the force acts vertically downwards toward the Earth's surface.
