### Front
How do you calculate the decay constant ($\lambda$) given a radioactive decay graph?

### Back
- Key idea: Determine $t_{1/2}$ from the graph (time taken for activity to halve), then use the decay relationship.
- Formula / law: $\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{t_{1/2}}$
- When to use it: When asked for the decay constant or the probability of decay per unit time from an activity-time graph.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert the time units (e.g., days or hours) into standard SI units (seconds) before calculating $\lambda$.
