To find the ratio of the gravitational field strengths between Ganymede ($g_G$) and Europa ($g_E$), we use the formula for gravitational field strength at the surface of a spherical body:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the values from the table:**
*   **Ganymede:** $M_G = 1.5 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}$, $r_G = 2.6 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
*   **Europa:** $M_E = 5 \times 10^{22} \text{ kg}$, $r_E = 1.5 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$

**2. Set up the ratio $g_G : g_E$:**
$$\frac{g_G}{g_E} = \frac{\frac{GM_G}{r_G^2}}{\frac{GM_E}{r_E^2}}$$

The gravitational constant $G$ cancels out:
$$\frac{g_G}{g_E} = \frac{M_G}{M_E} \times \left( \frac{r_E}{r_G} \right)^2$$

**3. Substitute the values:**
*   Mass ratio: $\frac{1.5 \times 10^{23}}{5 \times 10^{22}} = \frac{15 \times 10^{22}}{5 \times 10^{22}} = 3$
*   Radius ratio squared: $\left( \frac{1.5 \times 10^6}{2.6 \times 10^6} \right)^2 = \left( \frac{1.5}{2.6} \right)^2 \approx (0.577)^2 \approx 0.333 \approx \frac{1}{3}$

**4. Calculate the final ratio:**
$$\frac{g_G}{g_E} = 3 \times \frac{1}{3} = 1$$

The ratio of the gravitational field strengths is $1:1$.

**Answer:** The correct option is **D) 1:1**.
