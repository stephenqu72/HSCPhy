### Front
What would be the implications for the Standard Model of Matter if quarks were discovered to have internal sub-structure?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The Standard Model would be reclassified as an "effective" (incomplete) theory, requiring a paradigm shift to incorporate the new, smaller "true" fundamental particles and their interactions.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When discussing the limits of the Standard Model, the nature of scientific progress, or the definition of fundamental particles.
- **Common trap:** Treating the Standard Model as an absolute truth; always frame it as a *model* subject to revision based on new experimental evidence.
