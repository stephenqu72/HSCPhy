To answer this question effectively for an HSC Physics exam, you need to link the historical failure of Classical Physics to the birth of Quantum Theory.

### Answer
The ultraviolet catastrophe refers to the failure of classical electromagnetic theory to accurately predict the energy spectrum of blackbody radiation. According to the classical Rayleigh-Jeans law, the intensity of radiation emitted by a blackbody should increase towards infinity as the wavelength decreases (i.e., as frequency increases into the ultraviolet range). This implies that an object at room temperature would emit infinite amounts of ultraviolet light, violating the Law of Conservation of Energy.

Max Planck resolved this in 1900 by proposing that energy is not continuous, but instead emitted or absorbed in discrete "packets" or "quanta." He introduced his relation, $E = hf$, where $E$ is the energy, $f$ is the frequency, and $h$ is Planck's constant. By quantizing energy, Planck showed that the probability of emitting high-frequency (ultraviolet) photons becomes extremely low, as their individual energy requirement is prohibitively high. This successfully matched experimental blackbody radiation curves and marked the beginning of quantum mechanics.

### How to solve this
To secure full marks (4 marks), your response must cover four specific pillars:
1. **The classical prediction:** Mention the Rayleigh-Jeans law or classical wave theory and how it predicted intensity should rise towards infinity at short wavelengths.
2. **The physical violation:** Explicitly state that this violates the Law of Conservation of Energy (as it would require infinite power).
3. **The resolution:** Identify Max Planck's contribution, specifically the concept of **quantization**. 
4. **The mechanism:** Explain how $E = hf$ restricts the emission of high-frequency radiation, effectively "taming" the spectrum to match experimental observations.
