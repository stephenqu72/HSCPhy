### Front
What do the direction and density of electric field lines represent?

### Back
- Key idea: Field lines point in the direction of the force on a positive test charge; line density represents field strength ($E$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E = \frac{F}{q}$; lines emerge from positive, terminate on negative.
- When to use it: To determine charge polarity and identify regions of varying field intensity in diagrams.
- Common trap: Assuming inward arrows point toward a positive charge (inward is always negative).
