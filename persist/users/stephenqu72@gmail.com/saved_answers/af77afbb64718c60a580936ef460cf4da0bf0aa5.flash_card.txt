### Front
How do you calculate the linear speed ($v$) of an object undergoing uniform circular motion when given its angular velocity ($\omega$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Linear speed is the product of the radius and the angular velocity.
- Formula: $v = r\omega$
- When to use it: Any circular motion problem requiring a conversion from angular to linear velocity (e.g., clock hands, rotating gears).
- Common trap: Forgetting to ensure units for radius ($r$) are consistent (e.g., converting $\text{cm}$ to $\text{m}$ if SI units are required).
