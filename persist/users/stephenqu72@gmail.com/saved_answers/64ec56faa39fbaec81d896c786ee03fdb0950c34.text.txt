To determine the correct relationship, we must look at the physics formula for the gravitational field strength ($g$) at the surface of a planet:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Where:
*   $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
*   $M$ is the mass of the planet.
*   $r$ is the radius of the planet.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the variables:** The question asks for the relationship between the mass ($M$) and the gravitational field ($g$) for planets with the **same radius** ($r$).
2.  **Simplify the expression:** Since $G$ is a constant and $r$ is constant, we can group them into a single constant $k = \frac{G}{r^2}$. The equation becomes:
    $$g = k \times M$$
3.  **Analyze the relationship:** This is a **linear relationship** of the form $y = mx$ (where $y$ is $g$ and $x$ is $M$). As the mass of the planet increases, the gravitational field strength increases proportionally at a constant rate.
4.  **Select the graph:** 
    *   (A) shows a negative linear relationship (inverse).
    *   (B) shows an exponential or quadratic increase.
    *   (C) shows an inverse relationship.
    *   (D) shows a **direct linear relationship**, which matches our equation $g \propto M$.

**Answer:** The correct graph is **(D)**.
