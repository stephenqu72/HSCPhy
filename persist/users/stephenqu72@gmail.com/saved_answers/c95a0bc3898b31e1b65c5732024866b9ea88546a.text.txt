### Answer
As the magnet rotates, the copper disc will also begin to rotate in the same direction (anticlockwise). This occurs due to **electromagnetic induction** and **Lenz's Law**. As the magnet moves, the magnetic flux through the region of the disc directly beneath the magnet changes. This changing magnetic flux induces **eddy currents** within the conductive copper disc. According to Lenz's Law, these eddy currents create their own magnetic fields that oppose the change in magnetic flux that produced them. Consequently, a force is exerted on the disc that drags it along in the direction of the magnet's motion to minimize the relative motion between the magnet and the disc.

### How to Solve This
To secure full marks for a 4-mark HSC physics question on this topic, you must link the physical principles in a logical chain:

1.  **Identify the cause (Change in Flux):** State that the rotating magnet creates a changing magnetic flux ($\Delta\Phi$) through the copper disc.
2.  **Explain the induced effect (Faraday's Law):** Mention that this changing flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) in the disc, which drives circular **eddy currents** within the metal.
3.  **Apply the opposition principle (Lenz's Law):** Explicitly reference Lenz’s Law, explaining that the direction of the eddy currents is such that they create a magnetic field that opposes the relative motion of the magnet.
4.  **State the outcome:** Explain that this interaction results in a magnetic force (Lorentz force) acting on the disc, causing it to experience a torque that makes it follow the magnet's rotation.

**Key terminology to include:**
*   Changing magnetic flux
*   Eddy currents
*   Lenz's Law
*   Opposition to relative motion
