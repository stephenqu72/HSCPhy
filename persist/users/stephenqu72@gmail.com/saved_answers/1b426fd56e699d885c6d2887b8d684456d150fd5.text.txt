To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of Special Relativity, specifically the definitions of **proper time** and **proper distance**.

### 1. Identify Proper Distance
Proper distance is defined as the distance between two points measured in a reference frame where both points are at rest. Since Earth and Mars are essentially stationary relative to each other (in the context of this physics problem), the distance $L_0$ between them measured by an observer on Earth or Mars is the **proper distance**.
- The spacecraft is moving at $0.8c$ relative to Earth and Mars. Therefore, the distance measured by the observer in the spacecraft is length-contracted.
- Thus, $D$ is the **contracted distance**.

### 2. Identify Proper Time
Proper time ($\Delta t_0$) is defined as the time interval between two events measured by an observer who is at the **same location** for both events. 
- In the spacecraft's reference frame, the start of the journey (passing Earth) and the end of the journey (passing Mars) occur at the exact same location (the position of the spacecraft itself). 
- Because the spacecraft observer is present at both "events" at the same point in their frame, the time interval $T$ they measure is the **proper time interval**.

### Conclusion
- $T$ is the proper time interval.
- $D$ is the contracted distance.
