The correct answer is **A. A testable scientific explanation.**

### Explanation
To succeed in HSC Science, it is crucial to distinguish between key scientific terms:

*   **Hypothesis:** This is a proposed explanation for a phenomenon that can be tested through experimentation or observation. It is an initial starting point for further investigation. Because it is a "prediction" or "proposed explanation," its core requirement is that it must be **testable** and falsifiable.
*   **Scientific Theory:** This corresponds more closely to option B. A theory is a well-substantiated explanation that has been repeatedly confirmed through observation and experimentation.
*   **Options C and D:** These are distractors. Whether a scientist is famous or whether an idea is "widely believed" has no bearing on its status as a scientific hypothesis. Science relies on evidence and methodology rather than authority or popularity.

When approaching questions about the "Nature of Science" in the HSC, always look for the option that emphasizes **empirical testing** and **falsifiability**. Since a hypothesis is the tentative beginning of the scientific method, "testable" is the defining characteristic.
