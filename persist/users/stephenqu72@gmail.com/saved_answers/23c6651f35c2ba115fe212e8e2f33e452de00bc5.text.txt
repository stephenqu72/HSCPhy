To determine the correct statement for a particle moving in a circle with uniform speed (Uniform Circular Motion), let's evaluate each option based on the physics of circular motion.

### Answer
The correct statement is **A: The kinetic energy of the particle is constant.**

---

### Explanation

**1. Kinetic Energy (Option A):**
Kinetic energy is defined by the formula $K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$. In uniform circular motion, the *speed* ($v$) of the particle is constant, and the mass ($m$) is constant. Since $v$ and $m$ do not change, the kinetic energy remains constant. This makes statement **A** correct.

**2. Net Force (Option B):**
For an object moving in a circle, the net force is the **centripetal force**, which is always directed toward the center of the circle. The direction of motion (velocity) is always tangent to the circle. Since the force (radial) is perpendicular to the motion (tangential), the force is **not** in the same direction as the motion. This makes statement **B** incorrect.

**3. Momentum (Option C):**
Momentum is a vector quantity: $\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$. While the *magnitude* of the momentum is constant (because speed is constant), the *direction* of the velocity vector is constantly changing as the particle moves along the circular path. Because the direction changes, the momentum vector is **not** constant. This makes statement **C** incorrect.

**4. Displacement (Option D):**
Displacement is a vector pointing from the initial position to the final position. In circular motion, the net force is directed toward the center of the circle. Over any significant arc of the circle, the displacement does not align with the radial centripetal force. This makes statement **D** incorrect.

### Summary
*   **A** is correct because speed determines kinetic energy.
*   **B** is incorrect because centripetal force is perpendicular to velocity.
*   **C** is incorrect because velocity changes direction.
*   **D** is incorrect as it describes geometric properties inconsistent with circular dynamics.
