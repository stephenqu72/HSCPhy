### Answer

**(a) Initial Force**
The magnitude of the magnetic force $F$ is calculated using the formula $F = qvB\sin(\theta)$.
Given:
- $q = 7.5 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{C}$
- $v = 6.0 \times 10^3 \, \text{m s}^{-1}$
- $B = 3.6 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{T}$
- $\theta = 90^\circ$ (the velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field)

$$F = (7.5 \times 10^{-6}) \times (6.0 \times 10^3) \times (3.6 \times 10^{-4}) \times \sin(90^\circ)$$
$$F = 1.62 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{N}$$

Using the Right-Hand Rule: Pointing fingers in the direction of velocity (right) and curling them into the page (magnetic field direction), the thumb points **upward**. 
**The initial force is $1.62 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{N}$ directed vertically upwards.**

**(b) Subsequent Motion**
The magnetic force acts as a centripetal force ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$), causing the particle to undergo **uniform circular motion**.
We find the radius $r$ using the equality $qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$:
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$
$$r = \frac{(4.0 \times 10^{-15}) \times (6.0 \times 10^3)}{(7.5 \times 10^{-6}) \times (3.6 \times 10^{-4})}$$
$$r = \frac{2.4 \times 10^{-11}}{2.7 \times 10^{-9}} \approx 8.89 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m} = 8.89 \, \text{mm}$$
The particle will move in a **circular path with a radius of $8.89 \, \text{mm}$** at a constant speed of $6.0 \times 10^3 \, \text{m s}^{-1}$.
