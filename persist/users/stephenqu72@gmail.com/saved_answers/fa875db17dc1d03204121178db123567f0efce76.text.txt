### Explanation
To solve this problem, consider these three physics principles:

1.  **Direction of the field:** By definition, electric field lines represent the path a positive test charge would take. Since field lines always point away from positive charges and towards negative charges, the field between the plates must point from the top (positive) plate to the bottom (negative) plate. This eliminates option **C**, which shows the field pointing upwards.

2.  **Uniformity:** Between two large, parallel, oppositely charged plates, the electric field is **uniform**. This means the field lines should be parallel to each other and evenly spaced within the central region. Options **B** and **D** show horizontal lines, which are incorrect for a vertical potential difference.

3.  **Fringing (Edge Effects):** While the field is uniform in the center, the lines "bulge" or curve outwards at the edges of the plates. This phenomenon is known as the "fringing effect." 
    *   Diagram **A** correctly shows the uniform, parallel vertical lines in the middle and the characteristic curved field lines at the outer edges of the plates.

Therefore, diagram **A** accurately depicts the physical reality of the electric field between two charged parallel plates.
