To determine the new centripetal force, we use the formula for the magnitude of the centripetal force:

$$F = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

where:
*   $m$ is the mass of the object
*   $v$ is the speed (velocity)
*   $r$ is the radius of the circular path

### Step-by-step solution:

1.  **Define the initial state:**
    The initial force is $F = \frac{mv^2}{r}$.

2.  **Define the changes to the variables:**
    *   The mass is doubled: $m' = 2m$
    *   The radius is halved: $r' = \frac{1}{2}r$
    *   The speed is tripled: $v' = 3v$

3.  **Calculate the new force ($F'$):**
    Substitute the new values into the formula:
    $$F' = \frac{m'(v')^2}{r'}$$
    $$F' = \frac{(2m)(3v)^2}{\frac{1}{2}r}$$

4.  **Simplify the expression:**
    *   First, square the speed: $(3v)^2 = 9v^2$
    *   Now substitute back: $F' = \frac{(2m)(9v^2)}{0.5r}$
    *   Multiply the constants: $2 \times 9 = 18$
    *   Divide by $0.5$: $\frac{18}{0.5} = 36$
    *   Therefore: $F' = 36 \times \left( \frac{mv^2}{r} \right)$

5.  **Conclusion:**
    Since the original force $F = \frac{mv^2}{r}$, the new force is $F' = 36F$.
