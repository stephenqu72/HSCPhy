To solve this problem, we must equate the magnetic torque produced by the current in the galvanometer to the restoring torque of the torsion spring.

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   Number of turns, $N = 100$
*   Dimensions of the square coil: $3.0\text{ cm} \times 3.0\text{ cm} = 0.03\text{ m} \times 0.03\text{ m}$
*   Area of the coil, $A = 0.03 \times 0.03 = 9.0 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m}^2$
*   Magnetic field strength, $B = 0.54\text{ T}$
*   Torsion constant, $k = 0.034\text{ Nm/rad}$
*   Angle of deflection, $\theta = 18^\circ$

### 2. Convert units:
The formula for the restoring torque uses radians. We must convert the deflection angle from degrees to radians:
$$\theta_{\text{rad}} = 18^\circ \times \left( \frac{\pi}{180^\circ} \right) = \frac{\pi}{10} \approx 0.31416\text{ rad}$$

### 3. Set up the equilibrium condition:
At equilibrium, the magnetic torque ($\tau_m$) equals the restoring torque ($\tau_s$):
$$\tau_m = \tau_s$$
The formula for magnetic torque in a radial magnetic field is $\tau_m = NIAB$. The formula for restoring torque is $\tau_s = k\theta$.
$$NIAB = k\theta$$

### 4. Solve for current ($I$):
$$I = \frac{k\theta}{NAB}$$
Substitute the known values:
$$I = \frac{0.034 \times (\frac{\pi}{10})}{100 \times (9.0 \times 10^{-4}) \times 0.54}$$
$$I = \frac{0.010681}{0.0486}$$
$$I \approx 0.21977\text{ A}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $0.22\text{ A}$.

**Answer:** The correct option is **B. 0.22 A**.
