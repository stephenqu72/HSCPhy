To excel at this 7-mark HSC Physics question, you must demonstrate a deep understanding of the "Working Scientifically" skills. This is not just a description of what you did; it is a critical analysis of *why* your experimental design was robust.

### How to Answer this Question

To achieve full marks, use the following structure:

1.  **Introduce your investigation:** Briefly name the experiment (e.g., "The investigation into the relationship between the angle of inclination and the coefficient of friction" or "Measuring the speed of light using a microwave").
2.  **Identify and Justify Variables:** 
    *   Explicitly state your **independent variable** (what you changed), **dependent variable** (what you measured), and **controlled variables** (what you kept constant).
    *   **Justification:** Explain *why* these variables are necessary. For example: "The temperature was kept constant because, as per $PV=nRT$, it would otherwise affect the pressure, introducing a systematic error."
3.  **Evaluate Experimental Controls:** 
    *   Discuss the effectiveness of your controls. Did they successfully isolate the independent variable?
    *   Discuss how you addressed potential errors. Mentioning how you mitigated systematic errors (e.g., zero-setting a balance) and random errors (e.g., repeating trials to calculate a mean) is crucial for justifying **reliability**.
4.  **Connect to Validity:** Explicitly link your procedure to **validity**. Validity is achieved if your experiment actually tests the hypothesis by ensuring only the independent variable affects the dependent variable. If you failed to control a significant variable, explain the impact and how you minimized it.

---

### Example Response (Drafting strategy)

*   **Context:** Investigation of the relationship between the length of a pendulum ($l$) and its period ($T$).
*   **Validity:** I justified the use of a small amplitude ($< 10^\circ$) to ensure the motion approximated Simple Harmonic Motion ($SHM$). By controlling the mass of the bob and the string length, I ensured that $T \propto \sqrt{l}$ was the only relationship being tested, fulfilling the definition of a valid experiment.
*   **Reliability:** I justified the use of multiple trials for each length to calculate a mean period. This reduced the impact of human reaction time (a random error). I evaluated the stopwatch precision as the limiting factor and accounted for this by using an electronic photogate, which improved the consistency of the data collected.

---

### Key Tips for the 7-Mark Response:
*   **Use Subject-Specific Vocabulary:** Use terms like *validity, reliability, accuracy, systematic error, random error, controlled variables,* and *precision*.
*   **Be Critical:** "Evaluation" means you must judge your own work. Don't just say "I kept the temperature constant." Say: "By maintaining a constant ambient temperature, I minimized the risk of thermal expansion in the apparatus, thereby ensuring that the change in length was solely attributable to the independent variable."
*   **Structure:** Use clear paragraphs or bullet points to ensure the marker can easily identify your justification and evaluation.
