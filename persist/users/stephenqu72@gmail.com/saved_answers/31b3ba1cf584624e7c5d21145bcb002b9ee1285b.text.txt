### Answer

James Clerk Maxwell made a foundational contribution to physics by unifying electricity, magnetism, and optics into a single theoretical framework. His key contributions include:

*   **Maxwell’s Equations:** He synthesized existing laws (such as Gauss’s Laws, Ampère’s Law, and Faraday’s Law) and introduced the "displacement current" term. This allowed for the mathematical prediction of self-propagating electromagnetic waves.
*   **The Nature of Light:** By calculating the speed of these predicted electromagnetic waves using the constants of permittivity ($\epsilon_0$) and permeability ($\mu_0$) of free space, he found the velocity to be:
    $$v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$$
*   **The Prediction:** The value he calculated for $v$ was approximately $3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$, which matched the experimentally determined speed of light at the time. This led him to correctly propose that light is an **electromagnetic wave**.
*   **Significance:** This ended the need for a mechanical "luminiferous aether" to transmit light through a medium, as the field itself could propagate through a vacuum, fundamentally changing our understanding of the universe.
