To solve this problem, we must understand the motion of a charged particle moving perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field.

### Explanation
When a charged particle of charge $q$ and mass $m$ moves with velocity $v$ into a magnetic field $B$ (perpendicular to its velocity), it experiences a magnetic force that acts as a centripetal force, causing it to travel in a circular path. The force is given by $F = qvB$, and the centripetal force is $F = \frac{mv^2}{r}$.

Equating these two expressions:
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

We can solve for the radius $r$:
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

From this formula, we can see that the radius $r$ is **inversely proportional** to the magnetic field strength $B$ ($r \propto \frac{1}{B}$), assuming mass $m$, velocity $v$, and charge $q$ remain constant.

If the magnitude of the magnetic field is doubled ($B_{new} = 2B$), the new radius $r_{new}$ will be:
$$r_{new} = \frac{mv}{q(2B)}$$
$$r_{new} = \frac{1}{2} \times \left( \frac{mv}{qB} \right)$$
$$r_{new} = \frac{r}{2}$$

### Answer
The correct option is **B**.
