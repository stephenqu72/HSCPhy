To solve this problem, we must refer to the physics formula for the magnetic force per unit length ($F/L$) between two infinitely long, parallel, current-carrying conductors:

$$ \frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r} $$

Where:
*   $F$ is the magnitude of the force.
*   $L$ is the length of the conductors.
*   $\mu_0$ is the permeability of free space.
*   $I_1$ and $I_2$ are the currents in the two conductors.
*   $r$ is the separation distance between the conductors.

### Analysis
The relationship we are investigating is between the force $F$ and the distance $r$. Assuming the currents $I_1$ and $I_2$ are kept constant, we can see from the formula that:
$$ F \propto \frac{1}{r} $$

This is an **inverse relationship**. Mathematically, as the distance $r$ increases, the force $F$ decreases according to a reciprocal function (a hyperbola). 
*   When $r$ is very small, $F$ is very large (approaching infinity).
*   When $r$ is very large, $F$ is very small (approaching zero).
