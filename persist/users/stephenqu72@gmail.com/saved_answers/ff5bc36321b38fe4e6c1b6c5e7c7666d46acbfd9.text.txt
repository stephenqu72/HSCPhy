### Answer and Explanation

**a) How does the Bohr model explain the production of this spectrum? (3 marks)**

*   **Explanation:** The Bohr model posits that electrons exist in discrete, quantized energy levels (stationary states) around the nucleus. When an electron transitions from a higher energy level ($n_{high}$) to a lower energy level ($n_{low}$), it emits a photon with energy equal to the difference between these two levels.
*   **The Physics:** This is represented by the equation: $\Delta E = E_{high} - E_{low} = hf$. Because these energy levels are discrete and specific for hydrogen, only specific, distinct frequencies (and thus colors) of light can be emitted, resulting in the observed line emission spectrum rather than a continuous spectrum.

**b) Analyse the contribution of Schrödinger to the current model of the atom. (2 marks)**

*   **Explanation:** While Bohr treated electrons as particles in defined orbits, Erwin Schrödinger introduced the quantum mechanical model. He proposed that electrons exhibit wave-like properties.
*   **The Physics:** By developing the wave equation (the Schrödinger equation), he demonstrated that electrons exist in "orbitals"—regions of space where there is a high probability of finding an electron, rather than fixed paths. This introduced the concept of electron probability clouds and established the foundation for modern quantum mechanics.

**c) Draw a labelled diagram of a helium-4 atom. (3 marks)**

*   **To draw this:**
    1.  **Nucleus:** Draw a central cluster containing 2 protons ($p^+$) and 2 neutrons ($n^0$). 
        *   **Fundamental force:** Label the **Strong Nuclear Force** acting between these nucleons (keeping the nucleus together).
    2.  **Electron Cloud:** Draw two electrons ($e^-$) orbiting or surrounding the nucleus.
        *   **Fundamental force:** Label the **Electromagnetic Force** acting between the positively charged nucleus and the negatively charged electrons (binding the electrons to the atom).
        *   *(Optional: Note that Gravity is negligible at this scale).*

### How to solve these types of questions:
1.  **For part (a):** Always link "quantized energy levels" to the "emission of a photon." Use the phrase "discrete energy transitions" for full marks.
2.  **For part (b):** The key shift to mention is from "deterministic orbits" (Bohr) to "probabilistic orbitals" (Schrödinger). Use terms like "wave-particle duality" and "probability density."
3.  **For part (c):** Ensure you draw the nucleus explicitly showing the 4 nucleons (2p, 2n). Correctly identifying the **Strong Nuclear Force** (binding nucleons) and the **Electromagnetic Force** (binding electrons) is critical for scoring all 3 marks.
