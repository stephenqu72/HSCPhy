### Front
How do you determine the magnetic polarity of a solenoid and the subsequent alignment of a nearby magnet?

### Back
- Key idea: Current-carrying coils create a magnetic field; opposite poles attract.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Right-Hand Grip Rule (fingers = current direction, thumb = North pole).
- When to use it: To determine field direction in solenoids and predict magnet orientation.
- Common trap: Confusing conventional current (positive to negative) with electron flow, or forgetting that the North pole of a magnet points in the direction of the external field lines (towards the solenoid's South pole).
