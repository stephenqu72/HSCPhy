This question focuses on the **Blackbody Radiation curve**, a fundamental topic in the HSC Physics module on the Nature of Light.

### Answer
This graph represents the **Blackbody Radiation spectrum** (or Planck’s distribution) for an object at two different temperatures. Curve A represents an object at a higher temperature than the object represented by curve B.

**Significance:**
1.  **Failure of Classical Physics:** Classical physics (specifically the Rayleigh-Jeans law) predicted that as wavelength decreases (frequency increases), the intensity should increase infinitely, leading to the "ultraviolet catastrophe." This graph demonstrates that intensity reaches a peak and then drops off, which classical wave theory could not explain.
2.  **Planck’s Hypothesis:** Max Planck resolved this by proposing that energy is not continuous but is emitted in discrete packets called quanta, given by $E = hf$.
3.  **Wien’s Displacement Law:** As the temperature increases, the peak wavelength $(\lambda_{max})$ shifts toward shorter wavelengths (blue-shift). Since Curve A has a lower peak wavelength than Curve B, it corresponds to a higher temperature. 
4.  **Stefan-Boltzmann Law:** The total area under the curve represents the total power radiated. Because the area under Curve A is greater, it confirms that a hotter object radiates significantly more energy than a cooler one.
