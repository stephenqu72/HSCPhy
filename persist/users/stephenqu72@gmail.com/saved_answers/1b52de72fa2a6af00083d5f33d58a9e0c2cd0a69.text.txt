### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) Gravitational**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to recall the core components of the **Standard Model of particle physics**:

1.  **The Standard Model** is a theoretical framework that describes three of the four fundamental forces in the universe and classifies all known elementary particles.
2.  The forces described by the Standard Model are:
    *   **Electromagnetic force:** Mediated by the photon.
    *   **Strong nuclear force:** Mediated by the gluon.
    *   **Weak nuclear force:** Mediated by $W$ and $Z$ bosons.
3.  **Gravity**, while being one of the four fundamental forces, is described by **General Relativity** (Einstein's theory of gravity). Currently, there is no widely accepted quantum field theory that successfully incorporates gravity into the Standard Model. Physicists often look for a theoretical particle called the "graviton" to mediate this force, but it has not been observed, and gravity remains outside the scope of the Standard Model.

Therefore, because gravity does not fit into the quantum framework of the Standard Model like the other three forces do, (A) is the correct choice.
