To determine the path of the electron in the electric field, let’s break down the physics step-by-step.

### Answer
The correct answer is **C. A parabolic path up the page**.

### Explanation

1.  **Analyze the Force:**
    The electric field lines point downwards. By definition, an electric field points in the direction a positive test charge would move. Therefore, the force on a positive charge would be downward. Since an electron is **negatively charged**, it experiences a force in the opposite direction of the electric field lines. Thus, the electron is subjected to an upward force.

2.  **Analyze the Motion:**
    *   **Horizontal direction:** There is no force acting in the horizontal direction, so the horizontal velocity $v_x$ remains constant.
    *   **Vertical direction:** The electron experiences a constant upward force $F = qE$. According to Newton’s Second Law ($F=ma$), this results in a constant upward acceleration $a_y$. 
    *   This is identical to the physics of projectile motion in a gravitational field, where an object has constant horizontal velocity and constant vertical acceleration.

3.  **Determine the Path:**
    The combination of constant horizontal motion and constant vertical acceleration results in a **parabolic path**. Since the force is directed upwards, the electron will be deflected "up the page."

### Summary of Physics Principles
*   **Force on a charge:** $F = qE$. Because the electron's charge $q$ is negative, the force is directed opposite to the field $E$.
*   **Kinematics:** Constant horizontal velocity + constant vertical acceleration = parabolic trajectory.
