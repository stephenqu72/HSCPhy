### Explanation
In the late 1800s, physicists believed that light, like sound, required a physical medium to travel through. They called this hypothetical medium the **luminiferous aether**. It was believed that the aether filled all of space and acted as the absolute reference frame for light waves.

If the aether existed, the Earth—as it orbits the Sun—would be moving through this "aether wind." This motion would cause the speed of light to appear slightly different depending on the direction of travel relative to the aether, much like a swimmer in a river experiences different speeds when swimming with or against the current.

The **Michelson-Morley experiment** was specifically designed to:
