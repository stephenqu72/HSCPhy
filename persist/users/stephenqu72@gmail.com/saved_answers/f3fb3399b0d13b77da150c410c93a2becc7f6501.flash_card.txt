### Front
How do the gravitational potential energy ($U$) and kinetic energy ($K$) of a satellite change as it moves to a lower circular orbit?

### Back
- Key idea: Decreasing orbital radius $r$ causes $U$ to become more negative (decrease) and $K$ to increase.
- Formula: $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$ and $K = \frac{GMm}{2r}$
- When to use it: For satellite orbital transitions or energy changes.
- Common trap: Assuming $U$ increases because "energy is being lost"; remember $U$ is negative, so a smaller $r$ makes $U$ a larger negative number (a decrease).
