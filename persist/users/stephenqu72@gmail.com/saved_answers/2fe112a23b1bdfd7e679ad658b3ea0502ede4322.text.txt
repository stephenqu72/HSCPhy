The correct answer is **D. frequency.**

### Explanation

This question tests your understanding of the **Doppler Effect**, a core concept in HSC Physics. 

1.  **The Concept:** The Doppler Effect describes the change in frequency (and wavelength) of a wave in relation to an observer who is moving relative to the wave source. 
2.  **Why it happens:** As the fire engine (the source) moves towards Alex (the observer), each successive sound wave is emitted from a position closer to Alex than the previous one. This "bunches up" the wavefronts, shortening the wavelength.
3.  **The Relationship:** Because the speed of sound $v$ in a medium is constant, we use the wave equation:
    $$v = f \lambda$$
    where $f$ is the frequency and $\lambda$ is the wavelength.
    If the speed $v$ remains constant and the wavelength $\lambda$ decreases as the source approaches, the frequency $f$ must **increase** to maintain the equality. This is why a siren sounds higher-pitched as it approaches you.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **A (speed):** The speed of sound is determined entirely by the properties of the medium (temperature, pressure, and density of the air), not by the motion of the source.
*   **B (period):** Since period $T$ is the inverse of frequency ($T = 1/f$), an increase in frequency results in a **decrease** in period.
*   **C (amplitude):** Amplitude relates to the loudness (intensity) of the sound. While the sound gets louder as the fire engine gets closer, this is due to proximity, not the Doppler Effect. The question specifically asks for the change resulting from the approach (the relative motion), for which the defining characteristic is the shift in frequency.
