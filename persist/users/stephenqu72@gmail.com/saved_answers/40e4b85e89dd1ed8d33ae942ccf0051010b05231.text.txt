To solve this problem, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, which is defined by the formula:

$$F = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

### Step 1: Identify the given values
*   Mass 1 ($m_1$) = $1.0\text{ kg}$
*   Mass 2 ($m_2$) = $100\text{ kg}$
*   Distance ($r$) = $10\text{ cm} = 0.10\text{ m}$ (It is crucial to convert centimeters to meters for SI consistency)
*   Gravitational constant ($G$) = $6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2\text{ kg}^{-2}$

### Step 2: Substitute values into the formula
$$F = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (1.0) \times (100)}{(0.10)^2}$$

### Step 3: Perform the calculation
1. Calculate the numerator: $(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times 100 = 6.67 \times 10^{-9}$
2. Calculate the denominator: $(0.10)^2 = 0.01 = 10^{-2}$
3. Divide the numerator by the denominator:
$$F = \frac{6.67 \times 10^{-9}}{10^{-2}}$$
$$F = 6.67 \times 10^{-9 - (-2)}$$
$$F = 6.67 \times 10^{-7}\text{ N}$$
