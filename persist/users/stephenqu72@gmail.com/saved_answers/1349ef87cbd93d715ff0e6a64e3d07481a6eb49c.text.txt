To solve this problem, we apply the **Law of Conservation of Energy** for an ideal transformer. In an ideal transformer, the total power input from the primary coil must equal the total power output from all secondary coils combined:

$$P_{\text{primary}} = P_{\text{secondary}_1} + P_{\text{secondary}_2}$$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Calculate the input power ($P_1$):**
    Using the formula $P = VI$, we have:
    $$P_1 = V_1 \times I_1 = 240\text{ V} \times 1.0\text{ A} = 240\text{ W}$$

2.  **Determine the voltages in the secondary coils:**
    The ratio of voltage to the number of turns is constant for all coils in a transformer ($V/N = \text{constant}$).
    From the primary coil: $V_1 / N_1 = 240 / 40 = 6\text{ V per turn}$.
    *   For the first secondary coil ($N_2 = 10$): $V_2 = 10 \times 6 = 60\text{ V}$.
    *   For the second secondary coil ($N_3 = 20$): $V_3 = 20 \times 6 = 120\text{ V}$.

3.  **Use the conservation of energy equation:**
    $$P_1 = (V_2 \times I_2) + (V_3 \times I_3)$$
    Substitute the known values:
    $$240 = (60 \times 0.5) + (120 \times I_3)$$
    $$240 = 30 + 120 \times I_3$$

4.  **Solve for $I_3$:**
    $$240 - 30 = 120 \times I_3$$
    $$210 = 120 \times I_3$$
    $$I_3 = 210 / 120$$
    $$I_3 = 1.75\text{ A}$$
