### Front
What is the formula for the normal force ($F_{\text{seat}}$) exerted on an occupant at the top of a vertical circular track (hill)?

### Back
- Key idea: The net centripetal force is the difference between gravity and the normal force.
- Formula / law: $F_{\text{seat}} = m(g - \frac{v^2}{r})$
- When to use it: Circular motion problems involving vertical hills or convex curves.
- Common trap: Assuming $F_{\text{seat}} = mg$. Remember that at the top of a hill, gravity helps provide the centripetal force, so the seat provides less support than the occupant's weight.
