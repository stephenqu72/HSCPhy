The correct answer is **(A)**.

### Explanation

To answer this question, we must look at the historical contributions of James Clerk Maxwell regarding Electromagnetism:

*   **(B) Unified the fields of Electricity, magnetism and light:** This is correct. Maxwell’s equations provided the theoretical framework that unified electricity and magnetism into a single electromagnetic field and predicted that light is an electromagnetic wave.
*   **(C) Calculated the speed of light:** This is correct. From his equations, Maxwell derived the constant $c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\epsilon_0 \mu_0}}$. When he plugged in the values for the permittivity of free space ($\epsilon_0$) and the permeability of free space ($\mu_0$), the result was approximately $3 \times 10^8$ m/s, which matched the experimentally determined speed of light.
*   **(D) Explained how EMR propagates:** This is correct. Maxwell proposed that oscillating electric and magnetic fields sustain each other, allowing electromagnetic radiation (EMR) to propagate through space as a self-sustaining wave.
*   **(A) Proved that EMR exists beyond the visible spectrum:** This is **not** a contribution of Maxwell. While Maxwell's theory *predicted* the existence of EMR outside the visible spectrum, the experimental proof was provided by **Heinrich Hertz** in 1887, who generated and detected radio waves, confirming Maxwell's theoretical predictions.

Therefore, since the question asks for what was **not** a major contribution (in the sense of an experimental proof or discovery), (A) is the correct choice.
