### Front
In uniform circular motion, which physical quantity remains constant and why?

### Back
- Key idea: Kinetic energy ($K$) is a scalar; it depends on speed, not direction.
- Formula / law: $K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$ (where $v$ is constant speed).
- When to use it: To identify invariants in circular dynamics.
- Common trap: Assuming momentum is constant; momentum is a vector and changes because velocity's *direction* constantly changes.
