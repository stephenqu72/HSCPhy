### Part (a)
**Answer:** The two variables that determine the luminosity ($L$) of a star are its **radius** ($R$) and its **surface temperature** ($T$).

**Explanation:** This is derived from the Stefan-Boltzmann Law, expressed by the formula:
$$L = 4\pi R^2 \sigma T^4$$
where $\sigma$ is the Stefan-Boltzmann constant. Because $4\pi\sigma$ is constant, the luminosity of a star is directly proportional to its surface area ($R^2$) and its temperature raised to the fourth power ($T^4$).
