To solve this question, we need to understand the underlying physical process of **magnetic braking**, which is a key concept in the Physics module regarding Electromagnetism.

### Answer
The correct answer is **C) eddy currents and heat energy**.

### Explanation
1.  **The Process of Magnetic Braking:** Magnetic braking occurs when a conductor (like a metal disc or wheel) moves through a non-uniform magnetic field or past a stationary magnet. According to **Lenz's Law**, the change in magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) in the conductor, which drives circulating loops of electrical current known as **eddy currents**.
2.  **Lenz’s Law and Conservation of Energy:** Lenz’s Law states that the direction of these eddy currents creates a magnetic field that opposes the motion that caused them. This opposing force acts as a brake on the moving object.
3.  **Energy Transformation:** The kinetic energy of the moving object is used to perform work against the magnetic force. As the eddy currents flow through the resistive material of the conductor, they dissipate energy in the form of **thermal energy (heat)** due to the Joule heating effect ($P = I^2R$).
4.  **Evaluating Options:**
    *   **A, B, and D:** While magnetic fields are involved, they are not the "product" or the final form of energy into which kinetic energy is converted. The conversion process relies on the creation of eddy currents, which subsequently generate heat. Therefore, option C is the most accurate description of the energy transformation process required by the Law of Conservation of Energy in this context.
