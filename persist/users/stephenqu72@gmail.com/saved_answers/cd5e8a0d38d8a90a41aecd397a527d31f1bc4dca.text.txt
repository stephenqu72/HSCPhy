### Answer
The correct answer is **(B) $3.0 \times 10^{-18} \text{ kg}$**.

### Explanation
To find the amount of substance that has decayed, we must first determine the amount of substance **remaining** after the given time, and then subtract that from the initial amount.

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Initial mass ($N_0$) = $6.0 \times 10^{-18} \text{ kg}$
*   Half-life ($t_{1/2}$) = $6 \text{ hours}$
*   Time elapsed ($t$) = $4 \text{ hours}$

**2. Use the radioactive decay formula:**
The mass remaining ($N_t$) after time $t$ is given by:
$$N_t = N_0 \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{\frac{t}{t_{1/2}}}$$

Substitute the values:
$$N_t = 6.0 \times 10^{-18} \times \left( 0.5 \right)^{\frac{4}{6}}$$
$$N_t = 6.0 \times 10^{-18} \times \left( 0.5 \right)^{0.6667}$$
$$N_t \approx 6.0 \times 10^{-18} \times 0.62996$$
$$N_t \approx 3.7798 \times 10^{-18} \text{ kg}$$

**3. Calculate the amount decayed:**
The amount that has decayed is the initial mass minus the remaining mass:
$$\text{Decayed} = N_0 - N_t$$
$$\text{Decayed} = 6.0 \times 10^{-18} - 3.7798 \times 10^{-18}$$
$$\text{Decayed} \approx 2.22 \times 10^{-18} \text{ kg}$$
