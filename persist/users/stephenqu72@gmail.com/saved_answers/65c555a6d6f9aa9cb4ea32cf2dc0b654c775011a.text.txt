### Answer
To determine the velocity of the ball immediately before impact, we use the kinematic equation for motion under constant acceleration due to gravity:

$$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$

Assuming the ball is dropped from rest ($u = 0\text{ m/s}$), the acceleration $a = g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$, and the displacement $s = H = 1.2\text{ m}$:

$$v^2 = 0^2 + 2 \times 9.8 \times 1.2$$
$$v^2 = 23.52$$
$$v = \sqrt{23.52} \approx 4.85\text{ m/s}$$

The velocity of the ball immediately before impact is approximately **$4.85\text{ m/s}$ downwards**.

To solve this HSC physics problem, follow these steps:

1.  **Identify the variables:** You are given the height $H = 1.2\text{ m}$. In physics problems involving a ball being dropped, the initial velocity ($u$) is typically $0\text{ m/s}$ unless otherwise stated. We use the acceleration due to gravity, $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$.
2.  **Select the correct formula:** Since we are dealing with motion under constant acceleration and we need to find the final velocity ($v$) without knowing the time taken, the most efficient formula is:
    $$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$
3.  **Substitution:** Plug the known values into the equation. $u = 0$, $a = 9.8$, and $s = 1.2$.
4.  **Solve:** Calculate the value of $v^2$ and then take the square root to find $v$. 
5.  **Alternative Method:** You could also use the Principle of Conservation of Energy, where the potential energy at the top equals the kinetic energy at the bottom:
    $$mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
    Dividing both sides by mass ($m$), you get $gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2$, which simplifies to $v = \sqrt{2gh}$. Using this: $v = \sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 1.2} = 4.85\text{ m/s}$. Both methods yield the same result.
