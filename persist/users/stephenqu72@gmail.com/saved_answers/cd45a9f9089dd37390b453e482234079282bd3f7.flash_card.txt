### Front
How do you calculate the relative velocity of two objects moving at relativistic speeds (significant fractions of $c$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Velocities do not add linearly; the relative velocity must not exceed $c$.
- Formula: $u' = \frac{v_A + v_B}{1 + \frac{v_A v_B}{c^2}}$
- When to use it: When finding the velocity of an object as observed from a frame that is itself moving at a relativistic speed.
- Common trap: Using Galilean addition ($v_A + v_B$), which violates the second postulate of Special Relativity.
