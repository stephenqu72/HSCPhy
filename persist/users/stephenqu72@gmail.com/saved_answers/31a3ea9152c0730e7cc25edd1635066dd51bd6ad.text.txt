To solve this problem, we need to apply Kepler’s Third Law to find the mass of the planet and then use the escape velocity formula.

### 1. Identify the given values
*   Radius of orbit ($r$) = $4.46 \times 10^8 \text{ m}$
*   Period ($T$) = $1.5 \times 10^8 \text{ s}$
*   Radius of planet ($R$) = $4500 \text{ km} = 4.5 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
*   Gravitational constant ($G$) = $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$

### 2. Determine the mass of the planet ($M$)
Using Kepler’s Third Law:
$$\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$$
Rearranging for $M$:
$$M = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{G T^2}$$
Substitute the values:
$$M = \frac{4 \times \pi^2 \times (4.46 \times 10^8)^3}{6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times (1.5 \times 10^8)^2}$$
$$M \approx \frac{4 \times 9.8696 \times 8.87 \times 10^{25}}{6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 2.25 \times 10^{16}} \approx \frac{3.50 \times 10^{27}}{1.50 \times 10^6} \approx 2.33 \times 10^{21} \text{ kg}$$

### 3. Calculate escape velocity ($v_e$)
The formula for escape velocity is:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$$
Substitute the values:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 2.33 \times 10^{21}}{4.5 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{3.11 \times 10^{11}}{4.5 \times 10^6}} \approx \sqrt{69111} \approx 262.89 \text{ m/s}$$

Rounding this result, we get $2.63 \times 10^2 \text{ m/s}$.
