### Front
In symmetrical projectile motion, how do you determine the time taken to reach the maximum height ($t_{up}$) from the total flight time ($T$)?

### Back
- Key idea: The vertical motion is symmetrical; gravity acts equally on the ascent and descent.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $t_{up} = \frac{T}{2}$
- When to use it: Whenever an object is launched and lands at the same vertical level (ignoring air resistance).
- Common trap: Forgetting that a stroboscopic photo with $n$ images has $n-1$ time intervals. Always calculate total time $T$ as $(n-1) \times \Delta t$ before dividing by 2.
