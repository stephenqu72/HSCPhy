To solve this problem, we first resolve the initial velocity into its vertical ($v_{y0}$) and horizontal ($v_x$) components:
- $v_{y0} = v \sin(\theta) = 28 \sin(30^\circ) = 14\text{ m/s}$
- $v_x = v \cos(\theta) = 28 \cos(30^\circ) = 24.25\text{ m/s}$

### Part (a)
The ball returns to its initial height when the vertical displacement is $\Delta y = 0$. Using the displacement formula $\Delta y = v_{y0}t + \frac{1}{2}a_y t^2$, where $a_y = -9.8\text{ m/s}^2$:
$$0 = 14t - 4.9t^2$$
$$t(14 - 4.9t) = 0$$
Since $t \neq 0$, we have $t = \frac{14}{4.9} \approx 2.86\text{ s}$.

### Part (b)
1. **Find the time ($t$) when the ball is at $0.50\text{ m}$:**
   The displacement $\Delta y = 0.50 - 1.0 = -0.5\text{ m}$.
   $$-0.5 = 14t - 4.9t^2 \implies 4.9t^2 - 14t - 0.5 = 0$$
   Using the quadratic formula $t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
   $$t = \frac{14 \pm \sqrt{(-14)^2 - 4(4.9)(-0.5)}}{9.8} = \frac{14 \pm \sqrt{196 + 9.8}}{9.8} \approx \frac{14 + 14.346}{9.8} \approx 2.89\text{ s}$$

2. **Find the horizontal distance ($x_b$) of the ball at $t = 2.89\text{ s}$:**
   $$x_b = v_x t = 24.25 \times 2.89 \approx 70.08\text{ m}$$

3. **Find the distance the fielder runs ($d_f$):**
   The fielder starts at $85\text{ m}$ and catches it at $70.08\text{ m}$.
   $$d_f = 85 - 70.08 = 14.92\text{ m}$$

4. **Calculate the fielder's acceleration ($a_f$):**
   Using $d_f = u_f t + \frac{1}{2} a_f t^2$ where $u_f = 0$:
   $$14.92 = \frac{1}{2} a_f (2.89)^2$$
   $$a_f = \frac{14.92 \times 2}{8.35} \approx 3.57\text{ m/s}^2$$

The magnitude of her acceleration is approximately **$3.57\text{ m/s}^2$**.
