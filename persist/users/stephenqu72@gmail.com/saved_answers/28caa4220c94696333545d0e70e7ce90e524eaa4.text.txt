To determine the correct graph, we must look at the physics formula for the gravitational field strength ($g$) at the surface of a planet.

### Explanation
The gravitational field strength $g$ at the surface of a planet is given by:
$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Where:
*   $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
*   $M$ is the mass of the planet.
*   $r$ is the radius of the planet.

The question asks for the relationship between the mass ($M$) and the surface gravitational field ($g$) for planets with the **same radius**. This means $r$ is a constant. We can rewrite the formula as:
$$g = \left( \frac{G}{r^2} \right) M$$

Since $G$ and $r$ are both constants, the term in the parentheses is also a constant (let’s call it $k$). Therefore, the equation simplifies to:
$$g = kM$$

This is in the form of a linear equation ($y = mx$), where $g$ is directly proportional to $M$. A direct proportionality on a graph is represented by a straight line passing through the origin.

Comparing this to the given options:
*   (A) shows an inverse linear relationship.
*   (B) shows an exponential/curved relationship.
*   (C) shows an inverse relationship.
*   (D) shows a **straight line passing through the origin**, which represents a direct proportionality.

Therefore, the correct graph is **(D)**.
