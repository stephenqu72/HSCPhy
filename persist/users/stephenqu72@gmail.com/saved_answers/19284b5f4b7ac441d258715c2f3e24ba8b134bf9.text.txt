Hello! As an HSC Physics teacher, I will guide you through this classic circular motion problem.

### Part (a)
The two forces acting on the ball are:
1. **Weight ($mg$):** Acting vertically downwards.
2. **Tension ($T$):** Acting along the string towards the point of attachment on the rod.

---

### Part (b)
**1. Geometry:** The radius $r = L \sin \theta$, where $L = 0.75\text{ m}$ and $\theta = 25^\circ$.
$$r = 0.75 \sin(25^\circ) \approx 0.317\text{ m}$$

**2. Force Analysis:**
*   Vertical components balance: $T \cos \theta = mg$
*   Horizontal components provide the centripetal force: $T \sin \theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$

**3. Derivation:** Dividing the two equations:
$$\tan \theta = \frac{v^2}{rg} \implies v = \sqrt{rg \tan \theta}$$

**4. Calculation:**
$$v = \sqrt{(0.317)(9.8)(\tan 25^\circ)} \approx \sqrt{1.446} \approx 1.20\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

---

### Part (c)
In this scenario, we have two tensions $T_1$ (top) and $T_2$ (bottom). Let $\theta = 30^\circ$.

**1. Vertical Equilibrium:**
The sum of vertical components must equal weight:
$$T_1 \cos \theta - T_2 \cos \theta = mg$$
$$(T_1 - T_2) \cos(30^\circ) = (1.8)(9.8)$$
$$T_1 - T_2 = \frac{17.64}{\cos(30^\circ)} \approx 20.37\text{ N} \quad \text{--- (Eq 1)}$$

**2. Centripetal Force:**
$r = 0.75 \sin(30^\circ) = 0.375\text{ m}$.
$$(T_1 + T_2) \sin \theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
$$(T_1 + T_2) \sin(30^\circ) = \frac{1.8 \times 3.0^2}{0.375} = 43.2$$
$$T_1 + T_2 = \frac{43.2}{\sin(30^\circ)} = 86.4\text{ N} \quad \text{--- (Eq 2)}$$

**3. Solve Simultaneous Equations:**
Adding Eq 1 and Eq 2:
$$2T_1 = 106.77 \implies T_1 \approx 53.39\text{ N}$$
Substituting into Eq 2:
$$T_2 = 86.4 - 53.39 \approx 33.01\text{ N}$$

**Answer:** $T_1 \approx 53.4\text{ N}$ and $T_2 \approx 33.0\text{ N}$.
