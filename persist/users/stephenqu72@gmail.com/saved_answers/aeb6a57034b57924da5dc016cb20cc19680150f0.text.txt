The correct answer is **B. the wave nature of light**.

### Explanation

To understand why this is the case, we look at the fundamental properties of waves versus particles in the context of Physics:

1.  **Transverse Waves:** Polarization is a phenomenon that only occurs for transverse waves. A transverse wave is one where the oscillation of the medium (or the field, in the case of electromagnetic waves) occurs perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation. 
2.  **The Mechanism:** Unpolarized light consists of electromagnetic oscillations in many different planes. When this light passes through a polarizing filter, only the oscillations aligned with the filter's transmission axis are allowed to pass through. This restricts the wave to a single plane of oscillation.
3.  **Why not particles?** Particles (or corpuscles, as defined in Newton’s theory) travel in straight lines and do not possess "planes of oscillation" that can be filtered out. The act of filtering based on orientation is a uniquely wave-based characteristic.
4.  **HSC Context:** In the study of the nature of light, we often contrast phenomena:
    *   **Wave nature:** Demonstrated by interference, diffraction, and **polarization**.
    *   **Particle nature:** Demonstrated by the photoelectric effect and Compton scattering.

Because polarization specifically identifies the directional orientation of oscillations within a wave, it serves as conclusive evidence that light behaves as a transverse wave.
