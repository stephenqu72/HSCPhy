### Explanation

To answer this question, we need to distinguish between the contributions of Michael Faraday and James Clerk Maxwell:

*   **(A), (C), and (D)** are all core pillars of Maxwell's work. Maxwell unified the existing laws of electricity and magnetism into a single mathematical framework (Maxwell's Equations). From these equations, he derived the existence of electromagnetic waves and showed that their velocity $v$ is related to the permeability of free space ($\mu_0$) and the permittivity of free space ($\epsilon_0$) by the formula:
    $$v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$$
    This calculation famously showed that the speed of electromagnetic waves matches the speed of light, leading to the conclusion that light is an electromagnetic wave.

*   **(B) Prediction of electromotive force:** This is **not** a contribution of Maxwell. The phenomenon of electromagnetic induction and the concept of electromotive force (EMF) were discovered and described by Michael Faraday in 1831. While Maxwell later incorporated Faraday’s Law into his set of equations (as one of the four equations), he did not *predict* the existence of EMF; it was an experimental discovery made by Faraday years before Maxwell published his theory.

Therefore, because the question asks for what was **not** a contribution, (B) is the correct choice.
