### Front
What does a "redshift" in the spectral lines of a distant galaxy indicate about its motion relative to Earth?

### Back
- Key idea: Light waves are stretched due to the Doppler Effect, shifting spectral lines toward longer (red) wavelengths.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Doppler Effect / Hubble’s Law.
- When to use it: To determine the radial velocity of celestial objects; provides evidence for the expansion of the universe.
- Common trap: Mistaking redshift for the physical size of an object or an intrinsic change in color rather than a shift in spectral position.
