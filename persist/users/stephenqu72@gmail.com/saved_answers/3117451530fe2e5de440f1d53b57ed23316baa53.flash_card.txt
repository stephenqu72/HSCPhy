### Front
If an object rotating in a circle maintains a constant frequency of revolution, how does a change in the radius of rotation affect its angular velocity?

### Back
- Key idea: Angular velocity ($\omega$) is solely dependent on the frequency of rotation ($f$), not the radius ($r$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\omega = 2\pi f$. No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Any scenario involving circular motion where frequency is stated to be constant.
- Common trap: Confusing angular velocity ($\omega$) with linear velocity ($v = r\omega$); changing the radius will change linear velocity, but has no effect on angular velocity.
