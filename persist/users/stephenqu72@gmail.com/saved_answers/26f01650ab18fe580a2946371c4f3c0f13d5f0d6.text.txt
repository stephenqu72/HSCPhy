### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) none of the above.**

### Explanation
To solve this, we need to apply the two fundamental postulates of Einstein's Special Theory of Relativity:

1.  **The Principle of Relativity:** The laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames of reference.
2.  **The Constancy of the Speed of Light:** The speed of light in a vacuum, denoted as $c$, is constant for all inertial observers, regardless of the motion of the light source or the motion of the observer.

Let's evaluate the options based on these principles:

*   **(A) The speed of the observer:** According to the second postulate, no matter how fast an observer is moving relative to the light source, they will always measure the speed of light as exactly $c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$. Therefore, the observer's speed does not change the measured value.
*   **(B) The speed of the object emitting the light:** Similarly, the speed of the source does not affect the speed of the light it emits. Whether the source is stationary or moving at relativistic speeds, the light it emits will travel through a vacuum at the constant speed $c$.
*   **(C) The type of electromagnetic radiation:** All electromagnetic radiation (radio waves, visible light, X-rays, etc.) consists of photons. In a vacuum, all photons travel at the same constant speed $c$, regardless of their frequency or wavelength. 
*   **(D) None of the above:** Since none of the factors listed in (A), (B), or (C) have any effect on the measured speed of light in a vacuum, (D) is the correct choice.

In the HSC syllabus, it is crucial to remember that $c$ is an **invariant constant** in physics.
