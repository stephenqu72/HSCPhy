### Part (a): Determine the distance to P
To find the distance as measured by observers on Earth, we use the standard velocity formula $v = d/t$ in the Earth's frame.

**Given:**
*   $v = 0.8c = 0.8 \times 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s} = 2.4 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
*   $t = 15 \text{ years}$

First, convert years to seconds:
$$15 \text{ years} \times 365.25 \text{ days/year} \times 24 \text{ h/day} \times 3600 \text{ s/h} \approx 4.73 \times 10^8 \text{ s}$$

Now calculate $d$:
$$d = v \times t = (2.4 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}) \times (4.73 \times 10^8 \text{ s}) \approx 1.136 \times 10^{17} \text{ m}$$

### Part (b): Show the astronaut ages 9 years
We use the time dilation formula: $t_v = \gamma t_0$, where $t_v$ is the dilated time (Earth) and $t_0$ is the proper time (astronaut).

**1. Calculate Lorentz factor ($\gamma$):**
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.8)^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.64}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.36}} = \frac{1}{0.6} = 1.667$$

**2. Solve for proper time ($t_0$):**
$$t_0 = \frac{t_v}{\gamma} = \frac{15}{1.667} = 15 \times 0.6 = 9 \text{ years}$$
The astronaut ages exactly 9 years.

---

### Part (c): Explain why the astronaut ages 9 years
The astronaut considers themselves to be at rest. From their frame of reference, the planet P is rushing towards them at $0.8c$. Because of **length contraction**, the distance to the planet is contracted.

**1. Calculate contracted distance ($L$):**
$$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2} = L_0 \times (0.6)$$
Where $L_0$ is the distance measured from Earth ($12 \text{ light-years}$). 
$$L = 12 \times 0.6 = 7.2 \text{ light-years}$$

**2. Calculate time from the astronaut's perspective:**
$$t = \frac{L}{v} = \frac{7.2 \text{ light-years}}{0.8c} = 9 \text{ years}$$

**Conclusion:** The astronaut ages 9 years because they perceive the distance to the planet as being shorter (length contraction). Both frames agree on the outcome, even if the "reason" (time dilation vs. length contraction) differs.
