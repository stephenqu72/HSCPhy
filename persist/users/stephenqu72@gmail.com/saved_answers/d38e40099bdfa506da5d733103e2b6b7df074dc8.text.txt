### Explanation
The concept of wave-particle duality suggests that all quantum entities exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties depending on the experiment being performed.

1.  **Photoelectric Effect:** In this phenomenon, light (which was traditionally thought to be a wave) behaves as a stream of discrete packets of energy called "photons." The interaction between a single photon and a single electron demonstrates the **particle property** of light. Because the energy of the photon is given by $E = hf$ and it strikes an electron in a one-to-one collision, it confirms the particle nature.
2.  **Electron Diffraction:** Electrons are traditionally considered particles with mass and charge. However, experiments (such as the Davisson-Germer experiment) demonstrate that electrons can produce diffraction patterns when passed through a crystal lattice. Diffraction is an interference phenomenon characteristic of waves. According to the de Broglie hypothesis, electrons have a wavelength $\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$, where $p$ is the momentum. This observation confirms the **wave property** of matter.

Comparing this to the options:
- Photoelectric effect = Particle property
- Electron diffraction = Wave property

This matches row **C**.
