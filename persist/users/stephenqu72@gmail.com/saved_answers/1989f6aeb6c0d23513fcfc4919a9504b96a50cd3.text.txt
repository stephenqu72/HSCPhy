To solve this question, we must apply knowledge of atomic spectra, the Bohr model, and the Doppler effect.

### (a) Explanation of Bohr's Model
Bohr’s model postulates that electrons in atoms exist in discrete, quantized energy levels. When white light (from the incandescent filament) passes through hydrogen gas, electrons in the ground state absorb photons to transition to higher energy levels. Because the energy difference between these levels is quantized, only specific wavelengths of light are absorbed, resulting in the dark lines seen in spectrum $A$. This provides experimental evidence for the existence of discrete, stable energy levels in the atom, rather than a continuous range of energies.

### (b) Differences in Spectra
*   **Star Croesus:** The spectrum is identical to the reference spectrum $A$. The lines are at the exact same wavelengths as those produced by stationary hydrogen.
*   **Star Dromus:** The absorption lines are shifted towards shorter wavelengths (the blue/violet end of the spectrum) compared to the reference spectrum $A$.

### (c) Motion of the Stars
*   **Star Croesus:** The spectrum shows no shift relative to the reference spectrum $A$. Therefore, there is no significant radial velocity component; the star is stationary relative to Earth (or its motion is purely perpendicular to our line of sight).
*   **Star Dromus:** The spectrum exhibits a "blueshift." According to the Doppler effect, when a light source moves toward an observer, the frequency increases and the wavelength decreases. Thus, Star Dromus is moving towards Earth.
