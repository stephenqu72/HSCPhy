### Front
How can a rotating space station simulate Earth’s gravity ($g$), and what is the relationship between the station's radius ($r$) and its period of rotation ($T$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Artificial gravity is generated by centripetal acceleration ($a_c$). For an occupant to experience Earth-like gravity, the floor must exert a normal force providing $a_c = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$.
- Formula / law / constant: $a_c = \frac{4\pi^2 r}{T^2}$ where $a_c = g$.
- When to use it: Any problem involving rotating spacecraft or space stations designed to simulate gravity.
- Common trap: Confusing angular velocity ($\omega$) with the period ($T$). Ensure $T$ is in seconds and $r$ is in meters before calculating.
