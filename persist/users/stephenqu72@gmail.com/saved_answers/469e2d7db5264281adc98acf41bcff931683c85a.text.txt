### Part 1: Newton’s Third Law Explanation
Newton’s Third Law states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. In the context of two parallel current-carrying wires, each wire creates a magnetic field that exerts a force on the other wire. If the currents are in the same direction, the wires attract each other; if in opposite directions, they repel. The force exerted by wire 1 on wire 2 is equal in magnitude and opposite in direction to the force exerted by wire 2 on wire 1, regardless of the difference in currents (though in this problem, the currents are equal).

### Part 2: Calculation
To find the force between the wires, we use the formula for the force per unit length between two parallel conductors:
$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2 \pi r}$$

**Given variables:**
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \, \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A}$
- $I_1 = I_2 = 0.375 \, \text{A}$
- $r = 13.5 \, \text{mm} = 0.0135 \, \text{m}$
- $L = 0.65 \, \text{m}$

**Step-by-step Calculation:**
1. Calculate the force per unit length:
$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times (0.375) \times (0.375)}{2\pi \times 0.0135}$$
$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 0.140625}{0.0135}$$
$$\frac{F}{L} \approx 2.0833 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{N/m}$$

2. Calculate the total force $F$ by multiplying by the length $L$:
$$F = \left(\frac{F}{L}\right) \times L$$
$$F = (2.0833 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{N/m}) \times 0.65 \, \text{m}$$
$$F \approx 1.354 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{N}$$

**Final Answer:**
The force is approximately $1.35 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{N}$.
