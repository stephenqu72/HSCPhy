### Front
What is the nature and direction of the net force acting on an object undergoing uniform circular motion?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The net force is the centripetal force ($F_c$), which acts perpendicular to the velocity vector to change the object's direction.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $F_{net} = F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$; direction is always toward the center of the circular path.
- **When to use it:** Whenever an object moves at a constant speed along a curved or circular trajectory.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the *normal force* or a specific component of gravity as being synonymous with the *net centripetal force*. The centripetal force is the vector sum of all radial forces.
