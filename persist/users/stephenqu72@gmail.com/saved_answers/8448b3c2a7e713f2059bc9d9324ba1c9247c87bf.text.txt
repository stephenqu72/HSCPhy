To solve this problem, we use the formula for centripetal acceleration ($a_c$), which describes the acceleration of an object moving in a circular path at a constant speed $v$:

$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the initial state:**
    The truck travels at speed $v_i$ on a curve with radius $R$. The initial centripetal acceleration is:
    $$a_1 = \frac{v_i^2}{R}$$

2.  **Identify the final state:**
    The truck maintains the same speed $v_i$, but the radius of the curve increases to $3R$. The new centripetal acceleration is:
    $$a_2 = \frac{v_i^2}{3R}$$

3.  **Compare the two accelerations:**
    We can express $a_2$ in terms of $a_1$ by factoring out the constants:
    $$a_2 = \frac{1}{3} \times \left( \frac{v_i^2}{R} \right)$$
    $$a_2 = \frac{1}{3} a_1$$

4.  **Conclusion:**
    The new acceleration $a_2$ is one-third of the original acceleration $a_1$. Therefore, the magnitude of the centripetal acceleration decreases by a factor of 3.
