To calculate the wavelength of the photon released when an electron transitions from $n = 3$ to $n = 1$ in a Hydrogen atom, we use the Rydberg formula:

$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$

Where:
- $R_H \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \, \text{m}^{-1}$ (Rydberg constant)
- $n_f = 1$
- $n_i = 3$

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1. **Substitute the values into the formula:**
   $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{1}{1^2} - \frac{1}{3^2} \right)$$
   $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( 1 - \frac{1}{9} \right)$$
   $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{8}{9} \right)$$

2. **Calculate the value:**
   $$\frac{1}{\lambda} \approx 9.751 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}^{-1}$$

3. **Invert to solve for $\lambda$:**
   $$\lambda = \frac{1}{9.751 \times 10^6} \approx 1.0255 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}$$

4. **Convert to nanometers (nm):**
   $$1.0255 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m} \times 10^9 \, \text{nm/m} \approx 102.55 \, \text{nm}$$
   Rounding to three significant figures, we get $103 \, \text{nm}$.

*   Wavelength must be a **positive** value because it is a physical distance. This immediately eliminates options A and B.
*   Option D uses the unit $\text{Gm}^{-1}$ (inverse gigameters), which is not a standard unit for wavelength, and the magnitude is incorrect.
*   Option C ($103 \, \text{nm}$) matches our calculated value.

The correct answer is **C**.
