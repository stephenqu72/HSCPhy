To determine the correct answer, we need to apply the physics of circular motion to the two children on the rotating roundabout.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Analyze Centripetal Acceleration ($a_c$)**
The formula for centripetal acceleration is:
$$a_c = r\omega^2$$
Where:
- $r$ is the radius (distance from the axis of rotation).
- $\omega$ is the angular velocity.

Because the children are on the same rotating roundabout, they share the same angular velocity ($\omega$). We are also told both children are sitting at the same distance ($r = 2.5\text{ m}$) from the center. Since $r$ and $\omega$ are identical for both children, their centripetal accelerations must be equal:
$$a_c(P) = a_c(Q)$$

**2. Analyze Centripetal Force ($F_c$)**
The formula for centripetal force is:
$$F_c = ma_c$$
Where $m$ is the mass of the object. We know:
- $m_P = 36\text{ kg}$
- $m_Q = 45\text{ kg}$
- Since $a_c(P) = a_c(Q)$, we can compare the forces:
$$F_c(P) = 36 \cdot a_c$$
$$F_c(Q) = 45 \cdot a_c$$

Since $45 > 36$, it follows that $F_c(Q) > F_c(P)$, which is equivalent to $P < Q$ for the centripetal force.

**Conclusion:**
- Centripetal acceleration: $P = Q$
- Centripetal force: $P < Q$

Comparing this to the given options, **(B)** is the correct statement.
