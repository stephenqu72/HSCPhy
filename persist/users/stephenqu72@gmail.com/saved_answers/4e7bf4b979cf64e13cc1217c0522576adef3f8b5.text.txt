To solve these problems, we use the principles of universal gravitation. First, let's list our known values and convert them to SI units:
*   Mass of Earth ($M$): $5.97 \times 10^{24}\text{ kg}$
*   Mass of comet ($m$): $2.1 \times 10^{11}\text{ kg}$
*   Distance ($r$): $11.6\text{ million km} = 11.6 \times 10^6\text{ km} = 1.16 \times 10^{10}\text{ m}$
*   Gravitational constant ($G$): $6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2\text{ kg}^{-2}$

---

### (a) Force between the comet and the Earth
We use Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation: $F = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$

$$F = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24}) \times (2.1 \times 10^{11})}{(1.16 \times 10^{10})^2}$$
$$F = \frac{8.36 \times 10^{25}}{1.346 \times 10^{20}} \approx 6.21 \times 10^5\text{ N}$$

---

### (b) Theoretical orbital velocity for a circular orbit
For an object to maintain a circular orbit, the gravitational force provides the centripetal force: $\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$, which simplifies to $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24})}{1.16 \times 10^{10}}}$$
$$v = \sqrt{3.43 \times 10^4} \approx 185.3\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

---

### (c) Escape velocity and explanation
The formula for escape velocity is $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$

$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24})}{1.16 \times 10^{10}}}$$
$$v_e = \sqrt{6.86 \times 10^4} \approx 262.0\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

**Explanation:** The actual velocity of the comet is $10\text{ km s}^{-1} = 10,000\text{ m s}^{-1}$. Since $10,000\text{ m s}^{-1} > 262.0\text{ m s}^{-1}$, the comet's speed significantly exceeds the escape velocity of the Earth at that distance. Therefore, the Earth's gravity is insufficient to capture the comet into an orbit, causing it to follow a hyperbolic trajectory past the Earth.
