To solve this problem, we use Einstein's mass-energy equivalence principle, $E = mc^2$.

### Part (a)
**Objective:** Calculate the mass ($m$) converted to energy per second.
1. **Identify the constants:**
   - Power (energy per second) $E = 3.86 \times 10^{26} \text{ J}$
   - Speed of light $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
2. **Rearrange the formula:** 
   $$m = \frac{E}{c^2}$$
3. **Calculation:**
   $$m = \frac{3.86 \times 10^{26}}{(3.00 \times 10^8)^2}$$
   $$m = \frac{3.86 \times 10^{26}}{9.00 \times 10^{16}}$$
   $$m \approx 4.29 \times 10^9 \text{ kg}$$

**Answer (a):** Approximately $4.29 \times 10^9 \text{ kg}$ of matter is converted into energy each second.

### Part (b)
**Objective:** Determine the number of helium-4 ($He$-$4$) nuclei produced per second.

*Note for students:* In the proton-proton chain reaction, mass is lost during the fusion of hydrogen into helium. Specifically, the conversion of $4 \text{ protons}$ into $1 \text{ helium nucleus}$ releases approximately $4.27 \times 10^{-12} \text{ J}$ of energy (or a mass defect of $\approx 0.0475 \text{ u}$). 

1. **Energy released per $He$-$4$ nucleus:**
   The mass defect ($\Delta m$) for one $He$-$4$ nucleus produced from 4 protons ($4 \times 1.6726 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg} \approx 6.6904 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$) is:
   $$\Delta m = (4 \times \text{mass of proton}) - \text{mass of } He\text{-}4$$
   $$\Delta m = (6.6904 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) - (6.648 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) = 4.24 \times 10^{-29} \text{ kg}$$
   Energy released per fusion event: $E_{fusion} = \Delta m c^2$
   $$E_{fusion} = (4.24 \times 10^{-29}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)^2 \approx 3.816 \times 10^{-12} \text{ J}$$

2. **Number of nuclei:**
   $$\text{Number} = \frac{\text{Total Energy per second}}{\text{Energy per fusion}}$$
   $$\text{Number} = \frac{3.86 \times 10^{26}}{3.816 \times 10^{-12}} \approx 1.01 \times 10^{38} \text{ nuclei}$$

**Answer (b):** Approximately $1.01 \times 10^{38}$ helium-4 nuclei are produced per second.
