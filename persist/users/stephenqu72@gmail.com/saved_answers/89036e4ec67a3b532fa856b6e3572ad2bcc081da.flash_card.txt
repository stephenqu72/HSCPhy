### Front
How does surface gravity ($g$) change if a planet’s mass ($M$) and radius ($r$) are both halved?

### Back
- Key idea: Surface gravity is proportional to mass and inversely proportional to the square of the radius ($g \propto M/r^2$).
- Formula: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- When to use it: Any orbital or gravitational field strength calculation involving planetary bodies.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the radius reduction. If $r$ becomes $1/2$, the denominator becomes $1/4$, effectively multiplying the gravity by $4$. (Final result: $g' = 2g$).
