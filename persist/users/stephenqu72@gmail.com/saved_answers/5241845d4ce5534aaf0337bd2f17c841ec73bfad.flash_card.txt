### Front
How do you calculate the gravitational field strength ($g$) of a planet?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational field strength depends on the mass of the planet and the square of the distance from its centre.
- **Formula:** $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- **When to use it:** To determine acceleration due to gravity at a specific distance from a celestial body.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert distance ($r$) from kilometres to metres before squaring, or neglecting to add the planet's radius if given an altitude above the surface ($r = R_{planet} + h$).
