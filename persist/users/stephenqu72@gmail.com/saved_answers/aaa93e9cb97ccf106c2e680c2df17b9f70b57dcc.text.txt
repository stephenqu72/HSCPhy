To solve this projectile motion problem, we first identify our known variables using the standard coordinate system (up is positive, ground level is $0\text{ m}$):

- Initial height $y_0 = 0.8\text{ m}$
- Initial velocity $v = 17\text{ ms}^{-1}$
- Launch angle $\theta = 45^\circ$
- Acceleration $a_y = -9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$
- Initial vertical velocity $u_y = 17 \sin(45^\circ) \approx 12.021\text{ ms}^{-1}$
- Initial horizontal velocity $u_x = 17 \cos(45^\circ) \approx 12.021\text{ ms}^{-1}$

---

### (a) How long did it take for the bottle rocket to reach the ground?

We use the displacement formula for the vertical direction:
$$y = y_0 + u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$$
Setting the final height $y = 0$:
$$0 = 0.8 + 12.021t - 4.9t^2$$
Rearranging into a quadratic equation:
$$4.9t^2 - 12.021t - 0.8 = 0$$
Using the quadratic formula $t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$$t = \frac{12.021 \pm \sqrt{(-12.021)^2 - 4(4.9)(-0.8)}}{9.8}$$
$$t = \frac{12.021 \pm \sqrt{144.50 + 15.68}}{9.8} = \frac{12.021 \pm 12.657}{9.8}$$
Taking the positive result: $t \approx 2.518\text{ s}$. 
Rounding to two significant figures, it took **$2.52\text{ s}$**.

---

### (b) Calculate the final speed of the rocket?

The final speed $v_f$ is the magnitude of the final velocity vector: $v_f = \sqrt{v_{xf}^2 + v_{yf}^2}$.

1. **Horizontal velocity:** $v_{xf} = u_x = 12.021\text{ ms}^{-1}$ (remains constant).
2. **Vertical velocity:** $v_{yf} = u_y + a_y t$
   $$v_{yf} = 12.021 + (-9.8)(2.518) \approx 12.021 - 24.676 = -12.655\text{ ms}^{-1}$$
3. **Resultant speed:**
   $$v_f = \sqrt{12.021^2 + (-12.655)^2} = \sqrt{144.50 + 160.15} = \sqrt{304.65} \approx 17.45\text{ ms}^{-1}$$

The final speed of the rocket is approximately **$17.5\text{ ms}^{-1}$**.
