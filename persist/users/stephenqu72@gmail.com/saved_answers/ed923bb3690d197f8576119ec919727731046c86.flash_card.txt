### Front
How does the recession velocity vs. distance graph indicate an accelerating universe?

### Back
- Key idea: The graph curves upward (the gradient increases), showing that distant galaxies are moving faster than predicted by a linear Hubble Law.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = H_0d$ (Hubble’s Law) represents the linear baseline; an accelerating universe deviates upward from this line.
- When to use it: To interpret cosmic expansion models based on Type Ia supernova data.
- Common trap: Confusing an upward-curving graph (accelerating expansion) with a downward-curving graph (decelerating expansion).
