### Answer and Explanation

To solve this problem, we first extract the given values:
- Radius $r = 0.300\text{ m}$
- Frequency $f = 450\text{ rev/min} = \frac{450}{60}\text{ rev/s} = 7.5\text{ rev/s}$
- Mass $m = 0.025\text{ kg}$

#### (a) What is the angular speed of the sock in rad/s?
The angular speed $\omega$ is related to the frequency $f$ by the formula:
$$\omega = 2\pi f$$
Substituting the value for $f$:
$$\omega = 2 \times \pi \times 7.5$$
$$\omega = 15\pi \approx 47.12\text{ rad/s}$$

#### (b) Calculate the magnitude and direction of the centripetal force on the sock.
The centripetal force $F_c$ is calculated using the formula:
$$F_c = mr\omega^2$$
Substituting our known values:
$$F_c = 0.025 \times 0.300 \times (15\pi)^2$$
$$F_c = 0.0075 \times 225\pi^2$$
$$F_c \approx 0.0075 \times 2220.66 \approx 16.65\text{ N}$$

**Direction:** The direction of the centripetal force is always directed **radially inward** toward the centre of the drum (the axis of rotation).

---

### How to solve this type of problem:
1. **Unit Conversion:** Always ensure your frequency is in revolutions per second (Hz) before calculating angular speed. Note that $1\text{ rev} = 2\pi\text{ radians}$.
2. **Standard Formulas:** Memorize the relationships between linear velocity ($v$), angular velocity ($\omega$), and centripetal acceleration/force. Using $\omega$ directly in $F = mr\omega^2$ is often faster than converting to $v$ first.
3. **Conceptual Understanding:** Remember that "centripetal" literally means "center-seeking." Whenever an object moves in a circle, the net force must point toward the center of that circle.
