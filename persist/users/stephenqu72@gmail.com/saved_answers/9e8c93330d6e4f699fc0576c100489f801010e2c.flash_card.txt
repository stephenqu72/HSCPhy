### Front
How do you determine the charge polarity induced on a conductor moving through a magnetic field?

### Back
- Key idea: Use the Lorentz force ($F = qv \times B$) to determine the path of charge carriers.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Right-hand rule (positive charge) or Left-hand rule (electrons); $F = qvB\sin\theta$.
- When to use it: When a conductor moves through an external magnetic field (Electromagnetic Induction/Motor Effect).
- Common trap: Forgetting that electrons move in the opposite direction to "conventional current" when using hand rules.
