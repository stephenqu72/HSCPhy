The correct answer is **A. Hubble’s Law**.

### Explanation:

To solve this problem, we need to interpret the visual data provided in the spectral lines of galaxies compared to the "Expected Result" (a laboratory reference spectrum).

1.  **Analyze the diagrams:** The diagrams display absorption spectra. In the "Close Galaxies," the black lines are shifted slightly towards the red end of the spectrum compared to the "Expected Result." In "Distant Galaxies," these lines are shifted even further towards the red end. This phenomenon is known as **Redshift**.
2.  **Relate Redshift to Physics:** According to the Doppler effect for light, when a light source moves away from an observer, its light waves are stretched, resulting in an increase in wavelength (a shift towards the red end of the spectrum).
3.  **Identify Hubble’s Law:** Edwin Hubble observed that virtually all galaxies are redshifted and, crucially, that the degree of redshift (recessional velocity) is proportional to the distance of the galaxy from Earth. This relationship, expressed as $v = H_0 d$, is known as **Hubble's Law**, which serves as primary observational evidence for the expansion of the universe.
4.  **Evaluate other options:**
    *   **Millikan’s Oil Drop** experiment was used to determine the elementary charge of an electron.
    *   **Bohr’s Hydrogen Atom** model explains atomic line spectra based on electron energy levels but does not describe galactic recession.
    *   **Einstein’s Special Relativity** deals with physics in inertial frames of reference, particularly at high speeds, and while it incorporates time dilation and length contraction, the specific observation of galactic distance-velocity proportionality is the hallmark of Hubble's work.
