To solve this problem, we must apply the formula for gravitational potential energy ($U$) of a mass $m$ at a distance $r$ from the center of a planet with mass $M$:

$$U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Understand the Geometry:** The problem states the diagram is "drawn to scale." We need to determine the distances of $X$ and $Y$ from the center of the Earth.
    *   Let $R$ be the radius of the Earth.
    *   By measuring the diagram:
        *   The distance from the center of the Earth to point $X$ is approximately $2R$.
        *   The distance from the center of the Earth to point $Y$ is approximately $3R$.
        *   *(Note: Using a ruler on screen, the radius of Earth is 1 unit, the distance to $X$ is 2 units, and the distance to $Y$ is 3 units.)*

2.  **Set up the Ratio:**
    We want to find the ratio $\frac{U_x}{U_y}$:
    $$\frac{U_x}{U_y} = \frac{-\frac{GMm}{r_x}}{-\frac{GMm}{r_y}}$$
    The constants $G$, $M$, and $m$ cancel out:
    $$\frac{U_x}{U_y} = \frac{r_y}{r_x}$$

3.  **Substitute the Values:**
    From our scale measurement:
    *   $r_x = 2$
    *   $r_y = 3$
    
    Therefore:
    $$\frac{U_x}{U_y} = \frac{3}{2} = 1.5$$

4.  **Conclusion:**
    The ratio of the gravitational potential energy at $X$ to that at $Y$ is $1.5$. This corresponds to option **C**.

**Answer:**
The correct option is **C**.
