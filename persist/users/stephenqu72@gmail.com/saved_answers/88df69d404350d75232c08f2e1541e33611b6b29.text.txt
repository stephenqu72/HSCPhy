To solve this problem, we need to analyze the orbital mechanics of a planet in an elliptical orbit by looking at how its distance from the star changes as it moves along the path indicated.

### **Step-by-Step Solution**

**1. Identify the direction of motion and change in distance ($r$)**
Looking at the diagram, the star is located at one of the foci of the ellipse, specifically positioned towards the left side. The planet is currently on the upper part of the orbit, and the red arrow indicates that the planet is moving **towards the left** (counter-clockwise). 

As the planet moves from its current position toward the left, it is moving closer to the star (approaching the periapsis, or the closest point in the orbit). Therefore, the orbital radius $r$ is **decreasing**.

**2. Analyze Gravitational Potential Energy ($U$)**
The formula for gravitational potential energy is:
$$U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$$
Where:
*   $G$ is the gravitational constant.
*   $M$ is the mass of the star.
*   $m$ is the mass of the planet.
*   $r$ is the distance between them.

Since $r$ is **decreasing**, the magnitude of the fraction $\frac{GMm}{r}$ is **increasing**. However, because of the negative sign in the formula, as this value gets larger, the total energy becomes "more negative" (e.g., moving from $-10\text{ J}$ to $-20\text{ J}$). In mathematics and physics, moving toward a more negative value is defined as a **decrease**. 
Therefore, **gravitational potential energy is decreasing.**

**3. Analyze Kinetic Energy ($K$)**
According to the law of conservation of mechanical energy, the total energy $E$ of the orbit remains constant:
$$E = K + U = \text{constant}$$
If the gravitational potential energy $U$ is decreasing (becoming more negative), the kinetic energy $K$ **must increase** to keep the total energy $E$ constant. As the planet falls closer to the star, it speeds up.

**4. Analyze Acceleration ($a$)**
The magnitude of the gravitational acceleration experienced by the planet is given by:
$$a = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$
Since the distance $r$ is **decreasing**, the denominator $r^2$ is also decreasing. This causes the overall value of the acceleration $a$ to **increase**.

**5. Analyze Total Energy ($E$)**
In an isolated system like this, there are no external torques or non-conservative forces (like friction) acting on the planet. Therefore, the total mechanical energy $E$ remains **constant**.

### **Conclusion**
*   **A. acceleration:** Increasing
*   **B. gravitational potential energy: Decreasing**
*   **C. kinetic energy:** Increasing
*   **D. total energy:** Constant

The correct answer is **B**.
