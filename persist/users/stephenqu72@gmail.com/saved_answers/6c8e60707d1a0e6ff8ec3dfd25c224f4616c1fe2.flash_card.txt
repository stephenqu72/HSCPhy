### Front
Why does banking a track allow a cyclist to travel faster around a corner compared to a flat track?

### Back
- Key idea: The horizontal component of the normal force ($F_N \sin\theta$) provides additional centripetal force, reducing reliance on friction.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{c} = F_N \sin\theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: Any question involving circular motion on banked surfaces (cyclists, cars, planes).
- Common trap: Forgetting that friction is *not* entirely eliminated; it is merely supplemented by the normal force component.
