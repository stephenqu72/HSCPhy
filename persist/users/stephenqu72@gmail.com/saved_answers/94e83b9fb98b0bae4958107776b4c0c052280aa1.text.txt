### Answer
One common risk in a projectile motion experiment is the potential for injury from the projectile (e.g., a steel ball or marble) rolling off the lab bench or striking someone. To manage this risk, I ensured the projectile was launched into a designated "landing zone" padded with soft material, such as a foam block or folded towel. Additionally, I cleared the surrounding floor area of any obstacles to prevent trips or falls while students were moving to measure the landing point, ensuring a safe working environment.

### How to solve this question
This is a standard "Working Scientifically" question frequently found in HSC Physics exams. To secure full marks (2 marks), you must follow these steps:

1.  **Identify a specific risk:** Do not be vague. Instead of saying "it was dangerous," specify *what* the danger was (e.g., "tripping over equipment," "being hit by the projectile," or "broken glass").
2.  **Describe the mitigation strategy:** Clearly explain the *action* taken to reduce or eliminate that risk.
3.  **Link the two:** Ensure your explanation explicitly links the identified hazard to the safety measure implemented.

**Teacher's Tip:** When discussing experimental risks, always think about the **HAZARD** (the source of harm) and the **CONTROL MEASURE** (how you managed it). Use the format: "A risk identified was [Hazard]. To manage this, I [Control Measure]." This structure ensures you hit the marking criteria immediately.
