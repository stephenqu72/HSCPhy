### Answer

**(a)** In deep space, occupants are in a state of **microgravity** (often referred to as weightlessness) because they are effectively in constant free-fall while orbiting or moving through space. Without gravity to hold them to the floor, they would float. Magnetic boots are used to provide an artificial "grip" onto the steel floor, allowing occupants to walk and remain stationary without drifting away.

**(b)** To find the period of rotation, we use the formula for centripetal acceleration:
$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} = \frac{4\pi^2 r}{T^2}$$
Given that $a_c = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$ (equivalent to Earth's gravity) and $r = 500\text{ m}$, we rearrange for the period $T$:
$$T = \sqrt{\frac{4\pi^2 r}{a_c}} = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{500}{9.8}}$$
$$T \approx 2\pi \sqrt{51.02} \approx 2\pi \times 7.14$$
$$T \approx 44.87\text{ seconds}$$

    *   **Substitute values:** Plug in $r = 500\text{ m}$ and $a = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$.
    *   **Algebraic manipulation:** Solve for $T$. Always ensure your units are consistent (meters and seconds are SI standard, so no conversion is required).
