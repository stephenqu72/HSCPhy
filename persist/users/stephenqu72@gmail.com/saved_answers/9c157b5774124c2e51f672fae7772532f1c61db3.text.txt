To solve this problem, you need to understand the relationship between the **binding energy per nucleon** and the stability of a nucleus.

1.  **The Binding Energy Curve:** The stability of a nucleus is indicated by its binding energy per nucleon. The highest point on the curve is held by Iron ($Fe^{56}$), which is the most stable nucleus. 
2.  **Nuclear Fission:** Nuclei that are heavier than iron (like $U^{235}$) have a lower binding energy per nucleon than intermediate nuclei. They can release energy by undergoing **fission**, where a heavy nucleus splits into smaller, more stable nuclei with higher binding energy per nucleon. Thus, $U^{235}$ undergoes fission.
3.  **Nuclear Fusion:** Nuclei that are lighter than iron (like $O^{16}$) have lower binding energy per nucleon than intermediate nuclei. They can release energy by undergoing **fusion**, where smaller, lighter nuclei combine to form a larger, more stable nucleus with higher binding energy per nucleon. Thus, $O^{16}$ can participate in fusion reactions to form more stable elements (moving toward the peak of the curve).

*   $O^{16}$ is relatively light, so it undergoes **fusion**.
