To solve this problem, we must apply the laws of conservation of mass number and atomic number in a nuclear decay equation.

### Answer
The correct option is **(C)**.

### Explanation
An alpha particle ($\alpha$) is a helium nucleus, represented as $^4_2\text{He}$. When a nucleus undergoes alpha decay, it emits this particle, meaning the parent nucleus loses 4 units of mass and 2 units of charge (atomic number).

The general equation for alpha decay is:
$${}^{A}_{Z}\text{X} \rightarrow {}^{A-4}_{Z-2}\text{Y} + {}^{4}_{2}\text{He}$$

1.  **Identify the Parent Nucleus:**
    Uranium-238 ($^{238}\text{U}$) has a mass number $A = 238$ and an atomic number $Z = 92$.

2.  **Apply Conservation Laws:**
    *   **Mass Number ($A$):** The daughter nucleus must have a mass number of $238 - 4 = 234$.
    *   **Atomic Number ($Z$):** The daughter nucleus must have an atomic number of $92 - 2 = 90$.

3.  **Conclusion:**
    The daughter nucleus has an atomic number of 90 and a mass number of 234. Looking at the options provided, **(C) 90 and 234** is the correct choice.
