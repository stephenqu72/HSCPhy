### Part (a): Mass vs. Weight
**Answer:** Mass is a measure of the amount of matter in an object and is constant regardless of location. Weight is the force exerted on an object by a gravitational field ($W = mg$), which varies depending on the strength of the local gravitational field.

**Explanation:** In physics, mass is a scalar quantity measured in kilograms (kg), representing inertia. Weight is a vector quantity (force) measured in Newtons (N). Always emphasize that weight is dependent on gravity, while mass is an intrinsic property.

---

### Part (b): Is the spacecraft weightless?
**Answer:** No, the spacecraft is not weightless. 

**Explanation:** "Weightlessness" (or apparent weightlessness) refers to a state of freefall where there is no normal reaction force acting on an object. However, the gravitational force (weight) is still acting on the spacecraft and its occupants; this force is exactly what provides the centripetal acceleration required to keep the spacecraft in orbit.

---

### Part (c): Calculation of Gravitational Potential Energy
**Formula:** The gravitational potential energy $U$ of a system of two masses is given by:
$$U = -\frac{G m_1 m_2}{r}$$

**Given data:**
- $G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$
- $m_1 = 1.84 \times 10^{30} \text{ kg}$
- $m_2 = 2.25 \times 10^{30} \text{ kg}$
- $r = 150 \times 10^6 \text{ km} = 150 \times 10^9 \text{ m}$

**Calculation:**
$$U = -\frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \times (1.84 \times 10^{30}) \times (2.25 \times 10^{30})}{150 \times 10^9}$$
$$U = -\frac{2.7622 \times 10^{50}}{1.5 \times 10^{11}}$$
$$U \approx -1.84 \times 10^{39} \text{ J}$$

**Methodology:** Always convert distance into SI units (meters) first. The negative sign is crucial as it indicates a bound system where energy must be added to separate the stars to infinity.
