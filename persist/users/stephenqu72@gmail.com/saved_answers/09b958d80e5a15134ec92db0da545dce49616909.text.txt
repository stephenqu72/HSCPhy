### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) binding energy**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, you need to understand the concept of nuclear stability and the forces acting within the nucleus.

1.  **The Concept:** The protons and neutrons (collectively known as nucleons) inside an atomic nucleus are held together by the **strong nuclear force**. This force is incredibly powerful at very short distances, overcoming the electrostatic repulsion between positively charged protons.
2.  **Definition of Binding Energy:** Because the strong nuclear force is attractive, the nucleons are in a lower energy state when they are bound together in a nucleus compared to when they are isolated. Therefore, to separate a nucleus into its individual constituent nucleons, you must add energy to the system to "break" these bonds. This specific amount of energy required to disassemble the nucleus is known as the **binding energy**.
3.  **Why the others are incorrect:**
    *   **(A) The CNO cycle** is a process of stellar nucleosynthesis where stars convert hydrogen into helium; it is not a term used for the energy required to disassemble a nucleus.
    *   **(B) Gravitational potential energy** relates to the interaction between masses, which is negligible on the nuclear scale compared to the strong nuclear force.
    *   **(C) Elastic potential energy** is energy stored in objects (like springs) due to compression or extension, which is not the force mechanism behind nuclear binding.

By understanding that energy must be *inputted* to overcome the strong nuclear force that holds the nucleus together, you can identify that **binding energy** is the correct terminology for this requirement.
