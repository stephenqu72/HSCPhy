### Front
How does the electric field strength ($E$) between parallel plates change as the plate separation ($d$) increases, given a constant voltage source ($V$)?

### Back
- Key idea: $E$ is inversely proportional to $d$ ($E \propto 1/d$); the relationship follows a reciprocal curve (hyperbola).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E = \frac{V}{d}$; No fixed constant.
- When to use it: When analyzing capacitors connected to a constant voltage source where plate distance is varied.
- Common trap: Assuming a linear decrease; the relationship is non-linear (inverse).
