### Front
Under what condition does nuclear fission release energy, according to the binding energy per nucleon curve?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Fission releases energy only if the products have a higher average binding energy per nucleon than the parent nucleus (moving toward the peak at Iron-56).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Binding Energy per Nucleon vs. Mass Number ($A$) graph.
- **When to use it:** To determine if a fission or fusion reaction is exothermic (energy-releasing) or endothermic (energy-absorbing).
- **Common trap:** Assuming all fission reactions release energy; light nuclei (like Helium) actually require an energy input to undergo fission because they move *down* the binding energy curve.
