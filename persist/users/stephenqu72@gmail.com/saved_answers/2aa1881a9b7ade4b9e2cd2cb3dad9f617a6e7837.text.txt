To solve this problem, we must apply **Hubble’s Law**, which describes the relationship between the distance of a galaxy ($d$) and its recessional velocity ($v$). 

Hubble’s Law is expressed as:
$$v = H_0 \cdot d$$
where $H_0$ is the Hubble constant.

1.  **Identify the mathematical relationship:** The equation $v = H_0 \cdot d$ is in the form of a linear equation ($y = mx$), where $v$ is the vertical axis, $d$ is the horizontal axis, and $H_0$ is the slope. Therefore, the graph of recessional velocity versus distance must be a **straight line passing through the origin**.
2.  **Evaluate the axes:** We must check the units used on the axes to ensure physical consistency. 
    *   Graph A uses units of $\times 10^6 \text{ pc}$ (megaparsecs) for distance. This is the standard unit used in Hubble's Law.
    *   Graph B uses light years. While velocity and distance are proportional regardless of the distance unit, physicists conventionally define the Hubble constant relative to megaparsecs (Mpc).
