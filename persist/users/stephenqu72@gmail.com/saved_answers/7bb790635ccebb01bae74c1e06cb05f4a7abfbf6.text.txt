To calculate the de Broglie wavelength of the proton, we use the de Broglie relation:

$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$$

Where:
*   $\lambda$ is the de Broglie wavelength (m)
*   $h$ is Planck's constant, approximately $6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$
*   $p$ is the momentum of the particle ($5.1 \times 10^{-19} \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$)

### Step-by-step Calculation:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   $p = 5.1 \times 10^{-19} \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$
    *   $h \approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$

2.  **Substitute into the formula:**
    $$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{5.1 \times 10^{-19}}$$

3.  **Perform the division:**
    $$\lambda \approx 1.299 \times 10^{-15} \text{ m}$$

4.  **Rounding:**
    Rounding to two significant figures (matching the precision of the given momentum), we get $\lambda \approx 1.3 \times 10^{-15} \text{ m}$.
