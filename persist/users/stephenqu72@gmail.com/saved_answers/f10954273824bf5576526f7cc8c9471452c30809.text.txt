### Answer
The correct answer is **(D)**.

### Explanation
To determine the correct answer, we must evaluate the scientific validity of each option regarding nuclear fusion technology:

*   **(A) Incorrect:** Nuclear fusion is extremely energy-dense. In fact, it releases significantly more energy per unit mass than nuclear fission. This is the very reason scientists are attempting to harness it for power generation.
*   **(B) Incorrect:** The primary fuel for fusion (deuterium) can be extracted from seawater, and tritium can be bred from lithium. These resources are effectively abundant, meaning there is no shortage of fuel.
*   **(C) Incorrect:** While the neutrons produced in some fusion reactions can make reactor materials radioactive, the products of the fusion reaction itself (such as helium) are not radioactive. This is a significant safety advantage over fission, which produces long-lived, highly radioactive waste products.
*   **(D) Correct:** Nuclear fusion requires overcoming the electrostatic repulsion (Coulomb barrier) between positively charged nuclei. To achieve this, temperatures in the range of millions of degrees Celsius are required, along with sufficient pressure and confinement time. Maintaining these extreme conditions (plasma confinement) in a stable and controlled manner for a net-energy-producing reaction is one of the greatest technological challenges in physics today.
