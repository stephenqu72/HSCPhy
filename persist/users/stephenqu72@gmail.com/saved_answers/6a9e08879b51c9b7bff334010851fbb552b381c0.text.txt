### Answer
James Clerk Maxwell’s primary contribution was the synthesis and unification of existing laws of electricity and magnetism into a single, cohesive mathematical framework known as **Maxwell’s Equations**.

Key aspects of his contribution include:
1.  **Unification of Electromagnetic Phenomena:** He mathematically consolidated Gauss’s Law, Gauss’s Law for Magnetism, Faraday’s Law of Induction, and Ampère’s Law (with his critical addition), proving that electricity and magnetism are two aspects of a single electromagnetic field.
2.  **The Displacement Current:** Maxwell recognized that the existing version of Ampère’s Law was incomplete. He introduced the "displacement current" ($I_d = \epsilon_0 \frac{d\Phi_E}{dt}$), which accounts for the magnetic field generated by changing electric fields, even in the absence of a conduction current.
3.  **Prediction of Electromagnetic Waves:** By combining his equations, he derived the wave equation, showing that oscillating electric and magnetic fields propagate through space as a self-sustaining electromagnetic wave.
4.  **The Velocity of Light:** Maxwell calculated the speed of these electromagnetic waves using the constants of permittivity ($\epsilon_0$) and permeability ($\mu_0$):
    $$v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0}}$$
    The resulting value matched the experimentally measured speed of light, leading him to the revolutionary conclusion that light is an electromagnetic wave.
