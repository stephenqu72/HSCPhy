To solve this problem, we use Einstein's mass-energy equivalence formula: 

$$E = mc^2$$

where:
- $E$ is the energy produced in Joules ($J$)
- $m$ is the mass lost in kilograms ($kg$)
- $c$ is the speed of light, approximately $3.00 \times 10^8 \, m/s$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Calculate the total energy ($E$) released:**
The bomb has a yield of 11 tonnes of TNT, and each tonne produces $4.2 \times 10^9 \, J$.
$$E = 11 \times 4.2 \times 10^9 \, J = 4.62 \times 10^{10} \, J$$

**2. Rearrange the formula to solve for mass ($m$):**
$$m = \frac{E}{c^2}$$

**3. Substitute the values:**
Using $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \, m/s$:
$$m = \frac{4.62 \times 10^{10}}{(3.00 \times 10^8)^2}$$
$$m = \frac{4.62 \times 10^{10}}{9.00 \times 10^{16}}$$
$$m \approx 5.133 \times 10^{-7} \, kg$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches option (B).

**Correct Answer:** **(B)**
