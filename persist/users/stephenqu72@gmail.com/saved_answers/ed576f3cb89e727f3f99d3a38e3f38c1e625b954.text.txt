To identify the correct answer, let's analyze the function of the components in a nuclear fission reactor:

1.  **Component X (Moderator):** In a water-moderated fission reactor, the substance surrounding the fuel rods (often water or graphite) acts as a **moderator**. Its purpose is to collide with the high-speed neutrons produced by fission, reducing their kinetic energy. This "slows down" the neutrons so that they have a higher probability of being captured by $U-235$ nuclei to sustain the chain reaction.
2.  **Component Y (Control Rods):** These are inserted into the core to regulate the rate of the chain reaction. They are made of materials (such as boron or cadmium) that are highly effective at **absorbing neutrons**. By inserting or withdrawing these rods, operators can control the number of available neutrons, effectively controlling the power output or shutting down the reactor.
