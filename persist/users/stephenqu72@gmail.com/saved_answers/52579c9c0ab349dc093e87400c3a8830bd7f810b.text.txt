To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of circular motion and the conservation of energy.

### Step 1: Determine the required speed at the top of the loop
For passengers to feel "weightless" at the top of the loop, the normal force $N$ exerted by the seat must be zero. The only force providing the centripetal force at the top is gravity ($mg$).
Setting the net force equal to the centripetal force:
$$F_{net} = mg = \frac{mv_{top}^2}{r}$$
Solving for the speed at the top ($v_{top}$):
$$v_{top}^2 = gr$$
Given the diameter of the loop is $10\text{ m}$, the radius $r = 5\text{ m}$. Thus, $v_{top}^2 = 5g$.

### Step 2: Use the Conservation of Energy
We need to find the speed at the bottom of the loop ($v_{bottom}$) that allows the coaster to reach this state. We set the potential energy at the bottom to $0$. The total energy at the bottom must equal the total energy at the top.
$$E_{bottom} = E_{top}$$
$$\frac{1}{2}mv_{bottom}^2 = \frac{1}{2}mv_{top}^2 + mgh$$
The height $h$ at the top is the diameter of the loop: $h = 2r = 10\text{ m}$.
Substituting the values:
$$\frac{1}{2}mv_{bottom}^2 = \frac{1}{2}m(5g) + mg(10)$$
Divide by $m$ and multiply by $2$:
$$v_{bottom}^2 = 5g + 20g$$
$$v_{bottom}^2 = 25g$$
$$v_{bottom} = \sqrt{25g}$$
