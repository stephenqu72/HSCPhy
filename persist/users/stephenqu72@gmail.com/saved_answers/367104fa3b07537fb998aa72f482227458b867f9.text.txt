### Answer
The correct answer is **B. $1/4 F$**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we look at the standard formula for centripetal force:
$$F = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Where:
* $m$ is the mass of the object.
* $v$ is the velocity.
* $r$ is the radius of the circular path.

In this scenario, the mass ($m$) and the radius of the drum ($r$) remain constant. Therefore, we can express the relationship between force and velocity as:
$$F \propto v^2$$

If the new velocity becomes $v' = \frac{1}{2}v$, we substitute this into the proportional relationship to find the new force, $F'$:
$$F' \propto (v')^2$$
$$F' \propto (\frac{1}{2}v)^2$$
$$F' \propto \frac{1}{4}v^2$$

Since the original force $F$ is proportional to $v^2$, we can see that the new force is:
$$F' = \frac{1}{4}F$$

This demonstrates that when the velocity is halved, the centripetal force is reduced to one-quarter of its original value due to the squared relationship in the formula.
