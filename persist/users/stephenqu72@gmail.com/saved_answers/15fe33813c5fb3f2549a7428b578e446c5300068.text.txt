To solve this problem, we use the **Rydberg formula** for hydrogen, which relates the wavelength ($\lambda$) of emitted light to the energy levels of an electron transition:

$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$

Where:
*   $R_H$ is the Rydberg constant, approximately $1.097 \times 10^7 \, \text{m}^{-1}$.
*   $n_f$ is the final energy level ($n = 2$).
*   $n_i$ is the initial energy level ($n = 5$).

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1.  **Identify the values:** $n_f = 2$, $n_i = 5$.
2.  **Substitute into the formula:**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{1}{2^2} - \frac{1}{5^2} \right)$$
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{25} \right)$$
3.  **Calculate the fraction:**
    $$\left( \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{25} \right) = \left( 0.25 - 0.04 \right) = 0.21$$
4.  **Solve for $1/\lambda$:**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \times 0.21 \approx 2.3037 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}^{-1}$$
5.  **Solve for $\lambda$:**
    $$\lambda = \frac{1}{2.3037 \times 10^6} \approx 4.34 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}$$
