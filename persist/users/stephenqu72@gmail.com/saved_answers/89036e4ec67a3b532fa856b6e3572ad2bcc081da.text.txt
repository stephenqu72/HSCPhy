To solve this problem, we use the formula for the surface gravity of a planet, which is derived from Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

where:
*   $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
*   $M$ is the mass of the planet.
*   $r$ is the radius of the planet.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Define the initial state:** 
    The initial surface gravity is $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$.

2.  **Define the new state:** 
    The problem states that both the mass and the radius are halved. Let $M'$ and $r'$ be the new mass and radius:
    *   $M' = \frac{M}{2}$
    *   $r' = \frac{r}{2}$

3.  **Substitute into the formula:** 
    Calculate the new surface gravity $g'$ by substituting $M'$ and $r'$ into the gravity equation:
    $$g' = \frac{G \cdot M'}{(r')^2}$$
    $$g' = \frac{G \cdot (\frac{M}{2})}{(\frac{r}{2})^2}$$

4.  **Simplify the expression:**
    $$g' = \frac{\frac{GM}{2}}{\frac{r^2}{4}}$$
    $$g' = \frac{GM}{2} \cdot \frac{4}{r^2}$$
    $$g' = 2 \cdot \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

5.  **Relate back to the original gravity:**
    Since $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$, we can substitute $g$ into our result:
    $$g' = 2g$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct choice is **(D)**.
