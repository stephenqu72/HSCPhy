To solve this projectile motion problem, we must analyze the horizontal and vertical components of the motion separately.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. Identify the vertical velocity at the peak:**
The spear reaches its maximum height at $t = 1.5\text{ s}$. At this point, the vertical component of velocity is zero ($v_y = 0$). Using the equation $v_y = u_y + a_y t$, where $a_y = -9.8\text{ m/s}^2$:
$$0 = u_y - (9.8)(1.5)$$
$$u_y = 14.7\text{ m/s}$$

**2. Relate horizontal and vertical velocity at launch:**
The spear is launched at $45^\circ$. Therefore:
$$\tan(45^\circ) = \frac{u_y}{u_x}$$
Since $\tan(45^\circ) = 1$, we have $u_x = u_y = 14.7\text{ m/s}$. Because there is no air resistance, $u_x$ remains constant throughout the flight.

**3. Analyze the landing condition:**
The spear lands at $60^\circ$ to the horizontal. Let $v_{yf}$ be the final vertical velocity at landing. 
$$\tan(60^\circ) = \frac{|v_{yf}|}{u_x}$$
$$|v_{yf}| = u_x \cdot \tan(60^\circ)$$
$$|v_{yf}| = 14.7 \cdot \sqrt{3} \approx 14.7 \cdot 1.732 \approx 25.46\text{ m/s}$$

**4. Calculate the time from peak to landing:**
From the peak (where $v_y = 0$) to the ground, the time taken ($t_2$) can be found using $v_{yf} = v_{y,peak} + a_y t_2$:
$$-25.46 = 0 - 9.8 \cdot t_2$$
$$t_2 = \frac{25.46}{9.8} \approx 2.60\text{ s}$$

**5. Calculate total time of flight:**
Total time $T = t_1 (\text{launch to peak}) + t_2 (\text{peak to landing})$:
$$T = 1.5 + 2.6 = 4.1\text{ s}$$
