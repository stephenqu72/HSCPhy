### Answer
The correct answer is **(B)**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we need to apply **Faraday's Law of Induction** and **Lenz's Law**.

1.  **Requirement for a resistive force:** For a resistive force to act on the magnet, there must be an induced current flowing through the solenoid. According to Faraday’s Law, a changing magnetic flux through a closed conducting loop induces an electromotive force (EMF). If the circuit is open (as in diagrams C and D), no current flows, and therefore no significant magnetic force is exerted on the falling magnet. Thus, we only consider (A) and (B).

2.  **Faraday's Law:** The magnitude of the induced EMF ($\mathcal{E}$) is proportional to the number of turns in the coil ($N$) and the rate of change of magnetic flux ($\Phi$):
    $$\mathcal{E} = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$$

3.  **Determining the Greatest Force:**
    *   In both (A) and (B), the circuit is closed, allowing current to flow.
    *   The induced current creates a magnetic field that opposes the motion of the magnet (Lenz's Law). The magnitude of the induced current is directly proportional to the induced EMF.
    *   Because the rate of change of magnetic flux is determined by the speed of the magnet, and the magnet is dropped from the same height in both scenarios, the primary differentiator is the number of turns ($N$).
    *   Since option (B) has $500$ coils compared to the $200$ coils in option (A), the induced EMF in (B) will be significantly greater. A larger EMF leads to a larger induced current, which in turn generates a stronger opposing magnetic field.

Consequently, the solenoid with the higher number of turns (500) will experience a much greater magnetic braking (resistive) force.
