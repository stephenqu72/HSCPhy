### Answer

**(a)** The centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) of an object moving in a circular path is given by the formula:
$$a_c = r\omega^2$$
where $r$ is the radius of the circular path (distance from the centre) and $\omega$ is the angular velocity of the turntable.

**(b)** The block remains in circular motion because static friction acts as the centripetal force required to keep the block moving in a circle. As the angular velocity $\omega$ increases, the required centripetal force ($F_c = m r \omega^2$) also increases. Static friction has a maximum limit ($F_{f,max} = \mu_s N$). Once the required centripetal force exceeds this maximum possible value of static friction, the frictional force is no longer sufficient to maintain circular motion, and the block begins to slide outward.

### How to solve this

1.  **For part (a):** Recognize that centripetal acceleration relates linear acceleration to angular parameters. You are expected to state the formula $a_c = v^2/r$ and substitute $v = r\omega$ to derive $a_c = r\omega^2$. Two marks are awarded here for the correct formula and identification of the variables.
2.  **For part (b):** This is a conceptual physics question that tests your understanding of circular motion dynamics.
    *   **Identify the force:** State that static friction provides the centripetal force.
    *   **Establish the relationship:** Link the increase in $\omega$ to an increase in the required centripetal force ($F_c \propto \omega^2$).
    *   **The "breaking point":** Explain that friction is a limiting force. When the demand for centripetal force exceeds the maximum available static friction, the block can no longer stay in its circular path, resulting in sliding. Mentioning the limit of static friction is crucial for the full 3 marks.
