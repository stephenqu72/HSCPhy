### Front
What is the quark composition of a meson according to the Standard Model?

### Back
- Key idea: Mesons are composite hadrons formed by the strong nuclear force.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $q\bar{q}$ (one quark and one antiquark).
- When to use it: When classifying subatomic particles or determining the constituent structure of hadrons.
- Common trap: Confusing mesons ($q\bar{q}$) with baryons ($qqq$) or mistaking them for fundamental particles like leptons.
