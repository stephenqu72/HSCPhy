The correct answer is **d) W and Z particles**.

### Explanation

To solve this question, you need to recall the Standard Model of particle physics, specifically the exchange particles (gauge bosons) associated with the four fundamental forces of nature:

1.  **Strong Nuclear Force:** Carried by **gluons**. It binds quarks together to form protons and neutrons.
2.  **Electromagnetic Force:** Carried by **photons**. It acts between charged particles.
3.  **Weak Nuclear Force:** Carried by the **$W^+$**, **$W^-$**, and **$Z^0$** bosons. These particles are responsible for processes like beta decay.
4.  **Gravitational Force:** Though not yet fully integrated into the Standard Model, the theoretical carrier is the **graviton**.

Regarding the other options:
*   The **Higgs boson** is not a gauge boson; it is a scalar boson responsible for providing mass to other elementary particles via the Higgs field.
*   **Photons** carry the electromagnetic force, not the weak force.
*   **Gluons** carry the strong force.

Therefore, identifying the exchange particles for each fundamental force is a key requirement for the HSC Physics module "From the Universe to the Atom."
