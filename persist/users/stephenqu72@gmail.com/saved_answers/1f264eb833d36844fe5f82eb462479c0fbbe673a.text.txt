To calculate the relativistic momentum of an electron, we must account for the effects of special relativity since the electron is traveling at a significant fraction of the speed of light ($v = 0.8c$).

### 1. Identify the relevant formula
The relativistic momentum $p$ is given by:
$$p = \gamma mv$$
Where:
- $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$ (The Lorentz factor)
- $m$ is the rest mass of an electron ($m \approx 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$)
- $v$ is the velocity ($0.8c$)

### 2. Calculate the Lorentz factor ($\gamma$)
Given $v = 0.8c$:
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.8)^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.64}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.36}} = \frac{1}{0.6} = 1.666...$$

### 3. Calculate the momentum ($p$)
Now, substitute the values into the momentum formula:
$$p = 1.666... \times (9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}) \times (0.8 \times 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s})$$

First, calculate $v$:
$$v = 0.8 \times 3.00 \times 10^8 = 2.4 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$$

Now calculate $p$:
$$p = 1.666... \times 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \times 2.4 \times 10^8$$
$$p \approx 3.644 \times 10^{-22} \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches **(B)**.

### Answer
The correct option is **(B)**.
