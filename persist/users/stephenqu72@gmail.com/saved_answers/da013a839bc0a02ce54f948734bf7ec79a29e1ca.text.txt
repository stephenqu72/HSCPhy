To solve this problem, we need to determine how much of the original Iodine-131 ($^{131}\text{I}$) has decayed, as the decayed portion is what transforms into Xenon-131 ($^{131}\text{Xe}$).

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Determine the number of half-lives that have elapsed:**
The total time elapsed is $16$ hours, and the half-life of $^{131}\text{I}$ is $8$ hours.
$$\text{Number of half-lives} (n) = \frac{\text{Total time}}{\text{Half-life}} = \frac{16}{8} = 2$$

**2. Calculate the remaining amount of Iodine-131:**
After each half-life, the amount of the radioisotope is halved.
- After $8$ hours ($1$ half-life): $24.0\text{ mg} \times 0.5 = 12.0\text{ mg}$
- After $16$ hours ($2$ half-lives): $12.0\text{ mg} \times 0.5 = 6.0\text{ mg}$
So, $6.0\text{ mg}$ of $^{131}\text{I}$ remains in the body.

**3. Calculate the amount of Xenon-131 produced:**
The Law of Conservation of Mass tells us that the amount of Xenon produced must equal the amount of Iodine that has decayed.
$$\text{Amount decayed} = \text{Initial amount} - \text{Remaining amount}$$
$$\text{Amount of } ^{131}\text{Xe} = 24.0\text{ mg} - 6.0\text{ mg} = 18.0\text{ mg}$$

Comparing this to the given options, the correct answer is **C**.

### Summary of logic:
*   Identify the total time elapsed relative to the half-life.
*   Calculate the quantity of the parent isotope remaining after the specified time.
*   Subtract the remaining parent quantity from the initial quantity to find the quantity of the daughter isotope produced.
