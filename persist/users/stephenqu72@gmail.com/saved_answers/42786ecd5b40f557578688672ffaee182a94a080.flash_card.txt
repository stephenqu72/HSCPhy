### Front
Why does a rocket not need to reach the theoretical escape velocity to leave Earth’s gravitational field?

### Back
- Key idea: Continuous thrust allows a spacecraft to "climb" out of the gravitational potential well, rather than requiring an impulsive "jump."
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$ (Escape velocity formula)
- When to use it: When analyzing energy requirements for leaving a gravitational field with ongoing propulsion.
- Common trap: Assuming escape velocity is a mandatory instantaneous speed for all spacecraft; it is only required for *unpowered* projectiles.
