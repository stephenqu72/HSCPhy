To solve this problem, we must apply the formula for centripetal force while ensuring all units are consistent with the SI system.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Mass ($m$) = $1300\text{ kg}$
*   Radius ($r$) = $13\text{ m}$
*   Velocity ($v$) = $36\text{ km/h}$

**2. Convert velocity to SI units (m/s):**
Physics formulas require speed in meters per second. To convert from $\text{km/h}$ to $\text{m/s}$, divide by $3.6$:
$$v = \frac{36}{3.6} = 10\text{ m/s}$$

**3. Apply the centripetal force formula:**
The magnitude of the centripetal force ($F_c$) is given by:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

**4. Substitute the values and calculate:**
$$F_c = \frac{1300 \times (10)^2}{13}$$
$$F_c = \frac{1300 \times 100}{13}$$
$$F_c = \frac{130000}{13}$$
$$F_c = 10000\text{ N}$$

The magnitude of the centripetal force on the car is $10,000\text{ N}$ (or $1.0 \times 10^4\text{ N}$).
