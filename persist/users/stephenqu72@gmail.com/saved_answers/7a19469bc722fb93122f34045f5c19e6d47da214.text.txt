### Recommended Answer: The Muon Decay Experiment

A classic piece of evidence for the special theory of relativity is the observation of muon decay.

1. **Prediction:** According to special relativity, time dilation occurs for objects moving at relativistic speeds relative to an observer. The time dilation formula is $t_v = \frac{t_0}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$, where $t_v$ is the dilated time, $t_0$ is the proper time (the muon's half-life at rest), $v$ is the velocity, and $c$ is the speed of light.
2. **Experiment:** Muons are unstable subatomic particles created in the upper atmosphere by cosmic rays. They travel toward the Earth's surface at speeds very close to $c$. 
3. **Observation:** With a very short half-life of approximately $2.2 \times 10^{-6}$ seconds, classical physics predicts that most muons should decay long before they reach the surface. However, detectors on the ground observe a significantly higher number of muons than expected.
4. **Conclusion:** Because the muons are moving at relativistic speeds, their "internal clocks" slow down relative to an observer on Earth. This time dilation allows them to travel further within their measured half-life before decaying, providing direct experimental confirmation of Einstein's prediction.

*   **The "4-Mark" Rule:** Whenever you see a 4-mark question, you must provide enough detail to justify the full allocation. A one-sentence answer will not suffice. You need to follow a structure:
    *   **Identify** the phenomenon (e.g., time dilation).
    *   **Contextualize** the experiment (e.g., muons in the atmosphere).
    *   **Explain the mechanism** (e.g., describe the high velocity and the difference between proper time and dilated time).
    *   **Link back** to the theory (e.g., show how the experimental result validates the theory of special relativity).
*   **Use Terminology:** Use key terms like "relativistic speeds," "time dilation," "proper time," and "half-life."
