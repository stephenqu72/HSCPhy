### Front
How do you determine the decay constant ($\lambda$) from a radioactive decay graph and what is the crucial unit requirement?

### Back
- Key idea: Use the half-life ($t_{1/2}$) where activity $A(t) = 0.5 A_0$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{t_{1/2}}$
- When to use it: To calculate the rate of decay when given a time-based decay curve.
- Common trap: Failing to convert the time units from the graph (e.g., days or years) into SI units (seconds) before calculating $\lambda$.
