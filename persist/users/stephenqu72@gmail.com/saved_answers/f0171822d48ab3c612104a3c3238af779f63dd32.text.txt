To determine the age of the rock, we use the radioactive decay law:

$$N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$$

Where:
*   $N(t) = 8\text{ g}$ (the remaining mass)
*   $N_0 = 800\text{ g}$ (the starting mass)
*   $\lambda = 0.0054\text{ years}^{-1}$ (the decay constant)
*   $t$ = the time (age of the rock)

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $t$:**
    $$\frac{N(t)}{N_0} = e^{-\lambda t}$$
    Take the natural logarithm ($\ln$) of both sides:
    $$\ln\left(\frac{N(t)}{N_0}\right) = -\lambda t$$
    $$t = \frac{\ln\left(\frac{N(t)}{N_0}\right)}{-\lambda}$$

2.  **Substitute the given values:**
    $$t = \frac{\ln\left(\frac{8}{800}\right)}{-0.0054}$$
    $$t = \frac{\ln(0.01)}{-0.0054}$$

3.  **Calculate the value:**
    Knowing that $\ln(0.01) \approx -4.605$:
    $$t \approx \frac{-4.605}{-0.0054}$$
    $$t \approx 852.77\text{ years}$$

Comparing our result to the options provided, the closest answer is **852 years**.
