The correct answer is **(A) quarter.**

### Explanation

To solve this problem, we use the formula for gravitational field strength ($g$) at the surface of a spherical body:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Where:
- $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
- $M$ is the mass of the Earth.
- $r$ is the radius of the Earth.

**Step-by-step logic:**

1.  **Identify the relationship:** The formula shows that gravitational field strength is inversely proportional to the square of the radius ($g \propto \frac{1}{r^2}$).
2.  **Apply the change:** The problem states that the radius $r$ is doubled, meaning the new radius $r'$ is $2r$.
3.  **Substitute into the proportionality:**
    $$g' \propto \frac{1}{(2r)^2}$$
    $$g' \propto \frac{1}{4r^2}$$
4.  **Compare to the original:** Since $g \propto \frac{1}{r^2}$, the new field strength $g'$ is $\frac{1}{4}$ of the original field strength $g$.

Therefore, if the radius doubles while the mass remains constant, the gravitational field strength becomes a **quarter** of its original value.
