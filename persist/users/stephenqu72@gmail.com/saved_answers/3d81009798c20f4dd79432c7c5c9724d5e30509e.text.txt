To solve this projectile motion problem, we assume the initial height is $0\text{ m}$, the ceiling height $y = 10\text{ m}$, $v = 20\text{ m s}^{-1}$, and acceleration due to gravity $g = 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$.

### (a) Maximum angle to avoid a foul
A foul occurs if the peak of the ball's trajectory reaches or exceeds $10\text{ m}$. We want the maximum vertical displacement $y_{max}$ to be exactly $10\text{ m}$.
Using the kinematic equation for vertical motion:
$$v_y^2 = u_y^2 - 2gy$$
At the maximum height, the vertical velocity $v_y = 0$. The initial vertical velocity is $u_y = v \sin \theta$.
$$0 = (v \sin \theta)^2 - 2gy$$
$$(v \sin \theta)^2 = 2gy$$
$$v \sin \theta = \sqrt{2gy}$$
$$\sin \theta = \frac{\sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 10}}{20} = \frac{\sqrt{196}}{20} = \frac{14}{20} = 0.7$$
$$\theta = \arcsin(0.7) \approx 44.43^\circ$$
The maximum angle to avoid a foul is approximately **$44.4^\circ$**.

---

### (b) Velocity of the ball after $2.0\text{ s}$
We need the horizontal and vertical components of velocity at $t = 2.0\text{ s}$.
Using $\theta \approx 44.43^\circ$, $\sin \theta = 0.7$, and $\cos \theta = \sqrt{1 - 0.7^2} = \sqrt{0.51} \approx 0.714$.

**1. Horizontal velocity ($v_x$):**
Horizontal velocity remains constant.
$$v_x = v \cos \theta = 20 \times 0.714 = 14.28\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

**2. Vertical velocity ($v_y$):**
Using $v_y = u_y - gt$:
$$v_y = (20 \times 0.7) - (9.8 \times 2.0)$$
$$v_y = 14 - 19.6 = -5.6\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

**3. Resultant velocity ($v$):**
$$v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2} = \sqrt{14.28^2 + (-5.6)^2} \approx \sqrt{203.9 + 31.36} \approx \sqrt{235.26} \approx 15.34\text{ m s}^{-1}$$
The angle $\alpha$ relative to the horizontal:
$$\alpha = \arctan\left(\frac{-5.6}{14.28}\right) \approx -21.4^\circ$$
The velocity is approximately **$15.3\text{ m s}^{-1}$ at $21.4^\circ$ below the horizontal**.
