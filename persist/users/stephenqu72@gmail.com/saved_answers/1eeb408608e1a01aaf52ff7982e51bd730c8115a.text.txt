To determine the correct answer, we need to analyze the motion of a projectile launched horizontally.

### Analysis of the Motion
For a ball thrown horizontally from a cliff of height $h$:

1.  **Vertical Motion:** The time of flight $t$ depends only on the height $h$ and gravity $g$. Using the equation $h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$, we can see that:
    $$t = \sqrt{\frac{2h}{g}}$$
    Since the height $h$ and gravity $g$ are the same for both balls, **the time of flight $t$ remains constant** for both objects.

2.  **Horizontal Motion:** The horizontal range $R$ is determined by the horizontal velocity $v_x$ and the time of flight:
    $$R = v_x \times t$$

### Solving the Problem
*   For the first ball: $R_1 = v \times t$
*   For the second ball: The new velocity is $v_2 = 3v$. Since the time $t$ is unchanged, the new range $R_2$ is:
    $$R_2 = (3v) \times t$$
    $$R_2 = 3(v \times t)$$
    $$R_2 = 3R$$

Because the horizontal speed is directly proportional to the range (given a constant time of flight), tripling the initial horizontal speed results in a range that is also tripled.

**Correct Option: (C) $3R$**
