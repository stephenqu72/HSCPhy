To solve this question, you need to understand the historical context of the "Ultraviolet Catastrophe" and how Max Planck solved the problem of black-body radiation.

1.  **The Problem:** Classical physics could not explain the spectrum of radiation emitted by a black body. Classical theory (the Rayleigh-Jeans law) predicted that an object should emit an infinite amount of energy at ultraviolet frequencies, which was physically impossible.
2.  **Planck’s Hypothesis:** In 1900, Max Planck proposed that the energy of oscillators in the walls of a black body is not continuous, but rather **quantised**. He stated that energy can only be emitted or absorbed in discrete "packets" or "quanta," defined by the equation:
    $$E = nhf$$
    where $n$ is an integer, $h$ is Planck's constant, and $f$ is the frequency of the radiation.
3.  **Evaluating the Options:**

    *   (B) is incorrect: This describes the work of **Albert Einstein**, who extended Planck's idea to explain the photoelectric effect.
    *   (C) is incorrect: Planck solved the black-body radiation problem, but he did not explain the photoelectric effect; Einstein did that.
    *   (D) is **correct**: It accurately reflects Planck's hypothesis that the energy exchange between the electromagnetic field and the matter (the walls of the cavity) occurs in discrete, quantised amounts.
