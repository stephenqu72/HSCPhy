To find the energy of a photon given its wavelength, we use the Planck-Einstein relation:

$$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$

### Given values:
*   Planck’s constant ($h$) $\approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$
*   Speed of light ($c$) $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
*   Wavelength ($\lambda$) $= 3.5 \times 10^{-2} \text{ m}$

### Step-by-step Calculation:
1.  **Substitute the values into the formula:**
    $$E = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)}{3.5 \times 10^{-2}}$$

2.  **Calculate the numerator:**
    $$(6.626 \times 3.00) \times 10^{-34 + 8} = 19.878 \times 10^{-26} \text{ J m}$$

3.  **Divide by the wavelength:**
    $$E = \frac{19.878 \times 10^{-26}}{3.5 \times 10^{-2}}$$
    $$E \approx 5.679 \times 10^{-24} \text{ J}$$

### Explanation
To solve problems involving photon energy, always remember the relationship between energy, frequency, and wavelength. Since $v = f\lambda$ and for light $c = f\lambda$, we can substitute $f = \frac{c}{\lambda}$ into $E=hf$ to derive $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$. When performing these calculations, ensure your units for speed ($m/s$) and wavelength ($m$) are consistent to arrive at the energy in Joules ($J$).
