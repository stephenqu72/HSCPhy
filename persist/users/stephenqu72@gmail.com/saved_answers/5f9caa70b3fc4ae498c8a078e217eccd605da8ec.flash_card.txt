### Front
How do you calculate the mass ($M$) of a central body using the orbital radius ($r$) and orbital speed ($v$) of a satellite?

### Back
- Key idea: Equate gravitational force to centripetal force ($F_g = F_c$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} \implies M = \frac{v^2r}{G}$
- When to use it: To find the mass of a central body given the satellite's orbital parameters.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., km/s to m/s) or using the diameter instead of the radius ($r$).
