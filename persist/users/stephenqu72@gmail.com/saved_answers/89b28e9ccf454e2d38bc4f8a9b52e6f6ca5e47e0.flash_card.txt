### Front
What is the total energy released during an electron-positron annihilation, and how is it calculated?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Mass-energy equivalence where total rest mass of both particles is converted into photons.
- **Formula / law:** $E = mc^2$; Rest mass of $e^- / e^+ \approx 0.511 \text{ MeV}$.
- **When to use it:** When calculating energy produced by matter-antimatter annihilation (Total energy = $2 \times 0.511 \text{ MeV} = 1.022 \text{ MeV}$).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to account for *both* particles; remember to multiply the single particle mass/energy by two.
