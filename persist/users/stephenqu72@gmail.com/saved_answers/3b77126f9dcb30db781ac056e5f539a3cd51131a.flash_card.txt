### Front
How can you maximize the torque produced by a force applied to a lever?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Maximize the lever arm distance and ensure the force is applied perpendicularly to the lever arm.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\tau = Fd\sin(\theta)$; No fixed constant.
- **When to use it:** Problems involving mechanical advantage, levers, or rotational equilibrium.
- **Common trap:** Confusing "vertical" force with "perpendicular" force; always ensure the angle $\theta$ is relative to the lever arm, not just the ground.
