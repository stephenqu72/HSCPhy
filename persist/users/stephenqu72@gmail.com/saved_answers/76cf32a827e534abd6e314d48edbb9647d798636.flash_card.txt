### Front
How do you calculate the time ($t$) required for a radioactive sample to decay to a specific mass ($N_t$) given an initial mass ($N_0$) and half-life ($T_{1/2}$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Radioactive decay follows an exponential relationship where the mass halves every $T_{1/2}$ interval.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $N(t) = N_0(0.5)^{\frac{t}{T_{1/2}}}$; use $\ln$ to solve for $t$: $t = T_{1/2} \times \frac{\ln(N_t/N_0)}{\ln(0.5)}$
- When to use it: Any decay problem where mass/activity changes over time and half-life is known or can be determined from a graph.
- Common trap: Forgetting to ensure units for $N_t$ and $N_0$ are identical (e.g., converting g to mg) before substituting into the formula.
