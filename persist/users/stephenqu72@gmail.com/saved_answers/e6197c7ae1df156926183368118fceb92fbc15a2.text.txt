To answer this question, we must understand the historical context of Heinrich Hertz’s experiments on electromagnetic waves and how he serendipitously discovered the photoelectric effect.

### Analysis of the Physics
1.  **Hertz's Experiment:** Hertz created sparks in a transmitter, which generated electromagnetic waves that induced smaller sparks in a receiver loop.
2.  **The Photoelectric Effect:** Hertz observed that the spark in the receiver was brighter and more easily produced when the receiver was illuminated by the ultraviolet (UV) light produced by the transmitter's own spark. This occurred because the UV light ejected electrons from the surface of the metal receiver (the photoelectric effect), making it easier for the spark to jump across the air gap.
3.  **The Role of Glass:** Glass is opaque to UV radiation. When a glass sheet is placed between the transmitter and the receiver, it blocks the UV light reaching the receiver.
4.  **The Consequence:** Because the UV light is blocked, the photoelectric effect that was assisting the ionization of the air gap in the receiver is diminished or removed. Consequently, the receiver requires a higher potential difference to produce a spark, or, in the context of the observed spark length, the spark becomes harder to produce or shorter in length because the "priming" effect of the UV radiation is gone.

### Evaluating the Options
*   (A) Radio waves are not blocked by glass; this is incorrect.
*   (B) While UV waves *are* blocked, the observation consistent with the photoelectric effect refers to the impact on the sparks (the physical measurement).
*   (C) This would imply the glass enhances the process, which is incorrect.
*   (D) This is the correct observation. Blocking the UV light stops the photoelectric emission from the receiver's gap, reducing the ease with which the spark jumps, resulting in a shorter maximum spark length.

**Correct Answer: (D)**

### How to solve this
1.  **Identify the Phenomenon:** Recognize that the "photoelectric effect" mentioned in the question refers to the accidental discovery Hertz made while observing that UV light enhanced spark production.
2.  **Recall Material Properties:** Know that glass acts as a filter that blocks UV radiation but allows radio waves to pass through.
3.  **Connect Causality:** Link the presence of UV light to the facilitation of the spark in the receiver. If the UV light is blocked by the glass, the process that made the spark easier to create is removed. Therefore, the spark performance (length) must decrease.
