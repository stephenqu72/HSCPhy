### Front
How do Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation and Kepler’s Third Law combine to explain satellite orbital motion?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Newton provides the mechanical force balance ($F_g = F_c$) for orbital velocity, while Kepler defines the mathematical relationship between orbital radius and period.
- **Formula / law:** $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$ and $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$
- **When to use it:** When asked to "discuss" or "explain" the theoretical framework behind satellite placement, altitude, or orbital stability.
- **Common trap:** Only defining the laws without linking them to the *mechanics* (force balance) or failing to mention that geostationary orbits depend specifically on the $r^3/T^2$ constant.
