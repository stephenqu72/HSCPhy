### Front
How did Millikan determine the elementary charge ($e$) without needing to know the exact mass of individual oil drops?

### Back
- Key idea: Analyzing the greatest common divisor of charge changes ($\Delta q$) on a single drop.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $mg = q \frac{V}{d}$; $q = ne$
- When to use it: To find the fundamental unit of charge despite uncertainties in drop mass/radius.
- Common trap: Assuming the total charge of a single drop was calculated directly from its mass; Millikan actually used the *differences* in charge states to isolate $e$.
