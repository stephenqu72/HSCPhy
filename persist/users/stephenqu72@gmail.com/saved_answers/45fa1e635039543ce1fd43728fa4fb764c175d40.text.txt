To determine the centripetal acceleration of a carriage on the Ferris wheel, we follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the given information
*   **Diameter ($d$):** $40\text{ m}$. Therefore, the radius ($r$) is $r = \frac{d}{2} = 20\text{ m}$.
*   **Period ($T$):** The time for one revolution is $40\text{ s}$.

### 2. Choose the correct formula
The formula for centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) is:
$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$$
Alternatively, using the angular velocity formula where $v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$, we get:
$$a_c = \frac{4\pi^2 r}{T^2}$$

### 3. Perform the calculation
Using the second version of the formula:
*   $r = 20\text{ m}$
*   $T = 40\text{ s}$

$$a_c = \frac{4 \times \pi^2 \times 20}{40^2}$$
$$a_c = \frac{80\pi^2}{1600}$$
$$a_c = \frac{\pi^2}{20}$$
Since $\pi^2 \approx 9.87$:
$$a_c \approx \frac{9.87}{20} \approx 0.4935\text{ ms}^{-2}$$

Rounding to one decimal place, we get $0.5\text{ ms}^{-2}$.

### Conclusion
The correct answer is **A**.
