To determine which expression results in the least percentage uncertainty, we calculate the percentage uncertainty for each operation.

Given:
$P = 30 \pm 1\text{ g}$
$Q = 20 \pm 1\text{ g}$

### 1. Addition ($P + Q$) and Subtraction ($P - Q$)
When adding or subtracting, absolute uncertainties are added:
$\Delta(P \pm Q) = \Delta P + \Delta Q = 1 + 1 = 2\text{ g}$

*   **For $P + Q$:** Value $= 30 + 20 = 50\text{ g}$.
    Percentage uncertainty $= \frac{2}{50} \times 100\% = 4\%$.
*   **For $P - Q$:** Value $= 30 - 20 = 10\text{ g}$.
    Percentage uncertainty $= \frac{2}{10} \times 100\% = 20\%$.

### 2. Multiplication ($P \times Q$) and Division ($P/Q$)
When multiplying or dividing, percentage uncertainties are added:
$\text{Percentage uncertainty} = \left( \frac{\Delta P}{P} \times 100\% \right) + \left( \frac{\Delta Q}{Q} \times 100\% \right)$

*   $\frac{\Delta P}{P} \times 100\% = \frac{1}{30} \times 100\% \approx 3.33\%$
*   $\frac{\Delta Q}{Q} \times 100\% = \frac{1}{20} \times 100\% = 5\%$

*   **For $P \times Q$:** Percentage uncertainty $\approx 3.33\% + 5\% = 8.33\%$.
*   **For $P/Q$:** Percentage uncertainty $\approx 3.33\% + 5\% = 8.33\%$.

### Conclusion
Comparing the results:
*   (A) $P + Q$: $4\%$
*   (B) $P - Q$: $20\%$
*   (C) $P \times Q$: $8.33\%$
*   (D) $P/Q$: $8.33\%$

The expression with the least percentage uncertainty is **(A)**.
