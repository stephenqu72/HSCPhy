To determine the correct direction, we use the **Right-Hand Rule** for the magnetic force acting on moving charges (or the motor effect principle applied to charge carriers).

### Explanation:
1.  **Identify the Magnetic Field ($B$):** The magnetic field lines point from the North pole ($N$) to the South pole ($S$). In this diagram, the $N$ pole is at the bottom right and the $S$ pole is at the top left, so the magnetic field points from **bottom-right to top-left**.
2.  **Understand the Physics:** When a conductor moves through a magnetic field, the free electrons inside the conductor experience a magnetic force given by $F = q(v \times B)$.
    *   If the **top end** of the conductor is to become **positively charged**, electrons must be pushed toward the **bottom end** of the conductor.
    *   Since electrons are negatively charged, the magnetic force on the electrons must be directed downwards.
3.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule (for positive charges):**
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field (from $N$ to $S$, or bottom-right to top-left).
    *   For a **positive charge** to experience a force **upward** (to accumulate at the top end), point your thumb in the direction of the force (upward).
    *   Your palm (or the cross product $v \times B$) will indicate the required velocity direction.
4.  **Alternatively, use the Left-Hand Rule (for electrons):**
    *   Point your index finger in the direction of the magnetic field (towards the top-left).
    *   Point your middle finger in the direction of the force on the electrons (to make the top positive, electrons must move down, so point your middle finger **down**).
    *   Your thumb will point in the direction of the velocity ($v$). Following this orientation, your thumb points toward **direction 4**.

If the conductor moves in direction **4**, the magnetic force on the free electrons will push them to the bottom, leaving the top end with a net positive charge.

**Correct Answer:** D (Direction 4)
