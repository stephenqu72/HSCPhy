### Front
On a frictionless banked track, what provides the centripetal force required for a vehicle to undergo circular motion?

### Back
- Key idea: The horizontal component of the normal force ($F_N \sin\theta$) acts as the centripetal force.
- Formula / law: $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} = F_N \sin\theta$ and $F_N \cos\theta = mg$.
- When to use it: Whenever a vehicle turns on a banked curve without relying on friction.
- Common trap: Thinking the centripetal force is a separate "extra" force; it is actually a component of the existing normal force.
