### Answer and Explanation

This problem requires the application of the **Law of Conservation of Energy** and the principles of **Circular Motion**.

Given:
*   Mass $m = 250\text{ g} = 0.25\text{ kg}$
*   Radius of loop $r = 0.20\text{ m}$ (Diameter $= 0.40\text{ m}$)
*   Velocity at B $v_B = 3.0\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   $g \approx 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$

---

#### a. Calculate the value of $h$
Since there is no friction or air resistance, the total mechanical energy is conserved: $E_{initial} = E_{final}$.
At point A, the car is at rest ($KE = 0$), so $E_A = PE = mgh$.
At point B, the car has height $h_B = 2r = 0.40\text{ m}$ and speed $v_B$, so $E_B = mgh_B + \frac{1}{2}mv_B^2$.

$$mgh = mgh_B + \frac{1}{2}mv_B^2$$
Canceling $m$ from both sides:
$$gh = g(0.40) + \frac{1}{2}(3.0)^2$$
$$9.8h = 9.8(0.40) + 4.5$$
$$9.8h = 3.92 + 4.5 = 8.42$$
$$h = \frac{8.42}{9.8} \approx 0.859\text{ m}$$

**Answer:** $h \approx 0.86\text{ m}$

---

#### b. Calculate the magnitude of the normal reaction force at point B
At point B (top of the loop), both gravity ($mg$) and the normal force ($N$) act downwards toward the center of the circle. Their resultant provides the centripetal force:

$$F_{net} = \frac{mv_B^2}{r}$$
$$N + mg = \frac{mv_B^2}{r}$$
$$N = \frac{mv_B^2}{r} - mg$$
$$N = \frac{0.25 \times (3.0)^2}{0.20} - (0.25 \times 9.8)$$
$$N = \frac{2.25}{0.20} - 2.45 = 11.25 - 2.45 = 8.8\text{ N}$$

**Answer:** $8.8\text{ N}$

---

#### c. Explain why the car does not fall
The car does not fall because it maintains a speed high enough that the centripetal force required to keep it in circular motion is greater than the gravitational force acting on it. Specifically, because the normal force $N$ is positive ($N = 8.8\text{ N}$), the track is exerting a downward force on the car to keep it on the circular path. As long as $v_B^2 > rg$ (or $v_B > \sqrt{rg}$), the car will remain in contact with the track.
