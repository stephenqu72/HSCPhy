To find the minimum adhesive force required at point 3, we must apply the principles of uniform circular motion.

### Physics Principles
1. **Centripetal Force ($F_c$):** Any object moving in a circle of radius $R$ at an angular velocity $\omega$ requires a net centripetal force directed toward the center of rotation:
   $$F_{net} = m \omega^2 R$$
2. **Force Analysis at Point 3:** At point 3 (the bottom of the wheel), two radial forces act on the clay:
   *   The adhesive force ($F_{adh}$) pulling the clay **upward** toward the center of the wheel.
   *   The gravitational force ($mg$) pulling the clay **downward** away from the center.

### Solving the Problem
Since the net force must be directed toward the center (upward at point 3) to sustain circular motion, we write the equation for the net force:
$$F_{net} = F_{adh} - mg$$

Rearranging this to solve for the adhesive force ($F_{adh}$):
$$F_{adh} = F_{net} + mg$$

Substituting the expression for centripetal force ($F_{net} = m \omega^2 R$):
$$F_{adh} = m \omega^2 R + mg$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **(B)**.

### Reasoning
At point 3, the adhesive force must perform a "double duty": it must provide the necessary centripetal force to keep the object moving in a circle, AND it must counteract the weight of the object that is pulling it away from the wheel. Therefore, the adhesive force must be greater than the centripetal force alone, leading to the sum of the two terms.
