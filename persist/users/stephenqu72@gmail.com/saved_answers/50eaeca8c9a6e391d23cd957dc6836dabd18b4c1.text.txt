To determine the correct S.I. unit for gravitational field strength, we look at its definition.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) $ms^{-2}$**.

*(Note: While $Nkg^{-1}$ is also a valid and commonly used S.I. unit for gravitational field strength, $ms^{-2}$ is the equivalent unit representing acceleration due to gravity, which is the standard representation in this context.)*

---

### How to solve it
1.  **Define Gravitational Field Strength ($g$):** Gravitational field strength is defined as the gravitational force per unit mass acting on an object at a specific point in a gravitational field. This can be expressed by the formula:
    $$g = \frac{F}{m}$$
    Where $F$ is the force (measured in Newtons, $N$) and $m$ is the mass (measured in kilograms, $kg$). Therefore, $g$ can be expressed in units of $Nkg^{-1}$.

2.  **Relate to Acceleration:** From Newton's Second Law, we know that force is mass times acceleration ($F = ma$). If we rearrange this for acceleration, we get $a = F/m$. Since the force of gravity is the weight of an object ($W = mg$), the acceleration due to gravity is equivalent to the gravitational field strength.
    *   Since $F = ma$, the units are $N = kg \times ms^{-2}$.
    *   Rearranging for acceleration ($a$):
    $$a = \frac{N}{kg} = ms^{-2}$$

3.  **Evaluate the options:**
    *   (A) $kg$ is the unit for mass.
    *   (B) $N$ is the unit for force.
    *   (C) $J$ (Joules) is the unit for energy or work.
    *   (D) $ms^{-2}$ is the unit for acceleration, which is equivalent to the units of gravitational field strength ($Nkg^{-1}$).
