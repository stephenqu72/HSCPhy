To solve this physics problem, we need to calculate the magnitude of the electrostatic force acting on an electron placed in a uniform electric field created by parallel charged plates.

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   Voltage ($V$) = $6.0\text{ V}$
*   Distance ($d$) = $150\text{ cm} = 1.5\text{ m}$ (We must convert centimeters to meters for SI units)
*   Charge of an electron ($q$) = $1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$ (This is a standard physical constant)

### 2. Relevant Formulas:
*   The electric field strength ($E$) between parallel plates is given by:
    $$E = \frac{V}{d}$$
*   The magnitude of the force ($F$) acting on a charge in an electric field is given by:
    $$F = qE$$
*   Combining these, the formula for force is:
    $$F = \frac{qV}{d}$$

### 3. Calculation:
Substitute the values into the combined formula:
$$F = \frac{(1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (6.0\text{ V})}{1.5\text{ m}}$$
$$F = \frac{9.6 \times 10^{-19}}{1.5}\text{ N}$$
$$F = 6.4 \times 10^{-19}\text{ N}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct choice is **(A)**.

***

### Summary for Students:
When solving problems involving parallel plates, always ensure your distance is in **meters** ($150\text{ cm} = 1.5\text{ m}$). By calculating the electric field first and then applying the force formula, you ensure that you are accounting for the influence of the voltage potential across the specific gap between the plates.
