### Front
How does the required centripetal force ($F_c$) change when a car transitions from a flat track to a banked track at the same constant speed and radius?

### Back
- Key idea: The required centripetal force is a kinematic requirement determined solely by $m$, $v$, and $r$.
- Formula / law: $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: When determining the net inward force necessary for circular motion.
- Common trap: Confusing the *source* of the force (friction vs. normal force) with the *total magnitude* of the force required. The total force remains constant if $m$, $v$, and $r$ are unchanged.
