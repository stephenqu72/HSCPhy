### Front
Why does a coin on a rotating disc eventually slide off, and which coin (A or B) slides off first if $r_A > r_B$?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Static friction provides the centripetal force ($F_c$); sliding occurs when $F_c$ exceeds $\mu mg$.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $F_c = mr\omega^2$ and $r\omega^2 \le \mu g$.
- **When to use it:** Problems involving objects in horizontal circular motion held by friction.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that objects further from the center (larger $r$) reach the critical sliding velocity ($\omega$) first because they require more centripetal force for the same $\omega$.
