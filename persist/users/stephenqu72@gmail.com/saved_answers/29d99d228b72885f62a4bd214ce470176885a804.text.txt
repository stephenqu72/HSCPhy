To find the minimum speed $v$ for a bicycle on a banked track, we must consider the forces acting on the bike: gravity ($mg$), the normal force ($N$), and the force of static friction ($f_s = \mu_s N$).

### Explanation
1.  **Identify the forces:** Since we are looking for the **minimum** speed, the bike has a tendency to slide **down** the slope. Therefore, the static friction force $f_s$ acts **up** the slope to prevent this slipping.
2.  **Resolve forces:** We set up a coordinate system where the radial direction (towards the center of the turn) is horizontal and the vertical direction is vertical.
    *   **Vertical direction (no acceleration):** The vertical components of the normal force and friction must balance gravity.
        $$N \cos \theta + f_s \sin \theta = mg$$
        Substituting $f_s = \mu_s N$:
        $$N(\cos \theta + \mu_s \sin \theta) = mg \implies N = \frac{mg}{\cos \theta + \mu_s \sin \theta}$$
    *   **Horizontal direction (centripetal acceleration):** The horizontal components of the normal force and friction provide the centripetal force $\frac{mv^2}{r}$.
        $$N \sin \theta - f_s \cos \theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
        Substituting $f_s = \mu_s N$:
        $$N(\sin \theta - \mu_s \cos \theta) = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
3.  **Combine the equations:** Divide the horizontal equation by the vertical equation to eliminate $N$ and $m$:
    $$\frac{mv^2/r}{mg} = \frac{N(\sin \theta - \mu_s \cos \theta)}{N(\cos \theta + \mu_s \sin \theta)}$$
    $$\frac{v^2}{rg} = \frac{\sin \theta - \mu_s \cos \theta}{\cos \theta + \mu_s \sin \theta}$$
4.  **Simplify:** Divide the numerator and denominator of the right side by $\cos \theta$:
    $$\frac{v^2}{rg} = \frac{\tan \theta - \mu_s}{1 + \mu_s \tan \theta}$$
    $$v = \sqrt{gr \left( \frac{\tan \theta - \mu_s}{1 + \mu_s \tan \theta} \right)}$$

Comparing this to the given options, we find that (B) is the correct expression. Note that (D) represents the **maximum** speed, where friction acts down the slope to prevent the bike from sliding up.

### Answer
The correct option is **(B)**.
