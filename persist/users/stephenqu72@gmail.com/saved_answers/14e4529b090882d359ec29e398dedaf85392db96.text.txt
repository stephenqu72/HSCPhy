To determine the orbital period of planet $Y$, we use Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion, which states that the square of the orbital period $T$ is proportional to the cube of the orbital radius $r$:
$$\frac{T_X^2}{r_X^3} = \frac{T_Y^2}{r_Y^3}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Determine the orbital period of Planet $X$ ($T_X$):**
We are told that in $5$ years, planet $X$ has rotated through $120^\circ$. 
Since a full orbit is $360^\circ$, the fraction of the orbit completed is:
$$\frac{120^\circ}{360^\circ} = \frac{1}{3} \text{ of an orbit}$$
If it takes $5$ years to complete $\frac{1}{3}$ of an orbit, the total time for one full orbit ($T_X$) is:
$$T_X = 5 \text{ years} \times 3 = 15 \text{ years}$$

**2. Relate the radii of the orbits:**
The ratio of the radii is $r_X : r_Y = 2 : 5$. Therefore, we can write:
$$\frac{r_Y}{r_X} = \frac{5}{2} = 2.5$$

**3. Apply Kepler's Third Law:**
Rearranging the formula to solve for $T_Y$:
$$\left( \frac{T_Y}{T_X} \right)^2 = \left( \frac{r_Y}{r_X} \right)^3$$
$$\frac{T_Y}{T_X} = \sqrt{\left( \frac{r_Y}{r_X} \right)^3} = \left( \frac{r_Y}{r_X} \right)^{3/2}$$
$$T_Y = T_X \times (2.5)^{3/2}$$

**4. Calculate the final value:**
$$T_Y = 15 \times \sqrt{2.5^3}$$
$$T_Y = 15 \times \sqrt{15.625}$$
$$T_Y = 15 \times 3.9528... \approx 59.29 \text{ years}$$

**Answer:** Planet $Y$ takes approximately **$59.3$ years** to orbit the star.
