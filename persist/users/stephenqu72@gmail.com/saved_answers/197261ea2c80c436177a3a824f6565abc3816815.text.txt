To solve this problem, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which states that the wavelength at which a black body emits maximum intensity ($\lambda_{max}$) is inversely proportional to its absolute temperature ($T$):

$$\lambda_{max} T = b$$

where $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant ($b \approx 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$).

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the relationship:**
Since the constant $b$ remains the same for both states of the black body, we can set up the following ratio:
$$\lambda_1 T_1 = \lambda_2 T_2$$
Rearranging to solve for the new wavelength $\lambda_2$:
$$\lambda_2 = \frac{\lambda_1 T_1}{T_2}$$

**2. List the known values:**
*   Initial wavelength $\lambda_1 = 7.00 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$
*   Initial temperature $T_1 = 4140 \text{ K}$
*   The temperature is increased by $1000 \text{ K}$, so the new temperature $T_2 = 4140 \text{ K} + 1000 \text{ K} = 5140 \text{ K}$

**3. Calculate the new wavelength:**
$$\lambda_2 = \frac{(7.00 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}) \times (4140 \text{ K})}{5140 \text{ K}}$$
$$\lambda_2 \approx \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}}{5140 \text{ K}}$$
$$\lambda_2 \approx 5.638 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$$

Rounding to three significant figures, we get $5.64 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$. Comparing this to the provided options, it matches option **(B)**.

**Answer:** (B) $5.65 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$
