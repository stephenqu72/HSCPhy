To solve this problem, we must identify the relationship between the orbital radius ($r$) and the orbital period ($T$) for planets orbiting the same central star. This is governed by Kepler's Third Law:

$$\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$$

However, the question asks about the **acceleration due to gravity** ($a_g$) experienced by the planets. For a planet in a circular orbit, the centripetal acceleration is provided by the gravitational force:
$$a_g = \frac{v^2}{r} = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Since both planets orbit the same star (same $M$), the ratio of the accelerations is:
$$\frac{a_{Endo}}{a_{Exo}} = \frac{GM / r_{Endo}^2}{GM / r_{Exo}^2} = \left( \frac{r_{Exo}}{r_{Endo}} \right)^2$$

$$r_{Endo} = 6.2 \times 10^8 \text{ m}$$
$$r_{Exo} = 1.56 \times 10^9 \text{ m} = 15.6 \times 10^8 \text{ m}$$
$$\text{Ratio } \frac{r_{Exo}}{r_{Endo}} = \frac{15.6 \times 10^8}{6.2 \times 10^8} \approx 2.516$$

**Step 2: Calculate the ratio of accelerations.**
$$\frac{a_{Endo}}{a_{Exo}} = (2.516)^2 \approx 6.33$$

This means the acceleration due to gravity experienced by Endo is approximately 6.3 times greater than that experienced by Exo.
