The physics principle involved is the **Doppler Effect**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, you need to understand how the relative motion between a source of waves (the fire truck siren) and an observer (Chris) affects the observed frequency.

1.  **Doppler Effect Principles:** When a source moves towards an observer, the wavefronts are "compressed," leading to a shorter wavelength and a higher frequency ($f_{observed} > f_{source}$). When the source moves away, the wavefronts are "stretched," leading to a longer wavelength and a lower frequency ($f_{observed} < f_{source}$).
2.  **Sketching the Graph:** 
    *   Since the speed of the truck is constant, the shift in frequency will also be constant while the truck is on either side of Chris. This is why you draw horizontal lines.
    *   The "towards" section must be placed **above** the $500\text{ Hz}$ dotted line.
    *   The "away" section must be placed **below** the $500\text{ Hz}$ dotted line.
3.  **Naming the Principle:** The phenomenon where the frequency of a wave changes due to the relative motion between the source and the observer is universally known in physics as the Doppler Effect.
