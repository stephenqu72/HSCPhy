To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of circular motion in a horizontal plane.

### (a) Direction of the resultant force
Since the ball is performing uniform circular motion in a horizontal circle, the resultant force (the net force) must be the **centripetal force**. This force is directed horizontally toward the center of the circular path.

### (b) Construct the weight vector
The only two forces acting on the ball are the normal force $N$ and the weight $mg$.
- Vertical equilibrium: The vertical component of $N$ must balance the weight $mg$. Thus, $N \sin\theta = mg$.
- Your arrow for weight must point **vertically downwards** from the ball, with a length such that if you complete the rectangle, the normal force $N$ acts as the diagonal.

### (c) Derivation
1. **Vertical forces:** The ball is not accelerating vertically, so the vertical component of the normal force must equal the weight:
   $$N \sin\theta = mg \implies N = \frac{mg}{\sin\theta}$$
2. **Horizontal forces:** The horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force $F$:
   $$F = N \cos\theta$$
3. **Substitute:** Substitute the expression for $N$ into the centripetal force equation:
   $$F = \left(\frac{mg}{\sin\theta}\right) \cos\theta = \frac{mg}{\tan\theta}$$
