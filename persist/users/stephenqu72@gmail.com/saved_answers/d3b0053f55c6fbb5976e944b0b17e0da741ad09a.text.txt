To solve this problem, we need to determine the total charge passing through the wire in the given time and then find how many individual electrons constitute that charge.

### Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Potential difference ($V$) = $240\text{ V}$
*   Resistance ($R$) = $12\text{ }\Omega$
*   Time ($t$) = $5.0\text{ s}$
*   Charge of a single electron ($e$) = $1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
*   (Note: The length of the wire is extraneous information not required to solve this problem.)

**2. Calculate the current ($I$):**
Using Ohm’s Law, $V = IR$, we can rearrange to solve for current:
$$I = \frac{V}{R} = \frac{240\text{ V}}{12\text{ }\Omega} = 20\text{ A}$$

**3. Calculate the total charge ($Q$):**
Current is the rate of flow of charge ($I = \frac{Q}{t}$), so the total charge that passes in $5.0$ seconds is:
$$Q = I \times t = 20\text{ A} \times 5.0\text{ s} = 100\text{ C}$$

**4. Calculate the number of electrons ($n$):**
The total charge is equal to the number of electrons multiplied by the charge per electron ($Q = n \times e$):
$$n = \frac{Q}{e}$$
$$n = \frac{100\text{ C}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C/electron}}$$
$$n = 6.25 \times 10^{20} \text{ electrons}$$

Rounding to match the significant figures provided in the options, we get $6.2 \times 10^{20}$.

**Conclusion:**
Comparing our result to the given choices, the correct option is **(D)**.
