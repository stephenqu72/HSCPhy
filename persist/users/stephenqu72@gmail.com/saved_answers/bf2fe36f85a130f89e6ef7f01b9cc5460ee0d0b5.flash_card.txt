### Front
How does the value of the Hubble constant ($H_0$) affect the estimated age of the universe ($t$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The age of the universe is inversely proportional to the Hubble constant; a smaller $H_0$ implies a greater age.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $t \approx \frac{1}{H_0}$
- **When to use it:** When calculating the age of the universe or comparing age estimates based on different measurements of the expansion rate.
- **Common trap:** Assuming a smaller $H_0$ makes the universe younger; remember that a slower expansion rate means it took longer to reach the current size.
