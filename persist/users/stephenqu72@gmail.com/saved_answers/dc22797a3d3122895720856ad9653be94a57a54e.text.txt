### Answer

**a) Calculate the threshold frequency for iron.**

To find the threshold frequency ($f_0$), we use the work function equation $\Phi = h f_0$.
Given $\Phi = 4.5 \text{ eV}$, we must first convert this to Joules using $1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$.
$$\Phi = 4.5 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} = 7.209 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$
Using Planck’s constant $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$:
$$f_0 = \frac{\Phi}{h} = \frac{7.209 \times 10^{-19}}{6.626 \times 10^{-34}} \approx 1.09 \times 10^{15} \text{ Hz}$$

**b) Determine the initial velocity ($u$).**

1.  **Plotting:** Plot the points $(3, 1.3)$, $(6, 0.97)$, $(9, 0.66)$, $(12, 0.36)$, and $(15, 0.05)$ on the provided axes.
2.  **Line of Best Fit:** Drawing a straight line through these points, the intercept on the $v^2$ axis (where displacement $s=0$) represents $u^2$.
3.  **Calculation:** Extending the line to the y-axis, the intercept is approximately $1.63 \times 10^{12} \text{ m}^2\text{s}^{-2}$.
4.  **Initial velocity:**
    $$u = \sqrt{1.63 \times 10^{12}} \approx 1.28 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s}$$
