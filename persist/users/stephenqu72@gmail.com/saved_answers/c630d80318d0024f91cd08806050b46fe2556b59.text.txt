To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of projectile motion and the conservation of mechanical energy.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. Identify the physics at maximum height:**
At the maximum height of a projectile's path, the vertical component of the velocity is zero. Therefore, the velocity of the ball at this point is equal to its horizontal velocity component, which remains constant throughout the flight:
$$v_{max\_height} = v_x = v_0 \cos(\theta)$$
where $v_0$ is the initial velocity and $\theta = 35^\circ$.

**2. Use the Kinetic Energy formula:**
Given the kinetic energy at maximum height $K_{top} = 22 \text{ J}$ and the mass $m = 600 \text{ g} = 0.6 \text{ kg}$, we can find the velocity at this point ($v_x$):
$$K = \frac{1}{2} m v_x^2$$
$$22 = \frac{1}{2} \times 0.6 \times v_x^2$$
$$22 = 0.3 \times v_x^2$$
$$v_x^2 = \frac{22}{0.3} \approx 73.333$$
$$v_x = \sqrt{73.333} \approx 8.5635 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$

**3. Relate $v_x$ to initial velocity $v_0$:**
Since $v_x = v_0 \cos(35^\circ)$, we rearrange to solve for $v_0$:
$$v_0 = \frac{v_x}{\cos(35^\circ)}$$
$$v_0 = \frac{8.5635}{\cos(35^\circ)}$$
Using $\cos(35^\circ) \approx 0.81915$:
$$v_0 \approx \frac{8.5635}{0.81915} \approx 10.454 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches option (B).

**Answer:**
The correct answer is **(B) 10.45 ms⁻¹**.
