### Front
How do you calculate average speed from a displacement-time graph?

### Back
- Key idea: Average speed is the total path distance (scalar) divided by total time.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v_{avg} = \frac{\text{total distance}}{\text{total time}}$
- When to use it: To find the rate of motion when direction changes (unlike velocity, which uses displacement).
- Common trap: Confusing total distance with displacement. You must add the absolute values of all movements, including those in the negative direction.
