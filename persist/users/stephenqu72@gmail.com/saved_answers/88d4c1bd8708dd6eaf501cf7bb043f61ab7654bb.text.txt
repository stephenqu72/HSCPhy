Hello! As an HSC Physics teacher, I'm happy to guide you through this problem. This is a classic "circular motion on a banked track" question. Let's break it down.

### (a) Free Body Diagram
To solve this, visualize the cyclist as a point mass on an incline.
1. **Gravity ($mg$):** Acts vertically downwards from the centre of mass.
2. **Normal Force ($N$):** Acts perpendicular to the surface of the banked track, pointing upwards and inwards towards the center of the turn.
3. **Net Force ($F_{net}$):** Since the cyclist is moving in a horizontal circle, the net force must be directed **horizontally towards the center of the circle**. This net force is the horizontal component of the normal force ($N \sin \theta$).

**Diagram description:** Draw an inclined line. Place a dot on it. Draw an arrow pointing straight down ($mg$) and an arrow perpendicular to the incline ($N$). Draw a horizontal dashed arrow pointing towards the center of the circle and label it "Net Force ($F_{net}$ or $F_c$)".

---

### (b) Calculation
To find the net force (which is the centripetal force $F_c$), we look at the vertical and horizontal components of the forces:
1. **Vertical equilibrium:** The vertical component of the normal force must balance gravity:
   $$N \cos \theta = mg$$
2. **Horizontal force:** The horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force:
   $$F_{net} = N \sin \theta$$

From the first equation, $N = \frac{mg}{\cos \theta}$. Substitute this into the second equation:
$$F_{net} = \left(\frac{mg}{\cos \theta}\right) \sin \theta = mg \tan \theta$$

**Given values:**
- $m = 70\text{ kg}$
- $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$
- $\theta = 25^\circ$

**Calculation:**
$$F_{net} = 70 \times 9.8 \times \tan(25^\circ)$$
$$F_{net} = 686 \times 0.4663...$$
$$F_{net} \approx 319.7\text{ N}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (consistent with the provided data):
$$F_{net} \approx 320\text{ N}$$

**Summary:** The net force required to keep the cyclist in uniform circular motion at this specific angle is determined by the gravitational force and the banking angle, resulting in approximately $320\text{ N}$ directed horizontally toward the center of the track.
