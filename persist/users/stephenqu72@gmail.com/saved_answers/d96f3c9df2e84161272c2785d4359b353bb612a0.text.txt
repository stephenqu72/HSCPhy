The correct answer is **D. diffraction of electrons through a crystal**.

### Explanation
To determine which phenomenon provides evidence for the wave-like behavior of electrons, we must look for evidence of **interference** or **diffraction**, which are defining characteristics of wave motion.

1.  **Analyze the options:**
    *   **A. Photoelectric effect:** This provides evidence for the **particle-like** nature of light (photons), where light transfers energy to electrons in discrete packets.
    *   **B & C. Atomic emission and absorption spectra:** These provide evidence for the quantization of energy levels within an atom (Bohr’s model). While the *stability* of these orbits is explained by the de Broglie hypothesis, the spectral lines themselves are primarily evidence of discrete energy transitions, not direct visual evidence of electron diffraction.
    *   **D. Diffraction of electrons through a crystal:** When electrons are directed at a crystal lattice (such as in the Davisson-Germer experiment), they produce a diffraction pattern similar to X-rays. Diffraction is a property unique to waves. The spacing of the atoms in the crystal acts as a diffraction grating, and the resulting interference pattern can only be explained if the electrons exhibit wave-like properties with a de Broglie wavelength defined by:
        $$\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$$
        where $h$ is Planck's constant and $p$ is the momentum of the electron.

2.  **Conclusion:** The observation of interference patterns (diffraction) when electrons interact with a crystal lattice provides direct experimental confirmation of de Broglie's wave-particle duality hypothesis for matter.
