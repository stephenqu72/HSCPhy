### Front
How do you calculate the magnitude of a resultant force when multiple forces act along perpendicular ($x$ and $y$) axes?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Resolve all forces into their orthogonal components, find the net force for each axis, and combine them using vector addition.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $R = \sqrt{R_x^2 + R_y^2}$ (where $R_x$ and $R_y$ are net component forces).
- **When to use it:** When dealing with multiple concurrent forces acting at right angles.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to assign negative signs to forces acting in opposite directions (e.g., treating all forces as positive), which leads to incorrect $R_x$ or $R_y$ values.
