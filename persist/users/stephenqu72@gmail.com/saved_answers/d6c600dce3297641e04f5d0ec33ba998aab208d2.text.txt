### Teacher's Guide and Solution

This question tests your understanding of **charged particle motion in magnetic fields**, the **properties of ionizing radiation**, and the **nature of radioactive decay**.

---

#### a. Purpose of the lead block
**Answer:** The lead block acts as a collimator. It absorbs radiation emitted in all directions and forces the particles to exit through a small aperture, creating a narrow, directional beam of radiation for the investigation.

**Explanation:** In physics experiments involving radiation, we need a controlled beam. Lead is dense and has a high atomic number, making it an excellent shielding material to stop ionizing radiation.

#### b. Identify the straight-line radiation
**Answer:** Gamma ($\gamma$) radiation. 
**Reason:** Gamma rays are high-energy electromagnetic radiation with no electric charge. Since the magnetic force on a moving charge is $F = qvB\sin(\theta)$, a particle with charge $q = 0$ experiences zero magnetic force and thus travels in a straight line.

#### c. Nature of the radiation emitted
**Answer:** Gamma rays are high-frequency, short-wavelength electromagnetic photons emitted by an excited nucleus as it transitions to a lower energy state.
**Explanation:** According to the Law of Conservation of Energy, the energy released during a nuclear transition (the mass-energy equivalence $E = \Delta mc^2$) is carried away by the photon. The nucleus loses potential energy, and that energy is conserved by being converted into the radiant energy of the gamma photon.

#### d. Identify the curved particles
**Answer:**
*   **Sample X:** Beta-plus ($\beta^+$) particles (positrons). Using the Right-Hand Rule (RHR), the magnetic field is into the page ($\otimes$), the velocity is to the right, and the upward force suggests a positive charge.
*   **Sample Y:** Beta-minus ($\beta^-$) particles (electrons). Using the Left-Hand Rule (or RHR with a negative charge), the downward force indicates a negative charge.

**Explanation:** 
1.  Apply the Right-Hand Rule: Point your fingers into the page (B-field) and your thumb to the right (velocity). For a **positive** charge, the palm pushes **up**. Since the curve for X is upward, it must be positively charged ($\beta^+$).
2.  For a **negative** charge, the force is reversed. The downward curve for Y indicates a negative charge ($\beta^-$). 
3.  *Note:* Alpha particles are much heavier and would have a much larger radius of curvature than beta particles. Given the tight curves shown, these are clearly beta particles.
