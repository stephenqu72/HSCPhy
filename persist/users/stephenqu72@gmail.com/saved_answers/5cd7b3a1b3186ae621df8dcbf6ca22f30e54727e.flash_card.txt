### Front
How does the mechanical energy of a magnet change as it moves from free fall into a copper cylinder?

### Back
- Key idea: Mechanical energy is not conserved; kinetic energy decreases as it is converted into thermal energy via eddy currents.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Lenz's Law (and $I^2R$ heating).
- When to use it: Analyzing magnetic braking or electromagnetic induction in a conductor.
- Common trap: Stating that energy is "lost" from the system; energy is actually dissipated/transformed into heat, satisfying the Law of Conservation of Energy.
