### Front
How can you increase the reliability of period ($T$) measurements for a pendulum to reduce random error?

### Back
- Key idea: Timing multiple oscillations ($n$) and dividing the total time by $n$ spreads the human reaction time error over a larger total value.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $T = \frac{t_{total}}{n}$
- When to use it: Whenever using a manual stopwatch to measure a periodic event.
- Common trap: Confusing **reliability** (consistent results/reducing random error) with **validity** (correct method/controlling variables) or **accuracy** (proximity to the true value).
