### (c) Calculation of Magnetic Field Strength ($B$)
The motor effect force on a coil is given by $\tau = nIABr$, where the student is applying a force at a distance. More specifically, for a rectangular coil of width $w$ being stopped by a force $F$ applied at the edge, the torque produced by the magnetic field is $\tau = nIABw$ and the counter-torque by the external force is $\tau = F \times \frac{w}{2}$. Equating these:
$$F \cdot \frac{w}{2} = nIABw$$
$$F = 2nI B \cdot (\text{effective length})$$
Alternatively, treating this as a balance of forces on the wire segment: The force on one side of the coil is $F_{side} = nIBL$. Since the student applies a force to stop the rotation, we relate the measured force to the torque balance. Given the geometry, the force $F$ acts at the edge (distance $d = 0.05 \text{ m}$ from the axis). The torque equation is:
$$\tau = F \times d = nIBL \times d$$
Using $n = 50$, $L = 0.2 \text{ m}$, and $d = 0.05 \text{ m}$:
$$F = (nBLd) \cdot I \implies \text{Slope} = nBLd$$
From the graph, pick two points on the line, e.g., $(0, 0)$ and $(4, 0.097)$:
$$\text{Slope} = \frac{0.097 - 0}{4 - 0} = 0.02425 \text{ N/A}$$
Equate to variables:
$$0.02425 = 50 \times B \times 0.2 \times 0.05$$
$$0.02425 = 0.5 \times B$$
$$B = \frac{0.02425}{0.5} = 0.0485 \text{ T}$$
