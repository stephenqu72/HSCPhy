### Front
How do you determine the charge ($q$) of a stationary oil drop suspended between charged parallel plates?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Static equilibrium where electric force ($F_E$) balances gravitational force ($F_g$).
- **Formula / law / constant:** $qE = mg$ or $q = \frac{mg}{E}$; $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$.
- **When to use it:** When an object with mass is suspended at rest in a uniform electric field.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the direction of force on a negative charge; remember $F_E$ is opposite to the field direction for a negative charge.
