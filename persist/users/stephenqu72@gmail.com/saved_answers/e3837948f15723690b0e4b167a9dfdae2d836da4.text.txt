To calculate Planck's constant based on the student's experimental data, we use the relationship defined by the photoelectric effect equation at the threshold frequency.

### Explanation and Working

The work function ($\Phi$) is defined as the minimum energy required to remove an electron from the surface of a metal. This is related to the threshold frequency ($f_0$) by the equation:

$$\Phi = h f_0$$

Where:
*   $\Phi$ is the work function (given as $3.2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$)
*   $h$ is Planck's constant (the value we need to determine)
*   $f_0$ is the threshold frequency (given as $6.5 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$)

**Step 1: Rearrange the formula to solve for $h$**
$$h = \frac{\Phi}{f_0}$$

**Step 2: Substitute the given values**
$$h = \frac{3.2 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}}{6.5 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}}$$

**Step 3: Calculate the result**
$$h \approx 4.923 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (consistent with the provided data):
$$h \approx 4.9 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$$
