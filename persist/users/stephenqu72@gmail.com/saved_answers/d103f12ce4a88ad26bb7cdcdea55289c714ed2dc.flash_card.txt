### Front
What defines an object in "freefall," and what is the primary constraint on its path?

### Back
- **Key idea:** An object is in freefall if gravity is the *only* force acting upon it.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation: $F_g = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$.
- **When to use it:** To determine if an object's trajectory is purely gravitational (e.g., orbits, ballistic paths).
- **Common trap:** Assuming an object moving away from Earth is not in freefall; freefall simply means no non-gravitational forces (like thrust or air resistance) are present. Gravity always curves a path toward the mass source, regardless of velocity.
