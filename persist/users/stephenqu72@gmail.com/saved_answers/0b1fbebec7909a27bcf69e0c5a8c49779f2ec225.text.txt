To solve this problem, we need to apply the physics of a charged particle moving in a magnetic field. When an electron enters a magnetic field perpendicularly, the magnetic force acts as the centripetal force, causing it to travel in a circular path.

### 1. Identify the relevant formulas
The magnetic force $F_B$ on a charged particle moving with velocity $v$ perpendicular to a magnetic field $B$ is given by:
$$F_B = qvB$$

Since this force provides the centripetal force $F_c$ for circular motion ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$), we can equate them:
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Rearranging for the radius $r$:
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

### 2. Identify the constants and given values
*   Mass of an electron ($m$): $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
*   Charge of an electron ($q$): $1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
*   Velocity ($v$): $6.5 \times 10^3 \text{ ms}^{-1}$
*   Magnetic Field ($B$): $2.4 \times 10^{-3} \text{ T}$

### 3. Calculation
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}) \times (6.5 \times 10^3 \text{ ms}^{-1})}{(1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \times (2.4 \times 10^{-3} \text{ T})}$$

$$r = \frac{5.9215 \times 10^{-27}}{3.8448 \times 10^{-22}} \text{ m}$$

$$r \approx 1.54 \times 10^{-5} \text{ m}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches option **(C)**.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(C)**.
