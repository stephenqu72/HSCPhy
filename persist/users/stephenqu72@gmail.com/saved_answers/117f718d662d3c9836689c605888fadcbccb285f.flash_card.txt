### Front
In circular orbital motion, what is the direction of a satellite's acceleration vector and why?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration is always centripetal (directed toward the center of the orbit).
- Formula / law / constant: Newton’s Second Law ($F_{net} = ma$) and Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation.
- When to use it: Any question asking for the direction of force or acceleration for an object in a circular orbit.
- Common trap: Confusing the direction of acceleration (center-seeking) with the direction of velocity (tangent to the orbit).
