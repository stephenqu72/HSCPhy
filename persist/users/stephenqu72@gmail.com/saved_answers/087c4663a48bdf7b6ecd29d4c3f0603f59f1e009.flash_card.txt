### Front
Why can spherical celestial bodies (e.g., Earth and Sun) be treated as point masses in Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation?

### Back
- Key idea: The **Shell Theorem** allows a spherically symmetric body to be treated as if all its mass is concentrated at its center of mass.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F = G \frac{Mm}{d^2}$
- When to use it: When the distance ($d$) between objects is very large compared to the radii of the objects.
- Common trap: Assuming the law only applies to "tiny" objects; it applies to large spheres as long as they are treated as point masses.
