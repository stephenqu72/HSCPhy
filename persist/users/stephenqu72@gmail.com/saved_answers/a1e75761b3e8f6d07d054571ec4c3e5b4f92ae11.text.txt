To solve this problem, we need to calculate the magnetic flux through a cross-section of an air-filled solenoid.

### Given Data:
*   Turns per metre ($n$) = $4000 \text{ m}^{-1}$
*   Area ($A$) = $0.01 \text{ m}^2$
*   Current ($I$) = $6 \text{ A}$
*   Permeability of free space ($\mu_0$) = $4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Calculate the Magnetic Field ($B$):**
    The magnetic field inside a long, tightly wound solenoid is given by the formula:
    $$B = \mu_0 n I$$
    Substitute the known values:
    $$B = (4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times 4000 \times 6$$
    $$B = (4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times 24000$$
    $$B = 9.6\pi \times 10^{-3} \text{ T} \approx 0.03016 \text{ T}$$

2.  **Calculate the Magnetic Flux ($\Phi$):**
    Magnetic flux is defined as the product of the magnetic field and the area through which it passes ($ \Phi = B \cdot A $). Note that for a single cross-section of the solenoid, we use the area of the coil:
    $$\Phi = B \times A$$
    $$\Phi = (9.6\pi \times 10^{-3} \text{ T}) \times 0.01 \text{ m}^2$$
    $$\Phi = 9.6\pi \times 10^{-5} \text{ Wb}$$
    $$\Phi \approx 3.016 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb}$$

Comparing this result to the provided options, the correct answer is **(A)**.

### Explanation:
*   First, we determine the strength of the magnetic field produced by the solenoid. Because it is an air-filled solenoid, we use $\mu_0$. 
*   We use the formula for a solenoid field, $B = \mu_0 n I$, where $n$ is the number of turns per unit length.
*   Once $B$ is found, we calculate the flux $\Phi$ by multiplying $B$ by the cross-sectional area $A$. 
*   The result $3.01 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb}$ matches option (A).
