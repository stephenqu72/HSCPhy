To determine the correct answer, we must use the formula for centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) of an object moving in a circular path:

$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the initial state:**
    Let the initial acceleration be $a_1$, where:
    $$a_1 = \frac{v^2}{r}$$

2.  **Define the new state:**
    The speed is doubled ($v_{new} = 2v$) and the radius is halved ($r_{new} = \frac{r}{2}$).

3.  **Calculate the new acceleration ($a_2$):**
    Substitute the new values into the formula:
    $$a_2 = \frac{(v_{new})^2}{r_{new}}$$
    $$a_2 = \frac{(2v)^2}{\frac{r}{2}}$$

4.  **Simplify the expression:**
    $$a_2 = \frac{4v^2}{\frac{r}{2}}$$
    Since dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal:
    $$a_2 = 4v^2 \cdot \frac{2}{r}$$
    $$a_2 = 8 \cdot \left( \frac{v^2}{r} \right)$$

5.  **Compare with the initial acceleration:**
    Since $a_1 = \frac{v^2}{r}$, we can substitute it into the equation for $a_2$:
    $$a_2 = 8 \cdot a_1$$
