This question refers to the famous **Hafele-Keating experiment**. Here are the answers and the explanation to help you master this concept for your HSC Physics exam.

### Answers

**a) What was the experiment trying to measure?**
The experiment was designed to test the predictions of Einstein's theories of relativity (Special and General Relativity) regarding time dilation. Specifically, it sought to demonstrate that time passes at different rates for observers moving at different velocities (Special Relativity/Kinematic Time Dilation) and in different gravitational potentials (General Relativity/Gravitational Time Dilation).

**b) Why were four clocks used on both the planes and at the Observatory?**
To increase the reliability and precision of the data by reducing the impact of random errors or individual clock malfunctions. By using multiple clocks, researchers could calculate an average and ensure that the detected time difference was a consistent physical phenomenon rather than an artifact of a single faulty instrument.

**c) Identify one other example of experimental evidence supporting the prediction of Special Relativity and indicate which prediction is supported.**
*   **Example:** The observation of muons produced in the upper atmosphere.
*   **Prediction supported:** Time dilation. Muons have a very short half-life and should decay before reaching the Earth's surface. However, because they travel at speeds close to the speed of light ($c$), time dilation causes their internal "clocks" to slow down from our perspective, allowing them to survive long enough to be detected at sea level.

### How to solve it (Teacher's Tips)
*   **For part (a):** Always link time dilation back to the two core theories. In the Hafele-Keating experiment, the clocks on the plane moving East experienced a different total effect compared to those moving West, due to the combination of their velocity relative to the Earth's rotation and their altitude.
*   **For part (b):** This is a standard scientific method question. Whenever you see a question about "why use multiple pieces of equipment," think about **reliability** and **precision**. It reduces the uncertainty of the measurement.
*   **For part (c):** You need to memorize at least two standard proofs for relativity. Besides the Hafele-Keating experiment and muon decay, you could also discuss the **precise synchronization of GPS satellites**, which must adjust for both Special Relativity (velocity) and General Relativity (gravity) to maintain accurate time.
