### Front
How is the energy released during a nuclear fission reaction calculated from mass measurements?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Energy released ($E$) is equivalent to the mass defect ($\Delta m$) lost during the reaction, governed by mass-energy equivalence ($E=mc^2$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\Delta m = \sum m_{\text{reactants}} - \sum m_{\text{products}}$; $E = \Delta m \times 931.5 \, \text{MeV}/u$.
- **When to use it:** When given isotopic masses before and after a nuclear reaction to determine total energy output.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to account for all neutrons produced (e.g., if 3 neutrons are released, ensure they are included in the product mass).
