To solve this problem, we apply the principles of electromagnetism, specifically the interaction between a current-carrying conductor and an external magnetic field.

### Answer
1. **Explanation:** When the current $I$ flows upwards through the wire, it generates a circular magnetic field around the wire (by the Right-Hand Grip Rule). This wire is situated within the external magnetic field $B$ produced by the permanent bar magnet, which points from the North pole to the South pole. According to the motor effect, a current-carrying wire placed in an external magnetic field experiences a force $F = IlB \sin \theta$. Because the wire is carrying current through the external magnetic field, it experiences a magnetic force that causes it to move.
2. **Direction:** Using the Right-Hand Rule (or Fleming's Left-Hand Rule for motors):
   - **Current ($I$):** Upwards.
   - **Magnetic Field ($B$):** From the North pole of the magnet towards the South pole (pointing towards the wire).
   - **Force ($F$):** Pointing the fingers of your left hand: point your thumb up (current), fingers towards the wire from the magnet (field), and your palm faces the direction of the force. The force on the wire is directed **away from the magnet** (to the left in the diagram).
