To answer this question effectively, you need to apply the concept of the **Doppler Effect** for a moving observer.

### Answer
When the car moves towards the stationary police car, the observer intercepts more wave fronts per unit of time than they would if they were stationary. Although the speed of sound in the air remains constant, the **relative velocity** between the observer and the sound waves increases. Because the observer is moving into the waves, they encounter the wave crests more frequently. Since pitch is directly related to the observed frequency ($f_{obs} = f_s \frac{v + v_{obs}}{v}$), the increase in the rate of wave interception is perceived by the human ear as an increase in pitch.

### How to solve it (HSC Teacher Tips)
To secure all 3 marks for this physics explanation, you must structure your answer logically:

1.  **Identify the phenomenon:** Start by naming the **Doppler Effect**. State clearly that it occurs because the motion of the observer changes the rate at which they encounter wave fronts.
2.  **Clarify the constants and variables:** Explicitly state that the **speed of sound ($v$)** remains constant in the medium (air). This is a common pitfall; students often mistakenly think the sound itself speeds up, but it is the *relative* speed that changes.
3.  **Explain the mechanism of frequency:** Explain that as you move towards the source, you cover the distance between wave crests faster. Mathematically, the frequency $f = \frac{v}{\lambda}$. Since the wavelength ($\lambda$) emitted by the source stays the same, but the relative velocity at which the observer meets the wave increases, the **observed frequency** ($f_{obs}$) increases.
4.  **Connect to pitch:** Conclude by explicitly stating that in acoustics, a higher frequency corresponds to a higher pitch, which explains the observation described in the question.
