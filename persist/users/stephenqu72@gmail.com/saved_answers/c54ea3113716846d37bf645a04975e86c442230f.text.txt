To determine the correct answer, we must understand the physics of **refractive index** and its relationship to wavelength.

### Explanation

1.  **Dispersion:** Dispersion occurs because the refractive index ($n$) of a medium, such as glass, is dependent on the frequency (or wavelength) of the light passing through it. This is known as *chromatic dispersion*.
2.  **Snell's Law:** According to Snell's Law, $n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2)$, light with a higher refractive index will be refracted (bent) more significantly when moving from a less dense medium (air) to a more dense medium (glass).
3.  **Wavelength dependence:** In glass, the refractive index is higher for shorter wavelengths (violet light) and lower for longer wavelengths (red light). Therefore, violet light experiences more bending (refraction) than red light.
4.  **The Prism:** When white light enters a prism, the different colors travel at different speeds within the glass and thus bend by different amounts. Violet light bends the most towards the base of the prism, while red light bends the least.
5.  **Analyzing the options:** We are looking for the diagram where violet is bent at a steeper angle toward the base than red.
