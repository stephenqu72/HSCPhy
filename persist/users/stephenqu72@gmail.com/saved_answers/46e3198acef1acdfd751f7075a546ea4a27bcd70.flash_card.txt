### Front
How do you relate the orbital periods ($T$) and semi-major axes ($r$) of two objects orbiting the same central mass?

### Back
- Key idea: Kepler’s Third Law states the ratio $T^2/r^3$ is constant for all bodies orbiting the same central mass.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\frac{T_1^2}{r_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{r_2^3}$ (or $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$)
- When to use it: Any problem involving comparative orbital mechanics or calculating unknown periods/radii.
- Common trap: Forgetting to cube the radius ratio or failing to take the square root of the final answer.
