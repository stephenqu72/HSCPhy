### Front
How do you relate the orbital periods and radii of two objects orbiting the same central mass?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \text{constant}$ or $\frac{r_1^3}{T_1^2} = \frac{r_2^3}{T_2^2}$.
- **When to use it:** When comparing two objects orbiting the same central body (e.g., planets around a star or satellites around a planet).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units to be consistent (e.g., using days for one period and seconds for another) or failing to cube the radius and square the period correctly.
