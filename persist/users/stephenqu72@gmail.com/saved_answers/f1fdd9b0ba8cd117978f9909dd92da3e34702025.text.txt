To find the wavelength of the emitted photon when an electron transitions from energy level $n_i = 3$ to $n_f = 1$ in a hydrogen atom, we use the Rydberg formula for the hydrogen spectrum:

$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$

### Step-by-step Solution:

1.  **Identify the constants and variables:**
    *   Rydberg constant, $R_H \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \text{ m}^{-1}$
    *   Initial energy level, $n_i = 3$
    *   Final energy level, $n_f = 1$

2.  **Substitute the values into the formula:**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \times \left( \frac{1}{1^2} - \frac{1}{3^2} \right)$$
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \times \left( 1 - \frac{1}{9} \right)$$
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \times \left( \frac{8}{9} \right)$$

3.  **Calculate the value:**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} \approx 9.751 \times 10^6 \text{ m}^{-1}$$
    $$\lambda = \frac{1}{9.751 \times 10^6} \approx 1.0255 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$$

4.  **Convert to nanometers (nm):**
    Since $1 \text{ nm} = 10^{-9} \text{ m}$, we multiply by $10^9$:
    $$\lambda \approx 102.55 \text{ nm}$$
    Rounding this value, we get $1.03 \times 10^2 \text{ nm}$.
