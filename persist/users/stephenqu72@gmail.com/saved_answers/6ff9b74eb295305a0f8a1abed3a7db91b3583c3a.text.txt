To solve this problem, we must apply the physics of orbital mechanics. For a satellite in a circular orbit around a central body:

1.  **Total Mechanical Energy ($E_{total}$):**
    $$E_{total} = K + U = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$$
    As the orbital radius $r$ increases, the total energy becomes less negative (it increases toward zero). This matches the original provided graph, where the points trend upward from $-6$ toward $0$.

2.  **Potential Energy ($U$):**
    $$U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$$
    This is always negative. As $r$ increases, $U$ becomes less negative (it increases toward zero).

3.  **Kinetic Energy ($K$):**
    $$K = \frac{GMm}{2r}$$
    This is always positive. As $r$ increases, $K$ decreases (it approaches zero from above).

4.  **The Relationship:**
    Crucially, for any circular orbit:
    $$E_{total} = K + U$$
    Since $U = -2K$, it follows that $E_{total} = -K$. Or, put simply, **$K = -U / 2$**.
