### Front
Does the mass of a satellite affect its required orbital velocity for a circular orbit at a specific altitude?

### Back
- Key idea: Orbital velocity is independent of the satellite's mass ($m$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$
- When to use it: Any question involving circular orbits around a central mass.
- Common trap: Assuming $m$ affects $v$ or $T$ because it appears in the force balance equation ($F_g = F_c$); it cancels out on both sides.
