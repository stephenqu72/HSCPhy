To find the angular velocity of the satellite, we use the fundamental relationship between angular velocity ($\omega$), the angle subtended in one full orbit ($2\pi$ radians), and the time period of that orbit ($T$).

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given information:**
    *   Time period ($T$) = $3.00$ days.
    *   Note: The orbital radius is provided ($823 \text{ km}$ above the surface), but it is **extraneous information** because the angular velocity depends only on the time taken to complete a full revolution.

2.  **Convert the time period into SI units (seconds):**
    *   $1 \text{ day} = 24 \text{ hours} \times 60 \text{ minutes} \times 60 \text{ seconds} = 86,400 \text{ seconds}$.
    *   $T = 3.00 \times 86,400 \text{ s} = 259,200 \text{ s}$.

3.  **Apply the formula for angular velocity:**
    $$\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T}$$

4.  **Calculate the value:**
    $$\omega = \frac{2 \times \pi}{259,200 \text{ s}}$$
    $$\omega \approx \frac{6.283185}{259,200} \text{ rad/s}$$
    $$\omega \approx 2.424 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches option **C**.

**Answer:** The correct option is **C**.
