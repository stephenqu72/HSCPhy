To determine the correct answer, we need to analyze the motion of a projectile at its point of maximum height.

### Answer
The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we look at the physics of projectile motion:

1.  **Vertical component of velocity ($v_y$):** As a projectile moves upward, gravity acts against its motion, causing the vertical velocity to decrease until it momentarily stops at the peak of its trajectory. At the exact moment the projectile reaches its **maximum height**, its vertical velocity is $v_y = 0$. After this point, it begins to accelerate downwards.
2.  **Acceleration ($a$):** In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), the only force acting on the object throughout its entire flight is gravity. Gravity exerts a constant downward acceleration on the object. This acceleration is equal to $g$ (the acceleration due to free fall), and it remains constant at $g$ regardless of the projectile's position, including at the peak of the trajectory. 

Comparing this to the options:
*   At max height, $v_y = 0$.
*   At max height, $a = g$.

This corresponds to row **D** in the table.
