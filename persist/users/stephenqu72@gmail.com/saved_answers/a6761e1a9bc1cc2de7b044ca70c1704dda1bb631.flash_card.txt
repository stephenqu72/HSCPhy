### Front
How do you calculate the required radius ($r$) for a rotating space station to simulate a specific artificial gravity ($a_c$) given a rotation period ($T$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Artificial gravity is generated by the centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) of a rotating frame; set $a_c$ equal to $9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$.
- **Formula:** $r = \frac{a_c T^2}{4\pi^2}$
- **When to use it:** When calculating dimensions for space stations or centrifuges to achieve Earth-equivalent gravity.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to square the period ($T^2$) or using linear velocity ($v$) instead of period when time is the given variable.
