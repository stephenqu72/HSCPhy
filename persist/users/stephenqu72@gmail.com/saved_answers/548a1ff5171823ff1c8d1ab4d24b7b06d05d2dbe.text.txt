### Explanation

To solve this question, you need to understand the historical context of the corpuscular theory of light (championed by Isaac Newton) and the specific physical predictions it made.

1.  **Analyze the Corpuscular Model:** This model proposed that light consists of tiny "corpuscles" (particles). 
    *   **A (Travel through a vacuum):** Particles can move through a vacuum (like how bullets travel through space), so this is consistent with the model.
    *   **B (Reflection):** Newton explained reflection by treating light particles like elastic billiard balls bouncing off a surface, where the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection. This is consistent.
    *   **D (Refraction/Bending):** Newton explained refraction by suggesting that particles of light were "pulled" into a denser medium by gravitational-like forces, causing them to change direction. This is consistent with his model.

2.  **Evaluate the "Failure" of the Model:**
    *   **C (Slower in water than air):** To explain refraction (bending) using the corpuscular model, Newton's theory required that light particles **speed up** when entering a denser medium (like water or glass). He argued that the attractive forces of the medium accelerated the particles. 
    *   Later, Foucault’s experiment (1850) measured the speed of light and proved that light actually travels **slower** in a denser medium than in a vacuum/air. Because the corpuscular model mathematically predicted that light should speed up, and the experimental evidence proved it slowed down, the model could not correctly explain this observation.

Therefore, the observation that light travels slower in water than in air contradicted the core mathematical derivation of the corpuscular model.
