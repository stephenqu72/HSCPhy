To solve this problem, we use the physics principle of **torque** ($\tau$), which is the rotational equivalent of force.

### Part (a)
**Calculation:**
The formula for torque is:
$$\tau = rF \sin(\theta)$$
Where:
- $r$ is the length of the lever arm (spanner) = $46\text{ cm} = 0.46\text{ m}$.
- $F$ is the applied force = $92\text{ N}$.
- $\theta$ is the angle between the force and the spanner. Since the force is applied "perpendicular to the radius," $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

$$\tau = 0.46\text{ m} \times 92\text{ N} \times 1$$
$$\tau = 42.32\text{ Nm}$$

**Answer:** The torque applied is $42.32\text{ Nm}$.

---

### Part (b)
**Calculation:**
We need to produce the same torque ($\tau = 42.32\text{ Nm}$) while the force is applied at an angle of $\theta = 50^\circ$ to the spanner. Rearranging the torque formula to solve for the new force ($F_{new}$):
$$\tau = r F_{new} \sin(50^\circ)$$
$$F_{new} = \frac{\tau}{r \sin(50^\circ)}$$

Substituting the values:
$$F_{new} = \frac{42.32}{0.46 \times \sin(50^\circ)}$$
$$F_{new} = \frac{42.32}{0.46 \times 0.766}$$
$$F_{new} = \frac{42.32}{0.35236}$$
$$F_{new} \approx 120.1\text{ N}$$

**Answer:** The astronaut would need to apply approximately $120.1\text{ N}$ of force.

---

### How to solve this type of question:
1. **Unit Conversion:** Always ensure the lever arm length is in standard SI units (meters). Converting $46\text{ cm}$ to $0.46\text{ m}$ is a common pitfall.
2. **Formula Selection:** Recognize that torque is maximized when the force is applied perpendicularly ($\sin(90^\circ) = 1$).
3. **Algebraic Manipulation:** When given a new angle, treat the torque as a constant and rearrange the equation $\tau = rF\sin(\theta)$ to isolate the unknown variable ($F$).
4. **Significant Figures:** Maintain consistency with the input values provided in the question.
