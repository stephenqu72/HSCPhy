### Answer
The experiment cannot differentiate between the two scenarios because of **Einstein’s Equivalence Principle**. This principle states that the effects of a uniform gravitational field are locally indistinguishable from the effects of uniform acceleration.

*   **Person A's explanation:** Person A, believing they are on a planet, would attribute the pen's motion to the **gravitational force** of the planet pulling the pen toward the floor. In this reference frame, the pen is in free fall within a gravitational field.
*   **Person B's explanation:** Person B, believing the craft is accelerating, would explain that the pen is not actually "falling." Instead, because the craft is accelerating upward, the **floor is rushing up to meet the pen**, which is essentially at rest (or moving at constant velocity) due to its inertia. From an inertial perspective outside the craft, the pen stays still while the floor accelerates toward it.

### How to solve this
To solve this, you must apply the core concepts of General Relativity:
1.  **Identify the principle:** Recognize that the scenario is a classic application of the Equivalence Principle. You are essentially inside an "Einstein Elevator."
2.  **Define the two frames:**
    *   **Gravitational frame:** Stationary in a gravitational field $g$. Objects accelerate toward the floor at $a = g$ because of the curvature of spacetime.
    *   **Accelerating frame:** In empty space accelerating at $a$. Objects remain stationary (Newton's First Law), and the floor accelerates toward them.
3.  **Synthesize:** Explain that because the physical observations (the pen hitting the floor) are identical in both cases, no local experiment—whether dropping a pen, measuring weight, or light bending—can distinguish between being at rest in a gravitational field and undergoing constant acceleration.
