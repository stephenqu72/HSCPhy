To solve this problem, we use Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle, which relates the energy released in a nuclear reaction to the mass lost during that reaction.

### Explanation and Calculation

**1. Identify the relevant formula:**
The relationship between mass and energy is given by:
$$E = \Delta m c^2$$
Where:
*   $E$ is the energy produced (in Joules, $J$)
*   $\Delta m$ is the mass decrease (in kilograms, $kg$)
*   $c$ is the speed of light in a vacuum, approximately $3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

**2. Rearrange the formula to solve for mass decrease ($\Delta m$):**
$$\Delta m = \frac{E}{c^2}$$

**3. Convert units and substitute values:**
The energy provided is $1.3 \text{ MJ}$. Since $1 \text{ MJ} = 10^6 \text{ J}$, the energy $E = 1.3 \times 10^6 \text{ J}$.
$$\Delta m = \frac{1.3 \times 10^6}{(3.00 \times 10^8)^2}$$
$$\Delta m = \frac{1.3 \times 10^6}{9.00 \times 10^{16}}$$
$$\Delta m \approx 1.44 \times 10^{-11} \text{ kg}$$

**Answer:**
The estimated mass decrease resulting from this fusion reaction is approximately $1.44 \times 10^{-11} \text{ kg}$.
