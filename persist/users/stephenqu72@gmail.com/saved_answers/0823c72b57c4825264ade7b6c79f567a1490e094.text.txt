To solve this problem, we need to understand the principles of **beta-minus decay** ($\beta^-$).

### Explanation

1.  **Understand Beta-minus Decay:** In beta-minus decay, a neutron inside the nucleus converts into a proton and an electron (the beta particle). The electron is ejected from the nucleus.
    *   The mass number ($A$) remains the same because a neutron becomes a proton.
    *   The atomic number ($Z$) increases by 1 because there is one additional proton.
    *   The general equation is: $_{Z}^{A}\text{X} \rightarrow {_{Z+1}^{A}\text{Y}} + {_{-1}^{0}\text{e}}$

2.  **Analyze the Options:**
    *   **Lithium (Li)** has an atomic number of 3. In beta decay, the resulting element must have an atomic number of $3 + 1 = 4$.
    *   Element 4 on the periodic table is **Beryllium (Be)**.
    *   Therefore, we are looking for a reaction that starts with Lithium, produces Beryllium (atomic number 4), and releases a beta particle (${_{-1}^{0}\text{e}}$).

3.  **Evaluate the choices:**
    *   **A:** Shows Boron ($B$, atomic number 5). Incorrect.
    *   **B:** Shows alpha decay/transmutation, not beta decay. Incorrect.
    *   **C:** Shows positron emission (${_{+1}^{0}\text{e}}$) and creates Helium. Incorrect.
    *   **D:** Shows $_{3}^{8}\text{Li} \rightarrow {_{4}^{8}\text{Be}} + {_{-1}^{0}\text{e}}$.
        *   Conservation of mass: $8 = 8 + 0$ (Correct).
        *   Conservation of charge: $3 = 4 + (-1)$ (Correct).

**Conclusion:**
Equation D correctly represents the beta decay of Lithium-8 into Beryllium-8.

**Correct Answer:** **D**
