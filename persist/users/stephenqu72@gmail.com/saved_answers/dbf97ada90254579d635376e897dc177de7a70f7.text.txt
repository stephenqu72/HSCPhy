To answer a 7-mark question like this, you must demonstrate a deep understanding of electromagnetism by linking theoretical laws to practical technology. "Analyse" requires you to explain the *how* and *why*—not just name-drop concepts.

### Suggested Response

**1. Laws and Principles**

*   **Oersted’s Principle:** Discovered when Oersted observed a compass needle deflecting near a current-carrying wire. It established that electric current creates a magnetic field. Mathematically, the field $B$ around a wire is related to current $I$ by $B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$. This is the fundamental basis for all electromagnetism.
*   **Faraday’s Law of Induction:** Faraday discovered that a changing magnetic flux through a circuit induces an electromotive force (EMF). It is defined as $\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi_B}{\Delta t}$. This principle shows the reciprocal nature of the interaction: magnetic fields can be used to generate electric current.
*   **Lenz’s Law:** This provides the direction of the induced current in Faraday’s law (represented by the negative sign in the equation). It states that the induced current will create a magnetic field that opposes the change in flux that produced it, ensuring the **Law of Conservation of Energy** is maintained.

**2. Applications**

*   **Electric Motors:** These use the **Motor Effect**, where a current-carrying conductor in an external magnetic field experiences a force $F = BIL \sin \theta$. By converting electrical energy into mechanical energy, motors allow us to run household appliances, industrial machinery, and electric vehicles.
*   **Electrical Generators:** These utilize Faraday’s Law of Induction. By rotating a coil within a magnetic field (using mechanical energy from wind, steam, or water), a time-varying magnetic flux is created, inducing an alternating current ($AC$). This is the foundation of our modern power grid, enabling the large-scale distribution of electricity.

3.  **Use Physics Terminology:** Ensure you use specific terms like *magnetic flux*, *induced EMF*, and *Lorentz force* to demonstrate high-level syllabus mastery.
