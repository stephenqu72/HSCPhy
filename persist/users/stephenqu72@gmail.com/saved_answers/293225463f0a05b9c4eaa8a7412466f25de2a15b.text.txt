$$W = mg$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

    On Earth, the weight is given as $W$ and the standard acceleration due to gravity is approximately $g = 9.8 \text{ ms}^{-2}$. Therefore, we can express the mass ($m$) of the object as:
    $$m = \frac{W}{9.8}$$

2.  **Apply to the new environment:**
    When the object is taken to Kashyyyk, its mass ($m$) remains constant (mass is an intrinsic property of the object and does not change with location). The new acceleration due to gravity is given as $a$. Let the new weight be $W_{new}$:
    $$W_{new} = m \times a$$

3.  **Substitute the mass:**
    Substitute the expression for mass derived from the Earth data into the equation for the new weight:
    $$W_{new} = \left( \frac{W}{9.8} \right) \times a$$
    $$W_{new} = \frac{aW}{9.8} \text{ N}$$
