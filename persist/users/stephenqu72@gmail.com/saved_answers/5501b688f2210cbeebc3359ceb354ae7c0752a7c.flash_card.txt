### Front
How do you determine the elastic potential energy ($U$) stored in a spring using a Force-displacement ($F-x$) graph?

### Back
- **Key idea:** $U$ is equal to the area under the $F-x$ graph.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $U = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$ (area of a triangle); Hooke’s Law ($F=kx$).
- **When to use it:** When calculating energy stored due to compression or extension from an equilibrium position.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting the $\frac{1}{2}$ factor or failing to convert units (e.g., cm to m) before calculating.
