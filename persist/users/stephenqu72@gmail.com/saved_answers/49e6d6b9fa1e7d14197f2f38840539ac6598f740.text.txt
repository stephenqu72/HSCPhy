The correct answer is **(A) Galactic red shift**.

### Explanation

To solve this question, you need to recall Edwin Hubble’s contributions to cosmology in the context of the Big Bang Theory:

1.  **Hubble's Observation:** By observing the light spectra from distant galaxies, Hubble discovered that the spectral lines were consistently shifted toward the red end of the spectrum (longer wavelengths). This phenomenon is known as **galactic red shift**.
2.  **The Physical Meaning:** According to the Doppler effect (for light), a shift toward longer wavelengths occurs when an object is moving away from the observer. Hubble observed that almost all distant galaxies are moving away from us, and that their speed of recession is proportional to their distance from Earth, a relationship now known as **Hubble’s Law** ($v = H_0 d$).
3.  **Support for the Big Bang:** This observation provided the first empirical evidence that the universe is expanding. If the universe is expanding today, it implies that it was smaller, denser, and hotter in the past, which is the foundational premise of the Big Bang Theory.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **(B) Galactic blue shift:** This would indicate that galaxies are moving *towards* us, which contradicts the expansion of the universe (with the exception of local galaxies like Andromeda).
*   **(C) Microwave background radiation:** While this is major evidence for the Big Bang, it was discovered by Penzias and Wilson in 1964, not by Hubble.
*   **(D) The chemical composition of stars:** While important to astrophysics, this relates to stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis rather than the specific discovery Hubble is famous for regarding the expansion of the universe.
