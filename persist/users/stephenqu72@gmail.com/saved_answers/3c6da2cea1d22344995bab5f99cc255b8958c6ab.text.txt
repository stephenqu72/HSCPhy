### Explanation
To solve this, we must evaluate the standard applications associated with each region of the electromagnetic spectrum:

1.  **Infra-red:** This region is associated with heat. Thermal cameras detect infra-red radiation emitted by objects due to their temperature. Therefore, "thermal imaging" is the correct association.
2.  **Visible:** This region corresponds to light detectable by the human eye. It is the basis for standard light microscopy (optical microscopy).
3.  **Ultraviolet (UV):** UV radiation has high enough energy to damage the DNA of microorganisms. It is commonly used for water sterilisation (disinfection).
4.  **X-rays:** Because X-rays have high energy and short wavelengths, they can penetrate soft tissues but are absorbed by denser materials like bone, making them the standard tool for medical imaging (e.g., bone X-rays).

**Evaluating the options:**
*   **A** is incorrect because UV is not primarily used for optical microscopy (visible light is).
*   **B** is incorrect because water sterilisation is not performed by infra-red.
*   **C** is incorrect because optical microscopy is not performed by infra-red.
*   **D** correctly matches all four: **Infra-red** (thermal imaging), **Visible** (optical microscopy), **Ultraviolet** (water sterilisation), and **X-rays** (medical imaging).
