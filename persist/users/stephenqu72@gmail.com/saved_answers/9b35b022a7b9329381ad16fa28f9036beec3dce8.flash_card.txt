### Front
If the distance ($r$) from the centre of a planet is doubled, how does the magnitude of the satellite's gravitational potential energy ($U$) change?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational potential energy is inversely proportional to distance ($U \propto 1/r$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$
- When to use it: Any scenario involving moving an object between different orbital radii or distances from a point mass.
- Common trap: Confusing algebraic increase (moving from a more negative to a less negative number) with magnitude; always calculate the ratio of the magnitudes ($|U|$).
