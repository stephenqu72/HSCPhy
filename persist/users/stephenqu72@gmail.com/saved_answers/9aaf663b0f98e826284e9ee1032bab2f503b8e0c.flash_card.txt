### Front
How does horizontal velocity affect the time of flight for a projectile launched horizontally from a height?

### Back
- Key idea: Horizontal and vertical motions are independent. Time of flight is determined solely by vertical displacement ($h$) and gravity ($g$).
- Formula / law / constant: $t = \sqrt{\frac{2h}{g}}$
- When to use it: Any horizontal projectile motion problem where time is required.
- Common trap: Assuming that a higher horizontal velocity results in a longer time of flight.
