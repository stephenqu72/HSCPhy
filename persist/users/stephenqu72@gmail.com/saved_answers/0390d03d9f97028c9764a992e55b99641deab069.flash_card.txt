### Front
What determines the direction of the net force on an object undergoing uniform circular motion?

### Back
- Key idea: The net force is the **centripetal force**, which must always point toward the center of the circular path.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{net} = F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ or $F_c = mr\omega^2$.
- When to use it: Whenever an object moves in a circular path (e.g., banked tracks, conical pendulums, or satellites).
- Common trap: Mistaking the direction of the net force for one of the individual component forces (like the normal force or gravity) rather than their vector resultant.
