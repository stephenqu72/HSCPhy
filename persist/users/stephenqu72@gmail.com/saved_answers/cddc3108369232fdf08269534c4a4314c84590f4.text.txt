### Answer

The physics law is **Lenz’s Law**.

Lenz’s Law states that the direction of the induced current in a conductor is such that it creates a magnetic field that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it. 

In the diagram on the right, the North pole of the magnet is moving towards the coil. According to Lenz's Law, the coil must induce a current that creates a North pole on the face of the coil closest to the magnet to repel it and oppose the inward motion. Using the right-hand grip rule, a current flowing in that direction would result in a deflection of the meter in a specific direction (as seen on the left). The diagram on the right shows the meter deflecting in the opposite direction, which implies a magnetic field is being created that attracts the magnet. This would violate the Law of Conservation of Energy because it would accelerate the magnet toward the coil, increasing its kinetic energy without external work, which is physically impossible.
