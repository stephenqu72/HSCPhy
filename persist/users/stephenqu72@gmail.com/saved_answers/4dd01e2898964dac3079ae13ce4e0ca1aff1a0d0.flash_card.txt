### Front
How do you ensure experimental **validity** and **reliability**, and can an experiment be one without the other?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Validity = fair test (controlling variables); Reliability = consistency (repetition and averaging).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When evaluating or designing a first-hand investigation.
- **Common trap:** Confusing "reliable" (consistent results) with "valid" (results that actually test the hypothesis). An experiment with systematic error (e.g., faulty calibration) is reliable but invalid.
