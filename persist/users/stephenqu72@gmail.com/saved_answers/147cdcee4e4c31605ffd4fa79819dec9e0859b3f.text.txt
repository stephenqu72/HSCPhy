The correct answer is **(B) It is moving away from us.**

### Explanation

To solve this problem, you need to understand the **Doppler Effect** as it applies to light in astrophysics.

1.  **The Doppler Effect:** Just as the sound of a siren changes pitch depending on whether it is moving toward or away from an observer, the light from a celestial object shifts in wavelength based on its relative motion.
2.  **Redshift:** When an object is moving away from the observer, the light waves it emits are "stretched out." Since red light has a longer wavelength than blue or violet light, this stretching causes the spectral lines to shift toward the red end of the electromagnetic spectrum. This phenomenon is called **redshift**.
3.  **Large Redshift:** A *large* redshift indicates that the object is moving away from the observer at a very high velocity. According to Hubble's Law, the greater the redshift of a distant galaxy, the faster it is receding from us.
4.  **Why other options are incorrect:**
    *   (A) Moving toward us would cause a "blueshift" (compression of light waves).
    *   (C) A stationary object would show no shift in its spectral lines.
    *   (D) Redshift is an indicator of motion (velocity), not the physical size of the object.

In the context of the HSC Physics syllabus (specifically the "Astrophysics" module), redshift is a fundamental piece of evidence for the expansion of the universe.
