### Front
What are the three broad categories of fundamental particles in the Standard Model?

### Back
- Key idea: Fundamental particles have no internal sub-components and are categorized as Quarks, Leptons, and Bosons.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: To distinguish between fundamental particles (building blocks) and composite particles (hadrons: baryons and mesons).
- Common trap: Confusing composite particles (protons, neutrons, hadrons) for fundamental particles; protons and neutrons contain quarks and are therefore not fundamental.
