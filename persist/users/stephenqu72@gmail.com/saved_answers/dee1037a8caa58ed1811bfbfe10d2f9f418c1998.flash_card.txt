### Front
What is the net reaction of the CNO cycle, and why does it contribute minimally to the Sun’s energy output?

### Back
- Key idea: The CNO cycle uses Carbon, Nitrogen, and Oxygen as catalysts to fuse four Hydrogen nuclei into one Helium nucleus; it requires higher temperatures than the p-p chain due to stronger Coulomb repulsion.
- Formula / law / constant: $4(^1_1H) \rightarrow ^4_2He + 2e^+ + 2\nu_e + \text{energy}$
- When to use it: Explaining stellar nucleosynthesis in stars more massive than the Sun ($>1.3 M_{\text{sun}}$).
- Common trap: Mistaking the intermediate C, N, and O nuclei for reactants; they are catalysts and are regenerated, not consumed.
