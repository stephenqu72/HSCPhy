To find the velocity of a car on a banked track with no friction (also known as the "design speed"), we use the physics of circular motion.

### 1. Identify the given information:
*   Radius of the track ($r$): $4.5\text{ km} = 4500\text{ m}$
*   Banking angle ($\theta$): $8^\circ$
*   Acceleration due to gravity ($g$): $9.8\text{ m/s}^2$
*   Total mass ($m$): $1375\text{ kg} + 75\text{ kg} = 1450\text{ kg}$ (Note: In this specific derivation, the mass cancels out).

### 2. The Physics Principle:
When there is no friction, the horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force required to keep the car moving in a circle.
*   Vertical equilibrium: $N\cos\theta = mg$
*   Horizontal centripetal force: $N\sin\theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$

By dividing the two equations ($N\sin\theta / N\cos\theta = \frac{mv^2}{r} / mg$), we get the formula for design speed:
$$\tan\theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

### 3. Calculation:
Rearrange the formula to solve for velocity ($v$):
$$v = \sqrt{rg \tan\theta}$$

Substitute the values:
$$v = \sqrt{4500 \times 9.8 \times \tan(8^\circ)}$$
$$v = \sqrt{44100 \times 0.14054}$$
$$v = \sqrt{6197.8}$$
$$v \approx 78.726\text{ m/s}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct choice is **(C)**.

### 4. Summary of steps:
1.  Recognize that for a frictionless banked turn, the mass is irrelevant.
2.  Use the standard formula derived from the balance of forces: $v = \sqrt{rg \tan\theta}$.
3.  Ensure the radius is converted from kilometers to meters ($4.5\text{ km} = 4500\text{ m}$).
4.  Perform the calculation and round to the appropriate number of significant figures.
