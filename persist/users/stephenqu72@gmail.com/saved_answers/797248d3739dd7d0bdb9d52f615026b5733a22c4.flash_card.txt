### Front
How do you determine the direction of the magnetic force on a moving charged particle?

### Back
- Key idea: Use the Right-Hand Rule (or vector cross-product).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$
- When to use it: Whenever a charged particle moves through an external magnetic field.
- Common trap: For negative charges (e.g., electrons), the force direction is opposite to the palm/result of the Right-Hand Rule.
