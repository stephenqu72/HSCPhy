### Front
Explain the condition for "apparent weightlessness" in a parabolic flight (zero-G arc).

### Back
- **Key idea:** Apparent weightlessness occurs when the passenger and aircraft are in freefall, accelerating downwards at $g$. Because there is no normal force ($F_N = 0$), the passenger feels weightless despite still being in Earth's gravitational field.
- **Formula / law / constant:** $F_c = F_g \rightarrow \frac{mv^2}{r} = mg \rightarrow r = \frac{v^2}{g}$
- **When to use it:** To calculate the required flight radius for weightlessness or to explain why gravity is not zero during "zero-G" maneuvers.
- **Common trap:** Confusing "apparent weightlessness" with the total absence of gravity; the gravitational force ($F_g$) is still acting on the passenger.
