### Front
How do you calculate the decay constant ($\lambda$) using a radioactive decay graph?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The decay constant is inversely proportional to the half-life ($t_{1/2}$).
- **Formula:** $\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{t_{1/2}}$
- **When to use it:** When you need to determine the rate of decay from a mass-time or activity-time graph.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the units of time (e.g., years vs. seconds) or failing to verify the "halving" point from $t=0$ if the curve is shifted.
