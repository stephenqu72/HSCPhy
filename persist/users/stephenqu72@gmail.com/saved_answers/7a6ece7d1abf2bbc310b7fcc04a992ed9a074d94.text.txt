To determine the correct answer, let's analyze the nuclear process shown in the diagram.

### Answer
The correct option is **D) Fission Reaction**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we need to look at the characteristics of the reaction presented:

1.  **Reactants:** A heavy nucleus, Uranium-235 ($^{235}_{92}\text{U}$), is being struck by a neutron ($n$). This is the classic trigger for induced nuclear fission.
2.  **Process:** The heavy nucleus splits into two smaller, lighter nuclei: Barium-139 ($^{139}_{56}\text{Ba}$) and Krypton-95 ($^{95}_{36}\text{Kr}$).
3.  **Products:** The reaction releases additional neutrons (in this case, two) and a significant amount of energy ($200 \text{ MeV}$).

**Why it is not the others:**
*   **Beta Decay** involves the emission of an electron or positron from a nucleus to change a neutron to a proton or vice versa.
*   **Alpha Decay** involves the emission of a helium nucleus ($^{4}_{2}\text{He}$) from a heavy nucleus.
*   **Fusion Reaction** involves the joining of two light nuclei to form a single, heavier nucleus (the opposite of what is shown here).

Since the process involves a heavy nucleus splitting into smaller fragments upon neutron bombardment, it is defined as **nuclear fission**.
