*   The velocity of the particle is $v = 0.9999c$.
*   The lifetime observed by the scientist (in the laboratory frame) is the dilated time, $\Delta t = 1.0 \times 10^{-6}\text{ s}$.
*   We need to find the proper time, $\Delta t_0$, which is the lifetime of the particle in its own rest frame (if it were stationary).

### 2. The Time Dilation Formula
The relationship between dilated time and proper time is given by:
$$\Delta t = \gamma \Delta t_0$$
Where $\gamma$ (the Lorentz factor) is defined as:
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$

### 3. Step-by-Step Calculation
First, calculate the Lorentz factor $\gamma$:
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.9999)^2}}$$
$$1 - (0.9999)^2 = 1 - 0.99980001 = 0.00019999$$
$$\sqrt{0.00019999} \approx 0.01414178$$
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{0.01414178} \approx 70.71$$

Now, rearrange the time dilation formula to solve for the proper time $\Delta t_0$:
$$\Delta t_0 = \frac{\Delta t}{\gamma}$$
$$\Delta t_0 = \frac{1.0 \times 10^{-6}}{70.71}$$
