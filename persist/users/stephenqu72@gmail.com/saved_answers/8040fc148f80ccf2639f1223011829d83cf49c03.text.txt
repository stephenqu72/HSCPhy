To solve this problem, we must identify the forces acting on the rod and apply Newton's Second Law along the plane of the rails.

### Given Data
- Mass $m = 500\text{ g} = 0.5\text{ kg}$
- Length $L = 30\text{ cm} = 0.3\text{ m}$
- Angle $\theta = 60^\circ$
- Current $I = 2.8\text{ A}$
- Magnetic field $B = 2.5\text{ T}$
- Gravity $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$

---

### (a) Force due to the magnetic field
The magnetic force $F_B$ is calculated using the formula $F_B = BIL\sin(\phi)$, where $\phi$ is the angle between the current and the magnetic field. Since the field is perpendicular to the rod, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.
$$F_B = 2.5 \times 2.8 \times 0.3 = 2.1\text{ N}$$
**Direction:** Using the Right-Hand Rule, with current flowing down the rod and the magnetic field pointing outwards, the force is directed **up the slope of the rails**.

### (b) Initial acceleration of the rod
To find acceleration, we perform a force balance along the rails. The two forces acting along the rails are the magnetic force ($F_B$ up) and the component of weight ($mg\sin(60^\circ)$ down).
Sum of forces: $F_{net} = F_B - mg\sin(60^\circ)$
$$F_{net} = 2.1 - (0.5 \times 9.8 \times \sin(60^\circ))$$
$$F_{net} = 2.1 - 4.2435 \approx -2.1435\text{ N}$$
Using $F = ma$:
$$a = \frac{-2.1435}{0.5} \approx -4.29\text{ m/s}^2$$
The negative sign indicates the net acceleration is directed **down the rails**.

### (c) Acceleration with the switch open
With the switch open, the current $I = 0$, meaning the magnetic force $F_B = 0$. The only force acting along the rails is the component of gravity.
The acceleration is now:
$$a = g\sin(60^\circ) = 9.8 \times 0.866 \approx 8.49\text{ m/s}^2 \text{ (down the rails)}$$
**Explanation:** With the switch open, the electromagnetic force that previously acted up the rails is removed. The rod is now only subject to the component of gravity acting down the incline. Consequently, the rod will accelerate significantly faster down the rails compared to when the switch was closed.
