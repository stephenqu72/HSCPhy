### Answer
The vital component missing in this simple motor is the **split-ring commutator**.

**Function of the commutator:**
In a standard manufactured DC motor, the split-ring commutator reverses the direction of the current through the coil every half-turn (every $180^\circ$). This ensures that the torque exerted on the coil remains in the same rotational direction, allowing the motor to spin continuously.

**Why this student motor works without it:**
This motor uses a "half-stripped" wire technique as a substitute. By removing the insulation from only half of the wire's circumference at one end of the coil, the electrical contact with the safety pin (the brush) is broken for the second half of the rotation. During this "off" phase, there is no current, and thus no magnetic force; however, the coil continues to rotate due to its **inertia**. When the contact is re-established after the half-turn, the current flows again, and the magnetic force pushes the coil in the same direction, maintaining continuous rotation without needing a mechanical split-ring.
