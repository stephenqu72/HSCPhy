### Answer
The correct answer is **(D)**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must understand the fundamental physical requirements for nuclear fusion compared to nuclear fission.

1.  **Analyze the options:**
    *   **(A) Incorrect:** Nuclear fusion actually releases significantly *more* energy per nucleon than nuclear fission. It is the process that powers stars.
    *   **(B) Incorrect:** The fuel for fusion (such as isotopes of hydrogen like deuterium and tritium) is extremely abundant on Earth, particularly in our oceans. This is one of the primary reasons scientists are pursuing fusion as a long-term energy solution.
    *   **(C) Incorrect:** While some components of a fusion reactor can become activated (radioactive) due to neutron bombardment, the products of the fusion reaction itself (like Helium-4) are not radioactive. Fusion is considered much "cleaner" than fission, which produces long-lived radioactive waste.
    *   **(D) Correct:** Nuclear fusion requires overcoming the **Coulomb barrier**—the massive electrostatic repulsion between positively charged nuclei. To force nuclei close enough for the strong nuclear force to take over, extreme conditions are required: temperatures of millions of degrees (like in the Sun's core) or massive pressures (inertial confinement). Creating and maintaining these conditions in a stable, controlled way for sustained commercial energy production is the primary technical hurdle that scientists are still working to overcome (e.g., in projects like ITER).

**Summary for HSC revision:** 
Remember the distinction: **Fission** (splitting atoms) is currently used because it is easier to control using thermal neutrons. **Fusion** (combining atoms) is the future goal, but it is currently hindered by the engineering difficulty of achieving and sustaining the extreme temperatures and pressures needed to overcome the electrostatic repulsion of nuclei.
