### Front
Why can ultrasound waves not be polarised?

### Back
- Key idea: Polarisation is a property unique to transverse waves (oscillations perpendicular to propagation). Ultrasound waves are longitudinal (oscillations parallel to propagation).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: To distinguish between wave types; longitudinal waves cannot have their oscillation orientation restricted.
- Common trap: Assuming all wave phenomena (like interference or diffraction) apply to all wave types; polarisation requires perpendicular oscillation.
