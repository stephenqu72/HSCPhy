To determine the energy change for the transition of an electron from $n=4$ to $n=3$, we follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the energy values
From the provided table:
*   Energy at $n=4$: $E_4 = 2.04 \times 10^{-18}\text{ J}$
*   Energy at $n=3$: $E_3 = 1.94 \times 10^{-18}\text{ J}$

### 2. Calculate the difference in energy
The change in energy ($\Delta E$) is given by the final state minus the initial state:
$$\Delta E = E_{\text{final}} - E_{\text{initial}}$$
$$\Delta E = E_3 - E_4$$
$$\Delta E = 1.94 \times 10^{-18}\text{ J} - 2.04 \times 10^{-18}\text{ J}$$
$$\Delta E = -0.10 \times 10^{-18}\text{ J}$$
Converting this to standard scientific notation:
$$\Delta E = -1.00 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$$

### 3. Interpret the result
*   The **magnitude** of the energy change is $1.00 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$.
*   Because the electron is moving from a higher energy level ($n=4$) to a lower energy level ($n=3$), the atom must lose energy to conserve energy. This energy is released in the form of a photon. Therefore, the energy is **emitted**.
