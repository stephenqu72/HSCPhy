### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. Recall the relationship between frequency ($f$) and wavelength ($\lambda$):**
The speed of light ($c$) is approximately $3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$. The formula is:
$$\lambda = \frac{c}{f}$$

**2. Calculate the wavelength for $9.5 \text{ GHz}$:**
First, convert $9.5 \text{ GHz}$ to Hertz: $9.5 \times 10^9 \text{ Hz}$.
$$\lambda_1 = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{9.5 \times 10^9} \approx 0.0316 \text{ m}$$
In scientific notation, this is approximately $3.2 \times 10^{-2} \text{ m}$.

**3. Calculate the wavelength for $300 \text{ GHz}$:**
Convert $300 \text{ GHz}$ to Hertz: $300 \times 10^9 = 3 \times 10^{11} \text{ Hz}$.
$$\lambda_2 = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{3 \times 10^{11}} = 1 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$$
