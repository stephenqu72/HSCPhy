### Explanation

To solve this, we must recall the definition and fundamental properties of a **photon** as taught in the HSC Physics module "From the Universe to the Atom":

1.  **Definition:** A photon is a discrete "packet" or quantum of electromagnetic energy. 
2.  **Evaluating the options:**
    *   **(A) "It is a positive electron":** This is incorrect. A "positive electron" is a positron, which is the antimatter counterpart to an electron and has mass.
    *   **(B) "It has no rest mass and travels at a speed of $3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m s}^{-1}$ in vacuum":** This is **correct**. By definition, photons are massless particles that travel at the speed of light ($c \approx 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m s}^{-1}$) in a vacuum. Because they are massless, they cannot exist at rest.
    *   **(C) "It has rest mass and particle nature of electromagnetic radiation":** This is incorrect because photons have **zero** rest mass. While they do represent the particle nature of electromagnetic radiation, the claim about rest mass makes this statement false.
    *   **(D) "It describes the wave nature of light":** This is incorrect. The concept of the photon is specifically used to describe the **particle nature** of light (as seen in phenomena like the photoelectric effect), whereas light's wave nature is described by parameters such as wavelength, frequency, and diffraction/interference patterns.

Therefore, statement (B) is the only scientifically accurate description.
