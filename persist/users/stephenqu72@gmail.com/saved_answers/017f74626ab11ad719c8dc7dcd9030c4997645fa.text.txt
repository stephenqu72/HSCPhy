To solve this problem, we must first determine the velocity of the ions as they enter the drift region and then calculate the time taken for each isotope to cross the $1.54\text{ m}$ distance.

### 1. Physics Principles
The work done by the electric field equals the kinetic energy gained by the ion:
$$qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \implies v = \sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}}$$
The time taken to travel the drift region of length $L$ is:
$$t = \frac{L}{v} = L \sqrt{\frac{m}{2qV}}$$

### 2. Given Data
- $V = 17500\text{ V}$
- $L = 1.54\text{ m}$
- $q = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$ (singly ionised)
- $1\text{ u} = 1.66 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$
- $m_{16} = 16 \times 1.66 \times 10^{-27} = 2.656 \times 10^{-26}\text{ kg}$
- $m_{18} = 18 \times 1.66 \times 10^{-27} = 2.988 \times 10^{-26}\text{ kg}$

### 3. Calculations
For $^{16}\text{O}$:
$$t_{16} = 1.54 \sqrt{\frac{2.656 \times 10^{-26}}{2 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \times 17500}} \approx 1.54 \times 4.887 \times 10^{-7} \approx 7.526 \times 10^{-7}\text{ s} = 752.6\text{ ns}$$

For $^{18}\text{O}$:
$$t_{18} = 1.54 \sqrt{\frac{2.988 \times 10^{-26}}{2 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \times 17500}} \approx 1.54 \times 5.183 \times 10^{-7} \approx 7.982 \times 10^{-7}\text{ s} = 798.2\text{ ns}$$

### 4. Conclusion
The difference in arrival time ($\Delta t$) is:
$$\Delta t = 798.2\text{ ns} - 752.6\text{ ns} = 45.6\text{ ns}$$

Since $45.6\text{ ns} < 125\text{ ns}$, the student **cannot** separate the isotopes using this specific apparatus setup.
