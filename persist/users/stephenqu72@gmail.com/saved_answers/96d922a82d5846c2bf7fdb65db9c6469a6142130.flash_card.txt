### Front
How do you calculate the work required to move a satellite from one circular orbit to another?

### Back
- Key idea: Work done equals the change in total mechanical energy ($\Delta E$).
- Formula: $W = E_2 - E_1 = \left( -\frac{GMm}{2r_2} \right) - \left( -\frac{GMm}{2r_1} \right)$
- When to use it: Whenever a satellite changes its orbital radius.
- Common trap: Forgetting that total energy ($E$) is negative; also, ensure $r$ is the distance from the *centre* of the planet (i.e., $r = R_E + h$).
