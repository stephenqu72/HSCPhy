### Front
How do you determine the escape velocity of a planet given the orbital data (radius $r$, period $T$) of its satellite?

### Back
- Key idea: Use orbital mechanics to find the central mass, then apply the energy conservation principle for escape velocity.
- Formula / law / constant: 
  1. Kepler’s Third Law: $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$
  2. Escape Velocity: $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$
- When to use it: When required to calculate planetary properties (mass or escape velocity) from satellite orbital data.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert units (e.g., $R$ from km to m) or confusing the orbital radius ($r$) with the planet's radius ($R$).
