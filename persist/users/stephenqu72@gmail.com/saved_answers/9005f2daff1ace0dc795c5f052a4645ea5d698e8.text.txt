To identify the correct graph for an inverse-square law, we must look at the mathematical relationship described.

### Answer
The correct graph is **D**.

### Explanation
An inverse-square law states that a physical quantity ($y$) is inversely proportional to the square of the distance ($x$) from the source. Mathematically, this is expressed as:
$$y \propto \frac{1}{x^2}$$
or
$$y = \frac{k}{x^2}$$
(where $k$ is a constant of proportionality).

To determine which graph is correct, let's analyze the behavior of this function:
1. **As $x$ increases:** As the distance $x$ gets larger, the value of $x^2$ increases rapidly. Consequently, $\frac{1}{x^2}$ gets smaller, meaning $y$ approaches zero. This represents a downward-sloping curve that flattens out as it moves to the right.
2. **As $x$ approaches zero:** As $x$ becomes very small, $x^2$ becomes extremely small, causing $y$ to approach infinity (the graph shoots upwards near the y-axis).

**Evaluating the choices:**
* **A:** This shows an exponential or quadratic growth ($y = x^2$ or $y = e^x$).
* **B:** This shows a linear relationship ($y = mx$).
* **C:** This shows a square root relationship ($y = \sqrt{x}$), which increases but flattens out.
* **D:** This shows the classic "decaying" curve characteristic of inverse relationships, where $y$ is high when $x$ is small and $y$ drops rapidly as $x$ increases, consistent with $y = \frac{1}{x^2}$.
