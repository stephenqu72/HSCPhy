### Answer

**(a) State Lenz's Law:**
Lenz's Law states that the direction of an induced electromotive force (EMF) and the resulting induced current is such that it creates a magnetic field that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it.

**(b) Explanation:**
1. **Change in Flux:** Initially, there is a strong magnetic flux pointing into the page through the copper ring. When the external magnetic field is turned off, the magnetic flux through the ring decreases rapidly.
2. **Opposition (Lenz's Law):** To obey Lenz's Law, the ring must oppose this decrease in flux. Since the original field was "into the page" and is decreasing, the ring must produce its *own* magnetic field directed "into the page" to try to maintain the original flux.
3. **Induced Current:** According to the right-hand grip rule, to create a magnetic field that points into the page inside the loop, the induced current must flow in a **clockwise** direction.

### How to solve this
When tackling HSC questions involving induction, follow this systematic mental checklist:

*   **Step 1: Identify the "Change."** What is happening to the flux? In this case, the field is being removed, so the magnetic flux (pointing into the page) is decreasing.
*   **Step 2: Determine the "Goal."** The ring wants to *oppose* the change. If the flux is decreasing, the ring wants to add more flux in the same direction to keep things as they were.
*   **Step 3: Define the Direction.** If the ring needs to create a magnetic field "into the page," point your right thumb into the page. Your fingers will naturally curl in the **clockwise** direction.
*   **Step 4: Structure your HSC response.** Notice how the explanation above links directly back to the definition provided in part (a). Always explicitly mention "opposing the change in flux" to gain full marks.
