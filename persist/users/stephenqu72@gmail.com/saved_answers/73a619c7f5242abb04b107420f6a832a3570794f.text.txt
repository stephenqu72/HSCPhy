### Explanation
To determine the SI unit for the magnetic field ($B$), we use the formula for the magnetic force ($F$) acting on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field:

$$F = BIL \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
*   $F$ is the magnetic force (measured in Newtons, $N$)
*   $B$ is the magnetic field (measured in Teslas, $T$)
*   $I$ is the current (measured in Amperes, $A$)
*   $L$ is the length of the wire (measured in meters, $m$)
*   $\sin(\theta)$ is a dimensionless quantity.

To find the units of the magnetic field ($B$), we rearrange the formula to solve for $B$:

$$B = \frac{F}{IL}$$

Now, substitute the SI units for each variable into the equation:

*   Units of $F$ = $N$
*   Units of $I$ = $A$
*   Units of $L$ = $m$

Therefore, the SI unit for $B$ (Tesla) is:

$$1\ T = \frac{1\ N}{1\ A \cdot 1\ m}$$

Looking at the options provided:
*   (A) $\frac{Ampere}{Newton}$ is incorrect.
*   (B) $\frac{Newton}{Ampere}$ is incorrect.
*   (C) $\frac{Joules}{Ampere \cdot Newton}$ is incorrect.
*   (D) $\frac{Newton}{Ampere \cdot meter}$ matches our derived units.
