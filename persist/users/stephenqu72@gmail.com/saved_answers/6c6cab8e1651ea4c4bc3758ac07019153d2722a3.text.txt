### (a) Identify the component labelled X.
**Answer:** Slip ring.

**Explanation:** In an AC generator, slip rings are used to maintain a continuous electrical connection between the rotating coil and the external circuit, allowing the current to reverse direction every half-turn. (Distinguish this from a split-ring commutator used in DC motors).

### (b) Explain how an alternating emf is induced within the coil as it is rotated.
**Answer:**
1. As the coil rotates in the uniform magnetic field, the magnetic flux passing through the area of the coil changes over time.
2. According to Faraday’s Law of Induction, an emf is induced equal to the rate of change of magnetic flux linkage, $\mathcal{E} = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$.
3. Because the coil rotates, the angle between the normal to the coil and the magnetic field changes sinusoidally. This causes the magnetic flux $\Phi = BA \cos(\theta)$ to vary periodically. As the sides of the coil move across the field lines, the direction of the induced current reverses every half-rotation (as dictated by Lenz's Law), resulting in an alternating emf.

### (c) The frequency of the generator is doubled. Draw the resulting voltage output.
**Explanation:** 
Doubling the frequency ($f$) has two effects:
1. **Time period ($T$):** Since $T = \frac{1}{f}$, doubling the frequency halves the period. The original wave completed one full cycle in $20\text{ ms}$. The new wave will complete one full cycle in $10\text{ ms}$.
2. **Peak Voltage ($V_{peak}$):** The induced emf is given by $\mathcal{E} = N B A \omega \sin(\omega t)$, where $\omega = 2\pi f$. Since $\omega$ is proportional to frequency, doubling the frequency also doubles the peak voltage from $25\text{ kV}$ to $50\text{ kV}$.
