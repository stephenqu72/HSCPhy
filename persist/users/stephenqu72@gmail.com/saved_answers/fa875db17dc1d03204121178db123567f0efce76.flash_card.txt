### Front
What are the three defining characteristics of the electric field between two large, oppositely charged parallel plates?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The field is uniform in the center and non-uniform at the edges.
- **Figure:** Parallel, evenly spaced field lines between plates; "fringing" (curving) lines at the edges.
- **When to use it:** To determine the direction and nature of a constant electric field (e.g., in a Millikan oil drop or cathode ray tube experiment).
- **Common trap:** Assuming the field is perfectly uniform all the way to the edges (ignoring fringing) or getting the direction wrong (remember: positive to negative).
