To solve this problem, we must analyze the projectile's horizontal and vertical components of motion independently.

### 1. Identify the given information:
*   Horizontal velocity ($v_x$) is constant: $v_x = 13.0\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Vertical displacement ($\Delta y$) at impact: $15.0\text{ m}$
*   Angle of impact with the vertical wall: $45.0^\circ$
*   Acceleration due to gravity ($g$): $9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$ (downward)

### 2. Determine the vertical velocity at impact:
At the point of impact, the angle of $45.0^\circ$ is measured between the velocity vector and the vertical wall. This means the ratio of horizontal to vertical velocity is:
$$\tan(45.0^\circ) = \frac{v_x}{|v_y|}$$
Since $\tan(45.0^\circ) = 1$, the vertical velocity magnitude $|v_y|$ at impact must equal the horizontal velocity:
$$|v_y| = 13.0\text{ m s}^{-1}$$
*(Note: Since the projectile is moving downwards to strike the wall at a point above the launch height, the vertical velocity is $-13.0\text{ m s}^{-1}$.)*

### 3. Find the initial vertical velocity ($u_y$):
Using the kinematic equation $v_y^2 = u_y^2 + 2a\Delta y$:
$$(-13.0)^2 = u_y^2 + 2(-9.8)(15.0)$$
$$169 = u_y^2 - 294$$
$$u_y^2 = 463 \implies u_y = \sqrt{463} \approx 21.517\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

### 4. Find the time of flight ($t$):
Using $v_y = u_y + at$:
$$-13.0 = 21.517 - 9.8t$$
$$-34.517 = -9.8t$$
$$t = \frac{34.517}{9.8} \approx 3.522\text{ s}$$

### 5. Calculate horizontal distance ($d$):
$$d = v_x \times t$$
$$d = 13.0 \times 3.522 \approx 45.786\text{ m}$$

Rounding to 3 significant figures, the launch site is **$45.8\text{ m}$** from the wall.

### Explanation of the method:
1. **Constant Horizontal Motion:** Recognize that the horizontal velocity remains $13.0\text{ m s}^{-1}$ throughout the flight.
2. **Impact Geometry:** Use the impact angle to find the vertical velocity component at the moment of collision.
3. **Kinematics:** Use the vertical displacement and the vertical velocities to find the time elapsed using constant acceleration formulae.
4. **Distance:** Multiply the horizontal velocity by the time of flight to find the horizontal displacement.
