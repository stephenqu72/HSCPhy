To solve for the de Broglie wavelength of the motor car, we use the de Broglie equation:

$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$

Where:
*   $h$ is Planck's constant ($\approx 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$)
*   $m$ is the mass of the object ($663 \text{ kg}$)
*   $v$ is the velocity of the object ($10 \text{ m s}^{-1}$)

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   $h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$
    *   $m = 663 \text{ kg} = 6.63 \times 10^2 \text{ kg}$
    *   $v = 10 \text{ m s}^{-1} = 10^1 \text{ m s}^{-1}$

2.  **Calculate the momentum ($p$):**
    $$p = m \times v$$
    $$p = (6.63 \times 10^2) \times (10^1) = 6.63 \times 10^3 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$$

3.  **Calculate the wavelength ($\lambda$):**
    $$\lambda = \frac{6.63 \times 10^{-34}}{6.63 \times 10^3}$$
    $$\lambda = \frac{6.63}{6.63} \times 10^{-34 - 3}$$
    $$\lambda = 1 \times 10^{-37} \text{ m}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, $10^{-37} \text{ m}$ is the correct answer. Therefore, **Option A** is correct.
