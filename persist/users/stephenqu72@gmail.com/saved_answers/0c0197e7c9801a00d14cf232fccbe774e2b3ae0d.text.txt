To solve this problem, we must understand the relationship between weight, mass, and the acceleration due to gravity, defined by the formula:
$$W = mg$$
where $W$ is weight (in Newtons), $m$ is the mass (in kilograms), and $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity (in $\text{m s}^{-2}$).

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Determine the mass of the object:**
The mass of an object is constant regardless of location. We can find the mass using the information given for Earth, where the acceleration due to gravity is approximately $g_{\text{Earth}} = 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$.
$$m = \frac{W_{\text{Earth}}}{g_{\text{Earth}}}$$
$$m = \frac{256 \text{ N}}{9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}} \approx 26.12 \text{ kg}$$

**2. Calculate the acceleration due to gravity on planet $X$ ($g_X$):**
Using the weight of the object on planet $X$ ($W_X = 92 \text{ N}$) and the mass we just calculated, we rearrange the weight formula to solve for $g_X$:
$$g_X = \frac{W_X}{m}$$
$$g_X = \frac{92 \text{ N}}{26.12 \text{ kg}}$$
$$g_X \approx 3.522 \text{ m s}^{-2}$$

**3. Conclusion:**
Comparing our result to the given options, $3.522 \text{ m s}^{-2}$ is closest to $3.5 \text{ m s}^{-2}$.
