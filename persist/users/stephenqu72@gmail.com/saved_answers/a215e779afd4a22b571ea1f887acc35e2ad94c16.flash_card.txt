### Front
How do the Gravitational Potential Energy ($U_g$) and Total Mechanical Energy ($E$) of a satellite change when it moves into a lower circular orbit (e.g., due to atmospheric drag)?

### Back
- Key idea: As a satellite descends, it moves deeper into the Earth's gravitational potential well. Both $U_g$ and $E$ decrease (become more negative).
- Formula / law: $U_g = -\frac{GMm}{r}$ and $E = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$
- When to use it: To determine energy changes during orbital decay or when moving between circular orbits.
- Common trap: Assuming that because kinetic energy increases, the total energy must also increase. In a decaying orbit, energy is dissipated (e.g., via friction), so total energy decreases.
