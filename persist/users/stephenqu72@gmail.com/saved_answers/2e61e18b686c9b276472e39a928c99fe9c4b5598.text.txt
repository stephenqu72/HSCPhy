To solve this problem, we rely on the physics of uniform circular motion. 

### Part (a): Determining the ratio of speeds
The speed $v$ of an object moving in a circle of radius $r$ with a period $T$ is given by:
$$v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$$

We are given that the time for one revolution ($T$) is the same for both masses. Therefore, the ratio of the speeds is:
$$\frac{v_A}{v_B} = \frac{\frac{2\pi r_A}{T}}{\frac{2\pi r_B}{T}} = \frac{r_A}{r_B}$$

Given $r_A = 18\text{ cm}$ and $r_B = 36\text{ cm}$:
$$\frac{v_A}{v_B} = \frac{18}{36} = 0.5$$

---

### Part (b): Determining the ratio of centripetal forces
The formula for centripetal force is $F_c = m\omega^2r$, where $\omega$ is the angular velocity. Since $\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T}$ and the periods are identical, both masses have the same angular velocity ($\omega_A = \omega_B$).

The ratio of the centripetal forces is:
$$\frac{F_A}{F_B} = \frac{m_A \omega^2 r_A}{m_B \omega^2 r_B} = \left( \frac{m_A}{m_B} \right) \left( \frac{r_A}{r_B} \right)$$

*Note: The question states "If the mass of B is 3 times greater than mass B", which is a typo. Assuming it meant "the mass of B is 3 times greater than mass A" ($m_B = 3m_A$):*

$$\frac{m_A}{m_B} = \frac{1}{3}$$
$$\frac{r_A}{r_B} = \frac{18}{36} = \frac{1}{2}$$

Substituting these values:
$$\frac{F_A}{F_B} = \left( \frac{1}{3} \right) \times \left( \frac{1}{2} \right) = \frac{1}{6} \approx 0.167$$

### Explanation
1. **For (a):** Recognize that for a constant period, speed is directly proportional to the radius. Doubling the radius doubles the circumference that must be covered in the same amount of time.
2. **For (b):** Use the angular velocity form of centripetal force ($F = m\omega^2r$) because $\omega$ is constant for both objects, making it the simplest variable to work with. Multiply the mass ratio by the radius ratio.
