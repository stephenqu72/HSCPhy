### Answer

To determine the tidal effect of a celestial body on Earth, we must look at the **tidal force**, which is the difference in gravitational pull across the diameter of the Earth. The tidal force $F_T$ is proportional to the mass of the body $M$ and inversely proportional to the cube of the distance $r$ from the Earth:
$$F_T \propto \frac{M}{r^3}$$

We can calculate the ratio of the tidal force exerted by Jupiter ($F_{T,J}$) to the tidal force exerted by the Moon ($F_{T,M}$):
$$\frac{F_{T,J}}{F_{T,M}} = \frac{M_J}{M_M} \times \left( \frac{r_M}{r_J} \right)^3$$

Given:
- $M_J = 1.9 \times 10^{27} \text{ kg}$
- $M_M = 7.3 \times 10^{22} \text{ kg}$
- $r_M = 3.6 \times 10^8 \text{ m}$
- $r_J = 5.9 \times 10^{11} \text{ m}$

Substitute the values:
$$\frac{F_{T,J}}{F_{T,M}} = \left( \frac{1.9 \times 10^{27}}{7.3 \times 10^{22}} \right) \times \left( \frac{3.6 \times 10^8}{5.9 \times 10^{11}} \right)^3$$
$$\frac{F_{T,J}}{F_{T,M}} \approx 26,027 \times (0.00061)^3$$
$$\frac{F_{T,J}}{F_{T,M}} \approx 26,027 \times (2.27 \times 10^{-10}) \approx 5.9 \times 10^{-6}$$

**Conclusion:** The tidal force exerted by Jupiter is approximately $6 \times 10^{-6}$ (or 0.0006%) of the tidal force exerted by the Moon. Because the tidal effect drops off with the cube of the distance, Jupiter’s massive distance significantly outweighs its greater mass, rendering its effect on Earth's tides negligible.

### How to solve it
1.  **Understand the Physics:** Tides are caused by the *gradient* of the gravitational field, not just the magnitude of the gravitational pull. This is why the tidal force relates to $1/r^3$ rather than the standard inverse-square law ($1/r^2$).
2.  **Set up the Ratio:** A common mistake is to compare gravitational pull ($GM/r^2$). You must use the tidal force relationship. Setting up a ratio allows you to compare the two bodies without needing to calculate absolute forces in Newtons, which simplifies the math.
3.  **Perform the Calculation:** Ensure you cube the distance ratio correctly. The distance ratio $\frac{3.6 \times 10^8}{5.9 \times 10^{11}}$ is a very small number; once cubed, it becomes extremely small, which explains why the "negligible" claim holds true.
4.  **Structure the Argument:** As an HSC student, ensure you explicitly state that while Jupiter has a much larger mass, the dependence of tidal forces on the cube of the distance is the governing factor.
