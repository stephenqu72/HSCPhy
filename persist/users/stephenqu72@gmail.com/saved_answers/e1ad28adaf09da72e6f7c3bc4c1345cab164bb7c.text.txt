To solve this problem, we use the transformer turn ratio formula and the efficiency formula.

### Given Data:
- Efficiency ($\eta$) = $80\% = 0.8$
- Primary turns ($N_p$) = $7000$
- Secondary turns ($N_s$) = $175$
- Primary voltage ($V_p$) = $240\text{ V}$
- Secondary current ($I_s$) = $8.0\text{ A}$

---

### Step 1: Calculate Secondary Voltage ($V_s$)
The transformer equation relates voltage and turns ratio:
$$\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$

Rearranging for $V_s$:
$$V_s = V_p \times \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$
$$V_s = 240 \times \frac{175}{7000}$$
$$V_s = 240 \times 0.025 = 6.0\text{ V}$$

### Step 2: Calculate Primary Current ($I_p$)
Efficiency ($\eta$) is defined as the ratio of output power ($P_s$) to input power ($P_p$):
$$\eta = \frac{P_s}{P_p} = \frac{V_s I_s}{V_p I_p}$$

Rearranging to solve for $I_p$:
$$I_p = \frac{V_s I_s}{\eta V_p}$$
$$I_p = \frac{6.0 \times 8.0}{0.8 \times 240}$$
$$I_p = \frac{48}{192} = 0.25\text{ A}$$

Converting to milliamperes ($mA$):
$$0.25\text{ A} \times 1000 = 250\text{ mA}$$

### Conclusion:
The primary current is $250\text{ mA}$ and the secondary voltage is $6.0\text{ V}$. Comparing this to the given options, the correct choice is **A**.
