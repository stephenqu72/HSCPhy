To find the magnitude of the force on an electron in a uniform electric field between two parallel plates, we follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the given variables
*   Potential difference ($V$) = $100\text{ V}$
*   Distance between plates ($d$) = $2\text{ mm} = 2 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$
*   Charge of an electron ($q$) = $1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$ (standard physical constant)

### 2. Determine the Electric Field ($E$)
The electric field between parallel plates is uniform and is given by the formula:
$$E = \frac{V}{d}$$
Substituting the given values:
$$E = \frac{100\text{ V}}{2 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}} = 5 \times 10^4\text{ V/m}$$

### 3. Calculate the Electric Force ($F$)
The force exerted on a charge in an electric field is given by:
$$F = qE$$
Substituting our values:
$$F = (1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (5 \times 10^4\text{ V/m})$$
$$F = 8.0 \times 10^{-15}\text{ N}$$

**Note:** The position of the electron ("halfway between the plates") is irrelevant here because the electric field between ideal parallel plates is **uniform**, meaning the force is the same regardless of the electron's position.

### Conclusion
Comparing our result to the options provided:
A. $8.0 \times 10^{-18}\text{ N}$
B. $1.6 \times 10^{-17}\text{ N}$
C. $8.0 \times 10^{-15}\text{ N}$
D. $1.6 \times 10^{-14}\text{ N}$

The correct answer is **C**.
