### Front
How do momentum and kinetic energy behave during a perfectly inelastic collision?

### Back
- Key idea: Total momentum is conserved, but kinetic energy is lost (converted to internal/thermal energy).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\sum p_i = \sum p_f$ ; $\Delta KE = KE_{initial} - KE_{final}$
- When to use it: When objects "stick together," "coalesce," or move with a "common final velocity" after impact.
- Common trap: Assuming kinetic energy is conserved (it only remains constant in *elastic* collisions) or forgetting to use the combined mass ($m_1 + m_2$) for the final velocity calculation.
