### Answers and Explanations

#### (a) Define "geostationary"
**Answer:** A geostationary satellite is one that orbits above Earth's equator with a period of exactly one sidereal day ($23.93$ hours or $\approx 86\,164$ seconds). Because its angular velocity matches Earth's, it remains fixed at a constant position relative to a point on the Earth's surface.

**Teacher's Tip:** To score the full mark, you must mention two things: the **period** (matching Earth's rotation) and the **apparent stationary position** relative to the ground.

---

#### (b) Calculate the orbital radius
**Explanation:** We use Kepler’s Third Law, which relates the period $T$ to the orbital radius $r$:
$$\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$$
Rearranging for $r$: $r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{GMT^2}{4\pi^2}}$

**Constants:**
*   $G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N m}^2 \text{kg}^{-2}$
*   $M = 5.97 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg}$ (Mass of Earth)
*   $T = 86\,164 \, \text{s}$ (One sidereal day)

**Calculation:**
$$r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11})(5.97 \times 10^{24})(86\,164)^2}{4\pi^2}}$$
$$r \approx \sqrt[3]{7.54 \times 10^{22}}$$
$$r \approx 4.22 \times 10^7 \, \text{m} \text{ (or } 42\,200 \, \text{km)}$$

---

#### (c) Kepler’s Second Law and Conservation of Energy
**Answer:** Kepler’s Second Law states that a line segment joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time. This is a direct consequence of the **Conservation of Angular Momentum**. While the Law of Conservation of Energy is also relevant (total mechanical energy remains constant as the satellite moves between gravitational potential energy and kinetic energy), Kepler's Second Law specifically describes the spatial symmetry of the orbit. In an elliptical orbit, as the satellite gets closer to the central body, it speeds up to maintain its angular momentum, satisfying the "equal areas" rule.

**Teacher's Tip:** Ensure you link the change in orbital speed to the exchange between potential and kinetic energy if you choose to include energy conservation in your explanation.
