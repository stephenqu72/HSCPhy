To answer this question, we must apply the principles of projectile motion, specifically the independence of perpendicular motion components.

### Answer
The motion of the apple can be broken down into three independent components:

1.  **Horizontal Component (North):** Since the car was moving at a uniform velocity to the north, the apple retains this constant velocity in the northern direction throughout its flight due to inertia (assuming air resistance is negligible). There is no force acting in this direction.
2.  **Horizontal Component (East):** The apple was thrown horizontally to the east. It maintains this constant velocity in the eastern direction throughout its flight as there is no horizontal force (ignoring air resistance) to accelerate or decelerate it in this direction.
3.  **Vertical Component:** As soon as the apple is released, it is under the influence of the constant downward force of gravity. This results in uniform acceleration $g$ ($9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ downwards). The vertical motion is characterized by an initial vertical velocity of zero (if thrown perfectly horizontally) and a linear increase in downward velocity over time.
