To solve this physics problem, we need to use the formula for acceleration due to gravity on the surface of a planet:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Where:
*   $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity
*   $G$ is the gravitational constant
*   $M$ is the mass of the planet
*   $r$ is the radius of the planet

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the given information:**
*   The gravity of both planets is equal: $g_1 = g_2$
*   Planet 1 has 4 times the mass of Planet 2: $M_1 = 4M_2$

**2. Set up the equality:**
Since $g_1 = g_2$, we can write:
$$\frac{GM_1}{r_1^2} = \frac{GM_2}{r_2^2}$$

**3. Simplify the equation:**
We can cancel out the gravitational constant $G$ from both sides:
$$\frac{M_1}{r_1^2} = \frac{M_2}{r_2^2}$$

**4. Substitute the known mass relationship:**
Substitute $M_1 = 4M_2$ into the equation:
$$\frac{4M_2}{r_1^2} = \frac{M_2}{r_2^2}$$

**5. Solve for the radius relationship:**
Divide both sides by $M_2$:
$$\frac{4}{r_1^2} = \frac{1}{r_2^2}$$

Now, rearrange to isolate the radii:
$$4r_2^2 = r_1^2$$

Take the square root of both sides:
$$\sqrt{4r_2^2} = \sqrt{r_1^2}$$
$$2r_2 = r_1$$

**Conclusion:**
The radius of Planet 1 is twice the radius of Planet 2 ($r_1 = 2r_2$).
