### Answer

To improve motor performance and mitigate issues regarding torque and back EMF, several engineering modifications can be implemented:

1.  **Increasing the Number of Coils (Armature Windings):** By using multiple coils wound on a cylindrical iron core (an armature), the motor maintains a more consistent torque. As one coil rotates out of the position of maximum torque, another enters it, preventing the "dead spots" seen in simple motors and ensuring smooth rotational speed.
2.  **Using a Radial Magnetic Field:** Instead of flat pole pieces, using curved (concave) permanent magnets or electromagnets ensures that the magnetic field lines are always perpendicular to the current-carrying wires in the armature. Since torque is defined by $\tau = nBIA \cos(\theta)$, keeping the field radial keeps the coil in the position of maximum torque throughout the rotation.
3.  **Increasing the Strength of the Magnetic Field ($B$):** Using stronger permanent magnets (e.g., Neodymium) or electromagnets with higher current capacity increases the magnetic flux density. This directly increases the maximum torque ($\tau = nBIA$) and enhances the magnitude of the back EMF induced, which helps regulate the motor's operating speed.
4.  **Increasing the Number of Turns ($n$) in the Coil:** Adding more turns to the armature coil increases the total flux linkage and the magnetic force experienced by the coil, significantly boosting both starting and running torque.
5.  **Using a Laminated Iron Core:** To address back EMF efficiency, the armature should be built on a laminated iron core. This reduces the circulation of **eddy currents** within the core, which would otherwise generate unwanted heat and oppose the motion, thereby improving the efficiency of the motor and reducing energy losses.
6.  **Optimizing Commutation:** Using more commutator segments ensures that current is always directed in the most efficient path through the armature, reducing sparking and power loss, which maintains a more stable torque output.

### How to solve this question
1.  **Analyze the context:** The question mentions a "variable resistor." This is a clue that the motor faces high starting current (because back EMF is zero at zero velocity). Your answer should address how structural improvements remove the need for such external protections.
2.  **Focus on the Physics principles:** 
    *   **Torque:** Recall $\tau = nBIA \sin(\theta)$ (or $\cos(\theta)$ depending on geometry). To improve torque, you must increase $n, B, I,$ or $A$.
    *   **Back EMF:** Recall $\epsilon = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$. Back EMF is necessary to limit current at high speeds. 
3.  **Structure the response:** Since this is an 8-mark question, aim for 4 distinct, well-explained points. Use technical vocabulary (e.g., *armature, flux density, radial field, eddy currents*).
4.  **Connect concepts:** Explain *why* the change works. For example, do not just say "use curved magnets," say "use curved magnets *to ensure a radial field, which keeps the force perpendicular to the coil for maximum, constant torque*."
