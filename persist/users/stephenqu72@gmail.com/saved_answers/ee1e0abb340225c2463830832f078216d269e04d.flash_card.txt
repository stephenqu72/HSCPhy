### Front
How does the shape of a standing wave on a fixed string change when the frequency is tripled?

### Back
- Key idea: The number of loops (antinodes) corresponds to the harmonic number ($n$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = f\lambda$; for a fixed string, $L = n \frac{\lambda}{2}$.
- When to use it: When frequency is changed on a string with fixed tension and density (constant $v$).
- Common trap: Forgetting that tripling the frequency creates the 3rd harmonic (3 loops), not 3 wavelengths.
