To achieve full marks on this 6-mark HSC Physics question, you need to link specific features of the electromagnetic spectrum to the physical characteristics of a star.

### Answer
The properties of a star can be determined by analyzing the light it emits, which provides data on temperature, chemical composition, and motion:

1.  **Temperature (Wien’s Law):** The continuous spectrum of a star approximates a blackbody curve. By determining the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) of the spectrum, we can calculate the star's surface temperature ($T$) using Wien’s Displacement Law:
    $$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$
    Where $b$ is Wien’s constant. A peak shifted toward the blue end indicates a hotter star, while a peak shifted toward the red end indicates a cooler star.

2.  **Chemical Composition (Absorption Lines):** As light passes through the star’s cooler outer atmosphere, specific elements absorb photons at characteristic wavelengths, creating dark absorption lines (Fraunhofer lines). By comparing these lines to known laboratory spectra, astronomers can identify the chemical elements present in the stellar atmosphere.

3.  **Radial Velocity (Doppler Effect):** By comparing the observed wavelength ($\lambda_{obs}$) of known absorption lines to their rest wavelength ($\lambda_{rest}$), the Doppler effect can be used to determine the star’s radial velocity ($v$):
    $$\frac{\Delta\lambda}{\lambda_{rest}} = \frac{v}{c}$$
    A redshift indicates the star is moving away, while a blueshift indicates it is moving toward the observer.

4.  **Stellar Rotation and Pressure:** The width of the absorption lines can reveal additional information. Broadening of lines can indicate high stellar rotation speeds (due to the Doppler effect on opposite sides of the star) or high surface gravity (pressure broadening), which helps in determining luminosity classes.

*   **Identify the "Key Concepts":** For a 6-mark question, aim for at least 3 distinct "pillars" of information (Temperature, Composition, and Motion).
*   **Use Physics Terminology:** Ensure you use the correct terminology such as *blackbody radiation*, *absorption lines*, *Doppler shift*, and *Wien’s Law*.
*   **Show Equations:** Including relevant formulas like Wien’s Law or the Doppler shift formula adds mathematical rigour, which is highly valued in HSC physics marking criteria.
*   **Structure:** Use headings or clear, concise bullet points to ensure the examiner can easily identify how you have addressed each property of the star.
