### Answer
The correct answer is **B) The velocity of electromagnetic waves.**

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to be familiar with James Clerk Maxwell’s contributions to the unification of electricity and magnetism, a core component of the HSC Physics curriculum ("Electromagnetism" module).

1.  **Maxwell's Equations:** Maxwell unified existing laws (Gauss's laws, Ampere's law, and Faraday's law) into a set of four equations. Through these, he predicted that changing electric and magnetic fields would propagate through space as a self-sustaining wave.
2.  **The Prediction:** A key result of Maxwell’s derivation was the calculation of the speed of these waves using the constants of free space: the permittivity of free space ($\epsilon_0$) and the permeability of free space ($\mu_0$). He calculated the speed $v$ using the formula:
    $$v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$$
    When he calculated this value, it resulted in approximately $3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$, which matched the known experimental value for the speed of light. This led him to the ground-breaking prediction that **light itself is an electromagnetic wave**.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **A) The wave behaviour of particles:** This is associated with de Broglie (matter waves).
*   **C) The relationship between black bodies and their surface temperature:** This relates to Wien's Displacement Law or the Stefan-Boltzmann Law (topics within "Quanta to Quarks").
*   **D) The wavelengths produced by transmission between energy levels of atoms:** This is associated with the Bohr model of the atom and the Rydberg formula.
