### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of the **First Postulate of Special Relativity**.

1.  **Length Contraction:** The phenomenon described is length contraction. According to Einstein, an object moving relative to an observer will appear to be contracted (shortened) in the direction of motion. Because both the platform and the train are in relative motion to one another, each observer measures the other object as being shorter than their own "proper length."
2.  **The Equivalence of Inertial Frames:** A fundamental pillar of Special Relativity is that the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames of reference. There is no "preferred" or "absolute" frame of reference. Therefore, the observer on the platform and the observer on the train are both correct within their own frames of reference. The apparent contradiction is resolved by acknowledging that "length" is not an absolute property but a relative one that depends on the motion of the observer.
3.  **Evaluating the distractors:**
    *   **(B) and (D):** These are incorrect because, in Special Relativity, any inertial frame (a frame moving at a constant velocity) is considered perfectly valid. Moving frames are not "invalid."
    *   **(C):** This is incorrect because the speed of light is a constant ($c$) in all inertial frames, regardless of the motion of the source or the observer.

By accepting that all inertial frames are equally valid, we understand that both observers are simply measuring the length of an object that is moving relative to them, leading to the valid observation of length contraction from both perspectives.
