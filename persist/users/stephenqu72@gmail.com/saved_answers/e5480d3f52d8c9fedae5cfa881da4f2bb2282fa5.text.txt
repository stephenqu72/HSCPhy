To find the speed of the electron, we must apply the principle of **Conservation of Energy**.

### Explanation
When an electron is accelerated through a potential difference ($V$), its electrical potential energy is converted entirely into kinetic energy ($K$). 

1. **The Energy Equation:**
   The work done on the electron is $W = qV$. This energy appears as kinetic energy:
   $$qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
   Where:
   - $q = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$ (charge of an electron)
   - $V = 3200\text{ V}$ (potential difference)
   - $m = 9.109 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$ (mass of an electron)
   - $v = \text{speed of the electron}$

2. **Note on the Magnetic Field:**
   In this scenario, the magnetic field is perpendicular to the electron's path. While a magnetic field exerts a force on a moving charge ($F = qvB\sin\theta$), this force acts perpendicular to the direction of motion. Therefore, the magnetic field **does no work** on the electron and does not change its speed; it only changes its direction (causing it to curve). We can safely ignore the magnetic field strength for this specific calculation.

3. **Calculation:**
   Rearrange the energy equation to solve for $v$:
   $$v = \sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}}$$
   Substitute the known values:
   $$v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (3200\text{ V})}{9.109 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}}}$$
   $$v = \sqrt{\frac{1.025 \times 10^{-15}}{9.109 \times 10^{-31}}}$$
   $$v = \sqrt{1.125 \times 10^{15}}$$
   $$v \approx 3.35 \times 10^7\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **C**.
