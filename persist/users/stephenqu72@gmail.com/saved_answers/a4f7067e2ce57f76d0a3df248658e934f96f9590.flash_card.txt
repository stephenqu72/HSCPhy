### Front
In uniform circular satellite motion, what are the instantaneous directions of the velocity and centripetal acceleration vectors?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Velocity is tangential to the path; centripetal acceleration is directed radially toward the center of the orbit.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\vec{v} \perp \vec{a_c}$. No fixed constant.
- **When to use it:** Whenever identifying the direction of forces or motion for an object orbiting a central body.
- **Common trap:** Confusing "tangential" with "outward" or incorrectly assuming acceleration is in the direction of motion (it is perpendicular).
