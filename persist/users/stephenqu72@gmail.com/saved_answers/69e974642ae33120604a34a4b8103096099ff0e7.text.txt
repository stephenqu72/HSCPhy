### Answer
The correct answer is **D. Discovery of radio waves**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to be familiar with the history of electromagnetism as outlined in the HSC Physics syllabus regarding James Clerk Maxwell and Heinrich Hertz.

1.  **Maxwell's Contributions (A, B, and C):** James Clerk Maxwell is famous for synthesizing existing laws of electricity and magnetism into a set of four equations (Maxwell's Equations). Through these equations, he mathematically unified electricity and magnetism into a single field: electromagnetism. He also derived a wave equation from these, which allowed him to **predict the existence of electromagnetic waves** and calculate their speed to be approximately equal to the speed of light ($c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$), leading to his hypothesis that light itself is an electromagnetic wave.

2.  **The "NOT" Contribution (D):** While Maxwell provided the theoretical framework for electromagnetic waves, it was **Heinrich Hertz** who experimentally proved their existence years later. Hertz is credited with the **discovery of radio waves** through his experiments in the late 1880s, where he successfully generated and detected electromagnetic waves in a laboratory setting.

Since the question asks for what Maxwell did *not* do, option D is the correct choice because the discovery (experimental proof) belongs to Hertz, not Maxwell.
