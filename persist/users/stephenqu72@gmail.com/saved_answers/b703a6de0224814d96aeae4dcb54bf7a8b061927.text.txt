### Answer

The process described by the equation is the **proton-proton chain reaction**.

The reaction occurs in three primary steps:

1.  Two protons fuse to form a deuterium nucleus ($^2_1\text{H}$), releasing a positron ($^0_{+1}\text{e}$) and a neutrino ($\nu$):
    $$^1_1\text{H} + ^1_1\text{H} \rightarrow ^2_1\text{H} + ^0_{+1}\text{e} + \nu$$
2.  The deuterium nucleus fuses with another proton to form a helium-3 nucleus ($^3_2\text{He}$), releasing a gamma-ray photon ($\gamma$):
    $$^2_1\text{H} + ^1_1\text{H} \rightarrow ^3_2\text{He} + \gamma$$
3.  Two helium-3 nuclei fuse to form a stable helium-4 nucleus ($^4_2\text{He}$), releasing two protons back into the stellar environment:
    $$^3_2\text{He} + ^3_2\text{He} \rightarrow ^4_2\text{He} + ^1_1\text{H} + ^1_1\text{H}$$

*(Note: To achieve the net equation provided in the prompt, steps 1 and 2 must occur twice each for every one instance of step 3.)*

### How to solve it

To secure full marks for this 4-mark question, you must demonstrate knowledge of the specific nuclear fusion sequence that powers stars like our Sun:

1.  **Identification (1 mark):** Correctly identify the process as the "proton-proton chain." Be careful not to confuse this with the CNO cycle, which occurs in more massive stars.
2.  **Structural Outline (3 marks):** You need to outline the three distinct stages of the fusion process. 
    *   **Step 1:** Focus on the creation of deuterium. Remember the conservation laws; a proton changing to a neutron necessitates the emission of a positron and a neutrino.
    *   **Step 2:** Focus on the formation of Helium-3. This is a radiative process involving the release of high-energy photons.
    *   **Step 3:** Focus on the final formation of Helium-4. This stage is crucial because it accounts for the final products and illustrates how protons are "recycled" in the reaction.

**HSC Tip:** Always ensure your subscripts (atomic number) and superscripts (mass number) are balanced on both sides of every equation you write. In nuclear physics questions, examiners look for strict adherence to the laws of conservation of charge and nucleon number.
