To explain why the total energy of an orbiting satellite is negative, we must consider the two components of its mechanical energy: kinetic energy and gravitational potential energy.

### Answer
The total energy $E_{total}$ of a satellite in a stable circular orbit is the sum of its kinetic energy ($K$) and gravitational potential energy ($U$):
$$E_{total} = K + U$$

1.  **Gravitational Potential Energy ($U$):** By convention, the gravitational potential energy is defined as zero at an infinite distance from the Earth. Because gravity is an attractive force, work must be done against the gravitational field to move an object to infinity. Therefore, the potential energy at any finite distance $r$ is negative: 
    $$U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$$
2.  **Kinetic Energy ($K$):** For a satellite in a circular orbit, the centripetal force is provided by the gravitational force:
    $$\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GMm}{r^2} \implies mv^2 = \frac{GMm}{r}$$
    Since $K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$, we substitute the expression above:
    $$K = \frac{GMm}{2r}$$
3.  **Total Energy:** Summing these gives:
    $$E_{total} = \frac{GMm}{2r} + \left( -\frac{GMm}{r} \right) = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$$

Because $G, M, m,$ and $r$ are all positive values, the resulting total energy $E_{total}$ is always negative. This negative value indicates that the satellite is **bound** to the Earth; it does not have enough energy to escape the gravitational pull and reach infinity (where the potential energy would be zero).

### How to solve this
When approaching HSC Physics questions like this (worth 4 marks), follow this structure:
*   **Define the components:** Explicitly state that total energy is the sum of kinetic and potential energy.
*   **Use the reference point:** Explain that potential energy is negative because we define $U=0$ at infinity.
*   **Mathematical derivation:** Show the relationship between $K$ and $U$ using circular orbit physics ($F_c = F_g$).
*   **Physical interpretation:** Conclude by stating that a negative total energy signifies a "bound system."
