### Answer

**(a)**
To calculate the temperature, we use Wien’s Displacement Law:
$$\lambda_{max} T = 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}$$

1. From the graph, the peak intensity occurs at $\lambda_{max} = 1.0 \text{ } \mu\text{m} = 1.0 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$.
2. Substitute the values into the formula:
$$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}}{1.0 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}}$$
$$T = 2898 \text{ K}$$

**(b)**
The classical wave theory of light predicted the "ultraviolet catastrophe," which suggested that intensity should increase infinitely as wavelength decreases (the Rayleigh-Jeans law). This contradicted experimental observations, which showed the intensity drops toward zero at short wavelengths. Max Planck resolved this by proposing that electromagnetic energy is emitted in discrete "quanta" ($E = hf$). This quantized approach successfully modeled the curve, forming the foundation for the particle model of light (photons).
