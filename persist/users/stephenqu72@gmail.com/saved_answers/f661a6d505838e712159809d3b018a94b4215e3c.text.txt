### Answer
The correct answer is **A. The neutron**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you must recall the key historical experiments that led to our current understanding of the atom:

1.  **James Chadwick (1932):** He bombarded beryllium atoms with alpha particles and discovered a neutral radiation that had a mass similar to the proton. He concluded that these were **neutrons**, particles with no charge located in the nucleus. This solved the discrepancy between the atomic number and the atomic mass of elements.
2.  **Ernest Rutherford:** He discovered the **atomic nucleus** through his famous gold foil experiment, where he observed alpha particles being deflected by a small, dense, positively charged center.
3.  **Robert Millikan:** He performed the oil-drop experiment to determine the precise **charge of an electron**.
4.  **J.J. Thomson:** He used cathode ray tubes to measure the **charge-to-mass ratio ($e/m$) of an electron**.

By reviewing the historical milestones in the HSC Physics syllabus regarding the development of the atomic model, we can confirm that Chadwick is specifically associated with the discovery of the neutron.
