### Explanation
To determine the answer, we must recall how Edwin Hubble provided the empirical evidence that led to the theory of an expanding Universe.

1.  **The Role of the Doppler Effect:** When a source of light moves away from an observer, the light waves are "stretched," shifting the spectrum toward longer wavelengths (the red end of the visible spectrum). This is known as a **redshift**.
2.  **Hubble's Observations:** In the 1920s, Edwin Hubble observed the spectra of distant galaxies. He noticed that the spectral lines (the "fingerprints" of elements like hydrogen) were systematically shifted toward the red end.
3.  **The Conclusion:** By applying the **Doppler effect** to this light, Hubble concluded that these galaxies were moving away from Earth. Furthermore, he discovered that the speed at which they were receding was proportional to their distance from us (Hubble's Law). This linear relationship is the primary evidence for the expansion of the Universe.
