To calculate the force exerted on the dust particle, we need to determine the strength of the uniform electric field between the plates and then use the formula for electrostatic force.

### 1. Identify the given values
*   Charge of the particle ($q$): $-1.00 \times 10^{-8} \text{ C}$
*   Potential difference ($V$): $50.0 \text{ kV} = 50.0 \times 10^3 \text{ V}$
*   Distance between plates ($d$): $47.5 \text{ cm} = 0.475 \text{ m}$

### 2. Formulas required
*   The magnitude of the electric field ($E$) between parallel plates is: 
    $$E = \frac{V}{d}$$
*   The magnitude of the electrostatic force ($F$) acting on a charge is: 
    $$F = qE$$
    (Combining these, we get $F = \frac{qV}{d}$)

### 3. Calculation
First, calculate the electric field strength:
$$E = \frac{50,000 \text{ V}}{0.475 \text{ m}} \approx 105,263.16 \text{ V/m}$$

Next, calculate the magnitude of the force:
$$F = |q| \times E$$
$$F = (1.00 \times 10^{-8} \text{ C}) \times 105,263.16 \text{ V/m}$$
$$F \approx 1.05 \times 10^{-3} \text{ N}$$

**Answer:**
The magnitude of the force exerted on the dust particle is approximately **$1.05 \times 10^{-3} \text{ N}$** directed towards the positively charged plate.

***

### Explanation of the approach:
1.  **Understand the physics:** The setup describes a region of uniform electric field between two parallel plates. The potential difference across the plates creates this field.
2.  **Units:** Ensure all units are in SI units (Volts and meters). Always convert $kV$ to $V$ and $cm$ to $m$.
3.  **Calculation:** Use the relationship $E = V/d$ to find the field intensity, then multiply by the magnitude of the charge $q$ to find the force. Since the particle is negatively charged, it will be attracted to the positive plate.
