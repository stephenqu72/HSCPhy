### Explanation
To determine why **A** is the correct answer, we use the Conservation of Energy principle. 

1.  **Energy Approach:** The total mechanical energy at the point of projection equals the total mechanical energy at impact (since air resistance is ignored).
    $$E_{initial} = E_{final}$$
    $$mgh + \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2 = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2$$
    Notice that mass ($m$) appears in every term. We can divide both sides by $m$:
    $$gh + \frac{1}{2}v_i^2 = \frac{1}{2}v_f^2$$
    $$v_f = \sqrt{v_i^2 + 2gh}$$
2.  **Conclusion:** Because $v_i$ (initial speed) and $h$ (height above ground) are the same for both particles, the final speed $v_f$ must be the same for both, regardless of their mass or launch angle.
