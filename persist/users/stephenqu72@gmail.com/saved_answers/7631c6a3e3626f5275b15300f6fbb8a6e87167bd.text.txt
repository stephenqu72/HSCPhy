To solve this problem, we must analyze the forces acting on the metal rod in its equilibrium state.

### 1. Identify the Forces
There are three forces acting on the rod:
1. **Weight ($W = mg$):** Acting vertically downwards.
2. **Tension ($T$):** Acting along the wire at an angle of $15^\circ$ to the vertical.
3. **Magnetic Force ($F_B = IlB\sin\theta$):** Since the current is horizontal and the magnetic field is vertical, the angle between them is $90^\circ$, so $F_B = IlB$. This force acts horizontally.

### 2. Force Diagram
At equilibrium, the sum of forces is zero. We can represent this with a vector triangle or by resolving components:
*   Horizontal components: $T\sin(15^\circ) = F_B$
*   Vertical components: $T\cos(15^\circ) = mg$

### 3. Calculation
Divide the horizontal equation by the vertical equation:
$$\frac{T\sin(15^\circ)}{T\cos(15^\circ)} = \frac{F_B}{mg}$$
$$\tan(15^\circ) = \frac{IlB}{mg}$$

Rearranging for the magnetic field strength $B$:
$$B = \frac{mg\tan(15^\circ)}{Il}$$

**Given values:**
*   $m = 0.5\text{ kg}$
*   $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$
*   $I = 4.5\text{ A}$
*   $l = 0.9\text{ m}$
*   $\theta = 15^\circ$

Substitute the values into the formula:
$$B = \frac{0.5 \times 9.8 \times \tan(15^\circ)}{4.5 \times 0.9}$$
$$B = \frac{4.9 \times 0.2679}{4.05}$$
$$B \approx 0.324\text{ T}$$

**Answer:** The strength of the magnetic field is approximately **$0.324\text{ T}$**.
