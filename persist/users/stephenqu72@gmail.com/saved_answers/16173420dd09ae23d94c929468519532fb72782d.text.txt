### Explanation
To solve this, we must identify the historical context and the physical principles presented in the diagram:

1.  **Identify the Scientist and Theory:** The diagram shows an electromagnetic wave, which is a mathematical model famously proposed by **James Clerk Maxwell** in 1865. Maxwell unified electricity and magnetism into a set of equations describing how oscillating electric and magnetic fields regenerate each other.
2.  **Analyze the Options:**
    *   **A is incorrect:** While 19th-century scientists (including Maxwell) hypothesized the existence of the "luminiferous aether" as a medium for light waves, modern physics has rejected this concept. More importantly, the specific diagram of an electromagnetic wave represents a self-propagating wave in a vacuum, not vibrations of a physical medium.
    *   **B is incorrect:** The diagram shows the electric ($E$) and magnetic ($B$) fields oscillating in phase and traveling in the *same* direction (the direction of propagation).
    *   **C is correct:** Maxwell’s theory demonstrated that an accelerating (oscillating) charge produces a time-varying electric field, which in turn induces a time-varying magnetic field (and vice versa). This process makes the electromagnetic wave "self-propagating," meaning it does not require a medium to travel.
    *   **D is incorrect:** Although Einstein used Maxwell’s equations to formulate his Special Theory of Relativity, the specific theory of classical electromagnetism shown here is attributed to Maxwell, not Einstein.

**Key Physics Concept:** Electromagnetic waves consist of oscillating electric and magnetic fields that are perpendicular to each other and to the direction of wave propagation. Because the changing magnetic field induces a changing electric field, and the changing electric field induces a magnetic field, the wave sustains itself as it moves through space at the speed of light, $c$.
