To determine the correct answer, we must apply **Wien's Displacement Law**, which describes the relationship between the temperature of a black body and the wavelength at which it emits the most radiation.

### Explanation
Wien's Displacement Law is expressed by the formula:
$$\lambda_{peak} \cdot T = b$$
where $b$ is Wien's displacement constant ($2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$). 

This formula tells us that the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{peak}$) is **inversely proportional** to the absolute temperature ($T$) of the body:
$$\lambda_{peak} \propto \frac{1}{T}$$

1. **Initial state:** The source has temperature $T_1$ and peak wavelength $\lambda_1$.
   $$\lambda_1 \propto \frac{1}{T_1}$$
2. **Final state:** The temperature is increased to $T_2 = 2T_1$. Let the new peak wavelength be $\lambda_2$.
   $$\lambda_2 \propto \frac{1}{T_2} = \frac{1}{2T_1}$$
3. **Comparison:** By comparing $\lambda_2$ to $\lambda_1$:
   $$\lambda_2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \left(\frac{1}{T_1}\right) = \frac{\lambda_1}{2}$$

When the absolute temperature of a black body is doubled, the peak wavelength of the emitted radiation is halved.

**Answer:** (B) The peak wavelength would become $\frac{\lambda_1}{2}$.
