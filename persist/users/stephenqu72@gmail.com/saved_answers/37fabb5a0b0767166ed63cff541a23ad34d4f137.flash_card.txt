### Front
State Einstein’s Equivalence Principle regarding gravitational fields and acceleration.

### Back
- Key idea: The physical effects of a uniform gravitational field are locally indistinguishable from the effects of uniform acceleration.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure
- When to use it: Analyzing "Einstein elevator" scenarios, weightlessness, or explaining why light bends in a gravitational field.
- Common trap: Forgetting the word **locally**; over large distances, tidal forces (differences in gravity) can distinguish between the two.
