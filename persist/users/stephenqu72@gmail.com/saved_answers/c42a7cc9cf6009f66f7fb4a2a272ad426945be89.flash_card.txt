### Front
What is the defining characteristic of a scientific hypothesis in the context of the Scientific Method?

### Back
- Key idea: It is a tentative, testable explanation for a phenomenon.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When identifying the starting point of a scientific investigation.
- Common trap: Confusing a "hypothesis" (unproven, testable) with a "theory" (well-tested, broadly accepted explanation).
