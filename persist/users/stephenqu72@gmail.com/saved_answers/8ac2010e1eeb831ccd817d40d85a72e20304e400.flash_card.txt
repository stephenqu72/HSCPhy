### Front
What variables determine the maximum height ($h_{max}$) of a projectile in a vacuum?

### Back
- Key idea: Maximum height is determined by the initial vertical velocity component.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $h_{max} = \frac{u^2 \sin^2 \theta}{2g}$
- When to use it: To calculate the peak vertical displacement of a projectile when air resistance is neglected.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the sine of the angle or confusing $u$ (total launch speed) with $u_y$ (vertical component).
