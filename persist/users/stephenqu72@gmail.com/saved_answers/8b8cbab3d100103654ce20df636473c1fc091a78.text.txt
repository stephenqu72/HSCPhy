To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of energy conservation in an electric field and the mechanics of circular motion in a magnetic field.

### 1. Step-by-Step Solution

**Step 1: Calculate the velocity of the proton exiting the electric field.**
The work done on the proton by the electric field equals its change in kinetic energy. Since the proton starts from rest:
$$qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
Rearranging for velocity $v$:
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}}$$
Using standard values for a proton ($q = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$, $m = 1.673 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$) and $V = 10,000\text{ V}$:
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times 10,000}{1.673 \times 10^{-27}}} \approx 1.383 \times 10^6\text{ m/s}$$

**Step 2: Calculate the radius of curvature in the magnetic field.**
When the proton enters the magnetic field, the magnetic force acts as the centripetal force:
$$F_B = F_c \implies qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Solving for the radius $r$:
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$
Substitute the expression for $v$ from Step 1 into this equation:
$$r = \frac{m}{qB} \sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}} = \frac{1}{B} \sqrt{\frac{2mV}{q}}$$

**Step 3: Calculate the final numerical value.**
Using $B = 2.0 \times 10^{-2}\text{ T}$:
$$r = \frac{1}{2.0 \times 10^{-2}} \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (1.673 \times 10^{-27}) \times 10,000}{1.602 \times 10^{-19}}}$$
$$r = 50 \times \sqrt{2.088 \times 10^{-4}} \approx 50 \times 0.01445$$
$$r \approx 0.7225\text{ m}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (as per the given values):
**The radius of curvature is $0.72\text{ m}$.**

---

### 2. Explanation of the Method
*   **Energy Conservation:** We assume all electric potential energy is converted into kinetic energy because the proton starts with negligible velocity. 
*   **Circular Motion:** Because the magnetic field is perpendicular to the velocity, the proton undergoes uniform circular motion. The magnetic force ($qvB$) provides the required centripetal force ($\frac{mv^2}{r}$).
*   **Mathematical shortcut:** By substituting the energy equation into the radius equation, we simplify the calculation and reduce the potential for rounding errors.
