### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Answer:** The pattern shown is an electron diffraction pattern. In classical physics, electrons were considered strictly as point-like particles with a definite trajectory. Particles following classical mechanics cannot produce an interference/diffraction pattern; such patterns are a hallmark of wave-like behaviour. This observation provides experimental evidence for de Broglie’s hypothesis, which states that matter has wave properties governed by the relation $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$. The electron beam is diffracting off the periodic lattice of the crystal, just as X-rays (which are waves) would, thereby demonstrating that electrons exhibit wave-particle duality, a phenomenon classical physics cannot account for.

**How to solve:** To earn full marks, you must link the physical observation (diffraction pattern) to the underlying theoretical limitation. 
1. Identify the pattern as **diffraction**.
2. State that diffraction is a characteristic property of **waves**.
3. Conclude that this forces a rejection of the classical view of the electron as merely a **particle**, supporting the concept of **wave-particle duality**.

#### Part (b)
**Answer:** Erwin Schrödinger moved away from the Bohr model’s idea of defined "planetary" orbits for electrons. He contributed the wave mechanical model, which describes electrons using mathematical wave functions, $\psi$. Instead of fixed paths, these functions define regions of space (orbitals) where there is a high *probability* of finding an electron. The "electron cloud" representation visualizes this probability density, where the density of the dots represents the likelihood of the electron's presence at a particular position. This successfully explained atomic stability and spectral lines without requiring electrons to "orbit" in a way that would classically lose energy through radiation.

**How to solve:** To earn full marks, focus on the transition from deterministic to probabilistic models.
1. Mention the **wave equation** ($\psi$).
2. Explain the shift from **defined orbits** (Bohr model) to **probability distributions/orbitals**.
3. Explain that the electron cloud model represents the **probability density** of finding an electron, rather than a physical trajectory.
