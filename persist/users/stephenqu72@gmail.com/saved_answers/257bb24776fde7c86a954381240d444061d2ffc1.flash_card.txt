### Front
Why is the net force on a satellite orbiting multiple celestial bodies never zero?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational force acts as an external force at all points in the path, causing constant centripetal acceleration or changes in velocity.
- **Law:** Newton’s First Law ($F_{net} = ma$). For $F_{net} = 0$, an object must remain in inertial motion (constant velocity).
- **When to use it:** When analyzing orbital trajectories, satellite flybys, or interpreting diagrams of celestial motion.
- **Common trap:** Assuming that because an object is "in orbit" or following a specific path, it is in equilibrium. Gravity is always present unless the object is at an infinite distance or a specific, unstable Lagrangian point.
