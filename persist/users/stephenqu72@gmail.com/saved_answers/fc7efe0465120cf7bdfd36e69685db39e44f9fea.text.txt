### Part a: Explanation
**Answer:** The voltage is stepped up to reduce the current flowing through the transmission lines. Since power loss due to heating in the wires is given by $P_{loss} = I^2R$, reducing the current $I$ by a factor $n$ results in a reduction of power loss by a factor of $n^2$. This makes the transmission of electrical energy much more efficient over long distances.

**How to solve:** This is a conceptual question. Always link the "step-up" process to the **reduction of current** and subsequently the **reduction of energy loss** as heat ($I^2R$ losses).

### Part b: Calculation of Current
**Answer:**
Given:
- Power $P = 500\text{ MW} = 500 \times 10^6\text{ W}$
- Transmission Voltage $V = 500\text{ kV} = 500 \times 10^3\text{ V}$

Using the formula $P = VI$:
$$I = \frac{P}{V} = \frac{500 \times 10^6\text{ W}}{500 \times 10^3\text{ V}} = 1000\text{ A}$$
To convert to kA:
$$1000\text{ A} = 1.0\text{ kA}$$

**How to solve:** Identify the power and the transmission voltage. Rearrange the power formula $P = VI$ to solve for $I$. Ensure your units are converted to standard SI units (Watts and Volts) before calculating, then convert the final answer to kA as requested by the box.

### Part c: Determine Maximum Power Available
**Answer:**
1. Calculate the power lost as heat in the transmission lines:
   $$P_{loss} = I^2R = (1000\text{ A})^2 \times 30.0\text{ }\Omega$$
   $$P_{loss} = 1,000,000 \times 30.0 = 30,000,000\text{ W} = 30\text{ MW}$$
2. Subtract the lost power from the initial generated power:
   $$P_{available} = P_{total} - P_{loss}$$
   $$P_{available} = 500\text{ MW} - 30\text{ MW} = 470\text{ MW}$$

**How to solve:** This is a multi-step calculation. First, find the power dissipated by the resistance of the wires using $P = I^2R$. Then, subtract this "wasted" power from the total power supplied by the generator to find the remaining power available at the destination.
