To solve this problem, we use the relationship between the electric field ($E$), the potential difference (voltage, $V$), and the distance between the electrodes ($d$). 

### Explanation:
The formula for the electric field between two parallel plates (which serves as a model for the gap in a spark plug) is:
$$E = \frac{V}{d}$$

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Voltage ($V$) = $16000\text{ V}$
*   Distance ($d$) = $0.4\text{ mm}$

**2. Convert units to SI units:**
In physics calculations, it is essential to work in standard SI units (meters).
*   $d = 0.4\text{ mm} = 0.4 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m} = 0.0004\text{ m}$

**3. Substitute the values into the formula:**
$$E = \frac{16000}{0.0004}$$

To simplify this division, we can write $0.0004$ as $4 \times 10^{-4}$:
$$E = \frac{16000}{4 \times 10^{-4}}$$
$$E = 4000 \times 10^4$$
$$E = 40,000,000\text{ V/m} \text{ (or N/C)}$$

Looking at the options provided, $40,000,000\text{ NC}^{-1}$ corresponds to option (D).

### Answer:
The correct answer is **(D)**.
