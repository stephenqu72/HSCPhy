### Part (a): How did Einstein explain the photoelectric effect?

**Answer:**
Einstein proposed that light consists of discrete packets of energy called **photons**, where the energy of each photon is given by $E = hf$. He explained that the photoelectric effect occurs through a one-to-one interaction where a single photon transfers all its energy to a single electron in the metal. If the photon's energy is greater than the metal's **work function** ($\Phi$), the electron is ejected with kinetic energy. This explained why the effect depends on the frequency of light rather than intensity, as a single photon must reach the threshold frequency to overcome the work function.

**How to solve:**
To gain full marks, ensure you mention:
1. **The Particle Model:** Light acts as photons, not just a continuous wave.
2. **Quantization:** $E = hf$.
3. **The Mechanism:** The one-to-one interaction and the threshold frequency required to overcome the work function ($\Phi$).

### Part (b): Determine the wavelength of the light in nanometres.

**Answer:**
To find the wavelength, we use the conservation of energy equation for the photoelectric effect:
$$hf = \Phi + E_k$$
Where $E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.

**Step 1: Calculate the kinetic energy ($E_k$) in Joules.**
Mass of electron ($m$) = $9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
Velocity ($v$) = $1.042 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s}$
$$E_k = 0.5 \times (9.109 \times 10^{-31}) \times (1.042 \times 10^6)^2 \approx 4.945 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

**Step 2: Convert the work function ($\Phi$) to Joules.**
$1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$
$$\Phi = 3.82 \text{ eV} \times (1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J/eV}) \approx 6.120 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

**Step 3: Calculate total photon energy ($E = hf$).**
$$E = 4.945 \times 10^{-19} + 6.120 \times 10^{-19} = 1.1065 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J}$$

**Step 4: Calculate the wavelength ($\lambda$) using $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$.**
$h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ Js}$, $c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
$$\lambda = \frac{hc}{E} = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3.0 \times 10^8)}{1.1065 \times 10^{-18}} \approx 1.796 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$$
$$\lambda \approx 180 \text{ nm}$$

**How to solve:**
1. Always convert all units to SI (Joules, kg, m/s) before performing calculations.
2. Remember that the incident energy of the photon must equal the work required to remove the electron plus the kinetic energy of the released electron.
3. Be careful with your rounding during intermediate steps to maintain precision for the final answer.
