### Answer

**(a) Comparison and Transmutation**

*   **Alpha ($\alpha$) decay:** Consists of a helium nucleus ($^{4}_{2}\text{He}$). It is the most massive and highly ionizing, but has low penetration (stopped by paper). During transmutation, it reduces the atomic number $Z$ by 2 and the mass number $A$ by 4, creating a new element.
*   **Beta ($\beta$) decay:** Consists of an electron ($^{0}_{-1}e$) or positron ($^{0}_{+1}e$). It has moderate ionization and penetration (stopped by thin aluminum). In $\beta^-$ decay, a neutron converts to a proton, increasing $Z$ by 1 while $A$ remains constant.
*   **Gamma ($\gamma$) decay:** High-energy electromagnetic radiation. It has the lowest ionization but highest penetration (requires thick lead/concrete). $\gamma$ emission does not change $Z$ or $A$; it is typically released following $\alpha$ or $\beta$ decay to move the nucleus from an excited state to a lower energy state. 

**Conclusion:** Alpha and beta decay cause transmutation by fundamentally changing the number of protons in the nucleus. Gamma decay does not cause transmutation but acts as a mechanism to stabilize the nucleus post-transmutation.

**(b) Calculation**

The formula for radioactive decay is:
$$N = N_0 \times \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n$$
Where:
*   $N_0 = 10\text{ g}$
*   $n = 3\text{ half-lives}$

Calculation:
$$N = 10 \times \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^3$$
$$N = 10 \times \frac{1}{8}$$
$$N = 1.25\text{ g}$$
The remaining mass is $1.25\text{ grams}$.
