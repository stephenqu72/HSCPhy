To answer this 6-mark HSC Physics question, you must demonstrate a clear understanding of how these two specific areas of Einstein's work challenged the prevailing classical physics view of light.

### Sample Answer

At the start of the 20th century, light was firmly established as an electromagnetic wave (Maxwell's theory). Einstein’s work initiated a paradigm shift by forcing physicists to accept that light possesses both wave and particle characteristics.

**1. The Photoelectric Effect (Particle Nature)**
Classical wave theory failed to explain the photoelectric effect because it predicted that increasing intensity (brightness) should eventually eject electrons, regardless of frequency. Einstein proposed that light consists of discrete packets of energy called photons, where the energy of a photon is given by $E = hf$.
*   This explained why only light above a specific threshold frequency could eject electrons: a single photon must have enough energy to overcome the metal's work function ($\Phi$).
*   Increasing intensity only increases the number of photons (and thus the number of ejected electrons), not their kinetic energy. This provided experimental evidence that light acts as a particle during interaction with matter.

**2. Special Relativity (The Nature of Light)**
Einstein’s Special Relativity established that the speed of light $c$ is a universal constant for all inertial observers. By showing that light does not require a medium (the luminiferous aether) to propagate, Einstein removed the classical requirement for light to behave strictly like a mechanical wave in a medium. Furthermore, the mass-energy equivalence ($E=mc^2$) and the relativistic momentum of light (which lacks rest mass) reinforced the idea that light carries momentum, a property traditionally associated with particles.

**Conclusion**
Together, these theories forced the reconceptualisation of light from a purely wave-based model to wave-particle duality. Light was no longer seen as just an oscillation of the aether, but as something that travels as a wave and interacts as a particle (a quantum of energy).

### How to solve this question
1.  **Analyze the Command Word:** "How did..." implies a causal relationship. You must explain the *mechanism* of the changes.
2.  **Structure:** Use two distinct sections (one for the Photoelectric Effect, one for Relativity) to ensure you address both parts of the prompt.
3.  **Use Physics Terminology:** For full marks, include key terms: *quantization*, *work function*, *threshold frequency*, *wave-particle duality*, and *universal constant*.
4.  **Connect to "Reconceptualisation":** The final "so what?" is crucial. Do not just list the theories; explicitly state that the model changed from a "classical wave" to a "wave-particle" model.
5.  **Mark Allocation:** 6 marks indicate an extended response. Spend roughly 3 marks on the photoelectric effect and 3 marks on relativity/synthesis.
