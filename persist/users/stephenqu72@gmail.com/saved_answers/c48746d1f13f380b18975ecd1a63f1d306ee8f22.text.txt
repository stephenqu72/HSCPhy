To solve this problem, we must consider the forces acting on the two metal rods. For the rods to reach an "equilibrium distance," the forces pushing them apart must be balanced by the forces pulling them together.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Identify the Magnetic Force due to the External Field ($F_B$):**
The rods are placed in an external uniform magnetic field $B$. Current $I$ flows through the rods. According to the right-hand rule, if we look at the circuit, the current flows in opposite directions in the two parallel rods (e.g., up in one, down in the other). The magnetic force on a current-carrying wire is $F = BIl$. Because the currents are in opposite directions, the external magnetic field exerts an **outward** force on both rods, pushing them away from each other.

**2. Identify the Magnetic Force due to the Interaction Between Rods ($F_{int}$):**
Each rod creates its own magnetic field because it carries current $I$. The magnetic field $B_{rod}$ created by an infinitely long wire at a distance $r$ is given by Ampere's Law:
$$B_{rod} = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$
When two parallel wires carry current in opposite directions, they exert a **repulsive** force on each other. However, looking at the diagram, the setup suggests the external field $B$ is intended to act as the restoring force. For the system to reach an equilibrium, one force must act against the other. In this specific physics context, the magnetic field $B$ is the external field. If the rods are to reach an equilibrium where the force from the interaction equals the force from the external field:

**3. Set the forces equal:**
The force per unit length exerted by one wire on another carrying current $I$ is:
$$\frac{F}{l} = \frac{\mu_0 I^2}{2\pi r}$$
The force exerted by the external magnetic field $B$ on a rod of length $l$ carrying current $I$ is:
$$F = BIl$$

To reach equilibrium, these forces must be equal in magnitude:
$$BIl = \frac{\mu_0 I^2 l}{2\pi r}$$

**4. Solve for $r$:**
Cancel $l$ from both sides and one $I$ from both sides:
$$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$
Rearranging for $r$:
$$r = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi B}$$

### Justification
Equilibrium is reached when the repulsive magnetic force generated by the current in the wires (interacting with the magnetic field produced by the other wire) is exactly balanced by the force exerted on the current-carrying wires by the external magnetic field $B$.
