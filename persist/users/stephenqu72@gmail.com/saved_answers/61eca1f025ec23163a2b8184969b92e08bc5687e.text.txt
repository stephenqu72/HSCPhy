The correct answer is **C. Maxwell**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to be familiar with the history of physics, specifically the development of electromagnetic theory, which is a core component of the HSC Physics syllabus (specifically Module 4: Electricity and Magnetism).

1.  **James Clerk Maxwell** formulated a set of equations, now known as **Maxwell's Equations**, which mathematically unified electricity and magnetism. He demonstrated that electric and magnetic fields travel through space as waves moving at the speed of light. This led him to the groundbreaking conclusion that light itself is an electromagnetic wave.
2.  **Einstein** is best known for his theories of Special and General Relativity and his work on the photoelectric effect.
3.  **Huygens** is known for his wave theory of light and the principle of secondary wavelets.
4.  **Newton** is famous for his laws of motion and universal gravitation.

Because Maxwell was the scientist responsible for the mathematical synthesis of electric and magnetic phenomena, C is the correct choice.
