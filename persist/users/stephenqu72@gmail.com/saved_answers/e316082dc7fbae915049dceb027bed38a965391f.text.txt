To find the approximate total binding energy for a nucleus of Tungsten-184 ($^{184}_{74}\text{W}$), follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the number of nucleons
The notation $^{184}_{74}\text{W}$ indicates that the mass number (number of nucleons, $A$) is **184**.

### 2. Determine the binding energy per nucleon from the graph
Locate $A = 184$ on the horizontal axis of the graph. Looking at the curve, we find the corresponding y-value (average binding energy per nucleon). For $A$ around 180–190, the curve is at approximately **8 MeV** per nucleon.

### 3. Calculate the total binding energy in MeV
The total binding energy ($BE_{total}$) is calculated by multiplying the number of nucleons ($A$) by the binding energy per nucleon:
$$BE_{total} \approx 184 \times 8\text{ MeV} = 1472\text{ MeV}$$

### 4. Convert the energy to Joules
We are given that $1\text{ eV} = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$. Therefore, $1\text{ MeV} = 1.6 \times 10^{-13}\text{ J}$.
$$BE_{total} \approx 1472 \times (1.6 \times 10^{-13}\text{ J})$$
$$BE_{total} \approx 2355.2 \times 10^{-13}\text{ J}$$
$$BE_{total} \approx 235.5 \times 10^{-12}\text{ J}$$

Since the prefix pico ($p$) is $10^{-12}$, the energy is approximately **235 pJ**.
