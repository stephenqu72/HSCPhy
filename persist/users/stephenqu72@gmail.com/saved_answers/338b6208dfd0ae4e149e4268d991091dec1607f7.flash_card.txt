### Front
Sequence the chronological stages of the early Universe according to the standard Big Bang model (Inflation, Nucleosynthesis, Decoupling, Particle/Antiparticle formation).

### Back
- Key idea: The universe evolved from a hot, dense state to a transparent state.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Chronological order: II (Inflation) $\rightarrow$ III (Particles) $\rightarrow$ I (Nucleosynthesis) $\rightarrow$ IV (Decoupling).
- When to use it: To answer timeline-based questions regarding the Big Bang theory or Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation.
- Common trap: Confusing the order of Inflation ($10^{-36}$s) and the era of particle formation; remember that Inflation is the "trigger" for the expansion that preceded the cooling necessary for matter formation.
