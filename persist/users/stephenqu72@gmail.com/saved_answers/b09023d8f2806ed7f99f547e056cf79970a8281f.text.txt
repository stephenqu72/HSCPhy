To solve this problem, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation. The gravitational field strength $g$ at a distance $d$ from the centre of a mass $M$ is given by the formula:

$$g = \frac{GM}{d^2}$$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the relationship:** Since $G$ and $M$ are constants, the gravitational field strength $g$ is inversely proportional to the square of the distance from the centre of the planet ($g \propto \frac{1}{d^2}$).
    
2.  **Initial state:** At distance $d_1 = r$, the field strength is $g_1 = 9.76\text{ N kg}^{-1}$.
    
3.  **Final state:** We are asked for the field strength at an *altitude* of $2r$. It is a common HSC trap to confuse "altitude" with "distance from the centre". 
    *   The total distance from the centre of the Earth is the radius plus the altitude.
    *   $d_2 = r + 2r = 3r$.
    
4.  **Apply the inverse-square law:**
    $$\frac{g_2}{g_1} = \frac{\frac{1}{d_2^2}}{\frac{1}{d_1^2}} = \left( \frac{d_1}{d_2} \right)^2$$
    Substitute the known values:
    $$\frac{g_2}{9.76} = \left( \frac{r}{3r} \right)^2$$
    $$\frac{g_2}{9.76} = \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^2 = \frac{1}{9}$$
    
5.  **Calculate the result:**
    $$g_2 = \frac{9.76}{9} \approx 1.0844\dots \text{ N kg}^{-1}$$

Rounding to two decimal places, we get $1.08\text{ N kg}^{-1}$.

**Correct Option: A**
