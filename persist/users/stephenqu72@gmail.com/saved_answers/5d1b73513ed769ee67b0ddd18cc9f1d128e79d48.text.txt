To solve this problem, we use the fundamental definition of magnetic flux.

### 1. Identify the given information
*   Magnetic flux ($\Phi$) = $1.25 \times 10^{-5}\text{ Wb}$
*   Magnetic flux density ($B$) = $1.25\text{ mT}$

First, we must convert the magnetic flux density into standard SI units (Tesla):
$$B = 1.25 \times 10^{-3}\text{ T}$$

### 2. The Formula
The relationship between magnetic flux, magnetic flux density, and the area ($A$) of a loop (assuming the area is perpendicular to the field) is given by:
$$\Phi = B \times A$$

### 3. Calculation
We need to solve for the area ($A$):
$$A = \frac{\Phi}{B}$$

Substitute the values:
$$A = \frac{1.25 \times 10^{-5}\text{ Wb}}{1.25 \times 10^{-3}\text{ T}}$$

Divide the coefficients and the powers of 10:
$$A = \left(\frac{1.25}{1.25}\right) \times 10^{-5 - (-3)}$$
$$A = 1 \times 10^{-2}\text{ m}^2$$
$$A = 0.010\text{ m}^2$$

Comparing this result to the given options, we can see that it matches **(B)**.

***

### Summary of steps:
1.  **Extract variables:** Always note down $\Phi$ and $B$.
2.  **Unit Consistency:** Convert milli-Tesla (mT) to Tesla (T) by multiplying by $10^{-3}$. This is a common trap in HSC exams!
3.  **Rearrange:** Use $\Phi = BA$ to isolate $A$.
4.  **Solve:** Perform the division and select the correct option.
