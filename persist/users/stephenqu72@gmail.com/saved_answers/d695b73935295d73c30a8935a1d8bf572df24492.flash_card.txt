### Front
What are the defining characteristics and the governing formula for an object undergoing uniform circular motion?

### Back
- **Key idea:** An object moving in a circle at constant speed experiences a centripetal force directed toward the centre due to constant change in velocity (acceleration).
- **Formula:** $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- **When to use it:** When calculating the net force required to maintain an object's circular path (e.g., cars on curves, satellite orbits).
- **Common trap:** Centripetal force is not a "new" force; it is the *resultant* of existing forces (like friction, tension, or gravity).
