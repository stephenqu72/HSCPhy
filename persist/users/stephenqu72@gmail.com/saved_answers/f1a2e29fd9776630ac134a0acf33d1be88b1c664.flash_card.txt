### Front
How do you determine the direction of the magnetic field produced by a current-carrying wire?

### Back
- Key idea: Use the Right-Hand Grip Rule. Point the right thumb in the direction of conventional current; fingers curl in the direction of the $B$-field.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Right-Hand Grip Rule.
- When to use it: Whenever determining the magnetic field direction generated by a current (moving charges).
- Common trap: Forgetting that conventional current flows opposite to the direction of electron flow.
