To determine how the interference pattern changes, we must refer to the mathematical formula for the fringe separation (also known as fringe width) in Young’s double-slit experiment:

$$\Delta x = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$

Where:
- $\Delta x$ is the distance between adjacent bright fringes (the separation).
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the light used.
- $L$ is the distance from the slits to the screen.
- $d$ is the distance between the two slits.

### Explanation
1. **Identify the variables:** In this problem, the slit separation ($d$) and the distance to the screen ($L$) remain constant. Only the wavelength ($\lambda$) is changing.
2. **Analyze the relationship:** The formula shows that the fringe separation ($\Delta x$) is directly proportional to the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the light source ($\Delta x \propto \lambda$).
3. **Compare the wavelengths:** The green laser has a wavelength of $532\text{ nm}$ and the red laser has a wavelength of $633\text{ nm}$. Since the wavelength is increasing ($633\text{ nm} > 532\text{ nm}$), the fringe separation ($\Delta x$) must also increase.
4. **Conclusion:** Because the fringes are more widely spaced, the alternating bands of bright light become more separated.

Comparing this to the provided options:
(A) Incorrect: The pattern depends on the wavelength.
(B) Incorrect: Brightness is related to the intensity of the source, not the wavelength.
(C) Incorrect: This would happen if the wavelength decreased.
(D) **Correct:** An increase in wavelength results in an increase in fringe separation.

**Correct Answer: (D)**
