### Front
How is the linear speed ($v$) of an object in uniform circular motion related to its angular velocity ($\omega$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Linear speed is the product of the radius and angular velocity.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = r\omega$
- When to use it: To convert between rotational motion ($\text{rad s}^{-1}$) and linear tangential motion ($\text{m s}^{-1}$) for an object at a fixed radius.
- Common trap: Forgetting to ensure units for radius (e.g., cm to m) and time are consistent with the required answer units.
