### Answer
The correct answer is **(C)**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we need to understand James Clerk Maxwell's contribution to electromagnetism, specifically the formulation of Maxwell's equations.

1.  **Context:** Before Maxwell, scientists like Gauss, Ampère, and Faraday had established various laws describing electricity and magnetism separately. Maxwell's genius was in unifying these and identifying a missing link.
2.  **The "Missing Link":** Maxwell realized that Ampère's Law (which states that an electric current produces a magnetic field) was incomplete. He hypothesized that a **varying (changing) electric field** must also produce a magnetic field. This is known as the **displacement current**.
3.  **Why (C) is the answer:** By adding this term to Ampère's Law, Maxwell created the symmetry needed to predict the existence of electromagnetic waves. His equations demonstrated that a changing electric field creates a magnetic field and a changing magnetic field creates an electric field, allowing waves to propagate through space at the speed of light.

**Why the others are incorrect/imprecise:**
*   **(A)** This describes Gauss's Law for Magnetism ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0$), which was a known property of magnetic fields established before Maxwell. While he used it, it is not the *key* addition he made to formulate his comprehensive theory.
*   **(B)** While true that moving charges (currents) create magnetic fields, this is essentially a restatement of existing laws (like the Biot-Savart Law) that Maxwell incorporated, rather than his revolutionary formulation.
*   **(D)** This statement is factually incorrect; electric field lines originate on positive charges and terminate on negative charges.
