### Front
How does the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation support the Big Bang model?

### Back
- Key idea: The CMB is the "afterglow" of the Big Bang, representing the thermal radiation left over from when the universe cooled enough for neutral atoms to form (recombination).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Black-body spectrum at ~2.7 K (isotropic).
- When to use it: To provide evidence for the hot, dense, early state of the universe.
- Common trap: Forgetting to mention it is "isotropic" (uniform in all directions) or failing to identify it as black-body radiation.
