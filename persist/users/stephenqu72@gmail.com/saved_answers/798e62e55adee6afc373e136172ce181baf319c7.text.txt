To solve this problem, we first identify the given values:
- Mass of the rocket, $m = 0.120\text{ kg}$
- Initial velocity, $u = 0\text{ m/s}$
- Final velocity, $v = 22\text{ m/s}$
- Time elapsed, $t = 1.9\text{ s}$
- Moon mass, $M = 7.35 \times 10^{22}\text{ kg}$
- Moon radius, $R = 1.74 \times 10^{6}\text{ m}$
- Gravitational constant, $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ Nm}^2\text{kg}^{-2}$

---

### (a) Calculate the resultant force
We use Newton's Second Law, $F = ma$, where $a = \frac{v - u}{t}$.
$$a = \frac{22 - 0}{1.9} \approx 11.58\text{ m/s}^2$$
$$F = 0.120 \times 11.58 = 1.39\text{ N}$$

### (b) Calculate the work done
Work done is equal to the change in kinetic energy: $W = \Delta EK = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.
$$W = \frac{1}{2} \times 0.120 \times (22)^2 = 0.06 \times 484 = 29.04\text{ J}$$

### (c) Calculate the height
Assuming constant acceleration, we use $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$:
$$s = 0 + \frac{1}{2} \times 11.58 \times (1.9)^2 = 0.5 \times 11.58 \times 3.61 = 20.9\text{ m}$$

### (d) Explain if the rocket escapes
To escape, the rocket's kinetic energy at $1.9\text{ s}$ must be greater than or equal to the gravitational potential energy required to move from the surface to infinity.
The escape velocity $v_e$ is given by $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$.
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 7.35 \times 10^{22}}{1.74 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{9.80 \times 10^{12}}{1.74 \times 10^6}} = \sqrt{5.63 \times 10^6} \approx 2373\text{ m/s}$$
Since the rocket's velocity ($22\text{ m/s}$) is significantly lower than the escape velocity ($2373\text{ m/s}$), it will not escape the Moon's gravitational field. It will eventually fall back to the surface.
