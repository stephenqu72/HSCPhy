To solve this problem, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, which is given by the formula:

$$F = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the initial state:**
    The initial force is $F = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$.

2.  **Determine the changes:**
    *   **Distance:** The distance is "decreased by one third". This means the new distance $r_1$ is $r - \frac{1}{3}r = \frac{2}{3}r$.
    *   **Mass:** The mass of one object is increased by a factor of 4. Let's say the new mass $m_1'$ is $4m_1$. The other mass $m_2$ remains unchanged.

3.  **Set up the equation for the new force ($F_1$):**
    Substitute the new values into the gravitational formula:
    $$F_1 = \frac{G (4m_1) m_2}{(\frac{2}{3}r)^2}$$

4.  **Simplify the expression:**
    $$F_1 = \frac{4 \cdot G m_1 m_2}{\frac{4}{9}r^2}$$
    To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal:
    $$F_1 = 4 \cdot \frac{9}{4} \cdot \left( \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)$$
    $$F_1 = 9 \cdot F$$

**Conclusion:**
Comparing our result to the given options, $F_1 = 9F$.
