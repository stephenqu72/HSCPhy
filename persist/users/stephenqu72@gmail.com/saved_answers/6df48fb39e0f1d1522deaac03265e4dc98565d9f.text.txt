The correct answer is **A. X-rays**.

### Explanation
To determine which form of electromagnetic radiation has the highest energy per photon, we refer to the fundamental relationship between the energy of a photon ($E$) and its frequency ($f$) or wavelength ($\lambda$):

1.  **The Formula:** The energy of a photon is given by the equation:
    $$E = hf$$
    where $h$ is Planck's constant. Since frequency and wavelength are related by $c = f\lambda$ (where $c$ is the speed of light), we can also express this as:
    $$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$

2.  **The Relationship:** From these equations, it is clear that energy is directly proportional to frequency ($E \propto f$) and inversely proportional to wavelength ($E \propto \frac{1}{\lambda}$). This means that radiation with the **highest frequency** and the **shortest wavelength** will have the highest energy per photon.

3.  **The Electromagnetic Spectrum:** If we compare the options provided:
    *   **Microwaves:** Longest wavelength, lowest frequency, lowest energy.
    *   **Infrared:** Longer wavelength than UV and X-rays, lower energy.
    *   **Ultraviolet:** Shorter wavelength than the above, but longer wavelength than X-rays.
    *   **X-rays:** Shortest wavelength, highest frequency, and therefore highest energy among the choices given.

By looking at the electromagnetic spectrum, X-rays occupy a position toward the high-frequency, short-wavelength end compared to the other options listed. Therefore, X-rays possess the highest energy per photon.
