To find the frequency of the magnetic flux variation, we first need to determine the period ($T$) of the wave from the provided graph.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the Period ($T$):** The period is the time taken for one complete cycle of the wave. Looking at the graph, the wave starts at its maximum value at $t = 0\text{ s}$. The next time it reaches its maximum value is at $t = 0.04\text{ s}$. Therefore, the period $T = 0.04\text{ s}$.
    *   *Alternatively*, you can measure the distance between two consecutive zero-crossings in the same direction or two consecutive troughs. From $0.02\text{ s}$ to $0.06\text{ s}$ (if extended) would also be $0.04\text{ s}$.

2.  **Use the Frequency Formula:** The relationship between frequency ($f$) and period ($T$) is given by:
    $$f = \frac{1}{T}$$

3.  **Calculate:**
    $$f = \frac{1}{0.04\text{ s}}$$
    $$f = 25\text{ Hz}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **D**.

**Answer:** D. $25\text{ Hz}$
