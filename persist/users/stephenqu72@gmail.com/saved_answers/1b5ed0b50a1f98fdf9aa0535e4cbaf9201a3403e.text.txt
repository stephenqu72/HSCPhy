### Answer
The diagram depicts a **nuclear fission chain reaction**. 

1.  **The Process:** A neutron strikes a large, unstable nucleus (such as Uranium-235), causing it to become excited and undergo fission. The nucleus splits into two smaller daughter nuclei, releasing a large amount of energy and several additional neutrons. These neutrons then strike other nearby nuclei, triggering further fission events, which creates the exponential chain reaction shown.
2.  **Source of Energy:** The energy produced originates from a phenomenon known as the **mass defect**. When the parent nucleus splits, the sum of the masses of the resulting daughter nuclei and the emitted neutrons is slightly less than the mass of the original nucleus and incident neutron. According to Einstein's mass-energy equivalence principle, $E = mc^2$, this "missing" mass is converted into a vast amount of kinetic and thermal energy.

### How to solve this
To achieve full marks (6 marks) for a question like this in an HSC exam, you must provide a comprehensive explanation that links the visual evidence to nuclear physics principles:

*   **Identify the reaction (2 marks):** Explicitly name the process as a "fission chain reaction." Point out that it is self-sustaining because the neutrons released from one event trigger the next.
*   **Explain the mechanism (2 marks):** Briefly describe the physical steps: the absorption of a neutron, the instability of the heavy nucleus, the splitting into smaller fragments, and the release of additional neutrons.
*   **Identify the source of energy (2 marks):** You must mention the **mass defect**. It is crucial to state that mass is "lost" during the reaction and converted into energy using the equation $E = mc^2$. Mentioning that binding energy per nucleon is higher for the daughter nuclei compared to the parent nucleus (making the products more stable) adds depth and ensures full credit.
