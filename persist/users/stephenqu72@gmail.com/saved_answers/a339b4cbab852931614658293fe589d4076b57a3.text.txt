To determine the correct relationship between the launch angle ($\theta$) and the time of flight ($t$) for a projectile on level ground, we look at the standard kinematic equation for time of flight:

$$t = \frac{2v_0 \sin(\theta)}{g}$$

Where:
- $v_0$ is the initial launch velocity.
- $\theta$ is the launch angle with respect to the horizontal.
- $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity.

### Analysis:
1. **At $\theta = 0^\circ$**: The sine of $0^\circ$ is $0$, so the time of flight is $0$. This means the graph must start at the origin $(0,0)$.
2. **As $\theta$ increases**: The function $\sin(\theta)$ starts at $0$ and increases. However, the slope of the $\sin(\theta)$ curve is steep near $0^\circ$ and becomes less steep as it approaches $90^\circ$ (the derivative of $\sin(\theta)$ is $\cos(\theta)$, which decreases from $1$ to $0$ as $\theta$ increases from $0^\circ$ to $90^\circ$).
3. **Graph shape**: The curve should start at the origin, increase with a decreasing gradient (concave down), and reach its maximum value at $\theta = 90^\circ$.

Comparing this to the provided options:
- **(A)** represents a linear relationship, which is incorrect.
- **(B)** shows a curve starting at the origin and increasing with a decreasing gradient (concave down), which matches the mathematical behavior of the sine function.
- **(C)** shows an increasing gradient (concave up), which is incorrect.
- **(D)** shows a decreasing relationship, which is incorrect.

Therefore, the correct graph is **(B)**.
