### Front
How does the maximum speed of a car on a flat, unbanked curve depend on the forces present?

### Back
- Key idea: Centripetal force is provided entirely by static friction ($F_f = \frac{mv^2}{r}$).
- Formula: $v = \sqrt{\frac{\mu F_N r}{m}}$ or $v = \sqrt{\mu gr}$.
- When to use it: For any flat, horizontal circular motion where friction is the sole inward force.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the normal force ($F_N$) equals $mg$ only on a flat road; it changes on banked tracks.
