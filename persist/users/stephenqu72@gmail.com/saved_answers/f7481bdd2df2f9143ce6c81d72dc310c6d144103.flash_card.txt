### Front
How does acceleration change for a ball rolling down a curved ramp onto a table, and what is its acceleration in the air?

### Back
- **Key idea:** On the ramp, acceleration $a = g \sin\theta$ decreases as $\theta$ decreases (flattening curve). In the air, acceleration is constant ($g$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $a = g \sin\theta$ (ramp); $a = g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ (projectile).
- **When to use it:** When analyzing motion involving transitions from curved surfaces to free-fall projectile motion.
- **Common trap:** Assuming acceleration is constant while on the ramp; it only becomes constant once the projectile is in free-fall.
