### Front
How does Hubble’s Law provide evidence for the Big Bang Theory, and how is recessional velocity measured?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Hubble’s Law shows a linear relationship between a galaxy's distance and its recessional velocity, implying the universe is expanding.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $z = \frac{\Delta \lambda}{\lambda} \approx \frac{v}{c}$ (Redshift formula).
- **When to use it:** When asked for evidence of the expanding universe or calculating the velocity of a distant object based on observed spectral shifts.
- **Common trap:** Confusing redshift with simple light absorption; remember it is a **Doppler effect** caused by the stretching of space/motion, not a change in the light source's intrinsic color.
