To solve this problem, we use the principles of uniform circular motion. The centripetal force required to keep an object of mass $m$ moving in a circle of radius $r$ with angular velocity $\omega$ is given by the formula:
$$F_c = m r \omega^2$$

### (a) Calculate the magnitude of angular velocity of the disc.

**Step 1: Identify the variables for Object A.**
*   Mass ($m_A$) = $2\text{ kg}$
*   Radius ($r_A$) = $0.2\text{ m}$
*   Centripetal force ($F_A$) = $160\text{ N}$

**Step 2: Rearrange the formula to solve for $\omega$.**
$$160 = 2 \times 0.2 \times \omega^2$$
$$160 = 0.4 \times \omega^2$$
$$\omega^2 = \frac{160}{0.4} = 400$$
$$\omega = \sqrt{400} = 20\text{ rad/s}$$

**Answer:** The angular velocity of the disc is $20\text{ rad/s}$.

### (b) Calculate the magnitude of the centripetal force required to keep object B remaining in uniform circular motion.

**Step 1: Identify the variables for Object B.**
*   Mass ($m_B$) = $5\text{ kg}$
*   Angular velocity ($\omega$) = $20\text{ rad/s}$ (same as the disc)
*   Radius ($r_B$) = $0.2\text{ m} + 1.3\text{ m} = 1.5\text{ m}$ (the distance from the centre)

**Step 2: Calculate the centripetal force using $F_c = m r \omega^2$.**
$$F_B = 5 \times 1.5 \times (20)^2$$
$$F_B = 7.5 \times 400$$
$$F_B = 3000\text{ N}$$

**Answer:** The centripetal force required for object B is $3000\text{ N}$.
