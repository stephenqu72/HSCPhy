To determine the correct graph, we must analyze the physics relationship between the force on parallel conductors and the distance separating them.

### Analysis and Explanation

1.  **The Formula:** The magnitude of the force per unit length $F/L$ between two long parallel wires carrying currents $I_1$ and $I_2$ separated by a distance $d$ is given by the formula:
    $$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi d}$$
    Since the currents $I_1$ and $I_2$ are constant, we can say that the force $F$ is inversely proportional to the distance $d$:
    $$F \propto \frac{1}{d}$$

2.  **The Change Over Time:** The problem states that the distance $d$ is decreasing at a steady rate. We can express this as:
    $$d(t) = d_0 - vt$$
    where $d_0$ is the initial distance and $v$ is the constant speed at which they approach each other.

3.  **Substituting the variables:** Substituting the expression for $d(t)$ into our proportionality formula, we get:
    $$F(t) \propto \frac{1}{d_0 - vt}$$

4.  **Interpreting the Graph:** 
    *   As $t$ increases, the denominator $(d_0 - vt)$ decreases.
    *   As the denominator decreases, the value of the entire fraction ($F$) increases.
    *   Crucially, because the distance is in the denominator, the relationship is non-linear (it is a reciprocal function). As the wires get closer ($d \to 0$), the force approaches infinity. The slope of the graph must therefore increase as time progresses.

Looking at the options:
*   (A) shows an increasing value but a decreasing gradient.
*   (B) shows a linear relationship, which is incorrect.
*   (C) shows an increasing value with an **increasing gradient**, which matches the behavior of a reciprocal function $1/x$ as $x$ approaches 0.
*   (D) shows a constant force, which is incorrect.

Therefore, the correct answer is **(C)**.
