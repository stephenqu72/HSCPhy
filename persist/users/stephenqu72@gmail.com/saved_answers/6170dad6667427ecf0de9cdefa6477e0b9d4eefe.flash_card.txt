### Front
Who performed the first quantitative measurement of the speed of light by observing the eclipses of Jupiter’s moon, Io?

### Back
- Key idea: Ole Romer used the varying orbital distances between Earth and Jupiter to prove light has a finite speed.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When identifying historical experiments regarding the finite speed of light in the solar system.
- Common trap: Confusing Romer (astronomical/orbital) with Foucault or Fizeau (terrestrial/rotating devices).
