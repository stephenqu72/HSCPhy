### Front
In Rømer’s method for measuring the speed of light, why does the observed orbital period of Jupiter’s moon, Io, vary throughout Earth’s orbit?

### Back
- Key idea: The time taken for light to traverse the changing distance between Earth and Jupiter causes a "lag" or "lead" in observed eclipse timings.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \frac{\Delta d}{\Delta t}$ (Speed of light constant).
- When to use it: To explain why orbital periods appear longer when Earth moves away from the planet and shorter when moving toward it.
- Common trap: Confusing the *actual* orbital period of the moon (which remains constant) with the *observed* interval (which changes due to Earth's motion).
