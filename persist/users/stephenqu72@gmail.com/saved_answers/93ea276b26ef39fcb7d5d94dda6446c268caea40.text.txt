### Answer
The correct answer is **A) Galactic Red Shift**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we need to recall the specific contributions of Edwin Hubble to our understanding of the universe:

1.  **Hubble's Observation:** By analyzing the spectra of distant galaxies, Hubble observed that the light from almost all galaxies was shifted toward the red end of the electromagnetic spectrum. This phenomenon is known as **Galactic Red Shift**.
2.  **The Interpretation:** The red shift indicates that these galaxies are moving away from us. Because he observed that the more distant a galaxy is, the faster it is receding (Hubble's Law), he concluded that the universe itself is expanding. This expansion is a cornerstone of the Big Bang Theory, as it implies that if we rewind time, the universe must have originated from a single, dense, hot point.
3.  **Why the other options are incorrect:**
    *   **B) Galactic Blue Shift:** This would indicate that galaxies are moving *toward* us, which contradicts Hubble's observations of the expanding universe.
    *   **C) Abundance of light elements:** While this is major evidence for the Big Bang, it was primarily predicted and confirmed through nuclear physics and observations by other scientists (like Alpher and Herman), not by Hubble’s optical observations.
    *   **D) Microwave Background Radiation:** This was discovered by Penzias and Wilson in 1964, long after Hubble's primary work on expansion, and it provides a different line of evidence (the "afterglow" of the Big Bang).

**Strategy Tip:** When studying Cosmology for the HSC, ensure you can distinguish between the different pieces of evidence for the Big Bang. Hubble is the key figure for **expansion/redshift**, whereas Penzias and Wilson are the key figures for **Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation**.
