To answer this question, we must identify the "fundamental particles" that make up an atom of Helium ($He$).

### Answer:
The correct answer is **(A) 6**.

### Explanation:
1.  **Identify the components:** An atom is composed of three primary subatomic particles: **protons**, **neutrons**, and **electrons**.
2.  **Determine the composition of a standard Helium-4 atom:**
    *   **Protons:** Helium has an atomic number of 2, meaning it contains **2 protons**.
    *   **Neutrons:** The most common isotope of Helium is Helium-4, which has a mass number of 4. Since the mass number is the sum of protons and neutrons ($4 - 2 = 2$), it contains **2 neutrons**.
    *   **Electrons:** In a neutral atom, the number of electrons equals the number of protons. Therefore, it contains **2 electrons**.
3.  **Sum the particles:**
    *   Total fundamental particles = (Number of protons) + (Number of neutrons) + (Number of electrons)
    *   Total = $2 + 2 + 2 = 6$

By counting the particles identified in the model provided (2 electrons in orbit and 4 particles in the nucleus), we arrive at a total of 6.
