### Front
A charge $q$ is accelerated from rest through a potential difference $V$. How does its final velocity $v$ relate to $V$?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Energy conservation (Electrical Potential Energy = Kinetic Energy).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$; therefore, $v \propto \sqrt{V}$.
- **When to use it:** To find the final speed of an electron or ion accelerated by an electric field.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to square root the ratio of the potential differences when calculating a change in velocity.
