### Front
Derive the expression for the tension ($F_T$) in a string holding a mass $m$ undergoing vertical circular motion of radius $r$ and velocity $v$ at the top and bottom of its path.

### Back
- Key idea: Net centripetal force ($\sum F_c$) equals $\frac{mv^2}{r}$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: 
  - Top: $F_T = \frac{mv^2}{r} - mg$
  - Bottom: $F_T = \frac{mv^2}{r} + mg$
- When to use it: Problems involving vertical circular motion where gravity acts parallel to the centripetal force.
- Common trap: Forgetting that gravity assists the centripetal force at the top but opposes it at the bottom.
