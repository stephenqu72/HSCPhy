### Front
How do horizontal and vertical velocity components behave in projectile motion (neglecting air resistance)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Horizontal velocity is constant; vertical velocity changes due to constant downward acceleration ($g$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Newton’s First Law ($a_x = 0$); Vertical $v_y$ changes by $-9.8 \, \text{m/s}$ every second.
- **When to use it:** To analyze velocity vector diagrams at different points of a trajectory.
- **Common trap:** Assuming horizontal velocity changes or that vertical velocity is zero at the start of a ground-launched projectile.
