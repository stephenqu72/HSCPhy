### Front
How do you derive the orbital speed $v$ for two equal masses $m$ rotating around a common centre at a radius $r$ if their gravitational attraction provides the required centripetal force?

### Back
- Key idea: Equate centripetal force ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$) with the gravitational force ($F_g = \frac{Gm_1m_2}{d^2}$).
- Formula: $v = \sqrt{\frac{Gm}{4r}}$ (where $d = 2r$ is the separation distance).
- When to use it: Circular motion problems where gravity is the sole provider of centripetal force.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the separation distance $d$ in the denominator of Newton's Law is $2r$, not $r$.
