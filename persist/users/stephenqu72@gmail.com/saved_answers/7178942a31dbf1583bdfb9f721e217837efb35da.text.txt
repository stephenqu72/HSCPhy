### Solution and Explanation

To solve projectile motion problems, we must always remember that horizontal velocity remains constant throughout the flight (ignoring air resistance), while vertical velocity changes due to gravity ($g = 9.8 \text{ ms}^{-2}$).

#### Part (a)
At the maximum height of a projectile's path, the vertical component of velocity is zero. Therefore, the speed at maximum height is entirely equal to the horizontal velocity component ($v_x$).
Since $v_x$ is constant throughout the motion:
$$v_x = v \cos(\theta) = 20 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$
Where $v$ is the initial launch speed and $\theta = 8^\circ$.
$$v = \frac{20}{\cos(8^\circ)} \approx \frac{20}{0.9903} \approx 20.197 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$
Rounding to significant figures (or 2 decimal places), the speed is **$20.20 \text{ ms}^{-1}$**.

#### Part (b)
First, find the initial vertical velocity ($v_y$):
$$v_y = v \sin(\theta) = 20.197 \times \sin(8^\circ) \approx 2.809 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$
To find the horizontal distance, we need the total time of flight. We use the vertical displacement formula $s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$, where $s_y = -3.0 \text{ m}$ (downward from the launch point):
$$-3.0 = 2.809t - 4.9t^2$$
$$4.9t^2 - 2.809t - 3.0 = 0$$
Using the quadratic formula $t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$$t = \frac{2.809 + \sqrt{(-2.809)^2 - 4(4.9)(-3.0)}}{9.8}$$
$$t = \frac{2.809 + \sqrt{7.89 + 58.8}}{9.8} \approx \frac{2.809 + 8.167}{9.8} \approx 1.12 \text{ seconds}$$
Now, find the horizontal distance ($R$):
$$R = v_x \times t = 20 \times 1.12 = 22.4 \text{ m}$$
The ball will hit the ground at a horizontal distance of **$22.4 \text{ m}$**.
