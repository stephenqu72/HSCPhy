To find the magnitude of the induced electromotive force (emf), we use Faraday's Law of Induction:
$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$$
where $\Phi = BA \cos(\theta)$.

### 1. Identify the given information:
*   Number of turns, $N = 100$
*   Radius of the coil, $r = 2.0\text{ cm} = 0.02\text{ m}$
*   Area of the coil, $A = \pi r^2 = \pi(0.02)^2 \approx 1.2566 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}^2$
*   The angle given is between the magnetic field and the **plane of the coil** ($60^\circ$). The angle $\theta$ in the flux formula must be the angle between the magnetic field and the **normal** to the coil. Therefore, $\theta = 90^\circ - 60^\circ = 30^\circ$.
*   From the graph, the rate of change of the magnetic field ($\frac{\Delta B}{\Delta t}$) is the gradient:
    $$\frac{\Delta B}{\Delta t} = \frac{0.5 - 0.0}{0.10 - 0.0} = 5.0\text{ T/s}$$

### 2. Set up the calculation:
Substituting the expression for flux into Faraday's law:
$$\varepsilon = -N A \cos(\theta) \frac{\Delta B}{\Delta t}$$

Now, plug in the values:
$$\varepsilon = -(100) \times (1.2566 \times 10^{-3}) \times \cos(30^\circ) \times 5.0$$
$$\varepsilon = -(100) \times (1.2566 \times 10^{-3}) \times 0.866 \times 5.0$$
$$\varepsilon \approx -0.544\text{ V}$$

The magnitude is approximately $0.54\text{ V}$. Looking at the options provided, (B) $-0.54\text{ V}$ is the correct answer.

### Summary of steps:
1.  **Calculate the area** of the circular loop.
2.  **Determine the effective angle**: Ensure you use the complement of the angle given because the formula requires the angle with the normal.
3.  **Find the rate of change** of the magnetic field from the slope of the provided graph.
4.  **Apply Faraday's Law** to compute the induced emf.
