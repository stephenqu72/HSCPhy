### Front
How do particle accelerators validate the Standard Model, and what is the underlying physical principle?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Accelerators recreate early-universe conditions by converting kinetic energy into mass to detect high-energy particles (e.g., Higgs Boson).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $E=mc^2$ (Mass-energy equivalence).
- **When to use it:** When asked to "assess" or explain the role of accelerators in particle physics or the early universe.
- **Common trap:** Failing to explicitly link the high energy of the accelerator to the requirement of producing massive, short-lived particles; or omitting the specific example (Higgs Boson).
