To solve these types of torque problems in HSC Physics, remember that **torque** ($\tau$) is defined as the product of the force and the perpendicular distance from the pivot point: $\tau = F \times d$. By convention, counter-clockwise torque is positive and clockwise torque is negative. Equilibrium occurs when the sum of torques is zero ($\sum \tau = 0$).

### (a) Diagram
Draw a horizontal line representing the plywood board. Place a triangle under the center as the pivot. Place $m_A = 23\text{ kg}$ at $d_A = 0.25\text{ m}$ to the left and $m_B = 12\text{ kg}$ at $d_B = 0.123\text{ m}$ to the right. Label the forces acting downwards as $F_A = m_A g$ and $F_B = m_B g$.

### (b) Calculation of Net Torque
1. **Assumptions**: Assume $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$ and the board is perfectly horizontal.
2. **Convert units**: $d_A = 0.25\text{ m}$, $d_B = 0.123\text{ m}$.
3. **Torque A (Counter-clockwise)**: $\tau_A = (23 \times 9.8) \times 0.25 = 56.35\text{ Nm}$.
4. **Torque B (Clockwise)**: $\tau_B = (12 \times 9.8) \times 0.123 = 14.47\text{ Nm}$.
5. **Net Torque**: $\tau_{net} = \tau_A - \tau_B = 56.35 - 14.47 = 41.88\text{ Nm}$ (counter-clockwise).

### (c) Ways to balance
To balance, we need $\tau_{net} = 0$.
1. **Increase distance**: Move Mass B further to the right. Since $\tau_A$ is $56.35\text{ Nm}$, the new distance $d_B$ would be $d_B = 56.35 / (12 \times 9.8) \approx 0.479\text{ m}$.
2. **Increase mass**: Keep the position the same and add more mass to side B. The required mass $m_B$ would be $m_B = 56.35 / (9.8 \times 0.123) \approx 46.74\text{ kg}$.

### (d) Adding Mass C
For equilibrium, the total clockwise torque must equal the counter-clockwise torque.
1. The system has a net CCW torque of $41.88\text{ Nm}$ from part (b). To balance, Mass C ($6\text{ kg}$) must be placed on the **right side** to provide clockwise torque.
2. Let $d_C$ be the distance of Mass C from the pivot:
   $$F_C \times d_C = 41.88$$
   $$(6 \times 9.8) \times d_C = 41.88$$
   $$58.8 \times d_C = 41.88$$
   $$d_C = 41.88 / 58.8 \approx 0.712\text{ m}$$
Mass C must be placed $0.712\text{ m}$ (or $71.2\text{ cm}$) to the right of the pivot.
