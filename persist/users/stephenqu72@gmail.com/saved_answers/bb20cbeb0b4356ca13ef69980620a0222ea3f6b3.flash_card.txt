### Front
When can the gravitational force between two masses be used to derive the orbital velocity of a circular path?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational force ($F_g$) provides the necessary centripetal force ($F_c$) for circular motion.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$ (where $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2/\text{kg}^2$)
- When to use it: To calculate orbital speed or radius for objects in stable circular motion under gravity.
- Common trap: Assuming this effect is observable with laboratory-scale masses; the gravitational force is usually far too weak to overcome friction or air resistance.
