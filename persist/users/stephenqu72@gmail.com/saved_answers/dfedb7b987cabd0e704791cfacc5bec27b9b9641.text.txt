### **Explanation**

To solve this question, we must apply our knowledge of the fundamental characteristics of electromagnetic (EM) waves:

1.  **Nature of Fields:** An electromagnetic wave consists of two distinct fields—an electric field ($E$) and a magnetic field ($B$). 
2.  **Oscillation:** These fields are not "constant" (ruling out option C); they oscillate in time and space as the wave propagates.
3.  **Orientation:** A defining characteristic of an EM wave is that the electric field and the magnetic field are **perpendicular** to each other ($E \perp B$). Furthermore, both of these fields are perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation.
4.  **Comparison of Options:**
    *   **A is correct:** It accurately identifies that the fields are oscillating and perpendicular.
    *   **B is incorrect:** While an accelerating charge is the *source* of an EM wave, the wave itself is a self-propagating oscillation of fields, not "accelerating fields."
    *   **C is incorrect:** The fields must be oscillating, not constant.
    *   **D is incorrect:** The fields are perpendicular, not parallel.

In HSC Physics, you should visualize this as the electric field oscillating in one plane (e.g., the vertical plane) and the magnetic field oscillating in a plane at $90^\circ$ to it (e.g., the horizontal plane), both traveling through space at the speed of light, $c$.
