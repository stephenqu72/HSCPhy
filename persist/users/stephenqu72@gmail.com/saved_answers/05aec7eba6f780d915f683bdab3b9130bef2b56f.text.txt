To determine the direction of the deflection of the electron beam, we use the **Right-Hand Rule for Magnetic Force** (often referred to as the "Right-Hand Palm Rule" or the "Left-Hand Rule" for negative charges).

### The Answer
The correct answer is **(D) Upwards**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we apply the magnetic force formula $F = qvB \sin\theta$, where the direction is determined by the cross product. Specifically, for a charged particle, we use the following steps:

1.  **Identify the Magnetic Field ($B$):** The magnetic field lines travel from the North pole ($N$) to the South pole ($S$). In this diagram, the field points from left to right.
2.  **Identify the Velocity ($v$):** The electron beam is moving away from the viewer (into the page or towards the back of the gap between the poles).
3.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule:** 
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field (to the right, towards the $S$ pole).
    *   Point your thumb in the direction of the **conventional current**. Since electrons are negatively charged, conventional current flows in the *opposite* direction of the electrons. If the electrons are moving into the page, the conventional current is effectively moving out of the page (towards you).
    *   Your palm (or the force vector) will face **upwards**.
4.  **Alternative (Left-Hand Rule):** If you use the Left-Hand Rule specifically for negative charges:
    *   Point your index finger in the direction of the magnetic field (right).
    *   Point your middle finger in the direction of the electron velocity (into the page).
    *   Your thumb will point **upwards**, indicating the direction of the magnetic force.

Because the force acting on the electrons is directed upwards, the beam will be deflected upwards.
