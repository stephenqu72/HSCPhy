To determine which bicycle wheel has the largest torque acting on it, we use the formula for torque ($\tau$):

$$\tau = r F \sin(\theta)$$

where:
*   $r$ is the distance from the pivot point to the point where the force is applied.
*   $F$ is the magnitude of the applied force.
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the force vector and the radial vector.
*   Equivalently, torque can be expressed as $\tau = r_{\perp} F$, where $r_{\perp}$ is the "lever arm" (the perpendicular distance from the pivot to the line of action of the force).

### Analysis of the Options:

*   **Option A:** The force $F$ is applied at distance $r$ and is perpendicular to the radius. The lever arm is the full radius $r$. 
    $$\tau_A = r \times F = rF$$
*   **Option B:** The force $0.5F$ is applied at distance $r$ and is perpendicular to the radius. 
    $$\tau_B = r \times 0.5F = 0.5rF$$
*   **Option C:** The force $F$ is applied at a distance closer to the center than the rim. Because the lever arm ($r_{\perp}$) is less than $r$, the torque is less than $rF$.
    $$\tau_C < rF$$
*   **Option D:** The force $F$ is applied at distance $r$, but at an angle. The effective lever arm is $r \sin(\theta)$, where $\theta < 90^{\circ}$. Therefore, $\sin(\theta) < 1$.
    $$\tau_D = r F \sin(\theta) < rF$$

**Conclusion:**
Comparing the results, Option A provides the maximum product of the applied force and its perpendicular distance from the pivot point.

**Answer:** A
