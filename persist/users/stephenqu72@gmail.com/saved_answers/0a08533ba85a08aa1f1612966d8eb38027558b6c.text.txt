To "assess" in an HSC Physics context means to make a judgment of value, quality, outcomes, or results. You must provide a balanced argument that weighs the significance of Einstein’s work against the pre-existing scientific understanding.

### Answer
Before Einstein, light was almost exclusively understood through Maxwell’s electromagnetic wave theory, which successfully explained interference, diffraction, and polarization. However, this classical model failed to explain the photoelectric effect, where the kinetic energy of emitted electrons depended on the frequency of light rather than its intensity.

Einstein’s 1905 paper proposed that light consists of discrete "quanta" (later called photons), with energy $E = hf$. This was a profound conceptual shift:
1. **Resolution of the Photoelectric Effect:** Einstein showed that light interacts with matter as particles. If the frequency $f$ of incident light is below the threshold frequency $f_0$ of the metal, no electrons are ejected, regardless of intensity. This overturned the classical wave prediction.
2. **Quantization:** By introducing the photon, Einstein provided the foundational basis for quantum mechanics. This work earned him the Nobel Prize and forced the scientific community to accept the **wave-particle duality** of light.
3. **Impact:** The work moved physics away from purely continuous classical models toward a probabilistic, quantum model. It allowed for the development of modern technologies such as solar cells, CCD sensors, and lasers. 

In conclusion, Einstein’s work was the catalyst that reconciled the apparent contradictions between wave and particle behavior. It did not invalidate Maxwell’s wave theory, but rather complemented it, proving that light exhibits properties of both, depending on the context of the observation.
