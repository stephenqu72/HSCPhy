To find the magnitude of the induced current in the rod, we follow a two-step physics process: calculating the induced electromotive force (EMF) and then applying Ohm's Law.

### 1. Identify the given values
*   Magnetic field strength ($B$): $0.33\text{ T}$
*   Length of the rod within the magnetic field ($l$): $30\text{ cm} = 0.30\text{ m}$ (Note: Use the distance between the rails, as that defines the loop's width).
*   Velocity ($v$): $6.2\text{ m/s}$
*   Resistance ($R$): $9.0\text{ }\Omega$

### 2. Calculate the Induced EMF ($\varepsilon$)
The motional EMF induced in a conductor moving through a magnetic field is given by the formula:
$$\varepsilon = B \cdot l \cdot v$$

Substituting the values:
$$\varepsilon = 0.33 \times 0.30 \times 6.2$$
$$\varepsilon = 0.6138\text{ V}$$

### 3. Calculate the Induced Current ($I$)
Using Ohm's Law, $I = \frac{V}{R}$ (where $V = \varepsilon$):
$$I = \frac{0.6138}{9.0}$$
$$I = 0.0682\text{ A}$$

### 4. Convert to milliamperes (mA)
To convert Amperes to milliamperes, multiply by $1000$:
$$0.0682\text{ A} \times 1000 = 68.2\text{ mA}$$

Rounding to the nearest option provided, we get **$68\text{ mA}$**.
