#### Part (b)
**Calculation:**
To find the orbital period, we use Kepler's Third Law: $\frac{T^2}{r^3} = k$. For a planet orbiting a star, this is $\frac{T^2}{r^3} = \frac{1}{M_{star}}$.
*Note:* The mass of C-187 is not explicitly given, but in standard HSC Physics questions of this type, if mass isn't provided, we often assume the mass of the host star is approximately solar mass ($1 M_{sun}$) for simplicity, or we check if the orbit is defined relative to the Earth-Sun system. 
Using $T^2 \propto r^3$:
$$T_{planet}^2 = r^3$$
$$T = \sqrt{(0.125)^3} = \sqrt{0.001953125} \approx 0.04419 \text{ years}$$
To convert to Earth days:
$$0.04419 \times 365.25 \approx 16.14 \text{ days}$$
Rounding to the nearest day, the year is **16 days**.
