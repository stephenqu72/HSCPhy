### Front
Why is a planet's orbital speed independent of its own mass, and how do you calculate it?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The centripetal force required for orbit is provided by the gravitational force; the planet's mass ($m$) cancels out when equating the two.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$
- **When to use it:** When determining the velocity of an object in a circular orbit around a central mass ($M$).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that $M$ refers to the **mass of the central body**, not the orbiting satellite.
