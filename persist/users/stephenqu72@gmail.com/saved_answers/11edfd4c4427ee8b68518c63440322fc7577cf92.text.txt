### Part (a)
**Answer:**
Rutherford concluded that the atom consists of a tiny, dense, positively charged nucleus surrounded by mostly empty space. 
1. **Most alpha particles passed straight through the gold foil:** This indicated that the atom is composed mostly of empty space.
2. **A small fraction of alpha particles were deflected at large angles:** This suggested that they were repelled by a concentrated, positively charged mass (the nucleus) at the center of the atom.
3. **Very few particles bounced straight back:** This confirmed the nucleus is extremely dense and contains almost all of the atom's mass, as only a direct collision with a massive body could cause such a reflection.

**How to solve:** "Justify" requires you to link the *observations* of the Geiger-Marsden experiment to the *conclusions* Rutherford drew. You must explain the "why"—don't just state the model; explain how the behavior of the alpha particles forced him to abandon the previous model.

### Part (c)
**Answer:**
The main problem with the Rutherford model is that, according to classical electromagnetic theory, an accelerating charged particle (the electron) should continuously emit electromagnetic radiation. As the electron loses energy by radiating, it would spiral into the nucleus, causing the atom to be unstable and collapse almost instantaneously. This contradicted the observation that atoms are stable.

**How to solve:** "Outline the main problem" requires you to identify the conflict between the model and classical physics. Focus on two key points: 
1. Classical physics predicts electrons (accelerating charges) lose energy via radiation.
2. This leads to the collapse of the atom, which is experimentally untrue (atoms are stable).
