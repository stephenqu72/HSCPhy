To find the net force on the middle wire, we use the formula for the force per unit length between two parallel wires:
$$F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 L}{2\pi r}$$

Given:
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \, \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A}$
- Length of wires $L = 0.2 \, \text{m}$
- Middle wire current $I_2 = 3 \, \text{A}$

### 1. Force from the left wire ($I_1 = 2 \, \text{A}$, $r = 0.02 \, \text{m}$)
Since both currents are flowing in the same direction (into the page, denoted by 'X'), they attract each other.
$$F_1 = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(2)(3)(0.2)}{2\pi(0.02)} = \frac{(2 \times 10^{-7})(6)(0.2)}{0.02} = 1.2 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{N}$$
Since they attract, the middle wire is pulled **to the left**.

### 2. Force from the right wire ($I_3 = 4 \, \text{A}$, $r = 0.03 \, \text{m}$)
The middle wire current ($3 \, \text{A}$) and the right wire current ($4 \, \text{A}$) are both flowing into the page, so they also attract each other.
$$F_3 = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(3)(4)(0.2)}{2\pi(0.03)} = \frac{(2 \times 10^{-7})(12)(0.2)}{0.03} = 1.6 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{N}$$
Since they attract, the middle wire is pulled **to the right**.

### 3. Net Force
Calculate the resultant force:
$$F_{\text{net}} = F_3 - F_1 = 1.6 \times 10^{-5} - 1.2 \times 10^{-5} = 0.4 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{N}$$
Because the force to the right ($1.6 \times 10^{-5}$) is greater than the force to the left ($1.2 \times 10^{-5}$), the net force is **$0.4 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{N}$ to the right**.

The correct option is **(C)**.
