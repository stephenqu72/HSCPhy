### Front
In ideal projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), how does the projectile's speed change over time, and why?

### Back
- Key idea: Speed is the magnitude of the velocity vector; while horizontal velocity ($v_x$) is constant, vertical velocity ($v_y$) changes due to constant acceleration ($g$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$ and $a_y = g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$.
- When to use it: To determine velocity or speed at any point in a projectile's flight path.
- Common trap: Assuming speed is constant because $v_x$ is constant, or assuming speed is zero at the peak (only $v_y$ is zero).
