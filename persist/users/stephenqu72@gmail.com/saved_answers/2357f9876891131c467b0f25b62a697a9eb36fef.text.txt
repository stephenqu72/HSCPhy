**Answer:**
The spacing between adjacent bright bands will **increase**.

**Explanation:**
To solve this, we use the formula for double-slit interference (or the spacing between fringes in diffraction patterns):
$$\Delta x = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$
Where:
*   $\Delta x$ is the fringe spacing (distance between adjacent bright bands).
*   $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the light source.
*   $L$ is the distance from the slits to the screen.
*   $d$ is the distance between the slits.

Since $L$ and $d$ remain constant in this experiment, the fringe spacing $\Delta x$ is directly proportional to the wavelength $\lambda$ ($\Delta x \propto \lambda$). 

The wavelength of red light is significantly longer than the wavelength of green light. Because the wavelength has increased, the spacing $\Delta x$ between the adjacent bright bands must also increase.
