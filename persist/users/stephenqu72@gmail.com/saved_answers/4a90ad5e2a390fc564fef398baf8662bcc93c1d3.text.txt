### Explanation

#### Question 14
To solve this, we must identify the physical phenomena occurring in Young's Double Slit Experiment (YDSE):
1.  **Diffraction:** As the monochromatic light passes through the narrow slits, the waves spread out. This spreading of waves as they encounter an aperture is known as diffraction. Without this, the light would simply pass through as two straight beams.
2.  **Interference:** Once the light has diffracted from the two slits, the two wavefronts overlap. Where crest meets crest (or trough meets trough), **constructive interference** creates a bright fringe. Where crest meets trough, **destructive interference** creates a dark fringe. 
The resulting pattern of bright and dark fringes on the screen is the definitive evidence of the wave nature of light, caused by the combination of these two properties.

#### Question 15
To solve this, we must look at the historical context of physics in 1801:
*   Before 1801, the **corpuscular model** (championed by Isaac Newton) proposed that light consisted of streams of particles (corpuscles). 
*   Young's experiment demonstrated a phenomenon (interference) that could *only* be explained by wave mechanics. 
*   Because the corpuscular model could not account for interference patterns, it was largely discarded in favor of a **mechanical wave model** (the idea that light required a medium, the "luminiferous ether," to propagate). 
*   Options (B), (C), and (D) are incorrect because the electromagnetic wave model and the quantum model were developments that occurred much later (Maxwell in the 1860s and Einstein/Planck in the early 1900s, respectively).
