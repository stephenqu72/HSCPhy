To solve this projectile motion problem, we break the initial velocity vector into horizontal ($v_x$) and vertical ($v_y$) components.
Given: $v = 64\text{ m s}^{-1}$, $\theta = 7.00^\circ$ (downwards), $d = 11.9\text{ m}$.
Components:
$v_x = v \cos(7.00^\circ) = 64 \times 0.9925 = 63.52\text{ m s}^{-1}$
$v_y = -v \sin(7.00^\circ) = -64 \times 0.1219 = -7.80\text{ m s}^{-1}$ (negative because it is directed downwards).

---

### (a) Calculate the time it takes the tennis ball to reach the net.
The horizontal velocity remains constant as there is no air resistance.
$$t = \frac{d}{v_x} = \frac{11.9}{63.52} \approx 0.187\text{ s}$$

### (b) Show that the tennis ball passes over the net.
We need to find the vertical displacement $y$ at $t = 0.187\text{ s}$ using the equation of motion $y = v_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$, where $a_y = -9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$.
$$y = (-7.80)(0.187) + \frac{1}{2}(-9.8)(0.187)^2$$
$$y = -1.4586 - 0.1714 = -1.63\text{ m}$$
The height of the ball above the ground is the initial height minus the vertical displacement:
$$h = h_0 - 1.63 = 2.80 - 1.63 = 1.17\text{ m}$$
Since $1.17\text{ m} > 0.910\text{ m}$, the ball passes over the net.

### (c) Determine the velocity of the tennis ball as it strikes the ground.
First, find the time to hit the ground ($y = -2.80\text{ m}$):
$$-2.80 = -7.80t - 4.9t^2 \implies 4.9t^2 + 7.80t - 2.80 = 0$$
Using the quadratic formula $t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$$t = \frac{-7.80 + \sqrt{7.80^2 - 4(4.9)(-2.80)}}{9.8} \approx 0.298\text{ s}$$
Now, find the final vertical velocity $v_f$:
$$v_f = v_y + a_y t = -7.80 + (-9.8)(0.298) = -10.72\text{ m s}^{-1}$$
The magnitude of final velocity $v_{total}$ is:
$$v_{total} = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_f^2} = \sqrt{63.52^2 + (-10.72)^2} \approx 64.4\text{ m s}^{-1}$$
