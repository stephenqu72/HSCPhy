To solve this problem, we use the principle of **Conservation of Mechanical Energy**.

### Step 1: Identify the energies
The total energy $E$ at any point in the gravitational field of a planet of mass $M$ and radius $R$ is given by:
$$E = K + U = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{GMm}{r}$$
where $r$ is the distance from the center of the planet.

*   **At the surface ($r = R$):** The launch speed is $v = \frac{3}{4}v_e$. Since the escape speed is $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$, we have $v^2 = \frac{9}{16} \cdot \frac{2GM}{R} = \frac{9GM}{8R}$.
    $$E_{surface} = \frac{1}{2}m\left(\frac{9GM}{8R}\right) - \frac{GMm}{R} = \frac{9GMm}{16R} - \frac{16GMm}{16R} = -\frac{7GMm}{16R}$$
*   **At maximum height ($r = R + h$):** The velocity $v = 0$.
    $$E_{max} = 0 - \frac{GMm}{R + h}$$

### Step 2: Equate energies
By conservation of energy, $E_{surface} = E_{max}$:
$$-\frac{7GMm}{16R} = -\frac{GMm}{R + h}$$
$$\frac{7}{16R} = \frac{1}{R + h}$$
$$16R = 7(R + h)$$
$$16R = 7R + 7h$$
$$9R = 7h$$
$$h = \frac{9}{7}R$$

The maximum height reached from the surface is $\frac{9}{7}R$. This corresponds to option **C**.

### Explanation Summary
1. Define total mechanical energy as the sum of kinetic and gravitational potential energy.
2. Substitute the given launch velocity ($3/4$ of escape speed) into the energy equation at the surface.
3. Set the total energy at the surface equal to the total potential energy at the maximum height (where kinetic energy is zero).
4. Solve the resulting algebraic equation for the height $h$.
