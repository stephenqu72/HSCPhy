### Front
What are two key pieces of evidence that baryons (e.g., protons/neutrons) are not fundamental particles?

### Back
- Key idea: 
    1. **Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS):** High-energy electrons scatter off internal "partons" (quarks) rather than behaving as a single point-charge. 
    2. **The Quark Model/Classification:** The existence of fractional quark charges ($+2/3, -1/3$) accounts for the integer charges of hadrons, and the "Eightfold Way" symmetry predicted the existence of previously unknown particles (e.g., $\Omega^-$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Any question asking for evidence of substructure in hadrons or why the Standard Model proposes quarks.
- Common trap: Don’t just name the experiments; explain *why* they prove substructure (i.e., point-like scattering and integer charge summing).
