### Front
Derive the expression for the vertical velocity ($v_y$) of an electron traversing a uniform electric field of length $l$ with horizontal velocity $v_x$.

### Back
- Key idea: Uniform acceleration in the vertical direction while horizontal velocity remains constant.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v_y = \frac{eEl}{mv_x}$ (derived from $F=ma$ and $v=u+at$).
- When to use it: Projectile-like motion of a charged particle entering a parallel plate electric field perpendicularly.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the time spent in the field ($t = l/v_x$) depends on the constant horizontal velocity.
