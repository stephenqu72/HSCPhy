### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the initial and final states from the question text:**
The question asks for the change in energy when the altitude is **reduced** from $14000\text{ km}$ to $4000\text{ km}$.
*   Initial altitude ($h_{\text{initial}}$) $= 14000\text{ km}$
*   Final altitude ($h_{\text{final}}$) $= 4000\text{ km}$

**2. Extract the corresponding energy values from the graph:**
To find these values, locate the altitude on the horizontal $x$-axis, move vertically to reach the plotted curve, and then move horizontally to read the value on the vertical $y$-axis ($U$).

*   **For $14000\text{ km}$:** Find $14000$ on the x-axis. Moving up to the curve and across to the y-axis, we can see the point lies exactly on the grid line representing:
    $$U_{\text{initial}} \approx -3.0 \times 10^9\text{ J}$$
*   **For $4000\text{ km}$:** Find $4000$ on the x-axis (this is one grid square to the left of the $5000$ mark). Moving up to the curve and across to the y-axis, we see the point lies at:
    $$U_{\text{final}} \approx -5.8 \times 10^9\text{ J}$$

**3. Calculate the change in gravitational potential energy ($\Delta U$):**
The "change" in any quantity is always calculated as $\text{Final Value} - \text{Initial Value}$.
$$\Delta U = U_{\text{final}} - U_{\text{initial}}$$

Substitute the values we extracted:
$$\Delta U = (-5.8 \times 10^9\text{ J}) - (-3.0 \times 10^9\text{ J})$$
$$\Delta U = -5.8 \times 10^9\text{ J} + 3.0 \times 10^9\text{ J}$$
$$\Delta U = -2.8 \times 10^9\text{ J}$$

**Conclusion:**
Since the altitude decreased, the satellite moved closer to the Earth, meaning it lost potential energy (the value became more negative). The calculated change of $-2.8 \times 10^9\text{ J}$ matches option **B**.
