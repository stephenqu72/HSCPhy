To determine the correct answer, we must analyze the physical properties of the electromagnetic spectrum as we move from Radio waves (left) to Gamma-rays (right).

### Answer
The correct statement is **C. Wavelength decreases, energy increases.**

---

### Explanation
To solve this, we use the fundamental wave equation and the photon energy equation:

1.  **Wavelength relationship:** The wave equation is $v = f\lambda$. Since all electromagnetic waves travel at the speed of light ($c$) in a vacuum, we have $c = f\lambda$, or $\lambda = \frac{c}{f}$. As you move from radio waves to gamma rays, the frequency ($f$) increases significantly. Because $\lambda$ and $f$ are inversely proportional, an increase in frequency results in a **decrease in wavelength**.
2.  **Energy relationship:** The energy of a photon is given by Planck's equation: $E = hf$, where $h$ is Planck's constant and $f$ is the frequency. As we move to the right (toward gamma rays), the frequency increases. Therefore, the energy of the photons must **increase**.
3.  **Alternative view using wavelength:** We can also use the equation $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$. This shows that energy and wavelength are inversely proportional. As the wavelength ($\lambda$) gets smaller (decreases), the energy ($E$) must get larger (increases).

By observing the spectrum provided:
*   **Radio waves** have long wavelengths and low energy.
*   **Gamma-rays** have extremely short wavelengths and high energy.
*   Therefore, moving from left to right, wavelength decreases and energy increases.
