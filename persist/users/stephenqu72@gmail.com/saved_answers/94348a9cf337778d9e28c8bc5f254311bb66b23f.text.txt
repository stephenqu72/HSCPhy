To solve this problem, we must apply our knowledge of stellar evolution and the physical characteristics of star groups on the Hertzsprung-Russell (HR) diagram.

### Explanation

1.  **Analyze the HR Diagram Regions:**
    *   **Region A:** This is the **Main Sequence**. Stars in this region are fusing hydrogen into helium in their cores via the **p-p chain reaction** (or the CNO cycle for more massive stars).
    *   **Region B:** This is the **Red Giant Branch**. These are evolved stars that have exhausted the hydrogen in their cores and are now burning helium in their cores via the **triple-alpha process**.
    *   **Region C:** This is the **Supergiant/Giant** region. These stars have also moved off the main sequence and are in advanced stages of evolution, where helium fusion (the triple-alpha process) occurs.
    *   **Region D:** These are **White Dwarfs**. These are stellar remnants that no longer perform nuclear fusion.

2.  **Evaluate the Reactions:**
    *   **(i) p-p Chain reaction:** This is the primary process of hydrogen fusion occurring in the cores of stars on the **Main Sequence (A)**.
    *   **(ii) Triple-alpha reaction:** This process fuses helium into carbon and occurs in the core or shell of evolved, cooler stars, specifically the **Red Giants (B)** and more luminous **Giants/Supergiants (C)**.

3.  **Conclusion:**
    *   The p-p chain reaction predominates in group **A**.
    *   The triple-alpha reaction predominates in groups **B and C**.

Matching these conclusions with the provided options:
*   A: A | A and C (Incorrect)
*   B: D | B and C (Incorrect)
*   C: A | B and C (**Correct**)
*   D: D | A and C (Incorrect)

**The correct answer is C.**
