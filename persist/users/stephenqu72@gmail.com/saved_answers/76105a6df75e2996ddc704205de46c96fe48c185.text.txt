The correct answer is **(C)**.

### Explanation

To solve this, we need to analyze the forces acting on a projectile (the ball) in the absence of significant air resistance:

1.  **Analyze the forces at maximum height:** When the ball reaches its maximum height, its instantaneous vertical velocity is $0$ $m/s$. However, it is still under the influence of gravity. The only force acting on the ball throughout its entire flight (ignoring air resistance) is its weight, which is $F_g = mg$, directed vertically downwards.
2.  **Evaluate the options:**
    *   **(A) Incorrect:** The weight of the ball acts on it even at the peak. Therefore, there is a net force.
    *   **(B) Incorrect:** Balanced forces mean the net force is zero. As established, there is a non-zero gravitational force.
    *   **(C) Correct:** The moment after the ball is thrown, the force acting on the ball is its weight ($mg$) directed downwards. At the maximum height, the force acting on the ball is still its weight ($mg$) directed downwards. Therefore, the forces are identical.
    *   **(D) Incorrect:** The net force is gravity, which acts downwards for the entire duration of the flight. It never acts upwards, nor does it change direction at the peak.

**HSC Teacher Tip:** Students often confuse "zero velocity" with "zero acceleration" or "zero net force." Remember that at the top of a trajectory, $v = 0$ $m/s$, but acceleration remains constant at $g \approx 9.8$ $m/s^2$ downwards.
