To achieve full marks on this question, you must demonstrate a clear understanding of how James Clerk Maxwell unified the existing knowledge of electromagnetism.

### Answer
Maxwell’s primary contribution was the unification of electricity and magnetism into a single, cohesive mathematical framework known as **Maxwell’s Equations**. His key contributions include:

1.  **The Unification of Electromagnetism:** He consolidated the laws of Gauss, Ampère, and Faraday into a set of four partial differential equations. This provided a complete mathematical description of how electric and magnetic fields interact.
2.  **The Concept of Displacement Current:** Maxwell identified a missing term in Ampère’s Law. He proposed that a changing electric field creates a magnetic field, just as a changing magnetic field creates an electric field. This "displacement current" was crucial for the theoretical consistency of electromagnetism.
3.  **The Prediction of Electromagnetic Waves:** By combining his equations, Maxwell derived a wave equation and calculated the speed of these electromagnetic waves. He found this speed to be approximately $3 \times 10^8$ m/s, which matched the known speed of light, leading him to correctly propose that light is an electromagnetic wave.
