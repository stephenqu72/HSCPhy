### Front
What physical principle dictates why a planet moves faster at perihelion than at aphelion?

### Back
- Key idea: Conservation of angular momentum and the conservation of total mechanical energy.
- Formula / law: Kepler’s Second Law; $L = mvr \sin(\theta) = \text{constant}$; $E_{\text{total}} = U + K = \text{constant}$.
- When to use it: To explain changes in orbital speed due to varying distances from a central mass.
- Common trap: Assuming the orbital speed is constant (circular motion) rather than accounting for the trade-off between gravitational potential energy and kinetic energy.
