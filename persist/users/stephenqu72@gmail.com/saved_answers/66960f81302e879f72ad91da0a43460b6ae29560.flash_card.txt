### Front
How do you calculate the displacement of an object undergoing constant acceleration when initial velocity and time are known?

### Back
- Key idea: Kinematic equations of motion (displacement).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$ (where $g \approx 9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$).
- When to use it: To find displacement or height when time, initial velocity, and constant acceleration are known.
- Common trap: Ignoring sign conventions (e.g., failing to define the direction of motion as positive vs. gravity acting downwards).
