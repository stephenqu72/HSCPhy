### Front
Which of the following is a fundamental particle according to the Standard Model: Hadron, Neutron, Photon, or Proton?

### Back
- Key idea: Fundamental particles (elementary particles) have no internal substructure and are not composed of smaller parts.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When classifying particles as either "elementary" (e.g., quarks, leptons, gauge bosons) or "composite" (e.g., hadrons like protons and neutrons).
- Common trap: Confusing composite particles (made of quarks) with fundamental particles; remember that all hadrons are composite.
