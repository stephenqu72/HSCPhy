To solve this problem, we use the principles of wave-particle duality, specifically Young's double-slit experiment equations and the de Broglie wavelength formula.

### Part a) Show that the de Broglie wavelength is $2.4 \times 10^{-10}\text{ m}$

**Given data:**
- Slit separation ($d$) = $5 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m}$
- Distance to screen ($L$) = $2.5\text{ m}$
- Fringe separation ($\Delta x$) = $1.2 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}$

**Formula:**
For double-slit interference, the fringe separation is given by:
$$\Delta x = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$

**Calculation:**
Rearrange the formula to solve for the wavelength ($\lambda$):
$$\lambda = \frac{\Delta x \cdot d}{L}$$
$$\lambda = \frac{(1.2 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}) \times (5 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m})}{2.5\text{ m}}$$
$$\lambda = \frac{6.0 \times 10^{-10}}{2.5}\text{ m}$$
$$\lambda = 2.4 \times 10^{-10}\text{ m}$$
This confirms the required value.

### Part b) Calculate the velocity of the electrons

**Formula:**
The de Broglie wavelength relates to momentum ($p$) via:
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$
Where:
- $h$ (Planck's constant) $\approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J s}$
- $m$ (mass of an electron) $\approx 9.109 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$

**Calculation:**
Rearrange to solve for $v$:
$$v = \frac{h}{m \lambda}$$
$$v = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{(9.109 \times 10^{-31}) \times (2.4 \times 10^{-10})}$$
$$v = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{2.186 \times 10^{-40}}$$
$$v \approx 3.03 \times 10^6\text{ m/s}$$

The velocity of the electrons is approximately $3.03 \times 10^6\text{ m/s}$.
