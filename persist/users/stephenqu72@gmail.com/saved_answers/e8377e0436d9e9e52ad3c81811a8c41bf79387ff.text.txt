To find the magnitude of the force exerted on a charged particle moving through a magnetic field, we use the formula for the Lorentz force:

$$F = qvB \sin(\theta)$$

### Breakdown of Variables:
1.  **$q$ (Charge of the electron):** The elementary charge is $1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$.
2.  **$v$ (Velocity):** The problem states $3.0 \times 10^3 \text{ ms}^{-1}$, which is $3000 \text{ ms}^{-1}$.
3.  **$B$ (Magnetic field strength):** The problem states $2 \times 10^{-2} \text{ T}$, which is $0.02 \text{ T}$.
4.  **$\theta$ (Angle):** The particle passes at $90^\circ$ relative to the magnetic field.

### Substituting into the formula:
$$F = (1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times (3000) \times (0.02) \times \sin(90^\circ)$$

**Correct Answer: (A)**
