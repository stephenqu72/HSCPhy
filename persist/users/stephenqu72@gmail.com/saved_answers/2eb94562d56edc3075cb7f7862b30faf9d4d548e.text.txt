To solve this problem, we first identify the given values:
*   Side length $s = 12.00\text{ cm} = 0.12\text{ m}$
*   Area $A = s^2 = (0.12)^2 = 0.0144\text{ m}^2$
*   Number of turns $N = 8$
*   Current $I = 3.00\text{ A}$
*   Magnetic field strength $B = 2.50 \times 10^{-4}\text{ T}$ (Note: The prompt image says $10^4\text{ T}$, but physically, a standard physics problem would imply $10^{-4}\text{ T}$. I will use $2.50 \times 10^{-4}\text{ T}$ for a realistic calculation).

### (a) Calculate the torque
The formula for torque is $\tau = NIAB \sin(\theta)$, where $\theta$ is the angle between the **normal to the coil** and the magnetic field.
In the diagram, the angle between the coil plane and the field is $40^\circ$, so the angle between the normal and the field is $90^\circ - 40^\circ = 50^\circ$.
$$\tau = 8 \times 3.00 \times 0.0144 \times (2.50 \times 10^{-4}) \times \sin(50^\circ)$$
$$\tau \approx 8.64 \times 10^{-5} \times 0.766 \approx 6.62 \times 10^{-5}\text{ Nm}$$

### (b) Determine the change in flux
Magnetic flux is $\Phi = B A \cos(\theta)$, where $\theta$ is the angle between the normal and the field.
Initial angle $\theta_i = 50^\circ$.
Final angle $\theta_f = 50^\circ + 30^\circ = 80^\circ$.
$$\Delta \Phi = BA(\cos(80^\circ) - \cos(50^\circ))$$
$$\Delta \Phi = (2.50 \times 10^{-4}) \times 0.0144 \times (0.1736 - 0.6428)$$
$$\Delta \Phi \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-6} \times (-0.4692) \approx -1.69 \times 10^{-6}\text{ Wb}$$

### (c) Calculate the rate of rotation
The peak voltage $V_p = NBA\omega$. The problem specifies a peak-to-peak voltage of $2400\text{ V}$, so peak voltage $V_p = 1200\text{ V}$.
$$\omega = \frac{V_p}{NBA} = \frac{1200}{8 \times (2.50 \times 10^{-4}) \times 0.0144}$$
$$\omega = \frac{1200}{2.88 \times 10^{-5}} \approx 4.17 \times 10^7\text{ rad/s}$$
To convert to revolutions per minute (RPM):
$$\text{RPM} = \frac{\omega \times 60}{2\pi} \approx \frac{4.17 \times 10^7 \times 60}{6.28} \approx 3.98 \times 10^8\text{ RPM}$$
