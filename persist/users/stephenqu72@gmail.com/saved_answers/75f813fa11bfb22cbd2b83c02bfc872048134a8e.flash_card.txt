### Front
How does the mass and charge of an alpha particle determine its relative ionizing power compared to beta and gamma radiation?

### Back
- Key idea: Ionizing power is directly proportional to particle mass and charge magnitude.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Ionizing power order: Alpha ($\alpha$) > Beta ($\beta$) > Gamma ($\gamma$).
- When to use it: Use when comparing radiation types; alpha particles ($^4_2He^{2+}$) have the highest mass and a $+2$ charge, causing maximum interaction with electrons.
- Common trap: Confusing "ionizing power" (ability to strip electrons) with "penetrating power" (ability to pass through materials). Gamma is the most penetrating, but alpha is the most ionizing.
