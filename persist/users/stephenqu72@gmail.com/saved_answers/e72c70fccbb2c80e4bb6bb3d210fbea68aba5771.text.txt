To determine the most appropriate measure of uncertainty for this ammeter, we need to analyze the scale of the instrument.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the scale intervals:** Look at the major markings on the ammeter. There is a major marking at 0, 5, 10, 15, and 20.
2.  **Determine the minor divisions:** Between the major marking 0 and 5, there are 5 subdivisions. This means each small tick mark represents:
    $$\text{Value per division} = \frac{5 - 0}{5} = 1 \text{ A}$$
3.  **Determine the resolution (smallest scale division):** The smallest scale division of this ammeter is $1 \text{ A}$.
4.  **Determine the uncertainty:** For an analogue instrument like this, the standard practice for the uncertainty (or reading error) is to take **half of the smallest scale division**.
    $$\text{Uncertainty} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{smallest scale division}$$
    $$\text{Uncertainty} = \frac{1}{2} \times 1 \text{ A} = 0.5 \text{ A}$$

While some might argue for the full scale division in cases of poor parallax or needle thickness, in HSC physics, the half-division rule is the convention for estimating the uncertainty of analogue measurements. Therefore, $0.5 \text{ A}$ is the most appropriate measure provided in the options.
