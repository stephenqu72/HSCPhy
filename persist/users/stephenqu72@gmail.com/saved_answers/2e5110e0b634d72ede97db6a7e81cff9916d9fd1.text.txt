To solve this problem, we apply **Malus's Law**, which describes the change in intensity of polarized light when it passes through an analyzer.

### Explanation and Calculation

1.  **Understand the Physics:**
    *   The light incident on the first polarizer is unpolarized with intensity $I_0$.
    *   After passing through the first polarizer (the polariser), the intensity of the light becomes $I_1 = \frac{1}{2}I_0$.
    *   Malus's Law states that when this polarized light passes through a second filter (the analyzer) with its axis at an angle $\theta$ to the polarization direction of the incoming light, the transmitted intensity $I$ is given by:
        $$I = I_1 \cos^2(\theta)$$
    *   Substituting $I_1 = \frac{1}{2}I_0$, we get:
        $$I = \frac{1}{2}I_0 \cos^2(\theta)$$

2.  **Set up the Equation:**
    The question asks for the angle $\theta$ such that the final intensity $I$ is $30\%$ of the original incident intensity $I_0$.
    $$I = 0.30 I_0$$

    Equating the two expressions for $I$:
    $$0.30 I_0 = \frac{1}{2} I_0 \cos^2(\theta)$$

3.  **Solve for $\theta$:**
    *   Divide both sides by $I_0$:
        $$0.30 = 0.5 \cos^2(\theta)$$
    *   Divide by $0.5$:
        $$0.6 = \cos^2(\theta)$$
    *   Take the square root of both sides:
        $$\cos(\theta) = \sqrt{0.6} \approx 0.7746$$
    *   Find the inverse cosine ($\arccos$):
        $$\theta = \arccos(0.7746) \approx 39.23^\circ$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the closest value is $39^\circ$.

**Correct Option: (C)**
