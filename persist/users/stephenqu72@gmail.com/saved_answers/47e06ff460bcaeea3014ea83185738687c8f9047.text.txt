### Answer
The correct answer is **(A)**.

### Explanation
To determine which unit is equivalent to a joule ($J$), we must look at the definitions of energy and work in physics.

1.  **Define the Work formula:** Work ($W$) is defined as the force ($F$) applied over a distance ($d$). The formula is:
    $$W = F \times d$$
    Since work is a form of energy, its unit is the Joule ($J$), and the unit for force is the Newton ($N$), while the unit for distance is the meter ($m$). Therefore:
    $$1 J = 1 N \cdot m$$

2.  **Break down the Newton:** According to Newton's Second Law, force is mass ($m$) times acceleration ($a$):
    $$F = m \times a$$
    The units for mass are kilograms ($kg$) and the units for acceleration are meters per second squared ($m/s^2$ or $m \cdot s^{-2}$). Therefore:
    $$1 N = 1 kg \cdot m \cdot s^{-2}$$

3.  **Combine the units:** Substitute the breakdown of the Newton back into the equation for the Joule:
    $$1 J = (1 kg \cdot m \cdot s^{-2}) \cdot m$$
    $$1 J = 1 kg \cdot m^2 \cdot s^{-2}$$

Comparing this result to the given options:
*   (A) $kg m^2 s^{-2}$ matches our derived result.
*   (B) $kg m s^{-2}$ is the unit for Force (Newton).
*   (C) $N m^{-2}$ is the unit for Pressure (Pascal).
*   (D) $N m^2$ is not a standard fundamental SI unit.

Thus, **(A)** is the correct choice.
