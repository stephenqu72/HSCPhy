### Front
Why does the observable universe consist almost entirely of matter rather than equal parts matter and antimatter?

### Back
- Key idea: Baryon asymmetry (CP-violation) created a tiny excess of matter during the early universe, preventing total annihilation.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Explaining the matter-antimatter discrepancy or the early evolution of the Big Bang.
- Common trap: Confusing matter creation with stellar nucleosynthesis (fusion/supernovae); fusion rearranges existing matter but does not create it.
