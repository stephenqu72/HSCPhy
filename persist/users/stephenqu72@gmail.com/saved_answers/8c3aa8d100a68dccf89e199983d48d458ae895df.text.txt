To determine which wavelength is not part of the Balmer series, we use the Rydberg formula for the hydrogen atom:

$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$

For the **Balmer series**, the final energy level is always $n_f = 2$. Therefore, the formula becomes:

$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{2^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right) = R_H \left( \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$

where $n_i = 3, 4, 5, \dots$ and $R_H \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \, \text{m}^{-1}$.

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1.  **For $n_i = 3$ (H-alpha line):**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{9} \right) = R_H \left( \frac{5}{36} \right) \implies \lambda \approx 656 \, \text{nm}$$
    This matches option D.

2.  **For $n_i = 4$ (H-beta line):**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{16} \right) = R_H \left( \frac{3}{16} \right) \implies \lambda \approx 486 \, \text{nm}$$
    This matches option C.

3.  **For $n_i = 5$ (H-gamma line):**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{25} \right) = R_H \left( \frac{21}{100} \right) \implies \lambda \approx 434 \, \text{nm}$$
    This matches option B.

4.  **For $n_i = 6$ (H-delta line):**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{36} \right) = R_H \left( \frac{8}{36} \right) \implies \lambda \approx 410 \, \text{nm}$$

Comparing these results to the options provided:
*   656 nm (D), 486 nm (C), and 434 nm (B) are clearly part of the Balmer series.
*   **421 nm (A)** does not correspond to any transition where $n_i$ is an integer in the Balmer series.

**Answer:** A
