### Answer

The spectra provide evidence for the expansion of the universe by demonstrating a **redshift**. By comparing the absorption lines of the distant galaxy to the reference spectrum of the Sun, it is observable that the lines in the galaxy's spectrum have shifted toward the longer wavelength (red) end of the spectrum.

1.  **Support for Hubble’s Law:** Hubble’s Law states that the velocity of recession of a galaxy is proportional to its distance ($v = H_0d$). The redshift indicates that the galaxy is moving away from us. Because the magnitude of this redshift correlates to the recession velocity, it confirms that the universe is expanding uniformly as described by Hubble's Law.
2.  **Support for the Big Bang Theory:** The observation of galactic redshift implies that if the universe is expanding, it must have been smaller and more dense in the past. This provides direct observational evidence that the universe originated from a singular, hot, dense state—the Big Bang—and has been expanding ever since.
