To solve this problem, we need to analyze the vertical component of the projectile's motion.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Identify the given variables:**
*   Initial velocity $v = 49\text{ ms}^{-1}$
*   Maximum height $h = 105\text{ m}$
*   Acceleration due to gravity $g \approx 9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$
*   At maximum height, the vertical component of velocity is $v_y = 0\text{ ms}^{-1}$.

**2. Use the kinematic equation for vertical motion:**
We use the formula relating initial vertical velocity ($v_{0y}$), final vertical velocity, acceleration, and displacement:
$$v_y^2 = v_{0y}^2 - 2gh$$
Since $v_y = 0$ at the peak:
$$0 = v_{0y}^2 - 2gh$$
$$v_{0y} = \sqrt{2gh}$$

**3. Calculate the initial vertical velocity ($v_{0y}$):**
$$v_{0y} = \sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 105}$$
$$v_{0y} = \sqrt{2058} \approx 45.365\text{ ms}^{-1}$$

**4. Relate vertical velocity to the launch angle ($\theta$):**
The vertical component of the launch velocity is given by $v_{0y} = v \sin(\theta)$.
Rearranging to solve for $\theta$:
$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{v_{0y}}{v}$$
$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{45.365}{49}$$
$$\sin(\theta) \approx 0.9258$$

**5. Calculate the angle:**
$$\theta = \arcsin(0.9258)$$
$$\theta \approx 67.78^\circ$$

Rounding to the nearest degree, we get **$68^\circ$**.

Comparing this result to the given options:
A) $45^\circ$
B) $58^\circ$
**C) $68^\circ$**
D) $93^\circ$

The correct option is **C**.
