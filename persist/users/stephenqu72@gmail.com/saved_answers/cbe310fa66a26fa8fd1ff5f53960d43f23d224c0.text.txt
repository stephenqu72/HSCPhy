The correct answer is **(C) Rutherford Model**.

### Explanation

To solve this question, you need to understand the historical development of the atomic model within the HSC Physics syllabus:

1.  **The Context:** At the time, the prevailing model was J.J. Thomson’s **Plum Pudding Model**, which suggested that an atom consisted of a sphere of positive charge with negatively charged electrons embedded within it like plums in a pudding.
2.  **The Experiment:** The Geiger-Marsden experiment (also known as the gold foil experiment) involved firing high-energy alpha particles at a thin sheet of gold.
3.  **The Observation:** While most alpha particles passed straight through, a small percentage were deflected at large angles, and some even bounced directly backward. This was entirely inconsistent with the Plum Pudding Model, which predicted that the positive charge would be too diffuse to cause such significant scattering.
4.  **The Conclusion:** Ernest Rutherford concluded that the atom must consist of a very small, dense, and positively charged **nucleus** containing most of the atom's mass, surrounded by mostly empty space occupied by electrons. This became known as the **Rutherford Model** (or the nuclear model).

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **(A)** The Rutherford-Bohr model built upon Rutherford’s work by introducing quantized energy levels, but the Geiger-Marsden experiment specifically established the existence of the nucleus, not the quantized orbits.
*   **(B)** The Plum Pudding Model is what the experiment **refuted**, not confirmed.
*   **(D)** The Electron Cloud Model is a much later development based on quantum mechanics, which was not the result of this experiment.
