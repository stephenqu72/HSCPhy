### Front
How do you determine the relationship between the mass ($M$) and radius ($r$) of two planets with equal surface gravity ($g$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Surface gravity is directly proportional to mass and inversely proportional to the square of the radius.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$ (Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation)
- When to use it: When comparing gravitational field strengths of different celestial bodies.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the radius or incorrectly rearranging the proportionality (e.g., $M \propto r$ instead of $M \propto r^2$).
