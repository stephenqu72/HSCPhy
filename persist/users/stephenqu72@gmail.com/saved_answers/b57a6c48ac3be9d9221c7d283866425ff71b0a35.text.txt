To solve this projectile motion problem, we must analyze the vertical motion of the paper plane independently of its horizontal motion.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) 2.16 s**.

### Explanation
In projectile motion, horizontal and vertical motions are independent. Because the plane is thrown **horizontally**, its initial vertical velocity ($u_y$) is $0 \text{ m s}^{-1}$.

We use the kinematic equation for displacement in the vertical direction:
$$s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$$

Where:
*   $s_y = 22.8 \text{ m}$ (vertical displacement/height)
*   $u_y = 0 \text{ m s}^{-1}$ (initial vertical velocity)
*   $a_y = g = 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$ (acceleration due to gravity)
*   $t = ?$ (time taken)

Substituting the values into the equation:
$$22.8 = (0)t + \frac{1}{2}(9.8)t^2$$
$$22.8 = 4.9t^2$$

Now, solve for $t^2$:
$$t^2 = \frac{22.8}{4.9}$$
$$t^2 \approx 4.653$$

Finally, take the square root to find $t$:
$$t = \sqrt{4.653}$$
$$t \approx 2.157 \text{ s}$$

Rounding to three significant figures, we get **2.16 s**, which matches option (A).
