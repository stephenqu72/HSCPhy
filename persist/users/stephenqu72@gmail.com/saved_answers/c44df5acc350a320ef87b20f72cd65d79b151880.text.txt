### Answer
The correct answer is **B. $1.5R$**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we use the formula for gravitational acceleration at the surface of a planet:
$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$
where $G$ is the gravitational constant, $M$ is the mass of the planet, and $r$ is its radius.

**1. Establish the ratio for Earth:**
For Earth, let mass be $M_E$ and radius be $R$. The acceleration due to gravity is:
$$g_E = \frac{GM_E}{R^2} \approx 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$$

**2. Set up the equation for Phobetor:**
We are given:
- Mass of Phobetor ($M_P$) $= 4M_E$
- Gravity of Phobetor ($g_P$) $= 18 \text{ m s}^{-2}$
- Radius of Phobetor $= r_P$

Using the formula for Phobetor:
$$g_P = \frac{G(4M_E)}{r_P^2} = 18$$

**3. Compare the two planets:**
Divide the equation for Phobetor by the equation for Earth to eliminate $G$ and $M_E$:
$$\frac{g_P}{g_E} = \frac{\frac{4GM_E}{r_P^2}}{\frac{GM_E}{R^2}}$$
$$\frac{18}{9.8} = 4 \times \frac{R^2}{r_P^2}$$

Calculating the ratio:
$$1.837 \approx 4 \times \frac{R^2}{r_P^2}$$
$$\frac{R^2}{r_P^2} \approx \frac{1.837}{4} \approx 0.459$$

Taking the square root of both sides:
$$\frac{R}{r_P} \approx \sqrt{0.459} \approx 0.677$$
$$r_P \approx \frac{R}{0.677} \approx 1.477R$$

Rounding to the nearest option provided, $1.477R$ is closest to $1.5R$.
