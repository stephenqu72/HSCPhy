### Front
Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation: How does the gravitational force ($F$) between two bodies change with respect to the distance ($r$) between their centres?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational force is **inversely proportional** to the square of the distance ($F \propto 1/r^2$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $F = \frac{G M m}{r^2}$; $G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N m}^2/\text{kg}^2$.
- **When to use it:** Calculating orbital forces, satellite motion, and planetary gravity.
- **Common trap:** Confusing direct proportionality with inverse proportionality; forgetting to square the distance ($r$); confusing the Universal Gravitational Constant ($G$) with acceleration due to gravity ($g$).
