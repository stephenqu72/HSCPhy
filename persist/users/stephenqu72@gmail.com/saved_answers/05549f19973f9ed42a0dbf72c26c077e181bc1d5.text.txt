### Answer
The correct answer is **B. The motor effect.**

### Explanation
To solve this question, we need to identify the phenomenon where a current-carrying conductor placed in an external magnetic field experiences a force. This force is defined by the formula:
$$F = BIL\sin(\theta)$$
where:
*   $F$ is the magnetic force (Newtons)
*   $B$ is the magnetic field strength (Tesla)
*   $I$ is the current (Amperes)
*   $L$ is the length of the conductor (meters)
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the conductor and the magnetic field lines.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **A. Faraday's law:** This relates to electromagnetic induction, where a *changing* magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) in a circuit. It is the principle behind generators, not the force on a conductor.
*   **C. Lenz’s law:** This states that the direction of an induced current is always such that it opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it (linked to the conservation of energy).
*   **D. The photoelectric effect:** This refers to the emission of electrons when electromagnetic radiation (such as light) hits a material. It is a quantum phenomenon, not related to magnetic forces on conductors.

The **motor effect** is the fundamental principle behind the operation of DC motors, where this force is used to create rotational motion.
