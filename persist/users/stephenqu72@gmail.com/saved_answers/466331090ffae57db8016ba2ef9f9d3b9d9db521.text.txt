To find the temperature of the blackbody, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which relates the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) of the radiation emitted by a blackbody to its absolute temperature ($T$).

### Formula
The relationship is defined as:
$$\lambda_{max} T = b$$
Where:
*   $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength in meters ($m$).
*   $T$ is the temperature in Kelvin ($K$).
*   $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m}\cdot\text{K}$.

### Step-by-Step Calculation

1.  **Convert the wavelength to meters:**
    The given wavelength is $345 \, \text{nm}$. Since $1 \, \text{nm} = 10^{-9} \, \text{m}$:
    $$\lambda_{max} = 345 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{m} = 3.45 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}$$

2.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $T$:**
    $$T = \frac{b}{\lambda_{max}}$$

3.  **Substitute the values:**
    $$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m}\cdot\text{K}}{3.45 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}}$$

4.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$T \approx 0.840 \times 10^{4} \, \text{K}$$

    $$T = 8.40 \times 10^{3} \, \text{K}$$
