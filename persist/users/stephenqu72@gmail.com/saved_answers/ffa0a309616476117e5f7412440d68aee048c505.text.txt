To find the magnetic flux through the coil, we use the formula for magnetic flux ($\Phi$):

$$\Phi = B \cdot A \cdot \cos(\theta)$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   Magnetic field strength ($B$): $20.0\text{ T}$
    *   Dimensions of the coil: $2.0\text{ cm}$ and $5.0\text{ cm}$.
    *   Area ($A$): $2.0\text{ cm} \times 5.0\text{ cm} = 10\text{ cm}^2$. 
    *   Convert the area to SI units (square meters): $10\text{ cm}^2 = 10 \times (10^{-2}\text{ m})^2 = 10 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m}^2 = 1.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}^2$.

2.  **Determine the angle ($\theta$):**
    The formula $\Phi = BA\cos(\theta)$ requires $\theta$ to be the angle between the **magnetic field** and the **normal to the plane of the coil**. 
    *   The problem states the coil lies at $30^\circ$ to the magnetic field.
    *   The angle between the plane and the normal is $90^\circ$.
    *   Therefore, the angle $\theta$ between the magnetic field and the normal is $90^\circ - 30^\circ = 60^\circ$.

3.  **Calculate the flux:**
    $$\Phi = (20.0\text{ T}) \times (1.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}^2) \times \cos(60^\circ)$$
    $$\Phi = 20.0 \times 0.001 \times 0.5$$
    $$\Phi = 0.01\text{ Wb}$$

4.  **Convert to milliwebers (mWb):**
    $0.01\text{ Wb} = 10\text{ mWb}$.
