### Front
What is the primary physical cause of orbital decay for satellites in Low Earth Orbit (LEO)?

### Back
- Key idea: Atmospheric drag caused by residual gas particles in the thermosphere colliding with the satellite.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Explaining why LEO satellite altitudes decrease over time or calculating energy loss in non-vacuum orbits.
- Common trap: Mistaking the strong gravitational pull as the cause of decay; gravity is the centripetal force required to maintain the orbit, not the cause of altitude loss.
