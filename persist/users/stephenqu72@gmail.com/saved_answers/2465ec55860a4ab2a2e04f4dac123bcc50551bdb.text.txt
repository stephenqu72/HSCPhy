To solve this problem, we need to find the decay constant ($\lambda$) of a radioactive source given its half-life.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the given information:**
    *   The problem states that the activity of the source becomes one-half of its initial value after $360\text{ s}$. This means the half-life ($t_{1/2}$) of the nuclide is $360\text{ s}$.
    *   $t_{1/2} = 360\text{ s}$.

2.  **Recall the relevant formula:**
    The relationship between the half-life ($t_{1/2}$) and the decay constant ($\lambda$) is given by:
    $$t_{1/2} = \frac{\ln(2)}{\lambda}$$
    Rearranging this formula to solve for $\lambda$:
    $$\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{t_{1/2}}$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    Substitute the known value of $t_{1/2}$ into the equation:
    $$\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{360\text{ s}}$$
    Using the approximation $\ln(2) \approx 0.693$:
    $$\lambda \approx \frac{0.693147}{360\text{ s}}$$
    $$\lambda \approx 1.9254 \times 10^{-3}\text{ s}^{-1}$$

4.  **Compare with the options:**
    Rounding our result to three significant figures gives $1.93 \times 10^{-3}\text{ s}^{-1}$, which matches option **A**.

**Correct Answer: A**
