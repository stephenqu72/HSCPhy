### Front
What observation provided the critical evidence for Hubble’s Law regarding the expansion of the universe?

### Back
- Key idea: Redshift of stellar spectra (absorption lines shifting toward longer wavelengths).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = H_0 d$ ($H_0 \approx 70 \text{ km/s/Mpc}$).
- When to use it: To calculate the recessional velocity of a galaxy or its distance based on observed redshift.
- Common trap: Confusing "distance indicators" (Cepheid variables) with "recession evidence" (spectral redshift).
