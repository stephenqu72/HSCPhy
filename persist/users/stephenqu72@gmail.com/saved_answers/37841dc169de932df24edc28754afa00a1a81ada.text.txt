To determine the number of distinct spectral lines produced when an electron transitions from the $n=4$ energy level to the ground state ($n=1$), we must identify all possible downward transitions.

### Answer
The correct option is **(C) 6**.

### Explanation
When an electron is at the $n=4$ energy level, it can return to the ground state ($n=1$) either directly or through intermediate energy levels. Each unique transition releases a photon with a specific energy, corresponding to a distinct spectral line.

We can list the possible transitions:
1.  **From $n=4$:**
    *   $4 \rightarrow 3$
    *   $4 \rightarrow 2$
    *   $4 \rightarrow 1$
2.  **From $n=3$:**
    *   $3 \rightarrow 2$
    *   $3 \rightarrow 1$
3.  **From $n=2$:**
    *   $2 \rightarrow 1$

By counting these, we find a total of $3 + 2 + 1 = 6$ possible transitions.

**Mathematical Formula:**
For an electron transitioning from an initial energy level $n$ to the ground state, the number of distinct spectral lines $N$ can be calculated using the formula:
$$N = \frac{n(n - 1)}{2}$$

Substituting $n = 4$:
$$N = \frac{4(4 - 1)}{2} = \frac{4 \times 3}{2} = \frac{12}{2} = 6$$

This formula accounts for all possible combinations of transitions between the energy levels.
