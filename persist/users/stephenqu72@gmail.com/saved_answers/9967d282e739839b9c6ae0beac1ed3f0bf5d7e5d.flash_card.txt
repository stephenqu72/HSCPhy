### Front
What are Kepler’s Three Laws of Planetary Motion?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Describes the kinematics of planetary motion around the Sun.
- **Laws:** 
  1. **Orbits:** Planets move in ellipses with the Sun at one focus.
  2. **Areas:** A line connecting the planet and Sun sweeps out equal areas in equal time.
  3. **Periods:** The ratio $T^2 / r^3$ is a constant for all planets orbiting the same body.
- **When to use it:** Solving orbital mechanics problems involving planetary motion or satellite periods.
- **Common trap:** Confusing Kepler's Laws (kinematics) with Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation (dynamics/force).
