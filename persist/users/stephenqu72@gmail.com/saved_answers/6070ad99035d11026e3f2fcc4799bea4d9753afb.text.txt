### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a): Explain why the shape of the path is circular.

**Answer:**
When the cathode ray particles (electrons) enter the uniform magnetic field, they experience a magnetic force given by the Lorentz force law: $F = qvB \sin(\theta)$. Since the velocity of the particles is perpendicular to the magnetic field ($\theta = 90^\circ$), the force is always perpendicular to the direction of motion. This force acts as a centripetal force, continuously changing the direction of the particle's velocity without changing its speed. Because the force is constant in magnitude and always directed towards a central point relative to the motion, the particle follows a circular path.

**Teacher's Tip:** To score full marks here, you must mention two key physics principles: 
1. The force is always **perpendicular** to the velocity.
2. This force acts as a **centripetal force**.

#### Part (b): Calculate the speed of these cathode ray particles.

**Explanation:**
To find the speed, we equate the magnetic force to the centripetal force. 
The magnetic force is $F_B = qvB$.
The centripetal force is $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$.

Setting them equal:
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Solving for velocity ($v$):
$$v = \frac{qBr}{m}$$

We use the known charge-to-mass ratio of an electron, which is $\frac{q}{m} \approx 1.76 \times 10^{11} \text{ C kg}^{-1}$.
Given values:
- $B = 5.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ T}$
- $r = 70.0 \text{ mm} = 0.070 \text{ m}$

**Calculation:**
$$v = (1.76 \times 10^{11}) \times (5.0 \times 10^{-3}) \times (0.070)$$
$$v = 1.76 \times 10^{11} \times 3.5 \times 10^{-4}$$
$$v = 6.16 \times 10^7 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

Rounding to two significant figures (consistent with the provided data):
**$v = 6.2 \times 10^7 \text{ m s}^{-1}$**
