### Part a) Discussion of Energy and Work

**Answer:**
*   **Ferris Wheel:** The passenger moves in a vertical circle at constant speed. Kinetic energy ($E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$) remains constant because the speed is constant. Gravitational potential energy ($E_p = mgh$) fluctuates as the passenger's height changes. Consequently, the total mechanical energy fluctuates. Work is done by the motor/gravity; as the passenger rises, positive work is done against gravity (increasing $E_p$); as they descend, gravity does positive work (decreasing $E_p$).
*   **Spinning Cone:** The passenger moves in a horizontal circle at a constant speed and constant height. Therefore, both $E_k$ and $E_p$ remain constant throughout the motion. The total mechanical energy is constant. Since $E_{total}$ is constant, the net work done on the passenger is zero (Work-Energy Theorem). While individual forces (normal force, friction, gravity) act on the passenger, their vector sum provides the centripetal force, and because the displacement is always perpendicular to the net force required for circular motion, no net work is done.

### Part b) Calculation of Speeds

**How to solve:**
1.  **Geometry:** The radius $r$ of the circular path at height $h = 20.0$ m with angle $\theta = 30.0^\circ$ is $r = h \tan(30^\circ) = 20.0 \times 0.577 = 11.547$ m.
2.  **Forces:** Resolve forces vertically and horizontally. $N \sin \theta \pm f \cos \theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ and $N \cos \theta \mp f \sin \theta = mg$, where $f = \mu N$.
3.  **Minimum Speed ($v_{min}$):** Friction acts **up** the wall ($f = \mu N$).
    *   $N \cos \theta + \mu N \sin \theta = mg \implies N = \frac{mg}{\cos \theta + \mu \sin \theta}$
    *   $N \sin \theta - \mu N \cos \theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
    *   $v_{min} = \sqrt{rg \frac{\sin \theta - \mu \cos \theta}{\cos \theta + \mu \sin \theta}}$
    *   $v_{min} = \sqrt{11.547 \times 9.8 \times \frac{0.5 - 0.65 \times 0.866}{0.866 + 0.65 \times 0.5}} = \sqrt{113.16 \times \frac{-0.0629}{1.191}} \approx 0$ m/s (The negative result indicates the cone is steep enough that static friction alone prevents slipping at very low speeds).
4.  **Maximum Speed ($v_{max}$):** Friction acts **down** the wall ($f = \mu N$).
    *   $N \cos \theta - \mu N \sin \theta = mg \implies N = \frac{mg}{\cos \theta - \mu \sin \theta}$
    *   $N \sin \theta + \mu N \cos \theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
    *   $v_{max} = \sqrt{rg \frac{\sin \theta + \mu \cos \theta}{\cos \theta - \mu \sin \theta}}$
    *   $v_{max} = \sqrt{11.547 \times 9.8 \times \frac{0.5 + 0.65 \times 0.866}{0.866 - 0.65 \times 0.5}} = \sqrt{113.16 \times \frac{1.0629}{0.541}} = \sqrt{222.25} \approx 14.9$ m/s.
