### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) Predicted the existence of electromagnetic waves**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must look at James Clerk Maxwell’s significant contributions to the field of Electromagnetism:

1.  **Maxwell’s Equations:** Maxwell unified the laws of electricity and magnetism into a single set of four equations. He introduced the "displacement current" term, which showed that a changing electric field produces a magnetic field, just as a changing magnetic field (Faraday's Law) produces an electric field.
2.  **Theoretical Prediction:** By combining these equations, Maxwell showed that these oscillating fields would propagate through space as a transverse wave. He calculated the speed of these waves using the constants of permittivity ($\epsilon_0$) and permeability ($\mu_0$):
    $$v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$$
3.  **The Connection to Light:** Upon calculating this value, he found it to be approximately $3 \times 10^8 \text{ m s}^{-1}$, which matched the known speed of light. This led him to the landmark conclusion that **light itself is an electromagnetic wave**.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **(B):** While Maxwell calculated the theoretical speed of light based on constants, he did not experimentally measure it; experimental measurements were conducted by scientists like Fizeau and Foucault.
*   **(C):** The existence of radio waves was experimentally proven by Heinrich Hertz, who built upon Maxwell's theoretical predictions.
*   **(D):** Gauss's Law for magnetism states that there are no magnetic monopoles, but this is a fundamental observation/mathematical construct that predates Maxwell; it is one of the equations he *formalized*, rather than a proof he "discovered" in the context of his own unique contribution.
