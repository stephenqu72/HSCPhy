### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Analyze the Magnetic Flux:** 
    The magnetic flux $\Phi$ through the coil is given by $\Phi = BA \cos(\theta)$, where $\theta$ is the angle between the magnetic field vector and the normal to the coil's surface. Initially, the field is perpendicular to the plane, so the flux is at a maximum. 
    As the coil begins to rotate clockwise as shown, the area of the coil "projected" perpendicular to the magnetic field decreases. Consequently, the magnetic flux through the coil is **decreasing**.

2.  **Apply Lenz’s Law:** 
    Lenz’s Law states that the induced current will create a magnetic field that opposes the *change* in flux. Since the downward flux is decreasing, the induced current must create a magnetic field pointing **downwards** (to replenish the "lost" flux).

3.  **Determine Current Direction (Right-Hand Grip Rule):**
    Using your right hand, point your thumb in the direction of the desired induced magnetic field (downward). Your fingers will curl in the direction of the induced current.
