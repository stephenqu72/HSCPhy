To solve this problem, we must analyze the torque ($\tau$) acting on both systems using the formula:
$$\tau = rF\sin(\theta)$$
where $r$ is the radius (lever arm length), $F$ is the force, and $\theta$ is the angle between the force vector and the radial vector.

### 1. Analysis of the Bicycle Pedal
*   **Mechanism:** The force is applied vertically by the foot. As the pedal rotates through one revolution ($360^\circ$), the angle between the force vector (downward) and the lever arm ($0.25\text{ m}$) changes continuously.
*   **Torque behavior:** The torque is given by $\tau = rF\sin(\phi)$, where $\phi$ is the angle between the vertical force and the pedal arm. 
*   **Variation:** The torque varies sinusoidally. It is maximum when the pedal is horizontal ($\phi = 90^\circ$) and zero when the pedal is at the top or bottom of the cycle ($\phi = 0^\circ$ or $180^\circ$). Over one revolution, the torque oscillates between $+rF$ and $-rF$ (if considering direction) or simply fluctuates between $0$ and $rF$ if considering magnitude.

### 2. Analysis of the Generator Coil
*   **Mechanism:** The coil rotates in a magnetic field. The torque here relates to the magnetic torque: $\tau = NIAB\sin(\theta)$, where $\theta$ is the angle between the normal to the coil and the magnetic field.
*   **Torque behavior:** In the diagram, the force shown is a physical force acting on the coil edge. As the coil rotates, the "lever arm" (the perpendicular distance from the pivot to the line of action of the force) changes.
*   **Variation:** Similar to the bicycle, the torque follows a sinusoidal pattern based on the orientation of the coil relative to the field/force. However, in a standard generator, the torque is maximal when the plane of the coil is parallel to the magnetic field and zero when the plane of the coil is perpendicular to the field.

### Comparison and Contrast
*   **Compare:** Both systems experience a **sinusoidal variation** in torque throughout one revolution due to the changing effective perpendicular distance (lever arm) of the force application as the angle changes.
*   **Contrast:** The bicycle pedal's torque is driven by external mechanical input (gravity/muscle force) and is designed to provide rotational energy, whereas the generator coil's torque is an internal magnetic torque (often a restoring torque). Furthermore, the specific phase of the maximum torque depends on the initial orientation of the mechanical setup in both cases.
