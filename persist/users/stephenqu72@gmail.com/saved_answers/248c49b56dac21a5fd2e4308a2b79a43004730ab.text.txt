To determine the correct statement regarding an object in uniform circular motion, we must recall the definition and mechanics of such motion.

### Analysis of the Physics
1.  **Uniform Circular Motion Definition:** "Uniform" implies that the **speed** of the object is constant. However, because the object is moving in a circle, its direction is constantly changing. Since velocity is a vector quantity (magnitude + direction), a change in direction means the **velocity is not constant**.
2.  **The Role of Centripetal Force:** For an object to move in a circle, there must be a net force acting on it. This force is called the **centripetal force**.
3.  **Direction of the Force:** To keep an object in uniform circular motion, this force must act **perpendicular** to the instantaneous velocity of the object (pointing towards the center of the circle).
    *   If the force were *parallel* to the velocity (Option A), it would cause the speed to increase or decrease (acceleration or deceleration).
    *   By acting *perpendicular* to the velocity, the force changes the direction of the motion without changing the magnitude of the velocity (the speed).

### Evaluating the Options
*   **A is incorrect:** A force parallel to velocity changes speed, which contradicts "uniform" circular motion.
*   **B is incorrect:** Velocity is not constant in circular motion because the direction is constantly changing.
*   **C is correct:** In uniform circular motion, the speed remains constant, and the centripetal force acts in a direction perpendicular to the velocity to facilitate the circular path.
*   **D is incorrect:** Velocity is not constant in circular motion.

Therefore, the correct statement is **C**.

### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Check Velocity vs. Speed:** Always remember that "constant velocity" implies no change in speed *and* no change in direction. Since circular motion requires a change in direction, "constant velocity" is almost never the answer for circular motion.
2.  **Identify the Force Direction:** Recall that the centripetal force ($F_c$) is always directed toward the center of the circle, while the velocity vector ($v$) is tangent to the path. These two vectors are always perpendicular ($90^\circ$ to each other).
3.  **Eliminate options:** Quickly discard any option suggesting the velocity is constant, as that would describe linear motion with zero acceleration.
