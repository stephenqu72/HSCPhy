To solve this problem, we must identify the physical meaning of the variables in the given diffraction equation: $d \sin \theta = \lambda$.

1.  **Identify $d$:**
    In the context of the Young's double-slit experiment (or diffraction grating), $d$ represents the **slit separation** (the distance between the two slits). The problem explicitly states that the light passes through "two slits $1\ \mu\text{m}$ apart." Therefore, $d = 1\ \mu\text{m}$.

2.  **Identify $\theta$:**
    The angle $\theta$ represents the angle from the central maximum to the specific maximum being observed. By constructing a right-angled triangle between the slits and the screen:
    *   The **opposite** side of the triangle is the distance from the central maximum to the first maximum, which is $0.08\ \text{m}$.
    *   The **adjacent** side of the triangle is the perpendicular distance from the slits to the screen, which is $0.3\ \text{m}$.
    
    Using basic trigonometry, the definition of tangent is:
    $$\tan \theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = \frac{0.08}{0.3}$$
    Solving for $\theta$:
    $$\theta = \tan^{-1} \left( \frac{0.08}{0.3} \right)$$

3.  **Conclusion:**
    Comparing these values to the provided options:
    *   $d = 1\ \mu\text{m}$
    *   $\theta = \tan^{-1} \left( \frac{0.08}{0.3} \right)$
    
    This matches **Option C**.
