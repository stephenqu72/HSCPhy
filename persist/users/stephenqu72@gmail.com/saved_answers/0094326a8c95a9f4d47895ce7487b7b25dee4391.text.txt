To determine which side of the conductor has the greatest concentration of electrons, we apply the **Right-Hand Rule** (or the Left-Hand Rule for negative charges) to the free electrons moving within the conductor.

### Explanation:
1.  **Identify the physical principle**: When a conductor moves through a magnetic field, the free electrons within it experience a magnetic force given by the Lorentz force law: $\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$.
2.  **Determine the direction of the force**:
    *   The velocity vector $\vec{v}$ is pointing **downward**.
    *   The magnetic field vector $\vec{B}$ is pointing **into the page**.
    *   Using the Right-Hand Rule for a **positive charge**: Point your fingers in the direction of velocity (down), curl them into the page (direction of $B$), and your thumb points to the **left** (towards side $P$).
    *   Since electrons carry a **negative charge** ($q$ is negative), the force acts in the opposite direction. Therefore, the magnetic force on the electrons pushes them to the **right** (towards side $R$).
3.  **Conclusion**: As the conductor moves downward, the magnetic force causes electrons to migrate towards the right side of the conductor, resulting in a higher concentration of electrons at side $R$.

The correct answer is **(C)**.
