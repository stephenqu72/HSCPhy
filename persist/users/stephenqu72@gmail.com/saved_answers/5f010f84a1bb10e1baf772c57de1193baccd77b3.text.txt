### Answer
**None of the above.**

There is a technical inaccuracy in the phrasing of the question provided in the image. The force that holds quarks together is the **Strong Nuclear Force**, and the exchange particle (gauge boson) that mediates this force is the **gluon**. 

However, a **gluon is not a meson**. A meson is a subatomic particle composed of one quark and one antiquark. A gluon is an elementary particle (a gauge boson) that carries the strong color charge. Because "Gluon" is the only option that correctly identifies the particle responsible for holding quarks together, it is the intended answer, but it is scientifically misclassified as a "meson" in the question text.

2.  **Identify the Exchange Particle:** In the Standard Model, forces are mediated by gauge bosons.
