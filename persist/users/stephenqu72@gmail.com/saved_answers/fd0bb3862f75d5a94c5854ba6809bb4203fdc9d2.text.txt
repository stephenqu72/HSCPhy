#### Question 8
**Correct Answer: (A)**

**Explanation:**
Faraday’s Law of Induction states that the induced electromotive force (EMF), $\varepsilon$, is proportional to the negative rate of change of magnetic flux, $\Phi$:
$$\varepsilon = - \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$$

To find the correct EMF graph, we look at the slope (gradient) of the given magnetic flux graph:
1.  **At $t=0$:** The flux is at a negative maximum. The slope is momentarily zero (the tangent is horizontal), so the EMF starts at $0$.
2.  **Between $t=0$ and the first quarter mark:** The flux is increasing (becoming less negative). The gradient is positive. Because of the negative sign in Faraday's Law ($-\frac{d\Phi}{dt}$), the EMF must be **negative**.
3.  **At the first quarter mark:** The flux passes through zero. The slope is at its maximum positive value. Therefore, the EMF must be at its **negative maximum**.

Comparing these features to the options, graph (A) shows the EMF starting at 0, going to a negative maximum, crossing through zero, going to a positive maximum, and returning to zero. This matches the derivative behavior of the flux graph.
