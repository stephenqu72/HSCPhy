### Solutions

**a.** Using the conservation of energy, the work done by the electric field equals the change in kinetic energy:
$$eV_0 = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \implies v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV_0}{m}}$$

**b.** A magnetic field exerts a force on a moving charge given by $F = qvB$. According to the Right-Hand Rule, for a negative electron moving into a magnetic field directed into the page, the force is always perpendicular to the direction of motion. Because this force (the centripetal force) is always perpendicular to the velocity, it changes the direction but not the speed, resulting in uniform circular motion.

**c.** Setting the magnetic force equal to the centripetal force:
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r} \implies eBv = \frac{mv^2}{r} \implies eB = \frac{mv}{r}$$
Rearranging to show the relationship:
$$\frac{e}{m} = \frac{v}{Br}$$

**d.** 
- **Independent variable:** Voltage ($V_0$)
- **Dependent variable:** Radius squared ($r^2$) (Note: $r$ is measured, but $r^2$ is the plotted variable).
- **Controlled variable:** Magnetic field strength ($B$)

**e.** 
- $0.052^2 \approx 0.0027$
- $0.059^2 \approx 0.0035$
- $0.072^2 \approx 0.0052$

**f. & g. Calculation of Gradient:**
Using the points $(0.0013, 500)$ and $(0.0052, 2000)$:
$$\text{Gradient} = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{2000 - 500}{0.0052 - 0.0013} = \frac{1500}{0.0039} \approx 3.85 \times 10^5 \text{ V/m}^2$$

**h. Finding $e/m$:**
From the provided equation: $V_0 = (\frac{eB^2}{2m})r^2$.
The gradient of a $V_0$ vs $r^2$ graph is $k = \frac{eB^2}{2m}$.
Given $B = 2.0 \text{ mT} = 0.002 \text{ T}$:
$$\frac{e}{m} = \frac{2 \times \text{gradient}}{B^2} = \frac{2 \times 3.85 \times 10^5}{(0.002)^2} = \frac{7.7 \times 10^5}{4 \times 10^{-6}} = 1.925 \times 10^{11} \text{ C/kg}$$

### How to solve this
1. **Understand Energy Conservation:** Part (a) is a classic energy transformation problem where potential energy ($eV$) becomes kinetic energy ($1/2mv^2$).
2. **Apply Circular Motion Principles:** In part (b), recognize that the Lorentz force acts as the centripetal force. A key point is to state that the force is always perpendicular to velocity.
3. **Control Variables:** In part (d), identifying variables is essential. Since we are testing how $V_0$ affects the path, $V_0$ must be the independent variable.
4. **Graphing:** When calculating the gradient (part g), always use the line of best fit, not just the data points. Ensure your units are consistent (convert mT to T).
5. **Algebraic Manipulation:** In part (h), the goal is to map the physical equation $y = mx + c$ to the provided formula $V_0 = (\frac{eB^2}{2m})r^2$. Since $V_0$ is on the $y$-axis and $r^2$ is on the $x$-axis, the slope is $\frac{eB^2}{2m}$.
