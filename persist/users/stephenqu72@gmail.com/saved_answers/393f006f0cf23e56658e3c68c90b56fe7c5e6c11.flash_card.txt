### Front
In the Standard Model, what distinguishes leptons from hadrons (baryons/mesons)?

### Back
- Key idea: Leptons are fundamental (elementary) particles, whereas hadrons are composite particles made of quarks.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When classifying subatomic particles; remember leptons do not experience the strong nuclear force.
- Common trap: Mistaking muons as "non-particles" or misidentifying composite particles (hadrons) as fundamental.
