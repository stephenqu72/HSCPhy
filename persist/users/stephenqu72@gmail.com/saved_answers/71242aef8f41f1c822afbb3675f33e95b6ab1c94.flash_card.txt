### Front
How do you calculate the orbital velocity of a planet assuming a circular orbit?

### Back
- Key idea: Orbital velocity is the circumference of the orbit divided by the orbital period.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$
- When to use it: To determine the constant tangential speed of an object in a uniform circular orbit.
- Common trap: Failing to convert units into SI (e.g., leaving radius in km or time in days/hours) before calculating.
