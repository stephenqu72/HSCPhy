To solve this problem, we need to calculate the torque exerted on the tyre.

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   Force ($F$) = $1.40 \times 10^3 \text{ N} = 1400 \text{ N}$
*   Diameter ($d$) = $45 \text{ cm} = 0.45 \text{ m}$

### 2. Determine the radius:
The formula for torque uses the lever arm, which in the case of a tyre rotating on the ground, is the radius ($r$) of the tyre.
$$r = \frac{d}{2} = \frac{0.45 \text{ m}}{2} = 0.225 \text{ m}$$

### 3. Apply the torque formula:
Torque ($\tau$) is defined as the product of the force and the perpendicular distance from the pivot point:
$$\tau = F \times r$$
$$\tau = 1400 \text{ N} \times 0.225 \text{ m}$$
$$\tau = 315 \text{ Nm}$$

Comparing this result to the options provided:
*   A. $31.0 \text{ Nm}$
*   B. $315 \text{ Nm}$
*   C. $630 \text{ Nm}$
*   D. $6220 \text{ Nm}$

The correct option is **B**.

### Explanation for Students:
When solving torque problems, always remember to convert your units to SI units first. Physics problems often give diameters in centimetres, but the torque formula requires the radius in metres. By converting $45 \text{ cm}$ to $0.225 \text{ m}$, you ensure the final result is in Newton-metres ($\text{Nm}$). If you had accidentally used the diameter as the radius, you would have arrived at $630 \text{ Nm}$ (Option C), which is a common distractor!
