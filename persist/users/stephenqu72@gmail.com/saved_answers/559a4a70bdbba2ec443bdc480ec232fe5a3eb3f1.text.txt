To solve this projectile motion problem, we first resolve the initial velocity into vertical and horizontal components.
Initial velocity $u = 150\text{ m/s}$, angle $\theta = 20^\circ$.
Vertical component: $u_y = u \sin(20^\circ) = 150 \times \sin(20^\circ) \approx 51.303\text{ m/s}$.

### Part (a)
**1. Time to reach maximum height ($t_{up}$):**
At maximum height, the vertical velocity $v_y = 0$. Using $v_y = u_y + a_y t$:
$$0 = 51.303 - 9.8 \times t_{up}$$
$$t_{up} = \frac{51.303}{9.8} \approx 5.235\text{ s}$$

**2. Maximum height above the water ($H$):**
The displacement from the launch point is $\Delta y = u_y t_{up} + \frac{1}{2} a_y t_{up}^2$:
$$\Delta y = (51.303 \times 5.235) - (0.5 \times 9.8 \times 5.235^2) \approx 268.58 - 134.29 = 134.29\text{ m}$$
Since the cliff is $100\text{ m}$ high, the maximum height above the water is:
$$H = 100 + 134.29 = 234.29\text{ m}$$

---

### Part (b)
**Time to fall from maximum height ($t_{down}$):**
To find the time to fall from the peak ($234.29\text{ m}$) to the water level ($0\text{ m}$), we use the displacement formula starting from the peak where $u_{peak} = 0$:
$$\Delta y = u_{peak} t_{down} + \frac{1}{2} g t_{down}^2$$
$$-234.29 = 0 - \frac{1}{2} \times 9.8 \times t_{down}^2$$
$$234.29 = 4.9 \times t_{down}^2$$
$$t_{down}^2 = \frac{234.29}{4.9} \approx 47.81$$
$$t_{down} = \sqrt{47.81} \approx 6.91\text{ s}$$
