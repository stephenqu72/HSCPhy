To solve this problem, we use the formula for the escape velocity ($v_e$) of a planet:

$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

Where:
- $G$ is the universal gravitational constant ($6.67 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{m}^3 \text{kg}^{-1} \text{s}^{-2}$)
- $M$ is the mass of the planet
- $r$ is the radius of the planet

### Step-by-Step Calculation

**1. Calculate Escape Velocity for Mercury ($v_{e, \text{Mercury}}$):**
$$v_{e, \text{Mercury}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (3.30 \times 10^{23})}{2.44 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v_{e, \text{Mercury}} = \sqrt{\frac{4.4022 \times 10^{13}}{2.44 \times 10^6}} \approx \sqrt{1.804 \times 10^7} \approx 4247 \, \text{m/s} \approx 4.25 \, \text{km/s}$$

**2. Calculate Escape Velocity for Venus ($v_{e, \text{Venus}}$):**
$$v_{e, \text{Venus}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (4.87 \times 10^{24})}{6.02 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v_{e, \text{Venus}} = \sqrt{\frac{6.50086 \times 10^{14}}{6.02 \times 10^6}} \approx \sqrt{1.080 \times 10^8} \approx 10392 \, \text{m/s} \approx 10.39 \, \text{km/s}$$

**3. Comparison:**
Comparing the two values, the escape velocity of Venus ($10.39 \, \text{km/s}$) is significantly higher than that of Mercury ($4.25 \, \text{km/s}$). Specifically, the escape velocity of Venus is approximately $2.44$ times greater than that of Mercury.
