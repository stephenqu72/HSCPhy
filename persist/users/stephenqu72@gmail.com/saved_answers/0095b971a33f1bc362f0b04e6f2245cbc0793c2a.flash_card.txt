### Front
What is the formula and procedure for calculating the escape velocity ($v_e$) of a planet?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The minimum initial velocity required for an object to escape a planet's gravitational field, derived from the conservation of energy (kinetic + gravitational potential = 0).
- **Formula:** $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$ (where $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$).
- **When to use it:** When asked to calculate the speed required to leave a celestial body's surface or compare escape velocities between planets.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert the radius ($r$) from km to meters (m) or failing to provide a comparative ratio/statement when asked to "compare."
