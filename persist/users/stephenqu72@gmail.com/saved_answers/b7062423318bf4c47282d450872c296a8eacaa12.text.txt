### Answer

To generate torque in an AC induction motor, the following physical principles occur:

1.  **Rotating Magnetic Field:** When a three-phase AC supply is connected to the stator windings, it creates a rotating magnetic field ($RMF$) that spins at a constant synchronous speed.
2.  **Electromagnetic Induction:** This rotating magnetic field sweeps across the stationary conductive bars of the rotor. According to Faraday's Law, this changing magnetic flux induces an electromotive force ($EMF$) in the rotor bars.
3.  **Induced Current:** Because the rotor bars form a closed circuit (often via end rings), the induced $EMF$ drives a current through the rotor.
4.  **Lenz’s Law and Force:** The current-carrying rotor conductors now experience a force when placed within the stator's magnetic field, consistent with the Motor Effect ($F = BIL \sin \theta$). Lenz’s Law dictates that the direction of this induced current creates a magnetic field that opposes the change that caused it; thus, the rotor attempts to "catch up" to the rotating magnetic field to reduce the relative motion.
5.  **Torque Generation:** This interaction between the stator’s magnetic field and the current induced in the rotor creates a mechanical torque, causing the rotor to spin in the same direction as the rotating magnetic field.
