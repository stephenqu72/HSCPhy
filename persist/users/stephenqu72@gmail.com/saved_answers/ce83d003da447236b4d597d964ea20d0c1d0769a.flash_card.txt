### Front
Calculate the net force on a current-carrying rod of mass $0.1\text{ kg}$, length $1\text{ m}$, carrying $2\text{ A}$ of current in a $10\text{ T}$ magnetic field (where $F_B$ and $F_g$ act in the same direction).

### Back
- Key idea: Superposition of magnetic force ($F_B = BIL\sin\theta$) and weight ($F_g = mg$).
- Formula: $F_{net} = BIL\sin\theta + mg$
- When to use it: When a current-carrying conductor is subject to both a uniform magnetic field and a gravitational field.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert mass units (e.g., grams to kilograms) or neglecting the vector direction (ensuring $F_B$ and $F_g$ are correctly aligned).
