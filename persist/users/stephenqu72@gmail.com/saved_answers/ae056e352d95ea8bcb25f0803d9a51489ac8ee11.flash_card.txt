### Front
How does the acceleration due to gravity ($a_g$) of a planet in a circular orbit relate to its orbital radius ($r$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational acceleration is inversely proportional to the square of the orbital radius ($a_g \propto 1/r^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$ (derived from Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation and centripetal acceleration).
- When to use it: To compare the gravitational pull on two bodies orbiting the same central mass.
- Common trap: Confusing this with Kepler’s Third Law ($r^3/T^2 = k$), which relates radius to orbital *period*, not acceleration.
