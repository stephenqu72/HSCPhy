To solve this problem, we use the kinematics of projectile motion. Let the vertical displacement be $y$ and the horizontal displacement be $x$. The initial vertical velocity $u_y = 0$. The time taken to cover horizontal distance $d$ is $t = \frac{d}{v}$.

### Part 1: Gravity only
The particle falls $y = 0.2\text{ m}$. Using $y = \frac{1}{2}at^2$, where $a = g$:
$$0.2 = \frac{1}{2}g \left( \frac{d}{v} \right)^2 \implies \frac{d^2}{v^2} = \frac{0.4}{g}$$

### Part 2: Gravity + Electric Field
The particle is now fired at $2v$ and hits the upper surface at $y' = 0.2\text{ m}$. The horizontal distance is $x' = \frac{3d}{2}$.
The time taken is $t' = \frac{x'}{v'} = \frac{1.5d}{2v} = 0.75 \frac{d}{v}$.

Let the net vertical acceleration be $a_{net}$. Since the particle moves upward, the electric force $F_E$ must be directed upwards, opposing gravity:
$$a_{net} = \frac{F_E - mg}{m}$$
Using the displacement equation for the new path:
$$0.2 = \frac{1}{2} a_{net} (t')^2$$
$$0.2 = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{F_E - mg}{m} \right) \left( 0.75 \frac{d}{v} \right)^2$$
Substitute $\frac{d^2}{v^2} = \frac{0.4}{g}$ into the equation:
$$0.2 = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{F_E - mg}{m} \right) (0.5625) \left( \frac{0.4}{g} \right)$$
$$0.2 = \frac{F_E - mg}{m} (0.1125) \left( \frac{1}{g} \right)$$
$$0.2g = 0.1125 \left( \frac{F_E - mg}{m} \right)$$
$$\frac{0.2}{0.1125}g = \frac{F_E}{m} - g$$
$$\frac{2000}{1125}g = \frac{F_E}{m} - g \implies \frac{16}{9}g = \frac{F_E}{m} - g$$
$$\frac{F_E}{m} = \frac{16}{9}g + g = \frac{25}{9}g$$
$$F_E = \frac{25mg}{9}$$

The correct answer is **(D)**.
