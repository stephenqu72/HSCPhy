### Front
What defines an **inertial frame of reference**?

### Back
- Key idea: A coordinate system that is not accelerating (stationary or moving at a constant velocity) where Newton's Laws hold true.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s First Law of Motion.
- When to use it: Analyzing motion in classical mechanics and establishing the baseline for Special Relativity.
- Common trap: Thinking a frame is non-inertial just because it is moving; it must be **accelerating** to be non-inertial (and thus require fictitious forces).
