To solve this problem, we apply the **Principle of Moments**. For an object to be in rotational equilibrium, the sum of the clockwise torques must equal the sum of the counter-clockwise torques about the pivot point:

$$\sum \tau_{clockwise} = \sum \tau_{counter-clockwise}$$

The torque ($\tau$) is defined as the product of the force (weight, $mg$) and the perpendicular distance ($r$) from the pivot: $\tau = m \cdot g \cdot r$. Since gravity $g$ acts on all masses, it cancels out from both sides of the equation.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the counter-clockwise (left) torques:**
    There are two masses on the left side of the pivot: $m_1$ and $m_2$.
    - $m_1 = 150\text{ g}$, $r_1 = 49.0\text{ cm}$
    - $m_2 = 100\text{ g}$, $r_2 = 30.0\text{ cm}$
    
    The total counter-clockwise torque is:
    $$\tau_{ccw} = (m_1 \cdot r_1) + (m_2 \cdot r_2)$$
    $$\tau_{ccw} = (150 \cdot 49.0) + (100 \cdot 30.0)$$
    $$\tau_{ccw} = 7350 + 3000 = 10350\text{ g}\cdot\text{cm}$$

2.  **Identify the clockwise (right) torque:**
    There is one mass on the right side: $m_3$.
    - $r_3 = 39.0\text{ cm}$
    
    The clockwise torque is:
    $$\tau_{cw} = m_3 \cdot r_3 = m_3 \cdot 39.0$$

3.  **Equate and solve for $m_3$:**
    $$m_3 \cdot 39.0 = 10350$$
    $$m_3 = \frac{10350}{39.0}$$
    $$m_3 \approx 265.38\text{ g}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, **265 g** is the correct value.
