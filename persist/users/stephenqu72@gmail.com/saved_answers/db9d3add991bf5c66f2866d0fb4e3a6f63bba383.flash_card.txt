### Front
How is a nuclear fission chain reaction kept at a stable, self-sustaining "critical" state in a reactor?

### Back
- Key idea: Control rods (e.g., Boron/Cadmium) absorb excess neutrons to ensure exactly one neutron from each fission event induces a subsequent fission, preventing exponential growth.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: ${}_{0}^{1}n + {}_{92}^{235}U \rightarrow {}_{56}^{141}Ba + {}_{36}^{92}Kr + 3{}_{0}^{1}n$
- When to use it: When explaining nuclear reactor safety or the difference between a bomb (uncontrolled) and a power plant (controlled).
- Common trap: Confusing "moderators" (which slow neutrons to increase fission probability) with "control rods" (which absorb neutrons to stop the reaction).
