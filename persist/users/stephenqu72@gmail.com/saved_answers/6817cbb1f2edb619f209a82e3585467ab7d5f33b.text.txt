### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Answer:**
The centripetal acceleration $a_c$ of the block is given by the formula:
$$a_c = r\omega^2$$
To calculate this, you multiply the radius $r$ (in meters) by the square of the angular velocity $\omega$ (in radians per second).

**How to solve:**
Recall the relationship between linear velocity $v$ and angular velocity $\omega$, which is $v = r\omega$. Since centripetal acceleration is defined as $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$, substituting $r\omega$ for $v$ gives:
$$a_c = \frac{(r\omega)^2}{r} = \frac{r^2\omega^2}{r} = r\omega^2$$

#### Part (b)
**Answer:**
The block undergoes circular motion because the static frictional force between the block and the turntable surface acts as the centripetal force ($F_{net} = F_f$). As the angular velocity $\omega$ increases, the required centripetal force ($F_c = mr\omega^2$) increases. Static friction has a maximum limit ($F_{f,max} = \mu N$). When the required centripetal force exceeds this maximum possible static frictional force, the friction can no longer provide the acceleration necessary to keep the block in circular motion, causing the block to slide.

**How to solve:**
1.  **Identify the force:** State that static friction is the centripetal force providing the necessary acceleration for circular motion.
2.  **Explain the trend:** As $\omega$ increases, the required centripetal force $m r \omega^2$ increases.
3.  **Identify the limit:** Friction is a limiting force. Once $m r \omega^2 > \mu m g$, the surface can no longer provide enough force to maintain the circular path, resulting in slipping.
