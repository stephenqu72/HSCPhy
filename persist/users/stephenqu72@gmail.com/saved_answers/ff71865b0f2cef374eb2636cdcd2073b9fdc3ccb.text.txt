The correct answer is **D. Position of the peg.**

### Explanation:

To identify the independent variable in an experiment, you must determine what the student is intentionally changing or manipulating to see how it affects the other variables (the dependent variables).

1.  **Understand the setup:** The student has a rotating disc with three separate "peg holes" at different distances from the center (axle). By placing a peg into these different holes, the student is varying the **radius ($r$)** of the circular path.
2.  **Analyze the objective:** The goal is to investigate the relationship between the radius, angular velocity, and linear velocity.
3.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   **Independent Variable:** This is what the student controls. By selecting different peg holes, the student is controlling the distance from the axle, which is the **position of the peg** (the radius).
    *   **Dependent Variables:** These are the variables that change as a result of changing the radius, such as the linear velocity ($v = r\omega$) of the peg as it rotates.
    *   **Controlled Variables:** The angular velocity ($\omega$) is typically kept constant by the motor driving the turntable, while the distance to the wall is a constant part of the apparatus setup and does not change during the trial.

Since the student chooses which hole to place the peg in to observe the effect on the motion, the position of the peg is the independent variable.
