To determine the threshold wavelength required to eject an electron from the surface of aluminium, we use the relationship between energy and wavelength in the photoelectric effect.

### Answer
The correct option is **(D) $3.04 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$**.

### Explanation
1. **Understand the Physics:** The work function ($\Phi$) is the minimum energy required to remove an electron from a metal surface. This is related to the threshold frequency ($f_0$) and threshold wavelength ($\lambda_0$) by the equation:
   $$\Phi = \frac{hc}{\lambda_0}$$
   Where:
   - $h$ is Planck's constant ($6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$)
   - $c$ is the speed of light ($3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$)
   - $\Phi$ is the work function in Joules.

2. **Convert Units:** The work function is given in electron-volts (eV). We must convert this to Joules (J) using the conversion factor $1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$.
   $$\Phi = 4.08 \text{ eV} \times (1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J/eV}) = 6.536 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

3. **Rearrange and Calculate:** Rearrange the formula to solve for wavelength ($\lambda$):
   $$\lambda = \frac{hc}{\Phi}$$
   $$\lambda = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s})}{6.536 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}}$$
   $$\lambda \approx \frac{1.9878 \times 10^{-25}}{6.536 \times 10^{-19}} \text{ m}$$
   $$\lambda \approx 3.041 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$$

Rounding to three significant figures gives $3.04 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$, which matches option (D).
