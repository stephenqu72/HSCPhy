### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) The alternating bands of bright light would become more separated.**

### Explanation
To solve this, we need to use the mathematical relationship for Young's Double Slit experiment. The separation between adjacent bright fringes (the fringe width, denoted by $\Delta x$) is given by the formula:

$$\Delta x = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$

Where:
*   $\Delta x$ = fringe separation (the distance between adjacent bright bands)
*   $\lambda$ = wavelength of the light used
*   $L$ = distance from the slits to the screen
*   $d$ = distance between the two slits

**Step-by-step reasoning:**
1.  **Identify the variables:** The experiment setup ($L$ and $d$) remains unchanged. The only variable being altered is the wavelength ($\lambda$).
2.  **Analyze the relationship:** Looking at the formula, we can see that the fringe separation $\Delta x$ is **directly proportional** to the wavelength $\lambda$ (i.e., $\Delta x \propto \lambda$).
3.  **Apply the change:** You are changing the laser from green ($\lambda = 532\text{ nm}$) to red ($\lambda = 633\text{ nm}$). Since $633\text{ nm} > 532\text{ nm}$, the wavelength has increased.
4.  **Conclusion:** Because the wavelength has increased, and $\Delta x$ is directly proportional to $\lambda$, the distance between the bright bands ($\Delta x$) must also increase. Therefore, the bands become more separated (spread out).
