To solve this problem, we use the formula for the force per unit length between two parallel current-carrying wires:

$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$

### Given values:
*   Permeability of free space, $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \, \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A}$
*   Current in the first wire, $I_1 = 1 \, \text{A}$
*   Current in the second wire, $I_2 = 1 \, \text{A}$
*   Distance between the wires, $r = 1 \, \text{m}$

### Step-by-step Calculation:
1.  **Substitute the values into the formula:**
    $$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times 1 \times 1}{2\pi \times 1}$$

2.  **Simplify the expression:**
    *   The $\pi$ terms cancel out.
    *   $4 / 2 = 2$.
    *   The result is $2 \times 10^{-7}$.

3.  **Determine the units:**
    Since the question asks for the "force per metre length," the unit is Newtons per metre, written as $\text{N/m}$ or $\text{Nm}^{-1}$.
