To solve this problem, we must analyze the physics of circular motion on a banked track where the normal force provides the necessary centripetal force in the absence of friction.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
For a car on a banked track with no friction, the centripetal force $F_c$ is provided by the horizontal component of the normal force $N$.
- The vertical component balances gravity: $N \cos \theta = mg$
- The horizontal component provides centripetal force: $N \sin \theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$

Dividing these equations gives the standard banked track formula:
$$\tan \theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

### 2. Convert Units
The speed $v$ is given in km/h. We must convert this to SI units (m/s):
$$v = 50 \text{ km/h} = \frac{50}{3.6} \approx 13.889 \text{ m/s}$$

### 3. Calculate the Radius of Motion
The radius $r$ is the total horizontal distance from the centre of the track to the car.
- The track starts at $30\text{ m}$.
- The car is at a height $h$ on a $20^{\circ}$ slope. The horizontal distance from the start of the banking to the car is $x = \frac{h}{\tan 20^{\circ}}$.
- Therefore, $r = 30 + \frac{h}{\tan 20^{\circ}}$.

### 4. Solve for $h$
Substitute the variables into the banked track formula:
$$\tan 20^{\circ} = \frac{v^2}{(30 + \frac{h}{\tan 20^{\circ}})g}$$

Rearranging for $r$:
$$r = \frac{v^2}{g \tan 20^{\circ}} = \frac{13.889^2}{9.8 \times \tan 20^{\circ}} \approx \frac{192.9}{3.567} \approx 54.08 \text{ m}$$

Now find $h$ using the relationship $r = 30 + \frac{h}{\tan 20^{\circ}}$:
$$54.08 = 30 + \frac{h}{\tan 20^{\circ}}$$
$$24.08 = \frac{h}{\tan 20^{\circ}}$$
$$h = 24.08 \times \tan 20^{\circ} \approx 24.08 \times 0.364 \approx 8.76 \text{ m}$$

**Answer:** The car will travel safely at a height of approximately **$8.76\text{ m}$**.
