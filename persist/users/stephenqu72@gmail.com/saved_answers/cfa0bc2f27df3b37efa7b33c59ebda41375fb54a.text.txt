To determine the correct expression for the centripetal force in this scenario, we must analyze the forces acting on the mass $m$.

### Explanation

1.  **Identify the Forces:** There are two forces acting on the mass:
    *   The gravitational force, $mg$, acting vertically downwards.
    *   The normal force, $N$, acting perpendicular to the surface of the banked track.

2.  **Define the Direction of Centripetal Force:** Since the object is undergoing circular motion on a horizontal track, the net centripetal force must act **horizontally** towards the center of the circular path.

3.  **Resolve the Normal Force ($N$):** We can split the normal force $N$ into two perpendicular components:
    *   The **vertical component**: $N \cos\theta$. This must balance the weight of the object ($mg$) so that the mass maintains a constant height. Thus, $N \cos\theta = mg$.
    *   The **horizontal component**: $N \sin\theta$. This component points towards the center of the circular path. 

4.  **Conclusion:** Because the horizontal component of the normal force ($N \sin\theta$) is the only horizontal force acting on the mass, it provides the required centripetal force for the circular motion.

Therefore, the correct expression is $N \sin\theta$.

**Correct Option: (A)**

### How to solve it
*   **Draw a Free Body Diagram (FBD):** Always draw the forces $N$ and $mg$.
*   **Choose your axes:** Use a standard coordinate system where the x-axis is horizontal (the direction of acceleration) and the y-axis is vertical (the direction of equilibrium).
*   **Apply Newton's Second Law:**
    *   Vertical: $\sum F_y = N \cos\theta - mg = 0$
    *   Horizontal: $\sum F_x = N \sin\theta = F_{centripetal} = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
*   By projecting $N$ onto the horizontal axis, you identify that $N \sin\theta$ is the net force providing the centripetal acceleration.
