To solve this problem, we use the formula for the magnetic force $F$ on a charged particle moving through a magnetic field:

$$F = qvB\sin(\theta)$$

Since both particles move at right angles to the magnetic field, $\sin(90^{\circ}) = 1$, so the formula simplifies to $F = qvB$.

### Step 1: Define the variables for the alpha particle
Let $q_{\alpha}$, $v_{\alpha}$, and $B_{\alpha}$ be the charge, velocity, and magnetic flux density for the alpha particle. The force on the alpha particle is:
$$F = q_{\alpha} v_{\alpha} B_{\alpha}$$

### Step 2: Define the variables for the beta particle
We know the following relationships:
*   The charge of an alpha particle is $+2e$. The charge of a beta particle (an electron) is $-e$. In terms of magnitude, $|q_{\beta}| = 0.5 \times |q_{\alpha}|$.
*   The velocity of the beta particle is $v_{\beta} = 10v_{\alpha}$.
*   The magnetic field is $B_{\beta} = 0.5B_{\alpha}$.

### Step 3: Calculate the force on the beta particle ($F_{\beta}$)
Substitute these values into the force formula:
$$F_{\beta} = |q_{\beta}| \cdot v_{\beta} \cdot B_{\beta}$$
$$F_{\beta} = (0.5 q_{\alpha}) \cdot (10 v_{\alpha}) \cdot (0.5 B_{\alpha})$$

Now, group the numerical coefficients:
$$F_{\beta} = (0.5 \times 10 \times 0.5) \cdot (q_{\alpha} v_{\alpha} B_{\alpha})$$
$$F_{\beta} = 2.5 \cdot (q_{\alpha} v_{\alpha} B_{\alpha})$$
