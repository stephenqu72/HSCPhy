The correct answer is **(C)**.

### Explanation

To find the mass of an electron, physicists required two key pieces of information:

1.  **The charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$):** This was first determined by J.J. Thomson in 1897 using his cathode ray tube experiment. By measuring the deflection of electron beams in known electric and magnetic fields, he found that the value of $e/m_e$ was approximately $1.76 \times 10^{11} \text{ C/kg}$.
2.  **The fundamental charge of the electron ($e$):** In 1909, Robert Millikan performed the famous **Oil Drop Experiment**. By balancing the gravitational, buoyant, and electrostatic forces on tiny charged oil droplets, he was able to determine the magnitude of the elementary charge, $e \approx 1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$.

Once both values were known, the mass of the electron ($m_e$) could be calculated by rearranging the charge-to-mass ratio:

$$m_e = \frac{e}{(e/m_e)}$$

While Thomson provided the ratio, it was the specific measurement of the charge $e$ from **Millikan’s oil drop experiment** that finally allowed scientists to isolate and calculate the mass of the electron.
