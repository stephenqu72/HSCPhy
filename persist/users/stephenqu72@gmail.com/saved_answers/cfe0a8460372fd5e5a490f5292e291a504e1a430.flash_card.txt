### Front
How do you determine the ratio of gravitational field strengths ($g_1:g_2$) between two spherical bodies given their masses and radii?

### Back
- Key idea: Use the proportionality $g \propto \frac{M}{r^2}$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$; ratio formula: $\frac{g_1}{g_2} = \frac{M_1}{M_2} \times (\frac{r_2}{r_1})^2$.
- When to use it: Whenever comparing gravitational acceleration on the surface of planets or moons.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the radius ratio or inverting the radii ($r_2/r_1$ vs $r_1/r_2$).
