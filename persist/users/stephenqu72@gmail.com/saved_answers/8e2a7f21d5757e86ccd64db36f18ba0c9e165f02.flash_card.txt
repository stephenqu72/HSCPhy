### Front
When the Earth exerts a gravitational force on the Moon, does the Earth accelerate? Why?

### Back
- Key idea: According to Newton’s Third Law, the Moon exerts an equal and opposite force on the Earth. By Newton’s Second Law ($F=ma$), this force causes the Earth to accelerate toward the Moon.
- Formula / law / constant: Newton’s 2nd Law ($F=ma$) and 3rd Law (Action-Reaction).
- When to use it: Any question involving the relative motion or forces between two celestial bodies.
- Common trap: Assuming that because the Earth's acceleration is negligible, the force on it is zero. The forces are always equal; only the accelerations differ due to the massive difference in $m$.
