To calculate the work function ($\Phi$) of copper, we use the relationship between the threshold frequency ($f_0$) and the energy required to eject an electron from the metal surface, defined by Einstein's photoelectric equation:

$$\Phi = h f_0$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the given values and constants:**
*   Threshold frequency ($f_0$): $1.0 \times 10^9 \text{ MHz}$
*   First, convert MHz to Hz: $1 \text{ MHz} = 10^6 \text{ Hz}$.
*   Therefore, $f_0 = 1.0 \times 10^9 \times 10^6 = 1.0 \times 10^{15} \text{ Hz}$.
*   Planck's constant ($h$): $6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$

**2. Calculate the work function in Joules:**
$$\Phi = (6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}) \times (1.0 \times 10^{15} \text{ Hz})$$
$$\Phi = 6.626 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

**3. Convert Joules to electron-volts (eV):**
To convert from Joules to eV, divide by the elementary charge ($1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$):
$$\Phi (\text{eV}) = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}}{1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J/eV}}$$
$$\Phi \approx 4.136 \text{ eV}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get **4.1 eV**.
