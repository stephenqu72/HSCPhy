To solve this problem, we need to track the nuclear reactions involving the transmutation of Silicon-28 into Phosphorus. This is a common application of Neutron Transmutation Doping (NTD) in semiconductor physics.

### Answer

The process involves two main stages: neutron capture followed by beta-minus decay.

1.  **Neutron Capture:** Silicon-28 captures a neutron to become Silicon-29:
    $${}^{28}_{14}\text{Si} + {}^{1}_{0}\text{n} \rightarrow {}^{29}_{14}\text{Si} + \gamma$$
    *(Note: Silicon-29 then captures another neutron to become unstable Silicon-31)*:
    $${}^{29}_{14}\text{Si} + {}^{1}_{0}\text{n} \rightarrow {}^{31}_{14}\text{Si} + \gamma$$

2.  **Beta Decay:** The unstable Silicon-31 nucleus undergoes $\beta^-$ decay to transform into Phosphorus-31:
    $${}^{31}_{14}\text{Si} \rightarrow {}^{31}_{15}\text{P} + {}^{0}_{-1}e + \bar{\nu}_e$$

### How to solve this
To achieve full marks (3 marks), you must demonstrate an understanding of nuclear conservation laws (conservation of atomic number $Z$ and mass number $A$):

*   **Step 1: Identify the starting point.** You start with Silicon ($Z=14$). When it captures a neutron ($A=1, Z=0$), the mass number increases by 1.
*   **Step 2: Understand the sequence.** The silicon absorbs neutrons until it becomes an unstable isotope ($^{31}\text{Si}$).
*   **Step 3: Apply Beta Decay rules.** Beta-minus ($\beta^-$) decay occurs when a neutron in the nucleus converts into a proton, emitting an electron ($^{0}_{-1}e$) and an antineutrino ($\bar{\nu}_e$). 
    *   The mass number ($A$) stays the same (31).
    *   The atomic number ($Z$) increases by 1 ($14 \rightarrow 15$), which changes the element from Silicon to Phosphorus.
*   **Step 4: Check your work.** Ensure that the sum of the top numbers ($A$) and bottom numbers ($Z$) are balanced on both sides of every equation.
