### Step 1: Calculate the wavelengths produced by the transitions
In a gas discharge tube, electrons transition from higher energy levels to lower ones, emitting photons where the energy of the photon $E = \Delta E$. The wavelength $\lambda$ is given by $\lambda = \frac{hc}{\Delta E}$, where $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J s}$ and $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}$.

The energy levels are: $E_4 = 4.92 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}$, $E_3 = 9.29 \times 10^{-20} \, \text{J}$, $E_2 = 3.52 \times 10^{-20} \, \text{J}$, and $E_1 = 0$.

*   **Transition $4 \rightarrow 3$:** $\Delta E = (4.92 - 0.929) \times 10^{-19} = 3.991 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}$
    $\lambda_{43} = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34})(3 \times 10^8)}{3.991 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 498 \, \text{nm}$
*   **Transition $4 \rightarrow 2$:** $\Delta E = (4.92 - 0.352) \times 10^{-19} = 4.568 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}$
    $\lambda_{42} = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34})(3 \times 10^8)}{4.568 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 435 \, \text{nm}$
*   **Transition $3 \rightarrow 2$:** $\Delta E = (9.29 - 3.52) \times 10^{-20} = 5.77 \times 10^{-20} \, \text{J}$
    $\lambda_{32} = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34})(3 \times 10^8)}{5.77 \times 10^{-20}} \approx 3445 \, \text{nm}$ (Infrared, not visible)

*Note: The question specifies three visible wavelengths; assuming transitions to level 1 were omitted or transitions to level 2 are the primary visible ones.*

### Step 2: Determine the wavelength from the double-slit experiment
For the first-order maximum ($n=1$), the double-slit formula is:
$$d \sin \theta = n \lambda$$
Given $d = 1.0 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{m}$ and $\theta = 2.32^\circ$:
$$\lambda = d \sin \theta = (1.0 \times 10^{-5}) \times \sin(2.32^\circ)$$
$$\lambda \approx (1.0 \times 10^{-5}) \times 0.04048 \approx 4.048 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m} = 405 \, \text{nm}$$

### Conclusion
Comparing our calculated values, the experimental wavelength is approximately $405 \, \text{nm}$. Re-evaluating the transition $4 \rightarrow 1$:
$\Delta E = 4.92 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J} \implies \lambda = \frac{1.988 \times 10^{-25}}{4.92 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 404 \, \text{nm}$.
