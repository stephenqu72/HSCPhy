### Part (a)
**Answer:**
Rutherford’s gold foil experiment showed that while most alpha particles passed through the foil undeflected, a small proportion were deflected at large angles, and a very few were reflected directly backward. This contradicted the prevailing Thomson "plum pudding" model. Rutherford concluded that the atom consists of a tiny, dense, positively charged nucleus at the center containing most of the atom's mass. Because most particles passed through, the atom must be mostly empty space. The large deflections indicated strong electrostatic repulsion between the positive alpha particles and the concentrated positive charge of the nucleus.

**How to solve:**
To get 3 marks, you must link the observations to the deductions:
1.  **Observation:** Most particles passed through. **Deduction:** The atom is mostly empty space.
2.  **Observation:** Some particles deflected at large angles. **Deduction:** There is a concentrated, dense, positive charge (the nucleus).
3.  **Conclusion:** This forced the shift from a uniform charge distribution to a nuclear model.

### Part (b)
**Answer:**
Before Chadwick, it was observed that the mass of a nucleus was greater than the sum of its protons (e.g., Helium has 2 protons but 4 units of mass). When Chadwick bombarded beryllium with alpha particles, he observed the emission of a neutral radiation that could eject protons from paraffin wax. By applying the laws of conservation of momentum and energy to these collisions, he determined that the neutral radiation consisted of particles with a mass approximately equal to that of a proton. These were named neutrons, solving the discrepancy in atomic mass.

**How to solve:**
To get 3 marks, follow this logical progression:
1.  **The Problem:** Mention the discrepancy between atomic number and atomic mass (the "missing mass" issue).
2.  **The Experiment:** Describe the bombardment of beryllium and the resulting "neutral" radiation.
3.  **The Conclusion:** Explain how the interaction with paraffin wax proved the existence of a neutral particle with mass, confirming the presence of the neutron within the nucleus.
