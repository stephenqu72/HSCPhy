### (b) Explanation and Prediction
**Physics Principles:**
When a motor rotates, the coil moves through a magnetic field. According to Faraday’s Law and Lenz’s Law, this induces a **back electromotive force (back EMF)**. The net voltage ($V_{net}$) across the coil is the difference between the supply voltage ($V_{supply}$) and the back EMF ($\varepsilon_{back}$):
$$V_{net} = V_{supply} - \varepsilon_{back}$$
As the speed of the motor increases, the rate of change of magnetic flux through the coil increases, causing the back EMF to increase. Consequently, $V_{net}$ decreases as the motor speeds up.

**Prediction:**
The maximum operating speed occurs when the back EMF equals the supply voltage ($V_{supply} = 12.0\text{ V}$). At this point, the net voltage across the coil would be **$0\text{ V}$**, because no current would flow to provide further torque to accelerate the motor.

### (c) Evaluating the student's conclusion
**Evaluation:**
The student's conclusion is **incorrect**.

**Reasoning:**
*   **Mathematical evidence:** For a relationship to be "directly proportional," the graph must be a straight line passing through the origin ($y = mx$). 
*   **Observation:** The data points do not form a straight line; the rate of change in voltage is not constant as speed increases. Furthermore, at a speed of $0\text{ rpm}$, the voltage is $12\text{ V}$, not $0\text{ V}$. Therefore, the relationship is non-linear and does not satisfy the conditions for direct proportionality.
