### Answer
1. **Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) Radiation:** This is the uniform, faint glow of microwave radiation detected from every direction in the sky. It is considered the "afterglow" of the Big Bang, representing the moment the universe cooled enough for atoms to form and photons to travel freely (decoupling).
2. **Hubble’s Law (Redshift of Galaxies):** Observations show that distant galaxies are moving away from us at speeds proportional to their distance. This provides evidence that the universe is currently expanding, implying that at some point in the past, all matter was concentrated at a single, extremely dense point.
3. **Abundance of Light Elements:** The Big Bang model predicts the specific primordial ratio of light elements in the universe, specifically hydrogen, helium, and trace amounts of lithium. The observed ratios in the oldest stars and gas clouds align precisely with the theoretical predictions of Big Bang Nucleosynthesis.
