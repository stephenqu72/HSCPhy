### Answer

The four fundamental forces of nature are essential to the structure and interactions of the universe:

1.  **Strong Nuclear Force:** This is the strongest of the four forces. Its role is to bind quarks together to form protons and neutrons, and to bind protons and neutrons together within an atomic nucleus, overcoming the electrostatic repulsion between positively charged protons.
2.  **Electromagnetic Force:** This force acts between particles that possess electric charge. Its role is to govern interactions between charged particles, such as binding electrons to the nucleus to form atoms, enabling the formation of chemical bonds, and mediating all electric and magnetic phenomena.
3.  **Weak Nuclear Force:** This force is responsible for particle decay and radioactivity, specifically beta decay. It plays a crucial role in nuclear fusion processes, such as those that power the Sun, by allowing for the transformation of one type of quark into another.
4.  **Gravitational Force:** Although the weakest of the four forces, it has an infinite range and is always attractive. Its role is to govern the motion of planets, stars, and galaxies, and it is responsible for the large-scale structure of the universe by attracting all objects with mass.

To answer a question like this in an HSC Physics examination, follow these steps:

1.  **Analyze the Verb:** The command word is "Outline." This means you must provide a brief description of the main features or functions of each force. You do not need to provide deep mathematical derivations, but you must clearly state what each force "does."
2.  **Allocate Marks:** The question is worth 4 marks. This typically implies that you should provide a distinct, accurate point for each of the four forces.
3.  **Use Specific Terminology:** Ensure you mention the key physical domain of each force. For instance, clearly distinguish between the "nuclear" forces (strong and weak) and the "long-range" forces (electromagnetic and gravitational).
4.  **Structure for Clarity:** Use a list format or clear paragraphs to ensure the examiner can easily identify your four distinct points. This ensures you satisfy the marking criteria for all 4 marks.
