To find the separation $d$ between the two wires, we use the formula for the force per unit length between two parallel current-carrying wires:

$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2 \pi d}$$

### Given data:
*   Force per unit length $\frac{F}{L} = 5.5 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N m}^{-1}$
*   Current in Wire X, $I_1 = 2 \text{ A}$
*   Current in Wire Y, $I_2 = 3 \text{ A}$
*   Permeability of free space, $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T m A}^{-1}$

### Step-by-step Solution:

    $$d = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2 \pi (F/L)}$$

    $$d = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T m A}^{-1}) \times 2 \text{ A} \times 3 \text{ A}}{2 \pi \times 5.5 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N m}^{-1}}$$

3.  **Simplify the expression:**
    Notice that $\frac{4\pi}{2\pi} = 2$:
    $$d = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 2 \times 3}{5.5 \times 10^{-5}}$$

    $$d = \frac{12}{5.5} \times 10^{-2} \text{ m}$$
