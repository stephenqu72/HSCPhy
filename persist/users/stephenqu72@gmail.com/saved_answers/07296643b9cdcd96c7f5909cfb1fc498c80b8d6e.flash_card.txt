### Front
Describe the horizontal and vertical acceleration components of a projectile in ideal motion (ignoring air resistance).

### Back
- Key idea: Horizontal motion is constant velocity; vertical motion is constant acceleration.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_x = 0$; $a_y = g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ (downward).
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem (e.g., trajectory, range, or flight time calculations).
- Common trap: Assuming velocity is zero at the peak of the flight; only the *vertical* velocity component is zero, while the horizontal velocity remains constant.
