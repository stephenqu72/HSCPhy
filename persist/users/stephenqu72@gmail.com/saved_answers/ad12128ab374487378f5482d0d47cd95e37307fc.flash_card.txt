### Front
How does measuring 20 rotations instead of 10 affect the reliability of a circular motion experiment?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Increasing the number of rotations reduces the relative percentage uncertainty caused by human reaction time.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** Whenever asked to justify experimental design improvements regarding timing or period measurements.
- **Common trap:** Stating it "eliminates" error; it only *minimizes* the impact of reaction time error relative to the total time measured.
