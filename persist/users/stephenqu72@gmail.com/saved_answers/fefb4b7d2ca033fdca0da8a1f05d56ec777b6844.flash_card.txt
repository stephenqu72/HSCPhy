### Front
How must net forces be balanced for an oil droplet moving at a **constant speed** in both downward and upward directions?

### Back
- Key idea: Net force ($\sum F$) must be zero (Newton's First Law).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Downwards: $F_G = F_F$; Upwards: $F_E = F_G + F_F$
- When to use it: Whenever a problem specifies "constant speed," "terminal velocity," or "equilibrium."
- Common trap: Forgetting that the direction of friction ($F_F$) must always oppose the direction of motion.
