### Answer

**(a) The two postulates of Einstein's special theory of relativity are:**

1. The laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames of reference.
2. The speed of light in a vacuum, $c$, is constant for all observers, regardless of the motion of the light source or the observer.

**(b) Calculation:**

First, we determine the proper length ($L_0$) of the spaceship using the first scenario:
$$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
$$3.57 = L_0 \sqrt{1 - (0.850)^2}$$
$$3.57 = L_0 \sqrt{1 - 0.7225}$$
$$3.57 = L_0 \sqrt{0.2775}$$
$$L_0 = \frac{3.57}{0.52678} \approx 6.777 \text{ m}$$

Now, we use this proper length to find the new velocity ($v$) when the measured length is $L = 2.50 \text{ m}$:
$$2.50 = 6.777 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
$$\frac{2.50}{6.777} = \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
$$0.3689 = \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
$$0.1361 = 1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$$
$$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 0.8639$$
$$\frac{v}{c} = \sqrt{0.8639} \approx 0.929$$
The speed is $0.929c$.
