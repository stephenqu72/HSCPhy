To calculate the energy released during this fission reaction, we need to determine the mass defect ($\Delta m$) and convert it into energy ($E$) using Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle.

### Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Identify the masses of all particles involved:**
*   Mass of $n \approx 1.6749 \times 10^{-27}$ kg (standard value).
*   Mass of ${}^{235}\text{U} = 3.9017 \times 10^{-25}$ kg
*   Mass of ${}^{141}\text{Ba} = 2.28922 \times 10^{-25}$ kg
*   Mass of ${}^{92}\text{Kr} = 1.57534 \times 10^{-25}$ kg

**2. Calculate the mass of the reactants and products:**
*   **Reactants:** Mass of ${}^{235}\text{U}$ + Mass of $1$ neutron
    $$M_{\text{reactants}} = 3.9017 \times 10^{-25} + 0.016749 \times 10^{-25} = 3.918449 \times 10^{-25} \text{ kg}$$
*   **Products:** Mass of ${}^{141}\text{Ba}$ + Mass of ${}^{92}\text{Kr}$ + Mass of $3$ neutrons
    $$M_{\text{products}} = 2.28922 \times 10^{-25} + 1.57534 \times 10^{-25} + 3(0.016749 \times 10^{-25})$$
    $$M_{\text{products}} = 3.86456 \times 10^{-25} + 0.050247 \times 10^{-25} = 3.914807 \times 10^{-25} \text{ kg}$$

**3. Calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$):**
$$\Delta m = M_{\text{reactants}} - M_{\text{products}}$$
$$\Delta m = 3.918449 \times 10^{-25} - 3.914807 \times 10^{-25} = 3.642 \times 10^{-28} \text{ kg}$$

**4. Calculate Energy in Joules using $E = \Delta m c^2$:**
(Using $c = 2.9979 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$)
$$E = (3.642 \times 10^{-28}) \times (2.9979 \times 10^8)^2$$
$$E \approx 3.273 \times 10^{-11} \text{ J}$$

**5. Convert Joules to MeV:**
Knowing $1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$, then $1 \text{ MeV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-13} \text{ J}$.
$$E (\text{MeV}) = \frac{3.273 \times 10^{-11} \text{ J}}{1.602 \times 10^{-13} \text{ J/MeV}} \approx 204.3 \text{ MeV}$$

**Final Answer:** The energy released is approximately **204 MeV**.
