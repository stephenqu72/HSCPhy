To solve this problem, we apply the principles of electromagnetism related to the DC motor effect.

### Part (a): Determine the torque on the side $WZ$

**1. Understand the physics:**
The force on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field is given by $F = nBIL\sin(\theta)$, where:
- $n = 20$ (number of turns)
- $B = 2.0\text{ T}$
- $I = 2.5\text{ A}$
- $L = 0.04\text{ m}$ (length of the side)
- $\theta$ is the angle between the wire and the magnetic field. Since the field is horizontal and the coil is flat, the angle is $90^\circ$, so $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

**2. Calculate the force ($F$):**
$$F = 20 \times 2.0 \times 2.5 \times 0.04 = 4.0\text{ N}$$

**3. Calculate the torque ($\tau$):**
Torque is defined as $\tau = F \times r$, where $r$ is the perpendicular distance from the axis of rotation. The axis of rotation is exactly in the middle of the square. Therefore, the distance $r$ from the axis to the side $WZ$ is half the width of the coil:
$$r = \frac{0.04\text{ m}}{2} = 0.02\text{ m}$$
$$\tau = 4.0\text{ N} \times 0.02\text{ m} = 0.08\text{ Nm}$$

**Answer:** The torque on side $WZ$ is $0.08\text{ Nm}$.

### Part (b): Explain the continuous movement of the coil

The movement of a DC motor is continuous due to the **split-ring commutator**.

1. **Torque Variation:** As the coil rotates, the torque ($\tau = nBIA\cos\theta$) changes. When the coil is in the horizontal position (as shown), the torque is at its maximum because the force is perpendicular to the lever arm. As the coil rotates to the vertical position, the torque drops to zero.
2. **Role of the Commutator:** Without a commutator, the coil would stop or oscillate once it reached the vertical position. As the coil passes the vertical position, the split-ring commutator reverses the direction of the current flowing through the coil sides ($WZ$ and $XY$).
3. **Continuous Rotation:** By reversing the current, the direction of the magnetic force on each side is reversed relative to the axis of rotation. This ensures that the torque acts in the same rotational direction for every half-turn, preventing the coil from stalling and allowing it to continue spinning.
