### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must apply the physical principles governing electric fields between parallel plates:

1.  **Uniform Electric Field:** In the space between two large, parallel, oppositely charged plates, the electric field strength ($E$) is uniform (constant). This means that at any point between the plates, the electric field has the same magnitude and direction.

2.  **Force on a Charge:** The magnitude of the electric force ($F$) acting on a charge ($q$) placed in an electric field is given by the formula:
    $$F = qE$$

3.  **Analysis:**
    *   Since the electric field $E$ is constant between the plates, and the charge $Q$ is constant, the magnitude of the force $F$ must also be constant.
    *   As the charge moves from distance $0$ (at point X) to distance $d$ (at point Y), the force acting on it does not increase, decrease, or change based on its position.
    *   Graph **C** shows a constant, non-zero value for $F$ regardless of the distance from the starting plate. Therefore, it is the only graph that correctly represents a constant force.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **A:** Suggests the force increases linearly with distance, which is incorrect for a uniform field.
*   **B:** Suggests the force decreases linearly, which is incorrect.
*   **D:** Suggests an inverse-square or decay relationship (like a point charge), which applies to isolated point charges, not the uniform field created by parallel plates.
