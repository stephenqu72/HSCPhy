### Answer
Transformers operate based on the principle of **Faraday’s Law of Induction**. When an alternating current (AC) is applied to the primary coil (in this case, $120\text{V}$ at $60\text{Hz}$), it creates a changing magnetic flux within the transformer's iron core. This changing magnetic flux passes through the secondary coil, inducing an alternating electromotive force (EMF) across it (the $12\text{VAC}$ output).

By applying the transformer equation $\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$, we can see that since the input voltage is $120\text{V}$ and the output is $12\text{V}$, this is a step-down transformer where the primary coil has ten times as many turns as the secondary coil. Furthermore, the efficiency can be evaluated by comparing input power ($P_{in} = V_p \times I_p = 120\text{V} \times 0.55\text{A} = 66\text{W}$) to output power ($12\text{W}$). Since $P_{out} < P_{in}$, energy is being dissipated as heat due to factors like eddy currents and resistance.
