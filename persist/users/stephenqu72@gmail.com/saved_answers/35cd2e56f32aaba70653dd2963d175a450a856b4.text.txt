### Answer

In the Standard Model of particle physics, quarks are fundamental fermions that serve as the building blocks of matter. Their nature and role can be outlined as follows:

*   **Fundamental Nature:** Quarks are elementary particles that possess fractional electric charges (either $+\frac{2}{3}e$ or $-\frac{1}{3}e$). There are six "flavours" of quarks: up, down, charm, strange, top, and bottom. 
*   **Confinement:** Quarks are never found in isolation due to a property called color confinement. They are always bound together by the strong nuclear force, which is mediated by particles called gluons.
*   **Role in Hadrons:** Quarks combine to form composite particles known as hadrons. These are categorized into:
    *   **Baryons:** Composed of three quarks (e.g., protons consist of two up quarks and one down quark [$uud$], and neutrons consist of one up quark and two down quarks [$udd$]).
    *   **Mesons:** Composed of a quark-antiquark pair.
*   **Force Interactions:** Quarks are the only fundamental particles that participate in all four fundamental interactions: gravity, electromagnetism, the weak nuclear force, and the strong nuclear force.
