### Front
In a comparison between two tracks starting at the same height, how does a track with a deeper "dip" (lower vertical drop) affect arrival time compared to a flatter track?

### Back
- Key idea: The track with the lower minimum height converts more gravitational potential energy into kinetic energy, resulting in a higher average velocity that overcomes any extra path length.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Conservation of Mechanical Energy ($mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$)
- When to use it: Comparing speeds or arrival times in paths where vertical displacement varies but friction is negligible.
- Common trap: Assuming the longer path length (due to the curve) will automatically result in a slower arrival time.
