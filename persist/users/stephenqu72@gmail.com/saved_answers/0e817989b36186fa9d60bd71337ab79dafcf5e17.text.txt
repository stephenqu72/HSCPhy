### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Answer:** An inertial frame of reference is a frame of reference that is not accelerating; it is either at rest or moving at a constant velocity. In such a frame, Newton’s First Law of Motion holds true—an object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force. 
**Everyday example:** A person standing on the ground watching a train pass by at a constant speed (ignoring the Earth's rotation) is considered to be in an inertial frame of reference.

#### Part (b)
**Answer:**
We use the length contraction formula:
$$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
Where:
- Proper length $L_0 = 220\text{ m}$
- Contracted length $L = 70\text{ m}$
- $c$ is the speed of light.

Rearranging for $v$:
$$\frac{L}{L_0} = \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
$$\left(\frac{70}{220}\right)^2 = 1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$$
$$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 1 - \left(\frac{7}{22}\right)^2$$
$$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 1 - 0.1012$$
$$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 0.8988$$
$$\frac{v}{c} = \sqrt{0.8988} \approx 0.948$$
$$v \approx 0.948c$$

The spaceship must be travelling at approximately $0.948c$ (or $94.8\%$ of the speed of light).

**Explanation:** This is a classic Special Relativity problem. 
1. Identify the **proper length** ($L_0$), which is the length measured in the object's rest frame ($220\text{ m}$).
2. Identify the **contracted length** ($L$), which is the length measured by an observer moving relative to the object ($70\text{ m}$).
3. Substitute these values into the length contraction formula and solve algebraically for $v$. Ensure your final answer is expressed as a fraction or percentage of $c$.
