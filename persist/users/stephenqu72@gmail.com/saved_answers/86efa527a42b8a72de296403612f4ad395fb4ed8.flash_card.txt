### Front
How does "experimental uncertainty" differ from an "experimental mistake" in the context of HSC Physics?

### Back
- Key idea: Uncertainty is an inherent, unavoidable limit in measurement precision, whereas a mistake is an avoidable human error that should be excluded from data.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When evaluating the reliability of data or reporting results as $x \pm \Delta x$.
- Common trap: Treating mistakes (e.g., misreading a scale) as uncertainty; mistakes must be removed from the data set, while uncertainty is quantified and included.
