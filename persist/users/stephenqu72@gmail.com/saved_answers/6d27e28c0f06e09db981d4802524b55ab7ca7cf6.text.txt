### Answer

Magnetic braking works through the principle of electromagnetic induction. As a conductor (such as a metal wheel or disc) moves through a non-uniform magnetic field, the changing magnetic flux experienced by the conductor induces circular **eddy currents** within it, as described by Faraday’s Law. According to Lenz’s Law, these eddy currents create their own magnetic field that opposes the original motion of the conductor, thereby exerting a retarding force that slows the object down.

Regarding the conservation of energy, the kinetic energy of the moving object is not simply destroyed; it is transformed. The induced eddy currents encounter electrical resistance within the material of the conductor, causing the energy to be dissipated as thermal energy (heat). Thus, the initial mechanical kinetic energy of the system is converted into internal thermal energy, consistent with the Law of Conservation of Energy.
