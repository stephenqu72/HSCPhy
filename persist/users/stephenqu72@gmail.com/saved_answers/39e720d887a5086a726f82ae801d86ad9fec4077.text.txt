To determine the direction of the induced current, we must apply **Lenz’s Law**, which states that the direction of the induced current is such that it creates a magnetic field that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. When the plate is released from A (moving into the magnetic field):**
*   As the plate swings from A toward the center, it enters the region of the uniform magnetic field (directed into the page).
*   The magnetic flux through the plate increases.
*   According to Lenz's Law, the induced current must create a magnetic field pointing **out of the page** to oppose this increase.
*   Using the right-hand grip rule, a magnetic field pointing out of the page corresponds to an **anticlockwise** current.

**2. When the plate returns to A (moving out of the magnetic field):**
*   After swinging to position B and turning back, the plate moves from the center toward position A, effectively exiting the magnetic field region.
*   The magnetic flux through the plate decreases.
*   According to Lenz's Law, the induced current must create a magnetic field pointing **into the page** to oppose this decrease.
*   Using the right-hand grip rule, a magnetic field pointing into the page corresponds to a **clockwise** current.

**Conclusion:**
When released from A, the current is anticlockwise. When returning to A, the current is clockwise. This matches option **C**.
