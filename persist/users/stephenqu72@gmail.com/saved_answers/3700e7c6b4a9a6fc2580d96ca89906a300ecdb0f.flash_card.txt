### Front
Why must a geostationary satellite orbit directly above the Earth's equator?

### Back
- Key idea: The satellite's orbital plane must coincide with the Earth's equatorial plane so that the gravitational force (directed toward Earth's center) provides the centripetal force required to maintain a fixed position relative to the surface.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Law of Universal Gravitation ($F_g = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$) providing the centripetal force ($F_c = mr\omega^2$).
- When to use it: Any question regarding satellite orbits, geostationary requirements, or orbital period/radius calculations.
- Common trap: Confusing orbital radius ($r$) with altitude ($h$). Always remember $r = h + R_E$.
