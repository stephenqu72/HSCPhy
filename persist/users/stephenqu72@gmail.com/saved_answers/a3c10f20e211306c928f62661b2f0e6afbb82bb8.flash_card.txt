### Front
How do you calculate the angular velocity ($\omega$) of an object given arc length ($s$), radius ($r$), and time ($t$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Angular velocity is the rate of change of angular displacement ($\theta$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\omega = \frac{\theta}{t}$, where $\theta = \frac{s}{r}$. (Convert $\theta$ from radians to degrees if units require it: $\text{rad} \times \frac{180^\circ}{\pi}$).
- **When to use it:** When solving circular motion problems involving linear distance traveled along a curved path.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert time into SI units (seconds) or failing to distinguish between radians and degrees based on the requested unit.
