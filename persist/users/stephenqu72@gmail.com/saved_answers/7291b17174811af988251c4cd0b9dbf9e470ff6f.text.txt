### Part a: Calculation of Final Velocity
**Physics Principle:** In any collision, the total momentum of a closed system is conserved.
$$\sum p_{before} = \sum p_{after}$$
$$(m_c \times u_c) + (m_v \times u_v) = (m_c \times v_c) + (m_v \times v_v)$$

**Working:**
Let the mass of the car $m_c = 1200\text{ kg}$ and the van $m_v = 2200\text{ kg}$.
Initial velocities: $u_c = 10\text{ m s}^{-1}$, $u_v = 0\text{ m s}^{-1}$.
Final velocities: $v_c = ?$, $v_v = 6.5\text{ m s}^{-1}$.

$$(1200 \times 10) + (2200 \times 0) = (1200 \times v_c) + (2200 \times 6.5)$$
$$12000 = 1200v_c + 14300$$
$$1200v_c = 12000 - 14300$$
$$1200v_c = -2300$$
$$v_c = -1.916... \approx -1.92\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

**Answer:** The car is moving at $1.92\text{ m s}^{-1}$ to the **left** (the negative sign indicates the direction opposite to the initial motion).

---

### Part b: Elastic vs Inelastic
**Explanation:** To determine if the collision is elastic, we compare total kinetic energy ($E_k$) before and after the collision.
$E_{k, before} = \frac{1}{2}m_c u_c^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_v u_v^2 = \frac{1}{2}(1200)(10^2) = 60,000\text{ J}$
$E_{k, after} = \frac{1}{2}m_c v_c^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_v v_v^2 = \frac{1}{2}(1200)(-1.917^2) + \frac{1}{2}(2200)(6.5^2) \approx 2202 + 46475 = 48,677\text{ J}$

Since $E_{k, before} \neq E_{k, after}$ (specifically, kinetic energy is lost), this is an **inelastic collision**. Energy is dissipated, likely through heat, sound, and deformation of the vehicle bodies.

### Part c: Average Force
**Physics Principle:** Impulse ($F\Delta t = \Delta p$). $F = \frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t} = \frac{m(v - u)}{\Delta t}$. Time $\Delta t = 40\text{ ms} = 0.04\text{ s}$.

**i. Force on van by car:**
$\Delta p_{van} = m_v(v_v - u_v) = 2200(6.5 - 0) = 14300\text{ kg m s}^{-1}$
$F = \frac{14300}{0.04} = 357,500\text{ N}$
The direction is to the **right** (positive direction).

**ii. Force on car by van:**
According to Newton's Third Law, the force on the car by the van must be equal in magnitude and opposite in direction to the force on the van by the car.
Magnitude: **$357,500\text{ N}$**
Direction: **To the left**.
