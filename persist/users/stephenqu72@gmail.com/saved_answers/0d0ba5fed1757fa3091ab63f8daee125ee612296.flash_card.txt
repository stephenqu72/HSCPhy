### Front
When four point charges ($+q, -q, -q, +q$) are placed at the corners of a square, what is the electric potential along the axes passing through the center of the square?

### Back
- Key idea: Superposition of scalar potential ($V = \sum k \frac{q_i}{r_i}$) results in total cancellation due to symmetry.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $V = k\frac{q}{r}$ (Principle of Superposition).
- When to use it: To determine equipotential lines or regions of zero potential in multi-charge configurations.
- Common trap: Confusing scalar electric potential with vector electric field (which would not be zero everywhere on those axes).
