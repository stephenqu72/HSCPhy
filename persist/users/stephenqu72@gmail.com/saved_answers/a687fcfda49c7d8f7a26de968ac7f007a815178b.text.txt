To solve this problem, we must analyze the motion of a charged particle in a uniform electric field. This is a classic projectile motion scenario where the electric force acts as the constant acceleration.

### Explanation

1.  **Analyze the motion:** The particle enters the field with a constant horizontal velocity $u$. Because there is no force acting in the horizontal direction (neglecting air resistance and gravity), the horizontal distance $X$ is governed by the simple constant velocity formula: $X = u \times t$.
2.  **Determine the constraints:** To find the time $t$ that the particle remains in the field, we must look at the vertical motion. The particle starts from the top plate and moves towards the bottom plate. The distance between the plates (let's call it $d$) is the vertical displacement $s$ in the vertical kinematic equation.
3.  **Use Kinematics:** The vertical motion is subject to a constant electric force $F = qE$, leading to a constant vertical acceleration $a = \frac{qE}{m}$. To relate the vertical displacement $s$ (which is $d$) to the time $t$, we use the displacement-time equation for constant acceleration:
    $$s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$
    In this case, since the initial vertical velocity is zero, this simplifies to $d = \frac{1}{2}at^2$.
4.  **Connecting the parts:** By using this formula to solve for time ($t = \sqrt{\frac{2d}{a}}$), we can then substitute $t$ into the horizontal motion equation $X = ut$ to calculate the distance $X$. Therefore, the displacement formula is the fundamental tool needed to bridge the gap between the vertical traversal and the horizontal distance.

Option (D) is the correct formula because it allows us to calculate the time the particle spends in the electric field based on its vertical displacement, which is necessary to determine the horizontal distance $X$.

**Answer:** (D)
