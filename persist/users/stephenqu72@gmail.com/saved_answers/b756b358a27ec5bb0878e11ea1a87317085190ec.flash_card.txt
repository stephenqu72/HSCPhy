### Front
How does the average binding energy per nucleon graph indicate nuclear stability?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The higher the average binding energy per nucleon, the more stable the nucleus.
- **Figure:** The Binding Energy per Nucleon vs. Mass Number ($A$) graph; the peak occurs at $A \approx 60$ ($Fe-56$).
- **When to use it:** To compare the stability of different isotopes or to determine if a nucleus will undergo fusion (moving toward the peak from the left) or fission (moving toward the peak from the right).
- **Common trap:** Assuming higher mass number always means higher binding energy; the curve actually declines for nuclei heavier than $Fe-56$.
