### Front
Derive Kepler’s Third Law and explain how the mass of a central body ($M$) is determined from a $T^2$ vs $r^3$ graph.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Equate gravitational force ($F_g = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$) to centripetal force ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$) and substitute $v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$.
- **Formula:** $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$ or $T^2 = \left(\frac{4\pi^2}{GM}\right)r^3$.
- **When to use it:** When calculating orbital periods, radii, or the mass of a central body using satellite data.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units to SI (seconds for $T$ and meters for $r$) before calculating $M$. Ensure $T^2$ is on the $y$-axis and $r^3$ is on the $x$-axis to equate the gradient to $\frac{4\pi^2}{GM}$.
