### Answer
Millikan’s oil drop experiment provided the first accurate measurement of the elementary charge ($e$). By spraying oil droplets between two charged plates, he observed their motion using a microscope.

**1. The Mechanics of the Experiment:**
As droplets fell through the hole, they acquired a negative charge due to friction or radiation. By adjusting the voltage ($V$) across the plates, Millikan created a uniform electric field ($E = V/d$) that exerted an upward force ($F_E = qE$) on the charged droplets, opposing the downward force of gravity ($F_g = mg$).

When a droplet was held stationary (suspended), the forces were balanced:
$$F_g = F_E$$
$$mg = qE$$
Since $E = V/d$, the charge on the drop could be calculated as:
$$q = \frac{mgd}{V}$$

**2. Interpretation and Contribution to Atomic Structure:**
By varying the voltage and observing many droplets, Millikan found that the calculated charges ($q$) were always integer multiples of a specific base value:
$$q = n \cdot e$$
(where $n = 1, 2, 3, \dots$ and $e \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$).

This result proved that electric charge is **quantized** rather than continuous. This was a pivotal moment in understanding the structure of matter, as it confirmed that the electron is a fundamental subatomic particle with a discrete, indivisible unit of charge, directly supporting the particle model of the atom.
