### Explanation
To solve this, we must identify the components of a standard cathode ray tube (CRT):

1.  **The Cathode (Filament):** The coiled structure on the far left is the cathode, which is heated to emit electrons via thermionic emission.
2.  **The Elements marked $Y$:** These consist of the **control grid** (the cup-like structure surrounding the filament) and the **anode** (the cylindrical structure next to it).
    *   The **control grid** (the first part of $Y$) has a negative potential relative to the cathode. By adjusting the potential of this grid, the number of electrons allowed to pass through towards the screen is regulated. This directly controls the **brightness or intensity** of the spot on the screen.
    *   The **anode** (the second part of $Y$) is set to a high positive potential to accelerate the electrons.
3.  **The Plates:** The horizontal plates at the end of the tube are responsible for vertical deflection (moving the beam up or down).

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **(A)** While the anode is part of the $Y$ assembly and contributes to acceleration, in standard CRT diagrams of this type, the combination of the control grid and the acceleration assembly is primarily known for controlling beam current. However, in the context of typical HSC physics questions regarding the "control grid" portion of $Y$, its specific function is to regulate beam current (intensity).
*   **(C) and (D)** These functions are performed by the deflection plates (the horizontal/vertical metal plates), not the components labeled $Y$ near the source of the electrons.

Therefore, the specific role of the components labeled $Y$ (most notably the control grid) is to adjust the intensity of the electron beam.
