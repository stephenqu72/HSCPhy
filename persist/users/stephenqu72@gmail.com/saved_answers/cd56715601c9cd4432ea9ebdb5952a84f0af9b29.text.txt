### Part (b): Back EMF
**Answer:**
As the motor spins, the coil is rotating within an external magnetic field, which causes the magnetic flux through the coil to change over time. According to **Faraday’s Law**, this change in flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) in the coil. **Lenz’s Law** dictates that this induced EMF (the "back EMF") will act in the opposite direction to the applied supply voltage. 

As the motor spins faster, the rate of change of magnetic flux increases, leading to a higher back EMF. This effectively reduces the net voltage across the coil ($V_{net} = V_{supply} - \mathcal{E}_{back}$), which reduces the current. Consequently, the motor reaches a steady state where the back EMF limits the motor's speed, preventing it from accelerating indefinitely.

**How to solve:**
1.  **Identify the cause:** Link the motion of the coil in the magnetic field to the changing magnetic flux.
2.  **Reference Laws:** Explicitly mention Faraday’s Law (induction) and Lenz’s Law (the direction of the induced EMF).
3.  **Explain the limiting effect:** Explain how the back EMF opposes the supply voltage, thereby reducing the net current and creating a "self-regulating" mechanism that stabilizes the rotational speed.
