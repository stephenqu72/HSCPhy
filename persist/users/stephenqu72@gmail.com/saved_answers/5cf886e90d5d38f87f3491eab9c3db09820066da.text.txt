The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation
Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle is expressed by the famous equation:
$$E = mc^2$$

To understand why **D** is the most accurate description:

*   **Understanding $E = mc^2$:** This equation tells us that mass ($m$) and energy ($E$) are manifestations of the same underlying physical entity. Specifically, mass is considered "condensed" energy. When a system loses mass (as seen in nuclear fission or fusion), that mass is converted into a proportional amount of energy.
*   **Evaluating A:** This is incorrect. Mass and energy are directly linked, not independent.
*   **Evaluating B:** This is incorrect. The "rest mass" ($m_0$) of an object is an **invariant** quantity, meaning it remains the same regardless of the observer's inertial frame of reference.
*   **Evaluating C:** This is slightly inaccurate. While they are equivalent, they are not "identical" in their properties or units of measurement; rather, mass is a *form* of energy that can be converted into other forms (like kinetic or thermal energy).
*   **Evaluating D:** This captures the core essence of the theory in the context of HSC Physics. It states that mass is fundamentally a form of energy. Because $c^2$ (the speed of light squared) is an enormous constant, even a tiny amount of mass contains a vast amount of potential energy.
