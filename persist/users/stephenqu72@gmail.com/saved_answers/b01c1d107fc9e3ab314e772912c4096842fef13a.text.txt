To solve this problem, we must apply the de Broglie wavelength formula, which relates the wave-like property of a particle (its wavelength) to its momentum.

### Answer
The correct statement is **C: The neutron has a shorter wavelength because its mass is greater.**

### Explanation
1.  **The Formula:** The de Broglie wavelength ($\lambda$) is given by:
    $$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$
    Where:
    - $h$ is Planck's constant (a constant value).
    - $p$ is momentum ($p = mv$).
    - $m$ is the mass of the particle.
    - $v$ is the velocity (speed) of the particle.

2.  **Analyze the Variables:**
    - The problem states that both the proton and the neutron travel at the **same speed** ($v$).
    - Planck's constant ($h$) is obviously the same for both.
    - Therefore, the wavelength ($\lambda$) is inversely proportional to the mass ($m$):
      $$\lambda \propto \frac{1}{m}$$

3.  **Compare Masses:**
    - A neutron is slightly more massive than a proton ($m_n > m_p$).

4.  **Conclusion:**
    - Since the neutron has a greater mass ($m$), and mass is in the denominator of the de Broglie equation, the neutron must have a smaller wavelength compared to the proton. 
    - Conversely, the proton has a smaller mass, resulting in a longer wavelength.
    - Looking at the options, statement **C** correctly identifies that the neutron has a shorter wavelength because its mass is greater.
