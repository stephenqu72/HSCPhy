### Answer
The correct answer is **(C)**.

### Explanation
To determine the nature of Planck's constant, we look at the fundamental equation Max Planck developed to describe the quantization of energy in thermal radiation:

$$E = hf$$

Where:
*   $E$ is the energy of a photon (measured in Joules, $\text{J}$)
*   $h$ is Planck's constant (measured in $\text{J s}$)
*   $f$ is the frequency of the radiation (measured in Hertz or $\text{s}^{-1}$)

If we rearrange this equation to solve for $h$:
$$h = \frac{E}{f}$$

This demonstrates that Planck's constant is the constant of proportionality—or the **ratio**—between the energy of electromagnetic radiation and its frequency.

*   **(B)** is a reference to the **Universal Gravitational Constant ($G$)** used in Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation.
*   **(D)** relates to the **Boltzmann constant ($k_B$)**, which links the average kinetic energy of gas particles to temperature.
