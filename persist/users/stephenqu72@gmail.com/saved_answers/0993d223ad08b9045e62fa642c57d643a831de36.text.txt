### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) Bohr**.

### Explanation
To determine which planet has the lowest escape velocity, we use the formula for escape velocity ($v_e$):

$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

Where:
- $G$ is the gravitational constant (a constant value).
- $M$ is the mass of the planet.
- $r$ is the radius of the planet.

From this formula, we can see that:
1. $v_e$ is **directly proportional** to the square root of the mass ($\sqrt{M}$). A smaller mass leads to a smaller escape velocity.
2. $v_e$ is **inversely proportional** to the square root of the radius ($\sqrt{r}$). A larger radius leads to a smaller escape velocity.

To find the lowest escape velocity, we are looking for the planet with the **smallest mass** and the **largest radius**.

Let's evaluate the options:
*   **(A) Newton:** $M = 5 \times 10^{24}$, $r = 8000$
*   **(B) Einstein:** $M = 8 \times 10^{24}$, $r = 7800$ (Higher mass and smaller radius than A, so $v_e$ is higher).
*   **(C) Bohr:** $M = 5 \times 10^{24}$, $r = 9500$ (Same mass as A, but a larger radius, so $v_e$ must be lower than A).
*   **(D) Hawkins:** $M = 8 \times 10^{24}$, $r = 10200$ (Larger mass than C, and since the mass is $1.6 \times$ greater while the radius is only $\approx 1.07 \times$ greater, the $v_e$ will be higher than C).

Comparing the two planets with the smallest mass ($5 \times 10^{24}$ kg), which are Newton and Bohr, Bohr has the larger radius ($9500$ km vs $8000$ km). Since a larger radius reduces the escape velocity, **Bohr** will have the lowest escape velocity of all four planets.
