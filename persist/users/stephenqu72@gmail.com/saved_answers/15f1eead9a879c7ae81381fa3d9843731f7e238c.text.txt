To solve this problem, we apply Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle, defined by the equation $E = mc^2$.

### Explanation
1.  **Understand the physics:** When an electron and a positron annihilate, their entire mass is converted into energy (usually in the form of two gamma-ray photons).
2.  **Identify the particles:** 
    *   The mass of an electron is approximately $m_e \approx 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$.
    *   The mass of a positron is identical to that of an electron, so $m_p \approx 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$.
3.  **Calculate total mass:**
    *   Total mass $m_{total} = m_e + m_p = 2 \times (9.11 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}) = 1.822 \times 10^{-30}\text{ kg}$.
4.  **Use the mass-energy equivalence formula:**
    *   $E = mc^2$
    *   $c$ (the speed of light) $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$.
    *   $E = (1.822 \times 10^{-30}\text{ kg}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s})^2$
    *   $E = (1.822 \times 10^{-30}) \times (9.00 \times 10^{16})$
    *   $E \approx 1.6398 \times 10^{-13}\text{ J}$

Rounding this result gives us $1.6 \times 10^{-13}\text{ J}$.
