### Front
Explain the physics of a cyclist on a banked track at varying speeds.

### Back
- **Key idea:** At the design speed, the horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force. At lower speeds, static friction is required to prevent sliding.
- **Formula:** $\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$
- **When to use it:** To explain the mechanics of circular motion on banked surfaces and why riders must adjust their lean angle relative to speed.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the cyclist *always* leans perpendicular; they only do so at the design speed to avoid relying on friction.
