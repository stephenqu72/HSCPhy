### Explanation
To solve this, we must evaluate each statement against Kepler's three Laws of Planetary Motion:

*   **Kepler's First Law (Law of Ellipses):** Planets orbit the Sun in an ellipse, with the Sun at one of the two foci. **Statement B** aligns with this law.
*   **Kepler's Second Law (Law of Equal Areas):** A line segment joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time. A direct consequence of this is that a planet must move faster when it is closer to the Sun (perihelion) and slower when it is further away (aphelion). **Statement A** describes this law.
*   **Kepler's Third Law (Law of Harmonies):** The square of the orbital period ($T$) of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis ($r$) of its orbit. This is expressed as $\frac{T^2}{r^3} = k$, where $k$ is a constant for all planets orbiting the same body. Since $k$ is constant for the Solar System, the ratio for Venus and Mercury must be the same. **Statement D** aligns with this law.

**Statement C** discusses "total orbital energy" and "kinetic energy." While orbital mechanics involves these concepts (where total energy $E = K + U$ is negative for a bound orbit), this relationship is a result of Newtonian mechanics and the Law of Universal Gravitation, not one of Kepler’s empirical Laws of Planetary Motion. Therefore, Statement C is the correct choice because it is **not** one of Kepler's three laws.
