To solve this problem, we apply the principles of special relativity, specifically **time dilation**.

### Explanation

1.  **Identify the Frames of Reference:**
    *   The "proper time" ($t_0$) is the time interval measured by an observer in the same frame of reference as the event. In this case, the astronaut is cleaning inside the ship, so the astronaut’s clock measures the proper time.
    *   The "dilated time" ($t$) is the time interval measured by an observer in a different frame of reference moving at a velocity $v$ relative to the event. Mission control on Earth is observing the astronaut moving at $v = 0.9c$. Thus, the 30 minutes recorded by Earth is the dilated time $t$.

2.  **State the Time Dilation Formula:**
    $$t = \gamma t_0 = \frac{t_0}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$
    Where:
    *   $t = 30$ minutes
    *   $v = 0.9c$
    *   $t_0 = ?$ (the time the astronaut cleaned for)

3.  **Perform the Calculation:**
    First, find the Lorentz factor ($\gamma$):
    $$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.9)^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.81}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.19}} \approx \frac{1}{0.4359} \approx 2.294$$

    Now, rearrange the formula to solve for $t_0$:
    $$t_0 = \frac{t}{\gamma} = t \times \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
    $$t_0 = 30 \times \sqrt{0.19}$$
    $$t_0 = 30 \times 0.4359$$
    $$t_0 \approx 13.077 \text{ minutes}$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number, we get 13 minutes.
