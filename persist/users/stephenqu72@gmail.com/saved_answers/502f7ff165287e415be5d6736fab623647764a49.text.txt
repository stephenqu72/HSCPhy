To solve this problem, we must understand the relationship between a charged particle's motion and a magnetic field. When a particle enters a magnetic field perpendicularly, the magnetic force acts as a centripetal force.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the governing equation:** 
    The magnetic force ($F_B = qvB$) provides the centripetal force ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$):
    $$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

2.  **Rearrange for the radius ($r$):**
    Canceling $v$ from both sides and solving for $r$:
    $$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

3.  **Analyze the "Identical Path" condition:**
    If the second particle follows an *identical path*, its radius $r$ must remain constant. We compare the initial state ($r = \frac{mv}{qB}$) to the second particle's state ($r'$):
    $$r' = \frac{m' v'}{q' B'}$$
    Given: $B' = 2B$ and $v' = \frac{1}{2}v$. 
    For $r' = r$, we set the equations equal:
    $$\frac{mv}{qB} = \frac{m' (\frac{1}{2}v)}{q' (2B)}$$

4.  **Simplify:**
    Cancel $v$ and $B$ from both sides:
    $$\frac{m}{q} = \frac{m'}{4q'}$$
    $$\frac{m'}{q'} = 4 \left( \frac{m}{q} \right)$$

5.  **Evaluate the Options:**
    We need to check which option maintains the equality $\frac{m'}{q'} = \frac{4m}{q}$:
    *   **A:** $\frac{m}{q} \neq \frac{4m}{q}$ (Incorrect)
    *   **B:** $\frac{2m}{0.5q} = 4 \left( \frac{m}{q} \right)$ (Correct!)
    *   **C:** $\frac{4m}{q} = 4 \left( \frac{m}{q} \right)$ (Correct!)
