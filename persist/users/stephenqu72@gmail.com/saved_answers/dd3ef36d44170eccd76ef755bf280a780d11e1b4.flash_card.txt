### Front
How do initial launch angles affect the properties of a projectile (Height, Time of Flight, Range)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Vertical velocity ($v \sin\theta$) determines height and time of flight; the angle closest to $45^\circ$ maximizes horizontal range.
- **Formula / law:** 
  - $h_{max} = \frac{(v\sin\theta)^2}{2g}$
  - $t_{flight} = \frac{2v\sin\theta}{g}$
  - $R = \frac{v^2\sin(2\theta)}{g}$
- **When to use it:** Analyzing projectile motion graphs where initial velocity is constant but launch angles differ.
- **Common trap:** Assuming a steeper angle always results in a greater range; high angles produce height but sacrifice horizontal distance.
