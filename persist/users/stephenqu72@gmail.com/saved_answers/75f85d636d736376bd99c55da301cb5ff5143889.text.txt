### (a) Calculate the number of turns of wire on the secondary coil.

**Answer:** 400 turns.

**Explanation:**
For an ideal transformer, the ratio of voltages is equal to the ratio of the number of turns in the coils. We use the transformer equation:
$$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$
Where:
- $V_p = 12\text{ V}$ (Primary voltage)
- $V_s = 240\text{ V}$ (Secondary voltage)
- $N_p = 20\text{ turns}$ (Primary turns)
- $N_s = ?$ (Secondary turns)

Rearranging for $N_s$:
$$N_s = N_p \times \frac{V_s}{V_p}$$
$$N_s = 20 \times \frac{240}{12}$$
$$N_s = 20 \times 20 = 400\text{ turns}$$

### (b) Explain the purpose of the soft iron core.

**Answer:** The purpose of the soft iron core is to provide a path of low magnetic reluctance (high magnetic permeability) for the magnetic flux. It effectively "channels" or concentrates the magnetic field lines generated by the primary coil so that they pass through the secondary coil, ensuring maximum magnetic flux linkage and minimizing magnetic flux leakage.

---

### (c) The core is made of many thin sheets of soft iron glued together with insulating material. Explain why it is constructed this way.

**Answer:** This construction is designed to minimize energy losses due to **eddy currents**. As the magnetic flux changes within the core, it induces circular currents (eddy currents) in the conducting iron. By laminating the core (using thin sheets separated by insulation), the physical path for these currents is broken or restricted, which significantly reduces the magnitude of the eddy currents. This prevents the core from overheating and reduces energy dissipation as heat, thereby increasing the efficiency of the transformer.
