To determine which diagram represents the weakest gravitational field strength at point X, we use the formula for gravitational field strength $g$:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

where:
- $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
- $M$ is the mass of the planet.
- $r$ is the distance from the center of the planet to the point.

Since $G$ is constant, we can compare the values of $\frac{M}{r^2}$ for each option. The smallest value will correspond to the weakest gravitational field.

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

*   **Option A:** $M = 2M$, $r = 2r$
    $$\frac{2}{(2)^2} = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5$$

*   **Option B:** $M = 0.5M$, $r = 0.5r$
    $$\frac{0.5}{(0.5)^2} = \frac{0.5}{0.25} = 2.0$$

*   **Option C:** $M = 3M$, $r = 1.5r$
    $$\frac{3}{(1.5)^2} = \frac{3}{2.25} \approx 1.33$$

*   **Option D:** $M = 4M$, $r = 2r$
    $$\frac{4}{(2)^2} = \frac{4}{4} = 1.0$$

### Conclusion:
Comparing the calculated values:
- A: $0.5$
- B: $2.0$
- C: $1.33$
- D: $1.0$

The smallest value is $0.5$, which corresponds to **Option A**. Therefore, diagram A represents the weakest gravitational field strength.
