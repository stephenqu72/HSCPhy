To solve this problem, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which relates the absolute temperature of a black body to the wavelength at which it emits the most radiation ($\lambda_{max}$).

### Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Convert temperature to Kelvin:**
Wien's Law requires the temperature to be in Kelvin.
$$T = 35.6^\circ\text{C} + 273.15 = 308.75\text{ K}$$

**2. State Wien’s Displacement Law:**
The relationship is given by the formula:
$$\lambda_{max} T = b$$
where $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$.

**3. Calculate the wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$):**
$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}\cdot\text{K}}{308.75\text{ K}}$$
$$\lambda_{max} \approx 9.386 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}$$

**4. Convert the result to nanometers (nm):**
Since $1\text{ m} = 10^9\text{ nm}$:
$$\lambda_{max} \approx 9.386 \times 10^{-6} \times 10^9\text{ nm}$$
$$\lambda_{max} \approx 9386\text{ nm}$$

**5. Compare with options:**
Rounding our result to two decimal places gives $9.39 \times 10^3\text{ nm}$. Option **B ($9.40 \times 10^3\text{ nm}$)** is the closest value.

### Answer
The correct option is **B**.
