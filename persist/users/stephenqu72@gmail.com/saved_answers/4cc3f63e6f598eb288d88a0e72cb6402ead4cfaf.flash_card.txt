### Front
Given two radioactive samples with equal activity ($A$), what is the relationship between their decay constants ($\lambda$) and the number of nuclei ($N$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Activity is the product of the decay constant and the number of radioactive nuclei.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $A = \lambda N$ (Inverse proportionality: $N \propto 1/\lambda$)
- When to use it: When comparing two samples with equal activity to determine the ratio of their nuclei or decay constants.
- Common trap: Forgetting that a higher decay constant means fewer nuclei are required to produce the same activity.
