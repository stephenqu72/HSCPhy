To solve this problem, we use **Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion**, which states that the square of the orbital period ($T$) of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis ($r$) of its orbit.

### The Formula
When we measure the period in **Earth years** and the distance in **astronomical units (AU)**, the constant of proportionality is 1. The formula is expressed as:
$$T^2 = r^3$$

### The Calculation
1.  **Identify the given value:** The orbital period $T$ is $40,000$ years.
2.  **Substitute into the formula:** 
    $$(40,000)^2 = r^3$$
3.  **Calculate $T^2$:** 
    $$40,000^2 = (4 \times 10^4)^2 = 16 \times 10^8 = 1,600,000,000$$
4.  **Solve for $r$:** We need to find the cube root of $1.6 \times 10^9$:
    $$r = \sqrt[3]{1,600,000,000}$$
    
    To estimate this without a calculator:
    *   We know $100^3 = 1,000,000$ (too small).
    *   We know $1000^3 = 1,000,000,000$ (close).
    *   Since $1.6 \times 10^9$ is slightly larger than $10^9$, the cube root must be slightly larger than $1000$. 
    *   Let's check $1170^3$: $1170 \times 1170 \approx 1.37 \times 10^6$. Then $1.37 \times 10^6 \times 1170 \approx 1.6 \times 10^9$.

Therefore, $r \approx 1170$ AU.
