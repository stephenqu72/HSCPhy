### Front
How does the trajectory of a projectile change when air resistance is introduced compared to an ideal vacuum?

### Back
- Key idea: Air resistance acts opposite to velocity, causing a reduction in range and peak height and creating an asymmetric trajectory.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Asymmetry: the descent is steeper than the ascent; $F_{drag} \propto v^2$.
- When to use it: When analyzing real-world projectile motion vs. idealised parabolic motion in physics questions.
- Common trap: Assuming the path remains a perfect parabola; the horizontal velocity is not constant when air resistance is present.
