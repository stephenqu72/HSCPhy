### Front
On a frictionless banked track, which specific vector represents the net force acting on a vehicle in uniform circular motion?

### Back
- Key idea: The net force (centripetal force) must point horizontally toward the center of the circular path.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\vec{F}_{net} = \vec{F}_{gravity} + \vec{F}_{normal}$ (Vector sum results in a horizontal vector).
- When to use it: To identify or calculate forces acting on objects undergoing circular motion on an incline.
- Common trap: Mistaking the Normal force (perpendicular to the track) for the Net force (horizontal toward the center).
