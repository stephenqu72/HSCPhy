### Front
How do you calculate the force required to produce a specific torque at a given angle and distance from a pivot?

### Back
- Key idea: Torque ($\tau$) is the rotational equivalent of force; only the perpendicular component of force produces torque.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\tau = rF\sin(\theta)$
- When to use it: When calculating forces acting at an angle to a lever arm to achieve rotational equilibrium or motion.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert distance ($r$) from centimeters to meters and failing to account for the $\sin(\theta)$ component when the force is not perpendicular.
