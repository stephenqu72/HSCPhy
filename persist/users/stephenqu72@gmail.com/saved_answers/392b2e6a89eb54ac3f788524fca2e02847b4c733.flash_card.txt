### Front
How does the direction of force on a charge in an electric field relate to the electric field lines?

### Back
- Key idea: A positive charge experiences a force in the direction of the field lines ($\vec{E}$); a negative charge experiences a force in the opposite direction.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\vec{F} = q\vec{E}$
- When to use it: To determine the direction of motion or acceleration of a charged particle placed in an electric field.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the force on a *negative* charge (like an electron) is anti-parallel to the field direction.
