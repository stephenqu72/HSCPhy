### Teacher's Solution and Explanation

#### Part (a)
To solve this, we must first equate the electrical potential energy provided by the potential difference to the kinetic energy gained by the electron, then use the de Broglie wavelength formula.

**Step 1:** Calculate the kinetic energy ($E_k$) of the electron.
$E_k = qV = (1.602 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C}) \times 75 \, \text{V} = 1.2015 \times 10^{-17} \, \text{J}$.

**Step 2:** Use the de Broglie wavelength formula: $\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2mE_k}}$.
Given:
$h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J s}$
$m = 9.109 \times 10^{-31} \, \text{kg}$

$$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{\sqrt{2 \times (9.109 \times 10^{-31}) \times (1.2015 \times 10^{-17})}}$$
$$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{\sqrt{2.189 \times 10^{-47}}} = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{4.679 \times 10^{-24}} \approx 1.416 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{m}$$

Converting to nanometers: $1.416 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{m} = 0.1416 \, \text{nm} \approx 0.142 \, \text{nm}$. (Q.E.D.)

#### Part (b)
**Explanation:** 
The graph shows a diffraction pattern, characterized by a central maximum and a secondary maximum. Diffraction is a wave-like property. Classically, particles would pass through the hole and be detected in a straight line. The fact that the electrons are detected at angles away from the central axis indicates they are behaving like waves.

**Validation of de Broglie’s Hypothesis:**
1. **Wave-particle duality:** The electrons exhibit diffraction when passing through an aperture comparable to their wavelength (the radius of $0.50 \, \text{nm}$ is of the same order of magnitude as the wavelength calculated in (a)).
2. **Quantitative evidence:** The first minimum in a circular diffraction pattern occurs at $\sin(\theta) \approx 1.22 \frac{\lambda}{d}$, where $d$ is the diameter ($1.0 \, \text{nm}$). Using our calculated $\lambda = 0.142 \, \text{nm}$, we find $\sin(\theta) \approx 1.22 \times \frac{0.142}{1.0} \approx 0.173$. The angle $\theta \approx \arcsin(0.173) \approx 10^\circ$. Looking at the graph, the intensity minimum indeed occurs near $10^\circ-12^\circ$. This agreement between the theoretical wave prediction and the experimental data provides strong validation of the de Broglie hypothesis.
