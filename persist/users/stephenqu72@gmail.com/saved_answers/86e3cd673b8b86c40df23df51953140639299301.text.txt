The correct answer is **C**.

### Explanation
The **electron-volt (eV)** is a unit of energy commonly used in atomic and nuclear physics. By definition, $1\text{ eV}$ is the amount of kinetic energy gained by a single electron accelerating from rest through an electric potential difference of $1\text{ volt}$.

To calculate the value in Joules (the SI unit for energy), we use the formula for work done on a charge:
$$W = qV$$

Where:
*   $q$ is the elementary charge of an electron ($e \approx 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$)
*   $V$ is the potential difference ($1\text{ V}$)

Substituting these values:
$$W = (1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (1\text{ V})$$
$$W = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$$

Therefore, $1\text{ eV} = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$.
