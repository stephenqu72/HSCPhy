To determine the correct expression for the total energy of an orbiting satellite, we analyze the energy components in a gravitational field.

### Analysis
For a satellite of mass $m$ in a circular orbit of radius $r$ around a planet of mass $M$:

1.  **Gravitational Potential Energy ($U$):** 
    The potential energy is given by:
    $$U = -\frac{G M m}{r}$$

2.  **Kinetic Energy ($E_K$):** 
    For a stable circular orbit, the centripetal force is provided by gravity:
    $$\frac{m v^2}{r} = \frac{G M m}{r^2}$$
    Multiplying both sides by $\frac{r}{2}$, we get:
    $$\frac{1}{2} m v^2 = \frac{G M m}{2r}$$
    Thus, kinetic energy is:
    $$E_K = \frac{G M m}{2r}$$

3.  **Total Energy ($E_{\text{total}}$):**
    The total energy is the sum of kinetic and potential energy:
    $$E_{\text{total}} = E_K + U$$
    $$E_{\text{total}} = \frac{G M m}{2r} + \left( -\frac{G M m}{r} \right)$$
    $$E_{\text{total}} = \frac{G M m}{2r} - \frac{2 G M m}{2r} = -\frac{G M m}{2r}$$

### Comparison
*   Comparing $E_{\text{total}} = -\frac{G M m}{2r}$ with our expression for $E_K$, we see that $E_{\text{total}} = -E_K$. This does not match option B (which suggests half).
*   Comparing $E_{\text{total}} = -\frac{G M m}{2r}$ with our expression for $U = -\frac{G M m}{r}$, we see that:
    $$E_{\text{total}} = \frac{1}{2} \left( -\frac{G M m}{r} \right) = \frac{U}{2}$$

Therefore, the correct expression is $E_{\text{total}} = \frac{U}{2}$.
