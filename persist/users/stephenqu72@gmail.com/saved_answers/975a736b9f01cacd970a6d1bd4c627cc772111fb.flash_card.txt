### Front
How does the interaction of a mass differ across gravitational, electric, and magnetic fields?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational fields interact with all mass; electric and magnetic fields only act on electric charges. A neutral mass is unaffected by electric or magnetic fields.
- **Formula / law:** 
  - Gravitational: $F_g = mg$ 
  - Electric: $F_E = qE$
  - Magnetic: $F_B = qvB\sin\theta$
- **When to use it:** When asked to "critically analyse" the effect of different fields on a particle/object.
- **Common trap:** Assuming magnetic fields do work on charges; they only change direction, never speed or kinetic energy, because the force is always perpendicular to velocity.
