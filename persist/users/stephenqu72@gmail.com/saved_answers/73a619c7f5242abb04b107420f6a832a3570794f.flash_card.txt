### Front
What is the SI unit for magnetic field ($B$) derived from the magnetic force on a current-carrying wire?

### Back
- Key idea: 1 Tesla ($T$) = 1 Newton per Ampere-meter.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F = BIL \sin(\theta) \rightarrow B = \frac{F}{IL}$
- When to use it: To determine or convert units for magnetic field strength problems.
- Common trap: Forgetting to include the length ($m$) in the denominator or confusing it with magnetic flux ($\Phi$).
