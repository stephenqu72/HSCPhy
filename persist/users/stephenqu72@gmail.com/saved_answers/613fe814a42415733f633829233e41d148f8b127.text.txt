### Answer

**a) Calculation**

First, convert the values to standard SI units (meters):
- Wavelength $\lambda = 545 \text{ nm} = 545 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$
- Rulings $N = 1.2 \times 10^3 \text{ lines/cm} = 1.2 \times 10^5 \text{ lines/m}$

The grating spacing $d$ is:
$$d = \frac{1}{N} = \frac{1}{1.2 \times 10^5} \approx 8.333 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$$

Using the diffraction grating formula $d \sin(\theta) = n\lambda$ for the third-order ($n=3$):
$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{n\lambda}{d} = \frac{3 \times 545 \times 10^{-9}}{8.333 \times 10^{-6}}$$
$$\sin(\theta) = 0.1962$$
$$\theta = \arcsin(0.1962) \approx 11.31^\circ$$

**b) Explanation**
When monochromatic light passes through the multiple slits of a diffraction grating, it undergoes diffraction. As the light waves emerge from each slit, they act as point sources of coherent light. These secondary wavefronts overlap and undergo **superposition**.
- **Bright spots (constructive interference):** Occur where light waves from adjacent slits arrive at the screen in phase (the path difference between waves is an integer multiple of the wavelength, $n\lambda$). Their amplitudes add together to create increased intensity.
- **Dark spots (destructive interference):** Occur where light waves arrive out of phase (typically a path difference of odd half-multiples of the wavelength). The crests of one wave coincide with the troughs of another, cancelling each other out.

### How to solve this
1.  **Identify variables:** Always convert diffraction grating "lines per unit length" to the distance between slits $d$ (measured in meters). Remember $d = \frac{1}{\text{lines per meter}}$.
2.  **Apply the formula:** Use the grating equation $d \sin(\theta) = n\lambda$. Ensure your calculator is in "Degree" mode when finding $\theta$.
3.  **Conceptual Reasoning:** For the explanation part (part b), ensure you mention the key physics terms: **coherent sources**, **superposition**, **path difference**, **constructive interference**, and **destructive interference**. These are the "keywords" HSC markers look for.
