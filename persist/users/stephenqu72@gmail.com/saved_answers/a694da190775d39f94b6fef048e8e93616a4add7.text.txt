### Answer

In an electric motor, the primary energy transformation is the conversion of **electrical energy** into **mechanical (kinetic) energy**. 

The process involves the following stages:
1. **Electrical Input:** Electrical energy is supplied to the motor’s coil (armature) via a power source.
2. **Energy Transformation:** As current flows through the coil in the presence of an external magnetic field, a magnetic force acts on the current-carrying wires (the motor effect). This results in the transformation of electrical energy into mechanical work, causing the motor shaft to rotate.
3. **Energy Dissipation:** Due to factors such as resistance in the wires (causing heating), friction in the bearings, and air resistance, some of the input energy is inevitably transferred as **thermal energy (heat)** and sound energy, which are considered "waste" outputs.

To secure full marks (3 marks) on this HSC-style question, you must demonstrate a clear understanding of the energy flow:

1. **Identify the primary transformation:** You must state that electrical energy is converted into kinetic or mechanical energy. This is the core principle of a motor.
2. **Explain the process:** Briefly mention how this happens (e.g., the interaction between the current and the magnetic field creating a torque).
3. **Account for losses:** To achieve the final mark, you must acknowledge the Law of Conservation of Energy. Mention that no system is 100% efficient; therefore, energy is also dissipated into the surroundings as heat and sound.

**Teacher's Tip:** In physics, "transformation" refers to the change from one type of energy to another (e.g., electrical to mechanical), while "transfer" refers to the movement of energy from one place or object to another (e.g., from the source to the motor, and finally to the environment as heat). Using both terms accurately helps differentiate your answer.
