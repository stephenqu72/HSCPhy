### Front
What is the quark composition of the $\Lambda^0$ baryon, and why is it distinct from a neutron?

### Back
- Key idea: The $\Lambda^0$ is a neutral baryon ($uds$) that contains a strange quark.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Charge summation: $u(+\frac{2}{3}) + d(-\frac{1}{3}) + s(-\frac{1}{3}) = 0$.
- When to use it: To identify baryons based on charge conservation and relative mass (strange quarks are heavier than $u$ and $d$).
- Common trap: Confusing it with the neutron ($udd$), which also has a charge of $0$ but lacks the heavier strange quark.
