### b) Calculate the person's average speed for the entire journey.

**Answer:** $1.33\text{ m/s}$

**Explanation:** Average speed is defined as the **total distance** traveled divided by the **total time** taken. Unlike displacement, distance is a scalar and always adds up regardless of direction.
1.  From $0\text{--}2\text{ s}$: Distance $= 5\text{ m}$
2.  From $2\text{--}4\text{ s}$: Distance $= 0\text{ m}$
3.  From $4\text{--}6\text{ s}$: Distance $= 5\text{ m}$ (moving from $5$ back to $0$)
4.  From $6\text{--}8\text{ s}$: Distance $= 3\text{ m}$ (moving from $0$ to $-3$)
5.  From $8\text{--}10\text{ s}$: Distance $= 0\text{ m}$
6.  From $10\text{--}12\text{ s}$: Distance $= 3\text{ m}$ (moving from $-3$ to $0$)

Total distance $= 5 + 0 + 5 + 3 + 0 + 3 = 16\text{ m}$.
Total time $= 12\text{ s}$.
Average speed $= \frac{16\text{ m}}{12\text{ s}} \approx 1.333... \text{ m/s}$. Rounded to two decimal places, this is $1.33\text{ m/s}$.

### c) Propose a reason for the negative displacement values shown in the graph.

**Answer:** The person has changed their direction of motion and moved past their initial starting position.

**Explanation:** In physics, displacement is a vector quantity. The positive and negative signs indicate direction relative to the reference point (the starting position, $x=0$). A negative displacement means the person has traveled in the opposite direction to their initial motion, moving behind the origin or "starting line."
