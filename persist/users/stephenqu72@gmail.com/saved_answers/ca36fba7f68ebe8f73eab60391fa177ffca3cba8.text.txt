To solve this problem, we must apply the principle of a **Velocity Selector**.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
For a charged particle to maintain a straight-line path while moving through perpendicular electric ($E$) and magnetic ($B$) fields, the net force acting on the particle must be zero. This means the magnitude of the electric force ($F_E$) must exactly balance the magnitude of the magnetic force ($F_B$):
$$F_E = F_B$$

Substituting the standard formulas for these forces:
$$qE = qvB$$

Since the charge $q$ is on both sides, we can divide by $q$:
$$E = vB$$
Rearranging to solve for the magnetic field $B$:
$$B = \frac{E}{v}$$

### 2. Identify the Given Values
*   Charge $q = 1.00 \times 10^{-3} \text{ C}$ (Note: As shown in the derivation, the magnitude of the charge is actually irrelevant here as it cancels out).
*   Electric field $E = 1.20 \times 10^{-3} \text{ NC}^{-1}$
*   Velocity $v = 6.50 \times 10^3 \text{ ms}^{-1}$

### 3. Calculation
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$B = \frac{1.20 \times 10^{-3}}{6.50 \times 10^3}$$
$$B \approx 0.1846 \times 10^{-6} \text{ T}$$
$$B \approx 1.846 \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}$$

Rounding to three significant figures, we get $1.85 \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}$.

### Conclusion
Comparing our result to the given options, the correct answer is **B**.
