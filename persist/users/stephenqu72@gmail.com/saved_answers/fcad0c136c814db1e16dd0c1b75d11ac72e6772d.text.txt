To answer this 9-mark question, you must demonstrate how scientific knowledge is non-linear and cumulative—Newton’s quote highlights that scientific progress relies on building upon the work of predecessors. You need to "evaluate," meaning you must judge the validity of this statement by showing how each atomic model was improved or refined by new empirical evidence.

### Model Answer

The development of the atomic model strongly validates Newton’s observation, as each transition was necessitated by experimental evidence that the preceding model could not explain.

*   **Dalton’s Billiard Ball Model (Early 1800s):** Dalton proposed that atoms were indivisible, solid spheres. This provided a foundational framework for chemical reactions. However, it was incomplete.
*   **Thomson’s Plum Pudding Model (1897):** Following his discovery of the electron using cathode ray tubes, J.J. Thomson "stood on the shoulders" of Dalton by showing the atom was divisible and contained subatomic particles. He proposed a sphere of positive charge with electrons embedded in it.
*   **Rutherford’s Nuclear Model (1911):** Rutherford’s Geiger-Marsden gold foil experiment provided evidence that contradicted the Plum Pudding model. The observation that some alpha particles deflected at large angles forced the conclusion that the atom has a tiny, dense, positively charged nucleus. Rutherford built upon Thomson’s work by refining the internal structure.
*   **Bohr’s Planetary Model (1913):** Rutherford’s model could not explain why electrons didn't spiral into the nucleus (classical physics predicted they should lose energy). Bohr incorporated Planck’s quantum theory, suggesting electrons exist in fixed energy levels. This explained the atomic emission spectra, showing that new theoretical tools allowed scientists to "see further."
*   **Schrödinger/Quantum Mechanical Model (1926):** Finally, building upon the wave-particle duality (de Broglie), Schrödinger introduced the concept of electron probability clouds (orbitals) rather than fixed orbits. This represents the current understanding, illustrating how we constantly refine models as our capacity to detect evidence (like subatomic wave properties) improves.

**Conclusion:** The statement is highly accurate. Each scientist relied on the failures and successes of their predecessors to propose superior models. Atomic theory is not a stagnant set of facts, but a dynamic, iterative process of discovery.

### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Structure your essay:** Use a chronological approach. Each paragraph should follow the sequence: *Existing model $\rightarrow$ Flaw/New evidence $\rightarrow$ Improved model*.
2.  **Use the "Evaluation":** Don't just list facts. Constantly refer back to the prompt. Use phrases like, "This confirms Newton's statement because..." or "This highlights the iterative nature of scientific progress."
3.  **Aim for Depth:** For 9 marks, you need at least 4-5 major milestones. Ensure you explicitly mention the *experimental evidence* (e.g., Gold Foil experiment, Emission Spectra) that acted as the catalyst for change.
