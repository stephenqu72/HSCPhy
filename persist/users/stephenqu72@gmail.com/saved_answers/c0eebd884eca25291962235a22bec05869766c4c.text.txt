### Answer
The correct answer is **C. $1.0\ c$**.

### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of Einstein’s Second Postulate of Special Relativity. The postulate states that the speed of light in a vacuum ($c$) is constant for all inertial observers, regardless of the motion of the source or the observer.

1.  **Understand the nature of the signal:** A "microwave message" consists of electromagnetic radiation. Electromagnetic radiation travels through a vacuum at the speed of light, $c$.
2.  **Apply Special Relativity:** According to the principles of Special Relativity, the speed of light is an absolute constant in all inertial frames of reference. It does not obey standard Galilean velocity addition (where you might incorrectly add the speeds of the spaceship and the microwave, i.e., $c + 0.4c = 1.4c$). 
3.  **Conclusion:** Even though the spaceship is moving toward the Earth at $0.4\ c$, the relative velocity between the spaceship and the incoming microwave signal will still be exactly $c$. Therefore, the speed at which the spaceship receives the message is $1.0\ c$.
