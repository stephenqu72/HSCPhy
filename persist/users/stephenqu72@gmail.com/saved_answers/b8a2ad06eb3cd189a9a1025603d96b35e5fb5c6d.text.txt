To determine which quantity is equal to the gradient of the line, we use the physics formula for the magnetic force $F$ acting on a current-carrying wire of length $L$ placed in a uniform magnetic field $B$:

$$F = BIL \sin(\theta)$$

Assuming the wire is placed perpendicular to the magnetic field (which is standard for these types of questions unless otherwise stated), $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$, simplifying the equation to:

$$F = (BL) \times I$$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Analyze the Equation:** Compare the formula $F = (BL) \times I$ to the linear equation $y = mx + c$.
    *   The variable plotted on the $y$-axis is Force ($F$).
    *   The variable plotted on the $x$-axis is Current ($I$).
    *   Since the graph passes through the origin, the intercept $c = 0$.
2.  **Identify the Gradient:** In the linear relationship $F = (BL)I$, the gradient $m$ is the coefficient of $I$. 
    *   Therefore, $m = B \times L$.
3.  **Conclusion:** The gradient of the graph represents the product of the magnetic field strength $B$ and the length of the wire $L$.
