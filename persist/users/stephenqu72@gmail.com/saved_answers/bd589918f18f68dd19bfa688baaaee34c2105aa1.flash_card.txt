### Front
What does the acceleration-time graph of a projectile look like in the absence of air resistance (taking 'up' as positive)?

### Back
- Key idea: Projectiles experience a constant downward gravitational acceleration.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a = -g = -9.8\text{ m/s}^2$ (a horizontal line below the time axis).
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem assuming no air resistance.
- Common trap: Confusing the acceleration-time graph with the velocity-time graph (which has a negative gradient) or the displacement-time graph (which is parabolic).
