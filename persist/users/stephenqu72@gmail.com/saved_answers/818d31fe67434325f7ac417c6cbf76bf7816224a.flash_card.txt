### Front
Distinguish between "accuracy" and "precision" in experimental measurements.

### Back
- Key idea: Accuracy is proximity to the true/accepted value; precision is the consistency/grouping of repeated measurements.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Evaluating the quality of experimental data or analyzing sets of results (e.g., target-style diagrams).
- Common trap: Assuming high precision implies high accuracy; a measurement can be highly consistent (precise) but consistently wrong (not accurate) due to systematic error.
