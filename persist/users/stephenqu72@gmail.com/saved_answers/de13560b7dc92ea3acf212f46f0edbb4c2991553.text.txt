To answer a 6-mark "Assess" question, you must provide a balanced judgement that highlights both the strengths (effectiveness) and the limitations (ineffectiveness) of the model in explaining physical phenomena.

### Suggested Answer
The Bohr-Rutherford model was a significant advancement in atomic physics, though it was ultimately incomplete.

**Strengths (Effectiveness):**
1. **Rutherford’s contribution:** The nuclear model effectively accounted for the Geiger-Marsden experiment. The large-angle scattering of alpha particles indicated that the positive charge and mass were concentrated in a tiny, dense nucleus, solving the instability issues of the previous Thomson "plum pudding" model.
2. **Bohr’s contribution:** Bohr successfully applied Planck’s quantum theory to explain the stability of atoms. By proposing that electrons occupy discrete, stable energy levels (stationary states) where they do not radiate energy, he resolved the classical electrodynamic paradox that an accelerating charge should spiral into the nucleus.
3. **Spectral lines:** Bohr’s model provided a quantitative explanation for the emission spectrum of hydrogen. By linking photon emission to energy transitions between shells ($\Delta E = E_2 - E_1 = hf$), it accurately predicted the wavelengths of the Balmer series.

**Limitations (Ineffectiveness):**
1. **Multi-electron atoms:** The model failed to predict the spectral lines of atoms with more than one electron, as it could not account for electron-electron repulsions.
2. **Relative intensities:** It could not explain the varying intensities of spectral lines.
3. **Zeeman effect:** It failed to explain the splitting of spectral lines in the presence of a magnetic field (the Zeeman effect).
4. **Theoretical inconsistency:** It was an "ad hoc" theory that blended classical mechanics with quantum postulates without a deeper theoretical basis for *why* orbits were quantized.

**Conclusion:**
While the Bohr-Rutherford model was highly effective as a transitionary framework—explaining atomic stability and hydrogen’s spectra—it was limited in scope, eventually being superseded by the Schrödinger wave-mechanical model.

### How to solve this question
1.  **Understand the Verb:** "Assess" means to make a judgment based on criteria. You must give evidence for the model's success (strengths) and its failures (limitations).
2.  **Structure:** Use a clear structure. Start with the Rutherford aspect (nuclear atom), move to the Bohr aspect (quantized energy levels), then transition to the shortcomings.
3.  **Use Specifics:** Always mention specific experiments (e.g., Geiger-Marsden alpha scattering, hydrogen emission spectra) to gain full marks.
4.  **Balance:** Since it is a 6-mark question, spend roughly 3 marks on the successes and 3 marks on the failures.
