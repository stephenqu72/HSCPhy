### Front
How does the gravitational force between two objects change if one mass increases by a factor of 4 and the distance between their centres decreases by one-third?

### Back
- Key idea: Proportionality in Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation ($F \propto m$ and $F \propto 1/r^2$).
- Formula / law / constant: $F = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$
- When to use it: To calculate the change in gravitational force when mass or separation distance varies.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the change in distance (e.g., $(2/3)^2 = 4/9$) and misinterpreting "decreased by one-third" as the new distance being $1/3$ rather than $2/3$.
