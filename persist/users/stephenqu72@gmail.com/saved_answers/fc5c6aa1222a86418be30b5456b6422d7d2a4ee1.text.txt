To determine the direction of the force on the electron, we use the **Right-Hand Rule (RHR)** for magnetic forces, keeping in mind that the electron has a **negative charge**.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(D) Out of the page**.

### Explanation
To solve this, follow these steps using the Right-Hand Rule:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   The magnetic field ($\vec{B}$) is pointing **up the page**.
    *   The velocity of the electron ($v$) is pointing to the **left** (it is moving into the field from the right).
    *   The charge of an electron is **negative** ($q < 0$).

2.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule (for a positive charge):**
    *   Point your right-hand fingers in the direction of the velocity (to the **left**).
    *   Curl your fingers toward the direction of the magnetic field ($\vec{B}$) (upward).
    *   Your thumb now points **into the page**. This is the direction the force *would* be if the charge were positive.

3.  **Adjust for the negative charge:**
    *   Since the electron has a negative charge, the force will be in the **opposite direction** of what your right-hand thumb indicated.
    *   Opposite to "into the page" is **out of the page**.

Alternatively, you can use the left-hand rule directly for electrons (negative charges), where the thumb points to the force, the index finger to the magnetic field, and the middle finger to the velocity. 

Using the Lorentz force formula $\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$, because $q$ is negative, the direction of the cross product $\vec{v} \times \vec{B}$ is flipped. Since $\vec{v}$ (left) crossed with $\vec{B}$ (up) results in a vector pointing "into the page," multiplying by the negative charge $q$ forces the result to point **out of the page**.
