To find the wavelength of the emitted photon when an electron transitions from $n=3$ to the ground state ($n=1$) in a hydrogen atom, we use the **Rydberg formula**:

$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   The Rydberg constant $R_H \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \text{ m}^{-1}$.
    *   The final energy level $n_f = 1$ (ground state).
    *   The initial energy level $n_i = 3$.

2.  **Substitute into the formula:**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{1}{1^2} - \frac{1}{3^2} \right)$$
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( 1 - \frac{1}{9} \right)$$
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{8}{9} \right)$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \times 0.8889 \approx 9.75 \times 10^6 \text{ m}^{-1}$$
    $$\lambda = \frac{1}{9.75 \times 10^6} \approx 1.0256 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$$

4.  **Convert to nanometers (nm):**
    Since $1 \text{ m} = 10^9 \text{ nm}$:
    $$\lambda \approx 1.0256 \times 10^{-7} \times 10^9 \text{ nm} = 102.56 \text{ nm}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, $102.56 \text{ nm}$ is closest to **105 nm**.

**Correct Option: (C)**
