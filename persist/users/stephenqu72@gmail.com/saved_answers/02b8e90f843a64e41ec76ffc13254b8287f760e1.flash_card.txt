### Front
What is the formula for gravitational field strength ($g$) at a distance $r$ from the centre of a planet with mass $M$?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational field strength is the gravitational force per unit mass.
- Formula: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- When to use it: To calculate the acceleration due to gravity at a specific point in a radial gravitational field.
- Common trap: Confusing it with the formula for gravitational force ($F = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$); remember that $g$ is independent of the mass of the object in the field.
