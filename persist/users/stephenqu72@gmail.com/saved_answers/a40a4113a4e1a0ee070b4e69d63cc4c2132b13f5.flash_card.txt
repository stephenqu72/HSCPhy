### Front
What is the general term for composite particles formed by the combination of two or more quarks held together by the strong nuclear force?

### Back
- Key idea: Hadrons are composite particles made of quarks; they include baryons (3 quarks) and mesons (quark-antiquark).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When identifying particles that experience the strong nuclear force in the Standard Model.
- Common trap: Confusing Hadrons (composite particles) with Leptons (fundamental particles like electrons that do not experience the strong force).
