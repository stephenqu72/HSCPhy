### Answer

Newton’s corpuscular (particle) model and Huygens’ wave model provided competing explanations for the observable properties of light:

1.  **Reflection:** 
    *   **Newton:** Particles of light bounce off surfaces like perfectly elastic spheres. The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection due to conservation of momentum.
    *   **Huygens:** Wavefronts strike a surface, and each point on the wavefront acts as a source of secondary wavelets. The geometry of the reflected wavelets results in the angle of incidence equaling the angle of reflection.

2.  **Refraction:**
    *   **Newton:** Newton argued light particles are attracted toward a denser medium by a force, causing them to accelerate as they enter, which explains why the angle of refraction is smaller than the angle of incidence.
    *   **Huygens:** Huygens proposed that light travels slower in a denser medium. Using his principle that every point on a wavefront is a source of spherical wavelets, he showed that the change in speed causes the wavefront to change direction (bend) towards the normal.

3.  **Rectilinear Propagation (Shadows):**
    *   **Newton:** Because light particles have mass/momentum and travel in straight lines unless acted upon by a force, they form sharp-edged shadows, consistent with the observed linear path of light.
    *   **Huygens:** Huygens explained this by noting that waves travel in straight lines perpendicular to the wavefront. While he struggled initially with diffraction, he explained that shadows are formed because the wave fronts are effectively planar, traveling in a straight trajectory away from the source.
