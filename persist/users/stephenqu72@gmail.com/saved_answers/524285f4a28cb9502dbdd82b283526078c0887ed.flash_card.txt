### Front
A planet orbits a star in an elliptical path. Compare the kinetic energy ($E_k$) of the planet at perihelion ($P$) versus aphelion ($Q$).

### Back
- Key idea: Kinetic energy is highest at perihelion because the planet's orbital speed is greatest when closest to the star.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Kepler’s Second Law (Equal areas in equal times); $E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.
- When to use it: To determine energy changes in elliptical orbits or orbital velocity variations.
- Common trap: Confusing "more negative" potential energy ($U$) with "smaller magnitude"—$U$ is actually lower (more negative) when the planet is closer to the star.
