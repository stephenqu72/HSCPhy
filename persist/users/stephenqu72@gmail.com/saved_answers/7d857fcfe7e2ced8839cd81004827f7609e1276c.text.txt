To solve for the magnetic permeability constant $\mu_0$, we use the formula for the force per unit length between two long, parallel, current-carrying wires:

$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$

Since $I_1 = I_2 = I = 25.5\text{ A}$, the formula becomes:
$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I^2}{2\pi r}$$

### Step 1: Convert all values to SI units
*   Length of wires $L = 70.0\text{ cm} = 0.700\text{ m}$
*   Separation $r = 0.50\text{ cm} = 0.0050\text{ m}$
*   Change in mass $\Delta m = 0.500\text{ g} = 0.000500\text{ kg}$
*   Force $F = \Delta m \cdot g = 0.000500\text{ kg} \times 9.8\text{ m/s}^2 = 0.0049\text{ N}$ (using $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$)

### Step 2: Rearrange the formula to solve for $\mu_0$
$$\mu_0 = \frac{F \cdot 2\pi r}{L \cdot I^2}$$

### Step 3: Substitute the values
$$\mu_0 = \frac{0.0049 \times 2 \times \pi \times 0.0050}{0.700 \times (25.5)^2}$$
$$\mu_0 = \frac{0.000153938}{455.3925} \approx 3.38 \times 10^{-7} \approx 3.4 \times 10^{-7}$$

The correct answer is **(A)**.
