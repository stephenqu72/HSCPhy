To solve this problem, we must identify the condition for a charged particle to travel through a "velocity selector" (crossed electric and magnetic fields) without being deflected.

### 1. The Physics Principle
For the electron to pass through undeflected, the net force on it must be zero. This means the magnitude of the electric force ($F_E$) must equal the magnitude of the magnetic force ($F_B$):
$$F_E = F_B$$

Using the fundamental equations for these forces:
- Electric force: $F_E = qE$
- Magnetic force: $F_B = qvB \sin(\theta)$

Since the magnetic field is perpendicular to the velocity of the electron, $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$. Thus:
$$qE = qvB$$
$$v = \frac{E}{B}$$

### 2. Finding the Electric Field ($E$)
The electric field between two parallel plates is given by $E = \frac{V}{d}$, where $V$ is the potential difference and $d$ is the distance between the plates.
- $V = 400\text{ V}$
- $d = 20.0\text{ mm} = 0.020\text{ m}$

$$E = \frac{400}{0.020} = 20,000\text{ V/m} = 2.0 \times 10^4\text{ V/m}$$

### 3. Calculating the Speed ($v$)
Now, substitute $E$ and the given magnetic field $B = 8.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ T}$ into the velocity equation:
$$v = \frac{2.0 \times 10^4}{8.0 \times 10^{-3}}$$
$$v = 0.25 \times 10^7\text{ m/s}$$
$$v = 2.5 \times 10^6\text{ m/s}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **D**.

### Summary of Steps
1. Set the electric force equal to the magnetic force: $qE = qvB$.
2. Simplify to find $v = \frac{E}{B}$.
3. Calculate $E$ using $E = \frac{V}{d}$.
4. Divide $E$ by $B$ to obtain the velocity.
