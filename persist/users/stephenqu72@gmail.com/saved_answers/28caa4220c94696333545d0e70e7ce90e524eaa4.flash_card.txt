### Front
For planets of equal radius, what is the graphical relationship between planetary mass ($M$) and surface gravitational field strength ($g$)?

### Back
- Key idea: $g$ is directly proportional to $M$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2} \rightarrow g \propto M$ (Linear graph through the origin).
- When to use it: Comparing surface gravity across different planets where radius is held constant.
- Common trap: Confusing direct proportionality ($g \propto M$) with inverse-square proportionality ($g \propto 1/r^2$).
