To solve this problem, we apply **Newton's Second Law of Motion**, which states that the net force acting on an object is equal to the product of its mass and its acceleration:

$$F_{net} = ma$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given variables:**
    *   Mass ($m$) = $87\text{ kg}$
    *   Net Force ($F_{net}$) = $26\text{ N}$

2.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for acceleration ($a$):**
    $$a = \frac{F_{net}}{m}$$

3.  **Substitute the values into the formula:**
    $$a = \frac{26\text{ N}}{87\text{ kg}}$$

4.  **Calculate the result:**
    $$a \approx 0.29885\text{ m s}^{-2}$$

Rounding this to one significant figure (as per the precision of the options provided), we get approximately $0.3\text{ m s}^{-2}$.
