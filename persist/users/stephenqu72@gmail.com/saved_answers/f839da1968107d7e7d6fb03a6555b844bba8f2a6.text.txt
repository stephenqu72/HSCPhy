### Explanation

To find the wavelength of the emitted photon during an electron transition, follow these steps:

**1. Determine the energy difference ($\Delta E$) between the levels:**
The energy of the electron at $n=4$ is $E_4 = -0.85\text{ eV}$ and at $n=1$ is $E_1 = -13.6\text{ eV}$.
The energy of the emitted photon is equal to the difference between these two states:
$$\Delta E = E_{\text{initial}} - E_{\text{final}}$$
$$\Delta E = -0.85\text{ eV} - (-13.6\text{ eV}) = 12.75\text{ eV}$$

**2. Convert the energy from eV to Joules:**
Since $1\text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$:
$$\Delta E = 12.75 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J} \approx 2.04255 \times 10^{-18}\text{ J}$$

**3. Use the photon energy formula to find the wavelength ($\lambda$):**
The relationship between energy and wavelength is given by $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$, which rearranges to:
$$\lambda = \frac{hc}{\Delta E}$$
Where:
- $h$ (Planck's constant) $\approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$
- $c$ (speed of light) $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$

Plugging in the values:
$$\lambda = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)}{2.04255 \times 10^{-18}}$$
$$\lambda \approx \frac{1.9878 \times 10^{-25}}{2.04255 \times 10^{-18}}$$
$$\lambda \approx 9.73 \times 10^{-8}\text{ m}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, **C ($9.72 \times 10^{-8}\text{ m}$)** is the correct choice (the slight variation is due to rounding constants).
