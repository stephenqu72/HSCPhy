### Front
What are the directional properties of instantaneous velocity ($\vec{v}$) and centripetal acceleration ($\vec{a}$) for an object in uniform circular motion?

### Back
- Key idea: Velocity is tangential; acceleration is centripetal (radial).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\vec{v} \perp \vec{a}$; $\vec{a}$ points toward the center.
- When to use it: Any uniform circular motion problem requiring vector direction analysis.
- Common trap: Assuming acceleration is in the direction of motion (it is always perpendicular to velocity).
