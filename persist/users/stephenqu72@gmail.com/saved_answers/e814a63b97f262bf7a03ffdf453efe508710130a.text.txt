To solve this problem, we need to calculate the frequency of the photon emitted when an electron transitions from a higher energy state ($n=5$) to a lower energy state ($n=3$).

### 1. Identify the Energy Values
From the provided energy level diagram for mercury:
*   Energy at $n=5$ ($E_5$) = $-3.71 \text{ eV}$
*   Energy at $n=3$ ($E_3$) = $-5.52 \text{ eV}$

### 2. Calculate the Energy Difference ($\Delta E$)
The energy of the emitted photon is equal to the difference between the two energy levels:
$$\Delta E = E_5 - E_3$$
$$\Delta E = -3.71 \text{ eV} - (-5.52 \text{ eV})$$
$$\Delta E = 1.81 \text{ eV}$$

### 3. Convert Energy from eV to Joules
Since the standard unit for frequency calculations involves Joules, we convert the energy using the conversion factor $1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$:
$$\Delta E = 1.81 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$
$$\Delta E \approx 2.90 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

### 4. Calculate the Frequency ($f$)
Using the photon energy formula $E = hf$, where $h$ is Planck's constant ($6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$):
$$f = \frac{\Delta E}{h}$$
$$f = \frac{2.90 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}}{6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}}$$
$$f \approx 4.377 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $4.4 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$.

Comparing this to the given options:
(A) $1.81 \text{ Hz}$
(B) $4.4 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$
(C) $2.89 \times 10^{-19} \text{ Hz}$
(D) $7.4 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$

The correct option is **(B)**.
