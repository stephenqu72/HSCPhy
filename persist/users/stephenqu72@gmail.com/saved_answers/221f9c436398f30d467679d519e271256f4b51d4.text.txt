To solve this problem, we need to relate the de Broglie wavelength to the kinetic energy gained by a charged particle accelerating through a potential difference.

### Explanation:

1.  **De Broglie wavelength formula:** The de Broglie wavelength $\lambda$ of a particle with momentum $p$ is given by:
    $$\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$$
    where $h$ is Planck's constant.

2.  **Relating momentum to kinetic energy:** The kinetic energy $E_k$ of a particle with mass $m$ and momentum $p$ is given by:
    $$E_k = \frac{p^2}{2m}$$
    Rearranging for momentum, we get:
    $$p = \sqrt{2m E_k}$$

3.  **Energy gained from a potential difference:** A charge $q$ accelerated from rest through a potential difference $V_A$ gains kinetic energy equal to the work done by the electric field:
    $$E_k = q V_A$$

4.  **Substituting into the wavelength equation:** Substitute the expression for $E_k$ into the momentum equation, and then into the wavelength formula:
    $$p = \sqrt{2m q V_A}$$
    $$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2m q V_A}}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches option (B).

### Answer:
The correct option is **(B)**.
