To solve this problem, we use the relationship between the energy of a photon and its wavelength, given by the formula:

$$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$

Where:
- $h$ (Planck's constant) $\approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$
- $c$ (speed of light) $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
- $\lambda$ (wavelength) $= 640 \text{ nm} = 640 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$

### Part a: Calculation

1.  **Calculate energy in Joules:**
    $$E = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)}{640 \times 10^{-9}}$$
    $$E \approx 3.1059 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

2.  **Convert Joules to eV:**
    Since $1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$, divide the result by $1.602 \times 10^{-19}$:
    $$E(\text{eV}) = \frac{3.1059 \times 10^{-19}}{1.602 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 1.9388 \text{ eV}$$
    Rounding to two decimal places, we get **$1.94 \text{ eV}$**.
