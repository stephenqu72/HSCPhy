To answer this question, we must apply the principles of the Lorentz force acting on charged particles moving through a magnetic field.

### Explanation of the Physics
When different types of radiation pass through a uniform magnetic field (perpendicular to the direction of motion), they behave differently based on their charge and mass:

1.  **Gamma ($\gamma$) radiation:** These are high-energy photons. They have no charge, so they are not affected by the magnetic field and continue in a **straight line**.
2.  **Alpha ($\alpha$) particles:** These are helium nuclei ($He^{2+}$). They have a positive charge and a relatively large mass. They will be deflected in a wide arc according to the right-hand rule.
3.  **Beta ($\beta$) particles:** These are electrons ($e^-$) or positrons ($e^+$). Because they have a much smaller mass than alpha particles (despite their charge), they experience a significantly tighter deflection curve compared to alpha particles for the same momentum.
