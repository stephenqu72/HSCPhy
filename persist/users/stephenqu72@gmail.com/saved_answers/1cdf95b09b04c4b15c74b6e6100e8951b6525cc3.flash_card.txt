### Front
How does an electric field ($E$) deflect a charged particle, and what determines the direction of the force ($F$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Charged particles experience a force $F = qE$. Opposites attract, likes repel.
- **Formula / law:** $F = qE$, where $q$ is charge and $E$ is the field vector (points from $+$ to $-$).
- **When to use it:** To identify unknown radiation (alpha/beta) or calculate the path of charged particles between parallel plates.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the direction of the electric field ($+$ to $-$) with the direction of electron flow ($- \rightarrow +$); always check the polarity of the plates provided in the diagram.
