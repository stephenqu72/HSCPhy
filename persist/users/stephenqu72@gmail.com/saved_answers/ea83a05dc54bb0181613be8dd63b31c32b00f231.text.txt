### Answer

**(a) Describe how a transformer works and explain how the iron core increases efficiency.**

A transformer operates on the principle of **Faraday’s Law of Induction**. An alternating current (AC) in the primary coil creates a continuously changing magnetic flux. This flux is linked to the secondary coil via the iron core. According to Faraday’s Law, the changing magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) or voltage in the secondary coil.

The iron core increases efficiency by providing a path of high magnetic permeability. This ensures that almost all the magnetic flux generated by the primary coil is confined and "guided" to the secondary coil (minimizing flux leakage). By concentrating the magnetic field, the core maximizes the linkage between the coils, ensuring that the induced EMF is as high as possible.

**(b) Determine the output current.**

*   **Given:**
    *   Input Voltage ($V_p$) = $20\,000\text{ V}$
    *   Input Current ($I_p$) = $100\text{ A}$
    *   Output Voltage ($V_s$) = $12\,400\text{ V}$
    *   Efficiency ($\eta$) = $90\% = 0.90$

*   **Calculation:**
    Efficiency is the ratio of output power ($P_s$) to input power ($P_p$):
    $$\eta = \frac{P_s}{P_p}$$
    $$0.90 = \frac{V_s \times I_s}{V_p \times I_p}$$
    $$0.90 = \frac{12\,400 \times I_s}{20\,000 \times 100}$$
    $$0.90 = \frac{12\,400 \times I_s}{2\,000\,000}$$
    $$I_s = \frac{0.90 \times 2\,000\,000}{12\,400}$$
    $$I_s \approx 145.16\text{ A}$$

The output current is approximately **$145.16\text{ A}$**.
