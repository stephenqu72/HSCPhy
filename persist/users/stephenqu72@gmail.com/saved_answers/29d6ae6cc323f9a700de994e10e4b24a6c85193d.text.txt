### Answer
The correct answer is **C. $80\text{ m s}^{-1}$**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, you need to apply the principles of **projectile motion**. 

1.  **Analyze the components of motion:** Projectile motion is divided into two independent components:
    *   **Vertical motion ($y$):** Influenced by gravity ($g \approx 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$), which causes the object to accelerate downwards.
    *   **Horizontal motion ($x$):** In the absence of air resistance (which is assumed in standard HSC physics problems unless otherwise stated), there are no forces acting in the horizontal direction.

2.  **Apply Newton’s First Law:** According to Newton’s First Law of Motion, an object in motion will maintain a constant velocity unless acted upon by a net external force. Since there is no horizontal force acting on the paintball after it is fired, the horizontal component of the velocity remains constant throughout the entire flight.

3.  **Conclusion:** The initial horizontal velocity is given as $80\text{ m s}^{-1}$. Because the horizontal acceleration is $0\text{ m s}^{-2}$, the horizontal velocity at any point in the flight—including just before impact—must also be $80\text{ m s}^{-1}$. The height of $10\text{ m}$ and the distance of $25\text{ m}$ are provided as distractors; they would be necessary to calculate the vertical velocity or the time of flight, but they do not affect the horizontal velocity.
