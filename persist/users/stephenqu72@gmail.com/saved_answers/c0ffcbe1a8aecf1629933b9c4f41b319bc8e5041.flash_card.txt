### Front
In a free-body diagram of a satellite in a stable circular orbit, which force(s) should be drawn?

### Back
- Key idea: Only the gravitational force ($F_g$) acting toward the center of the orbit.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_g = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$; Newton’s First Law.
- When to use it: Whenever drawing a free-body diagram for an object in uniform circular motion under gravity.
- Common trap: Including a "centripetal force" as an additional vector or drawing a propulsive force/normal force. Centripetal force is the *result* of gravity, not a separate physical force.
