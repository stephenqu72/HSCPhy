### Front
Why does the mechanical energy gained by a satellite not equal the chemical energy expended by its launch rocket, and does this violate the conservation of energy?

### Back
- Key idea: Energy is not "lost"; it is transformed into non-mechanical forms (heat, sound, exhaust KE) during launch. The total energy of the closed system (rocket + satellite + environment) remains constant.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Law of Conservation of Energy; $E_{total} = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$.
- When to use it: To explain discrepancies between fuel input and orbital energy gains in satellite launch scenarios.
- Common trap: Assuming the satellite’s final mechanical energy should equal the fuel's chemical energy, ignoring significant losses to the surroundings.
