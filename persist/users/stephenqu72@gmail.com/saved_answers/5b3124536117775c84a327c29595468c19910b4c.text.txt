To determine which situation produces the greatest average current, we must apply Faraday’s Law of Induction, which states that the induced electromotive force (EMF) is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux ($\Phi$) through the coil:

$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$$

The magnetic flux is given by $\Phi = BA \cos \theta$, where $\theta$ is the angle between the magnetic field lines and the normal to the coil's surface. A current $I$ is induced when there is a change in the flux passing through the coil.

### Analysis of the options:

*   **(A)** The magnet rotates about its vertical center axis. The magnetic field lines emitted from the poles remain largely directed toward or away from the coil. Since the magnetic field $B$ and the angle $\theta$ relative to the coil do not change significantly, there is minimal change in flux ($\Delta \Phi \approx 0$).
*   **(B)** The coil rotates about its vertical axis. As it spins, the angle $\theta$ between the magnetic field lines (which are horizontal) and the normal to the coil changes continuously from $0^\circ$ to $90^\circ$ and beyond. This causes a significant, periodic change in magnetic flux, inducing an alternating current.
*   **(C)** The coil rotates about the horizontal axis passing through the center of the magnet. Because the magnetic field lines are generally parallel to this axis, the flux remains near zero throughout the rotation (the field lines are always parallel to the plane of the coil, so $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\cos 90^\circ = 0$). Very little to no current is induced.
*   **(D)** The magnet rotates about its long horizontal axis. Due to the symmetry of the bar magnet, the magnetic field lines in the region of the coil remain essentially unchanged as the magnet spins on its own axis. Therefore, the flux does not change, and no significant current is induced.

### Conclusion:
Option **(B)** creates the greatest change in magnetic flux over time ($\Delta \Phi / \Delta t$) by changing the orientation of the coil relative to the magnetic field. This results in the largest induced EMF and, consequently, the greatest average current.

**Correct Answer: (B)**
