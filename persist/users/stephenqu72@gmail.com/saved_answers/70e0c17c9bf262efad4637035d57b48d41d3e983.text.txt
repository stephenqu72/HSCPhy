### Model Answer

Satellite orbits are primarily determined by their altitude, which dictates their orbital period and the area of the Earth's surface they can observe.

1.  **Low Earth Orbits (LEO):** These satellites orbit at low altitudes (typically $200–2,000 \text{ km}$). Because they are close to Earth, they move at high velocities to maintain orbit, resulting in a short orbital period (often around 90 minutes). Their proximity allows for high-resolution imaging and low-latency communication. **Uses:** Earth observation (mapping, weather monitoring, spy satellites) and the International Space Station.
2.  **Medium Earth Orbits (MEO):** These exist between LEO and geostationary altitudes. They are commonly used for navigation systems. By using a constellation of satellites in MEO, a receiver on the ground can maintain contact with multiple satellites simultaneously to triangulate precise positions. **Uses:** Global Positioning Systems (GPS).
3.  **Geostationary Orbits (GEO):** These satellites orbit directly above the equator at an altitude of approximately $35,800 \text{ km}$. At this specific distance, their orbital period matches the Earth's rotation period (24 hours). Consequently, they appear fixed at a single point in the sky relative to an observer on the ground. **Uses:** Satellite TV broadcasting and continuous weather monitoring, as they provide a stable link and a constant view of a large portion of the Earth’s surface.

### How to solve this
*   **Deconstruct the Verb:** The command word "Describe" requires you to provide the characteristics of the orbits, not just list them. You must explain *how* the altitude affects the orbit and *why* that makes it suitable for a specific use.
*   **Structure:** Use clear headings or distinct paragraphs for LEO, MEO, and GEO. This makes your response easy for a marker to follow and ensures you hit all criteria of the marking rubric.
*   **The "Link":** The prompt explicitly asks you to "relate satellite orbits to their uses." If you describe the physics but forget the applications (or vice versa), you will lose marks.
*   **Key Physics Principles:** Mentioning the relationship between altitude and orbital period (Kepler’s Third Law) demonstrates a deeper understanding of the physics involved, which is essential for higher-band responses.
