### Answer

According to the Standard Model of particle physics, both protons and neutrons are baryons composed of three quarks held together by the strong nuclear force.

**1. Composition and Charge:**
*   A **proton** is composed of two up quarks ($u$) and one down quark ($d$), giving it a quark composition of $uud$. The charges of these quarks are $+\frac{2}{3}e$ and $-\frac{1}{3}e$ respectively. Thus, the total charge is:
    $$+\frac{2}{3}e + \frac{2}{3}e - \frac{1}{3}e = +1e$$
*   A **neutron** is composed of one up quark and two down quarks, giving it a quark composition of $udd$. Its total charge is:
    $$+\frac{2}{3}e - \frac{1}{3}e - \frac{1}{3}e = 0$$

**2. Mass:**
*   While both particles are made of three quarks, their masses differ slightly. A neutron is approximately $0.14\%$ heavier than a proton. This difference is primarily attributed to the "flavor" of the quarks; a down quark has a slightly higher intrinsic mass than an up quark. Additionally, the complex interactions of the strong force (gluon exchange) and the energy associated with these quark-gluon interactions contribute to the total mass of the hadrons.
