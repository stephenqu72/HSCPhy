To solve this problem, we use the formula for the fringe spacing (the distance between adjacent maxima) in a double-slit interference pattern:

$$\Delta x = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$

Where:
- $\Delta x$ is the fringe spacing.
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the light.
- $L$ is the distance to the screen.
- $d$ is the distance between the slits.

### Experiment 1: Changing Wavelength ($\lambda$)
*   **Physics:** Red light has a longer wavelength than blue light ($\lambda_{red} > \lambda_{blue}$).
*   **Prediction:** Since $\Delta x \propto \lambda$, the red light will have a larger fringe spacing (wider pattern) and the blue light will have a smaller fringe spacing (narrower pattern).
*   **Comparison:** Graph $X$ is wider (the peaks are further apart) than Graph $W$. Therefore, **Graph $X$ is Red light** and **Graph $W$ is Blue light**.

### Experiment 2: Changing Slit Distance ($d$)
*   **Physics:** The fringe spacing is inversely proportional to the slit separation ($\Delta x \propto 1/d$).
*   **Prediction:** A *larger* slit distance ($d$) results in a *smaller* fringe spacing ($\Delta x$), meaning the pattern becomes narrower. A *smaller* slit distance ($d$) results in a *larger* fringe spacing ($\Delta x$), meaning the pattern becomes wider.
*   **Comparison:** Graph $Z$ is wider (peaks further apart) than Graph $Y$. Therefore, **Graph $Z$ corresponds to a Small slit distance** and **Graph $Y$ corresponds to a Large slit distance**.

### Conclusion
- Experiment 1: $W$ = Blue, $X$ = Red
- Experiment 2: $Y$ = Large slit distance, $Z$ = Small slit distance

Matching these findings with the provided options:
- (D) identifies $W$ as Blue, $X$ as Red, $Y$ as Large slit distance, and $Z$ as Small slit distance.
