### Explanation
To determine which vector diagram is correct, we must analyze the forces acting on the cyclist when they are moving **faster** than the design speed of a banked track.

1.  **Understand "Design Speed":** The design speed is the specific speed where the horizontal component of the normal force ($N \sin \theta$) provides the exact centripetal force required to maintain circular motion, meaning no friction is required.
2.  **Analyze the "Faster than Design Speed" scenario:**
    *   If the cyclist travels faster than the design speed, the required centripetal force ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$) increases.
    *   The horizontal component of the normal force alone is insufficient to keep the cyclist on the track radius. The cyclist effectively "wants" to slide **up** the bank.
    *   To prevent this outward sliding, static friction must act **down the slope** of the track to provide additional centripetal force.
3.  **Evaluating the forces:**
    *   **Gravity ($mg$):** Always acts vertically downward.
    *   **Normal Force ($N$):** Always acts perpendicular to the surface of the track.
    *   **Friction ($f$):** Since the cyclist is moving too fast and tends to slide up the slope, friction must act down the slope to oppose this motion.
4.  **Identifying the correct vector diagram:**
    *   Diagram **C** shows the Normal force pointing perpendicular to the slope and the friction force pointing **down the slope**. This combination creates a larger resultant horizontal force, providing the extra centripetal force needed for the higher speed.
    *   *Note:* If the cyclist were moving slower than the design speed, they would tend to slide down, and friction would point up the slope (which would look like diagram A).

Therefore, Diagram C correctly illustrates the forces when the cyclist is travelling faster than the design speed.
