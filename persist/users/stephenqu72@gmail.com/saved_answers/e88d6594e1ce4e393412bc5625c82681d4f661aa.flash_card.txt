### Front
How do you determine the direction of the magnetic field around a straight current-carrying wire?

### Back
- Key idea: The magnetic field forms concentric circles around the wire.
- Formula / law / figure: Right-Hand Grip Rule.
- When to use it: Whenever you need to identify the orientation of $B$-field lines relative to current direction $I$.
- Common trap: Using the left hand instead of the right, or confusing the current direction with electron flow (always use **conventional current**).
