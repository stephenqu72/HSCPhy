Assuming standard projectile motion parameters where the initial vertical velocity component is $v_y$ and the initial horizontal velocity component is $v_x$:

### Answer
The angle to the horizontal $\theta$ is calculated using the inverse tangent function of the ratio of the vertical velocity component to the horizontal velocity component:
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{v_y}{v_x}\right)$$

### How to solve it
1. **Identify the components:** Look at your data (or graph) to determine the initial vertical velocity ($v_y$) and the initial horizontal velocity ($v_x$). The horizontal velocity remains constant throughout the flight, while the vertical velocity changes due to gravity. You need the initial values ($t = 0$).
2. **Use Trigonometry:** In projectile motion, the velocity vector forms a right-angled triangle where the hypotenuse is the initial velocity ($v$), the opposite side is the vertical component ($v_y$), and the adjacent side is the horizontal component ($v_x$).
3. **Apply the formula:** Since $\tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$, we use $\tan(\theta) = \frac{v_y}{v_x}$.
4. **Calculate:** Take the inverse tangent of the ratio to find the angle in degrees. Ensure your calculator is set to **degrees mode** rather than radians.

**Example:** If $v_y = 20\text{ m/s}$ and $v_x = 34.6\text{ m/s}$:
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{20}{34.6}\right) \approx 30^\circ$$
