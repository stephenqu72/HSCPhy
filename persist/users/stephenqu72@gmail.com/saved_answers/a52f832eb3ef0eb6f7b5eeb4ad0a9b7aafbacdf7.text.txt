To determine the correct statement, we must analyze the distribution of current in the circuit when the switch is closed.

**1. Identify the circuit components:**
The circuit consists of a $12V$ source in series with a $20 \Omega$ resistor, followed by two resistors ($10 \Omega$ and $5 \Omega$) connected in parallel.

**2. Analyze the parallel section:**
In a parallel circuit, the voltage across each branch is the same. Let the voltage across the parallel combination be $V_p$.
According to Ohm's Law ($I = V/R$), the current through each resistor is:
- Current through the $10 \Omega$ resistor: $I_{10} = V_p / 10$
- Current through the $5 \Omega$ resistor: $I_{5} = V_p / 5$

Comparing these two, since $5 \Omega$ is half of $10 \Omega$, the current through the $5 \Omega$ resistor must be twice the current through the $10 \Omega$ resistor ($I_5 = 2 \times I_{10}$). This makes **statement (B) true**.

**3. Analyze the total current:**
The total current ($I_{total}$) of the circuit passes through the $20 \Omega$ resistor. This total current then splits into the $10 \Omega$ and $5 \Omega$ branches. Therefore:
- The total current ($I_{20}$) must be greater than the current in either individual parallel branch ($I_{10}$ or $I_5$).
- This invalidates statement (C) (since $I_5 < I_{20}$) and statement (D) (since $I_{10} < I_5$).
- Regarding (A): Since $I_{20} = I_{10} + I_5 = I_{10} + 2I_{10} = 3I_{10}$, the current through the $10 \Omega$ resistor is actually one-third of the current through the $20 \Omega$ resistor, not half.
