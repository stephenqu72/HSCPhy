### Answer

**(a)** The Balmer series consists of multiple lines because hydrogen atoms have many discrete energy levels ($n=3, 4, 5, \dots$). When excited electrons transition from these higher energy levels down to the $n=2$ level, they emit photons with energies corresponding to the difference between these levels. Since energy is quantized ($E = \Delta E = E_{initial} - E_{final}$), each specific transition produces a photon of a specific frequency and wavelength. Other series exist because electrons can transition to different lower energy levels: transitions to $n=1$ form the Lyman series (UV), and transitions to $n=3$ form the Paschen series (IR).

**(b)** The longest wavelength corresponds to the smallest energy transition within the series. For the Balmer series, this is the transition from $n=3$ to $n=2$.
Using the Rydberg formula:
$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$
Where $R_H \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \, \text{m}^{-1}$, $n_f = 2$, and $n_i = 3$:
$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{1}{2^2} - \frac{1}{3^2} \right)$$
$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{9} \right) = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{5}{36} \right)$$
$$\frac{1}{\lambda} \approx 1,523,611 \, \text{m}^{-1}$$
$$\lambda \approx 6.563 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m} = 656.3 \, \text{nm}$$
