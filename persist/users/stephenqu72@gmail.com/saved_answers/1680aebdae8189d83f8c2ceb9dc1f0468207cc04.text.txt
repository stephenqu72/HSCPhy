To determine how to increase the force on a current-carrying conductor, we refer to the physics formula for the magnetic force ($F$) acting on a wire:

$$F = BIl \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
*   $B$ is the magnetic field strength.
*   $I$ is the magnitude of the current.
*   $l$ is the length of the conductor within the magnetic field.
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the conductor and the magnetic field lines.

### Answer
Two ways to increase the force on the conductor are:
1.  **Increase the current ($I$)** flowing through the conductor.
2.  **Increase the strength of the magnetic field ($B$)** (for example, by using stronger magnets).
*(Other acceptable answers include increasing the length $l$ of the conductor within the field, or adjusting the angle $\theta$ to be closer to $90^{\circ}$, where $\sin(90^{\circ}) = 1$ is the maximum value).*

### How to solve this
1.  **Identify the relationship:** Always start by recalling the relevant physical principle. In the HSC Physics course, the force on a wire in a magnetic field is defined by the formula $F = BIl \sin(\theta)$.
2.  **Analyze the variables:** Look at the variables on the right side of the equation ($B$, $I$, $l$, and $\sin(\theta)$). If any of these values increase (or if the angle $\theta$ moves toward $90^{\circ}$), the resultant force $F$ will increase proportionally.
3.  **Formulate your response:** Since the question asks for two ways, pick two distinct variables from the formula and state that increasing them will increase the force. Ensure you specify exactly what is being changed (e.g., instead of just "change current," say "increase the current").
