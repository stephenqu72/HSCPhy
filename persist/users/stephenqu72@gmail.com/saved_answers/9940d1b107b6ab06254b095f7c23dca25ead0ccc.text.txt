The correct answer is **D. Balmer series**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to recall the relationship between electron energy transitions in a hydrogen atom and the resulting electromagnetic radiation. The series are defined by the energy level ($n_f$) to which an electron drops:

1.  **Lyman series ($n_f = 1$):** Transitions to the ground state involve large energy changes, resulting in high-frequency, high-energy photons in the **ultraviolet (UV)** region.
2.  **Balmer series ($n_f = 2$):** Transitions to the second energy level involve moderate energy changes. These specific transitions correspond to wavelengths ranging from approximately $380\text{ nm}$ to $700\text{ nm}$, which falls exactly within the **visible light** spectrum.
3.  **Paschen series ($n_f = 3$):** Transitions to the third energy level involve smaller energy gaps, resulting in lower-frequency photons in the **infrared (IR)** region.
4.  **Rydberg series:** This is not a specific spectral series name like the others, but rather refers to the constant ($R_H$) used in the Rydberg formula to calculate the wavelengths of all these lines.

In the HSC Physics curriculum, it is essential to memorize that the **Balmer series** is the set of lines associated with the visible emission spectrum of hydrogen.
