### Explanation
To solve this problem, we need to understand the relationship between the refractive index of a medium (like glass) and the properties of light.

1.  **Dispersion in a Prism:** When white light enters a prism, it undergoes refraction. The degree to which light bends is determined by the refractive index ($n$) of the material. Different colors (wavelengths) of light experience different refractive indices in glass; this is known as **dispersion**.
2.  **Cauchy’s Equation/Refractive Index:** Glass is a dispersive medium where the refractive index is dependent on the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the light. Specifically, shorter wavelengths experience a higher refractive index than longer wavelengths.
3.  **Analyzing the options:**
    *   **Violet light** has a shorter wavelength and a higher frequency than red light. Because it has a shorter wavelength, it experiences a higher refractive index in the glass, causing it to slow down more and bend (refract) at a sharper angle. This confirms **(A)** is correct.
    *   **(B) is incorrect** because red light has a *lower* frequency and longer wavelength than violet light.
    *   **(C) is incorrect** because red light travels *faster* in the glass than violet light. A higher speed in the medium results in less refraction (less bending).
    *   **(D) is incorrect** because the energy of a photon is given by $E = hf$. Since red light has a lower frequency ($f$) than violet light, its photons carry *less* energy, not more.

Therefore, because violet light has a shorter wavelength, it undergoes greater refraction, resulting in more bending as it passes through the prism.
