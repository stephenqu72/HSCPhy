To find the acceleration of the proton as it enters the magnetic field, we use the principles of electromagnetism and Newton's Second Law.

### 1. Identify the given values
*   Charge of a proton ($q$): $1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
*   Velocity of the proton ($v$): $1200\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Magnetic field strength ($B$): $3.00\text{ T}$
*   Mass of a proton ($m$): $1.673 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$

### 2. Calculate the Magnetic Force
The magnitude of the magnetic force ($F_B$) on a moving charge is given by:
$$F_B = qvB\sin(\theta)$$
Since the proton enters perpendicular to the field, $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.
$$F_B = (1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (1200\text{ m s}^{-1}) \times (3.00\text{ T})$$
$$F_B = 5.7672 \times 10^{-16}\text{ N}$$

### 3. Calculate the Acceleration
Using Newton's Second Law, $F = ma$, we rearrange for acceleration ($a$):
$$a = \frac{F}{m}$$
$$a = \frac{5.7672 \times 10^{-16}\text{ N}}{1.673 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}}$$
$$a \approx 3.447 \times 10^{11}\text{ m s}^{-2}$$
Rounding to three significant figures, we get $3.45 \times 10^{11}\text{ m s}^{-2}$.

### 4. Determine Direction
Using the Right-Hand Rule:
*   Point your fingers in the direction of velocity (up the page).
*   Curl your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field (out of the page).
*   Your thumb points to the **left**.
Since the force acts to the left, the acceleration is directed to the **left**.

The correct option is **D**.
