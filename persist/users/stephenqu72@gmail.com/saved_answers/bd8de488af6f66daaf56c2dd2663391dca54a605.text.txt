To solve this problem, we use **Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion**, which states that the square of the orbital period ($T$) of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis (orbital radius, $r$) of its orbit:

$$\frac{T^2}{r^3} = \text{constant}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the relationship:**
    For two planets orbiting the same star, we can write the ratio:
    $$\frac{T_B^2}{r_B^3} = \frac{T_A^2}{r_A^3}$$

2.  **Rearrange to find the ratio of radii:**
    $$\frac{r_B^3}{r_A^3} = \frac{T_B^2}{T_A^2}$$
    $$\left(\frac{r_B}{r_A}\right)^3 = \left(\frac{T_B}{T_A}\right)^2$$

3.  **Substitute the given information:**
    We are told that Planet B's period is 3.5 times longer than Planet A's, so $T_B = 3.5 \times T_A$, or $\frac{T_B}{T_A} = 3.5$.
    $$\left(\frac{r_B}{r_A}\right)^3 = (3.5)^2$$
    $$\left(\frac{r_B}{r_A}\right)^3 = 12.25$$

4.  **Solve for the ratio of radii:**
    Take the cube root of both sides:
    $$\frac{r_B}{r_A} = \sqrt[3]{12.25}$$
    $$\frac{r_B}{r_A} \approx 2.306$$

Comparing this result to the given options, **(A) 2.3** is the correct answer.
