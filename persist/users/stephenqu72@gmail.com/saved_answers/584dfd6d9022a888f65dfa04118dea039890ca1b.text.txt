### Answer

The dynamo torch and the AC induction motor operate on inverse electromagnetic principles:

**1. The Dynamo Torch (Generator):**
*   **Physics Principle:** Operates via electromagnetic induction (Faraday's Law). As the handle is squeezed, it rotates a permanent magnet relative to a fixed coil. This change in magnetic flux ($\Delta\Phi$) through the coil induces an electromotive force (EMF), generating current.
*   **Conservation of Energy:** Mechanical energy (work done by the hand) is converted into electrical energy, which then powers the bulb, where it is converted into light and heat energy.

**2. The AC Induction Motor:**
*   **Physics Principle:** Operates via the interaction of magnetic fields and induced currents (Lenz’s Law/Lorentz force). An AC supply to the stator coils creates a rotating magnetic field. This field induces an eddy current in the rotor (squirrel cage). According to Lenz’s Law, the induced current creates a magnetic field that opposes the change in flux, resulting in a force (Lorentz force) that causes the rotor to spin.
*   **Conservation of Energy:** Electrical energy supplied to the stator coils is converted into kinetic (mechanical) energy of the rotating rotor, with some energy loss as heat due to internal resistance and eddy currents.

**Comparison/Contrast:**
*   **Energy Transformation:** The dynamo converts mechanical energy to electrical, while the motor converts electrical energy to mechanical.
*   **Components:** The dynamo uses a permanent magnet and a stationary coil (generator), while the motor uses stationary electromagnets (stator) to induce rotation in a rotor.
