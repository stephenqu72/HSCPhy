### Answer and Explanation

#### Part a)
To find the energy released, we calculate the **mass defect** ($\Delta m$) between the initial reactants (four protons) and the final product (one helium nucleus).

1.  **Constants required:**
    *   Mass of a proton ($m_p$) $\approx 1.007276 \text{ amu}$
    *   Mass of a helium nucleus ($m_{He}$) $= 4.00153 \text{ amu}$
    *   Conversion factor: $1 \text{ amu} = 931.5 \text{ MeV}/c^2$

2.  **Calculate the mass of four protons:**
    $$m_{initial} = 4 \times 1.007276 \text{ amu} = 4.029104 \text{ amu}$$

3.  **Calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$):**
    $$\Delta m = m_{initial} - m_{final}$$
    $$\Delta m = 4.029104 \text{ amu} - 4.00153 \text{ amu} = 0.027574 \text{ amu}$$

4.  **Calculate energy released ($E$):**
    $$E = \Delta m \times 931.5 \text{ MeV/amu}$$
    $$E = 0.027574 \times 931.5 \approx 25.685 \text{ MeV}$$

**Result:** Approximately **25.69 MeV** of energy is released.

#### Part b)
As a main sequence star exhausts its hydrogen fuel, it evolves into the next stage (Red Giant branch). The changes in forces and reactions are as follows:

1.  **Nuclear Reactions:** Hydrogen fusion in the core slows down and ceases as the supply of hydrogen is depleted. The core contracts, causing the temperature to rise, which eventually triggers helium fusion (the "triple-alpha process") in the core and hydrogen fusion in a shell surrounding the core.
2.  **Forces:** During the main sequence, the star is in **hydrostatic equilibrium**, where the inward pull of gravity is balanced by the outward radiation pressure from fusion. 
3.  **Loss of Equilibrium:** When hydrogen fusion ceases, the outward radiation pressure drops, allowing gravity to overcome pressure and cause the core to contract. This contraction increases gravitational potential energy, heating the core. The resulting increase in energy output from the new shell fusion causes the outer layers of the star to expand significantly and cool, leading to the Red Giant phase.
