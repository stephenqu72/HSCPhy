### Front
In a horizontal conical pendulum experiment, how does the magnitude of the centripetal force ($F_c$) on the rotating mass relate to the hanging mass ($M$)?

### Back
- Key idea: The tension in the string provides the centripetal force, which is balanced by the weight of the hanging mass.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_c = Mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: Steady-state circular motion where the system is in equilibrium (vertical forces balance).
- Common trap: Forgetting that $M$ is the hanging mass and $m$ is the rotating mass; they are not necessarily equal.
