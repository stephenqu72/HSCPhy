### Front
What is the quark composition of a neutron, and how is its charge calculated?

### Back
- Key idea: A neutron is electrically neutral (charge = 0), composed of three valence quarks.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $q_{neutron} = d + d + u = (-\frac{1}{3}e) + (-\frac{1}{3}e) + (+\frac{2}{3}e) = 0$
- When to use it: When identifying subatomic particle structures or verifying conservation of charge in beta decay.
- Common trap: Confusing the neutron ($udd$) with the proton ($uud$).
