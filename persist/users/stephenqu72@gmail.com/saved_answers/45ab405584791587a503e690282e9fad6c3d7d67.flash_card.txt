### Front
How does a nucleus’s position relative to the "Band of Stability" determine its decay mode?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Nuclei decay to reach the band of stability by adjusting their $N/Z$ ratio.
- **Figure:** 
  - **Above band (high $N/Z$):** $\beta^-$ decay ($Z \rightarrow Z+1$).
  - **Below band (low $N/Z$):** $\beta^+$ decay or electron capture ($Z \rightarrow Z-1$).
  - **Heavy nuclei ($Z > 82$):** $\alpha$ decay ($A-4, Z-2$).
- **When to use it:** To predict the decay product of a radioactive isotope given its proton/neutron excess.
- **Common trap:** Confusing $\beta^-$ (neutron excess) with $\beta^+$ (proton excess); remember $\beta^-$ increases atomic number ($Z$), while $\beta^+$ decreases it.
