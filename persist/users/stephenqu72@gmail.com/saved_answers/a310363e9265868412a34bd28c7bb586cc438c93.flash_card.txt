### Front
How do the equipotential lines for two identical point charges differ from those of a single point charge?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Superposition of potentials causes equipotential lines to "stretch" and merge into a single "peanut" shape around both charges.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $V_{total} = k(\frac{q}{r_1} + \frac{q}{r_2})$; potential is a scalar sum.
- **When to use it:** Identifying the shape of equipotential lines for multi-charge systems.
- **Common trap:** Confusing equipotential lines (which form closed loops around charges) with electric field lines (which represent direction of force and originate/terminate at charges). Equipotential lines must always be perpendicular to field lines.
