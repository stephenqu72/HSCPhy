### Answer

**(a)** Albert will observe the **exit lamp** switch on before the **entrance lamp**. 

According to the principle of the **relativity of simultaneity**, events that are simultaneous in one inertial frame are not necessarily simultaneous in another frame moving relative to the first. As the train moves toward the exit lamp, the light from the exit lamp has a shorter distance to travel to reach Albert, while the light from the entrance lamp has a longer distance to travel to reach him (because he is moving away from the location where the light was emitted). Since the speed of light $c$ is constant for all observers, Albert sees the flash from the exit lamp first.

**(b)** To find the speed of the train as a percentage of $c$:
1. We identify the proper time interval $\Delta t_0$. Mileva is in the frame where the tunnel is stationary. The time measured by an observer in a frame where the two events (entering and exiting the tunnel) occur at different locations is the dilated time $\Delta t$. Therefore, $\Delta t = 4.39\text{ s}$ and the proper time $\Delta t_0$ is the interval measured by Albert, $\Delta t_0 = 2.00\text{ s}$.
2. Use the time dilation formula: 
   $$\Delta t = \frac{\Delta t_0}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$
3. Rearrange to solve for $v$:
   $$\frac{\Delta t_0}{\Delta t} = \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
   $$\left(\frac{2.00}{4.39}\right)^2 = 1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$$
   $$0.2075 \approx 1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$$
   $$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 1 - 0.2075 = 0.7925$$
   $$\frac{v}{c} = \sqrt{0.7925} \approx 0.890$$
4. The speed of the train is approximately **89.0% of the speed of light**.
