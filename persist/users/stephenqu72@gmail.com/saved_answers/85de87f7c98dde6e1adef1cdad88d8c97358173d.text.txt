To solve this, we must look at the fundamental equation for the current $I$ in a DC motor armature:

$$I = \frac{V - \varepsilon}{R}$$

Where:
*   $V$ is the supply voltage.
*   $\varepsilon$ is the back electromotive force (back emf).
*   $R$ is the internal resistance of the armature.

**1. Analysis of the physics:**
When a DC motor spins, the armature coils move through a magnetic field, inducing an emf. According to **Lenz's Law**, this induced emf (the back emf) acts in opposition to the supply voltage.
