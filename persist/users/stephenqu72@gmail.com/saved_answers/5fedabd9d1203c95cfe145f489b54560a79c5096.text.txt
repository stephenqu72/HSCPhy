The correct answer is **(D) less valid.**

### Explanation
To understand why, we must look at the physics of the "whirling mass" experiment.

1.  **The Physics:** The centripetal force ($F_c$) required to keep the mass $m$ in horizontal circular motion is provided by the tension in the string. In this setup, this tension is balanced by the weight of the hanging mass $M$ (i.e., $F_c = Mg$).
2.  **The Assumption:** The diagram shows the mass $m$ moving in a perfectly horizontal circle. For this to happen, the tension must be purely horizontal. However, in reality, gravity acts on mass $m$ ($mg$ downwards). For the mass to remain at a constant vertical level, the string must pull slightly upwards to counteract gravity. This means the path is actually a "conical pendulum" rather than a perfectly horizontal circle.
3.  **Validity:** In physics, **validity** refers to whether the experiment actually measures what it claims to measure. The experiment claims to measure the relationship between centripetal force and speed for uniform circular motion. By ignoring the vertical component of the tension (the force of gravity on $m$), the student is making an assumption that the motion is perfectly horizontal when it is not. Because the apparatus does not perfectly isolate the variables as intended by the theoretical model (due to the influence of gravity on the moving mass), the results do not accurately reflect the intended physical relationship. Therefore, the assumption reduces the **validity** of the investigation.
