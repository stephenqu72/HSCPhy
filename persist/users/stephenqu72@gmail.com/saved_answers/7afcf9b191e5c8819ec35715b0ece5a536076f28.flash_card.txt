### Front
What are the three forces acting on a rider in a "rotor" amusement ride once the floor has dropped?

### Back
- Key idea: Vertical forces are balanced ($f = mg$), while the horizontal force provides centripetal acceleration ($N = mv^2/r$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\sum F_y = 0$ (no vertical motion); $\sum F_x = F_{net} = ma_c$.
- When to use it: Circular motion problems involving static friction supporting an object against gravity.
- Common trap: Forgetting the vertical frictional force; incorrectly identifying the centripetal force as an additional physical force (it is the *resultant* of the Normal force).
