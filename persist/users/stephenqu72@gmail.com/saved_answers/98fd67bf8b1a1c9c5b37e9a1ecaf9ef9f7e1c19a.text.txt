To answer this question effectively for an HSC-level physics response, you must identify a clear contrast between a historical method (where reaction time and terrestrial distances were the limiting factors) and a modern, high-precision method (where lasers and frequency standards are used).

### Suggested Answer

A historical method to measure the speed of light is **Fizeau’s rotating toothed-wheel experiment**. Fizeau directed a beam of light at a distant mirror, which reflected the light back through the teeth of a rotating cogwheel. By adjusting the speed of the wheel, he could determine the time taken for light to travel the distance to the mirror and back by observing when the light was blocked by the next tooth. This method was limited by human reaction time (if done manually) or mechanical limitations and the difficulty of measuring precise distances and rotational speeds, leading to significant uncertainties.

In contrast, a contemporary method utilizes **laser interferometry and frequency standards**. The current definition of the meter is based on the distance light travels in a vacuum in a specific fraction of a second ($1 / 299,792,458$ s). Scientists use highly stable lasers where the frequency ($f$) and wavelength ($\lambda$) are measured with extreme precision using optical frequency combs. Because $c = f\lambda$, the speed of light can be determined to 9 significant figures. This method eliminates the mechanical limitations and human error inherent in historical experiments by relying on the fundamental properties of atomic transitions.

### How to solve this type of question
1.  **Identify the Requirement:** The keyword is "contrast." This means you must explicitly state the differences between the two methods, not just describe them individually.
2.  **Select Examples:**
    *   **Historical:** Fizeau (toothed wheel) or Foucault (rotating mirror) are standard HSC syllabus examples. Avoid Galileo’s lantern experiment unless you mention its failure to account for the speed of light.
    *   **Contemporary:** Focus on the modern definition of the meter or laser interferometry.
3.  **Structure the Argument:**
    *   **Methodology:** Briefly describe *how* they worked.
    *   **Limitations:** Highlight why the historical method was inaccurate (e.g., mechanical speed limits, terrestrial distances, human observation).
    *   **Technological Shift:** Emphasize how modern technology (lasers, electronics, atomic clocks) removed these limitations to provide high precision.
4.  **Mark Allocation:** Since this is a 4-mark question, aim for at least two clear points of contrast (e.g., methodology and precision/accuracy) to ensure you secure full marks.
