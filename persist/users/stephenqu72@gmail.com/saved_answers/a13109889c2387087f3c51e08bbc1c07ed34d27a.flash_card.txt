### Front
In projectile motion launched and landing at the same vertical height (neglecting air resistance), how does the kinetic energy at launch compare to the kinetic energy at impact?

### Back
- Key idea: Total mechanical energy is conserved throughout the flight.
- Formula / law: Law of Conservation of Energy ($E_{total} = KE + PE = \text{constant}$).
- When to use it: To determine energy or speed at specific points in a projectile's trajectory.
- Common trap: Assuming kinetic energy is zero at the peak (it remains equal to initial horizontal KE) or ignoring the vertical component of velocity.
