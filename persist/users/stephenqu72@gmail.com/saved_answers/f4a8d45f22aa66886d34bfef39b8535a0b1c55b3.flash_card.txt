### Front
"Account for" the differences in penetrating power and ionising ability between $\alpha, \beta,$ and $\gamma$ radiation.

### Back
- Key idea: Ionising ability is inversely proportional to penetrating power; both are determined by the particle's mass and charge.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When asked to explain how physical properties (charge/mass) result in observed radiation behavior.
- Common trap: Listing properties without linking them to the physical nature (mass/charge) of the radiation—you must explicitly state the cause-and-effect relationship.
