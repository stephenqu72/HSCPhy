To determine the ratio of the resistance of $R_1$ to the resistance of $R_2$, we use the provided formula:
$$R = \frac{\rho L}{A}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the variables for each resistor:**
Since both resistors are made of the same material, the resistivity $\rho$ is the same for both. Let the length and cross-sectional area of $R_2$ be $L$ and $A$, respectively.
*   For Resistor $R_2$: $R_2 = \frac{\rho L}{A}$
*   For Resistor $R_1$: The length is twice that of $R_2$ ($L_1 = 2L$), and the area is half that of $R_2$ ($A_1 = \frac{1}{2}A$).

**2. Express the resistance of $R_1$ in terms of $L$ and $A$:**
$$R_1 = \frac{\rho (2L)}{\frac{1}{2}A}$$

**3. Simplify the expression for $R_1$:**
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal:
$$R_1 = \rho \cdot 2L \cdot \frac{2}{A}$$
$$R_1 = 4 \left( \frac{\rho L}{A} \right)$$
$$R_1 = 4 R_2$$

**4. Find the ratio:**
The question asks for the ratio of the resistance of $R_1$ to $R_2$:
$$\frac{R_1}{R_2} = \frac{4 R_2}{R_2} = 4$$
