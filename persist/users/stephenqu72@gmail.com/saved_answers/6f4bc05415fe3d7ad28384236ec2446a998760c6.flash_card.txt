### Front
What is the difference in tension ($T_A - T_B$) for an object of mass $m$ moving at a constant speed in a vertical circle of radius $r$?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The net centripetal force is provided by the vector sum of tension and weight; at the bottom tension opposes weight, at the top it assists weight.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $T_A - T_B = 2mg$
- **When to use it:** When asked for the variation in tension between the highest and lowest points of a vertical circular path at constant speed.
- **Common trap:** Calculating $T_A$ and $T_B$ separately and rounding early; always simplify the expression algebraically first to avoid compounding errors.
