### Front
How does the time taken for an object falling from rest scale with its vertical displacement?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Displacement ($h$) is proportional to the square of time ($t^2$).
- **Formula:** $h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$ (where $u=0$).
- **When to use it:** Problems involving vertical motion under constant gravity where initial velocity is zero.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to take the square root; assuming a linear relationship (e.g., thinking 9x distance = 9x time instead of 3x time).
