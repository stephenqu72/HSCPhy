### Front
How can you identify a nuclear fission reaction in an exam diagram?

### Back
- **Key idea:** A heavy nucleus (e.g., U-235) is hit by a neutron and splits into two smaller nuclei, releasing additional neutrons and energy.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Conservation of nucleon number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$).
- **When to use it:** When analyzing reaction equations or diagrams showing one large nucleus splitting into fragments.
- **Common trap:** Confusing fission (splitting) with fusion (joining) or radioactive decay (spontaneous emission from a single nucleus).
