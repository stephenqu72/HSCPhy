### Answer
The correct answer is **A. 0 J**.

---

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we need to understand the relationship between work, force, and electric potential in a uniform electric field.

1.  **The Electric Field:** Between two large parallel plates, the electric field $E$ is uniform and directed from the positive plate to the negative plate.
2.  **Work and Potential:** The work $W$ done on a charge $q$ moving in an electric field is given by the formula:
    $$W = -q \Delta V$$
    where $\Delta V$ is the change in electric potential.
3.  **Equipotential Lines:** In a system of parallel plates, the equipotential lines are planes parallel to the plates themselves. This means that moving along a line that is *parallel* to the plates does not result in a change in electric potential ($\Delta V = 0$).
4.  **Application:** The question states that the proton is moved **parallel to the positive plate**. Because it is moving along an equipotential surface, there is no change in potential energy, and therefore, no work is done by the electric field on the proton.

Mathematically, the force exerted by the electric field on the proton is directed perpendicular to the plates. Since the displacement is parallel to the plates, the angle $\theta$ between the force vector and the displacement vector is $90^{\circ}$. Using the work formula $W = Fd \cos(\theta)$:
$$W = Fd \cos(90^{\circ}) = Fd(0) = 0 \text{ J}$$

Therefore, the work done is $0 \text{ J}$.
