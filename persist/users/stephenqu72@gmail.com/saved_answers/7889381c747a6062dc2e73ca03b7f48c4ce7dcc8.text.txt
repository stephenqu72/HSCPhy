To solve this problem, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation:
$$F = \frac{G M m}{r^2}$$
where $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N m}^2 \text{kg}^{-2}$, $M$ is the mass of the Sun, $m$ is the mass of the planet, and $r$ is the orbital radius.

### 1. Data Preparation
*   $M = 2.00 \times 10^{30} \, \text{kg}$
*   $m_{\text{Earth}} = 5.97 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg}$ (Standard physical constant)
*   $r_{\text{Earth}} = 1.50 \times 10^{11} \, \text{m}$
*   $m_{\text{Mercury}} = 3.29 \times 10^{23} \, \text{kg}$
*   $r_{\text{Mercury}} = 5.79 \times 10^{7} \, \text{km} = 5.79 \times 10^{10} \, \text{m}$

### 2. Calculation
**For Earth:**
$$F_{\text{Earth}} = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (2.00 \times 10^{30}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24})}{(1.50 \times 10^{11})^2} \approx 3.54 \times 10^{22} \, \text{N}$$

**For Mercury:**
$$F_{\text{Mercury}} = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (2.00 \times 10^{30}) \times (3.29 \times 10^{23})}{(5.79 \times 10^{10})^2} \approx 1.31 \times 10^{22} \, \text{N}$$

### 3. Comparison
To compare them, find the ratio:
$$\frac{F_{\text{Earth}}}{F_{\text{Mercury}}} = \frac{3.54 \times 10^{22}}{1.31 \times 10^{22}} \approx 2.70$$

**Conclusion:** The Sun exerts approximately $2.7$ times more gravitational force on Earth than it does on Mercury.
