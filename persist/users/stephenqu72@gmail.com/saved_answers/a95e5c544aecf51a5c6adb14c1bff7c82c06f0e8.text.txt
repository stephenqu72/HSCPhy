To solve this question, we need to compare the physical properties of an alpha particle ($\alpha$) and a beta particle ($\beta$).

### Analysis of the Particles
1.  **Alpha particle ($\alpha$):** An alpha particle consists of two protons and two neutrons, which is identical to a helium nucleus ($^4_2He^{2+}$).
    *   **Mass:** It is considered "heavy" relative to subatomic particles, with a mass of approximately $4\text{ u}$.
    *   **Charge:** It has a charge of $+2$ due to the two protons.
2.  **Beta particle ($\beta$):** A beta particle is a high-energy, high-speed electron (or positron) ejected from the nucleus.
    *   **Mass:** It is very "light" (negligible mass compared to the alpha particle).
    *   **Charge:** A standard beta particle ($\beta^-$) has a charge of $-1$.

### Evaluating the characteristics
*   **I (heavy):** This describes an alpha particle.
*   **II (light):** This describes a beta particle.
*   **III (charge +2):** This describes an alpha particle.
*   **IV (charge -1):** This describes a beta particle.
*   **V (charge +1):** This describes a positron (a type of beta particle, $\beta^+$), but generally, when comparing to "alpha particles," we look for the properties of the standard alpha.

Comparing these to the given options:
*   An alpha particle is **heavy (I)** and has a **charge of +2 (III)**.

Therefore, the correct characteristics are I and III. Looking at the choices provided, option (D) matches this result.

**Correct Answer: (D)**
