### Front
Why is the proton not considered a "fundamental particle" according to the Standard Model?

### Back
- Key idea: Fundamental particles are indivisible and have no internal substructure; the proton is a composite hadron.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When identifying particles based on their composition (protons are baryons made of three quarks: $uud$).
- Common trap: Assuming that because a proton is stable or carries charge, it must be fundamental. Stability and charge are not criteria for being fundamental.
