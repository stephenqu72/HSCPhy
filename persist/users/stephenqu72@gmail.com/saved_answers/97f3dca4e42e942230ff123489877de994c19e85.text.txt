The correct answer is **B. The temperature of the object.**

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to apply **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which describes the relationship between the temperature of a black body and the wavelength at which it emits the most radiation (the peak wavelength, $\lambda_{max}$).

Wien’s Displacement Law is mathematically expressed as:
$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$

Where:
*   $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength of emission.
*   $T$ is the absolute temperature of the black body (in Kelvin).
*   $b$ is Wien's displacement constant ($\approx 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$).

**Key Takeaways for your HSC Physics exam:**
1.  **Independence of Material:** A black body, by definition, is an idealized physical body that absorbs all incident electromagnetic radiation. Because it is an idealized model, the peak wavelength depends **only** on its temperature, not on the material from which it is constructed.
2.  **Inverse Relationship:** As the temperature ($T$) of the object increases, the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) decreases. This is why objects shift from "red hot" to "white hot" or "blue hot" as they get hotter.
3.  **Shape:** The shape of an object does not determine the spectral distribution of a perfect black body; therefore, options containing "shape" or "material" are incorrect.
