### Answer
Lenz's Law is a manifestation of the **Law of Conservation of Energy**. 

If the induced current were to flow in the same direction as the change in magnetic flux, it would create a magnetic field that reinforces the original change. This would cause the induced current to increase, creating an even stronger magnetic field, which would in turn further increase the current. This would lead to a perpetual increase in energy without any external work being done, which violates the Law of Conservation of Energy. Therefore, the induced current must flow in a direction that opposes the change in flux to ensure that energy is not created from nothing.

### How to solve this
To "justify" a physics law in an HSC exam, you are being asked to provide the fundamental physical principle that underlies it. When approaching this 3-mark question, follow this structure:

1.  **Identify the underlying principle:** Clearly state that Lenz's Law is an application of the Law of Conservation of Energy.
2.  **Use a proof by contradiction:** Explain what would happen if the law were *not* true (e.g., if the induced current reinforced the flux change).
3.  **Explain the consequence:** Describe how this violation would lead to an infinite energy gain (a "runaway" effect), which is physically impossible. This link between the induced current and the conservation of energy is the "justification" required to earn full marks.
