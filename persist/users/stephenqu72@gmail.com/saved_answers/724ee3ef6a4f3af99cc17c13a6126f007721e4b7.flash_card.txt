### Front
How do you calculate the decay constant ($\lambda$) from a half-life ($t_{1/2}$), and what does $\beta^-$ decay entail for a nucleus?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The decay constant is the probability of decay per unit time; $\beta^-$ decay increases atomic number ($Z$) by 1 while mass number ($A$) remains constant.
- **Formula / law:** $\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{t_{1/2}}$; ${}_Z^A X \rightarrow {}_{Z+1}^A Y + {}_{-1}^0e + \bar{\nu}_e$
- **When to use it:** When determining the rate of radioactive decay or predicting daughter nuclei in transmutation.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert $t_{1/2}$ into seconds (SI units) before calculating $\lambda$ in $\text{s}^{-1}$.
