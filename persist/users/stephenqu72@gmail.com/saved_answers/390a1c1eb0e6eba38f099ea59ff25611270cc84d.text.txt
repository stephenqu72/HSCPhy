To solve this problem, we need to apply the principles of Conservation of Energy and Circular Motion.

### 1. Conservation of Energy
We find the speed of the bucket at the top of the circle ($v_{top}$) using the conservation of energy principle.
The total mechanical energy at the bottom equals the total energy at the top:
$$E_{bottom} = E_{top}$$
$$\frac{1}{2}mv_{bottom}^2 = \frac{1}{2}mv_{top}^2 + mg(2r)$$
Divide by $m$ and rearrange for $v_{top}^2$:
$$v_{top}^2 = v_{bottom}^2 - 4gr$$
Given: $v_{bottom} = 12.1\text{ m/s}$, $r = 1.50\text{ m}$, $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$:
$$v_{top}^2 = (12.1)^2 - 4(9.8)(1.50) = 146.41 - 58.8 = 87.61\text{ m}^2/\text{s}^2$$

### 2. Tension at the Bottom ($T_{bottom}$)
At the bottom, tension acts upwards and weight acts downwards, providing the centripetal force:
$$T_{bottom} - mg = \frac{mv_{bottom}^2}{r}$$
$$T_{bottom} = m(g + \frac{v_{bottom}^2}{r}) = 1.20(9.8 + \frac{146.41}{1.50}) = 1.20(9.8 + 97.607) \approx 128.89\text{ N}$$

### 3. Tension at the Top ($T_{top}$)
At the top, both tension and weight act downwards:
$$T_{top} + mg = \frac{mv_{top}^2}{r}$$
$$T_{top} = m(\frac{v_{top}^2}{r} - g) = 1.20(\frac{87.61}{1.50} - 9.8) = 1.20(58.407 - 9.8) \approx 58.33\text{ N}$$

### 4. Difference in Tension
The difference in tension is:
$$\Delta T = T_{bottom} - T_{top} = 128.89 - 58.33 = 70.56\text{ N}$$
Rounding to the nearest whole number, we get $71\text{ N}$.

**Answer: (B) 71 N**
