### Front
Derive the formula for the "design speed" of a vehicle on a banked curve without friction.

### Back
- **Key idea:** The horizontal component of the normal force ($N\sin\theta$) provides the necessary centripetal force ($mv^2/r$) for circular motion.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v = \sqrt{rg\tan\theta}$
- **When to use it:** When a vehicle traverses a frictionless banked curve at the specific speed where no lateral friction is required.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that $N\cos\theta = mg$; students often incorrectly use $\sin\theta$ for the vertical component or ignore the angle's effect on the normal force.
