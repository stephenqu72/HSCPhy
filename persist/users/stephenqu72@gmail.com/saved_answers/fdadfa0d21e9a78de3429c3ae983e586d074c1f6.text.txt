### Answer

**Definitions:**
*   A **gravitational field** is a region of space surrounding a body with mass in which another mass experiences a force of attraction.
*   An **electric field** is a region of space surrounding an electrically charged particle in which another charge experiences an electric force.

**Similarities:**
*   Both fields are non-contact forces that act over a distance and follow an inverse-square law ($F \propto 1/r^2$).
*   Both can be represented by field lines where the density of the lines indicates the strength of the field.

**Differences:**
*   Gravitational fields only exert an **attractive** force on mass. In contrast, electric fields can exert both **attractive and repulsive** forces depending on the polarity of the charges (like charges repel, opposite charges attract).
*   Gravitational forces depend on the property of **mass**, whereas electric forces depend on the property of **electric charge**. Additionally, electric forces are significantly stronger than gravitational forces at the atomic level.
