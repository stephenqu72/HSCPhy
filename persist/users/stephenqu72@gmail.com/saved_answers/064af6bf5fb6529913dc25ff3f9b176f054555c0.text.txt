### Answer
An experimental observation that supports Huygens’ principle is the **spreading of a wave after it passes through a narrow aperture or around an obstacle**. Specifically, when water waves or light pass through a small opening (comparable to the wavelength), they do not continue in a straight line but instead bend and spread out, forming circular wavefronts on the other side. This occurs because every point on the wavefront at the aperture acts as a secondary source of spherical wavelets, as predicted by Huygens.

### How to solve this
To answer a question like this in an HSC Physics exam, you need to link the theoretical principle to a concrete physical observation:

1.  **Define the relevant part of the theory:** Recall that Huygens' principle states that every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary spherical wavelets. The new wavefront is the tangent surface to these wavelets.
2.  **Select a classic observation:** The most direct evidence for this in the context of diffraction is the transition of a plane wavefront into a curved (or spreading) wavefront when it encounters an obstacle or aperture. 
3.  **Draft the response:**
    *   **The Observation:** State clearly that waves spread or bend around corners/obstacles (diffraction).
    *   **The Connection:** Explain that this spreading happens because the points within the opening act as new point sources of waves (Huygens' secondary wavelets), which interfere to create the new, wider wavefront.
    *   **Check the marks:** Since this is a 2-mark question, one mark is typically awarded for identifying the observation (diffraction/spreading) and the second for correctly linking it to the mechanism of secondary wavelets (Huygens' principle).
