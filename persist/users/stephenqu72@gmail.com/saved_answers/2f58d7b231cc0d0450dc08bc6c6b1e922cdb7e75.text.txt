### 1. Explaining the Experimental Evidence

**The Geiger-Marsden (Rutherford) Experiment:**
*   **Observation:** Most alpha particles passed straight through the gold foil, while a small fraction were deflected at large angles or bounced directly backward.
*   **Feature:** This provides evidence for a tiny, dense, positively charged **nucleus** surrounded by mostly empty space.
*   **Forces:** The large-angle deflections are due to the **electrostatic (Coulombic) repulsion** between the positive nucleus and the positive alpha particles.

**The Hydrogen Spectrum (Bohr Model):**
*   **Observation:** The emission spectrum is discrete (only specific wavelengths/colors), not continuous.
*   **Feature:** Electrons exist in stable, quantized **energy levels** ($n=1, 2, 3, \dots$).
*   **Photons:** When an electron transitions from a higher energy level ($n_i$) to a lower one ($n_f$), it releases the energy difference as a **photon** of light. The energy of this photon is given by $E = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$. Because the energy levels are quantized, the emitted light can only exist at specific wavelengths.

### 2. Sample Calculation
Let's calculate the wavelength of the longest-wavelength line in the Balmer series ($n=3 \rightarrow n=2$). 
Using the Rydberg formula:
$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$
Where $R_H \approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \text{ m}^{-1}$. For $n_f=2$ and $n_i=3$:
$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{1}{2^2} - \frac{1}{3^2} \right) = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{9} \right)$$
$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \times 0.1389 \approx 1.5236 \times 10^6 \text{ m}^{-1}$$
$$\lambda \approx 6.56 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m} = 656 \text{ nm}$$
