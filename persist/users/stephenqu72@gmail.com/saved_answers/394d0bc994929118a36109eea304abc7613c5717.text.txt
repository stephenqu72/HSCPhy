### Question 4
**Answer: (A)**

**Explanation:**
To find the speed of the car at point Y, we use the Law of Conservation of Energy. Since we are told to ignore friction and air resistance, mechanical energy is conserved.

1.  At point X, the car is stationary, so its total mechanical energy is purely gravitational potential energy: $E_X = mgh$.
2.  At point Y, the car has reached the bottom of the hill (relative to the starting point X), so its potential energy is converted into kinetic energy: $E_Y = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.
3.  Setting these equal: 
    $$mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
4.  Cancel the mass ($m$) from both sides and solve for $v$:
    $$gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2$$
    $$v^2 = 2gh$$
    $$v = \sqrt{2gh}$$

### Question 5
**Answer: (B)**

**Explanation:**
This question tests your understanding of projectile motion kinematics.

1.  **Horizontal Velocity:** In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), there are no forces acting in the horizontal direction. According to Newton's First Law, an object in motion stays in motion with a constant velocity. Therefore, the horizontal component of the velocity remains constant throughout the entire flight from Y to Z.
2.  **Comparison:** Since the velocity at Y is entirely horizontal, the horizontal component of velocity at Z is exactly equal to the velocity at Y.
3.  **Why the others are wrong:** Option (A) and (C) are incorrect because the horizontal velocity does not change. Option (D) is incorrect because the horizontal velocity is determined by the drop from the hill ($h$), not the height of the cliff ($H$).
