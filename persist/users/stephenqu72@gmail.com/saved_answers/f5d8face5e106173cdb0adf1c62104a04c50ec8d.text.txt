To determine the correct adjustment, we use **Malus's Law**, which states that the intensity of transmitted light $I$ through a polariser is given by:
$$I = I_0 \cos^2(\theta)$$
where $I_0$ is the initial intensity and $\theta$ is the angle between the light's polarization axis and the polariser's transmission axis. The greatest reduction in intensity occurs when $\theta$ is closest to $90^\circ$ (which results in $I=0$).
