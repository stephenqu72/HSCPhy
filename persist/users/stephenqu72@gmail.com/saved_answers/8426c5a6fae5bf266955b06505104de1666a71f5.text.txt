### Part (b)
**Answer:** $0.75\text{ kg}$

**Explanation:**
For the rod to be in equilibrium (stationary), the sum of all upward forces must equal the sum of all downward forces.

1. **Calculate the magnetic force ($F_B$):**
   The formula for the force on a current-carrying wire is:
   $$F_B = BIL \sin(\theta)$$
   Since the wire is perpendicular to the magnetic field, $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.
   Given: $B = 2.3\text{ T}$, $I = 1.3\text{ A}$, $L = 0.50\text{ m}$.
   $$F_B = 2.3 \times 1.3 \times 0.50 = 1.495\text{ N}$$

2. **Set up the equilibrium equation:**
   The upward forces are the two tension forces ($T_1 + T_2$) and the magnetic force ($F_B$). The downward force is gravity ($mg$).
   $$F_{up} = F_{down}$$
   $$T_{total} + F_B = mg$$
   Given $T = 4.5\text{ N}$ per wire, the total tension is $4.5 + 4.5 = 9.0\text{ N}$.
   $$9.0 + 1.495 = m \times 9.8$$
   $$10.495 = m \times 9.8$$
   $$m = \frac{10.495}{9.8} \approx 1.07\text{ kg}$$
