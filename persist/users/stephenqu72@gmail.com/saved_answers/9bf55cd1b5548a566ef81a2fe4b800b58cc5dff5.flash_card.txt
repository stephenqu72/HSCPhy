### Front
Why does a projectile follow a parabolic path?

### Back
- Key idea: Independence of horizontal and vertical motion; constant horizontal velocity and constant vertical acceleration ($g$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $y = v_{0y}t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$ and $x = v_xt$
- When to use it: To explain the trajectory shape in the absence of air resistance.
- Common trap: Assuming horizontal velocity changes during flight; horizontal velocity remains constant (ignoring air resistance).
