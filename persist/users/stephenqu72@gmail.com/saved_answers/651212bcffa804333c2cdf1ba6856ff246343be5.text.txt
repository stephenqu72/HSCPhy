### Answer and Explanation

#### Question 13
**Answer: (C)**

**Explanation:**
In projectile motion, where air resistance is ignored, we treat horizontal and vertical motion as independent:
1.  **Horizontal motion:** There is no acceleration in the horizontal direction ($a_x = 0$). According to Newton’s First Law, if there is no net force acting horizontally, the horizontal velocity remains constant throughout the flight. Therefore, $v_x = \text{constant}$.
2.  **Vertical motion:** The force of gravity acts on the object, causing a constant downward acceleration ($a_y = g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$). Because there is acceleration, the vertical component of the velocity changes continuously.
3.  **Displacement:** Both horizontal and vertical displacement change over time as the ball moves, so (A) and (B) are incorrect.

#### Question 14
**Answer: (D)**

**Explanation:**
To make the ball land closer to the cliff, you need to reduce the horizontal range ($R$). The horizontal range is defined by $R = v_x \cdot t_{total}$.
*   **(A) Lower velocity:** Reducing the initial velocity reduces both components, decreasing $R$. This *will* make it land closer.
*   **(B) & (C) Changing the angle:** Starting at $45^\circ$ is the optimal angle for maximum range on level ground. Adjusting the angle away from $45^\circ$ (either higher or lower) will typically decrease the range in this context, making it land closer.
*   **(D) Greater horizontal velocity:** If you increase the horizontal component of the velocity ($v_x$), the ball will travel further horizontally in the same amount of time. Therefore, increasing the horizontal velocity will make the ball land *further away* from the cliff, not closer.
