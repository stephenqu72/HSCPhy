To solve this problem, we use the definition of magnetic flux ($\Phi$).

### 1. Identify the given information:
*   Magnetic field strength ($B$) = $2\text{ T}$
*   Radius of the loop ($r$) = $8\text{ cm}$ = $0.08\text{ m}$

### 2. The Formula for Magnetic Flux:
Magnetic flux is defined as the product of the magnetic field strength ($B$) and the area ($A$) perpendicular to the field lines that it passes through:
$$\Phi = B \cdot A \cdot \cos(\theta)$$
Since the loop is placed perpendicular to the field (the dots represent field lines coming out of the page), the angle $\theta$ between the area vector and the magnetic field is $0^\circ$. Because $\cos(0^\circ) = 1$, the formula simplifies to:
$$\Phi = B \cdot A$$

### 3. Calculation:
First, calculate the area ($A$) of the circular loop:
$$A = \pi \cdot r^2$$
$$A = \pi \cdot (0.08\text{ m})^2$$
$$A = \pi \cdot 0.0064\text{ m}^2 \approx 0.0201\text{ m}^2$$

Now, calculate the magnetic flux:
$$\Phi = B \cdot A$$
$$\Phi = 2\text{ T} \cdot (0.0064\pi\text{ m}^2)$$
$$\Phi \approx 2 \cdot 0.0201 \approx 0.0402\text{ Wb}$$

Rounding to the nearest provided option, we get $0.04\text{ Wb}$.

**Correct Answer:** **(A)**

### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Check units:** Always convert units to standard SI units (meters, Tesla, etc.) before calculating. Here, cm was converted to meters.
2.  **Determine the orientation:** Identify if the magnetic field is perpendicular to the area. If it is, use $\Phi = BA$. If it is at an angle, remember to include the $\cos(\theta)$ component.
3.  **Calculate the area:** Remember the standard area formulas for shapes ($A = \pi r^2$ for a circle, $A = l \cdot w$ for a rectangle).
