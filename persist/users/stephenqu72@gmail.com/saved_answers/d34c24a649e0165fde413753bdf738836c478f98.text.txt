To determine the correct answer, we must look at Faraday's Law of Induction, which governs the production of induced electromotive force (EMF), or voltage, in a generator.

### Explanation

The induced EMF ($\varepsilon$) in a rotating coil within a magnetic field is given by the formula:
$$\varepsilon = NBA\omega \sin(\omega t)$$

Where:
*   $N$ = Number of turns in the coil
*   $B$ = Magnetic field strength
*   $A$ = Area of the coil
*   $\omega$ = Angular velocity (how fast the coil is turning)

Looking at the options:
*   **(A) Increasing the area ($A$):** This directly increases $\varepsilon$.
*   **(B) Decreasing the resistance:** Resistance affects the *current* ($I = V/R$) that flows through a circuit, but it does not change the *induced EMF* (the voltage generated). The voltage generated depends solely on the physical parameters of the generator (magnetic field, area, turns, and speed).
*   **(C) Turning the coil faster ($\omega$):** This increases the rate of change of magnetic flux, thereby increasing $\varepsilon$.
*   **(D) Increasing the number of turns ($N$):** This directly increases $\varepsilon$.

Since decreasing the resistance of the coil only affects the current output rather than the voltage output generated by electromagnetic induction, option (B) is the correct answer.

**Answer:** (B)
