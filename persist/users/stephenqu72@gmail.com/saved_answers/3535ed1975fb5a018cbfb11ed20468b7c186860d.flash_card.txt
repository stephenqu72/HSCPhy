### Front
What is the physical origin of Kepler’s 2nd Law, and why is gravitational potential energy ($U$) negative?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kepler’s 2nd Law arises from the **conservation of angular momentum** due to gravity being a central force. The negative sign for $U$ indicates a **bound system** relative to zero potential at infinity.
- **Formula:** $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$
- **When to use it:** Use the conservation of angular momentum to explain orbital speed variations; use $U$ to calculate the energy required to change an object's orbital position or escape the field.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting the negative sign in $U$ or failing to convert kilometers to meters ($10^3$) when calculating orbital values.
