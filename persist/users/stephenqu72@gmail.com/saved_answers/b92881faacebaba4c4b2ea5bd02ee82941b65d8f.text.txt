### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must apply the fundamental physics principles of uniform circular motion:

1.  **Direction of Instantaneous Velocity**: The instantaneous velocity vector of an object in circular motion is always directed **tangent** to the path of the circle at the object's current position.
2.  **Direction of Centripetal Force**: The word "centripetal" literally means "center-seeking." Therefore, the centripetal force must always be directed **radially inward** toward the center of the orbit (in this case, the center of the Earth).
3.  **Orthogonality**: In uniform circular motion, the velocity vector and the centripetal force vector are always perpendicular ($90^\circ$ to each other).
