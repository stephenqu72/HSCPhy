To solve this problem, we must apply the physics of electromagnetism, specifically the interaction between a moving charged particle and a magnetic field produced by a current-carrying conductor.

1.  **Determine the direction of the magnetic field produced by $QR$:**
    The wire $QR$ carries a current. Using the **Right-Hand Grip Rule** (thumb pointing in the direction of conventional current from positive terminal through $Q$ to $R$), the magnetic field lines circulate around the wire. Above the wire, the magnetic field lines point towards the back of the chamber (the direction from $Y$ toward $X$).

2.  **Determine the force on the cathode ray:**
    A cathode ray consists of a beam of electrons (negatively charged). The force $F$ on a charge $q$ moving with velocity $v$ in a magnetic field $B$ is given by the Lorentz force law:
    $$F = q(v \times B)$$
    Since electrons are negatively charged, we use the **Left-Hand Rule** (or the Right-Hand Rule and reverse the result).
    *   **Thumb:** Points in the direction of the velocity of the electrons (from $Q$ to $R$).
    *   **Fingers:** Point in the direction of the magnetic field (into the page/back of the chamber).
    *   **Palm (for positive charge):** Points downward.
    *   **Back of hand (for negative charge/electrons):** Points upward toward $W$.

3.  **Effect of increasing resistance:**
    According to Ohm's Law ($V = IR$), if the resistance increases while the voltage remains constant, the current $I$ flowing through the conductor $QR$ decreases. 
    The magnitude of the magnetic field $B$ produced by the wire is directly proportional to the current ($B \propto I$). Therefore, decreasing the current reduces the strength of the magnetic field, which in turn reduces the magnetic force exerted on the cathode ray.

4.  **Conclusion:**
    Initially, the cathode ray is deflected upward towards $W$. When resistance is increased, the current drops, the magnetic field weakens, and the deflection decreases. This means the bright spot moves **downwards** from its initial position towards the undeflected path (closer to the wire). Therefore, the spot moves towards $Z$ (the direction of the wire).
