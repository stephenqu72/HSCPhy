### Answer

To increase the generated electromotive force (emf), the student could implement the following changes:

1.  **Increase the speed of the magnet's motion:** Faraday's Law of Induction states that the magnitude of the induced emf ($\varepsilon$) is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux ($\Phi$). By moving the magnet faster, the student increases the rate of change of magnetic flux ($\frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$), which directly increases the induced emf.
2.  **Increase the number of turns in the coil:** According to Faraday's Law, the induced emf is given by $\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$, where $N$ is the number of loops. Adding more turns of wire ensures that each turn experiences the changing magnetic flux, and since the emfs in each turn are connected in series, the total potential difference measured will increase proportionally to the number of turns. 

*(Alternatively, the student could use a stronger magnet to increase the magnetic flux density, thereby increasing the rate of change of flux.)*

### How to solve this
To solve this question, you must apply **Faraday's Law of Induction**, which is represented by the formula:

$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$$

*   **Understand the variables:** 
    *   $\varepsilon$ is the induced electromotive force (potential difference).
    *   $N$ is the number of turns in the coil.
    *   $\Delta\Phi$ is the change in magnetic flux.
    *   $\Delta t$ is the time taken for that change to occur.
*   **Identify the goal:** You need to maximize $\varepsilon$. Mathematically, this means you must either increase the numerator ($N$ or $\Delta\Phi$) or decrease the denominator ($\Delta t$).
*   **Translate to physical actions:**
    *   Increasing $N$: Use a coil with more loops.
    *   Increasing $\Delta\Phi$: Use a stronger magnet (increasing the magnetic field strength $B$).
    *   Decreasing $\Delta t$: Increase the relative velocity between the magnet and the loop (move the magnet faster).

When answering an HSC Physics "Discuss" question, ensure you clearly link your suggested change (e.g., "moving the magnet faster") to the underlying physical principle (e.g., "increasing the rate of change of magnetic flux") to earn the full marks.
