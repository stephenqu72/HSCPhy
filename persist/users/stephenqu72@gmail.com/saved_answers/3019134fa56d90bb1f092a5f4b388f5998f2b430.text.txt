To determine which galaxy is closest to us, we must apply the principles of the Doppler effect as it relates to cosmology (redshift).

### Answer
The correct answer is **B. V**.

### Explanation
1.  **The Concept of Redshift:** According to Hubble's Law, the universe is expanding, meaning that distant galaxies are moving away from us. When an object moves away, the light it emits is shifted toward the red end of the spectrum (longer wavelengths). The further away a galaxy is, the faster it is receding, and the greater its redshift will be.
2.  **Analyzing the Spectra:**
    *   **The Sun:** This is our local reference frame. The positions of the spectral lines here represent the "rest" wavelengths.
    *   **Redshift Detection:** A galaxy is "closest" if it has the **smallest redshift**, meaning its spectral lines are shifted the least amount away from their original positions compared to the Sun's spectrum.
3.  **Comparing the Galaxies:**
    *   **Galaxy U:** The lines are significantly shifted toward the red (right side).
    *   **Galaxy V:** The lines are shifted only slightly to the right compared to the Sun. This indicates a very small redshift.
    *   **Galaxy W:** The lines are shifted very far to the right, indicating a high redshift and a galaxy moving away from us very rapidly.
    *   **Galaxy X:** The lines are shifted toward the right, more than in Galaxy V.
4.  **Conclusion:** Because Galaxy V has spectral lines closest to the positions seen in the Sun's spectrum, it exhibits the smallest redshift. According to Hubble's Law, the smallest redshift implies the lowest recessional velocity, which corresponds to the galaxy being the closest to us of the options provided.
