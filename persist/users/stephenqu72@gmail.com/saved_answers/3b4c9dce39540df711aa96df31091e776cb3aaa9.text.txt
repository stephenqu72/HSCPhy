To determine the car's motion, we must compare the velocity at which the car is traveling ($v = 30\text{ m s}^{-1}$) to the **ideal velocity** (or "design speed") required for a car to negotiate a banked corner without the need for friction.

### 1. The Physics Principle
On a frictionless banked corner, the horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force required to turn the car:
$$F_c = N \sin(\theta) = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
The vertical component of the normal force balances the weight of the car:
$$N \cos(\theta) = mg$$

Dividing these two equations gives the formula for the ideal velocity:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg} \implies v = \sqrt{rg \tan(\theta)}$$

### 2. Calculation
Given:
- $r = 35\text{ m}$
- $g \approx 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$
- $\theta = 20^\circ$

Calculate the ideal velocity:
$$v_{\text{ideal}} = \sqrt{35 \times 9.8 \times \tan(20^\circ)}$$
$$v_{\text{ideal}} = \sqrt{343 \times 0.364}$$
$$v_{\text{ideal}} = \sqrt{124.85} \approx 11.17\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

### 3. Conclusion
The car is traveling at $30\text{ m s}^{-1}$, which is significantly faster than the design speed of approximately $11.17\text{ m s}^{-1}$. 

When a car travels faster than the design speed, the required centripetal force is larger than what the horizontal component of the normal force can provide at that specific angle. Consequently, the car will experience a net outward force relative to the turn, causing it to slide **up** the slope.

**Correct Answer: A**

### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Identify the system:** Recognize this as a circular motion problem involving a banked track.
2.  **Use the design speed formula:** Use $v = \sqrt{rg \tan(\theta)}$ to find the specific speed for which no friction is required.
3.  **Compare:** 
    *   If $v_{\text{actual}} = v_{\text{ideal}}$, the car stays at the same height.
    *   If $v_{\text{actual}} > v_{\text{ideal}}$, the car slides **up** the bank.
    *   If $v_{\text{actual}} < v_{\text{ideal}}$, the car slides **down** the bank.
