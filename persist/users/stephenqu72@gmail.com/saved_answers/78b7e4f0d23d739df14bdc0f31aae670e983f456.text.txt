### Answer
The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to understand the fundamental definition of an inertial frame of reference in classical and relativistic mechanics:

1.  **Definition:** An inertial frame of reference is a coordinate system in which Newton's First Law of Motion (the law of inertia) holds true. This law states that an object will remain at rest or continue to move with a constant velocity unless acted upon by a net external force.
2.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   **A is incorrect:** A frame that is accelerating is a *non-inertial* frame. In such frames, fictitious forces (or pseudo-forces) appear to act on objects.
    *   **B is incorrect:** Newton’s laws *do* hold true in an inertial frame. They fail or require modification (by adding fictitious forces) in non-inertial frames.
    *   **C is incorrect:** Inertial (fictitious) forces only appear in *non-inertial* (accelerating) frames.
    *   **D is correct:** By definition, an inertial frame is one that is not undergoing acceleration. This means it must either be stationary (at rest) or moving with a uniform (constant) velocity relative to other inertial frames.

When studying for the HSC Physics module "Advanced Mechanics" or "Special Relativity," remember: **Inertial = No acceleration**. If the observer is moving at a constant speed in a straight line, they are in an inertial frame.
