To solve this problem, we need to apply the radioactive decay law.

### 1. Identify the given values
*   Initial activity ($A_0$): $5640 \text{ counts/min}$
*   Final activity ($A_t$): $112 \text{ counts/min}$
*   Time elapsed ($t$): $12 \text{ hours} = 12 \times 60 \text{ minutes} = 720 \text{ minutes}$

### 2. Calculate the decay constant ($\lambda$)
The radioactive decay formula is:
$$A_t = A_0 e^{-\lambda t}$$

Rearranging to solve for $\lambda$:
$$\frac{A_t}{A_0} = e^{-\lambda t}$$
$$\ln\left(\frac{A_t}{A_0}\right) = -\lambda t$$
$$\lambda = \frac{-\ln\left(\frac{A_t}{A_0}\right)}{t}$$

Substitute the values:
$$\lambda = \frac{-\ln\left(\frac{112}{5640}\right)}{720}$$
$$\lambda \approx \frac{-\ln(0.019858)}{720} \approx \frac{3.919}{720} \approx 0.00544 \text{ min}^{-1}$$
