### Front
What is the origin of the centripetal force for a car traveling on a banked track?

### Back
- Key idea: The centripetal force is not an "extra" force; it is the **net resultant** of the horizontal components of the normal force (and frictional force, if present).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{net} = ma_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: To determine the required velocity, radius, or banking angle in circular motion problems.
- Common trap: Thinking centripetal force is an "additional" force acting on the car, rather than the vector sum of existing forces.
