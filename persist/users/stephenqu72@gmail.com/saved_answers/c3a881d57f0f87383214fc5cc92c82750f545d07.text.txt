### Solution and Explanation

To solve this problem, we use the fundamental relationships between potential difference, electric fields, and force on charged particles.

**Given data:**
*   Potential difference ($V$) = $250\text{ V}$
*   Distance ($d$) = $1.50\text{ mm} = 1.50 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$
*   Charge of an alpha particle ($q$) = $+2e = 2 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C} = 3.204 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
*   Mass of an alpha particle ($m$) $\approx 6.645 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$

---

#### a) Calculate the electric field ($E$)
The electric field between parallel plates is uniform and is defined by the formula:
$$E = \frac{V}{d}$$
Substituting the values:
$$E = \frac{250}{1.50 \times 10^{-3}} \approx 1.67 \times 10^5\text{ V m}^{-1} \text{ (or N C}^{-1})$$

#### b) Calculate the force ($F$) on the alpha particle
The electric force exerted on a charge $q$ in an electric field $E$ is given by:
$$F = qE$$
Using the charge of the alpha particle and the field calculated in part (a):
$$F = (3.204 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (1.667 \times 10^5\text{ V m}^{-1})$$
$$F \approx 5.34 \times 10^{-14}\text{ N}$$
The direction of the force is away from the positive plate toward the negative plate.

#### c) Describe quantitatively the effect of the field
"Quantitatively" means we must use physics principles to calculate the resulting motion. The field causes the alpha particle to accelerate ($a$) towards the negative plate according to Newton's Second Law ($F = ma$):
$$a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{5.34 \times 10^{-14}\text{ N}}{6.645 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}}$$
$$a \approx 8.04 \times 10^{12}\text{ m s}^{-2}$$
**Description:** The alpha particle will experience a constant, unidirectional acceleration of approximately $8.04 \times 10^{12}\text{ m s}^{-2}$ directed towards the negative plate, causing its velocity to increase linearly over time in the direction of the field.
