To solve this problem, we must first determine the initial velocity of the ball using energy conservation, then analyze its projectile motion, and finally calculate the required speed and displacement for the target cup.

### 1. Determine the Initial Velocity of the Ball
The work done by the spring ($W = 19.4 \text{ J}$) is converted into the kinetic energy ($KE$) of the ball.
$$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
Given $m = 0.046 \text{ kg}$ and $W = 19.4 \text{ J}$:
$$19.4 = \frac{1}{2}(0.046)v^2$$
$$v^2 = \frac{19.4 \times 2}{0.046} \approx 843.48$$
$$v \approx 29.04 \text{ m/s}$$

### 2. Analyze the Projectile Motion
We resolve the velocity into horizontal ($v_x$) and vertical ($v_y$) components:
$$v_x = v \cos(35^\circ) = 29.04 \cos(35^\circ) \approx 23.80 \text{ m/s}$$
$$v_y = v \sin(35^\circ) = 29.04 \sin(35^\circ) \approx 16.66 \text{ m/s}$$

Using the vertical displacement formula $y = v_y t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$, where $y = -2.6 \text{ m}$ (since it lands below the launch height):
$$-2.6 = 16.66t - 4.9t^2$$
$$4.9t^2 - 16.66t - 2.6 = 0$$
Using the quadratic formula $t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$$t = \frac{16.66 \pm \sqrt{(-16.66)^2 - 4(4.9)(-2.6)}}{9.8} \approx \frac{16.66 + 18.09}{9.8} \approx 3.55 \text{ s}$$

### 3. Determine Distance and Speed
The ball travels a horizontal distance ($R$) during time $t$:
$$R = v_x t = 23.80 \times 3.55 \approx 84.49 \text{ m}$$

The cup travels this same distance $R$ in the same time $t$ to be at the landing spot:
$$v_{cup} = \frac{R}{t} = \frac{84.49}{3.55} = 23.80 \text{ m/s}$$

**Final Answers:**
- The distance the target cup must travel is approximately **84.5 m**.
- The required speed of the target cup is approximately **23.8 m/s**.

*(Note: In this specific setup, because the cup starts at the base and travels to the landing point $R$ in time $t$, its speed must equal the horizontal component of the ball's velocity.)*
