### Front
In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), why does the velocity of the object constantly change?

### Back
- Key idea: Velocity is a vector quantity; its direction changes along the curved path, and gravity accelerates the vertical component.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s Second Law ($F = ma$); $a_y = g$ (constant).
- When to use it: To analyze motion in 2D kinematics problems.
- Common trap: Thinking that "velocity is changing" only refers to a change in speed; remember that direction is part of velocity.
