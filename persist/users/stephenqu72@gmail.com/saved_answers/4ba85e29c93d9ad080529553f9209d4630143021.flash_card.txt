### Front
What physical properties of a star can be determined through the analysis of its absorption spectrum?

### Back
- Key idea: Using spectral lines and continuum intensity to identify chemical composition, surface temperature, radial velocity, and rotation.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Wien’s Law ($\lambda_{max} T = \text{constant}$) and the Doppler Effect ($\frac{\Delta\lambda}{\lambda} = \frac{v}{c}$).
- When to use it: To explain how astronomers derive stellar characteristics from light without physical sampling.
- Common trap: Confusing **radial velocity** (a shift in the line's position/wavelength) with **stellar rotation** (the broadening of the line width).
