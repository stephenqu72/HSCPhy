### How to solve it
To earn full marks, ensure your sketch demonstrates these three physical principles:

*   **Peak Shift (Wien’s Law):** The peak wavelength $\lambda_{max}$ is inversely proportional to temperature ($T$). Since $6000K = 2 \times 3000K$, the peak wavelength for the 6000K object must appear at exactly half the wavelength of the peak for the 3000K object. Mathematically: 
    $$\lambda_{max} \propto \frac{1}{T}$$

Because the temperature is doubled, the peak height and total area under the 6000K curve should be significantly higher (theoretically $2^4 = 16$ times the total power/area) than the 3000K curve.
