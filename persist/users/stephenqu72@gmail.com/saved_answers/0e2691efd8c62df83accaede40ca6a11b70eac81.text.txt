### 1. Identify the given values
*   Height of the tower: $h$
*   Length of the blade: $r = \frac{h}{2}$
*   Total height of the launch point from the ground: $y = h + r = h + \frac{h}{2} = \frac{3h}{2}$
*   Horizontal distance (range): $d = \pi h^{\frac{3}{2}}$

### 2. Projectile Motion Equations
The fragment is released when the blade is at the very top and vertical, meaning it is moving horizontally.
*   **Vertical motion:** The time $t$ taken to hit the ground from height $y$ is found using $y = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$:
    $$t = \sqrt{\frac{2y}{g}} = \sqrt{\frac{2(3h/2)}{g}} = \sqrt{\frac{3h}{g}}$$
*   **Horizontal motion:** The range $d$ is given by $d = v_x t$:
    $$v_x = \frac{d}{t} = \frac{\pi h^{3/2}}{\sqrt{3h/g}} = \pi h \sqrt{\frac{gh}{3}}$$

### 3. Determine the Period ($T$)
The horizontal velocity $v_x$ of the tip of the blade is related to the angular velocity $\omega$ by $v_x = \omega r$.
*   Since $r = \frac{h}{2}$:
    $$\omega = \frac{v_x}{r} = \frac{\pi h \sqrt{\frac{gh}{3}}}{\frac{h}{2}} = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{gh}{3}}$$
*   The period $T$ is given by $T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega}$:
    $$T = \frac{2\pi}{2\pi \sqrt{\frac{gh}{3}}} = \sqrt{\frac{3}{g/h}} = \sqrt{\frac{3h}{g}}$$
