### Front
How do you calculate the gravitational acceleration ($g$) on a different celestial body using an object's weight and mass?

### Back
- Key idea: Mass is invariant; weight ($W$) is the product of mass ($m$) and local gravitational acceleration ($g$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $W = mg$ or $g = W/m$.
- When to use it: To determine local gravity when weight and mass are known, or when comparing an object's weight across different locations.
- Common trap: Confusing mass ($kg$) with weight ($N$) or assuming $g$ is always $9.8 \, m/s^2$ when the context involves other planets or moons.
