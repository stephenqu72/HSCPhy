To solve this problem, we apply the principles of electromagnetic induction, specifically Faraday's Law and Lenz's Law.

### (a) Calculation of induced EMF

**1. Identify the given variables:**
*   Magnetic field strength, $B = 0.80\text{ T}$
*   Side length of the square loop, $s = 37\text{ cm} = 0.37\text{ m}$
*   Time taken to enter the field, $\Delta t = 0.04\text{ s}$
*   The area of the loop $A = s^2 = (0.37\text{ m})^2 = 0.1369\text{ m}^2$

**2. Formula:**
Faraday's Law states that the magnitude of the induced EMF ($\varepsilon$) is the rate of change of magnetic flux ($\Phi$):
$$\varepsilon = \left| \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t} \right|$$
Since flux is $\Phi = B \cdot A$ and only the area inside the field is changing as the loop enters:
$$\varepsilon = \frac{B \cdot \Delta A}{\Delta t}$$

**3. Calculation:**
As the loop enters the field, the change in area ($\Delta A$) is the total area of the square, $0.1369\text{ m}^2$.
$$\varepsilon = \frac{0.80\text{ T} \times 0.1369\text{ m}^2}{0.04\text{ s}}$$
$$\varepsilon = \frac{0.10952}{0.04}$$
$$\varepsilon = 2.738\text{ V}$$
Rounding to appropriate significant figures (two, based on given values), we get **$2.7\text{ V}$**.

---

### (b) Direction of induced current

To determine the direction, we use **Lenz's Law**, which states that the induced current will create a magnetic field to oppose the change in flux.
1.  As the loop enters the magnetic field, the magnetic flux into the page is **increasing**.
2.  The loop will attempt to oppose this increase by creating its own magnetic field pointing **out of the page**.
3.  Using the **right-hand grip rule**, point your thumb out of the page; your fingers curl in a **counter-clockwise** direction.

**Answer:** The current flows in a **counter-clockwise** direction.
