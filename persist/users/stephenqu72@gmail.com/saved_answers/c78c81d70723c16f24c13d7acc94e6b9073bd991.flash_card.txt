### Front
How can you determine the work done **by an electric field** on a positive charge moving between two points?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The field does positive work when a positive charge moves from a region of higher potential ($V_{high}$) to lower potential ($V_{low}$).
- **Formula / law:** $W_{field} = q(V_i - V_f) = -\Delta U_e$.
- **When to use it:** To calculate energy changes or determine the direction of motion for a charged particle in an electric field.
- **Common trap:** Confusing work done *by the field* with work done *by an external agent*. If the charge moves toward higher potential, the field does negative work (the external agent does the work).
