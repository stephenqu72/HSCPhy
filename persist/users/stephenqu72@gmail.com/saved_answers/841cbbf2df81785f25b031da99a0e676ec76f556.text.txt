To determine the net magnetic field at point $P$, we must use the **Right-Hand Grip Rule** to determine the direction of the magnetic field generated by each wire individually.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Analyze the Top Wire:**
    *   Point the thumb of your right hand in the direction of the current (to the right).
    *   Your fingers will curl around the wire. Below the top wire (where point $P$ is located), your fingers point **into the page**. Therefore, the magnetic field from the top wire at $P$ is $Z$ into the page.

2.  **Analyze the Bottom Wire:**
    *   Again, point your thumb in the direction of the current (to the right).
    *   Your fingers curl around the wire. Above the bottom wire (where point $P$ is located), your fingers point **out of the page**. Therefore, the magnetic field from the bottom wire at $P$ is $Z$ out of the page.

3.  **Calculate the Net Field:**
    *   Since the wires are identical and point $P$ is exactly halfway between them, the magnitude of the field from both wires is the same ($Z$).
    *   Because the directions are opposite (one is "into" and one is "out of"), they cancel each other out:
        $$B_{net} = Z_{\text{into}} - Z_{\text{out}} = 0$$

The net magnetic field at point $P$ is $0$ Teslas.

**Correct Option: (B)**
