### Front
How do you calculate the maximum kinetic energy ($K_{max}$) of a $\beta^-$ particle resulting from a nuclear decay to an excited state?

### Back
- **Key idea:** $K_{max}$ is the total energy released ($Q$) minus the excitation energy ($E_x$) of the daughter nucleus.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $K_{max} = Q - E_x$; $1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$.
- **When to use it:** When a decay scheme shows the nucleus transitioning to an excited state rather than the ground state.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to subtract the specific $E_x$ of the relevant excited state, or confusing the antineutrino's energy (which takes the "missing" energy) with the $\beta$ particle's kinetic energy.
