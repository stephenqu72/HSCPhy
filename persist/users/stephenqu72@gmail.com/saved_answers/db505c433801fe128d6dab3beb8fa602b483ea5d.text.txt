### Part (a): Calculation
**The Question:** A current of $3.0 \times 10^{-2} \text{ A}$ was input into a transformer in which the secondary winding had 20 times the number of primary turns. Calculate the output current if there were no power loss.

**Solution:**
In an ideal transformer (no power loss), the power in the primary circuit equals the power in the secondary circuit ($P_p = P_s$). Since $P = VI$, we have $V_p I_p = V_s I_s$.
We also know the transformer ratio formula: $\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$.

By substituting the ratio into the power equation, we derive:
$$I_s = I_p \times \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$

**Given:**
- $I_p = 3.0 \times 10^{-2} \text{ A}$
- $\frac{N_s}{N_p} = 20 \implies \frac{N_p}{N_s} = \frac{1}{20}$

**Calculation:**
$$I_s = (3.0 \times 10^{-2} \text{ A}) \times \left(\frac{1}{20}\right)$$
$$I_s = \frac{0.03}{20} \text{ A}$$
$$I_s = 1.5 \times 10^{-3} \text{ A} \quad (\text{or } 0.0015 \text{ A})$$

### Part (b): Outline
**The Question:** Outline the role of transformers in household electrical goods.

**Explanation:**
The primary role of transformers in household goods is to adjust the voltage levels provided by the mains supply (which is typically $240 \text{ V}$ AC in Australia) to the specific requirements of the electronic components within the device.
- **Step-down transformers:** These are most common in household electronics (like phone chargers or laptops) to reduce the high mains voltage to a lower, safer DC voltage (after rectification) required by sensitive microchips and circuits.
- **Isolation/Safety:** Transformers can also act as an isolation barrier between the user and the mains power, increasing safety.
