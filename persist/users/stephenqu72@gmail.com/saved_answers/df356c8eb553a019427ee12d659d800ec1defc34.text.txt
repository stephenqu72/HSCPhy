To answer this question, we must apply Einstein’s Special Theory of Relativity. The phenomenon of muons reaching the surface is a classic demonstration of time dilation and length contraction.

### Answer
The observation that muons reach Earth's surface can be explained by two complementary relativistic effects:

1.  **From the Earth’s frame of reference:** Time dilation occurs. Because the muons are travelling at a significant fraction of the speed of light, their internal "clock" runs slower relative to an observer on Earth. Consequently, the muon’s lifetime is effectively extended, allowing it to cover the distance to the Earth's surface before it decays.
2.  **From the muon’s frame of reference:** Length contraction occurs. In the muon's frame, the muon is at rest, and the Earth's atmosphere is rushing towards it at nearly the speed of light. Due to length contraction, the distance between the upper atmosphere and the Earth's surface is measured to be significantly shorter. Thus, the muon can traverse this contracted distance within its normal, proper lifetime.
