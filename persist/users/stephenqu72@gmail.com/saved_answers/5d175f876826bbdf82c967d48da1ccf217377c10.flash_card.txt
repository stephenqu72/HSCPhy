### Front
Under what condition does an object experience an increased downward $g$-force (increased apparent weight)?

### Back
- Key idea: Apparent weight increases when the net acceleration is directed upwards.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $N = m(g + a)$ (Newton's Second Law)
- When to use it: Calculating "heaviness" in accelerating frames like elevators, rollercoasters, or aircraft maneuvers.
- Common trap: Confusing the direction of velocity with the direction of acceleration (e.g., moving upwards but slowing down results in downward acceleration and feeling lighter).
