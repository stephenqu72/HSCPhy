To solve this problem, we need to apply the physics of circular motion on a banked track without relying on friction.

### Physics Principles
When an object travels on a banked curve without friction, the centripetal force required for circular motion is provided entirely by the horizontal component of the normal force ($N$).

1.  **Vertical equilibrium:** The vertical component of the normal force balances the weight ($mg$).
    $$N \cos \theta = mg$$
2.  **Centripetal force:** The horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force ($F_c$).
    $$N \sin \theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Dividing the second equation by the first eliminates $N$ and $m$:
$$\frac{N \sin \theta}{N \cos \theta} = \frac{mv^2 / r}{mg}$$
$$\tan \theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

### Calculation
Given:
*   $\theta = 40.0^\circ$
*   $r = 20.0 \text{ m}$
*   $g = 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$

Rearranging to solve for velocity ($v$):
$$v = \sqrt{rg \tan \theta}$$
$$v = \sqrt{20.0 \times 9.8 \times \tan(40.0^\circ)}$$
$$v = \sqrt{196 \times 0.8391}$$
$$v \approx \sqrt{164.46} \approx 12.824 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

To convert this speed from $\text{m s}^{-1}$ to $\text{km h}^{-1}$, we multiply by $3.6$:
$$v_{\text{km/h}} = 12.824 \times 3.6 \approx 46.166 \text{ km h}^{-1}$$

Rounding to three significant figures, we get $46.2 \text{ km h}^{-1}$.
