### Front
Explain the physics of a moving observer encountering a stationary sound source (e.g., a car driving towards a police siren).

### Back
- **Key idea:** The **Doppler Effect**—moving towards a source increases the rate of wave-front interception, increasing observed frequency ($f_{obs}$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $f_{obs} = f_s \frac{v + v_{obs}}{v}$ (where $v$ is the constant speed of sound in air).
- **When to use it:** Whenever a source or observer is in motion relative to the medium to explain shifts in frequency/pitch.
- **Common trap:** Thinking the *speed of sound* changes. The speed of sound in the medium is constant; only the *relative velocity* between the observer and the waves changes.
