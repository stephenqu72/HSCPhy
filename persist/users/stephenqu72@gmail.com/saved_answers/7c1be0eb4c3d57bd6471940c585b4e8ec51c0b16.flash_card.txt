### Front
Where is a planet's gravitational potential energy (GPE) greatest in an elliptical orbit, and why?

### Back
- **Key idea:** GPE is at a maximum (closest to zero) when the distance ($r$) from the central mass is at its maximum (aphelion).
- **Formula:** $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$
- **When to use it:** To determine the relative potential energy of an object in a gravitational field based on its orbital position.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting the negative sign; a larger $r$ makes the fraction smaller, which makes the negative value "larger" (closer to zero).
