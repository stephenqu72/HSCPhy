1.  **Independent Variable:** This is the variable the student purposefully changes. The problem states, "The student changed the mass $M_1$". Therefore, $M_1$ is the independent variable.
2.  **Dependent Variable:** This is the variable being measured in response to the changes made to the independent variable. The problem states the student "measured the time taken for a complete revolution." The time taken for one revolution is the definition of the period ($T$). Therefore, the period is the dependent variable.
3.  **Controlled Variable:** These are the factors that must remain constant throughout the experiment to ensure a fair test. In this setup, the mass of the rotating object ($m_2$) and the radius of the circular path ($L$) should be kept constant.
