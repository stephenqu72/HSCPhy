### Answer and Explanation

**(a) What are the reactants and products of the CNO cycle? Justify your answer.**

*   **Answer:** The net reactant is four hydrogen nuclei ($^1_1H$) and the net product is one helium nucleus ($^4_2He$).
*   **Justification:** By observing the cycle, the inputs are four protons (entering in four separate reaction steps) and the outputs are one alpha particle ($^4_2He$) plus energy. The nuclei $^{12}C$, $^{13}N$, $^{13}C$, $^{14}N$, $^{15}O$, and $^{15}N$ are merely catalysts; they are consumed and regenerated throughout the cycle, resulting in no net change to their concentration. Therefore, the net reaction is:
    $$4(^1_1H) \rightarrow ^4_2He + 2(e^+) + 2(\nu_e) + \text{energy}$$

**(b) Identify the missing particle, $(X)$.**

*   **Answer:** $(X)$ is a positron, denoted as $^0_{+1}e$ (or $\beta^+$).
*   **Explanation:** In the reaction $^{13}_7N \rightarrow ^{13}_6C + (X) + \nu_e$, we must balance the atomic number (charge) and the mass number. 
    *   Mass number: $13 = 13 + A + 0 \Rightarrow A = 0$.
    *   Atomic number: $7 = 6 + Z + 0 \Rightarrow Z = 1$.
    A particle with mass number 0 and atomic number 1 is a positron.

**(c) Why is only 2% of the helium fused in the Sun using the CNO cycle?**

*   **Answer:** The CNO cycle has a significantly higher activation energy (or temperature dependence) compared to the proton-proton (p-p) chain.
*   **Explanation:** The CNO cycle involves collisions between protons and heavier nuclei (Carbon, Nitrogen, Oxygen). Because these nuclei have higher atomic numbers ($Z$) than hydrogen, the electrostatic (Coulomb) repulsion is much stronger. The Sun's core temperature is relatively low (approx. 15 million Kelvin), which is sufficient for the p-p chain to dominate, but insufficient for the CNO cycle to occur at a significant rate. The CNO cycle only becomes the dominant energy production method in stars much more massive than the Sun ($>1.3 M_{sun}$), where core temperatures are significantly higher.
