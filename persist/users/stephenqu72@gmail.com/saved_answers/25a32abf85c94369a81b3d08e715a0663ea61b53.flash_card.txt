### Front
On what factors does the orbital speed ($v$) of a satellite depend, and how does the satellite's own mass affect it?

### Back
- Key idea: Orbital speed is independent of the satellite's mass ($m$). It depends only on the mass of the central body ($M$) and the orbital radius ($r$).
- Formula / law / constant: $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$
- When to use it: To determine or compare the velocities of objects in circular orbits (e.g., satellites, planets).
- Common trap: Assuming a more massive satellite must travel slower or faster; mass ($m$) always cancels out in the derivation from $F_g = F_c$.
