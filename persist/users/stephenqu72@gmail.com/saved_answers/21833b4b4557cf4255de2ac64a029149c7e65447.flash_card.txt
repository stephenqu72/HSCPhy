### Front
How are sound waves and light waves classified based on their oscillation direction relative to energy propagation?

### Back
- Key idea: Sound is a longitudinal wave (parallel); light is a transverse wave (perpendicular).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When distinguishing wave types by particle/field displacement.
- Common trap: Confusing the medium requirement (mechanical vs. EM) with the direction of oscillation (longitudinal vs. transverse).
