The correct answer is **C. $c$**.

### Explanation
This question tests your understanding of the **second postulate of Special Relativity**, as proposed by Albert Einstein. 

1.  **The Second Postulate:** The speed of light in a vacuum ($c$) is a universal constant. It is independent of the motion of the source and independent of the motion of the observer.
2.  **Application:** In classical (Galilean) mechanics, you might be tempted to simply add the velocities (e.g., $0.7c + c = 1.7c$), but this does not apply to light. Because light has no mass and travels at the fundamental speed limit of the universe, no matter how fast the sender or the receiver is moving, the measurement of the speed of the light beam (in this case, a microwave beam, which is part of the electromagnetic spectrum) will always result in $c$.
3.  **Conclusion:** Even though the spaceships are moving toward each other at $0.7c$, the receiving spaceship will measure the microwave beam approaching it at exactly $c$ (approximately $3 \times 10^8$ m/s). 

Options A, B, and D are incorrect because they violate the fundamental principle that the speed of light is invariant in all inertial frames of reference.
