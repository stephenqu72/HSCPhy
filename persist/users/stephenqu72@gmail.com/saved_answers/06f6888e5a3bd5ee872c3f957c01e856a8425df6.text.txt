To solve this problem, we use the fundamental definition of electric potential difference ($V$). The potential difference between two points is defined as the work done ($W$) per unit charge ($q$) required to move a positive test charge between those points.

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Work done ($W$) = $2.4 \times 10^{-5} \text{ J}$
*   Charge ($q$) = $2 \text{ \mu C} = 2 \times 10^{-6} \text{ C}$ (Remember to always convert microcoulombs to coulombs by multiplying by $10^{-6}$).

**2. Use the formula:**
The relationship is given by:
$$V = \frac{W}{q}$$

**3. Perform the calculation:**
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$V = \frac{2.4 \times 10^{-5}}{2 \times 10^{-6}}$$

To simplify this, divide the coefficients ($2.4 / 2 = 1.2$) and subtract the exponents ($10^{-5} / 10^{-6} = 10^{-5 - (-6)} = 10^1$):
$$V = 1.2 \times 10^1$$
$$V = 12 \text{ V}$$
