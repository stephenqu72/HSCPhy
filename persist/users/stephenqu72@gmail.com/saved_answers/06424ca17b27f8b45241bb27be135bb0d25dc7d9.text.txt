To find the orbital velocity of Phobos, we use the formula for the orbital velocity of a satellite orbiting a central body:

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

where:
*   $v$ is the orbital velocity
*   $G$ is the gravitational constant ($6.67 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N m}^2 \text{kg}^{-2}$)
*   $M$ is the mass of the central body (Mars)
*   $r$ is the orbital radius

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Determine the mass of Mars ($M$):**
Since we don't know the mass of Mars directly, we use the data provided for Deimos to find it.
Rearranging the velocity formula: $v^2 = \frac{GM}{r} \implies GM = v^2 r$.
*   For Deimos: $v = 1.35 \times 10^3 \, \text{m s}^{-1}$ (converted from km/s) and $r = 2.35 \times 10^7 \, \text{m}$.
*   $GM = (1.35 \times 10^3)^2 \times (2.35 \times 10^7)$
*   $GM = 1.8225 \times 10^6 \times 2.35 \times 10^7 = 4.282875 \times 10^{13} \, \text{m}^3 \text{s}^{-2}$

**2. Calculate the orbital velocity of Phobos ($v_{\text{Phobos}}$):**
Now use the $GM$ value found above and the orbital radius of Phobos ($r = 9.38 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}$):
*   $v_{\text{Phobos}} = \sqrt{\frac{4.282875 \times 10^{13}}{9.38 \times 10^6}}$
*   $v_{\text{Phobos}} = \sqrt{4.566 \times 10^6}$
*   $v_{\text{Phobos}} \approx 2136.8 \, \text{m s}^{-1}$

**3. Convert to km/s:**
*   $v_{\text{Phobos}} \approx 2.14 \, \text{km s}^{-1}$
