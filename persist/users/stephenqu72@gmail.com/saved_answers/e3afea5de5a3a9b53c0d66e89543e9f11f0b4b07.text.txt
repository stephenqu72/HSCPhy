### Answer

Rutherford’s model proposed that the atom consists of a tiny, dense, positively charged nucleus at the center, containing almost all the mass of the atom, with electrons orbiting at a large distance. This accounts for the experimental evidence as follows:

1.  **Most alpha particles passed straight through:** This indicates that the atom is mostly empty space.
2.  **Small deflections:** These occur when alpha particles pass near the positive nucleus, experiencing electrostatic repulsion.
3.  **Large deflections (backscattering):** These occur only when an alpha particle has a "head-on" collision with the concentrated positive charge of the nucleus. Because the nucleus is extremely dense and positively charged, it exerts a strong repulsive force, sufficient to reflect the heavy, high-speed alpha particle backwards.
