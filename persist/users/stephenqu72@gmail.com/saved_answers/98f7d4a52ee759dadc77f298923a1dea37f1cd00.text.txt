To solve this question, we must understand the fundamental principles governing how charged particles behave in electromagnetic fields.

### Analysis of the Physics Principles

1.  **Magnetic Fields:**
    *   The force on a charged particle in a magnetic field is given by the Lorentz force formula: $\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$.
    *   Because this involves a cross product, the force is **always perpendicular** to both the velocity of the particle and the magnetic field.
    *   Crucially, if the velocity $v = 0$ (the particle is stationary), the force is zero. Thus, the particle must be in motion to experience a magnetic force.

2.  **Electric Fields:**
    *   The force on a charged particle in an electric field is given by: $\vec{F} = q\vec{E}$.
    *   This force is **parallel** (or anti-parallel) to the electric field lines.
    *   This force depends only on the charge and the field strength; it does **not** depend on the velocity of the particle. Therefore, the particle does not need to be in motion to experience this force; it experiences it simply by existing within the field.

### Evaluating the Options

*   **Option A:** Incorrect. Magnetic force requires motion, and electric force does not.

*   **Option D:** Incorrect. Magnetic force is perpendicular, and electric force does not require motion.

### Answer
The correct option is **C**.
