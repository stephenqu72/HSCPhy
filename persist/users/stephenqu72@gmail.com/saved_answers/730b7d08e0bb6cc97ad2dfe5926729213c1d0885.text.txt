To answer a 5-mark "discuss" question in HSC Physics, you must provide a structured argument that covers the scope, historical context, and the transformative impact of the scientist’s work.

### Answer

James Clerk Maxwell’s contribution was the unification of electricity and magnetism into a single, cohesive theory of electromagnetism. His significance can be summarized through the following key points:

1.  **Mathematical Unification:** Before Maxwell, electricity and magnetism were treated as separate phenomena (as seen in the works of Faraday, Ampère, and Gauss). Maxwell synthesized these experimental laws into a single set of four partial differential equations (Maxwell’s Equations). This provided the first formal, rigorous mathematical framework for the field.
2.  **The Displacement Current:** Maxwell’s most critical theoretical insight was the "displacement current." By adding this term to Ampère’s Law, he correctly predicted that a changing electric field generates a magnetic field, just as a changing magnetic field (Faraday’s Law) generates an electric field. This symmetry was the missing link in classical electromagnetism.
3.  **Prediction of Electromagnetic Waves:** By combining his equations, Maxwell derived a wave equation that showed that electric and magnetic fields propagate through space as a transverse wave. He calculated the speed of these waves to be $c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0}}$, which matched the experimentally determined speed of light.
4.  **Nature of Light:** Maxwell proposed that light is an electromagnetic wave. This was a paradigm shift, as it transitioned optics into the realm of electromagnetism, suggesting that light is just one frequency of a continuous electromagnetic spectrum.
5.  **Foundation for Modern Physics:** His work provided the necessary foundation for Hertz’s experimental verification of radio waves, which led to modern wireless communication. Furthermore, the constancy of the speed of light derived from his equations served as a fundamental postulate for Einstein’s theory of Special Relativity.

### How to solve this type of question
*   **The "Discuss" Directive:** This requires more than just listing facts. You must show the *significance*—i.e., explain the "before" and "after" state of physics.
*   **Structure for 5 marks:**
    *   **Introduction:** Briefly state his role in unifying the field.
    *   **Body Paragraphs:** Focus on specific breakthroughs (Mathematical synthesis $\rightarrow$ Displacement current $\rightarrow$ Wave prediction $\rightarrow$ Nature of light).
    *   **Impact:** Conclude by explaining the long-term technological or theoretical legacy (Relativity or telecommunications).
*   **Keywords:** Use terms like *unification*, *symmetry*, *propagation*, *transverse wave*, and *electromagnetic spectrum* to demonstrate deep syllabus knowledge.
