To solve this problem, we use the relationship between the work done on a charge by an electric field and the change in its kinetic energy.

### Explanation

When a charge $q$ moves through a potential difference $V$, the work done on the charge by the electric field is given by:
$$W = qV$$

According to the Work-Energy Theorem, this work done is equal to the change in the particle's kinetic energy ($\Delta EK$):
$$\Delta EK = qV$$

**1. Calculate the change in kinetic energy ($\Delta EK$):**
The kinetic energy increased from $2.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ J}$ to $6.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ J}$.
$$\Delta EK = EK_{final} - EK_{initial}$$
$$\Delta EK = (6.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ J}) - (2.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ J})$$
$$\Delta EK = 4.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ J}$$

**2. Rearrange the formula to solve for charge ($q$):**
$$q = \frac{\Delta EK}{V}$$

**3. Substitute the given values:**
Given $V = 500\text{ V}$:
$$q = \frac{4.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ J}}{500\text{ V}}$$
$$q = \frac{4.0 \times 10^{-5}}{5.0 \times 10^2}$$
$$q = 0.8 \times 10^{-7}\text{ C}$$
$$q = 8.0 \times 10^{-8}\text{ C}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct choice is **(B)**.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(B)**.
