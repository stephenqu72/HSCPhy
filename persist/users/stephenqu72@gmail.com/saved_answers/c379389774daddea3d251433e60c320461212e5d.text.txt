To solve this problem, we use the photoelectric equation, which describes the energy balance of the photoelectric effect:

$$E_k = hf - \Phi$$

Where:
- $E_k$ is the maximum kinetic energy of the emitted photoelectrons.
- $h$ is Planck's constant.
- $f$ is the frequency of the incident light.
- $\Phi$ is the work function of the metal (the minimum energy required to remove an electron).

### Analysis:
1.  **Identify the thresholds:** The x-intercept of each line represents the **threshold frequency** ($f_0$) of that specific metal. At this frequency, $E_k = 0$, meaning the energy of the incident photon is exactly equal to the work function ($\Phi = hf_0$).
    *   For metal $X$, the threshold frequency is $f_{0,X} < 8 \times 10^{14}$ Hz (looking at the graph, it appears to be at $4 \times 10^{14}$ Hz).
    *   For metal $Y$, the threshold frequency is exactly $f_{0,Y} = 8 \times 10^{14}$ Hz.

2.  **Evaluate the scenario:** The question asks us to consider incident light with a frequency of $f = 8 \times 10^{14}$ Hz.
    *   **For metal $X$:** Since $f > f_{0,X}$, the incident photons have more energy than the work function. Thus, $E_k > 0$, and photoelectrons are emitted with a specific maximum velocity.
    *   **For metal $Y$:** Since $f = f_{0,Y}$, the energy of the incident photons is exactly equal to the work function. According to the photoelectric equation, $E_k = hf - \Phi = 0$.

3.  **Conclusion:** Because $E_k = 0$ for metal $Y$ at this frequency, the photoelectrons emitted have essentially zero kinetic energy and therefore zero velocity. Since metal $X$ results in $E_k > 0$, its photoelectrons will have a greater velocity than those from metal $Y$. Conversely, the photoelectrons from metal $Y$ will have a **lower maximum velocity** compared to those from metal $X$.

Regarding options C and D, the photoelectric effect is an instantaneous process—it does not depend on a "time to gain energy" (which is a concept from the wave theory of light that was invalidated by the photoelectric effect). Therefore, A is the correct answer.
