To determine which planet has the greatest gravitational field strength at its surface, we use the formula for gravitational field strength $g$:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

where:
- $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
- $M$ is the mass of the planet.
- $r$ is the radius of the planet.

We can calculate the relative gravitational field strength for each planet by substituting their respective mass and radius values into the formula. Since $G$ is a constant for all, we focus on the ratio $\frac{M}{r^2}$.

1.  **Planet W**: $M = M, r = R$
    $$g_W = \frac{M}{R^2}$$

2.  **Planet X**: $M = M, r = 2R$
    $$g_X = \frac{M}{(2R)^2} = \frac{M}{4R^2} = 0.25 \frac{M}{R^2}$$

3.  **Planet Y**: $M = 2M, r = R$
    $$g_Y = \frac{2M}{R^2} = 2 \frac{M}{R^2}$$

4.  **Planet Z**: $M = 2M, r = 2R$
    $$g_Z = \frac{2M}{(2R)^2} = \frac{2M}{4R^2} = 0.5 \frac{M}{R^2}$$

Comparing the coefficients, Planet Y has a relative value of $2$, while the others are $1$, $0.25$, and $0.5$ respectively. Therefore, Planet Y has the greatest gravitational field strength.

**Correct Answer: C. Planet Y**
