### Front
In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), how does the horizontal velocity change during flight?

### Back
- Key idea: Horizontal velocity remains constant because horizontal acceleration is zero.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_x = 0 \text{ m s}^{-2}$
- When to use it: To determine horizontal velocity at any point in time during the projectile's trajectory.
- Common trap: Attempting to use vertical displacement or time of flight to calculate horizontal velocity; horizontal velocity is independent of these factors.
