### Explanation
To identify the independent variable in a scientific investigation, you must look for the factor that the experimenter deliberately changes or manipulates to observe its effect on the system.

1.  **Independent Variable:** This is the variable that you change. In this specific investigation, the question explicitly states that the "strength of a magnet is varied." Therefore, the strength of the magnet is the independent variable.
2.  **Dependent Variable:** This is the variable being measured or tested as a result of the changes. Here, the investigation aims to "determine the size of a generated current," making the size of the current the dependent variable.
3.  **Controlled Variables:** These are factors that must be kept constant to ensure a fair test (e.g., speed of the magnet or the number of turns in the coil). Options A and D represent potential controlled variables, while C represents the dependent variable.

Since option B directly corresponds to the factor being manipulated by the investigator, it is the correct answer.
