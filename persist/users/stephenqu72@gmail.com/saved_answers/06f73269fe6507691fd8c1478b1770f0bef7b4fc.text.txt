To solve this problem, we use Kepler's Third Law of planetary motion, which relates the orbital period $T$ and the orbital radius $r$ of a satellite to the mass $M$ of the central body.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

1.  **Identify the formula:**
    The relationship is given by:
    $$\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$$
    Rearranging to solve for the mass of the planet ($M$):
    $$M = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{GT^2}$$

2.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   Orbital period $T = 2.45 \times 10^7 \text{ s}$
    *   Orbital radius $r = 3.80 \times 10^{10} \text{ m}$
    *   Gravitational constant $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3 \text{ kg}^{-1} \text{ s}^{-2}$

3.  **Substitute the values into the equation:**
    $$M = \frac{4 \times \pi^2 \times (3.80 \times 10^{10})^3}{6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times (2.45 \times 10^7)^2}$$

4.  **Perform the calculation:**
    *   Numerator: $4 \times \pi^2 \times 5.4872 \times 10^{31} \approx 2.166 \times 10^{33}$
    *   Denominator: $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 6.0025 \times 10^{14} \approx 4.004 \times 10^4$
    *   $M \approx \frac{2.166 \times 10^{33}}{4.004 \times 10^4} \approx 5.41 \times 10^{28} \text{ kg}$
