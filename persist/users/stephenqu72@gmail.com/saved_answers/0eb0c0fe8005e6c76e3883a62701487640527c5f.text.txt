### Answer
The correct answer is **B. The motor effect**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we look at the interaction between electric currents and magnetic fields:

1.  **The Motor Effect:** This phenomenon describes the force exerted on a current-carrying conductor when it is placed within an external magnetic field. The magnitude of this force $F$ can be calculated using the formula:
    $$F = BIL \sin(\theta)$$
    where $B$ is the magnetic field strength, $I$ is the current, $L$ is the length of the conductor, and $\theta$ is the angle between the conductor and the magnetic field. This is the underlying principle behind electric motors.

2.  **Why the others are incorrect:**
    *   **A. Faraday’s Law:** This relates to electromagnetic induction, which explains how a changing magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) in a circuit (it is about generating electricity, not the physical force on a wire).
    *   **C. Lenz’s Law:** This is a direction rule for electromagnetic induction, stating that the direction of an induced current will oppose the change in magnetic flux that produced it.
    *   **D. The Photoelectric Effect:** This refers to the emission of electrons when electromagnetic radiation (light) hits a material; it is a quantum physics concept unrelated to current-carrying conductors in magnetic fields.

Always remember that the "motor effect" is essentially the conversion of electrical energy into mechanical work due to magnetic forces!
