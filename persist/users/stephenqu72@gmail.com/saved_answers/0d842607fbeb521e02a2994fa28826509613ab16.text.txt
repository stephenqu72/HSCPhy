To determine which projectile travels the furthest horizontal distance (the range), we use the physics formula for the range ($R$) of a projectile launched from level ground:

$$R = \frac{v^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$

Where:
- $v$ is the initial velocity
- $\theta$ is the angle of projection
- $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity (constant)

We need to calculate the value of $v^2 \sin(2\theta)$ for each option to compare the ranges.

**Analysis:**

*   **(A)** $v = 12$, $\theta = 20^\circ$
    $$12^2 \times \sin(40^\circ) = 144 \times 0.6428 \approx 92.56$$

*   **(B)** $v = 11$, $\theta = 30^\circ$
    $$11^2 \times \sin(60^\circ) = 121 \times 0.8660 \approx 104.79$$

*   **(C)** $v = 10$, $\theta = 20^\circ$
    $$10^2 \times \sin(40^\circ) = 100 \times 0.6428 \approx 64.28$$

*   **(D)** $v = 9$, $\theta = 45^\circ$
    $$9^2 \times \sin(90^\circ) = 81 \times 1 = 81.00$$

**Conclusion:**
Comparing the calculated values ($92.56, 104.79, 64.28, 81.00$), option **(B)** yields the highest value. Therefore, diagram (B) results in the furthest horizontal distance.
