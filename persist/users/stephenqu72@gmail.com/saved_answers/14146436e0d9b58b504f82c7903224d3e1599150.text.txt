To solve this problem, we must apply the virial theorem as it relates to a satellite in a stable, circular orbit around a central body.

### The Physics Principles
For a satellite of mass $m$ in a stable circular orbit at a distance $r$ from a central mass $M$:

1.  **Kinetic Energy ($E_k$):** Given by the formula $E_k = \frac{GMm}{2r}$.
2.  **Gravitational Potential Energy ($E_p$):** Given by the formula $E_p = -\frac{GMm}{r}$.
3.  **Total Mechanical Energy ($E_{total}$):** This is the sum of kinetic and potential energy:
    $$E_{total} = E_k + E_p$$
    Substituting the expressions above:
    $$E_{total} = \frac{GMm}{2r} + \left(-\frac{GMm}{r}\right) = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$$

### Relationships
From these formulas, we can derive two critical relationships:
*   $E_{total} = -E_k$
*   $E_p = -2E_k$

### Analysis of the Options
We are looking for an option where the potential energy is equal to $-2 \times E_k$ and the total energy is the sum of $E_k$ and $E_p$ (which will result in $E_{total} = -E_k$).

*   **Option A:** 
    *   $E_k = 40,000\text{ J}$
    *   $E_p = -80,000\text{ J}$ (Note: $-80,000 = -2 \times 40,000$, which is correct)
    *   $E_{total} = 40,000 + (-80,000) = -40,000\text{ J}$ (Note: $-40,000 = -E_k$, which is correct)
    *   **This matches all physical requirements.**

*   **Option B, C, and D:** These do not satisfy the relationship $E_p = -2E_k$ and $E_{total} = E_k + E_p$. For instance, in Option D, $E_k = 80,000$ implies $E_p$ should be $-160,000$, which is not listed.

**Correct Answer: A**
