To solve for the time of flight of a projectile landing at the same vertical level from which it was launched, we focus on the vertical component of its motion.

### **Answer**
The correct answer is **(A) 2.86 s**.

### **Explanation**
To find the time of flight ($t$), we use the kinematic equation for vertical displacement:
$$y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2} a_y t^2$$

Since the ball lands at the same height from which it was kicked, the total vertical displacement $y = 0$.
The initial vertical velocity $u_y$ is determined by the sine component of the initial velocity ($v$):
$$u_y = v \sin(\theta)$$
Given $v = 28\text{ ms}^{-1}$ and $\theta = 30^\circ$:
$$u_y = 28 \sin(30^\circ) = 28 \times 0.5 = 14\text{ ms}^{-1}$$

The vertical acceleration $a_y$ due to gravity is $-9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$ (taking upward as positive). Setting displacement to zero:
$$0 = (u_y)t + \frac{1}{2}(-g)t^2$$
We can factor out $t$:
$$0 = t(u_y - \frac{1}{2}gt)$$
Since $t \neq 0$ at the point of landing, we solve for $t$:
$$\frac{1}{2}gt = u_y$$
$$t = \frac{2u_y}{g}$$

Now, substitute the values:
$$t = \frac{2 \times 14}{9.8}$$
$$t = \frac{28}{9.8}$$
$$t \approx 2.857\text{ s}$$

Rounding to two decimal places, we get **2.86 s**.
