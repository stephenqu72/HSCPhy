### Front
How does the gravitational potential energy ($U$) of an object change if its distance ($r$) from the centre of a mass $M$ is quadrupled?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational potential energy is inversely proportional to the distance ($r$). If $r$ increases, the magnitude $|U|$ decreases.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$
- **When to use it:** To calculate energy changes in a gravitational field or when moving between orbital radii.
- **Common trap:** Confusing "mathematical increase" (becoming less negative, i.e., moving closer to zero) with "magnitude." Always check if the question asks for the change in the *value* ($U$ increases) or the *magnitude* ($|U|$ decreases).
