### Answer

Two limitations of the ideal transformer model and corresponding strategies to improve efficiency are:

1.  **Limitation: Eddy Currents.**
    *   **Description:** Changing magnetic flux in the iron core induces circular currents (eddy currents) within the core material itself, causing energy loss as heat due to the resistance of the iron.
    *   **Strategy:** The core is constructed using **laminated sheets** insulated from each other. This breaks the path of the eddy currents, significantly reducing their magnitude and the resulting heat dissipation.

2.  **Limitation: Resistance in Windings (Copper Loss).**
    *   **Description:** The copper wires used in the primary and secondary coils have inherent electrical resistance. As current flows through these coils, energy is dissipated as heat according to $P = I^2R$.
    *   **Strategy:** Use **low-resistance, thick copper wire** for the windings to minimize the power lost to heat.
