To find the gravitational field strength at the surface of Triton, we use the formula derived from Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

### Given Data:
- Gravitational constant, $G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$
- Mass of Triton, $M = 2.14 \times 10^{22} \text{ kg}$
- Radius of Triton, $r = 1.35 \times 10^{6} \text{ m}$

### Calculation:
1. Plug the values into the formula:
   $$g = \frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \times (2.14 \times 10^{22})}{(1.35 \times 10^{6})^2}$$

2. Calculate the numerator:
   $$(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \times (2.14 \times 10^{22}) \approx 1.428236 \times 10^{12}$$

3. Calculate the denominator (square of the radius):
   $$(1.35 \times 10^{6})^2 = 1.8225 \times 10^{12}$$

4. Divide the numerator by the denominator:
   $$g = \frac{1.428236 \times 10^{12}}{1.8225 \times 10^{12}} \approx 0.78366 \dots \text{ m s}^{-2}$$

Rounding to three significant figures, we get $0.784 \text{ m s}^{-2}$, which corresponds to option (A).

**Correct Answer: (A)**

### How to solve this type of problem:
1. **Identify the variables:** Always clearly list your $G$, $M$, and $r$. Ensure that all units are in SI units (kg, meters, seconds).
2. **Select the correct formula:** For surface gravity, you must use the distance from the center of the mass to the surface, which is the radius $r$. 
3. **Handle Scientific Notation:** Be careful when squaring numbers in scientific notation. Remember that $(a \times 10^b)^2 = a^2 \times 10^{2b}$.
4. **Verification:** Check the order of magnitude. If you get a result like $10^{17}$, you likely forgot to square the radius or used the wrong power of 10.
