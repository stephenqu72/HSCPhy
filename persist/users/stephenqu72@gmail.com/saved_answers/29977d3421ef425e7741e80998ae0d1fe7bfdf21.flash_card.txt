### Front
How do you calculate the magnetic field strength ($B$) acting on a moving charged particle?

### Back
- **Key idea:** A moving charge in a magnetic field experiences a Lorentz force perpendicular to its velocity and the field.
- **Formula:** $B = \frac{F}{qv \sin(\theta)}$
- **When to use it:** To find the magnetic field strength given a particle's force, charge, velocity, and angle of entry.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert the angle to radians or assuming $\sin(\theta) = 1$ when the particle is not entering perpendicular to the field.
