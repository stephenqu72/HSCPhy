To solve this problem, we need to analyze the motion of an electron moving through a uniform electric field between two charged parallel plates.

### Given Data:
*   Voltage ($V$) = $20\text{ V}$
*   Plate length ($L$) = $0.25\text{ m}$
*   Plate separation ($d$) = $0.20\text{ m}$
*   Initial horizontal velocity ($v_x$) = $3500\text{ m/s}$
*   Electron charge ($q$) = $1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
*   Electron mass ($m$) = $9.11 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. Determine the electric field ($E$):**
The electric field between parallel plates is given by:
$$E = \frac{V}{d} = \frac{20}{0.20} = 100\text{ V/m}$$

**2. Determine the acceleration of the electron ($a_y$):**
The force on the electron is $F = qE$. Using Newton’s second law ($F = ma_y$):
$$a_y = \frac{qE}{m} = \frac{(1.6 \times 10^{-19})(100)}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}} \approx 1.756 \times 10^{13}\text{ m/s}^2$$

**3. Determine the time ($t$) to travel the length of the plates:**
Since there is no horizontal force, the horizontal velocity is constant:
$$t = \frac{L}{v_x} = \frac{0.25}{3500} \approx 7.143 \times 10^{-5}\text{ s}$$

**4. Determine the vertical displacement ($y$):**
The electron starts at $y = 0$ (halfway) and moves towards the positive plate. Using $y = \frac{1}{2}a_y t^2$:
$$y = 0.5 \times (1.756 \times 10^{13}) \times (7.143 \times 10^{-5})^2 \approx 0.0448\text{ m}$$
Since the plates are separated by $0.2\text{ m}$, the halfway point is $0.1\text{ m}$ from the positive plate. Because $0.0448 < 0.1$, the electron hits the positive plate before it exits the length of the plates.

**5. Find the distance ($x$) along the plate where it impacts:**
We need the time $t_{impact}$ required to travel a vertical distance of $y = 0.1\text{ m}$:
$$0.1 = \frac{1}{2}a_y t_{impact}^2 \implies t_{impact} = \sqrt{\frac{0.2}{1.756 \times 10^{13}}} \approx 1.067 \times 10^{-7}\text{ s}$$
