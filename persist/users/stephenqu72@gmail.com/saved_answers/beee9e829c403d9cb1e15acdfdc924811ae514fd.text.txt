### Explanation
To determine the correct statement, let’s evaluate each option based on the physics of an AC induction motor:

*   **A. It has no moving parts:** This is incorrect. An induction motor consists of a stationary part called the *stator* and a rotating part called the *rotor*. The rotor is designed to spin, so it definitely has moving parts.
*   **B. It utilises a rotating magnetic field:** This is **correct**. In an induction motor, an AC supply is connected to the stator windings, which are arranged to create a magnetic field that rotates at a constant speed (known as the synchronous speed). This rotating magnetic field induces eddy currents in the rotor, leading to the motor's operation via Lenz's Law and the motor effect.
*   **C. It is the only type of motor using AC current:** This is incorrect. There are other types of AC motors, such as universal motors and synchronous motors, which also operate using AC electricity.
*   **D. It does not have a current-carrying conductor:** This is incorrect. The stator windings are conductors carrying AC, and the rotor (which is often a "squirrel cage" design) contains conductor bars in which currents are induced. The interaction between these currents and the magnetic field is what produces the torque.
