To find the age of the solar system using radioactive decay, we use the standard radioactive decay formula:

$$N(t) = N_0 \cdot 2^{-\frac{t}{T_{1/2}}}$$

Where:
*   $N(t)$ is the amount of parent material remaining.
*   $N_0$ is the initial amount of parent material.
*   $t$ is the time elapsed (the age of the sample).
*   $T_{1/2}$ is the half-life ($710 \times 10^6$ years, which is equivalent to $0.71 \times 10^9$ years).

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Determine the remaining fraction of Uranium-235:**
    The problem states that $99.609375\%$ of the Uranium has transmuted into Lead.
    Therefore, the remaining amount $N(t)$ is:
    $$N(t) = 100\% - 99.609375\% = 0.390625\%$$
    As a decimal, this is $0.00390625$.

2.  **Relate the fraction to the decay equation:**
    We want to find $t$ such that:
    $$\frac{N(t)}{N_0} = 0.00390625$$
    $$0.00390625 = \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n$$
    where $n$ is the number of half-lives passed ($n = \frac{t}{T_{1/2}}$).

3.  **Solve for $n$:**
    Notice that $0.00390625 = \frac{1}{256}$.
    Since $2^8 = 256$, then:
    $$\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^8 = \frac{1}{256} = 0.00390625$$
    So, $n = 8$ half-lives have passed.

4.  **Calculate the age $t$:**
    $$t = n \times T_{1/2}$$
    $$t = 8 \times 710 \times 10^6 \text{ years}$$
    $$t = 5680 \times 10^6 \text{ years}$$
    $$t = 5.680 \text{ billion years}$$
