### Front
What is the nuclear reaction process for beta-minus ($\beta^-$) decay, and how is it verified?

### Back
- **Key idea:** A neutron converts into a proton, an electron ($\beta^-$ particle), and an electron antineutrino.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Conservation of Mass Number ($A$) and Atomic Number ($Z$). Equation: $^A_Z X \rightarrow ^A_{Z+1} Y + ^0_{-1}e + \bar{\nu}_e$
- **When to use it:** When a nucleus has an excess of neutrons and you need to determine the daughter product.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that the atomic number $Z$ increases by 1 while the mass number $A$ remains constant.
