### Front
What is the function of a moderator in a nuclear fission reactor?

### Back
- Key idea: Moderators slow down "fast" neutrons into "thermal" (slow) neutrons via elastic collisions to increase the probability of inducing subsequent U-235 fission.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When asked to explain the mechanism of a self-sustaining chain reaction.
- Common trap: Stating the moderator "absorbs" neutrons; it must be clarified that the moderator *decelerates* neutrons, whereas *control rods* are used to *absorb* them.
