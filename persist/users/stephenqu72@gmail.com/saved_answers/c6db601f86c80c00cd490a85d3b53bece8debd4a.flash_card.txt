### Front
Which of Kepler’s Laws explains why a planet sweeps out equal areas in equal time intervals?

### Back
- Key idea: Orbital velocity varies; planets move faster at perihelion and slower at aphelion to cover equal area.
- Formula / law / constant: Kepler’s Second Law (Law of Equal Areas).
- When to use it: To determine or compare the time taken for an object to travel between two points in an elliptical orbit given area sectors.
- Common trap: Assuming orbital speed is constant; it is only constant in a perfectly circular orbit.
