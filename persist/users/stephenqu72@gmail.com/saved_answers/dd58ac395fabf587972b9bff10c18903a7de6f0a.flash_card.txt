### Front
Distinguish between Reliability, Validity, and Accuracy when evaluating an experimental procedure.

### Back
- **Key idea**: 
    - **Reliability**: Consistency of results (achieved via repetition/averaging to reduce random error).
    - **Validity**: Does the experiment measure what it intends to? (achieved by controlling variables to ensure a fair test).
    - **Accuracy**: Closeness to the true value (achieved by reducing systematic error and uncertainty).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure**: No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it**: When evaluating experimental designs, analyzing error sources, or comparing two different methods of measurement in HSC practical questions.
- **Common trap**: Confusing reliability with accuracy; repeating trials makes data more *reliable* (reduces random error), but if your equipment is poorly calibrated, the result remains *inaccurate* (systematic error).
