### Explanation
This is a classic demonstration of the **Relativity of Simultaneity**, a core concept in Einstein’s Special Theory of Relativity. To solve this, we must consider the perspective of the scientist on Earth (the stationary observer) relative to the motion of the capsule.

1.  **Reference Frames:** For an observer inside the space capsule, the light from the laser travels the same distance to both $P$ and $Q$ at the same speed ($c$). Therefore, within the capsule's frame of reference, the hatches open simultaneously.
2.  **The Scientist's Perspective:** The scientist on Earth observes the capsule moving to the right at a speed of $0.7c$. 
3.  **Light Path:** As the light travels toward the hatches, the capsule is also moving.
    *   Hatch $Q$ is moving **away** from the light source.
    *   Hatch $P$ is moving **towards** the light source.
4.  **Conclusion:** Because the speed of light is invariant (it is constant at $c$ for all observers regardless of their motion), the scientist sees the light reach hatch $P$ first because $P$ is "rushing" to meet the light beam, while the light has to "catch up" to the receding hatch $Q$.

Thus, according to the scientist on Earth, $P$ opens before $Q$.
