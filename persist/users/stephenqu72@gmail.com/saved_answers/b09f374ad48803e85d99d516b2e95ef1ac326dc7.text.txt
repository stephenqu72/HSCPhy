To solve this problem, we need to understand the relationship between the force on the current-carrying wires and the resulting torque on the motor's armature.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

1.  **Identify the force acting on the armature sides:**
    The problem states that each side perpendicular to the magnetic field experiences a force of $F = 0.275\text{ N}$. In a square loop motor, there are two sides perpendicular to the field that contribute to the torque (one side with current flowing into the page and one with current flowing out).

2.  **Determine the number of turns:**
    The coil consists of $n = 50$ turns. Since the force is given for "the sides," we must consider that the total force contributing to the torque is the force per side multiplied by the number of turns:
    $$F_{\text{total}} = 50 \times 0.275\text{ N} = 13.75\text{ N}$$

3.  **Use the Torque Formula:**
    Torque ($\tau$) is defined as the force multiplied by the lever arm distance ($d$):
    $$\tau = F_{\text{total}} \times d$$
    In a DC motor with radial magnets, the wires are effectively at a distance $r$ from the axis of rotation, so the total lever arm for the two sides is the width of the coil, $w = 2r$. However, we can also use the formula for torque on a coil: $\tau = nIBw \cdot l$.
    
    Given that $F = nBIl$, we know the force on one side of the coil is $F = 0.275\text{ N}$. The torque exerted by one side is $F \times r$, where $r$ is the distance from the axis of rotation to the side. 
    Since the two sides provide torque in the same rotational direction, the total torque is:
    $$\tau = 2 \times (F \times r) = F \times (2r) = F \times w$$
    Wait—we are not given the width ($w$). Let's look at the parameters: $F = 0.275\text{ N}$, $B = 0.35\text{ T}$, $I = 0.25\text{ A}$, $n = 50$.
    Using $F = nBIl \implies 0.275 = 50 \times 0.35 \times 0.25 \times l$:
    $$0.275 = 4.375 \times l \implies l = \frac{0.275}{4.375} \approx 0.0628\text{ m}$$
    
    Now, calculate the torque:
    $$\tau = nIBw \times l$$
    Since the loop is square, $w = l = 0.0628\text{ m}$.
    $$\tau = (n \times B \times I \times l) \times w$$
    $$\tau = F \times w = 0.275 \times 0.0628 \approx 0.01727\text{ Nm}$$

4.  **Conclusion:**
    Rounding to the appropriate significant figures, we get $0.017\text{ Nm}$.

The correct option is **(B)**.
