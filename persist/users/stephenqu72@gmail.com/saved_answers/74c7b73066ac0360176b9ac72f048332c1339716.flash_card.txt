### Front
How do you calculate the linear distance ($s$) travelled by a point on the rim of a rotating object given its radius ($r$) and total angular displacement ($\theta$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Linear distance is the arc length traced by the radius through a given angle.
- Formula: $s = r\theta$ (where $\theta$ is in radians).
- When to use it: To convert rotational kinematics (angular displacement) into linear displacement.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert degrees to radians if the angle is not provided in radians.
