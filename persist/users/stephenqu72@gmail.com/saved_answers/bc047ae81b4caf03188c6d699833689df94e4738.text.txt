To solve this question, we must systematically use the properties of subatomic particles to filter and identify them. We focus on three key criteria: charge (interaction with fields) and relative mass.

### How to solve:
1.  **Interaction with Electric/Magnetic Fields:** This is the most efficient first step. Charged particles (electrons and protons) are deflected by electric and magnetic fields, while neutral particles (neutrons) are not.
2.  **Direction of Deflection:** Once identified as charged, we differentiate by the direction of deflection. Electrons ($q = -e$) and protons ($q = +e$) move in opposite directions in an electric field.
3.  **Mass differentiation:** Use mass as a secondary confirmation. Protons are much more massive than electrons ($m_p \approx 1836 m_e$).

---

### Suggested Flow Chart

**Step 1: Pass particles through an electric field.**
*   **Result A:** Particle travels straight (no deflection).
    *   **Conclusion:** **Neutron** (Neutral charge).
*   **Result B:** Particle is deflected.
    *   *Proceed to Step 2.*

**Step 2: Determine direction of deflection in the electric field.**
*   **Deflected towards the negative plate:**
    *   **Conclusion:** **Proton** (Positive charge). *Mass feature: Large mass ($ \approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$).*
*   **Deflected towards the positive plate:**
    *   **Conclusion:** **Electron** (Negative charge). *Mass feature: Very small mass ($ \approx 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$).*

**Step 3: Confirm with Magnetic Field.**
*   Apply the Right-Hand Rule (for positive) or Left-Hand Rule (for negative). The force is $F = qvB\sin(\theta)$. Neutrons remain undeflected. Electrons and protons will deflect in opposite directions based on their opposite charges.
