### Front
How do you calculate the remaining mass of a radioactive sample at a specific time when given the half-life?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Radioactive decay is an exponential process where mass reduces by half every half-life ($t_{1/2}$).
- **Formula:** $N_t = N_0 \times (0.5)^n$, where $n = \frac{\text{total time}}{t_{1/2}}$.
- **When to use it:** To determine the remaining quantity or elapsed time in radioactive decay problems.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units (e.g., minutes to hours) before calculating the number of half-lives ($n$).
