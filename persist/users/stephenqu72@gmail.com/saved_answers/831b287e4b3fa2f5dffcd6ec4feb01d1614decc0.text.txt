### Explanation

To solve this, we must understand the definition of magnetic permeability ($\mu$). 

Magnetic permeability is a physical property that describes how a material responds to an external magnetic field. Specifically, it represents the material's ability to support the formation of a magnetic field within itself. 

1.  **Definition:** When a material is placed in an external magnetic field ($H$), it becomes magnetized. The resulting magnetic flux density ($B$) inside the material is given by the relationship:
    $$B = \mu H$$
    Where $\mu$ is the magnetic permeability of the material. Therefore, $\mu$ is effectively the ratio that measures how strongly the material contributes to the magnetic field strength inside it compared to a vacuum.

2.  **Evaluating the choices:**
    *   **(A)** is incorrect because the force between two current-carrying wires is determined by the magnetic field generated by the currents, and while $\mu_0$ (the permeability of free space) appears in the formula $F/L = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$, the constant itself is not the "measurement of force."
    *   **(B) is correct** as it accurately describes permeability as a measure of how the material reacts to (and supports) a magnetic field when placed within an external field.
    *   **(C)** is incorrect; while electromagnetism relates current and magnetism, permeability is a property of the material's magnetic response, not a description of the current flow itself.
    *   **(D)** is incorrect because "electromagnetic resistance" is not a standard term used to define permeability.

**Teacher's Tip:** Remember that $\mu_0$ is a universal constant (permeability of free space), while $\mu$ (without the subscript) refers to the permeability of a specific medium. Both characterize the degree to which a region or substance can be magnetized.
