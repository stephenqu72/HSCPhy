### Front
How do you calculate the shift in observed frequency when a sound source moves towards or away from a stationary observer?

### Back
- Key idea: The Doppler Effect (frequency changes due to relative motion).
- Formula: $f_{obs} = f_s \left( \frac{v}{v \mp v_s} \right)$
- When to use it: When a sound source is moving relative to a stationary observer.
- Common trap: Using the wrong sign in the denominator. Use **minus (−)** when the source moves **towards** (frequency increases) and **plus (+)** when moving **away** (frequency decreases).
