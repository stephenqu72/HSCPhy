### Front
How do you calculate the energy released in a nuclear fission reaction?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Energy released corresponds to the mass defect ($\Delta m$) between reactants and products, converted via Einstein's mass-energy equivalence.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $E = \Delta m c^2$ and $1 \, u \approx 931.5 \, \text{MeV}$.
- **When to use it:** Whenever calculating the energy yield of a nuclear reaction given isotopic or particle masses.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to include the mass of all emitted neutrons in the products (e.g., the 3 neutrons in a standard $^{235}\text{U}$ fission).
