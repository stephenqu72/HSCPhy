### Front
How does the orbital radius of a satellite change if its orbital period is doubled?

### Back
- Key idea: Kepler’s Third Law ($\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \text{constant}$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\frac{r_1^3}{T_1^2} = \frac{r_2^3}{T_2^2} \rightarrow r_2 = r_1 \sqrt[3]{(\frac{T_2}{T_1})^2}$.
- When to use it: To relate the orbital periods and radii of two objects orbiting the same central body.
- Common trap: Confusing **orbital radius** ($r$, measured from the planet's centre) with **altitude** ($h$, measured from the planet's surface). Remember: $r = h + R_{earth}$.
