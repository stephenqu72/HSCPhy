To determine the polarity of the charges, we must look at the direction of the electric field lines:

1.  **Fundamental Principle:** By convention, electric field lines always originate from a **positive** charge and terminate on a **negative** charge.
2.  **Analyze Charge L:** Observe the arrows attached to the lines connected to charge L. The arrows are pointing **towards** the center of the circle L. Because the field lines terminate at the charge, L must be **negative**.
3.  **Analyze Charge R:** Observe the arrows attached to the lines connected to charge R. The arrows are pointing **towards** the center of the circle R. Because the field lines also terminate at this charge, R must also be **negative**.
4.  **Conclusion:** Since the electric field lines point inward toward both L and R, both charges are negative.

Comparing this to the given options:
A) Incorrect: Field lines would point away from both.
B) **Correct**: Field lines point toward both negative charges.
C) Incorrect: Field lines would go from R to L.
D) Incorrect: Field lines would go from L to R.

The correct answer is **B**.
