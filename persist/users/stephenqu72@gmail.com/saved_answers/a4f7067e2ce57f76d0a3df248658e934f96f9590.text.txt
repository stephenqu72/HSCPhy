### Explanation
To solve this problem, we need to identify the directions of two vector quantities:

1.  **Orbital Velocity ($v$):** For an object in circular motion, the velocity vector is always directed **tangent** to the path of the orbit at the point where the object is located. Given the satellite is on the left side of the Earth, moving in a counter-clockwise direction (which is standard for such diagrams), the tangent at this position points **downwards** ($\downarrow$).
2.  **Centripetal Acceleration ($a_c$):** By definition, centripetal acceleration is the acceleration directed toward the center of the circular path. Since the Earth is at the center of the orbit and the satellite is to the left of the Earth, the acceleration vector points horizontally toward the center of the Earth, which is to the **right** ($\rightarrow$).

Comparing these directions to the table:
- Orbital Velocity: $\downarrow$
- Acceleration: $\rightarrow$

This corresponds exactly to row **C**.
