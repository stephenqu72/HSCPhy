### Front
How do you calculate the tangential speed of an object moving in a horizontal circle when provided with the tension ($T$) of the string?

### Back
- Key idea: The tension provides the centripetal force ($F_c$) required for circular motion.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $T = \frac{mv^2}{r} \implies v = \sqrt{\frac{Tr}{m}}$
- When to use it: Any horizontal circular motion problem involving tension and speed.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert mass ($m$) into standard SI units (kg) or using the wrong radius.
