### Answer
The correct statement is **D: The direction of the net force is perpendicular to the wall.**

### Explanation
To solve this, we must consider the forces acting on a person in the Rotor when the floor is removed:

1.  **Vertical forces:** The person is not moving vertically (they are not sliding down the wall). Therefore, the net vertical force is zero. Gravity ($F_g = mg$) acts downwards, and the frictional force ($f$) from the wall acts upwards. These two forces cancel each other out ($f = mg$).
2.  **Horizontal forces:** The person is undergoing uniform circular motion. According to Newton’s Second Law, an object in circular motion must have a net force acting toward the center of the circular path. This is known as the **centripetal force** ($F_c$).
3.  **The Net Force:** Since the vertical forces cancel out, the only remaining force that is not balanced is the normal force ($N$) exerted by the wall on the person. This normal force points directly toward the center of the drum (i.e., perpendicular to the wall). 

Because the net vertical force is zero, the total **net force** acting on the person is equivalent to the centripetal force. Therefore, the net force is directed horizontally toward the center of the drum, which is perpendicular to the wall.
