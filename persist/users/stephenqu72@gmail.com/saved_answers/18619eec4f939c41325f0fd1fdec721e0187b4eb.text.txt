### Answer
The correct answer is **A. $1500\text{ N}$**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must apply **Newton’s First Law of Motion**, which states that an object will maintain a constant velocity (zero acceleration) unless acted upon by a net external force.

1.  **Analyze the Motion:** The problem states that the aeroplane is flying in a "horizontal straight line at a constant speed". This indicates that the aeroplane is in a state of **equilibrium**.
2.  **Apply Newton's Second Law:** For any object in equilibrium, the net force acting on it in any direction must be zero. Mathematically, $\sum F = ma$. Since the velocity is constant, the acceleration $a$ is $0\text{ m s}^{-2}$. Therefore:
    $$\sum F = m \times 0 = 0\text{ N}$$
3.  **Horizontal Forces:** In the horizontal direction, there are two primary forces acting on the aeroplane: the forward horizontal thrust ($T$) and the horizontal resistance (drag/air resistance, $R$). 
4.  **Balance the Forces:**
    $$\sum F_{horizontal} = T - R = 0$$
    $$T = R$$
5.  **Conclusion:** Since the resistance force is given as $1500\text{ N}$, the forward horizontal thrust must be exactly equal to $1500\text{ N}$ to ensure the net force is zero and the speed remains constant.
