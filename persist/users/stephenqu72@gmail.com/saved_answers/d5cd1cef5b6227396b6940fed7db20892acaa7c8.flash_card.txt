### Front
In a system moving at a constant speed along an incline, what is the relationship between the parallel component of gravity and the frictional force?

### Back
- Key idea: Since velocity is constant, acceleration is zero; therefore, the net force along the slope is zero (equilibrium).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\sum F = 0$ (Newton’s First Law); $F_f = mg \sin(\theta)$.
- When to use it: To calculate friction or air resistance for an object moving at a constant velocity on a slope.
- Common trap: Forgetting to use $\sin(\theta)$ (parallel component) instead of $\cos(\theta)$ (perpendicular component).
