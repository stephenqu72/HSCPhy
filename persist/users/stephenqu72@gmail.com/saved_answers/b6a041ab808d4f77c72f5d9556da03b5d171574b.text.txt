To solve this problem, we must analyze the independent components of projectile motion.

### Explanation

In projectile motion with negligible air resistance, horizontal motion and vertical motion are independent of each other.

1.  **Time of Flight ($t$):** 
    The vertical motion is governed by gravity ($g$). When a stone is thrown horizontally, its initial vertical velocity ($u_y$) is $0$. Using the displacement formula for vertical motion:
    $$h = u_y t + \frac{1}{2}g t^2$$
    Since $u_y = 0$, this simplifies to:
    $$h = \frac{1}{2}g t^2 \implies t = \sqrt{\frac{2h}{g}}$$
    The time of flight depends only on the height of the cliff ($h$) and the acceleration due to gravity ($g$). It is **independent** of the horizontal initial velocity. Therefore, even if the speed is tripled to $3v$, the time of flight remains $t$.

2.  **Range ($R$):**
    The horizontal range is the distance covered horizontally at a constant velocity during the time of flight:
    $$R = v \times t$$
    When the initial horizontal velocity is changed from $v$ to $3v$, and the time of flight ($t$) remains the same, the new range ($R'$) becomes:
    $$R' = (3v) \times t = 3(v \times t) = 3R$$

Comparing these results to the options provided, the time of flight is $t$ and the range is $3R$. This corresponds to option (C).

### Correct Answer
