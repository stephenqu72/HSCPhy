### Answer

**(a) Outline a practical investigation to observe the emission spectra of gas discharge tubes.**
To observe the emission spectra:
1. Place a gas discharge tube (e.g., hydrogen or neon) into a power supply unit and switch it on to produce light.
2. Use a diffraction grating or a hand-held spectroscope to view the light emitted from the tube.
3. Observe the light as it passes through the grating, which acts to disperse the light into its constituent wavelengths, forming a series of discrete colored lines (the emission spectrum).

**(b) Identify ONE potential hazard associated with performing gas discharge tube investigations, and outline ONE safe work practice which addresses this hazard.**
*   **Hazard:** The gas discharge tube requires a high-voltage power supply to operate, which poses a significant risk of electric shock.
*   **Safe work practice:** Ensure the power supply is turned off before touching or replacing the discharge tube, and ensure the power supply unit is properly grounded and that all cables are insulated and intact.
