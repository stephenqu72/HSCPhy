### Answer

**1. Newton’s Particle Model (Corpuscular Theory)**
Newton proposed that light consists of a stream of tiny, massless particles called "corpuscles" emitted by light sources. He argued that these particles travel in straight lines, explaining shadow formation and reflection (as an elastic collision with a surface). He believed light travelled faster in denser media to account for refraction.

**2. Huygens’ Wave Model**
Huygens proposed that light is a longitudinal wave propagating through an all-encompassing medium called the "luminiferous aether." He used the principle that every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary spherical wavelets to explain reflection and refraction. He correctly predicted that light would travel slower in denser media.

**3. Experimental Evidence (Two examples)**
*   **Young’s Double Slit Experiment (Evidence for the Wave Model):** In 1801, Thomas Young passed light through two closely spaced narrow slits. The resulting interference pattern of bright and dark fringes on a screen could not be explained by particles colliding. It provided definitive evidence for the wave nature of light, as it demonstrated diffraction and constructive/destructive interference.
*   **Foucault’s Speed of Light Measurement (Evidence for the Wave Model):** In 1850, Léon Foucault measured the speed of light in water and found it to be slower than in air. This directly contradicted Newton’s corpuscular theory (which required a higher speed in denser media) and provided strong support for Huygens' wave model.

### Teacher's Guide: How to solve this
*   **Structure:** This is an 8-mark question, which suggests a balanced distribution: 2 marks for Newton, 2 marks for Huygens, and 4 marks for the experimental evidence (2 marks per experiment).
*   **Be Specific:** When describing the models, explicitly state the mechanism (particles vs. waves). 
*   **Connect to Evidence:** Ensure you explain *why* the experiment supports the model. For Young’s experiment, mention "interference patterns" or "diffraction." For Foucault, explicitly mention the speed comparison between air and water.
*   **Use Precise Terminology:** Keywords like "corpuscles," "luminiferous aether," "interference," and "refraction" are essential for full marks in an HSC-level response.
