To answer this question effectively, you need to apply Kepler’s Second Law and the principle of Conservation of Energy (or Conservation of Angular Momentum) to an elliptical orbit.

### Answer
As the satellite moves away from point $P$ (apogee), it loses kinetic energy and gains gravitational potential energy, causing its velocity to **decrease** until it reaches the furthest point. As it then moves towards the perigee (the point closest to Earth), it loses gravitational potential energy and gains kinetic energy, causing its velocity to **increase**. The velocity reaches its minimum at the apogee (point $P$) and its maximum at the perigee.

### Explanation
To secure full marks (3 marks), your response should cover these three logical points:

1.  **Identify the nature of the orbit:** Point $P$ represents the **apogee**, the point in the orbit furthest from the Earth. Because the orbit is highly elliptical, the satellite’s distance from the center of mass of the Earth changes significantly.
2.  **Apply Conservation of Energy:** The total mechanical energy of the satellite remains constant ($E = K + U$). As the satellite moves from $P$ toward perigee, its distance $r$ from Earth decreases. As $r$ decreases, the gravitational potential energy ($U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$) becomes more negative (decreases). To keep the total energy constant, the kinetic energy ($K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$) must increase, leading to an increase in velocity.
3.  **Apply Kepler’s Second Law:** Kepler’s Second Law states that a line segment joining a planet and the Sun (or satellite and Earth) sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time. For this to occur, the satellite must travel faster when it is closer to the Earth (perigee) and slower when it is further away (apogee at point $P$).

**Summary for your exam response:**
*   **At $P$ (apogee):** The satellite is at its maximum distance from Earth and has its minimum velocity.
*   **Moving toward perigee:** Gravitational potential energy is converted into kinetic energy, causing the velocity to increase.
*   **At perigee:** The satellite is at its minimum distance from Earth and has its maximum velocity.
