### Answer
The correct statement is **D. The components must not move relative to each other.**

### Explanation
To determine the correct answer, we must analyze the purpose of the Michelson-Morley experiment. The experiment was designed to detect the "aether wind"—the relative motion of the Earth through the hypothetical luminiferous aether.

1.  **The Scientific Principle:** The apparatus is an interferometer. It works by splitting a single beam of monochromatic light into two perpendicular paths, reflecting them off mirrors, and recombining them at the detector to produce an interference pattern. 
2.  **The Objective:** If the Earth were moving through an aether, the speed of light relative to the apparatus would be different in the direction of the "aether wind" versus perpendicular to it. This would cause a phase shift in the interference pattern as the entire apparatus was rotated.
3.  **Why D is critical:** The entire premise of the experiment relies on detecting a change in the interference pattern caused *only* by the Earth's motion through the aether. If the individual components (mirrors or the beam splitter) were moving relative to each other due to mechanical vibration, thermal expansion, or instability, the interference pattern would shift randomly. This would introduce "noise" that would make it impossible to distinguish between a change caused by the aether and a change caused by the instability of the apparatus itself. Therefore, the rigidity and stability of the apparatus are the fundamental requirements for the experiment to produce valid data.

**Why the others are incorrect:**
*   **A:** While usually true for convenience, it is not the *critical* basis; one could theoretically adjust for unequal intensities.
*   **B:** Mirrors 1 and 2 do not need to be identical; they just need to provide reliable reflections for their respective paths.
*   **C:** While the experiment often uses arms of equal length (or calibrated lengths), the critical requirement is the *stability* of these distances during rotation, not that they are equal by design. The test relies on *changes* in speed, not equality of path length.
