To determine the correct answer, we must look at the nuclear reactions involved in the Carbon-Nitrogen-Oxygen (CNO) cycle.

### Answer
The correct answer is **D. neutrino**.

### Explanation
The net process of the CNO cycle is the fusion of four protons ($^1\text{H}$) into one helium nucleus ($^4\text{He}$). The net reaction is given by:
$$4^1\text{H} \rightarrow ^4\text{He} + 2e^+ + 2\nu_e + \text{energy}$$

Where:
*   $4^1\text{H}$ are the four protons.
*   $^4\text{He}$ is the helium nucleus (alpha particle).
*   $2e^+$ are two positrons.
*   $2\nu_e$ are two electron neutrinos.

In the intermediate steps of the CNO cycle, protons are converted into neutrons (or vice versa) via beta-plus decay. In these beta-plus decay processes, a proton is converted into a neutron, emitting a positron ($e^+$) and an electron neutrino ($\nu_e$). While positrons are also produced, they quickly annihilate with electrons in the stellar core, turning into gamma-ray photons. The neutrinos, however, are fundamental particles that interact very weakly with matter and escape the star, carrying away a portion of the energy produced in the reaction. Among the options provided, the neutrino is the key fundamental particle emitted as a byproduct of this fusion process.

*   **A. Quark:** Quarks are constituents of protons and neutrons, not emitted during fusion.
*   **B. Electron:** Positrons are emitted, not electrons.
*   **C. Hydrogen atom:** This is a reactant, not a product.
