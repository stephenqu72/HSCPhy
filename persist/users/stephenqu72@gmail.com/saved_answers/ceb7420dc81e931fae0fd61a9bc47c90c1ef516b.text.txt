To solve this problem, we use Snell's Law and the relationship between the refractive index of a medium and the speed of light in that medium.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. Identify the given information:**
*   Angle of incidence ($i$) = $55^\circ$
*   Speed of light in vacuum ($c$) = $3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ ms}^{-1}$
*   Speed of blue light in glass ($v_b$) = $2.71 \times 10^8 \text{ ms}^{-1}$
*   Speed of red light in glass ($v_r$) = $2.78 \times 10^8 \text{ ms}^{-1}$

**2. Relate refractive index to speed:**
The refractive index ($n$) of a medium is given by $n = \frac{c}{v}$. 
According to Snell's Law: $n_1 \sin(i) = n_2 \sin(r)$.
Since the light is entering from air (assume $n_1 \approx 1$), the equation simplifies to:
$$\sin(r) = \frac{\sin(i)}{n} = \frac{\sin(i)}{c/v} = \frac{v \sin(i)}{c}$$

**3. Calculate the angle of refraction for each ray:**

*   **For the Blue Ray:**
    $$\sin(r_b) = \frac{2.71 \times 10^8 \times \sin(55^\circ)}{3.00 \times 10^8}$$
    $$\sin(r_b) = 0.9033 \times 0.81915 \approx 0.7400$$
    $$r_b = \arcsin(0.7400) \approx 47.73^\circ$$

*   **For the Red Ray:**
    $$\sin(r_r) = \frac{2.78 \times 10^8 \times \sin(55^\circ)}{3.00 \times 10^8}$$
    $$\sin(r_r) = 0.9267 \times 0.81915 \approx 0.7591$$
    $$r_r = \arcsin(0.7591) \approx 49.39^\circ$$

**4. Calculate the angular separation:**
The angular separation ($\Delta r$) is the difference between the two angles:
$$\Delta r = r_r - r_b$$
$$\Delta r = 49.39^\circ - 47.73^\circ = 1.66^\circ$$

**Answer:**
The angular separation between the red and blue rays is approximately **$1.66^\circ$**.
