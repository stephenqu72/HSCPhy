### (a) Calculation of Distance
**Answer:**
The time taken for the signal is $84 - 77 = 7 \text{ seconds}$.
Using the speed of light $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$:
$$d = v \times t = (3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}) \times (7 \text{ s}) = 2.10 \times 10^9 \text{ meters}$$

**Method:**
Identify that radio signals (GPS) travel at the speed of light. Calculate the time interval, then use the formula $d = vt$.

### (b) Determination of Orbital Altitude
**Answer:**
1. Period $T = \frac{1}{2} \text{ day} = 12 \text{ hours} = 43,200 \text{ seconds}$.
2. Use Kepler’s Third Law: $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$.
3. $r^3 = \frac{GM T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24}) \times (43200)^2}{4\pi^2} \approx 7.54 \times 10^{22} \text{ m}^3$.
4. $r \approx 2.66 \times 10^7 \text{ m}$.
5. Altitude $h = r - R_{Earth} \approx 2.66 \times 10^7 - 6.38 \times 10^6 \approx 2.02 \times 10^7 \text{ m}$ (or $\sim 20,200 \text{ km}$).

**Method:**
Apply Kepler’s Third Law for circular orbits. Ensure the period is in SI units (seconds) and remember that $r$ is the distance from the center of the Earth, so you must subtract the Earth’s radius to find the *altitude*.

### (c) Relativistic Effects
**Answer:**
GPS requires extreme timing precision because a tiny error in time translates to a massive error in position ($d = ct$). Two relativistic effects must be accounted for:
1. **Special Relativity (Time Dilation):** Satellites move at high velocities relative to the receiver, causing their clocks to tick slower by approximately $7 \mu s/day$.
2. **General Relativity (Gravitational Time Dilation):** Satellites are further from the Earth’s gravitational well (lower potential) than the receiver. According to GR, clocks closer to a mass tick slower, so the satellite clocks tick *faster* by approximately $45 \mu s/day$.
3. **Net Effect:** The combined effect results in satellite clocks running approximately $38 \mu s$ faster per day. Without adjusting the onboard clocks, the positional error would accumulate to kilometers per day. Engineers "pre-compensate" the clocks to account for this frequency shift.

**Method:**
Structure your answer by distinguishing between the two theories. Mention the velocity effect (Special Relativity) and the gravitational potential effect (General Relativity), and conclude with the necessity of synchronization to maintain locational accuracy.
