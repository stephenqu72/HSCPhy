To solve this problem, we need to calculate the work done to move a proton in a uniform electric field.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Potential difference between plates ($V_{total}$) = $240\text{ V}$
*   Distance between plates ($d_{total}$) = $102\text{ cm} = 1.02\text{ m}$
*   Distance moved by the proton ($\Delta d$) = $0.47\text{ m}$
*   Charge of a proton ($q$) = $1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$

**2. Calculate the Electric Field strength ($E$):**
In a parallel plate arrangement, the electric field is uniform and given by:
$$E = \frac{V_{total}}{d_{total}}$$
$$E = \frac{240\text{ V}}{1.02\text{ m}} \approx 235.29\text{ V/m}$$

**3. Calculate the potential difference ($\Delta V$) over the distance moved:**
The potential difference experienced by the proton over the distance $\Delta d$ is:
$$\Delta V = E \times \Delta d$$
$$\Delta V = 235.29\text{ V/m} \times 0.47\text{ m} \approx 110.59\text{ V}$$

**4. Calculate the work done ($W$):**
The work done on a charge moving through a potential difference is given by:
$$W = q \times \Delta V$$
$$W = (1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times 110.59\text{ V}$$
$$W \approx 1.77 \times 10^{-17}\text{ J}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches option (C).

**Correct Option: (C)**
