### ANSWER AND EXPLANATION

To solve this problem, we use Einstein's photoelectric equation:
$$E_k = hf - \Phi$$
where $E_k$ is the maximum kinetic energy, $h$ is Planck's constant, $f$ is the frequency, and $\Phi$ is the work function. This is in the form of a linear equation $y = mx + c$, where $y = E_k$, $x = f$, the gradient $m = h$, and the $y$-intercept $c = -\Phi$.

#### (a) Graphing
To plot the graph, place the frequency values on the horizontal axis and kinetic energy values on the vertical axis.
*   Plot the points: $(1.49, 7.30), (1.00, 3.97), (0.76, 2.33), (0.59, 1.34), (0.50, 0.66), (0.43, 0.23)$.
*   Draw a line of best fit through these points. The line should be straight, extending to the horizontal axis.

#### (b) Determine the work function ($\Phi$)
The work function is the magnitude of the $y$-intercept of your graph.
1.  Extend the line of best fit back to the $f$-axis (the $x$-intercept, $f_0$).
2.  Based on the data, the threshold frequency $f_0$ is approximately $0.40 \times 10^{15} \text{ Hz}$.
3.  Calculate $\Phi = h \times f_0$. Using $h \approx 4.14 \times 10^{-15} \text{ eV}\cdot\text{s}$:
    $$\Phi = 4.14 \times 10^{-15} \times 0.40 \times 10^{15} \approx 1.66 \text{ eV}$$
*(Note: Students should read the specific intercept from their own drawn line.)*

#### (c) Determine Planck's constant ($h$)
Planck's constant is the gradient ($m$) of the graph.
1.  Pick two points $(f_1, E_{k1})$ and $(f_2, E_{k2})$ on your line of best fit.
2.  Calculate the gradient: $m = \frac{\Delta E_k}{\Delta f}$.
3.  Using the provided data points:
    $$m = \frac{7.30 \times 10^{-19} - 0.23 \times 10^{-19}}{1.49 \times 10^{15} - 0.43 \times 10^{15}} = \frac{7.07 \times 10^{-19}}{1.06 \times 10^{15}} \approx 6.67 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$$
The value obtained should be close to the accepted value of $6.63 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$.
