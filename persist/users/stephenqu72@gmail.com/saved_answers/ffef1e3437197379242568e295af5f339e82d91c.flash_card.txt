### Front
State the principle and formula used to calculate the observed frequency change of a wave when the source moves relative to a stationary observer.

### Back
- Key idea: The **Doppler Effect**—the compression (moving towards) or rarefaction (moving away) of wavefronts.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $f_{obs} = f_s \left( \frac{v_w}{v_w \mp v_s} \right)$
- When to use it: Whenever a wave source is moving relative to a stationary observer.
- Common trap: Using the wrong sign in the denominator; remember that moving *towards* the observer reduces the denominator (creating a higher frequency).
