### Front
What is the definition of escape velocity in terms of mechanical energy?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The minimum initial velocity required to reach infinity with zero kinetic energy; the total mechanical energy must equal zero.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Conservation of Mechanical Energy: $K + U = 0 \implies \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{GmM}{r} = 0$
- **When to use it:** To determine the velocity needed to overcome a gravitational potential well regardless of direction or atmospheric conditions.
- **Common trap:** Assuming escape velocity is affected by atmospheric drag or that the object must have remaining kinetic energy at infinity.
