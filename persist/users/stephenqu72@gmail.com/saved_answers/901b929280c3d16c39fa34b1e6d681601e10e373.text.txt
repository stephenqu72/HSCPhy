To determine the correct statement, we must apply **Lenz's Law**, which states that the direction of an induced current is such that it creates a magnetic field that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

1.  **Analyze the Change in Flux:** As the North pole of the magnet moves upwards towards the horizontal coil, the magnetic flux passing through the coil increases. Specifically, the magnetic field lines directed upwards (out of the North pole) are increasing in density within the coil's area.
2.  **Determine the Opposing Magnetic Field:** According to Lenz's Law, the coil must "oppose" this increase. To resist the approaching North pole, the coil must create its own magnetic field that points downwards (to repel the North pole). 
3.  **Apply the Right-Hand Grip Rule:** Imagine grabbing the coil with your right hand such that your thumb points in the direction of the induced magnetic field (downwards, away from the magnet). Your curled fingers will point in the direction of the induced current.
4.  **Determine the Direction:** When looking from **above** the coil, your fingers will curl in an **anti-clockwise** direction to generate a magnetic field that points downwards through the center of the coil.

Therefore, when viewed from above, the induced current in the coil flows anti-clockwise to create an "induced North pole" on the underside of the coil, which repels the approaching North pole of the magnet.
