### 1. Analysis of the Modifications

**Modification A: Frequency is doubled**
*   The original waveform completes one full cycle in $20\text{ ms}$.
*   Frequency $f$ is related to period $T$ by $f = \frac{1}{T}$. 
*   If the frequency is doubled, the period must be halved.
*   The new period becomes $\frac{20\text{ ms}}{2} = 10\text{ ms}$. This means the generator will now complete two full cycles within the original $20\text{ ms}$ timeframe.

**Modification B: Split-ring commutator is added**
*   Slip rings produce an AC output (alternating current that swings between positive and negative).
*   A split-ring commutator acts as a mechanical rectifier. It reverses the connection to the external circuit every half-turn. This forces the negative portion of the sine wave to become positive. 
*   Therefore, the resulting output will be a "pulsating DC" waveform where all peaks are positive.
