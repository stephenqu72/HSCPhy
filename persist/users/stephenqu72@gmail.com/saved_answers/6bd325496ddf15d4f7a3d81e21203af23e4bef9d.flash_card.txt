### Front
How do you calculate the acceleration ($a$) of an object sliding on a frictionless inclined plane tilted at angle $\theta$?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Acceleration is the component of gravity acting parallel to the surface.
- **Formula:** $a = g \sin(\theta)$
- **When to use it:** Whenever an object moves on an incline and you need to resolve gravity into parallel and perpendicular components.
- **Common trap:** Using $\cos(\theta)$ instead of $\sin(\theta)$. Remember: $\sin$ for parallel (down the slope), $\cos$ for perpendicular (normal to the surface).
