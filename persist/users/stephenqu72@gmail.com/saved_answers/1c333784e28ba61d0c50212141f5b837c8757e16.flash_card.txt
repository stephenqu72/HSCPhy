### Front
In a uniform circular motion experiment with constant mass ($m$) and radius ($r$), what is the relationship between centripetal force ($F$) and velocity ($v$), and how is it represented graphically?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Centripetal force is directly proportional to the square of the velocity ($F \propto v^2$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $F = \frac{mv^2}{r}$. A graph of $F$ vs $v$ produces a parabola; $F$ vs $v^2$ produces a straight line.
- **When to use it:** When analyzing circular motion data to identify variables vs. constants or linearizing data for a line of best fit.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the parabolic curve of $F$ vs $v$ with a linear relationship; $F$ is not directly proportional to $v$, but to $v^2$.
