To determine the correct answer, we must analyze the energy transformations of the tennis ball as it moves from the bottom to the top of a vertical circle.

### Analysis
1.  **Energy Change:** As the ball moves from the bottom (lowest height) to the top (highest height) of its path, its gravitational potential energy ($U = mgh$) increases significantly. Even if the speed of the ball were constant, its mechanical energy would change due to the change in height. Therefore, the total mechanical energy of the ball is **not constant**.
2.  **Work-Energy Principle:** The Work-Energy Theorem states that the work done on an object is equal to the change in its mechanical energy ($\Delta E = W$). Since the potential energy of the ball increases as it moves to the top, the net work done on the ball must be non-zero. The tension in the string and gravity are forces acting on the ball; as it rises, work is being done on (or by) the system to change its potential energy.

### Answer
The correct option is **A**.

### Explanation
When an object moves vertically against gravity, it gains gravitational potential energy. Since $E_{total} = K + U$, and $U$ increases as the ball rises, the total mechanical energy is changing. According to the Work-Energy Theorem, a change in the mechanical energy of a system requires work to be done. Thus, the energy of the ball changes, and work is done on the ball.
