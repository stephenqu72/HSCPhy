To solve this physics problem, we apply the **Right-Hand Grip Rule** to determine the direction of the magnetic field generated by a current-carrying wire.

### How to solve it:
1. **Identify the Rule:** The Right-Hand Grip Rule states that if you point the thumb of your right hand in the direction of the conventional current ($I$), your curled fingers will indicate the direction of the magnetic field lines ($B$) encircling the wire.
2. **Apply the Rule:** Since the current is flowing to the **right**, point your right thumb to the right along the wire. Your fingers will curl "up" out of the page above the wire and "down" into the page below the wire.
3. **Represent the Field:** In a 2D diagram, we represent this as:
   - **Above the wire:** Draw circular loops or directional arrows pointing upwards and looping back around. To represent the 3D nature, we often draw concentric circles.
   - **Notation:** A standard way to show this is by using dots ($\cdot$) to represent the field coming out of the page (above the wire) and crosses ($\times$) to represent the field going into the page (below the wire).

### The Solution:
On your diagram, you should draw **concentric circles** centered on the wire. Place arrows on these circles pointing **counter-clockwise** (when viewed from the front/side) to indicate the direction of the magnetic field.

*   **Top half of circles:** Arrows should point to the left (coming out toward you).
*   **Bottom half of circles:** Arrows should point to the right (going into the page).
*   **Result:** You are effectively drawing circles that go *up and out* from above the wire and *down and into* the page below the wire.
