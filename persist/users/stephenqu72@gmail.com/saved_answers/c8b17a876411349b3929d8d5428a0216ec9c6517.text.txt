To determine the radius of curvature of the electron's path, we use the principle that the magnetic force acts as a centripetal force for a charged particle moving perpendicularly to a magnetic field.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
The magnetic force $F_B$ on a charged particle is given by:
$$F_B = qvB$$
Since this force causes the electron to travel in a circle, it must equal the centripetal force $F_c$:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Equating the two:
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Solving for the radius $r$:
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

### 2. Identify the Variables
*   Mass of an electron ($m$): $9.11 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$
*   Velocity ($v$): $1225\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Charge of an electron ($q$): $1.60 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
*   Magnetic field strength ($B$): $10.0\text{ mT} = 10.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ T} = 0.010\text{ T}$

### 3. Calculation
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}) \times (1225\text{ m s}^{-1})}{(1.60 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (0.010\text{ T})}$$
$$r = \frac{1.115975 \times 10^{-27}}{1.6 \times 10^{-21}}$$
$$r \approx 6.9748 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m}$$
