### Front
What defines an inertial frame of reference, and which of the following is an example: (a) a bus at constant velocity, or (b) a car turning a corner at constant speed?

### Back
- Key idea: A frame where Newton’s First Law holds; the frame must have zero acceleration ($a = 0$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s First Law of Motion ($a = 0$).
- When to use it: To determine if an observer’s perspective is valid for applying standard Newtonian mechanics without fictitious forces.
- Common trap: Assuming constant *speed* implies an inertial frame; if the *direction* changes (e.g., turning a corner), there is centripetal acceleration, making it a non-inertial frame. Answer: (a).
