### Solution and Explanation

#### Part a: Free Body Diagram
To represent the forces on one of the balls:
1. **Tension ($T$):** Draw an arrow along the string pointing diagonally upward and inward towards the ceiling.
2. **Gravitational force ($F_g$):** Draw an arrow pointing vertically downward from the center of the ball.
3. **Electrostatic force ($F_E$):** Draw an arrow pointing horizontally away from the other ball (outward).

---

#### Part b: Calculating Tension
The ball is in equilibrium, so the vertical components of the forces must sum to zero. 
The angle between the two strings is $60^\circ$, so the angle between one string and the vertical is $\theta = 30^\circ$.

The vertical component of the tension force ($T \cos \theta$) must balance the gravitational force ($F_g = mg$).
- Mass $m = 4.0\text{ g} = 0.004\text{ kg}$
- $g = 9.8\text{ N kg}^{-1}$
- $\theta = 30^\circ$

$$T \cos(30^\circ) = mg$$
$$T = \frac{mg}{\cos(30^\circ)}$$
$$T = \frac{0.004 \times 9.8}{\cos(30^\circ)}$$
$$T = \frac{0.0392}{0.8660} \approx 4.529 \times 10^{-2}\text{ N}$$
Rounding to two significant figures, we get $T = 4.5 \times 10^{-2}\text{ N}$.

---

#### Part c: Calculating Electrostatic Force
The horizontal component of the tension force ($T \sin \theta$) must balance the electrostatic force ($F_E$):

$$F_E = T \sin(30^\circ)$$
Using $T \approx 0.04529\text{ N}$:
$$F_E = 0.04529 \times \sin(30^\circ)$$
$$F_E = 0.04529 \times 0.5$$
$$F_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{-2}\text{ N}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, the magnitude of the electrostatic force is $2.3 \times 10^{-2}\text{ N}$.
