### Front
Derive the expression for the total mechanical energy ($E$) of a satellite of mass $m$ in a stable circular orbit around a central mass $M$ at radius $r$.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Total energy is the sum of kinetic energy ($K$) and gravitational potential energy ($U$). For circular orbits, $K = -\frac{1}{2}U$.
- **Formula:** $E = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$
- **When to use it:** To calculate the total orbital energy, the energy required to change orbits, or the binding energy of a satellite.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting the negative sign; the negative sign indicates the satellite is in a bound state. Also, confusing $r$ with the altitude above the Earth's surface ($r = R_E + h$).
