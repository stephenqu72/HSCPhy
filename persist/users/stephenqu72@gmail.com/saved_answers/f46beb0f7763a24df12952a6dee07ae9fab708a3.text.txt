To find the effect on the magnitude of the force between two parallel conductors, we must use the standard formula for the force per unit length $F/L$ between two parallel current-carrying wires:

$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$

Where:
- $\mu_0$ is the permeability of free space.
- $I_1$ and $I_2$ are the currents in the two conductors.
- $r$ is the separation distance between the conductors.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1. **Identify the initial state:** Let the initial force be $F_{initial} \propto \frac{I_1 \cdot I_2}{r}$.
2. **Apply the changes:**
   - Both currents are halved: $I_1 \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}I_1$ and $I_2 \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}I_2$.
   - The separation distance is halved: $r \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}r$.
3. **Substitute these into the proportionality:**
   $$F_{final} \propto \frac{(\frac{1}{2}I_1) \cdot (\frac{1}{2}I_2)}{\frac{1}{2}r}$$
4. **Simplify the expression:**
   $$F_{final} \propto \frac{\frac{1}{4} (I_1 \cdot I_2)}{\frac{1}{2} r}$$
   $$F_{final} \propto \frac{1}{4} \cdot 2 \cdot \frac{I_1 \cdot I_2}{r}$$
   $$F_{final} \propto \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{I_1 \cdot I_2}{r}$$
5. **Conclusion:** The new force is $\frac{1}{2}$ of the original force. Therefore, the magnitude will be halved.
