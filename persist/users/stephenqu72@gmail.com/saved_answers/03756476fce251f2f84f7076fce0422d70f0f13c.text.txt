### Explanation:

1.  **Identify the physical phenomenon:** As the coil rotates in the magnetic field, the sides of the coil cut through the magnetic flux lines. This induces an electromotive force (EMF) across the coil.
2.  **Determine the direction of the force on charges:** We use the right-hand palm rule for a positive charge moving in a magnetic field:
    *   Point your **fingers** in the direction of the magnetic field (from North to South, i.e., left to right).
    *   Point your **thumb** in the direction of the velocity of the wire (the left side of the coil is moving "up" as it rotates clockwise).
    *   Your **palm** (or the direction of the force on a positive charge) points towards the back of the coil for the left side, or more simply, we can look at the induced current direction.
    *   Alternatively, using the Right-Hand Rule: For the left side of the coil, the velocity is upward ($\vec{v}$) and the field is directed to the right ($\vec{B}$). The Lorentz force $\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$ on a positive charge is directed into the page/towards the back.
3.  **Analyze the accumulation:** Even though there is no "complete circuit" to allow for a steady current, the induced EMF acts as a battery. It creates a potential difference across the ends of the wire. Positive charges will be pushed by the magnetic force toward one end of the coil and accumulate there until the electric field created by the separation of charge balances the magnetic force (at which point, the net force is zero). 
4.  **Conclusion:** Based on the clockwise rotation and the orientation of the magnetic field, the left side of the coil moving upward will cause positive charges to accumulate at the end connected to terminal A.
