### Front
How do you calculate the orbital period of one planet given its radius and the orbital data of another planet?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kepler’s Third Law states that the square of the orbital period is directly proportional to the cube of the orbital radius ($T^2 \propto r^3$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\frac{T_1^2}{r_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{r_2^3}$
- **When to use it:** When comparing the orbital periods and radii of two objects orbiting the same central mass.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert partial orbits into the full period ($T$) before substituting into the formula, or failing to cube the radius ratio while squaring the period ratio.
