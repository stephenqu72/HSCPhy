### Front
How do you resolve projectile motion problems in the NSW HSC Physics course?

### Back
- Key idea: Treat horizontal and vertical motion as independent components.
- Formula / law / constant: Horizontal: $v_x = \text{constant}$; Vertical: $v_y = u_y + at$ and $s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2}at^2$ (where $a = g \approx 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$).
- When to use it: Any 2D motion problem involving objects moving under the influence of gravity (ignoring air resistance).
- Common trap: Assuming the horizontal velocity changes or forgetting that the initial vertical velocity ($u_y$) is $0\text{ m s}^{-1}$ for purely horizontal launches.
