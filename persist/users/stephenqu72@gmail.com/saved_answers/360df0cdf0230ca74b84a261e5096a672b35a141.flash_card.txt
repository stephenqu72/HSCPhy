### Front
What is the primary purpose of a split-ring commutator in a DC generator?

### Back
- Key idea: Rectification; it reverses the external circuit connections every half-rotation to ensure the output current flows in only one direction (DC).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Faraday’s Law of Induction ($\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$).
- When to use it: Whenever a question asks how a DC generator produces a unidirectional output from an AC-inducing coil.
- Common trap: Thinking the commutator "smooths" the current or increases the output voltage; it only rectifies the direction.
