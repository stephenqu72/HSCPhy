To solve this HSC physics problem, we use the formula for Young’s Double Slit Experiment, which relates the wavelength ($\lambda$), the slit separation ($d$), the fringe separation ($\Delta x$), and the distance to the screen ($L$).

### 1. The Diagram
When drawing the diagram, ensure you label:
*   The two slits separated by distance $d = 300\text{ }\mu\text{m}$.
*   The distance from the slits to the screen, $L = 4.3\text{ m}$.
*   The fringe pattern on the screen, indicating the distance between the central maximum ($m=0$) and the first bright fringe ($m=1$) as $\Delta x = 8\text{ mm}$.

### 2. Calculation
**Step 1: Identify and convert variables to SI units.**
*   Slit separation $d = 300\text{ }\mu\text{m} = 300 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m} = 3 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m}$
*   Distance to screen $L = 4.3\text{ m}$
*   Fringe separation $\Delta x = 8\text{ mm} = 8 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$

**Step 2: Use the fringe separation formula.**
The formula for the position of bright fringes is $\Delta x = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$. We rearrange this to solve for $\lambda$:
$$\lambda = \frac{\Delta x \cdot d}{L}$$

**Step 3: Substitute the values.**
$$\lambda = \frac{(8 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}) \times (3 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m})}{4.3\text{ m}}$$
$$\lambda = \frac{24 \times 10^{-7}}{4.3}\text{ m}$$
$$\lambda \approx 5.58 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m}$$

**Step 4: Convert to nanometers (optional but standard for light).**
$$\lambda \approx 558\text{ nm}$$

**Final Answer:** The wavelength of the laser is approximately $5.58 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m}$ or $558\text{ nm}$.
