### Front
What happens during the annihilation of an electron and a positron, and what is the total energy produced?

### Back
- Key idea: Mass-energy equivalence; total rest mass is converted into two photons.
- Formula / law / constant: $E = mc^2$; Rest mass energy of $e^-/e^+ \approx 0.511 \text{ MeV}$; Total energy $\approx 1.022 \text{ MeV}$.
- When to use it: Low-energy annihilation problems where initial kinetic energy is negligible.
- Common trap: Forgetting to add the rest mass energy of *both* particles (must sum $e^-$ and $e^+$).
