### Physics Analysis
1.  **Initial Conditions:** The projectile is launched horizontally. This means at $t = 0$, the initial vertical velocity component is $v_{y0} = 0$.
2.  **Vertical Motion:** Once the projectile is in the air, the only force acting on it (ignoring air resistance) is gravity. According to Newton's Second Law, this produces a constant downward acceleration, $a_y = g$.
3.  **Equation of Motion:** The vertical velocity $v_y$ at any time $t$ is given by the formula:
    $$v_y(t) = v_{y0} + a_y t$$
    Substituting our values:
    $$v_y(t) = 0 + gt = gt$$
4.  **Graph Interpretation:** Since $g$ (acceleration due to gravity) is a positive constant, the relationship between vertical velocity $v_y$ and time $t$ is a linear function $v_y = gt$. This is a straight line passing through the origin $(0,0)$ with a positive slope equal to $g$.
