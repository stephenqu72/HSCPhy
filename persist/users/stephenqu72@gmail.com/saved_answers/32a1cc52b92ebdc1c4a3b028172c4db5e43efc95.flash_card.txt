### Front
Why does the mechanical energy of a tennis ball moving in a vertical circle change, and how is this related to work?

### Back
- Key idea: Mechanical energy ($E = K + U$) is not conserved because gravitational potential energy ($U = mgh$) increases as the ball rises.
- Formula / law: Work-Energy Theorem ($\Delta E = W$).
- When to use it: When analyzing energy transformations in vertical circular motion or any path with changing height.
- Common trap: Assuming mechanical energy is conserved because the string tension acts perpendicular to motion; remember that gravity does negative work as the ball rises, causing a change in mechanical energy.
