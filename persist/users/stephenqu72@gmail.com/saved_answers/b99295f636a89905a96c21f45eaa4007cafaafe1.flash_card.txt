### Front
Derive the expression for escape velocity ($v_e$) from the surface of a planet.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Conservation of energy ($E_{surface} = E_{\infty} = 0$). The kinetic energy at the surface must overcome the total gravitational potential energy.
- **Formula:** $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$
- **When to use it:** To calculate the minimum velocity required for an object to escape a gravitational field and reach infinity with zero kinetic energy.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting the negative sign in the gravitational potential energy formula ($U = -GMm/r$) or failing to realize that $m$ (the projectile's mass) cancels out.
