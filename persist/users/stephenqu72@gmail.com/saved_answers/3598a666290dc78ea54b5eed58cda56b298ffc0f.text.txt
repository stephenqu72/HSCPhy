To solve this problem, we must relate the required centripetal acceleration to the rotational period of the spacecraft.

### Answer
To simulate Earth's gravity ($g = 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$) with a rotation period of at least $60 \text{ s}$, the radius of the spacecraft must be at least $894 \text{ meters}$.

---

### How to solve it
**1. Understand the physics:**
"Artificial gravity" is created by the centripetal acceleration required to keep the astronauts moving in a circle. The formula for centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) is:
$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$$
Since the tangential velocity $v$ relates to the period $T$ and radius $r$ by $v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$, we can substitute this into the acceleration formula:
$$a_c = \frac{(\frac{2\pi r}{T})^2}{r} = \frac{4\pi^2 r^2}{T^2 r} = \frac{4\pi^2 r}{T^2}$$

**2. Rearrange for the required variable ($r$):**
We are given $a_c = 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$ and the constraint $T \geq 60 \text{ s}$. To find the minimum radius, we use the minimum allowed period ($T = 60 \text{ s}$):
$$r = \frac{a_c T^2}{4\pi^2}$$

**3. Substitute the values:**
$$r = \frac{9.8 \times (60)^2}{4 \times \pi^2}$$
$$r = \frac{9.8 \times 3600}{39.478}$$
$$r \approx \frac{35280}{39.478} \approx 893.66 \text{ m}$$

**Conclusion:** 
The spacecraft requires a radius of at least $894 \text{ meters}$ (measured from the center of rotation to the floor of the crew quarters) to achieve an artificial gravity of $9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$ while maintaining a rotation period of at least $60 \text{ seconds}$.
