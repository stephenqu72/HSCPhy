To solve this physics problem, we need to identify the three fundamental types of spectra taught in the HSC Physics syllabus:

1.  **Emission Spectrum (Source I):** This spectrum shows distinct, bright lines on a dark background. This occurs when an excited gas emits specific discrete wavelengths of light due to electron transitions between quantized energy levels. This identifies **Source I as a gas discharge tube**.
2.  **Absorption Spectrum (Source II):** This spectrum shows a continuous range of colors (the rainbow) interrupted by dark lines. These dark lines occur when light from a continuous source passes through a cooler gas, which absorbs specific wavelengths. This is characteristic of **Source II (Sunlight)**, where the cooler outer layers of the Sun's atmosphere absorb specific frequencies from the continuous emission of the hotter core.
3.  **Continuous Spectrum (Source III):** This spectrum shows a continuous, uninterrupted band of color. This is produced by hot, dense objects (blackbody radiation), where atoms are so closely packed that their energy levels overlap, creating a smooth distribution of wavelengths. This identifies **Source III as an incandescent filament** (like an old-fashioned light bulb).

### Explanation Summary:
*   **Emission spectra** (bright lines) come from low-density gases (gas discharge tubes).
*   **Absorption spectra** (dark lines in a continuous background) come from light passing through a cooler gas (Sunlight).
*   **Continuous spectra** (smooth rainbow) come from hot, dense solids (incandescent filaments).
