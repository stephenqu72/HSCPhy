1.  **The Motor Effect:** The force $F$ on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field is given by $F = BIl \sin(\theta)$. For the motor to rotate continuously, the forces acting on the conductors in the armature must produce a **net torque** that consistently turns the rotor in the same direction.
2.  **Magnetic Field Orientation:** By placing the north poles diagonally opposite each other and the south poles diagonally opposite each other (a four-pole configuration), the magnetic flux is directed radially inward and outward towards the armature. 
3.  **Consistency of Torque:** If you were to place a north pole directly opposite a south pole in a four-pole setup, the field lines would interact in a way that creates opposing torques on different sides of the armature. By alternating the poles (N-S-N-S), the motor ensures that as the armature conductors pass under each pole, the current direction (controlled by the commutator) interacts with the magnetic field to consistently produce a force that pushes the armature in the same direction of rotation.
