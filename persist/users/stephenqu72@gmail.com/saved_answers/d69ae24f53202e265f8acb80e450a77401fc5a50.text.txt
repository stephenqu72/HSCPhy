### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Define the Process of Nuclear Fusion:** Nuclear fusion is a nuclear reaction where two light atomic nuclei (small nuclei) combine to form a heavier, more stable nucleus. Looking at the options, rows (C) and (D) correctly describe this process. Rows (A) and (B) describe nuclear *fission* (splitting a large nucleus).

2.  **Analyze the Change in Mass:** In nuclear fusion, the mass of the resulting nucleus is slightly *less* than the sum of the masses of the individual initial nuclei. This missing mass is known as the "mass defect" ($\Delta m$).

3.  **Apply Einstein’s Mass-Energy Equivalence:** According to Albert Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle, defined by the equation:
    $$E = \Delta m c^2$$
    where $E$ is the energy released, $\Delta m$ is the mass defect, and $c$ is the speed of light. The "missing" mass is converted into a significant amount of energy (typically released as kinetic energy of the products or gamma radiation). Because mass is converted into energy, the total mass of the particles **decreases**.

4.  **Conclusion:** We are looking for the row that identifies the process as "two small nuclei combining" and the change in mass as "decreases."
