### Explanation

To solve this question, you need to apply your knowledge of wave properties from the HSC Physics syllabus regarding the nature of light and electromagnetic radiation:

1.  **Nature of the Wave:** Polarization is a property exclusive to **transverse waves**. In transverse waves, the displacement of the medium (or the oscillation of the electric/magnetic fields in the case of light) is perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation. Longitudinal waves (like sound waves) oscillate parallel to the direction of propagation and cannot be polarized. This immediately allows you to eliminate options (B) and (D).
2.  **Definition of Polarization:** Unpolarized waves (such as light from a standard bulb) have oscillations occurring in many different planes perpendicular to the direction of propagation. A polarizing filter acts as a "gate" that restricts the oscillations of the wave to a single plane.
3.  **Conclusion:** Once the wave is passed through a polarizer, the oscillations are restricted to a single orientation. This process is defined as polarizing the wave, which matches the description in option (A): "Polarisation occurs when a transverse wave is allowed to vibrate in only one direction."

By identifying that only transverse waves can be polarized and that the process restricts oscillation to a single plane, (A) is the only scientifically accurate statement.
