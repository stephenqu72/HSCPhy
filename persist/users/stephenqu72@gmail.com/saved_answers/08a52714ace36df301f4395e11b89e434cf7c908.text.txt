To determine the wavelength of the laser light, we use the diffraction grating formula:

$$d \sin(\theta) = n\lambda$$

### 1. Identify the given values
*   **Grating density:** $600\text{ lines/mm} = 600,000\text{ lines/m}$.
    The slit separation $d$ is:
    $$d = \frac{1}{600,000} \approx 1.667 \times 10^{-6}\text{ m}$$
*   **Distance to screen ($L$):** $2.0\text{ m}$
*   **Position of first-order fringe ($x$):** $0.65\text{ m}$ (at $n = 1$)

### 2. Calculate the angle ($\theta$)
Using trigonometry for the right-angled triangle formed by the grating, the central fringe, and the first-order fringe:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{x}{L} = \frac{0.65}{2.0} = 0.325$$
$$\theta = \arctan(0.325) \approx 18.00^\circ$$

### 3. Calculate the wavelength ($\lambda$)
Using $n = 1$ for the first-order fringe:
$$\lambda = \frac{d \sin(\theta)}{n}$$
$$\lambda = (1.667 \times 10^{-6}) \times \sin(18.00^\circ) / 1$$
$$\lambda \approx 1.667 \times 10^{-6} \times 0.309$$
$$\lambda \approx 5.15 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m}$$

Converting to nanometers ($1\text{ nm} = 10^{-9}\text{ m}$):
$$\lambda \approx 515\text{ nm}$$

**Final Answer:** The wavelength of the laser light is approximately $515\text{ nm}$.

2.  **Find the angle ($\theta$):** The pattern shows the first-order maximum ($n=1$) at $0.65\text{ m}$. We use the geometry of the setup to find the angle of diffraction using $\tan(\theta) = \text{opposite}/\text{adjacent}$.
