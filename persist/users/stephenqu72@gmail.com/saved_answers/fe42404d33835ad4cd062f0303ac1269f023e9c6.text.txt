In the context of the Scientific Method, a **hypothesis** is defined as a proposed, tentative explanation for a phenomenon, observation, or problem. Because it is tentative, it must be testable through experimentation and observation. 

Here is why the other options are incorrect:
*   **(B)** describes a **scientific theory** or a **conclusion** derived from experiments, as it implies the explanation has already been verified and supported by evidence.
*   **(C)** describes a **scientific theory** or **scientific law**, which represent well-established, widely accepted explanations or descriptions of natural phenomena based on a massive body of accumulated evidence.
*   **(D)** is incorrect because a hypothesis is not defined by its mathematical correctness; it is defined by its ability to be tested empirically. Mathematical models can be used to support a hypothesis, but a hypothesis itself is a conceptual framework that awaits experimental validation.

To solve this, always remember the distinction in science: 
1.  **Hypothesis:** An "educated guess" or tentative explanation requiring testing.
2.  **Theory:** A well-substantiated explanation of some aspect of the natural world that has been repeatedly tested and confirmed.
