### Answer
The de Broglie wavelength is given by the equation:
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$
where:
*   $h$ is Planck's constant ($\approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$)
*   $m$ is the mass of the object (in kg)
*   $v$ is the velocity of the object (in m/s)

**Example calculation:**
Consider a cricket ball with a mass $m = 0.16 \text{ kg}$ moving at a velocity $v = 30 \text{ m/s}$.
$$\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{0.16 \times 30}$$
$$\lambda \approx 1.38 \times 10^{-34} \text{ m}$$

**Conclusion:**
This wavelength is unimaginably small—far smaller than the diameter of an atomic nucleus ($\approx 10^{-15} \text{ m}$). Because the wavelength is effectively zero compared to the dimensions of the object, wave properties such as diffraction or interference cannot be observed. Therefore, everyday objects behave purely according to classical mechanics.
