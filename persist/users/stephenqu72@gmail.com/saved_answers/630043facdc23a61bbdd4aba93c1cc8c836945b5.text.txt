To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of Circular Motion and the Conservation of Mechanical Energy.

### Step 1: Analyze the condition for "weightlessness"
At the top of the loop, the forces acting on the passenger are gravity ($mg$) acting downwards and the normal force ($N$) from the seat acting downwards. The centripetal force is provided by the sum of these forces:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} = N + mg$$
"Weightlessness" occurs when the normal force $N = 0$. In this state, the only force providing the centripetal acceleration is gravity. Therefore:
$$\frac{mv^2}{r} = mg$$
$$v^2 = rg$$

### Step 2: Use the Law of Conservation of Energy
Since there is no friction, the total mechanical energy at the start (Point A) is equal to the total mechanical energy at the top of the loop (Point B). Let the top of the loop be the reference level for potential energy ($h_B = 0$). Then the height at the start is $h_A = d$.

**At the start (Point A):**
- $v_A = 0$ (negligible speed)
- $E_{total, A} = mgh_A = mgd$

**At the top of the loop (Point B):**
- $v_B^2 = rg$
- $E_{total, B} = \frac{1}{2}mv_B^2 + mgh_B = \frac{1}{2}m(rg) + 0 = \frac{1}{2}mrg$

Equating the energies:
$$mgd = \frac{1}{2}mrg$$
$$d = \frac{r}{2}$$

### Step 3: Calculate the value
Given the radius $r = 8.0\text{ m}$:
$$d = \frac{8.0\text{ m}}{2} = 4.0\text{ m}$$

Comparing this to the provided options, the correct answer is **(A)**.
