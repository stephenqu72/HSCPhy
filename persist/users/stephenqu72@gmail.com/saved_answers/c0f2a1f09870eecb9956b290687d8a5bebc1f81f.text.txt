### Answer

#### 1. Geiger-Marsden (Gold Foil) Experiment
*   **Procedure:** A thin sheet of gold foil was bombarded with a stream of alpha particles ($\alpha$-particles) in a vacuum. A movable fluorescent screen (zinc sulfide) was used to detect the scattered particles.
*   **Observations:** Most $\alpha$-particles passed straight through, but a small fraction were deflected at large angles, and some even bounced directly backward.
*   **Significance:** This contradicted the "Plum Pudding" model. It proved that the atom is mostly empty space with a tiny, dense, positively charged **nucleus** at the center.

#### 2. ChadwickŌĆÖs Experiment
*   **Procedure:** Chadwick bombarded a beryllium target with $\alpha$-particles. This produced a highly penetrating, neutral radiation. He then allowed this radiation to strike a paraffin wax target, which ejected protons.
*   **Observations:** By measuring the velocities of the ejected protons and applying the conservation of momentum and energy, he calculated the mass of the neutral particles.
*   **Significance:** The mass was found to be approximately equal to that of a proton. This confirmed the existence of the **neutron**, solving the discrepancy between atomic number and atomic mass.
