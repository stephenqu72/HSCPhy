To determine which graph represents the relationship between the speed $v$ and the radius $r$ of an electron moving in a uniform magnetic field, we must apply the principles of circular motion and electromagnetism.

### Analysis and Solution

1.  **Identify the Forces:** When an electron moves perpendicularly through a uniform magnetic field, it experiences a magnetic Lorentz force. This force acts as a centripetal force, keeping the electron in a circular path. The magnitude of the magnetic force is given by:
    $$F_B = qvB$$
    where $q$ is the charge of the electron, $v$ is the velocity, and $B$ is the magnetic field strength.

2.  **Relate to Circular Motion:** The centripetal force required to maintain a circular path of radius $r$ is:
    $$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
    where $m$ is the mass of the electron.

3.  **Equate the Forces:** Since the magnetic force provides the centripetal force:
    $$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

4.  **Derive the Relationship:** We want to find the relationship between $v$ and $r$. Solving for $r$:
    $$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$
    Alternatively, solving for $v$:
    $$v = \left( \frac{qB}{m} \right) r$$

5.  **Conclusion:** In this equation, $q$ (charge), $B$ (magnetic field strength), and $m$ (mass) are all constants. Therefore, the velocity $v$ is directly proportional to the radius $r$ ($v \propto r$). 

A direct proportionality $v = kr$ (where $k$ is the constant $\frac{qB}{m}$) is represented by a **straight line passing through the origin**.

Looking at the options provided:
*   **Graph A** shows a linear relationship passing through the origin, which matches our derived equation $v \propto r$.

Therefore, the correct graph is **A**.
