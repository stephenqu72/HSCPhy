To solve this problem, we need to relate the linear speed of the car to the angular speed of its wheels.

### 1. Identify the given values
*   **Diameter ($d$):** $0.400\text{ m}$
*   **Radius ($r$):** The radius is half the diameter, so $r = \frac{0.400\text{ m}}{2} = 0.200\text{ m}$
*   **Linear speed ($v$):** $108\text{ km h}^{-1}$

### 2. Convert units to SI (meters and seconds)
Physics calculations in the HSC require standard SI units. We must convert $\text{km h}^{-1}$ to $\text{m s}^{-1}$:
$$v = 108 \times \frac{1000\text{ m}}{3600\text{ s}} = \frac{108}{3.6}\text{ m s}^{-1} = 30\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

### 3. Use the relationship between linear and angular speed
The formula relating linear speed ($v$) and angular speed ($\omega$) for a wheel rolling without slipping is:
$$v = r\omega$$

Rearranging the formula to solve for $\omega$:
$$\omega = \frac{v}{r}$$

### 4. Calculation
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$\omega = \frac{30\text{ m s}^{-1}}{0.200\text{ m}}$$
$$\omega = 150\text{ rad s}^{-1}$$

Comparing this result to the given options:
(A) $75\text{ rad s}^{-1}$
(B) $150\text{ rad s}^{-1}$
(C) $270\text{ rad s}^{-1}$
(D) $540\text{ rad s}^{-1}$

**The correct option is (B).**

### How to solve this type of problem:
1.  **Check your units:** Always ensure the linear speed is in $\text{m s}^{-1}$ and the distance (radius) is in meters. If the speed is in $\text{km h}^{-1}$, divide by $3.6$ to get $\text{m s}^{-1}$.
2.  **Radius vs. Diameter:** Read the question carefully to see if you are given the radius or the diameter. Always divide the diameter by $2$ to find the radius before using the formula $v = r\omega$.
3.  **Apply the formula:** Remember that the point of contact between the tire and the road has an instantaneous velocity equal to the car's velocity, allowing the use of $v = r\omega$.
