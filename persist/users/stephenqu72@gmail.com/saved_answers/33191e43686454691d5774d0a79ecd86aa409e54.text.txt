To secure full marks for this 9-mark HSC Physics response, you must move beyond simply describing the models. The verb **"Analyse"** requires you to identify the limitations of each model and explain how the subsequent scientist addressed those shortcomings, building a cohesive narrative of scientific progress.

### Sample Response

The atomic model evolved as scientists identified limitations in existing theories, leading to a progression that reconciled classical physics with quantum observations.

**Rutherford’s Nuclear Model:** Following the 1909 Geiger-Marsden experiment, Rutherford proposed that the atom consisted of a dense, positively charged nucleus surrounded by electrons. While this explained the deflection of alpha particles, it possessed a fatal flaw: according to classical electromagnetism, an accelerating charged electron should continuously emit radiation, lose energy, and spiral into the nucleus. This predicted an unstable atom, contradicting the observed stability of matter.

**Bohr’s Postulates:** Bohr resolved Rutherford's paradox by applying Planck’s quantum hypothesis. He proposed that electrons exist in discrete "stationary" energy states where they do not radiate energy. Transitions between these states occur via the absorption or emission of photons with energy $E = hf$. Bohr’s model successfully explained the spectral lines of hydrogen (Rydberg formula). However, it remained "semi-classical," lacking a theoretical justification for why orbits were quantized and failing to explain the intensity of spectral lines or the behavior of multi-electron atoms.

**De Broglie’s Hypothesis:** De Broglie transformed the field by proposing wave-particle duality, where matter particles possess a wavelength $\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$. This provided the physical basis for Bohr’s quantization. If an electron is a standing wave confined in a circular orbit, only orbits with a circumference equal to an integer multiple of the wavelength ($2\pi r = n\lambda$) can exist. This explained *why* energy levels are discrete: they represent stable standing wave patterns. This conceptual leap moved physics away from "planetary" orbits toward the modern quantum mechanical model, where electrons are defined by probability density functions (orbitals) rather than fixed paths.

### How to solve this question:

1.  **Structure the Argument:** Use a chronological approach. For each physicist, follow this pattern: **Key contribution** $\rightarrow$ **Scientific limitation identified** $\rightarrow$ **Transition to the next model**.
2.  **Focus on the "Why":** For 9 marks, simple facts (e.g., "Bohr had orbits") are insufficient. You must explain the *necessity* of the change. For example, explain *why* Rutherford's atom was unstable and *how* Bohr’s quantization solved the spectral problem.
3.  **Include Physics Principles:** Use key equations to demonstrate depth. Reference $E = hf$ for Bohr and $\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$ for de Broglie. Connecting these equations to the physical models is what differentiates a top-tier response.
4.  **Use Terminology:** Ensure you mention "stability," "quantization," "standing waves," "discrete energy levels," and "classical electromagnetism."
