### Front
What is the primary mechanism of radioactive decay occurring within the CNO cycle?

### Back
- Key idea: Beta-plus decay ($e^+$ emission).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $^{A}_{Z}X \rightarrow ^{A}_{Z-1}Y + e^+ + \nu_e$
- When to use it: To identify the process converting proton-rich intermediate nuclei (e.g., $^{13}N$, $^{15}O$) into more stable isotopes.
- Common trap: Confusing the proton capture steps (which emit gamma radiation) with the radioactive decay steps (which emit positrons/neutrinos).
