### Front
How do the graphical characteristics of projectile motion (ignoring air resistance) differ between horizontal and vertical components?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Horizontal motion is constant velocity; vertical motion is constant acceleration.
- **Formula / Law:** 
  - Horizontal: $v_x = \text{constant}$, $\Delta x = v_x t$
  - Vertical: $a_y = -g$, $v_y = u_y - gt$, $\Delta y = u_y t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$
- **When to use it:** To identify or sketch motion graphs ($x, v, a$ vs. $t$) for projectiles.
- **Common trap:** Assuming vertical velocity is zero at the start or that horizontal velocity changes due to gravity.
