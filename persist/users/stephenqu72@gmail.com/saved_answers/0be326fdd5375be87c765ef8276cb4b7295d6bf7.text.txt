To solve this, we must evaluate the historical context and the physical limitations of the Bohr model:

1.  **Understanding the Bohr Model:** Bohr proposed that electrons orbit the nucleus in stable, quantized energy levels. This model was highly successful in explaining the emission and absorption spectra of the hydrogen atom (a single-electron system).

In the HSC Physics curriculum, it is essential to remember that while Bohr provided the breakthrough of quantized energy levels, his model is restricted to single-electron systems because it cannot mathematically handle the interactions in multi-electron atoms.
