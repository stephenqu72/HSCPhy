### Front
In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), how does the horizontal component of velocity change over time?

### Back
- Key idea: Horizontal velocity remains constant because there is no horizontal acceleration.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton's First Law of Motion ($a_x = 0$).
- When to use it: To determine horizontal velocity at any point during a projectile's flight.
- Common trap: Assuming vertical displacement or gravity affects the horizontal velocity component.
