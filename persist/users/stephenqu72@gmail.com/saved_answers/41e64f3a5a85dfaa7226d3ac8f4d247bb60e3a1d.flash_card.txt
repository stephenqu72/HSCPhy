### Front
What are the fundamental constituents of all hadrons, and how are they classified within the Standard Model?

### Back
- Key idea: Hadrons are composite particles made of quarks held together by the strong nuclear force.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Baryons ($qqq$) and Mesons ($q\bar{q}$).
- When to use it: To classify subatomic particles or identify the composition of nucleons (protons/neutrons).
- Common trap: Confusing fundamental leptons (electrons/neutrinos) with hadrons or assuming gauge bosons (gluons) are constituent matter particles.
