### Answer

Both AC generators and DC motors consist of a current-carrying coil rotating within a magnetic field produced by permanent magnets or electromagnets. However, they differ significantly in their structural components and energy conversion processes:

*   **Structure:** An AC generator uses **slip rings** to maintain a continuous connection between the rotating coil and the external circuit, allowing the current direction to reverse every half-turn. In contrast, a DC motor uses a **split-ring commutator**, which reverses the direction of current in the coil every half-turn to ensure the torque remains in a consistent direction.
*   **Function:** An AC generator converts mechanical energy into electrical energy using the principle of **electromagnetic induction** (Faraday’s Law). A DC motor converts electrical energy into mechanical energy by utilizing the **motor effect**, where a force ($F = BIL\sin\theta$) is exerted on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field.
