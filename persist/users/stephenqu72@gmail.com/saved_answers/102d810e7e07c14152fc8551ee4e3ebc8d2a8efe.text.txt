### Explanation:
1.  **Understand the materials:**
    *   **$\alpha$-particles:** These consist of two protons and two neutrons (a helium nucleus). They are large and have a $+2$ charge, making them highly ionizing but very weakly penetrating. They are stopped by a thin sheet of paper or a few centimeters of air.
    *   **$\beta$-particles:** These are high-energy, high-speed electrons emitted from the nucleus. They are smaller than alpha particles and have a $-1$ charge. They can pass through paper but are generally stopped by a few millimeters of aluminum.
    *   **$\gamma$-rays:** These are high-frequency electromagnetic waves. They have no mass and no charge, making them highly penetrating. They can pass through paper and aluminum and require thick layers of dense material like lead or concrete to significantly reduce their intensity.

    *   **Radiation Z:** It passes through both the plastic and the aluminum and is only stopped by the thick $50\text{ mm}$ block of lead. This is the characteristic behavior of **$\gamma$-rays**.
