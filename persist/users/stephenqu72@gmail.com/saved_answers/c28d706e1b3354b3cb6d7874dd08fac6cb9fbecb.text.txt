To find the frequency of the wave shown on the oscilloscope, follow these steps:

### 1. Identify the Time Period ($T$)
First, observe the horizontal axis (time $t$ in seconds). The label "0.1" is positioned at the third vertical grid line from the left. This means each grid division represents:
$$\text{Time per division} = \frac{0.1 \text{ s}}{3} = \frac{1}{30} \text{ s} \approx 0.0333 \text{ s}$$

Now, count the number of grid divisions for one complete cycle. A full cycle starts at the peak (y-axis) and returns to the peak.
- The first peak is at $t = 0$.
- The second peak is at exactly 1.5 grid divisions (midway between the first and second grid line).
- Therefore, the period $T$ (time for one cycle) is $1.5$ divisions $\times (\frac{1}{30} \text{ s/division}) = 0.05 \text{ s}$.

Alternatively, count the total cycles:
- In the time interval of $0.1 \text{ s}$ (3 divisions), there are exactly 2 full cycles.
- This confirms $T = \frac{\text{total time}}{\text{number of cycles}} = \frac{0.1 \text{ s}}{2} = 0.05 \text{ s}$.

### 2. Calculate the Frequency ($f$)
Frequency is defined as the reciprocal of the time period:
$$f = \frac{1}{T}$$
$$f = \frac{1}{0.05 \text{ s}}$$
$$f = 20 \text{ Hz}$$

### Answer
The frequency is **20 Hz**.
