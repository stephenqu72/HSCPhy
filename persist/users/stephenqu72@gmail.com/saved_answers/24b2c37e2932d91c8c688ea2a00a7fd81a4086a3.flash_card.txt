### Front
How does changing the length ($L$) and cross-sectional area ($A$) of a conductor affect its resistance ($R$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Resistance is directly proportional to length and inversely proportional to cross-sectional area.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $R = \rho \frac{L}{A}$ (where $\rho$ = resistivity).
- When to use it: Whenever calculating the effect of changing the physical dimensions or material of a resistor.
- Common trap: Forgetting that area ($A$) is often given as $\pi r^2$; halving the radius results in a resistance increase by a factor of 4, not 2.
