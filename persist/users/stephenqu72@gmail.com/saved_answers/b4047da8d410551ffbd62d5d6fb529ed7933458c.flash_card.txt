### Front
How does the Normal Force ($F_N$) experienced by a driver at the peak of a hill compare to the trough of a valley for constant circular motion?

### Back
- Key idea: At the peak, $F_N$ is reduced by centripetal force ($F_N = mg - \frac{mv^2}{r}$); at the trough, $F_N$ is increased by centripetal force ($F_N = mg + \frac{mv^2}{r}$).
- Formula: $F_{net} = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ (Centripetal Force).
- When to use it: Any vertical circular motion problem involving apparent weight (Normal Force).
- Common trap: Forgetting that gravity ($mg$) always acts downwards; the direction of the net centripetal force must always point toward the center of the circle.
