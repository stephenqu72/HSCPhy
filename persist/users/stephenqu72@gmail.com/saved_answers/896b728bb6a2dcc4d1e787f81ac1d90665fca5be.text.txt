### Answer

**(a) Describe 'redshift'.**
Redshift occurs when light from an object moving away from the observer is shifted towards the longer wavelength (red) end of the electromagnetic spectrum. This is a result of the Doppler effect, where the light waves are "stretched" due to the relative motion of the source away from the observer. In an cosmological context, it is also caused by the expansion of the universe itself, which stretches the space through which light travels.

**(b) Name the scientist who used redshift to support the Big Bang Theory.**
Edwin Hubble.

### How to solve it

1.  **Part (a) Strategy (2 marks):**
    *   To get full marks, you need two distinct points:
        *   **The "What":** Explain that the wavelength of light increases (shifts toward the red end of the spectrum).
        *   **The "Why":** Explain that this is due to the object moving away from the observer (Doppler effect) or the expansion of the universe (cosmological redshift).
    *   *Teacher's Tip:* Always mention the relationship between velocity and wavelength: as an object moves away, the frequency decreases and the wavelength $\lambda$ increases.

2.  **Part (b) Strategy (1 mark):**
    *   This is a factual recall question. You must associate the observational evidence of expanding galaxies (redshift) with the astronomer who first quantified it.
    *   *Teacher's Tip:* Remember that Hubble observed that the redshift of galaxies is proportional to their distance from us—a discovery that became known as "Hubble's Law."
