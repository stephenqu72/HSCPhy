### Step 1: Motion on the table
In the frame of reference of the train, the table is smooth, meaning there is no friction. When the train accelerates to the left at $a = 3.0\text{ ms}^{-2}$, the ball experiences a pseudo-force to the right. Relative to the table, the ball remains stationary while the table accelerates underneath it. Effectively, the edge of the table moves towards the ball. 

The time $t_1$ taken for the edge of the table to reach the ball (covering a distance $d = 2.1\text{ m}$) is calculated using the kinematic equation $d = \frac{1}{2}at_1^2$:
$$2.1 = \frac{1}{2} \times 3.0 \times t_1^2$$
$$2.1 = 1.5 \times t_1^2$$
$$t_1^2 = 1.4 \implies t_1 = \sqrt{1.4} \approx 1.183\text{ s}$$

### Step 2: Projectile motion to the floor

$$t_2^2 = \frac{2.0}{9.8} \approx 0.204$$
