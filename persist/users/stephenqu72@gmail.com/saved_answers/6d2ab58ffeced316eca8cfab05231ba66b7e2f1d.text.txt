Unpolarised light (initial intensity $I_{in} = 10$) passing through the first polariser loses half its intensity.
$$I_1 = \frac{1}{2} I_{in} = \frac{1}{2} \times 10 = 5 \text{ units}$$

**2. Passing through Polariser 2:**

Using MalusŌĆÖs Law:
$$I_2 = I_1 \cos^2(20^\circ)$$
$$I_2 = 5 \times (\cos 20^\circ)^2 \approx 5 \times 0.8830 = 4.415 \text{ units}$$
