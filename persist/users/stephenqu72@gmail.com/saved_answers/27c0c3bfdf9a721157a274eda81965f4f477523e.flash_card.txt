### Front
In projectile motion, how do initial horizontal velocity and gravity affect the time an object takes to fall a vertical distance $h$?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Vertical and horizontal motions are independent; time of flight depends only on initial vertical velocity ($u_y$) and vertical displacement ($h$).
- **Formula:** $t = \sqrt{\frac{2h}{g}}$ (assuming $u_y = 0$).
- **When to use it:** To calculate time of flight for objects launched horizontally or dropped from rest.
- **Common trap:** Assuming horizontal velocity affects time of flight, or incorrectly thinking mass influences the rate of acceleration in a vacuum.
