To find the ratio of the centripetal force on $S_1$ to the centripetal force on $S_2$, we use the formula for centripetal force:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

### Step-by-step Solution:

1.  **Define the variables for $S_1$:**
    *   Mass = $m$
    *   Velocity = $v$
    *   Radius = $r$
    *   Force on $S_1$ ($F_1$) = $\frac{mv^2}{r}$

2.  **Define the variables for $S_2$:**
    *   Mass = $m$ (given equal mass)
    *   Velocity = $\frac{v}{\sqrt{2}}$
    *   Radius = $2r$
    *   Force on $S_2$ ($F_2$) = $\frac{m \left(\frac{v}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2}{2r}$

3.  **Simplify $F_2$:**
    *   $F_2 = \frac{m \left(\frac{v^2}{2}\right)}{2r}$
    *   $F_2 = \frac{mv^2}{4r}$

4.  **Calculate the ratio $F_1 : F_2$:**
    *   Ratio = $\frac{F_1}{F_2} = \frac{\frac{mv^2}{r}}{\frac{mv^2}{4r}}$
    *   The terms $m, v^2,$ and $r$ cancel out:
    *   Ratio = $\frac{1}{\frac{1}{4}} = 4$

The ratio of the centripetal force on $S_1$ to the centripetal force on $S_2$ is **4**.
