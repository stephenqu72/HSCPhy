### Part (b): Identify the transformer
**Answer:** The transformer is a **step-up transformer**.

**Explanation:**
A transformer is classified based on the relationship between its primary and secondary coils. The fundamental transformer equation is:
$$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$
Where $V$ is voltage and $N$ is the number of turns. If the number of turns in the secondary coil ($N_s$) is greater than the number of turns in the primary coil ($N_p$), the output voltage ($V_s$) will be greater than the input voltage ($V_p$). Since the secondary winding typically has significantly more turns than the primary in this context, it "steps up" the voltage.
