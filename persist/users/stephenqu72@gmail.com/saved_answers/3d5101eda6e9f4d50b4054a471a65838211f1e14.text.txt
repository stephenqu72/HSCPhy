To solve this problem, we need to balance the downward force of gravity with the upward electric force acting on the point charge.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
For a charge to be "exactly suspended," the net force on it must be zero. This means the magnitude of the electric force ($F_E$) must equal the magnitude of the gravitational force ($F_g$).

*   **Gravitational Force:** $F_g = mg$
*   **Electric Force:** $F_E = qE$
*   **Electric Field between parallel plates:** $E = \frac{V}{d}$
    (where $V$ is the potential difference and $d$ is the distance between plates)

Combining these, we get:
$$mg = q \frac{V}{d}$$

### 2. Rearrange to solve for $d$
$$d = \frac{qV}{mg}$$

### 3. Substitute the values
*   $m = 2.5 \times 10^{-6} \text{ kg}$
*   $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$
*   $q = 4.0 \times 10^{-8} \text{ C}$ (We use the magnitude of the charge)
*   $V = 35.0 \text{ V}$

$$d = \frac{(4.0 \times 10^{-8} \text{ C}) \times (35.0 \text{ V})}{(2.5 \times 10^{-6} \text{ kg}) \times (9.8 \text{ m/s}^2)}$$

$$d = \frac{1.4 \times 10^{-6}}{2.45 \times 10^{-5}}$$
$$d \approx 0.05714 \text{ meters}$$

### 4. Convert to the correct units
To match the options provided:
$$0.05714 \text{ m} = 57.14 \text{ mm}$$

Comparing this to the given options, **(B) 57 mm** is the correct answer.
