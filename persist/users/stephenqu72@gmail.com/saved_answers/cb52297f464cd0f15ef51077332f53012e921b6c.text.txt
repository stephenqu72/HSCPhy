As an HSC Physics teacher, I will guide you through this 8-mark transformer question.

### Part (a)
**Diagram Construction:**
To demonstrate a **step-up transformer**, you must ensure the secondary coil has more turns than the primary coil.
1.  **Core:** Draw a square or rectangular laminated iron core.
2.  **Primary side:** Connect the AC power supply to the coil with **300 turns**. Include an AC ammeter in series and an AC voltmeter in parallel with the supply.
3.  **Secondary side:** Place the coil with **600 turns** on the opposite side of the core. Connect an AC voltmeter across these terminals.
4.  **Labels:** Clearly label the iron core, primary coil ($N_p = 300$), secondary coil ($N_s = 600$), AC power supply, and meters.

### Part (b)
**Explanation:**
Faraday’s Law states that an induced electromotive force (EMF) is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux: $\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$.

In an ideal transformer, the iron core ensures that the magnetic flux $\Phi$ is shared perfectly between the primary and secondary coils.
1.  The primary voltage $V_p$ creates a changing flux in the core: $V_p = -N_p \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$.
2.  This same changing flux passes through the secondary coil, inducing a secondary voltage: $V_s = -N_s \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$.
3.  By dividing these two equations, we derive the transformer equation: 
$$\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$
Since $N_s > N_p$ in our setup, the output voltage $V_s$ will be stepped up relative to $V_p$.

### Part (c)
**Explanation:**
The experimental results will differ from the ideal transformer due to **energy losses** (efficiency $< 100\%$).
1.  **Resistance in coils:** The copper wire has internal resistance, causing energy loss as heat ($I^2R$ losses).
2.  **Eddy currents:** Even with an iron core, changing magnetic fields induce small currents within the core itself, leading to heating. 
3.  **Flux leakage:** Not all magnetic field lines generated by the primary coil will pass through the secondary coil, meaning the flux linkage is not 100% efficient.
4.  **Hysteresis:** Energy is lost as heat due to the continuous re-magnetization of the iron core as the AC current flips direction.

Because of these, the actual measured $V_s$ will be lower than the theoretical value calculated by the ideal transformer equation.
