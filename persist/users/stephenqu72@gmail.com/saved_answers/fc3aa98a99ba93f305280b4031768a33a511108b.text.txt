### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Answer:**
Maxwell predicted that electromagnetic waves are generated by oscillating electric charges (accelerating charges), which produce a time-varying electric field. According to Ampère-Maxwell's law, this changing electric field induces a time-varying magnetic field. Faraday’s law of induction then states that this changing magnetic field induces a further changing electric field. This self-propagating cycle allows the wave to move through space at the speed of light, $c$, calculated as:
$$c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$$
This theoretical prediction matched the known speed of light, leading Maxwell to conclude that light itself is an electromagnetic wave.

**How to solve:**
*   **Focus on the mechanism:** Explain the link between accelerating charges and the creation of fields.
*   **Identify the laws:** Explicitly mention the interplay between Faraday’s Law and the Ampère-Maxwell Law (the "self-sustaining" nature of the wave).
*   **Connect to the constant:** Mention the theoretical value of $c$ derived from physical constants $\mu_0$ and $\epsilon_0$.

#### Part (b)
**Answer:**
1.  **Surface Temperature:** Stars behave as near-perfect blackbodies. By observing the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) of a star's continuous spectrum, Wien's Displacement Law ($\lambda_{max} T = \text{constant}$) allows us to calculate the surface temperature.
2.  **Motion:** The Doppler effect causes the spectral lines of a star to shift. A redshift indicates the star is moving away, while a blueshift indicates it is moving toward the observer. The degree of the shift determines the radial velocity.
3.  **Chemical Composition:** Every element emits or absorbs light at specific, unique wavelengths (spectral fingerprints). By observing the absorption lines in a star’s spectrum, we can perform spectroscopic analysis to identify the presence of specific elements in the star's atmosphere.

*   **Structure:** Address the three distinct requirements (temperature, motion, composition) as individual points to ensure you pick up all 4 marks.
