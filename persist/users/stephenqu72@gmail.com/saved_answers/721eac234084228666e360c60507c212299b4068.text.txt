To solve this problem, we need to relate the work done by the electric field to the kinetic energy gained by the particle.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Understand the Work-Energy Principle:**
    When a charged particle $e$ is accelerated through a potential difference $V$ between two plates, the work done on the particle by the electric field is equal to its final kinetic energy $K$. 
    The formula for work done on a charge is:
    $$W = K = qV = eV$$
    From this, we can find the potential difference $V$:
    $$V = \frac{K}{e}$$

2.  **Relate Potential Difference to Electric Field:**
    In a uniform electric field $E$ between two parallel plates separated by a distance $d$, the potential difference $V$ is given by:
    $$V = Ed$$

3.  **Combine the Equations:**
    Substitute the expression for $V$ from the first step into the second step equation:
    $$\frac{K}{e} = Ed$$

4.  **Solve for $E$:**
    To isolate the electric field strength $E$, divide both sides by $d$:
    $$E = \frac{K}{ed}$$
