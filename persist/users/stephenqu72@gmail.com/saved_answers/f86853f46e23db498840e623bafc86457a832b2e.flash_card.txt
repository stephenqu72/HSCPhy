### Front
In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), how does the speed at the launch point compare to the speed at the impact point if both are at the same vertical height?

### Back
- Key idea: Total mechanical energy ($E_k + E_p$) is conserved throughout the trajectory.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Law of Conservation of Energy: $E_{total} = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + mgh$
- When to use it: To determine projectile speed, kinetic energy, or potential energy at symmetric points in a path.
- Common trap: Assuming kinetic energy is zero at the peak (the vertical component is zero, but horizontal velocity remains).
