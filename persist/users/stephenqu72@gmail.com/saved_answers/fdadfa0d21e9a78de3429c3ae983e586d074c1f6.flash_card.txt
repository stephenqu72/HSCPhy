### Front
Compare gravitational and electric fields in terms of force nature and mathematical relationship.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Both are inverse-square, non-contact fields; gravity is always attractive, while electric fields are both attractive and repulsive.
- **Formula / law / constant:** $F = G\frac{m_1m_2}{r^2}$ vs $F = k\frac{q_1q_2}{r^2}$; both follow $F \propto \frac{1}{r^2}$.
- **When to use it:** When asked to "compare," "contrast," or "outline" the properties of fundamental fields.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that gravity only acts as an attractive force, whereas electric fields depend on the sign of the charges.
