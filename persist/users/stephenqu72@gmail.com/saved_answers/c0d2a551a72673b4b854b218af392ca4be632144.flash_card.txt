### Front
How does the emission of one alpha particle and two beta-minus particles affect the atomic number ($Z$) and identity of a radioactive nucleus?

### Back
- Key idea: The atomic number remains unchanged, resulting in an isotope of the original element.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: 
  $\alpha$ decay: $Z \to Z-2$
  $\beta^-$ decay: $Z \to Z+1$
  Net change: $(Z-2) + 1 + 1 = Z$
- When to use it: To identify isotopic products in a radioactive decay series.
- Common trap: Confusing $\beta^-$ (electron) with $\beta^+$ (positron); $\beta^+$ decreases $Z$ by $1$.
