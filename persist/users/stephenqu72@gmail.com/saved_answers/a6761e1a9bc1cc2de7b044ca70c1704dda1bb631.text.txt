To solve this problem, we must relate the centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) required to simulate Earth's gravity to the rotational period ($T$) and the radius of rotation ($r$).

### Solution:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   Desired artificial gravity $a_c = 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$.
    *   Minimum period $T = 60\text{ s}$ (one minute).

2.  **Relate acceleration and period:**
    The formula for centripetal acceleration in terms of period is:
    $$a_c = \frac{4\pi^2 r}{T^2}$$

3.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for the radius ($r$):**
    $$r = \frac{a_c T^2}{4\pi^2}$$

4.  **Substitute the values:**
    $$r = \frac{9.8 \times (60)^2}{4 \times \pi^2}$$
    $$r = \frac{9.8 \times 3600}{39.478}$$
    $$r \approx \frac{35280}{39.478}$$
    $$r \approx 893.66\text{ m}$$

**Conclusion:**
To achieve an artificial gravity of $9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$ with a rotation period of at least one minute, the distance from the centre of mass to the crew quarters must be approximately **$894\text{ meters}$**.
