### Front
What is the "design speed" of a banked track, and which force component provides the necessary centripetal force?

### Back
- Key idea: At design speed, the horizontal component of the normal force provides all required centripetal force, removing the need for friction.
- Formula: $v = \sqrt{rg \tan \theta}$ (derived from $\tan \theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$)
- When to use it: To calculate the ideal speed for a car to corner on a banked track without relying on friction.
- Common trap: Assuming the normal force ($N$) *is* the centripetal force; it is actually the *horizontal component* ($N \sin \theta$) that acts as the centripetal force.
