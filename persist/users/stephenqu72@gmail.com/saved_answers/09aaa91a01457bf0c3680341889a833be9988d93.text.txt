To solve this problem, we need to calculate the wavelength of the photon emitted during the transition from the $n=4$ energy level to the $n=1$ energy level in a hydrogen atom.

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Identify the energy levels:**
From the provided diagram, the energy levels are:
- $E_4 = -0.85 \text{ eV}$
- $E_1 = -13.6 \text{ eV}$

**2. Calculate the change in energy ($\Delta E$):**
When an electron transitions from a higher energy level to a lower one, it emits a photon with energy equal to the difference between these two levels:
$$\Delta E = E_4 - E_1$$
$$\Delta E = -0.85 \text{ eV} - (-13.6 \text{ eV}) = 12.75 \text{ eV}$$

**3. Convert energy from eV to Joules:**
Since $1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$, we have:
$$\Delta E = 12.75 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J} \approx 2.04255 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J}$$

**4. Use the photon energy formula to find the wavelength ($\lambda$):**
The relationship between energy and wavelength is given by $\Delta E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$, which rearranges to:
$$\lambda = \frac{hc}{\Delta E}$$
Where:
- $h$ (Planck's constant) $\approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$
- $c$ (speed of light) $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

Substituting the values:
$$\lambda = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)}{2.04255 \times 10^{-18}}$$
$$\lambda \approx \frac{1.9878 \times 10^{-25}}{2.04255 \times 10^{-18}} \approx 9.732 \times 10^{-8} \text{ m}$$

**5. Convert meters to nanometers:**
Since $1 \text{ m} = 10^9 \text{ nm}$:
$$\lambda \approx 9.732 \times 10^{-8} \text{ m} \times 10^9 \text{ nm/m} \approx 97.32 \text{ nm}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, **(C) 97.3 nm** is the correct answer.
