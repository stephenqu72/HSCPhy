### Front
How do you calculate the mass of an object after a perfectly inelastic collision?

### Back
- Key idea: Total momentum is conserved in a closed system ($p_{\text{initial}} = p_{\text{final}}$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $m_1u_1 + m_2u_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v$
- When to use it: When two objects collide and stick together (inelastic collision).
- Common trap: Forgetting to sum the masses $(m_1 + m_2)$ for the final velocity term.
