To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of electromagnetism regarding current-carrying conductors and the motor effect.

### (a) Calculate the force per unit length between the wires.

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Voltage ($V$) = $2\text{ V}$
*   Resistance ($R$) = $0.40\text{ }\Omega$
*   Distance ($r$) = $25\text{ cm} = 0.25\text{ m}$
*   Permeability of free space ($\mu_0$) = $4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

**2. Calculate the current ($I$) in each wire using Ohm's Law ($V = IR$):**
$$I = \frac{V}{R} = \frac{2}{0.40} = 5\text{ A}$$

**3. Use the formula for force per unit length between two parallel conductors:**
$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$
Since $I_1 = I_2 = 5\text{ A}$:
$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times 5 \times 5}{2\pi \times 0.25}$$
$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 25}{0.25} = 2 \times 10^{-7} \times 100 = 2 \times 10^{-5}\text{ N/m}$$

### (b) Explain whether the wires are attracting or repelling each other.

**Explanation:**
The wires are **attracting** each other. According to the **Right-Hand Grip Rule**, the current flowing through the top wire creates a magnetic field that circles the wire. At the position of the bottom wire, this magnetic field points **into the page**. According to the **Right-Hand Rule for the Motor Effect** ($F = ILB\sin\theta$), if you place your fingers in the direction of the current (to the right) and curl them towards the magnetic field direction (into the page), your thumb points **upward** (towards the top wire). By Newton's Third Law, the top wire experiences an equal and opposite force downward. Thus, they attract.
