### Answer and Explanation

**a. Maxwell's Model of Light (2 marks)**
*   **Description:** Maxwell’s model proposes that light is an electromagnetic wave consisting of oscillating electric and magnetic fields that are perpendicular to each other and perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation.
*   **Predictions:** 
    1. Electromagnetic waves travel at a constant speed, $c$, which Maxwell calculated to be $3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$. 
    2. Light is an electromagnetic wave that does not require a medium to propagate, unlike mechanical waves.

**b. Einstein's Postulates (2 marks)**
*   **Postulates:** 
    1. The laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames of reference. 
    2. The speed of light in a vacuum ($c$) is constant for all observers, regardless of the motion of the source or the observer.
*   **Impact:** This overturned the Newtonian concept of absolute space and time, leading to the understanding that time and length are relative. It essentially dismantled the "aether" theory, confirming light is an independent electromagnetic field.

**c. Evidence for Special Relativity (2 marks)**
*   **Evidence:** Time dilation—where time passes more slowly for an object moving at relativistic speeds relative to a stationary observer. 
*   **Technology:** GPS satellites. Because satellites move at high speeds relative to Earth, they experience time dilation (along with general relativistic effects). Engineers must program clocks on GPS satellites to account for these time differences to ensure accurate positioning data on Earth.

**d. Length Contraction Calculation (2 marks)**
*   **Method:** We must identify the "proper length" ($L_0$), which is the length measured by a passenger inside the train (in the same rest frame as the carriage). The platform observer measures the "contracted length" ($L$).
*   **Formula:** $$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
*   **Calculation:**
    Given $L = 5.5 \text{ m}$ and $v = 0.95c$:
    $$5.5 = L_0 \sqrt{1 - (0.95)^2}$$
    $$5.5 = L_0 \sqrt{1 - 0.9025}$$
    $$5.5 = L_0 \sqrt{0.0975}$$
    $$5.5 = L_0 \times 0.31225$$
    $$L_0 = \frac{5.5}{0.31225} \approx 17.61 \text{ m}$$
*   **Result:** The passenger measures the carriage to be approximately **$17.61 \text{ m}$**.
