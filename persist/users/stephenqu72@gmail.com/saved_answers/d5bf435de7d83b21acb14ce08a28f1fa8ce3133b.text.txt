### Sample Answer: Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation (CMB)

*   **Select:** The Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation.
*   **Outline:** The CMB is the "afterglow" of the Big Bang—faint microwave radiation detected in all directions of the sky. It represents the point in time (about 380,000 years after the Big Bang) when the universe cooled enough for electrons and protons to form neutral hydrogen atoms, allowing photons to travel freely through space.
*   **Assess:** This is a highly compelling piece of evidence. Its existence was predicted by Big Bang models before its discovery, and its temperature profile (perfectly consistent with a blackbody at $2.7\,K$) is extremely difficult to explain through alternative theories like the Steady State model. Therefore, it provides strong, quantitative support for the theory of a hot, dense early universe.

### How to solve this question:

1.  **Structure for the marks:**
    *   **Mark 1 (Select and Outline):** Clearly name the piece of evidence (e.g., CMB, Redshift of galaxies, or Abundance of light elements) and describe what it is.
    *   **Mark 2 (Contextual link):** Briefly explain *why* this supports the Big Bang Theory (e.g., "it shows the universe was once hot and dense").
    *   **Mark 3 (Assessment):** You must make a judgment to "assess." Use words like "highly compelling," "consistent with predictions," or "provides strong support" to show why this evidence is valid and significant.

2.  **Top tips for the HSC:**
    *   **Choose the strongest evidence:** CMB and Hubble’s Law (redshift) are the standard "go-to" answers. Don't waste time on vague descriptions; be specific about the physics.
    *   **Follow the directive verbs:**
        *   **Outline:** Briefly describe.
        *   **Assess:** Make a judgment of the value or quality of the evidence. You cannot get full marks without evaluating how well it supports the theory.
