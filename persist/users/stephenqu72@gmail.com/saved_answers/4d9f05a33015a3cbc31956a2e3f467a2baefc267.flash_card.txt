### Front
For a satellite in an elliptical orbit, where does it have maximum kinetic energy and why?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kinetic energy is inversely proportional to the distance from the primary body (due to conservation of energy).
- **Formula / law:** Conservation of Mechanical Energy ($E_{total} = K + U = \text{constant}$).
- **When to use it:** To compare velocity or energy states at different points in an elliptical orbit (e.g., perigee vs. apogee).
- **Common trap:** Confusing "most negative" potential energy with "lowest" magnitude; at perigee, the satellite is moving fastest, even though its potential energy is at its minimum value.
