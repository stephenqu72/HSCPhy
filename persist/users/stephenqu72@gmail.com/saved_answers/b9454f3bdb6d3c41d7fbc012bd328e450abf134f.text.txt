To solve this problem, we need to distinguish between **average speed** (a scalar quantity) and **average velocity** (a vector quantity).

### 1. Calculate Average Speed
Average speed is defined as the total distance traveled divided by the total time taken:
$$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}}$$

For a semicircular path, the distance is half the circumference of a circle:
$$\text{Distance} = \pi \times r = \pi \times 200\text{ m} \approx 628.32\text{ m}$$
$$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{628.32\text{ m}}{50\text{ s}} \approx 12.57\text{ ms}^{-1}$$
Rounding to the nearest whole number provided in the options, we get **$13\text{ ms}^{-1}$**.

### 2. Calculate Magnitude of Average Velocity
The magnitude of average velocity is defined as the displacement divided by the total time taken:
$$\text{Average Velocity} = \frac{\text{Displacement}}{\text{Total Time}}$$

Displacement is the straight-line distance from the starting point to the ending point. For a semicircle, this is the diameter of the circle:
$$\text{Displacement} = 2 \times r = 2 \times 200\text{ m} = 400\text{ m}$$
$$\text{Average Velocity} = \frac{400\text{ m}}{50\text{ s}} = 8\text{ ms}^{-1}$$

### Conclusion
Comparing our results:
*   Magnitude of average velocity: $8\text{ ms}^{-1}$
*   Average speed: $13\text{ ms}^{-1}$

This corresponds to option **(C)**.
