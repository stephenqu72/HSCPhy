To determine the color of the star CX-234, we must find the peak wavelength of its black-body radiation spectrum using Wien's Displacement Law.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the relevant formula:**
    Wien's Displacement Law states:
    $$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$
    Where:
    - $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength in meters ($m$).
    - $b$ is Wien's displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}$.
    - $T$ is the absolute temperature in Kelvin ($K$).

2.  **Perform the calculation:**
    Given the temperature $T = 6400 \text{ K}$:
    $$\lambda_{max} = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}}{6400 \text{ K}}$$
    $$\lambda_{max} \approx 4.528 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$$

3.  **Convert to nanometers (nm):**
    Since $1 \text{ nm} = 1 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$:
    $$\lambda_{max} \approx 452.8 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m} = 452.8 \text{ nm}$$

4.  **Interpret the result using the diagram:**
    Looking at the provided spectrum diagram, the wavelength $452.8 \text{ nm}$ falls within the **blue** section of the visible light spectrum (which spans roughly $445 \text{ nm}$ to $475 \text{ nm}$ for the blue region).
