### Model Answer
Atomic clocks are sufficiently precise to measure the nanosecond differences in time passage predicted by Einstein’s theories.

1.  **Special Relativity (Velocity Time Dilation):** The Hafele-Keating experiment (1971) used highly accurate cesium-beam atomic clocks placed on commercial airliners flying around the world. Because the clocks were moving relative to a stationary observer on Earth, the moving clocks recorded a different elapsed time compared to the clocks left on the ground, matching the prediction of $t = \frac{t_0}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$.
2.  **General Relativity (Gravitational Time Dilation):** Atomic clocks have also verified that time runs slower closer to a massive body (where gravity is stronger). Experiments (such as those by Pound-Rebka or modern optical lattice clocks) have shown that clocks at different altitudes/gravitational potentials tick at different rates, consistent with the prediction that time passes faster at higher altitudes (lower gravitational potential).
