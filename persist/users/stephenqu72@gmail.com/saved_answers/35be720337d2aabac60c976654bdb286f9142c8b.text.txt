### Answer

**(a)**
*   **Red Giants:** This region is located in the top right section of the Hertzsprung-Russell (H-R) diagram (above the Main Sequence, towards the cooler temperatures).
*   **White Dwarfs:** This region is located in the bottom left section of the H-R diagram (below the Main Sequence, towards the hotter temperatures).

**(b)**
The luminosity $L$ of a star is given by the Stefan-Boltzmann law, expressed as:
$$L = 4\pi R^2 \sigma T^4$$
where $R$ is the radius, $\sigma$ is the Stefan-Boltzmann constant, and $T$ is the surface temperature.

From the H-R diagram, Red Giants are located at cool temperatures (low $T$) but possess high luminosity (high absolute magnitude represents low values on the vertical axis, but here, top is high luminosity). Conversely, White Dwarfs are located at high temperatures (high $T$) but possess very low luminosity (low down on the vertical axis). 

Since $R^2 \propto \frac{L}{T^4}$, the low temperature of a Red Giant requires a massive increase in surface area ($R^2$) to maintain its high luminosity. In contrast, the very high temperature of a White Dwarf means it can emit a significant amount of energy from a very small surface area. Thus, for a Red Giant to be as luminous as it is despite being cool, its radius $R$ must be significantly larger than that of a White Dwarf.

    *   Step 1: State the relationship $L \propto R^2 T^4$.
    *   Step 2: Compare the properties shown on the axes. Red Giants have low $T$ and high $L$.
    *   Step 3: Rearrange the formula to show that $R$ must be large when $T$ is small and $L$ is large.
    *   Step 4: Contrast this with the White Dwarf, where high $T$ and low $L$ necessitate a very small $R$.
