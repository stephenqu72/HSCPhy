### Front
What is the relationship between the normal force ($F_N$) and gravity ($mg$) for an object at the bottom of a vertical circular dip?

### Back
- Key idea: At the bottom of a dip, the normal force must provide both the upward support against gravity and the required centripetal force.
- Formula / law: $F_N = F_c + mg$ (or $\sum F = F_N - mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$)
- When to use it: Any vertical circular motion problem where the object is at the lowest point (e.g., a roller coaster or trolley in a dip).
- Common trap: Assuming $F_N = mg$. Always remember that the net force must point toward the center of the circle, making $F_N$ greater than $mg$ at the bottom.
