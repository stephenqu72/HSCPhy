### Explanation

To solve this, we need to consider the historical and theoretical context of James Clerk Maxwell's work in the 1860s:

1.  **Analysis of the Options:**
    *   **(A) Incorrect:** While Maxwell’s equations describe the behavior of charges, the "link between magnetism and electrons" (specifically the realization that magnetism arises from moving charges/electrons) was largely established through the work of Ampère and later refined by Lorentz.
    *   **(B) Incorrect:** Maxwell provided the theoretical framework that *predicted* the existence of electromagnetic waves, but he did not experimentally *discover* radio waves. That was achieved by Heinrich Hertz in the 1880s, confirming Maxwell's theory.
    *   **(C) Correct:** Maxwell demonstrated that the speed of the electromagnetic waves derived from his equations was $v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$, which matched the experimentally determined speed of light. This led him to the profound conclusion that light itself is an electromagnetic wave, and he predicted that other electromagnetic waves of different frequencies existed, forming an **electromagnetic spectrum**.
    *   **(D) Incorrect:** The idea that light is composed of "photons" (quanta of light) was proposed by Albert Einstein in 1905 to explain the photoelectric effect, decades after Maxwell's work.

2.  **Summary:**
    Maxwell’s greatest synthesis was unifying electricity, magnetism, and optics. By showing that light is an electromagnetic wave, he paved the way for the recognition of the entire electromagnetic spectrum.
