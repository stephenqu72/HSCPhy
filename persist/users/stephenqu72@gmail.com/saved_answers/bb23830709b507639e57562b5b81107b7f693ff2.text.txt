To find the speed of the object, we use the principles of Special Relativity regarding length contraction.

### Answer
The speed of the object is approximately $0.141c$ (or $14.1\%$ of the speed of light).

### Explanation
**1. Identify the relevant physics formula**
The length contraction formula is:
$$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
Where:
*   $L_0$ is the proper length (length measured by someone on the object).
*   $L$ is the contracted length (measured by an observer on Earth).
*   $v$ is the velocity of the object.
*   $c$ is the speed of light.

**2. Translate the problem into an equation**
The problem states there is a $1\%$ difference in length. This means the observed length $L$ is $99\%$ of the proper length $L_0$:
$$L = 0.99 L_0$$
Substituting this into the length contraction formula:
$$0.99 L_0 = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$

**3. Solve for $v$**
Divide both sides by $L_0$:
$$0.99 = \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
Square both sides to remove the square root:
$$0.9801 = 1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$$
Rearrange the equation:
$$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 1 - 0.9801$$
$$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 0.0199$$
Take the square root of both sides:
$$\frac{v}{c} = \sqrt{0.0199} \approx 0.14106$$
$$v \approx 0.141c$$

Thus, the object is travelling at approximately $14.1\%$ of the speed of light.
