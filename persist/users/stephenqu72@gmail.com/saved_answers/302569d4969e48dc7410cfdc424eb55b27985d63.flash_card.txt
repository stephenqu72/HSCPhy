### Front
Why are particles accelerated to near-light speeds before collisions in high-energy physics?

### Back
- **Key idea:** High kinetic energy is converted into mass ($E=mc^2$) to create new subatomic particles.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Einstein’s Mass-Energy Equivalence: $E = mc^2$.
- **When to use it:** To explain the purpose of particle accelerators and the production of massive, short-lived particles from kinetic energy.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the goal is to measure mass or test electromagnetic repulsion; the primary goal is mass-energy conversion.
