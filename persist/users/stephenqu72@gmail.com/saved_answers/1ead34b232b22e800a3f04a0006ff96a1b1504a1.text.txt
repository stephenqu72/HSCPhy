### Explanation
To solve this question, you must understand the specific mechanism proposed by Isaac Newton in his *corpuscular theory* of light to account for the phenomenon of refraction.

1.  **The Corpuscular Theory:** Newton proposed that light consisted of tiny particles (corpuscles).
2.  **The Mechanism for Refraction:** Newton observed that when light passes from a rarer medium (like air) to a denser medium (like glass or water), it bends *towards* the normal. He theorized that this occurred because there was an attractive force acting on the light particles at the boundary of the two media. 
3.  **The Logic:** Newton argued that the particles of the denser medium exerted a stronger gravitational-like attractive force on the light particles. As these light particles approached the interface, this force increased their velocity component perpendicular to the surface. This acceleration towards the normal caused the path of the light to bend, explaining refraction.
4.  **Evaluating the Distractors:**
    *   **B is incorrect** because while Newton predicted that light travels faster in a denser medium, he did not attribute refraction to the medium's density "forcing" a slowdown. In fact, his model required light to *speed up* in a denser medium to explain the change in direction.
    *   **C is incorrect** because scattering is a wave-based phenomenon associated with different concepts, not part of Newton's corpuscular explanation of refraction.
    *   **D is incorrect** because the angle of incidence is generally not equal to the angle of refraction (this only happens in specific cases like normal incidence where the angle is $0^\circ$); this statement describes the law of reflection, not refraction.

Therefore, **A** is the only statement that correctly reflects Newton's theoretical framework for refraction.
