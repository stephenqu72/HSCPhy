### Front
How does orbital altitude affect the period of a satellite, and which type of orbit (LEO or GEO) is best suited for surface mapping?

### Back
- Key idea: Orbital period increases with altitude; lower altitude allows for higher resolution imagery.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Kepler's Third Law ($T^2 \propto r^3$).
- When to use it: To determine orbital height based on timing requirements or imaging needs.
- Common trap: Confusing "geostationary" (fixed position, high altitude) with "low earth orbit" (frequent passes, low altitude).
