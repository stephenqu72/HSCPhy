To find the correct angle of the banked bend, we use the physics of circular motion for an object on a frictionless banked track.

### 1. Identifying the Physics Principles
When there is no sideways frictional force, the centripetal force required to keep the bicycle moving in a circle is provided entirely by the horizontal component of the normal force ($N$).

- The vertical component of the normal force balances the weight of the bicycle and rider: $N \cos(\theta) = mg$
- The horizontal component of the normal force provides the centripetal force: $N \sin(\theta) = \frac{mv^2}{r}$

By dividing the second equation by the first:
$$\frac{N \sin(\theta)}{N \cos(\theta)} = \frac{\frac{mv^2}{r}}{mg}$$
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

### 2. Calculation
Given values:
- Speed $v = 11 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
- Radius $r = 25 \text{ m}$
- Acceleration due to gravity $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$

Substitute the values into the formula:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{11^2}{25 \times 9.8}$$
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{121}{245}$$
$$\tan(\theta) \approx 0.49387$$

Now, calculate the angle $\theta$:
$$\theta = \arctan(0.49387)$$
$$\theta \approx 26.28^\circ$$

Comparing this result to the given options:
A. $2.6^\circ$
B. $10^\circ$
C. $26^\circ$
D. $30^\circ$

The value $26.28^\circ$ is closest to $26^\circ$.

**Correct Option: C**
