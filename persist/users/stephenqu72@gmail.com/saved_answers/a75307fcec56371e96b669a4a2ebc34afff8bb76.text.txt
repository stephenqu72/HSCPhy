### Teacher's Solution and Explanation

To solve this problem, we apply the principles of Special Relativity. We are given the speed of the train as $v = 0.9c$. The Lorentz factor, $\gamma$, is essential for these calculations:

$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.9^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.19}} \approx 2.294$$

---

#### (a) According to Observer A (Proper Time)
Observer A is in the same inertial frame as the laser and the mirror. The distance $L_0 = 2.3 \text{ m}$ is the **proper length**. The light travels to the mirror and back, covering a total distance of $2L_0 = 4.6 \text{ m}$.

Since the speed of light $c \approx 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$:
$$t_0 = \frac{2L_0}{c} = \frac{4.6}{3.0 \times 10^8} \approx 1.53 \times 10^{-8} \text{ s}$$

---

#### (b) According to Observer B
**i. Time taken:** 
Observer B measures the **dilated time** ($t$). Because the clock (the light pulse) is moving relative to Observer B, it appears to run slow:
$$t = \gamma t_0 \approx 2.294 \times 1.53 \times 10^{-8} \approx 3.51 \times 10^{-8} \text{ s}$$

**ii. Distance between laser and mirror:** 
Observer B measures **length contraction** in the direction of motion. The distance $L$ is:
$$L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma} = \frac{2.3}{2.294} \approx 1.00 \text{ m}$$

---

### Summary of steps for your exam:
1. **Identify the frame:** For Observer A, everything is stationary, so use standard kinematics ($d=vt$) with $c$.
2. **Use Lorentz transformations:** For Observer B, the time is dilated (longer than $t_0$) and the length is contracted (shorter than $L_0$).
3. **Accuracy:** Always keep your $\gamma$ value precise during intermediate steps to avoid rounding errors in your final answer.
