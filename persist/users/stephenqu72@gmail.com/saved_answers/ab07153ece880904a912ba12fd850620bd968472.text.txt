### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) The double-slit experiment with electrons.**

### Explanation
Louis de Broglie proposed the theory of **matter waves** (or de Broglie waves), suggesting that particles of matter, such as electrons, exhibit wave-like properties. His hypothesis is defined by the relationship:

$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$$

where:
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the particle.
- $h$ is Planck's constant.
- $p$ is the momentum of the particle.

To confirm this theory, evidence of electron interference or diffraction was required. 

1.  **(A) The double-slit experiment with electrons:** This is the direct evidence for de Broglie’s theory. When electrons are fired at a double-slit, they produce an interference pattern on a detector screen. This pattern is characteristic of waves, thereby demonstrating that matter (electrons) possesses wave-like properties. (Note: The Davisson-Germer experiment, involving electron diffraction from a nickel crystal, is also famous evidence for this).
2.  **(B) The photoelectric effect:** This provides evidence for the *particle* nature of light (photons), not the wave nature of matter.
3.  **(C) The Franck-Hertz experiment:** This provides evidence for the quantization of energy levels within atoms.
4.  **(D) The Millikan oil drop experiment:** This was used to determine the elementary charge of a single electron.
