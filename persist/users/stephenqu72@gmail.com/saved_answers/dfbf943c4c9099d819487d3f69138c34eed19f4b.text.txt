### Explanation: How to solve this
To solve this, we use a two-step approach:

1.  **Find the acceleration of the system:** Since both blocks are moving together as one unit, we treat them as a single object with a combined mass.
    *   Total mass $m_{total} = m_A + m_B = 4.0\text{ kg} + 1.0\text{ kg} = 5.0\text{ kg}$.
    *   Using Newton's Second Law ($F = ma$):
        $$a = \frac{F_{applied}}{m_{total}} = \frac{40\text{ N}}{5.0\text{ kg}} = 8.0\text{ m/s}^2$$

2.  **Calculate the force on individual blocks:** Now, focus on block B. The only horizontal force acting on block B is the force exerted by block A ($F_{on B by A}$). 
    *   For block B: $F_{on B by A} = m_B \times a$.
    *   $F_{on B by A} = 1.0\text{ kg} \times 8.0\text{ m/s}^2 = 8.0\text{ N}$.

3.  **Apply Newton's Third Law:** Newton’s Third Law states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. Therefore, the force exerted by block B on block A is equal in magnitude to the force exerted by block A on block B, but in the opposite direction.
