### How to solve this question
1.  **Select a specific investigation:** Choose one you have actually performed (e.g., measuring $g$ with a pendulum, investigating Snell's Law, or measuring the motor effect).
2.  **Define reliability:** Remember that in the context of HSC Science, **reliability** refers to the consistency of your results. If you repeat the experiment under the same conditions, do you get the same (or very similar) results?
3.  **Describe the process:**
    *   State the action: Mention repeating trials.
    *   State the evaluation: Explain that you compared the trials to ensure they were consistent (e.g., checking for a small range or standard deviation).
4.  **Structure for marks:** 
    *   One mark is usually awarded for mentioning the *method* of ensuring reliability (repeated trials). 
    *   The second mark is awarded for explaining *how* that method demonstrates consistency (e.g., "to identify outliers and calculate a mean").
