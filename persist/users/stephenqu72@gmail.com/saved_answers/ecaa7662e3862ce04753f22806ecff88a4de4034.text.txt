To find the magnetic flux through the coil, we use the standard formula for magnetic flux, $\Phi$:

$$\Phi = B \cdot A \cdot \cos(\theta)$$

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the variables provided:**
    *   Magnetic field strength ($B$) = $0.03\text{ T}$
    *   Sides of the rectangular coil: $0.04\text{ m}$ and $0.06\text{ m}$
    *   Area ($A$) = $0.04\text{ m} \times 0.06\text{ m} = 0.0024\text{ m}^2$ (or $2.4 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}^2$)

2.  **Determine the angle ($\theta$):**
    *   The formula uses $\theta$ as the angle between the magnetic field vector and the **normal** (perpendicular) to the surface of the coil.
    *   The problem states the *plane of the coil* is perpendicular to the field lines. This means the field lines are parallel to the normal of the coil surface.
    *   Therefore, $\theta = 0^\circ$, and $\cos(0^\circ) = 1$.

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$\Phi = 0.03\text{ T} \times 0.0024\text{ m}^2$$
    $$\Phi = 0.000072\text{ Wb}$$
    To express this in scientific notation:
    $$\Phi = 7.2 \times 10^{-5}\text{ Wb}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **(B)**.
