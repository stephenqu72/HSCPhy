### Question 9
**Answer:** (B) $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{1}$

**Explanation:**
For a satellite in a circular orbit, the centripetal force is provided by gravity:
$$\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$$
Solving for orbital speed $v$:
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$
This shows that orbital speed is inversely proportional to the square root of the orbital radius: $v \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{r}}$.

Let $v_A$ be the speed of Satellite A ($r_A = 3R$) and $v_B$ be the speed of Satellite B ($r_B = 9R$). The ratio is:
$$\frac{v_A}{v_B} = \frac{\sqrt{\frac{GM}{3R}}}{\sqrt{\frac{GM}{9R}}} = \sqrt{\frac{9R}{3R}} = \sqrt{3}$$
Thus, the ratio is $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{1}$.

### Question 10
**Answer:** (C) Move the satellite to a lower orbit

**Explanation:**
The gravitational field strength $g$ at a distance $r$ from the center of a planet is given by:
$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$
- $G$ is the gravitational constant and $M$ is the mass of the planet. These remain constant.
- The mass of the satellite ($m$) does not appear in this equation, meaning the field strength is independent of the satellite's mass. Therefore, (A) and (B) are incorrect.
- As $r$ decreases (moving to a lower orbit), the denominator $r^2$ decreases, which causes the gravitational field strength $g$ to increase. Therefore, (C) is the correct action to increase the field strength.
