To solve this problem, we must apply the two fundamental principles governing the polarization of light: Malus’s Law and the effect of an ideal polarizer on unpolarized light.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Passing through the first filter:**
    When unpolarized light with intensity $I$ passes through the first polarizing filter, the transmitted intensity $I_1$ is reduced by half, regardless of the orientation of the filter.
    $$I_1 = \frac{1}{2}I$$

2.  **Passing through the second filter:**
    Now, the light is linearly polarized with intensity $I_1 = 0.5I$. This light then passes through the second filter, which is at an angle of $\theta = 30^\circ$ to the first. According to **Malus's Law**, the transmitted intensity $I_2$ is given by:
    $$I_2 = I_1 \cos^2(\theta)$$

3.  **Calculation:**
    Substitute the known values into the equation:
    $$I_2 = (0.5I) \cdot \cos^2(30^\circ)$$
    Since $\cos(30^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$, then:
    $$\cos^2(30^\circ) = \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{3}{4} = 0.75$$
    Now, multiply this by the intensity after the first filter:
    $$I_2 = 0.5I \times 0.75$$
    $$I_2 = 0.375I$$

### Conclusion:
The intensity of the light passing through the second filter is $0.375I$. Therefore, the correct option is **(A)**.
