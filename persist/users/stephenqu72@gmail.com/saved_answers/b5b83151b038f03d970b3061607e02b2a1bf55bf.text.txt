### Answer

**1. The Geiger-Marsden (Rutherford) Gold Foil Experiment**
*   **Description:** Alpha particles were fired at a thin sheet of gold foil in a vacuum. A fluorescent screen was used to detect the scattering pattern of the particles.
*   **Contribution:** While most particles passed straight through, a small fraction were deflected at large angles, and some even bounced directly back. This led Ernest Rutherford to conclude that the atom consists of a tiny, dense, positively charged central region called the **nucleus**, which contains most of the atom's mass.

**2. Chadwick’s Discovery of the Neutron**
*   **Description:** James Chadwick bombarded beryllium targets with alpha particles, resulting in the emission of a neutral, high-penetration radiation. He then directed this radiation at paraffin wax, causing protons to be ejected.
*   **Contribution:** By applying the laws of conservation of momentum and energy to the ejected protons, Chadwick proved that the radiation consisted of particles with a mass approximately equal to that of a proton but with no electric charge. This confirmed the existence of the **neutron** within the nucleus, solving the discrepancy between atomic number and atomic mass.
