### Front
What are the two conditions required to verify if a nuclear equation is valid?

### Back
- Key idea: Conservation laws in nuclear reactions.
- Formula / law: 
  1. Conservation of Nucleon Number ($A_{left} = A_{right}$)
  2. Conservation of Atomic Number ($Z_{left} = Z_{right}$)
- When to use it: Whenever determining if a fission, fusion, or decay equation is physically possible.
- Common trap: Forgetting to multiply the nucleon/atomic numbers by the stoichiometric coefficient (e.g., $3^{1}_{0}\text{n}$ contributes 3 to $A$).
