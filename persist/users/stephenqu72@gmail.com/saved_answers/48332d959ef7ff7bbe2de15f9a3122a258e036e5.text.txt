To solve this projectile motion problem, we need to analyze the horizontal component of the motion. 

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Identify the given information:**
*   Initial velocity, $v = 50.0 \, \text{m/s}$
*   Range (horizontal displacement), $\Delta x = 85.0 \, \text{m}$
*   Time of flight, $t = 10.1 \, \text{s}$
*   Angle of projection, $\theta > 45^\circ$

**2. Use the horizontal motion equation:**
In projectile motion, there is no acceleration in the horizontal direction (assuming air resistance is negligible). Therefore, the horizontal velocity $v_x$ is constant:
$$v_x = v \cos(\theta)$$
The horizontal displacement is given by:
$$\Delta x = v_x \times t$$
Substituting the horizontal velocity expression:
$$\Delta x = (v \cos(\theta)) \times t$$

**3. Rearrange to solve for $\theta$:**
$$\cos(\theta) = \frac{\Delta x}{v \times t}$$
Substitute the known values:
$$\cos(\theta) = \frac{85.0}{50.0 \times 10.1}$$
$$\cos(\theta) = \frac{85.0}{505.0}$$
$$\cos(\theta) \approx 0.168317$$

**4. Find the angle:**
$$\theta = \arccos(0.168317)$$
$$\theta \approx 80.31^\circ$$
