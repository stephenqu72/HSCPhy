To find the number of photons emitted per minute, we need to determine the total energy emitted in one minute and divide it by the energy of a single photon.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Calculate the total energy ($E_{total}$) emitted in one minute:**
Power ($P$) is the energy per unit time ($P = E/t$). Therefore, $E = P \times t$.
Given $P = 2 \text{ W}$ (which is $2 \text{ J/s}$) and time $t = 60 \text{ s}$ (one minute):
$$E_{total} = 2 \text{ J/s} \times 60 \text{ s} = 120 \text{ J}$$

**2. Calculate the energy of a single photon ($E_{photon}$):**
The energy of a photon is given by the formula $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$, where:
*   $h$ (Planck's constant) $\approx 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$
*   $c$ (speed of light) $\approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
*   $\lambda$ (wavelength) $= 600 \text{ nm} = 600 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$

$$E_{photon} = \frac{(6.63 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3 \times 10^8)}{600 \times 10^{-9}}$$
$$E_{photon} = \frac{19.89 \times 10^{-26}}{6 \times 10^{-7}} \approx 3.315 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

**3. Calculate the number of photons ($n$):**
The number of photons is the total energy divided by the energy per photon:
$$n = \frac{E_{total}}{E_{photon}} = \frac{120}{3.315 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$n \approx 36.2 \times 10^{19} = 3.62 \times 10^{20}$$

**Correct Option: (C)**
