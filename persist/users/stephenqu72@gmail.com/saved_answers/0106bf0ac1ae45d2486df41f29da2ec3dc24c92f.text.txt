To solve this problem, we must analyze the forces acting on the car as it moves around a circular banked track without friction.

### 1. Identify the Forces and Variables
*   **Mass of the car:** $m$
*   **Radius of the curve:** $r = 90\text{ m}$
*   **Speed of the car:** $v = 60\text{ km/h}$. First, convert this to SI units (m/s):
    $$v = 60 \times \frac{1000}{3600} = \frac{60}{3.6} \approx 16.67\text{ m/s}$$
*   **Forces:**
    *   **Gravity ($mg$):** Acting vertically downwards.
    *   **Normal Force ($N$):** Acting perpendicular to the surface of the road.

### 2. Vector Resolution
To keep the car moving in a horizontal circle, the net force must be directed horizontally towards the center of the circle (the centripetal force). 
*   Resolve the Normal Force ($N$) into horizontal and vertical components:
    *   Vertical component: $N \cos\theta$
    *   Horizontal component: $N \sin\theta$

Since the car is not accelerating vertically, the vertical forces must balance:
$$N \cos\theta = mg \quad \text{--- (1)}$$
The horizontal component provides the centripetal force required for circular motion:
$$N \sin\theta = \frac{mv^2}{r} \quad \text{--- (2)}$$

### 3. Calculation
Divide equation (2) by equation (1) to eliminate $N$ and $m$:
$$\frac{N \sin\theta}{N \cos\theta} = \frac{\frac{mv^2}{r}}{mg}$$
$$\tan\theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

Now, substitute the values ($g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$):
$$\tan\theta = \frac{(16.67)^2}{90 \times 9.8}$$
$$\tan\theta = \frac{277.89}{882}$$
$$\tan\theta \approx 0.315$$
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}(0.315) \approx 17.48^\circ$$

**Final Answer:** The curve is banked at an angle of approximately $17.5^\circ$.
