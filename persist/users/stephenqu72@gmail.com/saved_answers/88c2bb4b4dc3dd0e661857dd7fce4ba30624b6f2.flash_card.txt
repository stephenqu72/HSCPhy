### Front
How does gravitational field strength ($g$) vary with distance ($r$) inside a uniform planet, and what does this look like on a graph?

### Back
- Key idea: $g$ is directly proportional to the distance from the centre ($g \propto r$).
- Formula: $g = \frac{GMr}{R^3}$
- When to use it: When calculating gravitational field strength for points *inside* a planet ($r \le R$).
- Common trap: Confusing the interior linear relationship ($g \propto r$) with the exterior inverse-square law ($g \propto 1/r^2$).
