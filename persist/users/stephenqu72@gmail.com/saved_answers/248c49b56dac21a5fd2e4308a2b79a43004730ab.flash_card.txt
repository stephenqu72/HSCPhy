### Front
In uniform circular motion, how is the direction of the centripetal force related to the object's instantaneous velocity?

### Back
- Key idea: The centripetal force is always directed toward the center of the circle, while velocity is tangential to the path.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: The force vector is **perpendicular ($90^\circ$)** to the velocity vector.
- When to use it: To determine why an object changes direction without changing speed.
- Common trap: Assuming the force changes the object's speed (it only changes the direction of the velocity).
