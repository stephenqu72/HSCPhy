### Front
How do you calculate the gravitational potential energy ($U$) of a satellite in orbit?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational potential energy is negative because work must be done to move an object to infinity (where $U=0$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$ (where $r = R_E + h$)
- When to use it: To calculate the energy state of an object within a gravitational field.
- Common trap: Forgetting to add the Earth's radius ($R_E$) to the satellite's altitude ($h$) to find the orbital radius ($r$).
