To solve this problem, we use Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion, which relates the orbital period $T$ to the orbital radius $r$ of a satellite orbiting the Earth.

### Constants required:
*   Gravitational constant, $G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$
*   Mass of Earth, $M = 5.972 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$
*   Radius of Earth, $R_E \approx 6371 \text{ km} = 6.371 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$

### Steps to solve:

1.  **Convert the period to SI units (seconds):**
    $T = 92 \text{ mins} = 92 \times 60 \text{ s} = 5520 \text{ s}$

2.  **Use Kepler’s Third Law formula:**
    The relationship is given by:
    $$\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$$
    Rearranging for $r$:
    $$r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{GMT^2}{4\pi^2}}$$

3.  **Calculate the orbital radius $r$:**
    $$r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.972 \times 10^{24}) \times (5520)^2}{4\pi^2}}$$
    $$r \approx \sqrt[3]{\frac{1.2148 \times 10^{19}}{39.478}}$$
    $$r \approx \sqrt[3]{3.077 \times 10^{17}} \approx 6,751,200 \text{ m} \approx 6751.2 \text{ km}$$

4.  **Find the height $h$ above Earth's surface:**
    The orbital radius $r$ is the sum of the Earth's radius $R_E$ and the height $h$ ($r = R_E + h$).
    $$h = r - R_E$$
    $$h \approx 6751.2 \text{ km} - 6371 \text{ km} = 380.2 \text{ km}$$
