To solve this problem, we must analyze the projectile motion of the car and the constant motion of truck B in the same frame of reference.

### 1. Identify the components of the car's initial velocity
The car is launched at $v = 22\text{ ms}^{-1}$ at an angle of $\theta = 38^\circ$.
*   Horizontal velocity: $v_x = v \cos(38^\circ) = 22 \cos(38^\circ) \approx 17.337\text{ ms}^{-1}$
*   Vertical velocity: $v_y = v \sin(38^\circ) = 22 \sin(38^\circ) \approx 13.548\text{ ms}^{-1}$

### 2. Determine the time of flight
The car lands when its vertical displacement is zero. Using the formula $\Delta y = v_y t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$ (taking $g = 9.8\text{ ms}^{-2}$):
$$0 = 13.548t - 4.9t^2$$
$$t = \frac{13.548}{4.9} \approx 2.765\text{ seconds}$$

### 3. Calculate horizontal displacements
**Car's displacement:**
$$\Delta x_{car} = v_x \times t = 17.337 \times 2.765 \approx 47.937\text{ m}$$

**Truck B's displacement:**
Truck B travels at a constant velocity of $10\text{ ms}^{-1}$ for the same time $t$:
$$\Delta x_{truck} = v_{truck} \times t = 10 \times 2.765 = 27.65\text{ m}$$

### 4. Determine landing position
At $t=0$, the front of truck B is $15\text{ m}$ ahead of the launch point.
*   The position of the back of truck B relative to the launch point at $t=0$ is $15\text{ m}$.
*   The position of the back of truck B at time $t$ is: $x_{back} = 15 + 27.65 = 42.65\text{ m}$.
*   The car lands at $x_{car} = 47.937\text{ m}$.

The distance from the back of truck B is the difference:
$$\text{Distance} = x_{car} - x_{back} = 47.937 - 42.65 = 5.287\text{ m}$$

**Answer:** The car lands approximately **5.29 m** past the back of truck B.

***

### Explanation of the approach:
1.  **Resolve vectors:** Always split projectile velocity into horizontal and vertical components immediately.
2.  **Use symmetry:** Since the truck ramp and the landing surface are presumably at the same height, use the vertical motion to find the "time of flight."
3.  **Frame of reference:** Calculate the final position of both the car and the object (truck B) relative to the same starting point ($t=0$). The difference between these two positions at the time of impact gives the landing point.
