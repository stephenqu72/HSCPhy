### Front
What is the mathematical relationship and graph shape of kinetic energy ($E_k$) versus time ($t$) for an object thrown vertically upwards?

### Back
- Key idea: $E_k$ follows a quadratic relationship forming an upward-opening parabola that reaches zero at the peak height.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E_k = \frac{1}{2}m(u - gt)^2$
- When to use it: Analyzing energy changes during vertical motion under constant gravity.
- Common trap: Assuming a linear relationship or a downward-opening parabola (which would incorrectly imply negative kinetic energy).
