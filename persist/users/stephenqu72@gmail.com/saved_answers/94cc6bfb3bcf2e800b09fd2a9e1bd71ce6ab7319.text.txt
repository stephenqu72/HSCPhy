### Answer

The four fundamental forces of nature govern all physical interactions in the universe:

1.  **Gravitational Force:** This is a long-range, attractive force acting between all objects with mass. It is responsible for the formation and structure of galaxies, planetary orbits, and the large-scale evolution of the universe.
2.  **Electromagnetic Force:** This force acts between particles with electric charge. It is responsible for holding electrons in orbit around atomic nuclei, the formation of chemical bonds (molecular structure), and all phenomena related to electricity, magnetism, and light.
3.  **Strong Nuclear Force:** This is a short-range, powerful force that binds quarks together to form protons and neutrons, and overcomes electrostatic repulsion to bind protons and neutrons together within the atomic nucleus.
4.  **Weak Nuclear Force:** This is a short-range force responsible for subatomic processes, such as radioactive beta decay, and it plays a critical role in the nuclear fusion reactions that power the Sun and other stars.
