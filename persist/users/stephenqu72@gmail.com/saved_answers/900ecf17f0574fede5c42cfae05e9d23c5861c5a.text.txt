To solve this problem, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation. The gravitational field strength $g$ at a distance $r$ from the centre of a mass $M$ is given by the formula:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the initial state:**
    At the Earth's surface, the distance from the centre is $r_1 = R$. The gravitational field strength is given as $g_1 = 9.76\text{ N kg}^{-1}$.
    So, $9.76 = \frac{GM}{R^2}$.

2.  **Determine the new distance:**
    The problem asks for the field strength at a distance $2R$ *above* the surface. This means the total distance from the centre of the Earth is the radius of the Earth plus the altitude:
    $$r_2 = R (\text{surface}) + 2R (\text{altitude}) = 3R$$

3.  **Calculate the new field strength ($g_2$):**
    Using the proportionality $g \propto \frac{1}{r^2}$, we can set up a ratio:
    $$\frac{g_2}{g_1} = \frac{\frac{GM}{(3R)^2}}{\frac{GM}{R^2}}$$
    $$\frac{g_2}{g_1} = \frac{R^2}{(3R)^2} = \frac{R^2}{9R^2} = \frac{1}{9}$$

4.  **Solve for $g_2$:**
    $$g_2 = \frac{g_1}{9}$$
    $$g_2 = \frac{9.76}{9}$$
    $$g_2 \approx 1.0844\dots\text{ N kg}^{-1}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the closest value is **1.08 N kg⁻¹**.

**Correct Option: A**
