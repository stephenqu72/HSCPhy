### Front
What is the correct nuclear equation for $\beta^-$ (beta-minus) decay and what conservation laws must it satisfy?

### Back
- **Key idea:** A neutron transforms into a proton, emitting an electron ($\beta^-$) and an electron antineutrino ($\bar{\nu}$).
- **Formula / law:** $^1_0n \rightarrow ^1_1p + ^0_{-1}\beta + \bar{\nu}$ (Conservation of mass number $A$ and atomic number $Z$).
- **When to use it:** When an unstable, neutron-rich nucleus decays to achieve a more stable proton-to-neutron ratio.
- **Common trap:** Confusing $\beta^-$ decay (emits antineutrino $\bar{\nu}$) with $\beta^+$ decay (emits neutrino $\nu$ and positron).
