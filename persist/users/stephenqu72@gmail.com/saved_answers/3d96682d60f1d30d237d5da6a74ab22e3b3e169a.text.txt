### Answer

**(a)** The induced EMF graph will be a **sine wave (or negative cosine wave)** that is $90^\circ$ (or $\pi/2$ radians) out of phase with the magnetic flux graph. Specifically, when the flux is at a maximum or minimum (gradient is zero), the EMF is zero. When the flux is passing through zero (gradient is at its steepest), the magnitude of the EMF is at its maximum. Starting from $t=0$, where the flux is at a maximum positive value, the EMF starts at zero and goes negative (following Faraday's Law, $\varepsilon = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$).

**(b)** If the loop is rotated faster:
1. The **amplitude** of the induced EMF will **increase** (because $\varepsilon_{max} = NBA\omega$, so increasing angular velocity $\omega$ increases the peak voltage).
2. The **frequency** of the induced EMF will **increase** (the wave will complete more cycles in the same amount of time).

### How to solve this

1.  **Understand the relationship between Flux ($\Phi$) and EMF ($\varepsilon$):** According to Faraday's Law of Induction, the induced EMF is proportional to the negative rate of change of magnetic flux:
    $$\varepsilon = - \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$$
2.  **Analyze the gradients:**
    *   Look at the peaks and troughs of the flux graph: at these points, the slope (gradient) is zero. Therefore, the EMF must be zero at these times.
    *   Look at the points where the flux graph crosses the time axis (x-intercepts): these are the points of steepest gradient. Therefore, the EMF must be at its maximum or minimum value at these times.
3.  **Sketching (Part a):** Since the flux graph is a cosine-like curve starting at a maximum, the derivative is a negative sine wave. Your graph should start at $(0,0)$, go to a negative peak, cross the axis where the flux is at a minimum, go to a positive peak, and return to zero.
4.  **Mathematical reasoning (Part b):** Recall the formula for EMF generated by a rotating coil: $\varepsilon = NBA\omega \sin(\omega t)$.
    *   $\omega$ (angular velocity) is directly proportional to how fast the loop rotates.
    *   Increasing $\omega$ increases the coefficient $NBA\omega$, resulting in a larger peak EMF (amplitude).
    *   Increasing $\omega$ also increases the frequency term inside the sine function ($\omega t$), meaning the oscillations occur more rapidly.
