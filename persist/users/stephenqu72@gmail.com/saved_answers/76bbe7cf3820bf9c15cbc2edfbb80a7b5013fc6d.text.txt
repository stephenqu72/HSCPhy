**Solution:**

### Part (c)
**Discussion:**
Hydrostatic equilibrium is the state of balance in a star where the inward force of gravity is perfectly balanced by the outward pressure generated by nuclear fusion in the core.

1.  **Gravity:** The massive core of Barnard’s star creates an immense inward gravitational pull, attempting to collapse the star.
2.  **Fusion (Pressure):** To counteract this, Barnard's star fuses hydrogen into helium in its core via the proton-proton chain. This release of energy creates a thermal pressure gradient that pushes outward.
3.  **Stability:** If the star compresses slightly, the core density and temperature rise, increasing the rate of fusion and thus the outward pressure, which pushes the star back to equilibrium.
4.  **Nuclear Equation:** The primary reaction is the proton-proton chain:
    $$4^1_1H \rightarrow ^4_2He + 2e^+ + 2\nu_e + \text{energy}$$
    (Or written as: $^1_1H + ^1_1H \rightarrow ^2_1H + e^+ + \nu_e$, followed by further steps to helium).

### How to solve:
*   **For (a) and (b):** You must memorize the axes of the HR diagram (Temperature on the x-axis, typically reversed; Luminosity on the y-axis). Recognizing that "red dwarf" and "M-class" imply the bottom-right of the main sequence is key.
*   **For (c):** Always link the concept of "equilibrium" to a "balance of forces." Never forget to explicitly state that the inward force is **gravity** and the outward force is **radiation/thermal pressure** from fusion. Always include the proton-proton chain equation as it is the signature process for low-mass stars like our Sun or Barnard's star.
