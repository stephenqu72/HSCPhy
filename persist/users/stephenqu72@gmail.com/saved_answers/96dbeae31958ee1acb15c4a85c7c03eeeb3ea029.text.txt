### Part (a)
**Answer:**
The Sun generates its energy through nuclear fusion in its core, where high temperatures and pressures allow hydrogen nuclei (protons) to fuse into helium nuclei. The mass of the resulting helium nucleus is slightly less than the sum of the masses of the original hydrogen nuclei. According to Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle, $E = \Delta mc^2$, this "lost" mass is converted into a tremendous amount of thermal and radiant energy. This energy travels to Earth in the form of electromagnetic radiation (EMR). Unlike mechanical waves, EMR consists of oscillating electric and magnetic fields that do not require a material medium to propagate, allowing the energy to travel through the vacuum of space at the speed of light ($c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$).

**How to solve:**
1. **Identify the source:** Mention "nuclear fusion" specifically—the proton-proton chain reaction.
2. **Link mass to energy:** Explicitly mention $E = \Delta mc^2$ to explain *how* mass becomes energy.
3. **Explain the travel:** Emphasize the wave nature of EMR. Because it is a self-propagating electromagnetic field, it requires no medium (unlike sound waves), which is the key to why it traverses the vacuum.

### Part (b)
**Answer:**
The Sun produces a full range of electromagnetic radiation because it behaves as a near-perfect blackbody radiator. The Sun’s surface (photosphere) has a temperature of approximately $5800 \text{ K}$. According to Planck’s law and Wien’s displacement law, a body at this temperature emits radiation across a continuous spectrum. While the peak intensity is in the visible light spectrum, the thermal motion of charged particles within the Sun ensures that photons are emitted across the entire spectrum, ranging from high-energy gamma rays and X-rays (from the core) to lower-energy infrared and radio waves.

**How to solve:**
1. **Model the Sun:** Use the term "blackbody radiation." This is the standard physical model for a star's emission.
2. **Explain the mechanism:** Relate the "full range" to the thermal kinetic energy of the particles at the Sun's surface. Because the Sun is very hot, the range of frequencies (and thus wavelengths) produced follows a continuous distribution curve (the Planck distribution).
3. **Connect to HSC terminology:** Linking the concept to the "thermal emission" of a hot object is the mark of a top-tier answer.
