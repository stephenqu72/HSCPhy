### Front
How does the inclusion of air resistance affect the time of flight and horizontal displacement of a projectile compared to a vacuum?

### Back
- Key idea: Air resistance acts as a resistive force opposing motion in both vertical and horizontal components.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{net} = ma$ and $s = u_yt + \frac{1}{2}at^2$. No fixed constant.
- When to use it: When comparing real-world projectile motion to ideal vacuum conditions.
- Common trap: Assuming air resistance only affects horizontal motion; remember it reduces downward acceleration, thereby increasing the time of flight.
