### Front
How do you calculate the centripetal force ($F_c$) acting on an object in circular motion, and what units must you use for the calculation?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Centripetal force is the net force acting towards the center of a circular path required to keep an object moving in a circle.
- **Formula / law:** $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- **When to use it:** Whenever an object moves in a uniform circular path and you need to determine the required force or identify the source of that force (e.g., tension, gravity, friction).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., speed must be in $\text{m/s}$, mass in $\text{kg}$, and radius in $\text{m}$) before calculating.
