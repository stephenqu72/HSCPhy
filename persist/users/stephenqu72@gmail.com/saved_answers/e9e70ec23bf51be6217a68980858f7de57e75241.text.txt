To determine which galaxy is closest to us, we need to apply the principles of the Doppler effect as it relates to cosmology (redshift).

### Answer
The correct answer is **B. $V$**.

### Explanation
1.  **Understanding Redshift:** In cosmology, light from distant galaxies is shifted toward the red end of the spectrum due to the expansion of the universe. This is known as **redshift**. The further away a galaxy is from Earth, the faster it is moving away from us (Hubble's Law), and the greater the shift of its spectral lines toward the red end.
2.  **Analyzing the Diagram:** 
    *   The "Sun" acts as our reference point (zero relative velocity). 
    *   The lines in the spectra of the galaxies are shifted relative to the position of the lines in the Sun's spectrum.
    *   A shift toward the right (toward the "Red" label) indicates that the galaxy is receding from us.
    *   The **closest** galaxy will show the **smallest amount of redshift** (the spectral lines will be closest to the positions seen in the Sun's spectrum).
3.  **Comparing the Galaxies:**
    *   **Galaxy $U$:** Significant shift toward the red.
    *   **Galaxy $V$:** Shows spectral lines almost perfectly aligned with the Sun's reference lines. This indicates a very small (or negligible) redshift, meaning it has a very low recessional velocity and is therefore the closest to us.
    *   **Galaxy $W$:** Large shift toward the red (farthest galaxy).
    *   **Galaxy $X$:** Moderate shift toward the red.

Since Galaxy $V$ exhibits the least amount of redshift compared to the reference spectrum of the Sun, it is the galaxy moving away the slowest, implying it is the closest to us.
