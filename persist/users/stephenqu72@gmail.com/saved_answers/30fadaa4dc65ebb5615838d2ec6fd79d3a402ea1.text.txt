### How to solve this type of problem:
1.  **Visualize the Path:** Always draw the velocity vector as a tangent to the curve at the specific point.
2.  **Separate Components:** Remember that $v_x$ is constant, while $v_y$ changes due to gravity. At the peak ($Y$), $v_y = 0$, so the net velocity is just $v_x$.
3.  **Identify Constant Acceleration:** In standard HSC physics projectile problems, acceleration is always $9.8 \, m/s^2$ downwards, regardless of the object's position or velocity. Avoid the common trap of thinking acceleration is zero at the peak!
