To solve this problem, we apply the principle of **Conservation of Energy**. 

### Explanation:

1.  **Energy Transformation**: When a charged particle is released from rest in a uniform electric field, the electric potential energy ($E_p$) lost by the particle is converted into kinetic energy ($E_k$).
2.  **Electric Potential Energy**: The work done on a charge $q$ moving through a potential difference $V$ is given by:
    $$E_p = qV$$
3.  **Kinetic Energy**: The kinetic energy of a particle with mass $m$ and velocity $v$ is:
    $$E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
4.  **Setting them equal**: Assuming no energy is lost, we set the potential energy equal to the kinetic energy:
    $$qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
5.  **Rearranging for the variables**:
    *   To isolate $v$: 
        $$v^2 = \frac{2qV}{m} \implies v = \sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}}$$
    *   To isolate $m$: 
        $$m = \frac{2qV}{v^2}$$

Looking at the options provided:
*   (A) $v = \sqrt{\frac{2q}{Vm}}$ is incorrect.
*   (B) $q = \frac{Vm}{v^2}$ is incorrect.
*   (C) $m = \frac{2qV}{v^2}$ matches our derived equation.
*   (D) $V = \frac{mv}{2q}$ is incorrect (it misses the square on the velocity).

The correct expression is **(C)**.

###ANSWER
The correct answer is **(C)**.
