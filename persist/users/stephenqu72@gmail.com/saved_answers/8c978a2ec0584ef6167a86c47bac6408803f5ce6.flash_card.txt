### Front
How do you relate the work done on an object moving in a vertical circle to its change in energy?

### Back
- Key idea: Net work done on an object equals its change in kinetic energy ($\Delta K$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Work-Energy Theorem: $W_{net} = \Delta E_k$.
- When to use it: Whenever a force causes a change in an object's speed, even if the total mechanical energy is also changing due to potential energy shifts.
- Common trap: Assuming work is zero or constant; work is a process, not a state function, and is required whenever kinetic energy changes.
