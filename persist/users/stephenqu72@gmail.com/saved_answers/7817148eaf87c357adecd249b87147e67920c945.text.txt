To solve this physics problem, we need to analyze the energy and forces acting on the alpha particle as it approaches the gold nucleus in a Rutherford scattering scenario.

### Analysis:

1.  **Electric Potential Energy:**
    The electric potential energy $U$ of a two-charge system is given by:
    $$U = \frac{k q_1 q_2}{r}$$
    Where $q_1$ is the charge of the alpha particle, $q_2$ is the charge of the gold nucleus, $k$ is Coulomb's constant, and $r$ is the distance between them. 
    As the alpha particle approaches the gold nucleus, the distance $r$ decreases. Since both charges are positive, $U$ is positive. As $r$ becomes smaller, the value of $U$ becomes larger. Therefore, the electric potential energy **increases**.

2.  **Magnitude of Momentum:**
    The total energy of the alpha particle is conserved. As the alpha particle approaches the nucleus, it experiences a repulsive electrostatic force. This force does negative work on the alpha particle, causing it to slow down. Kinetic energy ($K$) is converted into electric potential energy ($U$).
    Since kinetic energy is related to momentum ($p$) by the equation $K = \frac{p^2}{2m}$, if the kinetic energy decreases as the particle slows down, the magnitude of the momentum $p$ must also decrease. Therefore, the magnitude of the momentum **decreases**.

### Conclusion:
- The potential energy **increases**.
- The magnitude of momentum **decreases**.
