To determine the minimum refractive index of the liquid that allows light to cross the interface, we need to consider the condition for **Total Internal Reflection (TIR)**.

### Explanation
When light travels from a medium with a higher refractive index ($n_1$) to a medium with a lower refractive index ($n_2$), it can undergo total internal reflection if the angle of incidence exceeds the critical angle. 

The question asks for the condition where light will "just cross" the interface. This occurs exactly at the **critical angle** ($\theta_c$). At this point, the angle of refraction ($\theta_r$) into the liquid is $90^\circ$. 

Using **Snell's Law**:
$$n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2)$$

Where:
- $n_1 = 1.75$ (refractive index of glass)
- $\theta_1 = 62.0^\circ$ (angle of incidence)
- $n_2 = ?$ (refractive index of liquid)
- $\theta_2 = 90^\circ$ (angle of refraction for the light to just escape the interface)

Substitute the known values into the equation:
$$1.75 \times \sin(62.0^\circ) = n_2 \times \sin(90^\circ)$$

Since $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$:
$$n_2 = 1.75 \times \sin(62.0^\circ)$$

Using a calculator:
$$\sin(62.0^\circ) \approx 0.8829$$
$$n_2 = 1.75 \times 0.8829 \approx 1.545$$

Rounding this to two decimal places, we get $1.55$. Therefore, the value closest to our result is **B**.

### Answer
The correct option is **B**.
