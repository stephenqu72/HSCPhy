To determine the observed length of the spaceship, we use the principle of **length contraction** from Einstein’s Special Theory of Relativity.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) 16.3 m**.

### Explanation
When an object is moving at a relativistic speed relative to an observer, its length in the direction of motion is measured to be shorter than its proper length ($L_0$). The relationship is given by the formula:

$$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$

**1. Identify the given values:**
* Proper length ($L_0$) = $86.0\text{ m}$
* Velocity ($v$) = $2.7 \times 10^8\text{ ms}^{-1}$
* Speed of light ($c$) $\approx 3.0 \times 10^8\text{ ms}^{-1}$

**2. Calculate the ratio $\frac{v}{c}$:**
$$\frac{v}{c} = \frac{2.7 \times 10^8}{3.0 \times 10^8} = 0.9$$

**3. Apply the length contraction formula:**
$$L = 86.0 \times \sqrt{1 - (0.9)^2}$$
$$L = 86.0 \times \sqrt{1 - 0.81}$$
$$L = 86.0 \times \sqrt{0.19}$$
$$L = 86.0 \times 0.435889...$$
$$L \approx 37.486\text{ m}$$
