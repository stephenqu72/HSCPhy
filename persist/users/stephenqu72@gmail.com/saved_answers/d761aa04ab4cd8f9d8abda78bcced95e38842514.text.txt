To solve this problem, we need to consider the motion of a charged particle (a proton) moving in a magnetic field. When a charged particle moves perpendicular to a magnetic field, the magnetic force acts as a centripetal force, keeping the particle in a circular orbit.

### 1. Identify the relevant physics principles
The magnetic force $F_B$ acting on a charged particle is given by:
$$F_B = qvB$$
(Since the motion is circular, we assume the magnetic field is perpendicular to the velocity).

The centripetal force $F_c$ required to keep an object in circular motion is:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Equating the two ($F_B = F_c$):
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Solving for the magnetic field strength $B$:
$$B = \frac{mv}{qr}$$

### 2. Identify the constants and values
*   Mass of a proton ($m$): $1.673 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$
*   Charge of a proton ($q$): $1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
*   Velocity ($v$): $1.25 \times 10^8\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Radius ($r$): $82\text{ m}$

### 3. Calculation
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$B = \frac{(1.673 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}) \times (1.25 \times 10^8\text{ m s}^{-1})}{(1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (82\text{ m})}$$

$$B = \frac{2.09125 \times 10^{-19}}{1.31364 \times 10^{-17}}$$

$$B \approx 0.0159 \text{ T}$$

***Wait—correction on relativistic effects:***
In HSC Physics, for velocities as high as $1.25 \times 10^8\text{ m s}^{-1}$ (which is approx $0.4c$), we should consider if relativistic mass must be used.
The relativistic mass $m_v = \gamma m_0$, where $\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$.
$v/c = 1.25 \times 10^8 / 3 \times 10^8 \approx 0.4167$
$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.1736}} \approx 1.095$
New mass $m_v \approx 1.095 \times 1.673 \times 10^{-27} \approx 1.832 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$

Recalculating with relativistic mass:
$$B = \frac{(1.832 \times 10^{-27}) \times (1.25 \times 10^8)}{(1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times 82}$$
$$B \approx \frac{2.29 \times 10^{-19}}{1.3136 \times 10^{-17}} \approx 0.01743 \text{ T}$$

Rounding to 3 significant figures, we get $0.0174 \text{ T}$, which is closest to **(A) 0.0175 T**.
