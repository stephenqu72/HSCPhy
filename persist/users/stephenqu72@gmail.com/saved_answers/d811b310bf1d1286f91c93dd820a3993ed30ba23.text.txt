### Answer
Energy is conserved in an elliptical orbit because gravity is a **conservative force**. As the satellite moves along its elliptical path, it constantly undergoes a transformation between its gravitational potential energy ($U$) and its kinetic energy ($K$). 

When the satellite approaches the Earth (moving toward perigee), it speeds up, causing a decrease in gravitational potential energy and an equivalent increase in kinetic energy. Conversely, as the satellite moves away from the Earth (toward apogee), it slows down, causing an increase in potential energy and a decrease in kinetic energy. Throughout the entire orbit, the total mechanical energy ($E = K + U$) remains constant because there are no non-conservative forces (like air resistance) doing work on the system.
