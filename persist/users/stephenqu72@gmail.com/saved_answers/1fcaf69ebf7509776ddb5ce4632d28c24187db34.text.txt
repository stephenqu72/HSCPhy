### (a) Calculate the number of photons
**1. Identify the given values:**
* Energy required ($E_{total}$) = $5.50 \times 10^{-4} \text{ J}$
* Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $425 \text{ nm} = 425 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$
* Planck’s constant ($h$) = $6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$
* Speed of light ($c$) = $3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

**2. Formula:**
First, calculate the energy of one photon ($E_p$):
$$E_p = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$
Then, the number of photons ($N$) is:
$$N = \frac{E_{total}}{E_p}$$

**3. Calculation:**
$$E_p = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34})(3.00 \times 10^8)}{425 \times 10^{-9}} \approx 4.677 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$
$$N = \frac{5.50 \times 10^{-4}}{4.677 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 1.18 \times 10^{15} \text{ photons}$$

### (b) Calculate the maximum kinetic energy
**1. Concept:** Use Einstein’s photoelectric equation: $E_k = E_p - \phi$.
Since $\phi$ is in eV, it is easiest to convert $E_p$ to eV first ($1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$).

**2. Calculation:**
$$E_p \text{ (in eV)} = \frac{4.677 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}}{1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J/eV}} \approx 2.92 \text{ eV}$$
$$E_k = 2.92 \text{ eV} - 2.29 \text{ eV} = 0.63 \text{ eV}$$

### (c) Contrast the 1 W blue light and 1 W red light
**Explanation:** 
According to the particle model of light, the power of a beam is the total energy transferred per second ($P = N \times E_p$). 
* Since red light has a longer wavelength than blue light, each individual red photon has **lower energy** ($E_p = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$) than a blue photon. 
* To maintain the same power output of $1 \text{ W}$, a red laser must emit a **greater number of photons per second** compared to a blue laser.
