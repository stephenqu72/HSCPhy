### Front
Why is a linear model ($\tau = k\theta$) invalid for describing the relationship between torque and angle in a mechanical system?

### Back
- Key idea: Torque is defined by a sinusoidal function, not a linear one.
- Formula: $\tau = rF\sin(\theta)$
- When to use it: To determine the torque produced by an applied force at varying angles.
- Common trap: Assuming small-angle approximations (where $\sin\theta \approx \theta$ in radians) apply to large angles, leading to overestimation as $\theta$ approaches $90^\circ$.
