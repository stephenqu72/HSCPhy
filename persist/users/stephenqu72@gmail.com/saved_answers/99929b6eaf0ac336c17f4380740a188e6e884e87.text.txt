To determine the path of the electron, we must analyze the forces acting upon it.

### Answer
The correct answer is **C. A parabolic path up the page**.

### Explanation

1.  **Analyze the Force Direction**: The electric field lines (represented by arrows) point **down the page**. By definition, an electric field points in the direction a *positive* charge would be pushed. Since an electron is a *negatively* charged particle, it experiences an electrostatic force in the direction **opposite** to the electric field. Therefore, the force on the electron is directed **up the page**.
2.  **Determine the Nature of the Path**: 
    *   The electron enters the field with a constant horizontal velocity. 
    *   Once inside the field, there is no horizontal force, so its horizontal velocity remains constant.
    *   Simultaneously, there is a constant vertical force (upward) acting on the electron due to the electric field ($F = qE$). According to Newton’s Second Law ($F = ma$), this constant force produces a constant vertical acceleration.
    *   When an object has a constant velocity in one dimension and a constant acceleration in the perpendicular dimension, the resulting trajectory is a **parabola**.
3.  **Conclusion**: Because the electron is pulled upward by the electric field while moving forward, its path curves upward. Thus, it follows a parabolic path directed up the page.
