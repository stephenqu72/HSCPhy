To determine the net force on each wire, we apply the fundamental principle of electromagnetism: **parallel wires carrying current in the same direction attract each other, while parallel wires carrying current in opposite directions repel each other.**

Let the wires be labeled from left to right as $W_1$ ($2\text{ A}$, downwards), $W_2$ ($3\text{ A}$, upwards), and $W_3$ ($1\text{ A}$, upwards).

### 1. Analysis of forces:

*   **Wire 1 ($W_1$):**
    *   $W_1$ and $W_2$ have opposite currents (down vs. up), so they **repel**. $W_2$ pushes $W_1$ to the **left**.
    *   $W_1$ and $W_3$ have opposite currents (down vs. up), so they **repel**. $W_3$ pushes $W_1$ to the **left**.
    *   Since both forces are to the left, the net force on $W_1$ is to the **left**.

*   **Wire 2 ($W_2$):**
    *   $W_2$ and $W_1$ have opposite currents, so they **repel**. $W_1$ pushes $W_2$ to the **right**.
    *   $W_2$ and $W_3$ have currents in the same direction (both up), so they **attract**. $W_3$ pulls $W_2$ to the **right**.
    *   Since both forces are to the right, the net force on $W_2$ is to the **right**.

*   **Wire 3 ($W_3$):**
    *   $W_3$ and $W_1$ have opposite currents, so they **repel**. $W_1$ pushes $W_3$ to the **right**.
    *   $W_3$ and $W_2$ have currents in the same direction, so they **attract**. $W_2$ pulls $W_3$ to the **left**.
    *   The force from $W_2$ (attraction) is significantly stronger than the force from $W_1$ because $W_3$ is closer to $W_2$ than to $W_1$ and the current in $W_2$ is larger than in $W_1$. Therefore, the net force on $W_3$ is to the **left**.

### Conclusion:
The wires experiencing a net force to the left are $W_1$ and $W_3$. That makes a total of **2** wires.
