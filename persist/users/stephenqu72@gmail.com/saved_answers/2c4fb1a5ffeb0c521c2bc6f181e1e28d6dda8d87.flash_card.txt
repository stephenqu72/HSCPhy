### Front
What is the relationship between a hanging mass ($M$) and a mass ($m$) undergoing uniform horizontal circular motion in a tethered system at equilibrium?

### Back
- Key idea: The tension ($T$) in the string provides the centripetal force for the rotating mass and supports the weight of the hanging mass.
- Formula / law: $Mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: Circular motion problems involving a tethered system where the tension is equal to the weight of the suspended mass.
- Common trap: Forgetting that $g$ must be included when converting centripetal force to the weight of the hanging mass ($T = Mg$, not $T = M$).
