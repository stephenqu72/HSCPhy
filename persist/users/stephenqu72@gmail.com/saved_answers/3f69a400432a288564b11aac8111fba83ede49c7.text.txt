### Answer

The orbital radius is approximately $1.27 \times 10^7\text{ m}$ (or $12,700\text{ km}$).

---

### Explanation

To solve this problem, we use the formula for the gravitational field strength (acceleration due to gravity) at a distance $r$ from the centre of a planet:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

**1. Identify the known constants:**
*   Gravitational constant $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2\text{ kg}^{-2}$
*   Mass of the Earth $M = 5.97 \times 10^{24}\text{ kg}$
*   Target gravitational acceleration $g = 2.45\text{ ms}^{-2}$

**2. Rearrange the formula to solve for $r$:**
$$r^2 = \frac{GM}{g}$$
$$r = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{g}}$$

**3. Substitute the values:**
$$r = \sqrt{\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24})}{2.45}}$$
$$r = \sqrt{\frac{3.982 \times 10^{14}}{2.45}}$$
$$r = \sqrt{1.625 \times 10^{14}}$$
$$r \approx 1.275 \times 10^7\text{ m}$$

Rounding to match the precision of the given data, we get $1.27 \times 10^7\text{ m}$.
