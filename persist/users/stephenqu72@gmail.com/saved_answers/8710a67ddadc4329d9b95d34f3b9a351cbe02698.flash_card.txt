### Front
In vacuum projectile motion, what are the directions of velocity and acceleration at the peak (highest point) of the trajectory?

### Back
- Key idea: Velocity is horizontal (tangent to path); acceleration is constant and downward (gravity).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_y = -g \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ (downwards).
- When to use it: To determine projectile behavior at any point in the flight.
- Common trap: Assuming acceleration is zero at the peak because vertical velocity is zero.
