### Front
How is electric potential difference defined in terms of work and charge?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Potential difference ($V$) is the work done per unit charge to move a charge between two points.
- **Formula:** $V = \frac{W}{q}$
- **When to use it:** When calculating the energy required to move a charge within an electric field or potential difference between two points.
- **Common trap:** Failing to convert units (e.g., $\mu\text{C}$ to $\text{C}$ by multiplying by $10^{-6}$) and incorrect scientific notation handling with negative exponents.
