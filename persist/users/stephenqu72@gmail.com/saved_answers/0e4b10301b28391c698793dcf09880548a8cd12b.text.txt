### Answer
The correct answer is **(B)**.

### Explanation
To solve this, we need to distinguish between **Classical Electromagnetism** (Maxwell) and **Quantum Physics**:

1.  **Maxwell's Equations:** James Clerk Maxwell developed a set of equations that successfully unified electricity and magnetism. These equations describe light as a self-propagating **wave** of oscillating electric and magnetic fields. From his equations, one can derive:
    *   The wave nature of light (Option C).
    *   The speed of light in a vacuum, calculated as $c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$ (Option A).
    *   The existence of the entire electromagnetic spectrum, including radio waves, X-rays, etc. (Option D).

2.  **The Photoelectric Effect:** This phenomenon involves the emission of electrons from a metal surface when light shines upon it. Classical wave theory (Maxwell) failed to explain why the kinetic energy of the emitted electrons depends on the frequency of the light rather than its intensity. It was Albert Einstein who solved this in 1905 by proposing that light exists as discrete packets of energy called **photons** ($E = hf$). This effectively established the **particle nature** of light, which is a quantum mechanical concept that falls outside the scope of Maxwell’s classical wave-based equations.

Therefore, because Maxwell’s equations treat light strictly as a classical wave, they cannot account for the particle-like behavior observed in the photoelectric effect.
