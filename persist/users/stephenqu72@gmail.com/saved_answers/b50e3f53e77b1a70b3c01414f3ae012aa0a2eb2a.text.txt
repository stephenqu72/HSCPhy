### Part (b)
**Explanation and Calculation:**
To solve this, we use the formula for the force per unit length between two parallel wires:
$$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2 \pi d}$$

**1. Determine the force between $Y$ and $Z$:**
Since there is a force of attraction, currents in $Y$ and $Z$ must be in the same direction (both to the right).
The force on $Y$ due to $Z$ is:
$$\left(\frac{F}{L}\right)_{YZ} = \frac{(2 \times 10^{-7}) \times 20 \times 20}{0.30} = 2.667 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{N/m (upwards)}$$

**2. Determine the force required from $X$:**
For wire $Y$ to have zero net force, the force from $X$ must be equal in magnitude and opposite in direction to the force from $Z$. Since the force from $Z$ is upward (attraction), the force from $X$ must be **downward**. 
For an attractive force to be directed downwards (away from $X$), the current in $X$ must be in the **opposite direction** to $Y$. Since $Y$ is to the right, $X$ must be to the **left**.

**3. Calculate the magnitude of $I_X$:**
Set the force from $X$ equal to the force from $Z$:
$$\frac{(2 \times 10^{-7}) \times I_X \times 20}{0.75} = 2.667 \times 10^{-4}$$
$$I_X \times (5.333 \times 10^{-6}) = 2.667 \times 10^{-4}$$
$$I_X = \frac{2.667 \times 10^{-4}}{5.333 \times 10^{-6}} = 50 \, \text{A}$$

**Final Answer:** The current in $X$ is **50 A** flowing to the **left**.
