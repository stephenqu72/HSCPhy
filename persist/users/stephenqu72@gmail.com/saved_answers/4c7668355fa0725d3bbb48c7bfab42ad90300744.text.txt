### Answer

**(a)**
To block the light, the cogwheel must rotate such that a tooth moves into the position previously occupied by a gap during the time it takes for light to travel to the mirror and back.
*   Total distance $d = 2 \times 500\text{ m} = 1000\text{ m}$.
*   Using $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$, the time taken is $t = \frac{d}{c} = \frac{1000}{3.00 \times 10^8} \approx 3.33 \times 10^{-6}\text{ s}$.
*   There are 250 teeth and 250 gaps, totaling 500 divisions. The angle for one division is $\theta = \frac{2\pi}{500} = \frac{\pi}{250}\text{ rad}$.
*   Angular velocity $\omega = \frac{\theta}{t} = \frac{\pi / 250}{3.33 \times 10^{-6}} \approx 3770\text{ rad/s}$.

**(b) i.**
The lower rotation speed indicates that the light takes a longer time to travel to the mirror and back through the water than through the air. Since $t = \frac{d}{v}$, a larger time for the same distance implies that the velocity of light in water ($v_w$) is slower than the velocity of light in a vacuum/air ($v_a$). This confirms that $v_w < c$.

**(b) ii.**
Huygens' principle, which describes light as a wave, is supported by phenomena such as **diffraction** (the bending of light around obstacles or through slits) or **interference** (such as Young's Double Slit Experiment), which cannot be explained by a purely corpuscular (particle) model.
