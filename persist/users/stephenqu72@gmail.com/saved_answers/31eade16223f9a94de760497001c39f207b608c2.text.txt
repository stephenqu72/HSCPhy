To solve this problem, we use Albert Einstein's mass-energy equivalence principle, which relates the energy produced to the mass lost during a nuclear process.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the relationship:**
    The energy $E$ produced by a mass $m$ is given by the formula:
    $$E = mc^2$$
    Where $c$ is the speed of light, approximately $3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$.

2.  **Define the variables:**
    *   The power output is given as $P = 4.35 \times 10^{31} \text{ W}$.
    *   Since Power is energy per unit time ($P = E/t$), for a duration of $t = 1 \text{ second}$, the energy produced is:
        $$E = 4.35 \times 10^{31} \text{ J}$$

3.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for mass ($m$):**
    $$m = \frac{E}{c^2}$$

4.  **Substitute the values into the equation:**
    $$m = \frac{4.35 \times 10^{31} \text{ J}}{(3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s})^2}$$
    $$m = \frac{4.35 \times 10^{31}}{9.00 \times 10^{16}}$$
    $$m = 4.8333... \times 10^{14} \text{ kg}$$

Comparing our result to the options provided, we see that it matches option B.

**Answer:** The correct option is **B**.
