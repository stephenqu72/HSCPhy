### Front
How does the gravitational potential energy ($U$) of a projectile vary over time, and what is the characteristic shape of its graph?

### Back
- Key idea: $U$ is directly proportional to vertical displacement ($h$). Since $h(t)$ follows a quadratic function, $U(t)$ follows a downward-opening parabola.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $U = mgh$ and $h(t) = v_{0y}t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$.
- When to use it: To determine energy transformations or displacement-time relationships for projectiles.
- Common trap: Mistaking the graph for a displacement-time graph of velocity; remember that $U$ is always positive (relative to the launch plane) and parabolic, not linear.
