### Answer
There are two main pillars of evidence that support the Big Bang theory:

1.  **Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation (CMB):** This is the "afterglow" of the Big Bang. It is uniform, low-energy microwave radiation that permeates the entire universe. Its existence confirms that the early universe was extremely hot and dense, and has cooled as the universe expanded.
2.  **Hubble’s Law and Redshift:** Observations by Edwin Hubble showed that galaxies are moving away from us in all directions, with the recessional velocity being proportional to their distance ($v = H_0 d$). This indicates that the universe is expanding. By extrapolating this expansion backward in time, we arrive at a single point of origin, the Big Bang.

*(Optional additional point for depth: The observed abundance of light elements—specifically the ratio of Hydrogen to Helium—matches the predictions made by models of the early universe's nucleosynthesis.)*
