### Part (a)
**Answer:** 
The mass of the ruler is $143.1\text{ g}$ (or $0.1431\text{ kg}$). 
**Assumption:** The mass of the ruler is uniformly distributed (the centre of mass is at the $50.0\text{ cm}$ mark).

**Explanation:**
To find the mass, we use the principle of moments about the pivot point at $1.0\text{ cm}$. 
- The pivot is at $x_p = 1.0\text{ cm}$.
- The centre of mass of the uniform metre ruler is at $x_{cm} = 50.0\text{ cm}$.
- The balance is at $x_b = 30.0\text{ cm}$.
- Distance from pivot to centre of mass: $d_{cm} = 50.0 - 1.0 = 49.0\text{ cm}$.
- Distance from pivot to balance: $d_b = 30.0 - 1.0 = 29.0\text{ cm}$.
Sum of clockwise moments = Sum of anticlockwise moments about the pivot:
$$Mg(d_{cm}) = F_b(d_b)$$
Where $F_b$ is the force measured by the balance ($214.66 \times 10^{-3} \text{ kg} \times 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$).
Since $g$ cancels out:
$$M(49.0) = 214.66(29.0)$$
$$M = \frac{214.66 \times 29.0}{49.0} \approx 127.06\text{ g}$$
*Correction:* Wait, looking at the diagram, the balance measures the force at $30\text{ cm}$. The ruler mass $M$ acts at $50\text{ cm}$. 
$M \times (50-1) = 214.66 \times (30-1)$ 
$M \times 49 = 214.66 \times 29 \Rightarrow M = 127.06\text{ g}$.

**Answer:** The magnetic field is directed **out of the page** (towards the reader).

**Explanation:**
The balance reading increases as current increases. This means there is a downward force exerted on the balance. By Newton's Third Law, there is an **upward** magnetic force ($F = ILB$) acting on the wire. Using the Right-Hand Palm Rule, if the current is flowing from $P$ to $Q$ (left to right), for the force to be upward, the fingers (field) must point out of the page.

### Part (c)
**Answer:** $B \approx 0.024\text{ T}$

**Explanation:**
1. **Find $\sigma$:** This is the gradient of the graph of Force vs Current. The change in mass $\Delta M$ is $(238.5 - 237.5) \text{ g} = 1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ kg}$ over $\Delta I = (4.6 - 1.0) \text{ A} = 3.6 \text{ A}$.
   The change in force is $\Delta F = \Delta M \times g = 1.0 \times 10^{-3} \times 9.81 = 0.00981 \text{ N}$.
   $\sigma = \frac{\Delta F}{\Delta I} = \frac{0.00981}{3.6} = 0.002725 \text{ N/A}$.
2. **Find $L$:** The field covers the region from $85\text{ cm}$ to $90\text{ cm}$, so $L = 0.05 \text{ m}$.
3. **Calculate $B$:**
   $$B = \frac{\sigma}{3L} = \frac{0.002725}{3 \times 0.05} = \frac{0.002725}{0.15} \approx 0.018 \text{ T}$$
