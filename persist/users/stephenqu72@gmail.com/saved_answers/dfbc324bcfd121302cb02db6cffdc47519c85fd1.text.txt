### Solution and Explanation

To solve this physics problem, we must analyze the sphere in static equilibrium. The forces acting on the sphere are the gravitational force ($mg$ downwards), the electrostatic force ($F_E = qE$ horizontally to the right), and the tension in the thread ($T$ at an angle of $20^\circ$ to the vertical).

#### (a) Vector Diagram
In the space provided, you should draw the sphere and represent the three forces as vectors originating from it:
1.  **Weight ($mg$):** A vector pointing vertically downwards.
2.  **Electrostatic force ($F_E$):** A vector pointing horizontally to the right (towards the negative plate).
3.  **Tension ($T$):** A vector pointing along the thread, up and to the left.

The sum of these three vectors must form a closed triangle (or be equivalent to zero) because the sphere is in equilibrium.

---

#### (b) Calculation of the mass
To find the mass, we use the conditions for equilibrium: $\sum F_x = 0$ and $\sum F_y = 0$.

**1. Calculate the Electric Field ($E$):**
Between two parallel plates, $E = \frac{V}{d}$.
Given $V = 60\text{ V}$ and $d = 3.0\text{ cm} = 0.03\text{ m}$:
$$E = \frac{60}{0.03} = 2000\text{ V/m}$$

**2. Calculate the Electrostatic Force ($F_E$):**
$$F_E = qE = (2.5 \times 10^{-6}\text{ C}) \times 2000\text{ V/m} = 0.005\text{ N}$$

**3. Relate Forces to the Angle:**
Looking at the free-body diagram, the forces form a right-angled triangle where $\tan(\theta) = \frac{F_E}{mg}$, where $\theta = 20^\circ$.
Rearranging for $m$:
$$m = \frac{F_E}{g \cdot \tan(20^\circ)}$$

Using $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$:
$$m = \frac{0.005}{9.8 \times \tan(20^\circ)}$$
$$m \approx \frac{0.005}{9.8 \times 0.36397}$$
$$m \approx \frac{0.005}{3.567}$$
$$m \approx 0.0014017\text{ kg}$$

**Final Answer:**
The mass of the sphere is approximately $1.40 \times 10^{-3}\text{ kg}$ or $1.40\text{ g}$.
