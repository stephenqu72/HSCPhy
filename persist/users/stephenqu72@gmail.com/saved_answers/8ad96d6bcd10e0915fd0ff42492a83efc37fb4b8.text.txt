To solve this projectile motion problem, we must analyze the vertical and horizontal components of the motion separately.

### Part (a)
We are given:
- Initial speed $v = 53.0\text{ ms}^{-1}$
- Initial height $h_0 = 45.0\text{ m}$
- Maximum height $H_{max} = 175.0\text{ m}$ above the ground.

The height gained above the launch point is $\Delta y = 175.0 - 45.0 = 130.0\text{ m}$.
At maximum height, the vertical component of velocity $v_y = 0$. Using the kinematic equation $v_y^2 = (v \sin\theta)^2 - 2g\Delta y$:
$$0 = (53.0 \sin\theta)^2 - 2 \times 9.8 \times 130.0$$
$$(53.0 \sin\theta)^2 = 2548$$
$$53.0 \sin\theta = \sqrt{2548} \approx 50.4777$$
$$\sin\theta = \frac{50.4777}{53.0} \approx 0.9524$$
$$\theta = \arcsin(0.9524) \approx 72.25^\circ \approx 72.3^\circ$$
This confirms the required angle.

### Part (b)
First, we find the time of flight ($t_{total}$) to reach the range of $180.0\text{ m}$.
Vertical motion: $\Delta y = (v \sin\theta)t - 0.5gt^2$
$-45.0 = (53.0 \sin 72.3^\circ)t - 4.9t^2$
$-45.0 = 50.48t - 4.9t^2$
$$4.9t^2 - 50.48t - 45.0 = 0$$
Using the quadratic formula $t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$$t = \frac{50.48 + \sqrt{(-50.48)^2 - 4(4.9)(-45.0)}}{9.8} \approx \frac{50.48 + 58.48}{9.8} \approx 11.12\text{ s}$$

Now, consider the target. The target moves in a circle ($r=180\text{ m}$) with a period $T = 16.0\text{ s}$. 
The angular velocity is $\omega = \frac{2\pi}{16} = 0.3927\text{ rad s}^{-1}$.
The arrow must hit the target at a point $180\text{ m}$ from the tower. Since the arrow is fired East, it will hit the target when the target is at the East position.
The target starts South ($270^\circ$ or $3\pi/2\text{ rad}$) and moves anti-clockwise. To reach the East position ($0\text{ rad}$ or $2\pi\text{ rad}$), it must travel $90^\circ$ ($\pi/2\text{ rad}$).
Time taken for target to reach East position: $t_{target} = \frac{\pi/2}{0.3927} = 4.0\text{ s}$.
Since the arrow takes $11.12\text{ s}$ to reach the target, the player must fire when the target is at a position such that it reaches the East point exactly at $t = 11.12\text{ s}$.
The target reaches the East point every $16k + 4$ seconds. 
For the first hit: $t_{fire} + 11.12 = 4.0 + 16k$. 
To have a positive wait time, choose $k=1$: $t_{fire} = 20 - 11.12 = 8.88\text{ s}$.
