### Front
What are the conservation rules and the general equation for $\beta^-$ (beta-minus) decay?

### Back
- Key idea: A neutron converts into a proton and an electron (beta particle).
- Formula / law: 
    - Conservation: Mass number ($A$) stays constant; Atomic number ($Z$) increases by 1.
    - Equation: ${_{Z}^{A}\text{X} \rightarrow {_{Z+1}^{A}\text{Y}} + {_{-1}^{0}\text{e}} + \bar{\nu}_e}$
- When to use it: To identify daughter nuclei or balance nuclear decay equations where an electron is emitted.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the atomic number increases by 1 (it is often confused with alpha decay, where $Z$ decreases by 2).
