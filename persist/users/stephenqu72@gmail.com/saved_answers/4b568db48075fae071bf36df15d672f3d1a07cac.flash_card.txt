### Front
What determines the value of the constant $k$ in Kepler’s Third Law ($T^2 = kR^3$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The constant $k$ depends exclusively on the mass of the central body ($M$) being orbited.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $k = \frac{4\pi^2}{GM}$
- **When to use it:** When comparing orbits around different central masses or deriving orbital properties.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the orbiting planet's mass ($m$) affects $k$; it cancels out and has no effect.
