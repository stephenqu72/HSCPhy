### Explanation

To solve this question, we must identify the experimental setup used by J.J. Thomson to determine the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$) of the electron.

1.  **Analyze the options:**
    *   **Option A:** This is **Millikan's Oil Drop Experiment**. It was designed to measure the elementary charge of a single electron by balancing gravitational force against an upward electric force.
    *   **Option B:** This shows a **Cathode Ray Tube (CRT)**. Thomson used a modified cathode ray tube where he applied both electric and magnetic fields perpendicular to the path of the electron beam. By balancing the electric force ($F_E = qE$) and the magnetic force ($F_B = qvB$), he was able to determine the velocity of the electrons and subsequently their charge-to-mass ratio by measuring the deflection of the beam when only the magnetic field was applied.
    *   **Option C:** This is the **Michelson-Morley Interferometer**, used to detect the existence of the "luminiferous aether."
    *   **Option D:** This is the **Geiger-Marsden (Gold Foil) Experiment**, which provided evidence for the existence of a dense, positively charged nucleus within the atom.

2.  **Conclusion:** Thomson's experiment relied on the deflection of cathode rays (electrons) in vacuum tubes under the influence of electromagnetic fields. Therefore, the diagram of the Cathode Ray Tube (Option B) is the best model for his work.
