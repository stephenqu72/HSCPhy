The correct answer is **(B)**.

### Explanation

To solve this, we need to distinguish between the motion of objects *through* space and the evolution of space itself as described by the Big Bang theory and Hubble’s Law.

1.  **Analyze the options:**
    *   **(A) Movement of stars into the outer reaches:** This is incorrect. Galaxies are not "flying" through a pre-existing empty void like shrapnel from an explosion. If they were, the distribution would be different and we would be able to pinpoint a specific center of the explosion.
    *   **(B) Expansion of space itself:** This is the correct scientific model. According to the General Theory of Relativity and the Friedmann-Lemaître-Robertson-Walker (FLRW) metric, the fabric of spacetime itself is stretching. As space expands, the distance between distant galaxies increases over time, causing them to recede from each other. This is why we observe a redshift in the light from distant galaxies ($z = \frac{\Delta \lambda}{\lambda}$).
    *   **(C) Doppler effect of photons:** While we use the term "redshift" to describe the light, it is technically **cosmological redshift**, not a traditional Doppler effect. A Doppler effect occurs when an object moves *through* space. Cosmological redshift occurs because the wavelength of the photon is "stretched" as it travels through expanding space.
    *   **(D) Repulsive gravitational forces:** This is incorrect. Gravity is an attractive force. While "Dark Energy" acts as a repulsive force on a cosmological scale, it is not described as a "repulsive gravitational force between stars and galaxies."

2.  **Summary:** Hubble’s observations of receding galaxies are best explained by the metric expansion of space. Imagine a balloon with dots drawn on it; as you inflate the balloon, the distance between the dots increases, not because the dots are moving across the surface, but because the surface (space) itself is growing.
