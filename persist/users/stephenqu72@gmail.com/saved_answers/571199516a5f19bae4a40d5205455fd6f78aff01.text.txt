To solve this problem, we apply the principle of mass-energy equivalence ($E = mc^2$). In nuclear fusion, the mass of the reactants is greater than the mass of the products; this "lost" mass is the mass defect, which is converted into energy.

### (a) Calculate the mass defect
The mass defect ($\Delta m$) is the difference between the initial mass of reactants and the final mass of products.

**Reactants:** $2 \times$ mass of $^2_1\text{H} = 2 \times 2.0141\,\text{u} = 4.0282\,\text{u}$
**Products:** mass of $^3_2\text{He} + \text{mass of } ^1_0\text{n} = 3.0160\,\text{u} + 1.0087\,\text{u} = 4.0247\,\text{u}$

$$\Delta m = 4.0282\,\text{u} - 4.0247\,\text{u} = 0.0035\,\text{u}$$

---

### (b) Determine the energy released in Joules
To convert atomic mass units (u) to Joules, we use the conversion factor $1\,\text{u} \approx 1.6605 \times 10^{-27}\,\text{kg}$ and the speed of light $c \approx 2.998 \times 10^8\,\text{m/s}$.

1. Convert $\Delta m$ to kg:
   $$0.0035\,\text{u} \times 1.6605 \times 10^{-27}\,\text{kg/u} = 5.81175 \times 10^{-30}\,\text{kg}$$
2. Calculate energy using $E = \Delta m c^2$:
   $$E = (5.81175 \times 10^{-30}\,\text{kg}) \times (2.998 \times 10^8\,\text{m/s})^2$$
   $$E \approx 5.22 \times 10^{-13}\,\text{J}$$

---

### (c) Calculate the total energy released
The reaction provided involves **two** deuterium nuclei. If $1.5 \times 10^{25}$ deuterium nuclei undergo fusion, we must divide by 2 to find the number of fusion events.

1. Number of events:
   $$N = \frac{1.5 \times 10^{25}}{2} = 7.5 \times 10^{24} \text{ events}$$
2. Total energy:
   $$E_{\text{total}} = N \times E_{\text{single}}$$
   $$E_{\text{total}} = (7.5 \times 10^{24}) \times (5.22 \times 10^{-13}\,\text{J}) \approx 3.92 \times 10^{12}\,\text{J}$$
