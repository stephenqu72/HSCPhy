### Front
Define **Accuracy** vs. **Reliability (Precision)** in the context of experimental data.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Accuracy is proximity to the 'true' value; Reliability (Precision) is consistency/closeness of measurements to each other.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When evaluating experimental validity or determining how to improve data quality.
- **Common trap:** Confusing reliability with accuracy; a set of data can be highly reliable (consistent) but still inaccurate (systematic error).
