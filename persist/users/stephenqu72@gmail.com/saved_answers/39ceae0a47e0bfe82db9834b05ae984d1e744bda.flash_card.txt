### Front
How do you calculate the escape velocity ($v_e$) of an object from a massive body, and what principle of physics is it based on?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Escape velocity is the minimum speed required for an object to reach an infinite distance from a mass with zero kinetic energy.
- **Formula:** $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$ (Derived from the Conservation of Mechanical Energy: $E_k + U = 0$).
- **When to use it:** When calculating the speed needed to break free from a planet's gravitational field.
- **Common trap:** Using the distance from the Sun or a secondary body instead of the distance $r$ from the centre of the target planet.
