### Front
How does the gravitational field strength ($g$) change as a spacecraft moves further from the center of the Earth?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational field strength follows an inverse-square relationship with distance.
- Formula / law / constant: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$ (Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation)
- When to use it: To determine how $g$ varies with altitude or orbital radius.
- Common trap: Thinking $g$ depends on the mass of the spacecraft or the atmosphere; it only depends on the source mass ($M$) and distance ($r$).
