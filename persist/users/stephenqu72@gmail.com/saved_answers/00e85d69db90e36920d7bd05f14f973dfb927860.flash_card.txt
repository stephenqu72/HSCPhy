### Front
When assessing a radiation experiment, why does a 50% reduction in count rate after passing through aluminium invalidate a claim that the source is "pure beta"?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Beta particles are fully absorbed by aluminium; a 50% residual count indicates the presence of more penetrating radiation (likely gamma).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When analyzing experimental validity of radiation shielding or identifying unknown radioactive sources.
- **Common trap:** Assuming any decrease in count rate confirms the existence of the expected radiation type, rather than evaluating the *magnitude* of the decrease to identify mixed sources.
