To solve this problem, we need to calculate the magnetic flux ($\Phi$) passing through the wire loop.

### 1. Identify the relevant formula
The magnetic flux is defined as:
$$\Phi = B A \cos(\theta)$$
where:
*   $B$ is the magnetic field strength ($0.50\text{ T}$)
*   $A$ is the area of the loop ($4.0 \times 10^{-4}\text{ m}^2$)
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the **normal to the coil** (the vector perpendicular to the surface) and the **magnetic field lines**.

### 2. Determine the correct angle
The problem states that the plane of the coil is at an angle of $55^{\circ}$ to the magnetic field. However, the formula for magnetic flux requires the angle $\theta$ to be between the *normal* of the plane and the magnetic field.

Since the normal is perpendicular to the plane of the coil ($90^{\circ}$), we calculate $\theta$ as:
$$\theta = 90^{\circ} - 55^{\circ} = 35^{\circ}$$

### 3. Calculate the flux
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$\Phi = 0.50 \times (4.0 \times 10^{-4}) \times \cos(35^{\circ})$$
$$\Phi = 2.0 \times 10^{-4} \times 0.81915...$$
$$\Phi \approx 1.638 \times 10^{-4}\text{ Wb}$$

Rounding to the appropriate number of significant figures (consistent with the provided options), we get:
$$\Phi \approx 1.64 \times 10^{-4}\text{ Wb}$$
