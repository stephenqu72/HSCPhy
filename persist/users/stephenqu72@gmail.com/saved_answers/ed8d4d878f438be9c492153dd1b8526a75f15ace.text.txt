### Explanation
To arrive at the correct answer, we must distinguish between the various behaviours of waves:

*   **(A) Refraction:** This describes the change in direction (and speed) of a wave when it crosses the boundary between two different media (e.g., light moving from air into glass).
*   **(B) Interference/Superposition:** The addition of two or more coherent waves resulting in constructive or destructive interference is what creates stationary (standing) wave patterns.
*   **(C) Diffraction:** This is the phenomenon where waves spread out or bend as they pass through an aperture (opening) or around the edges of an obstacle. The degree of diffraction is most significant when the size of the obstacle or opening is comparable to the wavelength ($\lambda$) of the wave.
*   **(D) Dispersion:** This describes the separation of white light into its constituent colours (spectrum) due to the refractive index of a medium being dependent on the wavelength of the light.

Since **(C)** matches the physical definition of diffraction, it is the only correct option. 

### Tip for HSC Exams
In your exams, remember that diffraction is a "signature" wave property—if something exhibits diffraction, it confirms that it behaves as a wave. Always associate "diffraction" with the word **"bending"** or **"spreading"** around an edge.
