### Part (a): Calculation
**Step 1: Identify the given values.**
- Radius $r = 0.500\text{ m}$
- Magnetic field $B = 5.00 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$
- Area of the coil $A = \pi r^2 = \pi \times (0.500)^2 \approx 0.7854\text{ m}^2$

**Step 2: Define magnetic flux.**
The magnetic flux $\Phi$ is given by $\Phi = BA\cos(\theta)$, where $\theta$ is the angle between the magnetic field and the **normal** to the coil.

**Step 3: Calculate the change in flux.**
- Initial state: The coil is perpendicular to the field lines (as shown in the diagram), meaning the normal to the coil is parallel to the field. Thus, $\theta = 0^\circ$ and $\Phi_{initial} = BA$.
- Final state: The coil rotates $90^\circ$ so that its plane is parallel to the field lines. The normal is now perpendicular to the field, so $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\Phi_{final} = 0$.
- Change in flux $\Delta\Phi = |\Phi_{final} - \Phi_{initial}| = BA$.
- $\Delta\Phi = (5.00 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}) \times (0.7854\text{ m}^2) = 3.93 \times 10^{-5}\text{ Wb}$.

### Part (b): Graphing
**Explanation:**
- Faraday’s Law states that induced EMF ($\varepsilon$) is proportional to the negative rate of change of flux: $\varepsilon = -\frac{d\Phi}{dt}$.
- Since $\Phi = \Phi_{max}\cos(\omega t)$, the induced EMF will follow a sine function: $\varepsilon = \varepsilon_{max}\sin(\omega t)$.
- If we start at maximum positive flux ($\theta=0$), the rate of change is zero at the start. Therefore, the EMF graph must start at **zero** and move in the negative direction (or positive depending on your chosen direction of rotation, but a standard sine wave starting at zero is the core requirement).
- For 2 full rotations, you should draw two full cycles of a sine wave.
