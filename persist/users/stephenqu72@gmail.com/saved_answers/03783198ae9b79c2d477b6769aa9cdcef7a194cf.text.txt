### Answer and Explanation

This question assesses your understanding of the **Photoelectric Effect**, a crucial topic in the HSC Physics module "From the Universe to the Atom." To gain the full 4 marks, your discussion must contrast the classical wave theory of light with the quantum model (Einstein’s photon model).

#### Model Answer:
The apparatus demonstrates the photoelectric effect, where light incident on a metal surface causes the emission of electrons. This phenomenon could not be explained by the classical wave model of light, leading to the development of the quantum model.

1.  **Failure of Classical Wave Theory:** Classical theory predicted that the energy delivered by a light wave depends on its intensity (amplitude). It suggested that any frequency of light should eventually eject electrons if given enough time to "build up" energy, and that increasing intensity should increase the kinetic energy of emitted electrons.
2.  **Experimental Discrepancies:** Experiments showed that for any given metal, there is a **threshold frequency** ($f_0$) below which no electrons are emitted, regardless of how intense the light is. Furthermore, electrons are emitted instantaneously, and their maximum kinetic energy depends on the **frequency** of the light, not the intensity.
3.  **Einstein’s Quantum Solution:** Einstein proposed that light consists of discrete packets of energy called **photons**, where $E = hf$. A single photon interacts with a single electron. If the photon's energy ($hf$) is greater than the metal's **work function** ($\Phi$), an electron is emitted. 
4.  **Conclusion:** This model explains why frequency—not intensity—determines if emission occurs ($hf > \Phi$), satisfying the experimental results that the wave model could not reconcile.

#### How to solve this:
*   **Step 1: Identify the phenomenon.** Recognise the apparatus as the setup for the photoelectric effect.
*   **Step 2: State the limitations of the classical model.** Briefly explain that classical physics viewed light solely as a continuous wave and expected intensity to be the sole determinant of electron energy.
*   **Step 3: State the empirical evidence.** Mention the specific experimental observations that contradicted the wave theory, particularly the threshold frequency and the instantaneous nature of emission.
*   **Step 4: Explain the paradigm shift.** Introduce Einstein’s photon model. Use the equation $E = hf$ to demonstrate that energy is quantized. Explicitly link the frequency of light to the ability to overcome the work function ($\Phi$) of the metal.
