### Front
Explain the relationship between the gravitational forces two bodies exert on each other and distinguish between mass and weight.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Newton's Third Law (gravitational force is mutual) and the distinction between mass (invariant) and weight (location-dependent force).
- **Formula / law / constant:** Newton’s Third Law; $W = mg$.
- **When to use it:** When comparing forces between two bodies or calculating weight changes across different gravitational fields.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the larger planet exerts a "stronger" force on the smaller planet (forces are always equal in magnitude) or confusing mass with weight.
