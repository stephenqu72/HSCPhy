### Front
How do you calculate the work done on an electron accelerated through a potential difference?

### Back
- Key idea: Work done is equal to the change in electric potential energy.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $W = qV$, where $q = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$.
- When to use it: When a charged particle moves between two points with a known potential difference (voltage).
- Common trap: Forgetting to use the elementary charge constant ($e$) or miscalculating scientific notation powers.
