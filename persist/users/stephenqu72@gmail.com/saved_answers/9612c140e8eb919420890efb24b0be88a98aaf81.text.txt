To determine the correct answer, we must apply **Gauss's Law for Magnetism**.

### Answer
The correct answer is **C. Remains constant.**

### Explanation
1.  **Gauss's Law for Magnetism:** This fundamental law of electromagnetism states that the net magnetic flux $\Phi_B$ through any closed surface is always zero. This is mathematically expressed as:
    $$\oint_S \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} = 0$$
    This law is a consequence of the fact that magnetic monopoles do not exist; every magnetic field line that enters a closed surface must also exit it.

2.  **Analyzing the specific scenario:** The copper bottle is a closed surface. Even though the magnetic field is non-uniform and the field strength is increasing over time, the total number of magnetic field lines entering the left side of the bottle will always equal the total number of magnetic field lines exiting the right side.

3.  **Applying the sign convention:** The question defines "inwards" as positive. Let $\Phi_{in}$ be the flux entering the bottle and $\Phi_{out}$ be the flux exiting the bottle. The net magnetic flux $\Phi_{net}$ is:
    $$\Phi_{net} = \Phi_{in} - \Phi_{out}$$
    Since the number of field lines entering equals the number of field lines exiting at any given instant (due to the divergence-free nature of magnetic fields), $\Phi_{in} = \Phi_{out}$. Therefore:
    $$\Phi_{net} = 0$$
    
Regardless of how much the external magnetic field strength increases over time, the net magnetic flux through the closed surface remains exactly zero at every moment. Therefore, the value remains constant.
