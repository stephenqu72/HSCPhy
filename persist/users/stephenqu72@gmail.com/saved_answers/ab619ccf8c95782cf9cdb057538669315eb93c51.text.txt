To solve this physics problem, we need to determine how much of the original radioactive substance remains after a specific amount of time and how much daughter product has been formed.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Determine the number of half-lives elapsed:**
The total time elapsed is $276$ days, and the half-life ($t_{1/2}$) of polonium-210 is $138$ days. The number of half-lives ($n$) is calculated as:
$$n = \frac{\text{total time}}{\text{half-life}} = \frac{276}{138} = 2$$

**2. Calculate the remaining amount of Polonium-210:**
If we start with an initial amount of $N_0$ atoms:
* After $1$ half-life, we have $\frac{1}{2}N_0$ remaining.
* After $2$ half-lives, we have $\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2}N_0 = \frac{1}{4}N_0$ remaining.
So, the amount of Polonium-210 remaining is $0.25N_0$.

**3. Calculate the amount of Lead-206 produced:**
Since the sample was pure Polonium-210 to start with, any atom that has decayed must have turned into Lead-206.
$$\text{Lead-206} = \text{Initial amount} - \text{Remaining Polonium}$$
$$\text{Lead-206} = N_0 - 0.25N_0 = 0.75N_0$$
This is equivalent to $\frac{3}{4}N_0$.

**4. Find the ratio of Polonium-210 to Lead-206:**
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{\text{Polonium-210}}{\text{Lead-206}} = \frac{0.25N_0}{0.75N_0} = \frac{0.25}{0.75} = \frac{1}{3}$$

The ratio of polonium-210 atoms to lead-206 atoms is $1:3$.

Comparing this result to the given options:
A. $1:4$
B. $1:3$
C. $1:2$
D. $1:1$

The correct option is **B**.
