### How to solve this:
You need to structure your response by addressing each figure individually and then synthesizing how they collectively provide a consistent model for the origin of the universe.

**1. Figure 1: Hubble’s Law (Expansion of the Universe)**
*   **Observation:** The graph shows a linear relationship where the recessional velocity of galaxies increases proportionally with their distance ($v = H_0d$).
*   **Inference:** This indicates that the universe is not static; it is expanding. If the universe is expanding today, running the clock backward implies that all matter and energy originated from a single, infinitely dense point (the singularity).

**2. Figure 2: Redshift (Doppler Effect)**
*   **Observation:** Comparing the "Distanced star's spectra" to the "Sun's spectra," you can see the absorption lines are shifted toward the longer wavelength (red) end of the spectrum.
*   **Inference:** This is a Doppler redshift caused by the galaxies moving away from us. This provides the empirical data required for Hubble’s Law, confirming that galaxies are receding at high speeds, which is a key prediction of an expanding Big Bang universe.

**3. Figure 3: Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) Radiation**
*   **Observation:** The graph shows the intensity of radiation peaking at a specific wavelength ($0.12\text{ cm}$). This corresponds to a blackbody curve at a temperature of approximately $2.7\text{ K}$.
*   **Inference:** This is the "afterglow" of the Big Bang. Early in its history, the universe was hot and dense (opaque). As it expanded and cooled, it reached a point where photons could travel freely (recombination). The radiation has since been stretched by the expansion of space into the microwave part of the spectrum. The detection of this uniform background radiation is considered "smoking gun" evidence for the Big Bang.

### Suggested Answer
The Big Bang Theory is supported by these three independent lines of evidence, which describe a universe that is expanding, moving, and cooling from an initial hot, dense state.

**Hubble's Law (Figure 1):** The linear relationship between velocity and distance demonstrates that the universe is expanding. By observing that galaxies further away move faster, we conclude that space itself is stretching, suggesting that galaxies were much closer together in the past.

**Redshift (Figure 2):** The shift of absorption lines toward the red end of the spectrum provides the observational evidence for Figure 1. It confirms that distant galaxies are moving away from the observer. This validates the dynamic nature of the universe, moving away from the previously held static model.

**Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation (Figure 3):** The intensity curve represents the thermal radiation remnant from the early, hot phase of the universe. According to the Big Bang Theory, the universe should have been filled with high-energy radiation that cooled as the universe expanded. The observation of this specific blackbody spectrum (corresponding to a temperature of $2.7\text{ K}$) confirms the prediction that the universe underwent a transition from an opaque, hot state to the transparent, cool state we observe today.

Together, these observations refute static models and provide a cohesive history of a universe that began as a singularity and has evolved through expansion and cooling.
