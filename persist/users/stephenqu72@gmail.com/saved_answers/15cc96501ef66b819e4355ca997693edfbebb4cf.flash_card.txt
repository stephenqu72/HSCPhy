### Front
Which of Newton’s or Kepler’s laws describes the motion of a planet around the Sun, and which describes the force on an object on Earth's surface?

### Back
- Key idea: Kepler’s Laws describe planetary orbits; Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation describes the force between two masses.
- Formula / law: Kepler’s 3rd Law: $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$; Newton’s Law: $F = G\frac{m_1m_2}{r^2}$.
- When to use it: Use Kepler’s for satellite/planetary motion; use Newton’s for gravitational force calculations.
- Common trap: Confusing Kepler’s empirical observations of orbits with Newton’s dynamic explanation of gravitational force.
