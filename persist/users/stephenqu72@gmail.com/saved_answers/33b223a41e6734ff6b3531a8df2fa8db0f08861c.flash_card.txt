### Front
Where does a projectile reach its minimum speed and why?

### Back
- Key idea: Total speed is minimized at the peak (apex) of the trajectory because the vertical velocity component ($v_y$) is zero.
- Formula / law: $v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$; $v_x$ is constant, $v_y = 0$ at apex.
- When to use it: To identify points of minimum speed or calculate velocity at the apex in projectile motion problems.
- Common trap: Assuming speed is zero at the apex; the horizontal velocity ($v_x$) is still non-zero.
