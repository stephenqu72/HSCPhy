### Explanation
To solve this question, you must differentiate between Maxwell's **theoretical contributions** and the **experimental validation** performed by others.

*   **A is incorrect:** James Clerk Maxwell is famous for developing "Maxwell’s Equations," which mathematically unified the electric and magnetic fields into a single electromagnetic field.
*   **B is incorrect:** By manipulating his equations, Maxwell realized that a changing electric field would induce a changing magnetic field and vice versa, creating a self-propagating wave. He correctly predicted the existence of these electromagnetic waves.
*   **C is incorrect:** Maxwell’s theory allowed him to calculate the velocity of these waves using the constants of electricity ($\epsilon_0$) and magnetism ($\mu_0$). He found the speed to be:
    $$v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}}$$
    This value was essentially equal to the known speed of light, leading him to correctly hypothesize that light itself is an electromagnetic wave.
*   **D is correct:** Maxwell provided the **theoretical framework** for electromagnetism, but he did not perform the experiments to prove them. The existence of electromagnetic waves was experimentally **validated** (proven) by **Heinrich Hertz** in 1887, several years after Maxwell’s death. 

Therefore, Maxwell did not validate the existence of the waves; Hertz did.
