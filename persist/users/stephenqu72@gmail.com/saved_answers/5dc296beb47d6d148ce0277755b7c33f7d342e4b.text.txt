To solve this problem, we must use the formula for the orbital velocity of a satellite orbiting a central mass $M$.

### Explanation

1.  **Derive the Orbital Velocity Formula:**
    A satellite in a circular orbit is held in place by gravitational force acting as the centripetal force.
    $$\frac{mv^2}{R} = \frac{GMm}{R^2}$$
    By canceling the mass of the satellite ($m$) and one factor of the radius ($R$), we get:
    $$v^2 = \frac{GM}{R}$$
    $$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{R}}$$
    Crucially, note that the mass of the satellite ($m$) cancels out. This means the **orbital velocity is independent of the mass of the satellite.**

2.  **Compare Satellite A and Satellite B:**
    *   Satellite A has an orbital radius of $R_A = 4r$.
    *   Satellite B has an orbital radius of $R_B = r$.
    *   Since they orbit the same object, $G$ and $M$ are constant for both.

    The velocity of Satellite A is:
    $$v_A = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{4r}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{4}} \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

    The velocity of Satellite B is:
    $$v_B = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

3.  **Find the Ratio:**
    Comparing the two expressions:
    $$v_A = \frac{1}{2} v_B$$
    This can be rearranged to:
    $$v_B = 2v_A$$

This means the orbital velocity of satellite B is twice that of satellite A. Therefore, the correct option is **C**.
