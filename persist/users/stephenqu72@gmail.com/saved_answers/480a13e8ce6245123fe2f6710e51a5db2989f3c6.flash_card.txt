### Front
Explain why a stationary magnetometer near a stationary charged sphere reads zero, but registers a value when moving.

### Back
- Key idea: A magnetic field is only produced by moving electric charges (currents); stationary charges produce only an electric field.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $B = 0$ for static charges (Oersted's discovery).
- When to use it: When analyzing the relationship between charge, motion, and electromagnetism.
- Common trap: Forgetting that motion is relative; if the magnetometer moves relative to the static charge, the charge effectively acts as a moving current source.
