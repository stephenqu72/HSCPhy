### Front
For two satellites of different masses ($m$ and $2m$) in the same circular orbit around a planet, which quantity differs between them?

### Back
- Key idea: Orbital speed, period, and centripetal acceleration are independent of the satellite's mass. Momentum ($p = mv$) is directly proportional to mass.
- Formula / law / constant: $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$, $p = mv$
- When to use it: To compare orbital dynamics of satellites with varying masses at the same orbital radius.
- Common trap: Assuming that because the motion (speed/period) is the same, all associated physical quantities must also be identical.
