### Part a: Force on side EF
**Answer:**
The magnitude of the force is $0.032\text{ N}$ directed **upwards**.

**Explanation:**
1. **Identify the formula:** The force on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field is given by $F = nBIL\sin(\theta)$.
   - $n = 10$ (number of turns)
   - $B = 2.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ T}$
   - $I = 4.0\text{ A}$
   - $L = 0.04\text{ m}$ (length of the side)
   - $\theta = 90^{\circ}$ (since the wire is perpendicular to the magnetic field), $\sin(90^{\circ}) = 1$.
2. **Calculation:**
   $$F = 10 \times 2.0 \times 10^{-3} \times 4.0 \times 0.04 = 0.032\text{ N}$$
3. **Direction:** Use the **Right-Hand Rule** (or Fleming’s Left-Hand Rule for motors). Point your fingers from North to South, your thumb in the direction of current (from E to F), and your palm will face **upwards**.

### Part b: Average voltage during one-quarter revolution
**Answer:**
The magnitude of the average voltage is $0.0064\text{ V}$ (or $6.4\text{ mV}$).

**Explanation:**
1. **Identify the formula:** Faraday's Law states $\varepsilon_{avg} = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$.
2. **Find $\Delta \Phi$:** The flux changes from $\Phi = BA$ (when vertical) to $0$ (when horizontal) in one-quarter turn.
   - Area $A = 0.04 \times 0.04 = 0.0016\text{ m}^2$.
   - $\Delta \Phi = BA - 0 = (2.0 \times 10^{-3}) \times 0.0016 = 3.2 \times 10^{-6}\text{ Wb}$.
3. **Find $\Delta t$:** Two revolutions per second means the period $T = 0.5\text{ s}$. One-quarter revolution takes $\Delta t = \frac{T}{4} = 0.125\text{ s}$.
4. **Calculation:**
   $$\varepsilon_{avg} = 10 \times \frac{3.2 \times 10^{-6}}{0.125} = 0.0064\text{ V}$$

### Part c: Conversion to AC generator
**Answer:**
Replace the **split-ring commutator** with two **slip rings**.

**Justification:**
A split-ring commutator acts as a mechanical rectifier, reversing the connection to the external circuit every half-turn to ensure current always flows in the same direction (DC). Slip rings maintain a continuous connection to the same side of the coil throughout the rotation, allowing the induced current to reverse direction every half-turn, which is the definition of alternating current (AC).
