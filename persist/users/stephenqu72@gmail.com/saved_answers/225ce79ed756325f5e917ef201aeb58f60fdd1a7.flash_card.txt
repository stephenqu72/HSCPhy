### Front
How do you determine the mass ($M$) of a central planet using a graph of $r^3$ vs $T^2$ for its orbiting satellites?

### Back
- Key idea: The gradient of a $r^3$ vs $T^2$ graph is proportional to the central mass.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Kepler’s Third Law: $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2} \implies M = \frac{4\pi^2}{G} \times \text{gradient}$
- When to use it: When given orbital data for multiple satellites or a linear graph of $r^3$ against $T^2$.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., hours to seconds, km to m) before calculating the gradient or substituting into the formula.
