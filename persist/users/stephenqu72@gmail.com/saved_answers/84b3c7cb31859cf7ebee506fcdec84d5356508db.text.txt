To solve this problem, we must analyze the changes in the mass number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$) during radioactive decay as shown on the chart.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Analyze the start point:** The series begins at Uranium ($^{238}_{92}\text{U}$).
2.  **Determine the process for $X$:**
    *   The arrow goes from $Z=92$ (U) to $Z=90$ ($X$), and the mass number goes from $238$ to $234$.
    *   A change of $-2$ in the atomic number and $-4$ in the mass number signifies an **alpha ($\alpha$) decay**.
    *   The product has $Z=90$ and $A=234$. Looking at the periodic table (or knowing the series), the element with atomic number $90$ is Thorium (Th). So, $X = ^{234}_{90}\text{Th}$.
3.  **Determine the process for $Y$:**
    *   The arrow goes from $Z=90$ ($X$) to $Z=91$ ($Y$). The mass number remains constant at $234$.
    *   A change of $+1$ in the atomic number with no change in mass number signifies a **beta ($\beta^-$) decay**.
    *   The product has $Z=91$ and $A=234$. The element with atomic number $91$ is Protactinium (Pa). So, $Y = ^{234}_{91}\text{Pa}$.
4.  **Match with options:**
    *   $X$ is $^{234}_{90}\text{Th}$ produced by alpha decay.
    *   $Y$ is $^{234}_{91}\text{Pa}$ produced by beta decay.
