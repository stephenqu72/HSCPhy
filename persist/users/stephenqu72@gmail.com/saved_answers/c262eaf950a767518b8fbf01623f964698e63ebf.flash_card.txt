### Front
Why is a spacecraft in orbit NOT truly weightless?

### Back
- **Key idea:** "Weightlessness" (apparent weight) is the absence of a normal reaction force, but the gravitational force ($F_g$) still exists.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $F_g = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$; Gravitational force acts as the centripetal force.
- **When to use it:** When explaining why astronauts float in orbit despite being in the Earth’s gravitational field.
- **Common trap:** Confusing "apparent weightlessness" (free-fall) with the absence of gravity; gravity is what keeps the object in orbit.
