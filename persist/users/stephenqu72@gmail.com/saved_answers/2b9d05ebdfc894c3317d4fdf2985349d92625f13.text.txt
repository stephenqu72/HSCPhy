To find the energy of a photon emitted by the radio transmitter, we use the Planck-Einstein relation:

$$E = hf$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given variables:**
    The frequency ($f$) is given as $102.8 \text{ MHz}$. We must convert this into the SI unit, Hertz ($Hz$):
    $$f = 102.8 \times 10^6 \text{ Hz} = 1.028 \times 10^8 \text{ Hz}$$

2.  **Identify the constant:**
    We use Planck’s constant ($h$), which is approximately:
    $$h \approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    Substitute the values into the formula:
    $$E = (6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}) \times (1.028 \times 10^8 \text{ Hz})$$
    $$E \approx 6.811528 \times 10^{-26} \text{ J}$$

Rounding this to four significant figures gives $6.812 \times 10^{-26} \text{ J}$.

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches option **(B)**.

### Conclusion:
The correct answer is **(B)**.
