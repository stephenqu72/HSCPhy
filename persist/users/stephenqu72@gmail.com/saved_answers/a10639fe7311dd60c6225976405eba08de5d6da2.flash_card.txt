### Front
How do you represent the electric field between two oppositely charged parallel plates in a diagram?

### Back
- Key idea: The field is **uniform**, represented by straight, equally spaced parallel lines pointing from the positive plate to the negative plate.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E = \frac{V}{d}$ (Uniform field strength).
- When to use it: Whenever analyzing motion of charges or potential difference between parallel conductors.
- Common trap: Forgetting to draw "fringing" (curved lines) at the plate edges and assuming the field is uniform outside the plates.
