### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Definition:** Gravitational potential ($V$) at a point in a gravitational field is defined as the work done per unit mass by an external agent to move an object from a point of zero potential (infinity) to that specific point in the field. 
Mathematically, it is expressed as $V = \frac{U}{m}$, where $U$ is the gravitational potential energy and $m$ is the mass of the object. Note that gravitational potential is always negative, with its maximum value of zero at an infinite distance from the source of the field.

#### Part (b)
**Step-by-step Calculation:**

1.  **Understand the relationship between Potential and Potential Energy:**
    The gravitational potential energy is given by $U = mV = - \frac{GMm}{r}$. 
    The gravitational field strength is given by $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$.
    By comparing these, we can relate them to the distance $r$ from the center of the Moon:
    $$U = - \frac{GMm}{r} \implies |U| \propto \frac{1}{r}$$
    $$g = \frac{GM}{r^2} \implies g \propto \frac{1}{r^2}$$

2.  **Relate the distances using the potential energy condition:**
    We are given $U_A = 0.95 U_B$. Since both values are negative, this implies $|U_A| = 0.95 |U_B|$.
    Using the relationship $|U| \propto \frac{1}{r}$:
    $$\frac{1}{r_A} = 0.95 \left( \frac{1}{r_B} \right) \implies r_A = \frac{r_B}{0.95} \approx 1.0526 r_B$$

3.  **Calculate the gravitational field strength at A ($g_A$):**
    Using the relationship $g \propto \frac{1}{r^2}$:
    $$\frac{g_A}{g_B} = \frac{\frac{1}{r_A^2}}{\frac{1}{r_B^2}} = \left( \frac{r_B}{r_A} \right)^2$$
    Substitute $r_A = \frac{r_B}{0.95}$:
    $$\frac{g_A}{g_B} = \left( \frac{r_B}{\frac{r_B}{0.95}} \right)^2 = (0.95)^2$$
    $$g_A = 0.95^2 \times g_B$$
    $$g_A = 0.9025 \times 1.622 \text{ N/kg}$$
    $$g_A \approx 1.464 \text{ N/kg}$$

**Final Result:** The gravitational field strength at point $A$ is approximately $1.464 \text{ N/kg}$.
