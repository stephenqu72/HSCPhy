### Explanation
The phenomenon shown in the image is the **reflection of light**, governed by the Law of Reflection, which states that the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection ($i = r$).

1.  **Newton’s Corpuscular Model:** Newton proposed that light consists of particles (corpuscles). He explained reflection by suggesting that when particles approach a mirror, they experience a repulsive force perpendicular to the surface. This force reversed the normal component of the particle's velocity while keeping the tangential component constant, successfully predicting that the angle of incidence would equal the angle of reflection.
2.  **Huygens’ Wave Model:** Huygens proposed that light consists of waves traveling through an "aether." Using his principle of secondary wavelets (Huygens' Principle), he was able to derive the law of reflection geometrically by demonstrating that wavefronts striking a surface bounce back such that the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection.

Since both models successfully explained the observed behavior of reflection, option **B** is the only scientifically accurate statement.
