### Answer
The SI definition of the ampere is fundamentally based on the magnetic force of attraction or repulsion between two infinitely long, parallel wires carrying a current. Newton’s Third Law of Motion states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. 

When two parallel wires carry currents $I_1$ and $I_2$, each wire produces a magnetic field that exerts a force on the other. According to Ampere’s Force Law, the force per unit length $f$ exerted on the wires is given by:
$$f = \frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi d}$$
where $d$ is the separation distance. Because the force exerted by wire 1 on wire 2 is equal in magnitude and opposite in direction to the force exerted by wire 2 on wire 1, this interaction is a direct application of Newton’s Third Law. The definition of the ampere relies on this mutual, reciprocal interaction to provide a standard measure of electric current that is consistent with the conservation of momentum in physical systems.
