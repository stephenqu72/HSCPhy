To determine the correct statement, we must apply Einstein’s photoelectric equation, which relates the stopping voltage ($V_s$) to the frequency of incident light ($f$):

$$E_k = hf - \Phi$$
$$eV_s = hf - \Phi$$
$$V_s = \frac{h}{e}f - \frac{\Phi}{e}$$

This equation takes the linear form $y = mx + c$, where:
- The slope ($m$) is $\frac{h}{e}$.
- The y-intercept ($c$) is $-\frac{\Phi}{e}$, where $\Phi$ is the work function.

It indicates that the energy required to remove an electron from the metal (the work function) is $3.74\text{ eV}$.
