### Answer
The correct answer is **B. Direct observation of gravitational lensing**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we need to evaluate which options are considered primary observational pillars of the Big Bang Theory versus general phenomena in astrophysics.

*   **A. Direct measurement of $H_0$:** The Hubble constant ($H_0$) represents the rate of expansion of the universe. Measuring $H_0$ confirms that the universe is expanding, which is a foundational requirement for the Big Bang Theory.
*   **B. Direct observation of gravitational lensing:** Gravitational lensing is a phenomenon predicted by Einstein's General Theory of Relativity, occurring when light from a distant source is bent by the gravitational field of a massive object (like a galaxy cluster). While it confirms General Relativity, it is **not** direct evidence of the Big Bang's origin or expansion. It is a consequence of the distribution of mass-energy in the universe, not evidence for the hot, dense initial state of the universe.
*   **C. Measured abundance of H, He, and Li:** Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) predicts the specific ratios of light elements formed in the early universe. The observed abundance of hydrogen, helium, and lithium matches these predictions, making it one of the three strongest pieces of evidence for the Big Bang.
*   **D. CMBR at predicted $2.73\ K$:** The Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation (CMBR) is the "afterglow" of the Big Bang. The prediction that the universe would be filled with uniform radiation, currently cooled to approximately $2.73\ K$, is one of the most significant validations of the Big Bang model.

Since options A, C, and D are direct pillars of Big Bang cosmology and option B is a phenomenon explained by General Relativity independent of the expansion history of the universe, B is the correct choice.
