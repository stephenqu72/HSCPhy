To solve this problem, we apply the principles of electrostatics and dynamics. Since the ball is stationary, we know it is in a state of equilibrium, meaning the net force acting on it is zero.

### (a) Electric field strength
The electric field strength $E$ between two parallel plates is determined by the potential difference $V$ and the distance between the plates $d$. The formula is:
$$E = \frac{V}{d}$$

Given:
- $V = 430\text{ V}$
- $d = 1.4 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$

Calculation:
$$E = \frac{430}{1.4 \times 10^{-3}}$$
$$E \approx 3.07 \times 10^5\text{ V m}^{-1} \text{ (or N C}^{-1})$$

### (b) Mass of the ball
For the ball to remain stationary, the upward electric force $F_E$ must exactly balance the downward gravitational force $F_g$.

1. **Calculate the electric force:**
   $F_E = qE$
   $F_E = (4.0 \times 10^{-6}\text{ C}) \times (3.0714 \times 10^5\text{ V m}^{-1})$
   $F_E \approx 1.2286\text{ N}$

2. **Equate forces to find mass:**
   $F_g = mg \implies F_E = mg$
   $1.2286 = m \times 9.8$
   $m = \frac{1.2286}{9.8}$
   $m \approx 0.125\text{ kg}$

### How to solve:
1. **Identify the Physics:** Recognize that the uniform electric field between parallel plates is $E = V/d$.
2. **Equilibrium Condition:** If an object is stationary, the forces acting on it must sum to zero. Here, the weight force ($mg$) acts downwards, and the electric force ($qE$) acts upwards.
3. **Calculation:** Compute $E$ first, then use $qE = mg$ to isolate the mass $m$. Ensure your units are consistent (SI units: Coulombs, Volts, Meters, Newtons, Kilograms).
