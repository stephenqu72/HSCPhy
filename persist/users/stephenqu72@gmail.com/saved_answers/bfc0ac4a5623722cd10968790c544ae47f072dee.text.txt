To solve this problem, we must analyze the forces acting on the tether ball in uniform circular motion.

### Step 1: Identify the forces
The ball is subject to two main forces:
1.  **Gravity ($F_g = mg$)** acting vertically downwards.
2.  **Tension ($F_T$)** in the string acting at an angle $\theta$ from the vertical.

### Step 2: Resolve the forces
We resolve the tension $F_T$ into two components:
*   **Vertical component:** $F_{T,y} = F_T \cos \theta$. This must balance the weight of the ball so that there is no vertical acceleration:
    $$F_T \cos \theta = mg \implies F_T = \frac{mg}{\cos \theta}$$
*   **Horizontal component:** $F_{T,x} = F_T \sin \theta$. This component acts as the centripetal force ($F_c$) required to keep the ball in a horizontal circular path:
    $$F_T \sin \theta = F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

### Step 3: Combine equations
Divide the horizontal equation by the vertical equation:
$$\frac{F_T \sin \theta}{F_T \cos \theta} = \frac{mv^2/r}{mg}$$
$$\tan \theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

### Step 4: Evaluate the options
We are looking for an expression for mass ($m$). Let's look at the relationship between centripetal force and the vertical force (weight):
Since $F_c = F_T \sin \theta$ and $mg = F_T \cos \theta$, we can rearrange these to find the ratio:
$$\frac{F_c}{mg} = \frac{F_T \sin \theta}{F_T \cos \theta} = \tan \theta$$
Therefore, $mg = \frac{F_c}{\tan \theta}$. Solving for $m$:
$$m = \frac{F_c}{g \tan \theta}$$
