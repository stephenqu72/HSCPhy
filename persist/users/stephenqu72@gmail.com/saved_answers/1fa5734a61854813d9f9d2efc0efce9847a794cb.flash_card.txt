### Front
How do you graphically identify an inverse-square law relationship ($y \propto 1/x^2$)?

### Back
- Key idea: As $x$ increases, $y$ decreases rapidly. The curve is steep near the y-axis and approaches the x-axis (asymptote) as $x$ increases.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $y = k/x^2$; No fixed constant.
- When to use it: Applicable to field strength calculations (e.g., gravity, electric, or light intensity) relative to distance.
- Common trap: Confusing an inverse-square curve ($1/x^2$) with a linear ($x$) or direct-square ($x^2$) relationship.
