### Front
Which radioactive decay process involves the emission of a helium-4 nucleus?

### Back
- Key idea: Alpha decay involves the emission of a particle containing 2 protons and 2 neutrons.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: ${}^{A}_{Z}\text{X} \rightarrow {}^{A-4}_{Z-2}\text{Y} + {}^{4}_{2}\text{He}$
- When to use it: When identifying the parent-daughter relationship where the mass number decreases by 4 and atomic number by 2.
- Common trap: Confusing alpha particles with beta particles (electrons/positrons) or gamma rays (photons).
