To solve this problem, we need to calculate the energy of the photon emitted during the electron transition and compare it to the energy differences between the levels provided in the table.

### 1. Calculate the energy of the photon
The energy of a photon ($E$) is related to its wavelength ($\lambda$) by the equation:
$$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$

Where:
- $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J}\cdot\text{s}$ (Planck's constant)
- $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}$ (speed of light)
- $\lambda = 368 \, \text{nm} = 368 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{m}$

Substituting these values:
$$E = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34})(3.00 \times 10^8)}{368 \times 10^{-9}} \approx 5.40 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}$$

Convert Joules to electron-volts ($1 \, \text{eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}$):
$$E(\text{eV}) = \frac{5.40 \times 10^{-19}}{1.602 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 3.37 \, \text{eV}$$

### 2. Determine the energy difference between states
We look for a transition where $\Delta E = E_{\text{high}} - E_{\text{low}} \approx 3.37 \, \text{eV}$.

*   **(A) 2 $\rightarrow$ ground:** $-5.39 - (-13.9) = 8.51 \, \text{eV}$
*   **(B) 3 $\rightarrow$ ground:** $-2.02 - (-13.9) = 11.88 \, \text{eV}$
*   **(C) 3 $\rightarrow$ 2:** $-2.02 - (-5.39) = 3.37 \, \text{eV}$
*   **(D) 4 $\rightarrow$ 3:** $-1.05 - (-2.02) = 0.97 \, \text{eV}$

The transition **3 $\rightarrow$ 2** produces a photon with energy $3.37 \, \text{eV}$, which corresponds to a wavelength of $368 \, \text{nm}$.

**Correct Answer: (C)**
