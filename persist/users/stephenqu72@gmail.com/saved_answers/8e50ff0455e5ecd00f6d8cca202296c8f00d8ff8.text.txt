To solve this problem, we must treat the motion of the ball as a projectile-like motion on an inclined plane. The tilt of the table creates a constant gravitational acceleration component acting down the slope.

### (a) Calculate the net acceleration on the ball
On an inclined plane, the component of gravity acting down the slope is given by $a = g \sin(\theta)$.
Taking $g = 9.80\text{ m s}^{-2}$ and $\theta = 5.0^\circ$:
$$a = 9.80 \times \sin(5.0^\circ)$$
$$a \approx 9.80 \times 0.08715 \approx 0.854\text{ m s}^{-2}$$
The acceleration is directed down the slope (towards the side $YZ$).

### (b) Calculate the time it takes for the ball to reach the pocket
We define a coordinate system where the $x$-axis is parallel to the long side (no acceleration) and the $y$-axis is perpendicular to the long side (direction of constant acceleration).
The initial velocity $v = 1.10\text{ m s}^{-1}$ at $50.0^\circ$ relative to the long side gives:
- $v_x = 1.10 \cos(50.0^\circ) \approx 0.707\text{ m s}^{-1}$
- $v_y = 1.10 \sin(50.0^\circ) \approx 0.843\text{ m s}^{-1}$

The table's width (let's denote as $d_y$) is not explicitly provided. **However**, assuming the standard geometry of an HSC-style physics problem where we solve for the time $t$ relative to the acceleration $a$, if the pocket is at the far side (width $w$), we use $d_y = v_y t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$. 
*(Note: If the width is not given in the exam paper context, please verify if $d_y$ was provided. Assuming a standard pool table width of $1.0\text{ m}$ for calculation demonstration):*
If we assume the ball travels across the full width $w \approx 1.0\text{ m}$:
$$1.0 = 0.843t + 0.5(0.854)t^2$$
Solving $0.427t^2 + 0.843t - 1.0 = 0$ using the quadratic formula:
$$t = \frac{-0.843 + \sqrt{0.843^2 - 4(0.427)(-1)}}{2(0.427)} \approx 0.82\text{ s}$$

### (c) How far is the corner pocket from the position it was hit?
Using displacement formulas:
- $x = v_x t = 0.707 \times 0.82 \approx 0.58\text{ m}$
- $y = v_y t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2 = 1.0\text{ m}$ (the width)
Total distance $D = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} = \sqrt{0.58^2 + 1.0^2} \approx 1.16\text{ m}$.

***

**Teacher's Tip:** Always resolve vectors into components parallel and perpendicular to the slope. Acceleration only acts in the direction of the tilt, meaning the velocity component parallel to the tilt is constant.
