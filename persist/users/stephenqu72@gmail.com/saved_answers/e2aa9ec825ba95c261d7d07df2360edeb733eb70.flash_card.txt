### Front
What is the formula for calculating torque ($\tau$) and what does each variable represent?

### Back
- Key idea: Torque is the turning effect of a force.
- Formula: $\tau = rF\sin(\phi)$
    - $r$: distance from pivot to force application point.
    - $F$: magnitude of applied force.
    - $\phi$: angle between position vector $\vec{r}$ and force vector $\vec{F}$.
- When to use it: Whenever a force is applied to an object to cause rotation about a pivot.
- Common trap: Using the angle given in a diagram without verifying if it is the angle between the **position vector** and the **force vector** (ensure you use the perpendicular component).
