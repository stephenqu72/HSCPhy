### Answer
The different shapes of the paths arise because the force exerted on the electron acts differently relative to the direction of its motion in each case:

1.  **Electric Field (Figure 2a):** The electric force $F_E = qE$ is constant in both magnitude and direction (downwards towards the positive plate). As the electron travels through the field, this constant force acting perpendicular to its initial horizontal velocity causes it to follow a **parabolic path**, similar to a projectile in a gravitational field.
2.  **Magnetic Field (Figure 2b):** The magnetic force $F_B = qvB \sin\theta$ acts perpendicular to both the velocity vector and the magnetic field. Because the force is always perpendicular to the instantaneous velocity, it acts as a centripetal force. Since the magnitude of the velocity remains constant, the force direction rotates as the electron moves, resulting in a **circular path**.

### How to solve this
To achieve full marks in an HSC physics exam for this type of question, you must demonstrate an understanding of the relationship between force, motion, and geometry:

*   **Step 1: Identify the nature of the Electric Force.** Recall that in a uniform electric field, the force $F = qE$ is constant. Since the force is constant and initially perpendicular to the horizontal motion, it accelerates the particle in one direction only. This is the definition of projectile motion, which describes a parabola.
*   **Step 2: Identify the nature of the Magnetic Force.** Recognize that the magnetic force $F = qvB$ is always perpendicular to the velocity vector $v$. In physics, a force that is always perpendicular to the velocity results in uniform circular motion (the force changes the *direction* of the velocity but not its *magnitude*).
*   **Step 3: Compare and Contrast.** Clearly state that the electric force is constant in direction, while the magnetic force changes direction as the particle moves to stay perpendicular to the velocity. This distinction is the key to why one shape is a parabola and the other is a circle.
