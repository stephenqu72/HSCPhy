To find the mass of the ball, we use the formula for centripetal force involving angular velocity:

$$F = mr\omega^2$$

### 1. Identify the given values and convert to SI units
*   Centripetal force ($F$): $1.9\text{ N}$
*   Radius ($r$): $80\text{ cm} = 0.8\text{ m}$
*   Angular velocity ($\omega$): $4\pi\text{ rad s}^{-1}$

### 2. Rearrange the formula to solve for mass ($m$)
$$m = \frac{F}{r\omega^2}$$

### 3. Substitute the values into the equation
First, calculate the square of the angular velocity:
$$\omega^2 = (4\pi)^2 = 16\pi^2$$
Using $\pi \approx 3.14159$, $\pi^2 \approx 9.8696$:
$$\omega^2 \approx 16 \times 9.8696 \approx 157.91$$

Now, solve for $m$:
$$m = \frac{1.9}{0.8 \times 157.91}$$
$$m = \frac{1.9}{126.33}$$
$$m \approx 0.015039\dots\text{ kg}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $0.015\text{ kg}$.

Matching this result with the provided options:
(A) $0.19\text{ kg}$
(B) $0.15\text{ kg}$
(C) $0.019\text{ kg}$
(D) $0.015\text{ kg}$

The correct answer is **(D)**.
