### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Analyze the standing wave pattern:**
    A standing wave consists of nodes (points of zero displacement) and antinodes (points of maximum displacement). In the provided diagram, points A and B act as fixed ends (nodes). Between A and B, there are two complete "loops." 
    *   One full wavelength ($\lambda$) corresponds to two loops (a crest and a trough section).
    *   Since there are two loops, the distance between A and B ($L = 2.4 \text{ m}$) represents exactly two full wavelengths? No, let's look closer: a single loop is $\frac{\lambda}{2}$.
    *   There are 2 loops between A and B, so:
        $$L = 2 \times \left(\frac{\lambda}{2}\right) = \lambda$$
    *   Therefore, the wavelength $\lambda = 2.4 \text{ m}$.

2.  **Use the wave speed formula:**
    The speed of a wave ($v$) is related to its wavelength ($\lambda$) and period ($T$) by the formula:
    $$v = \frac{\lambda}{T}$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    *   We are given the period $T = 1.6 \text{ s}$.
    *   Substituting the values:
        $$v = \frac{2.4 \text{ m}}{1.6 \text{ s}}$$
        $$v = 1.5 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, we find that the speed is exactly $1.5 \text{ m s}^{-1}$.

**Correct Answer:**
**C. $1.5 \text{ m s}^{-1}$**
