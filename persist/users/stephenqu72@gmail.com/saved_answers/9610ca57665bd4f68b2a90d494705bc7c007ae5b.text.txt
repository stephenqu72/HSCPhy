To solve this problem, we must apply **Lenz's Law** and the principles of electromagnetic induction.

### Analysis
1.  **Open Switch:** When the switch is open, the rotating magnet induces an electromotive force (emf) across the coil. However, because the circuit is open, no current flows ($I = 0$). With no current, there is no magnetic field created by the coil, so there is no electromagnetic force acting on the magnet to oppose its rotation.
2.  **Closed Switch:** When the switch is closed, the changing magnetic flux through the coil (caused by the rotating magnet) induces an emf, which drives a current through the circuit. 
3.  **Lenz's Law:** According to Lenz's Law, the direction of the induced current is such that it opposes the change that created it. The induced current in the coil creates its own magnetic field. This field will exert a magnetic force on the rotating magnet that acts in the opposite direction to its motion. This is an application of the **conservation of energy**: the work done by the wind to rotate the magnet is now being converted into electrical energy in the circuit, which creates a "magnetic drag" or braking torque on the magnet.

### Conclusion
As the switch is closed, the induced current creates an opposing magnetic force (a braking effect) on the rotating magnet. This force acts against the direction of rotation, causing the spindle to slow down.
