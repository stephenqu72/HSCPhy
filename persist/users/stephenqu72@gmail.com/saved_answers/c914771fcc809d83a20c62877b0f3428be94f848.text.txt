### Explanation
To solve this, we need to understand the definition of an **electron volt (eV)**.

1.  **Definition**: An electron volt is a unit of **energy**. It is defined as the amount of kinetic energy gained by a single electron accelerating from rest through an electric potential difference of one volt ($1\text{ V}$).
2.  **Formula**: Since energy $E$ is calculated by the product of charge $q$ and potential difference $V$ ($E = qV$), and the elementary charge of an electron is approximately $1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$, we have:
    $$1\text{ eV} = (1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}) \times (1\text{ V}) = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$$
3.  **Units**: 
    *   The unit for energy is the **Joule (J)**.
    *   (A) Newtons ($N$) measure force.
    *   (B) Watts ($W$) measure power.
    *   (C) Volts ($V$) measure electric potential.

Therefore, $1\text{ eV}$ is equivalent to $1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ Joules}$.
