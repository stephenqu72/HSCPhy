To answer these questions, we apply core principles of electromagnetism and gravity.

### Question 1
**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
*   **Magnetic forces:** Magnetic poles exist as dipoles (North and South). Like poles (e.g., North and North) repel each other, while opposite poles (North and South) attract each other. Therefore, magnetic forces can result in either attraction or repulsion.
*   **Gravitational forces:** Gravity is a result of mass. Unlike electric charge or magnetic poles, mass is always positive. According to Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, the force is always directed toward the centers of mass, which means gravitational force is strictly attractive.

### Question 2
**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
To calculate the voltage (potential difference) between two parallel plates, we use the formula relating the electric field $E$, the voltage $V$, and the distance between the plates $d$:
$$V = E \times d$$

Given values:
*   $E = 2.0 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N C}^{-1}$
*   $d = 1.0 \times 10^{-2} \text{ m}$

Calculation:
$$V = (2.0 \times 10^{-4}) \times (1.0 \times 10^{-2})$$
$$V = 2.0 \times 10^{(-4 + -2)}$$
$$V = 2.0 \times 10^{-6} \text{ V}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, option B is the correct choice.
