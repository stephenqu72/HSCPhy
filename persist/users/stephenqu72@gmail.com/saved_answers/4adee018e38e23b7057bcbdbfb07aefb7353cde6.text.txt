To solve this problem, we need to understand the relationship between gravitational potential ($V$) and gravitational potential energy ($U$).

### 1. Direction of Motion
Gravitational potential $V$ is defined as the work done per unit mass to bring an object from infinity to a point. Near Earth, $V$ is zero at infinity and becomes increasingly negative as you get closer to the Earth's surface. 
*   Initial potential $V_1 = -15 \text{ MJ kg}^{-1}$
*   Final potential $V_2 = -65 \text{ MJ kg}^{-1}$

Since $-65 < -15$, the satellite is moving to a region of lower gravitational potential, which corresponds to moving **closer to the Earth**.

### 2. Change in Potential Energy
The gravitational potential energy $U$ is given by the formula:
$$U = mV$$
where $m$ is the mass of the object and $V$ is the gravitational potential.

*   Initial potential energy $U_1 = m \times V_1 = 80 \text{ kg} \times (-15 \text{ MJ kg}^{-1}) = -1200 \text{ MJ}$
*   Final potential energy $U_2 = m \times V_2 = 80 \text{ kg} \times (-65 \text{ MJ kg}^{-1}) = -5200 \text{ MJ}$

The change in potential energy ($\Delta U$) is:
$$\Delta U = U_2 - U_1$$
$$\Delta U = -5200 \text{ MJ} - (-1200 \text{ MJ})$$
$$\Delta U = -5200 \text{ MJ} + 1200 \text{ MJ} = -4000 \text{ MJ}$$

A change of $-4000 \text{ MJ}$ represents a **loss of $4000 \text{ MJ}$** of potential energy.

Comparing this result to the given options:
- Direction: Closer to the Earth
- Change: Loss of $4000 \text{ MJ}$

This corresponds to option **(B)**.
