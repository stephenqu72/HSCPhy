To solve this problem, we apply Newton’s Third Law and the principle of the motor effect.

### Explanation

1.  **Understand the Physics:** The electronic balance measures the "normal force" exerted by the magnet on the balance. When current flows through the wire, a magnetic force ($F_B$) is exerted on the wire. By Newton's Third Law, an equal and opposite force is exerted on the magnet.
    *   If the force on the magnet is directed **downward**, the balance reading increases.
    *   If the force on the magnet is directed **upward**, the balance reading decreases.

2.  **Analyze the Initial Change:**
    *   Initial reading (no current) $= 142.0\text{ g}$.
    *   Reading with $2.0\text{ A}$ (direction $XY$) $= 144.6\text{ g}$.
    *   The change in mass is $144.6 - 142.0 = +2.6\text{ g}$.
    *   This positive change means the magnetic force on the magnet is downward.

3.  **Determine the Force for the New Scenario:**
    *   The magnetic force is given by $F_B = BIl\sin\theta$. Since $B$, $l$, and $\theta$ are constant, the force is directly proportional to the current ($F_B \propto I$).
    *   For $2.0\text{ A}$, the change in mass is $2.6\text{ g}$.
    *   For $1.0\text{ A}$, the change in mass would be $\frac{2.6}{2.0} = 1.3\text{ g}$.
    *   Therefore, for $3.0\text{ A}$, the magnitude of the change in mass is $3 \times 1.3\text{ g} = 3.9\text{ g}$.

4.  **Account for Direction:**
    *   The current direction is now **$YX$**, which is the exact opposite of the initial direction $XY$.
    *   Because the current direction is reversed, the magnetic force on the magnet will also reverse.
    *   Since the original $2.0\text{ A}$ current (in direction $XY$) caused an **increase** in the reading, the $3.0\text{ A}$ current (in direction $YX$) will cause a **decrease** in the reading relative to the initial $142.0\text{ g}$.

5.  **Calculate Final Reading:**
    *   New Reading $= 142.0\text{ g} - 3.9\text{ g} = 138.1\text{ g}$.

The correct answer is **(A)**.
