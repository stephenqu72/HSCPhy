### Front
Why has the SI system shifted from artifact-based definitions to constant-based definitions?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Artifacts (e.g., the prototype kilogram) degrade over time; defining units by universal physical constants ensures stability, precision, and reproducibility anywhere in the universe.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant (applies to $c$, $h$, and $e$).
- **When to use it:** When asked about the reliability of measurement standards or the evolution of the SI unit system.
- **Common trap:** Focusing only on the definitions themselves rather than explaining the *reasoning* (instability of artifacts vs. invariance of fundamental constants).
