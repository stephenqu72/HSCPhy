### Front
What is the specific condition that triggers Beta-minus ($\beta^-$) decay?

### Back
- Key idea: An excess of neutrons relative to protons within a nucleus.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $n \rightarrow p + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e$
- When to use it: To identify the mechanism for increasing the proton-to-neutron ratio to achieve stability.
- Common trap: Confusing $\beta^-$ decay (excess neutrons) with $\beta^+$ decay (excess protons).
