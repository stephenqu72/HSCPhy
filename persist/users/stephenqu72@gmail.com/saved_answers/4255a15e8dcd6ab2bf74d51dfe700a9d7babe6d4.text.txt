To solve this problem, we need to understand the definition of angular velocity and how it relates to rotational frequency.

### Explanation

1.  **Define Angular Velocity ($\omega$):** The angular velocity of an object in circular motion is defined as the rate of change of its angular position. It is directly related to the frequency of rotation ($f$), which is the number of revolutions per unit time. The formula is:
    $$\omega = 2\pi f$$

2.  **Analyze the Variables:**
    *   The mass of the object is $250\text{ g}$, but this does not affect angular velocity.
    *   The length of the string ($r$) is changed from $2.0\text{ m}$ to $3.0\text{ m}$.
    *   The problem explicitly states that we are **maintaining the same rotational frequency** ($f = 100\text{ rev/min}$).

3.  **Evaluate the Relationship:**
    Looking at the formula $\omega = 2\pi f$, we can see that the angular velocity $\omega$ depends **only** on the frequency $f$. It is independent of the radius $r$ (the length of the string).

    Since the rotational frequency remains constant, the angular velocity must also remain constant, regardless of how much the string length is increased or decreased.

Therefore, the angular velocity will be the same as the original value.
