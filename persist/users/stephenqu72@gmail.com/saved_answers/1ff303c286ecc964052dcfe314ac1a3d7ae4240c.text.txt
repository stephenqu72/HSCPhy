### Explanation

To solve this, we must look at the standard formula for gravitational potential energy ($U$) between two masses:

$$U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$$

Where:
*   $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
*   $M$ is the mass of the Earth.
*   $m$ is the mass of the object.
*   $r$ is the distance between the center of the Earth and the object.

**Key features to consider:**

1.  **Negative values:** Because gravity is an attractive force, we define the gravitational potential energy to be zero at an infinite distance from the Earth ($r = \infty$). As the object gets closer to Earth (decreasing $r$), it loses potential energy, making $U$ negative. This eliminates graphs (A) and (B), which are in the positive $U$ region.
2.  **Inverse relationship:** The formula shows that $U$ is inversely proportional to $r$ ($U \propto -1/r$). 
    *   As $r$ increases, the value of $-GMm/r$ becomes less negative (approaching zero from below).
    *   As $r$ approaches the Earth's radius (small $r$), the value becomes a large negative number.
3.  **Shape:** Graph (C) shows $U$ starting as a large negative value at small $r$ and increasing toward zero as $r$ increases. This matches the mathematical behavior of the function $U = -k/r$. Graph (D) shows the magnitude increasing as distance increases, which is incorrect.
