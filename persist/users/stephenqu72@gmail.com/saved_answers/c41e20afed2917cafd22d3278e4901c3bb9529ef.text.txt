To determine the mass of the Sun, we use Kepler's Third Law as derived from Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation.

### 1. Identify the given variables
*   Orbital period ($T$): $365.25 \text{ days}$
*   Average orbital radius ($r$): $1.496 \times 10^{11} \text{ m}$
*   Gravitational constant ($G$): $6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{ kg}^{-2}$

### 2. Convert units to SI
The period $T$ must be in seconds:
$$T = 365.25 \times 24 \times 60 \times 60 = 31,557,600 \text{ s} \approx 3.156 \times 10^7 \text{ s}$$

### 3. Apply the formula
The relationship between mass ($M$), radius ($r$), and period ($T$) is given by:
$$T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{GM}$$
Rearranging to solve for the mass of the Sun ($M$):
$$M = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{GT^2}$$

### 4. Calculation
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$M = \frac{4 \times \pi^2 \times (1.496 \times 10^{11})^3}{(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \times (3.156 \times 10^7)^2}$$

First, calculate the numerator:
$$4 \times \pi^2 \times 3.348 \times 10^{33} \approx 1.322 \times 10^{35}$$

Next, calculate the denominator:
$$(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \times (9.960 \times 10^{14}) \approx 6.647 \times 10^4$$

Finally, divide:
$$M \approx \frac{1.322 \times 10^{35}}{6.647 \times 10^4} \approx 1.99 \times 10^{30} \text{ kg}$$
