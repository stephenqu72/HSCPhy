### Answer

**(a)** The rate of energy production is controlled by adjusting the depth of the **control rods** within the reactor core. These rods are made of materials (such as boron or cadmium) that are excellent at absorbing neutrons. To slow down or stop the chain reaction, the control rods are lowered further into the core to absorb more neutrons, thereby reducing the number of neutrons available to cause further fission of $^{235}U$. To increase the rate of energy production, the rods are raised, allowing more neutrons to participate in the chain reaction.

**(b)** 
1. **Calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$) for one fission reaction:**
   $$\Delta m = (\text{mass of reactants}) - (\text{mass of products})$$
   $$\Delta m = (235.04 + 1.008) - (140.91 + 91.93 + 3 \times 1.008)$$
   $$\Delta m = 236.048 - 236.084 = -0.036 \, \text{u}$$
   (Taking the absolute value for energy released: $0.036 \, \text{u}$)

2. **Convert mass defect to energy ($E$):**
   Using $1 \, \text{u} \approx 1.66 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{kg}$ and $E = mc^2$ (where $c = 3 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}$):
   $$\Delta m = 0.036 \times 1.66 \times 10^{-27} = 5.976 \times 10^{-29} \, \text{kg}$$
   $$E = 5.976 \times 10^{-29} \times (3 \times 10^8)^2 \approx 5.38 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{J per atom}$$

3. **Calculate total energy for 1 kg ($2.55 \times 10^{24}$ atoms):**
   $$E_{\text{total}} = 5.38 \times 10^{-12} \times 2.55 \times 10^{24} \approx 1.37 \times 10^{13} \, \text{J/kg}$$
   Convert to MJ: $1.37 \times 10^{7} \, \text{MJ/kg}$.

4. **Compare to coal ($10 \, \text{MJ/kg}$):**
   $$\text{Ratio} = \frac{1.37 \times 10^7}{10} = 1,370,000 \, \text{times greater.}$$
