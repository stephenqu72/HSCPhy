### Answer and Explanation

**Part a: Calculate the critical angle**

To find the critical angle ($c$), we use Snell's Law where the angle of refraction is $90^\circ$. The formula is:
$$\sin(c) = \frac{n_2}{n_1}$$
Where $n_1 = 1.52$ (glass) and $n_2 = 1.00$ (air).

1. Set up the equation:
   $$\sin(c) = \frac{1.00}{1.52} \approx 0.6579$$
2. Solve for $c$:
   $$c = \arcsin(0.6579) \approx 41.1^\circ$$

**Part b: Will the ray undergo total internal reflection?**

1. **Find the angle of incidence at side AC:**
   The ray enters the prism perpendicular to side AB, so it travels in a straight line until it hits side AC. Looking at the geometry of the triangle:
   - Angle $B = 90^\circ$
   - Angle $C = 45^\circ$
   - Therefore, Angle $A = 180^\circ - 90^\circ - 45^\circ = 45^\circ$.
   
   Because the incident ray is parallel to side BC, the angle between the ray and the normal at side AC is equal to the angle $A$ of the triangle.
   - Angle of incidence $i = 45^\circ$.

2. **Justify:**
   - Total Internal Reflection (TIR) occurs if the angle of incidence ($i$) is greater than the critical angle ($c$).
   - We found $c \approx 41.1^\circ$ and our angle of incidence $i = 45^\circ$.
   - Since $45^\circ > 41.1^\circ$, the condition for TIR is met.
   - **Conclusion:** Yes, the ray will undergo total internal reflection.
