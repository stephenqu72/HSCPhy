When an electron transitions from a higher energy level ($E_{initial} = -3.54\text{ eV}$) to a lower energy level ($E_{final} = -5.74\text{ eV}$), the energy of the emitted photon ($\Delta E$) is:
$$\Delta E = E_{initial} - E_{final}$$
$$\Delta E = -3.54\text{ eV} - (-5.74\text{ eV}) = 2.20\text{ eV}$$

**2. Convert the energy from eV to Joules:**
Using the conversion $1\text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$:
$$\Delta E = 2.20 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J} \approx 3.5244 \times 10^{-19}\text{ J}$$

**3. Calculate the wavelength of the photon:**
Using the Planck-Einstein relation $\Delta E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$, where $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J s}$ and $c = 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$:
$$\lambda = \frac{hc}{\Delta E}$$
$$\lambda = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)}{3.5244 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$\lambda \approx 5.64 \times 10^{-7}\text{ m} = 564\text{ nm}$$
