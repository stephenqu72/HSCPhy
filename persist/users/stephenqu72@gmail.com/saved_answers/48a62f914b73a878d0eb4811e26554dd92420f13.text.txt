To determine the force acting on the electron, we use the formula for the magnetic force on a moving charge:

$$F = qvB \sin(\theta)$$

### Step 1: Identify the variables
*   $q$ (charge of an electron) = $1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
*   $v$ (velocity) = $80 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   $B$ (magnetic field strength) = $20 \text{ T}$
*   $\theta$ (angle between velocity vector and magnetic field vector) = $90^\circ$ (since the electron travels "up" into a "rightwards" field)
*   $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$

### Step 2: Calculate the magnitude
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$F = (1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \times 80 \times 20 \times 1$$
$$F = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \times 1600$$
$$F = 2.56 \times 10^{-16} \text{ N}$$
Rounding to two significant figures, we get $F = 2.6 \times 10^{-16} \text{ N}$.

### Step 3: Determine the direction
We use the **Right-Hand Rule** (for positive charges):
1.  Point your fingers in the direction of velocity (up).
2.  Curl your fingers towards the magnetic field (right).
3.  Your thumb points **out of the page**.

**Crucial Step:** Because the electron is a **negative charge**, we must reverse the direction determined by the Right-Hand Rule. Therefore, the force on the electron is **into the page**.
