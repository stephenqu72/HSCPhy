To solve this problem, we use Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation, specifically the formula for gravitational field strength $g$:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

where:
- $G$ is the gravitational constant
- $M$ is the mass of the Earth
- $r$ is the distance from the **centre** of the Earth

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the initial state:**
    At the Earth's surface, the distance from the centre is $R$. The field strength is given as:
    $$g_1 = \frac{GM}{R^2} = 9.76 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$$

2.  **Determine the new distance:**
    The question asks for the field strength at a distance of $2R$ **above the surface**. 
    The total distance from the centre of the Earth ($r_2$) is the radius of the Earth ($R$) plus the height above the surface ($2R$):
    $$r_2 = R + 2R = 3R$$

3.  **Calculate the new field strength ($g_2$):**
    Substitute $r_2 = 3R$ into the formula:
    $$g_2 = \frac{GM}{(3R)^2} = \frac{GM}{9R^2}$$

4.  **Relate $g_2$ to $g_1$:**
    Since we know $g_1 = \frac{GM}{R^2} = 9.76$, we can substitute this into the equation for $g_2$:
    $$g_2 = \frac{1}{9} \times \left( \frac{GM}{R^2} \right)$$
    $$g_2 = \frac{9.76}{9}$$
    $$g_2 \approx 1.084 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the value is closest to **1.08 N kg$^{-1}$**.

**Correct Option: A**
