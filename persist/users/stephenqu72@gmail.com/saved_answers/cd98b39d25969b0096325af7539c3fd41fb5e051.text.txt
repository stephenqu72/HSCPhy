To solve this problem, we need to calculate the acceleration of an electron in a uniform electric field. 

### Answer
To show that the acceleration $a = 4 \times 10^{16} \text{ ms}^{-2}$, we use the relationship between electric force and Newton's Second Law.

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Voltage ($V$) = $5000 \text{ V}$
*   Distance ($d$) = $2 \text{ cm} = 0.02 \text{ m}$
*   Charge of an electron ($q$) = $1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
*   Mass of an electron ($m$) = $9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$

**2. Calculate the electric field strength ($E$):**
$$E = \frac{V}{d} = \frac{5000 \text{ V}}{0.02 \text{ m}} = 2.5 \times 10^5 \text{ V/m}$$

**3. Calculate the force ($F$) acting on the electron:**
$$F = qE = (1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \times (2.5 \times 10^5 \text{ V/m}) = 4.005 \times 10^{-14} \text{ N}$$

**4. Calculate the acceleration ($a$) using $F = ma$:**
$$a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{4.005 \times 10^{-14} \text{ N}}{9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}} \approx 4.39 \times 10^{16} \text{ ms}^{-2}$$
