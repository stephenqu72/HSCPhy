### Explanation

To understand Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence, we must look at the famous equation:
$$E = mc^2$$

Here is how we evaluate each option:

*   **A is incorrect:** Einstein's theory specifically demonstrates that mass and energy are **not** independent; they are fundamentally linked and can be converted into one another.
*   **B is incorrect:** "Rest mass" (invariant mass) is an intrinsic property of an object. It does not change regardless of the observer's inertial frame of reference. Only *relativistic mass* (a concept now less commonly used in modern physics) would vary with speed.
*   **C is incorrect:** While they are equivalent, saying they are "identical" is imprecise. They have different units and are not the same physical quantity; rather, mass is a concentrated form of energy.
*   **D is correct:** This is the most accurate physical interpretation of the relationship. It describes that mass is essentially energy in a highly condensed state. In processes like nuclear fission or fusion, a small amount of "lost" mass is converted into a massive amount of energy, confirming that mass is indeed a form of energy.

### How to approach this question:
When answering HSC Physics theory questions on relativity, focus on the distinction between *equivalence* and *identity*. Always remember that mass is not "the same thing" as energy, but rather that mass can be transformed into energy (and vice versa) according to the scale factor $c^2$.
