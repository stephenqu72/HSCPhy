To solve this problem, we rely on **Faraday's Law of Induction**, which states that an induced EMF ($\varepsilon$) is generated when there is a change in the magnetic flux ($\Phi$) through a loop:
$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$$
where magnetic flux is defined as $\Phi = BA \cos(\theta)$.

### Analysis of Movements

**Movement A: Rotation about the $x$-axis**
*   **Physics:** As the loop rotates, the angle $\theta$ between the magnetic field lines (pointing into the page) and the normal to the area of the loop changes continuously. Because $\cos(\theta)$ is changing, the magnetic flux through the loop changes.
*   **EMF:** The change in flux is sinusoidal. One full rotation results in one full cycle of an alternating EMF.
*   **Current:** Since the EMF is alternating, the induced current is **alternating**.

**Movement B: Moving parallel to the wire (Position 1 to 2)**
*   **Physics:** The magnetic field produced by a long straight wire at a distance $r$ is $B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$. Moving parallel to the wire means the distance $r$ from the wire remains constant for every point on the loop. Therefore, the magnetic field strength $B$ through the loop remains constant, and the area and orientation do not change.
*   **EMF:** Since $\Phi$ is constant, $\frac{d\Phi}{dt} = 0$. There is **no induced EMF**.
*   **Current:** **No current**.

**Movement C: Moving perpendicular to the wire (Position 2 to 3)**
*   **Physics:** Moving away from the wire increases the distance $r$ for all parts of the loop. As $r$ increases, the magnetic field $B$ decreases. Consequently, the magnetic flux through the loop decreases.
*   **EMF:** A change in flux induces an EMF. Since the motion is at a constant speed, the rate of change of flux is constant for a short duration, leading to a constant (non-zero) EMF value.
*   **Current:** According to Lenz’s Law, the induced current will create a magnetic field to oppose the decrease in flux (i.e., it tries to maintain the field pointing into the page). Using the Right-Hand Grip Rule, a **clockwise** current produces a magnetic field into the page.
