### Answer
Erwin Schrödinger’s model of the atom is a quantum mechanical model that describes the behavior of electrons as follows:

1.  **Wave-Particle Duality:** Schrödinger proposed that electrons do not move in fixed, circular orbits (as in the Bohr model) but instead behave as three-dimensional standing waves around the nucleus.
2.  **Probability Clouds (Orbitals):** Rather than defined paths, the model uses mathematical equations to define regions of space where there is a high probability of finding an electron. These regions are known as electron clouds or atomic orbitals.
3.  **Mathematical Basis:** The model is defined by the Schrödinger wave equation, which provides the energy levels of electrons and the shapes and orientations of these orbitals (e.g., $s$, $p$, $d$, $f$ orbitals) within the atom.
