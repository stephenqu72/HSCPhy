To solve this problem, we must analyze the motion of a proton moving through a uniform electric field. This is a classic projectile motion problem where the "gravity" is replaced by the electric force.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
*   **Electric Field Strength ($E$):** The electric field between parallel plates is $E = \frac{V}{d}$.
    *   $V = 100\text{ V}$
    *   $d = 20\text{ cm} = 0.2\text{ m}$
    *   $E = \frac{100}{0.2} = 500\text{ V m}^{-1}$
*   **Force and Acceleration:** The electric force on the proton is $F = qE = ma$, so the vertical acceleration is $a_y = \frac{qE}{m}$.
    *   Proton charge $q = 1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
    *   Proton mass $m = 1.673 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$
    *   $a_y = \frac{1.602 \times 10^{-19} \times 500}{1.673 \times 10^{-27}} \approx 4.788 \times 10^{10}\text{ m s}^{-2}$
*   **Velocity Components:**
    *   Horizontal velocity ($v_x$) is constant: $v_x = u$.
    *   Vertical velocity after time $t = 2 \times 10^{-6}\text{ s}$ is: $v_y = a_y t$.
    *   $v_y = (4.788 \times 10^{10}) \times (2 \times 10^{-6}) = 9.576 \times 10^4\text{ m s}^{-1}$.

### 2. Relate to the Direction
The problem states the velocity is at $45^\circ$ to the horizontal at $t = 2\text{ \mu s}$.
In a velocity vector triangle, $\tan(\theta) = \frac{v_y}{v_x}$.
Since $\theta = 45^\circ$, $\tan(45^\circ) = 1$, which means $v_y = v_x$.
Therefore, $u = v_y = 9.576 \times 10^4\text{ m s}^{-1}$.

### 3. Calculate the Magnitude
The resultant velocity $v$ is given by the Pythagorean theorem:
$$v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$$
Since $v_x = v_y = u$:
$$v = \sqrt{u^2 + u^2} = \sqrt{2u^2} = u\sqrt{2} \approx 1.414u$$

Given that $u = 9.576 \times 10^4\text{ m s}^{-1}$, we calculate the magnitude:
$$v = 1.414 \times 9.576 \times 10^4 \approx 1.354 \times 10^5\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

Comparing this to the options:
Option C is $1.35 \times 10^5\text{ m s}^{-1}$.

**Correct Answer:** C
