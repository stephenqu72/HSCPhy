### Answer and Explanation

#### Part a) Calculate the average EMF
To calculate the average induced EMF ($\varepsilon$), we use Faraday's Law of Induction, which states that the induced EMF is equal to the rate of change of magnetic flux ($\Phi_B$):
$$\varepsilon = - \frac{\Delta \Phi_B}{\Delta t}$$

**1. Identify given values:**
*   Side length of the square loop ($s$) = $0.040\text{ m}$
*   Area of the loop ($A$) = $s^2 = (0.040\text{ m})^2 = 0.0016\text{ m}^2$
*   Magnetic field strength ($B$) = $0.30\text{ T}$
*   Time interval ($\Delta t$) = $0.50\text{ s}$

**2. Calculate the change in flux ($\Delta \Phi_B$):**
*   Initial flux ($\Phi_{initial}$) = $0\text{ Wb}$ (loop is outside the field).
*   Final flux ($\Phi_{final}$) = $B \times A = 0.30\text{ T} \times 0.0016\text{ m}^2 = 0.00048\text{ Wb}$.
*   Change in flux ($\Delta \Phi_B$) = $0.00048\text{ Wb} - 0\text{ Wb} = 0.00048\text{ Wb}$.

**3. Calculate the EMF:**
$$\varepsilon = \frac{0.00048\text{ Wb}}{0.50\text{ s}} = 0.00096\text{ V}$$
The average EMF produced is **$9.6 \times 10^{-4}\text{ V}$** (or $0.96\text{ mV}$).

---

#### Part b) Direction of the induced current
According to **Lenz's Law**, the direction of the induced current will be such that it creates a magnetic field to oppose the change in magnetic flux. 

1.  As the loop moves into the field, the magnetic flux directed **out of the page** is **increasing**.
2.  To oppose this increase, the loop must create a magnetic field directed **into the page**.
3.  Using the Right-Hand Grip Rule, for a magnetic field pointing into the page, the current must flow in a **clockwise** direction.

**Direction:** Clockwise.
