### Front
Which of Kepler’s Laws of Planetary Motion dictates that the square of the orbital period is proportional to the cube of the orbital radius, and how is it expressed?

### Back
- Key idea: The ratio of the period squared to the semi-major axis cubed is constant for all bodies orbiting the same central mass.
- Formula / law / constant: Kepler’s Third Law: $\frac{T^2}{r^3} = k$ (where $k = \frac{4\pi^2}{GM}$).
- When to use it: To compare the orbital characteristics of two objects orbiting the same central body (e.g., planets around the Sun).
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., years to seconds, AU to metres) when calculating specific values for $k$.
