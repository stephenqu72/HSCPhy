### Explanation

When a wire of length $l$ moves through a magnetic field of strength $B$ with a velocity $v$, the induced electromotive force (EMF) or potential difference $V$ across the wire is given by the formula:

$$V = Blv \sin(\theta)$$

In this specific scenario, the question states that the wire is cutting the magnetic field at a **constant rate**. This implies two things:
1. The magnetic field strength $B$ is uniform and constant.
2. The length of the wire $l$ cutting the field is constant.
3. The velocity $v$ of the wire is constant.

Since $B$, $l$, and $v$ are all constants, the resulting induced potential difference $V$ must also be a **constant value**. 

Looking at the provided graphs:
*   **Graph A** shows $V$ decreasing over time.
*   **Graph B** shows $V$ increasing exponentially over time.
*   **Graph C** shows $V$ increasing linearly over time.
*   **Graph D** shows $V$ remaining at a constant value over time.

Since the potential difference is constant, Graph D is the only correct representation.

**Correct Answer: D**
