### Answer
The electromagnetic braking effect would be stronger in **Plate A**.

### Explanation and Justification
To solve this, we must apply **Lenz's Law** and the concept of **Eddy Currents**.

1.  **Mechanism of Braking:** As the metal plates enter the magnetic field $B$, the change in magnetic flux ($\Delta \Phi$) induces an electromotive force (EMF), which drives circular currents within the metal known as **eddy currents**. According to Lenz's Law, these currents create their own magnetic fields that oppose the motion of the plate, resulting in a resistive force (braking effect).
2.  **Plate A (Solid):** Because Plate A is a solid conductor, it provides a large, continuous area for eddy currents to flow. The magnitude of these currents is proportional to the area available and inversely proportional to the resistance ($I = V/R$). Large loops of current flow, generating a significant opposing magnetic force, which effectively slows down the plate.
3.  **Plate B (Slotted):** Plate B is composed of thin, vertical strips separated by air gaps (insulators). These slots significantly increase the electrical resistance of the path for potential eddy currents. By disrupting the physical paths that the large circular currents would take, the slots reduce the magnitude of the eddy currents.
4.  **Conclusion:** Since the braking force is directly related to the magnitude of the eddy currents induced, the significantly larger currents in the solid plate (Plate A) produce a much stronger electromagnetic braking force compared to the restricted currents in the slotted plate (Plate B).
