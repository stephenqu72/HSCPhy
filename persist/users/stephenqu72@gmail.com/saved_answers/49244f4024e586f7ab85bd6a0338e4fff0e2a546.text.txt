### Explanation
In HSC Physics, when discussing projectile motion, we assume the object is in free fall (ignoring air resistance). 

1.  **Identify the Forces:** Once the ball is projected from the cliff, it is no longer in contact with the launching mechanism. The only force acting upon it is the gravitational force ($F_g$), also known as weight.
2.  **Direction of Gravity:** Gravity always acts vertically downward towards the center of the Earth.
3.  **Independence of Motion:** While the ball has velocity in both the horizontal ($v_x$) and vertical ($v_y$) directions, force is a vector quantity defined by Newton's Second Law: $F_{net} = ma$. Since the only acceleration acting on the ball is the acceleration due to gravity ($g$), which acts vertically downward at all times during the flight, the net force must also be directed vertically downward.
4.  **Conclusion:** The horizontal velocity remains constant because there is no horizontal force acting on the ball (in the absence of air resistance). Consequently, the total force vector is always pointing straight down, as shown in diagram (B).
