To solve this problem, we use the formula for Young's double-slit interference pattern for bright fringes (maxima):

$$x_n = \frac{n \lambda L}{d}$$

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   Wavelength ($\lambda$) = $500 \text{ nm} = 500 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$
*   Distance from the slits to the screen ($L$) = $2 \text{ m}$
*   Distance between the slits ($d$) = $0.15 \text{ mm} = 0.15 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$
*   Order of the maxima ($n$) = $2$

### 2. Calculate the distance:
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$x_2 = \frac{2 \times (500 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}) \times (2 \text{ m})}{0.15 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}}$$

$$x_2 = \frac{2 \times 10^{-6}}{0.15 \times 10^{-3}}$$
$$x_2 = \frac{2}{0.15} \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$$
$$x_2 \approx 13.33 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$$
$$x_2 \approx 13.33 \text{ mm}$$

**Answer:** The distance from the central maxima to the 2nd order maxima is approximately **$13.33 \text{ mm}$**.

### Explanation for Students:
When approaching these problems, the most critical step is **unit conversion**. Always ensure that all your values are in standard SI units (meters) before plugging them into the equation. In this case, converting nanometers ($10^{-9}$) and millimeters ($10^{-3}$) to meters is essential. 

The formula $x_n = \frac{n \lambda L}{d}$ assumes that the distance to the screen is much larger than the slit separation, which allows us to use the small-angle approximation. Since $2 \text{ m} \gg 0.15 \text{ mm}$, this method is perfectly accurate for your HSC physics exam.
