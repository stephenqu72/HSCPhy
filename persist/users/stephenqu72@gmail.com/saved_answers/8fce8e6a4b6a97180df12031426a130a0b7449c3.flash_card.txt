### Front
Define the process of nuclear fission and identify the essential byproduct that enables a chain reaction.

### Back
- Key idea: A heavy, unstable nucleus (e.g., U-235) absorbs a neutron and splits into two smaller nuclei, releasing energy and additional neutrons.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $^{235}_{92}\text{U} + ^{1}_{0}n \rightarrow \text{Fission Fragments} + \text{neutrons} + \text{Energy}$
- When to use it: When identifying nuclear power generation processes or distinguishing fission from fusion (joining nuclei) or radioactive decay.
- Common trap: Confusing fission (splitting) with fusion (combining) or assuming the reaction only produces energy without triggering subsequent reactions via emitted neutrons.
