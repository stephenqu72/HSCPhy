To find the maximum speed of the truck, we must identify the physics principle governing a vehicle navigating a circular path.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principle
The centripetal force required to keep a vehicle moving in a circle of radius $r$ is provided by the sideways frictional force ($F_f$):
$$F_f = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
where:
- $m$ is the mass of the vehicle
- $v$ is the velocity
- $r$ is the radius of the corner

### 2. Set up the Ratios
Let the subscript 1 represent the car and the subscript 2 represent the truck.
For the car: $F_1 = \frac{m_1 v_1^2}{r}$
For the truck: $F_2 = \frac{m_2 v_2^2}{r}$

We are given the following relationships for the truck:
- $m_2 = 5m_1$
- $F_2 = 3F_1$

Substitute these into the truck's equation:
$$3F_1 = \frac{(5m_1) v_2^2}{r}$$

### 3. Solve for $v_2$
Since $F_1 = \frac{m_1 v_1^2}{r}$, we can substitute $F_1$ in the equation above:
$$3\left(\frac{m_1 v_1^2}{r}\right) = \frac{5m_1 v_2^2}{r}$$

Cancel out the common terms ($m_1$ and $r$) from both sides:
$$3v_1^2 = 5v_2^2$$
$$v_2^2 = \frac{3}{5} v_1^2$$
$$v_2 = v_1 \sqrt{\frac{3}{5}}$$

Given $v_1 = 80 \text{ km h}^{-1}$:
$$v_2 = 80 \times \sqrt{0.6}$$
$$v_2 \approx 80 \times 0.7746$$
$$v_2 \approx 61.97 \text{ km h}^{-1}$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number, we get $62 \text{ km h}^{-1}$.

**Correct Option: (B)**
