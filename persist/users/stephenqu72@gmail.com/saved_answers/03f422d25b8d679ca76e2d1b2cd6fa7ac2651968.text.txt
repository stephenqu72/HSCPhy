### Explanation
To solve this, we need to evaluate the definitions of momentum and kinetic energy in the context of uniform circular motion.

1.  **Kinetic Energy ($E_k$):** The formula for kinetic energy is $E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$. In a stable circular orbit, the speed ($v$) of the satellite is constant. Since the mass ($m$) of the ISS and its orbital speed are constant, the **kinetic energy remains constant**.
2.  **Momentum ($p$):** Momentum is a **vector quantity**, defined as $\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$. While the magnitude of the velocity (speed) is constant, the *direction* of the velocity vector is constantly changing because the ISS is following a circular path. Because the direction of the velocity changes at every point along the orbit, the momentum vector also changes. Therefore, **the momentum changes** throughout the orbital path.

*   **D is correct** because the momentum changes (due to the changing direction of the velocity vector) and the kinetic energy remains constant (due to the constant orbital speed).
