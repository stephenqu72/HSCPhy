### Answer
The magnetic flux density is $84.85\text{ T}$.

---

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we use the formula for magnetic flux ($\Phi$):

$$\Phi = BA \cos(\theta)$$

Where:
*   $\Phi$ is the magnetic flux ($0.15\text{ Wb}$)
*   $B$ is the magnetic flux density (the value we need to find)
*   $A$ is the area of the coil ($2.5 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}^2$)
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the **normal** to the coil and the magnetic field.

**Important Note:** The question states the coil is at $45^\circ$ to the magnetic field. In physics, the angle $\theta$ in the flux formula must be the angle between the *normal* (the perpendicular line) to the coil and the magnetic field lines. If the coil itself is at $45^\circ$ to the field, the angle between the normal and the field is $90^\circ - 45^\circ = 45^\circ$.

**Step-by-step Calculation:**

1.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $B$:**
    $$B = \frac{\Phi}{A \cos(\theta)}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values:**
    $$B = \frac{0.15}{(2.5 \times 10^{-3}) \cos(45^\circ)}$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$\cos(45^\circ) \approx 0.7071$$
    $$B = \frac{0.15}{(2.5 \times 10^{-3}) \times 0.7071}$$
    $$B = \frac{0.15}{0.0017677...}$$
    $$B \approx 84.85\text{ T}$$
