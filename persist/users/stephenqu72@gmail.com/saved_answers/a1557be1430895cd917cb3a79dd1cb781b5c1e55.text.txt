To determine the force experienced by the conductor, we use the **Right-Hand Palm Rule** (or Fleming's Left-Hand Rule).

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   **Current ($I$):** The symbol $\otimes$ indicates the current is flowing **into the page**.
    *   **Magnetic Field ($B$):** The arrows show the magnetic field is directed towards the **top-right** of the page.
    *   **Force ($F$):** We need to find the direction of the magnetic force acting on the conductor.

2.  **Apply the Right-Hand Palm Rule:**
    *   Point your **thumb** in the direction of the current (into the page).
    *   Point your **fingers** in the direction of the magnetic field (up and to the right).
    *   Your **palm** (the force) will now be facing in a direction perpendicular to both the current and the magnetic field.

3.  **Visualization:**
    *   If the current is into the page and the field is towards the top-right, rotating your hand accordingly results in the palm facing towards the **bottom-right**.
    *   Imagine the magnetic field vectors. To be perpendicular to those vectors, the force must point generally "down" or "right." Specifically, when the current is into the page and the field is at an angle, the force vector must be at a $90^{\circ}$ angle to the field vector. 
    *   Looking at the options provided, the vector pointing in the direction perpendicular to the magnetic field line (which is angled at roughly $45^{\circ}$ up-right) and consistent with the cross product $\vec{F} = I(\vec{L} \times \vec{B})$ is **D**.

**Conclusion:**
By applying the Right-Hand Rule, the force vector must be perpendicular to the magnetic field line. Option **D** is the only vector that points down and to the left (perpendicular to the up-right field lines).
