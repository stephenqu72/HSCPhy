### Explanation
1.  **Identify the physics principle:** When an electric current $I$ flows through a conductor with resistance $R$, electrical energy is dissipated as heat. This is known as Joule heating or resistive power loss.
2.  **State the relevant formula:** The formula for power loss ($P_{loss}$) in a wire is given by:
    $$P_{loss} = I^2 R$$
    where $I$ is the current flowing through the wire and $R$ is the resistance of the wire.
3.  **Analyze the relationship:** The problem states that the transmission line has a **constant resistance**. Therefore, $R$ is a constant. Looking at the equation $P_{loss} = I^2 R$, we can see that the power loss is directly proportional to the square of the current ($I^2$).
4.  **Evaluate the options:** 
    *   Since $P_{loss} \propto I^2$, option (D) is correct.
    *   Options (A) and (B) involve the transmission voltage. While voltage and current are related by $V=IR$, the power loss *in the wire itself* is specifically determined by the current flowing through that wire's resistance. Increasing the transmission voltage actually allows us to *decrease* the current for a given power delivery, which is the primary reason high voltage is used in transmission—to minimize the $I^2 R$ losses.

**Conclusion:** The power loss is proportional to the square of the transmission current.

**Correct Answer:** **(D)**
