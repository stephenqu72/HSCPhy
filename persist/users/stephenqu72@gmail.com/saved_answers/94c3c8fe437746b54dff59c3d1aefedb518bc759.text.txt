To determine if Atom X matches the unknown atom, we must calculate the wavelengths of light emitted by transitions between the energy levels of Atom X using the formula:
$$\Delta E = \frac{hc}{\lambda} \implies \lambda = \frac{hc}{\Delta E}$$
Where $hc \approx 1240 \text{ eV nm}$.

Let's test transitions to the $-3.60 \text{ eV}$ level:

**b. Explain how such an emission spectrum is produced.**

An emission spectrum is produced when electrons in an atom, which have been excited to higher energy levels (by absorbing energy from heat or electricity), return to lower energy states. As an electron drops from a higher energy level ($E_2$) to a lower energy level ($E_1$), it must lose energy to conserve energy. This energy is released as a single photon of electromagnetic radiation with energy $E = E_2 - E_1$. Since energy levels are discrete, only specific energy differences are possible, resulting in specific wavelengths of light appearing as distinct lines in the emission spectrum.
