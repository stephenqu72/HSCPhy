### Answer

*   **P-Q (Anode and Slits/Collimators):** These components act to accelerate the electrons (produced at the cathode) and collimate them into a narrow, well-defined beam. By passing through the slits, the cathode rays are directed into a straight path towards the rest of the apparatus.
*   **R-R' (Deflecting Plates):** These are parallel metal plates used to create a controlled electric field (and often paired with an external magnetic field in this experiment) to deflect the cathode ray beam. By varying the voltage across these plates, Thomson could measure the deflection of the electrons to determine their charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$).
*   **S (Fluorescent Screen):** This is a phosphorescent coating at the end of the tube. When the cathode ray beam strikes this surface, it produces a visible glowing spot, allowing the observer to detect the position of the beam and measure its deflection.
