### Explanation
To solve this problem, you need to apply your knowledge of **Baryogenesis** and the early universe:

1.  **The Matter-Antimatter Symmetry Problem:** According to the Standard Model of particle physics, matter and antimatter should have been produced in equal amounts in the early universe. If they were perfectly symmetrical, every particle of matter would have annihilated with a particle of antimatter, leaving behind nothing but pure radiation (photons).
2.  **The "Asymmetry":** Observation of our universe shows it is composed almost entirely of matter. This implies that there was a tiny, inherent preference for matter over antimatter (a process known as CP-violation) during the early stages of the Big Bang.
3.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   (A) is incorrect because energy cannot spontaneously create a universe of matter without a pre-existing broken symmetry.
    *   (B) and (D) are incorrect because fusion and supernovae rearrange existing matter into different elements; they do not create the fundamental subatomic matter that existed prior to the formation of the first stars.
