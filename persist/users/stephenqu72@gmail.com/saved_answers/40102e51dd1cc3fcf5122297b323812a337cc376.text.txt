### Part a) Explain why everyday transformers are not ideal.

To score full marks (3 marks) here, you need to identify and briefly explain the physical mechanisms that cause energy loss (heat) in a transformer:

1.  **Resistance in the Coils (Joule Heating):** The copper wires used to wind the primary and secondary coils have non-zero electrical resistance. When current flows through them, energy is dissipated as heat according to the formula $P = I^2R$.
2.  **Eddy Currents:** The changing magnetic flux in the iron core induces small circular currents (eddy currents) within the core itself. These currents produce heat in the core. *Teacher tip: Mention that laminated cores are used to minimize this.*
3.  **Magnetic Hysteresis:** Energy is lost as the iron core is repeatedly magnetized and demagnetized by the alternating current. The internal friction of the magnetic domains as they reorient generates thermal energy.
4.  **Flux Leakage:** Not all magnetic flux produced by the primary coil passes through the secondary coil; some leaks into the air, meaning the secondary coil does not capture 100% of the magnetic energy generated.

### Part b) Assuming all transformers are ideal, how much current is available for the neighbourhood?

**How to solve:**
In an "ideal" transformer system, we assume no power is lost during the transmission and transformation stages. Therefore, the total power generated ($P_{gen}$) is equal to the power available at the neighbourhood ($P_{neigh}$).

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   Total Power ($P$) = $250\text{ kW} = 250,000\text{ W}$
    *   Neighbourhood Voltage ($V_{neigh}$) = $1,300\text{ V}$

2.  **Use the power formula:**
    Since $P = VI$, we rearrange the formula to solve for current ($I$):
    $$I = \frac{P}{V}$$

3.  **Substitute the values:**
    $$I = \frac{250,000\text{ W}}{1,300\text{ V}}$$
    $$I \approx 192.3076... \text{ A}$$

**Final Answer:**
Rounding to a reasonable number of significant figures (consistent with the data provided), the current available is approximately **$192\text{ A}$**.
