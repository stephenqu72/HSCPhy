### Front
How does the value of the Hubble constant ($H_0$) determine the age of the universe?

### Back
- Key idea: The age of the universe ($t$) is inversely proportional to the Hubble constant ($H_0$). A smaller $H_0$ indicates a slower expansion rate, meaning the universe has taken longer to reach its current size.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $t \approx \frac{1}{H_0}$
- When to use it: Use this relationship to compare the relative ages of two universes with different expansion rates.
- Common trap: Assuming a smaller expansion rate ($H_0$) implies a younger universe; always remember that $t$ and $H_0$ are inversely related.
