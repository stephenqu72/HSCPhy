### Explanation

To solve this, we must look at the historical and scientific significance of Robert Millikan’s experiment within the Physics syllabus:

1.  **Understanding the Goal:** Millikan’s Oil Drop experiment was designed to determine the magnitude of the elementary electric charge. By observing the motion of charged oil droplets in a uniform electric field, Millikan found that the total charge on each droplet was always an integer multiple of a fundamental value ($q = ne$). This fundamental value was the charge of a single electron, approximately $1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$.
2.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   **(A) is correct:** Millikan successfully quantified (measured) the elementary charge of the electron.
    *   **(B) is incorrect:** The charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$) of the electron was established by J.J. Thomson using his cathode ray tube experiments, not by Millikan.
    *   **(C) is partially true but misleading:** While the experiment balanced gravitational force ($mg$) and electrostatic force ($qE$), this describes the *methodology* rather than the *significance* (the importance) of the experiment. In HSC physics, we focus on the outcome—the quantification of the electron's charge.
    *   **(D) is incorrect:** While Millikan’s data (the charge $e$) combined with Thomson's data (the ratio $e/m$) allowed scientists to calculate the *mass of an electron*, it did not directly enable the calculation of the mass of a proton in the way described.

**Summary for exam technique:** When a question asks why an experiment was "important," look for the scientific breakthrough or the fundamental constant it determined, rather than the mechanical steps used to perform it.
