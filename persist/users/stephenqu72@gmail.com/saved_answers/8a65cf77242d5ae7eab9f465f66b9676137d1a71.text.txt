To solve this problem, we need to calculate the mass defect ($\Delta m$) of the nuclear reaction and convert that mass into energy ($E$) using Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence formula, $E = mc^2$.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

**1. Calculate the Mass Defect ($\Delta m$):**
The mass defect is the difference between the total mass of the reactants and the total mass of the products:
$$\Delta m = (\text{mass of } ^{13}\text{C} + \text{mass of } ^1\text{H}) - (\text{mass of } ^{14}\text{N})$$

Using the values from the table:
$$\Delta m = (13.00335 + 1.007825) - 14.00307$$
$$\Delta m = 14.011175 - 14.00307 = 0.008105 \text{ amu}$$

**2. Convert mass from amu to kg:**
We use the conversion factor $1 \text{ amu} = 1.66054 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$:
$$\Delta m (\text{kg}) = 0.008105 \times 1.66054 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$$
$$\Delta m \approx 1.34587 \times 10^{-29} \text{ kg}$$

**3. Calculate Energy ($E$) in Joules:**
Use the formula $E = \Delta m c^2$, where $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$:
$$E = (1.34587 \times 10^{-29} \text{ kg}) \times (3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s})^2$$
$$E = 1.34587 \times 10^{-29} \times 9.00 \times 10^{16}$$
$$E \approx 1.211 \times 10^{-12} \text{ J}$$
