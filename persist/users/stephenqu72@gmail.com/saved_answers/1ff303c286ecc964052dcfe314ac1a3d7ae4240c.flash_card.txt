### Front
How does Gravitational Potential Energy ($U$) vary with distance ($r$) from a central mass, and what is the mathematical relationship?

### Back
- Key idea: $U$ is always negative and increases (becomes less negative) as $r$ increases, approaching zero at infinity.
- Formula / law / constant: $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$
- When to use it: When calculating the potential energy of a satellite or object within a gravitational field (beyond the Earth's surface).
- Common trap: Assuming $U$ is positive or that $U$ increases as $r$ increases (it approaches zero, but the magnitude decreases).
