### Answer
To convert electrical energy into mechanical energy (rotational kinetic energy), the following process occurs:

1.  **Stator Induction:** When an AC supply is connected to the stator windings, it creates a **rotating magnetic field (RMF)**.
2.  **Electromagnetic Induction:** This RMF cuts across the conductors in the rotor (squirrel cage). According to Faraday’s Law and Lenz’s Law, this induces an EMF and subsequently a current within the rotor bars.
3.  **Torque Production:** The induced current in the rotor creates its own magnetic field. This field interacts with the stator's rotating magnetic field, producing a Lorentz force (or torque) that causes the rotor to spin in the same direction as the RMF to minimize relative motion.
