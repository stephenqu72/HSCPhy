### Part a) Sketching the Intensity Graph

1.  **Intensity after $P_1$:** When unpolarised light of intensity $I_0$ passes through the first polarising filter ($P_1$), the intensity is halved. So, $I_1 = \frac{1}{2} I_0$.
2.  **Intensity after $P_2$:** The intensity at the detector follows Malus's Law: $I = I_1 \cos^2(\theta)$, where $\theta$ is the angle between the transmission axes of $P_1$ and $P_2$. Substituting $I_1$, we get:
    $$I = \frac{1}{2} I_0 \cos^2(\theta)$$
3.  **Graph features:**
    *   At $\theta = 0^\circ$ (aligned): $I = 0.5 I_0$.
    *   At $\theta = 90^\circ$ (crossed): $I = 0$.
    *   At $\theta = 180^\circ$ (aligned again): $I = 0.5 I_0$.
    *   **The curve:** The graph should be a smooth, symmetric "hump" shape starting at $0.5 I_0$ on the y-axis, dipping to $0$ at $90^\circ$, and rising back to $0.5 I_0$ at $180^\circ$.

### Part b) Inserting a Third Polariser ($P_{1.5}$)

When $P_2$ is set to $90^\circ$ relative to $P_1$, the light is extinguished. Introducing $P_{1.5}$ at $30^\circ$ to $P_1$ creates a "bridge" for the light:

1.  **After $P_1$:** Light intensity is $\frac{1}{2} I_0$.
2.  **After $P_{1.5}$ (at $\theta = 30^\circ$ to $P_1$):**
    Using Malus's Law: $I_{1.5} = (\frac{1}{2} I_0) \cos^2(30^\circ) = \frac{1}{2} I_0 \times (\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2})^2 = \frac{1}{2} I_0 \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{3}{8} I_0$.
3.  **After $P_2$ (at $\theta = 60^\circ$ to $P_{1.5}$):**
    Since $P_{1.5}$ is at $30^\circ$ and $P_2$ is at $90^\circ$ relative to $P_1$, the angle between $P_{1.5}$ and $P_2$ is $60^\circ$.
    $$I_{final} = I_{1.5} \cos^2(60^\circ) = \left(\frac{3}{8} I_0\right) \times \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{3}{8} I_0 \times \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{32} I_0$$
**Conclusion:** Because $I_{final} = \frac{3}{32} I_0$ (which is greater than zero), light now reaches the detector. The middle filter rotates the plane of polarisation, allowing a component of the light to pass through the final filter.
