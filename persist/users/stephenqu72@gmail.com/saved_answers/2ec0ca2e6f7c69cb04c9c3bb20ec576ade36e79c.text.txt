To determine the acceleration of the second proton, let's analyze the physics of a particle moving through combined electric and magnetic fields.

### Analysis
The total force (Lorentz force) acting on a proton of charge $q$ moving with velocity $\vec{v}$ in an electric field $\vec{E}$ and a magnetic field $\vec{B}$ is given by:
$$\vec{F} = q(\vec{E} + \vec{v} \times \vec{B})$$

**1. The first proton:**
The problem states that the velocity of the first proton remains unchanged. This means its acceleration is zero, which implies the net force is zero:
$$q(\vec{E} + \vec{v} \times \vec{B}) = 0 \implies \vec{E} = -\vec{v} \times \vec{B}$$
This establishes the relationship between the electric field and the magnetic field within the chamber.

**2. The second proton:**
The second proton enters with a velocity $2\vec{v}$. The net force acting on it is:
$$\vec{F}_{net} = q(\vec{E} + (2\vec{v}) \times \vec{B})$$
Substitute $\vec{E} = -\vec{v} \times \vec{B}$ into the equation:
$$\vec{F}_{net} = q(-\vec{v} \times \vec{B} + 2\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$$
$$\vec{F}_{net} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$$

**3. Determining the acceleration:**
Since acceleration $\vec{a} = \frac{\vec{F}_{net}}{m}$:
$$\vec{a} = \frac{q}{m}(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$$
Because $\vec{v}$ and $\vec{B}$ are constant vectors within the uniform fields, the cross product $\vec{v} \times \vec{B}$ is also a constant vector. Consequently, the acceleration $\vec{a}$ is constant in both magnitude and direction.

### Conclusion
The second proton experiences a constant net force, and therefore, a constant acceleration. The correct option is **B**.
