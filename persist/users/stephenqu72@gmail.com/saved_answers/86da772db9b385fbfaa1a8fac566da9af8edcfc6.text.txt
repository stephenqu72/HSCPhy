To determine which diagram shows the greatest torque, we must apply the definition of torque ($\tau$).

### Answer
The correct answer is **(D)**.

### Explanation
The formula for torque is:
$$\tau = rF \sin(\theta)$$
Where:
- $r$ is the distance from the pivot to the point where the force is applied (the lever arm).
- $F$ is the magnitude of the applied force.
- $\theta$ is the angle between the force vector and the radial line from the pivot to the application point.

To maximize the torque, we need to maximize the effective lever arm, which occurs when the force is applied perpendicular to the bar ($\theta = 90^\circ$, so $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$) and as far from the pivot as possible.

Let's evaluate each diagram:
*   **(A)** The forces are applied at an angle to the bar and closer to the pivot. This results in a smaller perpendicular component of the force and a smaller $r$.
*   **(B)** The forces are applied perpendicular to the bar, but the distance $r$ from the pivot is relatively small (closer to the center).
*   **(C)** The forces are applied at an angle and very close to the pivot, resulting in very low torque.
*   **(D)** The forces are applied perpendicular to the bar at the very ends of the bar. This provides the largest possible radial distance ($r$) from the pivot and the maximum possible angle ($\theta = 90^\circ$) between the force and the radius.

Because diagram (D) maximizes both the distance from the pivot and the angle at which the force acts, it produces the greatest torque.
