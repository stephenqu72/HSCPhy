To solve this problem, we apply the principles of Einstein's special relativity.

### 1. Identify the given information
*   The relativistic mass $m$ of the electron is 22 times its rest mass $m_0$: $m = 22m_0$.
*   The Lorentz factor $\gamma$ is defined by the ratio $\frac{m}{m_0}$. Therefore, $\gamma = 22$.
*   The rest mass energy of an electron ($E_0$) is a standard physics constant: $E_0 = m_0 c^2 \approx 0.511 \text{ MeV}$.

### 2. Conceptual approach
The total energy of a relativistic particle is given by $E_{total} = mc^2 = \gamma m_0 c^2$.
The kinetic energy ($K$) of a relativistic particle is the difference between its total energy and its rest mass energy:
$$K = E_{total} - E_0$$
$$K = \gamma m_0 c^2 - m_0 c^2 = (\gamma - 1) m_0 c^2$$

### 3. Calculation
*   We know $\gamma = 22$, so $(\gamma - 1) = 21$.
*   The rest energy of an electron in Joules is approximately $8.187 \times 10^{-14} \text{ J}$ (derived from $m_0 \approx 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$ and $c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$).
*   $K = 21 \times (m_0 c^2)$
*   $K = 21 \times 8.187 \times 10^{-14} \text{ J}$
*   $K \approx 1.719 \times 10^{-12} \text{ J}$

Rounding this to significant figures, we get $1.72 \times 10^{-12} \text{ J}$.
