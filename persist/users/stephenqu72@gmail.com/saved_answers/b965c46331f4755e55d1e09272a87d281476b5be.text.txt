To determine the correct answer, we must analyze the physics of circular motion for a carriage at the highest point of a vertical loop.

### Analysis
At the top of a circular track, a carriage (and its riders) undergoes circular motion. For an object to move in a circle, there must be a **net centripetal force** directed toward the center of the circle (which is downwards at the top of the loop).

The forces acting on the riders at the top of the track are:
1. The force of gravity, $mg$ (acting downwards).
2. The normal reaction force, $N$ (acting from the seat).

The net force providing the centripetal acceleration ($a_c = v^2/r$) is given by:
$$F_{net} = mg + N = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Rearranging for the reaction force $N$:
$$N = \frac{mv^2}{r} - mg$$

Now let's evaluate the options:
*   **A. All riders feel weightless:** This occurs if $N = 0$. This happens when $v^2/r = g$. This is a physically possible state (the minimum speed required to complete the loop).
*   **B. Upward reaction force:** This occurs if $N > 0$. This happens when the speed is high ($v^2/r > g$), meaning the carriage is pressing the rider into the seat. This is possible.
*   **C. Downward reaction force:** This occurs if $N < 0$. This means the seat is pulling the rider downward (e.g., if the riders are strapped in and moving very slowly). This is possible.
*   **D. Zero acceleration:** For an object moving in a circle, the acceleration is directed toward the center of the circle (centripetal acceleration). The magnitude of this acceleration is $a_c = v^2/r$. Since the carriage is moving along a circular path, it **must** have a non-zero centripetal acceleration. Therefore, it is impossible for the riders to have zero acceleration.

### Conclusion
The impossible situation is that the riders have zero acceleration, as they are undergoing circular motion.

**Correct Answer: D**
