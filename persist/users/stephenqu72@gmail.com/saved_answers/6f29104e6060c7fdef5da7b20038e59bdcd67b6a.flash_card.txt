### Front
What are the vertical velocity ($v_y$) and acceleration ($a$) of a projectile at its maximum height (ignoring air resistance)?

### Back
- Key idea: At the peak, vertical motion momentarily ceases, but gravity continues to act.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v_y = 0$; $a = g$ (downwards).
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem requiring state or kinematic analysis at the trajectory peak.
- Common trap: Assuming acceleration is zero because the vertical velocity is momentarily zero. Acceleration is constant throughout the entire flight.
