### Answer

The star is undergoing **nuclear fusion**, specifically the fusion of hydrogen nuclei into helium nuclei within its core. 

Regarding the effect on the star's mass: because the star is releasing a significant amount of energy into space, it must lose mass to account for this energy. According to Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence principle, $E = mc^2$, the energy released corresponds to a direct decrease in the star's total mass. Therefore, the mass of the star will **decrease** over time.

### How to solve this
To achieve full marks for this question, you need to address two distinct parts:

1.  **Identify the process:** In the context of a star, energy production is always attributed to nuclear fusion (the fusion of lighter elements like hydrogen into heavier elements like helium). Mentioning this term clearly is essential for the first mark.
2.  **Explain the mass change:** You must link the energy output to the concept of mass-energy equivalence ($E = mc^2$). Since energy is being radiated away from the star, the system is losing internal energy. Since energy and mass are equivalent, the loss of energy necessitates a corresponding loss of mass. Stating clearly that the mass "decreases" is vital for the second mark.

**Teacher's Tip:** When answering "explain" questions in HSC Physics, always try to use the relevant physical principle or law (in this case, mass-energy equivalence) to justify your reasoning. This shows the examiner you understand the underlying physics rather than just reciting facts.
