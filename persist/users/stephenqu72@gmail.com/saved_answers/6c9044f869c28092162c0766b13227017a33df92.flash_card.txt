### Front
How does $\beta^-$ (beta-minus) decay affect the position of a nuclide on a Neutron ($N$) vs. Atomic Number ($Z$) graph?

### Back
- Key idea: A neutron converts into a proton, increasing $Z$ by 1 and decreasing $N$ by 1.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Movement on an $N$ vs. $Z$ graph: $\Delta Z = +1$, $\Delta N = -1$ (one unit down and to the right).
- When to use it: To identify the daughter product of $\beta^-$ decay on a stability/nuclide graph.
- Common trap: Confusing $\beta^-$ (right/down) with $\beta^+$ decay (left/up) or alpha decay (diagonal move toward origin).
