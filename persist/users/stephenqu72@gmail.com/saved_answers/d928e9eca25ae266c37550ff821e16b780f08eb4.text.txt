### 1. Identify the precision of the measurements
*   **Length ($l$):** $2.3\text{ cm}$. A ruler graduated in cm has a precision of $\pm 0.1\text{ cm}$. The last digit (3) is the uncertain digit.
*   **Width ($w$):** $5.5\text{ mm}$. A ruler graduated in mm has a precision of $\pm 0.1\text{ mm}$ (or $\pm 0.01\text{ cm}$). The last digit (5) is the uncertain digit.

### 2. Standardize units
To add the values, they must be in the same units. Let's convert both to cm:
*   $l = 2.3\text{ cm}$
*   $w = 5.5\text{ mm} = 0.55\text{ cm}$

The formula for the perimeter of a rectangle is $P = 2l + 2w$.
*   $P = 2(2.3\text{ cm}) + 2(0.55\text{ cm})$
*   $P = 4.6\text{ cm} + 1.1\text{ cm}$
*   $P = 5.7\text{ cm}$

### 4. Applying the rule for precision
When adding or subtracting measurements, the final result should be rounded to the same **precision** (number of decimal places) as the *least precise* measurement.
*   $2.3\text{ cm}$ is precise to the **tenths** place (1 decimal place).
*   $0.55\text{ cm}$ is precise to the **hundredths** place (2 decimal places).

The least precise measurement is $2.3\text{ cm}$ (tenths place). Therefore, our result ($5.7\text{ cm}$) must be rounded to the **tenths place**.
