### Front
How do you determine the recessional velocity of a distant galaxy given its distance from Earth?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Hubble’s Law states that recessional velocity is directly proportional to distance.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v = H_0 d$; $H_0 \approx 70 \text{ km s}^{-1} \text{ Mpc}^{-1}$ (Hubble Constant).
- **When to use it:** When calculating the expansion rate of the universe or predicting the velocity of a galaxy at a specific distance.
- **Common trap:** Connecting data points "dot-to-dot" instead of drawing a line of best fit that passes through the origin $(0,0)$.
