To solve this problem, we need to determine the period of the marble's motion and then calculate the time taken to cover the specific angular displacement between point $Y$ and point $Z$.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Find the period ($T$) of the motion:**
The problem states that the marble completes $3$ rotations in $2$ seconds. The period is the time taken for one full rotation ($360^{\circ}$):
$$T = \frac{\text{total time}}{\text{number of rotations}} = \frac{2\text{ s}}{3} \approx 0.667\text{ s}$$

**2. Determine the fraction of the circle moved:**
The marble moves from $Y$ to $Z$, covering an angle of $120^{\circ}$. Since a full circle is $360^{\circ}$, the fraction of the circle covered is:
$$\text{Fraction} = \frac{120^{\circ}}{360^{\circ}} = \frac{1}{3}$$

**3. Calculate the time ($t$):**
Since the speed is constant, the time taken is simply the same fraction of the total period:
$$t = \text{Fraction} \times T$$
$$t = \frac{1}{3} \times \left( \frac{2}{3}\text{ s} \right)$$
$$t = \frac{2}{9}\text{ s} \approx 0.222\text{ s}$$

Rounding to two decimal places, we get $0.22$ seconds.

Comparing this result to the given options:
A. $0.11\text{ s}$
B. $0.16\text{ s}$
C. $0.20\text{ s}$
D. $0.22\text{ s}$

The correct answer is **D**.
