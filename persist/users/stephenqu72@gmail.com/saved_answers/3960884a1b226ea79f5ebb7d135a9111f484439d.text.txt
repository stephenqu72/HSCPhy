### Physics Principles
1. **Direction of Force:** Two parallel wires carrying current in the **same direction** experience an **attractive force**. If they carry current in **opposite directions**, they experience a **repulsive force**.
2. **Magnitude of Force:** The magnitude of the force per unit length $F/L$ between two long parallel conductors is given by the formula:
   $$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$
   where $I_1$ and $I_2$ are the currents, and $r$ is the distance between them. 
3. **Scale:** The problem states that the distance $r$ between the conductors is drawn to scale. By visual inspection of the image, the gap between the conductors in **Option A** is the smallest. Since $F \propto 1/r$, a smaller distance results in a greater force.
