To increase the distance between the bright and dark bands (also known as the fringe spacing, denoted as $\Delta x$) in a Young’s double-slit interference pattern, we must look at the standard interference equation:

$$\Delta x = \frac{\lambda L}{d}$$

Where:
*   $\Delta x$ is the fringe spacing (distance between adjacent bright bands).
*   $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the light used.
*   $L$ is the distance from the slits to the wall (screen).
*   $d$ is the distance between the two slits.

### How to solve this:
Since the question specifies that the wavelength ($\lambda$) cannot be changed, we look at the variables $L$ and $d$:

1.  **Increase the distance between the slits and the wall ($L$):** According to the equation, $\Delta x$ is directly proportional to $L$. If you move the wall further away from the black card, the value of $L$ increases, which results in a larger fringe spacing $\Delta x$.
2.  **Decrease the separation between the slits ($d$):** According to the equation, $\Delta x$ is inversely proportional to $d$. If you bring the two slits closer together, the value of $d$ decreases, which results in a larger fringe spacing $\Delta x$.

**Summary for your answer:**
To increase the distance between the bands, you could:
*   **Increase $L$:** Move the wall further away from the card with the slits. As $L$ increases, $\Delta x$ increases.
*   **Decrease $d$:** Bring the two slits closer together. As $d$ decreases, $\Delta x$ increases.
