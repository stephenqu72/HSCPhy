### Part a: Calculate the speed of the alpha particle

**The Physics:**
For the alpha particle to be at rest in the presence of both an electric and magnetic field, the net force must be zero. This is the principle of a **Velocity Selector**.
1. The electric force $F_E = qE$ acts in the direction of the electric field (from positive plate to negative plate).
2. The magnetic force $F_B = qvB \sin(\theta)$ acts perpendicular to both the velocity and the magnetic field. For the forces to balance, the magnetic force must be equal and opposite to the electric force.

Since they must balance ($F_E = F_B$):
$$qE = qvB$$
$$v = \frac{E}{B}$$

**Calculation:**
Given:
- $E = 50 \, \text{V/m}$
- $B = 0.25 \, \text{T}$

$$v = \frac{50}{0.25}$$
$$v = 200 \, \text{m/s}$$

*Note:* The length of the silk ($L$) and the radius of rotation are distractors for part (a), as the speed is determined solely by the balance of the fields.

### Part b: Describe the motion if the spider silk is cut

**The Physics:**
When the silk is cut, the centripetal force (provided by the tension in the string) vanishes. The alpha particle will then be governed solely by the electric and magnetic fields.

**Description:**
1. **Initial state:** At the moment the string is cut, the particle has a velocity $v = 200 \, \text{m/s}$. 
2. **Path:** Because the particle is moving in a magnetic field with a velocity component perpendicular to the field lines, it will experience a magnetic force $F = qvB$. This force acts as a centripetal force, causing the particle to undergo **uniform circular motion**.
3. **Dynamics:** The particle will move in a circular path with radius $r = \frac{mv}{qB}$. Because $E$ and $B$ are constant, once it moves away from the initial equilibrium point, the electric force and magnetic force will no longer be perfectly balanced, causing the particle to deviate from a straight line and curve.
