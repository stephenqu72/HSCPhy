To calculate the increase in momentum of the proton, we must use the relativistic momentum formula, as the velocities are a significant fraction of the speed of light ($c$).

### 1. Identify the relevant formulas
The relativistic momentum $p$ is given by:
$$p = \gamma mv = \frac{mv}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$
Where:
*   $m = 1.673 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$ (mass of a proton)
*   $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
*   $v$ is the velocity of the proton

The increase in momentum is $\Delta p = p_f - p_i$.

### 2. Calculation
**Step 1: Calculate initial momentum ($p_i$) at $v_i = 0.31c$**
$$\gamma_i = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.31)^2}} \approx \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.0961}} \approx 1.0505$$
$$p_i = \gamma_i m v_i = 1.0505 \times (1.673 \times 10^{-27}) \times (0.31 \times 3 \times 10^8)$$
$$p_i \approx 1.63 \times 10^{-19} \text{ Ns}$$

**Step 2: Calculate final momentum ($p_f$) at $v_f = 0.92c$**
$$\gamma_f = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.92)^2}} \approx \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.8464}} \approx 2.5516$$
$$p_f = \gamma_f m v_f = 2.5516 \times (1.673 \times 10^{-27}) \times (0.92 \times 3 \times 10^8)$$
$$p_f \approx 1.18 \times 10^{-18} \text{ Ns}$$

**Step 3: Calculate the change ($\Delta p$)**
$$\Delta p = 1.18 \times 10^{-18} - 0.163 \times 10^{-18} \approx 1.017 \times 10^{-18} \text{ Ns}$$

Comparing this to the provided options, the value is approximately $1.0 \times 10^{-18} \text{ Ns}$.

**Correct Option: C**

### How to solve this type of question:
1.  **Recognize the context:** When dealing with velocities greater than $\sim 0.1c$, always use the relativistic momentum formula rather than the classical $p=mv$.
2.  **Calculate Lorentz factors:** Calculate $\gamma$ separately for the initial and final velocities to keep your calculations clean.
3.  **Find the difference:** Subtract the initial relativistic momentum from the final relativistic momentum.
4.  **Significant Figures:** Ensure your mass of the proton and speed of light constants are accurate to at least 3 significant figures.
