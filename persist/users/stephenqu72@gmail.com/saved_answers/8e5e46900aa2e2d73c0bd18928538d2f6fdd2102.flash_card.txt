### Front
What are the quark compositions of a proton and a neutron, and how is their charge calculated?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Protons and neutrons are baryons composed of three quarks with fractional charges ($u = +2/3e$, $d = -1/3e$).
- **Formula / law:** Proton ($uud$) = $+2/3 + 2/3 - 1/3 = +1e$; Neutron ($udd$) = $+2/3 - 1/3 - 1/3 = 0e$.
- **When to use it:** Identifying subatomic structure or verifying conservation of charge in nuclear reactions.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the number of quarks (baryons have 3) or mixing up the signs of the up and down quark charges.
