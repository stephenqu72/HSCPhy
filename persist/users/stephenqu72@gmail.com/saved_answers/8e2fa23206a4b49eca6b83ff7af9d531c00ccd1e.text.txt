### Answer
The correct answer is **(C) the wave nature of the electron.**

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to recall the significance of the Davisson-Germer experiment in the context of the Physics "From the Universe to the Atom" module.

1.  **Context:** In 1924, Louis de Broglie hypothesized that if light could behave like both a wave and a particle (wave-particle duality), then matter particles like electrons should also exhibit wave-like properties. The de Broglie wavelength is given by:
    $$\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$$
    where $h$ is Planck's constant and $p$ is the momentum of the particle.

2.  **The Experiment:** Davisson and Germer conducted an experiment firing electrons at a crystalline nickel target. They observed a diffraction pattern in the scattered electrons. 

3.  **Conclusion:** Diffraction is a characteristic behavior of waves. By demonstrating that electrons could be diffracted by a crystal lattice (similar to how X-rays are diffracted), Davisson and Germer provided the first experimental confirmation of de Broglie’s hypothesis. 

4.  **Elimination of other choices:**
    *   (A) is associated with the Millikan Oil Drop experiment.
    *   (B) describes the nature of the electron already accepted through Thomson’s cathode ray tube experiments.
    *   (D) is associated with the Rutherford Gold Foil experiment.

Therefore, the Davisson-Germer experiment is specifically famous for proving the **wave nature of the electron**.
