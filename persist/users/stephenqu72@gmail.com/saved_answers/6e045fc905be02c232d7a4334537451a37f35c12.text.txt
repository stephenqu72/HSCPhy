To solve this problem, we apply the principle of **Length Contraction** from Einstein's Theory of Special Relativity. 

### 1. Identify the given information:
*   Proper length ($L_0$): The length measured when the object is stationary relative to the observer, which is $42\text{ m}$.
*   Velocity of the spaceship ($v$): $2.75 \times 10^8\text{ m s}^{-1}$.
*   Speed of light ($c$): $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8\text{ m s}^{-1}$.

### 2. The Formula:
The length contraction formula is:
$$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
where $L$ is the observed length of the object moving at velocity $v$.

### 3. Step-by-Step Calculation:
First, calculate the ratio $\frac{v}{c}$:
$$\frac{v}{c} = \frac{2.75 \times 10^8}{3.00 \times 10^8} \approx 0.91667$$

Now, square this value:
$$\left(\frac{v}{c}\right)^2 \approx (0.91667)^2 \approx 0.84028$$

Next, substitute this into the Lorentz factor part of the equation:
$$\sqrt{1 - 0.84028} = \sqrt{0.15972} \approx 0.39965$$

Finally, multiply by the proper length ($L_0$):
$$L = 42 \times 0.39965 \approx 16.785\text{ m}$$

Rounding to two decimal places, we get **$16.79\text{ m}$**.
