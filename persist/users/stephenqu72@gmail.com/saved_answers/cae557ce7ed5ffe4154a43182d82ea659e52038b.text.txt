To solve this problem, we need to relate the linear distance traveled by the tip of the blade to the angular velocity of the rotation.

### 1. Identify the given variables
*   Radius of the blade ($r$) = $1.5\text{ m}$
*   Arc length ($s$) = $2.5\text{ m}$
*   Time interval ($\Delta t$) = $0.5\text{ s}$

### 2. Calculate the angular displacement ($\theta$)
The relationship between arc length ($s$), radius ($r$), and angular displacement in radians ($\theta_{rad}$) is given by:
$$s = r\theta_{rad}$$
Rearranging for $\theta_{rad}$:
$$\theta_{rad} = \frac{s}{r} = \frac{2.5\text{ m}}{1.5\text{ m}} = 1.666... \text{ rad}$$

### 3. Convert angular displacement to degrees
Since the options are in degrees per second, we must convert the radians to degrees. 
Recall that $1\text{ rad} = \frac{180^\circ}{\pi}$.
$$\theta_{deg} = 1.666... \times \left(\frac{180}{\pi}\right) \approx 95.49^\circ$$

### 4. Calculate angular velocity ($\omega$)
Angular velocity is defined as the change in angular displacement over time:
$$\omega = \frac{\theta_{deg}}{\Delta t}$$
$$\omega = \frac{95.49^\circ}{0.5\text{ s}} \approx 190.98^\circ\text{ s}^{-1}$$

Rounding this to the nearest whole number gives $191^\circ\text{ s}^{-1}$.

Comparing this to the given options, the correct answer is **D**.
