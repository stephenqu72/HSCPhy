To answer this question effectively for the HSC Physics exam, you need to link the concept of the **strong nuclear force** to the observed stability of nuclei as shown on the $N$ vs $Z$ (Segrè) plot.

### Answer
The stability of nuclei is determined by the balance between the attractive strong nuclear force and the repulsive electrostatic force. The features of the nuclear force that explain this are:

1.  **Short-range nature:** The strong nuclear force is highly attractive but only acts over a very short range (approximately $1-2$ femtometres). It effectively binds nucleons together, counteracting the long-range electrostatic repulsion between protons.
2.  **Saturation:** The strong nuclear force is "saturated," meaning a nucleon only interacts with its immediate neighbours. As the number of protons ($Z$) increases, the electrostatic repulsion grows significantly due to its long-range nature, requiring more neutrons to provide extra attractive strong force without adding extra electrostatic repulsion.
3.  **Stability requirement:** For heavy nuclei, the increasing number of protons leads to an $N/Z$ ratio greater than $1$. The additional neutrons are required to increase the average separation between protons and provide additional strong force attraction, which is necessary to maintain nuclear stability against the disruptive electrostatic repulsion.

### How to solve it
1.  **Analyze the Graph:** Notice that the black "stable" line deviates above the $Z=N$ line as $Z$ increases. This tells you that larger nuclei require a higher neutron-to-proton ratio to remain stable.
2.  **Identify the Competition:** Recognize that there are two competing forces in the nucleus: the **strong nuclear force** (which is attractive and holds the nucleus together) and the **electrostatic (Coulomb) force** (which is repulsive and tries to push protons apart).
3.  **Structure for 3 Marks:**
    *   **Mark 1:** Describe the short-range, attractive nature of the strong nuclear force.
    *   **Mark 2:** Contrast this with the long-range, repulsive nature of the electrostatic force.
    *   **Mark 3:** Explain *why* the plot deviates from $Z=N$: as more protons are added, you need more neutrons (which contribute only to the strong force, not the electrostatic repulsion) to stabilize the nucleus.
