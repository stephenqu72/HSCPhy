To determine the correct expression for the charge $Q$ on an oil drop balanced in a Millikan oil drop experiment, we analyze the forces acting on the drop.

### Explanation:
1.  **Identify the forces:** For an oil drop to be "balanced" (stationary) between two parallel plates, the downward gravitational force must be exactly equal to the upward electric force.
    *   The gravitational force is given by $F_g = mg$, where $m$ is the mass of the drop and $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity.
    *   The electric force is given by $F_e = QE$, where $Q$ is the charge on the drop and $E$ is the electric field strength.

2.  **Relate the Electric Field to Voltage:** In a parallel plate capacitor, the electric field strength $E$ is related to the potential difference (voltage) $V$ across the plates and the distance $d$ between the plates by the formula:
    $$E = \frac{V}{d}$$

3.  **Equate the forces:** Since the drop is balanced, we set the forces equal to each other:
    $$F_e = F_g$$
    $$QE = mg$$

4.  **Substitute and Solve for $Q$:** Replace $E$ with $\frac{V}{d}$:
    $$Q \left( \frac{V}{d} \right) = mg$$
    To isolate $Q$, multiply both sides by $d$ and divide by $V$:
    $$Q = \frac{mgd}{V}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, we can see that it matches option **C**.

### Answer:
The correct option is **C**.
