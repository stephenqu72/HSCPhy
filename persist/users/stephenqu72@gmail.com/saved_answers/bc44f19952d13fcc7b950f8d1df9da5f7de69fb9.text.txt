### Answer
Einstein concluded that time is relative because the speed of light is constant for all observers. As shown in the diagrams, observer A sees the light travel a vertical path of length $2d$. However, because the box is moving relative to observer B, B perceives the light as traveling a longer, diagonal path. Since the speed of light ($c$) must be the same for both observers, and $speed = \frac{distance}{time}$, the observer who measures a longer distance (observer B) must also measure a longer duration of time ($t' > t$). This phenomenon is known as time dilation.

4.  **Conclude:** Identify this difference as the definition of time dilation—that time intervals measured between two events depend on the relative motion of the observer.
