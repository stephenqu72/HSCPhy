### Front
How do you calculate the local acceleration due to gravity ($g$) of a planet if you know an object's weight on Earth and on that planet?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Mass ($m$) is constant regardless of location; weight ($W$) varies with the local gravitational field ($g$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $W = mg \rightarrow g = \frac{W}{m}$
- **When to use it:** To find the gravity of an unknown location by first determining the object's mass from its Earth weight ($m = \frac{W_{Earth}}{9.8}$).
- **Common trap:** Assuming the object's *weight* remains constant; always solve for *mass* first as it is the invariant property.
