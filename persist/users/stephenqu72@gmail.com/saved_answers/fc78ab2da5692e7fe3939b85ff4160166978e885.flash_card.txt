### Front
How does the kinetic energy ($K$) of a satellite in a circular orbit change with its orbital radius ($r$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kinetic energy is inversely proportional to the orbital radius ($K \propto 1/r$).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $K = \frac{GMm}{2r}$
- **When to use it:** To determine changes in orbital speed or radius when given changes in kinetic energy (e.g., "energy increased by 25%").
- **Common trap:** Confusing this with total mechanical energy ($E = -GMm/2r$) or forgetting the factor of 2 in the denominator.
