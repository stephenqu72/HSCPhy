To solve this problem, we apply the principles of electromagnetic induction, specifically Faraday’s Law and Lenz’s Law.

### Given Data:
*   Dimensions: $l = 0.050\text{ m}$, $w = 0.035\text{ m}$
*   Area: $A = 0.050 \times 0.035 = 0.00175\text{ m}^2$
*   Magnetic field strength: $B = 0.2\text{ mT} = 0.2 \times 10^{-3}\text{ T}$
*   Frequency: $f = 50\text{ Hz}$
*   Period: $T = \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{50} = 0.02\text{ s}$

---

### a. Direction of current
Using Lenz's Law and the Right-Hand Rule: As the loop rotates into the magnetic field, the magnetic flux increases. The induced current creates a magnetic field to oppose this increase. According to the Fleming's Right-Hand Generator Rule, for the motion shown, the current flows **anticlockwise** (when viewed from above).

### b. Average EMF calculation
The average induced EMF ($\varepsilon_{avg}$) is given by Faraday's Law: $\varepsilon_{avg} = N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$. Assuming a single loop ($N=1$):
*   Initial flux ($\Phi_i$) = $0$ (loop is parallel to field)
*   Final flux ($\Phi_f$) = $B \times A = (0.2 \times 10^{-3}) \times 0.00175 = 3.5 \times 10^{-7}\text{ Wb}$
*   Time for quarter turn ($\Delta t$) = $\frac{T}{4} = \frac{0.02}{4} = 0.005\text{ s}$
$$\varepsilon_{avg} = \frac{3.5 \times 10^{-7} - 0}{0.005} = 7 \times 10^{-5}\text{ V} \text{ (or } 0.07\text{ mV)}$$

### c. Sketching the EMF
A DC generator with a split-ring commutator produces a "pulsating" output where the negative half-cycles are flipped to positive.
*   The wave will look like two "humps" per rotation (0 to $0.02\text{ s}$ is one rotation, so the graph covers $0$ to $0.04\text{ s}$).
*   It should be zero at $t=0, 0.01, 0.02, 0.03, 0.04\text{ s}$ and reach peaks at $0.005, 0.015, 0.025, 0.035\text{ s}$.

### d. Increasing the EMF
To increase the induced EMF, we look at $\varepsilon = NBA\omega \sin(\omega t)$. Two modifications are:
1.  **Increase the number of turns ($N$)** in the wire coil.
2.  **Increase the rotational frequency ($f$)** by spinning the loop faster.
*(Alternatively: Increase the magnetic field strength $B$ or the area of the loop $A$).*
