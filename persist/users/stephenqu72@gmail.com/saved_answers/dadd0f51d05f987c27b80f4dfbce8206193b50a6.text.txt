The correct answer is **C) Charged particles are accelerated by passing through a series of alternating electric fields.**

### Explanation

To solve this, we must consider the roles of electric and magnetic fields in physics:

1.  **Electric Fields ($E$):** According to the formula $F = qE$, an electric field exerts a force on a charged particle that is parallel to the field lines. This force does work on the particle, changing its kinetic energy and, therefore, its **velocity**. In a linear accelerator (linac) or a synchrotron, oscillating electric fields are timed (synchronized) so that the particle always experiences a forward push as it passes through the gaps between accelerating structures.
2.  **Magnetic Fields ($B$):** The magnetic force on a moving charge is given by the Lorentz force law: $F = qvB\sin\theta$. Crucially, this force is always perpendicular to the particle's velocity. Because the force is perpendicular to the direction of motion, **no work is done on the particle**. Consequently, magnetic fields change the *direction* of the particle (causing it to move in a circle or arc), but they **cannot change its speed**.

**Evaluating the other options:**
*   **A is incorrect** because a magnetic field perpendicular to the path acts as a centripetal force, which changes the particle's direction, not its velocity.
*   **B is incorrect** because particle accelerators rely on electromagnetic fields, not photon collisions.
*   **D is incorrect** because this describes a plasma or thermal process, not the controlled electromagnetic acceleration used in modern accelerators.

By process of elimination and understanding the fundamental difference between the effects of electric and magnetic fields on charged particles, **C** is the only physically accurate statement.
