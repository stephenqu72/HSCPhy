To find the number of turns on the secondary winding, we use the transformer turns ratio formula:

$$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$

### Given data:
*   Primary turns ($N_p$) = $2000$
*   Primary voltage ($V_p$) = $23\,000\text{ V}$
*   Secondary voltage ($V_s$) = $660\,000\text{ V}$

### Step-by-step solution:

1.  **Rearrange the formula** to solve for the secondary turns ($N_s$):
    $$N_s = N_p \times \frac{V_s}{V_p}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values** into the equation:
    $$N_s = 2000 \times \frac{660\,000}{23\,000}$$

3.  **Simplify and calculate:**
    $$N_s = 2000 \times \frac{660}{23}$$
    $$N_s \approx 2000 \times 28.6956...$$
    $$N_s \approx 57\,391.3$$

Since the number of turns must be a whole number, we round to the nearest integer:
**The number of turns on the secondary winding is $57\,391$.**
