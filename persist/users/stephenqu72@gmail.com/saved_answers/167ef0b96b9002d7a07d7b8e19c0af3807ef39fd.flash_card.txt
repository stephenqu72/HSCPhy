### Front
According to the Standard Model, how do the constituent particles of a Helium atom ($^4_2\text{He}$) differ in their fundamental nature?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Electrons are fundamental (leptons), while protons and neutrons are composite hadrons made of quarks.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Proton = $uud$ ($+1e$); Neutron = $udd$ ($0$); Gluons mediate the strong nuclear force.
- **When to use it:** When asked to explain the subatomic structure of matter using the Standard Model.
- **Common trap:** Assuming protons and neutrons are fundamental particles; always specify they are composed of quarks held by the strong force.
