To determine which arrow correctly represents alpha ($\alpha$) decay on the graph of Neutron number ($N$) versus Atomic number ($Z$), we must look at how an alpha particle affects the composition of a nucleus.

### Answer
The correct answer is **A**.

### Explanation
1.  **Understand Alpha Decay:** An alpha particle is a helium nucleus, consisting of 2 protons and 2 neutrons ($_{2}^{4}\text{He}$). When a nucleus undergoes alpha decay, it loses 2 protons and 2 neutrons.
    *   Change in atomic number ($\Delta Z$): $-2$
    *   Change in neutron number ($\Delta N$): $-2$

2.  **Analyze the Graph:** The graph plots Neutron number ($N$) on the y-axis and Atomic number ($Z$) on the x-axis. 
    *   A decrease in $Z$ means the point moves to the **left**.
    *   A decrease in $N$ means the point moves **down**.
    *   Therefore, the resultant movement on the graph must be downwards and to the left.

3.  **Evaluate the Options:**
    *   **Arrow A:** Points down and to the left. This represents a decrease in both $N$ and $Z$, which matches alpha decay.
    *   **Arrow B:** Points down and to the right. This represents a decrease in $N$ but an increase in $Z$ (e.g., positron emission).
    *   **Arrow C:** Points up and to the left. This represents an increase in $N$ but a decrease in $Z$ (e.g., electron capture).
    *   **Arrow D:** Points up and to the right. This represents an increase in both $N$ and $Z$.

Since alpha decay reduces both the atomic number and the neutron number by 2, the coordinate $(Z, N)$ shifts to $(Z-2, N-2)$, which is visually represented by arrow **A**.
