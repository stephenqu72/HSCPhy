### Front
What are the defining characteristics of leptons within the Standard Model?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Leptons are fundamental, point-like fermions that do not experience the strong nuclear force.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When asked to classify matter or distinguish between fundamental particles (leptons vs. quarks).
- **Common trap:** Confusing leptons with hadrons; remember that leptons are elementary particles (not made of quarks) and never interact via the strong force.
