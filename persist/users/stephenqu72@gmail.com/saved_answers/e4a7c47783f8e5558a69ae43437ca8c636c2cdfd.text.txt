To answer a 9-mark "justify" question in the HSC Physics syllabus (specifically Module 8: From the Universe to the Atom), you must provide a structured response that links experimental observations to the development of our understanding of particle physics and the Standard Model.

### Sample Answer

The statement is well-justified by the progression of experiments that allowed us to probe the nature of matter, forces, and fields:

1.  **The Thomson Experiment (Cathode Ray Tubes):** By applying electric and magnetic fields to cathode rays, J.J. Thomson observed the deflection of particles. This allowed him to calculate the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$) of the electron. This provided the first evidence that atoms are not indivisible and led to the discovery of subatomic particles.
2.  **The Millikan Oil Drop Experiment:** By observing the motion of charged oil droplets in a uniform electric field (balancing electrostatic force against gravity), Millikan determined the elementary charge of the electron. This experiment proved that electric charge is quantized, establishing a fundamental property of matter.
3.  **The Rutherford Gold Foil Experiment:** By firing alpha particles at a thin gold foil, Rutherford observed that while most passed through, some were deflected at large angles. This provided evidence for the existence of a dense, positively charged nucleus, fundamentally changing our understanding of atomic structure from the "plum pudding" model to a nuclear model.
4.  **Particle Accelerators (e.g., CERN's LHC):** Modern high-energy physics involves accelerating particles (like protons) to relativistic speeds and colliding them. By observing the collision debris in detectors using strong magnetic fields (to curve charged particle paths), scientists identified particles like the top quark and the Higgs boson. The observation of the Higgs boson confirms the interaction of particles with the Higgs field, explaining why particles possess mass.
5.  **Evidence of Antimatter:** Cloud chamber observations of cosmic rays and subsequent accelerator experiments revealed tracks that curved in the opposite direction to electrons, leading to the discovery of the positron ($e^+$). This confirmed the existence of antimatter, a direct implication of the Dirac equation and field theories.

In summary, the interaction of particles with external fields (electric/magnetic) has allowed for the precise measurement of their properties, while their interaction with each other in high-energy environments has revealed the fundamental forces and particles that compose the Standard Model.

---

### How to solve this type of question:
*   **Deconstruct the prompt:** The question asks you to "justify." This means you must show *how* the evidence confirms the statement. Do not just list facts; explain the *link* between the experimental result and the theoretical understanding.
*   **Use a thematic structure:** Group your points logically (e.g., historical atomic structure, discovery of charge, modern particle physics).
*   **Balance the marks:** Since this is a 9-mark question, aim for 3–4 detailed points. Each point should:
    *   Identify the experiment/observation.
    *   Explain the interaction (e.g., particle with a field).
    *   State the resulting conclusion about particle properties or physical processes.
*   **Check syllabus links:** Ensure you refer to specific syllabus components (e.g., properties of the atom, Standard Model, evidence for the Big Bang or particle interactions).
