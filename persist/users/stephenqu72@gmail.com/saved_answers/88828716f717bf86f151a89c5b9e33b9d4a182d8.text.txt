### Part (a)
**Reasoning:**
The gravitational force $F_g$ acting on the particle provides the centripetal force $F_c$ required to keep it in a circular orbit.
1. Gravitational force: $F_g = \frac{GMm}{R^2}$
2. Centripetal force: $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{R}$
Setting them equal: $\frac{GMm}{R^2} = \frac{mv^2}{R}$
Canceling $m$ from both sides and multiplying by $R^2$ gives $GM = Rv^2$.

### Part (b)
**Calculation:**
1. Radius $R = \frac{\text{diameter}}{2} = \frac{2.72 \times 10^8}{2} = 1.36 \times 10^8 \text{ m}$.
2. Using $GM = Rv^2$, find $v$: $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{R}} = \sqrt{\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(5.69 \times 10^{26})}{1.36 \times 10^8}} \approx 1.67 \times 10^4 \text{ m/s}$.
3. Period $T = \frac{2\pi R}{v} = \frac{2\pi(1.36 \times 10^8)}{1.67 \times 10^4} \approx 5.12 \times 10^4 \text{ s}$.

### Part (c)
**Explanation:**
Escape speed is the minimum speed required for an object to break free from the gravitational attraction of a massive body and never return (i.e., to reach an infinite distance with zero kinetic energy).

### Part (d)
**Deduction:**
1. Conservation of energy: $\frac{1}{2}mv_{es}^2 - \frac{GMm}{R} = 0$, so $\frac{1}{2}v_{es}^2 = \frac{GM}{R} \implies v_{es} = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$.
2. Since $g = \frac{GM}{R^2}$, then $GM = gR^2$.
3. Substitute $GM$ into the escape speed formula: $v_{es} = \sqrt{\frac{2(gR^2)}{R}} = \sqrt{2gR}$.
**Assumption:** The object is launched from the planet's surface and experiences no atmospheric drag/friction.

### Part (e)
**Calculation:**
1. $R = 1.7 \times 10^3 \text{ km} = 1.7 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$.
2. $g = 1.6 \text{ m/s}^2$.
3. $v_{es} = \sqrt{2 \times 1.6 \times 1.7 \times 10^6} = \sqrt{5.44 \times 10^6} \approx 2332 \text{ m/s}$ (or $2.3 \text{ km/s}$ to 2 sig figs).
