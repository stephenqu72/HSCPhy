### Solution and Explanation

#### Part a) Determine the spacing of the slits.

**1. Identify the relevant physics formula:**
The interference pattern for a double-slit experiment is governed by the formula for fringe spacing:
$$x = \frac{\lambda D}{d}$$
Where:
*   $x$ = fringe spacing (m)
*   $\lambda$ = wavelength of light (m)
*   $D$ = distance from slits to screen (m)
*   $d$ = spacing between slits (m)

**2. Relate the formula to the graph:**
The graph plots $x$ vs $D$. Since $x = (\frac{\lambda}{d})D$, this is a linear relationship ($y = mx$) where the gradient (slope) of the line is equal to $\frac{\lambda}{d}$.

**3. Calculate the gradient:**
Using the points $(0,0)$ and $(6, 13.2)$ from the graph:
$$\text{Gradient} = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta D} = \frac{13.2 \text{ mm} - 0 \text{ mm}}{6 \text{ m} - 0 \text{ m}} = 2.2 \text{ mm/m} = 2.2 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m/m}$$

**4. Solve for $d$:**
Given $\lambda = 620 \text{ nm} = 620 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$:
$$\text{Gradient} = \frac{\lambda}{d} \implies d = \frac{\lambda}{\text{Gradient}}$$
$$d = \frac{620 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}}{2.2 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m/m}} \approx 2.818 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}$$
Rounding to two significant figures (consistent with the wavelength), $d \approx 2.8 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}$ (or $0.28 \text{ mm}$).

#### Part b) If the experiment is repeated with the slit spacing doubled, state how the graph and the interference pattern change.

**1. Effect on the graph:**
Since the gradient of the graph is $\frac{\lambda}{d}$, doubling the slit spacing ($d_{new} = 2d$) will halve the gradient. Therefore, the slope of the line on the graph will decrease by a factor of 2, resulting in a line that is less steep (closer to the D-axis).

**2. Effect on the interference pattern:**
Since the fringe spacing $x$ is inversely proportional to $d$ ($x \propto \frac{1}{d}$), doubling the slit spacing will cause the fringes to become half as far apart. The interference pattern will appear more "compressed" or crowded.
