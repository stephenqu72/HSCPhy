### Front
How do you calculate the half-life of a particle moving at a relativistic speed ($v$) given its proper half-life ($t_0$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Time dilation causes the observed half-life of a moving particle to be longer than its proper half-life.
- **Formula:** $t = \gamma t_0 = \frac{t_0}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$
- **When to use it:** When the particle's speed is a significant fraction of the speed of light ($c$).
- **Common trap:** Confusing proper time ($t_0$) with dilated time ($t$); remember $t$ is always greater than $t_0$ for moving objects.
