To solve this problem, we must account for the relativistic effects on momentum since the electron is travelling at a significant fraction of the speed of light ($v = 0.8c$).

### Step-by-Step Explanation

**1. Identify the relevant formula**
In special relativity, the momentum $p$ of an object is given by:
$$p = \gamma mv$$
Where:
- $\gamma$ (Lorentz factor) $= \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$
- $m$ is the rest mass of the electron ($m_e \approx 9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$)
- $v$ is the velocity of the electron ($0.8c$)

**2. Calculate the Lorentz factor ($\gamma$)**
Given $v = 0.8c$:
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.8)^2}}$$
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.64}}$$
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.36}} = \frac{1}{0.6} = 1.666...$$

**3. Calculate the relativistic momentum ($p$)**
Substitute the values into the momentum formula:
$$p = \gamma m_e v$$
$$p = 1.666... \times (9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}) \times (0.8 \times 2.998 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s})$$

Using $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$:
$$p = 1.666... \times (9.109 \times 10^{-31}) \times (2.4 \times 10^8)$$
$$p \approx 3.6436 \times 10^{-22} \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$$

Rounding this to three significant figures gives $3.64 \times 10^{-22} \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$.

Comparing this to the given options, **(B)** is the correct choice.
