### Explanation

To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of electromagnetism regarding the motion of a charged particle in electric and magnetic fields.

**1. Determine the direction of the Electric Field ($E$):**
*   The electron is deflected towards the **bottom plate**.
*   The electric force on a negatively charged particle is in the opposite direction to the electric field ($F = qE$). Since the electron is negative, the force is downwards, which means the electric field must point **upwards (towards the top of the page)**.

**2. Determine the direction of the Magnetic Force ($F_B$):**
*   For the electron to pass through "undeflected," the magnetic force must exactly balance the electric force.
*   Since the electric force pulls the electron towards the bottom of the page, the magnetic force must pull the electron towards the **top of the page**.

**3. Apply the Right-Hand Rule (or Left-Hand Rule for electrons):**
*   Using the Right-Hand Rule (where the thumb is the direction of current $I$, fingers are the magnetic field $B$, and the palm is the force $F$):
    *   Since the electron is moving to the right, the conventional current $I$ is flowing to the **left**.
    *   Point your thumb to the left (current direction).
    *   Point your palm towards the top of the page (the direction required for the magnetic force $F_B$).
    *   Your fingers will point **out of the page**.
*   Alternatively, using Fleming’s Left-Hand Rule for a negative charge:
    *   First finger ($B$): ?
    *   Second finger ($I$): To the left (opposite to electron velocity).
    *   Thumb ($F$): Towards the top of the page.
    *   This forces the first finger to point **out of the page**.

**Conclusion:**
*   Electric field: Towards top of page.
*   Magnetic field: Out of page.

This corresponds to row **B**.
