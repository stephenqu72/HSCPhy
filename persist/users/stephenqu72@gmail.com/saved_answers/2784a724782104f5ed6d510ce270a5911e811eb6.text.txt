### Answer and Explanation

To solve these problems, we use the principles of electromagnetism related to current-carrying wires.

**Given values:**
- Current in wire X ($I_1$) = $3.0\text{ A}$
- Current in wire Y ($I_2$) = $5.0\text{ A}$
- Distance ($r$) = $0.17\text{ m}$
- Length ($L$) = $4.6\text{ m}$
- Permeability of free space ($\mu_0$) = $4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

---

#### Part (b): Determine the magnitude of the magnetic field strength on wire Y caused by wire X.
*(Note: It is logical to solve part (b) first, as the force depends on this magnetic field).*

The magnetic field ($B$) produced by a long straight wire at a distance $r$ is given by:
$$B = \frac{\mu_0 I_1}{2\pi r}$$

Substitute the known values:
$$B = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times 3.0}{2\pi \times 0.17}$$
$$B = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 3.0}{0.17}$$
$$B \approx 3.53 \times 10^{-6}\text{ T}$$

**Answer:** The magnetic field strength is approximately $3.5 \times 10^{-6}\text{ T}$.

---

#### Part (a): Determine the magnitude and direction of the force on wire Y caused by wire X.

**Magnitude:**
The force ($F$) on a wire of length $L$ in a magnetic field is $F = B I_2 L \sin(\theta)$. Since the wire is perpendicular to the field, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$. Using the values from part (b):
$$F = (3.53 \times 10^{-6}) \times 5.0 \times 4.6$$
$$F \approx 8.12 \times 10^{-5}\text{ N}$$

**Direction:**
Using the right-hand grip rule, wire X creates a magnetic field that points into the page at the position of wire Y. Applying the right-hand push rule (Fleming's Left-Hand Rule for force on a current-carrying wire), with current $I_2$ upwards and field $B$ into the page, the force points towards wire X. Since the currents are in the same direction, the wires **attract** each other.

**Answer:** The magnitude of the force is $8.1 \times 10^{-5}\text{ N}$ and the direction is attractive (towards wire X).
