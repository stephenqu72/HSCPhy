### Front
What are the two essential requirements for a scientific hypothesis?

### Back
- Key idea: A hypothesis must be **testable** and **falsifiable**.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: To distinguish a scientific hypothesis from an opinion, theory, or law during experimental design.
- Common trap: Confusing a hypothesis with a "scientific theory" (which is a well-tested, broad explanation) or assuming that a statement is a hypothesis just because it sounds logical or is proposed by an authority.
