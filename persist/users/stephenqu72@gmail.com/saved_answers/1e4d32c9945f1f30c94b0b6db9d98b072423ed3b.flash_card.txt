### Front
What is Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion and its simplified formula for orbital mechanics problems?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The square of an orbital period ($T$) is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis ($r$).
- **Formula / law:** $T^2 \propto r^3$ (or $T^2 = r^3$ when $T$ is in Earth years and $r$ is in AU).
- **When to use it:** To relate the distance of an object from a central body to its orbital period.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units to years/AU if using the simplified $T^2=r^3$ version, or failing to use the universal form ($T^2/r^3 = 4\pi^2 / GM$) when units are in SI (seconds/metres).
