### Front
In a spontaneous radioactive decay (e.g., alpha decay), what happens to the total mass and total binding energy of the system?

### Back
- Key idea: Mass defect is converted into kinetic energy, resulting in a more stable configuration with higher binding energy.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\Delta E = \Delta mc^2$ (Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence).
- When to use it: To determine energy changes in nuclear decay or fission/fusion reactions.
- Common trap: Confusing "binding energy per nucleon" with "total binding energy"—both increase when moving towards the iron-56 peak of stability.
