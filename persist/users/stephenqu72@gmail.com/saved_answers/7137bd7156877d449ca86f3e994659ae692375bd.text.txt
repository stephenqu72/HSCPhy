To calculate the gravitational force between two massive objects, we use **Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation**.

### Answer:
The gravitational force between the two supertankers is approximately $1.67 \times 10^3 \text{ N}$.

---

### Explanation:
To solve this, we use the formula:
$$F = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Gravitational constant $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{ kg}^{-2}$
*   Mass of the first tanker $m_1 = 5.00 \times 10^8 \text{ kg}$
*   Mass of the second tanker $m_2 = 5.00 \times 10^8 \text{ kg}$
*   Distance between centers $r = 100.00 \text{ m}$

**2. Substitute the values into the formula:**
$$F = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.00 \times 10^8) \times (5.00 \times 10^8)}{(100.00)^2}$$

**3. Perform the calculation:**
*   Numerator: $(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (25.00 \times 10^{16}) = 16.675 \times 10^6$
*   Denominator: $100^2 = 10,000 = 10^4$
*   Final division: $\frac{16.675 \times 10^6}{10^4} = 16.675 \times 10^2 = 1667.5 \text{ N}$

Rounding to three significant figures (consistent with the provided data), the force is **$1.67 \times 10^3 \text{ N}$**.
