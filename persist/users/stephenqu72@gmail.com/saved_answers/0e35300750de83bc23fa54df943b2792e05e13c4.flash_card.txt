### Front
How do you calculate the displacement of an object undergoing constant acceleration (e.g., free fall) given initial velocity ($u$), time ($t$), and acceleration ($a$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Kinematic equations for uniform acceleration.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$
- When to use it: To find displacement when acceleration is constant and you do not know the final velocity.
- Common trap: Forgetting to keep signs consistent—if you define "downwards" as positive, all downward vectors ($u, g, s$) must be positive.
