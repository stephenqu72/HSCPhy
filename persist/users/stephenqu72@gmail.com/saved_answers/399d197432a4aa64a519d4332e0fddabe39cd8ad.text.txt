To solve this problem, we need to relate the torque ($\tau$) applied to the spanner to the force ($F$), the length of the spanner ($r$), and the angle ($\theta$) at which the force is applied.

### 1. The Physics Principle
The formula for torque is:
$$\tau = r F \sin(\theta)$$

Given in the problem:
*   The length of the spanner, $r = 25.00 \text{ cm} = 0.25 \text{ m}$ (We must convert to SI units).
*   The graph plots $\tau$ (Torque) on the y-axis against $\sin(\theta)$ on the x-axis.

Comparing this to the equation of a straight line, $y = mx + c$:
*   $y = \tau$
*   $x = \sin(\theta)$
*   $m = rF$ (The gradient of the graph)

### 2. Calculate the Gradient ($m$)
We can pick two points from the line to find the slope $m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}$. Let's use $(0.14, 50)$ and $(0.59, 200)$:
$$m = \frac{200 - 50}{0.59 - 0.14}$$
$$m = \frac{150}{0.45} \approx 333.33 \text{ N}$$

### 3. Calculate the Force ($F$)
Since the gradient $m = rF$, we can solve for $F$:
$$333.33 = 0.25 \times F$$
$$F = \frac{333.33}{0.25}$$
$$F = 333.33 \times 4$$
$$F \approx 1333.3 \text{ N}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the magnitude of the force applied is $1333 \text{ N}$.
