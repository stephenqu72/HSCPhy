To ensure a first-hand investigation yields data that is both **valid** and **reliable**, the planning phase must address specific methodological requirements.

### Answer
To ensure **validity**, the investigation must be designed to accurately test the hypothesis by controlling variables. This includes:
*   **Identification of variables:** Clearly defining the independent variable (what is changed), the dependent variable (what is measured), and ensuring all other variables are strictly **controlled** to ensure they do not impact the results.
*   **Appropriate equipment:** Selecting instruments with sufficient precision to measure the required units accurately.
*   **Experimental design:** Ensuring the procedure directly addresses the aim of the experiment so that the data collected actually answers the research question.

To ensure **reliability**, the investigation must be designed to produce consistent results. This includes:
*   **Repetition and Replication:** Including multiple trials for each experimental condition to calculate a mean and identify outliers.
*   **Sample size:** Ensuring the sample size is large enough to be representative and minimize the impact of random error.
*   **Standardized procedure:** Creating a clear, step-by-step methodology that allows the experiment to be repeated exactly by others to see if the same results are achieved.

### How to solve this question
1.  **Understand the Verb "Discuss":** The command term "Discuss" requires you to provide points for and against, or in this case, to explore the *how* and *why* behind achieving validity and reliability. Do not just list points; explain how they contribute to the quality of the data.
2.  **Separate the Concepts:** Many students confuse these two terms. Always remember:
    *   **Validity** = Is the method *fair*? (Did I control my variables? Is the test measuring what it is supposed to?)
    *   **Reliability** = Is the data *consistent*? (Did I repeat the experiment? Is the result repeatable?)
3.  **Structure your response:** Use two clear sections (one for validity, one for reliability). This ensures the marker can see you have addressed both components of the question. 
4.  **Use Syllabus Terminology:** Use keywords such as "controlled variables," "independent variable," "dependent variable," "precision," "random error," and "sample size." This is exactly what HSC markers look for in high-scoring responses.
