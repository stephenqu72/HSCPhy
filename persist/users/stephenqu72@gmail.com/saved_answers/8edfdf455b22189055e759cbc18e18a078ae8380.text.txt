### Answer

To solve this, we must first note a crucial detail: the question states the distance is $2R$ **from the surface** of the planet. Assuming the planet is a sphere of radius $R$, the distance from the **centre** of the planet is $R + 2R = 3R$.

The gravitational field strength $g$ is given by the formula:
$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$
This shows that $g \propto \frac{1}{r^2}$.

1. **At $r = 3R$ (given as $2R$ from surface):** $g = 5 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$
2. **At $r = R$ (centre to surface):**
   Using the ratio method: $\frac{g_1}{g_2} = (\frac{r_2}{r_1})^2$.
   $g_R = 5 \times (\frac{3R}{R})^2 = 5 \times 9 = 45 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$. (Note: This is off the scale of the provided graph).
3. **At $r = 4R$:**
   $g_{4R} = 5 \times (\frac{3R}{4R})^2 = 5 \times (\frac{9}{16}) = 2.81 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$.
4. **At $r = 6R$:**
   $g_{6R} = 5 \times (\frac{3R}{6R})^2 = 5 \times (\frac{1}{2})^2 = 5 \times 0.25 = 1.25 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$.

### Explanation
*   **Identify the coordinate system:** The graph axis is labelled "Distance from centre of planet". Therefore, we must convert the "distance from surface" measurements into "distance from centre". If a point is $2R$ from the surface, its distance from the centre is $R (\text{radius}) + 2R = 3R$. 
*   **Apply the Inverse Square Law:** Since $g \propto \frac{1}{r^2}$, if you double the distance from the centre, the field strength drops to $1/4$ of its original value. If you triple the distance, it drops to $1/9$.
*   **Calculate:** 
    *   For $4R$ (from centre): The ratio of distances is $3:4$. The field strength is $5 \times (\frac{3}{4})^2 = 2.8125 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$.
    *   For $6R$ (from centre): The ratio of distances is $3:6$ (or $1:2$). The field strength is $5 \times (\frac{3}{6})^2 = 5 \times 0.25 = 1.25 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$.
