### Front
Why are circular tracks banked?

### Back
- Key idea: Banking redirects a component of the normal force ($N \sin \theta$) to provide the necessary centripetal force, reducing reliance on friction.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$; $N \sin \theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: Any scenario involving vehicles or objects moving on a curved path (e.g., velodromes, highways, aircraft banking).
- Common trap: Thinking banking *increases* the centripetal force required; it does not—it only changes *what force provides* that centripetal requirement.
