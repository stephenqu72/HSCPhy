### (a) What is meant by "red shift" and why does it happen?
**Answer:** Red shift is the observed increase in the wavelength of light (shifting it toward the red/longer end of the spectrum) emitted by an object moving away from an observer. It occurs due to the Doppler effect, where light waves emitted by a receding source are "stretched" over a greater distance as the source moves away, or in cosmology, due to the expansion of space-time itself stretching the light waves as they travel through the universe.

**Explanation:** You need two distinct points for the two marks: one for the definition (wavelength increases/shift toward red) and one for the mechanism (Doppler effect/expansion of space).

---

### (b) What is the significance of Hubble's work using the observed "red shift"?
**Answer:** Hubble's work demonstrated that the red shift of galaxies is proportional to their distance from Earth ($v = H_0d$). This provided observational evidence that the universe is expanding uniformly in all directions, which formed the foundation for the Big Bang Theory and the model of an evolving universe.

**Explanation:** Focus on the "big picture" impact. You must mention the **expansion of the universe** and the link between **distance and velocity**.
