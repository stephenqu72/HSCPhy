### Front
What are the changes to the mass number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$) of a parent nucleus following alpha decay?

### Back
- Key idea: An alpha particle ($_{2}^{4}\text{He}$) is emitted, resulting in the transmutation of the parent nucleus into a daughter nucleus.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $A_{daughter} = A_{parent} - 4$; $Z_{daughter} = Z_{parent} - 2$.
- When to use it: Whenever a radioactive nucleus emits an alpha particle to achieve stability.
- Common trap: Confusing alpha decay (subtract 2 from $Z$) with beta decay (add 1 to $Z$).
