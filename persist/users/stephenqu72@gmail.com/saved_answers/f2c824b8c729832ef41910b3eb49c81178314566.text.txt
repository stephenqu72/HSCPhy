To solve this problem, we must analyze the forces acting on the electron within the velocity selector.

### 1. Understanding the Physics
For the electron to travel **undeflected**, the net force must be zero. This means the magnitude of the electric force ($F_E$) must equal the magnitude of the magnetic force ($F_B$):
$$F_E = F_B$$
Using the formulas for these forces:
- Electric force: $F_E = qE = q \left( \frac{V}{d} \right)$
- Magnetic force: $F_B = qvB$

Equating them:
$$q \left( \frac{V}{d} \right) = qvB \implies v = \frac{V}{Bd}$$

### 2. Analyzing the Deflection at X
In the diagram, the electron is deflected **downwards** toward point X. To deflect downwards, the **downward force must be greater than the upward force**. 
- The electric field points from the positive plate to the negative plate (downward). Since the electron is negative, it experiences an **upward electric force** ($F_E = qE$).
- Using the right-hand rule for the magnetic force ($F_B = qvB$): With velocity to the right and magnetic field into the page, a positive charge would experience an upward force. However, because the **electron is negative**, it experiences a **downward magnetic force**.

For the electron to hit point X (downward deflection), the downward magnetic force must be greater than the upward electric force:
$$qvB > q \left( \frac{V}{d} \right)$$
$$vB > \frac{V}{d}$$

### 3. Evaluating the Options
We need an adjustment that makes the magnetic force stronger relative to the electric force ($vB > V/d$):

*   **(A) Decrease $V$**: If $V$ decreases, the term $\frac{V}{d}$ decreases. This makes the electric force smaller. The magnetic force remains dominant, pulling the electron downward toward X. **This is correct.**
*   **(B) Decrease $B$**: This decreases the magnetic force $qvB$, making the electron more likely to be pulled upward or remain undeflected.
*   **(C) Decrease $v$**: This decreases the magnetic force $qvB$, reducing the downward pull.
*   **(D) Decrease $d$**: Decreasing $d$ increases the electric field strength ($E = V/d$), which increases the upward electric force, pulling the electron away from X.

The correct answer is **(A)**.
