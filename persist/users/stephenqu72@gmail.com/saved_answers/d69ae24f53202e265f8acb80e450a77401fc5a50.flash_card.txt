### Front
In nuclear fusion, how does the total mass of the products compare to the reactants, and what principle explains the energy release?

### Back
- Key idea: Mass defect ($\Delta m$) is converted into energy.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E = \Delta m c^2$ (Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence).
- When to use it: When calculating energy released during nuclear reactions (fusion/fission) where mass is lost.
- Common trap: Assuming mass is conserved in nuclear reactions; in reality, total mass *decreases* as it is converted to energy.
