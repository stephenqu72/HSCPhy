### Front
How does the binding energy per nucleon change during nuclear fission and nuclear fusion?

### Back
- Key idea: Both processes result in an increase in binding energy per nucleon as nuclei move toward the peak of stability (near Iron-56).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Binding energy curve; $E = \Delta mc^2$.
- When to use it: When asked why energy is released in nuclear reactions or to compare the stability of reactants vs. products.
- Common trap: Assuming mass increases; in reality, total mass decreases (mass defect) and is converted into energy.
