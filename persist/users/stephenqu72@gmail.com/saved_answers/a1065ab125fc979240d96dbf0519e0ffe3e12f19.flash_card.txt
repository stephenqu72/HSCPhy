### Front
How can Kepler’s Third Law be used to determine the mass of a central body from a graph of $r^3$ vs $T^2$?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The gradient of the linear plot $r^3$ vs $T^2$ is equal to $\frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$.
- **Formula / law:** $\frac{r^3}{T^2} = \frac{GM}{4\pi^2}$
- **When to use it:** When given orbital period and radius data for multiple satellites orbiting the same central mass.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., periods to seconds, radii to metres) before calculating the gradient.
