### Front
Describe the speed-time graph of a projectile (ignoring air resistance) launched at an angle $\theta < 90^\circ$.

### Back
- Key idea: The speed graph is a hyperbola that reaches a non-zero minimum at the peak of flight.
- Formula / law: $S = \sqrt{(v \cos \theta)^2 + (v \sin \theta - gt)^2}$
- When to use it: Whenever identifying the magnitude of velocity (speed) rather than individual velocity components.
- Common trap: Assuming speed reaches zero at the peak; speed only reaches zero if the projectile is launched vertically ($\theta = 90^\circ$).
