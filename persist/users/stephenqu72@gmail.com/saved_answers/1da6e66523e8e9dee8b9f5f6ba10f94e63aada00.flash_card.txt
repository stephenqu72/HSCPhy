### Front
How does gravitational field strength ($g$) relate to the acceleration of an object in free fall?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational field strength is equivalent to the acceleration due to gravity at any given point.
- Formula / law: $g = \frac{F}{m} = a$ (where $a$ is acceleration in free fall). Units $\text{N kg}^{-1} = \text{m s}^{-2}$.
- When to use it: Any problem involving objects in free fall or determining gravity at a specific location.
- Common trap: Attempting to use the mass of the object in calculations; mass cancels out and is irrelevant for determining acceleration or field strength.
