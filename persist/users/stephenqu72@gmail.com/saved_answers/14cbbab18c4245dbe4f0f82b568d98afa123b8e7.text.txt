To solve this problem, we use the principle of conservation of energy for an ideal transformer.

### Answer
The correct option is **(D) 7.5 A**.

### Explanation
For a transformer that is $100\%$ efficient, the electrical power input in the primary coil is equal to the electrical power output in the secondary coil. The formula for power is $P = VI$, where $V$ is voltage and $I$ is current.

Therefore:
$$P_{\text{primary}} = P_{\text{secondary}}$$
$$V_p I_p = V_s I_s$$

Given the values from the question:
- Primary voltage ($V_p$) = $500\text{ V}$
- Primary current ($I_p$) = $1.5\text{ A}$
- Secondary voltage ($V_s$) = $100\text{ V}$
- Secondary current ($I_s$) = $?$

Substitute these values into the equation:
$$(500\text{ V}) \times (1.5\text{ A}) = (100\text{ V}) \times I_s$$
$$750\text{ W} = 100\text{ V} \times I_s$$

Now, solve for $I_s$:
$$I_s = \frac{750}{100}$$
$$I_s = 7.5\text{ A}$$

Since the voltage was stepped down (from $500\text{ V}$ to $100\text{ V}$), the current must step up proportionally to maintain the power balance, resulting in $7.5\text{ A}$.
