### Answer
The correct answer is **D**.

### Explanation
In any spontaneous nuclear reaction, the total mass of the products is slightly less than the total mass of the reactants. This missing mass is called the **mass defect** ($\Delta m$). According to Einstein's equation, this lost mass is converted into energy. 

1.  **Binding Energy Concept**: Binding energy is a measure of how tightly the nucleons are held together in a nucleus. A system with higher binding energy per nucleon is more stable.
2.  **Spontaneous Reactions**: For a nuclear reaction to be spontaneous (releasing energy), the products must be more stable than the reactants. This means the products must have a higher total binding energy than the reactants.
3.  **The Relationship**: The mass defect ($\Delta m$) observed in the reaction is the mass equivalent of the energy released ($Q$-value). This energy release corresponds exactly to the increase in the total binding energy of the system as it moves to a more stable state. Therefore, the mass defect is equivalent to the **binding energy gain** of the products compared to the reactants.

*   Option A and B are incorrect because they refer to static binding energies rather than the change in energy during a reaction.
*   Option C is incorrect because a "loss" in binding energy would imply the products are less stable (and would require an input of energy rather than being spontaneous).
