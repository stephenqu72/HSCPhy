### Front
How do you calculate and compare the gravitational force exerted by a central mass on two different orbiting bodies?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational force is directly proportional to the masses and inversely proportional to the square of the orbital radius.
- **Formula / law / constant:** Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation: $F = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$ where $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N m}^2 \text{kg}^{-2}$.
- **When to use it:** To determine the magnitude of gravitational attraction between celestial bodies.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., km to m) or neglecting to square the radius ($r^2$) in the denominator.
