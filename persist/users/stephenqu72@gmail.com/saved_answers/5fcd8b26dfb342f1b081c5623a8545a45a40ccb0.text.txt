### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must consider the force acting on a charged particle ($q$) moving with velocity ($v$) perpendicular to each type of field.

**1. Electric Field:**
When a charged particle enters a uniform electric field ($E$) perpendicularly, it experiences a constant force ($F = qE$) in the direction of the field (or opposite, depending on the charge). This is analogous to projectile motion in a gravitational field. The constant force acting perpendicular to the initial velocity results in a **parabolic path**.

**2. Magnetic Field:**
When a charged particle moves with a velocity ($v$) perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field ($B$), it experiences the Lorentz force ($F = qvB\sin\theta$). Since $\theta = 90^\circ$, the force is $F = qvB$. This force is always perpendicular to the direction of motion and the magnetic field. Because the force is always directed toward a central point (centripetal force), the particle follows a **circular path**.

**Summary:**
*   **Electric Field:** Constant force $\rightarrow$ Parabolic path.
*   **Magnetic Field:** Centripetal force $\rightarrow$ Circular path.

Matching these to the options, **Option C** is the only one that correctly identifies both.
