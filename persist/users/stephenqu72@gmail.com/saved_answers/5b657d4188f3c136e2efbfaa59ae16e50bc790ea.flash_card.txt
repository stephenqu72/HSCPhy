### Front
In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), how do the horizontal ($v_x$) and vertical ($v_y$) velocity components change during the flight?

### Back
- Key idea: Horizontal velocity is constant; vertical velocity changes due to constant gravitational acceleration.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v_x = u_x$ (constant); $v_y = u_y - gt$.
- When to use it: To determine velocity or position at any time $t$ during flight.
- Common trap: Assuming vertical velocity remains constant or that the magnitude of velocity is the same at the start and end of flight if the impact height differs from the launch height.
