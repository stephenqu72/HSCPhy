### Front
Explain the relationship between Kepler's 2nd Law and Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravity acts as a central force, exerting zero torque on a planet. This results in the conservation of angular momentum.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Law of Conservation of Angular Momentum ($L = mvr\sin\theta = \text{constant}$).
- **When to use it:** When discussing how orbital velocity changes with distance from the central mass (e.g., faster at perihelion, slower at aphelion).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to explicitly state that "zero torque" is the requirement for "conservation of angular momentum."
