### Front
How does the gravitational potential energy ($U$) of a satellite change when it moves from a distance $r$ to $2r$ from the center of a planet?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational potential energy increases (becomes "less negative") as distance $r$ increases.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$
- **When to use it:** To calculate energy changes when an object moves within a gravitational field.
- **Common trap:** Confusing a decrease in the *magnitude* of the binding energy with a decrease in *potential energy*. Moving further away from a planet always increases $U$ (bringing it closer to 0).
