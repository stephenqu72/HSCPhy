To solve this problem, we use the fundamental wave equation that relates the speed, frequency, and wavelength of an electromagnetic wave (such as a photon).

### 1. Identify the given values
*   **Frequency ($f$):** $5 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$
*   **Wavelength ($\lambda$):** $600 \text{ nm}$
    *   Since the speed of light is measured in $\text{m/s}$, we must convert the wavelength into meters:
    *   $600 \text{ nm} = 600 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m} = 6 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$

### 2. Apply the wave equation
The speed of a wave ($v$) is given by the formula:
$$v = f \times \lambda$$

### 3. Calculate
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$v = (5 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}) \times (6 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m})$$
$$v = 30 \times 10^{(14 - 7)} \text{ m/s}$$
$$v = 30 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}$$
To express this in standard scientific notation ($a \times 10^n$ where $1 \le a < 10$):
$$v = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$$

### Conclusion
Comparing this result to the provided options:
*   A) $2.8 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
*   B) $2.9 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
*   **C) $3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$**
*   D) $2.72 \times 10^9 \text{ m/s}$

The correct answer is **C**. This confirms the theoretical speed of light in a vacuum ($c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$).
