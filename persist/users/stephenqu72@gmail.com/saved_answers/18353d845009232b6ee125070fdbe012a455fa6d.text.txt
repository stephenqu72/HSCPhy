To excel in an HSC physics response like this, you must treat the term "Evaluate" as a directive to make a judgment based on criteria. You need to demonstrate the *significance* of Maxwell's work by showing how he synthesized existing knowledge and predicted new phenomena that fundamentally changed our understanding of the universe.

### Sample Answer

James Clerk Maxwell’s synthesis of electromagnetism is arguably the most significant achievement in classical physics because it unified disparate phenomena into a single, cohesive theoretical framework.

1.  **Synthesis of Existing Laws:** Before Maxwell, electricity and magnetism were treated as separate entities. Maxwell synthesized Gauss’s Laws, Ampère’s Law, and Faraday’s Law into a set of four partial differential equations. He recognized that these laws were incomplete as they stood, particularly in non-steady state conditions.
2.  **The Displacement Current:** Maxwell’s most brilliant contribution was the addition of the "displacement current" term to Ampère’s Law. He realized that a changing electric field creates a magnetic field, just as a changing magnetic field (Faraday’s Law) creates an electric field. This symmetry was the "missing piece" that allowed for the existence of self-propagating electromagnetic waves.
3.  **Prediction of Electromagnetic Waves:** By manipulating his equations, Maxwell derived the wave equation and calculated the theoretical speed of these waves:
    $$v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\epsilon_0 \mu_0}}$$
    Upon calculating this value, he found it matched the experimentally determined speed of light, leading him to correctly conclude that light itself is an electromagnetic wave.
4.  **Scientific Significance:** This discovery laid the foundation for modern technology (radio, television, radar, and optics) and resolved the long-standing mystery of the nature of light. Furthermore, the invariance of these equations under Lorentz transformations eventually necessitated the development of Einstein’s Special Relativity, showing that Maxwell’s work transcended classical physics and paved the way for modern physics.

### How to solve this:
1.  **Understand the Verb:** "Evaluate" requires you to state your position (that it is indeed highly significant) and provide evidence to justify that claim.
2.  **Structure your argument:**
    *   **Introduction:** State that it is a pivotal moment in physics, transitioning from fragmented laws to a unified field theory.
    *   **Body Paragraph 1 (Unification):** Mention the four equations.
    *   **Body Paragraph 2 (Innovation):** Focus on the displacement current. This shows depth of knowledge.
    *   **Body Paragraph 3 (The "Big Picture"):** Connect it to light and the speed of light calculation.
    *   **Conclusion:** Briefly mention how this work eventually led to Special Relativity, cementing its status as "most significant."
3.  **Check the Marks:** Since it is a 5-mark question, ensure you have at least 3-4 distinct logical points supported by specific physics terminology (e.g., displacement current, permittivity, permeability, unification).
