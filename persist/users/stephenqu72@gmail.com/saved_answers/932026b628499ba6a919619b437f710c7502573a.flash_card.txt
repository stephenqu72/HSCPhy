### Front
What provides the centripetal force for a car navigating a flat, horizontal circular bend?

### Back
- Key idea: Static friction between the tyres and the road provides the net inward force required for circular motion.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{net} = F_{friction} = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- When to use it: When calculating the maximum safe speed for a vehicle on a flat curve.
- Common trap: Assuming the normal force or engine force contributes to the lateral centripetal force.
