### Front
What is the theoretical impact on the Standard Model of Matter if quarks were discovered to be composite particles?

### Back
- Key idea: Quarks would lose their status as "elementary" (fundamental) particles, necessitating a theoretical expansion to explain their internal constituents and the new forces holding them together.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When discussing the limitations of the Standard Model or the history/nature of scientific discovery regarding subatomic structure.
- Common trap: Forgetting to mention that this requires a new framework/theory (Standard Model extension) to explain the new internal forces, rather than just saying the model is "wrong."
