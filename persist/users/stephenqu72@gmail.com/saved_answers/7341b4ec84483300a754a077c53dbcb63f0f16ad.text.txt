### Analysis of the Physics
When a particle of charge $q$ and mass $m$ moves with velocity $v$ perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field $B$, it undergoes uniform circular motion where the magnetic force provides the centripetal force:
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Solving for the radius of curvature $r$:
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$
Assuming the mass ($m$), velocity ($v$), and magnetic field strength ($B$) are constant for all particles, the relationship between radius ($r$) and charge ($q$) is:
$$r \propto \frac{1}{q}$$
This confirms that the radius is indeed **inversely proportional** to the charge.
