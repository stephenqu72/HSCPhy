To find the gravitational force acting on the satellite, we must recognize that the gravitational force provides the necessary **centripetal force** to keep the satellite in circular motion.

### 1. Identify the given variables
*   Mass of satellite ($m$): $3.50 \times 10^3 \text{ kg}$
*   Orbital radius ($r$): $9.17 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
*   Orbital period ($T$): $1.50 \text{ hours} = 1.50 \times 3600 \text{ s} = 5400 \text{ s}$

### 2. Relevant Formula
The centripetal force ($F_c$) required to keep an object in a circular orbit is given by:
$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Since the velocity $v$ is equal to the circumference divided by the period ($v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$), we substitute this into the formula:
$$F_c = \frac{m (\frac{2\pi r}{T})^2}{r} = \frac{4\pi^2 mr}{T^2}$$

### 3. Calculation
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$F = \frac{4 \times \pi^2 \times (3.50 \times 10^3 \text{ kg}) \times (9.17 \times 10^6 \text{ m})}{(5400 \text{ s})^2}$$

First, calculate the numerator:
$$4 \times \pi^2 \times 3.50 \times 10^3 \times 9.17 \times 10^6 \approx 1.267 \times 10^{13}$$

Next, calculate the denominator:
$$(5400)^2 = 2.916 \times 10^7$$

Finally, divide:
$$F = \frac{1.267 \times 10^{13}}{2.916 \times 10^7} \approx 4.345 \times 10^5 \text{ N}$$
