### Front
How does centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) change if an object’s speed doubles and its radius is halved?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration is directly proportional to $v^2$ and inversely proportional to $r$. Doubling $v$ quadruples $a_c$; halving $r$ doubles $a_c$. Total factor = $4 \times 2 = 8$.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$
- When to use it: Any uniform circular motion problem involving changes to velocity or orbital radius.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the velocity change ($2v$ becomes $4v^2$) or incorrectly handling the fraction in the denominator.
