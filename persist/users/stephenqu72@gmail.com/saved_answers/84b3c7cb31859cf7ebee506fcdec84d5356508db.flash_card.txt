### Front
How do $\alpha$ and $\beta^-$ decay affect the atomic number ($Z$) and mass number ($A$) on a decay series chart?

### Back
- Key idea: Radioactive decay conserves nucleon number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$).
- Formula / law: 
    - $\alpha$ decay: $Z \rightarrow Z-2$, $A \rightarrow A-4$.
    - $\beta^-$ decay: $Z \rightarrow Z+1$, $A$ remains constant.
- When to use it: To identify unknown isotopes or decay modes in a radioactive series.
- Common trap: Forgetting that $\beta^-$ decay involves a neutron turning into a proton, increasing $Z$ by 1, whereas positron emission ($\beta^+$) decreases $Z$ by 1.
