### Front
What are the SI units for acceleration and energy, and what is the relationship between their fundamental units?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration is the rate of velocity change ($\text{m s}^{-2}$); Energy is the capacity to do work ($J$).
- Formula / law: $a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}$ and $1 J = 1 \text{ kg m}^2 \text{ s}^{-2}$.
- When to use it: To verify unit consistency in calculations or to identify correct physical dimensions in multiple-choice questions.
- Common trap: Confusing acceleration ($\text{m s}^{-2}$) with velocity ($\text{m s}^{-1}$) or mistaking energy units for force ($N = \text{kg m s}^{-2}$).
