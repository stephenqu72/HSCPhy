To answer this question effectively, you need to connect Thomson’s experimental methodology with the logical inference that led to the discovery of the electron as a subatomic particle.

### Answer
**Experimental steps and observations:**
Thomson used a cathode ray tube (CRT) to subject a beam of cathode rays to both electric and magnetic fields. He measured the deflection of the beam and balanced the electric and magnetic forces to calculate the charge-to-mass ratio ($q/m$) of the particles. 

Crucially, Thomson repeated this experiment using different cathode materials and different residual gases within the tube. He observed that the $q/m$ ratio remained constant regardless of the material used to generate the rays or the gas present in the tube.

**Assessment of validity:**
Thomson's conclusion is considered valid because the constant value of the $q/m$ ratio provided strong evidence that the particles (electrons) were universal constituents of all matter. Since the particles were identical regardless of the source material, it logically followed that they were fundamental components present within all atoms, rather than being specific to the chemical properties of the cathode metal.
