### Front
Which subatomic particles are considered "fundamental" (elementary) in the Standard Model of matter?

### Back
- Key idea: Fundamental particles are those with no internal structure (not composed of smaller parts).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When identifying the building blocks of matter (Quarks and Leptons).
- Common trap: Confusing "Fermions" (a spin classification that includes composite baryons) or "Hadrons" (composite particles made of quarks) with fundamental particles.
