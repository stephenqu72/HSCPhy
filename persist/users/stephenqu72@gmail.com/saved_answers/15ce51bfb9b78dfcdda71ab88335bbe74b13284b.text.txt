### Answer

**a. Identify the force(s):**
The only force acting on the satellite is the **gravitational force** exerted by the Earth. This force acts in the direction of the center of the Earth (radially inward toward the Earth's center). This force acts as the centripetal force required to maintain the circular orbit.

**b. Calculate the period:**
1. Determine the total orbital radius ($r$):
   $$r = \text{Radius of Earth} + \text{Altitude} = 6.37 \times 10^6\text{ m} + 2.00 \times 10^7\text{ m} = 2.637 \times 10^7\text{ m}$$

2. Use Kepler's Third Law (or equate gravitational force to centripetal force $F_g = F_c$):
   $$\frac{G M m}{r^2} = \frac{m v^2}{r} = m r \left( \frac{2\pi}{T} \right)^2$$
   $$T = \sqrt{\frac{4 \pi^2 r^3}{G M}}$$

3. Substitute the values:
   $$T = \sqrt{\frac{4 \times \pi^2 \times (2.637 \times 10^7)^3}{6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 5.98 \times 10^{24}}}$$
   $$T = \sqrt{\frac{4 \times 9.8696 \times 1.834 \times 10^{22}}{3.98866 \times 10^{14}}}$$
   $$T = \sqrt{1.8143 \times 10^9} \approx 42594\text{ seconds}$$

4. Convert to hours (optional, but standard for satellite periods):
   $$T \approx 11.8\text{ hours}$$
