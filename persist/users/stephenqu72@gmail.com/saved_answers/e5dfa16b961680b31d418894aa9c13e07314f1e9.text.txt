### Answer

**(a)** The AC generator transforms **mechanical energy** (kinetic energy of the rotating coil) into **electrical energy** (an alternating current).

**(b)** Component **A** is a **split-ring commutator**. 
In a DC generator, as the coil rotates, the induced EMF would normally reverse direction every half-turn. The split-ring commutator acts as a mechanical rectifier. As the coil passes through the vertical position, the split-ring segments swap contact from one carbon brush to the other. This ensures that the current flowing out of the generator remains in the same direction, resulting in a unidirectional (DC) output.

### How to solve this

1.  **For part (a):** Always remember that generators are "input-mechanical, output-electrical." Motors are the exact opposite. Simply state the energy transformation clearly to secure the mark.
2.  **For part (b):** This is a 3-mark question, so you need to demonstrate a logical flow:
    *   **Identification (1 mark):** Correctly name component A as the "split-ring commutator."
    *   **The Problem (1 mark):** Explain *why* it is needed—that the induced EMF in the rotating coil is naturally alternating (sinusoidal).
    *   **The Solution (1 mark):** Explain *how* the component works—by reversing the connections to the external circuit every half-rotation, it effectively flips the negative half of the AC cycle to be positive, creating a constant direction of current.
