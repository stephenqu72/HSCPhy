To solve this problem, we must apply the principle of **moments** (torques) in equilibrium. For the forearm to remain stationary (in static equilibrium), the sum of the clockwise moments about the elbow pivot must equal the sum of the counter-clockwise moments about the same pivot.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the Pivot Point:** The elbow acts as the pivot point.
2.  **Determine the Forces and their Distances (Lever Arms):**
    *   The bicep force $F_B$ acts upwards at a distance of $50$ units from the pivot.
    *   The weight of the forearm ($30\text{ N}$) acts downwards at a distance of $50 + 90 = 140$ units from the pivot.
    *   The weight of the dumbbell ($140\text{ N}$) acts downwards at a distance of $50 + 90 + 160 = 300$ units from the pivot.
3.  **Set up the Equilibrium Equation:**
    The sum of clockwise moments = sum of counter-clockwise moments:
    $$\sum \tau_{clockwise} = \sum \tau_{counter-clockwise}$$
    $$F_B \times 50 = (30 \times 140) + (140 \times 300)$$

4.  **Calculate the Values:**
    *   Moment of forearm weight: $30 \times 140 = 4200$
    *   Moment of dumbbell weight: $140 \times 300 = 42000$
    *   Total clockwise moment: $4200 + 42000 = 46200$

5.  **Solve for $F_B$:**
    $$F_B \times 50 = 46200$$
    $$F_B = \frac{46200}{50}$$
    $$F_B = 924\text{ N}$$

Comparing our result to the given options, the correct answer is **(D)**.
