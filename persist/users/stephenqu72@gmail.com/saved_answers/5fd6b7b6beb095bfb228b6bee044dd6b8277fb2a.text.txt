To solve this problem, we must equate the kinetic energy imparted by the centrifuge to the energy required to overcome Earth's gravitational potential well (escape velocity).

### 1. Identify the given values:
*   Mass of payload ($m$) = $200\text{ kg}$
*   Radius of arm ($r$) = $50\text{ m}$
*   Mass of Earth ($M$) $\approx 5.97 \times 10^{24}\text{ kg}$
*   Radius of Earth ($R_E$) $\approx 6.37 \times 10^6\text{ m}$
*   Gravitational constant ($G$) $\approx 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

### 2. Determine escape velocity ($v_e$):
The escape velocity is given by the formula:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R_E}}$$
Substituting the values:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 5.97 \times 10^{24}}{6.37 \times 10^6}} \approx 11,186\text{ m/s}$$

### 3. Relate tangential velocity ($v$) to angular velocity ($\omega$):
At the moment of release, the tangential velocity of the payload must equal the required escape velocity:
$$v = r\omega \implies \omega = \frac{v_e}{r}$$

### 4. Calculate angular velocity ($\omega$):
$$\omega = \frac{11,186\text{ m/s}}{50\text{ m}}$$
$$\omega \approx 223.7\text{ rad/s}$$

***

### Explanation:
To solve this physics problem, we apply the principle of **Conservation of Energy**. The centrifuge must spin fast enough so that at the release point, the payload possesses the kinetic energy equivalent to that required to escape Earth's gravitational influence. 

1.  **Escape Velocity:** We calculate the speed required to escape Earth using the gravitational escape velocity formula.
2.  **Circular Motion:** Since the payload is released from the end of a rotating arm, its linear (tangential) velocity at the moment of release is defined by $v = r\omega$.
3.  **Final Calculation:** By rearranging the formula to $\omega = v/r$, we find the necessary angular velocity in radians per second.
