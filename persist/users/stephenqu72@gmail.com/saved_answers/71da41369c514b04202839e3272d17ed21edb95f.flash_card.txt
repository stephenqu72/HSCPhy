### Front
What two fundamental conservation laws must be applied to solve nuclear decay equations?

### Back
- Key idea: Total mass number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$) must remain constant on both sides of the equation.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Law of Conservation of Nucleon Number and Law of Conservation of Charge.
- When to use it: Whenever balancing radioactive decay equations (alpha, beta, or gamma).
- Common trap: Forgetting that an alpha particle ($\alpha$ or $_2^4\text{He}$) also carries mass and charge that must be included in the balancing.
