To solve this problem, we need to apply the physics of forces between current-carrying wires and Newton's Second Law of Motion.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
*   **Magnetic Force between parallel wires:** The force per unit length between two parallel conductors is given by the formula:
    $$F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 L}{2 \pi r}$$
    Where:
    *   $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \, \text{T m A}^{-1}$ (permeability of free space)
    *   $I_1 = 25.0 \, \text{A}$
    *   $I_2 = 50.0 \, \text{A}$
    *   $L = 2.00 \, \text{m}$ (length of the wires)
    *   $r = 2.00 \, \text{mm} = 2.00 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m}$ (distance of separation)
*   **Newton's Second Law:** $F = ma$, where $m$ is the mass of the trolley and $a = 0.200 \, \text{m s}^{-2}$.

### 2. Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step 1: Calculate the magnetic force ($F$)**
Substitute the given values into the force formula:
$$F = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times (25.0) \times (50.0) \times (2.00)}{2\pi \times (2.00 \times 10^{-3})}$$

Simplify the constants:
*   $\frac{4\pi}{2\pi} = 2$
*   $F = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 25.0 \times 50.0 \times 2.00}{2.00 \times 10^{-3}}$
*   $F = \frac{10^{-7} \times 2500 \times 2}{10^{-3}}$
*   $F = \frac{0.0005}{0.002} = 0.25 \, \text{N}$

**Step 2: Calculate the mass ($m$)**
Using Newton's Second Law, $F = ma$:
$$m = \frac{F}{a}$$
$$m = \frac{0.25 \, \text{N}}{0.200 \, \text{m s}^{-2}}$$
$$m = 1.25 \, \text{kg}$$

### Conclusion
The mass of each trolley is $1.25 \, \text{kg}$, which corresponds to option **(B)**.
