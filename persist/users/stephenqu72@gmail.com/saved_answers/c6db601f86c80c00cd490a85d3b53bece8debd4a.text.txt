### Explanation

To solve this question, you need to recall the definitions of Kepler's three laws of planetary motion:

1.  **Kepler’s First Law (Law of Ellipses):** States that all planets move in elliptical orbits, with the Sun at one focus. This describes the *shape* of the orbit.
2.  **Kepler’s Second Law (Law of Equal Areas):** States that a line segment joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out **equal areas during equal intervals of time**. This is exactly what the diagram depicts: because the shaded areas $Y$ and $Z$ are equal, the time taken for the comet to travel along the corresponding arcs ($W$ and $X$) must be identical.
3.  **Kepler’s Third Law (Law of Harmonies):** States that the square of the orbital period of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis of its orbit. This is represented by the mathematical relationship:
    $$\frac{T^2}{r^3} = k$$
    where $T$ is the orbital period and $r$ is the average distance from the Sun.
4.  **Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation:** Describes the force of attraction between two masses, given by:
    $$F = G\frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

Since the question specifically refers to the relationship between the **areas swept out** and the **time taken**, it is a direct application of Kepler’s Second Law.
