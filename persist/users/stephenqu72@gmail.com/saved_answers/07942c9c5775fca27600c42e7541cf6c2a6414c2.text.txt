To solve this problem, we apply **Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion**, which states that the square of the orbital period ($T$) of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis (radius, $r$) of its orbit:

$$T^2 \propto r^3 \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{T_X^2}{T_Y^2} = \frac{r_X^3}{r_Y^3}$$

### Step 1: Determine the orbital period of Planet $X$ ($T_X$)
We are told that planet $X$ rotates through $120^\circ$ in 5 years. Since a full orbit is $360^\circ$:
$$\frac{120^\circ}{360^\circ} = \frac{1}{3} \text{ of an orbit}$$
If it takes 5 years to complete $\frac{1}{3}$ of an orbit, the total period $T_X$ is:
$$T_X = 5 \times 3 = 15 \text{ years}$$

### Step 2: Use the ratio of the radii
The ratio of the radii is given as $r_X : r_Y = 2 : 5$. Therefore:
$$\frac{r_X}{r_Y} = \frac{2}{5}$$

### Step 3: Calculate the orbital period of Planet $Y$ ($T_Y$)
Substitute the known values into Kepler’s Third Law equation:
$$\frac{T_X^2}{T_Y^2} = \left(\frac{r_X}{r_Y}\right)^3$$
$$\frac{15^2}{T_Y^2} = \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^3$$
$$\frac{225}{T_Y^2} = \frac{8}{125}$$
Rearrange to solve for $T_Y^2$:
$$T_Y^2 = \frac{225 \times 125}{8}$$
$$T_Y^2 = \frac{28125}{8} = 3515.625$$
Taking the square root:
$$T_Y = \sqrt{3515.625} \approx 59.29 \text{ years}$$

**Answer:** Planet $Y$ takes approximately **59.3 years** to orbit the star.
