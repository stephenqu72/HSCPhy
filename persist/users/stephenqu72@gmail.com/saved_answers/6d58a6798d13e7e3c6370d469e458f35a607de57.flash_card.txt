### Front
How do you determine the frequency of a wave given its velocity ($v$) and a displacement-position graph?

### Back
- Key idea: Use the graph to identify the wavelength ($\lambda$), then use the universal wave equation.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = f\lambda \implies f = \frac{v}{\lambda}$
- When to use it: When given a displacement-position graph (where wavelength is the distance between consecutive crests/troughs) and wave speed.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert units (e.g., cm to m) before calculating, or confusing a displacement-position graph with a displacement-time graph (which would give the period $T$).
