### Front
How do you relate the orbital periods ($T$) and radii ($r$) of two objects orbiting the same central body?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kepler’s Third Law states that the ratio $T^2/r^3$ is constant for all bodies orbiting the same central mass.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $\frac{T_1^2}{r_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{r_2^3}$ (or $\frac{T^2}{r^3} = \frac{4\pi^2}{GM}$)
- **When to use it:** When comparing the motion of two satellites (moons, planets) around the same primary body.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to square the period ($T^2$) or cube the radius ($r^3$), or attempting to use the ratio for bodies orbiting different central masses.
