### Front
How does the gravitational acceleration ($g$) of a planet change if its mass triples and its radius is halved?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational field strength is directly proportional to mass ($M$) and inversely proportional to the square of the radius ($r^2$).
- Formula / law / constant: $g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- When to use it: To compare gravitational fields of different celestial bodies or determine surface gravity.
- Common trap: Forgetting to square the scaling factor of the radius (e.g., $(0.5)^2 = 0.25$, not $0.5$). Dividing by $0.25$ is equivalent to multiplying by $4$.
