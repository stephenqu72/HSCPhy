To solve this problem, we use **Malus's Law**, which describes how the intensity of polarized light changes when it passes through a polarizing filter (analyzer).

### The Formula
Malus's Law states:
$$I = I_0 \cos^2(\theta)$$

Where:
*   $I$ is the transmitted intensity ($15 \text{ W m}^{-2}$ in this problem).
*   $I_0$ is the initial intensity of the polarized light.
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the direction of the polarized light and the axis of the polarizing filter.

### How to Solve
We need to test the given options to see which pair of $(I_0, \theta)$ satisfies the equation $15 = I_0 \cos^2(\theta)$.

**Option A:** $I_0 = 20$, $\theta = 60^\circ$
$$I = 20 \times \cos^2(60^\circ) = 20 \times (0.5)^2 = 20 \times 0.25 = 5 \text{ W m}^{-2}$$
(Incorrect)

**Option B:** $I_0 = 20$, $\theta = 30^\circ$
$$I = 20 \times \cos^2(30^\circ) = 20 \times (\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2})^2 = 20 \times 0.75 = 15 \text{ W m}^{-2}$$
**(Correct)**

**Option C:** $I_0 = 30$, $\theta = 60^\circ$
$$I = 30 \times \cos^2(60^\circ) = 30 \times 0.25 = 7.5 \text{ W m}^{-2}$$
(Incorrect)

**Option D:** $I_0 = 30$, $\theta = 30^\circ$
$$I = 30 \times \cos^2(30^\circ) = 30 \times 0.75 = 22.5 \text{ W m}^{-2}$$
(Incorrect)

Since Option B yields the target intensity of $15 \text{ W m}^{-2}$, it is the correct answer.
