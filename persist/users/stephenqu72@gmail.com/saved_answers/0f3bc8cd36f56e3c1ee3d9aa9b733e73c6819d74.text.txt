### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Calculate the Mass Defect ($\Delta m$):**
The mass defect is the difference between the mass of the reactant and the sum of the masses of the products.
$$\text{Mass of reactant} = 8.005305 \text{ u}$$
$$\text{Mass of products} = 2 \times 4.002603 \text{ u} = 8.005206 \text{ u}$$
$$\Delta m = 8.005305 \text{ u} - 8.005206 \text{ u} = 0.000099 \text{ u}$$

**2. Convert Mass Defect to Energy:**
We use the conversion factor where $1 \text{ u} = 931.5 \text{ MeV/c}^2$.
$$E_{\text{total}} = 0.000099 \text{ u} \times 931.5 \text{ MeV/u} = 0.0922185 \text{ MeV}$$
To convert this to keV:
$$0.0922185 \text{ MeV} \times 1000 \text{ keV/MeV} \approx 92.2 \text{ keV}$$

Because the beryllium nucleus is initially at rest, the total energy released is shared equally as kinetic energy between the two identical alpha particles to conserve both energy and momentum.
$$KE_{\text{one alpha}} = \frac{E_{\text{total}}}{2} = \frac{92.2 \text{ keV}}{2} = 46.1 \text{ keV}$$
