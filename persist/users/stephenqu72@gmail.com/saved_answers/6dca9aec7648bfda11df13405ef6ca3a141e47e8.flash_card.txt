### Front
What are the defining characteristics of a nuclear equation representing gamma ($\gamma$) decay?

### Back
- **Key idea**: The nucleus transitions from an excited state to a lower energy state by emitting a high-energy photon, resulting in no change to atomic ($Z$) or mass ($A$) numbers.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure**: Conservation of Nucleon Number ($A_{in} = A_{out}$) and Atomic Number ($Z_{in} = Z_{out}$). Equation form: $^A_Z\text{X}^* \rightarrow ^A_Z\text{X} + ^0_0\gamma$
- **When to use it**: When identifying decay processes where the identity of the element remains unchanged.
- **Common trap**: Confusing gamma decay with alpha or beta decay; remember that gamma decay never causes transmutation (the parent and daughter nuclei are identical).
