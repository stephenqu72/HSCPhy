### Answer
The correct answer is **B. electric force and gravitational force**.

### Explanation
To solve this, you need to recall the setup of the **Millikan oil drop experiment**. Millikan suspended tiny charged oil droplets between two horizontal metal plates. 

1.  **Gravitational Force ($F_g$):** Every oil drop has mass, so it experiences a downward force due to gravity, calculated as $F_g = mg$.
2.  **Electric Force ($F_e$):** By applying a potential difference (voltage) across the plates, Millikan created a uniform electric field. Because the oil drops were charged (due to X-ray ionization), the electric field exerted an upward electric force on them, calculated as $F_e = qE$.

Millikan adjusted the voltage until the oil drop became **stationary** (or moved at a constant terminal velocity). When the drop is stationary, the net force on the particle is zero, meaning the upward electric force exactly balances the downward gravitational force:

$$F_e = F_g$$
$$qE = mg$$

Since there were no magnetic fields involved in this specific balancing mechanism, options involving magnetic force (A, C, and D) are incorrect.
