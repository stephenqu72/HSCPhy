### Front
How do you determine the remaining amount of a radioactive sample after a specific time period if the half-life ($T_{1/2}$) is known?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The activity or number of atoms halves every $T_{1/2}$.
- **Formula:** $N = N_0 \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^n$, where $n = \frac{t}{T_{1/2}}$
- **When to use it:** To calculate decay over multiple half-lives or to find the remaining fraction of a radioisotope.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that $n$ (the number of half-lives) must be a whole number for manual division, but can be a fraction (e.g., $9$ half-lives) when using the formula.
