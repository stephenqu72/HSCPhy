### Question 18
**Answer: D**

**Explanation:**
*   In experimental physics, measuring a single period ($T$) of a pendulum is prone to human reaction time error. Reaction time typically adds an uncertainty of approximately $0.1$ to $0.2$ seconds to any measurement.
*   By measuring the time for $n$ oscillations (in this case, $n=5$), the total time $t_{total} = n \times T$ is measured. The period is then calculated as $T = \frac{t_{total}}{n}$.
*   When you divide the total measured time by the number of oscillations, the absolute uncertainty of the reaction time is also divided by that number ($n$). Therefore, measuring multiple oscillations significantly reduces the relative uncertainty in the measurement of the period.
*   Options B and C are incorrect because the number of oscillations does not change the physical effects of air friction or the uncertainty of the length measurement (which is usually determined by a ruler/tape measure).
