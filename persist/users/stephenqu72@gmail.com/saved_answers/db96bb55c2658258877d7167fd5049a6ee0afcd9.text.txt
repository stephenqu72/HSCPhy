To solve this problem, we use the formula for gravitational field strength $g$ at the surface of a spherical body:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

where $G$ is the gravitational constant, $M$ is the mass, and $r$ is the radius of the body. 

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1. **Analyze Planet X:**
   - Mass $= M$
   - Diameter $= d$, so the radius $r_X = \frac{d}{2}$
   - Gravitational field strength $g_X = g = \frac{GM}{(\frac{d}{2})^2} = \frac{GM}{\frac{d^2}{4}} = \frac{4GM}{d^2}$

2. **Analyze Planet Y:**
   - Mass $= 2M$
   - Diameter $= 4d$, so the radius $r_Y = \frac{4d}{2} = 2d$
   - Gravitational field strength $g_Y = \frac{G(2M)}{(2d)^2} = \frac{2GM}{4d^2} = \frac{GM}{2d^2}$

3. **Compare $g_Y$ to $g_X$:**
   - We know $g = \frac{4GM}{d^2}$, which means $\frac{GM}{d^2} = \frac{g}{4}$
   - Substitute this into the expression for $g_Y$:
     $$g_Y = \frac{1}{2} \times \left( \frac{GM}{d^2} \right) = \frac{1}{2} \times \left( \frac{g}{4} \right) = \frac{g}{8} = 0.125g$$

Therefore, the gravitational field strength on planet Y is $0.125g$.
