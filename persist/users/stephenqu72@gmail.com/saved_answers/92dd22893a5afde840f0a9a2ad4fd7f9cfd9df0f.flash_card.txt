### Front
How does the work done by a gravitational force ($W_g$) relate to the gravitational potential energy ($U_g$) of an object moving between two points?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravity is a conservative force; work done by gravity is equal to the negative change in gravitational potential energy.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $W_g = -\Delta U_g = U_i - U_f$
- When to use it: To calculate energy changes in gravitational fields (e.g., satellite orbits, projectiles).
- Common trap: Confusing work done *by* gravity with work done *against* gravity (which is $+\Delta U_g$), or assuming total mechanical energy is conserved when external forces (like thrust) are present.
