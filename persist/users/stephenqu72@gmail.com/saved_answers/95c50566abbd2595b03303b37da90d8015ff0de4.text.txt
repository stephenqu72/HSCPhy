To determine the correct answer, we must examine the physics of a uniform electric field between two parallel plates.

### Explanation

1.  **The Electric Field ($E$):** For parallel plates connected to a constant voltage supply $V$ separated by a distance $d$, the electric field strength is uniform between the plates and is given by the formula:
    $$E = \frac{V}{d}$$
    Note that this formula shows that $E$ is independent of the position $x$ of the particle between the plates.

2.  **The Electric Force ($F$):** The force experienced by a charge $q$ in an electric field is given by:
    $$F = qE$$
    Substituting the expression for $E$, we get:
    $$F = q \cdot \frac{V}{d} = \frac{qV}{d}$$

3.  **Analyzing the variables:**
    *   **$V$ (Voltage):** As $V$ increases, $F$ increases (direct relationship).
    *   **$d$ (Distance between plates):** As $d$ increases, $F$ decreases (inverse relationship).
    *   **$x$ (Position):** Since $x$ does not appear in the final formula $F = \frac{qV}{d}$, the force is **independent** of the position of the charge between the plates. Therefore, changing $x$ does not increase or reduce the force.

4.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   A. Mentions $x$, which is incorrect because the force is independent of $x$.
    *   B. "Increasing $d$" would *reduce* the force, not increase it.
    *   C. Mentions $x$, which is incorrect.
    *   D. **"Decreasing $d$"** increases the force (since $d$ is in the denominator). **"Decreasing $V$"** reduces the force (since $V$ is in the numerator). This matches the physical relationships perfectly.

The correct row is **D**.
