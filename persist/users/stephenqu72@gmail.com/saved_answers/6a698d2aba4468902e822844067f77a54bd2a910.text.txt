To solve this problem, we use the principles of electromagnetic induction, specifically Faraday's Law and the definition of magnetic flux.

### 1. Calculate the cross-sectional area of the coil
Magnetic flux $\Phi$ is defined by the formula:
$$\Phi = B \cdot A \cdot \cos(\theta)$$
Given that the coil is perpendicular to the field, the angle between the normal to the coil and the field is $0^\circ$ (meaning $\cos(0) = 1$). We rearrange to solve for area $A$:
$$A = \frac{\Phi}{B}$$
Given:
- $\Phi = 8 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb}$
- $B = 2 \times 10^{-1} \text{ T}$

$$A = \frac{8 \times 10^{-4}}{2 \times 10^{-1}} = 4 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2$$

### 2. Calculate the induced EMF
Faraday's Law states that the average induced EMF ($\varepsilon$) is equal to the negative rate of change of magnetic flux linkage:
$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$$
Where:
- $N = 50$ (number of turns)
- Initial flux $\Phi_i = 8 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb}$
- When the coil rotates by $180^\circ$, the flux becomes $\Phi_f = -8 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb}$
- $\Delta t = 0.2 \text{ s}$

The change in flux is:
$$\Delta\Phi = \Phi_f - \Phi_i = -8 \times 10^{-4} - 8 \times 10^{-4} = -16 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb}$$

Now, calculate the EMF:
$$\varepsilon = -50 \times \left( \frac{-16 \times 10^{-4} \text{ Wb}}{0.2 \text{ s}} \right)$$
$$\varepsilon = 50 \times 8 \times 10^{-3} = 0.4 \text{ V}$$

**Final Answer:**
- The cross-sectional area of the coil is $4 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2$.
- The induced EMF is $0.4 \text{ V}$.
