### Front
How do you calculate the orbital radius or period of two satellites orbiting the same central body?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion.
- **Formula:** $\frac{r_1^3}{T_1^2} = \frac{r_2^3}{T_2^2}$
- **When to use it:** When comparing two objects orbiting the same central mass (e.g., moons around a planet).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to cube the radii or square the periods before rearranging; ensuring units for $T$ and $r$ are consistent on both sides.
