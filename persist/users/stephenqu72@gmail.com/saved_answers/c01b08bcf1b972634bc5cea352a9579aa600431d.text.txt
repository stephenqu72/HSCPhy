To solve this problem, we need to calculate the gravitational acceleration exerted by both the Earth and the Moon on a $1.00\text{ kg}$ object placed at the midpoint, then find the net acceleration.

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   Distance between Earth and Moon ($r_{total}$) = $3.84 \times 10^8\text{ m}$
*   Distance from midpoint to either body ($r$) = $\frac{3.84 \times 10^8}{2} = 1.92 \times 10^8\text{ m}$
*   Mass of Earth ($M_E$) = $6.0 \times 10^{24}\text{ kg}$
*   Mass of Moon ($M_M$) = $7.35 \times 10^{22}\text{ kg}$
*   Universal gravitational constant ($G$) = $6.674 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2\text{ kg}^{-2}$

### 2. Calculate the acceleration due to each body:
The acceleration due to gravity ($g$) is given by:
$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

**Acceleration towards Earth ($g_E$):**
$$g_E = \frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11})(6.0 \times 10^{24})}{(1.92 \times 10^8)^2}$$
$$g_E \approx \frac{4.0044 \times 10^{14}}{3.6864 \times 10^{16}} \approx 1.0862 \times 10^{-2}\text{ m s}^{-2}$$

**Acceleration towards the Moon ($g_M$):**
$$g_M = \frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11})(7.35 \times 10^{22})}{(1.92 \times 10^8)^2}$$
$$g_M \approx \frac{4.9054 \times 10^{12}}{3.6864 \times 10^{16}} \approx 0.1331 \times 10^{-2}\text{ m s}^{-2}$$

### 3. Calculate the net acceleration:
Since the forces pull in opposite directions, we subtract the smaller acceleration from the larger one:
$$\text{Net } g = g_E - g_M$$
$$\text{Net } g = (1.0862 \times 10^{-2}) - (0.1331 \times 10^{-2})$$
$$\text{Net } g \approx 0.9531 \times 10^{-2}\text{ m s}^{-2}$$
