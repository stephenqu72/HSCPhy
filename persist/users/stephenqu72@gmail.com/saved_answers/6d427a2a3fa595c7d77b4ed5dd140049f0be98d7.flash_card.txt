How do particle accelerators validate the Standard Model of matter?

### Back
- Key idea: High-energy collisions convert kinetic energy into mass, creating short-lived subatomic particles to verify theoretical predictions.
- Formula / law / constant: $E=mc^2$ (Mass-energy equivalence).
- When to use it: To explain experimental evidence for quarks, gauge bosons ($W, Z$), the Higgs boson, and conservation laws.
- Common trap: Merely listing discovery dates; you must explicitly link the accelerator technology (collision energy) to the theoretical Standard Model component it confirms.
