### (a) Plotting the graph
You would plot the force values (y-axis) against distance (x-axis). Given the relationship $F \propto \frac{1}{r}$, the resulting plot will be a **curve** (hyperbola), not a straight line.

### (b) Why it is inappropriate to draw a conclusion
It is difficult to determine the precise mathematical relationship from a curve by visual inspection alone. To verify an inverse relationship ($F \propto \frac{1}{r}$), we need to linearize the data.

### (c) Predict the relationship and add data
The theory predicts $F$ is inversely proportional to $r$. To test this, we should calculate $1/r$ (or $1/distance$).

The choice to plot $F$ against $1/r$ is effective because it linearizes the data. If the points form a straight line that passes through the origin, it provides graphical evidence that $F$ is directly proportional to $1/r$, confirming that $F$ is inversely proportional to $r$.
