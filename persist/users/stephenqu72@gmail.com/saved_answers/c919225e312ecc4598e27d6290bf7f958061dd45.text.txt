To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of circular motion and Newton’s Second Law.

### Part a: Resultant Force
At the lowest point of a vertical circle, the object is undergoing centripetal acceleration directed toward the center of the circle. The resultant force (the net force) is equal to the centripetal force, which must point **vertically upwards** toward the center of the circular path. 
*   **Action:** On Figure 14b, draw an arrow starting from the car and pointing directly upwards toward the center point.

### Part b: Calculating the Normal Reaction Force
To find the normal reaction force ($F_N$), we consider the forces acting on the car at point L:
1.  **Weight ($mg$):** Acting downwards.
2.  **Normal reaction ($F_N$):** Acting upwards.

The net force (resultant force) is the centripetal force ($F_c$), directed upwards:
$$F_{net} = F_N - mg = F_c$$

Rearranging to solve for $F_N$:
$$F_N = F_c + mg$$

**Step-by-step calculation:**
1. **Identify the variables:**
   - Mass ($m$) = $5.00 \text{ kg}$
   - Radius ($r$) = $4.00 \text{ m}$
   - Velocity ($v$) = $5.00 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
   - Acceleration due to gravity ($g$) $\approx 9.80 \text{ m s}^{-2}$

2. **Calculate the centripetal force ($F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$):**
   $$F_c = \frac{5.00 \times (5.00)^2}{4.00}$$
   $$F_c = \frac{5.00 \times 25.0}{4.00} = \frac{125}{4} = 31.25 \text{ N}$$

3. **Calculate the weight ($W = mg$):**
   $$W = 5.00 \times 9.80 = 49.0 \text{ N}$$

4. **Calculate $F_N$:**
   $$F_N = 31.25 + 49.0 = 80.25 \text{ N}$$

Rounding to appropriate significant figures based on the data (3 sig figs):
**The normal reaction force is $80.3 \text{ N}$.**
