### Front
How do alpha ($\alpha$) and beta-minus ($\beta^-$) decay appear on an $N-Z$ (Neutron vs. Proton) grid?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Alpha decay moves the nucleus diagonally down-left; Beta-minus decay moves it diagonally up-left.
- **Figure:** 
    - $\alpha$ decay: $\Delta N = -2, \Delta Z = -2$
    - $\beta^-$ decay: $\Delta N = -1, \Delta Z = +1$
- **When to use it:** To trace decay chains or identify daughter isotopes on a Segrè plot.
- **Common trap:** Confusing $\beta^-$ decay (neutron to proton) with $\beta^+$ decay (proton to neutron, which moves down-right).
