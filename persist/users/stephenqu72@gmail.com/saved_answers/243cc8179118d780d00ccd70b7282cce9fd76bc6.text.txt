To determine the direction of the force on the current-carrying wire, we use the **Right-Hand Rule** for the motor effect (also known as Fleming's Left-Hand Rule).

### Explanation
1.  **Determine the Direction of the Magnetic Field ($B$):** Magnetic field lines always point from the North pole ($N$) to the South pole ($S$). In this diagram, the magnets are arranged such that the magnetic field is directed from the left magnet's $S$ pole toward the right magnet's $N$ pole, but more importantly, looking at the space between them, the field flows from the left magnet's North pole toward the left and the right magnet's South pole toward the right. Actually, looking at the arrangement of two magnets placed side-by-side with the wire in the middle, the magnetic field is directed from **Left to Right** (from the first magnet's $N$ pole to the second magnet's $S$ pole).
2.  **Identify Current Direction ($I$):** The arrow on the wire indicates that the current $I$ is flowing **downwards**.
3.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule:**
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$): **To the right**.
    *   Point your thumb in the direction of the current ($I$): **Down**.
    *   Your palm (or the force exerted by the rule) faces **into the page**.
