### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) monochromatic**.

---

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must identify the physical phenomenon shown in the image. The image displays a clear **interference pattern** characterized by alternating bright and dark fringes (maxima and minima). This is the classic result of **Young’s Double Slit Experiment**.

1.  **Requirement for Interference:** For stable, observable interference fringes to be produced, the light source must be **coherent** (maintaining a constant phase relationship) and **monochromatic** (consisting of a single wavelength/color).
2.  **Why (A) is correct:** If the light were not monochromatic (i.e., if it were white light), different wavelengths would interfere constructively and destructively at different positions on the wall. This would cause the fringes to overlap and blur, resulting in a spectrum of colors rather than the distinct, sharp, monochromatic fringes seen in the provided image.
3.  **Evaluating other options:**
    *   **(B) Polarised:** While lasers are often polarized, polarization is not a requirement for an interference pattern to form.
    *   **(C) Intense:** Intensity affects the brightness of the pattern, but not its fundamental formation. A low-intensity coherent source would still produce the same interference pattern.
    *   **(D) Longitudinal:** Light is an electromagnetic wave, which is inherently **transverse**, not longitudinal. Furthermore, longitudinal waves (like sound) do not demonstrate polarization, though they can interfere; however, this is simply not a property of light.

Therefore, the defining characteristic required to create this specific, clear, monochromatic fringe pattern is that the light consists of a single wavelength.
