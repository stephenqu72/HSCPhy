### Answer
To ensure the reliability of secondary sources used during my Depth Studies, I employed a multi-faceted verification process:

1.  **Author and Publisher Credibility:** I checked the qualifications of the authors and the nature of the publishing body. I prioritized peer-reviewed journals, government reports, and academic institutions (e.g., universities or organizations like CSIRO) over general interest websites or opinion blogs, as these are subjected to rigorous vetting.
2.  **Cross-Referencing Data:** I compared the information or data found in one source against at least two other reputable independent sources. If multiple credible sources presented consistent data, the reliability of that information was increased.
3.  **Currency and Objectivity:** I ensured the information was up-to-date and relevant to the specific context of my study. I also evaluated the source for potential bias, ensuring the purpose of the publication was to inform or present scientific findings rather than to persuade or promote a commercial product.
4.  **Evidence-Based Methodology:** I examined the methodology described in the secondary research. I favored sources that explicitly stated their data collection and analysis procedures, allowing me to assess whether their conclusions were logically supported by the evidence provided.
