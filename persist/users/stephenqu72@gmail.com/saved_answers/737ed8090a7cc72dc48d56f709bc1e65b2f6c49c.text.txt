### Answer

**a. Name and explain the effect:**
*   **Name:** The effect is **dispersion**.
*   **Explanation:** White light is composed of a spectrum of different wavelengths (colours). When the white light enters the glass prism, it undergoes refraction. Because the refractive index of glass is wavelength-dependent (a phenomenon known as Cauchy’s equation), shorter wavelengths (such as violet/blue) travel more slowly in the medium and are refracted (bent) more significantly than longer wavelengths (such as red). This difference in the angle of refraction causes the component colours of the white light to spread out, resulting in the visible spectrum on the screen.

**b. Identify the colours:**
*   **Point X:** Red
*   **Point Y:** Violet

### How to solve this question

1.  **Understand the Physics Principle:** Dispersion occurs because the speed of light in a refractive medium varies with frequency. According to Snell's Law ($n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2$), if the refractive index $n$ changes for different wavelengths, the angle of refraction $\theta_2$ will change accordingly.
2.  **Analyze the Geometry:** Look at the deviation of the rays. The light ray that bends the *least* from its original path (closest to the top) has a lower refractive index and longer wavelength. The ray that bends the *most* (closest to the bottom at point Y) has a higher refractive index and shorter wavelength.
3.  **Recall the Visible Spectrum:** The mnemonic ROYGBIV (Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Indigo, Violet) helps here. Red light has the longest wavelength and is refracted the least, meaning it appears at the top (Point X). Violet light has the shortest wavelength and is refracted the most, meaning it appears at the bottom (Point Y).
