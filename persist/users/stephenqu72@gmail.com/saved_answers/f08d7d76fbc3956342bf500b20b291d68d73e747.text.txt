To determine which galaxy is closest to us, we must look at the **redshift** of the spectral lines.

### Answer
The correct answer is **B. Galaxy $V$**.

### Explanation
1.  **Understanding Redshift and Distance:** According to Hubble's Law, the further away a galaxy is from us, the faster it is moving away. This causes the light from the galaxy to experience a Doppler shift toward the red end of the spectrum (a "redshift"). Therefore, the galaxy that is **closest** to us will have the **smallest redshift** (i.e., its spectral lines will be shifted the least distance toward the red end compared to the stationary reference point of the Sun).
2.  **Analyzing the Diagram:** 
    *   The **Sun** provides the reference positions for the three spectral lines.
    *   **Galaxy $U$:** The lines are shifted significantly toward the red (right).
    *   **Galaxy $V$:** The lines are shifted only a very small amount toward the red compared to the Sun.
    *   **Galaxy $W$:** The lines are shifted the most toward the red (right), indicating it is the furthest away.
    *   **Galaxy $X$:** The lines are shifted toward the red, but further than those in Galaxy $V$.
3.  **Conclusion:** By comparing the positions of the spectral lines, Galaxy $V$ shows the smallest displacement from the reference lines of the Sun. A smaller redshift corresponds to a lower recessional velocity, which, according to Hubble's Law, indicates a smaller distance from Earth.
