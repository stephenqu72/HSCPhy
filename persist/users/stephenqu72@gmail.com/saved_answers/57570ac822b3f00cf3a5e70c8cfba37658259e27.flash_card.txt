### Front
How should you classify unpredictable, inconsistent variations in experimental data (such as a homemade launcher producing different velocities each trial)?

### Back
- Key idea: Random error.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: When data points fluctuate around a true value; use repeated trials and averaging to minimize.
- Common trap: Confusing it with "Systematic error," which is a consistent bias (e.g., equipment zero-offset) that cannot be reduced by averaging.
