### Front
Which particle categories are classified as fundamental according to the Standard Model, and which are composite?

### Back
- Key idea: Fundamental particles have no internal structure (quarks, leptons, gauge bosons, Higgs). Composite particles (hadrons/baryons) are made of quarks.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: To identify if a list of particles consists solely of elementary constituents.
- Common trap: Assuming protons, neutrons, and baryons are fundamental; always remember they are composite particles.
