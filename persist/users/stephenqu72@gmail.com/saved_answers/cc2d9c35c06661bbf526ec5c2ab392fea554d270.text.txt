To achieve full marks on an HSC Physics question like this, you must explicitly link the physics principles (Faraday’s and Lenz’s Laws) to the engineering solutions used to mitigate energy losses.

### Answer

Transformers operate based on **Faraday’s Law of Induction** and **Lenz’s Law**. An alternating current in the primary coil creates a changing magnetic flux, which is guided by a ferromagnetic core to the secondary coil, inducing an electromotive force (EMF) via mutual induction. Energy loss occurs primarily through heat. Key design features to improve efficiency include:

1.  **Laminated Cores:** The changing magnetic flux in the iron core induces circular currents known as **eddy currents** (a consequence of Faraday's Law). These cause significant resistive heating ($P = I^2R$). By constructing the core from thin, insulated laminated sheets, the path for these currents is restricted, significantly reducing energy loss.
2.  **Soft Iron Core:** To maximize magnetic flux linkage, a core with high magnetic permeability is used. A "soft" iron core can be easily magnetized and demagnetized, minimizing energy loss due to **magnetic hysteresis** (the energy required to realign magnetic domains in the core).
3.  **Low-resistance Copper Windings:** Coils are made of thick copper wire to minimize resistance ($R$). Since power loss due to resistance is given by $P = I^2R$, reducing resistance directly reduces the thermal energy lost during power transmission.
4.  **Flux Leakage Reduction:** The primary and secondary coils are often wound one on top of the other, or on a closed-loop core, to ensure that the magnetic flux produced by the primary coil is almost entirely captured by the secondary coil, ensuring efficient energy transfer.
