### Part (b): Controlled vs Uncontrolled Chain Reactions

To answer this, focus on the neutron economy and the role of the 3 neutrons produced in the reaction shown.

*   **Controlled Chain Reaction:** This occurs in a nuclear reactor. The reaction is regulated by control rods (e.g., boron or cadmium) that absorb excess neutrons. By ensuring that, on average, exactly one neutron from each fission event goes on to induce another fission, the energy release is kept constant and stable, allowing for steady power production.
*   **Uncontrolled Chain Reaction:** This occurs in a nuclear weapon. There is no moderation of the neutron flux. All three neutrons produced by the reaction are allowed to strike other U-235 nuclei, leading to an exponential increase in fission events. This causes an instantaneous, massive release of energy.
