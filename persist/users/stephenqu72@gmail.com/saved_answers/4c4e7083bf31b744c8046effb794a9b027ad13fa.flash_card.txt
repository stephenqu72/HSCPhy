### Front
Which of the following is NOT one of Kepler's three laws of planetary motion?
A) Law of Ellipses
B) Law of Harmonies ($T^2 \propto r^3$)
C) Orbits are parallel to the Sun's equatorial plane
D) Law of Equal Areas

### Back
- Key idea: Kepler’s laws describe the geometry, period, and speed of orbital motion, not the alignment with the Sun's rotation.
- Formula / law: 1st: Ellipses; 2nd: Equal areas in equal times; 3rd: $T^2/r^3 = k$.
- When to use it: Any question involving planetary or satellite orbital motion.
- Common trap: Assuming orbital planes must align with the central body’s equator (Kepler’s laws only define the shape and timing, not the spatial orientation of the orbital plane).
