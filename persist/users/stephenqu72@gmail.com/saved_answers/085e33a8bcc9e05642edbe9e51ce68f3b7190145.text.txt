### Explanation
To determine which statement is false, let's analyze each option based on the physics of a blackbody:

*   **(A) They absorb all electromagnetic radiation striking them:** This is the **definition** of a perfect blackbody. It reflects and transmits nothing, absorbing all incident radiation. This statement is **true**.
*   **(B) They only exist in theory:** In reality, no object is a *perfect* blackbody. While some objects (like stars or specialized laboratory cavities) approximate blackbody behavior very closely, a perfect blackbody is a theoretical idealization. This statement is **true**.
*   **(C) As the temperature of a blackbody increases the wavelength corresponding to peak intensity of the graph increases:** This contradicts **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which states:
    $$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$
    where $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength, $T$ is the absolute temperature, and $b$ is Wien's displacement constant. Because $T$ is in the denominator, as the temperature $T$ increases, the peak wavelength $\lambda_{max}$ must **decrease** (shifting toward the blue/ultraviolet end of the spectrum). Therefore, this statement is **false**.
*   **(D) They remit radiation over a range of frequencies:** Blackbody radiation curves (Planck's Law) show that a blackbody at a given temperature emits a continuous spectrum covering a broad range of frequencies/wavelengths, not just a single one. This statement is **true**.

Since the question asks for the false statement, **(C)** is the correct choice.
