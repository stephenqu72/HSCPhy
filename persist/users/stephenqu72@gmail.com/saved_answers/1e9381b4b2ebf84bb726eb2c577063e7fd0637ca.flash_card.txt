### Front
How do you calculate the mass of a central body ($M$) using orbital data ($r$ and $T$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Kepler’s Third Law relates the orbital period and radius to the central mass.
- **Formula:** $M = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{GT^2}$ where $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2/\text{kg}^2$.
- **When to use it:** When calculating the mass of a planet, star, or moon given the orbital characteristics of a satellite.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units to SI (meters and seconds) or forgetting to cube the radius ($r^3$) and square the period ($T^2$).
