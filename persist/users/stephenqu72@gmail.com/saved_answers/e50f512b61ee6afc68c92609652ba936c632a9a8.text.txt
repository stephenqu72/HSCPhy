### Explanation
To solve this question, you must understand the fundamental physical requirements for **diffraction** to occur. Diffraction is a wave phenomenon that occurs when waves encounter an obstacle or an aperture that is comparable in size to their wavelength.

1.  **X-rays:** We know that X-rays are part of the electromagnetic spectrum and are inherently waves. Because their wavelength is on the order of atomic spacing (angstroms), they are well-known for producing diffraction patterns when scattered by crystals.
2.  **Electrons:** Classically, electrons are considered "particles" with mass and charge. However, according to the **de Broglie hypothesis**, matter also exhibits wave-like properties, with a wavelength given by:
    $$\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$$
    where $h$ is Planck's constant and $p$ is the momentum of the particle.
3.  **The Comparison:** We can compare X-ray diffraction patterns (which occur because X-rays are waves) and electron diffraction patterns only if electrons also behave like waves. When electrons are accelerated to high velocities, their de Broglie wavelength becomes comparable to the spacing between atoms in a crystal lattice, allowing them to diffract just like X-rays.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **A:** While X-rays *can* exhibit particle-like properties (e.g., the photoelectric effect), this is irrelevant to why they produce *diffraction patterns*, which is a wave property.
*   **C:** This is factually incorrect; electrons are massive subatomic particles, while X-rays are massless photons.
*   **D:** While both can ionize matter, this has nothing to do with their ability to produce diffraction patterns.
