### Answer
The correct answer is **C. only transverse waves.**

### Explanation
1.  **Define Polarisation:** Polarization refers to the orientation of the oscillations of a wave. A wave is polarized if its oscillations occur in a single plane (e.g., vertical or horizontal) or in a specific pattern.
2.  **Transverse Waves:** In transverse waves, the particles (or fields, in the case of electromagnetic waves) oscillate in a direction perpendicular to the direction of energy propagation. Because these oscillations occur in a plane that is perpendicular to the wave's path, this plane can be restricted or "filtered" to a single orientation. Therefore, transverse waves can be polarized.
3.  **Longitudinal Waves:** In longitudinal waves (such as sound waves), the oscillations of the medium occur parallel to the direction of the wave's travel. Because the oscillations are along the same axis as the propagation, there is no "plane" of oscillation to filter. Consequently, longitudinal waves cannot be polarized.
4.  **Evaluate the options:**
    *   **A** is incorrect because sound (a longitudinal wave) cannot be polarized.
    *   **B** is incorrect because sound waves are longitudinal and lack the required directional geometry.
    *   **D** is incorrect because longitudinal waves are defined by parallel oscillation, which prohibits polarization.

By process of elimination and applying the physical principles of wave mechanics, polarization is a unique characteristic that distinguishes transverse waves from longitudinal waves.
