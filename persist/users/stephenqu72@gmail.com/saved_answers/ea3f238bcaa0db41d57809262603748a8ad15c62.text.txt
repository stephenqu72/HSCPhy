### Teacher's Solution and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Objective:** Show that $h_{max} = \frac{u^2 \sin^2 \theta}{2g}$.

**Step-by-step solution:**
1.  **Identify the vertical motion:** At the maximum height, the vertical component of the velocity, $v_y$, is $0$.
2.  **Use the kinematic equation:** We use $v_y^2 = u_y^2 + 2a_y \Delta y$.
3.  **Substitute components:**
    *   $v_y = 0$
    *   $u_y = u \sin \theta$
    *   $a_y = -g$
    *   $\Delta y = h_{max}$
4.  **Derive:**
    $$0 = (u \sin \theta)^2 - 2g h_{max}$$
    $$2g h_{max} = u^2 \sin^2 \theta$$
    $$h_{max} = \frac{u^2 \sin^2 \theta}{2g}$$

#### Part (b)
**Objective:** Determine if the projectile lands on, falls short of, or travels further than the $140.0\text{ m}$ target.

**Step-by-step solution:**
1.  **Identify the required formula:** The range $R$ of a projectile launched and landing at the same horizontal level is given by:
    $$R = \frac{u^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$
2.  **Plug in the values:**
    *   $u = 40.0\text{ m s}^{-1}$
    *   $\theta = 30^\circ$
    *   $g \approx 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$
3.  **Calculate the range:**
    $$R = \frac{40^2 \times \sin(2 \times 30^\circ)}{9.8}$$
    $$R = \frac{1600 \times \sin(60^\circ)}{9.8}$$
    $$R = \frac{1600 \times 0.866}{9.8} \approx 141.39\text{ m}$$
4.  **Conclusion:** Since the calculated range of $\approx 141.4\text{ m}$ is greater than the target distance of $140.0\text{ m}$, the projectile **travels further than the target**.
