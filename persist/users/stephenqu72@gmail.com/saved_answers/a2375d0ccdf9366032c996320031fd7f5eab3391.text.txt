To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of gravitation for a body of constant density, where the gravitational field strength $g$ follows different rules inside and outside the Earth.

### Part a: Finding the value of Y
The value $Y$ represents the gravitational field strength at the surface of the Earth ($r = R_E$). This is equivalent to the standard acceleration due to gravity, $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}$ (or $\text{N kg}^{-1}$).
**Answer: $9.8 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$**

---

### Part b: Why $g = 0$ at the centre
At the centre of the Earth, the gravitational field strength is zero because of the shell theorem and symmetry. For a sphere of constant density, any point at a distance $r$ from the centre is only affected by the mass contained *within* a sphere of radius $r$. As $r \to 0$, the enclosed mass $M_{enclosed} \to 0$. Furthermore, at the exact centre, the gravitational pull from the mass of the Earth in every direction is equal and opposite, causing all gravitational force vectors to cancel out.

---

### Part c: Increase in potential energy from centre to surface
For a sphere of constant density, the gravitational field strength inside the Earth ($r < R_E$) is given by $g(r) = \frac{g_{surface} \cdot r}{R_E}$. 
The potential energy difference is the work done against the gravitational field:
$$\Delta U = \int_{0}^{R_E} m \cdot g(r) \, dr = \int_{0}^{R_E} m \frac{g_{surface}}{R_E} \cdot r \, dr$$
$$\Delta U = \frac{m \cdot g_{surface}}{R_E} \left[ \frac{r^2}{2} \right]_{0}^{R_E} = \frac{m \cdot g_{surface} \cdot R_E}{2}$$

Given $m = 75 \text{ kg}$, $g_{surface} = 9.8 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$, and $R_E = 6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$:
$$\Delta U = \frac{75 \times 9.8 \times 6.37 \times 10^6}{2}$$
$$\Delta U \approx 2.34 \times 10^9 \text{ J}$$
