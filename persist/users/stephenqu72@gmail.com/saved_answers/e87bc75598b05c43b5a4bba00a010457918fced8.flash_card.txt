### Front
In Uniform Circular Motion, what is the relationship between the velocity vector and the centripetal acceleration vector?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) is always directed toward the center of the circular path and is perpendicular to the instantaneous velocity vector.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$; the acceleration vector is always at a $90^\circ$ angle to the velocity vector.
- **When to use it:** Whenever an object moves at a constant speed along a curved path to identify the direction of the net force.
- **Common trap:** Assuming acceleration is zero because speed is constant. Remember: velocity is a vector, so a change in *direction* equals acceleration.
