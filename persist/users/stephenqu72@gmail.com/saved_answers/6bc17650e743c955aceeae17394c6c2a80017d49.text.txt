The correct answer is **b) Inversely Proportional**.

### Explanation:
To determine the relationship between centripetal force ($F_c$) and the radius of curvature ($r$), we refer to the standard formula for centripetal force:

$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Where:
*   $m$ is the mass of the object.
*   $v$ is the velocity of the object.
*   $r$ is the radius of the circular path.

By examining this equation, we can see that the centripetal force is inversely proportional to the radius of the circle, assuming the mass and the velocity of the object remain constant. Mathematically, this is expressed as:

$$F_c \propto \frac{1}{r}$$

This means that if the radius of the path increases, the force required to keep the object in that circular motion decreases (and vice versa). Therefore, the relationship is inversely proportional.
