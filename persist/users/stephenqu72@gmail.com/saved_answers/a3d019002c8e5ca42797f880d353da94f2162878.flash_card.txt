### Front
Define the electron-volt (eV) and explain why it is the preferred unit in nuclear and particle physics.

### Back
- Key idea: Unit of energy representing the kinetic energy gained by an electron accelerated through a potential difference of 1V.
- Formula / constant: $1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$.
- When to use it: When dealing with extremely small energy values at the atomic/subatomic scale to avoid cumbersome scientific notation.
- Common trap: Confusing eV with Volts (a measure of potential difference) or assuming it is used for large-scale energy values.
