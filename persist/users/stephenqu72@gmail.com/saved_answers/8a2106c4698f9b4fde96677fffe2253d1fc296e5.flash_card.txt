### Front
How does apparent weight ($W$) compare in an elevator that is stationary, accelerating upwards, and accelerating downwards?

### Back
- Key idea: Apparent weight changes based on whether acceleration opposes or assists gravity.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F_{net} = ma \implies W_D < W_S < W_U$ (where $W_U = m(g+a)$ and $W_D = m(g-a)$); No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Problems involving "apparent weight," "feeling heavier/lighter," or tension in accelerating lifts.
- Common trap: Confusing the direction of **motion** with the direction of **acceleration** (e.g., a lift moving down can still be accelerating upwards).
