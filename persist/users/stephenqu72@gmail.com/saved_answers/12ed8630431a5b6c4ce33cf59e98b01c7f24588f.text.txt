To find the speed of the tips of the propeller, we need to calculate the linear velocity of an object moving in a circular path.

### 1. Identify the given variables:
*   Radius ($r$) = $8\text{ m}$ (the length of the blade)
*   Period ($T$) = $0.32\text{ s}$

### 2. Choose the relevant formula:
The speed ($v$) of an object moving in a circle is the circumference ($2\pi r$) divided by the time it takes to complete one revolution (the period, $T$):
$$v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$$

### 3. Perform the calculation:
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$v = \frac{2 \times \pi \times 8}{0.32}$$
$$v = \frac{16\pi}{0.32}$$
$$v \approx \frac{50.265}{0.32}$$
$$v \approx 157.08\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

Rounding to the appropriate number of significant figures (consistent with the provided options), we get $157\text{ m s}^{-1}$.

Comparing this to the given options:
(A) $79\text{ m s}^{-1}$
(B) $155\text{ m s}^{-1}$
(C) $157\text{ m s}^{-1}$
(D) $2035\text{ m s}^{-1}$

The correct answer is **(C)**.
