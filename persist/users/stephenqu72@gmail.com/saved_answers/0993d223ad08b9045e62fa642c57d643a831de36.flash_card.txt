### Front
How does mass ($M$) and radius ($r$) affect the escape velocity ($v_e$) of a planet?

### Back
- **Key idea:** $v_e$ decreases as mass decreases and radius increases.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$
- **When to use it:** To compare gravitational influence or calculate the minimum speed required to escape a celestial body's gravitational field.
- **Common trap:** Confusing direct proportionality with mass ($\sqrt{M}$) and inverse proportionality with radius ($1/\sqrt{r}$). Always check both variables simultaneously.
