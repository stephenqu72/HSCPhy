### Front
How do you calculate the initial vertical component of a projectile's velocity ($v_y$) given an initial velocity $v$ at an angle $\theta$ above the horizontal?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Resolution of vectors into perpendicular components using trigonometry.
- **Formula:** $v_y = v \sin \theta$
- **When to use it:** Whenever analyzing projectile motion to determine the initial vertical velocity for vertical displacement or time-of-flight equations.
- **Common trap:** Using $\cos$ instead of $\sin$ (remember: $\sin$ for vertical/opposite, $\cos$ for horizontal/adjacent).
