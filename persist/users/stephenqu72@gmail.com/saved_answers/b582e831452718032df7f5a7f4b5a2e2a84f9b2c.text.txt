### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must identify the definition of centripetal acceleration and how it applies to the point $S$ on the rotating tire.

1.  **Identify the Physics Principle**: The magnitude of centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) for an object moving in a circle is given by the formula:
    $$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} = r\omega^2$$
    where:
    *   $v$ is the linear velocity of the point.
    *   $r$ is the radius of the circular path.
    *   $\omega$ is the angular velocity.

2.  **Analyze the Motion**: The problem states that the tire is rotating at a **constant rate**. This means the angular velocity ($\omega$) is constant.
    *   Point $S$ is at a fixed distance from the center of the tire. Therefore, the radius ($r$) of its circular path is constant.
    *   Since both $r$ and $\omega$ are constant, the centripetal acceleration $a_c = r\omega^2$ must also remain constant throughout the entire revolution.

3.  **Evaluate the Graphs**:
    *   Graph (A) shows linear growth, which is incorrect.
    *   Graph (B) shows a horizontal line, representing a constant value over time. This matches our derivation that $a_c$ is constant.
    *   Graphs (C) and (D) show changing values, which would only occur if the tire were speeding up or slowing down.

Since the rotation rate is constant, the magnitude of the centripetal acceleration does not change as the tire rotates. Thus, graph (B) is the correct representation.
