To determine the force between two current-carrying wires, we use the formula for the magnitude of the force per unit length:

$$F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 l}{2\pi r}$$

Since $\mu_0$ and $2\pi$ are constants, the magnitude of the force is proportional to:
$$F \propto \frac{I_1 I_2 l}{r}$$

Let's calculate the relative strength for each option:

*   **(A)** $I_1 = I, I_2 = 2I, l = l, r = r$:
    $$F_A \propto \frac{(I)(2I)(l)}{r} = 2 \left( \frac{I^2 l}{r} \right)$$
*   **(B)** $I_1 = I, I_2 = I, l = l, r = 2r$:
    $$F_B \propto \frac{(I)(I)(l)}{2r} = 0.5 \left( \frac{I^2 l}{r} \right)$$
*   **(C)** $I_1 = I, I_2 = I, l = 2l, r = 2r$:
    $$F_C \propto \frac{(I)(I)(2l)}{2r} = 1 \left( \frac{I^2 l}{r} \right)$$
*   **(D)** $I_1 = 2I, I_2 = 2I, l = l, r = 2r$:
    $$F_D \propto \frac{(2I)(2I)(l)}{2r} = \frac{4I^2 l}{2r} = 2 \left( \frac{I^2 l}{r} \right)$$

Comparing the values:
1. Strongest: Options (A) and (D) are equal with a value of $2$.
2. Second strongest: Option (C) with a value of $1$.
3. Weakest: Option (B) with a value of $0.5$.

Since (A) and (D) are tied for the strongest, the next strongest value is (C).

**Answer:** (C)
