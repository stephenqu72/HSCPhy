### Front
How do the results of mechanical experiments compare between an inertial frame (at rest or uniform motion) and a non-inertial frame (accelerating)?

### Back
- Key idea: Experiments yield identical results in all **inertial frames** (rest or constant velocity), but differ in **non-inertial frames** due to the presence of fictitious forces.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Principle of Galilean Relativity.
- When to use it: Comparing physical laws or experimental outcomes across different moving reference frames.
- Common trap: Assuming an accelerating frame is an inertial frame, or failing to recognize that "uniform motion" and "at rest" are physically equivalent in terms of Newtonian mechanics.
