### Answer
A **real experiment** involves physical apparatus, empirical data collection, and testing in a controlled environment to verify a hypothesis. In contrast, a **thought experiment** (*Gedankenexperiment*) is a theoretical scenario performed in the mind to explore the logical consequences of a scientific principle.

Einstein utilized thought experiments because the phenomena of special relativity, such as time dilation and length contraction, involve objects moving at speeds approaching the speed of light ($c$), which were technologically impossible to achieve in his time. Thought experiments allowed him to isolate specific physical principles and test their logical consistency without the limitations of contemporary technology.

**Example:** In Einstein’s **"light clock"** thought experiment, he imagined a photon bouncing between two mirrors on a moving train. By comparing how an observer on the train and a stationary observer on the platform perceive the path of the light, he was able to logically deduce that time must pass at different rates for different observers, thus establishing the concept of time dilation.
