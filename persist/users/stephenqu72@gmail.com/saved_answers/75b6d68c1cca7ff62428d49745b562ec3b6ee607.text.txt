To determine which diagram best shows the diffraction of plane wavefronts at a single slit, we must apply the principles of Huygens' Principle.

### Explanation
1.  **Huygens' Principle:** Every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary spherical wavelets. When plane waves reach a slit, the slit acts as a point source (or a collection of point sources) for these secondary wavelets.
2.  **Diffraction:** As the waves pass through the opening, they spread out into the region behind the barrier. This bending or "spreading" of the wavefronts turns the initially flat (plane) wavefronts into curved (circular or semi-circular) wavefronts.
3.  **Analysis of Options:**
    *   **A:** Shows the wavefronts beginning to curve immediately at the edges of the slit, which is characteristic of diffraction as the waves emerge from the constrained opening into the wider space.
    *   **B:** Shows the wavefronts remaining relatively flat within the slit, which does not accurately represent the spreading effect of diffraction.
    *   **C:** Shows the wavefronts originating from a point *before* the slit or in a way that suggests a non-physical expansion.
    *   **D:** Shows a distorted pattern that implies reflection or a different wave behavior rather than standard single-slit diffraction.

Diagram **A** most accurately depicts the physical phenomenon where plane wavefronts encounter a slit and diffract into circular wavefronts propagating outwards from the aperture.

**Answer:** A
