To solve this problem, we apply **Lenz’s Law**, which states that the direction of an induced current is such that it creates a magnetic field that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Analyze the Change in Flux:** As the North pole of the magnet moves upwards towards the stationary horizontal coil, the magnetic flux through the coil increases in the upward direction.
2.  **Determine the Opposing Field:** According to Lenz's Law, the coil will attempt to oppose this increase in upward magnetic flux. To do this, the coil must create its own induced magnetic field pointing **downwards**.
3.  **Use the Right-Hand Grip Rule:** Point your right thumb in the direction of the induced magnetic field (downwards, away from you if you are looking from above). Your fingers will curl in the direction of the induced current.
4.  **Visualize the Current:** When looking at the coil from above, your fingers curl in a **clockwise** direction. This clockwise current creates a South pole on the top face of the coil, which is necessary to repel the approaching North pole of the magnet.

Comparing this to the given options:
*   (A) Anti-clockwise: Incorrect.
*   (B) Clockwise: Correct.
*   (C) No induced current: Incorrect, as there is a change in magnetic flux.
*   (D) Perpendicular to the movement: Incorrect; the current flows around the loop of the coil.

**Correct Option: (B)**
