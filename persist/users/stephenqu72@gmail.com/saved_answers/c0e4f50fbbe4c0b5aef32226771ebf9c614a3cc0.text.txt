To solve this problem, we use **Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion**, which states that for objects orbiting the same central body, the square of the orbital period ($T$) is directly proportional to the cube of the orbital radius ($r$):

$$\frac{r_1^3}{T_1^2} = \frac{r_2^3}{T_2^2}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Period of Io ($T_1$) = $1.77 \text{ days}$
*   Period of Europa ($T_2$) = $3.55 \text{ days}$
*   Radius of Europa ($r_2$) = $671,000 \text{ km}$
*   Radius of Io ($r_1$) = $?$

**2. Rearrange the formula to solve for $r_1$:**
$$\frac{r_1^3}{T_1^2} = \frac{r_2^3}{T_2^2}$$
$$r_1^3 = r_2^3 \times \frac{T_1^2}{T_2^2}$$
$$r_1 = \sqrt[3]{r_2^3 \times \left( \frac{T_1}{T_2} \right)^2}$$
Alternatively, simply:
$$r_1 = r_2 \times \left( \frac{T_1}{T_2} \right)^{2/3}$$

**3. Substitute the values into the equation:**
$$r_1 = 671,000 \times \left( \frac{1.77}{3.55} \right)^{2/3}$$

**4. Perform the calculation:**
*   First, find the ratio of the periods: $\frac{1.77}{3.55} \approx 0.49859$
*   Raise this to the power of $2/3$: $(0.49859)^{0.6667} \approx 0.6288$
*   Multiply by the radius of Europa: $671,000 \times 0.6288 \approx 421,909 \text{ km}$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches **(B)**.

The orbital radius of Io is approximately **421,909 km**.
