### Front
If a parent nuclide ${}^{A}_{Z}X$ undergoes an alpha decay followed by a beta-minus decay, versus a beta-minus decay followed by an alpha decay, how do the mass numbers of the final products compare?

### Back
- Key idea: The order of $\alpha$ and $\beta^-$ decay does not change the final mass number or atomic number of the resulting daughter product.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: To determine final products in decay chains; conservation of mass number ($A$) and atomic number ($Z$).
- Common trap: Assuming the final products are different isotopes; they are actually the same nuclide (isobars relative to the parent with $A-4$).
