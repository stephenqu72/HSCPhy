### Explanation
To determine the variables, we must look at how the experiment is designed:

1.  **Independent Variable:** This is the factor that the experimenter intentionally changes or manipulates. The prompt explicitly states, "changing only the length of the pendulum." Therefore, the **pendulum length** is the independent variable.
2.  **Dependent Variable:** This is the factor that is measured or observed as a result of changing the independent variable. The students measure the "time for five oscillations," which is used to calculate the period ($T$) of the pendulum. As the length ($l$) changes according to the formula $$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{l}{g}}$$ the period ($T$) will change. Thus, the **period of swing** is the dependent variable.
3.  **Controlled Variables:** These are the factors kept constant to ensure a fair test so they do not affect the results. The prompt explicitly states the students use a "constant mass and amplitude of swing." Therefore, **mass of the bob** and **amplitude of the swing** are the controlled variables.

Evaluating the options:
*   **A** is incorrect because it lists the independent and controlled variables incorrectly.
*   **B** is incorrect because the period is the result, not the variable being changed.
*   **C correctly identifies all three:** Length is changed, Period is measured, and Mass/Amplitude are kept constant.
*   **D** is incorrect because amplitude is stated as a controlled variable in the prompt, not a dependent variable.
