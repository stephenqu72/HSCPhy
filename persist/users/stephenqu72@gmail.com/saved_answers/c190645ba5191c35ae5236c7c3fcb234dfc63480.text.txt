To solve this problem, we need to calculate the magnetic force of repulsion between the two parallel wires and use Newton’s Second Law to determine the mass of the trolley.

### 1. Identify the Physics Principles
*   **Magnetic Force between two parallel wires:** The force per unit length $F/L$ between two long parallel wires separated by distance $r$ is given by:
    $$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$
*   **Newton’s Second Law:** $F = ma$, which implies $m = \frac{F}{a}$.

### 2. Given Data
*   Length of wires ($L$) = $2.00\text{ m}$
*   Current in first wire ($I_1$) = $25.0\text{ A}$
*   Current in second wire ($I_2$) = $50.0\text{ A}$
*   Separation distance ($r$) = $2.00\text{ mm} = 2.00 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$
*   Acceleration ($a$) = $0.200\text{ m s}^{-2}$
*   Permeability of free space ($\mu_0$) = $4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T m A}^{-1}$

### 3. Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step A: Calculate the force ($F$)**
Using the formula for magnetic force:
$$F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 L}{2\pi r}$$
Substitute the known values:
$$F = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times 25.0 \times 50.0 \times 2.00}{2\pi \times (2.00 \times 10^{-3})}$$
$$F = \frac{(2 \times 10^{-7}) \times 1250 \times 2.00}{2.00 \times 10^{-3}}$$
$$F = \frac{5 \times 10^{-4}}{2 \times 10^{-3}} = 0.25\text{ N}$$

**Step B: Calculate the mass ($m$)**
Using $F = ma$:
$$m = \frac{F}{a}$$
$$m = \frac{0.25\text{ N}}{0.200\text{ m s}^{-2}}$$
$$m = 1.25\text{ kg}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct choice is **(B)**.
