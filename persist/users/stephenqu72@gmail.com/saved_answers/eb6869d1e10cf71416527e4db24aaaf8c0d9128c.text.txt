To determine which action will increase the induced current in the ring, we must refer to **Faraday’s Law of Induction**.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) Increasing the speed of the magnet**.

### Explanation
Faraday’s Law of Induction states that the magnitude of the induced electromotive force (EMF), $\varepsilon$, in a circuit is directly proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux, $\Phi_B$, through that circuit:

$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi_B}{\Delta t}$$

1.  **Understanding Magnetic Flux:** Magnetic flux is essentially the measure of the number of magnetic field lines passing through the area of the wire ring. As the bar magnet moves closer to the ring, the magnetic field strength inside the ring increases, leading to a change in magnetic flux ($\Delta \Phi_B$).
2.  **Rate of Change:** The induced current $I$ is related to the EMF by Ohm's Law ($I = \frac{\varepsilon}{R}$). Therefore, to increase the current, we must increase the magnitude of the induced EMF. According to the formula, this is achieved by increasing the rate of change of flux ($\frac{\Delta \Phi_B}{\Delta t}$).
3.  **Applying the logic:**
    *   **(A) Increasing the speed of the magnet:** By moving the magnet faster, the magnetic flux through the loop changes in a shorter amount of time ($\Delta t$ decreases). This increases the rate $\frac{\Delta \Phi_B}{\Delta t}$, thereby increasing the induced EMF and the resulting current.
    *   **(B) Rotating the wire ring:** This would change the orientation, but unless the rate of flux change is specifically increased, it does not reliably increase current.
    *   **(C) Reversing the polarity:** This would only reverse the direction of the induced current (Lenz's Law), not change its magnitude.
    *   **(D) Moving the magnet away:** This would induce a current in the opposite direction but would not necessarily increase its magnitude compared to the original motion.

Thus, increasing the speed is the only option that directly increases the rate of change of magnetic flux.
