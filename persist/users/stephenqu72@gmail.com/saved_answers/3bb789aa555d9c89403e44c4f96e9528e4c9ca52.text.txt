To solve this problem, we use the photoelectric effect equations. 

**Given data:**
- Frequency of incident light, $f = 8.0 \times 10^{15} \text{ Hz}$
- Maximum kinetic energy, $E_{k(\text{max})} = 7.0 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$
- Planck's constant, $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$
- Elementary charge, $e = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$

---

### a) The potential required to stop electrons ($V_s$)
The stopping potential $V_s$ is defined by the work done by the electric field to stop the fastest electrons: $E_{k(\text{max})} = eV_s$.

$$V_s = \frac{E_{k(\text{max})}}{e} = \frac{7.0 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}}{1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}} \approx 4.37 \text{ V}$$

### b) The work function ($\Phi$)
Using Einstein’s photoelectric equation, $E_{k(\text{max})} = hf - \Phi$, we rearrange to solve for the work function $\Phi$:

$$\Phi = hf - E_{k(\text{max})}$$
$$\Phi = (6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s})(8.0 \times 10^{15} \text{ Hz}) - 7.0 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$
$$\Phi = 5.3008 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J} - 0.7 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J} = 4.6008 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J}$$
Rounding to two significant figures, $\Phi \approx 4.6 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J}$.

### c) The threshold frequency ($f_0$)
The threshold frequency is the frequency at which the energy of a photon equals the work function ($\Phi = hf_0$):

$$f_0 = \frac{\Phi}{h} = \frac{4.6008 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J}}{6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}} \approx 6.94 \times 10^{15} \text{ Hz}$$

---

### Summary of steps:
1. **For (a):** Recognize that kinetic energy in Joules is equivalent to the stopping potential multiplied by the elementary charge ($eV_s$). Divide the energy by the charge of an electron.
2. **For (b):** Apply the conservation of energy principle: energy of incident photon ($hf$) equals the sum of the work function ($\Phi$) and the maximum kinetic energy. Subtract $E_k$ from $hf$.
3. **For (c):** Use the definition of work function as the minimum energy required to eject an electron, which corresponds to the threshold frequency $f_0 = \Phi/h$.
