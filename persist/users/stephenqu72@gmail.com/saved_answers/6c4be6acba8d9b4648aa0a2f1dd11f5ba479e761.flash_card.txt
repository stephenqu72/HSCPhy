### Front
What is the mathematical condition required for an object to be in **static equilibrium**?

### Back
- Key idea: The vector sum of all external forces acting on the object must be zero, meaning no net acceleration occurs.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\sum F_x = 0$ and $\sum F_y = 0$ (or $\Sigma \vec{F} = 0$)
- When to use it: To calculate an unknown force magnitude or direction in a system of concurrent forces at rest.
- Common trap: Forgetting to assign positive/negative signs to directions (e.g., West as $-x$, South as $-y$) before summing components.
