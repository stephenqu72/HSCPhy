### Question 12 Solution
To find the Lorentz factor $\gamma$, we use the formula:
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}$$

**Given:**
- $v = 2.50 \times 10^8 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
- $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m s}^{-1}$

**Calculation:**
1. Calculate the ratio $\frac{v}{c}$:
$$\frac{v}{c} = \frac{2.50 \times 10^8}{3.00 \times 10^8} \approx 0.8333$$
2. Square the ratio:
$$\left(\frac{v}{c}\right)^2 \approx 0.6944$$
3. Calculate $1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$:
$$1 - 0.6944 = 0.3056$$
4. Calculate the square root:
$$\sqrt{0.3056} \approx 0.5528$$
5. Find the reciprocal:
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{0.5528} \approx 1.809$$

Rounding to three significant figures, we get **1.81**. The correct answer is **A**.

### Question 13 Solution
To find the power generated by the conversion of mass to energy, we use Einstein's mass-energy equivalence principle combined with the definition of power ($P = \frac{E}{t}$).

**Given:**
- Rate of mass conversion $\frac{\Delta m}{\Delta t} = 6.6 \times 10^9 \text{ kg s}^{-1}$
- $E = mc^2$, so $P = \frac{\Delta m}{\Delta t} c^2$
- $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m s}^{-1}$

**Calculation:**
$$P = (6.6 \times 10^9) \times (3.00 \times 10^8)^2$$
$$P = (6.6 \times 10^9) \times (9.00 \times 10^{16})$$
$$P = 59.4 \times 10^{25} \text{ W}$$
$$P \approx 5.94 \times 10^{26} \text{ W}$$

This is closest to **6.0 \times 10^{26} W**. Note that power is measured in Watts (W), eliminating options B and D. The correct answer is **C**.
