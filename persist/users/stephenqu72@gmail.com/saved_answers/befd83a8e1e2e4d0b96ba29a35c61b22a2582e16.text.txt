To determine the temperature of the blackbody, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which relates the absolute temperature $T$ of a blackbody to the wavelength $\lambda_{max}$ at which it emits the maximum intensity of radiation.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the peak wavelength from the graph:**
    Looking at the provided blackbody radiation curve, the peak intensity occurs at a wavelength of $600\text{ nm}$. Therefore, $\lambda_{max} = 600\text{ nm} = 600 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$.

2.  **Recall Wien’s Displacement Law:**
    The law is given by the formula:
    $$\lambda_{max} \cdot T = b$$
    where $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$.

3.  **Calculate the temperature:**
    Rearrange the formula to solve for $T$:
    $$T = \frac{b}{\lambda_{max}}$$
    $$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}\cdot\text{K}}{600 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}}$$
    $$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{6.0 \times 10^{-7}}\text{ K}$$
    $$T \approx 0.483 \times 10^{4}\text{ K}$$
    $$T \approx 4830\text{ K}$$

4.  **Conclusion:**
    Comparing our result to the given options, $4830\text{ K}$ matches option (A).

**Correct Option: (A)**
