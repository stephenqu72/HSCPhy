To determine the temperature of the object, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which describes the relationship between the temperature of a blackbody and the wavelength at which it emits its peak intensity.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$):**
    Looking at the provided graph, the peak of the curve occurs at a wavelength of approximately $600\text{ nm}$. Therefore, $\lambda_{max} = 600 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}$.

2.  **Apply Wien’s Displacement Law:**
    The formula is:
    $$\lambda_{max} T = b$$
    where:
    - $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength in meters ($m$)
    - $T$ is the absolute temperature in Kelvin ($K$)
    - $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}\cdot K$

3.  **Calculate the Temperature:**
    Rearranging the formula to solve for $T$:
    $$T = \frac{b}{\lambda_{max}}$$
    $$T = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}\cdot K}{600 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}}$$
    $$T \approx 4830\text{ K}$$

4.  **Conclusion:**
    Comparing this result to the given options, $4830\text{ K}$ matches option (A).

**Answer:** (A)
