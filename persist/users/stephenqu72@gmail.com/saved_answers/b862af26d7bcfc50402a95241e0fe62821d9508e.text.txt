### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to identify the standard components of a Direct Current (DC) motor and distinguish them from those of an Alternating Current (AC) generator or motor:

1.  **Magnets (Option A):** DC motors require a stationary magnetic field (the stator) to exert a force on the current-carrying coil.
2.  **Coil (Option B):** The coil (or armature) is the rotating part of the motor that carries the current and experiences the magnetic force, causing it to turn.
3.  **Slip-ring commutator (Option C):** This is the incorrect component. A **DC motor** uses a **split-ring commutator** (a single ring split into two halves). The split-ring commutator is essential in a DC motor because it reverses the direction of the current every half-turn, ensuring the motor continues to spin in the same direction. A "slip-ring" is used in **AC generators (alternators)** to maintain a continuous connection without reversing the current.
4.  **Brushes (Option D):** These are carbon blocks used in a DC motor to maintain electrical contact with the rotating split-ring commutator, allowing current to flow from the external circuit into the armature.

Because a DC motor requires a **split-ring** commutator, a "slip-ring commutator" is not a component of a DC motor.
