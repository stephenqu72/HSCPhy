### Front
How does the orbital velocity ($v$) of a satellite change as its orbital radius ($r$) increases?

### Back
- Key idea: Orbital velocity is inversely proportional to the square root of the orbital radius ($v \propto 1/\sqrt{r}$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \sqrt{GM/r}$
- When to use it: To compare the speeds of two objects orbiting the same central mass at different radii.
- Common trap: Forgetting the square root; assuming velocity is inversely proportional to $r$ rather than $\sqrt{r}$.
