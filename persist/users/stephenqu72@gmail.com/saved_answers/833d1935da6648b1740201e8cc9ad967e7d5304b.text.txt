### Answer
Transformers operate based on **Faraday’s Law of Induction** and **Lenz’s Law**. An alternating current in the primary coil creates a changing magnetic flux, which is guided by a soft iron core to the secondary coil, inducing an EMF. To maximize efficiency, the following design features are used to mitigate energy losses:

1.  **Resistance in coils (Joule heating):** Current flowing through copper wires leads to $P = I^2R$ power loss as heat.
    *   **Design solution:** Use low-resistance, thick copper wires to minimize $R$.
2.  **Eddy currents in the core:** The changing magnetic flux induces circular currents (eddy currents) in the conducting iron core, causing heating.
    *   **Design solution:** Use a **laminated core** (sheets of iron insulated by lacquer). This restricts the path of eddy currents, significantly reducing the resistive heating.
3.  **Magnetic flux leakage:** Not all flux produced by the primary coil links with the secondary coil.
    *   **Design solution:** Use a **soft iron core** that is continuous (often a closed-loop shape) to provide a path of low magnetic reluctance, ensuring efficient "coupling" of the magnetic flux.
4.  **Hysteresis loss:** The continuous reversing of the magnetic field in the core causes domains to realign, generating heat.
    *   **Design solution:** Use a **soft magnetic material** (like silicon steel) which has a narrow hysteresis loop, meaning less energy is wasted flipping magnetic domains.
