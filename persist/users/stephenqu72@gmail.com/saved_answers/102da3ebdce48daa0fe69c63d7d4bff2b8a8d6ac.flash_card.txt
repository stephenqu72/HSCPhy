### Front
How do you calculate the gravitational force ($F_g$) acting on a satellite in a stable circular orbit if only its mass ($m$), radius ($r$), and period ($T$) are known?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational force provides the centripetal force ($F_g = F_c$).
- Formula: $F_g = \frac{4\pi^2 mr}{T^2}$
- When to use it: When solving orbital mechanics problems where velocity ($v$) is not explicitly provided.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert the orbital period ($T$) into SI units (seconds).
