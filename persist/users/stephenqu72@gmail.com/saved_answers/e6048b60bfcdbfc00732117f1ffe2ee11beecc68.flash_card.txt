### Front
What are the changes to the atomic number ($Z$), mass number ($A$), and nucleon composition during beta-minus ($\beta^-$) decay?

### Back
- Key idea: A neutron converts into a proton, emitting an electron and an electron antineutrino.
- Formula / law: $n \rightarrow p + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e$ (Atomic number $Z \rightarrow Z+1$; Mass number $A$ remains constant).
- When to use it: To determine the daughter nucleus or nucleon changes following beta emission.
- Common trap: Mistaking it for $\beta^+$ decay (where $Z$ decreases by 1) or alpha decay (where $A$ decreases by 4).
