### Front
How do you derive the expression for the maximum height ($h_{max}$) of a projectile, and what is the formula for the horizontal range ($R$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Use vertical kinematic equations where $v_y = 0$ at peak height; use horizontal velocity and total time of flight for range.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: 
  $h_{max} = \frac{u^2 \sin^2 \theta}{2g}$
  $R = \frac{u^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$
- When to use it: $h_{max}$ for vertical displacement at peak; $R$ for horizontal distance on level ground.
- Common trap: The range formula $R = \frac{u^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$ only applies when the launch and landing heights are the same; otherwise, use $d_x = v_x t$.
