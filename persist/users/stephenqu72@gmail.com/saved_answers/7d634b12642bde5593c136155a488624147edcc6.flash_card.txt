### Front
How do you calculate the energy released during a nuclear reaction given the mass defect?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Mass-energy equivalence ($E=mc^2$); mass lost in a reaction is converted into kinetic energy/radiation.
- **Formula / law / constant:** $\Delta E = (\Delta m)c^2$; $1 \text{ amu} = 931.5 \text{ MeV}$; $1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$.
- **When to use it:** Whenever a nuclear reaction (fusion/fission/decay) occurs and the masses of reactants and products are provided.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to calculate the "mass defect" as the absolute difference (Reactants - Products) and confusing the units (ensure amu is converted to MeV before Joules).
