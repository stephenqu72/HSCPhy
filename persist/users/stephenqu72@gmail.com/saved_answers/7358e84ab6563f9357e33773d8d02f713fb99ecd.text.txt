1.  **Gravitational Potential Energy ($U$):** 
    The formula for gravitational potential energy is:
    $$U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$$
    Where $r$ is the distance between the center of the Earth and the Moon. 
    *   At **perigee** ($r = 362,570 \text{ km}$), $r$ is at its **minimum**. Since the potential energy is negative, a smaller distance magnitude means the value is "most negative" (a minimum).
    *   At **apogee** ($r = 405,410 \text{ km}$), $r$ is at its **maximum**. The value is "least negative" (a maximum).

2.  **Kinetic Energy ($K$) and Velocity ($v$):**
    In an elliptical orbit, the total mechanical energy ($E = K + U$) is conserved. 
    *   Since the total energy $E$ is constant, when the Moon is at its minimum distance (perigee), its gravitational potential energy is at its minimum. To conserve energy, the **kinetic energy must be at its maximum**. 
    *   Because $K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$, a maximum kinetic energy corresponds to a **maximum orbital velocity**.
