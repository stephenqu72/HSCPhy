### Front
What is the standard procedure to calculate power loss in a transmission line when the voltage drop ($\Delta V$) and total resistance ($R$) are known?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Power dissipated as heat due to the resistance of the transmission cables.
- **Formula:** $P_{loss} = \frac{(\Delta V)^2}{R}$ or $P_{loss} = I^2R$ (where $I = \frac{\Delta V}{R}$).
- **When to use it:** When calculating energy efficiency in electrical distribution systems.
- **Common trap:** Confusing the total voltage generated ($V_{gen}$) with the voltage drop ($\Delta V$). Ensure you only use $\Delta V$ when calculating loss across the line.
