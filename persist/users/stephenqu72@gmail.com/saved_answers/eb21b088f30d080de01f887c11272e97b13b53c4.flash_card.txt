### Front
How does the direction of force in projectile motion compare to uniform circular motion?

### Back
- **Key idea:** In projectile motion, the force (gravity) acts in a constant downward direction. In uniform circular motion, the force (centripetal) is dynamic, always directed toward the center and perpendicular to the instantaneous velocity.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** When asked to contrast the kinematics and force vectors of parabolic vs. circular trajectories.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the force in circular motion is constant in direction; it is only constant in *magnitude* (if speed is uniform), not direction.
