To solve this problem, we need to derive the expression for total mechanical energy $E$ of a mass $m$ in a stable circular orbit around a central mass $M$ at a radius $r$.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the components of total energy:**
The total energy $E$ is the sum of kinetic energy $K$ and gravitational potential energy $U$:
$$E = K + U$$
Where:
- $K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$
- $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$

**2. Use the condition for circular orbit:**
For a satellite to remain in a stable circular orbit, the centripetal force must be provided by the gravitational force:
$$F_c = F_g$$
$$\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$$

Multiplying both sides by $r$, we find an expression for $mv^2$:
$$mv^2 = \frac{GMm}{r}$$

**3. Express kinetic energy in terms of gravitational parameters:**
Divide the expression above by 2 to get $K$:
$$K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{GMm}{2r}$$

**4. Combine into the total energy equation:**
Now substitute $K$ and $U$ back into the total energy equation:
$$E = \left(\frac{GMm}{2r}\right) + \left(-\frac{GMm}{r}\right)$$

To add these, find a common denominator:
$$E = \frac{GMm}{2r} - \frac{2GMm}{2r}$$
$$E = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$$

This confirms the required result.
