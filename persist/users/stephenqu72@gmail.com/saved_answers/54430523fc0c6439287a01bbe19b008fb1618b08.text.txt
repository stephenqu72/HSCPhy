### Answer
Magnetic braking occurs when a conductor moves through a non-uniform magnetic field or relative to a magnetic field. According to Faraday’s Law, this changing magnetic flux induces an Electromotive Force (EMF) in the conductor. This induced EMF drives eddy currents within the conductor.

By Lenz's Law, these eddy currents create their own magnetic field that opposes the original change in flux, resulting in a retarding force (Lorentz force) that opposes the motion of the conductor, thus slowing it down.

Regarding the conservation of energy: Kinetic energy is the initial form of energy of the moving object. As the object slows down, this kinetic energy is converted into electrical energy (eddy currents) and subsequently dissipated as thermal energy (heat) within the conductor due to its internal resistance ($P = I^2R$). No energy is created or destroyed; it is simply transformed from mechanical kinetic energy into thermal energy.
