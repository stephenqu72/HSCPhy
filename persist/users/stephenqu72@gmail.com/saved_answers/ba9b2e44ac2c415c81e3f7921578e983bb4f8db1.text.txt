To determine the wavelength produced by the transition of an electron in a hydrogen atom from $n=3$ to $n=1$, we use the Rydberg formula:

$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   Rydberg constant ($R_H$) $\approx 1.097 \times 10^7 \, \text{m}^{-1}$
    *   Final energy level ($n_f$) = $1$
    *   Initial energy level ($n_i$) = $3$

2.  **Substitute the values into the equation:**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{1}{1^2} - \frac{1}{3^2} \right)$$
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( 1 - \frac{1}{9} \right)$$
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.097 \times 10^7 \left( \frac{8}{9} \right)$$

3.  **Calculate the value:**
    $$\frac{1}{\lambda} \approx 9.751 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}^{-1}$$
    $$\lambda = \frac{1}{9.751 \times 10^6} \approx 1.025 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}$$

4.  **Final Answer:**
    The wavelength is approximately $1.025 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}$ (or $102.5 \, \text{nm}$).

### How to solve this:
To tackle this type of HSC Physics problem, always follow these steps:
*   **Memorize the Rydberg Equation:** It is the standard tool for calculating wavelengths of spectral lines in hydrogen.
*   **Identify $n_i$ and $n_f$:** Ensure you distinguish between the initial energy level ($n_i$, where the electron starts) and the final energy level ($n_f$, where the electron ends).
*   **Check Units:** The constant $R_H$ is typically given in $\text{m}^{-1}$, meaning your answer for $\lambda$ will be in meters. Converting this to nanometers ($1 \, \text{nm} = 10^{-9} \, \text{m}$) is a common requirement in exams to make the number more readable.
