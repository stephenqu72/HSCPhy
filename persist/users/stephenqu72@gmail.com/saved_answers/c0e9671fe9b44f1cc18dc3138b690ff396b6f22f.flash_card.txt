### Front
Compare the source types (monopoles vs. dipoles) for gravitational, magnetic, and electric fields.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Gravitational fields are monopole-only; magnetic fields are dipole-only; electric fields can be both.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Gauss’s Law for Magnetism ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0$).
- **When to use it:** Identifying the fundamental nature of field sources in electromagnetism and gravitation.
- **Common trap:** Assuming magnetic monopoles exist; remember that breaking a magnet always results in two smaller dipoles.
