### Suggested Answer

Einstein’s Theory of Special Relativity presents significant constraints and unique consequences for interstellar space travel:

1.  **Time Dilation:** As a spacecraft approaches relativistic speeds ($v \rightarrow c$), time for the travelers slows down relative to an observer on Earth ($t = \gamma t_0$). This means astronauts could potentially travel to distant star systems within their own lifetimes, even if thousands of years have passed on Earth.
2.  **Length Contraction:** From the perspective of the travelers, the distance to the destination is contracted ($L = L_0 / \gamma$). This makes the journey appear shorter, effectively allowing the crew to reach their destination faster than classical physics would predict.
3.  **Energy Requirements:** The most significant challenge is the relativistic mass increase. As velocity increases, the energy required to accelerate the craft further approaches infinity ($E = \gamma mc^2$). This makes reaching speeds close to $c$ technologically impossible with current propulsion systems, as it requires an impractical amount of fuel.
4.  **Practical Implication:** These effects mean that while Special Relativity theoretically allows for human survival on long-duration journeys to distant stars, it creates a "one-way" social impact. Upon returning to Earth, the astronauts would find that everyone they knew has aged significantly or passed away, due to the temporal disparity.

1.  **Understand the Verb:** "Discuss" requires you to examine the impacts from different angles. You cannot simply list facts; you must show the relationship between the physical principles (Time Dilation, Length Contraction) and the practical realities of space travel.
2.  **Structure your argument:**
    *   **The "Pro":** Focus on Time Dilation and Length Contraction. Explain how these phenomena make travel to distant stars theoretically feasible for the astronauts themselves.
    *   **The "Con":** Focus on the energy/mass constraint. Use the concept of relativistic momentum or energy to explain why we cannot currently achieve these speeds.
3.  **Include key terms:** Use specific terminology like "Lorentz factor ($\gamma$)," "time dilation," "length contraction," and "relativistic speeds."
4.  **Mark Allocation:** This is a 4-mark question. Aim for at least 4 clear, linked points of discussion to ensure you hit the marking criteria for an "extended response" style answer.
