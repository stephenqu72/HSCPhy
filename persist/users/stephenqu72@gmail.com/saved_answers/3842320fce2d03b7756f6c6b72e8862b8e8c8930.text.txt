### Explanation

When an electron transitions from a higher energy level ($E_{upper}$) to a lower energy level ($E_{lower}$), a photon is emitted with energy $\Delta E = E_{upper} - E_{lower}$. 

The wavelength ($\lambda$) of this photon is related to its energy by the formula:
$$\Delta E = \frac{hc}{\lambda} \implies \lambda = \frac{hc}{\Delta E}$$

Using the values $h = 4.136 \times 10^{-15} \text{ eV s}$ and $c = 3.00 \times 10^{8} \text{ m/s}$, the constant $hc \approx 1240.8 \text{ eV nm}$. Thus:
$$\lambda (\text{nm}) = \frac{1240.8}{\Delta E (\text{eV})}$$

**Step-by-step testing of transitions:**

1.  **For A (48.3 nm):** $\Delta E = \frac{1240.8}{48.3} \approx 25.68 \text{ eV}$. Looking at the gaps: $30.6 - 4.9 = 25.7 \text{ eV}$. This is a valid transition ($n=2 \to n=5$ is absorption, but the energy gap exists).
