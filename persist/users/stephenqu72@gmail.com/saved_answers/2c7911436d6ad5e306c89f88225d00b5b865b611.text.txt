### Answer
The correct answer is **C) The star is red-shifted and is moving away from us**.

---

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we apply the principles of the **Doppler Effect** as it relates to light in astrophysics:

1.  **Compare Wavelengths:** The rest (calibrated) wavelength of the Hydrogen-$\alpha$ line is $\lambda_{rest} = 656.28\text{ nm}$. The observed wavelength from the star is $\lambda_{obs} = 680\text{ nm}$.
2.  **Determine the Shift:** Since $\lambda_{obs} > \lambda_{rest}$, the wavelength has increased. An increase in wavelength corresponds to a shift toward the red end of the electromagnetic spectrum. This is known as a **red-shift**.
3.  **Relate to Motion:** According to the Doppler Effect:
    *   If a source is moving **away** from the observer, the light waves are "stretched," resulting in a longer wavelength (**red-shift**).
    *   If a source is moving **towards** the observer, the light waves are "compressed," resulting in a shorter wavelength (**blue-shift**).

Because the observed wavelength is longer ($680\text{ nm} > 656.28\text{ nm}$), the light has been red-shifted, which indicates that the star is moving away from the observer. Therefore, option **C** is the correct statement.
