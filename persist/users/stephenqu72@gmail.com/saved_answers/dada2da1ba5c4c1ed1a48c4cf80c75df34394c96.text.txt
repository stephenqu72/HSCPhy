To find the wavelength of the light, we use the formula for Young's double-slit interference (or diffraction grating) for constructive interference (maxima):

$$d \sin(\theta) = n\lambda$$

### Given information:
*   Slit separation ($d$) = $5\ \mu\text{m} = 5 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{m}$
*   Angle ($\theta$) = $20^\circ$
*   Order ($n$) = $1$ (for the first maxima)

### Step-by-step Calculation:
1.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for wavelength ($\lambda$):**
    $$\lambda = \frac{d \sin(\theta)}{n}$$

2.  **Substitute the values into the equation:**
    $$\lambda = \frac{5 \times 10^{-6} \times \sin(20^\circ)}{1}$$

3.  **Calculate the sine value:**
    $\sin(20^\circ) \approx 0.342$

4.  **Complete the calculation:**
    $$\lambda = 5 \times 10^{-6} \times 0.342$$
    $$\lambda = 1.71 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{m}$$

5.  **Convert to the appropriate units:**
    Since $1 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{m} = 1\ \mu\text{m}$, then $1.71 \times 10^{-6}\ \text{m} = 1.71\ \mu\text{m}$.

Rounding to two significant figures, we get $1.7\ \mu\text{m}$.
