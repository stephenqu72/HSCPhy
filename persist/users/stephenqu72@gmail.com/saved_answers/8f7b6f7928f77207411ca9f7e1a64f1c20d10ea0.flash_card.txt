### Front
How did James Chadwick experimentally distinguish neutrons from gamma radiation when bombarding beryllium with alpha particles?

### Back
- Key idea: Neutrons possess mass and can knock protons out of paraffin wax; gamma rays (being massless) cannot.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Elastic collision conservation of momentum ($m_nv_n \approx m_pv_p$).
- When to use it: To explain the evidence for the discovery of the neutron (1932).
- Common trap: Assuming high penetrating power or neutral charge differentiates them; both radiation types share these traits, so only momentum-transfer (mass-dependent) collisions provide definitive proof.
