### Front
How does orbital speed $v$ depend on the orbital radius $r$ for a satellite in a circular orbit?

### Back
- Key idea: Orbital speed is inversely proportional to the square root of the orbital radius.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$ (derived from $F_c = F_g$).
- When to use it: To compare the velocities of two satellites at different orbital radii.
- Common trap: Forgetting the square root; thinking $v \propto \frac{1}{r}$ instead of $v \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{r}}$.
