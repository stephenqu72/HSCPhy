### Teacher's Explanation

This question tests your understanding of **circular motion** on a banked track. When a vehicle turns on a frictionless banked corner, the horizontal component of the normal force provides the necessary **centripetal force**.

#### Part (a): Vector Diagram
To solve this, you need to draw the forces acting on the object:
1.  **Weight ($mg$)**: A vector pointing vertically downwards from the center of the object.
2.  **Normal Force ($N$)**: A vector perpendicular to the surface of the bank, pointing upwards and outwards.
3.  **Resultant (Centripetal Force, $F_c$)**: The horizontal component of the normal force, pointing towards the center of the circular path.

**Visual description:** The normal force ($N$) is the hypotenuse. The vertical component ($N \cos \theta$) balances the weight ($mg$), and the horizontal component ($N \sin \theta$) equals the centripetal force ($mv^2/r$).

#### Part (b): Calculation
To find the banking angle ($\theta$) where friction is not required, we use the relationship:
$$\tan \theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

**Given values:**
*   Velocity ($v$) = $20\text{ m/s}$
*   Radius ($r$) = $344\text{ m}$
*   Gravity ($g$) $\approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$

**Calculation:**
1.  Substitute the values into the formula:
    $$\tan \theta = \frac{20^2}{344 \times 9.8}$$
    $$\tan \theta = \frac{400}{3371.2}$$
    $$\tan \theta \approx 0.11865$$

2.  Find the inverse tangent ($\arctan$):
    $$\theta = \arctan(0.11865)$$
    $$\theta \approx 6.77^\circ$$

**Final Answer:** The required banking angle is approximately $6.8^\circ$.
