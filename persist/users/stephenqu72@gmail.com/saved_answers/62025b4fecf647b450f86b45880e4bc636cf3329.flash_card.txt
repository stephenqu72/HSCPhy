### Front
How do you determine the amplitude and frequency from a displacement-time ($d$-$t$) graph?

### Back
- Key idea: Amplitude is max displacement from equilibrium ($d=0$); Period ($T$) is time for one full cycle.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $f = \frac{1}{T}$
- When to use it: To derive wave properties from a $d$-$t$ graph.
- Common trap: Confusing peak-to-peak distance with amplitude (amplitude is only from equilibrium to peak) or forgetting to convert milliseconds to seconds when calculating frequency.
