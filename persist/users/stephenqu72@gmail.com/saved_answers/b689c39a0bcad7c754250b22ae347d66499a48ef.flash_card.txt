### Front
What is the general term for composite subatomic particles made of two or more quarks held together by the strong nuclear force?

### Back
- Key idea: Hadrons are the umbrella classification for all quark-based particles (baryons and mesons).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Use when identifying particle classifications within the Standard Model.
- Common trap: Confusing "hadrons" (composite) with "leptons" (fundamental particles that do not contain quarks).
