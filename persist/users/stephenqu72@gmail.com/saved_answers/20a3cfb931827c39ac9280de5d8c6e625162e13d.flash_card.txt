### Front
How do you calculate the orbital period of a planet given its distance from the Sun?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The square of the orbital period ($T^2$) is directly proportional to the cube of the orbital radius ($r^3$).
- **Formula:** $\frac{T_1^2}{r_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{r_2^3}$ (or $\frac{T^2}{r^3} = k$)
- **When to use it:** When comparing the orbital motion of two bodies orbiting the same central mass.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to cube the radius ($r^3$) or square the period ($T^2$) before solving; ensure units are consistent (e.g., years and AU) on both sides.
