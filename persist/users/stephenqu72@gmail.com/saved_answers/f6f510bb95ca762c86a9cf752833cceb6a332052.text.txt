### Part (a): Calculate the decay constant ($\lambda$)

**The Concept:**
The decay constant $\lambda$ is related to the half-life ($t_{1/2}$) by the standard formula derived from the radioactive decay law:
$$\lambda = \frac{\ln(2)}{t_{1/2}}$$

**The Solution:**
Given $t_{1/2} = 8$ days:
$$\lambda = \frac{0.6931}{8 \text{ days}}$$
$$\lambda \approx 0.0866 \text{ day}^{-1}$$

*(Note: In HSC physics, unless specified, you can provide the answer in $\text{day}^{-1}$ as it matches the units provided in the question.)*

### Part (b): Determine how many nuclei remain after 76 days

**The Concept:**
We use the radioactive decay formula:
$$N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$$
Where:
- $N_0 = 1.2 \times 10^{13}$
- $\lambda = 0.0866 \text{ day}^{-1}$
- $t = 76 \text{ days}$

**The Solution:**
$$N(76) = (1.2 \times 10^{13}) \times e^{-(0.0866 \times 76)}$$
First, calculate the exponent: $-0.0866 \times 76 \approx -6.5816$
Next, calculate $e^{-6.5816} \approx 0.0013856$
Finally, calculate the final number:
$$N(76) = 1.2 \times 10^{13} \times 0.0013856 \approx 1.66 \times 10^{10}$$

**Alternative Method (Number of Half-lives):**
You can also calculate the number of half-lives ($n$):
$$n = \frac{76}{8} = 9.5$$
$$N = N_0 \times (0.5)^n$$
$$N = (1.2 \times 10^{13}) \times (0.5)^{9.5}$$
$$N = (1.2 \times 10^{13}) \times 0.0013856 \approx 1.66 \times 10^{10} \text{ nuclei}$$

Both methods confirm that approximately $1.66 \times 10^{10}$ nuclei remain.
