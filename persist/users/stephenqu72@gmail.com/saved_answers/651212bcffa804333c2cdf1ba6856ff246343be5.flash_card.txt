### Front
In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), how do the horizontal and vertical velocity components behave throughout the flight?

### Back
- Key idea: Horizontal velocity remains constant (no acceleration); vertical velocity changes due to gravity (constant acceleration).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_x = 0$, $a_y = g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ (downward).
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem to decouple the axes for calculations.
- Common trap: Assuming horizontal velocity changes or that $g$ affects horizontal motion.
