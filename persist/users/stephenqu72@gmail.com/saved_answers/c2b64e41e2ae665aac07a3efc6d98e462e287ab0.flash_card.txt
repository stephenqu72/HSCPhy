### Front
How does the energy release in nuclear reactions compare to chemical reactions in terms of mass-energy equivalence?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Chemical reactions involve negligible mass change, while nuclear reactions involve significant mass conversion ("mass defect") due to the strong nuclear force.
- **Formula / law / constant:** $E = \Delta mc^2$ ($1 \text{ u} \approx 1.6605 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$)
- **When to use it:** When calculating energy released from nuclear binding energy, fusion, or fission.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert atomic mass units (u) to kilograms (kg) before using the $E = mc^2$ formula.
