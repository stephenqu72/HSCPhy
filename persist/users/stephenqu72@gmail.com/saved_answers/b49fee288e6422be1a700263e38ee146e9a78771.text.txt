To solve this problem, we must apply the principle of **Conservation of Energy** and the concept of the **centre of mass (COM)**.

### Analysis
When a person is on a swing, they can increase the total mechanical energy of the system by performing internal work. 

1.  **Physics Principle:** By shifting their body mass (straightening legs or pulling up on the ropes) at the highest point of the swing (the amplitude), the rider raises their centre of mass. 
2.  **Work and Energy:** Raising the centre of mass against the force of gravity requires work. This work effectively adds **gravitational potential energy** ($PE_g = mgh$) to the system. 
3.  **Conversion:** As the rider swings back down, this additional potential energy is converted into kinetic energy ($KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$), resulting in a higher speed at the equilibrium position.
4.  **Evaluating the Options:**
    *   **A is incorrect:** Simply "straightening legs" doesn't create kinetic energy; it changes the potential energy.
    *   **B is correct:** Straightening legs and pulling on the ropes shifts the mass distribution upward, raising the centre of mass. This requires work, which is converted into increased mechanical energy (potential energy), leading to higher speeds on subsequent swings.
    *   **C is incorrect:** Pulling on the ropes does not create a torque around the pivot point that increases the swing's amplitude; it is about changing the height of the centre of mass relative to the pivot.
    *   **D is incorrect:** While centripetal force is involved in circular motion, the mechanism for increasing the *speed* of the swing is the input of energy through the movement of the centre of mass, not just the magnitude of centripetal acceleration.

### Conclusion
The correct statement is **B**. By adjusting their posture at the top of the swing, the rider raises their centre of mass, which increases the potential energy of the system, eventually resulting in an increase in speed.
