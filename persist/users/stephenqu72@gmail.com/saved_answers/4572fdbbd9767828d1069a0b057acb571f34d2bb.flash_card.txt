### Front
In a stable circular orbit, how do an object's momentum and kinetic energy change over time?

### Back
- Key idea: Kinetic energy is constant (scalar), but momentum changes (vector).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$ and $E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.
- When to use it: Circular orbital motion problems.
- Common trap: Assuming momentum is constant because speed is constant. Remember that momentum is a vector—since direction changes, momentum changes.
