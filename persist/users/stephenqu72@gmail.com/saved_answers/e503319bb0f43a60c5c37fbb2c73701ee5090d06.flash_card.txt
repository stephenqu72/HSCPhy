### Front
What is the formula for centripetal force ($F_c$) when given mass, radius, and frequency ($f$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Circular motion requires a net force directed toward the center of the path.
- Formula: $F_c = mr(2\pi f)^2$ or $F_c = mr\omega^2$
- When to use it: When an object undergoes uniform circular motion and you are provided with frequency or angular velocity rather than linear velocity.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert units to SI (kg, m) or failing to convert frequency (Hz) to angular velocity ($\omega = 2\pi f$) before squaring.
