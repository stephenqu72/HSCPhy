### Solution and Explanation

**Part (a): Calculate the current within the electromagnets**
To find the current in a solenoid, we use the formula for the magnetic field strength inside a long solenoid:
$$B = \mu_0 \frac{N}{L} I$$
Where:
- $B = 0.0188 \text{ T}$
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$
- $N = 1000 \text{ turns}$
- $L = 0.10 \text{ m}$ (converting $10.0 \text{ cm}$ to meters)

Rearranging for $I$:
$$I = \frac{B \cdot L}{\mu_0 \cdot N} = \frac{0.0188 \times 0.10}{4\pi \times 10^{-7} \times 1000} \approx 1.496 \text{ A} \approx 1.50 \text{ A}$$

**Part (b): Calculate the maximum torque produced by the motor**
The formula for torque on a motor coil is $\tau = n B A I \sin(\theta)$. For maximum torque, $\sin(\theta) = 1$.
- $n = 100 \text{ turns}$
- $B = 0.0188 \text{ T}$
- $A = \pi r^2 = \pi \times (0.15 \text{ m})^2 \approx 0.07068 \text{ m}^2$
- $I = 1.50 \text{ A}$

$$\tau = 100 \times 0.0188 \times 0.07068 \times 1.50 \approx 0.199 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$$

**Part (c): Why current increases when a load is placed on the motor**
As the motor turns, it acts as a generator, producing a back electromotive force (back EMF) that opposes the supply voltage. The net voltage is $V_{net} = V_{supply} - \text{Back EMF}$. When a load is applied, the motor slows down, which reduces the rate of change of magnetic flux through the coil, thereby decreasing the back EMF. Since the back EMF is lower, the potential difference across the armature increases, leading to an increase in current drawn from the supply to provide the extra torque required to maintain the load.

**Part (d): Hypothesise the behaviour of the motor with no load**
With no load, the motor will accelerate rapidly until it reaches its terminal speed. At this high speed, the back EMF generated by the rotating coil will be very close in magnitude to the supply voltage. Consequently, the current drawn will drop to a very small value, just enough to overcome internal friction and air resistance. The motor will run at a high, constant speed because the magnetic field is held constant by the parallel power supply.
