To solve this problem, we apply the principles of electromagnetism and kinematics.

### Part (a)
To calculate the acceleration, we use Newton’s Second Law ($F = ma$) combined with the formula for the electric force acting on a charge in an electric field ($F = qE$).

**Given:**
- Mass ($m$) = $2.0 \times 10^{-2}\text{ kg}$
- Charge ($q$) = $-1.2 \times 10^{-6}\text{ C}$
- Electric field ($E$) = $6000\text{ V m}^{-1}$

**Calculation:**
1. Find the magnitude of the electric force:
   $$F = |q| \times E$$
   $$F = (1.2 \times 10^{-6}\text{ C}) \times (6000\text{ V m}^{-1}) = 7.2 \times 10^{-3}\text{ N}$$
2. Calculate the acceleration ($a$):
   $$a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{7.2 \times 10^{-3}\text{ N}}{2.0 \times 10^{-2}\text{ kg}}$$
   $$a = 0.36\text{ m s}^{-2}$$

**Answer:** The acceleration is $0.36\text{ m s}^{-2}$ toward the positive plate.

### Part (b)
We need to find the final velocity of the droplet. Since the droplet enters the field with an initial vertical velocity ($v_y$) and experiences a horizontal acceleration ($a_x$), the motion must be treated as two-dimensional vector motion.

**Given:**
- Initial vertical velocity ($v_y$) = $0.25\text{ m s}^{-1}$ (constant, as we ignore gravity)
- Horizontal acceleration ($a_x$) = $0.36\text{ m s}^{-2}$
- Time ($t$) = $0.5\text{ s}$

**Calculation:**
1. Calculate the final horizontal velocity ($v_x$):
   $$v_x = u_x + a_x t = 0 + (0.36 \times 0.5) = 0.18\text{ m s}^{-1}$$
2. The vertical velocity ($v_y$) remains $0.25\text{ m s}^{-1}$.
3. Calculate the magnitude of the final velocity ($v$) using the Pythagorean theorem:
   $$v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$$
   $$v = \sqrt{(0.18)^2 + (0.25)^2}$$
   $$v = \sqrt{0.0324 + 0.0625} = \sqrt{0.0949} \approx 0.308\text{ m s}^{-1}$$

**Answer:** The final speed of the droplet is $0.31\text{ m s}^{-1}$ (rounded to two significant figures).
