To find the torque exerted on the disc, we use the fundamental definition of torque:

$$\tau = r F \sin(\phi)$$

Where:
*   $r$ is the distance from the pivot point to the point where the force is applied (the radius).
*   $F$ is the magnitude of the applied force.
*   $\phi$ is the angle between the position vector $\vec{r}$ and the force vector $\vec{F}$.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   Radius $r = 100 \text{ cm} = 1.0 \text{ m}$.
    *   Force $F = 100 \text{ N}$.
    *   The angle $\theta$ shown in the diagram is $30^\circ$. Looking at the geometry, the angle between the radius vector $\vec{r}$ (which is along the x-axis direction at that moment) and the force vector $\vec{F}$ (which is acting vertically upward) is $90^\circ - \theta$.
    *   Alternatively, using the perpendicular component of the force: The component of the force responsible for rotation is $F \cos(\theta)$, which is perpendicular to the radius $\vec{r}$.
    *   $\phi = 90^\circ - 30^\circ = 60^\circ$.

2.  **Calculate the torque:**
    Using the formula $\tau = r F \sin(\phi)$:
    $$\tau = (1.0 \text{ m}) \times (100 \text{ N}) \times \sin(60^\circ)$$
    $$\tau = 100 \times 0.866$$
    $$\tau = 86.6 \text{ Nm}$$

**Conclusion:**
The correct value describing the turning effect is $86.6 \text{ Nm}$. This matches option **(B)**.
