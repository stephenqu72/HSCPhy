To determine the length of the space station as perceived by the astronaut, we must apply the principles of **Special Relativity**, specifically **length contraction**.

### 1. Identify the given values
*   Proper length of the space station ($L_0$): $350\text{ m}$ (The length measured in its own rest frame).
*   Relative velocity ($v$): $0.13c$ (where $c$ is the speed of light).

### 2. The Physics Principle
According to Special Relativity, an object in motion relative to an observer will appear shorter in the direction of motion. The formula for length contraction is:
$$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
Where:
*   $L$ is the contracted length measured by the moving observer.
*   $L_0$ is the proper length.
*   $v$ is the relative velocity.

### 3. Calculation
Substitute the known values into the equation:
$$L = 350 \sqrt{1 - (0.13)^2}$$
$$L = 350 \sqrt{1 - 0.0169}$$
$$L = 350 \sqrt{0.9831}$$
$$L \approx 350 \times 0.9915$$
$$L \approx 347.03\text{ m}$$

Rounding to the nearest whole number provided in the options, we get $347\text{ m}$.

### 4. Conclusion
The astronaut, moving at $0.13c$ relative to the space station, will observe the station to be contracted along the direction of motion. Comparing our calculated result of approximately $347\text{ m}$ to the options, the correct answer is **A**.
