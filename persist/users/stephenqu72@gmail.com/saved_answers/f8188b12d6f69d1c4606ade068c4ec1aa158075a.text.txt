### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must understand the fundamental principles of projectile motion in the absence of air resistance:

1.  **Nature of Acceleration:** When an object is in projectile motion and air resistance is ignored, the only force acting on the object is gravity. According to Newton's Second Law ($F = ma$), this gravitational force provides a constant downward acceleration equal to the acceleration due to gravity, denoted as $g$ (approximately $9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$).

2.  **Constant Acceleration:** Because the force of gravity is constant in magnitude and direction at all points along the projectile's path, the acceleration remains constant at every point, including points $P, Q, R, S,$ and $T$.
    *   **At point R (the peak):** The vertical velocity is zero, but the acceleration is still $g$ downwards. Therefore, statement (A) and (C) are incorrect.
    *   **At point T (the end):** The acceleration is still $g$ downwards. It does not increase or change as the ball travels, so statement (B) is incorrect.

3.  **Conclusion:** Since the acceleration is always $g$ and is directed vertically downwards at every point along the trajectory, the magnitude and direction of the acceleration are identical at all points marked. This makes statement **(D)** the correct description.
