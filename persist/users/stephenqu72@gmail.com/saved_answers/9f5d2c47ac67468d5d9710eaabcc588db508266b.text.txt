To solve this problem, we use the principles of an ideal transformer. We are given the following values:
*   Primary turns ($N_p$) = $3000$
*   Secondary turns ($N_s$) = $150$
*   Primary voltage ($V_p$) = $240\text{ V}$
*   Secondary current ($I_s$) = $15\text{ A}$

### 1. Calculate Secondary Voltage ($V_s$)
The transformer equation relates the turns ratio to the voltage ratio:
$$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$
Rearranging for $V_s$:
$$V_s = V_p \times \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$
$$V_s = 240\text{ V} \times \frac{150}{3000}$$
$$V_s = 240 \times 0.05 = 12\text{ V}$$

### 2. Calculate Primary Current ($I_p$)
For an ideal transformer, we assume $100\%$ efficiency, meaning the primary power equals the secondary power ($P_p = P_s$). Since $P = V \times I$:
$$V_p \times I_p = V_s \times I_s$$
Rearranging for $I_p$:
$$I_p = \frac{V_s \times I_s}{V_p}$$
Alternatively, using the turns ratio property ($I_p/I_s = N_s/N_p$):
$$I_p = I_s \times \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$
$$I_p = 15\text{ A} \times \frac{150}{3000}$$
$$I_p = 15 \times 0.05 = 0.75\text{ A}$$

Comparing our results ($12\text{ V}$ and $0.75\text{ A}$) to the given options, the correct choice is **(C)**.
