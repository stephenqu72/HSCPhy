2.  **Use the classical relationship for time:**
    In the laboratory frame, the distance and the speed are measured directly. We do not need to apply time dilation here because the observer is measuring the time interval between two events (passing detector 1 and passing detector 2) that occur at fixed points within their own reference frame.
    The formula is:
    $$t = \frac{d}{v}$$
