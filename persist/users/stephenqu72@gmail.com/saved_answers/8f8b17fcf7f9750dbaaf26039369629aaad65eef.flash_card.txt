### Front
How do gravitational potential energy ($U$) and kinetic energy ($K$) of a satellite change as its orbital radius ($r$) increases?

### Back
- Key idea: As $r$ increases, $U$ becomes less negative (increases) and $K$ decreases.
- Formula / law: $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$ and $K = \frac{GMm}{2r}$.
- When to use it: To compare the energy states of satellites in different circular orbits.
- Common trap: Forgetting that $U$ is negative; a larger $r$ makes $U$ closer to zero, which means $U$ increases mathematically.
