### Answer

**(a) Describe the significance of the graph to our understanding of the universe.**
The graph represents **Hubble’s Law**, which demonstrates that the recessional velocity of galaxies is directly proportional to their distance from Earth. The significance is that it provides empirical evidence that the universe is expanding. By extrapolating this relationship back in time, it implies that the universe originated from a single, dense point, thereby supporting the **Big Bang Theory**.

**(b) How were the recessional velocities of galaxies determined?**
Recessional velocities are determined by observing the **redshift** in the light spectra of distant galaxies. As a galaxy moves away from an observer, the light it emits is shifted toward the longer (red) wavelengths of the electromagnetic spectrum due to the **Doppler effect**. By measuring the shift in known spectral lines (such as hydrogen absorption lines) and comparing them to laboratory standards, the velocity of the galaxy can be calculated using the formula:
$$z = \frac{\Delta \lambda}{\lambda} \approx \frac{v}{c}$$
where $z$ is the redshift, $\Delta \lambda$ is the change in wavelength, $\lambda$ is the rest wavelength, $v$ is the recessional velocity, and $c$ is the speed of light.
