### Answer
The farmer should choose the **Bucket Wheel** design with the largest diameter of **4 metres**.

**Justification:**
1.  **Design Choice:** The Bucket Wheel is more efficient than the Paddle Wheel because it utilizes the weight of the water (gravitational potential energy) in addition to the kinetic energy of the flow. The buckets "trap" the water, ensuring it exerts force over a larger portion of the wheel's rotation compared to the Paddle Wheel, which simply relies on the stream's flow hitting flat paddles.
2.  **Size Choice:** A larger diameter wheel (4 metres) increases the torque applied to the generator shaft. Since $Torque (\tau) = Force (F) \times radius (r)$, a larger radius for the same weight of water results in greater torque. Furthermore, a larger diameter provides a greater vertical distance for the water to fall, maximizing the gravitational potential energy ($PE_g = mgh$) converted into rotational kinetic energy.

### How to solve this
*   **Step 1: Identify the mechanism of energy transfer.** Look at how each wheel interacts with the water. The Paddle Wheel uses the velocity of the current (kinetic energy). The Bucket Wheel uses an overhead feed, meaning it captures the potential energy of the water dropping into the buckets as well as the kinetic energy of the stream.
*   **Step 2: Relate design to efficiency.** In physics, capturing more forms of energy (potential + kinetic) results in a higher overall efficiency. The Bucket Wheel is superior because it captures the weight of the water as it moves downwards.
*   **Step 3: Relate size to output.** In rotary motion, power output is related to torque ($P = \tau \omega$). By increasing the radius ($r$), you increase the lever arm of the force being applied, which increases torque. Therefore, the 4-metre wheel will provide the highest mechanical advantage to the generator.
