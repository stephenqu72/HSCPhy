### Answer
In the Standard Model, bosons act as force-carrier particles (or gauge bosons) that mediate interactions between matter particles (fermions). Each of the fundamental forces in the universe is transmitted by the exchange of specific bosons:

*   **Electromagnetic force:** Mediated by the **photon**, which governs interactions between electrically charged particles.
*   **Strong nuclear force:** Mediated by **gluons**, which bind quarks together to form protons, neutrons, and other hadrons.
*   **Weak nuclear force:** Mediated by the **$W$ and $Z$ bosons**, which are responsible for radioactive decay processes, such as beta decay.
*   **Higgs mechanism:** The **Higgs boson** is a unique scalar boson responsible for providing mass to other fundamental particles through their interaction with the Higgs field.
