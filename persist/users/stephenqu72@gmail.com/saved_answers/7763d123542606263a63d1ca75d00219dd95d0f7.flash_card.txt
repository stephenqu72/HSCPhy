### Front
How do you calculate the potential difference ($V$) between two parallel charged plates, and how do magnetic and gravitational forces fundamentally differ regarding polarity?

### Back
- Key idea: Potential difference is proportional to the electric field strength and distance; magnetic forces are dipolar (attract/repel), while gravity is monopolar (always attractive).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $V = E \times d$ (where $d$ is perpendicular distance).
- When to use it: When calculating potential difference in a uniform electric field between parallel plates.
- Common trap: Confusing vector directions—gravitational force is strictly attractive, whereas magnetic forces depend on pole orientation.
