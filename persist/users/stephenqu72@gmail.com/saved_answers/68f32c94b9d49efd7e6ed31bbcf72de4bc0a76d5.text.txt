### Explanation
To determine which discovery is linked to the expansion of the universe, we look at the historical evidence provided by Edwin Hubble in the 1920s:

1.  **The Role of the Doppler Effect:** Astronomers use spectroscopy to analyze the light coming from distant galaxies. By observing the spectral lines of elements (like hydrogen) within that light, they can compare them to known laboratory values. 
2.  **Redshift:** Hubble observed that for the vast majority of galaxies, these spectral lines were shifted toward the red end of the electromagnetic spectrum. This is known as **cosmological redshift**. 
3.  **The Link:** According to the Doppler effect, if a source of waves is moving away from an observer, the waves are stretched, resulting in a longer wavelength (a "redshift"). Hubble interpreted these redshifts as evidence that distant galaxies are receding from us. 
4.  **Hubble's Law:** By plotting the distance of galaxies against their recessional velocity (derived from the magnitude of the redshift), he demonstrated a direct relationship, $v = H_0 d$, which provides the fundamental evidence that the universe itself is expanding.

**Why the others are incorrect:**
*   **(B)** Gas and dust clouds (nebulae) are components of galaxies but are not the primary evidence for the *expansion* of the universe.
*   **(C)** Stellar color is related to temperature and stellar evolution (Hertzsprung-Russell diagram), not the expansion of space.
*   **(D)** While galaxies do move, the expansion of the universe is not a result of "moving through space" (like a projectile), but rather the expansion of space itself. Furthermore, this was discovered through observational data, not primary computer simulations.
