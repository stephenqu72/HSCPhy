### Teacher's Solution and Explanation

To solve these problems, we use the equations of motion under constant acceleration due to gravity ($g = 9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$). In projectile motion, vertical and horizontal components are independent.

---

#### Part (a)
**Question:** Calculate the vertical distance that the ball travels.

**Solution:**
We use the displacement formula for vertical motion starting from rest ($u_y = 0$):
$$s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2}g t^2$$
Given:
*   Initial vertical velocity ($u_y$) = $0\text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Time ($t$) = $3.5\text{ s}$
*   Gravity ($g$) = $9.8\text{ m s}^{-2}$

$$s_y = 0(3.5) + \frac{1}{2}(9.8)(3.5)^2$$
$$s_y = 4.9 \times 12.25$$
$$s_y = 60.025\text{ m}$$

**Answer:** The vertical distance is approximately **$60.0\text{ m}$**.

---

#### Part (b)
**Question:** Calculate the horizontal distance the phone travels.

**Solution:**
1. **Find the time of flight ($t$):**
   Using the vertical displacement formula:
   $$s_y = u_y t + \frac{1}{2}g t^2$$
   $$200 = 0(t) + \frac{1}{2}(9.8)t^2$$
   $$200 = 4.9t^2$$
   $$t^2 = \frac{200}{4.9} \approx 40.816$$
   $$t \approx 6.3887\text{ s}$$

2. **Calculate horizontal distance ($d_x$):**
   Since there is no air resistance, horizontal velocity ($v_x$) is constant:
   $$d_x = v_x \times t$$
   $$d_x = 50.0 \times 6.3887$$
   $$d_x \approx 319.435\text{ m}$$

**Answer:** The horizontal distance is approximately **$319\text{ m}$**.

---

### How to solve these types of problems:
1. **Identify the variables:** Separate your motion into vertical ($y$) and horizontal ($x$) components.
2. **Use the correct equation:** For vertical motion involving time and displacement, $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$ is the most reliable tool.
3. **Link components:** Time is the "bridge" between vertical and horizontal motion. If you have the vertical height, calculate the time of flight first, then use that time to find the horizontal range.
4. **Units and Sig Figs:** Always ensure your units ($m$, $s$) are consistent and round to an appropriate number of significant figures (usually 2 or 3 based on the question data).
