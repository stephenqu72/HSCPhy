### Front
What is Hubble’s Law, and what key evidence supports it?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The recessional velocity of a galaxy is directly proportional to its distance from the observer, indicating an expanding universe.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v = H_0 d$ ($v$: velocity, $d$: distance, $H_0$: Hubble constant).
- **When to use it:** When explaining the expansion of the universe or calculating the distance/velocity of distant galaxies using spectral data.
- **Common trap:** Confusing "recessional velocity" with the physical movement of a galaxy through space; it is actually the expansion of space itself carrying the galaxies apart.
