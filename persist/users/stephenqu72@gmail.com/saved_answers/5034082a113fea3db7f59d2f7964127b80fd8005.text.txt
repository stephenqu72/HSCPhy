To solve this problem, we first identify the given variables:
- Mass of Titan ($M$): $1.35 \times 10^{23}\text{ kg}$
- Radius of Titan ($r$): $2\,575\text{ km} = 2.575 \times 10^6\text{ m}$
- Gravitational constant ($G$): $6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N m}^2\text{ kg}^{-2}$

### a) Calculate the acceleration due to gravity ($g$)
The formula for acceleration due to gravity on the surface of a spherical body is:
$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Substituting the values:
$$g = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (1.35 \times 10^{23})}{(2.575 \times 10^6)^2}$$
$$g = \frac{9.0045 \times 10^{12}}{6.630625 \times 10^{12}}$$
$$g \approx 1.36\text{ m s}^{-2}$$

### b) Calculate the escape velocity ($v_e$)
The escape velocity is the minimum speed required for an object to escape the gravitational influence of a body. The formula is:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

Substituting the values:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (1.35 \times 10^{23})}{2.575 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{1.8009 \times 10^{13}}{2.575 \times 10^6}}$$
$$v_e = \sqrt{6\,993\,786.4}$$
$$v_e \approx 2\,645\text{ m s}^{-1} \text{ (or } 2.65\text{ km s}^{-1}\text{)}$$

### Teacher's Tips:
1. **Unit Conversion:** Always convert kilometers to meters ($10^3$) before substituting into physical formulas. Failing to do this is a very common source of error in HSC physics exams.
2. **Precision:** Keep at least 3 significant figures in your working out, as the input data is provided to 3 significant figures.
3. **Formula Recall:** If you forget the escape velocity formula, remember it is derived from equating the kinetic energy ($1/2 mv^2$) to the gravitational potential energy ($GMm/r$).
