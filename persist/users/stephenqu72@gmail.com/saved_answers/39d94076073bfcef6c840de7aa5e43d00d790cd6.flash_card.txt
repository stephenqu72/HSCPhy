### Front
What is the condition for a passenger to "feel weightless" at the top of a vertical circular loop?

### Back
- Key idea: The normal force ($N$) exerted by the seat on the passenger is zero.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $mg = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ or $v = \sqrt{gr}$.
- When to use it: To determine minimum entry heights or speeds for vertical loops where contact is just maintained.
- Common trap: Assuming $v=0$ at the top; if $v=0$, the passenger falls out. The passenger must maintain at least the centripetal speed required by gravity.
