### Answer
James Chadwick discovered the neutron in 1932 by bombarding a beryllium target with alpha particles ($\alpha$) emitted from a polonium source. This process produced a highly penetrating, neutral radiation.

When this radiation was directed at various targets, such as paraffin wax (which is rich in hydrogen atoms), it caused the emission of protons. By applying the laws of **conservation of momentum** and **conservation of energy** to the collisions between the unknown neutral radiation and the target protons, Chadwick was able to calculate the mass of the mysterious particles. He determined that the radiation consisted of particles with a mass approximately equal to that of a proton but with zero electric charge, leading to the identification of the neutron.
