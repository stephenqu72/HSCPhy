### Front
What are the changes to the atomic number ($Z$) and mass number ($A$) of a nucleus during alpha decay?

### Back
- Key idea: A parent nucleus emits an alpha particle ($^4_2\text{He}$), losing 2 protons and 2 neutrons.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A-4}_{Z-2} Y + ^4_2\text{He}$
- When to use it: To determine the daughter product of an alpha-emitting radioactive isotope.
- Common trap: Forgetting that the mass number ($A$) decreases by 4, not just 2 (the atomic number $Z$ decreases by 2).
