### Answer

Planck accounted for the shape of the black body radiation curve by proposing that energy is not emitted in a continuous wave, but rather in discrete "packets" or "quanta". He introduced the formula $E = nhf$, where $n$ is an integer, $h$ is Planck's constant, and $f$ is the frequency of the radiation. This hypothesis allowed him to derive a mathematical expression that perfectly matched the experimental data, effectively avoiding the "ultraviolet catastrophe".

Initially, this was not an accepted explanation because it directly contradicted the prevailing laws of classical physics. Classical electromagnetism at the time stated that energy was continuous and wave-like. Proposing that energy was quantized appeared to be a radical, ad-hoc mathematical trick rather than a physical reality, which made the physics community, including Planck himself, initially skeptical of its physical significance.
