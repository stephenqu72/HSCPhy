### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to identify the two pillars of observational evidence that support the Big Bang Theory and our current model of the expanding universe.

1.  **Evidence for the Big Bang (Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation - CMBR):** The Big Bang Theory posits that the universe began as a hot, dense singularity. As it expanded and cooled, radiation from that initial state eventually became "decoupled" from matter. This radiation has been stretched by the expansion of the universe into the microwave part of the electromagnetic spectrum. The discovery of this uniform, low-temperature ($2.7\text{ K}$) background radiation filling the universe is a major piece of evidence supporting the Big Bang.
2.  **Evidence for the Expanding Universe (Redshift):** Edwin Hubble observed that light from distant galaxies is shifted toward the red end of the spectrum. This "redshift" occurs because the galaxies are moving away from us. Importantly, Hubble's Law states that the velocity of recession $v$ is proportional to the distance $d$ ($v = H_0 d$). Therefore, light from galaxies that are **further away** shows a **larger redshift**, indicating they are receding faster.

**Why the other options are incorrect:**
*   **A and B:** Both are incorrect because they suggest the redshift is *smaller* for galaxies further away. This would imply the universe is slowing down or that the galaxies are not receding according to Hubble's Law.
*   **D:** This is incorrect because "blue shifts" would indicate that galaxies are moving *toward* us, which contradicts the evidence that the universe is expanding.
