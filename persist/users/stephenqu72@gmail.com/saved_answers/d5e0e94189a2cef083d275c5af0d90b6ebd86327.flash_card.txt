### Front
How do orbital velocity ($v$), period ($T$), and centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) change as a satellite's orbital radius ($r$) increases?

### Back
- Key idea: Larger orbits result in lower speeds and accelerations, but longer orbital periods.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: 
  - $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$
  - $T = \sqrt{\frac{4\pi^2r^3}{GM}}$ (Kepler’s 3rd Law)
  - $a_c = \frac{GM}{r^2}$
- When to use it: Circular satellite motion problems comparing two satellites at different altitudes.
- Common trap: Confusing "orbital radius" ($r$) with "altitude" ($h$). Remember: $r = R_{earth} + h$.
