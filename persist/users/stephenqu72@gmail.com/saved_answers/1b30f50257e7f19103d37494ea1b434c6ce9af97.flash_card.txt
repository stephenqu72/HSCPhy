### Front
For a projectile launched and landing at the same height (ignoring air resistance), how do the kinetic energies at launch and impact compare, and why?

### Back
- Key idea: Kinetic energy at launch equals kinetic energy at impact due to the Law of Conservation of Mechanical Energy.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E_{total} = E_k + U = \text{constant}$
- When to use it: Any projectile motion problem where the launch and landing heights are identical.
- Common trap: Assuming kinetic energy is zero at the peak (ignoring horizontal velocity) or ignoring that $v_x$ remains constant.
