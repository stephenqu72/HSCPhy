### Front
What is the fundamental difference between scalar and vector quantities, and how can you distinguish between them?

### Back
- Key idea: Scalars have magnitude only; vectors have both magnitude and direction.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Use when identifying physical quantities (e.g., distinguishing distance vs. displacement or speed vs. velocity).
- Common trap: Confusing scalars and vectors; remember that vector quantities always require a specified direction (e.g., "north" or "+") to be fully defined.
