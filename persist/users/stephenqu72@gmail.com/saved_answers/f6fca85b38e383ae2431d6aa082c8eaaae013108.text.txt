### Answer
When more appliances are turned on during the evening peak, the houses are connected in parallel, which decreases the total resistance of the load circuit. 

1.  **Current:** According to Ohm’s Law ($V = IR$), as the total resistance of the houses decreases, the total current ($I$) drawn from the transmission lines increases.
2.  **Energy Loss:** The transmission lines have a constant resistance ($R_{line}$). The power lost as heat in these lines is given by $P_{loss} = I^2 R_{line}$. Because the current increases, the power loss increases significantly (as a square of the current). Consequently, more energy is dissipated as heat in the transmission lines, reducing the efficiency of the energy distribution system.
3.  **Voltage:** Due to the increased current flowing through the transmission lines, there is a larger voltage drop ($V_{drop} = I R_{line}$) across the lines themselves. This results in a decrease in the terminal voltage delivered to the houses, which may lead to the "dimming" of lights or underperformance of appliances. 
4.  **Transformers:** To maintain a consistent output voltage despite the increased load, the system may require active regulation or the power station must increase output to compensate for the higher transmission losses.
