### Teacher's Guide: HSC Physics Depth Study

#### (a) Risk Assessment
To gain full marks for a risk assessment, you must identify the hazard, the potential consequence, and a clear control measure.

**Hazard:** Laser light (Class 2 or 3).
**Risk:** Permanent eye damage or temporary retinal burning if the beam is reflected into the eyes.
**Control:** 
1. Never look directly into the laser beam or its reflection. 
2. Ensure the laser is secured on a stable stand and not at eye level.
3. Use barriers or a beam stop to prevent the beam from traveling beyond the target area.
4. Use warning signs ("Laser in Use") to prevent others from entering the path.

#### (b) Explaining Interference
To answer this, describe the physics of superposition and path difference.

**The Explanation:**
When coherent light from the laser hits the double slit, the slits act as two point sources of light (Huygens' Principle). As the light waves spread out (diffract), they overlap and undergo **superposition**.
*   **Constructive Interference:** Where the path difference between the waves from the two slits is an integer multiple of the wavelength ($n\lambda$), the waves arrive in phase, resulting in bright fringes.
*   **Destructive Interference:** Where the path difference is a half-integer multiple of the wavelength $((n + 0.5)\lambda)$, the waves arrive exactly out of phase (crest meets trough), canceling each other out to create dark fringes.

**The Diagram:**
*   Draw two slits, $S_1$ and $S_2$.
*   Draw circular wavefronts emanating from both slits.
*   Show waves overlapping at a screen.
*   Mark points of intersection as "constructive" (bright) and gaps as "destructive" (dark).

---

### HOW TO SOLVE
1. **Part (a):** Always use a table format (Hazard | Risk | Control) in your mind, then translate it to clear, formal prose. HSC markers look for "safety-first" practices.
2. **Part (b):** You must mention that the light sources are **coherent** (same frequency and constant phase relationship). Without coherence, a stable interference pattern would not form. Use the term "path difference" as it is the key variable that determines the intensity of the light at any point on the screen.
