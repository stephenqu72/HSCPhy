### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a): Risk Assessment (3 marks)
To achieve full marks in an HSC Physics risk assessment, you must identify the **hazard**, the **risk** (how it could cause harm), and the **control measure** (how to prevent it).

*   **Hazard:** The red laser beam.
    *   **Risk:** Direct or reflected beam entering the eye can cause permanent retinal damage or blindness.
    *   **Control Measure:** Never look directly into the laser beam or at specular reflections. Use the laser in a controlled environment, ensure the beam is blocked at the end of its path (e.g., using a non-reflective screen), and wear appropriate laser safety goggles if necessary.
*   **Hazard:** Electrical equipment (laser power supply).
    *   **Risk:** Electric shock if cords are frayed or if used near liquids.
    *   **Control Measure:** Check cords for damage before use and ensure the workspace is dry.
*   **Hazard:** Darkness in the laboratory (required to see the pattern).
    *   **Risk:** Tripping or walking into furniture.
    *   **Control Measure:** Clearly mark the path of the beam and keep the area free of obstacles/clutter.

#### Part (b): Explanation of Interference (2 marks)
To explain this, you must describe the wave nature of light and the principle of superposition.

**Explanation:**
1.  **Diffraction:** As the monochromatic laser light passes through the two narrow slits, each slit acts as a new point source of light waves (Huygens' Principle).
2.  **Superposition:** These waves overlap (superpose) as they propagate toward the screen.
    *   **Constructive Interference:** Where the path difference between the waves from the two slits is an integer multiple of the wavelength ($n\lambda$), the waves arrive in phase, resulting in bright fringes (the red spots).
    *   **Destructive Interference:** Where the path difference is an odd multiple of half-wavelengths ($[n + 0.5]\lambda$), the waves arrive $180^\circ$ out of phase, canceling each other out to produce dark regions.

**Diagram requirements:**
Draw a standard double-slit diagram:
*   Two slits separated by distance $d$.
*   Light waves radiating from both slits as semicircular wavefronts.
*   Lines indicating where crests meet crests (constructive interference/bright fringe) and where crests meet troughs (destructive interference/dark fringe).
*   The path difference $\Delta L = d \sin(\theta)$.
