### Answer
Star Y is located on the main sequence, meaning it is currently fusing hydrogen into helium in its core. Once its core hydrogen is exhausted, it will move off the main sequence to become a giant or supergiant.

In contrast, star X is a low-mass star, specifically a red dwarf (found at the bottom right of the Hertzsprung-Russell diagram). Unlike star Y, star X will not transition off the main sequence in the same way because:

1.  **Convection:** Low-mass stars like star X are fully convective. This allows them to transport hydrogen from the outer layers into the core and distribute the helium produced throughout the entire star.
2.  **Lifetime:** Because of this efficient mixing and their relatively slow rate of hydrogen fusion, these stars consume their fuel very slowly.
3.  **Evolutionary Path:** Instead of expanding into a giant, star X will slowly burn through its entire supply of hydrogen over trillions of years, eventually becoming a helium white dwarf without ever passing through a giant phase.

### How to solve this question
To approach a 4-mark question like this in an HSC Physics exam, you should structure your response by demonstrating knowledge of stellar evolution and the specific properties of the Hertzsprung-Russell diagram:

*   **Identify the stars:** Start by identifying that Star Y is a massive/intermediate-mass main sequence star, while Star X is a low-mass main sequence star (red dwarf).
*   **Explain the standard transition:** Explain that "leaving the main sequence" refers to the exhaustion of hydrogen in the core, which leads to the expansion of outer layers as a star evolves into a red giant. 
*   **Contrast the physical mechanism:** Highlight the key difference for low-mass stars. Mention "full convection" as the critical mechanism. Because these stars are entirely convective, they do not develop an inert helium core surrounded by a hydrogen-burning shell (the structure required for the expansion seen in larger stars).
*   **Conclude the fate:** Explicitly state that because they burn their fuel completely and slowly, they bypass the giant phase entirely, moving directly toward the white dwarf stage.
