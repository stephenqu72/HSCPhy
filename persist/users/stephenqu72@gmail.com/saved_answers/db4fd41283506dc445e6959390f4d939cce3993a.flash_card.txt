### Front
In a system of connected objects moving together with the same acceleration, what is the relationship between their net forces and their masses?

### Back
- Key idea: Net force is directly proportional to mass ($F_{net} \propto m$) when acceleration is constant.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F = ma$
- When to use it: Comparing individual forces acting on different components within a single, unified system (e.g., blocks in contact).
- Common trap: Assuming all objects in a connected system experience the same net force; they share the same acceleration, but their net forces differ if their masses differ.
