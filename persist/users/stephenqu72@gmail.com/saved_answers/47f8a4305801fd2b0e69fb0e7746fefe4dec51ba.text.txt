### Explanation
To understand this question, we look at the fundamental properties of electromagnetic (EM) waves:

1.  **Oscillating Fields:** An electromagnetic wave is generated by the acceleration of electric charges. This process creates time-varying (oscillating) electric fields and magnetic fields. According to Maxwell's equations, a changing electric field induces a changing magnetic field, and vice versa. This self-propagating cycle allows the wave to travel through a vacuum.
2.  **Perpendicularity:** In an EM wave, the electric field vector ($E$) and the magnetic field vector ($B$) oscillate in directions that are perpendicular to each other. Furthermore, both of these fields are perpendicular to the direction in which the wave is traveling (the direction of energy propagation). This makes electromagnetic waves **transverse waves**.

**Evaluating the options:**
*   **A is correct:** It correctly identifies that the fields are both "oscillating" and "perpendicular."
*   **B is incorrect:** While the *source* of the wave requires accelerating charges, the fields themselves are described as oscillating, not "accelerating."
*   **C is incorrect:** The fields must be time-varying (oscillating) to propagate as a wave; constant fields would not form a wave.
*   **D is incorrect:** The fields are perpendicular to the direction of propagation and each other, not parallel.
