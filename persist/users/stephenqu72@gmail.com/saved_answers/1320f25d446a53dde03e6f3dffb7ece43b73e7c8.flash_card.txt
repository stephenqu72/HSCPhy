### Front
Why is a satellite in a circular orbit at a constant speed considered to be accelerating?

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration is the rate of change of **velocity** (a vector). Even if speed is constant, the continuous change in the **direction** of motion constitutes acceleration.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a = \frac{\Delta \vec{v}}{\Delta t}$.
- When to use it: Any scenario involving circular motion or changing direction.
- Common trap: Confusing "constant speed" with "constant velocity"; an object changing direction cannot have constant velocity and thus must be accelerating.
