### Front
How do you calculate the remaining mass of a radioactive sample over time when given the half-life ($t_{1/2}$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Radioactive decay is exponential; mass halves every $t_{1/2}$ interval.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $N = N_0(1/2)^n$, where $n = \text{total time} / t_{1/2}$.
- When to use it: To determine remaining quantity (mass, activity, or nuclei) after a specific duration.
- Common trap: Confusing "number of half-lives" ($n$) with the "total time" ($t$). Always calculate $n$ first.
