### Question 20(a)
**Answer:** Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity. Since velocity is a vector quantity (magnitude and direction), the satellite is accelerating because its **direction** of motion is constantly changing, even though its **speed** (magnitude of velocity) remains constant.

**How to solve:** Remember the definition of acceleration: $a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}$. In circular motion, the object is always changing direction toward the center, which constitutes acceleration.

### Question 20(b)
**(i) Arrow direction:** At point C (the top of the loop), the resultant force (centripetal force) must point **vertically downwards** toward the center of the loop.

**(ii) Two forces:**
1. Weight of the marble ($mg$) acting downwards.
2. Normal reaction force ($R$) from the track acting downwards on the marble.

**(iii) Deduce speed is approx $3.0\text{ ms}^{-1}$:**
**Explanation:** Use the Principle of Conservation of Energy. The potential energy at $A$ ($mgh_A$) is converted into kinetic energy at $C$ ($1/2 mv_C^2$) plus the potential energy at $C$ ($mgh_C$).
1. Total Energy at $A = mgh_A = 0.050 \times 9.81 \times 0.80 = 0.3924\text{ J}$.
2. Energy at $C = mgh_C + 1/2 mv_C^2 = (0.050 \times 9.81 \times 0.35) + 0.5 \times 0.050 \times v^2$.
3. $0.3924 = 0.171675 + 0.025v^2$.
4. $0.220725 = 0.025v^2 \implies v^2 = 8.829 \implies v \approx 2.97\text{ ms}^{-1} \approx 3.0\text{ ms}^{-1}$.

**(iv) Resultant force and reaction force:**
1. **Resultant force ($F_{net}$):** The resultant force at the top of the loop is the centripetal force required to keep the marble in a circle:
   $F_{net} = \frac{mv^2}{r}$ where $r = \text{diameter} / 2 = 0.175\text{ m}$.
   $F_{net} = \frac{0.050 \times (2.97)^2}{0.175} \approx \frac{0.441}{0.175} \approx 2.52\text{ N}$.
2. **Reaction force ($R$):** At point $C$, both gravity and the normal reaction act downward:
   $F_{net} = mg + R$
   $2.52 = (0.050 \times 9.81) + R$
   $2.52 = 0.49 + R$
   $R = 2.52 - 0.49 = 2.03\text{ N}$.
