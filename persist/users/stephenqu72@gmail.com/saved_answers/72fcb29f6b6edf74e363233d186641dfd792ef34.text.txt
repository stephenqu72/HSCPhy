To solve this problem, we use Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion, which states that the square of the orbital period $T$ is proportional to the cube of the orbital radius $r$:
$$\frac{T_X^2}{r_X^3} = \frac{T_Y^2}{r_Y^3}$$

### Step 1: Determine the orbital period of Planet $X$ ($T_X$)
We are told that in $5$ years, Planet $X$ rotates through $120^\circ$. Since a full orbit is $360^\circ$:
$$\frac{120^\circ}{360^\circ} = \frac{1}{3} \text{ of an orbit}$$
If it takes $5$ years to complete $\frac{1}{3}$ of an orbit, the total orbital period $T_X$ is:
$$T_X = 5 \times 3 = 15 \text{ years}$$

### Step 2: Use Kepler's Third Law to find the orbital period of Planet $Y$ ($T_Y$)
We are given the ratio of the radii $r_X : r_Y = 2 : 5$, which means $\frac{r_X}{r_Y} = \frac{2}{5}$.
Rearranging Kepler's Law:
$$\left( \frac{T_Y}{T_X} \right)^2 = \left( \frac{r_Y}{r_X} \right)^3$$
Substitute the known values:
$$\left( \frac{T_Y}{15} \right)^2 = \left( \frac{5}{2} \right)^3$$
$$\left( \frac{T_Y}{15} \right)^2 = \frac{125}{8} = 15.625$$
Take the square root of both sides:
$$\frac{T_Y}{15} = \sqrt{15.625} \approx 3.9528$$
$$T_Y = 15 \times 3.9528 \approx 59.29 \text{ years}$$

**Final Answer:**
It takes Planet $Y$ approximately **$59.3$ years** to complete one orbit.
