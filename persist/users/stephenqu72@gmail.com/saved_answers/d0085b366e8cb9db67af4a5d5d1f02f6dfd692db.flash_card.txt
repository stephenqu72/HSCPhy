### Front
How do you calculate the electrostatic force ($F$) on a charge ($q$) placed between two parallel plates with a potential difference ($V$) and separation distance ($d$)?

### Back
- Key idea: The electric field ($E$) is uniform; force is the product of charge and field strength.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F = qE = \frac{qV}{d}$
- When to use it: When finding the force on a charged particle in a uniform electric field.
- Common trap: Failing to convert separation distance ($d$) into SI units (meters).
