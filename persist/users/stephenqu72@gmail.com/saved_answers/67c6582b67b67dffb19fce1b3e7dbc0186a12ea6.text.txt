To determine the effect on the wire, we must analyze the magnetic field produced by the bar magnet and its interaction with the current-carrying wire.

### Answer
When the current is switched on, the wire will experience a **force causing it to move (deflect) towards the left**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, follow these steps using the principles of electromagnetism:

1.  **Identify the External Magnetic Field ($B$):** The bar magnet creates a magnetic field directed from the North pole to the South pole. Since the magnet is placed such that its side is facing the wire, the magnetic field lines ($B$) pass from the North face of the magnet towards the wire. In the region of the wire, the magnetic field is directed **towards the wire** (horizontally from right to left).
2.  **Identify the Current Direction ($I$):** The diagram shows the current $I$ flowing **upwards** through the vertical wire.
3.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule (Motor Effect):** The force $F$ on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field is determined by the right-hand rule (or Fleming's Left-Hand Rule).
    *   **Thumb:** Points in the direction of the current ($I$) — **Upwards**.
    *   **Fingers:** Point in the direction of the external magnetic field ($B$) — **Towards the left** (from the magnet towards the wire).
    *   **Palm:** The direction the palm faces indicates the direction of the force ($F$).
4.  **Conclusion:** Orienting your hand according to these vectors:
    *   Point your fingers horizontally to the left (towards the wire).
    *   Point your thumb vertically upwards.
    *   Your palm will face **out of the page/towards the left side of the diagram**. Therefore, the magnetic force exerted on the wire will push it away from the magnet, specifically in a direction determined by the cross product of the current vector and the magnetic field vector.

In this specific geometry, the magnetic field $B$ is directed towards the wire, the current $I$ is up, so the force $F = I(\vec{L} \times \vec{B})$ results in a force perpendicular to both, causing the wire to deflect.
