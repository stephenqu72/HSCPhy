### Front
When a projectile is dropped from a vehicle moving at constant velocity, why does the vehicle remain directly above the projectile during its flight?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Galilean relativity and horizontal inertia; the projectile retains the horizontal velocity of the vehicle at the moment of release.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** Newton's First Law (Inertia) and $v_x = \text{constant}$.
- **When to use it:** Problems involving objects released from moving frames of reference (e.g., planes, helicopters, moving carts).
- **Common trap:** Assuming the object "drops straight down" or loses its forward velocity upon release; air resistance is usually ignored unless stated otherwise.
