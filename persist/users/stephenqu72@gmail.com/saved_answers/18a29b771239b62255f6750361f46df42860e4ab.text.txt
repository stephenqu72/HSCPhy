To solve this problem, we use Kepler's Third Law of Planetary Motion, which relates the orbital period to the orbital radius.

### Part (a) Calculation

**1. Identify Constants and Variables:**
*   Orbital period $T = 1 \text{ hour } 40 \text{ minutes} = 100 \text{ minutes} = 6000 \text{ seconds}$.
*   Gravitational constant $G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}$.
*   Mass of Earth $M = 5.97 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$.
*   Radius of Earth $R_E \approx 6371 \text{ km} = 6.371 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$.

**2. Use Kepler's Third Law formula:**
$$T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{GM}$$
Rearranging for $r$ (orbital radius from the center of Earth):
$$r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{GMT^2}{4\pi^2}}$$

**3. Substitute the values:**
$$r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{(6.674 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24}) \times (6000)^2}{4\pi^2}}$$
$$r \approx \sqrt[3]{1.442 \times 10^{22}} \approx 2.434 \times 10^7 \text{ m} = 24,340 \text{ km}$$

**4. Calculate Altitude ($h$):**
The altitude is the distance from the Earth's surface, so subtract the Earth's radius:
$$h = r - R_E = 24,340 \text{ km} - 6,371 \text{ km} \approx 17,969 \text{ km}$$
*(Note: Given the context of the diagram showing LEO altitudes, the satellite is in a LEO orbit. The calculation above provides the precise value. In many HSC contexts, you might use $GM = 3.98 \times 10^{14} \text{ m}^3\text{s}^{-2}$ for the standard gravitational parameter.)*

### Part (b) Benefits
Since the satellite is in a Low Earth Orbit (LEO), two benefits include:
1.  **Lower Latency:** Because the satellite is closer to the surface, the time taken for a signal to travel to the satellite and back is significantly reduced compared to GEO satellites, making it ideal for high-speed communications.
2.  **Higher Resolution Imaging:** Being closer to the Earth allows for high-resolution photography and Earth observation, which is beneficial for meteorological monitoring and surveillance.
