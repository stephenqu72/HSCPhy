To find the angular velocity of the racing car, we use the relationship between linear velocity ($v$), angular velocity ($\omega$), and the radius ($r$) of the circular path:

$$v = r\omega$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given values:**
    *   Linear velocity ($v$) = $110\text{ km h}^{-1}$
    *   Radius ($r$) = $200.0\text{ m}$

2.  **Convert units:**
    The options provide the angular velocity in units of $\text{rad h}^{-1}$. Since the linear velocity is in $\text{km h}^{-1}$ and the radius is in meters, we must convert the radius from meters to kilometers to ensure the units are consistent.
    *   $r = 200.0\text{ m} = 0.2000\text{ km}$

3.  **Rearrange the formula to solve for $\omega$:**
    $$\omega = \frac{v}{r}$$

4.  **Perform the calculation:**
    $$\omega = \frac{110\text{ km h}^{-1}}{0.2000\text{ km}}$$
    $$\omega = 550\text{ rad h}^{-1}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, the correct answer is **(D)**.
