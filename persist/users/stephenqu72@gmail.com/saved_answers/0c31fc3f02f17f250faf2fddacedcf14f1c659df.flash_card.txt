### Front
What is Hubble’s Law and how is it derived from observational data?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Galaxies recede from Earth at a velocity directly proportional to their distance; this signifies a uniformly expanding universe.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $v = H_0d$; $H_0$ (Hubble Constant) is the gradient of a velocity ($km\,s^{-1}$) vs. distance ($Mpc$) graph.
- **When to use it:** To determine the recessional velocity of a distant galaxy or to estimate the rate of cosmic expansion ($H_0$).
- **Common trap:** Confusing the units ($km\,s^{-1}Mpc^{-1}$) or failing to draw the line of best fit through the origin $(0,0)$.
