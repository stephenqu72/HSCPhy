To determine the direction of the force acting on the wire, we use the **Right-Hand Rule** for the motor effect (also known as Fleming's Left-Hand Rule).

### Answer
The correct answer is **A. Up the page**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, follow these steps:

1.  **Identify the direction of the Magnetic Field ($B$):**
    The dots ($\bullet$) represent a magnetic field pointing **out of the page** towards you.

2.  **Identify the direction of the Conventional Current ($I$):**
    The diagram labels the left side with a plus ($+$) and the right side with a minus ($-$). Conventional current flows from positive to negative, so the current is moving from **left to right**.

3.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule (for force):**
    *   Point your fingers of your right hand in the direction of the current (to the right).
    *   Curl your fingers to point in the direction of the magnetic field (out of the page).
    *   Your thumb now points in the direction of the force.
    *   *Alternatively, using Fleming's Left-Hand Rule:*
        *   **FBI:** Point your **F**orefinger (first finger) in the direction of the Magnetic Field (out of the page).
        *   Point your **B**-middle finger (second finger) in the direction of the Current (to the right).
        *   Your **I**-thumb will point **up the page**.

Since your thumb is pointing towards the top of the page, the magnetic force exerted on the wire acts to move the wire **up the page**.
