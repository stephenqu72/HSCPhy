### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) Down, Down, Up**.

### Explanation
To solve this problem, you need to understand the quark composition of nucleons (protons and neutrons) and the charges associated with each quark flavor.

1.  **Recall Quark Charges:**
    *   An **Up ($u$)** quark has a charge of $+ \frac{2}{3}e$.
    *   A **Down ($d$)** quark has a charge of $- \frac{1}{3}e$.

2.  **Calculate the Charge for a Neutron:**
    A neutron is electrically neutral, meaning its total charge must be $0$. We can test the combinations:
    *   **For (A) Down, Down, Up ($ddu$):**
        $$\text{Total charge} = (-\frac{1}{3}) + (-\frac{1}{3}) + (+\frac{2}{3}) = 0$$
        Since the sum is $0$, this correctly represents a neutron.

    *   **For (B) Up, Up, Down ($uud$):**
        $$\text{Total charge} = (+\frac{2}{3}) + (+\frac{2}{3}) + (-\frac{1}{3}) = +1$$
        This is the composition of a proton.

    *   **For (C) and (D):** These involve other quarks (Strange or Charm) which are not part of the standard composition for nucleons in the ground state.

Therefore, the neutron consists of two down quarks and one up quark ($udd$).
