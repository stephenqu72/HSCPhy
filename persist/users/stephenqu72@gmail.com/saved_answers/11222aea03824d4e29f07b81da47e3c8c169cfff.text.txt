To answer this question, we must apply the definition of an **inertial frame of reference** from physics.

### Answer
The correct answer is **A: a bus travelling at constant velocity**.

### Explanation
An **inertial frame of reference** is a frame in which Newton’s First Law of Motion holds true. This means that an object at rest will remain at rest, and an object in motion will continue to move at a constant velocity (same speed and same direction) unless acted upon by a net external force.

Mathematically, for a frame to be inertial, the acceleration of the frame must be zero ($a = 0$). Let's analyze the options:

*   **A. A bus travelling at constant velocity:** Since the velocity is constant, the acceleration is zero ($a = 0$). This satisfies the requirement for an inertial frame of reference.
*   **B. An express train that is accelerating:** Because the train is accelerating, it is a non-inertial frame. In this frame, objects would appear to experience "fictitious forces" (e.g., you feel pushed back into your seat).
*   **C. A car turning a corner at a constant speed:** Even though the speed is constant, the *direction* is changing. A change in direction constitutes an acceleration (centripetal acceleration). Therefore, this is a non-inertial frame.
*   **D. A roller-coaster speeding up while heading down a slope:** This object has both a changing speed and a changing direction, meaning it is accelerating. Therefore, it is a non-inertial frame.

In summary, only the frame with $a = 0$ qualifies as an inertial frame of reference.
