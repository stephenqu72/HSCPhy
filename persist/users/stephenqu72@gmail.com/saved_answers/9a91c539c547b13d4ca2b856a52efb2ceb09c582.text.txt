### Answer and Explanation

**(a) The Ultraviolet Catastrophe (3 marks)**
*   **The Issue:** The Rayleigh-Jeans law predicts that the intensity of radiation emitted by a black body increases towards infinity as the wavelength decreases ($\lambda \rightarrow 0$). This is known as the "ultraviolet catastrophe."
*   **Physical Implication:** This creates a paradox where an object would emit an infinite amount of energy at high frequencies, which violates the law of conservation of energy.
*   **Conclusion:** This failure indicated that the existing classical model of physics, which treated light purely as a continuous wave and assumed energy could be distributed in any arbitrary amount, was fundamentally incomplete.

**(b) Max Planck’s Contribution (3 marks)**
*   **The Scientist:** Max Planck.
*   **The Solution:** Planck proposed that energy is not continuous but is instead emitted or absorbed in discrete "packets" or "quanta." 
*   **The Mechanism:** He introduced the formula $E = nhf$, where $h$ is Planck's constant. By restricting the oscillators to discrete energy levels, he prevented the "catastrophe" at short wavelengths because the probability of emitting high-energy (short wavelength) photons is suppressed by the exponential Boltzmann factor. This marked the birth of quantum mechanics.

**(c) Experimental Curve (1 mark)**
To draw the experimental curve on the graph:
*   Start at the origin $(0,0)$.
*   Rise to a peak intensity at a specific wavelength (determined by Wien’s Displacement Law).
*   Follow the Rayleigh-Jeans curve closely at long wavelengths (the infrared region).
*   *Note: Your drawn curve should show a peak and then fall back toward zero as it approaches shorter wavelengths, effectively "bending" away from the infinite climb of the Rayleigh-Jeans line.*

---

### How to approach this in an exam:
1.  **For (a):** Always use the term "Ultraviolet Catastrophe." Examiners look for this specific keyword. Mention that it predicted infinite energy at low wavelengths.
2.  **For (b):** Ensure you link Planck to the concept of **quantization**. Explain that energy is not continuous; that is the key conceptual pivot from classical to quantum physics.
3.  **For (c):** When drawing, ensure your curve passes through the origin $(0,0)$. The Rayleigh-Jeans curve does not reach the origin; the experimental curve must.
