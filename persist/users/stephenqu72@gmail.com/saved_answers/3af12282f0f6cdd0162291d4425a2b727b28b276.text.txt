To solve this question, we must understand the physical phenomenon occurring when a charged particle moves through a magnetic field and experiences a force.

### Answer
The correct answer is **(A) Motor effect**.

### Explanation
1.  **Understanding the Physics**: When a current-carrying conductor (or a moving charged particle) is placed in an external magnetic field, it experiences a force known as the **magnetic force** (or Lorentz force). This phenomenon is the fundamental principle behind the **Motor Effect**. 
2.  **The Right-Hand Palm Rule**: The right-hand palm rule is a mnemonic used specifically to determine the direction of the force exerted on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field. 
    *   The thumb points in the direction of the current (the flow of positive charge).
    *   The fingers point in the direction of the external magnetic field.
    *   The palm faces the direction of the magnetic force $F$ acting on the conductor.
3.  **Evaluating the Distractors**:
    *   **(B) Faraday's Law** relates to the induction of an electromotive force (EMF) when there is a *changing* magnetic flux.
    *   **(C) Lenz's Law** describes the direction of an *induced* current, which always opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it.
    *   **(D) Back EMF** is an induced EMF that opposes the change in current in an electric motor, also governed by Lenz's Law.

Since the question describes a force acting on a moving charge (current) in an external magnetic field, it is a direct application of the motor effect.
