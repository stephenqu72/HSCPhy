### Answer

**a. Explanation:**
According to Einstein’s theory of special relativity, length contraction occurs only in the direction of motion relative to an observer. Because the spaceship is moving at a relativistic speed ($0.7c$) relative to the technician, the length of the ship is contracted in the direction of its travel. However, because there is no relative motion in the perpendicular direction (the width), the transverse dimensions of the ship remain unaffected and are measured to be the same as their proper length.

**b. Calculation:**
Given:
- Observed length $L = 135\text{ m}$
- Velocity $v = 0.7c$
- Proper length $L_0 = ?$

First, calculate the Lorentz factor, $\gamma$:
$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.7)^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.49}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.51}} \approx 1.40028$$

Using the length contraction formula $L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma}$:
$$135 = \frac{L_0}{1.40028}$$
$$L_0 = 135 \times 1.40028 \approx 189.04\text{ m}$$

The length of the spaceship when stationary on Earth was approximately **189.04 m**.
