### Front
How does the speed of a projectile change throughout its flight, and what does its speed-time graph look like?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Speed is the magnitude of the velocity vector; it is at a minimum at the peak of the trajectory but never reaches zero (unless launched vertically).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** $S(t) = \sqrt{(v \cos \theta)^2 + (v \sin \theta - gt)^2}$. The graph is a smooth, parabolic-like curve (a hyperbola).
- **When to use it:** When asked to distinguish between velocity-time and speed-time graphs for projectile motion.
- **Common trap:** Assuming the speed-time graph is V-shaped (sharp corner) or that speed reaches zero at the peak.
