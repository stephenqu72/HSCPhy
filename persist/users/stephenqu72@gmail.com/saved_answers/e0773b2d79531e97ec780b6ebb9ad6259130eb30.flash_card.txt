### Front
For a satellite in a circular orbit, how does total mechanical energy ($E_{total}$) relate to gravitational potential energy ($U$)?

### Back
- Key idea: Total energy of an orbiting satellite is always half of its gravitational potential energy.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E_{total} = \frac{1}{2}U = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$
- When to use it: To calculate the energy required to change orbits or the total energy of a satellite in a stable circular path.
- Common trap: Forgetting the negative sign; the total energy is negative because the satellite is in a bound state.
