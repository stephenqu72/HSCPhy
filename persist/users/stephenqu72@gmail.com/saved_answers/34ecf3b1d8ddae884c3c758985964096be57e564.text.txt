### Answer

**(a)** Kepler’s First Law states that planets (or moons) move in elliptical orbits with the central body at one of the two foci. Because the orbit is elliptical rather than perfectly circular, the distance between the moon and the planet varies throughout the orbit. The value $5.67 \times 10^8\text{ m}$ represents the *average* radius (semi-major axis), not the instantaneous distance.

**(b)**
**Step 1: Calculate the orbital period ($T$)**
The circumference of the orbit is $C = 2\pi r$. Given $v = 2.50\text{ km s}^{-1} = 2500\text{ m s}^{-1}$ and $r = 5.67 \times 10^8\text{ m}$:
$$T = \frac{2\pi r}{v} = \frac{2 \times \pi \times 5.67 \times 10^8}{2500} \approx 1,424,800\text{ seconds}$$
(Or approximately $16.49$ days).

**Step 2: Calculate the mass of the planet ($M$)**
Using the formula for orbital velocity $v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$, we rearrange to solve for $M$:
$$M = \frac{v^2 r}{G}$$
Substituting the known values ($G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ m}^3\text{ kg}^{-1}\text{ s}^{-2}$):
$$M = \frac{(2500)^2 \times 5.67 \times 10^8}{6.67 \times 10^{-11}}$$
$$M = \frac{6.25 \times 10^6 \times 5.67 \times 10^8}{6.67 \times 10^{-11}} \approx 5.31 \times 10^{25}\text{ kg}$$
