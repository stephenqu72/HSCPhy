### Question 5: Calculation of Magnetic Field Strength

**1. Identify the variables:**
*   Number of conductors ($n$) = $7$
*   Current per conductor ($I_{single}$) = $0.50\text{ A}$
*   Total current ($I_{total}$) = $n \times I_{single} = 7 \times 0.50\text{ A} = 3.5\text{ A}$
*   Length ($L$) = $0.35\text{ m}$
*   Force ($F$) = $7.3\text{ mN} = 7.3 \times 10^{-3}\text{ N}$
*   Angle ($\theta$) between current and magnetic field = $90^\circ$ (perpendicular), so $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$

**2. Formula:**
The force on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field is given by:
$$F = B I L \sin(\theta)$$
Rearranging to solve for $B$:
$$B = \frac{F}{I \times L}$$

**3. Calculation:**
$$B = \frac{7.3 \times 10^{-3}\text{ N}}{3.5\text{ A} \times 0.35\text{ m}}$$
$$B = \frac{7.3 \times 10^{-3}}{1.225}$$
$$B \approx 5.959 \times 10^{-3}\text{ T} \approx 6.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ T}$$

**Answer:** The correct option is **B**.
