To determine the direction of the force acting on the current-carrying cable, we use the **Right-Hand Palm Rule** (or Fleming’s Left-Hand Rule).

### Explanation

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   **Current ($I$):** The symbol $\odot$ indicates that the current is flowing **out of the page** (towards you).
    *   **Magnetic Field ($B$):** The arrows show the magnetic field lines pointing **down and to the right**.
    *   **Force ($F$):** We need to determine the direction of the force, which is given by the formula:
        $$F = BIL \sin(\theta)$$
        The direction of the force is perpendicular to both the current and the magnetic field.

2.  **Apply the Right-Hand Palm Rule:**
    *   Point your **thumb** in the direction of the current (out of the page/towards your face).
    *   Point your **fingers** in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$) lines (down and to the right).
    *   Your **palm** will now be facing in the direction of the force ($F$).

3.  **Visualization:**
    *   With your thumb pointing at you and your fingers angled toward the bottom-right, your palm is forced to face toward the **top-left** direction.
    *   Looking at the provided options, arrow **Z** points in the top-left direction, perpendicular to the magnetic field lines.

**Conclusion:** The force acts in the direction indicated by arrow **Z**.

The correct answer is **D) Z**.
