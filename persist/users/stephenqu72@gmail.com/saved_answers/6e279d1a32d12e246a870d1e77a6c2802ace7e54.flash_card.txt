### Front
Explain the roles of the four fundamental forces in maintaining the structure and stability of an atom.

### Back
- **Key idea:** Atoms are held together by the electromagnetic force (electrons to nucleus) and the strong nuclear force (binds nucleons despite proton repulsion). The weak force dictates nuclear stability (decay), while gravity is negligible at the atomic scale.
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** Use when asked to explain atomic or nuclear stability/structure.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to explicitly state that the strong nuclear force must be strong enough to *overcome* the electrostatic repulsion between protons in the nucleus.
