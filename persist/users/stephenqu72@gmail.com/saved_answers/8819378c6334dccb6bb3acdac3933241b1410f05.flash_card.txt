### Front
What is the energy released by the annihilation of an electron and a positron?

### Back
- Key idea: Total mass of both particles is converted into energy ($E=mc^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E = 2 \times m_e c^2 \approx 1.022 \text{ MeV}$.
- When to use it: Whenever a matter-antimatter pair annihilation is described.
- Common trap: Forgetting that *both* particles contribute to the total mass, or using the mass of only one particle instead of the total pair.
