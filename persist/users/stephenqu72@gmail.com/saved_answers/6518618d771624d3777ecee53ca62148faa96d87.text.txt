### Model Answer

**Millikan’s Oil Drop Experiment:**
Robert Millikan’s experiment provided the definitive proof that electric charge is **quantized**. By measuring the motion of tiny charged oil droplets between charged plates in the presence of an electric field, he demonstrated that the charge $q$ of any droplet was always an integer multiple of a fundamental unit of charge $e$ (i.e., $q = ne$). This confirmed that the electron is a fundamental particle with a discrete, constant charge, providing essential data for calculating the mass of the electron when combined with Thomson’s charge-to-mass ratio. This solidified the existence of the electron as a subatomic building block of the atom.

**Schrödinger’s Contribution:**
Erwin Schrödinger moved physics beyond the limitations of the Bohr model (which treated electrons as particles in fixed circular orbits). He developed the **wave equation** (the Schrödinger equation), which describes the electron as a **probability wave**. This transitioned the atomic model from "orbits" to "orbitals"—regions of space where there is a high probability of finding an electron. His work established the **Quantum Mechanical Model** of the atom, introducing the concept that we cannot know the precise position and momentum of an electron simultaneously (Heisenberg’s uncertainty principle), thus defining the current cloud-like model of electron distribution.
