To solve this question, we must understand the evolutionary stage of a **T Tauri star** and how it appears on a Hertzsprung-Russell (H-R) diagram.

### Explanation

1.  **Understand T Tauri Stars:** T Tauri stars are "pre-main sequence" stars. They are protostars that have not yet begun nuclear fusion in their cores. Instead, they are gaining energy through gravitational contraction. Because they are still contracting, they are relatively cool (low temperature) but have large surface areas, which makes them quite luminous compared to main-sequence stars of similar temperature.
2.  **Analyze the H-R Diagram Positions:**
    *   **Main Sequence:** The diagonal line running from top-left to bottom-right represents stars burning hydrogen into helium in their cores.
    *   **Position C:** This region represents hot, highly luminous stars (Blue Giants/Supergiants).
    *   **Position B:** This region represents cool, luminous stars (Red Giants).
    *   **Position D:** This region represents cool, low-luminosity stars (Main sequence or Red Dwarfs).
    *   **Position A:** This region is located above the main sequence in the "cool" side of the diagram (lower temperatures, but higher luminosity than main sequence stars of the same temperature). 
3.  **Conclusion:** As T Tauri stars contract, they move from the right toward the main sequence. They are found in the region of high luminosity for their low surface temperature, which places them in the area designated as **A**.

The correct answer is **A**.
