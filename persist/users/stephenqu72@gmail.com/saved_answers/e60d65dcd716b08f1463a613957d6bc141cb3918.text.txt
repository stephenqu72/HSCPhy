To find the maximum speed a vehicle can safely travel around a flat curve, we rely on the centripetal force being provided entirely by the frictional force between the tires and the road.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the physics principles:**
    *   The centripetal force required for circular motion is $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$.
    *   The maximum frictional force available is $F_f = \mu N$, where $N$ is the normal force. On a flat road, $N = mg$.
    *   For the maximum speed, we set the centripetal force equal to the maximum frictional force:
        $$\frac{mv^2}{r} = \mu mg$$

2.  **Simplify the equation:**
    Notice that the mass $m$ cancels out from both sides:
    $$\frac{v^2}{r} = \mu g$$
    Rearranging to solve for velocity $v$:
    $$v = \sqrt{\mu gr}$$

3.  **Plug in the values:**
    *   $\mu = 0.4$
    *   $g \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$
    *   $r = 25 \, \text{m}$
    
    $$v = \sqrt{0.4 \times 9.8 \times 25}$$
    $$v = \sqrt{98}$$
    $$v \approx 9.9 \, \text{m/s}$$

4.  **Convert units:**
    The answer needs to be in $\text{km/h}$. To convert from $\text{m/s}$ to $\text{km/h}$, multiply by $3.6$:
    $$9.9 \, \text{m/s} \times 3.6 = 35.64 \, \text{km/h}$$

Rounding to the nearest option provided, the correct speed is approximately $36 \, \text{km/h}$.

**Correct Option: D**
