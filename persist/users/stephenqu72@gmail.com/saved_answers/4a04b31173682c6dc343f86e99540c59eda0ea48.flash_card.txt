### Front
In a conical pendulum or circular motion experiment where a hanging mass ($M$) provides tension to a rotating stopper ($m$), what does the gradient of the graph of $Mg$ vs $1/T^2$ represent?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The gradient represents the relationship $4\pi^2mR$.
- **Formula / law:** $Mg = (4\pi^2mR) \times \frac{1}{T^2}$
- **When to use it:** When asked to derive the mass of the rotating object ($m$) from experimental period ($T$) and radius ($R$) data.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert the mass $m$ from kg to grams or incorrectly assuming the hanging mass $M$ equals the rotating mass $m$.
