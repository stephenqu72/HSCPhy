To find the escape velocity of the asteroid, we use the standard escape velocity formula:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

### 1. Identify the missing mass ($M$)
To find the mass of Proxima Centauri, we use Kepler’s Third Law. The graph shows the period of the orbit ($T$). From $t=19$ to $t=57$ days, two full cycles occur.
- Period $T = 38 - 19 = 19$ days.
- Convert $T$ to seconds: $T = 19 \times 24 \times 3600 = 1,641,600 \text{ s}$.
- Radius $r = 2.55 \times 10^{13} \text{ m}$.

Kepler's Third Law states:
$$T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{GM}$$
Rearranging for $GM$:
$$GM = \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{T^2}$$

### 2. Substitute into the escape velocity formula
Substitute $GM$ into the $v_e$ equation:
$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2}{r} \cdot \left( \frac{4\pi^2 r^3}{T^2} \right)} = \sqrt{\frac{8\pi^2 r^2}{T^2}} = \frac{2\pi r \sqrt{2}}{T}$$

### 3. Calculation
$$v_e = \frac{2 \cdot \pi \cdot (2.55 \times 10^{13}) \cdot \sqrt{2}}{1,641,600}$$
$$v_e \approx \frac{2 \cdot 3.14159 \cdot 2.55 \times 10^{13} \cdot 1.4142}{1,641,600}$$
$$v_e \approx \frac{2.265 \times 10^{14}}{1,641,600}$$
$$v_e \approx 1.379 \times 10^8 \text{ ms}^{-1}$$

Rounding to three significant figures, we get $1.38 \times 10^8 \text{ ms}^{-1}$.
