### Front
How do you determine the spring constant ($k$) and elastic potential energy ($U$) from a force-compression graph?

### Back
- Key idea: $k$ is the gradient of the $F$ vs $x$ graph; $U$ is the area under the graph.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Hooke’s Law ($F=kx$) and Elastic Potential Energy ($U = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$).
- When to use it: Any mechanics problem involving ideal springs or linear elastic materials.
- Common trap: Failing to convert units (e.g., kN to N) or squaring the displacement incorrectly ($x^2$).
