### Front
On a rotating platform, how do the angular velocity ($\omega$) and linear velocity ($v$) compare for two people at different distances from the center?

### Back
- Key idea: Angular velocity is constant for a rigid body; linear velocity increases with distance from the center.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $v = r\omega$
- When to use it: Circular motion problems involving rotating platforms, carousels, or spinning discs.
- Common trap: Assuming that because the object is moving in a circle, all points have the same linear velocity. (Points further out must travel a larger circumference in the same time, thus having higher $v$).
