### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to recall the historical context of James Chadwick’s 1932 discovery of the neutron. 

1. **The Experimental Setup:** Chadwick bombarded a target of beryllium metal with particles emitted from a radioactive source ($X$). He observed that the beryllium then emitted a highly penetrating radiation.
2. **The Interaction:** When this mysterious, highly penetrating radiation struck a layer of paraffin wax (a substance rich in hydrogen/protons), it ejected protons from the wax. 
3. **The Physics:** By analyzing the conservation of momentum and energy in the collisions between the unknown radiation and the protons, Chadwick concluded that the radiation consisted of neutral particles with a mass approximately equal to that of a proton—these were neutrons.
4. **The Source ($X$):** In his actual experiment, Chadwick used **Polonium-210**, which is a well-known **alpha emitter**. The nuclear reaction occurring at the beryllium target is:
   $${}^{4}_{2}\text{He} + {}^{9}_{4}\text{Be} \rightarrow {}^{12}_{6}\text{C} + {}^{1}_{0}\text{n}$$
   where the alpha particle (${}^{4}_{2}\text{He}$) from the source hits the beryllium (${}^{9}_{4}\text{Be}$) to release a neutron (${}^{1}_{0}\text{n}$).

Therefore, for the neutrons to be produced from the beryllium target, the source $X$ must be an alpha emitter.
