To determine the speed of the proton as it exits the magnetic field, we must apply the physics of charged particles moving in uniform magnetic fields.

### Answer
The correct choice is **B. $1.2 \times 10^3\text{ m s}^{-1}$**.

### Explanation
When a charged particle, such as a proton, enters a uniform magnetic field, it experiences a magnetic force given by the Lorentz force law:
$$F = qvB\sin(\theta)$$

In this specific scenario:
1.  **Direction of Force:** The magnetic force acts perpendicular to the velocity of the proton at all times. Because the force is always perpendicular to the direction of motion, it acts as a **centripetal force**.
2.  **Work Done:** Since the magnetic force is always perpendicular to the displacement (velocity vector) of the particle, the work done by the magnetic field on the proton is zero ($W = F \cdot d \cos(90^\circ) = 0$).
3.  **Kinetic Energy:** According to the Work-Energy Theorem, because the magnetic field does no work on the particle, there is no change in the proton's kinetic energy.
4.  **Speed:** Since kinetic energy remains constant ($KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$), the speed of the proton remains constant throughout its path in the magnetic field.

Because the speed does not change, the proton exits the field with the exact same speed at which it entered. Therefore, the speed remains $1.2 \times 10^3\text{ m s}^{-1}$.
