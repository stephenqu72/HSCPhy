### Answer and Explanation

#### Part a) Calculation
To calculate the average induced EMF, we use Faraday’s Law of Induction, which states that the induced EMF is equal to the negative rate of change of magnetic flux linkage:
$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t}$$
Where:
- $N = 10$ (number of turns)
- $B = 2.0 \times 10^{-2} \text{ T}$
- $A = 1.6 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2$
- $\Delta t = 0.50 \text{ s}$

1. **Calculate the change in flux ($\Delta\Phi$):**
   At position X (outside), the flux is $\Phi_i = 0$.
   At position Y (inside), the flux is $\Phi_f = B \times A = (2.0 \times 10^{-2}) \times (1.6 \times 10^{-3}) = 3.2 \times 10^{-5} \text{ Wb}$.
   $\Delta\Phi = 3.2 \times 10^{-5} - 0 = 3.2 \times 10^{-5} \text{ Wb}$.

2. **Calculate the EMF:**
   $$\varepsilon = 10 \times \frac{3.2 \times 10^{-5}}{0.50}$$
   $$\varepsilon = 6.4 \times 10^{-4} \text{ V}$$
   *(Note: The magnitude is usually requested for average EMF calculations in this context.)*

#### Part b) Sketching the EMF
To determine the shape of the graph, we apply Faraday's Law ($\varepsilon = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$):

1. **Loop outside:** $\Phi = 0$, so $\varepsilon = 0$.
2. **Loop entering:** The flux increases linearly as the area enters the field. Since the rate of change is constant, the EMF will be a **constant non-zero value** (typically negative by Lenz’s Law).
3. **Loop inside:** The flux is constant ($B \times A$), so $\frac{d\Phi}{dt} = 0$. The EMF is $0$.
4. **Loop leaving:** The flux decreases at the same rate as it increased. This produces a **constant non-zero EMF** of the opposite sign to the "entering" phase.
5. **Loop outside:** Back to $0$.

**Sketch description:**
- From start to "entering": line at $0$.
- During "entering": a horizontal line at a constant value (e.g., below the axis).
- During "inside": a horizontal line at $0$.
- During "leaving": a horizontal line at a constant value of the opposite sign (e.g., above the axis).
- During "outside": back to $0$.
