To solve this problem, we must equate the required artificial gravity (centripetal acceleration) to the acceleration due to gravity on Earth, $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1. **Identify the given variables:**
   - Frequency ($f$) = $0.01\text{ Hz}$
   - Required acceleration ($a_c$) = $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$

2. **Recall the formula for centripetal acceleration:**
   The centripetal acceleration is given by $a_c = r\omega^2$, where $r$ is the radius and $\omega$ is the angular velocity in radians per second.

3. **Convert frequency to angular velocity:**
   Angular velocity $\omega$ is related to frequency $f$ by the equation:
   $$\omega = 2\pi f$$
   Substitute the given frequency:
   $$\omega = 2 \times \pi \times 0.01 = 0.02\pi\text{ rad/s}$$

4. **Rearrange the acceleration formula to solve for $r$:**
   $$a_c = r(2\pi f)^2$$
   $$r = \frac{a_c}{(2\pi f)^2}$$

5. **Substitute the values and calculate:**
   $$r = \frac{9.8}{(0.02\pi)^2}$$
   $$r = \frac{9.8}{0.0004\pi^2}$$
   $$r \approx \frac{9.8}{0.0039478}$$
   $$r \approx 2482.4\text{ m}$$

**Final Answer:**
The minimum radius required for the spacecraft is approximately **$2482\text{ meters}$** (or about $2.48\text{ km}$).

To solve this, we treat the normal force experienced by the occupants as the centripetal force required to keep them moving in a circle.
