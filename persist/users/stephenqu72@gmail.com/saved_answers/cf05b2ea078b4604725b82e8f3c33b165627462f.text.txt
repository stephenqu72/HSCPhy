To solve this problem, we must distinguish between angular velocity and instantaneous (linear) velocity for an object in circular motion.

### Answer
1. **Angular Velocity ($\omega$):** The angular velocity of point $X$ is equal to the angular velocity of point $Y$ ($\omega_X = \omega_Y$).
2. **Instantaneous Velocity ($v$):** The instantaneous velocity of point $X$ is twice that of point $Y$ ($v_X = 2v_Y$).

---

### Explanation
To arrive at this answer, consider the physics of rotational motion:

**1. Angular Velocity:**
Angular velocity is defined as the rate of change of the angle swept out by an object in circular motion ($\omega = \frac{\Delta\theta}{\Delta t}$). Since both $X$ and $Y$ are on the same rotating disc, they complete one full revolution in the same amount of time. Therefore, they share the same angular velocity regardless of their distance from the center.

**2. Instantaneous Velocity:**
The linear (instantaneous) velocity $v$ of a point on a rotating body is related to its angular velocity $\omega$ and its radius $r$ (distance from the center) by the formula:
$$v = r\omega$$
Given that the angular velocity $\omega$ is constant for both points, the linear velocity is directly proportional to the radius ($v \propto r$).
* Let the radius of point $Y$ be $r_Y = r$.
* The problem states $X$ is twice as far from the center as $Y$, so $r_X = 2r$.
* Substituting these into the formula:
  * For $Y$: $v_Y = r\omega$
  * For $X$: $v_X = (2r)\omega = 2(r\omega) = 2v_Y$

Thus, point $X$ has twice the instantaneous velocity of point $Y$.
