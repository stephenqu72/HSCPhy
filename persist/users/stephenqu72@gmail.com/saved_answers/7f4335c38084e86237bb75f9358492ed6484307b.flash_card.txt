### Front
According to the Principle of Relativity, how does the validity of Newton’s Second Law change when moving from a stationary frame to an inertial frame moving at a constant relativistic velocity ($v < c$)?

### Back
- Key idea: The laws of physics (including Newton's laws) are identical in all inertial frames of reference.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Principle of Relativity; $F_{net} = ma$
- When to use it: Comparing experimental results between stationary and uniformly moving (constant velocity) frames.
- Common trap: Assuming that high velocities (e.g., $0.75c$) change the fundamental laws of motion; they only affect measurements of time and length.
