To solve this problem, we must analyze the physics of a conical pendulum.

### Part a: Forces Acting on the Ball
There are two forces acting on the ball:
1.  **Gravitational Force ($mg$):** Acting vertically downwards from the center of the ball.
2.  **Tension Force ($T$):** Acting along the string, directed towards the point of suspension.

---

### Part b: Calculate the Speed of the Ball

**1. Identify the Geometry:**
The string length $L = 0.75\text{ m}$ and the angle $\theta = 25^\circ$ with the vertical. The radius $r$ of the horizontal circular path is:
$$r = L \sin(\theta) = 0.75 \sin(25^\circ) \approx 0.317\text{ m}$$

**2. Analyze the Forces:**
The vertical components of the forces must be balanced because there is no vertical acceleration:
$$T \cos(\theta) = mg \implies T = \frac{mg}{\cos(\theta)}$$
The horizontal component of the tension provides the centripetal force required for circular motion:
$$T \sin(\theta) = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

**3. Combine Equations:**
Substitute the expression for $T$ into the centripetal force equation:
$$\left( \frac{mg}{\cos(\theta)} \right) \sin(\theta) = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
$$mg \tan(\theta) = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$
Solving for $v$:
$$v = \sqrt{rg \tan(\theta)}$$

**4. Perform the Calculation:**
Using $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$:
$$v = \sqrt{(0.317) \times (9.8) \times \tan(25^\circ)}$$
$$v = \sqrt{0.317 \times 9.8 \times 0.4663}$$
$$v = \sqrt{1.4485}$$
$$v \approx 1.20\text{ m/s}$$

The speed of the ball is approximately **$1.20\text{ m/s}$**.
