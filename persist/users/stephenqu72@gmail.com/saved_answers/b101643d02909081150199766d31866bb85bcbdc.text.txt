### Answer
The brightness of the globe is determined by the total current flowing through the circuit, which is the vector sum of the current provided by the battery ($I_{battery}$) and the induced current ($I_{ind}$) generated by the changing magnetic flux.

The brightness changes as follows:
1.  **Initial entry:** As the loop enters the magnetic field, there is a change in magnetic flux ($\Delta \Phi_B$). According to Faraday's Law, an EMF is induced. By Lenz’s Law, the induced current flows in a direction that opposes the change in flux. In this case, the induced EMF acts against the battery's EMF, reducing the total current and causing the brightness to **decrease**.
2.  **Fully inside the field:** As the loop moves further into the field, the rate of change of flux decreases as the area enclosed by the field increases. Once the loop is fully inside the uniform magnetic field, the magnetic flux through the loop is constant ($\Delta \Phi_B = 0$). Consequently, the induced EMF drops to zero. The current is provided solely by the battery, causing the brightness to **increase** back to its baseline level.

### Explanation and Physics Principles
To solve this, we apply the following HSC Physics concepts:

*   **Faraday's Law:** $\varepsilon = - \frac{\Delta \Phi_B}{\Delta t}$. An EMF is induced only when the magnetic flux through the loop changes.
*   **Lenz's Law:** The induced EMF creates a current that opposes the change in flux. 
    *   As the loop enters the field, the flux into the page increases. The induced current creates a field *out* of the page, which opposes the battery current, reducing the total current and brightness.
*   **Magnetic Flux ($\Phi_B = B A_\perp$):** 
    *   At point $P$ (entry), the area ($A_\perp$) inside the field is increasing at a high rate as the circular loop enters, leading to a maximum induced EMF (opposing the battery).
    *   As the loop becomes fully submerged, the rate of change of the enclosed area decreases to zero. Because the magnetic field is **uniform**, once the loop is fully inside, the flux becomes constant, the induced EMF becomes zero, and the brightness reflects only the battery's power.
*   **The Graph:** The dip in the graph corresponds to the period where the loop is entering/traversing the boundary. The subsequent rise occurs as the loop completes its entry into the uniform field, where the counter-acting induced EMF disappears, returning the brightness to the level dictated by the battery alone.
