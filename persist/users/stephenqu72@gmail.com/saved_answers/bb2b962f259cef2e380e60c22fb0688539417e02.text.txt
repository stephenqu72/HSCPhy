To determine the correct direction of motion and the direction of the magnetic field, we must apply the principles of the Lorentz force acting on a charged particle.

### 1. Determining the Direction of Motion
The electron is moving in a spiral path. Since the electron is "slowing down" due to air resistance, its kinetic energy decreases, causing the radius of its circular path to decrease over time. By looking at the spiral, it starts from the outside and winds inwards. If you trace the path from the outer start towards the center, the motion is **clockwise**.

### 2. Determining the Direction of the Magnetic Field
We use the **Right-Hand Palm Rule** (or the Right-Hand Rule for forces). Remember that the rule is defined for a **positive** charge. Since an electron is **negative**, we must reverse the direction given by the rule.

*   **Force ($F$):** The magnetic force acts as the centripetal force, always pointing toward the center of the circle. At any point on the path, the force vector points inward.
*   **Velocity ($v$):** This is tangential to the path.
*   **Right-Hand Rule:** 
    *   Point your thumb in the direction of velocity ($v$).
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$).
    *   Your palm will face the direction of the force ($F$) for a *positive* charge. Since the electron is negative, the force will be on the back of your hand (or you simply flip the result).

Let's test **Option (B)**:
*   If the motion is clockwise and we assume the field is **out of the page**:
*   For an electron at the "top" of the circle, $v$ is to the right. 
*   If $B$ is out of the page, the Right-Hand Rule (for a positive charge) would give a force pointing *downwards*. 
*   Because the electron is negative, the force is the opposite: **upwards**, towards the center of the circle. This matches the required centripetal force.

Therefore, the electron is moving clockwise in a magnetic field directed out of the plane of the paper.

**Correct Option: (B)**

### How to solve this type of problem:
1.  **Identify the spiral direction:** Trace the path from the start point to the end point to determine if the rotation is clockwise or anti-clockwise.
2.  **Locate the Force:** Remember that in circular motion, the magnetic force is the centripetal force, which always points toward the center of curvature.
3.  **Apply the Right-Hand Rule:** Align your hand such that your thumb is the velocity and your palm is the force. If the particle is an electron, perform the standard rule and reverse the final magnetic field direction (or simply use your left hand for the force/velocity, keeping in mind the field direction stays consistent).
