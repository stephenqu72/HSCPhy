### Front
How do you determine the instantaneous direction of a particle on a transverse wave given the wave's direction of propagation?

### Back
- Key idea: Visualize the wave profile shifting slightly in the direction of wave velocity; the vertical displacement of the particle at its new horizontal position indicates its direction.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: To identify if a particle on a displacement-time or displacement-position graph is moving upwards or downwards.
- Common trap: Confusing the direction of the *wave propagation* with the direction of the *particle oscillation* (particles only move up and down, never left or right).
