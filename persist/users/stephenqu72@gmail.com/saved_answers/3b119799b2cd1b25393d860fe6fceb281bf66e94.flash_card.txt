### Front
How do you determine the minimum energy of a photon given a range of wavelengths?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Energy is inversely proportional to wavelength ($E \propto 1/\lambda$).
- **Formula:** $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$ (where $h \approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J s}$, $c \approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}$).
- **When to use it:** When calculating the energy of a photon associated with a specific wavelength or frequency.
- **Common trap:** Using the wrong wavelength; remember that **minimum** energy requires the **maximum** wavelength.
