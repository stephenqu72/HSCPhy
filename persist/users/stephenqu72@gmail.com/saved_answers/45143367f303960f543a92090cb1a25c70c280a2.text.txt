### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
To determine the speed $v$, one can adjust the electric field $E$ (using the voltage source) and the magnetic field $B$ simultaneously such that the beam passes through the apparatus undeflected ($d=0$). Under this condition, the electric force equals the magnetic force: $qE = qvB$. Thus, the velocity is $v = \frac{E}{B}$. Since $E = \frac{V}{d_{plates}}$, by knowing the voltage $V$ and plate separation, $v$ can be calculated.

#### Part (b)
The radius of curvature $r$ for a particle in a magnetic field is given by $r = \frac{mv}{qB}$, which implies $\frac{1}{r} = \frac{qB}{mv}$.
Using the formula $r = \frac{d}{1 - \cos(2\theta)}$, we calculate $\frac{1}{r} = \frac{1 - \cos(2\theta)}{d}$.
*Given the small angle approximation is not immediately obvious, we calculate $\frac{1}{r}$ using the provided $d$ and geometry.* However, since we are given $d$ and $w = 0.1$, $\tan\theta = \frac{d}{w}$. Thus $\theta = \arctan(\frac{d}{0.1})$.
For $B = 1.0 \times 10^{-6}$ T, $d = 1.667 \times 10^{-3}$ m:
$\theta = \arctan(0.01667) \approx 0.01667$ rad. $2\theta \approx 0.03334$ rad.
$1 - \cos(0.03334) \approx 0.0005556$.
$\frac{1}{r} = \frac{0.0005556}{1.667 \times 10^{-3}} \approx 0.333$ m$^{-1}$.

| 1.0 | 1.667 | 0.333 |
| 1.5 | 2.502 | 0.500 |

#### Part (c)
From the magnetic force equation $F = qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$, we derive $\frac{1}{r} = \left(\frac{q}{mv}\right)B$.
This is a linear equation of the form $y = mx$, where $y = \frac{1}{r}$ and $x = B$. The gradient is $\frac{q}{mv}$.
1. **Plot:** Plot $\frac{1}{r}$ (y-axis) vs $B$ (x-axis in Tesla).
2. **Gradient ($m$):** Using the values (e.g., $x=5 \times 10^{-6}, y=1.667$): $m = \frac{1.667}{5 \times 10^{-6}} = 3.33 \times 10^5$ C/kg$\cdot$m/s.
3. **Calculation:** Since $m = \frac{q}{mv}$, then $\frac{q}{m} = \text{gradient} \times v$.
Using $v = 10^5$ m/s:
$\frac{q}{m} = 3.33 \times 10^5 \times 10^5 = 3.33 \times 10^{10}$ C/kg.
