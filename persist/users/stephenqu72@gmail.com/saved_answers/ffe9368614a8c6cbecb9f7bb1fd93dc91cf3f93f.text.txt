To solve this problem, we must analyze the forces acting on the charged sphere in equilibrium.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

**1. Identify the given values:**
*   Charge $q = 2.3 \times 10^{-6}\text{ C}$
*   Voltage $V = 62\text{ V}$
*   Distance $d = 3.1\text{ cm} = 0.031\text{ m}$
*   Angle $\theta = 18^\circ$
*   Acceleration due to gravity $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$

**2. Calculate the electric field ($E$):**
Between parallel plates, the electric field is uniform and given by:
$$E = \frac{V}{d} = \frac{62}{0.031} = 2000\text{ N/C}$$

**3. Analyze the forces:**
The sphere is in equilibrium, so the vector sum of forces is zero. The three forces acting on it are:
*   Weight ($mg$) acting downwards.
*   Electric force ($F_E = qE$) acting horizontally.
*   Tension ($T$) acting along the string at an angle of $18^\circ$ from the vertical.

We can relate these forces using trigonometry:
$$\tan(\theta) = \frac{F_E}{mg}$$
Rearranging to solve for mass ($m$):
$$m = \frac{F_E}{g \cdot \tan(\theta)} = \frac{qE}{g \cdot \tan(\theta)}$$

**4. Perform the calculation:**
$$m = \frac{(2.3 \times 10^{-6}) \times 2000}{9.8 \times \tan(18^\circ)}$$
$$m = \frac{0.0046}{9.8 \times 0.3249}$$
$$m \approx \frac{0.0046}{3.184} \approx 0.0014446\text{ kg}$$

Converted to grams:
$$m \approx 1.44\text{ g}$$

**Answer:** The mass of the sphere is approximately $1.44 \times 10^{-3}\text{ kg}$ or $1.44\text{ g}$.
