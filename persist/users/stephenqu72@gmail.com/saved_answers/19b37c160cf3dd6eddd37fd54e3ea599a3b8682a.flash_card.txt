### Front
In projectile motion (ignoring air resistance), how do the direction and magnitude of the velocity and acceleration vectors change throughout the flight?

### Back
- Key idea: Velocity is tangent to the path; acceleration is constant and downward.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a_x = 0$, $a_y = -g$ ($9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ downward).
- When to use it: To determine the direction of vectors at any point (e.g., at peak height, $v_y = 0$ but $a \neq 0$).
- Common trap: Assuming acceleration is zero at the maximum height or that it changes direction along the path.
