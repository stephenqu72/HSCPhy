**Answer:** B. Hadrons

**Explanation:**

To solve this, you need to recall the Standard Model classification of particles, specifically the distinction between quarks and the composite particles they form.

1.  **Quarks** are fundamental particles that experience the strong nuclear force.
2.  Particles made of quarks are held together by the strong force (mediated by gluons). Any composite particle made of quarks is collectively referred to as a **Hadron**.
3.  **Hadrons** are further subdivided into two main types:
    *   **Baryons:** Composed of three quarks (e.g., protons and neutrons).
    *   **Mesons:** Composed of a quark and an antiquark pair.
4.  **Leptons** (Option C) are fundamental particles (like electrons and neutrinos) and are **not** made of quarks.

Since the question asks for the **general name** given to particles formed when two or more quarks combine, "Hadron" is the correct umbrella term that encompasses all such composite particles. While Baryons and Mesons are correct examples, "Hadron" is the most inclusive and precise general term for any particle made of quarks.
