### Answer
The correct answer is **B. Diffraction of light**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, you need to understand the fundamental difference between the historical models of light:

1.  **Huygens' Principle (Wave Model):** Christiaan Huygens proposed that light travels as a longitudinal wave. A hallmark of wave behavior is the ability to undergo **diffraction** (the spreading out of waves as they pass through an aperture or around an obstacle) and **interference**. The observation of light diffraction provided strong evidence that light behaves as a wave, which supported Huygens' model over the competing corpuscular (particle) theory proposed by Newton.

2.  **Evaluating the other options:**
    *   **A. Emission spectra:** These are explained by quantum mechanics and the movement of electrons between energy levels in atoms (Bohr model).
    *   **C. Black body radiation:** This was the problem that led Max Planck to propose the quantization of energy, which is a particle-like property of light.
    *   **D. The photoelectric effect:** Albert Einstein used this phenomenon to demonstrate that light can act as discrete packets of energy called "photons," providing evidence for the particle model of light (quantum theory).

Therefore, diffraction is the only phenomenon listed that is a direct consequence of wave mechanics, which is the core of Huygens' model.
