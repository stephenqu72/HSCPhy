### Part (a)
**Answer:** The paddle wheel will rotate faster. 

**Explanation:** Increasing the voltage between the cathode and anode increases the kinetic energy ($E_k$) given to the electrons (cathode rays) as they accelerate through the potential difference ($V$), according to $E_k = qV$. Since the velocity ($v$) of the particles is higher, they possess greater momentum ($p = mv$). When these faster-moving electrons strike the vanes of the paddle wheel, they transfer more kinetic energy to the wheel per unit time, resulting in a higher rotational speed.

### Part (b)
To answer this effectively for 4 marks, you must link each experimental setup to the specific property of cathode rays it demonstrates:

*   **Experiment W (Magnetic Field):** Demonstrates that cathode rays have a charge. When passed through a magnetic field, the beam is deflected (following the Lorentz force law, $F = qvB\sin\theta$), proving that cathode rays consist of charged particles.
*   **Experiment X (Metal Cross):** Demonstrates that cathode rays travel in straight lines. The opaque cross blocks the rays, casting a sharp shadow on the fluorescent screen at the end of the tube, confirming rectilinear propagation.
*   **Experiment Y (Electric Field):** Demonstrates the nature of the charge. The beam is deflected towards the positive plate, proving that cathode rays are negatively charged particles (later identified as electrons).
*   **Experiment Z (Paddle Wheel):** Demonstrates that cathode rays have mass and momentum. Because the paddle wheel moves when struck by the rays, it proves that cathode rays are not just waves of light but consist of particles with mass that can exert a mechanical force.
