### Answer

**1. Diffraction**
*   **Observation:** Light is observed to bend around corners or through small apertures, deviating from a strictly straight-line path.
*   **Explanation:** According to Huygens’ principle, every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary spherical wavelets. When light encounters an obstacle or aperture, these wavelets spread out into the "shadow" region. Particles traveling in straight lines cannot explain this bending, but wave propagation naturally accounts for the spreading of waves.

**2. Interference**
*   **Observation:** When two coherent light beams overlap, they produce a pattern of light and dark fringes (maxima and minima) on a screen.
*   **Explanation:** This is a phenomenon unique to waves, where the superposition of wave crests and troughs leads to constructive (reinforcement) and destructive (cancellation) interference. A particle model would predict that two beams of light simply add together to create a brighter patch, failing to explain the dark bands found in interference patterns.
