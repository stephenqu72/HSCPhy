To explain this observation, we must look at the **photoelectric effect**, a core concept in HSC Physics.

### Answer
The behaviour observed is due to the photoelectric effect. 
1. **Red Light:** Red light has a long wavelength and therefore a low frequency. Its energy per photon ($E = hf$) is lower than the work function ($\Phi$) of the zinc metal. Consequently, no electrons are ejected, the net negative charge remains, and the leaf stays extended.
2. **Ultraviolet (UV) Light:** UV light has a very high frequency. Its photon energy is greater than the work function of the zinc. This allows electrons (photoelectrons) to be ejected from the surface of the zinc plate. As the plate loses its negative charge, the excess electrons flow off the leaf and stem, neutralizing the electroscope, which causes the gold leaf to collapse.

### How to solve this
To secure full marks for a 4-mark response, structure your explanation logically:

*   **Identify the phenomenon:** Start by stating that the observations are evidence of the **photoelectric effect**, which demonstrates the particle nature of light.
*   **Define the threshold frequency:** Explain that for each metal, there exists a **threshold frequency** ($f_0$). Photoemission only occurs if the incident light frequency $f \ge f_0$.
*   **Explain the Red Light case:** Note that red light consists of photons with energy $E = hf$ that is less than the work function $\Phi$ of the zinc. Because the energy is insufficient to overcome the electrostatic binding energy of the electrons, no emission occurs.
*   **Explain the UV Light case:** Note that UV light has a high frequency, resulting in photons with energy $hf > \Phi$. The excess energy is converted into the kinetic energy of the emitted photoelectrons. The loss of electrons reduces the net negative charge on the zinc plate and the electroscope, causing the electrostatic repulsion between the stem and the leaf to vanish, so the leaf falls.
