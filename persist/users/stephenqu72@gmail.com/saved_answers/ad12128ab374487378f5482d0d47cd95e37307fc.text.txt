### (c) Reliability of 10 vs 20 rotations
The experiment using **20 rotations** is more reliable.

### (d) Derive an equation for radius
For mass $A$ to be in equilibrium, the tension provided by the weight of mass $B$ must equal the centripetal force required to keep mass $A$ in a circle.
1. The force of gravity on $B$ provides the centripetal force for $A$: $F_g = F_c \implies m_B g = \frac{m_A v^2}{r}$
2. Rearranging for $r$:
$$r = \frac{m_A v^2}{m_B g}$$
**Justification:** In uniform circular motion, the net force acting toward the center is the centripetal force. Since the mass $B$ is hanging stationary (balanced), its weight $m_B g$ acts as the tension force providing the centripetal acceleration $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$ to mass $A$.
