### Answer

*   **Bohr:** Proposed that electrons are discrete **particles** moving in fixed, circular orbits around the nucleus. He treated the electron as a classical object with a precise position and velocity, with specific energy levels defined by the principal quantum number, $n$.
*   **de Broglie:** Proposed that electrons exhibit **wave-particle duality**. He suggested that because electrons have mass and velocity, they also possess a wavelength, $\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$. This implies that electrons behave like standing waves confined to the orbits Bohr proposed, which explains why only specific energy levels are allowed.
*   **Schrödinger:** Proposed a **quantum mechanical model** where electrons are treated as three-dimensional probability clouds (orbitals) rather than fixed orbits. Using his wave equation, he described the electron as a wave function, $\psi$, where the square of the amplitude, $|\psi|^2$, represents the probability density of finding an electron at a specific point in space.
