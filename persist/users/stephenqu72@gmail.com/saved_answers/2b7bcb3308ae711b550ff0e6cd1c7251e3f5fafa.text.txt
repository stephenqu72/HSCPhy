To determine the direction of the magnetic field at point X, we use the **Right-Hand Grip Rule** for a solenoid.

### The Answer
The correct answer is **(A) left**.

### How to solve it
1.  **Understand the Right-Hand Grip Rule:** Place your right hand around the solenoid such that your fingers curl in the direction of the conventional current flow (from the positive terminal/bottom wire upwards into the coils).
2.  **Apply to the diagram:** 
    *   Looking at the first coil on the left, the current enters from the bottom and goes "up" and then "over" the top of the coil.
    *   Wrap your fingers in this direction: fingers pointing up at the front of the solenoid and curling over the top.
    *   Your extended thumb will point toward the **left**.
3.  **Analyze the Field Lines:** The magnetic field lines inside a solenoid point from the South pole to the North pole, and outside the solenoid, they loop back. 
    *   Since your thumb points left inside the coil, the North pole of the solenoid is on the left side.
    *   Magnetic field lines emerge from the North pole and loop around to enter the South pole.
    *   Point X is located near the right side of the solenoid (which is the South pole side). The field lines outside the coil move toward the South pole. Therefore, at point X, the magnetic field lines are directed toward the **left**.

### Summary of Physics Principle
The magnetic field direction $B$ for a solenoid is determined by the alignment of the current loops. By curling the fingers of the right hand in the direction of the current $I$, the thumb indicates the direction of the magnetic field vector inside the solenoid (toward the North pole). Because the magnetic field lines must form closed loops, the field lines outside the solenoid return toward the South pole, which is on the right, confirming that at point X, the field is directed to the left.
