### Front
Explain the "Cosmic Life Cycle" in the context of element synthesis.

### Back
- **Key idea:** The synthesis of elements progresses from light to heavy, driven by nuclear physics and validated by observational evidence (CMB, spectroscopy, gravitational waves).
- **Formula / law / constant / figure:** No fixed constant or figure.
- **When to use it:** To address "Discuss" or "Evaluate" essay questions regarding the origin of elements, linking Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, stellar evolution, and cataclysmic events.
- **Common trap:** Simply listing the fusion stages (e.g., "stars make carbon") without explaining the *interconnectedness*—specifically how nuclear physics constraints (binding energy) necessitate specific stellar life cycles and observational evidence.
