To solve this problem, we use **Wien’s Displacement Law**, which relates the absolute temperature of a blackbody to the wavelength at which it emits the most radiation.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the formula:** Wien's Displacement Law is given by:
    $$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$
    Where:
    *   $\lambda_{max}$ is the peak wavelength in meters ($m$).
    *   $b$ is Wien’s displacement constant, approximately $2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}\cdot\text{K}$.
    *   $T$ is the absolute temperature in Kelvin ($K$).

2.  **Convert temperature to Kelvin:**
    The given temperature is $37^\circ\text{C}$. To convert to Kelvin, add $273.15$:
    $$T = 37 + 273.15 = 310.15 \text{ K}$$

3.  **Perform the calculation:**
    Substitute the values into the formula:
    $$\lambda_{max} = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{310.15}$$
    $$\lambda_{max} \approx 9.344 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$$

4.  **Final Answer:**
    To express this in more common units, we can convert to micrometers ($\mu m$):
    $$9.344 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m} = 9.34 \text{ }\mu m$$

**Answer:** The peak wavelength of emitted radiation from the person is approximately $9.34 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$ (or $9.34 \text{ }\mu m$).
