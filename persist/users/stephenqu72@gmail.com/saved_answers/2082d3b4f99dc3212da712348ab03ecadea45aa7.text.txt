To answer this 6-mark HSC Physics question, you need to contrast the limitations of the classical wave theory with Einstein’s quantum model and explain the specific observations he successfully addressed.

### Answer
Einstein proposed that light consists of discrete packets of energy called photons, where the energy of each photon is given by $E = hf$. This quantum model resolved the contradictions of classical wave theory in the following ways:

1.  **Threshold Frequency:** Classical theory predicted that light of any frequency could eventually eject electrons given enough time. Einstein's model showed that a single photon must have enough energy to overcome the metal's work function ($\Phi$). If $hf < \Phi$, no emission occurs regardless of intensity, explaining why there is a threshold frequency.
2.  **Instantaneous Emission:** Because the interaction is a one-to-one collision between a photon and an electron, energy transfer is instantaneous. This explains why photoemission begins immediately upon illumination, rather than requiring time for an electron to accumulate energy from a wave.
3.  **Kinetic Energy and Frequency:** Einstein's photoelectric equation, $E_k = hf - \Phi$, shows that the maximum kinetic energy of ejected electrons depends linearly on the frequency of incident light. Classical theory incorrectly predicted that kinetic energy should depend on light intensity.
4.  **Intensity and Current:** Increasing the intensity of light (at a constant frequency) increases the *number* of photons per second, but not the energy per photon. Thus, higher intensity results in a higher number of ejected electrons (photo-current), but does not change the maximum kinetic energy of those electrons.
