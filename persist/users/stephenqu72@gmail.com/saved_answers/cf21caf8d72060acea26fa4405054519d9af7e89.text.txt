To determine the correct graph, we must look at the mathematical relationship between the gravitational field strength ($g$) and the distance ($r$) from the center of a planet.

### Explanation

According to Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation, the gravitational field strength at a distance $r$ from the center of a planet of mass $M$ is given by the formula:

$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$

Where:
- $G$ is the universal gravitational constant.
- $M$ is the mass of the planet.
- $r$ is the distance from the center of the planet.

From this equation, we can see that $g$ is inversely proportional to the square of the distance:
$$g \propto \frac{1}{r^2}$$

1.  **Inverse relationship:** As the distance $r$ increases, the gravitational field strength $g$ must decrease. This immediately eliminates graph C.
2.  **Non-linear (Inverse-Square):** Because the relationship involves $r^2$ in the denominator, the decrease is not linear (which eliminates graph D). 
3.  **Shape:** As $r$ gets very large, $g$ approaches zero, but never reaches it, creating a curve that flattens out as it moves along the x-axis. As $r$ gets smaller (approaching the surface), $g$ increases rapidly.

Graph **A** correctly depicts this inverse-square relationship: it starts high for small values of $r$ and curves downwards towards the x-axis as $r$ increases.

**Answer:** The correct graph is **A**.
