### Front
What are the two fundamental rules for sketching magnetic field lines between an $N$ and $S$ pole?

### Back
- Key idea: Field lines represent the direction of the magnetic force; they flow from North to South and indicate field strength via line density.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: "North to South" direction rule; lines originate at $N$, terminate at $S$.
- When to use it: Whenever drawing or interpreting magnetic field configurations or calculating force direction on a test compass.
- Common trap: Drawing lines that intersect or forgetting to include directional arrows on the curves.
