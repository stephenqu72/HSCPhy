To solve this problem, we must understand the fundamental relationship between the acceleration due to gravity and the gravitational field strength.

### Explanation

1.  **Definitions:** 
    *   The **gravitational field strength ($g$)** at any point in a gravitational field is defined as the gravitational force per unit mass exerted on an object at that point. Mathematically, it is given by:
        $$g = \frac{F}{m}$$
        where $F$ is the gravitational force and $m$ is the mass of the object. Its unit is $\text{N kg}^{-1}$.
    *   According to Newton's Second Law, the force acting on an object is $F = ma$, where $a$ is the acceleration of the object.
    *   When an object is in free fall, the only force acting on it is the gravitational force, so $F = mg$. 
    *   By substituting $F = ma$ into the definition of field strength, we get:
        $$g = \frac{ma}{m} = a$$
2.  **Concept:** The acceleration of an object in free fall at any location is exactly equal to the gravitational field strength at that location. The units $\text{m s}^{-2}$ (acceleration) and $\text{N kg}^{-1}$ (field strength) are equivalent, because $1 \text{ N} = 1 \text{ kg m s}^{-2}$, so $1 \text{ N kg}^{-1} = 1 \text{ m s}^{-2}$.
3.  **Calculation:** 
    *   Given acceleration $a = 25 \text{ m s}^{-2}$.
    *   Therefore, the gravitational field strength $g = 25 \text{ N kg}^{-1}$.
    *   The mass of the sphere ($5.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ kg}$) is irrelevant information (a "distractor") because gravity accelerates all objects at the same rate, regardless of their mass.
