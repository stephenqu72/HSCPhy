### Front
What evidence from the Standard Model proves that protons and neutrons are not fundamental particles?

### Back
- Key idea: Protons and neutrons are hadrons (baryons) composed of smaller sub-components called quarks.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Weak nuclear force interaction (e.g., $d \rightarrow u + W^-$).
- When to use it: When asked to justify why protons/neutrons are composite rather than elementary particles.
- Common trap: Confusing intrinsic properties (mass/charge) with structural evidence; beta decay is the proof of internal quark transformation.
