To answer this question effectively, you need to identify the key phenomena that provided evidence for the wave nature of light during the historical debate between corpuscular (particle) and wave models.

### Answer
Huygens’ wave model of light was supported by the following observations:

1.  **Reflection and Refraction:** Huygens successfully used his wave theory to explain the laws of reflection and the law of refraction (Snell's Law). He proposed that light waves spread out as wavefronts, and by applying the principle that every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary wavelets, he could derive how light paths change at boundaries.
2.  **Diffraction:** Light was observed to bend around corners or through small apertures. This is a characteristic property of waves (as seen in water waves), which the particle model of the time struggled to explain, as particles would be expected to travel in straight lines and create sharp shadows.
3.  **Interference:** Phenomena where light waves overlap to produce regions of constructive interference (bright fringes) and destructive interference (dark fringes) demonstrated the superposition of waves. This provided strong evidence that light behaves as a wave, as particles would simply collide and aggregate rather than cancel each other out.

*   **Use Terminology:** Ensure you mention "wavefronts," "superposition," or "secondary wavelets" to demonstrate a sophisticated understanding of Huygens' principle.
*   **Logical Flow:** Start with reflection/refraction as the foundational proof and then move to interference/diffraction as the more complex evidence that definitively separated the wave model from the particle model in the 19th century.
