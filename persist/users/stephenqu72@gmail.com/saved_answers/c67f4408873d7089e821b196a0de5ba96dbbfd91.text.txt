To find the current in the primary coil, we use the principles of an ideal transformer.

### Answer
The correct option is **(D) 4.2 mA**.

*(Note: Upon solving the calculation, the result is $4.166...$ mA. Rounding to two significant figures, we get 4.2 mA, matching option (D).)*

### Explanation
An ideal transformer is one where the input power equals the output power ($P_p = P_s$). Since $P = VI$, we have $V_p I_p = V_s I_s$. 

In an ideal transformer, the relationship between voltage, turns, and current is given by:
$$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s} = \frac{I_s}{I_p}$$

From the problem, we are given:
- The turns ratio $\frac{N_p}{N_s} = \frac{3}{5}$
- The secondary current $I_s = 2.5$ mA

Using the relationship between the turns ratio and the current ratio:
$$\frac{N_p}{N_s} = \frac{I_s}{I_p}$$

Substitute the known values into the equation:
$$\frac{3}{5} = \frac{2.5 \text{ mA}}{I_p}$$

To solve for $I_p$, rearrange the equation:
$$I_p = \frac{2.5 \text{ mA} \times 5}{3}$$
$$I_p = \frac{12.5}{3} \text{ mA}$$
$$I_p \approx 4.167 \text{ mA}$$

Rounding to two significant figures, we get **4.2 mA**, which corresponds to option **(D)**.
