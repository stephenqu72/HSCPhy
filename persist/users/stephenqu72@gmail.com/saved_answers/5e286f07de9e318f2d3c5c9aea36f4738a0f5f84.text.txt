To solve this problem, we will use the Bohr model for hydrogen atom energy transitions and the de Broglie wavelength formula.

---

### Part (a)

**1. Identify the formula:** The energy of a photon emitted during an electron transition is given by the Rydberg formula or by calculating the difference between energy levels:
$$E = E_i - E_f = -13.6 \left( \frac{1}{n_i^2} - \frac{1}{n_f^2} \right) \text{ eV}$$
For $n=4$ to $n=3$:
$$E = -13.6 \left( \frac{1}{4^2} - \frac{1}{3^2} \right) = -13.6 \left( \frac{1}{16} - \frac{1}{9} \right) = -13.6 \left( \frac{9-16}{144} \right) \approx 0.661 \text{ eV}$$

**2. Convert to Joules:**
$$E = 0.661 \times 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \approx 1.059 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

**3. Calculate Wavelength ($\lambda$):** Using $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$, where $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}$ and $c = 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$:
$$\lambda = \frac{hc}{E} = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34})(3 \times 10^8)}{1.059 \times 10^{-19}} \approx 1.88 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$$

**4. Conclusion:** Looking at the provided spectrum diagram, $1.88 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$ (or $1.88 \mu\text{m}$) falls within the **Infrared (IR)** waveband.

### Part (b)

**1. Identify the formula:** We use the de Broglie wavelength formula:
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{mv}$$
Where $m$ is the mass of an electron ($9.109 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$) and $\lambda = 1.87 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}$.

**2. Rearrange and solve for $v$:**
$$v = \frac{h}{m\lambda} = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{(9.109 \times 10^{-31})(1.87 \times 10^{-6})}$$
$$v \approx \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{1.703 \times 10^{-36}} \approx 389 \text{ m/s}$$

The electron would need to travel at approximately **$389 \text{ m/s}$**.
