To solve this problem, we must understand the fundamental properties of the Hertzsprung-Russell (H-R) diagram.

### Explanation
1.  **Understand the Axes:** In an H-R diagram, the **y-axis** represents **Luminosity** (or Absolute Magnitude) and the **x-axis** represents **Temperature** (decreasing from left to right).
2.  **Analyze the Trends:**
    *   **Arrow $Y$ (Main Sequence Trend):** This arrow points towards higher luminosity and higher temperature. Along the main sequence, stars with higher mass are hotter and more luminous. Therefore, $Y$ represents increasing **Mass**. Wait, let's look at the alternatives. Usually, moving down the main sequence towards the bottom right means stars have a longer **Life span** because they consume fuel more slowly. Thus, $Y$ corresponds to increasing **Life span**.
    *   **Arrow $X$:** This arrow points from the bottom right to the top left. Stars in the top left are hot and luminous. Moving along this path involves increasing stellar **Mass**.
    *   **Arrow $W$:** This arrow points towards the top left (high luminosity/temperature) and bottom right (low luminosity/temperature). Moving from the bottom right to the top left on the Main Sequence, stars become hotter. Thus, $W$ represents increasing **Temperature**.
    *   **Arrow $Z$:** This horizontal arrow indicates that as you move right (or left) along the diagram, the luminosity remains constant. This is the characteristic trait of changing stellar **Size** (radius) at a constant temperature/luminosity line (isoradius lines).

### Evaluating the Options:
*   We need to match the arrows to: Mass, Size, Temperature, and Life span.
*   **Arrow $Y$** points down the main sequence, which is the direction of increasing **Life span** (as stars get smaller, cooler, and less massive, they live much longer).
*   **Arrow $X$** points up the main sequence, the direction of increasing **Mass**.
*   **Arrow $W$** points toward higher temperatures (to the left), representing increasing **Temperature**.
*   **Arrow $Z$** (horizontal) follows lines of constant luminosity, which relates to **Size** (Radius).
