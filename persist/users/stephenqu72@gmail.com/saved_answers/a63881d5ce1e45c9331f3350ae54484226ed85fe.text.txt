To solve this problem, we apply the **Law of Conservation of Mechanical Energy**. Since there is no friction, the total mechanical energy (kinetic energy + gravitational potential energy) remains constant between point $A$ and point $B$.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Define the energy at points A and B:**
    Let $m$ be the mass of the carriage.
    *   At point $A$ (bottom of the loop), the height $h_A = 0$. The speed is $u$.
        $$E_A = \frac{1}{2}mu^2 + mg(0) = \frac{1}{2}mu^2$$
    *   At point $B$ (top of the loop), the height $h_B = 2r$ (the diameter of the circle). The speed is $v$.
        $$E_B = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + mg(2r) = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + 2mgr$$

2.  **Equate energies:**
    Because energy is conserved ($E_A = E_B$):
    $$\frac{1}{2}mu^2 = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + 2mgr$$
    Dividing by $m$ and multiplying by 2:
    $$u^2 = v^2 + 4gr$$
    $$u^2 - v^2 = 4gr$$

3.  **Use the algebraic identity:**
    The difference of two squares states that $u^2 - v^2 = (u - v)(u + v)$. Substituting this into our equation:
    $$(u - v)(u + v) = 4gr$$

4.  **Solve for the decrease in speed $(u - v)$:**
    The term $(u - v)$ represents the change (decrease) in speed between the bottom and the top of the loop:
    $$u - v = \frac{4gr}{u + v}$$
