To solve this problem, we apply the concept of **length contraction** from Einstein’s theory of Special Relativity.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the variables:**
    *   The rest length of the train ($L_0$) is $120\text{ m}$.
    *   The length of the platform ($L_p$) is $96\text{ m}$.
    *   An observer on the platform measures the train to have the same length as the platform. Therefore, the contracted length of the train ($L$) is $96\text{ m}$.

2.  **Recall the length contraction formula:**
    The relationship between the rest length ($L_0$) and the contracted length ($L$) observed by someone in a different reference frame is:
    $$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$

3.  **Substitute the known values:**
    $$96 = 120 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$

4.  **Solve for $v$:**
    *   Divide both sides by $120$:
        $$\frac{96}{120} = \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
        $$0.8 = \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
    *   Square both sides to remove the square root:
        $$0.64 = 1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}$$
    *   Rearrange the equation to solve for $\frac{v^2}{c^2}$:
        $$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 1 - 0.64$$
        $$\frac{v^2}{c^2} = 0.36$$
    *   Take the square root:
        $$\frac{v}{c} = \sqrt{0.36}$$
        $$\frac{v}{c} = 0.6$$
        $$v = 0.60c$$

**Conclusion:** The train must be traveling at $0.60c$. The correct option is **A**.
