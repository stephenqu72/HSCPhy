To answer this question, you must demonstrate an understanding of **Einstein’s Postulates of Special Relativity**, specifically the **Relativity of Simultaneity**.

### Answer
The discrepancy in observations is a fundamental consequence of the principle that the speed of light ($c$) is invariant for all inertial observers, regardless of the motion of the source or the observer.

1.  **For the scientist in the spaceship:** Because the source is equidistant from both clocks and the scientist is at rest relative to the entire apparatus, the light travels the same distance at the same speed ($c$). Therefore, the light reaches both clocks simultaneously.
2.  **For the observer on Earth:** The spaceship is moving at $0.8c$. From the Earth-based perspective, the back clock is moving *toward* the light pulse, while the front clock is moving *away* from it. Since the speed of light $c$ is constant, the light pulse travelling toward the rear clock has a shorter distance to cover to reach the detector, and the light pulse travelling toward the front clock has a longer distance to cover.
3.  **Conclusion:** Because the light travels different distances at the same speed $c$ to reach the two detectors in the Earth-based frame, the events are not simultaneous for the observer on Earth. The rear clock is activated before the front clock. This demonstrates that simultaneity is not absolute; it is relative to the observer's frame of reference.

### How to solve this
To secure full marks (4 marks) in an HSC Physics exam for this type of question, follow this structure:

*   **Mention the Postulate:** State clearly that the speed of light ($c$) is constant for all observers. This is the "engine" of your argument.
*   **Contrast the Frames:** Explicitly distinguish between the *proper frame* (the spaceship) and the *stationary frame* (Earth). 
*   **Explain the Geometry:** Use the concept of the moving clocks. Explain that from the Earth's frame, the "rear" clock moves to meet the incoming light beam, while the "front" clock moves away from its beam.
*   **Link back to Simultaneity:** Conclude that because the distance travelled by the light to each clock differs in the Earth frame, and the speed is constant, the time of arrival (the "event") must differ. This validates the concept of the **Relativity of Simultaneity**.
