### Answer
The correct answer is **(B)**.

### Explanation
To solve this question, we must apply the **Principle of Galilean Relativity**, which states that the laws of physics are identical in all **inertial frames of reference**.

1.  **Understand Inertial Frames:** An inertial frame of reference is one that is either at rest or moving with a constant velocity (uniform motion in a straight line). In such a frame, Newton's First Law (the law of inertia) holds true without the need for "fictitious" forces.
2.  **Compare the scenarios:**
    *   **At rest:** This is an inertial frame.
    *   **Uniform motion in a straight line:** This is also an inertial frame.
    *   **Accelerating:** This is a **non-inertial frame**. When an object is accelerating, an observer inside the frame will perceive "fictitious forces" (like the feeling of being pushed back when a train accelerates). These forces are not present when the train is at rest or moving at a constant velocity.
3.  **Analyze the options:**
    *   Because the train at rest and the train in uniform motion are both inertial frames, the results of mechanical experiments conducted in both will be identical. This invalidates options (C) and (D).
    *   Because the accelerating train is a non-inertial frame, the laws of physics appear to behave differently inside it compared to an inertial frame. Therefore, the results of experiments performed in an accelerating frame will differ from those performed in an inertial frame (at rest or in uniform motion). This makes **(B)** the correct choice. 
    *   Option (A) is incorrect because an accelerating frame is fundamentally different from an inertial frame (at rest), so results will not be identical.
