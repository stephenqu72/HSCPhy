### Answer
In Millikan's oil drop experiment, the charge of an electron was determined by creating a state of equilibrium for a charged oil drop suspended between two horizontal parallel plates.

1. **Gravity and Drag:** Initially, when the electric field is off, the oil drop falls under gravity ($F_g = mg$). It reaches a terminal velocity where the drag force ($F_d$) balances gravity, allowing the mass ($m$) of the drop to be calculated using Stokes' Law.
2. **The Electric Field:** When an electric field ($E$) is applied, an upward electric force ($F_e = qE$) acts on the charged drop.
3. **Equilibrium:** By adjusting the voltage across the plates, Millikan balanced the electric force with the gravitational force such that the drop remained stationary (or moved at a constant velocity). At this point of equilibrium:
   $$F_e = F_g$$
   $$qE = mg$$
4. **Calculating Charge:** Since $E = V/d$ (where $V$ is potential difference and $d$ is plate separation), the equation becomes:
   $$q = \frac{mgd}{V}$$
5. **Quantization:** By observing many drops, Millikan found that the values of $q$ were always integer multiples of a fundamental unit of charge ($q = ne$). This provided the definitive proof of the quantization of electric charge.
