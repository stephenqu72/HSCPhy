### Answer
The correct answer is **(a) The projectile launched at a smaller angle**.

### Explanation
To understand this, we must look at the physics of vertical motion in projectile motion. 

1.  **Vertical Velocity Component:** When a projectile is launched with an initial velocity $v$ at an angle $\theta$, the initial vertical component of the velocity is given by:
    $$v_y = v \sin(\theta)$$

2.  **Time to Maximum Height:** At the peak of its trajectory, the vertical velocity of the projectile is zero. Using the kinematic equation $v_f = v_i + at$, where $v_f = 0$, $v_i = v \sin(\theta)$, and $a = -g$ (acceleration due to gravity), we get:
    $$0 = v \sin(\theta) - gt$$
    Solving for $t$ (the time to reach maximum height):
    $$t = \frac{v \sin(\theta)}{g}$$

3.  **Comparing Angles:** Since $v$ and $g$ are constants for both projectiles, the time $t$ to reach maximum height depends directly on $\sin(\theta)$. 
    *   As $\theta$ increases (from $0^\circ$ to $90^\circ$), $\sin(\theta)$ increases.
    *   As $\theta$ decreases, $\sin(\theta)$ decreases.

Therefore, the projectile with the **smaller angle** will have a smaller initial vertical velocity component ($v \sin(\theta)$), which means it will reach its maximum height in a shorter amount of time.
