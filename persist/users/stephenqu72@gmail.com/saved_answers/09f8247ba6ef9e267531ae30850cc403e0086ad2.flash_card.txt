### Front
What is the quark composition and net charge of protons and neutrons?

### Back
- Key idea: Protons and neutrons are baryons (3 quarks) governed by the Standard Model.
- Formula: Proton ($uud$) = $+\frac{2}{3} + \frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = +1e$; Neutron ($udd$) = $+\frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = 0$.
- When to use it: Explaining subatomic particle structure or Beta decay processes.
- Common trap: Confusing the number of up vs. down quarks; remember that $uud$ is positive (proton) and $udd$ is neutral (neutron).
