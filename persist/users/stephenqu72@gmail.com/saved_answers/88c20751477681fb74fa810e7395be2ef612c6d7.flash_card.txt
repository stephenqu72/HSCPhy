### Front
Two objects of different masses exert gravitational forces on each other. What is the ratio of the force exerted by object A on object B to the force exerted by object B on object A?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational force is an interaction pair; forces are always equal in magnitude and opposite in direction.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s Third Law of Motion.
- When to use it: Any question comparing the mutual gravitational forces between two bodies.
- Common trap: Assuming the object with the larger mass exerts a larger gravitational force.
