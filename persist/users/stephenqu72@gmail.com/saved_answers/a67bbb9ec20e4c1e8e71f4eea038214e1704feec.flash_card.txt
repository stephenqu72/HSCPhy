### Front
How do you determine the direction of the magnetic field ($B$) around a straight current-carrying wire?

### Back
- Key idea: Use the Right-Hand Grip Rule.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Right-Hand Grip Rule: Thumb points in direction of conventional current ($I$); curled fingers show direction of $B$-field lines.
- When to use it: Whenever a diagram shows a straight wire carrying a current and requires the orientation of its circular magnetic field.
- Common trap: Forgetting to use the **right** hand (using the left hand reverses the field direction) or confusing conventional current with electron flow (electron flow is opposite to conventional current).
