### Front
Why do high-energy particle accelerators collide protons at relativistic speeds?

### Back
- Key idea: Kinetic energy of particles is converted into mass to create new, heavy particles.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $E = mc^2$ (Mass-energy equivalence)
- When to use it: To explain the purpose of experiments like the Large Hadron Collider (LHC).
- Common trap: Thinking the collision is intended to test the conservation of momentum or simply to overcome electromagnetic repulsion.
