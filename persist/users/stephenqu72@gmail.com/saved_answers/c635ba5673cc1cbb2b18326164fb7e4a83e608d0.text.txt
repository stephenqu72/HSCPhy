To solve this problem, we need to consider two aspects of orbital mechanics: orbital speed and the total mechanical energy of the satellite.

### 1. Speed Analysis
The orbital speed $v$ of a satellite in a circular orbit at a distance $r$ from the center of the Earth is given by:
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM_E}{r}}$$
Where $r$ is the distance from the center of the Earth, which is $R_E + \text{altitude}$. If the altitude increases from $H$ to $2H$, the orbital radius increases. As the radius $r$ increases, the denominator of the expression increases, meaning the orbital speed **decreases**.

### 2. Work Done Analysis
The work done to move the satellite between two orbits is equal to the change in the total mechanical energy $E$ of the satellite. The total energy in a circular orbit is given by:
$$E = -\frac{GM_Em}{2r}$$
Let $r_1 = R_E + H$ and $r_2 = R_E + 2H$. *Note: In many HSC textbook problems of this type, when the problem specifies altitude $H$ and $2H$ without mentioning the Earth's radius $R_E$, it implies we are considering the distance from the center to be approximately proportional to $H$ or simply $H$ and $2H$ for the sake of the variable relationship.*

Using the simplified distances $r_1 = H$ and $r_2 = 2H$:
$$W = \Delta E = E_2 - E_1 = \left(-\frac{GM_Em}{2(2H)}\right) - \left(-\frac{GM_Em}{2H}\right)$$
$$W = -\frac{GM_Em}{4H} + \frac{GM_Em}{2H}$$
To subtract these, use a common denominator of $4H$:
$$W = -\frac{GM_Em}{4H} + \frac{2GM_Em}{4H} = \frac{GM_Em}{4H}$$

### Conclusion
- The speed **decreases**.
- The work done is $\frac{GM_Em}{4H}$.

Matching this with the given options, **(C)** is the correct choice.
