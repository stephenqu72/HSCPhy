To determine which change will increase the ball's maximum height, we must look at the physics of projectile motion.

### The Physics Explanation
The maximum height ($H$) of a projectile depends solely on its initial vertical velocity component ($u_y$). Using the kinematic equation $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$, where the final vertical velocity $v_y = 0$ at the peak, $a = -g$, and $s = H$:

$$0 = u_y^2 - 2gH$$
$$H = \frac{u_y^2}{2g}$$

Since $u_y = u \sin(\theta)$ (where $u$ is the initial speed and $\theta$ is the angle of launch), the formula for maximum height becomes:
$$H = \frac{(u \sin \theta)^2}{2g}$$

### Evaluating the Options:
*   **A. Increasing the horizontal component:** This affects range but has no effect on the maximum height, as $H$ is independent of $u_x$.
*   **B. Decreasing the vertical component:** If $u_y$ decreases, $H$ will decrease.
*   **C. Increasing the angle of launch:** As the angle $\theta$ increases (up to $90^\circ$), the value of $\sin(\theta)$ increases. Since $H$ is proportional to $\sin^2(\theta)$, increasing the angle will increase the maximum height.
*   **D. Decreasing the angle of launch:** This would decrease the vertical component of the velocity, resulting in a lower maximum height.

Therefore, **Option C** is the correct answer.
