This question covers core HSC Physics concepts relating to circular motion, force, and experimental analysis.

### Answers and Explanations

**a. Why did the students take repeated time measurements?**
To calculate an average, which reduces the impact of random errors and increases the reliability of the data.

**b. What is the cause of this tension?**
The gravitational force (weight) acting on the hanging metal washers.

**c. Develop an equation between $Mg$ and the quantities listed.**
In this equilibrium state, the tension in the cord provides the centripetal force for the stopper: $T = F_c = \frac{4\pi^2 m R}{T^2}$. Since the tension is caused by the weight of the washers:
$$Mg = \frac{4\pi^2 m R}{T^2}$$

**d. Fill in the blank columns in Table 2.**
*Formulae:* $Mg = M \times 10$; $T = \frac{\text{Average time}}{20}$; $\frac{1}{T^2} = (\frac{1}{T})^2$.

**e. Graph**
Plot $Mg$ on the y-axis vs $1/T^2$ on the x-axis. The relationship is linear, so draw a straight line of best fit starting from near the origin. Ensure uncertainty bars of $\pm 0.1$ are marked horizontally on the $1/T^2$ points.

**f. Calculate the gradient**
Pick two points on your line of best fit $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$:
$$\text{Gradient} = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$$
Using the data points provided: $\text{Gradient} \approx \frac{5.4 - 3.0}{3.70 - 2.04} \approx \frac{2.4}{1.66} \approx 1.45 \text{ Ns}^2$.

**g. Show that $m \approx 50\text{ g}$**
From the equation in part (c), $Mg = (4\pi^2 m R) \times (\frac{1}{T^2})$. This matches the linear form $y = kx$, where $k$ (the gradient) $= 4\pi^2 m R$.
Given $R = 0.75\text{ m}$:
$$1.45 = 4 \times \pi^2 \times m \times 0.75$$
$$m = \frac{1.45}{3 \times \pi^2} \approx 0.049\text{ kg} = 49\text{ g} \approx 50\text{ g}$$
