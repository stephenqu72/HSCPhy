To find the magnetic field strength at a point equidistant between two parallel wires carrying current in the same direction, we use the right-hand grip rule and the formula for the magnetic field produced by a long straight wire:

$$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the setup:**
    *   Current in each wire ($I$) = $2\text{ A}$.
    *   Total distance between wires = $4\text{ cm} = 0.04\text{ m}$.
    *   Distance from each wire to the midpoint ($r$) = $\frac{0.04\text{ m}}{2} = 0.02\text{ m}$.
    *   Permeability of free space ($\mu_0$) = $4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$.

2.  **Determine the direction of the fields:**
    *   Imagine the wires are lying on your desk, both carrying current in the same direction (e.g., "up").
    *   Using the **right-hand grip rule**:
        *   For the left wire, the magnetic field at the midpoint points *into* the page.
        *   For the right wire, the magnetic field at the midpoint points *out of* the page.
    *   Because the currents are equal and the distances are equal, the magnitudes of the fields are identical, but their directions are opposite.

3.  **Calculate the net field:**
    *   Since the vectors point in exactly opposite directions, we subtract their magnitudes:
    $$B_{net} = B_1 - B_2$$
    *   Since $B_1 = B_2$:
    $$B_{net} = 0\text{ T}$$

### Conclusion:
The magnetic field from one wire cancels out the magnetic field from the other wire at the exact midpoint between them. Therefore, the total magnetic field strength is $0\text{ T}$.

The correct answer is **(A)**.
