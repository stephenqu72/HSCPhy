### Answer

The difference between a DC motor and a DC generator, in terms of the Law of Conservation of Energy, lies in the energy conversion process:

*   **DC Motor:** The device acts as a transducer that converts **electrical energy** (supplied by the battery/power source) into **mechanical kinetic energy** (motion of the vehicle). The current flowing through the coil in a magnetic field experiences a magnetic force (Lorentz force) that produces torque.
*   **DC Generator:** The device acts as a transducer that converts **mechanical kinetic energy** (provided by the vehicle's forward momentum) into **electrical energy**. As the coil is forced to rotate within the magnetic field, the changing magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) according to Faraday’s Law. This electrical energy can then be used to recharge the battery.

In both cases, energy is not created or destroyed; it is merely transformed from one form to another. In the "Brake" mode, the kinetic energy of the vehicle is depleted as it is converted into electrical energy, which results in a resistive force that slows the vehicle down.
