### Answer
The correct answer is **(B)**.

### Explanation
To solve for the magnitude of the uniform electric field ($E$) between two parallel plates, we use the formula:

$$E = \frac{V}{d}$$

Where:
*   $V$ is the potential difference between the plates (measured in Volts, V).
*   $d$ is the distance between the plates (measured in meters, m).

**Step 1: Identify the given values.**
*   Potential difference $V = 100\text{ V}$
*   Distance $d = 10\text{ cm}$

**Step 2: Convert units to SI base units.**
Physics formulas require SI units. Since the electric field is in $\text{V m}^{-1}$, we must convert the distance from centimeters to meters:
$$d = 10\text{ cm} = 0.1\text{ m} = 10^{-1}\text{ m}$$

**Step 3: Perform the calculation.**
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$E = \frac{100\text{ V}}{0.1\text{ m}}$$
$$E = 1000\text{ V m}^{-1}$$

**Step 4: Express in scientific notation.**
$$1000\text{ V m}^{-1} = 10^3\text{ V m}^{-1}$$

Comparing this to the provided options, it matches option **(B)**.
