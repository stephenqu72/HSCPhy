### Front
How do you calculate the gravitational force on an orbiting satellite given mass, radius, and period?

### Back
- Key idea: Gravitational force provides the necessary centripetal force ($F_g = F_c$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $F = \frac{4\pi^2 mr}{T^2}$
- When to use it: When solving orbital mechanics problems where velocity is not explicitly provided.
- Common trap: Failing to convert orbital period ($T$) from hours/minutes into seconds (SI units) before calculation.
