### Analysis of Contributions

1.  **Max Planck:** Planck's breakthrough came from his study of **black-body radiation**. He proposed that the energy of oscillators in the cavity walls of a black body is not continuous but is emitted or absorbed in discrete "quanta." His famous formula is $E = nhf$, where $n$ is an integer, $h$ is Planck's constant, and $f$ is frequency. This effectively means hot objects emit radiation in discrete amounts (quantized energy).

2.  **Albert Einstein:** Einstein extended Planck's idea to explain the **photoelectric effect**. He proposed that light itself consists of discrete packets of energy, which he later termed "photons." He argued that each photon carries an energy $E = hf$ and interacts with individual electrons in a metal, which explains why the energy of the emitted electrons depends on the frequency of the light rather than the intensity.
