### Front
Describe the shape and position of an acceleration-time graph for a projectile moving through the air (where 'up' is defined as positive).

### Back
- Key idea: Acceleration due to gravity is constant and acts in the opposite direction to the defined positive axis.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $a = -g \approx -9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$
- When to use it: To determine the motion profile of any projectile ignoring air resistance.
- Common trap: Assuming acceleration is zero at the peak of the flight (velocity is zero, but acceleration remains constant).
