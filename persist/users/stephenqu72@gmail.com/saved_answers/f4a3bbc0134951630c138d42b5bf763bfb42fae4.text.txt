To answer this question effectively for an HSC Physics exam, you must demonstrate a clear understanding of **Lenz's Law**, which states that the direction of an induced current is such that it creates a magnetic field that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it.

### Answer
Lenz's Law is a consequence of the **Law of Conservation of Energy**. When a magnet moves relative to a solenoid, the change in magnetic flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) and a current in the solenoid. This induced current creates its own magnetic field, which acts to oppose the motion of the magnet.

**Case: North pole moving towards a solenoid**
As a North pole approaches the solenoid, the magnetic flux through the coils increases. To oppose this increase, the solenoid induces a current that creates a North pole at the end of the solenoid closest to the magnet, resulting in a repulsive force.

**Labelled Diagram Description:**
*   **Magnet:** A bar magnet with the North ($N$) pole facing the solenoid. An arrow indicates movement towards the solenoid.
*   **Solenoid:** A coil of wire.
*   **Induced Polarity:** The face of the solenoid nearest the magnet is labelled $N$, and the far end is labelled $S$.
*   **Interaction:** A repulsive force vector is drawn between the $N$ pole of the magnet and the induced $N$ pole of the solenoid.

### How to solve this question
1.  **State Lenz's Law:** Start by defining the law clearly. It is the principle that the system "resists" the change.
2.  **Explain the cause:** Explicitly state that moving the magnet changes the magnetic flux ($\Phi$) through the solenoid coils, inducing an EMF ($\epsilon = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$).
3.  **Define the Polarity:** Explain that the induced magnetic field must oppose the motion. If the magnet is moving *towards* the coil, the coil mimics the magnet's pole to push it away. If moving *away*, the coil creates the opposite pole to attract it and pull it back.
4.  **Draw and Label:** A diagram is essential for the full 5 marks. Ensure you label:
    *   The poles of the permanent magnet.
    *   The direction of movement.
    *   The induced poles on the solenoid.
    *   The direction of the induced current (using the Right-Hand Grip Rule: if the North pole is induced, your thumb points towards the magnet, and your fingers curl in the direction of the current).
5.  **Relate to Energy:** Mentioning that this process requires work to be done, which is converted into electrical energy, demonstrates deep syllabus knowledge.
