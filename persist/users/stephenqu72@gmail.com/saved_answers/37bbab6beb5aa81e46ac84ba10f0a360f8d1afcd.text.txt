To derive the expression for the Schwarzschild radius, we must equate the escape velocity to the speed of light.

### Derivation

1.  **Conservation of Energy:** For an object of mass $m$ to escape the gravitational pull of a mass $M$ at a distance $r$, its total mechanical energy must be at least zero. The sum of its kinetic energy and gravitational potential energy is given by:
    $$\frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{GMm}{r} = 0$$

2.  **Escape Velocity:** Solving for the escape velocity ($v$), we get:
    $$\frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{GMm}{r}$$
    $$v^2 = \frac{2GM}{r}$$
    $$v = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

3.  **The Schwarzschild Condition:** By definition, at the event horizon (the Schwarzschild radius), the escape velocity is equal to the speed of light ($c$). Substituting $v = c$ and $r = r_s$:
    $$c = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r_s}}$$

4.  **Final Expression:** Squaring both sides and solving for $r_s$:
    $$c^2 = \frac{2GM}{r_s}$$
    $$r_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2}$$

### How to solve it
To solve this type of HSC Physics derivation, follow these steps:
*   **Identify the physics principle:** The problem is rooted in gravitational potential energy and kinetic energy.
*   **Set the boundary condition:** Recognize that the "event horizon" represents the limit where even light ($v=c$) cannot escape.
*   **Equate and Solve:** Set the kinetic energy expression equal to the magnitude of the gravitational potential energy, then isolate $r$ to find the Schwarzschild radius.
