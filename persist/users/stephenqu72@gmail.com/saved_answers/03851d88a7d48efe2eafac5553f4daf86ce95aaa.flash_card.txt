### Front
Distinguish between "validity" and "reliability" in the context of an HSC Physics first-hand investigation.

### Back
- Key idea: Validity refers to the fairness and accuracy of the experimental design (testing the hypothesis); Reliability refers to the consistency and repeatability of the data.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: No fixed constant or figure.
- When to use it: Use when evaluating experimental design, justifying methodology, or identifying sources of error in a depth study or practical task.
- Common trap: Confusing the two; remember **Validity = Fair test (controlled variables)**, **Reliability = Consistent results (multiple trials/replication)**.
