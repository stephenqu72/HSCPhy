To solve this problem, we use **Faraday's Law of Induction**, which states that the induced electromotive force (emf) $\varepsilon$ in a coil is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux through the coil:

$$\varepsilon = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$$

Since $\Phi = B \cdot A$ (where $B$ is the magnetic flux density and $A$ is the area of the coil), we can rewrite the equation as:
$$\varepsilon = -N \cdot A \cdot \frac{\Delta B}{\Delta t}$$

The question asks for the rate at which the magnetic flux changes, which is $\frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t}$. Let's rearrange the formula:
$$\left| \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t} \right| = \frac{|\varepsilon|}{N}$$

### Step-by-Step Calculation:

1. **Identify the given values:**
   - Induced emf ($\varepsilon$) = $28.5\text{ mV} = 28.5 \times 10^{-3}\text{ V}$
   - Number of turns ($N$) = $35$
   - Radius of the coil ($r$) = $50\text{ cm} = 0.5\text{ m}$ (Note: While the radius is provided, it is not required if we calculate the rate of change of flux directly, as $\Phi = BA$).

2. **Calculate the rate of change of magnetic flux:**
   $$\frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t} = \frac{\varepsilon}{N}$$
   $$\frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t} = \frac{28.5 \times 10^{-3}\text{ V}}{35}$$
   $$\frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t} = 8.1428... \times 10^{-4}\text{ Wb s}^{-1}$$
