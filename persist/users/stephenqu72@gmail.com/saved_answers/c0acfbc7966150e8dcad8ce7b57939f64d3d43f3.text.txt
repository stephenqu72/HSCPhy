#### Part (b)
**Answer:**
To minimize power loss ($P_{\text{loss}} = I^2 R$) during transmission, modern grids utilize the following strategies:

1.  **High Voltage Transmission:** By using step-up transformers to increase voltage, the current ($I$) is significantly decreased for a given power level ($P = VI$). Because power loss is proportional to the square of the current, reducing current leads to a massive reduction in heat loss in transmission lines.
2.  **Low Resistance Materials:** Transmission lines are made from materials with high electrical conductivity, such as aluminium (often reinforced with steel for mechanical strength), to keep the resistance ($R$) low.
3.  **Transformer Design:** To minimize energy loss within the transformer itself:
    *   **Laminated Cores:** The iron core is laminated with insulating material to reduce **eddy currents**, which would otherwise cause resistive heating.
    *   **Soft Iron Cores:** Using materials that are easily magnetized/demagnetized reduces hysteresis loss.
    *   **Cooling Systems:** Oil-filled tanks and cooling fins are used to dissipate heat generated by the remaining resistive losses in the copper windings.
