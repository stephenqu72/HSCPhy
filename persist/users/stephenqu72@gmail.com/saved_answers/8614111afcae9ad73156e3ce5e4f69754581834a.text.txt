To solve this problem, we apply **Snell's Law**, which states $n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2)$, where $n$ is the refractive index and $\theta$ is the angle relative to the normal.

### a. Angles of Refraction
Given $n_1 = 1.00$ (air), $\theta_1 = 30^\circ$, and $n_{prism}$ values for each color:

*   **Red:** $1.00 \times \sin(30^\circ) = 1.514 \times \sin(\theta_{r})$
    $$\sin(\theta_{r}) = \frac{0.5}{1.514} \approx 0.33025 \implies \theta_{r} \approx 19.28^\circ$$
*   **Blue:** $1.00 \times \sin(30^\circ) = 1.528 \times \sin(\theta_{b})$
    $$\sin(\theta_{b}) = \frac{0.5}{1.528} \approx 0.32723 \implies \theta_{b} \approx 19.11^\circ$$

**Red:** $19.28^\circ$, **Blue:** $19.11^\circ$

### b. Sketching to Surface B
To find the angle of incidence at Surface B, we use geometry. The ray enters Surface A and travels towards Surface B. In a $60^\circ-60^\circ-60^\circ$ prism, the angle between the rays and the normal at Surface B is determined by the apex angle. Since the ray bends *towards* the normal upon entering, and blue bends more than red, the blue ray will strike Surface B at a slightly larger angle relative to the normal than the red ray. Draw both rays inside the prism, with the blue ray deviating more from the original path than the red ray.

### c. Critical Angles
The critical angle $\theta_c$ is found using $\sin(\theta_c) = \frac{n_2}{n_1}$, where $n_2 = 1.00$ (air):

*   **Red:** $\sin(\theta_{c,r}) = \frac{1}{1.514} \approx 0.6605 \implies \theta_{c,r} \approx 41.34^\circ$
*   **Blue:** $\sin(\theta_{c,b}) = \frac{1}{1.528} \approx 0.6545 \implies \theta_{c,b} \approx 40.88^\circ$

**Red:** $41.34^\circ$, **Blue:** $40.88^\circ$
