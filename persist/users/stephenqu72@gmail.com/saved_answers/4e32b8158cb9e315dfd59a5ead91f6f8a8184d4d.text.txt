### Answer and Explanation

#### Part (a)
**Answer:**
The kinetic energy ($E_k$) gained by a particle with charge $q$ moving through a potential difference $V$ is given by $E_k = qV$.
For both the proton and the electron, the magnitude of the charge is the elementary charge, $e = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$.
$$E_k = (1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}) \times 400 \text{ V} = 6.408 \times 10^{-17} \text{ J}$$
Converting to electron-volts (eV): $400 \text{ eV}$.
Both the proton and the electron gain exactly **$6.41 \times 10^{-17} \text{ J}$** (or $400 \text{ eV}$) of kinetic energy.

**Explanation:** 
Since the work done by the electric field ($W = qV$) is converted entirely into kinetic energy for particles starting from rest, and both particles carry the same magnitude of charge, they gain the exact same amount of energy regardless of their mass.

#### Part (b)
**Answer:**
1.  **Calculate the Electric Field ($E$):**
    $$E = \frac{V}{d} = \frac{400 \text{ V}}{0.02 \text{ m}} = 20,000 \text{ V/m} \text{ (or N/C)}$$
2.  **Calculate Acceleration ($a$):**
    Using $F = ma$ and $F = qE$, we get $a = \frac{qE}{m}$.
    *   **Proton:** $m_p \approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
        $$a_p = \frac{(1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C})(20,000 \text{ N/C})}{1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}} \approx 1.92 \times 10^{12} \text{ m/s}^2$$
    *   **Electron:** $m_e \approx 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
        $$a_e = \frac{(1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C})(20,000 \text{ N/C})}{9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}} \approx 3.52 \times 10^{15} \text{ m/s}^2$$
3.  **Comparison:**
    $$\frac{a_e}{a_p} = \frac{m_p}{m_e} \approx \frac{1.67 \times 10^{-27}}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}} \approx 1833$$
The electron accelerates approximately **1833 times faster** than the proton due to its significantly smaller mass.

**Explanation:**
While the force applied to both particles is identical ($F = qE$), acceleration is inversely proportional to mass ($a = F/m$). Because the electron is $\approx 1833$ times lighter than the proton, its acceleration is proportionally greater by the same factor.
