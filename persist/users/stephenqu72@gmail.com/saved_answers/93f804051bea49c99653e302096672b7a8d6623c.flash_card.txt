### Front
How does an object's trajectory change when it has constant horizontal velocity and constant upward vertical acceleration?

### Back
- Key idea: The path is an upward-opening parabola resulting from the combination of linear horizontal motion and vertical acceleration ($y = \frac{1}{2}a_y t^2$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Newton’s Second Law ($F_{net} = ma$) and kinematic equations of motion.
- When to use it: To analyze projectile motion where net vertical force is non-zero and upward (e.g., a thrusting object).
- Common trap: Assuming the object follows a standard downward trajectory; if upward thrust $F_T > mg$, the vertical displacement is positive (upward).
