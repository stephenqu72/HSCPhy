To solve this problem, we apply **Kepler’s Third Law of Planetary Motion**, which states that the square of the orbital period ($T$) of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis (average orbital distance, $r$) of its orbit:

$$T^2 \propto r^3$$

Alternatively, for two planets orbiting the same star, we can write the ratio as:

$$\frac{T_A^2}{T_B^2} = \frac{r_A^3}{r_B^3}$$

### Step-by-Step Solution:

1.  **Identify the given information:**
    We are told that the orbital distance of planet A is six times that of planet B:
    $$r_A = 6r_B$$

2.  **Substitute the ratio into Kepler’s Third Law:**
    $$\frac{T_A^2}{T_B^2} = \left( \frac{r_A}{r_B} \right)^3$$
    Substituting $r_A = 6r_B$:
    $$\frac{T_A^2}{T_B^2} = (6)^3$$

3.  **Calculate the value:**
    $$6^3 = 6 \times 6 \times 6 = 216$$
    So, 
    $$\frac{T_A^2}{T_B^2} = 216$$
    Taking the square root of both sides to isolate the ratio of the periods:
    $$\frac{T_A}{T_B} = \sqrt{216}$$

4.  **Simplify the result:**
    $$\sqrt{216} = \sqrt{36 \times 6} = 6\sqrt{6} \approx 14.697$$
