The correct answer is **A. diffraction**.

### Explanation

To solve this, we need to analyze the behavior of the waves shown in the diagram:

1.  **Observation:** The diagram shows plane wavefronts (straight lines) approaching a barrier. As the waves reach the edge of the barrier, they do not simply continue in a straight line or bounce back. Instead, they "bend" around the corner of the barrier, resulting in circular (curved) wavefronts spreading into the region behind the barrier.
2.  **Definition of Diffraction:** Diffraction is the bending of waves around the corners of an obstacle or through an aperture into the region of geometrical shadow. The extent of this bending depends on the ratio of the wavelength ($\lambda$) to the size of the obstacle or opening. 
3.  **Evaluating the other options:**
    *   **Dispersion** refers to the separation of a wave into its constituent frequencies (like white light splitting into colors through a prism). This is not occurring here.
    *   **Refraction** is the change in direction of a wave due to a change in its speed as it passes from one medium to another (e.g., light moving from air into glass). The speed here is constant, and there is no change in medium.
    *   **Resonance** occurs when an object is forced to vibrate at its natural frequency, leading to an increase in amplitude. This is a property of oscillating systems, not wave propagation geometry.

Since the waves are bending around an edge, the phenomenon is clearly **diffraction**.
