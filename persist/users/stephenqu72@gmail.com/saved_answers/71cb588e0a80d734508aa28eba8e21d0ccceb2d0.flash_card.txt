### Front
How do you calculate the centripetal force acting on an object in uniform circular motion using angular velocity?

### Back
- Key idea: Centripetal force acts towards the center of the circle to maintain circular motion.
- Formula: $F_c = mr\omega^2$
- When to use it: When given the mass, radius, and angular velocity ($\omega$) of an object moving in a circle.
- Common trap: Forgetting to convert diameter to radius ($r = d/2$) or failing to square the angular velocity.
