To find the correct expression for the frequency of rotation of the conical pendulum, we must analyze the forces acting on the mass.

### Step-by-Step Derivation

1.  **Identify the forces:** The mass is subject to tension $T$ (acting along the string at angle $\theta$ to the vertical) and gravity $mg$ (acting downwards).
2.  **Resolve the forces:** 
    *   Vertical component: $T \cos \theta = mg$
    *   Horizontal component (providing centripetal force): $T \sin \theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
3.  **Relate to centripetal acceleration:** Dividing the horizontal equation by the vertical equation gives:
    $$\frac{T \sin \theta}{T \cos \theta} = \frac{\frac{mv^2}{r}}{mg}$$
    $$\tan \theta = \frac{v^2}{rg} \implies v^2 = rg \tan \theta$$
4.  **Incorporate frequency ($f$):** We know that for circular motion, the linear velocity is $v = 2\pi rf$. Substituting this into our expression for $v^2$:
    $$(2\pi rf)^2 = rg \tan \theta$$
    $$4\pi^2 r^2 f^2 = rg \tan \theta$$
5.  **Solve for $f$:**
    $$f^2 = \frac{rg \tan \theta}{4\pi^2 r^2} = \frac{g \tan \theta}{4\pi^2 r}$$
    Taking the square root of both sides:
    $$f = \sqrt{\frac{g \tan \theta}{4\pi^2 r}} = \frac{1}{2\pi} \sqrt{\frac{g \tan \theta}{r}} = \frac{\sqrt{g \tan \theta}}{2\pi \sqrt{r}}$$
