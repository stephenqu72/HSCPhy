### Explanation
To determine the correct answer, let's analyze the properties of projectile motion:

1.  **Acceleration:** When air resistance is ignored, the only force acting on the projectile is gravity. Gravity acts exclusively in the vertical direction. Therefore:
    *   Horizontal acceleration ($a_x$) = $0$
    *   Vertical acceleration ($a_y$) = $-g$ (approximately $-9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ downward)
    *   Because $g$ is constant, the acceleration is constant throughout the entire flight. This makes statement **D** correct. Statement **A** is incorrect because acceleration does not vary.

2.  **Velocity:**
    *   Horizontal velocity ($v_x$) remains constant throughout the flight because there is no horizontal force ($a_x = 0$).
    *   Vertical velocity ($v_y$) changes constantly due to the acceleration of gravity.
    *   Since $v_y$ changes, the total velocity also changes. Statement **B** is technically true because the vertical component varies; however, in physics multiple-choice questions, we look for the *most* definitive or descriptive statement. Statement **D** is a fundamental definition of projectile motion, whereas "vary" in B is somewhat ambiguous regarding the horizontal component.

3.  **At point $Y$ (the peak):**
    *   At the maximum height ($Y$), the vertical component of velocity ($v_y$) is $0$.
    *   However, the horizontal component of velocity ($v_x$) is **not** zero; the object is still moving forward. Therefore, the object does not "stop" (it would only stop if the entire velocity vector were zero). Statement **C** is incorrect.

**Conclusion:** Statement **D** is the most accurate description of the physics involved, as the acceleration due to gravity is a constant vector $g$ directed downwards at all points in the trajectory, including point $Y$.
