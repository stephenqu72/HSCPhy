### Part a) Explanation
**Answer:**
*   **Unpolarised light:** This is light where the electric field oscillations occur in all possible directions perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation.
*   **Polarised light:** This is light where the electric field oscillations are restricted to a single plane (or direction) perpendicular to the direction of propagation.

**How to solve it:**
To gain the 2 marks, you must define both terms clearly. Use the concept of the electric field vector. Think of unpolarised light as a "cone" of vibrations, while polarised light is a "single line" of vibration. A simple diagram showing random arrows for unpolarised and a single plane of arrows for polarised will guarantee full marks.

### Part b) Explanation
**Answer:**
Pilots often use Liquid Crystal Displays (LCDs) to view flight data. LCDs operate by emitting polarised light. If a pilot wears sunglasses with a polarising filter, the orientation of the filter might be perpendicular to the polarisation of the light coming from the LCD. This would cause the display to appear black or "blank out" due to Malus's Law, as the light intensity is reduced by $\cos^2(\theta)$. By not wearing polarising sunglasses, pilots ensure the flight data remains visible at all viewing angles.

**How to solve it:**
This is an application of wave theory. The key link is the connection between the **instrument panels (LCDs)** and **polarisation**. 
1. Identify that the LCDs produce polarised light.
2. Explain the interaction: If the sunglasses' filter axis is at $90^\circ$ (crossed) to the LCD's polarisation axis, the light is blocked.
3. Conclude that this would compromise safety, as the pilot would be unable to read critical flight instruments.
