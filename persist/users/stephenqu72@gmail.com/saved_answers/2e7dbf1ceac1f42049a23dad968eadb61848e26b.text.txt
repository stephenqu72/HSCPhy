To determine the age of the bone, we use the radioactive decay law, which relates the remaining amount of a substance to its initial amount and the number of half-lives that have elapsed.

### Answer
The age of the bone is **13,300 years** (to 3 significant figures).

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we use the following radioactive decay formula:
$$N(t) = N_0 \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{\frac{t}{T_{1/2}}}$$

Where:
*   $N(t) = 0.020\,g$ (the final amount of Carbon-14)
*   $N_0 = 0.10\,g$ (the initial amount of Carbon-14)
*   $T_{1/2} = 5730\,\text{years}$ (the half-life)
*   $t$ = the age of the bone we need to calculate.

**Step 1: Set up the equation**
$$0.020 = 0.10 \times \left( 0.5 \right)^{\frac{t}{5730}}$$

**Step 2: Isolate the exponential term**
Divide both sides by $0.10$:
$$\frac{0.020}{0.10} = 0.5^{\frac{t}{5730}}$$
$$0.2 = 0.5^{\frac{t}{5730}}$$

**Step 3: Solve for $t$ using logarithms**
Take the natural logarithm ($\ln$) of both sides:
$$\ln(0.2) = \ln\left( 0.5^{\frac{t}{5730}} \right)$$
Using the log power rule ($\ln(a^b) = b \ln(a)$):
$$\ln(0.2) = \frac{t}{5730} \times \ln(0.5)$$

Rearrange to solve for $t$:
$$t = \frac{\ln(0.2)}{\ln(0.5)} \times 5730$$

**Step 4: Calculate the final value**
Using a calculator:
$$t \approx 2.3219 \times 5730$$
$$t \approx 13304.5$$

Rounding to 3 significant figures, we get $13,300$ years.
