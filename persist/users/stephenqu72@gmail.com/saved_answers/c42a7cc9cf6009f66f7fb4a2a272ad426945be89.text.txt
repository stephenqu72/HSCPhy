### Explanation
In the context of the Scientific Method, it is essential to distinguish between a **hypothesis** and a **scientific theory**:

*   **A hypothesis (Option A):** This is a tentative, proposed explanation for a phenomenon that can be tested through experimentation or observation. The defining characteristic of a hypothesis is its **testability** (or falsifiability). It is the starting point of an investigation.
*   **A scientific theory (Option B):** This is an explanation that has been repeatedly tested, confirmed through observation and experimentation, and is accepted by the scientific community as a reliable account of the natural world. Therefore, a theory is a "well-tested explanation," not a hypothesis.
*   **Options C and D:** Science is based on empirical evidence, not the status of the individual proposing the idea (Option C) or mere popularity and belief (Option D). An idea does not become a scientific hypothesis simply because a famous person said it or because many people believe it to be true; it must be subject to rigorous testing.

When solving this type of question, always look for the keyword "testable," as it is the fundamental requirement for any scientific hypothesis.
