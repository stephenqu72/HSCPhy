### Front
What is the formula for the magnetic force ($F$) exerted between two long parallel current-carrying conductors of length ($L$) separated by a distance ($d$)?

### Back
- **Key idea:** The motor effect; the force arises because one wire sits in the magnetic field generated by the other.
- **Formula:** $F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 L}{2\pi d}$
- **Constants:** $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$
- **When to use it:** To calculate force between parallel wires or determine their separation distance.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to convert units to SI (e.g., cm to m, or mN to N) or confusing the "force per unit length" ($F/L = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi d}$) with the total force ($F$).
