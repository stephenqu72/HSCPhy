### Front
How do you calculate the energy released in a nuclear reaction like the fusion of two $^3_2\text{He}$ nuclei?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Energy released ($E$) is proportional to the mass defect ($\Delta m$) of the reaction.
- **Formula / law:** $E = \Delta m c^2$ or $E = \Delta m \times 931.5 \text{ MeV/u}$.
- **When to use it:** Whenever mass is converted into energy in nuclear reactions (fusion/fission).
- **Common trap:** Forgetting to account for the stoichiometric coefficients (e.g., multiplying the mass of the protons by 2) when calculating total reactant and product masses.
