To solve this problem, we need to calculate the magnetic force acting on the proton and then use Newton's Second Law ($F = ma$) to find the acceleration.

### 1. Identify the given values
*   Charge of a proton ($q$): $1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
*   Velocity ($v$): $1200 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
*   Magnetic field strength ($B$): $3.00 \text{ T}$
*   Mass of a proton ($m$): $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$

### 2. Calculate the Magnetic Force ($F$)
The formula for the magnetic force on a moving charge is:
$$F = qvB\sin(\theta)$$
Since the proton is fired perpendicularly into the magnetic field (coming out of the page), $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.
$$F = (1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times 1200 \times 3.00$$
$$F = 5.7672 \times 10^{-16} \text{ N}$$

### 3. Calculate the Acceleration ($a$)
Using Newton's Second Law, $a = \frac{F}{m}$:
$$a = \frac{5.7672 \times 10^{-16}}{1.67 \times 10^{-27}}$$
$$a \approx 3.4534 \times 10^{11} \text{ m s}^{-2}$$

### 4. Determine the direction
We use the **Right-Hand Rule** (for positive charges):
*   Point your fingers in the direction of the velocity ($v$): **Upwards** (towards the top of the page).
*   Curl your fingers in the direction of the magnetic field ($B$): **Out of the page** (towards you).
*   Your thumb points in the direction of the force ($F$): **To the right**.

Comparing our results to the options provided, the magnitude is $3.45 \times 10^{11} \text{ m s}^{-2}$ and the direction is to the right.

**Correct Option: C**
