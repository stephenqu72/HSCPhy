### Front
How do you calculate the remaining amount of a radioactive isotope after a specific time period?

### Back
- Key idea: Radioactive decay follows an exponential decrease based on the number of half-lives elapsed.
- Formula / law / constant: $N = N_0 \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n$, where $n = \frac{t}{t_{1/2}}$.
- When to use it: To determine the quantity or percentage of a radioactive substance remaining after time $t$.
- Common trap: Calculating the amount *remaining* but forgetting to subtract it from the initial amount (100% or $N_0$) if the question asks for the amount that has *decayed*.
