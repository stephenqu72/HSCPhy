Hello! As an HSC Physics teacher, I am happy to guide you through this problem. This question tests your understanding of **Circular Motion**, specifically the relationship between speed, radius, period, frequency, and centripetal force.

First, let's list our known variables:
*   Mass $m = 295\text{ kg}$
*   Radius $r = 24\text{ m}$
*   Tangential speed $v = 1.4\text{ km s}^{-1} = 1400\text{ m s}^{-1}$

---

### Part a: Time taken for one revolution (Period $T$)
The time taken to complete one full revolution is the period $T$. Since speed is distance over time, we use $v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$.
Rearranging for $T$:
$$T = \frac{2\pi r}{v}$$
$$T = \frac{2 \times \pi \times 24}{1400}$$
$$T \approx 0.1077\text{ s}$$

### Part b: Frequency in RPM
Frequency $f$ is the number of revolutions per second, given by $f = \frac{1}{T}$.
$$f = \frac{1}{0.1077} \approx 9.282\text{ Hz (revolutions per second)}$$
To convert revolutions per second to RPM (revolutions per minute), we multiply by 60:
$$\text{RPM} = f \times 60$$
$$\text{RPM} = 9.282 \times 60 \approx 556.9\text{ RPM}$$

### Part c: Centripetal force
The formula for centripetal force is $F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$.
Make sure to use standard units (meters and kilograms):
$$F_c = \frac{295 \times (1400)^2}{24}$$
$$F_c = \frac{295 \times 1,960,000}{24}$$
$$F_c = 24,091,666.67\text{ N}$$
The question asks for the answer in **MN (Meganewtons)**. $1\text{ MN} = 10^6\text{ N}$.
$$F_c \approx 24.09\text{ MN}$$

---

### Summary of Answers:
**a.** $0.108\text{ s}$ (or $0.11\text{ s}$ depending on sig figs)
**b.** $557\text{ RPM}$
**c.** $24.09\text{ MN}$
