### Answer
*   **Incandescent light bulbs** produce a **continuous spectrum**. Because they rely on thermal radiation (blackbody radiation), the atoms in the tungsten filament are in close proximity and undergo constant collisions. This causes a wide range of energy levels to overlap, resulting in the emission of light across all visible wavelengths, creating a smooth, uninterrupted rainbow of color.
*   **Discharge tubes** (such as neon or mercury lamps) produce an **emission (line) spectrum**. In these tubes, low-pressure gas atoms are excited by electrical discharge. Electrons jump to higher energy levels and emit photons of specific, discrete frequencies as they return to ground state. Because the energy levels in isolated gas atoms are quantized, the resulting spectrum consists of distinct, sharp lines of specific colors separated by dark regions.
