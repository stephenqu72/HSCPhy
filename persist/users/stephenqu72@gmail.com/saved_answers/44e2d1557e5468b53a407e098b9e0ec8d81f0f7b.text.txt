### Answer
The correct statement is **B. The lights turn on simultaneously for Bo.**

---

### Explanation
To solve this problem, we must apply the principles of Einstein’s **Special Relativity**, specifically the concept of the **Relativity of Simultaneity**.

1.  **Understand the frame of reference for Bo:** Bo is standing on the platform. At the instant the lights are emitted, Bo is equidistant from the two lamps (F and B). Because the speed of light $c$ is constant for all observers regardless of the motion of the source, and Bo is stationary relative to the points where the light is emitted, the light from both lamp F and lamp B must travel the exact same distance to reach Bo. Therefore, Bo observes the two flashes arriving at the same time.

2.  **Evaluate the options:**
    *   **Option A:** For Jo (inside the train), the lamps are at fixed positions in her frame. Since they are switched on simultaneously in the train's frame, they are simultaneous for Jo. While this is a true statement in relativity, the question asks what is correct **according to Bo** regarding the **perceived arrival times**.
    *   **Option B:** This is the correct interpretation of the situation from Bo's perspective. Because the events occur at positions equidistant to Bo and are observed at the same time, Bo concludes the events happened simultaneously.
    *   **Option C:** This is incorrect. In the train's frame, the events are simultaneous.
    *   **Option D:** This is incorrect. From Bo’s perspective, while the train is moving, the *emission* of the light is defined as occurring simultaneously for the platform observer who is equidistant to the points of emission. Once the light is emitted, the light from the back of the train (B) travels toward Bo while the back of the train moves *toward* that light's path, and the light from the front (F) travels toward Bo while the front of the train moves *away* from that light's path. However, because Bo was equidistant at the moment of emission, the light pulses reach Bo simultaneously.

**Key takeaway for HSC Physics:**
The Relativity of Simultaneity states that events that are simultaneous in one frame of reference are not necessarily simultaneous in another frame moving relative to the first. Because Bo is at the midpoint of the emission events in his frame, he measures them as occurring simultaneously.
