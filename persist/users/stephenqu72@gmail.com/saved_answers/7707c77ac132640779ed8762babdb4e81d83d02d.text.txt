To determine the change in electric field strength, we use the formula for the electric field $E$ between two parallel plates:

$$E = \frac{V}{d}$$

where:
- $V$ is the potential difference (voltage) between the plates.
- $d$ is the distance between the plates.

### Step-by-Step Explanation:

1.  **Identify the initial state:** Let the initial electric field be $E_1 = \frac{V}{d}$.
2.  **Apply the changes:**
    *   The voltage is doubled: $V_{new} = 2V$
    *   The distance is halved: $d_{new} = \frac{1}{2}d$
3.  **Calculate the new electric field ($E_2$):**
    $$E_2 = \frac{V_{new}}{d_{new}}$$
    $$E_2 = \frac{2V}{\frac{1}{2}d}$$
4.  **Simplify the expression:**
    Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal:
    $$E_2 = 2V \times \frac{2}{d} = 4 \times \frac{V}{d}$$
5.  **Conclusion:**
    Since $E_1 = \frac{V}{d}$, we can see that $E_2 = 4E_1$.

The electric field strength becomes four times greater. Therefore, the correct option is **D**.
