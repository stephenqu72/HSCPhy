### Answer
The correct answer is **B**.

### Explanation
Magnetic permeability, denoted by the Greek letter $\mu$, is a physical property of a material that describes how it responds to and interacts with a magnetic field. 

1.  **Conceptual definition:** Permeability is a measure of the ability of a material to support the formation of a magnetic field within itself. In other words, it represents the degree of magnetization that a material obtains in response to an applied magnetic field.
2.  **Mathematical relationship:** It is defined by the relationship between the magnetic flux density ($B$) and the magnetic field intensity ($H$):
    $$B = \mu H$$
    Because $B$ is the resulting magnetic field strength within the material and $H$ is the external magnetic field intensity, permeability determines how easily the material "permits" magnetic flux to pass through it.
3.  **Evaluating other options:**
    *   **A** refers to the force between current-carrying wires (Ampere's force law), which depends on permeability but is not the definition of permeability itself.
    *   **C** is incorrect because permeability relates to magnetic fields, not specifically the electric current flowing *through* the material.
    *   **D** is incorrect; there is no standard physical concept called "electromagnetic resistance" defined by permeability.
