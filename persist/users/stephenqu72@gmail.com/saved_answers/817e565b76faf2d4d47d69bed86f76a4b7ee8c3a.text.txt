The correct answer is **(A)**.

### Explanation

This diagram represents the **Geiger-Marsden experiment** (famously performed by Hans Geiger and Ernest Marsden under the direction of Ernest Rutherford). 

1.  **The Setup:** A radioactive source (Polonium, which emits alpha particles) is directed at a very thin sheet of gold foil. A fluorescent screen coated with zinc sulfide is placed around the foil to detect the path of the alpha particles (each impact creates a tiny flash of light).
2.  **The Observations:** Most alpha particles passed straight through the gold foil as expected by the "plum pudding" model of the atom. However, a small percentage were deflected at large angles, and some were even reflected directly back toward the source.
3.  **The Conclusion:** Rutherford reasoned that for such heavy, high-speed alpha particles to be deflected by an electrostatic force, they must be encountering a region of intense positive charge and concentrated mass. This led him to propose the **nuclear model of the atom**, where the vast majority of the atom's mass and its positive charge are concentrated in a tiny, dense central core called the **nucleus**.
