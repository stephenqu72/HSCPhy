### Front
Define magnetic permeability ($\mu$) and its physical significance.

### Back
- Key idea: A measure of a material's ability to support the formation of a magnetic field within itself when placed in an external magnetic field ($H$).
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $B = \mu H$
- When to use it: To determine the magnetic flux density ($B$) inside a specific medium compared to a vacuum.
- Common trap: Confusing $\mu$ (property of a specific medium) with $\mu_0$ (permeability of free space/vacuum).
