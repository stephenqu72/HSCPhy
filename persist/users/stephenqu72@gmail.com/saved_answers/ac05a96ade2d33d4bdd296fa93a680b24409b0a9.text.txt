To determine which particle is an electron, we must apply the physics of charged particles moving through a magnetic field.

### Analysis
1.  **Nature of the particle:** An electron is a **negatively charged** particle.
2.  **Magnetic force:** When a charged particle enters a magnetic field, it experiences a magnetic force given by the Lorentz force law: $\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$.
3.  **Right-Hand Rule:** 
    *   Point your fingers in the direction of velocity (Right).
    *   Point your palm in the direction of the magnetic field (into the page).
    *   For a **positive** charge, the thumb points in the direction of the force (Up).
    *   For a **negative** charge (like an electron), the force is in the opposite direction (Down).
4.  **Trajectory:** Because a magnetic force acts perpendicular to the velocity, it causes the particle to undergo circular motion (Curved path). Straight paths indicate neutral particles (no charge).

Applying the rule:
*   The velocity is to the right.
*   The magnetic field is into the page.
*   The force on a **positive** charge would be **Up**.
*   The force on an **electron (negative charge)** must be **Down**.
*   Since an electron is charged, it will be deflected, meaning its path will be **Curved**.

Comparing this to the table, **R1** is the only result that shows a "Down" deflection with a "Curved" path.

**Therefore, the correct answer is A.**

### How to solve it
*   **Step 1:** Identify the physical properties of an electron (negative charge, non-zero mass).
*   **Step 2:** Use the Right-Hand Rule to determine the direction of force for a positive charge moving right into a field directed into the page. You will find this force is upward.
*   **Step 3:** Since an electron is negative, reverse the direction of the force. The force on the electron must be downward.
*   **Step 4:** Recognize that a moving charged particle in a magnetic field experiences a constant force perpendicular to its velocity, resulting in a curved path.
*   **Step 5:** Match these characteristics (Downward, Curved) to the given table.
