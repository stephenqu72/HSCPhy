### Front
How do the kinetic and gravitational potential energy of an orbiting satellite change as it moves closer to Earth?

### Back
- Key idea: As $r$ decreases, potential energy ($U$) decreases (becomes more negative) and kinetic energy ($K$) increases (velocity increases).
- Formula / law: $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$ and Total Energy $E = K + U = \text{constant}$.
- When to use it: Any orbit problem involving changing orbital radius or eccentricity.
- Common trap: Thinking potential energy increases because the magnitude of $U$ grows; remember that becoming "more negative" is a decrease in value.
