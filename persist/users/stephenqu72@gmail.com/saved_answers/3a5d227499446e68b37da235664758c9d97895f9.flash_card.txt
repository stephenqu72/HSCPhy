### Front
What is the condition for "weightlessness" in a circular path at the top of a loop, and what formula defines the minimum speed required?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Weightlessness occurs when the normal force ($N$) is zero; the object is in free-fall as centripetal acceleration equals $g$.
- **Formula:** $v = \sqrt{gr}$
- **When to use it:** When calculating the minimum speed at the top of a vertical loop to prevent the object from falling off or to experience apparent weightlessness.
- **Common trap:** Confusing "weightlessness" with $g=0$. Gravity is still acting; it is the *reaction force* (normal force) that becomes zero.
