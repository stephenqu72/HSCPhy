### Front
How do you calculate the minimum escape velocity ($v$) of an electron (charge $e$) from a proton (charge $e$) at a distance $r$?

### Back
- **Key idea:** Conservation of energy; the electron escapes when its initial kinetic energy equals the magnitude of its electrostatic potential energy ($K + U_E = 0$).
- **Formula:** $v = \sqrt{\frac{2ke^2}{mr}}$, where $k = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}$.
- **When to use it:** To determine if a charged particle has sufficient energy to overcome a potential well.
- **Common trap:** Forgetting that an electron cannot physically achieve $v \ge c$ (speed of light); even if the math suggests a value, Special Relativity limits the particle's motion.
