### Front
How do you determine the polarity of an electric charge from a field line diagram?

### Back
- Key idea: Field lines originate from positive charges and terminate on negative charges.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: Arrows pointing *away* = positive (+); arrows pointing *into* = negative (−).
- When to use it: Whenever identifying charge types or plotting electric field configurations between point charges.
- Common trap: Confusing the direction of the force on a test charge (which follows the field lines) with the field direction itself; always remember the "test charge" is defined as a *positive* unit charge.
