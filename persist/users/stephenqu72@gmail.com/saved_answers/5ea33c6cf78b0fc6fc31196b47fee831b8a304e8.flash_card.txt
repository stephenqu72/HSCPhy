### Front
Two identical masses fall to a planet from the same initial distance; one starts from rest ($m_2$) and one has an initial horizontal velocity ($m_1$). Which has a higher impact speed?

### Back
- Key idea: Total mechanical energy ($E = U + K$) is conserved; since $m_1$ begins with higher initial total energy, it must possess higher total kinetic energy upon impact.
- Formula / law: $E_{total} = -\frac{GMm}{r} + \frac{1}{2}mv^2$
- When to use it: Comparing speeds or energies of objects moving within a gravitational field where initial states differ.
- Common trap: Assuming that having an initial horizontal velocity only contributes to the final velocity vector's direction, rather than increasing the object's total scalar speed.
