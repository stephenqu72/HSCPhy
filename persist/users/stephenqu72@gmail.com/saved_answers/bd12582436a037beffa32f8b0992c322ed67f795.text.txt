To solve this problem, we use the transformer turns ratio formula, which relates the voltage and the number of turns in the primary and secondary coils:

$$\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}$$

### Given data:
*   Primary voltage ($V_p$) = $240\text{ V}$
*   Secondary voltage ($V_s$) = $12\text{ V}$
*   Primary turns ($N_p$) = $300$
*   Input current = $10\text{ A}$ (Note: This is "distractor" information. It is not needed to find the number of turns, as the turns ratio depends only on the voltage ratio.)

### Step-by-step solution:
1.  **Rearrange the formula** to solve for the secondary turns ($N_s$):
    $$N_s = N_p \times \frac{V_s}{V_p}$$

2.  **Substitute the known values** into the equation:
    $$N_s = 300 \times \frac{12}{240}$$

3.  **Simplify the calculation**:
    $$\frac{12}{240} = \frac{1}{20}$$
    $$N_s = 300 \times \frac{1}{20}$$
    $$N_s = 15$$

**Conclusion:**
There are 15 turns in the secondary coil. The correct option is **(a)**.
