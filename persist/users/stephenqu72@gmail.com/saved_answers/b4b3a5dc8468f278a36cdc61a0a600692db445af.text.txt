To solve this problem, we use the formula for the magnetic force $F$ exerted on a moving charged particle:

$$F = qvB\sin(\theta)$$

### Given Data:
*   **Charge of a proton ($q$):** $1.602 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
*   **Speed of the proton ($v$):** Half the speed of light, where $c = 3.0 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$.
    $$v = 0.5 \times 3.0 \times 10^8\text{ m/s} = 1.5 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$$
*   **Magnetic field strength ($B$):** $2.0 \times 10^{-2}\text{ T}$
*   **Angle ($\theta$):** $7.5^\circ$

### Calculation:
Plug the values into the force equation:

$$F = (1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times (1.5 \times 10^8) \times (2.0 \times 10^{-2}) \times \sin(7.5^\circ)$$

1.  Calculate the product of the constants:
    $$(1.602 \times 10^{-19}) \times (1.5 \times 10^8) \times (2.0 \times 10^{-2}) = 4.806 \times 10^{-13}$$
2.  Calculate $\sin(7.5^\circ)$:
    $$\sin(7.5^\circ) \approx 0.130526$$
3.  Calculate the final force:
    $$F = 4.806 \times 10^{-13} \times 0.130526 \approx 6.273 \times 10^{-14}\text{ N}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, it matches **(B)**.

***Note on Physics Concepts:*** 
While the proton is traveling at relativistic speeds ($0.5c$), the formula for magnetic force $F = qvB\sin\theta$ remains valid for the instantaneous force. We do not need to account for relativistic mass here as the formula defines force in terms of the instantaneous velocity and charge.
