### Answer
The correct answer is **(D)**.

### Explanation
1.  **Understanding Length Contraction:** According to Einstein's theory of special relativity, an object moving at relativistic speeds relative to an observer will appear to be shortened in the direction of its motion. The formula for this contraction is:
    $$L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$
    where $L_0$ is the proper length (the length measured in the object's rest frame), $v$ is the velocity of the object, and $c$ is the speed of light.

2.  **Direction of Contraction:** The key principle here is that length contraction **only occurs parallel to the direction of motion**. Dimensions perpendicular to the direction of motion remain unaffected.

3.  **Analyzing the Geometry:** 
    *   The ball is moving at an angle of $45^\circ$ relative to the horizontal.
    *   Because the velocity vector is directed at this $45^\circ$ angle, the dimension of the ball along this path will be compressed.
    *   Option (A) shows contraction perpendicular to the path of motion, which is physically incorrect.
    *   Option (B) and (C) show no contraction, which would only be true if the ball were stationary or moving at non-relativistic speeds.
    *   Option (D) shows the ball flattened along the axis of its trajectory. Since the ball is moving at $45^\circ$ relative to the horizontal, the contraction occurs along that specific $45^\circ$ line, resulting in the flattened shape seen in (D).

By identifying that contraction happens strictly in the line of travel, we can visually conclude that the spherical shape must appear as an ellipse elongated perpendicular to the direction of motion and flattened along the path of motion.
