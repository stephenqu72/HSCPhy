### Front
How do you determine the half-life ($t_{1/2}$) of a radioisotope from an "Amount vs. Time" decay graph?

### Back
- Key idea: Half-life is the time required for the quantity of a radioactive isotope to decrease to 50% of its initial value.
- Formula / law / constant / figure: $N(t) = N_0(1/2)^{t/t_{1/2}}$
- When to use it: Whenever provided with an exponential decay curve of activity or count rate over time.
- Common trap: Failing to convert units (e.g., $\mu\text{s}$ to $\text{s}$) or confusing "half-life" with the time required to reach 0% (which is theoretically infinite).
