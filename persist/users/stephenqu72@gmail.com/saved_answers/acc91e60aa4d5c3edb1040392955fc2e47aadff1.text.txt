To calculate the gravitational potential energy of a satellite orbiting Earth, we use the formula:

$$U = -\frac{G M m}{r}$$

### 1. Identify the given values and physical constants:
*   Mass of the satellite ($m$): $6.50 \times 10^3 \text{ kg}$
*   Altitude of the satellite ($h$): $2.00 \times 10^3 \text{ km} = 2.00 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
*   Mass of the Earth ($M$): $5.97 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}$ (standard HSC reference value)
*   Radius of the Earth ($R_E$): $6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$ (standard HSC reference value)
*   Universal Gravitational Constant ($G$): $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

### 2. Determine the orbital radius ($r$):
The orbital radius $r$ is the sum of the Earth's radius and the altitude of the satellite:
$$r = R_E + h = (6.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}) + (2.00 \times 10^6 \text{ m}) = 8.37 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$$

### 3. Calculate the potential energy ($U$):
Substitute the values into the formula:
$$U = -\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24}) \times (6.50 \times 10^3)}{8.37 \times 10^6}$$

Calculating the numerator:
$$(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (5.97 \times 10^{24}) \times (6.50 \times 10^3) \approx 2.587 \times 10^{18}$$

Now divide by the radius:
$$U \approx -\frac{2.587 \times 10^{18}}{8.37 \times 10^6} \approx -3.09 \times 10^{11} \text{ J}$$

Comparing this result to the given options, $-3.09 \times 10^{11} \text{ J}$ is closest to $-3.11 \times 10^{11} \text{ J}$.

**Correct Option: (A)**
